Stachys appalachiana

Stachys appalachiana, commonly known as Appalachian betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
