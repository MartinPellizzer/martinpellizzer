Singaporandia macrophylla

Singaporandia macrophylla, commonly known as the Singapore flame tree, is a striking tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has broad, palmate leaves that are typically green with a glossy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is also used in reforestation projects in its native habitat.
