Sidalcea malviflora var. uliginosa

Sidalcea malviflora var. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This variety is adapted to wet conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
