Sorbus sativa

Sorbus sativa, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus sativa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive autumn foliage.
