Sorbus × tuzsoniana var. hemiptera

Sorbus × tuzsoniana var. hemiptera, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange to red berries. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
