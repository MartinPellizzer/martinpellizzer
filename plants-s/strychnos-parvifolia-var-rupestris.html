Strychnos parvifolia var. rupestris

Strychnos parvifolia var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock strychnos, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
