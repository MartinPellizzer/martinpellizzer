Selenicereus grandiflorus var. affinis

Selenicereus grandiflorus var. affinis, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant is notable for its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a strong fragrance. These flowers typically bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by moths and bats.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
