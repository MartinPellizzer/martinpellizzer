Sedum tibeticum var. stracheyi

Sedum tibeticum var. stracheyi, commonly known as the Tibetan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid and cold environments. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices and alpine meadows, where it is exposed to intense sunlight and low temperatures. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
