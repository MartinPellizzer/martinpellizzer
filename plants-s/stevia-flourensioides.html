Stevia flourensioides

Stevia flourensioides, commonly known as the golden stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a natural sweetener alternative.
