Sagina apetala var. valdiviana

Sagina apetala var. valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features small, succulent leaves that are densely packed, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are inconspicuous, usually white or pale green, and are clustered in small umbels.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to cool, humid climates and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
