Solanum bustillosii

Solanum bustillosii, commonly known as the Bustillos's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with dark green, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep purple color, containing several seeds.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
