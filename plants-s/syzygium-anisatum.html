Syzygium anisatum

Syzygium anisatum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or *Pterocarpus* species, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and capsule fruits containing winged seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Syzygium anisatum is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
