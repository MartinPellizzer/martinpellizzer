Schkuhria degenerica

Schkuhria degenerica, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Schkuhria degenerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
