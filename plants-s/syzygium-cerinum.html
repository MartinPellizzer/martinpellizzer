Syzygium cerinum

Syzygium cerinum, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellowish fruits resembling small silver coins. Syzygium cerinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
