Saccolabium blumei var. majus

Saccolabium blumei var. majus, commonly known as the showy saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
