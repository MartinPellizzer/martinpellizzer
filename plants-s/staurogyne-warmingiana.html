Staurogyne warmingiana

Staurogyne warmingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian water starwort, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface. Its small, green leaves are arranged opposite each other along the stems.

Staurogyne warmingiana is known for its ability to provide habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
