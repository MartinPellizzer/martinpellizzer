Saussurea lanuginosa var. glabrescens

Saussurea lanuginosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly saffron.

It is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silky layer of hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against harsh weather.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
