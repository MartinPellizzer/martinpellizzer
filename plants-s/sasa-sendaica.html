Sasa sendaica

Sasa sendaica, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly sweet aroma.

Sasa sendaica thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.
