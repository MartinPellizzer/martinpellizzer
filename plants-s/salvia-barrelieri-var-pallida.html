Salvia barrelieri var. pallida

Salvia barrelieri var. pallida, commonly known as the pale salvia or white salvia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a minty scent.

This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
