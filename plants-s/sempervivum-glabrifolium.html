Sempervivum glabrifolium

Sempervivum glabrifolium, commonly known as the stonecrop or hens and chicks, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sempervivum glabrifolium is also known for its ability to propagate easily through offsets, which contribute to its hardy and long-lived nature.
