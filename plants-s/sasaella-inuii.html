Sasaella inuii

Sasaella inuii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Sasaella inuii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
