Sarcostemma viminale subsp. orangeanum

Sarcostemma viminale subsp.

orangeanum, commonly known as the Orangeanum moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually pubescent beneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental berries and is often used in gardens and as a hedging plant.
