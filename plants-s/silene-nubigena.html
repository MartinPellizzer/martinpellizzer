Silene nubigena

Silene nubigena, commonly known as the cloudland catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene nubigena is known for its adaptability to high-altitude conditions and is often found in open, sunny areas.
