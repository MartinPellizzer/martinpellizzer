Shorea sericeiflora

Shorea sericeiflora, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, smooth, and often whitish bark, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is known for its hard, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is an important component of the forest ecosystem.
