Senecio monticola

Senecio monticola, commonly known as the mountain senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio monticola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
