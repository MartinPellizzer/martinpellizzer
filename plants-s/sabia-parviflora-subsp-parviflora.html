Sabia parviflora subsp. parviflora

Sabia parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sabia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
