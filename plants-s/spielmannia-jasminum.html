Spielmannia jasminum

Spielmannia jasminum, commonly known as the jasmine wood or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet scent at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
