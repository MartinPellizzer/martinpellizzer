Sorbus erubescens

Sorbus erubescens, commonly known as the red mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are a food source for various birds and wildlife.
