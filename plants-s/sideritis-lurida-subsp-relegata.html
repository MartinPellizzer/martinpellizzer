Sideritis lurida subsp. relegata

Sideritis lurida subsp.

relegata, commonly known as the Balkan ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and it blooms during the summer months.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine, and this subspecies is no exception, valued for its potential therapeutic properties.
