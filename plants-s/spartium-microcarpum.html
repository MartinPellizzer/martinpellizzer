Spartium microcarpum

Spartium microcarpum, commonly known as lesser sparrowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by narrow, bean-like pods containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Spartium microcarpum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
