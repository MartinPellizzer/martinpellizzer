Sabicea stipularioides

Sabicea stipularioides, commonly known as the stipular leaf vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules that are often mistaken for small leaves, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in racemes, and are usually white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
