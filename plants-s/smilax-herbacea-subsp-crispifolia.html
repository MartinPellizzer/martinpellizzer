Smilax herbacea subsp. crispifolia

Smilax herbacea subsp.

crispifolia, commonly known as the curly-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems that are covered in persistent, spiny bracts.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
