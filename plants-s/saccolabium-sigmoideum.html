Saccolabium sigmoideum

Saccolabium sigmoideum, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Saccolabium sigmoideum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
