Saussurea shonaiensis

Saussurea shonaiensis, commonly known as the Shonai swamp safflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in marshy or boggy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, and it is often found in the northern regions of Japan.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
