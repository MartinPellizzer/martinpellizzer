Sabicea hirsuta var. adpressa

Sabicea hirsuta var. adpressa, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
