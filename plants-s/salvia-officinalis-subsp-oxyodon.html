Salvia officinalis subsp. oxyodon

Salvia officinalis subsp.

oxyodon, commonly known as lemon sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is known for its strong lemony scent, which comes from the essential oils present in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

This subspecies is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
