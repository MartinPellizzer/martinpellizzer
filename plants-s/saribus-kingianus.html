Saribus kingianus

Saribus kingianus, commonly known as the King Island palm, is a flowering plant native to the island of King Island in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are a significant food source for local wildlife. Saribus kingianus is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in sandy soils with good drainage.

Its ecological importance makes it a key species in the conservation efforts of its native habitat.
