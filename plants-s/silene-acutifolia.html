Silene acutifolia

Silene acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene acutifolia is an annual or biennial plant, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
