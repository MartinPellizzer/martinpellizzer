Sempervivum canariense subsp. longithyrsum

Sempervivum canariense subsp.

longithyrsum, commonly known as the Canary Island houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are typically covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces tall, branched flower stems bearing clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
