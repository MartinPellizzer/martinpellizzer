Shiia sieboldii var. serratifolia

Shiia sieboldii var. serratifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive white bark that peels in papery strips. The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn bright yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in both ornamental horticulture and ecological landscapes.
