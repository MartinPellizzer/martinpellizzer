Statice flexuosa

Statice flexuosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

Statice flexuosa is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
