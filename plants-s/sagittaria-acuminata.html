Sagittaria acuminata

Sagittaria acuminata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that help it float and photosynthesize efficiently in aquatic environments. Its small white flowers appear above the water surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sagittaria acuminata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
