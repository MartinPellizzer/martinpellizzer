Scrophularia neesii

Scrophularia neesii, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments at high elevations.

Scrophularia neesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
