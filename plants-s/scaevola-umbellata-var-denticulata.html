Scaevola umbellata var. denticulata

Scaevola umbellata var. denticulata, commonly known as the Coast Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a small umbrella, hence its common name. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid environments.
