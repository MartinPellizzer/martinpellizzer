Sobralia gracilis

Sobralia gracilis, commonly known as the slender sobralia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sobralia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
