Swertia radiata var. macrophylla

Swertia radiata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
