Stephanotis chinensis

Stephanotis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble those of the common jasmine, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy flowers that bloom in summer, emitting a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. Its evergreen leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Stephanotis chinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
