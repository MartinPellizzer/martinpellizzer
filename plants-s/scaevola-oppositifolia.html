Scaevola oppositifolia

Scaevola oppositifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Scaevola oppositifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
