Senecio bustillosianus var. bustillosianus

Senecio bustillosianus var. bustillosianus, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
