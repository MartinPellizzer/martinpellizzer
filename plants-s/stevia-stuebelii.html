Stevia stuebelii

Stevia stuebelii, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains compounds such as steviol glycosides, which are used as natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Despite its medicinal uses, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
