Smilax plurifurcata

Smilax plurifurcata, commonly known as the many-branched catbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorned stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive waxy coating. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for wildlife.

Smilax plurifurcata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy, persistent growth habit.
