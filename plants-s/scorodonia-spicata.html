Scorodonia spicata

Scorodonia spicata, commonly known as the spike vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Scorodonia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
