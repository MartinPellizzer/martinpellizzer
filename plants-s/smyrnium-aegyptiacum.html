Smyrnium aegyptiacum

Smyrnium aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian cowplant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
