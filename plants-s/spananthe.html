Spananthe

Spananthe, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Spananthe, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Spananthe species are valued in horticulture for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated for their unique floral displays.
