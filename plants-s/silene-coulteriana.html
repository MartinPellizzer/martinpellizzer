Silene coulteriana

Silene coulteriana, commonly known as Coulter's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Silene coulteriana is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
