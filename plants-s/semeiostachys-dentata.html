Semeiostachys dentata

Semeiostachys dentata, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Semeiostachys dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
