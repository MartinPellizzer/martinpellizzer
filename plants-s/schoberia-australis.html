Schoberia australis

Schoberia australis, commonly known as the Australian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
