Salvia japonica var. crassifolia

Salvia japonica var. crassifolia, commonly known as Japanese sage or broadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are thick and fleshy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
