Sonchus otaviensis

Sonchus otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as a low-growing herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, spiny seeds.

Sonchus otaviensis is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
