Stephania truncata

Stephania truncata, commonly known as the Chinese herb *Fangji*, is a perennial climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, including stepharine and truncatine, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and mountainous regions.

Its root is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects.
