Salvia elsholtzioides

Salvia elsholtzioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Salvia elsholtzioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
