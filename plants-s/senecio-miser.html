Senecio miser

Senecio miser, commonly known as the weeping senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its susceptibility to certain pests and diseases.
