Sorghum leucostachyum

Sorghum leucostachyum, commonly known as white panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicle-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose, branched arrangements. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sorghum leucostachyum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
