Swartzia triptera

Swartzia triptera, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Swartzia triptera is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
