Senecio amabilis

Senecio amabilis, commonly known as the sweetstem groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features succulent, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a cluster at the tips of the stems, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
