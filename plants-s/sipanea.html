Sipanea

Sipanea, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Sipanea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
