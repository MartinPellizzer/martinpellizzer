Sphaerostigma dentatum subsp. campestris

Sphaerostigma dentatum subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and milky sap.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

This subspecies is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
