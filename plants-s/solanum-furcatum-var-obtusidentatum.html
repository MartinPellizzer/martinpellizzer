Solanum furcatum var. obtusidentatum

Solanum furcatum var. obtusidentatum, commonly known as the blunt-toothed nightshade, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, often containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
