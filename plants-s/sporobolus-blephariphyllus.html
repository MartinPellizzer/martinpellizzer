Sporobolus blephariphyllus

Sporobolus blephariphyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sanddrop, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in desert flora.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
