Sicyos semitonsus

Sicyos semitonsus, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

It produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit is a cylindrical, green, edible gourd that matures to a pale yellow or orange color. Native to the Americas, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
