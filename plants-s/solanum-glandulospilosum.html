Solanum glandulospilosum

Solanum glandulospilosum, commonly known as the glandular-spined nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
