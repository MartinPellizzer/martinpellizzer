Stramentopappus

Stramentopappus, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into seed-bearing capsules. Its unique feature is the presence of a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind.

Stramentopappus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
