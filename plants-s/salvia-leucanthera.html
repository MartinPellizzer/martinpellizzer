Salvia leucanthera

Salvia leucanthera, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of showy white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia leucanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in Native American cultures.
