Sphaeralcea orbiculata var. clementina

Sphaeralcea orbiculata var. clementina, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, with a rosette form that helps conserve moisture.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
