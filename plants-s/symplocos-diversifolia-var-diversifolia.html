Symplocos diversifolia var. diversifolia

Symplocos diversifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
