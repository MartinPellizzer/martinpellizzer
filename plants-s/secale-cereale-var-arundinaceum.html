Secale cereale var. arundinaceum

Secale cereale var. arundinaceum, commonly known as creeping rye grass, is a variant of rye that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial grass with slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its fibrous structure and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
