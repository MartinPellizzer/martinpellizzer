Symphytum asperrimum

Symphytum asperrimum, commonly known as the rough bugloss, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
