Syzygium ampliflorum

Syzygium ampliflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, reddish-brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

Syzygium ampliflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
