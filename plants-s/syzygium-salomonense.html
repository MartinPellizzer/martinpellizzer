Syzygium salomonense

Syzygium salomonense, commonly known as the Solomon's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the islands of the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red or orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.

Syzygium salomonense is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
