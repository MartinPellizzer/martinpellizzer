Senecio kebdanicus

Senecio kebdanicus, commonly known as the Keeldu rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing on cliffs or in dry, sun-exposed areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
