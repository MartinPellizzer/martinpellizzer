Stevia myricoides

Stevia myricoides, commonly known as sweetleaf or Mexican stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained attention as a sustainable alternative to artificial sweeteners.
