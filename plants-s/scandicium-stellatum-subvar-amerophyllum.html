Scandicium stellatum subvar. amerophyllum

Scandicium stellatum subvar. amerophyllum, commonly known as the American starflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
