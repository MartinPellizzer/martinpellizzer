Salix × erythroflexuosa

Salix × erythroflexuosa, commonly known as the red weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are typical of willow species. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is typically found in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.
