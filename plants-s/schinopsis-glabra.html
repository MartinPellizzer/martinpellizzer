Schinopsis glabra

Schinopsis glabra, commonly known as the white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by long, bean-like pods containing seeds. Schinopsis glabra is known for its hard, durable wood, which is valued for its strength and resistance to decay.

It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and dry forests.
