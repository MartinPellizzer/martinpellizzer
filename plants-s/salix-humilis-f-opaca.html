Salix humilis f. opaca

Salix humilis f. opaca, commonly known as the opaca willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark, almost black bark. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
