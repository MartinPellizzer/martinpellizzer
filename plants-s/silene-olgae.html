Silene olgae

Silene olgae, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in maritime climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene olgae is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand salty winds and poor soil conditions.
