Scutellaria × hybrida

Scutellaria × hybrida, commonly known as the blue skullcap, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Scutellaria × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
