Sabicea gracilis var. microcalyx

Sabicea gracilis var. microcalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sabicea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
