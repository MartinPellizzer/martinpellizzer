Stachys kuntzei

Stachys kuntzei, commonly known as Kuntze's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters.

Stachys kuntzei is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
