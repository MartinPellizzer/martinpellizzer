Schoenoplectus purshianus

Schoenoplectus purshianus, commonly known as the Pursh's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Schoenoplectus purshianus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
