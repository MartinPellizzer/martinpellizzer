Senecio glauciifolius

Senecio glauciifolius, commonly known as the blueleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
