Solanum indicum var. grandemunitum

Solanum indicum var. grandemunitum, commonly known as the large-fruited eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, purple to black berries that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The berries are typically harvested when fully mature, showcasing a deep, glossy color.
