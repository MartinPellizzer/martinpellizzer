Selaginella hellwigii

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous stem system with closely spaced, spirally arranged leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its sporophyte stage produces spores on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella hellwigii is adapted to thrive in humid climates and is often found in temperate regions with high rainfall.
