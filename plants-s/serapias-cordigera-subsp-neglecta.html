Serapias cordigera subsp. neglecta

Serapias cordigera subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the neglected heart's-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typical of the genus Serapias. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
