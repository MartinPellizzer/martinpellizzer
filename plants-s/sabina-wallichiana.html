Sabina wallichiana

Sabina wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan thuja, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub with a compact, conical shape. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green and closely arranged along the branches. Its cones are small and typically have three to four scales.

Sabina wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
