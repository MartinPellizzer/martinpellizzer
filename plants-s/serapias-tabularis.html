Serapias tabularis

Serapias tabularis, commonly known as the flatleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a short, upright stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often in shaded or rocky habitats.

Serapias tabularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
