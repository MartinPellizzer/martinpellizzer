Senecio congestus subsp. palustris

Senecio congestus subsp.

palustris, commonly known as the swamp senecio, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in southern Africa. It typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

The plant features upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
