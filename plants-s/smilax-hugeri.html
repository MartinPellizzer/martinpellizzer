Smilax hugeri

Smilax hugeri, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with thorny stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its berries, which are dark red when mature, are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Smilax hugeri is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its hardy and adaptable nature.
