Salvia officinalis var. thasia

Salvia officinalis var. thasia, commonly known as lemon sage, is a variant of the common sage plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves emit a distinct lemony scent when crushed, making it popular in culinary and aromatic applications. This variety is often used in herbal teas and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring minimal water once established.
