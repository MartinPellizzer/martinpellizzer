Stenocarpha filiformis var. epapposa

Stenocarpha filiformis var. epapposa, commonly known as the woolly phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
