Spiraea bethlehemensis

Spiraea bethlehemensis, commonly known as Bethlehem spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are borne on the previous year's wood. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Spiraea bethlehemensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
