Sida jacquinii

Sida jacquinii, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Sida jacquinii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for crop management.
