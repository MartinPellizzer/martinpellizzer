Sarcochilus indusiatus

Sarcochilus indusiatus, commonly known as the golden helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a robust, hairy rhizome and produces erect, leafy stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The flowers have a distinctive helmet-shaped lip, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Sarcochilus. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
