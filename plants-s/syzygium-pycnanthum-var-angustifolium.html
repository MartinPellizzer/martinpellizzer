Syzygium pycnanthum var. angustifolium

Syzygium pycnanthum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
