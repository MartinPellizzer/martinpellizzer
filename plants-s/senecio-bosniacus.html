Senecio bosniacus

Senecio bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio bosniacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
