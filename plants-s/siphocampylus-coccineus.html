Siphocampylus coccineus

Siphocampylus coccineus, commonly known as the redleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
