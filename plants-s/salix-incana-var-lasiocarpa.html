Salix incana var. lasiocarpa

Salix incana var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and silvery-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
