Stenotus lyallii

Stenotus lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's thistle, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a compact cluster.

Stenotus lyallii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
