Sarothra mutila

Sarothra mutila, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It blooms from spring to early summer and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Sarothra mutila is also notable for its historical association with the legend of the Scarlet Pimpernel, a British spy during World War I.
