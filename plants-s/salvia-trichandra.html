Salvia trichandra

Salvia trichandra, commonly known as the blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Salvia trichandra typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden landscapes.
