Satureja × benthamifolia

Satureja × benthamifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a hybrid shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often gray-green in color, and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs, and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its strong fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
