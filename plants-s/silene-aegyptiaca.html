Silene aegyptiaca

Silene aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene aegyptiaca thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
