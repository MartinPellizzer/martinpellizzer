Sedum cupressoides

Sedum cupressoides, commonly known as the blue stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including coastal and arid environments.
