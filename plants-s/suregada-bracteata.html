Suregada bracteata

Suregada bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and an important species in ecological studies.
