Sesbania macrocarpa var. picta

Sesbania macrocarpa var. picta, commonly known as the painted sesbania, is a striking tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, ornamental foliage with variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
