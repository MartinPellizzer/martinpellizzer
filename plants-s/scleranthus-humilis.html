Scleranthus humilis

Scleranthus humilis, commonly known as the common sicklegrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Scleranthus humilis is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
