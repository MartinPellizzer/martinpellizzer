Senecio linifoliaster

Senecio linifoliaster, commonly known as the silkyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio linifoliaster is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
