Scirpus eriophorum var. andrewsii

Scirpus eriophorum var. andrewsii, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower heads that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety thrives in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
