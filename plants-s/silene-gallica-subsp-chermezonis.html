Silene gallica subsp. chermezonis

Silene gallica subsp.

chermezonis, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in meadows and open slopes. Silene gallica subsp.

chermezonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
