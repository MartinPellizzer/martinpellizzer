Solanum nemorense

Solanum nemorense, commonly known as the marsh tomato, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Solanum nemorense is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
