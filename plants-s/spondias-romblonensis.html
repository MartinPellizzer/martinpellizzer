Spondias romblonensis

Spondias romblonensis, commonly known as Romblon mango, is a small tree native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Romblon.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits with a fibrous pulp.

It is well adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
