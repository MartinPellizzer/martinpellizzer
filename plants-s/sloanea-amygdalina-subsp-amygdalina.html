Sloanea amygdalina subsp. amygdalina

Sloanea amygdalina subsp.

amygdalina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum amygdalina*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a glossy dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and a shallow root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks in regions with a humid climate.
