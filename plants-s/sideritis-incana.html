Sideritis incana

Sideritis incana, commonly known as white beard mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis incana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
