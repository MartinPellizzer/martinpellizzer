Salvia marci

Salvia marci, commonly known as Marc's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fuzzy texture.

Salvia marci is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
