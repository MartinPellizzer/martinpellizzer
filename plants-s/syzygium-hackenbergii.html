Syzygium hackenbergii

Syzygium hackenbergii, commonly known as the Hackenberg's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Syzygium hackenbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
