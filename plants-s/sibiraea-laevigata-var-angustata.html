Sibiraea laevigata var. angustata

Sibiraea laevigata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf curlleaf mountain-ash, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
