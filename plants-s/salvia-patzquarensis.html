Salvia patzquarensis

Salvia patzquarensis, commonly known as the Patzcuarensis sage, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slight fuzzy texture, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia patzquarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in its native habitat.
