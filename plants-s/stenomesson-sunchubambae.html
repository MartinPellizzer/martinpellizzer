Stenomesson sunchubambae

Stenomesson sunchubambae, commonly known as the Sunchubamba cholla, is a unique cactus species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its arid habitat.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
