Scutellaria multiglandulosa

Scutellaria multiglandulosa, commonly known as the multiglandular skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
