Sanicula rubriflora

Sanicula rubriflora, commonly known as redflower sanicle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, often slightly toxic leaves, which contain various bioactive compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sanicula rubriflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
