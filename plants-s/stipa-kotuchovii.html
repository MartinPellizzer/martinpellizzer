Stipa kotuchovii

Stipa kotuchovii, commonly known as the Kotuchov's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is densely packed with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
