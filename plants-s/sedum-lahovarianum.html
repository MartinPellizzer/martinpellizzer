Sedum lahovarianum

Sedum lahovarianum, commonly known as the Lahovary's stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

Sedum lahovarianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
