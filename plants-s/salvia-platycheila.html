Salvia platycheila

Salvia platycheila, commonly known as the broadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, two-lipped purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia platycheila is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
