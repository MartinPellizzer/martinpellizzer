Satyrium hirtellum

Satyrium hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy satyrium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
