Staberoha disticha

Staberoha disticha, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early summer, followed by small, three-lobed seed pods.
