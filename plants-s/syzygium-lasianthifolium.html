Syzygium lasianthifolium

Syzygium lasianthifolium, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium lasianthifolium is also valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
