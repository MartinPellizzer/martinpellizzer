Senecio brachyantherus

Senecio brachyantherus, commonly known as the blue star, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms a low-growing rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its native habitat being limited.
