Smilacina amplexicaulis

Smilacina amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing false Solomon's seal, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its species name "amplexicaulis," meaning "embrace the stem.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found climbing over shrubs or trees.

Smilacina amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
