Struthiola dodecapetala var. kraussii

Struthiola dodecapetala var. kraussii, commonly known as the Krauss's star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
