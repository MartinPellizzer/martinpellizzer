Syzygium praetermissum

Syzygium praetermissum, commonly known as the Australian satinash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and myrtle species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Syzygium praetermissum is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
