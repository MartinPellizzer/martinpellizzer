Stelis quadrifida

Stelis quadrifida, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the inflorescence bears small, delicate flowers with a distinctive four-lobed structure.

Stelis quadrifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
