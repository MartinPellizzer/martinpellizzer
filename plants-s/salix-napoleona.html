Salix napoleona

Salix napoleona, commonly known as Napoleon's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in botanical studies.
