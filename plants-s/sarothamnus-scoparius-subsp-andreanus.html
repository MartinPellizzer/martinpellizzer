Sarothamnus scoparius subsp. andreanus

Sarothamnus scoparius subsp.

andreanus, commonly known as the Algerian thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both horticulture and traditional herbal uses.
