Senecio aksuensis

Senecio aksuensis, commonly known as the Aksu Senecio, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, specifically found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Senecio aksuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
