Senecio intactus

Senecio intactus, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Senecio intactus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
