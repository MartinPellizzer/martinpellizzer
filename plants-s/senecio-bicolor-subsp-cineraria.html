Senecio bicolor subsp. cineraria

Senecio bicolor subsp.

cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
