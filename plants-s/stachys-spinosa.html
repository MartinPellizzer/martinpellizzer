Stachys spinosa

Stachys spinosa, commonly known as the prickly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys spinosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
