Salvia texana var. canescens

Salvia texana var. canescens, commonly known as Texas sage or silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
