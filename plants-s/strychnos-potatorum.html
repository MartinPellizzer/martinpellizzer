Strychnos potatorum

Strychnos potatorum, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in traditional medicine, though it has been used in some cultures for its purported medicinal effects.
