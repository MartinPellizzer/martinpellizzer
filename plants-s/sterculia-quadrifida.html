Sterculia quadrifida

Sterculia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf sterculia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, often buttressed trunk. The leaves are palmately divided into four lobes, giving it its common name, and are typically large and glossy. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
