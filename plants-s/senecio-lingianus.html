Senecio lingianus

Senecio lingianus, commonly known as the Ling's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio lingianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
