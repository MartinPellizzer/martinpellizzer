Schefflera polydactylis

Schefflera polydactylis, commonly known as the giant schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets radiating from a central point. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Schefflera polydactylis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
