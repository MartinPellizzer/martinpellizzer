Senecio atropurpureus var. ulmeri

Senecio atropurpureus var. ulmeri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
