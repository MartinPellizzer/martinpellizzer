Spiraea hookeriana

Spiraea hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Spiraea hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
