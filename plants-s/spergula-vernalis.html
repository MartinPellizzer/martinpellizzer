Spergula vernalis

Spergula vernalis, commonly known as spring sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive V-shaped cross-section.

Spergula vernalis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
