Saxifraga paniculata var. canaliculata

Saxifraga paniculata var. canaliculata, commonly known as the rockfoam or canaliculate saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, featuring succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, arid conditions and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
