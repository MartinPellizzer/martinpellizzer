Senecio heterodoxus

Senecio heterodoxus, commonly known as the "Mexican hat" or "painted nettle," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, spiny stem with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a radiating pattern.

Senecio heterodoxus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
