Silene waldsteinii

Silene waldsteinii, commonly known as the Waldstein's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It has slender, upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

Silene waldsteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
