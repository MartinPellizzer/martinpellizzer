Syagrus cerqueirana

Syagrus cerqueirana, commonly known as the Cerrado palm, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open habitats such as savannas and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
