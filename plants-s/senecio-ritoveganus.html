Senecio ritoveganus

Senecio ritoveganus, commonly known as the St. George's Wort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
