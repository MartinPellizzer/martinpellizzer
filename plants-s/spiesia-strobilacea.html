Spiesia strobilacea

Spiesia strobilacea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are leathery, dark green on top, and silvery white underneath, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
