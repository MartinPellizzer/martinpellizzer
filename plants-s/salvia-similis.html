Salvia similis

Salvia similis, commonly known as the similar sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia similis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
