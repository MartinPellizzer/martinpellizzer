Salvia glumacea

Salvia glumacea, commonly known as slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
