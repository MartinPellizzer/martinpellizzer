Silene miniata

Silene miniata, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silene miniata is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period, often blooming in early summer.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
