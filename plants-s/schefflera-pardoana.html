Schefflera pardoana

Schefflera pardoana, commonly known as the Pardoana umbrella tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple branches that bear palmate leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green, glossy, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Schefflera pardoana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
