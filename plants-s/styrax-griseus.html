Styrax griseus

Styrax griseus, commonly known as the gray styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

Styrax griseus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
