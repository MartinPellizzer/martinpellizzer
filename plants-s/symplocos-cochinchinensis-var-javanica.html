Symplocos cochinchinensis var. javanica

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. javanica, commonly known as the Javanese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
