Senecio mbuluzensis

Senecio mbuluzensis, commonly known as the Mbuluzi groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
