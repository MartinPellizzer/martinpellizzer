Symplocos gardneriana

Symplocos gardneriana, commonly known as Gardner's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are edible for certain bird species.

Symplocos gardneriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
