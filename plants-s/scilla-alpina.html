Scilla alpina

Scilla alpina, commonly known as the alpine scilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often bulbous growth habit. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.
