Syzygium gitingense

Syzygium gitingense, commonly known as the Gitingense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Syzygium gitingense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its fruit is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its tart flavor and potential health benefits.
