Sarcostemma odoratum

Sarcostemma odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sarcostemma odoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
