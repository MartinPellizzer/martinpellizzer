Syringa reticulata subsp. amurensis

Syringa reticulata subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as the Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in forest edges and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
