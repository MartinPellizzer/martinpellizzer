Stapelia × comparabilis

Stapelia × comparabilis, commonly known as the African milk plant, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, waxy flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive, almost fleshy appearance. It is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.

Stapelia × comparabilis prefers full sun and well-draining soil, making it ideal for desert or rock garden settings.
