Solidago uniligulata var. levipes

Solidago uniligulata var. levipes, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
