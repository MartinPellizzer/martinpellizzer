Solanum tuberosum var. runa

Solanum tuberosum var. runa, commonly known as the Runa potato, is a botanical variety native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes elongated shapes and a wide range of skin colors, from white to purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits typical solanum features such as lobed leaves and solanaceous flowers. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cooler temperatures and well-drained soils.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
