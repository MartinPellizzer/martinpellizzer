Scutellaria incana

Scutellaria incana, commonly known as bluehead vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Scutellaria incana is native to regions of North America and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
