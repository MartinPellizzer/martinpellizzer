Stipagrostis brachypoda

Stipagrostis brachypoda, commonly known as the shortgrass needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand periods of drought.

Stipagrostis brachypoda plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
