Stachys peruviana

Stachys peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Stachys peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
