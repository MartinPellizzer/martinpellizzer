Stylosanthes plicata

Stylosanthes plicata, commonly known as narrowleaf stylo, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. It is often used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value and palatability.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
