Stipa rudis subsp. australis

Stipa rudis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with fine, needle-like leaves. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
