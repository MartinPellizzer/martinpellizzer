Solanum guerreroense

Solanum guerreroense, commonly known as Guerrero nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in the mountainous regions of Guerrero state, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
