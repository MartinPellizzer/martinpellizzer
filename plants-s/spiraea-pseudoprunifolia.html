Spiraea pseudoprunifolia

Spiraea pseudoprunifolia, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often reddish-brown and slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Spiraea pseudoprunifolia is also valued for its moderate growth habit and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
