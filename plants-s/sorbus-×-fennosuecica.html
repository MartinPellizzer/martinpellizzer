Sorbus × fennosuecica

Sorbus × fennosuecica, commonly known as the Swedish whitebeam, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Sorbus aria and Sorbus nemoralis.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its white to pale pink flowers and small, aggregate berries. The plant typically grows in boreal and temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and gray in younger trees.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
