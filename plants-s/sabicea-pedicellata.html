Sabicea pedicellata

Sabicea pedicellata, commonly known as the white-flowered sabicea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are known for their sweet fragrance, attracting various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution in forested areas.
