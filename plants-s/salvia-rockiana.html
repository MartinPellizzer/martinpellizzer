Salvia rockiana

Salvia rockiana, commonly known as the rock sage or rock sagebrush, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia rockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
