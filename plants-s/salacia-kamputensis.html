Salacia kamputensis

Salacia kamputensis, commonly known as the red salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and woody stems. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Salacia kamputensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
