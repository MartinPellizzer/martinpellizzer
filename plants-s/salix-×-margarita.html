Salix × margarita

Salix × margarita, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a unique visual appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are either male or female.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist environments.
