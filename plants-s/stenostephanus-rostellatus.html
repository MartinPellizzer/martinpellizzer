Stenostephanus rostellatus

Stenostephanus rostellatus, commonly known as the rostellate stephanus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
