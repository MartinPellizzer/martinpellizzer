Senecio pyrrhochrous

Senecio pyrrhochrous, commonly known as the red-stemmed groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and may have a slightly bitter taste.

Senecio pyrrhochrous is a hardy perennial that is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
