Stachys delphinensis

Stachys delphinensis, commonly known as waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. The plant features upright, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and have a distinctive blue or purple coloration.

Stachys delphinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
