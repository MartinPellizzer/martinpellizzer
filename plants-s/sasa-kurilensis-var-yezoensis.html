Sasa kurilensis var. yezoensis

Sasa kurilensis var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the Japanese islands of Hokkaido and the Kuril Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in temperate regions.
