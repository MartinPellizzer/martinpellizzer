Sarcochilus setosus

Sarcochilus setosus, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flower with a hairy, reddish-brown dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a rough, textured surface.

Sarcochilus setosus is known for its fragrant blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
