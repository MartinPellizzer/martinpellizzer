Sida demissa

Sida demissa, commonly known as smallleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida demissa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
