Sonchus graecus

Sonchus graecus, commonly known as Greek sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in temperate regions. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering heads produce small yellow flowers that develop into spiny, oval-shaped seeds.

Sonchus graecus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
