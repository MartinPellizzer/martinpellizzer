Stachys scaberula

Stachys scaberula, commonly known as rough hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rough texture and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
