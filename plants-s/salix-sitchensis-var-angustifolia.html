Salix sitchensis var. angustifolia

Salix sitchensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
