Selinum silaus

Selinum silaus, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy root system and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky, milky sap. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
