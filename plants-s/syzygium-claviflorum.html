Syzygium claviflorum

Syzygium claviflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Syzygium claviflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
