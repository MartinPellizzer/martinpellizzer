Scorzonera divaricata var. tatewakii

Scorzonera divaricata var. tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated tubular corolla, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
