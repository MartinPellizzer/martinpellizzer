Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii var. rubricentrus

Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii var. rubricentrus, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
