Salacia bangalensis

Salacia bangalensis, commonly known as the Bangalensis salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
