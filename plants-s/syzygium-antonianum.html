Syzygium antonianum

Syzygium antonianum, commonly known as the Antoanum apple or the Antoanum tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Syzygium antonianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
