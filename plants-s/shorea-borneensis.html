Shorea borneensis

Shorea borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter-colored underside.

Shorea borneensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
