Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. ullungdoense

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. ullungdoense, commonly known as the Ullungdoense honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, followed by orange-red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
