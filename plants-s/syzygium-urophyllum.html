Syzygium urophyllum

Syzygium urophyllum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and beauty. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

Syzygium urophyllum is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
