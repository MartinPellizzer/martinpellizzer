Setaria geminata

Setaria geminata, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and distinctive, hairy inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, while its seed heads are often clustered in loose, drooping panicles.

Setaria geminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
