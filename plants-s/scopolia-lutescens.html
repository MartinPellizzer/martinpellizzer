Scopolia lutescens

Scopolia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow scopolia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has a tuberous root system and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Scopolia lutescens is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
