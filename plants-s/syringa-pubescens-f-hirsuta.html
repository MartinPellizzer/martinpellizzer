Syringa pubescens f. hirsuta

Syringa pubescens f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woodbine, is a cultivar of the weigela plant characterized by its dense, soft, hairy foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, featuring clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are ovate and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
