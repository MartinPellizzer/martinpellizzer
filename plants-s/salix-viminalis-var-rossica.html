Salix viminalis var. rossica

Salix viminalis var. rossica, commonly known as Russian willow, is a variant of the common osier dogwood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its flexible, slender branches that are often used in basket weaving. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a pale underside.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows.
