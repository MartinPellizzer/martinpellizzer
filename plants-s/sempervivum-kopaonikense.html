Sempervivum kopaonikense

Sempervivum kopaonikense, commonly known as the Kopaonik houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Serbia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purple tinge. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum kopaonikense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
