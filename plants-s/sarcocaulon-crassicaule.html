Sarcocaulon crassicaule

Sarcocaulon crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Sarcocaulon crassicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
