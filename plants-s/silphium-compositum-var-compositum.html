Silphium compositum var. compositum

Silphium compositum var. compositum, commonly known as the composite silphium, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its historical significance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species for wetland restoration projects.
