Syzygium davaoense

Syzygium davaoense, commonly known as the Davao apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in local communities.
