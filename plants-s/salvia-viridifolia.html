Salvia viridifolia

Salvia viridifolia, commonly known as greenleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to violet flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional herbal medicine.
