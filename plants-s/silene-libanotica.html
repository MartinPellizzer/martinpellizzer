Silene libanotica

Silene libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
