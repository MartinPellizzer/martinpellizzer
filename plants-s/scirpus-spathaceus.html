Scirpus spathaceus

Scirpus spathaceus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, flat leaves that are often used in basket weaving. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus spathaceus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
