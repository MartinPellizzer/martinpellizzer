Scilla floribunda

Scilla floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Scilla floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and early blooming habit.
