Sida corchorifolia

Sida corchorifolia, commonly known as smallleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida corchorifolia produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
