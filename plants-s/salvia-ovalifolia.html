Salvia ovalifolia

Salvia ovalifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. Salvia ovalifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
