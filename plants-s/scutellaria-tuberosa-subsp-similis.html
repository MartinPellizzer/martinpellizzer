Scutellaria tuberosa subsp. similis

Scutellaria tuberosa subsp.

similis, commonly known as the similar tuberous scullion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
