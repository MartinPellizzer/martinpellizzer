Spiraea japonica var. acuminata

Spiraea japonica var. acuminata, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it popular in landscaping and floral arrangements.
