Spiranthes valerioi

Spiranthes valerioi, commonly known as the pink spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pale pink to white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature.

Spiranthes valerioi is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its delicate beauty and ecological importance in native habitats.
