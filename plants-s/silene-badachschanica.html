Silene badachschanica

Silene badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakshan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene badachschanica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
