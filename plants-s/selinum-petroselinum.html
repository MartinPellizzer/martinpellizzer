Selinum petroselinum

Selinum petroselinum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are widely used as a culinary herb and garnish. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic profile.

Parsley is also valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins A, C, and K.
