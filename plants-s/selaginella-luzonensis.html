Selaginella luzonensis

Selaginella luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with erect, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are usually located at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella luzonensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
