Scirpus sachalinensis

Scirpus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers, preferring moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus sachalinensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to aquatic environments.
