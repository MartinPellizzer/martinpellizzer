Spiraea capitata

Spiraea capitata, commonly known as the clustered spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea capitata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
