Syzygium prolatum

Syzygium prolatum, commonly known as the long-fruited tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow. Syzygium prolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
