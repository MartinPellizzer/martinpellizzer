Salvia dichroantha

Salvia dichroantha, commonly known as the red sage or African sage, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia dichroantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
