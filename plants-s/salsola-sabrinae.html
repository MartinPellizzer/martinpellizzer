Salsola sabrinae

Salsola sabrinae, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and disturbed soils. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spiny heads, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Salsola sabrinae is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
