Salvia baimaensis

Salvia baimaensis, commonly known as the Baima sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Salvia baimaensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
