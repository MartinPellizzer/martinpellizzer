Sclerobasis sonneratii

Sclerobasis sonneratii, commonly known as the Sonneratii spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and milky sap, which are typical features of the genus Sclerobasis. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are often clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a rough or hairy texture that aids in reducing water loss.
