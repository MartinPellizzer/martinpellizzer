Sarcochilus crepidiformis

Sarcochilus crepidiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed saxifrage orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its roots are often fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
