Senecio helianthemoides

Senecio helianthemoides, commonly known as the African daisy or red-hot cat's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-red bracts that resemble a cat's tail. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
