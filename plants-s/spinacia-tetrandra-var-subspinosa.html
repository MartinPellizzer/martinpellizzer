Spinacia tetrandra var. subspinosa

Spinacia tetrandra var. subspinosa, commonly known as the Chinese spinach or New Zealand spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and a mild, slightly tangy flavor. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value, rich in vitamins A and C. It prefers well-drained soil and can be grown in partial shade.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and ability to produce a harvest within a short growing season.
