Spilanthes grisea var. chodatana

Spilanthes grisea var. chodatana, commonly known as the yellow toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is known for its pungent, peppery scent and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some indigenous cultures.
