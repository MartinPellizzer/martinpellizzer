Sorghum myosurus

Sorghum myosurus, commonly known as the giant broom, is a tall, flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, brush-like clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist environments, such as wetlands and riverbanks, and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, pale-green to brown flowers in clusters.

Sorghum myosurus is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value as a habitat for various insects and birds.
