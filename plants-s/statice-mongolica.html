Statice mongolica

Statice mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Statice mongolica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
