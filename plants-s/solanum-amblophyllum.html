Solanum amblophyllum

Solanum amblophyllum, commonly known as the Malawi nightshade, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
