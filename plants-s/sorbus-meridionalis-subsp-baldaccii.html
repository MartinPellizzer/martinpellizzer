Sorbus meridionalis subsp. baldaccii

Sorbus meridionalis subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
