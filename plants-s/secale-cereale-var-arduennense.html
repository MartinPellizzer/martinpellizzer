Secale cereale var. arduennense

Secale cereale var. arduennense, commonly known as the Arduennense rye, is a variant of rye that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with florets. Its seeds are enclosed in protective husks, a feature common among cereal grains.

This variety is often studied for its potential in agricultural resilience and genetic diversity.
