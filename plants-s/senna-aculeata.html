Senna aculeata

Senna aculeata, commonly known as the prickly senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Senna aculeata is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
