Salvia hablitziana

Salvia hablitziana, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
