Serjania incana f. glabriuscula

Serjania incana f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-haired serjania, is a variant of the serjania genus native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
