Silene sendtneri

Silene sendtneri, commonly known as the Sendtner's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky slopes and meadows. Silene sendtneri has small, oval leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
