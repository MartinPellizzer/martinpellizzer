Strebanthus auriculatus

Strebanthus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
