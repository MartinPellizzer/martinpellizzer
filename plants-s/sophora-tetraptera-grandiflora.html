Sophora tetraptera grandiflora

Sophora tetraptera grandiflora, commonly known as the giant silkworm tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces long, slender pods containing fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in silk production.
