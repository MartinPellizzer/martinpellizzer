Sempervivum georgicum

Sempervivum georgicum, commonly known as the Georgian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Sempervivum georgicum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold winters and poor soil.
