Stirtonanthus insignis

Stirtonanthus insignis, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, adding contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Stirtonanthus insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
