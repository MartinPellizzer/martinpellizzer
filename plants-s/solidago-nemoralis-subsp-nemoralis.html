Solidago nemoralis subsp. nemoralis

Solidago nemoralis subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in woodland, meadows, and open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
