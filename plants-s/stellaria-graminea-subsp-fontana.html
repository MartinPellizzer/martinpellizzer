Stellaria graminea subsp. fontana

Stellaria graminea subsp.

fontana, commonly known as Fontana mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

The plant produces tiny, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
