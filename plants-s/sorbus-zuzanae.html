Sorbus zuzanae

Sorbus zuzanae, commonly known as the Zuzana's whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, including parts of the Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it often grows in rocky or woodland habitats.

Sorbus zuzanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
