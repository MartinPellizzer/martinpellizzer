Solanum rothelianum

Solanum rothelianum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it typically grows in shrubland or forested areas.

Solanum rothelianum is often found in regions with warm, humid climates.
