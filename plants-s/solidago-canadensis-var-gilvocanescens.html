Solidago canadensis var. gilvocanescens

Solidago canadensis var. gilvocanescens, commonly known as the golden beard or stiff goldenrod, is a variant of the common goldenrod.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a grayish-green appearance. This variety is known for its showy, late-summer blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained sites and full sun.
