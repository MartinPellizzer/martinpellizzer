Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. pastoris

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

pastoris, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features aromatic, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver underside.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception.
