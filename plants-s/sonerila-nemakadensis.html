Sonerila nemakadensis

Sonerila nemakadensis, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern region of India, particularly in the states of Meghalaya, Assam, and Manipur. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
