Setaria viridis var. purpurascens

Setaria viridis var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of the green foxtail grass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple tinge on the bracts and inflorescence, which sets it apart from the typical green variety. This plant is native to temperate regions of the Americas and thrives in disturbed soils and open areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often arranged in a loose, panicle-like structure.

Purple foxtail is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
