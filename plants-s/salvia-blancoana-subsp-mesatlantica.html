Salvia blancoana subsp. mesatlantica

Salvia blancoana subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies mesatlantica is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other subspecies of Salvia blancoana.
