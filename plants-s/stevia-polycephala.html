Stevia polycephala

Stevia polycephala, commonly known as the "Brazilian stevia," is a perennial shrub native to Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Stevia polycephala is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweeteners.
