Saxifraga aizoon var. orientalis

Saxifraga aizoon var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern rockfoil, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy texture, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
