Stachys hyssopifolia var. ambigua

Stachys hyssopifolia var. ambigua, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a minty aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
