Salsola zaidamica

Salsola zaidamica, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline and arid environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Salsola zaidamica plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
