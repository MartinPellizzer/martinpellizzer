Shorea laevifolia

Shorea laevifolia, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heavy, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Shorea laevifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
