Symplocos fimbriata

Symplocos fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a serrated margin, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that ripen to a reddish color. Symplocos fimbriata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
