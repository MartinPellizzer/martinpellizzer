Sagittanthera cyanelloides

Sagittanthera cyanelloides, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
