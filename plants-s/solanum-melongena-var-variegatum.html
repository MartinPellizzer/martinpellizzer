Solanum melongena var. variegatum

Solanum melongena var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated eggplant, is a cultivar of the eggplant species characterized by its distinctive leaf and stem patterns.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters with hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the fruit is a fleshy, egg-shaped berry that can vary in color from purple to white or green.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions.
