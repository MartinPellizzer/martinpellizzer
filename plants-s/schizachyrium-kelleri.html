Schizachyrium kelleri

Schizachyrium kelleri, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Schizachyrium kelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
