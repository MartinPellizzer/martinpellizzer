Senecio latiflorus var. lacunosus

Senecio latiflorus var. lacunosus, commonly known as the lacunar blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically linear and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
