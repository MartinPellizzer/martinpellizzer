Specularia pentagonia var. coa

Specularia pentagonia var. coa, commonly known as the Coa Pentagonal Catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
