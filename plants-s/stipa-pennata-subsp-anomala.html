Stipa pennata subsp. anomala

Stipa pennata subsp.

anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
