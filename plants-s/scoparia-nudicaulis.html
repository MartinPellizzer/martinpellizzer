Scoparia nudicaulis

Scoparia nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has small, fleshy, green leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
