Sorbus aucuparia subsp. camschatcensis

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

camschatcensis, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a subspecies of the rowan tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that are often consumed by birds.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has a compact, shrub-like form. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn bright red in the autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
