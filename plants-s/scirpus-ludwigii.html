Scirpus ludwigii

Scirpus ludwigii, commonly known as Ludwig's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and is important for wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus ludwigii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
