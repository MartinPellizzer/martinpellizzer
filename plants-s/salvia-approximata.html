Salvia approximata

Salvia approximata, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of showy violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
