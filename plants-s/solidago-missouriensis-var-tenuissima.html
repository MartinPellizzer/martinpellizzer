Solidago missouriensis var. tenuissima

Solidago missouriensis var. tenuissima, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
