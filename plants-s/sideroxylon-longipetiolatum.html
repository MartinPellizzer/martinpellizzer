Sideroxylon longipetiolatum

Sideroxylon longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
