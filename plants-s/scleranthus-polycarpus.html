Scleranthus polycarpus

Scleranthus polycarpus, commonly known as the many-seeded thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with five petals.

Scleranthus polycarpus is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
