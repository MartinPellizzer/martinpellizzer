Stipa crassiculmis subsp. heterotricha

Stipa crassiculmis subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the rough beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in clusters.

The leaves are narrow, rough, and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet cluster, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
