Spilanthes urens var. hispidula

Spilanthes urens var. hispidula, commonly known as hispid hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is known for its strong, pungent odor and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
