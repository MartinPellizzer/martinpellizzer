Salvia omeiana var. grandibracteata

Salvia omeiana var. grandibracteata, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. Its aromatic foliage and striking blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in warm, temperate climates.
