Solanum indicum

Solanum indicum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Africa, and parts of the Pacific. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It has hairy leaves and stems, and its growth habit is often sprawling or semi-woody.

Solanum indicum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value, but it can also be invasive in certain environments.
