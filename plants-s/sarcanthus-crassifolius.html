Sarcanthus crassifolius

Sarcanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved sarcanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in umbels, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Sarcanthus crassifolius is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
