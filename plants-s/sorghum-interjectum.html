Sorghum interjectum

Sorghum interjectum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and striking red or purple hues in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers arranged in a panicle, typical of the Sorghum genus. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable regions.
