Silene salangensis

Silene salangensis, commonly known as the Salang Valley catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene salangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
