Sasa paniculata f. subnobilis

Sasa paniculata f. subnobilis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species Sasa paniculata.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white to pale green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, upright shoots, often forming compact clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of East Asia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
