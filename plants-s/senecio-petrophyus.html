Senecio petrophyus

Senecio petrophyus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio petrophyus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
