Salix qinghaiensis

Salix qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically found in mountainous areas with cold climates. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Salix qinghaiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
