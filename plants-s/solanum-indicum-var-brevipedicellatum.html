Solanum indicum var. brevipedicellatum

Solanum indicum var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing toxic compounds such as solanine.

It is often found in moist, shady areas and is used in traditional medicine, though its consumption is generally discouraged due to its poisonous nature.
