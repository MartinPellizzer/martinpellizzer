Sorbus amurensis var. rufa

Sorbus amurensis var. rufa, commonly known as the Amur red rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reddish in color, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces berries that range in color from red to orange, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
