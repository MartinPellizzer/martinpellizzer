Stenorrhynchos polyanthum

Stenorrhynchos polyanthum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
