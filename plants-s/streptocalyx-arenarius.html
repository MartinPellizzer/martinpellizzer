Streptocalyx arenarius

Streptocalyx arenarius, commonly known as the sand dune hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray. Streptocalyx arenarius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
