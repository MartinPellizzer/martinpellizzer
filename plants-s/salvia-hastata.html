Salvia hastata

Salvia hastata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Salvia hastata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
