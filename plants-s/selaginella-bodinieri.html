Selaginella bodinieri

Selaginella bodinieri, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its whorled arrangement of small, scale-like leaves. It produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain both microspores and megaspores.

Selaginella bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
