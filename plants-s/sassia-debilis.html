Sassia debilis

Sassia debilis, commonly known as the slender sassia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in sandy soils. It is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
