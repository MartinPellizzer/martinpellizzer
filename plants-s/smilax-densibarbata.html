Smilax densibarbata

Smilax densibarbata, commonly known as the dense-barbed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and sharp, barbed spines, which help it climb and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Smilax densibarbata is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and thickets.
