Scalesia microcephala var. cordifolia

Scalesia microcephala var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped scaleia, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually broad and have a velvety texture, while its flowers attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in the unique ecosystems of the Galápagos.
