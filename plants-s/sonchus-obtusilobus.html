Sonchus obtusilobus

Sonchus obtusilobus, commonly known as blunt-lobed sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wetlands. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is considered an annual herb and is sometimes regarded as a weed in agricultural settings.
