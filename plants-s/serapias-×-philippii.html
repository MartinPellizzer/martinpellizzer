Serapias × philippii

Serapias × philippii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a cross between Serapias acaulis and Serapias auricoma.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in moist environments.

Serapias × philippii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with appropriate drainage and moisture conditions.
