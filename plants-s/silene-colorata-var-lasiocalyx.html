Silene colorata var. lasiocalyx

Silene colorata var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
