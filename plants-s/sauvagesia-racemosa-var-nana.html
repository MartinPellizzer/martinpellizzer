Sauvagesia racemosa var. nana

Sauvagesia racemosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf sauvagesia, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are ovate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
