Satureja graeca var. hispida

Satureja graeca var. hispida, commonly known as Greek mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong, peppery fragrance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
