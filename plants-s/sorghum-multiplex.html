Sorghum multiplex

Sorghum multiplex, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, adding visual interest.

Sorghum multiplex is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, showing resilience to drought conditions.
