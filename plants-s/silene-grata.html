Silene grata

Silene grata, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and branching.

Silene grata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
