Saussurea katoana

Saussurea katoana, commonly known as the Katoan saffron plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its unique and aromatic foliage, which contains compounds similar to those found in saffron. It thrives in alpine environments and is often found at high elevations with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
