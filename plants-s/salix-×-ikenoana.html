Salix × ikenoana

Salix × ikenoana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the autumn. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
