Spergulus

Spergulus, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and is typically found growing in coastal areas. The plant has a prostrate or erect growth habit, with linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Spergulus species are often used in sandy or saline soils due to their tolerance for harsh environmental conditions.

Its seeds are small and contain oils that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
