Silene graminifolia var. turgida

Silene graminifolia var. turgida, commonly known as the inflated catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, giving the plant its species name "graminifolia.". The variety turgida is distinguished by its inflated, bladder-like pods that remain on the plant after the flowers have faded.

This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
