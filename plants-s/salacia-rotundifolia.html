Salacia rotundifolia

Salacia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
