Sasa gombeiana

Sasa gombeiana, commonly known as the Gombe bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and form dense clumps, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.

Sasa gombeiana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
