Sagittaria cuneata f. equiloba

Sagittaria cuneata f. equiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are broad and often floating or submerged, with a distinctive notched base. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically in summer. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.
