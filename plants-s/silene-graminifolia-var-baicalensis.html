Silene graminifolia var. baicalensis

Silene graminifolia var. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical Silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
