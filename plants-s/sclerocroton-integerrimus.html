Sclerocroton integerrimus

Sclerocroton integerrimus, commonly known as the rough-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
