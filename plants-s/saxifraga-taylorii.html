Saxifraga taylorii

Saxifraga taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges in North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often fleshy and succulent, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.

Saxifraga taylorii is a low-growing plant, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in its harsh environment.
