Salix faxoniana

Salix faxoniana, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that hang gracefully from the crown. The leaves are oval and dark green, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by elongated fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist soils.
