Steiropteris parva

Steiropteris parva, commonly known as the small-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Steiropteris parva plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
