Sonchus lidii

Sonchus lidii, commonly known as the Armenian sowthistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a rosette of silver-velvet leaves with a distinctive central stem that bears yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, lobed leaves and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Sonchus lidii is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
