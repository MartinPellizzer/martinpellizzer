Scilla pratensis

Scilla pratensis, commonly known as meadow scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and typically grows in moist, grassy habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
