Silene melikjanii

Silene melikjanii, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Silene melikjanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
