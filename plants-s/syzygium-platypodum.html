Syzygium platypodum

Syzygium platypodum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Syzygium platypodum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species in the Myrtaceae family.
