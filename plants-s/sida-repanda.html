Sida repanda

Sida repanda, commonly known as silky sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. Its small, white flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
