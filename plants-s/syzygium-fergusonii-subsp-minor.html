Syzygium fergusonii subsp. minor

Syzygium fergusonii subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf satinwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
