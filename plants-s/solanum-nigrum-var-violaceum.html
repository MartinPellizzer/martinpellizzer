Solanum nigrum var. violaceum

Solanum nigrum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple black nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

This variety is notable for its striking purple foliage and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
