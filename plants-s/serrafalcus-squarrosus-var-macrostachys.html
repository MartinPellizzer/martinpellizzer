Serrafalcus squarrosus var. macrostachys

Serrafalcus squarrosus var. macrostachys, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where it forms a symbiotic relationship with its host.
