Sedum lazicum

Sedum lazicum, commonly known as the Armenian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and easy to care for.

Sedum lazicum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
