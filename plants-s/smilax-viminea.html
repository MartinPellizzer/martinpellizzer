Smilax viminea

Smilax viminea, commonly known as the creeping smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of sharp, spiny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Smilax viminea is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
