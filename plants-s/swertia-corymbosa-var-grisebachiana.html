Swertia corymbosa var. grisebachiana

Swertia corymbosa var. grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray-leaved swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a grayish or whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
