Solanum tuberosum var. saccharatum

Solanum tuberosum var. saccharatum, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a variant of the potato plant that belongs to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, sweet-tasting roots that are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. Unlike the typical potato, the sweet potato has a smooth, reddish or brownish skin and a moist, orange or yellow flesh. This plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is cultivated globally for its edible roots, which are used in various culinary applications.
