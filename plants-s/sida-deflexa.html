Sida deflexa

Sida deflexa, commonly known as the prostrate sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in a low, spreading habit, with hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The flowers are inconspicuous, often greenish or brownish, and are clustered in small heads. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
