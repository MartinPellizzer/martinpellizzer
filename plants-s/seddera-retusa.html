Seddera retusa

Seddera retusa, commonly known as the smooth seddera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Seddera retusa is typically found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display and hardy nature.
