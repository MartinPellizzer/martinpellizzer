Saurauia spadicea

Saurauia spadicea, commonly known as the white-flowered snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant and bloom in the spring. Saurauia spadicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers cool, humid conditions and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
