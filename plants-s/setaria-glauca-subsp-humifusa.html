Setaria glauca subsp. humifusa

Setaria glauca subsp.

humifusa, commonly known as bluegrass dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
