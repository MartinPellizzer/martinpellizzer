Schoenoplectus hallii

Schoenoplectus hallii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Schoenoplectus hallii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
