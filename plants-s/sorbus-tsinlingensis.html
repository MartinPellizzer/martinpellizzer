Sorbus tsinlingensis

Sorbus tsinlingensis, commonly known as the Tsingling whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries in the fall. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.
