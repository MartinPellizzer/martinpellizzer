Sesleria varia f. elongata

Sesleria varia f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
