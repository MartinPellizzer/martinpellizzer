Sideritis pauli subsp. calduchii

Sideritis pauli subsp.

calduchii, commonly known as the Calduch's sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pink to purple, attracting various pollinators.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
