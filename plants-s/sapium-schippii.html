Sapium schippii

Sapium schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's gouram, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Sapium schippii is also valued for its ability to purify water, making it popular in aquatic gardens.
