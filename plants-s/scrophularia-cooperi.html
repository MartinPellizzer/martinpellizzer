Scrophularia cooperi

Scrophularia cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's figwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many plants with showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. Its blooming period occurs from June to August, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
