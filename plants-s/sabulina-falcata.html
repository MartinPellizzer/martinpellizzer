Sabulina falcata

Sabulina falcata, commonly known as the bentleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves that are often curled or folded. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Sabulina falcata is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
