Serjania scopulifera

Serjania scopulifera, commonly known as the rock serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Serjania scopulifera is adapted to grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
