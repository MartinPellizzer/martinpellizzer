Silene scouleri var. pringlei

Silene scouleri var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
