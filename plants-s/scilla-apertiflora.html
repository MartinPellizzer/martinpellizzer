Scilla apertiflora

Scilla apertiflora, commonly known as the open-flowered scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in woodland and rocky habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge in late winter.

Scilla apertiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming flowers.
