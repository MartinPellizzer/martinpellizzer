Senecio vaginifolius

Senecio vaginifolius, commonly known as the Cape rose or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Senecio vaginifolius is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
