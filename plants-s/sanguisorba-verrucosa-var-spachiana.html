Sanguisorba verrucosa var. spachiana

Sanguisorba verrucosa var. spachiana, commonly known as the Spach's spurry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and bloom.
