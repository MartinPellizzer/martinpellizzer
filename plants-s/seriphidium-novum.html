Seriphidium novum

Seriphidium novum, commonly known as the New Zealand tarplant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and inland areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers in summer.

Seriphidium novum is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its adaptation to the region's unique climate.
