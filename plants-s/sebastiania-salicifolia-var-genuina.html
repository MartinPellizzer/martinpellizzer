Sebastiania salicifolia var. genuina

Sebastiania salicifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeard, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
