Specklinia pubipetala

Specklinia pubipetala, commonly known as the velvety pigmy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and its unique, hairy stems that give it its scientific name. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, velvety texture on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
