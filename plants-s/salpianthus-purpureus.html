Salpianthus purpureus

Salpianthus purpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf salpianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
