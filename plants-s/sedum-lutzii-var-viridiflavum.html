Sedum lutzii var. viridiflavum

Sedum lutzii var. viridiflavum, commonly known as the green-yellow stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tinge, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
