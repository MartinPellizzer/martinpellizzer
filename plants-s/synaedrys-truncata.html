Synaedrys truncata

Synaedrys truncata, commonly known as the rough-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has a compact rosette structure with fleshy, truncate (cut-off) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its succulent leaves help it store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

Synaedrys truncata is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
