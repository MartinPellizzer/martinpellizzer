Syzygium lancilimbum

Syzygium lancilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Syzygium lancilimbum thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
