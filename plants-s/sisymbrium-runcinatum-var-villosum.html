Sisymbrium runcinatum var. villosum

Sisymbrium runcinatum var. villosum, commonly known as hairy rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.

The plant is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
