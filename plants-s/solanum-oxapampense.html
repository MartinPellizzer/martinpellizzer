Solanum oxapampense

Solanum oxapampense, commonly known as the Oxapampa nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and it produces yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its habitat and chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and researchers studying plant adaptations to high-altitude environments.
