Salix × ustroniensis

Salix × ustroniensis, commonly known as the black willow.

It is a hybrid species within the willow genus, characterized by its dark, almost black bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting rapid growth and a shrub-like form. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers appearing on separate plants.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
