Statice duriuscula subsp. laeta

Statice duriuscula subsp.

laeta, commonly known as the cheerful statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, and purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
