Sideritis hirsuta subsp. vulgaris

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common beardtongue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic features, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
