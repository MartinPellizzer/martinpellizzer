Senecio ianthinus

Senecio ianthinus, commonly known as the blue-Eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flower heads.

Senecio ianthinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
