Schefflera tristis

Schefflera tristis, commonly known as the bitter sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a distinctive, umbrella-like pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Schefflera tristis is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
