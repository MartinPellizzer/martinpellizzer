Sorghum caffrorum var. albofuscum

Sorghum caffrorum var. albofuscum, commonly known as the white-leaf sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pale green to white leaves, which set it apart from other sorghum varieties. The plant typically grows as a tall, annual grass with robust stems and small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its grain or as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
