Solanum loxophyllum

Solanum loxophyllum, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are typically red or yellow when mature.

Solanum loxophyllum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
