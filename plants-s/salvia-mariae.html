Salvia mariae

Salvia mariae, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in gray-green, velvety leaves that are lance-shaped and aromatic. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or purple, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia mariae is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
