Spiraea confusa var. subglabra

Spiraea confusa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth, glabrous texture that distinguishes it from other varieties. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscape design for its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
