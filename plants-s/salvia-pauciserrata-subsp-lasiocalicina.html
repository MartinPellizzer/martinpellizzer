Salvia pauciserrata subsp. lasiocalicina

Salvia pauciserrata subsp.

lasiocalicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense whorls.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
