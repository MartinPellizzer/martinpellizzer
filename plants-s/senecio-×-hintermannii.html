Senecio × hintermannii

Senecio × hintermannii, commonly known as the hybrid African violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio serotinus and Senecio macroglossus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
