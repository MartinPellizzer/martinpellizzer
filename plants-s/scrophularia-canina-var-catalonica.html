Scrophularia canina var. catalonica

Scrophularia canina var. catalonica, commonly known as the Catalan dog's mouth, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its oval, hairy leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
