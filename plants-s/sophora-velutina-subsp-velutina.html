Sophora velutina subsp. velutina

Sophora velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a deciduous legume native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in elongated racemes, followed by pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and floodplains.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
