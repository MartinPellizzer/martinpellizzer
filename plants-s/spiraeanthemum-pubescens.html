Spiraeanthemum pubescens

Spiraeanthemum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spirea, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
