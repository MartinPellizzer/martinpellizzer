Schisandra lancifolia var. polycarpa

Schisandra lancifolia var. polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited five-fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces multiple red berries, which are rich in bioactive compounds and have been used in traditional medicine. Its fruits are typically harvested in late autumn and are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The species is notable for its adaptability to diverse soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
