Staphylea bumalda var. pubescens

Staphylea bumalda var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, nut-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
