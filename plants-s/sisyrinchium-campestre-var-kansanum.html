Sisyrinchium campestre var. kansanum

Sisyrinchium campestre var. kansanum, commonly known as the plains blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
