Senecio paucicephalus

Senecio paucicephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, featuring narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
