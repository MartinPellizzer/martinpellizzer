Salacia laevigata subsp. acuminata

Salacia laevigata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata salacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, orange-red berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
