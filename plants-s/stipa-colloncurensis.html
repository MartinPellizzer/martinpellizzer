Stipa colloncurensis

Stipa colloncurensis, commonly known as the Colloncure's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Stipa colloncurensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil.
