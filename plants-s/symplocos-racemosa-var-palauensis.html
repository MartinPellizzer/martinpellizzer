Symplocos racemosa var. palauensis

Symplocos racemosa var. palauensis, commonly known as the Palau holly, is a flowering plant native to the Caroline Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
