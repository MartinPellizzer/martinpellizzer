Saussurea henryi

Saussurea henryi, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and extensively branched.

Saussurea henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
