Salvia conradii

Salvia conradii, commonly known as the Conradi sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia conradii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
