Salvia farinacea

Salvia farinacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in partial to full sun. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Salvia farinacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
