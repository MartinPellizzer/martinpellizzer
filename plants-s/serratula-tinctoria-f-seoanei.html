Serratula tinctoria f. seoanei

Serratula tinctoria f. seoanei, commonly known as the purple serrated centaury, is a compact, herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, showy purple to violet flowers. The plant features serrated leaf margins, which give it its common name, and it is native to southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dyeing fabrics with its vibrant pigments.
