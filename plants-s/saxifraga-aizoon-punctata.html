Saxifraga aizoon punctata

Saxifraga aizoon punctata, commonly known as the spotted saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
