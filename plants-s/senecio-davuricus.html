Senecio davuricus

Senecio davuricus, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio davuricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
