Senecio varvarcensis var. pinnatus

Senecio varvarcensis var. pinnatus, commonly known as the pinnate senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, pinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it produces showy yellow or orange flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in certain regions.
