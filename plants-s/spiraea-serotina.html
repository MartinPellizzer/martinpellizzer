Spiraea serotina

Spiraea serotina, commonly known as late meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems with clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiraea serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
