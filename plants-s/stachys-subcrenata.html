Stachys subcrenata

Stachys subcrenata, commonly known as silver velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces erect, branching stems.

Stachys subcrenata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
