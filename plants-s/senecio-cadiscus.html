Senecio cadiscus

Senecio cadiscus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Senecio cadiscus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
