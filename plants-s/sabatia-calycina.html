Sabatia calycina

Sabatia calycina, commonly known as the pinkroot or smallflower sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodlands or along roadsides. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Sabatia calycina is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
