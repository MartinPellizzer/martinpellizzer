Serjania peruviana

Serjania peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian serjant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjania, which includes shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody capsules as fruit, containing several seeds.

Serjania peruviana is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
