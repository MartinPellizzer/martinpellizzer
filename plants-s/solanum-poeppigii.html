Solanum poeppigii

Solanum poeppigii, commonly known as the poeppig nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum poeppigii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
