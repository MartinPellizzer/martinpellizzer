Satureja brownei var. pilosiuscula

Satureja brownei var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
