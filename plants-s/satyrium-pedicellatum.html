Satyrium pedicellatum

Satyrium pedicellatum, commonly known as the purple tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
