Selinum agasylloides

Selinum agasylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry habitats.

Selinum agasylloides is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
