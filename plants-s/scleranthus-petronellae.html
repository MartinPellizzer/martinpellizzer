Scleranthus petronellae

Scleranthus petronellae, commonly known as the Petronellae Thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. Its stems are erect and covered with stiff hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Scleranthus petronellae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
