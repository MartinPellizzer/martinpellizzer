Scholtzia laciniata

Scholtzia laciniata, commonly known as the lacey scholtzia, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Scholtzia laciniata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
