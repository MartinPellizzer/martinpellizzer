Symphyotrichum subulatum var. ligulatum

Symphyotrichum subulatum var. ligulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
