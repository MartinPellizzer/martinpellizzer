Spergula jallui

Spergula jallui, commonly known as the Jallui sandwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is typically found in dry, sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Spergula jallui is often considered a rare and endemic species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
