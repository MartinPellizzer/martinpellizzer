Silene burchellii var. macropetala

Silene burchellii var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and open shrublands, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.

The variety macropetala is distinguished by its larger petals compared to the typical form of Silene burchellii.
