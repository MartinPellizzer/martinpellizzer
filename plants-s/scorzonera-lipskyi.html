Scorzonera lipskyi

Scorzonera lipskyi, commonly known as the Siberian scorzonera, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Scorzonera lipskyi is known for its edible tuberous roots, which are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
