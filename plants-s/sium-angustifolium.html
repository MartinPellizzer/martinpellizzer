Sium angustifolium

Sium angustifolium, commonly known as water parsnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically less than 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are cylindrical and often have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Sium angustifolium thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
