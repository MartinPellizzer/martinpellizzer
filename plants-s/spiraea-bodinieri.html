Spiraea bodinieri

Spiraea bodinieri, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of delicate pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include multiple stems, compound inflorescences, and a tendency to form dense, bushy growth.
