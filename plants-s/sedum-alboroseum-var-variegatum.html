Sedum alboroseum var. variegatum

Sedum alboroseum var. variegatum, commonly known as the "White Rose Stonecrop," is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white and green, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
