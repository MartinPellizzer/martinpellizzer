Symphyotrichum turbinellum

Symphyotrichum turbinellum, commonly known as the turbinellum heath.

This perennial herb is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its wiry stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine barrens. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
