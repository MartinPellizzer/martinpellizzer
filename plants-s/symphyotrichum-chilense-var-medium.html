Symphyotrichum chilense var. medium

Symphyotrichum chilense var. medium, commonly known as the Chilean blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
