Silene eranthema

Silene eranthema, commonly known as the common campion, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Silene eranthema is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
