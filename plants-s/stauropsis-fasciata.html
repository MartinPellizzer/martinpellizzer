Stauropsis fasciata

Stauropsis fasciata, commonly known as the broad-leafed stauropsis, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, adding visual interest. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
