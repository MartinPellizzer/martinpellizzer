Senecio loretensis

Senecio loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio loretensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
