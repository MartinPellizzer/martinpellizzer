Sansevieria sarmentosa

Sansevieria sarmentosa, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a succulent plant native to Africa.

It features long, upright leaves that are typically green with yellow or white variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on slender stalks.

Sansevieria sarmentosa is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and air-purifying capabilities.
