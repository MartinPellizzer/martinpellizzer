Scabiosa adzharica

Scabiosa adzharica, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa adzharica is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
