Syringa dilatata f. alba

Syringa dilatata f. alba, commonly known as the white lilac, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and seasonal display.
