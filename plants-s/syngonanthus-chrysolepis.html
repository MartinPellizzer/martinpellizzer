Syngonanthus chrysolepis

Syngonanthus chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow-green color, which distinguishes it from other mistletoe species. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds. It is native to parts of South America, particularly in regions with warm, humid climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree over time if left unchecked.
