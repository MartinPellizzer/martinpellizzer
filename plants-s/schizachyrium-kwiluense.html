Schizachyrium kwiluense

Schizachyrium kwiluense, commonly known as the Kwilu grass, is a perennial grass native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to brown in color.

Schizachyrium kwiluense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
