Stoerkia cranziana

Stoerkia cranziana, commonly known as the Cape goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Stoerkia cranziana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
