Smilax cocculoides var. lanceolata

Smilax cocculoides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf smilax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Smilacaceae.

It is a climbing or trailing vine characterized by its sharp, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the undergrowth vegetation.
