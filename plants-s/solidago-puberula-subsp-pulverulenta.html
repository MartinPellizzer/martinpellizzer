Solidago puberula subsp. pulverulenta

Solidago puberula subsp.

pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
