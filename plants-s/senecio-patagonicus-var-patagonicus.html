Senecio patagonicus var. patagonicus

Senecio patagonicus var. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
