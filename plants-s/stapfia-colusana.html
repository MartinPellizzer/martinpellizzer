Stapfia colusana

Stapfia colusana, commonly known as the white-stemmed stapfia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Stapfia colusana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
