Syzygium guineense subsp. huillense

Syzygium guineense subsp.

huillense, commonly known as the Huilliche sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, yellow-orange fruits.

The plant thrives in coastal and mountainous regions with well-drained soils and a temperate to subtropical climate. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and light grey.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and nutritional properties.
