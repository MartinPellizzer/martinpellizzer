Syzygium cambodianum

Syzygium cambodianum, commonly known as the Cambodian apple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance. Syzygium cambodianum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
