Salix odontophylla

Salix odontophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, which are typical of willow species.

Salix odontophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
