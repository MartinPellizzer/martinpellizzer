Sideritis georgica

Sideritis georgica, commonly known as Georgian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, blooming in summer.

Sideritis georgica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
