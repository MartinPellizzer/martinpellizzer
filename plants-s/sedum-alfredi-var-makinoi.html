Sedum alfredi var. makinoi

Sedum alfredi var. makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it both visually appealing and low maintenance.
