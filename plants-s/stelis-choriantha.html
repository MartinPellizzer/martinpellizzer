Stelis choriantha

Stelis choriantha, commonly known as the purple-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive purple-pink coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stelis choriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
