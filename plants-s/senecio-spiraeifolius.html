Senecio spiraeifolius

Senecio spiraeifolius, commonly known as the spiralleaf groundseller, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, spiraling leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
