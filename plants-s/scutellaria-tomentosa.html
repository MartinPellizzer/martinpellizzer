Scutellaria tomentosa

Scutellaria tomentosa, commonly known as wooly blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a square stem with opposite leaves.

Scutellaria tomentosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
