Syzygium oblongifolium

Syzygium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing sweet, acidic pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
