Squilla

Squilla, commonly known as the rock shell or sea squill, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its bulbous underground stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often pink or white and bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Squilla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
