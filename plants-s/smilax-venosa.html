Smilax venosa

Smilax venosa, commonly known as the veined arrowroot or red root, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of spiny stems, which help it to secure itself to supports. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern, contributing to its common name.

Smilax venosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
