Senecio leonensis

Senecio leonensis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique, feathery appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Senecio leonensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
