Syringa komarowii var. sargentiana

Syringa komarowii var. sargentiana, commonly known as the Sargent viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
