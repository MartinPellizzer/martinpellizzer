Salsola sieberi subsp. deschaseauxiana

Salsola sieberi subsp.

deschaseauxiana, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Its robust root system allows it to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone habitats.
