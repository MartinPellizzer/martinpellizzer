Scirpus validus

Scirpus validus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus validus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
