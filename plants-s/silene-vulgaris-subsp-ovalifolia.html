Silene vulgaris subsp. ovalifolia

Silene vulgaris subsp.

ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are oval-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
