Solanum pachytrichum

Solanum pachytrichum, commonly known as the thick-hairy nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
