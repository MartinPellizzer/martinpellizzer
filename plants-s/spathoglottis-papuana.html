Spathoglottis papuana

Spathoglottis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan lady's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces pseudobulbs and has long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Spathoglottis papuana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
