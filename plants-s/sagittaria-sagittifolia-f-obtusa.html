Sagittaria sagittifolia f. obtusa

Sagittaria sagittifolia f. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are typically floating or submerged, depending on water levels, and it is often used in aquatic gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
