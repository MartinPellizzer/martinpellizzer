Salix × poelliana

Salix × poelliana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix cinerea and Salix viminalis, characterized by its flexible branches and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its catkins are a notable feature, with male and female flowers appearing on separate plants.

Salix × poelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in horticulture for its adaptability and resilience.
