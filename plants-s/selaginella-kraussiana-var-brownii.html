Selaginella kraussiana var. brownii

Selaginella kraussiana var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's spikemoss, is a low-growing evergreen fern-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of scale-like leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are distinctive and resemble small, cone-like structures.

Selaginella kraussiana var. brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and resilient nature.
