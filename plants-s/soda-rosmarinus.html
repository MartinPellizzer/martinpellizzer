Soda rosmarinus

Soda rosmarinus, commonly known as sweet sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue or purple flowers. The plant contains essential oils, particularly camphor and cineole, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
