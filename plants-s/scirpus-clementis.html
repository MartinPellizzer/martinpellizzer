Scirpus clementis

Scirpus clementis, commonly known as the Clement's bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the top of its stem. Scirpus clementis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its presence helps in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
