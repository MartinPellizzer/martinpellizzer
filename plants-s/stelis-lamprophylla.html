Stelis lamprophylla

Stelis lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver hairs. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stelis lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
