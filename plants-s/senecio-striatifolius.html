Senecio striatifolius

Senecio striatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
