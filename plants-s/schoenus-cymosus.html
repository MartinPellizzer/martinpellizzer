Schoenus cymosus

Schoenus cymosus, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and stream banks. The plant has triangular stems that are often reddish in color and bear dense, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Schoenus cymosus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
