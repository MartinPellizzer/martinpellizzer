Sasa kurilensis var. cernua

Sasa kurilensis var. cernua, commonly known as the drooping fern grass, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often inconspicuous compared to the plant's vegetative structure.
