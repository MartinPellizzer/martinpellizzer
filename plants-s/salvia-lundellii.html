Salvia lundellii

Salvia lundellii, commonly known as Lundell's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and showy violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
