Sarcocaulon inerme

Sarcocaulon inerme, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
