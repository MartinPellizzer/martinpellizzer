Salvia gilliesii var. glandulosa

Salvia gilliesii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong scent when crushed. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are typically hairy and grow upright, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
