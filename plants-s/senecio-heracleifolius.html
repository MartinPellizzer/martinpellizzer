Senecio heracleifolius

Senecio heracleifolius, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
