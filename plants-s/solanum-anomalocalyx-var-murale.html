Solanum anomalocalyx var. murale

Solanum anomalocalyx var. murale, commonly known as the wall nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing on rocky walls or in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
