Sideritis montana subsp. remota

Sideritis montana subsp.

remota, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Sideritis montana subsp.

remota is valued for its medicinal uses and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
