Sideritis vestita

Sideritis vestita, commonly known as the white-haired ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis vestita is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
