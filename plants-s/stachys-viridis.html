Stachys viridis

Stachys viridis, commonly known as green betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine greenish or grayish pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Stachys viridis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
