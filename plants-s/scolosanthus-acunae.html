Scolosanthus acunae

Scolosanthus acunae, commonly known as the Andean fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Scolosanthus acunae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
