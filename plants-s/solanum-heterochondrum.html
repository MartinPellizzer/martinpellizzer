Solanum heterochondrum

Solanum heterochondrum, commonly known as the nightshade or desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by orange to red berries. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
