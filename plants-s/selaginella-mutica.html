Selaginella mutica

Selaginella mutica, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems covered in scale-like leaves. Selaginella mutica reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage.

It is often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks, showcasing its adaptability to various soil conditions.
