Sorocea opima

Sorocea opima, commonly known as the golden flame fern, is a rare and striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow fronds that contrast sharply with its dark green undersides. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its intricate frond structure consists of a central rachis with numerous pinnae, some of which are curled or twisted.

Sorocea opima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
