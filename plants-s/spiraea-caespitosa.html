Spiraea caespitosa

Spiraea caespitosa, commonly known as dwarf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Spiraea caespitosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
