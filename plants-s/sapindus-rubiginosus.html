Sapindus rubiginosus

Sapindus rubiginosus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to the subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces distinctive, spherical fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. The fruit's outer layer is known for its soapy texture, which gives the plant its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in various cultures.
