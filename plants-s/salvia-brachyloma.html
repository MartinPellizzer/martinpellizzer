Salvia brachyloma

Salvia brachyloma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Salvia brachyloma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
