Silene gallica var. myloptera

Silene gallica var. myloptera, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slight pubescence, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene gallica var. myloptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
