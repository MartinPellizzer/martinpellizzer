Scabiosa crenata subsp. macropoda

Scabiosa crenata subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaved scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and its role in supporting pollinators.
