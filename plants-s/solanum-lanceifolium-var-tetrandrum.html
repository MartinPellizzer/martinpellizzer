Solanum lanceifolium var. tetrandrum

Solanum lanceifolium var. tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-angled nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features woody stems with four ridges, giving it a distinctive angular appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are dark blue when mature and contain a high concentration of alkaloids.

This species is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun.
