Sporobolus inflatus

Sporobolus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated spikerush, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and cylindrical, inflated spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense stands. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

Sporobolus inflatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
