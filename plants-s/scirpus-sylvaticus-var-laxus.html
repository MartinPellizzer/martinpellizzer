Scirpus sylvaticus var. laxus

Scirpus sylvaticus var. laxus, commonly known as the loose spike rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as marshes, pond margins, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowering stems are usually more loosely arranged than in other varieties of Scirpus.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
