Saxifraga longistyla

Saxifraga longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Saxifraga longistyla thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

It is a perennial plant that is well adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
