Saccolabium pachyglossum

Saccolabium pachyglossum, commonly known as the broad-lipped saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and water-storing tissues. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
