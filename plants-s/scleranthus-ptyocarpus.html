Scleranthus ptyocarpus

Scleranthus ptyocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called achene, which are typically surrounded by a pappus for wind dispersal. Scleranthus ptyocarpus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native habitat.
