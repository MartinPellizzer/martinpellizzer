Saxifraga nervosa subsp. exarata

Saxifraga nervosa subsp.

exarata, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine veins.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer, typically arranged in a terminal cluster. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens as a hardy, ornamental plant.
