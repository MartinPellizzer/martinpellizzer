Silene filisecta

Silene filisecta, commonly known as the threadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is an annual herb that produces capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

Silene filisecta plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
