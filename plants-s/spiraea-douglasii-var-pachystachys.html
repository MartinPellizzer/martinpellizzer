Spiraea douglasii var. pachystachys

Spiraea douglasii var. pachystachys, commonly known as the Douglas spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, multi-stemmed growth with dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and seasonal color changes.
