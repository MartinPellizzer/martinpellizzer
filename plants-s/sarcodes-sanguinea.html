Sarcodes sanguinea

Sarcodes sanguinea, commonly known as the red milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals.

Sarcodes sanguinea plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
