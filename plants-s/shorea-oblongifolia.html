Shorea oblongifolia

Shorea oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leafed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and heavy, durable wood. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and is an important component of the forest canopy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Shorea oblongifolia plays a significant role in the ecological and economic systems of its native habitats.
