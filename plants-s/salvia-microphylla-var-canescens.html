Salvia microphylla var. canescens

Salvia microphylla var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
