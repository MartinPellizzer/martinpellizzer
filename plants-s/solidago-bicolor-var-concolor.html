Solidago bicolor var. concolor

Solidago bicolor var. concolor, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, disk-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
