Spinifex dioicus

Spinifex dioicus, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Spinifex dioicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
