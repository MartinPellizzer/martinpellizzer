Salvia wagneriana

Salvia wagneriana, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
