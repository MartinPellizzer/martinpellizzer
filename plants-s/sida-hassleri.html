Sida hassleri

Sida hassleri, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herb with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sida hassleri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
