Searsia krebsiana

Searsia krebsiana, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

The Cape snowball is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
