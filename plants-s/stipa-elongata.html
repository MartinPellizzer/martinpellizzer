Stipa elongata

Stipa elongata, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces spikelets that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Stipa elongata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
