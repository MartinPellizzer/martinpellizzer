Stevia resinosa

Stevia resinosa, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia resinosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
