Silene × hampeana

Silene × hampeana, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene × hampeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
