Sasaella viridistriata var. vagans

Sasaella viridistriata var. vagans, commonly known as the creeping bamboo, is a variant of the green-striated sasaella.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan. The plant features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, grassy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
