Salvia rigida f. media

Salvia rigida f. media, commonly known as the compact blue salvia, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces showy flower spikes in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
