Sporoxeia imparifolia

Sporoxeia imparifolia, commonly known as the irregular-leaved sporoxeia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are typically asymmetrical, with irregularly shaped lobes that set it apart from other members of the Sporoxeaceae family. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered along the stems.

The botanical study of Sporoxeia imparifolia provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of spore-producing plants in diverse ecological settings.
