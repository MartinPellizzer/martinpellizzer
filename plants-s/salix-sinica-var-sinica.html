Salix sinica var. sinica

Salix sinica var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces catkins, which are the primary floral structures, and it is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It prefers moist environments and is often found near rivers or in wetland areas.

The bark of Salix sinica var. sinica is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
