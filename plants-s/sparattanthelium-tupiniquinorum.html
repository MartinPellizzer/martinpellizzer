Sparattanthelium tupiniquinorum

Sparattanthelium tupiniquinorum, commonly known as the Tupiniquin Yellowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically yellow in color, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal climates with distinct wet and dry periods.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in native plant gardens.
