Scorzonera austriaca

Scorzonera austriaca, commonly known as Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, followed by spherical, spiky fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Scorzonera austriaca is valued for its edible roots, which have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.
