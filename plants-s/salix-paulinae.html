Salix paulinae

Salix paulinae, commonly known as the Pauline willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

Salix paulinae is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.
