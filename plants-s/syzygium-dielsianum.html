Syzygium dielsianum

Syzygium dielsianum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark is thick and rugged, often exuding a resinous scent.

Syzygium dielsianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
