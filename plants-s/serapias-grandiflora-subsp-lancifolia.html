Serapias grandiflora subsp. lancifolia

Serapias grandiflora subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in wet meadows and marshy habitats, often near water sources. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the moist environments it inhabits.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
