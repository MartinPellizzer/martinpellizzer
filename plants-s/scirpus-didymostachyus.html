Scirpus didymostachyus

Scirpus didymostachyus, commonly known as the two-stamen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with moist to wet soils. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, forming dense clumps with tall, cylindrical stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers.

Scirpus didymostachyus plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
