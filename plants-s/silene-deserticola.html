Silene deserticola

Silene deserticola, commonly known as the desert catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Silene deserticola is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

It plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators.
