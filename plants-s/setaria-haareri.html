Setaria haareri

Setaria haareri, commonly known as the African dogstail grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that resemble a dog's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Setaria haareri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in various environmental conditions.
