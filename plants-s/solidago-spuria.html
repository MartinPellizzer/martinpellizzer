Solidago spuria

Solidago spuria, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Solidago spuria plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
