Stigmatopteris pellucidopunctata

Stigmatopteris pellucidopunctata, commonly known as the translucent-punctate shield fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, shield-shaped fronds with a unique pattern of translucent dots along the veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in high humidity and partial shade. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a linear pattern on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
