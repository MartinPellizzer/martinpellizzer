Scirpus romanus

Scirpus romanus, commonly known as the Roman bulrush, is a tall, aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its stiff, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water and can tolerate a range of soil conditions. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus romanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and prevent erosion.
