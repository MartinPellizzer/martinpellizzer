Stenochlaena oleandrifolia

Stenochlaena oleandrifolia, commonly known as the creeping arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months.

The species is valued for its edible tubers and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
