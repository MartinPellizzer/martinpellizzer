Saccolabium tortifolium

Saccolabium tortifolium, commonly known as the twisted-leaf saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive twisted or curled appearance, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Saccolabium tortifolium is well adapted to low-nutrient soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
