Saxifraga lapeyrousei

Saxifraga lapeyrousei, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh conditions such as strong winds and limited soil nutrients. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Saxifraga lapeyrousei is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.
