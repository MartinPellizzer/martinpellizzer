Sisymbrium sinapistrum

Sisymbrium sinapistrum, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its foliage has a peppery scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
