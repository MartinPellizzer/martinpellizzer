Sphalanthus mussaendiflorus

Sphalanthus mussaendiflorus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, green, sour fruits that turn orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
