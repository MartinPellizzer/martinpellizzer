Syringa oblata var. donaldii

Syringa oblata var. donaldii, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and thrives in well-drained soil.

Syringa oblata var. donaldii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral displays.
