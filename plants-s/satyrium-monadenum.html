Satyrium monadenum

Satyrium monadenum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
