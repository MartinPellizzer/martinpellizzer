Sideroxylon obtusifolium subsp. buxifolium

Sideroxylon obtusifolium subsp.

buxifolium, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that resemble those of boxwood plants.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
