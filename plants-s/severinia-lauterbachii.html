Severinia lauterbachii

Severinia lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-black berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
