Solanum oblongum

Solanum oblongum, commonly known as the long pepper plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spiny fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its aromatic leaves and berries. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, elongated fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in various parts of the world.
