Silene insubrica

Silene insubrica, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Silene insubrica is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
