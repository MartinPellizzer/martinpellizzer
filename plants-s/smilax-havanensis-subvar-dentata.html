Smilax havanensis subvar. dentata

Smilax havanensis subvar. dentata, commonly known as the toothed smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
