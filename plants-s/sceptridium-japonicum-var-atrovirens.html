Sceptridium japonicum var. atrovirens

Sceptridium japonicum var. atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
