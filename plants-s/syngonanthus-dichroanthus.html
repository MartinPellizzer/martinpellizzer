Syngonanthus dichroanthus

Syngonanthus dichroanthus, commonly known as the two-colored flame tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its striking, bright red and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, dark brown fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
