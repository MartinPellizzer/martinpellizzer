Senecio nemorensis subsp. glabratus

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a taproot system and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
