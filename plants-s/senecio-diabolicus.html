Senecio diabolicus

Senecio diabolicus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features elongated, ribbed stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often adorned with spines and a reddish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
