Salvia linkiana

Salvia linkiana, commonly known as the Link's sage, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces vibrant violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
