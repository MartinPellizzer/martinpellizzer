Syngonanthus tenuis var. tenuis

Syngonanthus tenuis var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces white or pale green berries that are consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is native to parts of South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found.
