Salacia oblongifolia

Salacia oblongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf salacia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Salacia oblongifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
