Sebastiania brasiliensis var. polymorpha

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. polymorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright orange, star-shaped fruits. The fruits are edible, with a juicy, sweet pulp that contains numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
