Syzygium aporematum

Syzygium aporematum, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Syzygium aporematum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
