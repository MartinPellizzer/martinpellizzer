Salvia labellifera

Salvia labellifera, commonly known as the showy salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
