Styrax orizabensis

Styrax orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Styrax orizabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic resins.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
