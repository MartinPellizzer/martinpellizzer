Saldinia axillaris var. strigosa

Saldinia axillaris var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy saldinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while the stems are often branched and upright.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
