Seymeria falcata var. falcata

Seymeria falcata var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed seymeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
