Saxifraga aizoides var. aurantia

Saxifraga aizoides var. aurantia, commonly known as the orange saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces striking clusters of bright orange or red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
