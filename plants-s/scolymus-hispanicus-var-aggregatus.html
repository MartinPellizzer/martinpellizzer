Scolymus hispanicus var. aggregatus

Scolymus hispanicus var. aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
