Spiraea japonica var. formosana

Spiraea japonica var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, making it popular in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
