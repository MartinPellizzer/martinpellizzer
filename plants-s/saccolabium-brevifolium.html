Saccolabium brevifolium

Saccolabium brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
