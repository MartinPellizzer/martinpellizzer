Sesbania occidentalis var. multijuga

Sesbania occidentalis var. multijuga, commonly known as the West African sesbania, is a tropical legume native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions. It has a shallow root system and is known for its rapid growth, making it useful for soil stabilization.

Additionally, it is a nitrogen-fixing plant, contributing to soil fertility through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
