Symplocos naniflora

Symplocos naniflora, commonly known as the dwarf waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos naniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and year-round foliage.
