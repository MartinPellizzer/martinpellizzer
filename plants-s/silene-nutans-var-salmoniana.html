Silene nutans var. salmoniana

Silene nutans var. salmoniana, commonly known as the salmon pink campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to salmon-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
