Statice patens

Statice patens, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, showy flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, saline soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its flowers are typically pink, purple, or white, and they bloom from summer to autumn.

Statice patens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
