Senecio hachanus var. gabinetensis

Senecio hachanus var. gabinetensis, commonly known as the Hachan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
