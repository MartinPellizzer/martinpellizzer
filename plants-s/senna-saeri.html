Senna saeri

Senna saeri, commonly known as the mountain senna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Senna saeri typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential health benefits.
