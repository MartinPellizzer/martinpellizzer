Schizoglossum parvulum var. sessile

Schizoglossum parvulum var. sessile, commonly known as the small-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.
