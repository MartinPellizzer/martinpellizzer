Sanguisorba cretica

Sanguisorba cretica, commonly known as redroot sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features upright, branched stems that are covered with small, reddish-brown leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing small, pink to reddish-purple blossoms.

Sanguisorba cretica is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
