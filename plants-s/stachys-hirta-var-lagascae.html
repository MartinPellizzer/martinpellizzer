Stachys hirta var. lagascae

Stachys hirta var. lagascae, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and produces spikes of violet or blue-lilac flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
