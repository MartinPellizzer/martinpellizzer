Scutellaria subintegra

Scutellaria subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria subintegra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
