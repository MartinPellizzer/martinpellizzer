Sedum tsiangii var. torquatum

Sedum tsiangii var. torquatum, commonly known as the twisted stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often curl or twist, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
