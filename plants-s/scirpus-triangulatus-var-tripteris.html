Scirpus triangulatus var. tripteris

Scirpus triangulatus var. tripteris, commonly known as the triangular bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and typically greenish or brownish in color.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
