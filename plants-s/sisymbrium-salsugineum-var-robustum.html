Sisymbrium salsugineum var. robustum

Sisymbrium salsugineum var. robustum, commonly known as field radish, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
