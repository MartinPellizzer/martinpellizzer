Senecio taraxacoides var. nudatus

Senecio taraxacoides var. nudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are typically arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to arid landscape designs.
