Sciodaphyllum tremuloideum

Sciodaphyllum tremuloideum, commonly known as the trembling shadow pine, is a unique evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, scale-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests where it receives diffuse light. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
