Symplocos tribracteolata

Symplocos tribracteolata, commonly known as the three-bract elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark red or black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and dark green upper surface.

The fruit is edible and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
