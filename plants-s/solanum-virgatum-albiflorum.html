Solanum virgatum albiflorum

Solanum virgatum albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often blooming in the evening.

The fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
