Sutherlandia speciosa

Sutherlandia speciosa, commonly known as the cancer bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody, branching stems. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. Sutherlandia speciosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It has been traditionally used in African herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
