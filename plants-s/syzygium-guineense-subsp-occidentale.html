Syzygium guineense subsp. occidentale

Syzygium guineense subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the West African apple or red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is typically round or oval, with a bright red or purple rind and a segmented, juicy interior. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though the latter is less commonly consumed compared to other Syzygium species.
