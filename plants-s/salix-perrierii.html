Salix perrierii

Salix perrierii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The tree produces catkins as its primary floral structure, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning yellow in the fall.

Salix perrierii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
