Sarcostemma viminale subsp. odontolepis

Sarcostemma viminale subsp.

odontolepis, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It features woody stems with a distinctive, hairy texture and produces small, white or pale green flowers.

The plant bears yellowish-orange berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family.
