Sideroxylon foetidissimum

Sideroxylon foetidissimum, commonly known as the stinking ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a distinct, unpleasant odor.

The flowers are small and usually greenish-white, and the tree produces woody fruits containing seeds.
