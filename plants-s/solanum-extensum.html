Solanum extensum

Solanum extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spreading growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into rounded, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Solanum extensum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sunlight.
