Sutera dentatisepala

Sutera dentatisepala, commonly known as the serrated-sepal sutera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Suteraeaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, toothed sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Sutera dentatisepala is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or near streams.
