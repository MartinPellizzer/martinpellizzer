Scaevola parvifolia subsp. pilbarae

Scaevola parvifolia subsp.

pilbarae, commonly known as the Pilbara scaevola, is a flowering plant native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia. It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it suitable for coastal and dry garden settings.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
