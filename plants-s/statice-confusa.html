Statice confusa

Statice confusa, commonly known as the common statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many drought-tolerant species. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, brightly colored flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Statice confusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
