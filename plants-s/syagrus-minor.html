Syagrus minor

Syagrus minor, commonly known as the dwarf palm or Brazilian palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to Brazil.

It typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant features pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and feathery in appearance. Syagrus minor produces small, white flowers that develop into orange-red fruits, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
