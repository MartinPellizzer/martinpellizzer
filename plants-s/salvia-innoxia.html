Salvia innoxia

Salvia innoxia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a grayish-green color.

Salvia innoxia is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
