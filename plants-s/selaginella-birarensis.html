Selaginella birarensis

Selaginella birarensis, commonly known as the Birarensis spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and strobilus structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, contain spores that are dispersed by wind.

Selaginella birarensis is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and its potential use in horticulture.
