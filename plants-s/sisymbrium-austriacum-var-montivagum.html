Sisymbrium austriacum var. montivagum

Sisymbrium austriacum var. montivagum, commonly known as the Alpine mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in alpine and rocky environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
