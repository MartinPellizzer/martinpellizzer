Sisyrinchium chilense subsp. macranthum

Sisyrinchium chilense subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the Chilean blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to South America. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
