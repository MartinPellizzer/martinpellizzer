Sisymbrium irio var. dasycarpum

Sisymbrium irio var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, with small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
