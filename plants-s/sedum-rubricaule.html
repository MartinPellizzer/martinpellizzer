Sedum rubricaule

Sedum rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and reddish stems that become more pronounced in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum rubricaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
