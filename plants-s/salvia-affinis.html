Salvia affinis

Salvia affinis, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for gardens and pollinator habitats.
