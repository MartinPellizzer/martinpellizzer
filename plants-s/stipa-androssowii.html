Stipa androssowii

Stipa androssowii, commonly known as the Androssow's feathergrass, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa androssowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
