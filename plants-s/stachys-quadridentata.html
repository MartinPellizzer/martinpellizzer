Stachys quadridentata

Stachys quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys quadridentata is often found in moist, fertile soils and is adapted to humid climates.
