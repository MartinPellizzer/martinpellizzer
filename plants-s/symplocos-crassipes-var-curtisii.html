Symplocos crassipes var. curtisii

Symplocos crassipes var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
