Senecio castagneanus

Senecio castagneanus, commonly known as the Castagnean Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
