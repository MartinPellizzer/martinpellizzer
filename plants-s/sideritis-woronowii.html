Sideritis woronowii

Sideritis woronowii, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Sideritis woronowii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
