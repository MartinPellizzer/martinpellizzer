Saussurea Glabrescens

Saussurea glabrescens is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau. It is known for its tall, spindly stalks and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. This species is often found in rocky, arid environments where few plants can survive. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
