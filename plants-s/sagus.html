Sagus

Sagus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy blooms that emerge from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of ponds and lakes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The symbolic significance of Sagus in various cultures further highlights its botanical and cultural importance.
