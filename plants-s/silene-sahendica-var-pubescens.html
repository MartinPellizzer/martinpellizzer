Silene sahendica var. pubescens

Silene sahendica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine environments.
