Spiraea × watsoniana

Spiraea × watsoniana, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Spiraea × watsoniana thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
