Spondias graveolens

Spondias graveolens, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical tree belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit with a fragrant, yellowish-green exterior and a creamy, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, medicinal properties, and ornamental appeal.
