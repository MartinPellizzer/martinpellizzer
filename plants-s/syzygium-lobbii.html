Syzygium lobbii

Syzygium lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, sour berries that are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Syzygium lobbii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
