Solanum boldoense

Solanum boldoense, commonly known as Boldo bush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.
