Stemmatophyllum sessilifolium

Stemmatophyllum sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaf stemmatophyllum, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its short, sturdy stems and sessile leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters with soft sediment, where it can anchor itself firmly. The leaves are narrow and lack petioles, attaching directly to the stem, which helps reduce water resistance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered at the base of the plant.

This species plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
