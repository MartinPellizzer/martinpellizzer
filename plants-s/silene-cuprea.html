Silene cuprea

Silene cuprea, commonly known as red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Silene cuprea thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.

It is often found in moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
