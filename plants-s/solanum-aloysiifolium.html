Solanum aloysiifolium

Solanum aloysiifolium, commonly known as the Aloysiifolium nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and dark green, elongated leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption or cultivation in edible gardens.
