Saxifraga unguiculata subvar. aurea

Saxifraga unguiculata subvar. aurea, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

This species is often found in mountainous and tundra habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
