Scillopsis liliiflora

Scillopsis liliiflora, commonly known as the fragrant lily-of-the-valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers with a sweet, spicy fragrance. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and aromatic qualities.

Scillopsis liliiflora is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
