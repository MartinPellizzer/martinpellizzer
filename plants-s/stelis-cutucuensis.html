Stelis cutucuensis

Stelis cutucuensis, commonly known as the Cutucu orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Stelis cutucuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
