Solanum weberbaueri var. poscoanum

Solanum weberbaueri var. poscoanum, commonly known as the Poscoan nightshade, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. The fruit is a smooth, berry-like structure that ripens to a deep purple or black color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.
