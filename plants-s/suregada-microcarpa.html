Suregada microcarpa

Suregada microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited suregada, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, dry pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a forage plant.
