Solanum decemdentatum

Solanum decemdentatum, commonly known as the ten-toothed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny fruits and hairy leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in the Andes region.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for cultivation or consumption.
