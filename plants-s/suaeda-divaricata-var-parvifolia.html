Suaeda divaricata var. parvifolia

Suaeda divaricata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea blite, is a halophytic shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of the Middle East.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate high salinity.
