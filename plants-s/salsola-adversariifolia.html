Salsola adversariifolia

Salsola adversariifolia, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help retain water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are small and dry, facilitating wind dispersal in its native habitats.

Salsola adversariifolia is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and disturbed soils where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy or saline substrates.
