Solanum pereirae

Solanum pereirae, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and violet or purple flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum pereirae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
