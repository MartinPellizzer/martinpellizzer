Sporobolus sporobolus

Sporobolus sporobolus, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sporobolus sporobolus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
