Skimmia laureola subsp. multinervia

Skimmia laureola subsp.

multinervia, commonly known as the fragrant skimmia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It features glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late winter or early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and seasonal blooms.
