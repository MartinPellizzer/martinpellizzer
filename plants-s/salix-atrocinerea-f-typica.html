Salix atrocinerea f. typica

Salix atrocinerea f. typica, commonly known as black willow.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its dark, fissured bark and slender, willow-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its flowers are catkin-like, appearing in early spring, and it produces small, oval berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
