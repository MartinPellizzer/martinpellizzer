Solidago bicolor var. paniculata

Solidago bicolor var. paniculata, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are arranged in panicles, which are branched clusters, and they attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
