Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp. insularis

Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Mediterranean santolina, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and container gardening.
