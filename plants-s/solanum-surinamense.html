Solanum surinamense

Solanum surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers and produces berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a hairy texture.
