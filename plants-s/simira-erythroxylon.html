Simira erythroxylon

Simira erythroxylon, commonly known as the red wood or red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight trunk and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of tropical forest ecosystems.
