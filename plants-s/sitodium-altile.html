Sitodium altile

Sitodium altile, commonly known as the narrowleaf sitodium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Sitodium altile is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
