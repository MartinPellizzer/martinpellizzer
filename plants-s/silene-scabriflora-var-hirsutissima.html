Silene scabriflora var. hirsutissima

Silene scabriflora var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to the plant's distinctive appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
