Satureja reptans

Satureja reptans, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

Satureja reptans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
