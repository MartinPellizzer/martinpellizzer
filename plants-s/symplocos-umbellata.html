Symplocos umbellata

Symplocos umbellata, commonly known as the white-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bright blue berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Symplocos umbellata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
