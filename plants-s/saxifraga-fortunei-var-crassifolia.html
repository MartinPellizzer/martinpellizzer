Saxifraga fortunei var. crassifolia

Saxifraga fortunei var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
