Sempervivum × pyrenaicum

Sempervivum × pyrenaicum, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with fleshy, often bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a powdery coating. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
