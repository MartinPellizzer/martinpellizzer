Salvia trichoclada

Salvia trichoclada, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia trichoclada thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
