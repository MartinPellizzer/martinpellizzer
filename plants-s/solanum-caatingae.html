Solanum caatingae

Solanum caatingae, commonly known as the caatinga tomato, is a shrub native to Brazil's semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor. It thrives in poor, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
