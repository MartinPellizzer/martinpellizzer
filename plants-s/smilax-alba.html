Smilax alba

Smilax alba, commonly known as white smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. Smilax alba thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
