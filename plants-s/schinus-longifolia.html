Schinus longifolia

Schinus longifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its botanical profile. It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango.

Schinus longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
