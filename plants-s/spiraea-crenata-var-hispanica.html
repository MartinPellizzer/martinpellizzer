Spiraea crenata var. hispanica

Spiraea crenata var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
