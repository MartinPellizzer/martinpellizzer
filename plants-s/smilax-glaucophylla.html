Smilax glaucophylla

Smilax glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with spiny nodes and is characterized by its broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
