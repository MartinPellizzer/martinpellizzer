Solanum maternum

Solanum maternum, commonly known as the mother's tomato, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and berries. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh conditions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the fruits are small, fleshy berries that ripen to a red color.

Solanum maternum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
