Sorbus decipiens subsp. semiincisa

Sorbus decipiens subsp.

semiincisa, commonly known as the western mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
