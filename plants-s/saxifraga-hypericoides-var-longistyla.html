Saxifraga hypericoides var. longistyla

Saxifraga hypericoides var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright, branched stems that bear small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface and a serrated margin.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated staminodes, which give it its specific epithet "longistyla.".
