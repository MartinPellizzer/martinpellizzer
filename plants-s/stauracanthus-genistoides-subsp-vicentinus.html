Stauracanthus genistoides subsp. vicentinus

Stauracanthus genistoides subsp.

vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentine broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
