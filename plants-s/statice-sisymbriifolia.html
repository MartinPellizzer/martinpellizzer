Statice sisymbriifolia

Statice sisymbriifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes and other coastal habitats. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting hairs.

Statice sisymbriifolia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its long-lasting blooms and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
