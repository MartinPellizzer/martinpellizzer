Sideritis scordioides var. latifolia

Sideritis scordioides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Sideritis species are valued for their medicinal properties, with this variety being no exception.
