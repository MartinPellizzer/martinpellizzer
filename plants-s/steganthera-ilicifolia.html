Steganthera ilicifolia

Steganthera ilicifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved holly.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The leaves are leathery and have a distinctive rough texture, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
