Stachytarpheta andersonii

Stachytarpheta andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Stachytarpheta andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
