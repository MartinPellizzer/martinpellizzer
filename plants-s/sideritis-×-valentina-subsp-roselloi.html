Sideritis × valentina subsp. roselloi

Sideritis × valentina subsp.

roselloi, commonly known as the Rosello's sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
