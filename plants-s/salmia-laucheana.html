Salmia laucheana

Salmia laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's salvia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with two-lipped petals typical of the mint family.

Salmia laucheana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
