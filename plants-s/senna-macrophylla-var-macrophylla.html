Senna macrophylla var. macrophylla

Senna macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are bright green and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. These flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is the floral emblem of Australia.
