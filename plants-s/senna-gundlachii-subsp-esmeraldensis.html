Senna gundlachii subsp. esmeraldensis

Senna gundlachii subsp.

esmeraldensis, commonly known as the Emerald Senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
