Saussurea nipponica var. nikoensis

Saussurea nipponica var. nikoensis, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
