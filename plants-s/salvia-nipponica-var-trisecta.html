Salvia nipponica var. trisecta

Salvia nipponica var. trisecta, commonly known as the Japanese cutleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features striking, triangular, trifoliate leaves that are finely serrated and provide a distinctive visual appeal. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering.
