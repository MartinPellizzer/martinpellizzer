Stylidium schizanthum

Stylidium schizanthum, commonly known as the splintered sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting specific pollinators.

Stylidium schizanthum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability.
