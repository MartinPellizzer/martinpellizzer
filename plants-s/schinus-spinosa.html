Schinus spinosa

Schinus spinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red drupes. The plant is evergreen, with a bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruits are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Schinus spinosa is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
