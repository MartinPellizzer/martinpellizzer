Saxifraga greenei

Saxifraga greenei, commonly known as Greene's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in alpine and subalpine regions.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, often white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to retain water in harsh climates.

Saxifraga greenei is often found in meadows and on mountain slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
