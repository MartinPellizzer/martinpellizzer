Sagraea aphanantha

Sagraea aphanantha, commonly known as the slender sagraea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Sagraea aphanantha thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a notable species in the flora of Socotra.
