Saccharum pallidum

Saccharum pallidum, commonly known as the pale sugarcane.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, blade-like leaves. It produces a sugary sap that can be harvested for various industrial and commercial uses.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in warm climates with ample sunlight and water.
