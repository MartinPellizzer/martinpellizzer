Schizaea tenella

Schizaea tenella, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Schizaeaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping rhizomes and erect, spore-bearing cones called strobila. Its leaves are usually in pairs, with the lower pair being larger and more elongated.

Schizaea tenella plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
