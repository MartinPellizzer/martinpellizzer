Sclerolobium tinctorium

Sclerolobium tinctorium, commonly known as the crimson glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sclerolobium tinctorium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
