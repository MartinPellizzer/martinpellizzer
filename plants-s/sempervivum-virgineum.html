Sempervivum virgineum

Sempervivum virgineum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the European Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a reddish tint, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum virgineum is also known for its ability to survive harsh winter conditions.
