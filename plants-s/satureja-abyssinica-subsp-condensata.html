Satureja abyssinica subsp. condensata

Satureja abyssinica subsp.

condensata, commonly known as Ethiopian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often mint-scented foliage.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strongly aromatic, thyme-like scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
