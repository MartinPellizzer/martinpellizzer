Sasa kurilensis f. shirafu

Sasa kurilensis f. shirafu, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Shirafu bamboo, is a clump-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.
