Stevia veronicae var. gratioloides

Stevia veronicae var. gratioloides, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with small, glandular dots, which produce the plant's characteristic sweet taste. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Stevia veronicae var. gratioloides is also valued for its natural sweetening properties and is used in herbal teas and as a natural sugar substitute.
