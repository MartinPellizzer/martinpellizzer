Saxifraga moschata subf. aragonensis

Saxifraga moschata subf.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and it produces small, white or pink flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
