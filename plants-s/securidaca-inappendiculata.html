Securidaca inappendiculata

Securidaca inappendiculata, commonly known as the African blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The plant is also known for its latex sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
