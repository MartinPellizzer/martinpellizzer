Senecio hubertia

Senecio hubertia, commonly known as the blueweed or bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Senecio hubertia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
