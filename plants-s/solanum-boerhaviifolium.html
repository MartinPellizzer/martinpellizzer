Solanum boerhaviifolium

Solanum boerhaviifolium, commonly known as the Boerhavia leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a woody stem with glandular hairs. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
