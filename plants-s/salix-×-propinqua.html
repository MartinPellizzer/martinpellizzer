Salix × propinqua

Salix × propinqua, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green on top and pale white underneath. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, ponds, and wetlands.

Salix × propinqua is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
