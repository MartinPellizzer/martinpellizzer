Staurogyne griffithiana

Staurogyne griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface, with small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny, greenish-white blooms that appear in clusters.

Staurogyne griffithiana is valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and its role in maintaining water quality.
