Scindapsus annamicus

Scindapsus annamicus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shady conditions and is often used as a decorative foliage plant in indoor settings.

Scindapsus annamicus is also popular for its ability to purify air and its ease of care.
