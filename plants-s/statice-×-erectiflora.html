Statice × erectiflora

Statice × erectiflora, commonly known as sea lavender or saltbush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Statice × erectiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
