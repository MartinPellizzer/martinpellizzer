Silene karekirii

Silene karekirii, commonly known as the Karekiri catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene karekirii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
