Schisandra grandiflora

Schisandra grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in aromatic compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Schisandra grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
