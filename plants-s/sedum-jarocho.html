Sedum jarocho

Sedum jarocho, commonly known as Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for drought-tolerant landscapes.
