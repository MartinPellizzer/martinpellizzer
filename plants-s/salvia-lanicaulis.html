Salvia lanicaulis

Salvia lanicaulis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Salvia lanicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
