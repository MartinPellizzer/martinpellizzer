Salix caprea var. sericea

Salix caprea var. sericea, commonly known as silky willow, is a variant of the common goat willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray, hair-covered bark and long, slender catkins. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.
