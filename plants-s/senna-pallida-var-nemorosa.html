Senna pallida var. nemorosa

Senna pallida var. nemorosa, commonly known as the slender senna, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
