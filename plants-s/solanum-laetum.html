Solanum laetum

Solanum laetum, commonly known as the happy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. These berries are toxic to humans and animals but are used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
