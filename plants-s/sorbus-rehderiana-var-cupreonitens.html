Sorbus rehderiana var. cupreonitens

Sorbus rehderiana var. cupreonitens, commonly known as the copperleaf rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking copper-colored foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique coloration and ecological value make it a notable species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
