Styrax suberifolius var. fargesii

Styrax suberifolius var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
