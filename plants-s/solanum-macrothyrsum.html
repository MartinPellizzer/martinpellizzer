Solanum macrothyrsum

Solanum macrothyrsum, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Solanum macrothyrsum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
