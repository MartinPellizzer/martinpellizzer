Sapindus longifolius

Sapindus longifolius, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
