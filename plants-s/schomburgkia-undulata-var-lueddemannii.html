Schomburgkia undulata var. lueddemannii

Schomburgkia undulata var. lueddemannii, commonly known as the Lueddemann's spurred orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, fleshy, segmented pseudobulbs and its showy, spurred flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and can bloom multiple times a year under optimal conditions.

Schomburgkia undulata var. lueddemannii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
