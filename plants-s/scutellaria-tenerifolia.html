Scutellaria tenerifolia

Scutellaria tenerifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Scutellaria tenerifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
