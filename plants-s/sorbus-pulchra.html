Sorbus pulchra

Sorbus pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive serrated margin.

Sorbus pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
