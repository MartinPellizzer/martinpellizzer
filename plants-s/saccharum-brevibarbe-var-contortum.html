Saccharum brevibarbe var. contortum

Saccharum brevibarbe var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted bamboo grass, is a variant of the genus Saccharum, which includes several species of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This particular variety is characterized by its twisted or coiled nodes, which give it a distinctive appearance. It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves. The plant is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, swampy environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
