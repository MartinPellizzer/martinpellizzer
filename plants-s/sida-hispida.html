Sida hispida

Sida hispida, commonly known as prickly sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, sometimes prickly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida hispida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is used in traditional medicine and has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
