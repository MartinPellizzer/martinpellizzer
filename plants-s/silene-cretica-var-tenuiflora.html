Silene cretica var. tenuiflora

Silene cretica var. tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene cretica var. tenuiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
