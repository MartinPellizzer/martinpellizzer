Smilax china var. china

Smilax china var. china, commonly known as Chinese smilax, is a perennial climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem with thorny nodes and is characterized by its compound leaves that are usually trifoliate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense thickets.
