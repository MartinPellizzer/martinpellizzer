Stachys viticina

Stachys viticina, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stachys viticina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
