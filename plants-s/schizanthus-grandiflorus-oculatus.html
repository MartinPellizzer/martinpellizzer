Schizanthus grandiflorus-oculatus

Schizanthus grandiflorus-oculatus, commonly known as the common garden scorpionviolet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features showy, spurred petals that resemble the tail of a scorpion, giving it its common name. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Schizanthus grandiflorus-oculatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental flowers.
