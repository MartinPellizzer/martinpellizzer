Salvia meyeri

Salvia meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
