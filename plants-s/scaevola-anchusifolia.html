Scaevola anchusifolia

Scaevola anchusifolia, commonly known as the Anchusifolia fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
