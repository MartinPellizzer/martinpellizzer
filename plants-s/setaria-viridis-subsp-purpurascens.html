Setaria viridis subsp. purpurascens

Setaria viridis subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as purple false wheat, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential candidate for bioenergy research.
