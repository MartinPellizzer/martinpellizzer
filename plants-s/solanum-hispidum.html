Solanum hispidum

Solanum hispidum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Solanum hispidum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
