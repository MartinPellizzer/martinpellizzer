Scabiosa norica

Scabiosa norica, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have a distinctive five-petaled structure.

Scabiosa norica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
