Silene lagenocalyx var. purpurea

Silene lagenocalyx var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. This species is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of local flora.
