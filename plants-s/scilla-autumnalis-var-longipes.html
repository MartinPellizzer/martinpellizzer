Scilla autumnalis var. longipes

Scilla autumnalis var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed autumn scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
