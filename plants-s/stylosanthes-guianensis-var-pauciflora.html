Stylosanthes guianensis var. pauciflora

Stylosanthes guianensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Guianan stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage species for livestock. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and during drought periods.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
