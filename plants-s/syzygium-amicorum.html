Syzygium amicorum

Syzygium amicorum, commonly known as the friendship tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, red, fleshy fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Syzygium amicorum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
