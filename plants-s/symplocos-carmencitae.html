Symplocos carmencitae

Symplocos carmencitae, commonly known as Carmencita's wax myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the Ericaceae family.
