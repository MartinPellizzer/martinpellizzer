Scleranthus × heufleri

Scleranthus × heufleri, commonly known as the hybrid meadow sandwort, is a flowering plant that results from the cross between two species of the genus Scleranthus.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its foliage is usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Scleranthus × heufleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
