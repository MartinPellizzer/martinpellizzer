Syzygium burkillianum var. garcinifolioides

Syzygium burkillianum var. garcinifolioides, commonly known as the Burkill's tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often mistaken for those of the Garcinia genus. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, ovoid, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and aesthetic value.
