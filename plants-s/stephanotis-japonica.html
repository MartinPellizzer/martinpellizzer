Stephanotis japonica

Stephanotis japonica, commonly known as Japanese jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody stems that can climb or trail. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers are often used in traditional Japanese floral arrangements due to their beauty and scent.
