Solanum estradea

Solanum estradea, commonly known as the Estrade's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruit is a berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Solanum estradea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or shrublands.
