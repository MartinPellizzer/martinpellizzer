Scutellaria nana

Scutellaria nana, commonly known as dwarf skullcap, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Scutellaria nana is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
