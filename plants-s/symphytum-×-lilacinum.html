Symphytum × lilacinum

Symphytum × lilacinum, commonly known as the purple comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
