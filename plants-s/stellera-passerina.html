Stellera passerina

Stellera passerina, commonly known as the Pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of purple and white. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows from a short, thick rhizome. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are typically covered in fine hairs.

Stellera passerina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
