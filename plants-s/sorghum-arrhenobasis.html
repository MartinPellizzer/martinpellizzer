Sorghum arrhenobasis

Sorghum arrhenobasis, commonly known as giant broomcorn millet, is a tall, annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the male flowers typically appearing at the base of the stem. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in dry regions.

Its seeds are used for food, fodder, and industrial purposes, highlighting its agricultural importance.
