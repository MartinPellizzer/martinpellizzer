Sparganium affine var. boreale

Sparganium affine var. boreale, commonly known as the northern bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed species native to northern regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its cylindrical, spongy stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically submerged in water. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

This species is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
