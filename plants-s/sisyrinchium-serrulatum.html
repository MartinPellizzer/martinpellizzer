Sisyrinchium serrulatum

Sisyrinchium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium serrulatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
