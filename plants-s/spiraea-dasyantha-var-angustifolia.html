Spiraea dasyantha var. angustifolia

Spiraea dasyantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy texture, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
