Stillingia sylvatica var. linearifolia

Stillingia sylvatica var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stinging gentian, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are elongated and linear, giving it its varietal name, linearifolia. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
