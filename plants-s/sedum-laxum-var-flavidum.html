Sedum laxum var. flavidum

Sedum laxum var. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained environments. Its drought tolerance makes it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The varietal name "flavidum" refers to the yellow coloration of its flowers.
