Symplocos verrucosa

Symplocos verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf waxberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos verrucosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
