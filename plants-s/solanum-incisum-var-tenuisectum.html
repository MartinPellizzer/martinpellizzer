Solanum incisum var. tenuisectum

Solanum incisum var. tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a dark blue or black color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
