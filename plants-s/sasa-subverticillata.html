Sasa subverticillata

Sasa subverticillata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa subverticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
