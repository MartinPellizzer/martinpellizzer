Sorghum condensatum var. intermedium

Sorghum condensatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often used for forage and has potential in bioenergy applications.
