Streblosa maxima

Streblosa maxima, commonly known as the giant streblosa, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound inflorescence.

Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that can reach several feet in length, adding to its dramatic appearance. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are small and white, arranged in dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Streblosa maxima is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
