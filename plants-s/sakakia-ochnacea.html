Sakakia ochnacea

Sakakia ochnacea, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
