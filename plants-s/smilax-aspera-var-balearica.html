Smilax aspera var. balearica

Smilax aspera var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic catclaw, is a climbing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features woody stems with sharp, hooked thorns that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, asperous texture.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky, coastal environments.
