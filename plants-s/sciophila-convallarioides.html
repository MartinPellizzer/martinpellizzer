Sciophila convallarioides

Sciophila convallarioides, commonly known as the purple mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pale purple or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Sciophila convallarioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
