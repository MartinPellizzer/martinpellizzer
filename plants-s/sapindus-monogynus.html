Sapindus monogynus

Sapindus monogynus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and composed of 5–11 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. The fruits contain a single seed enclosed in a thick, fibrous husk, which is used in traditional practices.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in herbal medicine.
