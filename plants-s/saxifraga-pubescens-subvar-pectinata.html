Saxifraga pubescens subvar. pectinata

Saxifraga pubescens subvar. pectinata, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga pubescens subvar. pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
