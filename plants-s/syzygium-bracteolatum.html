Syzygium bracteolatum

Syzygium bracteolatum, commonly known as the white apple or white carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a yellowish-orange, elongated drupe with a sweet and sour flavor, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and medicinal uses.
