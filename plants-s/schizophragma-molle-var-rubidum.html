Schizophragma molle var. rubidum

Schizophragma molle var. rubidum, commonly known as the red-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, often with a reddish or purplish hue during the growing season. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black and are an important food source for birds.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other supports.
