Solanum heterandrum

Solanum heterandrum, commonly known as the red bignay, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Solanum heterandrum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a source of traditional food.
