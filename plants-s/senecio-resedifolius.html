Senecio resedifolius

Senecio resedifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are generally basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

Senecio resedifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
