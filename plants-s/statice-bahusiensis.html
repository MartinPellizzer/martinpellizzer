Statice bahusiensis

Statice bahusiensis, commonly known as the Bahusian glassflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, tightly packed flowers that resemble glass beads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Statice bahusiensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
