Smilacina bicolor

Smilacina bicolor, commonly known as the white and yellow smilacina, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features white and yellow variegation on the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Smilacina bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
