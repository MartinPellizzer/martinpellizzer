Securigera somalensis

Securigera somalensis, commonly known as the Somali milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub with small, compound leaves and produces yellow or pale green flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in semi-desert and coastal habitats.
