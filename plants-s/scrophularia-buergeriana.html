Scrophularia buergeriana

Scrophularia buergeriana, commonly known as Buerger's scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of pale pink to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
