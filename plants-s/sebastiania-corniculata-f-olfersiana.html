Sebastiania corniculata f. olfersiana

Sebastiania corniculata f. olfersiana, commonly known as the Olfer's flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical areas.
