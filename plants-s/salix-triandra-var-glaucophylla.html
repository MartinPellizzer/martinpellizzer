Salix triandra var. glaucophylla

Salix triandra var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla willow, is a variant of the common willow species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its branches are flexible and often used in basket-making and other traditional crafts.

The flowers appear in early spring, producing catkins that contribute to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
