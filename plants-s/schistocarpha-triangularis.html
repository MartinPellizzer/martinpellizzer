Schistocarpha triangularis

Schistocarpha triangularis, commonly known as the triangular carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, spiny stems and basal leaves that are triangular in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Schistocarpha triangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
