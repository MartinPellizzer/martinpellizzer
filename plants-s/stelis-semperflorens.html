Stelis semperflorens

Stelis semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom continuously throughout the year. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Stelis semperflorens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
