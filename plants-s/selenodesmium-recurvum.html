Selenodesmium recurvum

Selenodesmium recurvum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Selenodesmium recurvum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems and a high tolerance for drought.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
