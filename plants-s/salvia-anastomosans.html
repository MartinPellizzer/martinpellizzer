Salvia anastomosans

Salvia anastomosans, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia anastomosans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
