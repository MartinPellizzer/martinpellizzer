Syzygium nitidum

Syzygium nitidum, commonly known as the glossy apple or white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
