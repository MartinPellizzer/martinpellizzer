Syzygium guineense subsp. bamendae

Syzygium guineense subsp.

bamendae, commonly known as the Bamenda apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Cameroon. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers.

The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is popular in local markets.
