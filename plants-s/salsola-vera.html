Salsola vera

Salsola vera, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a hardy, perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers and has a robust root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically succulent and can store water, aiding in its drought tolerance.

Salsola vera is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but has ecological benefits, such as preventing soil erosion.
