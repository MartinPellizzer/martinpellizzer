Sorbus cretica subsp. danubialis

Sorbus cretica subsp.

danubialis, commonly known as the Danube rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland edges.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
