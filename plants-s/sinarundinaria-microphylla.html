Sinarundinaria microphylla

Sinarundinaria microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect culms that are typically less than 1 meter in height, making it ideal for small garden spaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and have a fine, grass-like texture.

Sinarundinaria microphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
