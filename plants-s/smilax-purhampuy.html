Smilax purhampuy

Smilax purhampuy, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and forest understories.

Smilax purhampuy is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
