Sphenopholis obtusata

Sphenopholis obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sphenophyllum, is a species of whisk fern belonging to the family Sphenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its primitive, fern-like appearance with simple, undivided leaves that are broad and ovate. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping stem that produces sporangia at the base of the leaves. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's structure reflects its ancient lineage, showcasing features similar to early vascular plants.
