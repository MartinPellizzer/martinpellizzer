Sorghum condensatum

Sorghum condensatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or purplish in color. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is adapted to dry conditions.

Sorghum condensatum is often used in traditional landscaping and has potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
