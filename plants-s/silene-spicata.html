Silene spicata

Silene spicata, commonly known as the spikeflower catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene spicata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
