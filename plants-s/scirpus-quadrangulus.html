Scirpus quadrangulus

Scirpus quadrangulus, commonly known as the four-angle bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, jointed stems that often have four distinct ridges, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually pink or reddish in color.

Scirpus quadrangulus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
