Stellaria latisepala

Stellaria latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that typically appear in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and opposite. The plant is native to regions in Asia, particularly China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Stellaria latisepala has a compact growth habit and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
