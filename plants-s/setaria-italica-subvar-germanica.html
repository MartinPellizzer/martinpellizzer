Setaria italica subvar. germanica

Setaria italica subvar. germanica, commonly known as German millet, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.

The seeds are used for bird food, animal feed, and in some traditional culinary applications.
