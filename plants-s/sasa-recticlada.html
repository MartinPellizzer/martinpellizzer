Sasa recticlada

Sasa recticlada, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or narrow-leaf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa recticlada is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
