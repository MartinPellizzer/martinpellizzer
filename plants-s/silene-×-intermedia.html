Silene × intermedia

Silene × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate catchfly, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, linear to oblanceolate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Silene × intermedia is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
