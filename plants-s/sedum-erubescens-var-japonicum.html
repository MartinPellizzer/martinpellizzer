Sedum erubescens var. japonicum

Sedum erubescens var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
