Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp. speciosa

Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained environments and is often found in coastal and inland areas.
