Scirpus sororius

Scirpus sororius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize wetland areas.

Scirpus sororius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
