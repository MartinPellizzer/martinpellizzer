Solidago shinnersii

Solidago shinnersii, commonly known as Shinners' goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
