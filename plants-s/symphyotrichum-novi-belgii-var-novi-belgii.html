Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. novi-belgii

Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. novi-belgii, commonly known as New York ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with purple to pinkish-purple flower clusters.

The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.
