Senegalia grandisiliqua

Senegalia grandisiliqua, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Senegalia grandisiliqua also has cultural and traditional uses, including as a source of fodder and in local medicine.
