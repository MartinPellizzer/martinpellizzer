Silene inflata var. foliosa

Silene inflata var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its inflated, bladder-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
