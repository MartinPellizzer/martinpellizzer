Selinum lineare

Selinum lineare, commonly known as the linear wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in herbal remedies.
