Senna pistaciifolia

Senna pistaciifolia, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sunlight.

Senna pistaciifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
