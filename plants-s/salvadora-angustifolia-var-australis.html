Salvadora angustifolia var. australis

Salvadora angustifolia var. australis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in semi-arid areas.
