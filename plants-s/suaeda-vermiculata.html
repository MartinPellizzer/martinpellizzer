Suaeda vermiculata

Suaeda vermiculata, commonly known as saltbush or sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Middle East, North Africa, and the Mediterranean.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is highly adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a valuable component of coastal and desert ecosystems. Its ability to thrive in harsh environments contributes to soil stabilization and erosion control.

Suaeda vermiculata is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
