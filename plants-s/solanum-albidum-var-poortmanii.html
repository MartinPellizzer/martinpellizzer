Solanum albidum var. poortmanii

Solanum albidum var. poortmanii, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) characterized by its distinctive white flowers and hairy foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa, typically growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its botanical features include alternate leaves with serrated edges and a short, stout stem.

It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
