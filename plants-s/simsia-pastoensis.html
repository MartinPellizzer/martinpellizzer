Simsia pastoensis

Simsia pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has striking yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk. It produces small, tubular disc flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Simsia pastoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
