Sarcanthus armeniacus

Sarcanthus armeniacus, commonly known as Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Sarcanthus armeniacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
