Sideritis bourgaei

Sideritis bourgaei, commonly known as the Balkan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. Sideritis bourgaei typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often at elevations above 1,000 meters.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds.
