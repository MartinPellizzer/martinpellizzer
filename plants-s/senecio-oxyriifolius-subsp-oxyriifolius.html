Senecio oxyriifolius subsp.

oxyriifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
