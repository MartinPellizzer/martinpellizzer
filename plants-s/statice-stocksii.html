Statice stocksii

Statice stocksii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice stocksii is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate harsh, maritime conditions.
