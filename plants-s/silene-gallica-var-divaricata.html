Silene gallica var. divaricata

Silene gallica var. divaricata, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
