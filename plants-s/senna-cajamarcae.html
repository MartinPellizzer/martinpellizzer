Senna cajamarcae

Senna cajamarcae, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

Senna cajamarcae is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
