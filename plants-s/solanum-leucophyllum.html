Solanum leucophyllum

Solanum leucophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by yellow berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Solanum leucophyllum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
