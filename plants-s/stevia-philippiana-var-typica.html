Stevia philippiana var. typica

Stevia philippiana var. typica, commonly known as Philippine stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the genus Stevia. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
