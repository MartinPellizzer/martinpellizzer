Shorea macroptera subsp. macropterifolia

Shorea macroptera subsp.

macropterifolia, commonly known as the giant ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in lowland to hill dipterocarp forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is often used in traditional medicine and construction.
