Stachys anisochila var. sendteri

Stachys anisochila var. sendteri, commonly known as the Sendter's betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
