Sutera cephalotes

Sutera cephalotes, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sutera cephalotes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
