Sabicea nobilis

Sabicea nobilis, commonly known as the noble sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
