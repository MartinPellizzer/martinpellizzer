Solanum xiphocephalum

Solanum xiphocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, and its flowers are usually purple or blue, with five petals. Solanum xiphocephalum produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
