Salvia officinalis subsp. officinalis

Salvia officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as common sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
