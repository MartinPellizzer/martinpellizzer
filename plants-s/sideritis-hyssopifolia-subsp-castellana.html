Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. castellana

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

castellana, commonly known as Castellana sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
