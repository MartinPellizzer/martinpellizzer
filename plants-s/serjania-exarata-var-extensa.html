Serjania exarata var. extensa

Serjania exarata var. extensa, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or wild starfruit, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
