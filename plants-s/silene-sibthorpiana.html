Silene sibthorpiana

Silene sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorp's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene sibthorpiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
