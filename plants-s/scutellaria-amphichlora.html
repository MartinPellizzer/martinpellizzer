Scutellaria amphichlora

Scutellaria amphichlora, commonly known as the two-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Scutellaria amphichlora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
