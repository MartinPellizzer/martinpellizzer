Sideritis leucantha var. bourgeana

Sideritis leucantha var. bourgeana, commonly known as the whiteflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
