Stylidium spathulatum subsp. glandulosum

Stylidium spathulatum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular stylidium, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its leaves are linear and covered with glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
