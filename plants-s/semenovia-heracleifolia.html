Semenovia heracleifolia

Semenovia heracleifolia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow or orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

Semenovia heracleifolia is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
