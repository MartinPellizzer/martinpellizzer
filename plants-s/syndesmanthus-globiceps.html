Syndesmanthus globiceps

Syndesmanthus globiceps, commonly known as the globe-headed syndesmanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are arranged in a dense, bushy pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
