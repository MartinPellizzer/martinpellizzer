Scelochilus brevis

Scelochilus brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. Native to tropical regions of South America, this species thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has fleshy leaves and produces a short, erect stem with a single flower at the apex.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Scelochilus brevis is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
