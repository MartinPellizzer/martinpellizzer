Serapias stenopetala

Serapias stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with narrow petals and a spurred lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of yellow or orange flowers.

Serapias stenopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
