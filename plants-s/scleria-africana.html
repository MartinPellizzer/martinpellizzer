Scleria africana

Scleria africana, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scleria africana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
