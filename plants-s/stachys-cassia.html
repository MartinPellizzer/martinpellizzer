Stachys cassia

Stachys cassia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Stachys cassia is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a valuable ornamental plant.
