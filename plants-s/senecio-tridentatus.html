Senecio tridentatus

Senecio tridentatus, commonly known as the triple-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with three distinct teeth along the margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
