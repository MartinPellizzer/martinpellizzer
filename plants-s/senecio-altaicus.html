Senecio altaicus

Senecio altaicus, commonly known as the Altai groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptation to harsh climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio altaicus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
