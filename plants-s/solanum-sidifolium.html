Solanum sidifolium

Solanum sidifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum sidifolium is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
