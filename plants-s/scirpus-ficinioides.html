Scirpus ficinioides

Scirpus ficinioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in water retention.

Scirpus ficinioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
