Solidago rigida var. magna

Solidago rigida var. magna, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters, with rigid, hairy stems that are often branched. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to moist soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
