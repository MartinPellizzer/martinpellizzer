Siphocampylus stellatus

Siphocampylus stellatus, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a star-like pattern on the petals. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Siphocampylus stellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
