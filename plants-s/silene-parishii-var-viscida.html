Silene parishii var. viscida

Silene parishii var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert environments.
