Strombocactus disciformis subsp. esperanzae

Strombocactus disciformis subsp.

esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza's discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slight bluish tint.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the apex of the stem. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
