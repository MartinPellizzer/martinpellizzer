Salvia filifolia

Salvia filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of showy blue to violet flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia filifolia is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
