Sommera cusucoana

Sommera cusucoana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Sommera cusucoana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
