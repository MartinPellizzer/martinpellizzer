Sebastiania edwalliana var. minor

Sebastiania edwalliana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf sebastiania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that appear in the summer months. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.
