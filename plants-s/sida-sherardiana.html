Sida sherardiana

Sida sherardiana, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas.

Sida sherardiana is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
