Sedum hungaricum

Sedum hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners.
