Stevia pauciflora

Stevia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green leaves and few, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food products.

Stevia pauciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
