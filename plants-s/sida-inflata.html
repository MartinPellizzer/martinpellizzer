Sida inflata

Sida inflata, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, balloon-like seed pods that contain numerous small, hairy seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in clusters.

Sida inflata is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
