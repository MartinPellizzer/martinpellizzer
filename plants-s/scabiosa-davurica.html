Scabiosa davurica

Scabiosa davurica, commonly known as the Siberian ladies' mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and produces clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Scabiosa davurica is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
