Stephanomeria myrioclada

Stephanomeria myrioclada, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are usually blue-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
