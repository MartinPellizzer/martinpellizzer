Symphyotrichum concinnum

Symphyotrichum concinnum, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and a conical flower head composed of both ray and disk flowers.

Symphyotrichum concinnum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
