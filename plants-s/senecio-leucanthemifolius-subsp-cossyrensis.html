Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. cossyrensis

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

cossyrensis, commonly known as the white thistle. It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers.

Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves and produces numerous flower heads that bloom in summer.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
