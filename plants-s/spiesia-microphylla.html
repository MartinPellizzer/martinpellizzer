Spiesia microphylla

Spiesia microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, alternate leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
