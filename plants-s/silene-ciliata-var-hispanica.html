Silene ciliata var. hispanica

Silene ciliata var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, branching stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
