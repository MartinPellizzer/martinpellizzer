Spondias malayana

Spondias malayana, commonly known as the Malaysian satinash or Malayan satinash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, round fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
