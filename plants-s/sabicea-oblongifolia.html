Sabicea oblongifolia

Sabicea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sabicea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are elongated and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Sabicea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
