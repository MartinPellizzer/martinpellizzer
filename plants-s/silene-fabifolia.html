Silene fabifolia

Silene fabifolia, commonly known as the field pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Silene fabifolia is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
