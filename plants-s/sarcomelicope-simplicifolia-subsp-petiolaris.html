Sarcomelicope simplicifolia subsp. petiolaris

Sarcomelicope simplicifolia subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated petioles, which set it apart from other members of the species.
