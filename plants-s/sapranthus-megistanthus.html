Sapranthus megistanthus

Sapranthus megistanthus, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the waterlogged environment, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
