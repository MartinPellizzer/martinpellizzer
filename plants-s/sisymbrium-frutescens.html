Sisymbrium frutescens

Sisymbrium frutescens, commonly known as wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sisymbrium frutescens is also known for its peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.
