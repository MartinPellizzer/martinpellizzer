Scleria sumatrensis

Scleria sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its grass-like appearance is characterized by narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers.

Scleria sumatrensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
