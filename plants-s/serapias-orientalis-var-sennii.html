Serapias orientalis var. sennii

Serapias orientalis var. sennii, commonly known as the Eastern Orchid Gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodland or scrubland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
