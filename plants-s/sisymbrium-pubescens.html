Sisymbrium pubescens

Sisymbrium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy brookweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are slender pods that release tiny seeds when mature.

Sisymbrium pubescens is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
