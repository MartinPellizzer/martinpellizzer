Salvia membranicalyx

Salvia membranicalyx, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
