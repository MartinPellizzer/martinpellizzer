Scutellaria nodulosa

Scutellaria nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria nodulosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
