Sisymbrium clandestinum

Sisymbrium clandestinum, commonly known as the secret broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other plants. Due to its parasitic nature, it can be harmful to agricultural crops, making it a concern for farmers.
