Salacia laevigata

Salacia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicorniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Salacia laevigata is often found in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salinity and fluctuating water levels.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
