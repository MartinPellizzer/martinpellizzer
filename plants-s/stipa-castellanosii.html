Stipa castellanosii

Stipa castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos' needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Stipa castellanosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
