Senecio pilotus var. angolensis

Senecio pilotus var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
