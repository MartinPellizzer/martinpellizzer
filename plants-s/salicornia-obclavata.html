Salicornia obclavata

Salicornia obclavata, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that are often covered in small, translucent bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Salicornia obclavata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
