Stachyurus yunnanensis var. obovatus

Stachyurus yunnanensis var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental, showy inflorescences that appear in early spring, consisting of clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically obovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in the fall. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
