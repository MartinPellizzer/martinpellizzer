Salomonia leptostachya

Salomonia leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed salomonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salomonia leptostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
