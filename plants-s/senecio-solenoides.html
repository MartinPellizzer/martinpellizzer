Senecio solenoides

Senecio solenoides, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, blue-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It produces seeds in small, dandelion-like capitula, which are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio solenoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
