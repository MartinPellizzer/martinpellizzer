Sarcomeris

Sarcomeris, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and features a woody stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
