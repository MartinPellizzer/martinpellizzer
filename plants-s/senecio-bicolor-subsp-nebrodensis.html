Senecio bicolor subsp. nebrodensis

Senecio bicolor subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodene hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrowly shaped.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
