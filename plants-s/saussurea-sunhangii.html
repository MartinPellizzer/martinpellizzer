Saussurea sunhangii

Saussurea sunhangii, commonly known as the Yushu saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and pale yellow to brownish flower heads. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Tibetan medicine, where it is used for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
