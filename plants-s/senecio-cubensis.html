Senecio cubensis

Senecio cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Cuba and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio cubensis is often used in traditional medicine, though its ecological role and conservation status remain understudied.
