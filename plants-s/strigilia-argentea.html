Strigilia argentea

Strigilia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf strigil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
