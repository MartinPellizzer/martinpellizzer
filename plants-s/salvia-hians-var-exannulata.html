Salvia hians var. exannulata

Salvia hians var. exannulata, commonly known as the Mexican sage or scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
