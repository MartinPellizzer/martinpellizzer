Senecio anthemifolius

Senecio anthemifolius, commonly known as the blueweed or bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue or grayish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

Senecio anthemifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
