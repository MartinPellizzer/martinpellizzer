Scrophularia paui

Scrophularia paui, commonly known as the purple scrophularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Scrophularia paui is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
