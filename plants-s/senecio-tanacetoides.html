Senecio tanacetoides

Senecio tanacetoides, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant is evergreen, with fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio tanacetoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
