Silene muscipula subsp. bracteosa

Silene muscipula subsp.

bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate bladdernut, is a flowering plant species native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color and bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by the presence of bracts beneath the flowers, which are modified leaves that aid in its identification.
