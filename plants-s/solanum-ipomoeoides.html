Solanum ipomoeoides

Solanum ipomoeoides, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.

Solanum ipomoeoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
