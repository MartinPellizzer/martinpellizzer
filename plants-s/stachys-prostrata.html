Stachys prostrata

Stachys prostrata, commonly known as creeping betony, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys prostrata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
