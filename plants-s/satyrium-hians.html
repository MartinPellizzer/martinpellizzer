Satyrium hians

Satyrium hians, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a complex inflorescence with a single, showy flower at the top of the stem, surrounded by a dense cluster of smaller, less conspicuous blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
