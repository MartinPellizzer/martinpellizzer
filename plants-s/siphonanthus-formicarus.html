Siphonanthus formicarus

Siphonanthus formicarus, commonly known as the ant plant, is a flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
