Sideritis scordioides var. grandiflora

Sideritis scordioides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
