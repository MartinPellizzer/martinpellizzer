Silene marginata f. hirtella

Silene marginata f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy-edge catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

This plant is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
