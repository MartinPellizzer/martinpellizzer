Salvia verbenaca var. serrata

Salvia verbenaca var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
