Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. contortum

Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. contortum, commonly known as twisted sugarcane.

This plant is a variant of sugarcane characterized by its twisted, coiled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
