Selaginella tylophora

Selaginella tylophora, commonly known as the climbing spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves that are typically linear to ovate in shape. Selaginella tylophora is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophylls.
