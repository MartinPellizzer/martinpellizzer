Sidalcea malviflora subsp. rostrata

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated checker-mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus.

The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.
