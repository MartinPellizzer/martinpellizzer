Simaba intermedia

Simaba intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. Its fruits are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Simaba intermedia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
