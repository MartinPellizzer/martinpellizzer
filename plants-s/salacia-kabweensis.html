Salacia kabweensis

Salacia kabweensis, commonly known as the Kabwe salacia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, Salacia kabweensis is considered a vulnerable species, facing threats from deforestation and habitat loss.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies in its native region.
