Salvia bicolor var. dichroa

Salvia bicolor var. dichroa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, two-toned flowers that range from blue to purple, with a white center. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia bicolor var. dichroa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
