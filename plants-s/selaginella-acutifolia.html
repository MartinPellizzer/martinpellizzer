Selaginella acutifolia

Selaginella acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems, giving it a grass-like appearance. Selaginella acutifolia is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its resilience to periodic drought.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
