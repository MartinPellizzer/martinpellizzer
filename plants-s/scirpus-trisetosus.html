Scirpus trisetosus

Scirpus trisetosus, commonly known as the three-bristled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters along the upper part of the stems. Scirpus trisetosus is adapted to moist or water-saturated soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in challenging wetland environments.
