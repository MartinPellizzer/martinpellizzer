Serratula kitaibelii

Serratula kitaibelii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Serratula kitaibelii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
