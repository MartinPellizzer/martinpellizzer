Syzygium prainianum subsp. minor

Syzygium prainianum subsp.

minor, commonly known as the small-fruited clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller fruits compared to the typical clove, with a smooth, reddish-brown surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
