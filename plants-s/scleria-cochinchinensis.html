Scleria cochinchinensis

Scleria cochinchinensis, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, often growing in wet or marshy environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scleria cochinchinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
