Spathoglottis montana

Spathoglottis montana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spatulate lip that attracts pollinators.

Spathoglottis montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
