Sauroglossum sellilabre

Sauroglossum sellilabre, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
