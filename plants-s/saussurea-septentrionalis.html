Saussurea septentrionalis

Saussurea septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
