Sarcochilus alatus

Sarcochilus alatus, commonly known as the flying sword orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive sword-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers with prominent, spurred petals. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Sarcochilus alatus is known for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
