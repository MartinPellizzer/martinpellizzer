Sorbus caucasica var. yaltirikii

Sorbus caucasica var. yaltirikii, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
