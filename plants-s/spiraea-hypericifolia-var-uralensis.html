Spiraea hypericifolia var. uralensis

Spiraea hypericifolia var. uralensis, commonly known as the Ural spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, multi-stemmed growth with dense clusters of small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins and a dark green upper surface. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
