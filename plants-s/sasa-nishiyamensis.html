Sasa nishiyamensis

Sasa nishiyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Sasa nishiyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and traditional Japanese garden design.
