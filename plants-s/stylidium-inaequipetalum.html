Stylidium inaequipetalum

Stylidium inaequipetalum, commonly known as the unequal-petaled sun-orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often resemble miniature sunflowers, with a distinct yellow or orange center. The plant has a short, erect stem and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in a basal rosette.

Stylidium inaequipetalum is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, typically in arid or semi-arid regions.
