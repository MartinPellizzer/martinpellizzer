Saracha punctata

Saracha punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Saracha punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
