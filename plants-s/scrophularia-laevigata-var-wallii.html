Scrophularia laevigata var. wallii

Scrophularia laevigata var. wallii, commonly known as the smooth scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
