Strophanthus letei

Strophanthus letei, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a woody vine that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red and orange. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic alkaloids.

Strophanthus letei is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a source of natural dyes.
