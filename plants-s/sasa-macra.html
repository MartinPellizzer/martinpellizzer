Sasa macra

Sasa macra, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Sasa macra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
