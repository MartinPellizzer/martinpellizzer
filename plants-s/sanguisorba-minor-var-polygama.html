Sanguisorba minor var. polygama

Sanguisorba minor var. polygama, commonly known as the narrowleaf burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features grass-like, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This perennial herb is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
