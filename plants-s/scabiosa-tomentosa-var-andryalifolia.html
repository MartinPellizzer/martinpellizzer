Scabiosa tomentosa var. andryalifolia

Scabiosa tomentosa var. andryalifolia, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a compact, mound-like growth habit with silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a woolly texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
