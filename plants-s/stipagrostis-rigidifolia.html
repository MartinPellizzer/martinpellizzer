Stipagrostis rigidifolia

Stipagrostis rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small organisms.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
