Salsola glabrescens var. apiculata

Salsola glabrescens var. apiculata, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, sour-tasting berries that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in saline soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it a common component of desert vegetation.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
