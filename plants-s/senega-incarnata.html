Senega incarnata

Senega incarnata, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a lady's slipper, which are clustered in loose racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow along the stem. Senega incarnata is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.

It typically thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
