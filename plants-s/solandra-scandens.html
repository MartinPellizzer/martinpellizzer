Solandra scandens

Solandra scandens, commonly known as the sweet scent vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with trailing stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Solandra scandens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms.
