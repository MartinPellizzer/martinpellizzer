Serjania nitidula

Serjania nitidula, commonly known as the glossy serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and are arranged in clusters along the branches.

Serjania nitidula is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
