Salix × hagensis

Salix × hagensis, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, cascading branches that droop gracefully. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a pointed tip. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

Salix × hagensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly found in wetland areas and along waterways.
