Spiraea bella var. pubicarpa

Spiraea bella var. pubicarpa, commonly known as the pubicarpa spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green and turns reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in landscaping.
