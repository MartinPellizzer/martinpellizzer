Silene setifolia

Silene setifolia, commonly known as the silky filbert or slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Silene setifolia typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.
