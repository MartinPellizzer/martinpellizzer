Senecio decorus

Senecio decorus, commonly known as the showy senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio decorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
