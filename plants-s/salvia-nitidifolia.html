Salvia nitidifolia

Salvia nitidifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint or sacred basil, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a minty aroma. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Salvia nitidifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
