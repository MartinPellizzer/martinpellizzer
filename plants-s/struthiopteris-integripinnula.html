Struthiopteris integripinnula

Struthiopteris integripinnula, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its massive, leathery fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length, with a distinctive, shield-like shape. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces sexually through spore dispersal.

Struthiopteris integripinnula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a dramatic visual impact in gardens.
