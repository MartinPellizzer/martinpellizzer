Selaginella rossii

Selaginella rossii, commonly known as the Rose of Sharon, is a species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae, characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage is perennial, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.
