Senecio pyrifolius

Senecio pyrifolius, commonly known as the fireleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, maple-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
