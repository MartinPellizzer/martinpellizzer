Sideritis tragoriganum f. brevispicata

Sideritis tragoriganum f. brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spurred rock sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its shorter floral spurs compared to the typical form of Sideritis tragoriganum.
