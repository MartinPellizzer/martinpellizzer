Saxifraga hypnoides var. cantabrica

Saxifraga hypnoides var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and limited soil nutrients.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
