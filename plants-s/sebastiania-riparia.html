Sebastiania riparia

Sebastiania riparia, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple to violet berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
