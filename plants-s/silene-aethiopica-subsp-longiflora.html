Silene aethiopica subsp. longiflora

Silene aethiopica subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the Ethiopian campion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Silene aethiopica subsp.

longiflora is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
