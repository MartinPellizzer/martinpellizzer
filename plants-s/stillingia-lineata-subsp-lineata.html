Stillingia lineata subsp. lineata

Stillingia lineata subsp.

lineata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
