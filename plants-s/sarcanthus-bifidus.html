Sarcanthus bifidus

Sarcanthus bifidus, commonly known as the bifid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed petals, which give it its scientific name "bifidus.". The plant typically grows in rocky soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, producing a range of colors from white to purple.

Sarcanthus bifidus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
