Spondias macrocarpa

Spondias macrocarpa, commonly known as the Natal plum or wild mango, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Spondias macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
