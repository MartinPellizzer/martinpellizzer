Sorbus alnifolia var. lyciocarpa

Sorbus alnifolia var. lyciocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by distinctive red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruiting bodies, known as pomes, are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
