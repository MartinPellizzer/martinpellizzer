Sedum ciliosum

Sedum ciliosum, commonly known as the wooly stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
