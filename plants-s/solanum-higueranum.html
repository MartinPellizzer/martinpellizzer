Solanum higueranum

Solanum higueranum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red as they mature. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem, glandular leaves, and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Solanum higueranum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
