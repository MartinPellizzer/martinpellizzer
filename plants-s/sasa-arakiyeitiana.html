Sasa arakiyeitiana

Sasa arakiyeitiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It forms dense clumps through underground rhizomes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem in its native habitat.
