Sempervivum brassaii

Sempervivum brassaii, commonly known as the Rock House Plant, is a succulent species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact rosette formation and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or violet during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sempervivum brassaii is also known for its ability to survive in harsh, rocky environments.
