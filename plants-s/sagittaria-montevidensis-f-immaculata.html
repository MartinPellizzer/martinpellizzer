Sagittaria montevidensis f. immaculata

Sagittaria montevidensis f. immaculata, commonly known as the white arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green, distinguishing it from other varieties. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in wetlands and ponds. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for water gardens and ecological restoration projects.
