Sicyos peninsularis

Sicyos peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Bottle-gourd, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It produces large, pale green, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

The fruit is edible when mature and has a mild, nutty flavor.
