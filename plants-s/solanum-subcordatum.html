Solanum subcordatum

Solanum subcordatum, commonly known as the blue potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Solanum subcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and potential medicinal properties.
