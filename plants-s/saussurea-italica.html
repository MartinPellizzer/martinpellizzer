Saussurea italica

Saussurea italica, commonly known as the Italian saffron or the "false saffron," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
