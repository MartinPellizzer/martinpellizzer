Spiraea cana var. typica

Spiraea cana var. typica, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, with a glossy texture, and the plant is known for its graceful, weeping habit. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
