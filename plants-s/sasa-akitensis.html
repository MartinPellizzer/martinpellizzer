Sasa akitensis

Sasa akitensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Akitan bamboo, is a clumping grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa akitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and traditional Japanese landscaping.
