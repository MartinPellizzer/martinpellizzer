Sieglingia seslerioides

Sieglingia seslerioides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, low-growing habit, often forming dense mats on rocky substrates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the stems. Its leaves are generally oval or lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

Sieglingia seslerioides is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
