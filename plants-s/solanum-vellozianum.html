Solanum vellozianum

Solanum vellozianum, commonly known as the Vellozian nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from yellow to red, which contain the seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
