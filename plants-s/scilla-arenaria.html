Scilla arenaria

Scilla arenaria, commonly known as sea scilla, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Scilla arenaria plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
