Semele pterygophora var. barretiana

Semele pterygophora var. barretiana, commonly known as the Barret's flame violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes specialized modified leaves called bracts.
