Sebipira

Sebipira, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to overharvesting, sebipira is now classified as an endangered species.
