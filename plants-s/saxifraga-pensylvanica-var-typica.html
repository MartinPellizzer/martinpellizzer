Saxifraga pensylvanica var. typica

Saxifraga pensylvanica var. typica, commonly known as Pennsylvania saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, shaded environments.
