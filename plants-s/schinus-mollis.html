Schinus mollis

Schinus mollis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Schinus mollis is known for its aromatic, pungent leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
