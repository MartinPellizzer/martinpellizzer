Setaria viridis subsp. ambigua

Setaria viridis subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as slender green millet, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
