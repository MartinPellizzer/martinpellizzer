Saturiastrum petraeum

Saturiastrum petraeum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions, with deep roots that help it access water. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Saturiastrum petraeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
