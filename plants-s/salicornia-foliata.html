Salicornia foliata

Salicornia foliata, commonly known as sea bean, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid and salty conditions. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often edible and have a slightly salty, crunchy texture, making it a valuable resource in certain cuisines.

Salicornia foliata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
