Stipa thessala

Stipa thessala, commonly known as the Balkan needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, bristle-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa thessala is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
