Solidago × lutescens

Solidago × lutescens, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in composite heads, with both ray and disk flowers contributing to its showy appearance.

Solidago × lutescens is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
