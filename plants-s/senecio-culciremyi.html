Senecio culciremyi

Senecio culciremyi, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as a small shrub with narrow, silver-gray leaves adapted to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
