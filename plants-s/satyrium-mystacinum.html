Satyrium mystacinum

Satyrium mystacinum, commonly known as the mistletoe orchid, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, drawing nutrients and water through its haustoria.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic lifestyle and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
