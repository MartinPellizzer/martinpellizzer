Spiraea japonica var. typica

Spiraea japonica var. typica, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or hedges.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and attractive fall foliage.
