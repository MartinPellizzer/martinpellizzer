Senna wislizeni var. villosa

Senna wislizeni var. villosa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
