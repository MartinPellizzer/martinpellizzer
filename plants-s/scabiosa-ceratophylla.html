Scabiosa ceratophylla

Scabiosa ceratophylla, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa ceratophylla is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
