Saponaria intermedia

Saponaria intermedia, commonly known as the middle saponaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

Saponaria intermedia is known for its mild soap-like scent, which gives it its common name.
