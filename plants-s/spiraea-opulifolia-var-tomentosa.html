Spiraea opulifolia var. tomentosa

Spiraea opulifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silver-leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
