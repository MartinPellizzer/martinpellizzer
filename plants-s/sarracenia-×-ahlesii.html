Sarracenia × ahlesii

Sarracenia × ahlesii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It features tall, upright pitchers that are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, designed to lure and trap insects. The plant's leaves curl into tubular structures that secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in wet, acidic soils and full sun.

Its striking appearance and unique ecological role make it a popular choice for both horticulture and botanical study.
