Solidago gonoclada

Solidago gonoclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinctive white or silver midrib, which helps in identifying the species.

Solidago gonoclada plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
