Staphylea japonica

Staphylea japonica, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that turn red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval with a smooth margin.

Staphylea japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
