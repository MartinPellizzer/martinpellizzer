Saxifraga diversifolia var. moorcroftiana

Saxifraga diversifolia var. moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
