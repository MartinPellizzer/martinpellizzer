Sideritis scordioides var. communis

Sideritis scordioides var. communis, commonly known as Greek oregano or wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
