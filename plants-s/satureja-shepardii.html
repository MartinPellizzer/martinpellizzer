Satureja shepardii

Satureja shepardii, commonly known as shepard's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features small, fragrant leaves and produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Satureja shepardii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its pungent, thyme-like aroma.
