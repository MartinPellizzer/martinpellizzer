Salvia verbenaca subsp. horminoides

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

horminoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
