Salvia rosea

Salvia rosea, commonly known as the rose sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, rose-pink to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
