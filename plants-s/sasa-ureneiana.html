Sasa ureneiana

Sasa ureneiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Sasa ureneiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
