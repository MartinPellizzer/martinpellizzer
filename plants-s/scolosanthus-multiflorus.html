Scolosanthus multiflorus

Scolosanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the multibract grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Scolosanthus multiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
