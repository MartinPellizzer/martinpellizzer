Sanamunda axillaris

Sanamunda axillaris, commonly known as the axillary sanamunda, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Sanamunda axillaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
