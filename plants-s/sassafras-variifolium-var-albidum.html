Sassafras variifolium var. albidum

Sassafras variifolium var. albidum, commonly known as the white sassafras, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves that are often white-veined and emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, spherical fruits. Its roots and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The white sassafras is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
