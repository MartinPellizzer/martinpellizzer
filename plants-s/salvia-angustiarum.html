Salvia angustiarum

Salvia angustiarum, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Salvia angustiarum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
