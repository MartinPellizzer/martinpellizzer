Serjania aluligera

Serjania aluligera, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjania, which is part of the order Malpighiales. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Serjania aluligera is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological importance.
