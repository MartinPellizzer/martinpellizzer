Saharanthus

Saharanthus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are small and succulent, helping to conserve water in dry conditions.

Saharanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in desert landscapes.
