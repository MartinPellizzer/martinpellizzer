Saxifraga ledifolia

Saxifraga ledifolia, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh alpine environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.

Saxifraga ledifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
