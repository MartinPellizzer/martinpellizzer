Silene morrisonmontana var. morrisonmontana

Silene morrisonmontana var. morrisonmontana, commonly known as Morrison's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid environments.
