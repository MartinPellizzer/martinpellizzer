Sophora galegoides

Sophora galegoides, commonly known as the purple pagoda tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
