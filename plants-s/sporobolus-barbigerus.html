Sporobolus barbigerus

Sporobolus barbigerus, commonly known as the bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Sporobolus barbigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
