Salvia gilliesii

Salvia gilliesii, commonly known as the Blue Salvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high elevations of New Zealand.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Salvia gilliesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
