Skimmia japonica f. veitchii

Skimmia japonica f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's skimmia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
