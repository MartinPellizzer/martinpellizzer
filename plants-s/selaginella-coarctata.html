Selaginella coarctata

Selaginella coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a distinctive rosette of spirally arranged, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its sporophyte stage produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella coarctata is notable for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in ecological succession in temperate and subtropical regions.
