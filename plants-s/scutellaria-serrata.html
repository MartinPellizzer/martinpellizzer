Scutellaria serrata

Scutellaria serrata, commonly known as the serrated skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and traditional medicinal uses.

Scutellaria serrata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
