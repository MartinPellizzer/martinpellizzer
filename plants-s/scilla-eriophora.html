Scilla eriophora

Scilla eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllis family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early spring display.
