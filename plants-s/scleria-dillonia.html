Scleria dillonia

Scleria dillonia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms during the summer months.

Scleria dillonia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
