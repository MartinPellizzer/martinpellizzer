Saponaria picta

Saponaria picta, commonly known as the painted carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine due to its saponin content. Saponaria picta typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
