Sebaea thodeana

Sebaea thodeana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, white flower clusters that resemble snowballs. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Sebaea thodeana is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and coastal environments.
