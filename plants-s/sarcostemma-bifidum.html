Sarcostemma bifidum

Sarcostemma bifidum, commonly known as the split-stem creeper, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
