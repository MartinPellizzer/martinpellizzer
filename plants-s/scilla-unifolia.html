Scilla unifolia

Scilla unifolia, commonly known as the slender scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, appearing after the flowers have faded.

Scilla unifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
