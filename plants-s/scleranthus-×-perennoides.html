Scleranthus × perennoides

Scleranthus × perennoides, commonly known as the perennial sow-thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
