Serratula insularis

Serratula insularis, commonly known as the island thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland habitats. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula insularis is often found in disturbed sites and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
