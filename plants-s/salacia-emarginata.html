Salacia emarginata

Salacia emarginata, commonly known as the serrated salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Salacia emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
