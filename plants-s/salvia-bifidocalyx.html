Salvia bifidocalyx

Salvia bifidocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
