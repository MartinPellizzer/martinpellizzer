Syringa reticulata var. longifolia

Syringa reticulata var. longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy floral display and attractive bark.
