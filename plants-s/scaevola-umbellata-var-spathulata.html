Scaevola umbellata var. spathulata

Scaevola umbellata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are ovate to spatulate in shape, giving the plant its specific epithet "spathulata," which refers to its broad, flat leaf form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
