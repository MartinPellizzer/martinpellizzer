Sadiria

Sadiria, commonly known as the bluebells of the desert, is a striking plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and water-storing tissues. Sadiria is often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds, where it thrives with minimal rainfall.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to xeriscaped gardens.
