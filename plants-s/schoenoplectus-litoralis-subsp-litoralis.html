Schoenoplectus litoralis subsp. litoralis

Schoenoplectus litoralis subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as salt marsh bulrush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is a perennial herb that thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries.

The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually unbranched and covered with dense, overlapping leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
