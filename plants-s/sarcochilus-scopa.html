Sarcochilus scopa

Sarcochilus scopa, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive member of the orchid family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its large, tree-like structure with a central stem and a crown of fronds. The plant features a unique, thickened rhizome that supports its upright growth. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native environment.

Sarcochilus scopa is also noted for its ability to produce spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.
