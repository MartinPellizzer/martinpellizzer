Schoenus pseudoloreus

Schoenus pseudoloreus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters that help stabilize shorelines. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Schoenus pseudoloreus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
