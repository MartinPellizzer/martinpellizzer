Scyphocoronis majus

Scyphocoronis majus, commonly known as the giant crownflower, is a striking member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Scyphocoronis majus is often found in open habitats like deserts and grasslands, where it thrives in full sunlight.
