Styppeiochloa gynoglossa

Styppeiochloa gynoglossa, commonly known as the narrowleaf styppeiochloa, is a grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
