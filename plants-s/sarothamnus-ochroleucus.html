Sarothamnus ochroleucus

Sarothamnus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Sarothamnus ochroleucus is adapted to rocky and arid soils, making it a hardy species often found in dry, sunny environments.
