Salix × expectata

Salix × expectata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and a dark green upper surface.

Salix × expectata thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.
