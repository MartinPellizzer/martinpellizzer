Senecio hercynicus subsp. dalmaticus

Senecio hercynicus subsp.

dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray, woolly leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to Mediterranean climates and arid conditions.
