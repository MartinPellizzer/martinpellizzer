Solanum pubescens

Solanum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically red when mature and contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum pubescens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
