Stachys aspera subsp. baicalensis

Stachys aspera subsp.

baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including Russia and Mongolia. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its subspecific name, *baicalensis*, refers to its origin in the Baikal region of Siberia.
