Sideritis ochroleuca subsp. tafraoutiana

Sideritis ochroleuca subsp.

tafraoutiana, commonly known as the Tafraoutian ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is native to North Africa, particularly Morocco, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and dense, whorled arrangement of narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are small, pale blue to white, and arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
