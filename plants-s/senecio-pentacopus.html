Senecio pentacopus

Senecio pentacopus, commonly known as the five-fingered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a creeping, rhizomatous stem that produces erect, branched shoots. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
