Scalesia gordilloi

Scalesia gordilloi, commonly known as the Galápagos giant daisy, is a striking plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant has a woody stem and produces numerous ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance. Scalesia gordilloi is adapted to the unique volcanic soils and climate of the Galápagos, thriving in full sun.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for native pollinators and its contribution to the island's distinctive flora.
