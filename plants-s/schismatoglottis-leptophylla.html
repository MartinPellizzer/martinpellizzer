Schismatoglottis leptophylla

Schismatoglottis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed schismatoglottis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on a short, erect stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
