Schizoloma nitens

Schizoloma nitens, commonly known as the translucent lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a unique botanical structure characterized by its delicate, leaf-like thallus.

It typically grows on tree bark and rock surfaces in humid, shaded environments, forming a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium. The thallus is thin and often has a waxy, translucent appearance, which helps in reducing water loss. It lacks distinct rhizines and is composed of a complex network of hyphae that support its growth.

This lichen is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to environmental pollutants.
