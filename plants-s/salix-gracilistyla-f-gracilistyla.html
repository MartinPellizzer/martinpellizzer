Salix gracilistyla f. gracilistyla

Salix gracilistyla f. gracilistyla, commonly known as the slender willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
