Syzygium putii

Syzygium putii, commonly known as the white flowered apple or white urchin, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Syzygium putii is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
