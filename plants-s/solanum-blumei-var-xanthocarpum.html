Solanum blumei var. xanthocarpum

Solanum blumei var. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers.

The fruit is distinctive, being yellow when ripe, and contains numerous seeds.
