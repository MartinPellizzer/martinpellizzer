Securidaca ecristata var. nitida

Securidaca ecristata var. nitida, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or scrub habitats.

The silky senna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
