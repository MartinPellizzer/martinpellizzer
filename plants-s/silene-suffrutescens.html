Silene suffrutescens

Silene suffrutescens, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to survive in arid conditions.
