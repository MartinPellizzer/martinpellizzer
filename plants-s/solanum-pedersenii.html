Solanum pedersenii

Solanum pedersenii, commonly known as the Pedersen's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
