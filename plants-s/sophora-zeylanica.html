Sophora zeylanica

Sophora zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules, enriching the soil.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
