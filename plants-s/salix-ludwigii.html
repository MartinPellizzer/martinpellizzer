Salix ludwigii

Salix ludwigii, commonly known as Ludwig's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

Salix ludwigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
