Streptanthus tortuosus var. oblongus

Streptanthus tortuosus var. oblongus, commonly known as the twisted stalked crucifer, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a glandular texture.
