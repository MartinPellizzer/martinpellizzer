Sasa samaniana f. villosula

Sasa samaniana f. villosula, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa samaniana species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and stout, hollow stems covered in a fine, hairy texture. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage.
