Salix chienii

Salix chienii, commonly known as Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

Salix chienii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
