Struthanthus complexus

Struthanthus complexus, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
