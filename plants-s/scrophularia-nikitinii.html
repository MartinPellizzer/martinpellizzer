Scrophularia nikitinii

Scrophularia nikitinii, commonly known as Nikitin's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, which are characteristic of the Scrophulariaceae family. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing small, black seeds.

Nikitin's figwort is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
