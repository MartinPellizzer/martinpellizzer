Saxifraga oppositifolia latina

Saxifraga oppositifolia latina, commonly known as the Arctic pink, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, fleshy leaves and vivid pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.

Its unique morphology and color make it a notable species in its habitat.
