Suaeda paradoxa

Suaeda paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding survival in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt tolerance makes it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems.

Suaeda paradoxa is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
