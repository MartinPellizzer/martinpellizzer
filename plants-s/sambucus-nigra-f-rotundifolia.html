Sambucus nigra f. rotundifolia

Sambucus nigra f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved elderberry, is a compact, ornamental variant of the elderberry plant.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively hardy, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and can be used in landscaping or as a decorative shrub.
