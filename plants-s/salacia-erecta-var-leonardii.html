Salacia erecta var. leonardii

Salacia erecta var. leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's salacia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
