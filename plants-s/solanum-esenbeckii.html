Solanum esenbeckii

Solanum esenbeckii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and toxic berries. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its fruits are small, yellow, and contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its poisonous nature, it is important to handle the plant with care.
