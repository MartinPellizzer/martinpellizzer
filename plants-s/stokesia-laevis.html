Stokesia laevis

Stokesia laevis, commonly known as the smooth stokesia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Stokesia laevis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
