Solanum giorgii

Solanum giorgii, commonly known as the Giorgi's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
