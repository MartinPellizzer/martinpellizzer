Sempervivum schlehanii var. dinaricum

Sempervivum schlehanii var. dinaricum, commonly known as the Dinaric houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially in sunny conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
