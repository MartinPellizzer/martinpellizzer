Selinum bupleurum

Selinum bupleurum, commonly known as Chinese thornapple, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, berry-like fruits. It is often found in rocky or forested areas and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
