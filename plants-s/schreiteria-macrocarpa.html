Schreiteria macrocarpa

Schreiteria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited schreiteria, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Schreiteria macrocarpa is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
