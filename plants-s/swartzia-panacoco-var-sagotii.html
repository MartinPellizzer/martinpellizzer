Swartzia panacoco var. sagotii

Swartzia panacoco var. sagotii, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 40 meters in height, characterized by its dark, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, leathery seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
