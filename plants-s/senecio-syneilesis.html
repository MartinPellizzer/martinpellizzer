Senecio syneilesis

Senecio syneilesis, commonly known as the sticky sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Senecio syneilesis is adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
