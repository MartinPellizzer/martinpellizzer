Sempervivum marmoreum subsp. matricum

Sempervivum marmoreum subsp.

matricum, commonly known as the Marble Sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive marbled or variegated pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
