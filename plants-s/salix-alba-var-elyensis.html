Salix alba var. elyensis

Salix alba var. elyensis, commonly known as the Ely's willow, is a variant of the white willow that is native to the British Isles.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically gray-green on the upper surface and pale white beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
