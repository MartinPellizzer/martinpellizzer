Scirpus sericeus

Scirpus sericeus, commonly known as silky bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in silky, white hairs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its feathery inflorescences appear in late summer and early fall, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus sericeus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
