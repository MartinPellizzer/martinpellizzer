Schefflera acuminata

Schefflera acuminata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant indoors.

Schefflera acuminata is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various light conditions.
