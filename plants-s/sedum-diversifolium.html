Sedum diversifolium

Sedum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that vary in shape and size, which is reflected in its scientific name. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Sedum diversifolium is drought-tolerant and is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
