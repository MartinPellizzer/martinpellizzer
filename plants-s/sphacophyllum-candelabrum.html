Sphacophyllum candelabrum

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, lobed thallus that often forms a candle-like shape, giving it its common name. Its color ranges from pale green to yellowish-green, and it lacks true leaves but has a photosynthetic cortex.

Sphacophyllum candelabrum is known for its slow growth and is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
