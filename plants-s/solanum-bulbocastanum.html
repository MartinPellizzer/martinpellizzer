Solanum bulbocastanum

Solanum bulbocastanum, commonly known as the bulbocastanum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
