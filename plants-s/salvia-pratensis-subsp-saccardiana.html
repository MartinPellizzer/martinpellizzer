Salvia pratensis subsp. saccardiana

Salvia pratensis subsp.

saccardiana, commonly known as the meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
