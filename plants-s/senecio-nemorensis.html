Senecio nemorensis

Senecio nemorensis, commonly known as the Mountain Everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
