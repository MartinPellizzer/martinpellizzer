Sorbus mougeotii var. typica

Sorbus mougeotii var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
