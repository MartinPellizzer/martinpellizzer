Silene integripetala

Silene integripetala, commonly known as the smooth-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene integripetala is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
