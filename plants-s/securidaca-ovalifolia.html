Securidaca ovalifolia

Securidaca ovalifolia, commonly known as the African spurge or African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive appearance.

It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
