Stachys burchellii

Stachys burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's hedge bindweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Stachys burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
