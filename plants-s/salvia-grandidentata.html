Salvia grandidentata

Salvia grandidentata, commonly known as plains sage, is a perennial herb native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

Salvia grandidentata is also an important nectar source for bees and other pollinators.
