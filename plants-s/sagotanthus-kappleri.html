Sagotanthus kappleri

Sagotanthus kappleri, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sagotanthus kappleri thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
