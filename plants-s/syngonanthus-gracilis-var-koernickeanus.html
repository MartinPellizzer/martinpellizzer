Syngonanthus gracilis var. koernickeanus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. koernickeanus, commonly known as the slender false heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, arranged in dense clusters along its stems. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in short inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens as a ground cover or in rockeries.
