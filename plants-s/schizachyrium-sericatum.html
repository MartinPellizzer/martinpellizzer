Schizachyrium sericatum

Schizachyrium sericatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Schizachyrium sericatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
