Sauropus decrescentifolia

Sauropus decrescentifolia, commonly known as the creeping sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often reddish or purple when young, gradually turning green as they mature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
