Strongylodon campenonii

Strongylodon campenonii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates and well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the tree produces small, round, orange fruits that contain seeds.

Strongylodon campenonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
