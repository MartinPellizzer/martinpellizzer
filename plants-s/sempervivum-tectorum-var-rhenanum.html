Sempervivum tectorum var. rhenanum

Sempervivum tectorum var. rhenanum, commonly known as the Rhenish houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold winters.
