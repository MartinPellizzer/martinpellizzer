Schizonotus sorbifolius

Schizonotus sorbifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and seasonal flowering.
