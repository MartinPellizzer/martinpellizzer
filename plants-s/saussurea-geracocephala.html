Saussurea geracocephala

Saussurea geracocephala, commonly known as the mountain saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
