Saussurea polyphylla

Saussurea polyphylla, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence.

Saussurea polyphylla is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
