Syzygium homichlophilum

Syzygium homichlophilum, commonly known as the purple-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple during the dry season. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with lenticels.

Syzygium homichlophilum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
