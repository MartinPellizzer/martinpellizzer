Solidago ovata

Solidago ovata, commonly known as lesser goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago ovata is a perennial herb that blooms from late summer to early autumn, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
