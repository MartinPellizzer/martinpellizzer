Selaginella jungermannioides

Selaginella jungermannioides, commonly known as the Jungermannioid selaginella, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It possesses a well-developed root system and produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures called microsporangia and megasporangia.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
