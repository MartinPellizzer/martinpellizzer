Syzygium megalophyllum

Syzygium megalophyllum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

Syzygium megalophyllum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
