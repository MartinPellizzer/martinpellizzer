Salacia staudtiana var. scaphodisca

Salacia staudtiana var. scaphodisca, commonly known as the scarlet salacia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while the stems are woody and often adorned with tendrils. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for horticulturists.
