Soliva triniifolia

Soliva triniifolia, commonly known as three-leaf soliva, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Soliva triniifolia is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
