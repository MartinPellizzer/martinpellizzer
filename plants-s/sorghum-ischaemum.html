Sorghum ischaemum

Sorghum ischaemum, commonly known as prairie dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to golden spikelets that are arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Sorghum ischaemum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
