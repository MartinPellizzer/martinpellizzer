Scopolia trichotoma

Scopolia trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy scopolia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. Scopolia trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
