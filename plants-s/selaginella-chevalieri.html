Selaginella chevalieri

Selaginella chevalieri, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, evergreen fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper part of the plant and contain spores.

Selaginella chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to survive in various soil types.
