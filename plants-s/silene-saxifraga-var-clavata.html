Silene saxifraga var. clavata

Silene saxifraga var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often clustered at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone soils.
