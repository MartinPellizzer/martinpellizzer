Solanum furcatum var. glabrum

Solanum furcatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, spherical berries that are toxic when ripe.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name "glabrum.".
