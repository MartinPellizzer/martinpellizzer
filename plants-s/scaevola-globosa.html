Scaevola globosa

Scaevola globosa, commonly known as the fairy fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs. This species is well adapted to sandy soils and salty coastal environments, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
