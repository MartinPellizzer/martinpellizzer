Syngonanthus densiflorus var. glabrescens

Syngonanthus densiflorus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers, which are often inconspicuous and not showy. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is characterized by its relatively smooth, hairless appearance compared to other variants of the species.
