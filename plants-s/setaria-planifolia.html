Setaria planifolia

Setaria planifolia, commonly known as flatleaf millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop. Its seeds are used for human consumption and animal feed, and it plays a role in traditional agriculture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other millet species.
