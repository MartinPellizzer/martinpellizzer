Stichorkis vestita

Stichorkis vestita, commonly known as the naked-stemmed stichorkis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem that is often covered in sparse foliage. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Stichorkis vestita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
