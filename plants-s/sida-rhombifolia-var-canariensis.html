Sida rhombifolia var. canariensis

Sida rhombifolia var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island sida, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous small seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
