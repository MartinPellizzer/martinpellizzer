Sophora macnabiana

Sophora macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically ranging from 5 to 9. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in racemose inflorescences.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.
