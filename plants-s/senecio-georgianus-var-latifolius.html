Senecio georgianus var. latifolius

Senecio georgianus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
