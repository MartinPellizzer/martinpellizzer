Swida schindleri

Swida schindleri, commonly known as the Schindler's swida, is a rare and elusive shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at elevations above 2,500 meters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and its restricted range, Swida schindleri is considered endangered and requires protection.
