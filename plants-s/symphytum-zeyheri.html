Symphytum zeyheri

Symphytum zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's comfrey, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for healing wounds and promoting tissue regeneration.
