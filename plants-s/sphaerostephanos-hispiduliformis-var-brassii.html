Sphaerostephanos hispiduliformis var. brassii

Sphaerostephanos hispiduliformis var. brassii, commonly known as the Brassii variant of the hairy stephanos, is a unique plant species characterized by its spherical or nearly spherical growth form.

It typically features a dense covering of fine, hairy structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The leaves are usually small and clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure. This variant is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in succulent and xerophytic plant systems.
