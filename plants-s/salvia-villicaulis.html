Salvia villicaulis

Salvia villicaulis, commonly known as the silky sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a soft, downy coating.

Salvia villicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
