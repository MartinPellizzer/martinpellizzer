Silene spergulifolia subsp. soskae

Silene spergulifolia subsp.

soskae, commonly known as the Soska's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of northern Europe and Siberia. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and fragile species in its native habitat.
