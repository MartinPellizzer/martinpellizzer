Sonchus picroides

Sonchus picroides, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it has been introduced to other regions, including North America. The plant is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Sonchus picroides is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, and it can sometimes become a weed in agricultural settings.
