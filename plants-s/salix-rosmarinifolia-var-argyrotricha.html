Salix rosmarinifolia var. argyrotricha

Salix rosmarinifolia var. argyrotricha, commonly known as silver-haired willow, is a variant of the rosmarinifolia willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces catkins in early spring, which are typical of willow species.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
