Salacia amentacea

Salacia amentacea, commonly known as the silverleaf salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in pendulous catkins. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salacia amentacea is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
