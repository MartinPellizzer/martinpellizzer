Sasa akagiensis

Sasa akagiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Sasa akagiensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
