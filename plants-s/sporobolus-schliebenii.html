Sporobolus schliebenii

Sporobolus schliebenii, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in the fall. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as plains, deserts, and along riverbanks.

Sporobolus schliebenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
