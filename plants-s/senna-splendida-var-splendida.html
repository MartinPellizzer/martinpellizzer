Senna splendida var. splendida

Senna splendida var. splendida, commonly known as the golden senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
