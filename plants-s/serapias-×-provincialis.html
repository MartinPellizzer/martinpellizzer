Serapias × provincialis

Serapias × provincialis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Serapias × provincialis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
