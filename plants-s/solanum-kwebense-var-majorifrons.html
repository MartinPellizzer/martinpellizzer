Solanum kwebense var. majorifrons

Solanum kwebense var. majorifrons, commonly known as the large-fruited eggplant, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces large, fleshy, egg-shaped fruits that are typically purple in color. The fruits are edible when ripe and have a mild, nutty flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes in its native habitat.
