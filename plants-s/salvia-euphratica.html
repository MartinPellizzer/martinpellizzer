Salvia euphratica

Salvia euphratica, commonly known as the desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Salvia euphratica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
