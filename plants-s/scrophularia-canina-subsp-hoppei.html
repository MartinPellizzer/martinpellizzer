Scrophularia canina subsp. hoppei

Scrophularia canina subsp.

hoppei, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of tubular, purple to pink flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
