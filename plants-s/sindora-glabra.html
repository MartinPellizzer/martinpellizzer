Sindora glabra

Sindora glabra, commonly known as the smooth sindora, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves with stipules. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Sindora glabra is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye due to its unique botanical properties.
