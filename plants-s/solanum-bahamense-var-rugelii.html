Solanum bahamense var. rugelii

Solanum bahamense var. rugelii, commonly known as the Bahamian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its foliage is generally hairy, and it has a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
