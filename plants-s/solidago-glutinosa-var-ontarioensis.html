Solidago glutinosa var. ontarioensis

Solidago glutinosa var. ontarioensis, commonly known as the Ontario sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, fluffy clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variant is distinguished by its sticky foliage and is often found in the Great Lakes region.
