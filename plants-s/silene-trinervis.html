Silene trinervis

Silene trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has alternate, linear leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its common name. Silene trinervis typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
