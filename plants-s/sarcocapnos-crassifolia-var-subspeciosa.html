Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. subspeciosa

Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. subspeciosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
