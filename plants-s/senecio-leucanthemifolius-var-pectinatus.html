Senecio leucanthemifolius var. pectinatus

Senecio leucanthemifolius var. pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
