Salix pseudowallichiana

Salix pseudowallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are an important food source for pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
