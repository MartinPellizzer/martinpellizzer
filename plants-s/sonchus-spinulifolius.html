Sonchus spinulifolius

Sonchus spinulifolius, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it has been naturalized in various parts of the world. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
