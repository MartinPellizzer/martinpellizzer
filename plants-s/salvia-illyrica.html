Salvia illyrica

Salvia illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
