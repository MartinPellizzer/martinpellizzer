Senecio medley-woodii

Senecio medley-woodii, commonly known as the Medley-Wood's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
