Seruneum glaucum

Seruneum glaucum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh mountain climate.

Seruneum glaucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
