Sabina horizontalis f. douglasii

Sabina horizontalis f. douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen with dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are usually dark blue when mature. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Cupressaceae family, which includes other juniper species.
