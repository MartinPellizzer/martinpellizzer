Saussurea acanthocarpa

Saussurea acanthocarpa, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Indian saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a unique yellow-orange resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dye. It thrives in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ecological and medicinal significance, it is also considered a rare and endangered species.
