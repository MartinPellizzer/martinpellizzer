Syagrus stenopetala

Syagrus stenopetala, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the slender palm, is a species of palm tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by dark red, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Syagrus stenopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
