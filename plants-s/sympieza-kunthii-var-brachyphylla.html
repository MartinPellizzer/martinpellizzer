Sympieza kunthii var. brachyphylla

Sympieza kunthii var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaf sympieza, is a rare shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
