Scleranthus annuus subsp. ruscinonensis

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonese thornweed, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the genus. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
