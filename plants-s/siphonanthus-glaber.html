Siphonanthus glaber

Siphonanthus glaber, commonly known as the naked-stemmed siphonanthus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Siphonanthus glaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
