Sideritis pusilla

Sideritis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf ironwort, is a small perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Sideritis pusilla is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
