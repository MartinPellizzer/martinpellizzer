Sigesbeckia somalensis

Sigesbeckia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Somalia and parts of East Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems covered with soft, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Sigesbeckia somalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
