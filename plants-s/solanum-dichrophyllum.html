Solanum dichrophyllum

Solanum dichrophyllum, commonly known as the two-colored nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned leaves, which are typically green on the upper surface and purple or reddish on the underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum dichrophyllum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streambanks.
