Syzygium cordifolium subsp. spissum

Syzygium cordifolium subsp.

spissum, commonly known as the thick-leaved clove. It is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are broader and more pointed than those of the typical species.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark red or black berries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and is often found in lowland forests.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its aromatic fruits.
