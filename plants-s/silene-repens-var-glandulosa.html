Silene repens var. glandulosa

Silene repens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular stoloniferous catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground and produce numerous flowering shoots. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is often found in sandy or disturbed soils across temperate regions.
