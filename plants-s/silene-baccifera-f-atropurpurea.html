Silene baccifera f. atropurpurea

Silene baccifera f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple or reddish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is often seen in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
