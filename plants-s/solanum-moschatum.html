Solanum moschatum

Solanum moschatum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange or red tuberous roots, which are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal have contributed to its widespread cultivation around the world.
