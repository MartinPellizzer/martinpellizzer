Stylophorum japonicum var. dissectum

Stylophorum japonicum var. dissectum, commonly known as Japanese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
