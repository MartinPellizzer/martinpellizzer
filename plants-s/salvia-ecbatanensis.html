Salvia ecbatanensis

Salvia ecbatanensis, commonly known as the Ecbatana sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia ecbatanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
