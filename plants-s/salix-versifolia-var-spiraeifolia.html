Salix versifolia var. spiraeifolia

Salix versifolia var. spiraeifolia, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
