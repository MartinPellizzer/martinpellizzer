Schlechtendalia speciosa

Schlechtendalia speciosa, commonly known as the pink glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Schlechtendalia speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
