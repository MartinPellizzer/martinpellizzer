Schefflera stolzii

Schefflera stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's umbrella plant, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, leathery leaves. The plant features a single trunk with a thick, woody base and produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Schefflera stolzii is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
