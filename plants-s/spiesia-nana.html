Spiesia nana

Spiesia nana, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
