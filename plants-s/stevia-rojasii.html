Stevia rojasii

Stevia rojasii, commonly known as the red stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its lance-shaped leaves, which are used to produce natural sweeteners. Stevia rojasii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
