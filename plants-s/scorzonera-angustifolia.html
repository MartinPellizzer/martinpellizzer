Scorzonera angustifolia

Scorzonera angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Scorzonera angustifolia is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cooler, well-drained conditions.
