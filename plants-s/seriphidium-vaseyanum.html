Seriphidium vaseyanum

Seriphidium vaseyanum, commonly known as the Persian tansy.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Seriphidium vaseyanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
