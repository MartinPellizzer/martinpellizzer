Scaphochlamys cordata

Scaphochlamys cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf scaphochlamys, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
