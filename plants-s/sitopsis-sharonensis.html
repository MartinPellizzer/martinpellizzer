Sitopsis sharonensis

Sitopsis sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon's sitopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Sharon Plain in Israel.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic plant of the region.
