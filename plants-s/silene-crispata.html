Silene crispata

Silene crispata, commonly known as the crinkled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, crinkled leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Silene crispata is a herbaceous perennial that produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous small seeds.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
