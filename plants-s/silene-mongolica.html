Silene mongolica

Silene mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Silene mongolica has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
