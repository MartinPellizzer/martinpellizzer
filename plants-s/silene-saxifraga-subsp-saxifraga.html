Silene saxifraga subsp. saxifraga

Silene saxifraga subsp.

saxifraga, commonly known as the rockpink, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy pink or magenta flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It is found across parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare or threatened species in many areas.
