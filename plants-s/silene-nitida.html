Silene nitida

Silene nitida, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has thin, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Silene nitida thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
