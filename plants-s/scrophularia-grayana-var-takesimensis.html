Scrophularia grayana var. takesimensis

Scrophularia grayana var. takesimensis, commonly known as the Korean heartworts, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
