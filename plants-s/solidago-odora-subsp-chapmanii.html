Solidago odora subsp. chapmanii

Solidago odora subsp.

chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's sweet goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
