Salsola forcipitata

Salsola forcipitata, commonly known as sandmat or desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It has a low, spreading growth habit and often forms dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Salsola forcipitata is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a common species in coastal and saline environments.
