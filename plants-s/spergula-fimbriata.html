Spergula fimbriata

Spergula fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it often grows in moist, calcareous soils.

Spergula fimbriata is typically found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.
