Solanum sisleyanum

Solanum sisleyanum, commonly known as the sisleyan nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
