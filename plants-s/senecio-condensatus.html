Senecio condensatus

Senecio condensatus, commonly known as the denseleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Senecio condensatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
