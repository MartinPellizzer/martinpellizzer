Sarcochilus pulvinifer

Sarcochilus pulvinifer, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet or purple flowers with prominent veining. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in woodland areas. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate appearance.

Sarcochilus pulvinifer is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native Australian flora.
