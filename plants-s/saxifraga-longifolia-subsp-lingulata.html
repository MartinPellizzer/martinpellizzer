Saxifraga longifolia subsp. lingulata

Saxifraga longifolia subsp.

lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals with a pale pink to white coloration.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
