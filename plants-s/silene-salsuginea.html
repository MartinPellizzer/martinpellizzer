Silene salsuginea

Silene salsuginea, commonly known as the desert sandplant, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing adaptations to extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water, and it has a fibrous root system that aids in stabilizing the soil.

Silene salsuginea is also known for its ability to survive in harsh environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
