Solanum nigrum var. oleraceum

Solanum nigrum var. oleraceum, commonly known as black nightshade or edible nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to heart-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in traditional cuisines.
