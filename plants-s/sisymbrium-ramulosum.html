Sisymbrium ramulosum

Sisymbrium ramulosum, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and a branched, leafless stem that often grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant lacks true leaves, relying instead on its stem for photosynthesis. It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially parasitizes the roots of other plants to obtain nutrients.

Sisymbrium ramulosum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.
