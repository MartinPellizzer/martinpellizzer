Solanum xanthocarpum

Solanum xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow fruit nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with five petals.

Solanum xanthocarpum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
