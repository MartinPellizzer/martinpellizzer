Spergularia media var. aptera

Spergularia media var. aptera, commonly known as the smooth sandspurry, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune systems and other arid habitats.

This plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
