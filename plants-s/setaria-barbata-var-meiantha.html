Setaria barbata var. meiantha

Setaria barbata var. meiantha, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
