Sorghum venustum

Sorghum venustum, commonly known as the celestial millet, is a strikingly tall grass species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, silver-blue leaves and clusters of fluffy, pinkish-purple inflorescences that resemble clouds. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive with minimal water, making it a resilient crop. Its unique appearance has made it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional ceremonies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
