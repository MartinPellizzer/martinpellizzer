Scutellaria improvisa

Scutellaria improvisa, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped blue or violet flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Scutellaria improvisa is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
