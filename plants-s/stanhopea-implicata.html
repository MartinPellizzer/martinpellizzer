Stanhopea implicata

Stanhopea implicata, commonly known as the fragrant stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted to the humid and shaded environments it inhabits.

Stanhopea implicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
