Syzygium australe

Syzygium australe, commonly known as the Australian brush cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Syzygium australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
