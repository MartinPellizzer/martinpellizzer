Solanum acroglossum

Solanum acroglossum, commonly known as the red-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red, tubular flowers that open in the evening. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum acroglossum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
