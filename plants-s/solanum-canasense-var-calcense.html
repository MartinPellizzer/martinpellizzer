Solanum canasense var. calcense

Solanum canasense var. calcense, commonly known as the calcicola nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme environments.
