Solanum denseaculeatum

Solanum denseaculeatum, commonly known as the dense-aculeate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
