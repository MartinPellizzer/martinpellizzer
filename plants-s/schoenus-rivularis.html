Schoenus rivularis

Schoenus rivularis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of rivers and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Schoenus rivularis is adapted to waterlogged conditions and is often found in temperate regions across the globe.
