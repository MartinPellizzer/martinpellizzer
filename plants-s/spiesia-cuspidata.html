Spiesia cuspidata

Spiesia cuspidata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and sturdy.

Spiesia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
