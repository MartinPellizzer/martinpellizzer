Solidago × alexanderi

Solidago × alexanderi, commonly known as Alexander's goldenrod, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Alexander's goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
