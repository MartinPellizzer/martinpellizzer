Symphytum bornmuelleri

Symphytum bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's comfrey, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its healing benefits.
