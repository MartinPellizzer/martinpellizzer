Sphacele paniculata

Sphacele paniculata, commonly known as the swamp panic, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that produces small, white to greenish flowers. The plant has alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Sphacele paniculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
