Salvia aucheri var. amethystea

Salvia aucheri var. amethystea, commonly known as Amethyst Salvia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of violet-blue, tubular flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive grayish-blue hue due to dense pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
