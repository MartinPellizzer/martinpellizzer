Sideritis villosa subsp. gossypina

Sideritis villosa subsp.

gossypina, commonly known as woolly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
