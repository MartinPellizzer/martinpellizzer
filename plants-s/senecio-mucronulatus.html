Senecio mucronulatus

Senecio mucronulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
