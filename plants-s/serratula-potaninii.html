Serratula potaninii

Serratula potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant features robust, hairy stems and deeply lobed, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Serratula potaninii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
