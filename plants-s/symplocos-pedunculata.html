Symplocos pedunculata

Symplocos pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red color.

Symplocos pedunculata is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.
