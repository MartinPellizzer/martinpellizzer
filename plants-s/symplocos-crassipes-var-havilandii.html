Symplocos crassipes var. havilandii

Symplocos crassipes var. havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's waxleaf, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
