Serratula sogdiana

Serratula sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually serrated, giving it the genus name "Serratula.".

The flowers are yellowish to reddish in color and bloom during the summer months.
