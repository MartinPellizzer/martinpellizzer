Symplocos obtusa var. obovata

Symplocos obtusa var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of the habitat.
