Scutellaria nepetifolia

Scutellaria nepetifolia, commonly known as heartworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Scutellaria nepetifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
