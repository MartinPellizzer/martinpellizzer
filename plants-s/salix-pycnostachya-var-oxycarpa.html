Salix pycnostachya var. oxycarpa

Salix pycnostachya var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
