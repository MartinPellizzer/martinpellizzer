Syncarpha argentea

Syncarpha argentea, commonly known as silverleaf synbranch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Syncarpha argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
