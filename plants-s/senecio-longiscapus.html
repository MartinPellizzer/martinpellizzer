Senecio longiscapus

Senecio longiscapus, commonly known as the long-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its elongated stems and narrow, linear leaves give it a unique appearance.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
