Squamellaria imberbis

Squamellaria imberbis, commonly known as the leafless morning glory, is a unique member of the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only stems and flowers present. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted for rapid growth and efficient photosynthesis through its modified stems.
