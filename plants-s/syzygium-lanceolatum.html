Syzygium lanceolatum

Syzygium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Myrtaceae family.
