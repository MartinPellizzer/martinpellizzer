Solanum nigrum var. subhastatum

Solanum nigrum var. subhastatum, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces white or pale green flowers.

This variant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
