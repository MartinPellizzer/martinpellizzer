Serpocaulon nanegalense

Serpocaulon nanegalense, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula serpocaulon, is a rare and endemic fern found in the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
