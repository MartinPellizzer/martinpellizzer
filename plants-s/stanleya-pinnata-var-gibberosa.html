Stanleya pinnata var. gibberosa

Stanleya pinnata var. gibberosa, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its taproot system allows it to access deep water sources, making it resilient in harsh climates.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
