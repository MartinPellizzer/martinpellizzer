Saxifraga nervosa subsp. lamottei

Saxifraga nervosa subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
