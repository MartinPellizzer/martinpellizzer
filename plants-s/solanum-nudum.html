Solanum nudum

Solanum nudum, commonly known as the naked nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a smooth, green berry that turns red when ripe.

Solanum nudum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and forest edges.
