Sedum × luteolum

Sedum × luteolum, commonly known as the orange stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a low-growing rosette. The plant produces clusters of bright orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
