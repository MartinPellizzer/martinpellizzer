Saccharum robustum

Saccharum robustum, commonly known as giant bamboo or wild sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, with sturdy, hollow stems that are often used in construction and as a source of fiber. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a rough texture and a sweet scent when crushed. Its rhizomes contain a high concentration of sugars, making it a potential source of biofuel and syrup.

Saccharum robustum thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
