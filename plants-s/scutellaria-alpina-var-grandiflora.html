Scutellaria alpina var. grandiflora

Scutellaria alpina var. grandiflora, commonly known as Alpine blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the mint family, Lamiaceae.
