Saussurea hybrida

Saussurea hybrida, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Saussurea hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
