Stickmannia ovalifolia

Stickmannia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf stickman plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble small, stick-like structures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Stickmannia ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unusual foliage.
