Saussurea matsumurae

Saussurea matsumurae, commonly known as Japanese yarrow or mountain pepper, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fibrous roots and clustered, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in mountainous regions and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health benefits.
