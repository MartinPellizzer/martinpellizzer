Schizachyrium tenuispicatum

Schizachyrium tenuispicatum, commonly known as narrow-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristly, awned flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Schizachyrium tenuispicatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
