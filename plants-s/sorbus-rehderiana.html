Sorbus rehderiana

Sorbus rehderiana, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
