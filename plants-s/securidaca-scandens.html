Securidaca scandens

Securidaca scandens, commonly known as the climbing senna, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across vegetation. The plant features pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

The botanical features of Securidaca scandens make it a notable species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
