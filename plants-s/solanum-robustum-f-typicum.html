Solanum robustum f. typicum

Solanum robustum f. typicum, commonly known as the robust nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into round, smooth berries. These berries are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
