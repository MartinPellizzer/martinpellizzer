Sinapis candollei

Sinapis candollei, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemose inflorescences, typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are siliques, which contain several seeds, and it is often grown as an ornamental or for its potential medicinal properties.

Sinapis candollei is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
