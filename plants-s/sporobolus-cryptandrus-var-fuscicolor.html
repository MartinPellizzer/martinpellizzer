Sporobolus cryptandrus var. fuscicolor

Sporobolus cryptandrus var. fuscicolor, commonly known as the blackseed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seed heads, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered and often remain attached to the plant even after the seeds have matured.
