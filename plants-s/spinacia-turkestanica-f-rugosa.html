Spinacia turkestanica f. rugosa

Spinacia turkestanica f. rugosa, commonly known as the rugose spinach, is a variant of the spinach genus characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaf surface.

This plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a unique culinary option. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
