Scabiosa albanensis

Scabiosa albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa albanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
