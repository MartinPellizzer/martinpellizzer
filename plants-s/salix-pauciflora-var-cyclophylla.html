Salix pauciflora var. cyclophylla

Salix pauciflora var. cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla willow, is a variant of the pauciflora willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
