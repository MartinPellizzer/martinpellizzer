Schwannia

Schwannia, commonly known as the "water spinach" or "rhubarb spinach," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, with Schwannia hirta being the most well-known. These plants are characterized by their succulent, edible leaves and stems, which are often used in culinary applications. Schwannia species are valued for their nutritional content and are cultivated in various parts of Asia and Africa.
