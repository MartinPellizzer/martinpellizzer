Salvia × hegelmaieri nothosubsp. accidentalis

Salvia × hegelmaieri nothosubsp.

accidentalis, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers, which are arranged in whorled inflorescences along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and aromatic, typical of the Salvia genus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
