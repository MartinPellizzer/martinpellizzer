Scrophularia marginata

Scrophularia marginata, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, purplish-pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
