Senecio contristatus

Senecio contristatus, commonly known as the weeping senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
