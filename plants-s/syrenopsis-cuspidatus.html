Syrenopsis cuspidatus

Syrenopsis cuspidatus, commonly known as the spiny marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized plant found in wetland habitats.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiny floral structures. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments, often near the edges of marshes or ponds. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-yellow, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Syrenopsis cuspidatus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting specific pollinators and contributing to wetland biodiversity.
