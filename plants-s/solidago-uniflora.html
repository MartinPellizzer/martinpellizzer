Solidago uniflora

Solidago uniflora, commonly known as the singleflower goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and streambanks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, yellow inflorescence composed of a single flower per stem, which blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago uniflora plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
