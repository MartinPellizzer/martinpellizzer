Streptanthus cordatus var. duranii

Streptanthus cordatus var. duranii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow or white flowers in early spring.
