Senecio sagittatus

Senecio sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio sagittatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
