Salix pyrocanthoides

Salix pyrocanthoides, commonly known as the firethorn willow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental thorns and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
