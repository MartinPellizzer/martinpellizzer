Sigmatochilus kinabaluensis

Sigmatochilus kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu fern, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively on Mount Kinabalu in Borneo.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in the cool, moist microhabitats of the mountain's upper elevations, often growing on rock faces or in shaded crevices. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and environmental changes.
