Stellaria graminea var. intermedia

Stellaria graminea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its delicate floral structures and adaptability to various soil conditions.
