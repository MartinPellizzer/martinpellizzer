Silene diclinis

Silene diclinis, commonly known as the two-rowed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silene diclinis has alternate, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance.
