Salvia breviconnectivata

Salvia breviconnectivata, commonly known as the short-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
