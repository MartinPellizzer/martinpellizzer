Symphyotrichum crenifolium

Symphyotrichum crenifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy purple to pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum crenifolium is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
