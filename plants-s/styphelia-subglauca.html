Styphelia subglauca

Styphelia subglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Styphelia subglauca thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal heaths.

It is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
