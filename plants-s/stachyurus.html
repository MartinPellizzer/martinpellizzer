Stachyurus

Stachyurus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Styracaceae.

It is native to China, Korea, and Japan, and is characterized by its unique, showy, fringe-like flowers that appear in early spring. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Stachyurus species are valued in horticulture for their ornamental value, particularly the delicate, cascading blooms that add visual interest to gardens.

They prefer well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making them suitable for a variety of garden settings.
