Smilax jacquinii

Smilax jacquinii, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Smilax jacquinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
