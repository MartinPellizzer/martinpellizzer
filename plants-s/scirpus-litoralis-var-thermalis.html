Scirpus litoralis var. thermalis

Scirpus litoralis var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedge, is a variant of the common flatsedge native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. Its growth is influenced by temperature and water availability, making it adapted to thermophilic conditions.

The botanical features of Scirpus litoralis var. thermalis make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
