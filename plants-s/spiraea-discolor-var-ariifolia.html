Spiraea discolor var. ariifolia

Spiraea discolor var. ariifolia, commonly known as the variegated weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of white flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically oval with a distinctive white or cream edge, creating a striking variegated appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
