Salvia phlomoides var. africana

Salvia phlomoides var. africana, commonly known as African sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
