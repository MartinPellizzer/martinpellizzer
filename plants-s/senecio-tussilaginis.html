Senecio tussilaginis

Senecio tussilaginis, commonly known as the coughwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Senecio tussilaginis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
