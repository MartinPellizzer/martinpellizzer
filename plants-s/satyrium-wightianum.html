Satyrium wightianum

Satyrium wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's satyrium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Satyrium wightianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
