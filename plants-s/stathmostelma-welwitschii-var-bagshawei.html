Stathmostelma welwitschii var. bagshawei

Stathmostelma welwitschii var. bagshawei, commonly known as the Namib Desert lichen, is a unique lichen species that thrives in the arid conditions of the Namib Desert.

It is a symbiotic composite of a fungus and a green alga, which allows it to survive in extreme environments with minimal water. The plant forms a crust-like growth on rock surfaces, contributing to soil stabilization and providing microhabitats for other desert organisms. Its structure is adapted to reduce water loss and maximize light absorption.

This species is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its resilience and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
