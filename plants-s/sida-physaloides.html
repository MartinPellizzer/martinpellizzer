Sida physaloides

Sida physaloides, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Sida physaloides is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also considered a weed in many regions due to its invasive potential and competitive nature.
