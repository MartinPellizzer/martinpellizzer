Sideritis ilicifolia subsp. cardoana

Sideritis ilicifolia subsp.

cardoana, commonly known as the Greek thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
