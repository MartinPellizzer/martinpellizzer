Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. concolor

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf false hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
