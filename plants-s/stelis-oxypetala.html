Stelis oxypetala

Stelis oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clustered, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain seeds.

Stelis oxypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
