Senecio subdiscoideus f. homogama

Senecio subdiscoideus f. homogama, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
