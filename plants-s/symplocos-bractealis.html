Symplocos bractealis

Symplocos bractealis, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are visually striking. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
