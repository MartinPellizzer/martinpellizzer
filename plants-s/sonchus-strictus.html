Sonchus strictus

Sonchus strictus, commonly known as narrowleaf sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the upper surface and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and borne in small clusters, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.
