Sorbaria sorbifolia var. glandulifolia

Sorbaria sorbifolia var. glandulifolia, commonly known as the glandular leaf sorbaria, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, often with glandular dots along the veins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
