Symphyotrichum divaricatum

Symphyotrichum divaricatum, commonly known as the slender New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy purple to pink blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Symphyotrichum divaricatum plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
