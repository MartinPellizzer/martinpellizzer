Solanum codonanthum

Solanum codonanthum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a red-orange color. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with simple, ovate leaves and small white flowers. Its fruits are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in various regions for both food and garden purposes.
