Serjania inscripta

Serjania inscripta, commonly known as the inscribed serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes several tropical shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by various bird species.

Serjania inscripta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
