Saussurea jucunda

Saussurea jucunda, commonly known as the Himalayan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, spiny leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is an important species in the flora of its native region.
