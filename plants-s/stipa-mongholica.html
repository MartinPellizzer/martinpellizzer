Stipa mongholica

Stipa mongholica, commonly known as the Mongolian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Stipa mongholica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
