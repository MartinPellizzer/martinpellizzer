Symplocos cochinchinensis var. molobros

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. molobros, commonly known as the Molobros tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with a leathery texture and a serrated margin. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
