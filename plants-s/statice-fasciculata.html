Statice fasciculata

Statice fasciculata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Statice fasciculata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in floral arrangements and traditional medicine.
