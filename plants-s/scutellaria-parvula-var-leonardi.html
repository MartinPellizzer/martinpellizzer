Scutellaria parvula var. leonardi

Scutellaria parvula var. leonardi, commonly known as Leonard's small skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, two-lipped blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
