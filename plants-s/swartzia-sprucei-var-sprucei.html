Swartzia sprucei var. sprucei

Swartzia sprucei var. sprucei, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
