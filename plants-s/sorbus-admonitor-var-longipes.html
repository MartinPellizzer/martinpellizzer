Sorbus admonitor var. longipes

Sorbus admonitor var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin, and they turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. This plant is typically found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
