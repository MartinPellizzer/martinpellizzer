Stachys hissarica subsp. turkestanica

Stachys hissarica subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ecological significance.
