Spergula vernalis var. macrocarpa

Spergula vernalis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded spring sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The variety macrocarpa is distinguished by its relatively large seeds compared to other varieties of Spergula vernalis.
