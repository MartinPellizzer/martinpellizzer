Syzygium watsonianum

Syzygium watsonianum, commonly known as the Watson's apple, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor.

Syzygium watsonianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
