Sedum rupestre

Sedum rupestre, commonly known as stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. It is a perennial species with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Sedum rupestre is widely used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
