Saccharum alopecuroides

Saccharum alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is not as economically significant as other sugarcane species.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
