Solanum montanum

Solanum montanum, commonly known as mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum montanum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
