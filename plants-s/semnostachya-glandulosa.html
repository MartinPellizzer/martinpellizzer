Semnostachya glandulosa

Semnostachya glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
