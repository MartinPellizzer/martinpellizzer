Sida burchelii

Sida burchelii, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Sida burchelii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
