Sorindeia madagascariensis var. arborea

Sorindeia madagascariensis var. arborea, commonly known as the Madagascar tree sorindeia, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, round fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils within its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
