Segetella vulgaris

Segetella vulgaris, commonly known as the common water clover, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its bright green, floating leaves that are divided into three narrow lobes, resembling a cloverleaf shape. The plant typically grows in dense mats on the water surface, often in still or slow-moving water bodies. Segetella vulgaris plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.

It is a perennial species that spreads through vegetative reproduction, forming extensive colonies in suitable conditions.
