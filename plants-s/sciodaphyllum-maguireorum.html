Sciodaphyllum maguireorum

Sciodaphyllum maguireorum, commonly known as the Moore's ghost fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that exhibit a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. This plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or among dense foliage. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sciodaphyllum maguireorum is considered a conservation concern.
