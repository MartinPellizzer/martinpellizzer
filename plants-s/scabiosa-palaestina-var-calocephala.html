Scabiosa palaestina var. calocephala

Scabiosa palaestina var. calocephala, commonly known as the Palestine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly lobed shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
