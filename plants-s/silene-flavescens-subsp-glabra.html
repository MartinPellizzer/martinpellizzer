Silene flavescens subsp. glabra

Silene flavescens subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The subspecies glabra is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as dunes and grasslands.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a short growing season, flowering in late spring to early summer.
