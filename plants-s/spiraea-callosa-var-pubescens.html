Spiraea callosa var. pubescens

Spiraea callosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
