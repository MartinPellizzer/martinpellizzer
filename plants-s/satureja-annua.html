Satureja annua

Satureja annua, commonly known as annual savory, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual plant with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, four-lobed fruits that contain the seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and antimicrobial properties.

Satureja annua thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and herb gardens.
