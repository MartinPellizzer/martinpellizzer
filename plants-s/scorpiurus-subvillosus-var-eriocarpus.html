Scorpiurus subvillosus var. eriocarpus

Scorpiurus subvillosus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are small, spherical drupes that mature to a reddish color.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in the eastern United States.
