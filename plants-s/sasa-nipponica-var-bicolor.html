Sasa nipponica var. bicolor

Sasa nipponica var. bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bicolor sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique bicoloration and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
