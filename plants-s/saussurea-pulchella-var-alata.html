Saussurea pulchella var. alata

Saussurea pulchella var. alata, commonly known as the winged saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces feathery, wing-like bracts that give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
