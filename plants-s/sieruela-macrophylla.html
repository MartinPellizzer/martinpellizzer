Sieruela macrophylla

Sieruela macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sieruela, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
