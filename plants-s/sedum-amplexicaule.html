Sedum amplexicaule

Sedum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping sedum, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
