Syzygium micklethwaitii

Syzygium micklethwaitii, commonly known as the Micklethwait's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family, native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling apples in appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
