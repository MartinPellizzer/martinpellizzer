Stapelia praetermissa var. luteola

Stapelia praetermissa var. luteola, commonly known as the Yellow Star Plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem covered in a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators like moths. Its leaves are typically green and succulent, adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
