Subpilocereus fricii

Subpilocereus fricii, commonly known as the Frici's night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in sparse spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. These flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
