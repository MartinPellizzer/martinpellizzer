Sapindus frutescens

Sapindus frutescens, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound, and its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and the bark is often rough and fissured.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
