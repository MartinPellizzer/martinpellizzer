Syringa reticulata subsp. pekinensis

Syringa reticulata subsp.

pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The bark of mature plants becomes fissured and reddish-brown, adding visual interest throughout the year.
