Silphium dentatum f. nodum

Silphium dentatum f. nodum, commonly known as the nodular silphium, is a rare and ancient plant with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy stem and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique growth habit includes the formation of nodular structures along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
