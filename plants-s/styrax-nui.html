Styrax nui

Styrax nui, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy surface.

Styrax nui typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
