Sorbus zolyomii

Sorbus zolyomii, commonly known as the Hungarian rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
