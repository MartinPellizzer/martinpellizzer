Silene alpina

Silene alpina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Silene alpina has a compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
