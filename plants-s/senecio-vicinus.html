Senecio vicinus

Senecio vicinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer months.

Senecio vicinus is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
