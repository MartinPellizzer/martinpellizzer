Scutellaria orientalis var. microphylla

Scutellaria orientalis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are typically tubular at the base and flare out into a wide, hood-like shape.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
