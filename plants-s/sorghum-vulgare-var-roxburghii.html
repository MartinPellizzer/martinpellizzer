Sorghum vulgare var. roxburghii

Sorghum vulgare var. roxburghii, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to Africa and cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a short-statured grass with narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy crop for arid environments. Its grains are used for food, animal feed, and traditional brewing.

The plant's botanical features include fibrous roots, a robust stem, and small, two-lobed leaves.
