Schima confertiflora

Schima confertiflora, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Schima confertiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
