Syringa henryi

Syringa henryi, commonly known as Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Syringa henryi prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
