Senecio angavonensis

Senecio angavonensis, commonly known as the Angavo Mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its foliage is usually narrow and grayish-green, with small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution.
