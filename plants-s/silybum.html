Silybum

Silybum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant contains a compound called silymarin, which is known for its potential liver-protecting properties. Silybum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its seeds are often used in herbal remedies and as a source of natural antioxidants.
