Solidago taurica

Solidago taurica, commonly known as the Crimean goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching.

Solidago taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
