Sugerokia japonica var. racemosa

Sugerokia japonica var. racemosa, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its preference for partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
