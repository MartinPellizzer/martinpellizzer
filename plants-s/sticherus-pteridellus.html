Sticherus pteridellus

Sticherus pteridellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's intricate leaf structure and preference for high humidity make it a unique subject for botanical study.
