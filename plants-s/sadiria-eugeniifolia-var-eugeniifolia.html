Sadiria eugeniifolia var. eugeniifolia

Sadiria eugeniifolia var. eugeniifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces striking red or purple spikes of flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of wetland ecosystems.
