Serratula divaricata

Serratula divaricata, commonly known as the variable sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually serrated and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its common name.
