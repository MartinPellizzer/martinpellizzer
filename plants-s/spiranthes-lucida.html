Spiranthes lucida

Spiranthes lucida, commonly known as the white fringed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
