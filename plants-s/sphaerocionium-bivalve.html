Sphaerocionium bivalve

Sphaerocionium bivalve, commonly known as the golden globular cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, spherical stem covered in dense, spiny bracts that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and enabling survival in harsh environments.

The plant's ability to store water in its fleshy stem makes it well-adapted to desert conditions.
