Stachys cretica subsp. cassia

Stachys cretica subsp.

cassia, commonly known as redstem betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
