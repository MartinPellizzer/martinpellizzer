Salvia subpatens

Salvia subpatens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia subpatens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
