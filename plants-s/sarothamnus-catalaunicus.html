Sarothamnus catalaunicus

Sarothamnus catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan thornbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Sarothamnus catalaunicus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
