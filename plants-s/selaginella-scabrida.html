Selaginella scabrida

Selaginella scabrida, commonly known as the scaly spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of closely packed, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. Its sporophyte stage is characterized by a short, unbranched stem with spirally arranged leaves.

Selaginella scabrida is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
