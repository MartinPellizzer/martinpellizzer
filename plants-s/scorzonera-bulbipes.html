Scorzonera bulbipes

Scorzonera bulbipes, commonly known as the mountain scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine, or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features a robust root system and produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are often covered in fine hairs.

Scorzonera bulbipes is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat various ailments.
