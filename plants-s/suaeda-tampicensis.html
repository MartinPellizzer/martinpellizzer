Suaeda tampicensis

Suaeda tampicensis, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Suaeda tampicensis plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
