Senna pendula var. eriocarpa

Senna pendula var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
