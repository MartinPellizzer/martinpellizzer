Syzygium heterocladum

Syzygium heterocladum, commonly known as the variable syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Syzygium heterocladum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
