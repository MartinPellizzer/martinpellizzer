Senecio leptoschizus var. leptocladus

Senecio leptoschizus var. leptocladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
