Silene inamoena

Silene inamoena, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene inamoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering habit.
