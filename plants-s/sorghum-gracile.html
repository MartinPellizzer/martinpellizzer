Sorghum gracile

Sorghum gracile, commonly known as slender sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in savannas and grasslands.

Sorghum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
