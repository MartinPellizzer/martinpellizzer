Selaginella ciliauricula

Selaginella ciliauricula, commonly known as the silky spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant features delicate, thread-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella ciliauricula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
