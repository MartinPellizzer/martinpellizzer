Sceptridium negeri

Sceptridium negeri, commonly known as the Malay cliff fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, upright fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with a dark green, leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky cliffs or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sceptridium negeri is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
