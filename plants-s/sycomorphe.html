Sycomorphe

Sycomorphe, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens across Europe and North America.
