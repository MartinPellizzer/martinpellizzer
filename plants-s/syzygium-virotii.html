Syzygium virotii

Syzygium virotii, commonly known as the Kalamata apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet-tart flavor. Syzygium virotii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
