Stachys saxicola subsp. platyodon

Stachys saxicola subsp.

platyodon, commonly known as the broad-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to rocky and dry habitats in the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.

It is well adapted to poor soils and is often found in limestone or gravelly areas.
