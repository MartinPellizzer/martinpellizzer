Syzygium lauterbachianum var. phaeophloium

Syzygium lauterbachianum var. phaeophloium, commonly known as the black-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves with prominent, dark veins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
