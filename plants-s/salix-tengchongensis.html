Salix tengchongensis

Salix tengchongensis, commonly known as Tengchong willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China, specifically the Tengchong region in Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, mountainous environments, often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
