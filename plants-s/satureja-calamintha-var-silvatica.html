Satureja calamintha var. silvatica

Satureja calamintha var. silvatica, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, known for their pungent, peppery scent.
