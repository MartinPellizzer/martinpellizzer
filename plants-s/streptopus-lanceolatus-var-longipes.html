Streptopus lanceolatus var. longipes

Streptopus lanceolatus var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and typically blue or purple, arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

This variety is distinguished by its longer stems compared to other varieties of Streptopus.
