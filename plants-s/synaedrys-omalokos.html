Synaedrys omalokos

Synaedrys omalokos, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
