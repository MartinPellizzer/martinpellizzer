Serjania mexicana

Serjania mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican serjania, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually white or pink and appear in clusters.

Serjania mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
