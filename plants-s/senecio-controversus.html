Senecio controversus

Senecio controversus, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and low-maintenance landscaping.
