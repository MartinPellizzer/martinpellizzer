Syringa buxifolia

Syringa buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, panicle-shaped clusters of white or pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa buxifolia is also valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
