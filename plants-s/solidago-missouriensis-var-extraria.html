Solidago missouriensis var. extraria

Solidago missouriensis var. extraria, commonly known as the Missouri goldenrod.

This variant is a perennial herb native to the central United States, characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
