Sesuvium acutifolium

Sesuvium acutifolium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a decorative touch to coastal landscapes. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines and salt marshes.

Sesuvium acutifolium plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
