Syzygium stenocladum

Syzygium stenocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The tree produces edible, sour berries that are used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Syzygium stenocladum is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
