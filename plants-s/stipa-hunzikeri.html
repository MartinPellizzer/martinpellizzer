Stipa hunzikeri

Stipa hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, feathery panicles that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage is often silver-gray, giving it a distinctive appearance that helps it withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Stipa hunzikeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
