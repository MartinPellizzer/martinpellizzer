Schoenorchis hungii

Schoenorchis hungii, commonly known as the Chinese ghost orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include a slender stem and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
