Sidalcea neomexicana subsp. diehlii

Sidalcea neomexicana subsp.

diehlii, commonly known as the Desert Mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils in desert regions.

The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
