Solanum tuberosum var. rossicum

Solanum tuberosum var. rossicum, commonly known as the Rossicum potato, is a botanical variety of the common potato.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple and white striped tubers, which are rich in nutrients and have a unique flavor profile. This variety is native to the Andes region and is adapted to cooler climates. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces foliage with spiny leaves.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use.
