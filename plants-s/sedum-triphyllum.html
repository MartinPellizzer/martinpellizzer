Sedum triphyllum

Sedum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leafed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Sedum triphyllum is drought-tolerant and has adaptations that allow it to store water efficiently.
