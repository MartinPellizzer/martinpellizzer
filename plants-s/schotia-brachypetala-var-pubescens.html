Schotia brachypetala var. pubescens

Schotia brachypetala var. pubescens, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
