Scleranthus umagellus

Scleranthus umagellus, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dunes and along shorelines.

Scleranthus umagellus is adapted to salt spray and is an important part of the coastal flora.
