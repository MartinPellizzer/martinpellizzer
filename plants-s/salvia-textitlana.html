Salvia textitlana

Salvia textitlana, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia textitlana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
