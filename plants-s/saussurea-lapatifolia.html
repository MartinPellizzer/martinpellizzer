Saussurea lapatifolia

Saussurea lapatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
