Senegalia seigleri

Senegalia seigleri, commonly known as the desert shrub or red acacia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are typically small and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted to disperse in dry environments.

Senegalia seigleri is well-suited to harsh climates and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
