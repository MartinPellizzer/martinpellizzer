Salacia soyauxii

Salacia soyauxii, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as those in the genus Ficus. Its unique botanical features include a lack of true roots and a specialized mode of nutrient absorption from its host.

Salacia soyauxii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
