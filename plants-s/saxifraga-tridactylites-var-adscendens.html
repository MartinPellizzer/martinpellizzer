Saxifraga tridactylites var. adscendens

Saxifraga tridactylites var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky and barren environments, often growing on slopes and cliffs where other plants struggle to survive. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its low-growing habit and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it well adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils.

It is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
