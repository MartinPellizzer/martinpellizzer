Sciaphila sumatrana

Sciaphila sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran shade plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with fleshy roots adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and dark green, and it produces small, intricate flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

Due to habitat loss, Sciaphila sumatrana is considered endangered and is of significant interest in botanical conservation efforts.
