Syzygium rubrovenium

Syzygium rubrovenium, commonly known as the red-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent red veins. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour fruits resembling tamarinds. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its tart flavor and nutritional content.

Syzygium rubrovenium thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.
