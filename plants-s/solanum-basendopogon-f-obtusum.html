Solanum basendopogon f. obtusum

Solanum basendopogon f. obtusum, commonly known as the blunt-leaved nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade genus Solanum.

It is characterized by its broad, obtuse leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
