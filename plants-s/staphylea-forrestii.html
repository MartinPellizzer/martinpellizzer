Staphylea forrestii

Staphylea forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Staphyleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Staphylea forrestii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
