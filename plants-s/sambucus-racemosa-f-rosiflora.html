Sambucus racemosa f. rosiflora

Sambucus racemosa f. rosiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered elderberry, is a flowering plant in the honeysuckle family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include smooth, reddish bark and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and wetlands across eastern North America.
