Saussurea filicifolia

Saussurea filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and arid conditions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in the soil and store moisture.

Saussurea filicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
