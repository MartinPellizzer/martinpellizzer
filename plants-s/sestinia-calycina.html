Sestinia calycina

Sestinia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sestinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its botanical features include a robust stem and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Sestinia calycina is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
