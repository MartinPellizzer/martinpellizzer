Staphylea pinnata

Staphylea pinnata, commonly known as the common staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Staphylea pinnata is often found in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
