Syzygium vulcanicum

Syzygium vulcanicum, commonly known as the Christmas peach, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The tree produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are sweet and juicy, often consumed fresh or used in cooking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Syzygium vulcanicum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
