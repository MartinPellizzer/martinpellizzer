Synaedrys debaryana

Synaedrys debaryana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Synaedrys debaryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
