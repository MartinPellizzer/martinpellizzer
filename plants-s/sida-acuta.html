Sida acuta

Sida acuta, commonly known as smallleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Sida acuta is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
