Samanea filipes

Samanea filipes, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching trunk and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, fluffy, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.

Samanea filipes plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
