Scirpus soloniensis

Scirpus soloniensis, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and capable of extensive underground growth, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus soloniensis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
