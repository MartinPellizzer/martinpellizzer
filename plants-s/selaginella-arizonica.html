Selaginella arizonica

Selaginella arizonica, commonly known as Arizona clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage.

Selaginella arizonica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
