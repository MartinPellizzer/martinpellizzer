Saxifraga aizoon var. major

Saxifraga aizoon var. major, commonly known as the Sierra stonecrop, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.
