Serjania nipensis

Serjania nipensis, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Serjania nipensis produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
