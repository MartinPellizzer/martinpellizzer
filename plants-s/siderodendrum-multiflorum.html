Siderodendrum multiflorum

Siderodendrum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered ironwood, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Siderodendrum multiflorum is often cultivated for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens and as a shade tree.
