Sida malvaviscus

Sida malvaviscus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its hairy, velvety texture gives it its common name, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida malvaviscus is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
