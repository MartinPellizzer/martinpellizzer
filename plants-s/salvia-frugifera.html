Salvia frugifera

Salvia frugifera, commonly known as the "cotton bush," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that resemble cotton, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia frugifera is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
