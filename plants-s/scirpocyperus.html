Scirpocyperus

Scirpocyperus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plants typically grow in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Scirpocyperus species are often used in wetland restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal in water gardens and bog landscapes.
