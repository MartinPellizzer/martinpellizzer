Saxifraga pedemontana var. pulvinaris

Saxifraga pedemontana var. pulvinaris, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where conditions are harsh. The plant features upright stems with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in rocky crevices and meadows.
