Salvia ballotiflora var. pinguifolia

Salvia ballotiflora var. pinguifolia, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
