Salvia chiapensis

Salvia chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapan sage, is a perennial herb native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a minty scent.

Salvia chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
