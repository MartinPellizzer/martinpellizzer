Sophia filipes

Sophia filipes, commonly known as the slender sophia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
