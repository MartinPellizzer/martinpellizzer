Solanum barbisetum

Solanum barbisetum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features hairy, spiny stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

Solanum barbisetum is characterized by its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
