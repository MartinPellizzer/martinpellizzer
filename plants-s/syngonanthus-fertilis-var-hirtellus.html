Syngonanthus fertilis var. hirtellus

Syngonanthus fertilis var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy fertilis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
