Spiraea decumbens var. canescens

Spiraea decumbens var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf spiraea, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-gray underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. Native to arid regions of North America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
