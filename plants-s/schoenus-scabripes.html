Schoenus scabripes

Schoenus scabripes, commonly known as the rough flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, three-lobed flowers.

Schoenus scabripes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
