Salix petiolaris f. lejocarpa

Salix petiolaris f. lejocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a unique horticultural form of the willow genus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are an important food source for pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
