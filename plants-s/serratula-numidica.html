Serratula numidica

Serratula numidica, commonly known as the Numidian sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like structure. The plant features serrated leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Serratula numidica is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
