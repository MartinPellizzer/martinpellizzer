Scorzonera austriaca var. hebecarpa

Scorzonera austriaca var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera austriaca var. hebecarpa thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.
