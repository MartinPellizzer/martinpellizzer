Stachys canescens

Stachys canescens, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

Stachys canescens is valued in herbal medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
