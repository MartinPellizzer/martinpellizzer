Schoenus pungens

Schoenus pungens, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Schoenus pungens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
