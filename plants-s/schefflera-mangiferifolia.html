Schefflera mangiferifolia

Schefflera mangiferifolia, commonly known as the mango-leaved schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are palmately arranged with 5 to 7 leaflets resembling the shape of a mango leaf. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Schefflera mangiferifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
