Scirpus macounii

Scirpus macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus macounii plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
