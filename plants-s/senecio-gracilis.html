Senecio gracilis

Senecio gracilis, commonly known as slender groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
