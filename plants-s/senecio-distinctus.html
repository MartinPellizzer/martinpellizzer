Senecio distinctus

Senecio distinctus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio distinctus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
