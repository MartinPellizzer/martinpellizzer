Scopolia boveana

Scopolia boveana, commonly known as the Bovean scopolia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Scopolia boveana is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display.
