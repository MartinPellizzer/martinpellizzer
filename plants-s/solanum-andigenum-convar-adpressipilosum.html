Solanum andigenum convar. adpressipilosum

Solanum andigenum convar. adpressipilosum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glandular, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

This species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
