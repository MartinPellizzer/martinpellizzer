Streptanthus asper

Streptanthus asper, commonly known as the roughpoded whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that remains closed until mature, at which point it opens to release seeds.

Streptanthus asper is native to North America and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.
