Schefflera arboricola

Schefflera arboricola, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown of palmate leaves that radiate from a central stem. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries, though these are not typically consumed by humans.

The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
