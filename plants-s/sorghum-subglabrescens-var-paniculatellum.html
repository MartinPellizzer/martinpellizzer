Sorghum subglabrescens var. paniculatellum

Sorghum subglabrescens var. paniculatellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with sturdy stems that can reach several feet in height.

This variety is of interest for its potential in forage and erosion control in marginal lands.
