Silene vivianii subsp. viscida

Silene vivianii subsp.

viscida, commonly known as the sticky catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
