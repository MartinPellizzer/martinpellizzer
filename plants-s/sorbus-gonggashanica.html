Sorbus gonggashanica

Sorbus gonggashanica, commonly known as the Gonggashan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
