Stachys germanica subsp. dasyanthes

Stachys germanica subsp.

dasyanthes, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
