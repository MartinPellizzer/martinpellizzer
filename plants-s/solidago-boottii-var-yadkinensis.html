Solidago boottii var. yadkinensis

Solidago boottii var. yadkinensis, commonly known as the Yadkin goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and forest edges. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges.

The flower heads are golden-yellow, composed of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by bracts, blooming in late summer to early autumn.
