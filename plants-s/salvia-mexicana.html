Salvia mexicana

Salvia mexicana, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia mexicana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
