Salvia pauciserrata subsp. erythrocalicina

Salvia pauciserrata subsp.

erythrocalicina, commonly known as the red calyx sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces striking red or pink bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is a prized plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
