Shorea singkawang subsp. singkawang

Shorea singkawang subsp.

singkawang, commonly known as the Singkawang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Borneo. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture.

The plant typically grows in lowland to hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse forest ecosystems.
