Sisyrinchium adenostemon

Sisyrinchium adenostemon, commonly known as the woolly bluecup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called caryopses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
