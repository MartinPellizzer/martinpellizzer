Sinarundinaria madagascariensis

Sinarundinaria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth surface and a fibrous structure. It is native to Madagascar and is adapted to the island's unique climate and soil conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role and potential use in sustainable construction.
