Senecillis purpurata

Senecillis purpurata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, tubular purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, cone-like flower head. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Senecillis purpurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
