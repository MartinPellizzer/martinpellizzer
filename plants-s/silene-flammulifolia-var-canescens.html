Silene flammulifolia var. canescens

Silene flammulifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, which are arranged in dense, showy clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in its dry habitat. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar adaptations to arid environments.

Its structure and appearance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and low-water gardening practices.
