Schinus dependens var. obovata

Schinus dependens var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant features compound leaves with obovate leaflets that are glossy and dark green on top. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
