Schomburgkia tibicinis var. grandiflora

Schomburgkia tibicinis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
