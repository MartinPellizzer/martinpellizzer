Spiranthes × neglecta

Spiranthes × neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wet meadows and woodlands. It produces small, white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes × neglecta is a perennial herb that is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
