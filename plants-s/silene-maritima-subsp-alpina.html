Silene maritima subsp. alpina

Silene maritima subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it is adapted to cold and windy conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
