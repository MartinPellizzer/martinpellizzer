Schinus polygama

Schinus polygama, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. Schinus polygama is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and showy flowers.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
