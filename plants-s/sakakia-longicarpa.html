Sakakia longicarpa

Sakakia longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited sakakia, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in spring.

The fruit is a follicle that elongates and becomes woody, containing several seeds.
