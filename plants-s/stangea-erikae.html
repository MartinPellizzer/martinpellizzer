Stangea erikae

Stangea erikae, commonly known as the Ericae stangea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in a dense, rounded shape.

Stangea erikae is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
