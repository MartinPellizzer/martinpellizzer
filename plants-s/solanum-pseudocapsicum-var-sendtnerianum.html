Solanum pseudocapsicum var. sendtnerianum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. sendtnerianum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or Bhut Jolokia, is a variant of the Capsicum genus within the nightshade family.

It is native to the Eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five lobes, and it produces small, round berries that mature to a bright red color.

This variety is notable for its extremely high capsaicin content, making it one of the hottest chili peppers in the world.
