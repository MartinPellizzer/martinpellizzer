Solanum arrebenta

Solanum arrebenta, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can be hairy, while its fruits are small, berry-like, and turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum arrebenta is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance.
