Stellaria falcata

Stellaria falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf starwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds enclosed in a capsule. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Stellaria falcata is primarily a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
