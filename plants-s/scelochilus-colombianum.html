Scelochilus colombianum

Scelochilus colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are pollinated primarily by small flies and bees.

Scelochilus colombianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and research purposes.
