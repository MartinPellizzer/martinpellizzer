Scleria terrestris var. thomsoniana

Scleria terrestris var. thomsoniana, commonly known as the Thomson's bulbous grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and delicate floral display.
