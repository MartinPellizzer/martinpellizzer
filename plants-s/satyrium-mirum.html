Satyrium mirum

Satyrium mirum, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the late summer or early autumn. The plant's flowers are characterized by their intricate, often colorful patterns and are adapted for specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees and wasps. Satyrium mirum prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and pollination strategy make it an important subject in the study of plant-animal interactions.
