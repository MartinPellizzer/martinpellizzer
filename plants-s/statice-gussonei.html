Statice gussonei

Statice gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces spikes of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to tolerate saline and well-drained soils.

Statice gussonei is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh maritime conditions.
