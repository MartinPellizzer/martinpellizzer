Solidago mexicana

Solidago mexicana, commonly known as Mexican goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Mexican goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
