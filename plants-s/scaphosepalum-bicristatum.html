Scaphosepalum bicristatum

Scaphosepalum bicristatum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex structure with a showy, spurred labellum.
