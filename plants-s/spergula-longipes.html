Spergula longipes

Spergula longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Spergula longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
