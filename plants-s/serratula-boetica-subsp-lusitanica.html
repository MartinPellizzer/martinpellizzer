Serratula boetica subsp. lusitanica

Serratula boetica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian serrata, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs.
