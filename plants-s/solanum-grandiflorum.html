Solanum grandiflorum

Solanum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a purple or violet throat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Solanum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
