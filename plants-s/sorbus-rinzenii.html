Sorbus rinzenii

Sorbus rinzenii, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by white to pink berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
