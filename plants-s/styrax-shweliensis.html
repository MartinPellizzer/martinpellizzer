Styrax shweliensis

Styrax shweliensis, commonly known as the Shweli styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap, which has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Styrax shweliensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
