Solanum asterocormum

Solanum asterocormum, commonly known as the star-shaped nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to purple berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
