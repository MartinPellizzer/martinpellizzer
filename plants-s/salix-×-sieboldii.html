Salix × sieboldii

Salix × sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
