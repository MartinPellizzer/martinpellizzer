Serratula pumila

Serratula pumila, commonly known as the rough serratula, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a fibrous root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
