Solanum odoriferum

Solanum odoriferum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible tuberous roots, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive leaves.

Solanum odoriferum is also valued for its culinary uses, particularly in tropical regions.
