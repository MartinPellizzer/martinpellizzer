Scorzonera semicana

Scorzonera semicana, commonly known as the lesser scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive violet or purple hue at the base, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scorzonera semicana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
