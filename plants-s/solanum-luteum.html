Solanum luteum

Solanum luteum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by berries that mature to a red or orange color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Solanum luteum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
