Solanum cuspistorta

Solanum cuspistorta, commonly known as the curly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
