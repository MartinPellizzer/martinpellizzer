Stachys riederi var. hispidula

Stachys riederi var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
