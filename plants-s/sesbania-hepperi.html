Sesbania hepperi

Sesbania hepperi, commonly known as the Hepper's sesbania, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Sesbania hepperi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
