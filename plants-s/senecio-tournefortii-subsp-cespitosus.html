Senecio tournefortii subsp. cespitosus

Senecio tournefortii subsp.

cespitosus, commonly known as the Tufted Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant features basal rosettes of blue-green, woolly leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single terminal head of small, daisy-like flowers.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh, low-nutrient conditions.
