Spathoglottis soutteriana

Spathoglottis soutteriana, commonly known as the Soutter's ginger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and conservation efforts are ongoing to protect its natural habitat.
