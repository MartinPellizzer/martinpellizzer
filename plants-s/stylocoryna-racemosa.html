Stylocoryna racemosa

Stylocoryna racemosa, commonly known as the clustered coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
