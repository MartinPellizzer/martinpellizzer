Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. somedana

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

somedana, commonly known as the Sardinian sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Sardinia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oblong seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
