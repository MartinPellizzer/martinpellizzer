Selaginella australiensis var. sciuroides

Selaginella australiensis var. sciuroides, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to colonize difficult terrain.
