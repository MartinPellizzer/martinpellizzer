Syzygium cornifolium

Syzygium cornifolium, commonly known as the horned flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Syzygium cornifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant foliage and floral display.
