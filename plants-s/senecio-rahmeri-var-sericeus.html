Senecio rahmeri var. sericeus

Senecio rahmeri var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
