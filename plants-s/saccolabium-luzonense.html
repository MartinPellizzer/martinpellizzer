Saccolabium luzonense

Saccolabium luzonense, commonly known as the Luzon legume, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and is adapted to the humid tropical climate of Luzon. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
