Solanum violaceum var. multiflorum

Solanum violaceum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the purple eggplant or violet nightshade, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its violet to purple flowers and edible, eggplant-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
