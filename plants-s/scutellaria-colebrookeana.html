Scutellaria colebrookeana

Scutellaria colebrookeana, commonly known as the blue scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly Nepal and Bhutan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
