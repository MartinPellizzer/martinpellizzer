Sarcoglottis catharinensis

Sarcoglottis catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette.

Sarcoglottis catharinensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
