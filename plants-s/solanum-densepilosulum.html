Solanum densepilosulum

Solanum densepilosulum, commonly known as the dense-hair nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and produces small, violet-colored flowers. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
