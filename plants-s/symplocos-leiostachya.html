Symplocos leiostachya

Symplocos leiostachya, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces showy, violet-black berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Symplocos leiostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
