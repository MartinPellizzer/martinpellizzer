Sonchus multiflorus

Sonchus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered sow thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

The species is often considered a weed but has some traditional medicinal uses.
