Smilax kwangsiensis

Smilax kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi smilax, is a perennial vine native to southern China.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and compound leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax kwangsiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
