Sempervivum surculosum

Sempervivum surculosum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, basal leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish or greenish hue. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the common name "everlasting.".
