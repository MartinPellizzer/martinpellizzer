Saccolabium giganteum var. petotianum

Saccolabium giganteum var. petotianum, commonly known as the Mexican giant legume, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flower clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread.
