Syzygium claviflorum var. riparium

Syzygium claviflorum var. riparium, commonly known as the river syzygium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows near water bodies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a deep red, making it an attractive ornamental plant.
