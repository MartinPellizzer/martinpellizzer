Solanum duchartrei

Solanum duchartrei, commonly known as the Madagascar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with hairy stems and elliptical leaves. Its berries are initially green and turn red when ripe, containing toxic compounds that deter herbivores.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
