Schoenocaulon drummondii

Schoenocaulon drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's mountain-mint, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

Schoenocaulon drummondii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
