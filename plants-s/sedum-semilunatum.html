Sedum semilunatum

Sedum semilunatum, commonly known as the moonstone sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
