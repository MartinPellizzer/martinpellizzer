Sasa ruscifolia

Sasa ruscifolia, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, jointed culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Sasa ruscifolia thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
