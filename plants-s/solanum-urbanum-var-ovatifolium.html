Solanum urbanum var. ovatifolium

Solanum urbanum var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the summer. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep blue or black color, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
