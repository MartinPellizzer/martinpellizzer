Senegalia lasiophylla

Senegalia lasiophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and feathery, silvery-green foliage that reflects sunlight, reducing water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are followed by long, thin pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Senegalia lasiophylla is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.
