Salvia longipes

Salvia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

Salvia longipes is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
