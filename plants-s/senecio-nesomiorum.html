Senecio nesomiorum

Senecio nesomiorum, commonly known as the Neomier Mountain Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
