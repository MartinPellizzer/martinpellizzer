Solanum lagoense

Solanum lagoense, commonly known as the Lake Victoria nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshades. The plant features hairy stems and ovate, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
