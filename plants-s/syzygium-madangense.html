Syzygium madangense

Syzygium madangense, commonly known as the Madang apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its fruits are not only valued for their taste but also for their potential medicinal properties.
