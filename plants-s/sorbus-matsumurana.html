Sorbus matsumurana

Sorbus matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries that persist into winter. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Sorbus matsumurana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
