Saxifraga hirculus f. intermedia

Saxifraga hirculus f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockfoam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers with five petals. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic, with a rough, textured surface.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
