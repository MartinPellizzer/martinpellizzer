Swertia bimaculata

Swertia bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves with a distinctive two-spotted pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Swertia bimaculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
