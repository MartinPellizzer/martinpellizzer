Setaria glauca var. aurea

Setaria glauca var. aurea, commonly known as golden foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces distinctive spikelets that are often golden or reddish in hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
