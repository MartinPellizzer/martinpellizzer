Strophopappus sellowianus

Strophopappus sellowianus, commonly known as the Sellow's sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a dense central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the sunflower genus. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Strophopappus sellowianus is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
