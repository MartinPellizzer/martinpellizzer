Sophora longipes

Sophora longipes, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping racemes of fragrant, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, rectangular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sophora longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
