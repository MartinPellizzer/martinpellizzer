Sceptridium nipponicum

Sceptridium nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese fern, is a delicate and rare species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, finely divided fronds that typically grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the underside of its leaves rather than flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
