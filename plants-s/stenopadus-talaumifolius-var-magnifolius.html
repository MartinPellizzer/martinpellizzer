Stenopadus talaumifolius var. magnifolius

Stenopadus talaumifolius var. magnifolius, commonly known as the magnifolius stenopadus, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, often with a deep green color and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its dramatic foliage and delicate floral display.
