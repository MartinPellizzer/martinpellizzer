Saccolabium curvifolium var. luteum

Saccolabium curvifolium var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow leafed saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its pods are slender and curved.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant yellow blooms.
