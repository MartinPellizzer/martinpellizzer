Scrophularia xanthoglossa var. hispidula

Scrophularia xanthoglossa var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy yellow lips, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance.

The flowers are yellow with two lips, the lower lip being larger and more prominent, and they bloom in early to mid-summer.
