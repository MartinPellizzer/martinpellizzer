Sorbus aucuparia var. alpestris

Sorbus aucuparia var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine whitebeam, is a variant of the rowan tree native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its white to pale pink flowers and clusters of red berries. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in autumn. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
