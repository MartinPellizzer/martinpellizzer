Solanum laciniatum f. viridicaule

Solanum laciniatum f. viridicaule, commonly known as the green-stemmed nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a yellow or orange color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of South America.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
