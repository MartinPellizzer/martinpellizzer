Senecillis fauriei

Senecillis fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing bright yellow to orange composite blooms that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual impact.
