Suaeda torreyana var. ramosissima

Suaeda torreyana var. ramosissima, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The plant's adaptations to extreme conditions make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
