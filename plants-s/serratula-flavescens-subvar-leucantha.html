Serratula flavescens subvar. leucantha

Serratula flavescens subvar. leucantha, commonly known as white serrated wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
