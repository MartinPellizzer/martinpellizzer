Sedum pososepalum

Sedum pososepalum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and well-draining conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and can store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments.

Sedum pososepalum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
