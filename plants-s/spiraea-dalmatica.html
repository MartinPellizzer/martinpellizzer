Spiraea dalmatica

Spiraea dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lance-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea dalmatica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
