Semecarpus cassuvium

Semecarpus cassuvium, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or simply chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are encased in a spiny husk, containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its shade-providing canopy.
