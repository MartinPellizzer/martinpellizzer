Schinus terebinthifolia var. glazioviana

Schinus terebinthifolia var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal uses.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to drought.
