Solanum repens

Solanum repens, commonly known as creeping nightshade, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
