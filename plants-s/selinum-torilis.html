Selinum torilis

Selinum torilis, commonly known as smooth chervil, is a small annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It grows to a height of about 20-40 cm, with slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its seeds are tiny, oval, and have a distinctive ridged surface.

Selinum torilis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
