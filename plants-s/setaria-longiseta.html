Setaria longiseta

Setaria longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired foxtail, is a grass species native to various parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristly, hair-like bracts. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread rapidly.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
