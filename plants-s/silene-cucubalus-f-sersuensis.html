Silene cucubalus f. sersuensis

Silene cucubalus f. sersuensis, commonly known as the Himalayan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Silene cucubalus f. sersuensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous habitats across Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.
