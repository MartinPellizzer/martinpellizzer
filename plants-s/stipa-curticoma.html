Stipa curticoma

Stipa curticoma, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted clumps of foliage. The leaves are narrow, rigid, and often have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet. This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and erosion control.
