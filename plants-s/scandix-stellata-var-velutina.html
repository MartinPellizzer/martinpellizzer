Scandix stellata var. velutina

Scandix stellata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, glandular underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Apiaceae family.
