Senecio triplipartitus

Senecio triplipartitus, commonly known as the three-parted groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in heads, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
