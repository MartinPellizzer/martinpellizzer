Stipa tianschanica var. pubescens

Stipa tianschanica var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
