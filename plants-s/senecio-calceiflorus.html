Senecio calceiflorus

Senecio calceiflorus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, blue-green foliage and yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across open landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
