Salix crataegifolia

Salix crataegifolia, commonly known as the thimblewillow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female, facilitating wind pollination. Its habitat includes wetlands, stream banks, and moist woodlands, where it thrives in poorly drained soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
