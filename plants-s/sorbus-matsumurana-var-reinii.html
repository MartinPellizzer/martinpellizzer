Sorbus matsumurana var. reinii

Sorbus matsumurana var. reinii, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a variant of the matsumurana whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
