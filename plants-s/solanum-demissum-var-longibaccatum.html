Solanum demissum var. longibaccatum

Solanum demissum var. longibaccatum, commonly known as the long-seeded nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits containing toxic compounds. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
