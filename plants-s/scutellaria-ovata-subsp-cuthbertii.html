Scutellaria ovata subsp. cuthbertii

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

cuthbertii, commonly known as the Cuthbert's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
