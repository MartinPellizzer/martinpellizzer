Solanum rupincola

Solanum rupincola, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
