Solanum tuberosum var. norfolcicum

Solanum tuberosum var. norfolcicum, commonly known as the Norfolk Island potato, is a variant of the common potato native to Norfolk Island in the South Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its starchy tubers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in a subtropical climate and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

This variety is notable for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
