Sobralia ruckeri

Sobralia ruckeri, commonly known as the Rucker's sobralia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces pendulous, raceme-like clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
