Spermolepis gigantea

Spermolepis gigantea, commonly known as the giant spermolepis, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical heads, making it a standout in its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
