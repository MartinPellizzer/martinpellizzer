Sinocalamus beecheyanus var. pubescens

Sinocalamus beecheyanus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sinocalamus beecheyanus species native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its dense, pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slightly rough texture. It grows as a clump-forming bamboo, often reaching heights of several meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
