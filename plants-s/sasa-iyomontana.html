Sasa iyomontana

Sasa iyomontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa iyomontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket weaving.
