Senecio chionopappus

Senecio chionopappus, commonly known as the snow pappus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit, characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble snowflakes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
