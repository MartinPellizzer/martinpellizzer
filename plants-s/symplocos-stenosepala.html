Symplocos stenosepala

Symplocos stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled evergreen.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos stenosepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
