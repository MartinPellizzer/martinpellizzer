Sophora gypsophila var. guadalupensis

Sophora gypsophila var. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems.
