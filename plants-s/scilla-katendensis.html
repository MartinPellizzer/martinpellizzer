Scilla katendensis

Scilla katendensis, commonly known as the Katend's scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and slightly hairy, emerging from a bulbous base.

Scilla katendensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
