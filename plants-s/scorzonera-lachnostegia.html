Scorzonera lachnostegia

Scorzonera lachnostegia, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera lachnostegia thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
