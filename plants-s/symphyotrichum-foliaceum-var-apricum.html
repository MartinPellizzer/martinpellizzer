Symphyotrichum foliaceum var. apricum

Symphyotrichum foliaceum var. apricum, commonly known as the spring blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
