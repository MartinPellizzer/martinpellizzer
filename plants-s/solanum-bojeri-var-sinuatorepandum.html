Solanum bojeri var. sinuatorepandum

Solanum bojeri var. sinuatorepandum, commonly known as the sinuatorepandum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
