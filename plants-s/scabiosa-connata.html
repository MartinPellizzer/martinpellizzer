Scabiosa connata

Scabiosa connata, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Scabiosa connata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
