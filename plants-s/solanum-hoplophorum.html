Solanum hoplophorum

Solanum hoplophorum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

Solanum hoplophorum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
