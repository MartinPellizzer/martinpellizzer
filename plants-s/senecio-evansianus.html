Senecio evansianus

Senecio evansianus, commonly known as the Evans' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
