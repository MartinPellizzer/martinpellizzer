Stipa mongholica var. barbellata

Stipa mongholica var. barbellata, commonly known as the Mongolian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, panicle-like structure with long, narrow spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
