Symplocos bakeri

Symplocos bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's waxleaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the western Ghats of India, characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a waxy coating that gives them a shiny appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
