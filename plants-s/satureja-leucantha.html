Satureja leucantha

Satureja leucantha, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Satureja leucantha is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in Native American medicine and rituals.
