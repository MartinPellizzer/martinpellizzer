Stachys aspera

Stachys aspera, commonly known as rough hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
