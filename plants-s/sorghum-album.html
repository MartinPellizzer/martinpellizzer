Sorghum album

Sorghum album, commonly known as white sorghum, is a tall annual grass native to Africa.

It produces large, fluffy panicles that contain numerous small, white seeds. The plant is characterized by its robust stems and broad, blade-like leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
