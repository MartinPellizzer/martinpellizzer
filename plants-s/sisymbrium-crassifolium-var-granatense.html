Sisymbrium crassifolium var. granatense

Sisymbrium crassifolium var. granatense, commonly known as the Granatense Mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
