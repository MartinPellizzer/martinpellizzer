Sophronitis kautskyi

Sophronitis kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky's orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns and a unique structure that facilitates specialized pollination. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, velvety texture, which aids in moisture retention.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Sophronitis kautskyi is considered a conservation concern.
