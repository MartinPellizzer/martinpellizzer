Saxifraga groenlandica var. quinquefida

Saxifraga groenlandica var. quinquefida, commonly known as the Greenland saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
