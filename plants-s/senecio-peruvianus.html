Senecio peruvianus

Senecio peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Senecio peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
