Syringa villosa var. pubescens

Syringa villosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its ornamental value lies in its showy flowers and unique foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
