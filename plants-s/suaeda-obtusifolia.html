Suaeda obtusifolia

Suaeda obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluish saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its salt tolerance allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems.

Suaeda obtusifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in saline habitats.
