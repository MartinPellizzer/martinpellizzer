Stylidium roseonanum

Stylidium roseonanum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are often pink or reddish in color. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Stylidium roseonanum is often found in heathlands and is a popular subject for botanists and horticulturists due to its striking appearance and ecological significance.
