Silene maximowicziana

Silene maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene maximowicziana is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for respiratory and skin conditions.
