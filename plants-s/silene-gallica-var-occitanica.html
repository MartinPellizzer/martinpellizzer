Silene gallica var. occitanica

Silene gallica var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly France and Italy.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
