Salacia granulata

Salacia granulata, commonly known as the rough salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Salacia granulata is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
