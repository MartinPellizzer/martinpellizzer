Statice papillata

Statice papillata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Statice papillata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in dried floral arrangements.
