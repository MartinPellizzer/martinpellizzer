Spiraea arborea

Spiraea arborea, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea arborea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
