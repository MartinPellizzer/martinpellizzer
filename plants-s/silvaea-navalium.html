Silvaea navalium

Silvaea navalium, commonly known as water hawthorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Silvaea navalium plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
