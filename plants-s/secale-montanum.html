Secale montanum

Secale montanum, commonly known as mountain ryegrass, is a flowering plant species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Secale montanum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and rocky areas.
