Scleranthus praecox

Scleranthus praecox, commonly known as early sow-thistle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Scleranthus praecox is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
