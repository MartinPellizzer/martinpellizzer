Spiraea × reflexa

Spiraea × reflexa, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching stems covered in small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
