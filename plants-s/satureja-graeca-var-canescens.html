Satureja graeca var. canescens

Satureja graeca var. canescens, commonly known as Greek savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, slightly peppery flavor.

Satureja graeca var. canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
