Senecio clivorum

Senecio clivorum, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its hardy nature.

Senecio clivorum is often used in alpine gardens due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
