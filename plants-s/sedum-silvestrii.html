Sedum silvestrii

Sedum silvestrii, commonly known as the forest stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, star-shaped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Sedum silvestrii is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
