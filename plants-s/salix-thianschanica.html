Salix thianschanica

Salix thianschanica, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Salix thianschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
