Satureja angustifolia

Satureja angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme.

Satureja angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pungent, sage-like aroma.
