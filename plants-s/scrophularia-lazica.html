Scrophularia lazica

Scrophularia lazica, commonly known as Caucasian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Scrophularia lazica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
