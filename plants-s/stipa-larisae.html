Stipa larisae

Stipa larisae, commonly known as the narrowleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or brown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its common name.

Stipa larisae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying alpine flora.
