Syagrus campicola

Syagrus campicola, commonly known as the coastal palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Syagrus campicola is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
