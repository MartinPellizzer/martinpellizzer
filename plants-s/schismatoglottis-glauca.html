Schismatoglottis glauca

Schismatoglottis glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaved schismatoglottis, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, it is characterized by its glossy, dark green to bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Schismatoglottis glauca is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled greenhouse conditions.
