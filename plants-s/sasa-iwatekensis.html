Sasa iwatekensis

Sasa iwatekensis, commonly known as the Iwate bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense foliage. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

Sasa iwatekensis exhibits rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and form thick mats over time.
