Silene transsilvanica

Silene transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene transsilvanica is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
