Solanum gilo var. ellipsoideum

Solanum gilo var. ellipsoideum, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its oval-shaped, spiny fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers and has a bushy growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses.

Its berries are sometimes used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
