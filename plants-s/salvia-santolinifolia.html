Salvia santolinifolia

Salvia santolinifolia, commonly known as the santolinifolia sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-green, fuzzy foliage. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
