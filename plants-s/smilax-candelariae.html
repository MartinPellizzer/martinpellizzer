Smilax candelariae

Smilax candelariae, commonly known as Candelaria smilax, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with a distinctive appearance, featuring spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Smilax candelariae thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
