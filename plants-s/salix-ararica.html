Salix ararica

Salix ararica, commonly known as the Araric willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America. This shrub or small tree typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along rivers, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces catkin-like flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
