Selaginella cognata

Selaginella cognata, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a rosette-like structure with spirally arranged leaves that are often needle-like in appearance. Selaginella cognata reproduces via spores, which are produced in cone-like structures called strobila.

It is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
