Solanum pseudocapsicum pilosum

Solanum pseudocapsicum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, green berries. These berries turn red when mature and contain toxic seeds, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in disturbed soils.
