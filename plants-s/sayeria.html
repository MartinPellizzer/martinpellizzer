Sayeria

Sayeria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Sayeria species typically thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-draining soil. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Sayeria caerulea, which is known for its striking blue flowers.
