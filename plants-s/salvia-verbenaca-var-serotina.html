Salvia verbenaca var. serotina

Salvia verbenaca var. serotina, commonly known as lateflower blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable plant for both gardens and natural habitats.
