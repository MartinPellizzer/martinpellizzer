Sinarundinaria hirsuta

Sinarundinaria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough, textured surface.

Sinarundinaria hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
