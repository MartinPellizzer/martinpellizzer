Syzygium variolosum

Syzygium variolosum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium variolosum is often found in rainforests and coastal areas, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
