Syzygium rizalense

Syzygium rizalense, commonly known as the Rizal's syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in local traditional medicine.
