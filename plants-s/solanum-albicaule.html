Solanum albicaule

Solanum albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white-tinged stems and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
