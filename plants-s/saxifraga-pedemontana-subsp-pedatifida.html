Saxifraga pedemontana subsp. pedatifida

Saxifraga pedemontana subsp.

pedatifida, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually appealing addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
