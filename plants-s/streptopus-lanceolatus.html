Streptopus lanceolatus

Streptopus lanceolatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead outline, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Streptopus lanceolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
