Sepalosaccus strumatus

Sepalosaccus strumatus, commonly known as the swordleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, upright cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
