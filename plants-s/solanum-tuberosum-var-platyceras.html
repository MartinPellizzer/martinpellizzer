Solanum tuberosum var. platyceras

Solanum tuberosum var. platyceras, commonly known as the flat-seeded potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its distinctively flat and broad seeds.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that produces violet flowers and is native to the Andes region of South America. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flat-seeded potato is valued for its unique seed morphology, which can be important for breeding programs and genetic diversity.
