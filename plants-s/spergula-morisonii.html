Spergula morisonii

Spergula morisonii, commonly known as the Morison's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Spergula morisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
