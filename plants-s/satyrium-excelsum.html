Satyrium excelsum

Satyrium excelsum, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as cloud forests or mountainous areas. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, adapted to capture sunlight in its natural habitat.

Satyrium excelsum is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes specialized petals and sepals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
