Scirpus cyperinus var. brachypodus

Scirpus cyperinus var. brachypodus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in marshy or waterlogged soils and is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
