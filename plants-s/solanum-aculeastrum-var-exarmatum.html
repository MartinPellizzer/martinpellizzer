Solanum aculeastrum var. exarmatum

Solanum aculeastrum var. exarmatum, commonly known as the smooth nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, toxic berries that are edible when ripe.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
