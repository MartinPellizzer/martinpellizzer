It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella usta is also notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of the lycophyte group.
