Sorbus torminalis f. obtecta

Sorbus torminalis f. obtecta, commonly known as the glossy whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains largely unblemished by pests or diseases. The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a smooth, waxy surface that gives them a glossy appearance. This cultivar produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
