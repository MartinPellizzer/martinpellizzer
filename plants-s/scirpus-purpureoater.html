Scirpus purpureoater

Scirpus purpureoater, commonly known as the purple-tipped bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale purple to reddish flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Scirpus purpureoater is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
