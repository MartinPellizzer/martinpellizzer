Secale montanum subsp. rhodopaeum

Secale montanum subsp.

rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Rhodope rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and culms that are often hairy.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that are often pubescent. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native region.
