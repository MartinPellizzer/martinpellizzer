Spiranthes tenuiflora

Spiranthes tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of flowers that resemble a twisted ribbon. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
