Saxifraga calyciflora

Saxifraga calyciflora, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in meadows and rocky areas. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for its alpine habitat, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
