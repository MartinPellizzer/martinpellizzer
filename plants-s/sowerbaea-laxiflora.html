Sowerbaea laxiflora

Sowerbaea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered cycad, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in cone-like structures, typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
