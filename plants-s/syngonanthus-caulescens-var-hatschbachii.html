Syngonanthus caulescens var. hatschbachii

Syngonanthus caulescens var. hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbachii sedge, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to waterlogged soils.
