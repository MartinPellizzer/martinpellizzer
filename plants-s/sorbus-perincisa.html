Sorbus perincisa

Sorbus perincisa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are clustered in spherical heads. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Sorbus perincisa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
