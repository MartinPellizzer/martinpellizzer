Senecio vagus var. vagus

Senecio vagus var. vagus, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring narrow, linear leaves and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
