Sebaea pusilla

Sebaea pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sebaea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Sebaea pusilla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
