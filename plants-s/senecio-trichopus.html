Senecio trichopus

Senecio trichopus, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It has a hairy stem and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio trichopus is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
