Sabina turkestanica

Sabina turkestanica, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk, is a species of juniper native to Central Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has scale-like leaves and produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The botanical features of Sabina turkestanica make it well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
