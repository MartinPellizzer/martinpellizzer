Schoenoxiphium strictum

Schoenoxiphium strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
