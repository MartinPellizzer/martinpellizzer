Sphaeranthus tetraphyllus

Sphaeranthus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a spherical shape.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
