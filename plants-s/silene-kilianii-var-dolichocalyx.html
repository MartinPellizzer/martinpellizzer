Silene kilianii var. dolichocalyx

Silene kilianii var. dolichocalyx, commonly known as the long-stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to high-altitude environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
