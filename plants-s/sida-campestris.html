Sida campestris

Sida campestris, commonly known as field sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, dry fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds.

Sida campestris is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
