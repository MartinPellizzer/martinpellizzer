Salvia privoides var. garberi

Salvia privoides var. garberi, commonly known as Garber's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue, which are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Garber's sage is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
