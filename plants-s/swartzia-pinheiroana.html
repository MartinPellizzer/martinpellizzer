Swartzia pinheiroana

Swartzia pinheiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic leaves. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
