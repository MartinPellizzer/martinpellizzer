Scaevola koenigii var. glabra

Scaevola koenigii var. glabra, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
