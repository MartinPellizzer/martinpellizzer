Setaria bifida

Setaria bifida, commonly known as split beardgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Setaria bifida is a hardy perennial that can thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
