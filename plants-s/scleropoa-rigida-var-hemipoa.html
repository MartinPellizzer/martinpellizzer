Scleropoa rigida var. hemipoa

Scleropoa rigida var. hemipoa, commonly known as the rigid grass or stiff grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
