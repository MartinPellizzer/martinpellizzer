Setaria sphacelata subsp. pyropea

Setaria sphacelata subsp.

pyropea, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
