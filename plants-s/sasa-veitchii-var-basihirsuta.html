Sasa veitchii var. basihirsuta

Sasa veitchii var. basihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa veitchii species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming, evergreen bamboo characterized by its stiff, erect culms that are covered in a dense layer of short, hairy pubescence. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth, waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique texture and foliage.
