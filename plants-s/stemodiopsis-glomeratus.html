Stemodiopsis glomeratus

Stemodiopsis glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered stemodia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has a short, upright stem with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
