Saldanhaea myriantha var. pubescens

Saldanhaea myriantha var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mountain heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
