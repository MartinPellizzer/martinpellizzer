Stalagmitis macrophylla

Stalagmitis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its sturdy, branching stems. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand periods of drought.

Stalagmitis macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in warm climates.
