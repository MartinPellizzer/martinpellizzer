Senftenbergia moritziana

Senftenbergia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's orchid, is a rare and exquisite species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a compact growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Senftenbergia moritziana is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
