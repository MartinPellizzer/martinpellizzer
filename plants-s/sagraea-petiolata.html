Sagraea petiolata

Sagraea petiolata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sagraea petiolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
