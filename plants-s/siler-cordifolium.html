Siler cordifolium

Siler cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf siler, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its stems are often square in cross-section.

Siler cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
