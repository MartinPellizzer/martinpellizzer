Syzygium inophyllum var. bernardii

Syzygium inophyllum var. bernardii, commonly known as the Kauri apple or *Pseudophasca inophylla*, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the wet tropical forests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fragrant, white flowers are followed by small, fleshy, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible but have a strong, musky aroma.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
