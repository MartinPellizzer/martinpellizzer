Solms-laubachia gamosepala

Solms-laubachia gamosepala, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals fused at the base. The plant has a fibrous root system and slender, branching stems that grow from the base. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Solms-laubachia gamosepala thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
