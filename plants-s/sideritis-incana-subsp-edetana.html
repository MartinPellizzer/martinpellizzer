Sideritis incana subsp. edetana

Sideritis incana subsp.

edetana, commonly known as the alpine beard vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
