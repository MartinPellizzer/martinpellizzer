Stipa pennata appendiculata

Stipa pennata appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of fine, silver-green foliage that are covered with short, silky hairs. The plant produces slender, branched spikelets that are often adorned with small, awned bracts, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Stipa pennata appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
