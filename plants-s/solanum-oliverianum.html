Solanum oliverianum

Solanum oliverianum, commonly known as Oliver's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, green berries. These berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to both animals and humans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
