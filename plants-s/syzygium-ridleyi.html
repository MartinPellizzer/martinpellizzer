Syzygium ridleyi

Syzygium ridleyi, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. Syzygium ridleyi is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
