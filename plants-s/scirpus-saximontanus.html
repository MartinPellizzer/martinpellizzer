Scirpus saximontanus

Scirpus saximontanus, commonly known as the saximon tule, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural strength.

Scirpus saximontanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
