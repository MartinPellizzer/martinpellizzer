Sarcoyucca valida

Sarcoyucca valida, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 20 feet, adorned with long, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal panicle. Sarcoyucca valida is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
