Salvia applanata

Salvia applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are violet to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

Salvia applanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
