Sempervivum paivae

Sempervivum paivae, commonly known as the Canary Island houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or violet during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Sempervivum paivae is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
