Simsia grandiflora

Simsia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Simsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange ray flowers surrounded by a ring of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Simsia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
