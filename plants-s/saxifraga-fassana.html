Saxifraga fassana

Saxifraga fassana, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. It is often found at high altitudes, where it withstands harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga fassana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
