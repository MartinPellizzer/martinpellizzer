Silene volubilitana

Silene volubilitana, commonly known as the climbing catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene volubilitana is known for its ability to climb using its twining stems, which helps it reach sunlight.
