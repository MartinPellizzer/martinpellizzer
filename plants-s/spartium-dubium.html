Spartium dubium

Spartium dubium, commonly known as the lesser sparrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. Spartium dubium is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
