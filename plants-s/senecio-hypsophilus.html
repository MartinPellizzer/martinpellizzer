Senecio hypsophilus

Senecio hypsophilus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, blue to purple-blue leaves and produces small, blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens.
