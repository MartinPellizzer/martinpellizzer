Sambucus canadensis f. atroflavula

Sambucus canadensis f. atroflavula, commonly known as the black-flowered elderberry, is a botanical variant of the common elderberry.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with the white or pale flowers of the typical form. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are compound, with 5 to 7 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The fruit is a dark blue to black berry, which is edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
