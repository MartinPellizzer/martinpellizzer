Saussurea alpina var. vulgaris

Saussurea alpina var. vulgaris, commonly known as the alpine saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
