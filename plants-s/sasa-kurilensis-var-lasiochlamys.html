Sasa kurilensis var. lasiochlamys

Sasa kurilensis var. lasiochlamys, commonly known as the hairy-leaf sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
