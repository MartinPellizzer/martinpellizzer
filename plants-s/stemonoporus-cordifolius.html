Stemonoporus cordifolius

Stemonoporus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved stemonoporus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
