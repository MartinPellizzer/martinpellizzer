Sedum borissovae

Sedum borissovae, commonly known as the Borissov's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum borissovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
