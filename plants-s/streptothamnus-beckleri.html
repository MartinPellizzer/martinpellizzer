Streptothamnus beckleri

Streptothamnus beckleri, commonly known as the Beckler's rockrose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss.

Streptothamnus beckleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
