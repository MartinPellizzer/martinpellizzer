Syzygium cymosum var. montanum

Syzygium cymosum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain clove, is a variant of the clove tree native to mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic, reddish-brown buds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and elevated terrain. Its botanical features include simple, elliptical leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its unique aroma and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
