Sida congensis

Sida congensis, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Sida congensis is known for its fibrous stems, which are used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.
