Scelochilus stenochilus

Scelochilus stenochilus, commonly known as the narrow-lipped scelochilus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in shaded, moist environments, and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive narrow lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Scelochilus stenochilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
