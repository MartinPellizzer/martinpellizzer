Setaria dioica

Setaria dioica, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelets that bear both male and female flowers, which are typically separated on different plants. The plant produces distinctive yellowish to golden spikelets that are often used as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas.

Setaria dioica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seed and rhizome propagation.
