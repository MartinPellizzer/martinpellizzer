Sauvetrea peruviana

Sauvetrea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sauvetrea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
