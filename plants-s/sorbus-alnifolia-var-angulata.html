Sorbus alnifolia var. angulata

Sorbus alnifolia var. angulata, commonly known as the angular rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, angular leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
