Senecio californicus var. laxior

Senecio californicus var. laxior, commonly known as the looseleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to California.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose, daisy-like heads.

This variety is characterized by its more loosely arranged foliage compared to other Senecio species.
