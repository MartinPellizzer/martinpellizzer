Scrophularia appendiculata

Scrophularia appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate figwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
