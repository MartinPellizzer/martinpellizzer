Struthiola aurea

Struthiola aurea, commonly known as the golden struthiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Struthiola aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
