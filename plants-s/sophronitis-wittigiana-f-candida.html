Sophronitis wittigiana f. candida

Sophronitis wittigiana f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast with its green leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
