Stachys brachyclada var. punctata

Stachys brachyclada var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or reddish markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and a valuable component of local ecosystems.
