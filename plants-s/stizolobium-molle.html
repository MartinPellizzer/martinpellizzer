Stizolobium molle

Stizolobium molle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with oval, glossy leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
