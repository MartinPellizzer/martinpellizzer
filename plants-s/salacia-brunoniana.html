Salacia brunoniana

Salacia brunoniana, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Salacia brunoniana is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
