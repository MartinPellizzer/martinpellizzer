Scirpus minutus

Scirpus minutus, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.
