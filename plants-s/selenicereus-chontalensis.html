Selenicereus chontalensis

Selenicereus chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontalensis moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in its natural arid environment.

Selenicereus chontalensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and floral display.
