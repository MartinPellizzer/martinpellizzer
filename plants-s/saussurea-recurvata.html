Saussurea recurvata

Saussurea recurvata, commonly known as the Himalayan black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark, aromatic seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique flavor and medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
