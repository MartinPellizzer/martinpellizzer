Siccobaccatus

Siccobaccatus, commonly known as the desert thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. The plant produces yellow or white flowers, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Siccobaccatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
