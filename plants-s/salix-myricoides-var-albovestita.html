Salix myricoides var. albovestita

Salix myricoides var. albovestita, commonly known as the white-haired willow, is a variant of the silky willow characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescent foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a soft, silvery appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its unique foliage.
