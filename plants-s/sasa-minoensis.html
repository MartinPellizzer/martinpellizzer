Sasa minoensis

Sasa minoensis, commonly known as the Mino bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa minoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
