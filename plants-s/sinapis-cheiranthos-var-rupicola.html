Sinapis cheiranthos var. rupicola

Sinapis cheiranthos var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are small and capsule-like, typical of the family, and are dispersed by wind.
