Sperlingia verticillata

Sperlingia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often emerging from the base in a rosette formation.

Sperlingia verticillata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
