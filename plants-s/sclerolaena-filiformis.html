Sclerolaena filiformis

Sclerolaena filiformis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its thin, thread-like stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with succulent tissues that store water. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Sclerolaena filiformis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
