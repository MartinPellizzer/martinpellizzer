Sesbania mossambicensis subsp. minimiflora

Sesbania mossambicensis subsp.

minimiflora, commonly known as the small-flower sesbania, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately compound with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. It has a short growth season and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
