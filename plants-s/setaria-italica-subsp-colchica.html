Setaria italica subsp. colchica

Setaria italica subsp.

colchica, commonly known as Italian millet, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the seeds are tiny and enclosed in protective husks.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines across southern Europe.
