Solanum lycocarpum f. angustifolium

Solanum lycocarpum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a variant of the Brazilian cherry tree.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
