Salvia adenostachya

Salvia adenostachya, commonly known as the desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Salvia adenostachya is also valued in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
