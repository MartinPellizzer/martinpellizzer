Senecio adamantinus var. integrifolius

Senecio adamantinus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a woolly texture, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
