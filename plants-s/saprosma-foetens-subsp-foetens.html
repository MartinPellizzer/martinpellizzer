Saprosma foetens subsp. foetens

Saprosma foetens subsp.

foetens, commonly known as the stinking elder, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, often pungent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but have a strong, disagreeable flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is notable for its distinctive odor, which is a key feature in its common name.
