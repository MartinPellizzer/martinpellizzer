Saracha peruviana

Saracha peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Saracha peruviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some Andean communities.
