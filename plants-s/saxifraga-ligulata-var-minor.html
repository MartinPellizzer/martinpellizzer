Saxifraga ligulata var. minor

Saxifraga ligulata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features a low-growing rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a dense cluster at the end of each leafy stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
