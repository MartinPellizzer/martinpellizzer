Stachys alopecuros

Stachys alopecuros, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species often found in disturbed or moist soils.
