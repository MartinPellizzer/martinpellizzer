Solidago acaulis

Solidago acaulis, commonly known as the tufted goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, leafless stem that bears dense clusters of yellow flowers at the top. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open woodlands or meadows. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago acaulis is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
