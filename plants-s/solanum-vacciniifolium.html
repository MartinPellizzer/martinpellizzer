Solanum vacciniifolium

Solanum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, red, currant-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, spreading foliage. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture, while the flowers are usually white or pale blue.

The fruit is edible, though it is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor.
