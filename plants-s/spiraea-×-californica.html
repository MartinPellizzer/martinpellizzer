Spiraea × californica

Spiraea × californica, commonly known as California weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features arching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a compact, rounded form. Its foliage is dark green and turns reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.
