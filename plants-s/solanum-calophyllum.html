Solanum calophyllum

Solanum calophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and the fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains toxic compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
