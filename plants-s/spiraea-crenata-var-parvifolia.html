Spiraea crenata var. parvifolia

Spiraea crenata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf meadow spirea, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
