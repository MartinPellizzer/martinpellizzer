Scleria variegata

Scleria variegata, commonly known as the variegated bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Scleria variegata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
