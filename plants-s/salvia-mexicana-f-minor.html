Salvia mexicana f. minor

Salvia mexicana f. minor, commonly known as Mexican sage or blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
