Saxifraga davidii

Saxifraga davidii, commonly known as David's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
