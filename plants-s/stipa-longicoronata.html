Stipa longicoronata

Stipa longicoronata, commonly known as the long-crowned grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it is adapted to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of florets.

Stipa longicoronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
