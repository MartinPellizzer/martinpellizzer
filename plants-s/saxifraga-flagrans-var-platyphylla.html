Saxifraga flagrans var. platyphylla

Saxifraga flagrans var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
