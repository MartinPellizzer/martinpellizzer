Salvia officinalis subsp. major

Salvia officinalis subsp.

major, commonly known as Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is aromatic, with a strong, peppery scent, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and herbal remedies.
