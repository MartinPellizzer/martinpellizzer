Sedum morrisonense

Sedum morrisonense, commonly known as Morrison's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
