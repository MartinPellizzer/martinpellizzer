Scorzonera cilicia

Scorzonera cilicia, commonly known as the Persian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera cilicia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.
