Struthanthus crassipes

Struthanthus crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments, often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
