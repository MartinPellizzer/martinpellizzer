Stipa capillacea var. parviflora

Stipa capillacea var. parviflora, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tufted, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, pale florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant landscaping plant.

The variety parviflora is distinguished by its smaller flower clusters compared to the typical form of S. capillacea.
