Sinningia stapelioides

Sinningia stapelioides, commonly known as the "Stapelioid" or "Paddle Plant," is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive, honey-scented aroma. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
