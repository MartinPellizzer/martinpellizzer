Senecillis jacquemontiana

Senecillis jacquemontiana, commonly known as the Jacquemont's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners interested in succulent plants.
