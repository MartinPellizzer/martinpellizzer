Sisymbrium terrestre

Sisymbrium terrestre, commonly known as the common winter cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated seed pods. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds.
