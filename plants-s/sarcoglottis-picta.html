Sarcoglottis picta

Sarcoglottis picta, commonly known as the painted sarcoglottis, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaf patterns. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a prominent midrib and can produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Its tuberous roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Sarcoglottis picta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.
