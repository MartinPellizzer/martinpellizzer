Sedum album var. purpureum

Sedum album var. purpureum, commonly known as purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

This variety is noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for xeriscaping.
