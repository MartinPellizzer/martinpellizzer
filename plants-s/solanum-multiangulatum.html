Solanum multiangulatum

Solanum multiangulatum, commonly known as the angular nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, though they are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Solanum multiangulatum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
