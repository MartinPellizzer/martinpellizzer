Senecio okopanus

Senecio okopanus, commonly known as the Okopan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio okopanus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
