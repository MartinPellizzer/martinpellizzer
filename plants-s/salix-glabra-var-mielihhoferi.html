Salix glabra var. mielihhoferi

Salix glabra var. mielihhoferi, commonly known as the Mielihhofer's weeping willow, is a rare variant of the weeping willow native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a pale green upper surface and a hairy underside. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
