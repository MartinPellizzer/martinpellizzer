Solanum coerulescens

Solanum coerulescens, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Solanum coerulescens is a member of the nightshade family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.
