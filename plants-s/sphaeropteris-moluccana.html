Sphaeropteris moluccana

Sphaeropteris moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan cycad, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones for reproduction. Its leaves are typically a deep green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Sphaeropteris moluccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine in some cultures.
