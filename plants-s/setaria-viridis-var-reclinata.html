Setaria viridis var. reclinata

Setaria viridis var. reclinata, commonly known as the reclining green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to Africa.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variant is often used as a forage crop and has ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
