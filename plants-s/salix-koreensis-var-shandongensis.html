Salix koreensis var. shandongensis

Salix koreensis var. shandongensis, commonly known as the Shandong weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
