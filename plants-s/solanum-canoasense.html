Solanum canoasense

Solanum canoasense, commonly known as the Canoas nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
