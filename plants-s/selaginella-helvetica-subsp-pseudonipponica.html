Selaginella helvetica subsp. pseudonipponica

Selaginella helvetica subsp.

pseudonipponica, commonly known as the European clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both spore and seed production, making it a heterosporous species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in humid conditions.

Its morphology includes a creeping stem with adventitious roots, and it is often found in the understory of temperate woodlands across Europe and parts of Asia.
