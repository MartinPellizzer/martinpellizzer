Sedum ebracteatum subsp. typicum

Sedum ebracteatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette, adapted for water storage and reducing transpiration.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically on stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
