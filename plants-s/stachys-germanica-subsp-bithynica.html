Stachys germanica subsp. bithynica

Stachys germanica subsp.

bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian betony, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, forming dense spikes of violet to purple blossoms.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
