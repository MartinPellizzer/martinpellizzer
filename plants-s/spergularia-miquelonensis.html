Spergularia miquelonensis

Spergularia miquelonensis, commonly known as the Falkland Islands sandspurr, is a flowering plant native to the subantarctic region, particularly the Falkland Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Spergularia miquelonensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is part of the local flora unique to its geographic range.
