Scirpus × igaensis

Scirpus × igaensis, commonly known as the hybrid reed, is a wetland plant that thrives in marshy and aquatic environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Scirpus holoschoenus and Scirpus calycatus, showcasing a combination of traits from both parents. The plant typically grows in dense stands, with tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Scirpus × igaensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
