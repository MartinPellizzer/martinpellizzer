Scilla grandifolia

Scilla grandifolia, commonly known as the giant squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbels and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Scilla grandifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
