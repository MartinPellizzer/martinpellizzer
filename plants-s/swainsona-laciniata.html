Swainsona laciniata

Swainsona laciniata, commonly known as the lacey swainsona, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves with finely divided, feathery lobes. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
