Senecio hookeri

Senecio hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
