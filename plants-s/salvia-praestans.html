Salvia praestans

Salvia praestans, commonly known as the skyblue salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
