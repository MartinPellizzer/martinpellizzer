Sanguisorba vulgaris f. magnolii

Sanguisorba vulgaris f. magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its striking, rose-like inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
