Siraitia borneensis

Siraitia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean monkfruit, is a vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the genus Siraitia, which includes other species of passionflower-like plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops orange to red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its fruits are not typically consumed by humans but are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Siraitia borneensis make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological research.
