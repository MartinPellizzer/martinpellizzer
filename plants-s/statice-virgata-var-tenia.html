Statice virgata var. tenia

Statice virgata var. tenia, commonly known as sea lavender or coastal lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to tolerate salty air and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats.

Statice virgata var. tenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
