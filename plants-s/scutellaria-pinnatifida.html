Scutellaria pinnatifida

Scutellaria pinnatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.
