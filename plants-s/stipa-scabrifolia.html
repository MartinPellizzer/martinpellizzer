Stipa scabrifolia

Stipa scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Stipa scabrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
