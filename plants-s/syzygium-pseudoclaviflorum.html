Syzygium pseudoclaviflorum

Syzygium pseudoclaviflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a dense canopy that provides shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
