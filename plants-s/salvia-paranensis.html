Salvia paranensis

Salvia paranensis, commonly known as the Parana sage, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Salvia paranensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential therapeutic properties.
