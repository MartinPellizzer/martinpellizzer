Sonchus spinosus

Sonchus spinosus, commonly known as spiny sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped, sparsely hairy, and have a bitter taste, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Sonchus spinosus is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
