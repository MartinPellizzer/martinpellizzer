Silene persica

Silene persica, commonly known as Persian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene persica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
