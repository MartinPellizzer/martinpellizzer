Stereoxylon

Stereoxylon, commonly known as the genus of fossilized wood, refers to a type of ancient plant material preserved through the process of petrification.

It is characterized by its cellular structure, which retains the original anatomical features of the wood from ancient plants. Stereoxylon is primarily found in sedimentary rock layers and provides valuable insights into the morphology and evolution of prehistoric flora. The study of stereoxylon helps scientists understand past climates and ecosystems.

It is an important resource for paleobotanists and geologists in reconstructing Earth's biological history.
