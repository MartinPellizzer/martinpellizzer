Syzygium tetragonum

Syzygium tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled clove or quadrangular clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, four-angled stem and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by reddish-brown, clove-like fruits. Syzygium tetragonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
