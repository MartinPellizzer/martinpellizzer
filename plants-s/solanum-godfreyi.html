Solanum godfreyi

Solanum godfreyi, commonly known as Godfrey's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Solanum godfreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
