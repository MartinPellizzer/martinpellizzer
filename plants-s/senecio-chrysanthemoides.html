Senecio chrysanthemoides

Senecio chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio chrysanthemoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
