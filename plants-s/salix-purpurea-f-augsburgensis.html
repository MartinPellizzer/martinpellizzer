Salix purpurea f. augsburgensis

Salix purpurea f. augsburgensis, commonly known as the Augsburg weeping willow, is a unique variant of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade and habitat for wildlife.
