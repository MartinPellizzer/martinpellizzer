Solanum warmingii

Solanum warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows as an herbaceous shrub.

Solanum warmingii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
