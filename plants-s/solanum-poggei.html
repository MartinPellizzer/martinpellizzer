Solanum poggei

Solanum poggei, commonly known as the Pogge's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum poggei is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
