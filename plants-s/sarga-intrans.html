Sarga intrans

Sarga intrans, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
