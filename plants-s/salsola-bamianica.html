Salsola bamianica

Salsola bamianica, commonly known as the Tibetan saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Salsola bamianica plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
