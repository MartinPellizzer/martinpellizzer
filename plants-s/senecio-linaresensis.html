Senecio linaresensis

Senecio linaresensis, commonly known as the Linares groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
