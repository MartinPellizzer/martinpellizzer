Stipa trichophylla

Stipa trichophylla, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Stipa trichophylla is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
