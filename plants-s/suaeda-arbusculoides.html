Suaeda arbusculoides

Suaeda arbusculoides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to extreme drought conditions through its succulent leaves and deep root system. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its salt tolerance makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Suaeda arbusculoides plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
