Salvia tehuacana

Salvia tehuacana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Salvia tehuacana is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
