Saccolabium sylvestre

Saccolabium sylvestre, commonly known as the wild senna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Saccolabium sylvestre is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
