Shorea glauca

Shorea glauca, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and smooth, pale grey bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Shorea glauca is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
