Syringodea discolor

Syringodea discolor, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the warmer months.

Syringodea discolor is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
