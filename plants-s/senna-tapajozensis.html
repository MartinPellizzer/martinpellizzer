Senna tapajozensis

Senna tapajozensis, commonly known as the Amazon senna, is a shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, and it produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-filled segments.

Senna tapajozensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
