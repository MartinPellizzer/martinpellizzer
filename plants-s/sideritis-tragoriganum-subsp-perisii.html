Sideritis tragoriganum subsp. perisii

Sideritis tragoriganum subsp.

perisii, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
