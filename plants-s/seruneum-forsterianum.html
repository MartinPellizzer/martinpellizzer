Seruneum forsterianum

Seruneum forsterianum, commonly known as the Forster's seruneum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens during that time.
