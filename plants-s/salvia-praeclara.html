Salvia praeclara

Salvia praeclara, commonly known as the showy sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
