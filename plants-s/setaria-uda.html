Setaria uda

Setaria uda, commonly known as slender bottlebrush grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its seed heads are often brightly colored, ranging from red to brown.

Setaria uda is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a forage plant for livestock.
