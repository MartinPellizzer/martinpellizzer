Saelanthus ternatus

Saelanthus ternatus, commonly known as the three-lobed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive three-lobed shape, contributing to its common name.

Saelanthus ternatus is adapted to survive in sandy and saline soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
