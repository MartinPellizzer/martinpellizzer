Serapias vomeracea subsp. guadarramica

Serapias vomeracea subsp.

guadarramica, commonly known as the Guadarramica sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
