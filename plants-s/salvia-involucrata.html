Salvia involucrata

Salvia involucrata, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Salvia involucrata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
