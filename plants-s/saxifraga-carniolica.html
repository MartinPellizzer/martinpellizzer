Saxifraga carniolica

Saxifraga carniolica, commonly known as the Carniola saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitude.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
