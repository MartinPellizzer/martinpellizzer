Sterculia rupestris

Sterculia rupestris, commonly known as the rock sterculia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
