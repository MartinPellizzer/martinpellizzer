Sorbus pannonica

Sorbus pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, rocky slopes, and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
