Serissa serissoides

Serissa serissoides, commonly known as the silver serissa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Serissa serissoides is also valued for its use in bonsai cultivation due to its ability to withstand pruning and shaping.
