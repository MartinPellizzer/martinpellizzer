Scorpiurus muricatus var. breviaculeatus

Scorpiurus muricatus var. breviaculeatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and typically have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry meadows.
