Sarothamnus scoparius f. sulphureus

Sarothamnus scoparius f. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur-flowered thyme shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
