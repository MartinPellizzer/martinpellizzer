Salix viadrina

Salix viadrina, commonly known as the Italian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
