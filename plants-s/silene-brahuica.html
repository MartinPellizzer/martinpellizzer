Silene brahuica

Silene brahuica, commonly known as the Brahui silene, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene brahuica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
