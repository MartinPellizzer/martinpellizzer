Senecio septilobus

Senecio septilobus, commonly known as the seven-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a head. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to arid conditions.
