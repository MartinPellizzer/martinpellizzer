Salix rhamnifolia subsp. rhamnifolia

Salix rhamnifolia subsp.

rhamnifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of the Americas. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks, wetlands, and other riparian habitats.

The plant features slender, often zigzagging branches and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. Its catkins are yellowish-green, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
