Sisymbrium bursifolium

Sisymbrium bursifolium, commonly known as bladderpod, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its small, rounded pods that resemble bladders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. It produces yellow flowers and has a fibrous root system.

Sisymbrium bursifolium is an annual plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
