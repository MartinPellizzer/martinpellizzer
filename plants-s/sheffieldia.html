Sheffieldia

Sheffieldia, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, pendulous catkins. The plant belongs to the Betulaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Sheffieldia typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 meters and produces small, triangular leaves that turn golden in autumn.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
