Sorbus heilingensis

Sorbus heilingensis, commonly known as the Austrian mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans.

It thrives in rocky or mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
