Senecio glandulosohirtellus

Senecio glandulosohirtellus, commonly known as the stickyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features glandular hairs on its leaves, which give it a sticky texture and aid in water retention. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its flowers are yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.
