Stigmatococca solanacea

Stigmatococca solanacea, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary significance.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in horticulture and requires careful handling.
