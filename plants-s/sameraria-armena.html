Sameraria armena

Sameraria armena, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sameraria armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
