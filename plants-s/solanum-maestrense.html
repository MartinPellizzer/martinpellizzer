Solanum maestrense

Solanum maestrense, commonly known as the Maestrense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Solanum maestrense belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.
