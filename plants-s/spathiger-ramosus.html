Spathiger ramosus

Spathiger ramosus, commonly known as the branched spathiger, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves, which are arranged in a distinctive, branching pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spathes, giving it its scientific name.
