Strombosia gossweileri

Strombosia gossweileri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
