Salvia fulgens var. boucheana

Salvia fulgens var. boucheana, commonly known as the red flame salvia, is a striking perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround clusters of small, tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
