Salvia officinalis var. castellana

Salvia officinalis var. castellana, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a cultivar of the common sage plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, gray-green foliage, and showy, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in summer. The plant is highly aromatic, with a strong mint-like scent, and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean landscapes.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a versatile plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
