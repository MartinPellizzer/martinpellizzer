Schizophragma crassum var. hsitaoianum

Schizophragma crassum var. hsitaoianum, commonly known as the Hsitaoianum honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden use.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for humans and wildlife.
