Setaria gracilis var. pauciseta

Setaria gracilis var. pauciseta, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, hard seeds.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to wetland environments.
