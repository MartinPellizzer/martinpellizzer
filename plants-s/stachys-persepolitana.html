Stachys persepolitana

Stachys persepolitana, commonly known as Persian betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and produces clusters of purple or pink flowers. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Stachys persepolitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
