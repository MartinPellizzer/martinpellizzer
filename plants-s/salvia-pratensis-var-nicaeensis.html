Salvia pratensis var. nicaeensis

Salvia pratensis var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Balkan clary or Balkan salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
