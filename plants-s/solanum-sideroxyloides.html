Solanum sideroxyloides

Solanum sideroxyloides, commonly known as the ironwood nightshade, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
