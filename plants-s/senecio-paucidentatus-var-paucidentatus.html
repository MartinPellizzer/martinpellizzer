Senecio paucidentatus var. paucidentatus

Senecio paucidentatus var. paucidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
