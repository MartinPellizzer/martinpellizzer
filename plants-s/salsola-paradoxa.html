Salsola paradoxa

Salsola paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or subshrub that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and succulent, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Salsola paradoxa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion.
