Scutellaria tenuipetiolata

Scutellaria tenuipetiolata, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scutellaria tenuipetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
