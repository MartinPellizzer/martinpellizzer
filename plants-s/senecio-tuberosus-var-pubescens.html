Senecio tuberosus var. pubescens

Senecio tuberosus var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvety groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
