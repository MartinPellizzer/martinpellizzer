Salvia schizochila

Salvia schizochila, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
