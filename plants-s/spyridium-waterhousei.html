Spyridium waterhousei

Spyridium waterhousei, commonly known as the waterhouse's spyridium, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps it conserve water in its arid environment.

Spyridium waterhousei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
