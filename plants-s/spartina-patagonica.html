Spartina patagonica

Spartina patagonica, commonly known as giant cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize sandy or muddy soils. This plant thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. Its rhizomes spread rapidly, allowing it to form extensive, monotypic stands.

Spartina patagonica plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by reducing erosion and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
