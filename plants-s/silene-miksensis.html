Silene miksensis

Silene miksensis, commonly known as the Miksens' catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene miksensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
