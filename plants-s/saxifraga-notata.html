Saxifraga notata

Saxifraga notata, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, basal leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga notata is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
