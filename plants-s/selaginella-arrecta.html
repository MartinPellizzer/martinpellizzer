Selaginella arrecta

Selaginella arrecta, commonly known as the upright clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Selaginella arrecta is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its capacity to retain moisture.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
