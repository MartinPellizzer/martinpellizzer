Salmea eupatoria

Salmea eupatoria, commonly known as the common tarragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in sauces and dressings.

Salmea eupatoria thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
