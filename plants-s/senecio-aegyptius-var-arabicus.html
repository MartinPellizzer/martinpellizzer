Senecio aegyptius var. arabicus

Senecio aegyptius var. arabicus, commonly known as the Egyptian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a succulent shrub with fleshy, ribbed stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to dry environments and can thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
