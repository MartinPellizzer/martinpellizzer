Sambucus simpsonii

Sambucus simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly serrated edge.

Simpson's elderberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
