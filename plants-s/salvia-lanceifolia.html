Salvia lanceifolia

Salvia lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and spikes of showy violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia lanceifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
