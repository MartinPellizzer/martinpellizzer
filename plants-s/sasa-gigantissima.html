Sasa gigantissima

Sasa gigantissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 12 meters. The plant has a dense, grass-like foliage with long, narrow leaves that are usually a bright green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa gigantissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Japanese culture.
