Selinum trinia

Selinum trinia, commonly known as the lesser calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Selinum trinia is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
