Scaphosepalum duplex

Scaphosepalum duplex, commonly known as the double-sheath orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of two sheathing bracts that enclose the floral spike. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure, featuring a bilobed lip and spurred column. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
