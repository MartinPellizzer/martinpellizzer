Senecio hirthii

Senecio hirthii, commonly known as the Hirth's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio hirthii is generally drought-tolerant and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
