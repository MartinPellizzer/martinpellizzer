Sidalcea delphiniifolia var. humilis

Sidalcea delphiniifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the lowleaf soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its small, pink to white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
