Scirpus margaritifer

Scirpus margaritifer, commonly known as the pearlgrass, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Scirpus margaritifer thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or saturated soils.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
