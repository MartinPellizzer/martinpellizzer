Searsia montana

Searsia montana, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Searsia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
