Scaevola microcarpa

Scaevola microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically retained on the plant for an extended period. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Scaevola microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
