Sorbus pseudosemi-incisa

Sorbus pseudosemi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathians. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. Its fruit is a small, aggregate berry that turns red when mature, providing food for various bird species.

It typically grows in rocky or wooded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
