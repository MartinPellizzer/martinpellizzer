Sempervivum reginae-amaliae

Sempervivum reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum reginae-amaliae is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
