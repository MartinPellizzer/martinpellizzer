Sideritis gossypina var. brevidentata

Sideritis gossypina var. brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more densely arranged teeth on the leaves compared to other varieties of Sideritis.
