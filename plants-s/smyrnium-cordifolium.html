Smyrnium cordifolium

Smyrnium cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf cowbane, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to both livestock and humans.
