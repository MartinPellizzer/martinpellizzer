Solanum obispoense

Solanum obispoense, commonly known as the Obispo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom at night. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

Solanum obispoense is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
