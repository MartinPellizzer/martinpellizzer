Stylidium eglandulosum

Stylidium eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless spurred orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Stylidium eglandulosum is a short-lived perennial, often found in coastal or inland areas with well-drained soils.
