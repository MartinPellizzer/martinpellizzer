Smilax cuspidata

Smilax cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with stout, spiny stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids.

Smilax cuspidata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
