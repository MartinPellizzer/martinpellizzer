Stobaea viscosa

Stobaea viscosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, fluffy flower heads. The plant features hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Stobaea viscosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and suitability for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
