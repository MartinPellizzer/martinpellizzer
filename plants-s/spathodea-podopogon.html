Spathodea podopogon

Spathodea podopogon, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous bright red or orange bracts. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
