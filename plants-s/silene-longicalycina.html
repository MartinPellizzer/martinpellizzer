Silene longicalycina

Silene longicalycina, commonly known as the long calyx catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the northern regions of Europe and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

Silene longicalycina is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
