Senebiera pinnatifida var. incisa

Senebiera pinnatifida var. incisa, commonly known as the serrated senebiera, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its foliage is typically dark green with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
