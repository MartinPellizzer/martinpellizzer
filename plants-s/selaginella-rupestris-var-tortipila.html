Selaginella rupestris var. tortipila

Selaginella rupestris var. tortipila, commonly known as the twisted-sheath spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphology.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant with a creeping rhizome and erect, branched stems. The leaves are spirally arranged, linear, and often have a distinct, twisted base that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
