Satureja pilosa subsp. origanita

Satureja pilosa subsp.

origanita, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval leaves and clusters of tiny white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
