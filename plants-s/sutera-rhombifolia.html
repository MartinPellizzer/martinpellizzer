Sutera rhombifolia

Sutera rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic sutera, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive rhombic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Sutera rhombifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
