Schkuhria anthemoidea var. guatemalensis

Schkuhria anthemoidea var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical leaf structures. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate, with hairy surfaces. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
