Salix glaucophylloides var. glaucophylla

Salix glaucophylloides var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla willow, is a variant of the glaucophylloides willow, native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in riparian zones and moist soils, showing adaptability to wetland environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a dense covering of fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
