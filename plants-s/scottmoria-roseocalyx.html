Scottmoria roseocalyx

Scottmoria roseocalyx, commonly known as the rose-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly veined, rose-pink petals and a slender, elegant floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex arrangement of sepals and petals that enhance its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its adaptability to humid, shaded environments.
