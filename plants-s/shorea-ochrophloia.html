Shorea ochrophloia

Shorea ochrophloia, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
