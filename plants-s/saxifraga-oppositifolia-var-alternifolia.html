Saxifraga oppositifolia var. alternifolia

Saxifraga oppositifolia var. alternifolia, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive, alternate arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variant is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
