Sida sonneratiana

Sida sonneratiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and agricultural areas.

Sida sonneratiana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in many regions.
