Scorzonera rigida

Scorzonera rigida, commonly known as the rigid scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy root system that is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow to orange flowers with five petals. Scorzonera rigida typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
