Schizocodon intercedens

Schizocodon intercedens, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Schizocodon intercedens is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
