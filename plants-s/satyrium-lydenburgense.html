Satyrium lydenburgense

Satyrium lydenburgense, commonly known as the Lydenburg orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and usually have a distinctive coloration, with a mix of white, green, and purple hues.

Satyrium lydenburgense is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
