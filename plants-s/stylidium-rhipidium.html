Stylidium rhipidium

Stylidium rhipidium, commonly known as the spurred sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble miniature suns. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the back of the petal.

Stylidium rhipidium is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, disturbed areas, and sandy plains.
