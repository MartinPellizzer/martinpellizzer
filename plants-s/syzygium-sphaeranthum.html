Syzygium sphaeranthum

Syzygium sphaeranthum, commonly known as the round-fruited clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces round, reddish fruits that contain a single seed.

Syzygium sphaeranthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
