Stichorkis plantaginea

Stichorkis plantaginea, commonly known as the broad-leaved stichorkis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a woolly texture. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stichorkis plantaginea is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
