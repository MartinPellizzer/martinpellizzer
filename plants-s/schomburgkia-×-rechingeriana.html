Schomburgkia × rechingeriana

Schomburgkia × rechingeriana, commonly known as the hybrid cycad, is a unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Schomburgkia lewisii and Zamioculcas zamiifolia.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive appearance, featuring a thick, fleshy trunk and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking visual appeal. It prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Despite its ornamental value, it is important to note that cycads are considered living fossils and have ancient evolutionary origins.
