Sasa kashidensis var. hashimotoi

Sasa kashidensis var. hashimotoi, commonly known as Hashimoto's bamboo, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features slender, hollow stalks with a smooth, dark green surface. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its graceful appearance.
