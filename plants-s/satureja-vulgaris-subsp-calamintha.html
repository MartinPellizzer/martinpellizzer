Satureja vulgaris subsp. calamintha

Satureja vulgaris subsp.

calamintha, commonly known as California mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a pungent, mint-like fragrance.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
