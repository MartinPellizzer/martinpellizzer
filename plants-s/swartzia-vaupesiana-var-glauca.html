Swartzia vaupesiana var. glauca

Swartzia vaupesiana var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous swartzia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, glaucous bark and lanceolate, leathery leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
