Saxifraga integrifolia var. apetala

Saxifraga integrifolia var. apetala, commonly known as the barren saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that often lack petals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically entire, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates.
