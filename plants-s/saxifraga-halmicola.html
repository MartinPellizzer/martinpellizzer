Saxifraga halmicola

Saxifraga halmicola, commonly known as the Halmikola saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and North America.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments, where it forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often succulent and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Saxifraga halmicola is well adapted to cold temperatures and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
