Salvia verbenaca var. ovatocrenata

Salvia verbenaca var. ovatocrenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in gray-green, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential source of essential oils.
