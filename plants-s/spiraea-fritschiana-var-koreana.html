Spiraea fritschiana var. koreana

Spiraea fritschiana var. koreana, commonly known as Korean spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and blooms in late spring to early summer.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
