Solanum andigenum convar. puca-mata

Solanum andigenum convar. puca-mata, commonly known as the purple potato, is a wild relative of the domesticated potato native to the Andes.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with tubers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen.

The tubers are valued for their unique color and potential nutritional properties.
