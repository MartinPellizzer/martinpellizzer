Silene rostrata

Silene rostrata, commonly known as the beakweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene rostrata is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a resilient, drought-tolerant species.
