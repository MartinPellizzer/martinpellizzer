Solidago gymnospermoides

Solidago gymnospermoides, commonly known as the gymnosperm-like goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering head composed of many small disc flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
