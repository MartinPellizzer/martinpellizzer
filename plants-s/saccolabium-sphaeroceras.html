Saccolabium sphaeroceras

Saccolabium sphaeroceras, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Saccolabium sphaeroceras is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
