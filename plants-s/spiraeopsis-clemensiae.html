Spiraeopsis clemensiae

Spiraeopsis clemensiae, commonly known as the dwarf spiraeo, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a compact, mounding growth habit. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Spiraeopsis clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
