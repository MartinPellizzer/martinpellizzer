Salvia molina

Salvia molina, commonly known as woolly bluecurls, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, preferring full sun.

Salvia molina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
