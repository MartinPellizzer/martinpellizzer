Stelis modesta

Stelis modesta, commonly known as the modest orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and symbiotic relationship with specific fungi.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Stelis modesta is considered a rare and endangered species.
