Senecio hookerianus

Senecio hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio hookerianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to specific ecological niches in its native habitat.
