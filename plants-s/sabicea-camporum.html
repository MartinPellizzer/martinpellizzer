Sabicea camporum

Sabicea camporum, commonly known as the Camps Hill acacia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Sabicea camporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
