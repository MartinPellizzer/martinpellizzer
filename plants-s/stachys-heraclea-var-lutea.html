Stachys heraclea var. lutea

Stachys heraclea var. lutea, commonly known as yellow betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in whorls along the upper stem, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Yellow betony is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
