Sigesbeckia caspia

Sigesbeckia caspia, commonly known as the Caspian sigesbeckia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in capitula and bloom in the summer months.

Sigesbeckia caspia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
