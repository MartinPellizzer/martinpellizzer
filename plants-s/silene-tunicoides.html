Silene tunicoides

Silene tunicoides, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette and can store water, aiding in its survival in humid conditions.

Silene tunicoides is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.
