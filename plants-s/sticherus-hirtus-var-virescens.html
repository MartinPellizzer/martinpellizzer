Sticherus hirtus var. virescens

Sticherus hirtus var. virescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed sticherus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
