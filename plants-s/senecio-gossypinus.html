Senecio gossypinus

Senecio gossypinus, commonly known as the cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio gossypinus is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
