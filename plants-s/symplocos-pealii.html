Symplocos pealii

Symplocos pealii, commonly known as the Peal's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Symplocos pealii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
