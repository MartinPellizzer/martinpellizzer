Salvia ancistrocarpha

Salvia ancistrocarpha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The genus Salvia is known for its aromatic compounds, which contribute to the plant's distinctive scent and medicinal value.
