Sophora velutina var. scandens

Sophora velutina var. scandens, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a climbing legume native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, green, bean-like pods that contain flattened, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along fences or trellises.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management.
