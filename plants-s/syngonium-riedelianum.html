Syngonium riedelianum

Syngonium riedelianum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive arrowhead pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Syngonium riedelianum is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
