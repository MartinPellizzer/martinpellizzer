Symplocos apicalis var. glabrifolia

Symplocos apicalis var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sand cherry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are generally smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in the fall.

Symplocos apicalis var. glabrifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
