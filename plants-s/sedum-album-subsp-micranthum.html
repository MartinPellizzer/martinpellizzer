Sedum album subsp. micranthum

Sedum album subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
