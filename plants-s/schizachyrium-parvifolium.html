Schizachyrium parvifolium

Schizachyrium parvifolium, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens. Its seed heads are fluffy and often persist through winter, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Schizachyrium parvifolium plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
