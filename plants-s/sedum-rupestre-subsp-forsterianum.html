Sedum rupestre subsp. forsterianum

Sedum rupestre subsp.

forsterianum, commonly known as the Forster's stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is well adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and poor, well-draining conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, arid environments.
