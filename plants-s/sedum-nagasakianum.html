Sedum nagasakianum

Sedum nagasakianum, commonly known as the Nagasaki stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is popular among gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
