Sapindus peruvianus var. dombeyanus

Sapindus peruvianus var. dombeyanus, commonly known as the Peruvian soapnut tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain spiny seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit has been historically used as a natural soap due to its saponin content.
