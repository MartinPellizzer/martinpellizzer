Serjania pedicellaris

Serjania pedicellaris, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, reddish bark and elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

Serjania pedicellaris is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
