Salsola merkulowiczii

Salsola merkulowiczii, commonly known as Russian thornweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and deserts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dry, triangular seeds.

It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
