Spiranthes viridiflora

Spiranthes viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and elongated inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spiral pattern, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

Spiranthes viridiflora is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters along the edges of streams or in swampy areas.
