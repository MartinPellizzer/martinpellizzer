Scutellaria cristata

Scutellaria cristata, commonly known as the crowned skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves and small, blue to violet flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.

Scutellaria cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
