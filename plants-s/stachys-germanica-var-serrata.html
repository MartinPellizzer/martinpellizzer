Stachys germanica var. serrata

Stachys germanica var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
