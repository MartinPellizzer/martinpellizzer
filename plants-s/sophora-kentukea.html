Sophora kentukea

Sophora kentukea, commonly known as Kentucky coffeetree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain hard, inedible seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat diversity.
