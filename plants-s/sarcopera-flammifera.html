Sarcopera flammifera

Sarcopera flammifera, commonly known as the flameplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
