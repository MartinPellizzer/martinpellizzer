Silene subvinosa

Silene subvinosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene subvinosa is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
