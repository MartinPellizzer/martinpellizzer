Salix futura f. futura

Salix futura f. futura, commonly known as the future willow, is a rare and elusive plant species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind rather than insects. Its growth is often restricted to specific microhabitats, such as moist, shaded areas near streams or in forest understories.
