Statice virgata var. gracillima

Statice virgata var. gracillima, commonly known as slender sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
