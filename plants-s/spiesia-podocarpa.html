Spiesia podocarpa

Spiesia podocarpa, commonly known as the mountain gum or broad-leafed gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the temperate regions of New South Wales and Victoria. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
