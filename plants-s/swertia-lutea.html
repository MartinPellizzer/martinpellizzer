Swertia lutea

Swertia lutea, commonly known as the yellow swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. Swertia lutea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential health benefits.
