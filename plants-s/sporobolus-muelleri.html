Sporobolus muelleri

Sporobolus muelleri, commonly known as mueller's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Sporobolus muelleri is a valuable component of native grasslands and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
