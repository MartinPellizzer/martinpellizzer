Schizochilus lilacinus

Schizochilus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light absorption.

Schizochilus lilacinus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
