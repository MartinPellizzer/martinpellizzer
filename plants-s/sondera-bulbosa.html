Sondera bulbosa

Sondera bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous sondera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bulb-like underground stem, which stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are often narrow and grass-like, adapting to its environment by minimizing water loss.

Sondera bulbosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
