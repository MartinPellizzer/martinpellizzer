Silene gallica var. subsecunda

Silene gallica var. subsecunda, commonly known as the lesser campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, and its stems are often hairy. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
