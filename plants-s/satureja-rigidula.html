Satureja rigidula

Satureja rigidula, commonly known as rigid mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pink or violet and arranged in whorls.

Satureja rigidula is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
