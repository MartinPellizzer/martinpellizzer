Smilax elegans subsp. osmastonii

Smilax elegans subsp.

osmastonii, commonly known as the osmaston's arrowwood, is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
