Sapium subrotundifolium

Sapium subrotundifolium, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red, lantern-like fruits that are visually striking. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Sapium subrotundifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
