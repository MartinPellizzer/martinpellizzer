Shorea polyandra

Shorea polyandra, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its durable, heavy, and pale-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Shorea polyandra is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
