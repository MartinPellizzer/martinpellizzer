Salix tangii var. angustifolia

Salix tangii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
