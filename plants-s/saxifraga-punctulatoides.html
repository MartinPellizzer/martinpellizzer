Saxifraga punctulatoides

Saxifraga punctulatoides, commonly known as the spotted saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas where the climate is cold and harsh. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat.

Saxifraga punctulatoides is well-adapted to extreme environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to cold and drought.
