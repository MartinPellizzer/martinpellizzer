Schinus cabrerae

Schinus cabrerae, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy green on the upper surface. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Schinus cabrerae is also known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
