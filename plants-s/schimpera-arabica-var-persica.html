Schimpera arabica var. persica

Schimpera arabica var. persica, commonly known as the Persian coffee plant, is a variant of the coffee genus native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, red berries containing the coffee beans. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
