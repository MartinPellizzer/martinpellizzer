Simsia bicentenarialis

Simsia bicentenarialis, commonly known as the bicentennial sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its scientific name honors the bicentennial anniversary of the United States, reflecting its historical significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
