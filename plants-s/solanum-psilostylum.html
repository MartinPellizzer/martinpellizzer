Solanum psilostylum

Solanum psilostylum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum psilostylum is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests or along stream banks.
