Silene dioica var. zetlandica

Silene dioica var. zetlandica, commonly known as Zetland catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands. It has slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves.

Zetland catchfly is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
