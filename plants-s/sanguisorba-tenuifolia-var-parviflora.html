Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. parviflora

Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as narrowleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered pinkish-red flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. parviflora is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
