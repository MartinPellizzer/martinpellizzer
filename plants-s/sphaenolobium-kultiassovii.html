Sphaenolobium kultiassovii

Sphaenolobium kultiassovii, commonly known as the golden wattle of the Northern Territory, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and is often found in sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which are clustered in spherical heads. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
