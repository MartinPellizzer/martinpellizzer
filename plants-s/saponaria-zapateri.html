Saponaria zapateri

Saponaria zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Saponaria zapateri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
