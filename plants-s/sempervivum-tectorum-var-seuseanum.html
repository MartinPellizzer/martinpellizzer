Sempervivum tectorum var. seuseanum

Sempervivum tectorum var. seuseanum, commonly known as the Seusean houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green or silver-colored leaves. The plant is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens and as a houseplant.
