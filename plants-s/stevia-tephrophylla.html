Stevia tephrophylla

Stevia tephrophylla, commonly known as the dwarf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental value, it is often grown as a garden plant.
