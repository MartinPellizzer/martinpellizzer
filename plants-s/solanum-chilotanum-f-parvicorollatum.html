Solanum chilotanum f. parvicorollatum

Solanum chilotanum f. parvicorollatum, commonly known as the small corolla tomato, is a variant of the wild tomato species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tomato species.

Its morphology and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and potential agricultural use.
