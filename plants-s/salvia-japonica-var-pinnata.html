Salvia japonica var. pinnata

Salvia japonica var. pinnata, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, and the leaves are often pinnately divided, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and aromatic foliage.
