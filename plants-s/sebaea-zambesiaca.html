Sebaea zambesiaca

Sebaea zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
