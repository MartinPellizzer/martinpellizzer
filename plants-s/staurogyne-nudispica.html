Staurogyne nudispica

Staurogyne nudispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in South America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or just at the water's surface. Its roots help stabilize sediment and improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.

Staurogyne nudispica plays an important role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
