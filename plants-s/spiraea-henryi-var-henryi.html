Spiraea henryi var. henryi

Spiraea henryi var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has finely divided, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Spiraea henryi var. henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant form and seasonal interest.
