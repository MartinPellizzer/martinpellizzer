Scutellaria cisvolgensis

Scutellaria cisvolgensis, commonly known as the Trans-Caucasian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Trans-Caucasus.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Scutellaria cisvolgensis is notable for its medicinal properties, though its uses are not widely documented in traditional medicine.
