Sideritis imbricata

Sideritis imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled rockrose, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis imbricata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
