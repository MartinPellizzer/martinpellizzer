Sutera burchellii

Sutera burchellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers, which bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Sutera burchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
