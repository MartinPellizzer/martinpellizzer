Spathanthus bicolor

Spathanthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that typically display a combination of yellow and purple hues. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its elegant, drooping inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Spathanthus bicolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
