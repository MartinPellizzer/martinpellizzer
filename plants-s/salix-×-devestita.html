Salix × devestita

Salix × devestita, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, often riparian habitats, such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive grayish-white coloration that gives it its common name.

Salix × devestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
