Silene anglica

Silene anglica, commonly known as the English pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces capsules as its fruit.

Silene anglica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
