Salix matsudana f. umbraculifera

Salix matsudana f. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Chinese cliff maple, is a deciduous flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, umbrella-shaped crown, which gives it a striking appearance in the landscape. The plant features slender, zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
