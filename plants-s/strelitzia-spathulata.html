Strelitzia spathulata

Strelitzia spathulata, commonly known as the Orange Banana.

It is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa, characterized by its large, colorful, banana-like leaves and distinctive orange and yellow flowers. The flowers are arranged in a striking spiral pattern, forming a unique inflorescence that resembles a bird's beak. The plant is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade to full sun.
