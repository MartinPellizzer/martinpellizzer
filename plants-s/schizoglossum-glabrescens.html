Schizoglossum glabrescens

Schizoglossum glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Schizoglossum glabrescens is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in pine rockland habitats.
