Synaphea petiolaris subsp. simplex

Synaphea petiolaris subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the slender synaphea, is a rare and delicate fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves that give it a lacy appearance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but endangered species.
