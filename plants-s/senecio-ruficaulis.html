Senecio ruficaulis

Senecio ruficaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
