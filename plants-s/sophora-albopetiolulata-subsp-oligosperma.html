Sophora albopetiolulata subsp. oligosperma

Sophora albopetiolulata subsp.

oligosperma, commonly known as the white-veined sophora, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its compound leaves with white veins and small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant produces elliptical, flat pods that contain a single, elongated seed. It is native to China and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
