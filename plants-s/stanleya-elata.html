Stanleya elata

Stanleya elata, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are often branched. Stanleya elata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
