Sasa sirakurensis

Sasa sirakurensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It forms dense clumps and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

Sasa sirakurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
