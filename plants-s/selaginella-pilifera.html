Selaginella pilifera

Selaginella pilifera, commonly known as the feather moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Selaginella pilifera is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
