Sorghum provinciale

Sorghum provinciale, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a robust annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and upright stems, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, which are often pale green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Sorghum provinciale is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited rainfall.
