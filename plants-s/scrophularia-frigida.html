Scrophularia frigida

Scrophularia frigida, commonly known as the cold weather scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has erect, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, clustered in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Scrophularia frigida is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats.
