Schellolepis cuspidata

Schellolepis cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock-daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

Schellolepis cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
