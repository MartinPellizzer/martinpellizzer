Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. endressii

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

endressii, commonly known as Endress's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its upright stems and small, tubular flowers. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
