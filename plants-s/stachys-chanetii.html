Stachys chanetii

Stachys chanetii, commonly known as the rock betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, forming distinctive spikes of purple or blue-violet blooms.

Stachys chanetii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
