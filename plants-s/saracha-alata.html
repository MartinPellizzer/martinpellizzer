Saracha alata

Saracha alata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various culinary applications. Saracha alata thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutrition and agricultural diversity.
