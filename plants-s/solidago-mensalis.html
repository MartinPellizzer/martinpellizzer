Solidago mensalis

Solidago mensalis, commonly known as the early goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago mensalis is an important component of native plant communities and supports a variety of wildlife.
