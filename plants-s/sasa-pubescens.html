Sasa pubescens

Sasa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa pubescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, moist environments.
