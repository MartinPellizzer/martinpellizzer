Silene montana var. typica

Silene montana var. typica, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it has a compact growth habit.

Silene montana var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
