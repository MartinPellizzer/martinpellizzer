Sophora glauca var. albescens

Sophora glauca var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese wisteria, is a variant of the Chinese wisteria native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pendulous clusters of flowers. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
