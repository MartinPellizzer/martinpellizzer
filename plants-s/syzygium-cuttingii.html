Syzygium cuttingii

Syzygium cuttingii, commonly known as the Cutting's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Syzygium cuttingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
