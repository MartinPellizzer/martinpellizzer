Seruneum elongatum

Seruneum elongatum, commonly known as the slender seruneum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Seruneum elongatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
