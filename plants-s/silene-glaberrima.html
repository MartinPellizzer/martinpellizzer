Silene glaberrima

Silene glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene glaberrima is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
