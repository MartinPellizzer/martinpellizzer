Syzygium pachyrrachis

Syzygium pachyrrachis, commonly known as the thick-leafed apple.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Syzygium pachyrrachis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
