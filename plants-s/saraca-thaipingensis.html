Saraca thaipingensis

Saraca thaipingensis, commonly known as the Thai flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
