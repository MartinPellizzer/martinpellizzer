Sporobolus jonesii

Sporobolus jonesii, commonly known as Jones' sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion makes it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
