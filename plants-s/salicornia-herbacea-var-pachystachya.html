Salicornia herbacea var. pachystachya

Salicornia herbacea var. pachystachya, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high levels of salt in the soil and water. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often red or pink in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and the stems are edible, often used in culinary applications.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for both its ecological role and nutritional content.
