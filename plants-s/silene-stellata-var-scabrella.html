Silene stellata var. scabrella

Silene stellata var. scabrella, commonly known as the rough starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
