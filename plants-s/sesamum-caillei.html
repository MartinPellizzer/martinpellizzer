Sesamum caillei

Sesamum caillei, commonly known as the Cameroon sesame, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sesamum caillei is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the region's flora and agricultural practices.
