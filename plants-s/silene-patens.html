Silene patens

Silene patens, commonly known as the nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
