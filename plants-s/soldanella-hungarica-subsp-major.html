Soldanella hungarica subsp. major

Soldanella hungarica subsp.

major, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
