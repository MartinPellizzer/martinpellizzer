Schizachyrium sanguineum var. hirtiflorum

Schizachyrium sanguineum var. hirtiflorum, commonly known as stiff-flowered bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil make it an ecologically valuable species.
