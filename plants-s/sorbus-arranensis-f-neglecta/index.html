Sorbus Arranensis F. Neglecta

Sorbus arranensis f. neglecta is a rare and lesser-known variant of the rowan tree. Found primarily in the Scottish islands, it thrives in coastal and mountainous environments. This form is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and reduced flower clusters. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and overlooked by many botanists. Studying this plant offers insights into adaptation and biodiversity in extreme climates.
