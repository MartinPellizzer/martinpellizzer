Sorindeia acutifolia

Sorindeia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sorindeia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
