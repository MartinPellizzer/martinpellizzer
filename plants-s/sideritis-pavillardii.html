Sideritis pavillardii

Sideritis pavillardii, commonly known as the Greek heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Sideritis pavillardii thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
