Senecio boivinii

Senecio boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and woolly, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
