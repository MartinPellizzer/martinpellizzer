Stachys pseudopinardii

Stachys pseudopinardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Stachys pseudopinardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
