Solidago pubens

Solidago pubens, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine pubescence.

Rough goldenrod is a hardy perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods and meadows.
