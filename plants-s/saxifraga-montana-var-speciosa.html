Saxifraga montana var. speciosa

Saxifraga montana var. speciosa, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

Saxifraga montana var. speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
