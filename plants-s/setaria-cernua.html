Setaria cernua

Setaria cernua, commonly known as bentgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria cernua is also notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, contributing to its resilience and adaptability.
