Senegalia sakalava var. sakalava

Senegalia sakalava var. sakalava, commonly known as the Sakalava acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
