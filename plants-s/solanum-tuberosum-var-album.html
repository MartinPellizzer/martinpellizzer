Solanum tuberosum var. album

Solanum tuberosum var. album, commonly known as the white potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its pale, often white or cream-colored tubers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces showy, violet or blue flowers and has compound leaves with lobed margins. Its tubers are typically less starchy and have a milder flavor compared to the standard potato variety.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in certain cuisines.
