Scleranthus micranthus

Scleranthus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, white flowers bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture.

Scleranthus micranthus is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
