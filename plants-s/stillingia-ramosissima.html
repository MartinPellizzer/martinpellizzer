Stillingia ramosissima

Stillingia ramosissima, commonly known as the redroot or redstem, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its branching stems and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant produces dark red or black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
