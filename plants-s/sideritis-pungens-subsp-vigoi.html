Sideritis pungens subsp. vigoi

Sideritis pungens subsp.

vigoi, commonly known as the Sicilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
