Salvia verbenaca subsp. oblongata

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
