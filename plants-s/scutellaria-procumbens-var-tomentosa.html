Scutellaria procumbens var. tomentosa

Scutellaria procumbens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.
