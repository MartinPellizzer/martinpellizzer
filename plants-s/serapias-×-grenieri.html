Serapias × grenieri

Serapias × grenieri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the genus Serapias, is a captivating plant that combines the characteristics of its parent species.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in rocky and sunny environments. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers that resemble orchids, with intricate patterns and a distinct lip. It is a perennial herb that produces multiple stems with clustered blooms, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique and striking appearance.
