Styrax japonicus f. pendulus

Styrax japonicus f. pendulus, commonly known as the weeping Japanese styrax, is a unique cultivar of the Styrax japonicus species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red, glossy berries. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its distinctive form and seasonal interest.
