Stachys spreitzenhoferi subsp. virella

Stachys spreitzenhoferi subsp.

virella, commonly known as the greenleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem.

The species is notable for its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.
