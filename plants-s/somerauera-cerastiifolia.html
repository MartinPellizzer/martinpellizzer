Somerauera cerastiifolia

Somerauera cerastiifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
