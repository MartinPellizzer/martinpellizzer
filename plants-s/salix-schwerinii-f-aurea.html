Salix schwerinii f. aurea

Salix schwerinii f. aurea, commonly known as the golden weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its striking golden foliage.

It is a deciduous tree with long, cascading branches that create a dramatic, flowing appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn, adding seasonal color to the landscape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade in suitable environments.
