Sonchus viridis

Sonchus viridis, commonly known as common groundsel, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a hairy stem that is often branched and grows up to 60 cm in height. The plant has alternate, spoon-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. It produces small, oval seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap.
