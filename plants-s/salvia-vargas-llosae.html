Salvia vargas-llosae

Salvia vargas-llosae, commonly known as the Peruvian blue sage, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in dense whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
