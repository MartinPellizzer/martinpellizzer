Stachys maleolens

Stachys maleolens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red stem deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive reddish stems that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys maleolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
