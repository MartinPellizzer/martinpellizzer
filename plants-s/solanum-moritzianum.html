Solanum moritzianum

Solanum moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive corolla.

The fruit is a spherical berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.
