Spirella robinsonii

Spirella robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's spirea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is usually dark green and dense, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

Spirella robinsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
