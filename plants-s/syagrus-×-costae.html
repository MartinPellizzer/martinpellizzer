Syagrus × costae

Syagrus × costae, commonly known as the hybrid palm or the costae palm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus oleracea.

It is a tall, slender palm with a distinctive crown of long, narrow leaves that give it an elegant appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. Syagrus × costae is native to Brazil and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil types.
