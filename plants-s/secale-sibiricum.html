Secale sibiricum

Secale sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian rye grass, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
