Salvia colorata

Salvia colorata, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia colorata typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
