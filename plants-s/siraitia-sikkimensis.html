Siraitia sikkimensis

Siraitia sikkimensis, commonly known as the giant pumpkin or kheer, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces enormous fruits that can weigh several kilograms, often reaching lengths of over 30 centimeters. Its fruit is used in traditional cuisine, particularly in Indian and Tibetan dishes.

Botanically, it is notable for its high water content and unique nutritional profile.
