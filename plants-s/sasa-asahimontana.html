Sasa asahimontana

Sasa asahimontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and is native to Japan, particularly in the mountainous regions of Honshu and Kyushu. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and provide a lush appearance in shaded environments.

Sasa asahimontana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
