Syspone

Syspone, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the remote regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble the appearance of ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, emitting a faint, sweet fragrance. Syspone thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and mysterious appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
