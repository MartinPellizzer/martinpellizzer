Solanum toliaraea

Solanum toliaraea, commonly known as the Toliara nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces berries that are yellow when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. Solanum toliaraea is often found in dry, scrubland habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
