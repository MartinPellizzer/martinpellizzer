Solanum nesophilum

Solanum nesophilum, commonly known as the highland nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are star-shaped with five petals.

The fruit is a small, egg-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
