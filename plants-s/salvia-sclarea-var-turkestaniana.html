Salvia sclarea var. turkestaniana

Salvia sclarea var. turkestaniana, commonly known as Turkistan blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
