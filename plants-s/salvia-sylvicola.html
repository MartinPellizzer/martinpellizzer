Salvia sylvicola

Salvia sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia sylvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
