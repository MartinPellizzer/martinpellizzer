Salvia purpusii

Salvia purpusii, commonly known as the purple sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia purpusii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
