Semecarpus micranthus

Semecarpus micranthus, commonly known as the small-fruited bedellia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Saponaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
