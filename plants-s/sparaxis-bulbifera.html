Sparaxis bulbifera

Sparaxis bulbifera, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, orange-red flowers that resemble small fireworks, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sparaxis bulbifera is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through division or by sowing its seeds.
