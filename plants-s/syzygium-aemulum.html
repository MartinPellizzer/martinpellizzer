Syzygium aemulum

Syzygium aemulum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
