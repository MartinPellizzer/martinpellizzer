Scleranthus anisochaitos

Scleranthus anisochaitos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus anisochaitos is adapted to arid conditions and has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
