Scrophularia domingensis

Scrophularia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips, and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
