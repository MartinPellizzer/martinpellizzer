Salacia forsteniana

Salacia forsteniana, commonly known as the yellow salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, Salacia forsteniana is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
