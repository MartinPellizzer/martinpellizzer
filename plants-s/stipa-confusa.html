Stipa confusa

Stipa confusa, commonly known as the confused feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and tufted inflorescences that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Stipa confusa is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
