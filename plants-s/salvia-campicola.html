Salvia campicola

Salvia campicola, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Salvia campicola is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and low maintenance requirements.
