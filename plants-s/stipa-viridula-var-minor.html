Stipa viridula var. minor

Stipa viridula var. minor, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
