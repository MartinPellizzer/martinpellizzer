Sarracenia × williamsii

Sarracenia × williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular-shaped leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are often adorned with white or yellow markings. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique trapping mechanism.
