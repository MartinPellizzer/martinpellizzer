Stickmannia pubescens

Stickmannia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stickman plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented stems that resemble a stick figure, hence its common name. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils.

Stickmannia pubescens is often cultivated for its unusual and decorative appearance in succulent gardens.
