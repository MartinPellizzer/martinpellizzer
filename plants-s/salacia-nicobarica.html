Salacia nicobarica

Salacia nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar salacia, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of its family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a slightly hairy underside.

Salacia nicobarica is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory.
