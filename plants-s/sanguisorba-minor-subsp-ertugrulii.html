Sanguisorba minor subsp. ertugrulii

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

ertugrulii, commonly known as the Turkish sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers.

The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Sorrel family, Cyperaceae.
