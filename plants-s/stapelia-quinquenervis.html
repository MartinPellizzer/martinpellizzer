Stapelia quinquenervis

Stapelia quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, waxy texture. These flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as flies and beetles.

Stapelia quinquenervis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
