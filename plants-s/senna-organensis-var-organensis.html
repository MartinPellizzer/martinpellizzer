Senna organensis var. organensis

Senna organensis var. organensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
