Scrophularia scopolii var. smyrnaea

Scrophularia scopolii var. smyrnaea, commonly known as Smyrna scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is of interest in botany due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
