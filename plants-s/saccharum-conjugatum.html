Saccharum conjugatum

Saccharum conjugatum, commonly known as the clustered bamboo or sweetgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures for its aromatic leaves. Its growth habit and floral structure make it a unique species within the genus Saccharum.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
