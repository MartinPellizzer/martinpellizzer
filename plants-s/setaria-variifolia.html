Setaria variifolia

Setaria variifolia, commonly known as variable foxtail, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched inflorescence that resembles a foxtail, with spikelets that are often green to reddish in color. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and flat, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria variifolia is often considered a weed but is also studied for its adaptability and potential ecological roles.
