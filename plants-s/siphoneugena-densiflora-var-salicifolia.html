Siphoneugena densiflora var. salicifolia

Siphoneugena densiflora var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf siphonostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
