Solanum cremastocarpum

Solanum cremastocarpum, commonly known as the cremastocarpum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fruiting bodies known as cremastocarps, which are unique in structure. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and often branched.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes.
