Sideritis hirtula

Sideritis hirtula, commonly known as woolly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis hirtula is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
