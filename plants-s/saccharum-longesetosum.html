Saccharum longesetosum

Saccharum longesetosum, commonly known as the long-haired sugarcane.

It is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense, fibrous structure. The plant is notable for its high sugar content and is cultivated for both commercial sugar production and traditional uses. Its leaves are long and narrow, often covered with fine, hair-like structures that give it its common name.

Saccharum longesetosum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soils for optimal growth.
