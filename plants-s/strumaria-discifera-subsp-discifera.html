Strumaria discifera subsp. discifera

Strumaria discifera subsp.

discifera, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
