Staphylea emodi

Staphylea emodi, commonly known as the Kashmiri snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at high altitudes.

Staphylea emodi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
