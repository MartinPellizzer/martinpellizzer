Sorbaria stellipila var. typica

Sorbaria stellipila var. typica, commonly known as the whitebeard barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological landscapes and as a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
