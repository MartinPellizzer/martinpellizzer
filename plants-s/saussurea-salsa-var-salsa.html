Saussurea salsa var. salsa

Saussurea salsa var. salsa, commonly known as the desert gentian, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
