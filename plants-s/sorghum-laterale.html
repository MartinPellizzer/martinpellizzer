Sorghum laterale

Sorghum laterale, commonly known as the bush sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or purplish in color. It is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food, animal feed, and biofuel production.

Sorghum laterale is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
