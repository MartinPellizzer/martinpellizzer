Smilacina racemosa subsp. amplexicaulis

Smilacina racemosa subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as broadleaf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States. It features climbing stems with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs.

The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are often clasping the stem, giving it its subspecies name. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in racemes, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant produces yellowish berries that mature in the summer, providing food for various wildlife species.
