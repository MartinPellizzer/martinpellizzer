Senna bacillaris

Senna bacillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture. Senna bacillaris is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It also serves as a source of food for certain insects and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
