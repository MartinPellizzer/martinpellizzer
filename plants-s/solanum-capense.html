Solanum capense

Solanum capense, commonly known as the Cape nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Solanum capense is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
