Sisymbrium argutum

Sisymbrium argutum, commonly known as the sharppod wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a distinctive, sometimes prickly texture.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
