Senecio prenanthifolius

Senecio prenanthifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
