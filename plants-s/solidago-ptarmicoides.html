Solidago ptarmicoides

Solidago ptarmicoides, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and streambanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
