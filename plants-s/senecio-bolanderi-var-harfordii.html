Senecio bolanderi var. harfordii

Senecio bolanderi var. harfordii, commonly known as Harford's brittlestem, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers in the spring, which are arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
