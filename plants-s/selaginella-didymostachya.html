Selaginella didymostachya

Selaginella didymostachya, commonly known as the two-stemmed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, branching stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include strobila, which produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella didymostachya is valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
