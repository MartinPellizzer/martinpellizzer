Syzygium grayi

Syzygium grayi, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe.

Syzygium grayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
