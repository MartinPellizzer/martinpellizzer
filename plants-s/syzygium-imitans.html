Syzygium imitans

Syzygium imitans, commonly known as the "false apple" or "sour apple," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, sour yellow or red berries. Syzygium imitans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
