Senegalia polhillii

Senegalia polhillii, commonly known as the polhillii acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or pale green flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Senegalia polhillii is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
