Sparganium simplex var. longissimum

Sparganium simplex var. longissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating on the water surface.

This variant is often found in shallow waters and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
