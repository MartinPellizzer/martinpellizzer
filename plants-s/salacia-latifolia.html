Salacia latifolia

Salacia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
