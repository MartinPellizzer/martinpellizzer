Smilax bella

Smilax bella, commonly known as the scarlet smilax, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, waxy, yellow-green to red stems with sharp, recurved spines. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Smilax bella is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
