Senecio pectinatus

Senecio pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio pectinatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
