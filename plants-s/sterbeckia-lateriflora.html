Sterbeckia lateriflora

Sterbeckia lateriflora, commonly known as the lateriflora bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in mild climates.
