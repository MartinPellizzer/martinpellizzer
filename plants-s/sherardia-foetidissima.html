Sherardia foetidissima

Sherardia foetidissima, commonly known as the stinkwood or stink bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark that emits a strong, unpleasant odor when disturbed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodland and rocky areas, and is adapted to both moist and dry conditions.
