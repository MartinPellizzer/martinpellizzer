Spiraea fritschiana var. villosa

Spiraea fritschiana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This ornamental plant is often used in garden landscapes for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
