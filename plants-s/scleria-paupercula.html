Scleria paupercula

Scleria paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or estuarine environments. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually unbranched, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Scleria paupercula is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
