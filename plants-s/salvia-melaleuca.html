Salvia melaleuca

Salvia melaleuca, commonly known as the silver salvia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Salvia melaleuca is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
