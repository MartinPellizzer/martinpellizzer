Stemoptera lilacina

Stemoptera lilacina, commonly known as the lilac lace fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with a soft, lilac-colored hue, giving it its common name. The plant's stems are typically slender and branched, allowing it to climb and anchor itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is characterized by a fine, lace-like texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Stemoptera lilacina thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light for optimal growth.
