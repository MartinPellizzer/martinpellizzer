Sanguisorba × takahashihideoi

Sanguisorba × takahashihideoi, commonly known as the Japanese teasel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble a broom, which are a key feature in its ornamental appeal. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall texture and appearance.
