Serruria diffusa

Serruria diffusa, commonly known as the Cape rose or red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth and dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight serration along the edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
