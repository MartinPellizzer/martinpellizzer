Solanum panamense

Solanum panamense, commonly known as the Panama nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they ripen. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
