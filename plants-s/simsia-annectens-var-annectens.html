Simsia annectens var. annectens

Simsia annectens var. annectens, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically bright green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bristly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
