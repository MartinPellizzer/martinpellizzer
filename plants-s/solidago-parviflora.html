Solidago parviflora

Solidago parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, and it thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
