Sempervivum thomasii

Sempervivum thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Sempervivum thomasii is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.
