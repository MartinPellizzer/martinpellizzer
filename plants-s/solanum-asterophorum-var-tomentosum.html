Solanum asterophorum var. tomentosum

Solanum asterophorum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The berries it produces are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
