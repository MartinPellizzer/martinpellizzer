Solanum puchupuchense

Solanum puchupuchense, commonly known as the Puchupuchu tomato, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and oval, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known member of the nightshade family.
