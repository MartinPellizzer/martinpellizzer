Stachytarpheta angustifolia var. elatior

Stachytarpheta angustifolia var. elatior, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
