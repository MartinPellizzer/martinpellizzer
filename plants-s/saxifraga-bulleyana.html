Saxifraga bulleyana

Saxifraga bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulley's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga bulleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
