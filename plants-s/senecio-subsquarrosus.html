Senecio subsquarrosus

Senecio subsquarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
