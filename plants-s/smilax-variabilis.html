Smilax variabilis

Smilax variabilis, commonly known as the variable greenbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax variabilis is a persistent and aggressive plant, capable of forming dense thickets.
