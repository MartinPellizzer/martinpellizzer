Selaginella roxburghii var. roxburghii

Selaginella roxburghii var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves and a branching structure that resembles a moss-like appearance. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

This plant is also noted for its ability to survive in arid conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience.
