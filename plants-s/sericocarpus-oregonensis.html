Sericocarpus oregonensis

Sericocarpus oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Sericocarpus oregonensis thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, where it forms dense mats.
