Saccharum nareya

Saccharum nareya, commonly known as the Nareya grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, fibrous stalks and long, narrow leaves. This plant is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in marshy or swampy environments. Its roots are used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

The plant is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and biofuel production.
