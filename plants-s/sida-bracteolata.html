Sida bracteolata

Sida bracteolata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sida bracteolata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
