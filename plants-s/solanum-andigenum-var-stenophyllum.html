Solanum andigenum var. stenophyllum

Solanum andigenum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
