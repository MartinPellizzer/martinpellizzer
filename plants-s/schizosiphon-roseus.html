Schizosiphon roseus

Schizosiphon roseus, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems that often turn red as the plant matures. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Schizosiphon roseus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
