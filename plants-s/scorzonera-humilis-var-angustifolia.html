Scorzonera humilis var. angustifolia

Scorzonera humilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Scorzonera humilis var. angustifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
