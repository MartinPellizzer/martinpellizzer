Skimmia intermedia

Skimmia intermedia, commonly known as Japanese skimmia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features glossy, dark green oval leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Skimmia intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
