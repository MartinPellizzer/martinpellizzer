Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. blancoana

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as Mexican lavender or blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue to violet flowers.

The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional herbal remedies.
