Saussurea pseudosimpsoniana

Saussurea pseudosimpsoniana, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant is notable for its tall, sparsely leafed stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in arid and cold environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
