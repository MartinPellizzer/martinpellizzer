Schizostachyum munroi var. decompositum

Schizostachyum munroi var. decompositum, commonly known as the decomposed bamboo, is a rare variant of the bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fibrous, and often fragmented culms that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
