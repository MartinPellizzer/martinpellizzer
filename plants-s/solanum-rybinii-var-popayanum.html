Solanum rybinii var. popayanum

Solanum rybinii var. popayanum, commonly known as the Popayan nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in regions with a warm, arid climate. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
