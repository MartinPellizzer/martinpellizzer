Saxifraga cordillerarum var. decipiens

Saxifraga cordillerarum var. decipiens, commonly known as the Sierra saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low growth habit, making it a hardy perennial.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and high-altitude ecosystems.
