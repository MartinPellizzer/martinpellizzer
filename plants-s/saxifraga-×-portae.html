Saxifraga × portae

Saxifraga × portae, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, often white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Saxifraga × portae is also noted for its ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a resilient addition to rock gardens.
