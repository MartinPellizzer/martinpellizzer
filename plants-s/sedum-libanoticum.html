Sedum libanoticum

Sedum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps store water and reduce transpiration. It produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance.

Sedum libanoticum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
