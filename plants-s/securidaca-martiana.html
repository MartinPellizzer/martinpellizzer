Securidaca martiana

Securidaca martiana, commonly known as the African spurge or false spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.

The leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
