Syzygium pseudocalcicola

Syzygium pseudocalcicola, commonly known as the calcicola apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Syzygium pseudocalcicola is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to grow in calcareous soils.
