Sasa admirabilis

Sasa admirabilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or silver bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa admirabilis is known for its ability to form dense clumps, which can provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
