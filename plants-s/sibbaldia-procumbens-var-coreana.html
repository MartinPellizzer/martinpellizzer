Sibbaldia procumbens var. coreana

Sibbaldia procumbens var. coreana, commonly known as Korean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It typically features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous branched stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
