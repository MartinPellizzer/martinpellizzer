Swartzia minutiflora

Swartzia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf flowered swartzia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces small, hard pods that contain its seeds.

Swartzia minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
