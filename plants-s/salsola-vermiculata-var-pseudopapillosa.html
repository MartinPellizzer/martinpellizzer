Salsola vermiculata var. pseudopapillosa

Salsola vermiculata var. pseudopapillosa, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically perennial and forms low, spreading mats, which help it survive in harsh, dry environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.
