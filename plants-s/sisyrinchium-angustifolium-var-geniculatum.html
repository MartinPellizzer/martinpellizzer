Sisyrinchium angustifolium var. geniculatum

Sisyrinchium angustifolium var. geniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow up to 30 centimeters tall, adorned with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers with bright yellow centers, typical of the Iridaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
