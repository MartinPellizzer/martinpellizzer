Sasa hatsuroana

Sasa hatsuroana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Sasa hatsuroana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
