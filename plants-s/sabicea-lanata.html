Sabicea lanata

Sabicea lanata, commonly known as the woolly sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly foliage, which gives it its scientific name "lanata," meaning "woolly" in Latin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sabicea lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
