Sabicea purpurea

Sabicea purpurea, commonly known as purple sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
