Silene neoladyginae

Silene neoladyginae, commonly known as the Neoladyginae campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, typically at high elevations.

Silene neoladyginae is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine environments.
