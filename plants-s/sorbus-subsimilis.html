Sorbus subsimilis

Sorbus subsimilis, commonly known as the similar rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
