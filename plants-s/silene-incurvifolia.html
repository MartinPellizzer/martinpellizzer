Silene incurvifolia

Silene incurvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene incurvifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
