Senecio xanthopappus

Senecio xanthopappus, commonly known as the yellow pappus senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically yellow, with a prominent pappus that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained conditions to flourish.
