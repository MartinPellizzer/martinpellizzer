Sauropus concinnus

Sauropus concinnus, commonly known as the silverleaf sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by round, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
