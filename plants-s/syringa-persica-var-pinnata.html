Syringa persica var. pinnata

Syringa persica var. pinnata, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with a feathery appearance, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Syringa persica var. pinnata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
