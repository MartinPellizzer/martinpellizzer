Swartzia calophylla

Swartzia calophylla, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is often found in humid lowland forests. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Swartzia calophylla is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
