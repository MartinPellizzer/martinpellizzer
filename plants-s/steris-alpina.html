Steris alpina

Steris alpina, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

Steris alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
