Salix ilicifolia

Salix ilicifolia, commonly known as the goat willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, fragrant, and hairy leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is known for its early spring bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Salix ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
