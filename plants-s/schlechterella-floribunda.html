Schlechterella floribunda

Schlechterella floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowering period occurs during the summer months. Schlechterella floribunda is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
