Sempervivum tectorum var. corymbosum

Sempervivum tectorum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks bearing clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, and it is often used as a ground cover in arid or rocky landscapes.
