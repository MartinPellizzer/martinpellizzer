Syringa × chinensis var. dubia

Syringa × chinensis var. dubia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
