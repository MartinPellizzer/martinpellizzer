Sorbus schneideriana

Sorbus schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are a food source for various birds and animals. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
