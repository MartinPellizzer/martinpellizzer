Sorbus devoniensis

Sorbus devoniensis, commonly known as the Devonshire rowan, is a small deciduous tree native to the UK.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and turn a striking orange-red in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in British horticulture.
