Sarmentaria

Sarmentaria, commonly known as the trailing arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Sarmentaria species are typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by berries.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
