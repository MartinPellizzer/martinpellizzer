Swartzia rugosa

Swartzia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-surfaced ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and rugged, fissured bark. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Swartzia rugosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
