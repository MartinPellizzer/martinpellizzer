Schizachyrium vallsii

Schizachyrium vallsii, commonly known as Valls' switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually golden to brown in hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Schizachyrium vallsii is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control due to its ecological benefits.
