Searsia dregeana

Searsia dregeana, commonly known as the silver tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
