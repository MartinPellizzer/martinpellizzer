Stomatanthes loefgrenii

Stomatanthes loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures used for capturing small aquatic organisms.

It is native to freshwater habitats in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still water, often found in marshes and ponds. Its morphology includes small, floating leaves with specialized bladders that aid in predation.
