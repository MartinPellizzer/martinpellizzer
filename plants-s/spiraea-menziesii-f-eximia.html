Spiraea menziesii f. eximia

Spiraea menziesii f. eximia, commonly known as the exclamation spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of white, flat-topped flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green color that turns yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
