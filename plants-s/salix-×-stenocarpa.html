Salix × stenocarpa

Salix × stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 3–8 cm long and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same inflorescence. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks, wet meadows, and floodplains, often forming dense colonies.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
