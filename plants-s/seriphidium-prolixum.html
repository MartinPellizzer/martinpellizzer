Seriphidium prolixum

Seriphidium prolixum, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
