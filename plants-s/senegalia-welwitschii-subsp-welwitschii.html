Senegalia welwitschii subsp. welwitschii

Senegalia welwitschii subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the welwitsch acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in winter.

The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can tolerate poor soils.

The wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.
