Sorbus confusa var. fallacina

Sorbus confusa var. fallacina, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by bright orange or red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and turn red or orange in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
