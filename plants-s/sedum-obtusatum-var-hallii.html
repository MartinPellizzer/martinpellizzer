Sedum obtusatum var. hallii

Sedum obtusatum var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
