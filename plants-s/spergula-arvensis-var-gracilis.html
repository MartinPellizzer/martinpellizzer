Spergula arvensis var. gracilis

Spergula arvensis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender field sedge, is a grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wet meadows.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems.
