Scleranthus drogeanus

Scleranthus drogeanus, commonly known as the desert sandstem, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves adapted to conserve water. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow, with five petals and a distinctive central crown of stamens. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
