Senna mensicola

Senna mensicola, commonly known as moonflower senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Senna mensicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
