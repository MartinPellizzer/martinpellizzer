Syzygium guamense

Syzygium guamense, commonly known as the Guam apple or white apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Guam.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet and tart flavor.

Syzygium guamense is adapted to the tropical climate of Guam and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
