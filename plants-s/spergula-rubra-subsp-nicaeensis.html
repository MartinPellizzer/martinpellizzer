Spergula rubra subsp. nicaeensis

Spergula rubra subsp.

nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its subspecies is distinguished by its reddish coloration, particularly in the inflorescence and leaf bases.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
