Stegnogramma pilosa var. alabamensis

Stegnogramma pilosa var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama hairy stegmin, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
