Salix × sepulcralis

Salix × sepulcralis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive drooping appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × sepulcralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
