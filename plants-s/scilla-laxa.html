Scilla laxa

Scilla laxa, commonly known as the loose scilla, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Scilla laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
