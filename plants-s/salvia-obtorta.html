Salvia obtorta

Salvia obtorta, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia obtorta is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
