Scrophularia laevigata subsp. valentina

Scrophularia laevigata subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually purple or pink, with five petals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
