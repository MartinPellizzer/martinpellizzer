Stapelia grandiflora var. lineata

Stapelia grandiflora var. lineata, commonly known as the spotted carrion flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, meaty, yellowish-green flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor. The leaves are typically green with white or pale markings, and the plant is known for its ability to store water in its stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

This plant is often cultivated for its unique appearance and interesting ecological adaptations.
