Serratula marginata

Serratula marginata, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Serratula marginata is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, somewhat bitter scent.
