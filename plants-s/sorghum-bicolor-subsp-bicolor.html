Sorghum bicolor subsp. bicolor

Sorghum bicolor subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it an important staple crop in many arid regions. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and protein, used for food, animal feed, and brewing.

The plant also has cultural and traditional significance in various parts of the world.
