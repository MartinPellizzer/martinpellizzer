Stachys pyramidalis

Stachys pyramidalis, commonly known as pyramidale or redstem deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Stachys pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
