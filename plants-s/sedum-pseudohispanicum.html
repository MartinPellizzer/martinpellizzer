Sedum pseudohispanicum

Sedum pseudohispanicum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.

Sedum pseudohispanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in drought-prone landscapes.
