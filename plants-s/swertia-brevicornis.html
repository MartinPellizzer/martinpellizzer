Swertia brevicornis

Swertia brevicornis, commonly known as the dwarf swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
