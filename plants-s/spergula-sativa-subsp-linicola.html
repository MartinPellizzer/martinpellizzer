Spergula sativa subsp. linicola

Spergula sativa subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the Linseed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, triangular stems with small, scale-like leaves, and produces pale green to brownish spikelets that contain tiny seeds. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed or nutrient-rich environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain grassland ecosystems.
