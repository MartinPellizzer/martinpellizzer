Schenkia japonica

Schenkia japonica, commonly known as Japanese spurge, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It is adapted to thrive in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Schenkia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
