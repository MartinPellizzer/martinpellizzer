Staurogyne tenera

Staurogyne tenera, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant has thin, branching stems that can grow in both water and moist soil, making it adaptable to various aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value.

Staurogyne tenera plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
