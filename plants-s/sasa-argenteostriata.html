Sasa argenteostriata

Sasa argenteostriata, commonly known as silver-striped bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a silvery-white stripe, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa argenteostriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese and Chinese gardens.
