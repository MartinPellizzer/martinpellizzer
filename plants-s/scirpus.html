Scirpus

Scirpus, commonly known as bulrush, is a genus of flowering plants in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds and lakes. Scirpus species are characterized by their tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. They play a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Their rhizomes often form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological structure of wetland ecosystems.
