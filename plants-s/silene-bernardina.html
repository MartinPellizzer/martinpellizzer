Silene bernardina

Silene bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene bernardina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
