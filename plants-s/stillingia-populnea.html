Stillingia populnea

Stillingia populnea, commonly known as the redstem stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. It produces dark purple to black berries that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
