Saxifraga ligulata var. ciliata

Saxifraga ligulata var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or tundra environments. The plant features succulent, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, cold climates. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a compact cluster, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
