Syzygium tenuifolium

Syzygium tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a drupe, typically red or orange when ripe, and is edible, though often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
