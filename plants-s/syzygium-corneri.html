Syzygium corneri

Syzygium corneri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the summer months. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Syzygium corneri thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
