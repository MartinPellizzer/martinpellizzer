Selliguea pothifolia

Selliguea pothifolia, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial evergreen fern that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and clustered fronds. The plant's fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, glossy appearance. It is often found growing on rocky substrates or in crevices, where it forms dense mats.
