Spiesia caucasica

Spiesia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic.

Spiesia caucasica is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
