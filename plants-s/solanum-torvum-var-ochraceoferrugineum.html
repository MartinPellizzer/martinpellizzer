Solanum torvum var. ochraceoferrugineum

Solanum torvum var. ochraceoferrugineum, commonly known as the ochraceoferrugineum nightshade, is a species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a sprawling shrub or vine characterized by its pubescent stems and alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, known for its diverse array of economically important plants, including tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
