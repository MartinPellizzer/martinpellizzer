Sterculia pruriens

Sterculia pruriens, commonly known as the itching nut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds. The plant is notable for its rough, often irritating bark, which gives it its common name.

It is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses in various indigenous communities.
