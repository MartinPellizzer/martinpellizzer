Secale vernum

Secale vernum, commonly known as spring wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.
