Salvia suffruticosa var. ballsiana

Salvia suffruticosa var. ballsiana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, woody stems covered in dense, soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces striking, showy violet to purple flower spikes in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
