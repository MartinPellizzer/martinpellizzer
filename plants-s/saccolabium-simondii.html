Saccolabium simondii

Saccolabium simondii, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, spiny fruits and narrow, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Saccolabium simondii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
