Sloanea paucinervia

Sloanea paucinervia, commonly known as the slender sloanea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Sloanea paucinervia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its wood is also valued for its fine grain and durability in traditional craftsmanship.
