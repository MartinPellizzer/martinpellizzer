Streptanthus glandulosus var. secundus

Streptanthus glandulosus var. secundus, commonly known as the glandular streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces clusters of yellow or white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is notable for its glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.
