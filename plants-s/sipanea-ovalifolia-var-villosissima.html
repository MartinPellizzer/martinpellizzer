Sipanea ovalifolia var. villosissima

Sipanea ovalifolia var. villosissima, commonly known as the hairy ovalleaf sipanea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
