Salix glabricarpa

Salix glabricarpa, commonly known as the smooth-barked willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and slender, drooping branches. The leaves are elliptic to lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a silvery underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian areas.
