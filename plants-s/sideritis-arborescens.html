Sideritis arborescens

Sideritis arborescens, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Sideritis arborescens is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
