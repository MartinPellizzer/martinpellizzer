Syzygium chloroleucum

Syzygium chloroleucum, commonly known as the white-leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glabrous, and pale green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and sour flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
