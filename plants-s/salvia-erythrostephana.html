Salvia erythrostephana

Salvia erythrostephana, commonly known as the redstephan salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia erythrostephana is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
