Solidago jejunifolia

Solidago jejunifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its root system is fibrous and shallow.

Solidago jejunifolia plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
