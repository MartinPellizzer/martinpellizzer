Salvia rosulata

Salvia rosulata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, tubular purple flowers arranged in conical clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Salvia rosulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
