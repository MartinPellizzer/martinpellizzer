Silene pugionifolia

Silene pugionifolia, commonly known as the sword-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Silene pugionifolia is often found in alpine or coastal regions, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
