Sempervivum gaudinii

Sempervivum gaudinii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Sempervivum gaudinii is also known for its ability to survive in cold climates and is often used in alpine gardens.
