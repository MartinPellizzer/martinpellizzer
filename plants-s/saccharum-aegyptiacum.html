Saccharum aegyptiacum

Saccharum aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Nile Valley.

It features elongated, narrow leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green flowers at the top of its stalks. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its historical significance includes use in ancient Egypt for sugar production and as a source of fiber.
