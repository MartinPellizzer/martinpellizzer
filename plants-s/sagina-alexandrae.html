Sagina alexandrae

Sagina alexandrae, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths on the ground, often in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.

Sagina alexandrae is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
