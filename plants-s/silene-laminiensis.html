Silene laminiensis

Silene laminiensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters tall and is often found in rocky soils or open habitats. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Silene laminiensis is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
