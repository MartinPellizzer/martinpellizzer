Sebaea crassulifolia

Sebaea crassulifolia, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, sandy soils.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
