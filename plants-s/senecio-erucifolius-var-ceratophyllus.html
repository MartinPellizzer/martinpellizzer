Senecio erucifolius var. ceratophyllus

Senecio erucifolius var. ceratophyllus, commonly known as the horned evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, leaf-like structures that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
