Sorbus cretica f. cuneifolia

Sorbus cretica f. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
