Sida rupicola var. grandiflora

Sida rupicola var. grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower seablush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Sida rupicola var. grandiflora is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
