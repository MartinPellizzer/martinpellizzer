Solanum cinnamomeum

Solanum cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Solanum cinnamomeum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
