Senna alexandrina var. obtusata

Senna alexandrina var. obtusata, commonly known as Egyptian senna or sickle senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat, ribbed pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their laxative and anti-inflammatory properties.
