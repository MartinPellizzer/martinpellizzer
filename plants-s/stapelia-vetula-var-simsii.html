Stapelia vetula var. simsii

Stapelia vetula var. simsii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its pale green, translucent, and often wrinkled leaves that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its low maintenance and unusual appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
