Solanum inaequiradians

Solanum inaequiradians, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum inaequiradians is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering pattern.
