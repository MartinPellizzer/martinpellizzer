Saxifraga × rifaea

Saxifraga × rifaea, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in a corymbous inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers shady to partially shaded environments.

Saxifraga × rifaea is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
