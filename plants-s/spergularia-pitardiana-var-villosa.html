Spergularia pitardiana var. villosa

Spergularia pitardiana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, fleshy leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
