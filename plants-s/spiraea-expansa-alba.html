Spiraea expansa alba

Spiraea expansa alba, commonly known as white meadowsweet, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Spiraea expansa alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
