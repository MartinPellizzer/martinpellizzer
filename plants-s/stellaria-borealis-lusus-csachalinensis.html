Stellaria borealis lusus csachalinensis

Stellaria borealis lusus csachalinensis, commonly known as the Siberian starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh, cold conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
