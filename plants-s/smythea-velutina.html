Smythea velutina

Smythea velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf smythea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. Smythea velutina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
