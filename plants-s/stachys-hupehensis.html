Stachys hupehensis

Stachys hupehensis, commonly known as Chinese mint or purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Stachys hupehensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.
