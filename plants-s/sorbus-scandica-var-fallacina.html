Sorbus scandica var. fallacina

Sorbus scandica var. fallacina, commonly known as the creeping whitebeam, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white, apple-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
