Symplocos acuminatissima

Symplocos acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Symplocos acuminatissima thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
