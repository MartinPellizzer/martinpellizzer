Senecio neoviscidulus

Senecio neoviscidulus, commonly known as the sticky leaf senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy, and often sticky leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
