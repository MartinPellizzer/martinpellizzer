Stoebe fusca

Stoebe fusca, commonly known as hoary mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stoebe fusca is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
