Sabina pseudothurifera

Sabina pseudothurifera, commonly known as the Japanese thuja, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically borne on the female plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its slow growth rate and adaptability to various climates contribute to its widespread cultivation.
