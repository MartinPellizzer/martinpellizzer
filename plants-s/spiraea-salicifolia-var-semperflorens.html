Spiraea salicifolia var. semperflorens

Spiraea salicifolia var. semperflorens, commonly known as everblooming weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features arching, willow-like branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its continuous flowering and graceful form in gardens and landscapes.
