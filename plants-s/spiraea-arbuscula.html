Spiraea arbuscula

Spiraea arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features dense, rounded clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea arbuscula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
