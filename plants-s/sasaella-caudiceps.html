Sasaella caudiceps

Sasaella caudiceps, commonly known as the long-headed sasa, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a common component of moist, shaded forest understories.

Sasaella caudiceps is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
