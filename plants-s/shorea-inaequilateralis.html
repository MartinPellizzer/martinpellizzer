Shorea inaequilateralis

Shorea inaequilateralis, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and forest structure.
