Senecio belgaumensis

Senecio belgaumensis, commonly known as the Belgaum groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
