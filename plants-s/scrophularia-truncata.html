Scrophularia truncata

Scrophularia truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply notched or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem and are usually pale purple or pink in color.

Scrophularia truncata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
