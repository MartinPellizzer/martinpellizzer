Senega graebiana

Senega graebiana, commonly known as the Florida gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Senega graebiana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
