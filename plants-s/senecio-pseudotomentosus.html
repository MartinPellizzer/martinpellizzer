Senecio pseudotomentosus

Senecio pseudotomentosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a sprawling growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in arid regions.
