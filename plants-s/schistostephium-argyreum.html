Schistostephium argyreum

Schistostephium argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf chamaedorea, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clustered, orange-red fruits. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
