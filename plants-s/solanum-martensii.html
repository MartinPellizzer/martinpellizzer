Solanum martensii

Solanum martensii, commonly known as Martens's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, rounded berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally not cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
