Scutellaria sulphurea

Scutellaria sulphurea, commonly known as golden skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild, mint-like scent.

Scutellaria sulphurea is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
