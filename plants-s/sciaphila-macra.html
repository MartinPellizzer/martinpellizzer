Sciaphila macra

Sciaphila macra, commonly known as the giant ground orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its unique morphology includes fleshy, strap-like leaves and a single, erect stem that supports its impressive blooms.

Sciaphila macra is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
