Scrophularia nodosa var. americana

Scrophularia nodosa var. americana, commonly known as American beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
