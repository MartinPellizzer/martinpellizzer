Sericocalyx scaber

Sericocalyx scaber, commonly known as the rough-hair mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of rough, scaly hairs, giving it its scientific name. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The berries it bears are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
