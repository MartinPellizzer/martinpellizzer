Schinus molle var. huigan

Schinus molle var. huigan, commonly known as the huigan tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, red berries that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a resinous sap when damaged.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
