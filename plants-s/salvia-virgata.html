Salvia virgata

Salvia virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its showy flowers, which range from violet to blue, bloom in whorls along the stem during the summer months.

Salvia virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
