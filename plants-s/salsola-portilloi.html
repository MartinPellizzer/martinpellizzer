Salsola portilloi

Salsola portilloi, commonly known as Portillo's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant has small, spine-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish and appear in clusters.

Salsola portilloi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
