Sonchus glandulosus

Sonchus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sticky coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
