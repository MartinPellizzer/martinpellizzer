Saussurea intermedia

Saussurea intermedia, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and yellow to orange-colored flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
