Spiraea alpina var. dahurica

Spiraea alpina var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and oval-shaped, with a serrated margin.

Spiraea alpina var. dahurica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.
