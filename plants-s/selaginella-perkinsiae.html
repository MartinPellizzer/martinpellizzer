Selaginella perkinsiae

Selaginella perkinsiae, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky crevices and shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, moss-like foliage and compact growth habit. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella perkinsiae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
