Stachys trinervis

Stachys trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys trinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
