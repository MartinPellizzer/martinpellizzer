Specklinia vierlingii

Specklinia vierlingii, commonly known as the Vierling's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, a feature typical of cycads, and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
