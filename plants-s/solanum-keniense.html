Solanum keniense

Solanum keniense, commonly known as Kenyan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption by humans and animals.
