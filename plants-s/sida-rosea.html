Sida rosea

Sida rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually ovate or lance-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Sida rosea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
