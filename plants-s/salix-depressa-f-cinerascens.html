Salix depressa f. cinerascens

Salix depressa f. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens willow, is a shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate growth habit.

It features grayish-brown, papery bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lanceolate, and covered with a fine, silvery down, contributing to its unique visual appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in riparian zones.
