Streptanthus inflatus

Streptanthus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant typically grows in dry, open habitats.

Streptanthus inflatus is known for its unique inflated seed pods that persist through the winter, adding to its ecological and botanical interest.
