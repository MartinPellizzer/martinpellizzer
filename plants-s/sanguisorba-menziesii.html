Sanguisorba menziesii

Sanguisorba menziesii, commonly known as redroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Sanguisorba menziesii is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
