Scleria rehmannii

Scleria rehmannii, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Scleria rehmannii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
