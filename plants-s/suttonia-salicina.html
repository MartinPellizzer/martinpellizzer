Suttonia salicina

Suttonia salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf suttonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Suttonia salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
