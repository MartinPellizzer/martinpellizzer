Salvia trichostyle

Salvia trichostyle, commonly known as the wooly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The genus Salvia, to which it belongs, is known for its medicinal and ornamental value across various species.
