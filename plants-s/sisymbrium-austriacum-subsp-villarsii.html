Sisymbrium austriacum subsp. villarsii

Sisymbrium austriacum subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Austrian brookweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is adapted to wetland environments.
