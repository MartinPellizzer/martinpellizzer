Sciaphila clemensiae

Sciaphila clemensiae, commonly known as the cloud forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species, often threatened by habitat destruction.
