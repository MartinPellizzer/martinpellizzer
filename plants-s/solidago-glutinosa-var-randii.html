Solidago glutinosa var. randii

Solidago glutinosa var. randii, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its showy inflorescences attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
