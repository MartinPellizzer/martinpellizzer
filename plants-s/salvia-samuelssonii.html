Salvia samuelssonii

Salvia samuelssonii, commonly known as Samuelson's sage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
