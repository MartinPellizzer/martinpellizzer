Sorbus margittaiana

Sorbus margittaiana, commonly known as the Mongolian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
