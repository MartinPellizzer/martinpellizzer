Stachys leucoglossa subsp. samothracica

Stachys leucoglossa subsp.

samothracica, commonly known as the silver-haired betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features showy, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the upper stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a silvery-gray appearance due to dense pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
