Samyda lunana

Samyda lunana, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Samyda lunana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
