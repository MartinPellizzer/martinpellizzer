Sambucus racemosa f. leucocarpa

Sambucus racemosa f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited elderberry, is a variant of the common elderberry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are white or pale yellow, distinguishing it from the typical red or black berries of other elderberry varieties.

It is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
