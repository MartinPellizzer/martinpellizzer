Senecio × londinensis

Senecio × londinensis, commonly known as the hybrid African violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio serotinus and Senecio hirtus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
