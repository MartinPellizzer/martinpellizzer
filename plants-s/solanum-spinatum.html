Solanum spinatum

Solanum spinatum, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum spinatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and can be toxic if ingested.
