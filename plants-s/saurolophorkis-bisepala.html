Saurolophorkis bisepala

Saurolophorkis bisepala, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often clustered at the base, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers bloom in clusters, showcasing vibrant colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
