Seidenfadeniella filiformis

Seidenfadeniella filiformis, commonly known as the slender spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
