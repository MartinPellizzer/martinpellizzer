Salsola marginata

Salsola marginata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, prostrate growth habit and small, greenish flowers. The plant has succulent leaves and stems that help it conserve water in harsh environments. It often forms dense mats, which can compete with native vegetation.

Salsola marginata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
