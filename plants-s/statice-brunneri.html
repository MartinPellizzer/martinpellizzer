Statice brunneri

Statice brunneri, commonly known as Brunner's glassweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps in water retention.

Statice brunneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
