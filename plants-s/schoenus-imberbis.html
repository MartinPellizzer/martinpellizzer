Schoenus imberbis

Schoenus imberbis, commonly known as the hairless spikerush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, often growing in shallow water or moist soil.
