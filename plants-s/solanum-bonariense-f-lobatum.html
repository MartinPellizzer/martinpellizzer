Solanum bonariense f. lobatum

Solanum bonariense f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
