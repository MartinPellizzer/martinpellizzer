Scleranthus brachyodon

Scleranthus brachyodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scleranthus brachyodon is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal or arid regions.
