Sisyrinchium californicum

Sisyrinchium californicum, commonly known as the California blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue or purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its small size and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
