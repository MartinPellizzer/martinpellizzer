Simocheilus klotzschianus var. glabrifolius

Simocheilus klotzschianus var. glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, pond margins, and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
