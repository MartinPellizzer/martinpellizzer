Salacia transvaalensis

Salacia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal salacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring.

Salacia transvaalensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and in woodland areas.
