Sarcopodium cymbidioides

Sarcopodium cymbidioides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Sarcopodium cymbidioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
