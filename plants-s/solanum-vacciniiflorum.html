Solanum vacciniiflorum

Solanum vacciniiflorum, commonly known as the redcurrant shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Solanum vacciniiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
