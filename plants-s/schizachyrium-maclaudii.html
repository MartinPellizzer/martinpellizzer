Schizachyrium maclaudii

Schizachyrium maclaudii, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in dry to mesic conditions.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall, and its slender, spike-like flower heads that appear in late summer. This plant is well adapted to prairie ecosystems and is often used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil. Schizachyrium maclaudii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
