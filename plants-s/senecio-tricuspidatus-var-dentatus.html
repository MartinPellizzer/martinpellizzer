Senecio tricuspidatus var. dentatus

Senecio tricuspidatus var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
