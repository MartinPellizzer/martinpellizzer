Solanum ferox var. repandum

Solanum ferox var. repandum, commonly known as the spreading nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that change from green to yellowish-red as they mature, often containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas with ample sunlight.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
