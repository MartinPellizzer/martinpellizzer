Sedum versadense subsp. villadioides

Sedum versadense subsp.

villadioides, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
