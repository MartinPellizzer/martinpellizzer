Syzygium coralinum

Syzygium coralinum, commonly known as the coral apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its attractive, coral-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

The fruit, which is small and edible, is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
