Silphium asteriscus var. asteriscus

Silphium asteriscus var. asteriscus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its historical significance and ecological value, it is considered a valuable native plant for restoration and conservation efforts.
