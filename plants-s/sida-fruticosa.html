Sida fruticosa

Sida fruticosa, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. Sida fruticosa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
