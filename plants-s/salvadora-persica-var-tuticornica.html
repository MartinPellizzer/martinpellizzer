Salvadora persica var. tuticornica

Salvadora persica var. tuticornica, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains seeds used in herbal remedies.

The tree is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for arid landscapes.
