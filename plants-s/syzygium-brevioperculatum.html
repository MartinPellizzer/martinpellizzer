Syzygium brevioperculatum

Syzygium brevioperculatum, commonly known as the short-capped tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
