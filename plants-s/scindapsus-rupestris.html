Scindapsus rupestris

Scindapsus rupestris, commonly known as the silver vine, is a compact, trailing evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking silver or white midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus rupestris is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
