Sida lindheimeri

Sida lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's mallot, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Sida lindheimeri is often found in prairies, roadsides, and along creek banks.
