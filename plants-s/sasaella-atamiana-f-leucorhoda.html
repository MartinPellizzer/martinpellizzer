Sasaella atamiana f. leucorhoda

Sasaella atamiana f. leucorhoda, commonly known as the white-flowered sasaella, is a variant of the sasaella genus native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
