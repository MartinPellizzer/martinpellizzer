Selaginella cuspidata

Selaginella cuspidata, commonly known as the spiky clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Selaginella cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
