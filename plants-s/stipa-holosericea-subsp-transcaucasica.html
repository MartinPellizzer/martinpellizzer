Stipa holosericea subsp. transcaucasica

Stipa holosericea subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spikelet that is densely packed with small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and landscaping purposes.
