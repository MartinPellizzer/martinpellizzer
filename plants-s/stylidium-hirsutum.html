Stylidium hirsutum

Stylidium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features a distinctive trigger mechanism in its flowers, which helps in pollination by attracting insects. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
