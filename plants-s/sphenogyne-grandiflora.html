Sphenogyne grandiflora

Sphenogyne grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sphenogyne, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It is often found in woodland areas and rocky slopes.

Sphenogyne grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
