Selinum pyrenaeum var. benthamineum

Selinum pyrenaeum var. benthamineum, commonly known as the bentham's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
