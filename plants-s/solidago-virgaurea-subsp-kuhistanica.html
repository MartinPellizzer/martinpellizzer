Solidago virgaurea subsp. kuhistanica

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

kuhistanica, commonly known as the Kuhistan goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
