Smilax munita

Smilax munita, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
