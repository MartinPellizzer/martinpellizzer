Selaginella furcillifolia

Selaginella furcillifolia, commonly known as the forked-leaved spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its lance-shaped, often forked leaves that are arranged spirally around the stem. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella furcillifolia is known for its resilience and ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
