Salix desertorum

Salix desertorum, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Salix desertorum is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
