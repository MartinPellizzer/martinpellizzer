Salvia halophila

Salvia halophila, commonly known as sea sage, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

Salvia halophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
