Sasaella tsukubensis

Sasaella tsukubensis, commonly known as the Tsukubanuki bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
