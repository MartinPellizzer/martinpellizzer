Sarothamnus virgatus

Sarothamnus virgatus, commonly known as the slender thyme or silver thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological gardening.
