Sorghum gidarba

Sorghum gidarba, commonly known as millet, is a type of grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in dry and hot climates, making it a staple crop in many arid areas. The plant produces small, oval-shaped grains that are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. Sorghum gidarba is often used for both human consumption and animal feed.

Its versatility and resilience make it an important crop for food security and sustainable agriculture.
