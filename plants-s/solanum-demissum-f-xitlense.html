Solanum demissum f. xitlense

Solanum demissum f. xitlense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berry-like fruits, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
