Syringa laciniata

Syringa laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Syringa laciniata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its ornate foliage and showy floral displays.
