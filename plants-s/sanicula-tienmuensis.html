Sanicula tienmuensis

Sanicula tienmuensis, commonly known as the Chinese sanicle, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features a slender, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Sanicula tienmuensis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
