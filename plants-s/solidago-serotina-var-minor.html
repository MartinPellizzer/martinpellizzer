Solidago serotina var. minor

Solidago serotina var. minor, commonly known as the lesser late goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, downy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, fluffy clusters at the ends of the stems.
