Sphaerocoryne gracilis subsp. gracilis

Sphaerocoryne gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender sphaerocoryne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and they are clustered in umbels.

The subspecies is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
