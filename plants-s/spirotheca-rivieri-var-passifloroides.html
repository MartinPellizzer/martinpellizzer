Spirotheca rivieri var. passifloroides

Spirotheca rivieri var. passifloroides, commonly known as the Passifloroid Passiflora, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
