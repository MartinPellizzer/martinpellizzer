Setaria leucopila

Setaria leucopila, commonly known as white beard grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to white florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Setaria leucopila is a hardy perennial that is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance.
