Serjania tortuosa

Serjania tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its woody, climbing or shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Serjania tortuosa is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
