Solanum saxatile

Solanum saxatile, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces smooth, spherical berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on maturity. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats.

Solanum saxatile contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
