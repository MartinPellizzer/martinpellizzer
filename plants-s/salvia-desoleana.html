Salvia desoleana

Salvia desoleana, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia desoleana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
