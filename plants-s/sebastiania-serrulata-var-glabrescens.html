Sebastiania serrulata var. glabrescens

Sebastiania serrulata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sebastiania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
