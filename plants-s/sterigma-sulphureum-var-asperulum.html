Sterigma sulphureum var. asperulum

Sterigma sulphureum var. asperulum, commonly known as the rough yellow lichen, is a member of the family Lecanoraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow thallus, which is typically crustose in growth form. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contributes to its unique chemical composition. It thrives in dry, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or bark.

Its surface is rough and uneven, giving it the specific epithet *asperulum*.
