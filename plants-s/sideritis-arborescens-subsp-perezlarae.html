Sideritis arborescens subsp. perezlarae

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

perezlarae, commonly known as the rock senna, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and wastelands.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
