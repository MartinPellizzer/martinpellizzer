Solanum stenotomum f. piticana

Solanum stenotomum f. piticana, commonly known as the Piticana nightshade, is a botanical variant of the species Solanum stenotomum.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and small, dark purple berries. This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
