Solanum mariae

Solanum mariae, commonly known as Marie's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
