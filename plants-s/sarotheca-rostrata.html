Sarotheca rostrata

Sarotheca rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Sarotheca rostrata plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
