Saxifraga × schachtii

Saxifraga × schachtii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

Saxifraga × schachtii is also noted for its ability to form hybrids with other saxifrage species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
