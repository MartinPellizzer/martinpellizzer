Sonerila brandisiana

Sonerila brandisiana, commonly known as the Malayan starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble starfruits, though they are not related to the common starfruit (Carambola). Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
