Salvia cacomensis

Salvia cacomensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cacomensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
