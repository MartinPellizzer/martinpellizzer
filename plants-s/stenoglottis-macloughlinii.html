Stenoglottis macloughlinii

Stenoglottis macloughlinii, commonly known as the Macloughlin's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with pale green to yellowish leaves and a single, elongated flower spike that bears a single, showy white or pale pink flower. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, often growing in association with specific fungal symbionts. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat destruction and limited distribution.

This species is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal.
