Sideroxylon hookeri

Sideroxylon hookeri, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Sideroxylon hookeri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
