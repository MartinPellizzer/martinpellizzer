Stisseria rufa

Stisseria rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered stisseria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Stisseria rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
