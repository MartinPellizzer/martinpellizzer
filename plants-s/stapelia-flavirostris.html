Stapelia flavirostris

Stapelia flavirostris, commonly known as the yellow beak stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often ribbed stems. The plant produces striking, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive beak-like shape, which are primarily pollinated by insects. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Stapelia flavirostris is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unusual appearance and low maintenance requirements.
