Senecio bupleuroides

Senecio bupleuroides, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Senecio bupleuroides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
