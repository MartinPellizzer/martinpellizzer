Serapias atrorubens

Serapias atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically a deep red or orange-red in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Serapias atrorubens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
