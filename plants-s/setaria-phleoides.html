Setaria phleoides

Setaria phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with bracts that are often shorter than the flowering branches.

Setaria phleoides is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
