Schefflera rugosifolia

Schefflera rugosifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often rough or hairy to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscape design.
