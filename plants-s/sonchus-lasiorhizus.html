Sonchus lasiorhizus

Sonchus lasiorhizus, commonly known as roughleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are rough to the touch and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
