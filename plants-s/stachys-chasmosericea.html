Stachys chasmosericea

Stachys chasmosericea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
