Styrax argenteus var. hintonii

Styrax argenteus var. hintonii, commonly known as the silver flame or silver styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white, velvety foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value lies in its unique leaf coloration and showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
