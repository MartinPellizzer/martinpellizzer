Sebastiania hispida var. schuechiana

Sebastiania hispida var. schuechiana, commonly known as the Schuechian's sebastiania, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
