Synsepalum cerasiferum

Synsepalum cerasiferum, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tart flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
