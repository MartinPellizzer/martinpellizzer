Senecio helenitis subsp. macrochaetus

Senecio helenitis subsp.

macrochaetus, commonly known as the giant helene's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
