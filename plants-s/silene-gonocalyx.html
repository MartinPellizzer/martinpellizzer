Silene gonocalyx

Silene gonocalyx, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Silene gonocalyx thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in open fields or along roadsides.

Its short lifespan and rapid growth make it a common weed in many areas.
