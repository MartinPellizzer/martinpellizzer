Suaeda cochlearifolia

Suaeda cochlearifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and the Middle East. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or muddy soils and is often found in estuaries and tidal zones.

Suaeda cochlearifolia plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.
