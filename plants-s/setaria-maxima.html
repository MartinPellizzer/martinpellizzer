Setaria maxima

Setaria maxima, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a highly versatile and resilient cereal grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant is known for its rapid growth cycle and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important crop in arid and semi-arid regions. Its small, hardy grains are used for food, animal feed, and even as a source of fiber.

Setaria maxima is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and essential minerals.
