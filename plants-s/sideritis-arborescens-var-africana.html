Sideritis arborescens var. africana

Sideritis arborescens var. africana, commonly known as African rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
