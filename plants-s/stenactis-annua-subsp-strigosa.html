Stenactis annua subsp. strigosa

Stenactis annua subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the woolly annual stenactis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, strigose leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a simple structure typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for herbivores.
