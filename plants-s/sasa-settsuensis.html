Sasa settsuensis

Sasa settsuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Settsu bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps through its rhizomatous growth habit, making it well-suited for erosion control in mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa settsuensis is often used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens for its ornamental value and ability to create natural, serene environments.
