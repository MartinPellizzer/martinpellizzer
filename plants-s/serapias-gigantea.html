Serapias gigantea

Serapias gigantea, commonly known as the giant sand orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Serapias gigantea is known for its long blooming period, often flowering from late spring through early autumn.
