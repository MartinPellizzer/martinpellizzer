Sarracenia alata

Sarracenia alata, commonly known as the winged pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its uniquely shaped, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-like structures. These pitchers are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, which help in attracting insects. The plant's pitchers secrete a sticky substance to trap prey, which is then digested by enzymes.

Sarracenia alata thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
