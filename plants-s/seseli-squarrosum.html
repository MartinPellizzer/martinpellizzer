Seseli squarrosum

Seseli squarrosum, commonly known as the rough seseli, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Seseli squarrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering habit.
