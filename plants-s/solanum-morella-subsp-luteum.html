Solanum morella subsp. luteum

Solanum morella subsp.

luteum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, yellow berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
