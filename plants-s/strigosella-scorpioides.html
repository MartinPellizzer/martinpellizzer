Strigosella scorpioides

Strigosella scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters that resemble a scorpion's tail, giving it its common name.

The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions.
