Salvia patriciae

Salvia patriciae, commonly known as Patricia's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of showy violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia patriciae is also noted for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
