Speculantha vernalis

Speculantha vernalis, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Speculantha vernalis is valued in horticulture for its early spring bloom and ability to thrive in partial shade.
