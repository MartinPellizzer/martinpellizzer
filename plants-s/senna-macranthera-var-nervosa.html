Senna macranthera var. nervosa

Senna macranthera var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with distinct, narrow veins, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
