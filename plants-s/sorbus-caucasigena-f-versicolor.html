Sorbus caucasigena f. versicolor

Sorbus caucasigena f. versicolor, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a variant of the Caucasian whitebeam, a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which exhibits a range of colors from white to green and even pink, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
