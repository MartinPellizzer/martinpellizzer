Symplocos glabra

Symplocos glabra, commonly known as the smooth symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.

Symplocos glabra thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories.
