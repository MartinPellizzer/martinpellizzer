Syringa vulgaris f. purpurea

Syringa vulgaris f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a fragrant deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically a striking purple to violet color, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the vibrant blooms.

It is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8 and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
