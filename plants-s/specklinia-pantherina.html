Specklinia pantherina

Specklinia pantherina, commonly known as the panther's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic plant, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and often have a speckled or mottled appearance, giving it its common name.

Specklinia pantherina is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in terrariums.
