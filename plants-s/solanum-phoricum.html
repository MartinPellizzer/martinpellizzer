Solanum phoricum

Solanum phoricum, commonly known as the rock tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and red, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Solanum phoricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines.
