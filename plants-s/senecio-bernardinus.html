Senecio bernardinus

Senecio bernardinus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in coastal and mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
