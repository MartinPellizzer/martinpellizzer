Selaginella setigera

Selaginella setigera, commonly known as the stair-step moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its segmented, stair-like structure, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Selaginella setigera is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
