Solanum euacanthum

Solanum euacanthum, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are usually ovate with spiny margins, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The species belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
