Schima lowii

Schima lowii, commonly known as the Chinese teak or the Chinese schima, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing seeds. Schima lowii is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in mountainous regions of China and Vietnam.
