Setaria viridis f. misera

Setaria viridis f. misera, commonly known as dwarf green millet, is a variant of the common green millet, characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to the standard species.

It is a C4 plant, which allows it to efficiently fix carbon dioxide under high light and temperature conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing tiny, greenish-brown seeds. It is often cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional agriculture due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
