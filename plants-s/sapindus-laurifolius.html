Sapindus laurifolius

Sapindus laurifolius, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, soap-like pulp. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural soap.
