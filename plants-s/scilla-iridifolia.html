Scilla iridifolia

Scilla iridifolia, commonly known as the blue scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a touch of color to woodland and garden settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and long-lasting flowers.
