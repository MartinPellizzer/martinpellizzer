Strumaria linguifolia

Strumaria linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue-leaved strumaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often described as tongue-shaped, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Strumaria linguifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
