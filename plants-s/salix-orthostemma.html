Salix orthostemma

Salix orthostemma, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and wetlands.

Salix orthostemma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
