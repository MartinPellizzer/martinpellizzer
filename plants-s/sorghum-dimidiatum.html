Sorghum dimidiatum

Sorghum dimidiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of flowers, which are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sorghum dimidiatum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological landscapes.
