Syringa cordifolia

Syringa cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers range in color from white to pale pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Syringa cordifolia is relatively low-maintenance and suitable for gardens and landscapes.
