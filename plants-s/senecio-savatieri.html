Senecio savatieri

Senecio savatieri, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio savatieri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
