Smilax sieboldii f. inermis

Smilax sieboldii f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to East Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its smooth, thornless stems, which distinguish it from other smilax species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
