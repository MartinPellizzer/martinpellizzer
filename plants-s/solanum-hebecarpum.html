Solanum hebecarpum

Solanum hebecarpum, commonly known as the slender-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Solanum hebecarpum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
