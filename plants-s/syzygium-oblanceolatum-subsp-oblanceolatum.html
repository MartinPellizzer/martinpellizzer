Syzygium oblanceolatum subsp. oblanceolatum

Syzygium oblanceolatum subsp.

oblanceolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
