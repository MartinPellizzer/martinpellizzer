Syzygium paludosum

Syzygium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium paludosum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
