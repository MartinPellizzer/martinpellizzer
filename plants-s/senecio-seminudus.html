Senecio seminudus

Senecio seminudus, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio seminudus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
