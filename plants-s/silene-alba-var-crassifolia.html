Silene alba var. crassifolia

Silene alba var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved white campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
