Suaeda monodiana

Suaeda monodiana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, often growing in coastal dunes or salt flats. Its structure includes a prostrate or ascending growth habit with branching stems.

Suaeda monodiana plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
