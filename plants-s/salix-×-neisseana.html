Salix × neisseana

Salix × neisseana, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, often giving it a weeping appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a bright green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

Salix × neisseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
