Salacia bussei

Salacia bussei, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, fragrant blooms throughout the year.

Salacia bussei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
