Sorbus folgneri

Sorbus folgneri, commonly known as the Sierra snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus folgneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
