Solanum asterosetosum

Solanum asterosetosum, commonly known as the star-hair nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. Its leaves are typically oval with a rough texture, and it produces small, oval berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
