Stachys aculeolata var. afromontana

Stachys aculeolata var. afromontana, commonly known as the East African hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of East Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with glandular hairs, giving them a slightly sticky texture.

This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.
