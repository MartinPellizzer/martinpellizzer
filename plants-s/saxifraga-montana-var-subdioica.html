Saxifraga montana var. subdioica

Saxifraga montana var. subdioica, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cold, high-altitude conditions.
