Spiranthera peltata

Spiranthera peltata, commonly known as the wax flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Spiranthera peltata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
