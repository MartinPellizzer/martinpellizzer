Silene halophila

Silene halophila, commonly known as saltweed or sea blite, is a flowering plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain water in arid or coastal conditions. Silene halophila is often found in salt marshes, dunes, and other coastal habitats.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
