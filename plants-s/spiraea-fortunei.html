Spiraea fortunei

Spiraea fortunei, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Its leaves are ovate and turn yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Spiraea fortunei is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
