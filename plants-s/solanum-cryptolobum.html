Solanum cryptolobum

Solanum cryptolobum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
