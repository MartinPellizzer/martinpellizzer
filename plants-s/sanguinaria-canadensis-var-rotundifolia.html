Sanguinaria canadensis var. rotundifolia

Sanguinaria canadensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the red-pink winter bloomer, is a variant of the bloodroot plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which exudes from the rhizome when the stem is broken. The plant features a single, showy white flower with a red spathe, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a waxy texture.

This variant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
