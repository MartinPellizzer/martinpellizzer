Salsola seriphioides

Salsola seriphioides, commonly known as sandmat or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the world.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

Salsola seriphioides is often found in sandy soils and can play a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
