Simocheilus articulatus

Simocheilus articulatus, commonly known as the articulated sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Its distinctive feature is the articulation of its stems, which gives it its common name.
