Symplocos syringoides

Symplocos syringoides, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds during the colder months. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and turn a striking red or orange in the fall.

Symplocos syringoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
