Silerium nodiflorum

Silerium nodiflorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in moist, woodland habitats. Its root system is fibrous, and it spreads by rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Silerium nodiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
