Soyeria

Soyeria, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, blue-gray leaves and produces clusters of small, blue flowers that bloom in the summer. Soyeria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
