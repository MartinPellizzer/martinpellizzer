Sanguisorba officinalis f. macrophylla

Sanguisorba officinalis f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
