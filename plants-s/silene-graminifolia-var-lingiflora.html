Silene graminifolia var. lingiflora

Silene graminifolia var. lingiflora, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
