Sclerocaryopsis spinocarpos

Sclerocaryopsis spinocarpos, commonly known as the spiny carpos bean, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its woody, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, where it thrives in sandy soils. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical structure of the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves.
