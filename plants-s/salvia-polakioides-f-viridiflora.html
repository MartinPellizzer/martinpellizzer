Salvia polakioides f. viridiflora

Salvia polakioides f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered sage, is a rare and attractive perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its bright blue to violet flowers. The plant produces compact clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry gardens.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in unique and hardy plants.
