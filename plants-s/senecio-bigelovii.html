Senecio bigelovii

Senecio bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio bigelovii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
