Struthiopteris eburnea

Struthiopteris eburnea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Struthiopteris eburnea plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
