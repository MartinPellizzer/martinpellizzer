Saussurea glabrata

Saussurea glabrata, commonly known as the smooth saffron.

It is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence, and it blooms during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
