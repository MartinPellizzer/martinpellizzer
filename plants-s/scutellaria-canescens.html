Scutellaria canescens

Scutellaria canescens, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Scutellaria canescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
