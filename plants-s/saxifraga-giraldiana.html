Saxifraga giraldiana

Saxifraga giraldiana, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga giraldiana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.
