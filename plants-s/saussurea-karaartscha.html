Saussurea karaartscha

Saussurea karaartscha, commonly known as the Karakarachan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are often mistaken for grains of saffron, though it is not related to the true saffron plant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
