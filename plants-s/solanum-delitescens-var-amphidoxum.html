Solanum delitescens var. amphidoxum

Solanum delitescens var. amphidoxum, commonly known as the "ghost pepper" or "chocolate pepper," is a variant of the Solanum delitescens species.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that turn a deep red or orange as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and a strong, pungent odor.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in culinary applications due to its spicy flavor.
