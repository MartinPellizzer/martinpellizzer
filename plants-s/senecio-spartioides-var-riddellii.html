Senecio spartioides var. riddellii

Senecio spartioides var. riddellii, commonly known as the Riddell's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy florets arranged in a discoid head.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
