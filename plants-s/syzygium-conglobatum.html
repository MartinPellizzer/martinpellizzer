Syzygium conglobatum

Syzygium conglobatum, commonly known as the clustered clove or small clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong clove-like aroma. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic in shape, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Syzygium conglobatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic essential oils.
