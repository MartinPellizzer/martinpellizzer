Sonerila hyssopifolia

Sonerila hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Sonerila hyssopifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
