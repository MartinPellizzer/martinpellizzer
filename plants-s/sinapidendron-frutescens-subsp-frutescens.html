Sinapidendron frutescens subsp. frutescens

Sinapidendron frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy red or orange flowers.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is adapted to humid, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
