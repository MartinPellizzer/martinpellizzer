Sison divaricatum

Sison divaricatum, commonly known as the African sison, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sison divaricatum thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
