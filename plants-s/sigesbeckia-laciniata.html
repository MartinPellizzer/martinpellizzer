Sigesbeckia laciniata

Sigesbeckia laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine hairs and produce clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are distinctive, being deeply lobed and often appearing cut or frayed. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
