Sida esculenta

Sida esculenta, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsules containing numerous small, round seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and okra.
