Serratula coronata subsp. insularis

Serratula coronata subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island crownwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to the typical S. coronata. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
