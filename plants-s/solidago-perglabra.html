Solidago perglabra

Solidago perglabra, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Solidago perglabra plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
