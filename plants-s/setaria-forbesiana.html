Setaria forbesiana

Setaria forbesiana, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, helping to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
