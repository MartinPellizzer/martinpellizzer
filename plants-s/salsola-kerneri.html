Salsola kerneri

Salsola kerneri, commonly known as Russian thorn, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with dense clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought and salt.

Salsola kerneri is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
