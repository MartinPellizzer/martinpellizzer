Sipanea radicans

Sipanea radicans, commonly known as the creeping sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sipanea radicans thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water once established.
