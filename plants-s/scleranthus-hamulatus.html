Scleranthus hamulatus

Scleranthus hamulatus, commonly known as the hooked sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, and its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

The species is characterized by its distinctive hooked awns on the seeds, which aid in seed dispersal.
