Spathodea laevis

Spathodea laevis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish petals arranged in a spathiform structure. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
