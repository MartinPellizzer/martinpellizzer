Saussurea japonica var. pteroclada

Saussurea japonica var. pteroclada, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow or Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
