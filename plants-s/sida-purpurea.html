Sida purpurea

Sida purpurea, commonly known as purpleleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features hairy, alternate leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its capsules are hairy and contain numerous tiny seeds.

Sida purpurea is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
