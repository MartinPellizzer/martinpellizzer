Stevia pereyrae

Stevia pereyrae, commonly known as the Peruvian stevia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its unique properties, it has gained interest for both culinary and medicinal uses.
