Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. flexuosa

Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible meadow spiraea, is a flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a dark green color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
