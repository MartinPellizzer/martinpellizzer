Salix × violacea

Salix × violacea, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive, drooping appearance. The plant produces catkins as its primary floral structure, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a rough texture with a dark green upper surface.

Native to parts of Asia and Europe, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and seasonal beauty.
