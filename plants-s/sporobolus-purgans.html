Sporobolus purgans

Sporobolus purgans, commonly known as the prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open fields.

Sporobolus purgans is adapted to withstand drought and is a valuable component of native grassland ecosystems.
