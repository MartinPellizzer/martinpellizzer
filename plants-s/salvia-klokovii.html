Salvia klokovii

Salvia klokovii, commonly known as the Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue-lilac flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia klokovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
