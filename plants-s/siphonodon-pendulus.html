Siphonodon pendulus

Siphonodon pendulus, commonly known as the hanging siphon, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous stems that droop gracefully from its base, often forming a cascading effect. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Siphonodon pendulus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its visual elegance in botanical gardens.
