Syngonium sparreorum

Syngonium sparreorum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that vary in color from bright green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. Syngonium sparreorum thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
