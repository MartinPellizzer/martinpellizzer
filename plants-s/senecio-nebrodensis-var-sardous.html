Senecio nebrodensis var. sardous

Senecio nebrodensis var. sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
