Syzygium johnsonii

Syzygium johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
