Solanum tetrachondrum var. subintegrum

Solanum tetrachondrum var. subintegrum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in umbels, and the plant produces small, globose berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
