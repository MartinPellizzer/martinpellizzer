Saxifraga lingulata lantoscana

Saxifraga lingulata lantoscana, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and linear, with small, white or pinkish flowers clustered at the ends of its stems. It is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and is often used in rock gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
