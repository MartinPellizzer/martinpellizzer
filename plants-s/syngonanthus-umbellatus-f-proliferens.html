Syngonanthus umbellatus f. proliferens

Syngonanthus umbellatus f. proliferens, commonly known as the spreading umbrella plant, is a unique variant of the native Hawaiian plant Syngonanthus umbellatus.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and the presence of numerous small, white, umbellate inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of Hawaiian rainforests. It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This fern-like plant is notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to the structural complexity of its habitat.
