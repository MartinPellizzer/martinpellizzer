Sychnosepalum sagotianum

Sychnosepalum sagotianum, commonly known as the Sagot's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, pale pink to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are highly specialized, with intricate structures that facilitate pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction and its rarity, Sychnosepalum sagotianum is considered critically endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
