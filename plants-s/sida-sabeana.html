Sida sabeana

Sida sabeana, commonly known as desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Sida sabeana is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots and the ability to store water in its stems.
