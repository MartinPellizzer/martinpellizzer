Salvia stenophylla var. subintegra

Salvia stenophylla var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a silvery underside, and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
