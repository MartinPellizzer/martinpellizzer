Solanum polytrichum var. longifolium

Solanum polytrichum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
