Sanguisorba minor subsp. polygama

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

polygama, commonly known as redroot or bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Sanguisorba minor subsp.

polygama is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
