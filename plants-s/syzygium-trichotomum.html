Syzygium trichotomum

Syzygium trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and small, edible, reddish berries. Its leaves are glossy and alternate, while its bark is smooth and pale.

Syzygium trichotomum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic essential oils.
