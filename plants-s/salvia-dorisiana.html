Salvia dorisiana

Salvia dorisiana, commonly known as the Doris's sage, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia dorisiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
