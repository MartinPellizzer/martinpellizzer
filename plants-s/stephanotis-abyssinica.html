Stephanotis abyssinica

Stephanotis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy green surface and a delicate, trailing growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
