Solanum verbascifolium f. eriocarpum

Solanum verbascifolium f. eriocarpum, commonly known as the woollyleaf nightshade, is a variant of the species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and somewhat rare plant within the Solanaceae family.
