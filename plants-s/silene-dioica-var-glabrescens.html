Silene dioica var. glabrescens

Silene dioica var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which become less prominent as the plant matures.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
