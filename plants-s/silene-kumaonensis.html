Silene kumaonensis

Silene kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its environment.

Silene kumaonensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
