Solanum storkii

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spiny fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and they appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Solanum storkii is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
