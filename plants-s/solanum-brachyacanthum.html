Solanum brachyacanthum

Solanum brachyacanthum, commonly known as the short-armed nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and China, and typically grows in forested or moist environments. The plant features small, green leaves with hairy undersides and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.

Solanum brachyacanthum is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
