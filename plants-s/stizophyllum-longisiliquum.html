Stizophyllum longisiliquum

Stizophyllum longisiliquum, commonly known as the long-siliquous stizophyllum, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender siliqua fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has small, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique fruit structure and ecological adaptations.
