Selinum aromaticum

Selinum aromaticum, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, umbrella-shaped flower clusters.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that develop into slender, seed-containing fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Sweet cicely is often cultivated for its delicate, anise-like flavor, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and herbal practices.
