Scutellaria elliptica var. hirsuta

Scutellaria elliptica var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy elliptic skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
