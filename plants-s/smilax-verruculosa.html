Smilax verruculosa

Smilax verruculosa, commonly known as the rough smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves that are typically dark green and hairy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

Smilax verruculosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
