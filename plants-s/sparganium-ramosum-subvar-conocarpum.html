Sparganium ramosum subvar. conocarpum

Sparganium ramosum subvar. conocarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bur reed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, branching stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and strap-shaped, often floating on the water surface.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in aquatic environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
