Silene dioica var. smithii

Silene dioica var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
