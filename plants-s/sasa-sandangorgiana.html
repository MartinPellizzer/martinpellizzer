Sasa sandangorgiana

Sasa sandangorgiana, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the Philippines.

It is a clump-forming perennial plant that grows in dense thickets, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Sasa sandangorgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
