Solanum tuberosum var. flavescens

Solanum tuberosum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato characterized by its yellow or golden skin and flesh.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller than standard potatoes and have a distinct, mild flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible tubers.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and adaptability to various growing conditions.
