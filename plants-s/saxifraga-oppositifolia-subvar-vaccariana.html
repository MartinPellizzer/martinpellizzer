Saxifraga oppositifolia subvar. vaccariana

Saxifraga oppositifolia subvar. vaccariana, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This subvariety is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in extreme climates.
