Salvia unguella

Salvia unguella, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia unguella is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought-resistant properties.
