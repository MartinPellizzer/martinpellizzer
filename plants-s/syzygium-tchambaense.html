Syzygium tchambaense

Syzygium tchambaense, commonly known as the African cherry or tchamba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C. It is valued for its ornamental beauty, medicinal properties, and use in traditional medicine.

The tree is also an important source of timber and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
