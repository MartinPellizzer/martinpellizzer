Selaginella panchghaniana

Selaginella panchghaniana, commonly known as the Panchghani spike moss, is a small, evergreen plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves arranged in spiral patterns around the stems. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It exhibits a unique ability to survive in low-light conditions and has adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Selaginella panchghaniana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
