Sisymbrium mollissimum var. glaberrimum

Sisymbrium mollissimum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
