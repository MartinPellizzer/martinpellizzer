Serapias × duffortii

Serapias × duffortii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias nemorensis and Serapias auricoma.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Serapias × duffortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
