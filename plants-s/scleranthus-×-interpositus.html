Scleranthus × interpositus

Scleranthus × interpositus, commonly known as the hybrid meadow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations compared to its parent species.
