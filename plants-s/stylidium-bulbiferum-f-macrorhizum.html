Stylidium bulbiferum f. macrorhizum

Stylidium bulbiferum f. macrorhizum, commonly known as the large-rooted burrgrass, is a unique variant of the Stylium genus native to Australia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, seed-bearing structures that resemble small bulbs or seed pods. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation.
