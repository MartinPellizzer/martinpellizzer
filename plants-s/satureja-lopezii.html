Satureja lopezii

Satureja lopezii, commonly known as Lopez's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, fragrant leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The flowers, which are pink to purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
