Senecio tenuifolius var. crucifolius

Senecio tenuifolius var. crucifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
