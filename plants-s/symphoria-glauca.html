Symphoria glauca

Symphoria glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its striking bluish-gray foliage. The leaves are needle-like, short, and tightly packed, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
