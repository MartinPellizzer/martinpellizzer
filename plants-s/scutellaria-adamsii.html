Scutellaria adamsii

Scutellaria adamsii, commonly known as Adams' blueflag, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Adams' blueflag is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
