Sophora sumatrana

Sophora sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Sophora, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain flat, hard seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
