Salsola luederitzensis

Salsola luederitzensis, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Salsola luederitzensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
