Saxifraga × trifurcatoides

Saxifraga × trifurcatoides, commonly known as the three-lobed saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is a perennial species that is valued in rock gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
