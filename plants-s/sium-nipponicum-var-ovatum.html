Sium nipponicum var. ovatum

Sium nipponicum var. ovatum, commonly known as Japanese water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, hollow stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, with white petals that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
