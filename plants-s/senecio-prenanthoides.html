Senecio prenanthoides

Senecio prenanthoides, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring.

Senecio prenanthoides is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
