Stuckenia chakassiensis

Stuckenia chakassiensis, commonly known as the Chakassian waterplantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, and it produces small, greenish flowers that emerge from the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can influence local aquatic ecosystems.

Stuckenia chakassiensis plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
