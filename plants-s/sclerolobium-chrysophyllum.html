Sclerolobium chrysophyllum

Sclerolobium chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a dense, spreading crown and thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry conditions. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is the national floral emblem of Australia.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
