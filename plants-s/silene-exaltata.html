Silene exaltata

Silene exaltata, commonly known as the "mountain catchfly," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Silene exaltata is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
