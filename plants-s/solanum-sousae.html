Solanum sousae

Solanum sousae, commonly known as the Socotra star gooseberry, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped yellow fruits. The plant has small, dark green leaves and produces flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Solanum sousae is adapted to the arid and harsh conditions of its native habitat, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
