Sechium peruvianum

Sechium peruvianum, commonly known as the Cucurbita maxima, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vining plant that produces large, green, and often lobed leaves. The plant bears yellow flowers that give way to orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is typically eaten raw or cooked, and it is valued for its nutritional content.

The plant is native to South America and is cultivated in various tropical and subtropical regions.
