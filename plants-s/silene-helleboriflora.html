Silene helleboriflora

Silene helleboriflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
