Sicyos microcarpus var. trilobata

Sicyos microcarpus var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed burr gourd, is a variant of the burr gourd species native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, angular stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, three-lobed fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust, vining growth habit.

The varietal name *trilobata* refers to the distinctive three-lobed shape of its fruit.
