Spiranthes decipiens

Spiranthes decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive spire orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its flowers are small and white, often with a faint greenish tinge, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The deceptive spire orchid is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its unique floral structure that resembles a spire.
