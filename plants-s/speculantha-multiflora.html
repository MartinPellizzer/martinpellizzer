Speculantha multiflora

Speculantha multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered speculantha, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
