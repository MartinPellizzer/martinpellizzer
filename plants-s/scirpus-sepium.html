Scirpus sepium

Scirpus sepium, commonly known as bulrush, is a tall, aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshes.

It has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thick, woolly layer of hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and usually have a distinctive white midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Scirpus sepium plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
