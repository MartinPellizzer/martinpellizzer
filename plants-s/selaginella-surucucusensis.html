Selaginella surucucusensis

Selaginella surucucusensis, commonly known as the Surucucus spikemoss, is a rare and endangered vascular plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its diminutive, spiky leaves and a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its conservation status highlights the importance of protecting its habitat from deforestation and habitat degradation.
