Socotora visciformis

Socotora visciformis, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk that often appears almost tree-like. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible dates, which are a significant food source in the region. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ecological and cultural importance, it is also considered a vulnerable species.
