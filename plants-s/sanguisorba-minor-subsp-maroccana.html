Sanguisorba minor subsp. maroccana

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan burnet, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It features upright, branched stems that are covered in dense, silky grayish-white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
