Scaphochlamys argentea

Scaphochlamys argentea, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of silvery, scale-like hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

The silver shield fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
