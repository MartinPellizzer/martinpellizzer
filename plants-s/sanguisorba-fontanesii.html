Sanguisorba fontanesii

Sanguisorba fontanesii, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Sanguisorba fontanesii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
