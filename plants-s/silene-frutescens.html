Silene frutescens

Silene frutescens, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats including meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, and it has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Silene frutescens is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
