Stevia hintonii

Stevia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has gained interest in both botanical research and alternative medicine.
