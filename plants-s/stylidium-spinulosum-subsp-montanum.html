Stylidium spinulosum subsp. montanum

Stylidium spinulosum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain stickseed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
