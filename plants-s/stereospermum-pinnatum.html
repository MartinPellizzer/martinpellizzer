Stereospermum pinnatum

Stereospermum pinnatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a reddish hue. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange or red fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

The wood of Stereospermum pinnatum is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal.
