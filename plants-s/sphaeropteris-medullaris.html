Sphaeropteris medullaris

Sphaeropteris medullaris, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant's trunk is composed of a dense, spongy tissue that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Native to Southeast Asia, it is often found in tropical rainforests and coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
