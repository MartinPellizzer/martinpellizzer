Sievekingia cristata

Sievekingia cristata, commonly known as the cristate sieveplant, is a unique member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Native to South Africa, it thrives in arid and rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
