Scoria ovata

Scoria ovata, commonly known as the oval scoria, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped, and often hollow stems.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is typically found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant's foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Scoria ovata is known for its ability to retain water, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
