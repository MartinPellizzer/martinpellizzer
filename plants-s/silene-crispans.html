Silene crispans

Silene crispans, commonly known as the crinkled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are crinkled and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene crispans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
