Senecio galeottii

Senecio galeottii, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and succulent-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio galeottii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
