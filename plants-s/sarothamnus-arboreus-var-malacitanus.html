Sarothamnus arboreus var. malacitanus

Sarothamnus arboreus var. malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan rosemary, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to coastal and arid climates.
