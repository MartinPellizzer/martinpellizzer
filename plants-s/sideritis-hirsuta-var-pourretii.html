Sideritis hirsuta var. pourretii

Sideritis hirsuta var. pourretii, commonly known as the rough hairy ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native environment.
