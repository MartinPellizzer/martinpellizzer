Sesleria hungarica

Sesleria hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It produces delicate white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer.

Sesleria hungarica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
