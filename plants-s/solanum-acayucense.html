Solanum acayucense

Solanum acayucense, commonly known as the Acayucense nightshade, is a species native to parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem.

Solanum acayucense is part of the nightshade family, which includes many plants with both culinary and medicinal uses.
