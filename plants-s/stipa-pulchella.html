Stipa pulchella

Stipa pulchella, commonly known as the purplegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced in the autumn.

Stipa pulchella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
