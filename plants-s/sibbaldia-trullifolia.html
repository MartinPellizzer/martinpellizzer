Sibbaldia trullifolia

Sibbaldia trullifolia, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and northern China.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Sibbaldia trullifolia is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions with high elevations.
