Scabiosa atropurpurea var. minor

Scabiosa atropurpurea var. minor, commonly known as lesser scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, often growing in clumps or spreading across the ground. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and as a nectar source for pollinators.
