Selinum podolicum

Selinum podolicum, commonly known as the Polish chervil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. Selinum podolicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
