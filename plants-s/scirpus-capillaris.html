Scirpus capillaris

Scirpus capillaris, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus capillaris plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
