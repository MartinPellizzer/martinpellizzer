Saussurea caerulea

Saussurea caerulea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Saussurea caerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
