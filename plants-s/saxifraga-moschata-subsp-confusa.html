Saxifraga moschata subsp. confusa

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically features rosettes of glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging garden conditions.
