Senecio correvonianus

Senecio correvonianus, commonly known as the Correvon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio correvonianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
