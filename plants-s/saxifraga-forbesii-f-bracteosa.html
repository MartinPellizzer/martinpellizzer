Saxifraga forbesii f. bracteosa

Saxifraga forbesii f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and its ability to add a touch of delicate beauty to high-altitude landscapes.
