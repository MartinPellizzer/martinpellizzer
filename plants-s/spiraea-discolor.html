Spiraea discolor

Spiraea discolor, commonly known as the whitebeard spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Spiraea discolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
