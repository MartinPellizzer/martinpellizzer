Solanum atriplicifolium var. minus

Solanum atriplicifolium var. minus, commonly known as the lesser Mexican nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its chemical composition, which includes various alkaloids and glycosides.
