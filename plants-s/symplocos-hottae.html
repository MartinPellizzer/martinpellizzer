Symplocos hottae

Symplocos hottae, commonly known as the Hotta's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish wax. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. Symplocos hottae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
