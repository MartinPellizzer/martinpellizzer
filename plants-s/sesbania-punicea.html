Sesbania punicea

Sesbania punicea, commonly known as the crimson sesban, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it visually distinctive. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Sesbania punicea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
