Scirpus loefgrenii

Scirpus loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, playing a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus loefgrenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
