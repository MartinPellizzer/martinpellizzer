Senecio croaticus

Senecio croaticus, commonly known as the Croatian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
