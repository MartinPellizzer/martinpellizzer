Solanum maranguense

Solanum maranguense, commonly known as the Maranguense nightshade, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.
