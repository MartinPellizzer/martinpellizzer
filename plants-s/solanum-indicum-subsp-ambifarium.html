Solanum indicum subsp. ambifarium

Solanum indicum subsp.

ambifarium, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
