Sabina recurva var. densa

Sabina recurva var. densa, commonly known as the dense juniper, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving the plant a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces blue-gray berries that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and winter interest.
