Sonchus tenerrimus subvar. dianae

Sonchus tenerrimus subvar. dianae, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
