Siparuna thea

Siparuna thea, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Siparuna thea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a shade tree in urban landscapes.
