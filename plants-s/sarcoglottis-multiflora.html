Sarcoglottis multiflora

Sarcoglottis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered sarcoglottis, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish flowers that are typically borne in spadices. The plant produces berries that are yellow when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
