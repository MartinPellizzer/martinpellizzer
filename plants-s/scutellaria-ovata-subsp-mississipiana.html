Scutellaria ovata subsp. mississipiana

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

mississipiana, commonly known as the Mississippi skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, two-lipped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
