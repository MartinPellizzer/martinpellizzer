Salix kungmuensis

Salix kungmuensis, commonly known as the Kungmu willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
