Saxifraga lingulata subsp. lantoscana

Saxifraga lingulata subsp.

lantoscana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
