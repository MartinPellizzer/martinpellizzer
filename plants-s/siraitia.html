Siraitia

Siraitia, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, round, orange-colored fruits that can weigh up to several hundred pounds. The plant has large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are cultivated for their size and are often used in culinary and decorative contexts.

The plant requires a warm climate and well-drained soil to thrive.
