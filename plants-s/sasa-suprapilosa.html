Sasa suprapilosa

Sasa suprapilosa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and is often found in cool, temperate regions.

Sasa suprapilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
