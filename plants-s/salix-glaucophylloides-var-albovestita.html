Salix glaucophylloides var. albovestita

Salix glaucophylloides var. albovestita, commonly known as the white-haired willow, is a variant of the glaucophylloides willow species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescent leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
