Sisymbrium cartilagineum

Sisymbrium cartilagineum, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
