Stevia gratioloides

Stevia gratioloides, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweet herb, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested for use in herbal teas and as a sugar substitute.

Stevia gratioloides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
