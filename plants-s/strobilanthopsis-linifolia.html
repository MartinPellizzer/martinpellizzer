Strobilanthopsis linifolia

Strobilanthopsis linifolia, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Strobilanthopsis linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
