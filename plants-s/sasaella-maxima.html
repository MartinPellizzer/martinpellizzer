Sasaella maxima

Sasaella maxima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense clumps.

Sasaella maxima is valued for its structural strength and has been used in traditional construction and crafts in its native habitat.
