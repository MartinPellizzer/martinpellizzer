Senecio queenslandicus

Senecio queenslandicus, commonly known as the Queensland groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, often succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
