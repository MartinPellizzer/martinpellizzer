Sageretia ramosa

Sageretia ramosa, commonly known as the slender sageretia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Sageretia ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
