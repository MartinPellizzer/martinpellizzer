Spiranthes pyramidalis

Spiranthes pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it the common name "spiral" or "spike-rush.".

Spiranthes pyramidalis is often found in open, sunny habitats and is an important species for pollinators such as bees and moths.
