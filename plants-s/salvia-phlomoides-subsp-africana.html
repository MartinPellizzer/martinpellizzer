Salvia phlomoides subsp. africana

Salvia phlomoides subsp.

africana, commonly known as African sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, and it produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
