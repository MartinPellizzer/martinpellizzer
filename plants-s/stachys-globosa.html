Stachys globosa

Stachys globosa, commonly known as globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Stachys globosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
