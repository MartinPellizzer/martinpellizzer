Silene graminifolia f. setifolia

Silene graminifolia f. setifolia, commonly known as the slender stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, thread-like stems and small, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. This ferny appearance gives it a distinctive, delicate look, making it a notable species in its native range.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
