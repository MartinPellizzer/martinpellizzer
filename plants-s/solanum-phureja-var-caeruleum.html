Solanum phureja var. caeruleum

Solanum phureja var. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue potato or purple potato, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces tuberous roots that are typically blue or purple in color, which are edible when properly prepared. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other potato species.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both agriculture and horticulture.
