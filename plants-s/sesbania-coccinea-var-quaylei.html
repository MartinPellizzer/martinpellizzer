Sesbania coccinea var. quaylei

Sesbania coccinea var. quaylei, commonly known as the red sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
