Solanum tuberosum f. wila-imilla

Solanum tuberosum f. wila-imilla, commonly known as the Wila-Imilla potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes a distinct coloration and texture. This variety is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it has been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, such as alternate leaves and a solanum-like flower structure.

Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance and yield.
