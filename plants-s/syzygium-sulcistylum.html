Syzygium sulcistylum

Syzygium sulcistylum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits. Its bark is often rugged and may have a reddish or brownish color.

Syzygium sulcistylum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
