Scorzonera sardoa

Scorzonera sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated corolla, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera sardoa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
