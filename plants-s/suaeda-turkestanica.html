Suaeda turkestanica

Suaeda turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sea-blush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and saline environments in Central Asia.

It thrives in coastal and inland salt marshes, demonstrating a remarkable ability to tolerate high salinity levels. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds adapted for dispersal in dry conditions.

Suaeda turkestanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
