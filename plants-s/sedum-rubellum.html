Sedum rubellum

Sedum rubellum, commonly known as the red sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It also serves as a food source for certain pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
