Solidago carnosa

Solidago carnosa, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Solidago carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
