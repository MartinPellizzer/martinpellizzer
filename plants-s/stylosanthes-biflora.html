Stylosanthes biflora

Stylosanthes biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage plant.

Stylosanthes biflora has deep roots that help in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
