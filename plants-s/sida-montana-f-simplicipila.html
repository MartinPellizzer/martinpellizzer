Sida montana f. simplicipila

Sida montana f. simplicipila, commonly known as the smoothleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
