Scilla longipes

Scilla longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed scilla, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky habitats and coastal areas. The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Scilla longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
