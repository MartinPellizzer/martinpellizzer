Salvia orientalis

Salvia orientalis, commonly known as oriental sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
