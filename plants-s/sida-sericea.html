Sida sericea

Sida sericea, commonly known as silky sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, silky foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida sericea thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
