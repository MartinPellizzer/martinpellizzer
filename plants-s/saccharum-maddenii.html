Saccharum maddenii

Saccharum maddenii, commonly known as the Madden grass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Saccharum maddenii is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the wetland ecosystems in its native range.
