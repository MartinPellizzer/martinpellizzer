Senecio homoplasticus var. tomentellus

Senecio homoplasticus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features dense, woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species in its native ecosystem.
