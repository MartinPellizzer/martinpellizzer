Sida rhombifolia var. riparia

Sida rhombifolia var. riparia, commonly known as river sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant has alternate, rhombic-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its botanical features include staminate and pistillate flowers on separate plants, making it monoecious.

It is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil along aquatic habitats.
