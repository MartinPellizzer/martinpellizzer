Salix melanopsis var. gracilipes

Salix melanopsis var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
