Senecio subulatus var. typicus

Senecio subulatus var. typicus, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, blue-gray leaves that form a low-growing mat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
