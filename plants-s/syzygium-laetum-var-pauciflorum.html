Syzygium laetum var. pauciflorum

Syzygium laetum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the rare-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its unique, clustered inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
