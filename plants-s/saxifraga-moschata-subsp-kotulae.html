Saxifraga moschata subsp. kotulae

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

kotulae, commonly known as the Kotula's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
