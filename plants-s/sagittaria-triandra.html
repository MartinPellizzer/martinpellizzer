Sagittaria triandra

Sagittaria triandra, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with narrow, ribbon-like leaves submerged beneath the water. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom above the water, typically in late summer. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Sagittaria triandra is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
