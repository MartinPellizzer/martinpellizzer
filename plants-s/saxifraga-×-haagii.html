Saxifraga × haagii

Saxifraga × haagii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full to partial sunlight. Its foliage consists of rounded, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga × haagii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
