Scoparia brasiliensis

Scoparia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Scoparia brasiliensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
