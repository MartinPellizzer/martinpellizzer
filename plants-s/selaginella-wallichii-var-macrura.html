Selaginella wallichii var. macrura

Selaginella wallichii var. macrura, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping stems that produce clusters of spirally arranged leaves. The leaves are typically elongated and have a distinct midrib, giving the plant a grass-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its specialized water retention structures.
