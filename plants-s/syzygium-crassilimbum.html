Syzygium crassilimbum

Syzygium crassilimbum, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticulture and forestry.
