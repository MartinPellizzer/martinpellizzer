Sasa hosomiana

Sasa hosomiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, often growing in mountainous regions. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Sasa hosomiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
