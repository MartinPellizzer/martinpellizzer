Savia elegans

Savia elegans, commonly known as the elegant spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking yellow-orange cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and dense foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
