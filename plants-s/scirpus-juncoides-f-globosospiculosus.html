Scirpus juncoides f. globosospiculosus

Scirpus juncoides f. globosospiculosus, commonly known as the globular bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, with the flowers clustered in compact, globular heads. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and ecological role in stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
