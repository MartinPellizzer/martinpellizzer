Scoparia excelsa

Scoparia excelsa, commonly known as the tall goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Scoparia excelsa is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
