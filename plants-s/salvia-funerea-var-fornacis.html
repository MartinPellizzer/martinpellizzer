Salvia funerea var. fornacis

Salvia funerea var. fornacis, commonly known as the black sage.

This plant is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically violet to blue-purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
