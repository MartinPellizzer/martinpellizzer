Salvia hajastana

Salvia hajastana, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia hajastana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
