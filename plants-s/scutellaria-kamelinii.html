Scutellaria kamelinii

Scutellaria kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scutellaria kamelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
