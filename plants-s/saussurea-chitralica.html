Saussurea chitralica

Saussurea chitralica, commonly known as the Chitral saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Karakoram ranges.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, yellowish-red flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters.
