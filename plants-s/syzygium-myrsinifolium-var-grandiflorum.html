Syzygium myrsinifolium var. grandiflorum

Syzygium myrsinifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The flowers are showy, white to pale pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
