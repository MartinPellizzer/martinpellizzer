Silene parryi

Silene parryi, commonly known as Parry's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as mountain slopes and open woodlands. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Silene parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to arid environments.
