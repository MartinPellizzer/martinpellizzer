Sempervivum boissieri

Sempervivum boissieri, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Sempervivum boissieri is also known for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions.
