Salix × jaccardii

Salix × jaccardii, commonly known as the Jaccard willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
