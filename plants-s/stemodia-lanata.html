Stemodia lanata

Stemodia lanata, commonly known as the woolly stemodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
