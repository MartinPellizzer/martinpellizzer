Silene colorata subsp. bipartita

Silene colorata subsp.

bipartita, commonly known as the two-colored catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral coloration, which may play a role in pollinator attraction.
