Solanum belizense

Solanum belizense, commonly known as the Belize nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum belizense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
