Statice grandiflora

Statice grandiflora, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. It is a hardy perennial that blooms from summer to early autumn.

Its ability to tolerate salt and drought makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and floral arrangements.
