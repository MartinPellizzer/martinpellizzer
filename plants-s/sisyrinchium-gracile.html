Sisyrinchium gracile

Sisyrinchium gracile, commonly known as slender blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early summer and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Sisyrinchium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
