Securidaca sulcata

Securidaca sulcata, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
