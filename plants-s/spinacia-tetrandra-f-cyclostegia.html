Spinacia tetrandra f. cyclostegia

Spinacia tetrandra f. cyclostegia, commonly known as the broad-leafed spinach, is a cultivated form of spinach characterized by its large, broad leaves.

It is a variant of the species Spinacia tetrandra, which is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamins. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include a short, upright stem and a rosette of broad, lobed leaves.
