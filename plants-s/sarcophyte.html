Sarcophyte

Sarcophyte, commonly known as the "sarcophyte" or "sarcophytes," is a term used in botany to describe certain types of plants that have a unique reproductive structure.

These plants typically produce a fleshy, often colorful fruit that encloses the seeds. The sarcophyte's fruit is usually thick and may contain a gelatinous substance that helps in seed dispersal. Some species of sarcophytes are found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The term is sometimes used interchangeably with other plant groups that share similar fruit characteristics.
