Solanum hirtellum

Solanum hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
