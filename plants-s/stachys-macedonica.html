Stachys macedonica

Stachys macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
