Selago glutinosa subsp. glutinosa

Selago glutinosa subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky wallaby tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its sticky, resinous sap, which gives it its common name.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both coastal and inland environments.
