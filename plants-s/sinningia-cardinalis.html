Sinningia cardinalis

Sinningia cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal gloxinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pink to red, with a contrasting white or yellow throat, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.

Sinningia cardinalis is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to care for in a greenhouse or indoor setting.
