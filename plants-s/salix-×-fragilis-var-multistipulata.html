Salix × fragilis var. multistipulata

Salix × fragilis var. multistipulata, commonly known as the weeping willow with multiple stipules, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate trees. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
