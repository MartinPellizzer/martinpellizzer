Sideritis erythrantha subsp. erythrantha

Sideritis erythrantha subsp.

erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwort, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
