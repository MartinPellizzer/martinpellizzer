Sloanea Papuana

Papuan sloanea, scientifically known as Sloanea papuana, is a rare and unique plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia. This evergreen shrub belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and faces significant threats from deforestation and climate change. Despite its ecological importance, little is known about its biological traits and reproductive habits. Conservation efforts are urgently needed to protect this rare species and preserve its natural habitat.
