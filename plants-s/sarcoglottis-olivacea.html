Sarcoglottis olivacea

Sarcoglottis olivacea, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and the Philippines. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces striking, pendulous flowers with a greenish-yellow hue. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments.

Sarcoglottis olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
