Solidago flexicaulis var. latifolia

Solidago flexicaulis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.
