Swertia nitida subsp. cusickii

Swertia nitida subsp.

cusickii, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
