Scirpus triqueter f. conglomeratus

Scirpus triqueter f. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit, which forms tall, cylindrical stems with narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
