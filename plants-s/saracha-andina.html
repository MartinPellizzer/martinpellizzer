Saracha andina

Saracha andina, commonly known as the Andean thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, contains seeds and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Saracha andina thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andes.
