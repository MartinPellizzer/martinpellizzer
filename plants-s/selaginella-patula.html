Selaginella patula

Selaginella patula, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, velvety foliage and a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions. Selaginella patula possesses specialized structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Its adaptability and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
