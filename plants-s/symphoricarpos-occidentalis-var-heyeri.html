Symphoricarpos occidentalis var. heyeri

Symphoricarpos occidentalis var. heyeri, commonly known as the western snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
