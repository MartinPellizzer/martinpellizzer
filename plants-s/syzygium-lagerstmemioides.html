Syzygium lagerstmemioides

Syzygium lagerstmemioides, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to apples, though they are botanically distinct. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cultures.
