Serratula coronata f. alpina

Serratula coronata f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine crownwort, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, upright stems that bear clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
