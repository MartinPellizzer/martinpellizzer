Stellaria crassifolia var. grandiflora

Stellaria crassifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved starwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
