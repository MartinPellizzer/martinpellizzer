Scleranthus capitulatus

Scleranthus capitulatus, commonly known as the clustered sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus capitulatus is characterized by its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
