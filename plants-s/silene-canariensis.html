Silene canariensis

Silene canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island pink, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene canariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
