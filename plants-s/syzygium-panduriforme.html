Syzygium panduriforme

Syzygium panduriforme, commonly known as the long-leaf apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, red or yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Syzygium panduriforme is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
