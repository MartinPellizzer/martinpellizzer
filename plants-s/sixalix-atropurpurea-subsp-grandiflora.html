Sixalix atropurpurea subsp. grandiflora

Sixalix atropurpurea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the purple-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
