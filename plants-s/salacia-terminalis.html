Salacia terminalis

Salacia terminalis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its bark is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Salacia terminalis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
