Solanum melongena var. serpentinum

Solanum melongena var. serpentinum, commonly known as the serpentini eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, serpentine-shaped fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces deep purple, glossy skin with a slightly bitter taste, making it a unique variety for culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its distinctive appearance and flavor, often used in local cuisines across its native regions.
