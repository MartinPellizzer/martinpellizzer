Sinapis mairei

Sinapis mairei, commonly known as the Maire's mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, triangular pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are edible and have a pungent, mustard-like flavor.

Sinapis mairei thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
