Syringa velutina var. venosa

Syringa velutina var. venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sweetwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, softly hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
