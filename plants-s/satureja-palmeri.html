Satureja palmeri

Satureja palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's savory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Satureja palmeri is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to arid conditions.
