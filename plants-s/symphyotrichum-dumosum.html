Symphyotrichum dumosum

Symphyotrichum dumosum, commonly known as the dwarf coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, producing pink to purple petals and a prominent yellow center.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting native pollinators.
