Salsola praemontana

Salsola praemontana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

Salsola praemontana plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
