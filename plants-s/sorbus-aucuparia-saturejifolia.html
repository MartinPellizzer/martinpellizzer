Sorbus aucuparia saturejifolia

Sorbus aucuparia saturejifolia, commonly known as the red mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often serrated and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
