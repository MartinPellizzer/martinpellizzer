Symplocos pendula var. hirtistylis

Symplocos pendula var. hirtistylis, commonly known as the hairy-styled flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, with elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are borne on long, drooping inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The variant is notable for its hairy stamens, which contribute to its specific name "hirtistylis.".
