Sebastiania stipulacea

Sebastiania stipulacea, commonly known as the wax tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are sometimes used as a natural dye due to their vibrant color.

Sebastiania stipulacea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
