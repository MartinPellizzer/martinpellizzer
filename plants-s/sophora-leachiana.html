Sophora leachiana

Sophora leachiana, commonly known as the blue sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Sophora leachiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
