Savia maculata

Savia maculata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that features thick, fleshy leaves with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
