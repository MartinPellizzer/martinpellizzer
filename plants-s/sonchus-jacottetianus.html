Sonchus jacottetianus

Sonchus jacottetianus, commonly known as the Jacottet's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bitter taste, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

It is considered a weed in some areas but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
