Solanum collinum

Solanum collinum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.

Solanum collinum is often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
