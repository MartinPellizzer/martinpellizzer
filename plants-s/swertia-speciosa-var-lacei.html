Swertia speciosa var. lacei

Swertia speciosa var. lacei, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
