Scilla speciosa

Scilla speciosa, commonly known as the showy squill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of many bulbous plants. Scilla speciosa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit, which adds color to gardens during the transition from winter to spring.
