Smilacina smithii

Smilacina smithii, commonly known as Smith's greenbriar, is a perennial climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that can climb and twine around other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy appearance.

Smilacina smithii is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
