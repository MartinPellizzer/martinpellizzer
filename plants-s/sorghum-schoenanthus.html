Sorghum schoenanthus

Sorghum schoenanthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces edible seeds that are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant has cultural significance in many traditional societies.
