Solanum nigrum var. genuinum

Solanum nigrum var. genuinum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical, black berries when mature. These berries contain toxic alkaloids and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, waste areas, and along roadsides.
