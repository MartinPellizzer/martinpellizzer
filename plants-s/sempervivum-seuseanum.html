Sempervivum seuseanum

Sempervivum seuseanum, commonly known as the Swiss mountain houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh mountain environments.
