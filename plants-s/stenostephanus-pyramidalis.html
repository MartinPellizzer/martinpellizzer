Stenostephanus pyramidalis

Stenostephanus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
