Syzygium crassiflorum

Syzygium crassiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Syzygium crassiflorum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
