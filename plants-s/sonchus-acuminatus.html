Sonchus acuminatus

Sonchus acuminatus, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that resemble those of other dandelion-like plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across North America. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

The species is sometimes considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
