Siolmatra brasiliensis

Siolmatra brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Siolmatra brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
