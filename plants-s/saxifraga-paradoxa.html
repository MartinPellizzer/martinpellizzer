Saxifraga paradoxa

Saxifraga paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of fleshy, waxy leaves that help conserve moisture in cold, harsh environments. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in clusters during the short growing season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga paradoxa is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and has a unique ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.
