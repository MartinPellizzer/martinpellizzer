Senecio extensus

Senecio extensus, commonly known as the spreading African daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio extensus is also noted for its ability to produce a large number of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
