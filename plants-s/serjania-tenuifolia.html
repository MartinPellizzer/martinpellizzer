Serjania tenuifolia

Serjania tenuifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or yellow serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, particularly at night. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Serjania tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
