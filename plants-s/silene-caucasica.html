Silene caucasica

Silene caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Silene caucasica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
