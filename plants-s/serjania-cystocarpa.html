Serjania cystocarpa

Serjania cystocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing hard, shiny seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
