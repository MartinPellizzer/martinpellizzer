Saussurea rupestris

Saussurea rupestris, commonly known as the rock saffron or desert saffron, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and yellow-orange ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Saussurea rupestris is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
