Solanum pentheri

Solanum pentheri, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum pentheri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
