Silene vulgaris

Silene vulgaris, commonly known as blushing grass or common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene vulgaris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is considered a herbaceous annual or biennial plant.
