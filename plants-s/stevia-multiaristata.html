Stevia multiaristata

Stevia multiaristata, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.

Stevia multiaristata is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to attract beneficial insects.
