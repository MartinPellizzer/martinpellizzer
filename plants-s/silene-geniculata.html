Silene geniculata

Silene geniculata, commonly known as the bent-stem catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silene geniculata produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.
