Serapias anthropophora

Serapias anthropophora, commonly known as the human-faced orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a prominent central lip that resembles a human face, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in open habitats.

Serapias anthropophora is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
