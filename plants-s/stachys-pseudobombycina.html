Stachys pseudobombycina

Stachys pseudobombycina, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Stachys pseudobombycina is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
