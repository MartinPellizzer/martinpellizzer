Silene fortunei var. bilobata

Silene fortunei var. bilobata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada silene, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, open habitats at high elevations. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

It is adapted to cold, dry conditions and plays a role in local pollination ecosystems.
