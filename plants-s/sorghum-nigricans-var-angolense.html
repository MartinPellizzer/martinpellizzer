Sorghum nigricans var. angolense

Sorghum nigricans var. angolense, commonly known as black millet, is a tropical grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black-colored grains, which are encased in a hard outer shell. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and the plant is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The botanical features of Sorghum nigricans var. angolense make it a resilient crop with potential for use in both food and industrial applications.
