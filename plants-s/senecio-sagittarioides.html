Senecio sagittarioides

Senecio sagittarioides, commonly known as the arrowhead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use.

Senecio sagittarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its distinctive foliage and delicate blooms.
