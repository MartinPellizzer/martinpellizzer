Smilax leucocarpa

Smilax leucocarpa, commonly known as white-fruited catbriar, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Smilax in the family Smilacaceae.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems and the presence of sharp, spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, yellow to orange in color, and contain several seeds.

Smilax leucocarpa is native to the southeastern United States and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
