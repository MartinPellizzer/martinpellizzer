Stachys ocymastrum var. rifana

Stachys ocymastrum var. rifana, commonly known as Rifan mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
