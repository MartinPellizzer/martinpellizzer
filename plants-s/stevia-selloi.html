Stevia selloi

Stevia selloi, commonly known as Peruvian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as a sugar substitute. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia selloi is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
