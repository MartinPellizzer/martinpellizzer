Sedum vietnamense

Sedum vietnamense, commonly known as Vietnamese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and star-shaped, usually appearing in shades of pink or white.

Sedum vietnamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
