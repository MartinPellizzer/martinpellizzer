Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp. alpina

Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine skullcap, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, two-lipped flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp.

alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
