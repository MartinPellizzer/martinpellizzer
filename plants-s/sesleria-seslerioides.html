Sesleria seslerioides

Sesleria seslerioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, with a reddish tinge at the base. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flower spikes during the late spring to early summer season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to add texture and color to garden settings.
