Shortia galacifolia

Shortia galacifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender stems. Its habitat typically includes moist, acidic soils in forest understories, and it thrives in shaded environments.

Shortia galacifolia is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
