Senecio nemorensis subsp. apuanus

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

apuanus, commonly known as the Apuan Alps groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
