Silene scaposa var. lobata

Silene scaposa var. lobata, commonly known as the lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive lobed base, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas.

The plant is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
