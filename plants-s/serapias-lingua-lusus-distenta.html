Serapias lingua lusus distenta

Serapias lingua lusus distenta, commonly known as the "tongue orchid," is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, tongue-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
