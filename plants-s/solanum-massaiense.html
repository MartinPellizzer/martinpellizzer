Solanum massaiense

Solanum massaiense, commonly known as the Massai tomato, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Kenya and Tanzania.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant has a strong, somewhat aromatic scent.
