Silene waltonii

Silene waltonii, commonly known as the Walton's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh habitat.

Silene waltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
