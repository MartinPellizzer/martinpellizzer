Senna siamea

Senna siamea, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 12 to 20 pairs of ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
