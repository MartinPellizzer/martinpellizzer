Subpilocereus margaritensis var. micranthus

Subpilocereus margaritensis var. micranthus, commonly known as the miniature night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems that form a compact, bushy plant. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and night-blooming flowers.
