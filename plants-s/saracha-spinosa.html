Saracha spinosa

Saracha spinosa, commonly known as the spiny saracha, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in harsh environmental conditions.

Saracha spinosa is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.
