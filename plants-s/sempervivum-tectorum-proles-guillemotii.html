Sempervivum tectorum proles guillemotii

Sempervivum tectorum proles guillemotii, commonly known as the Guillemotii houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a houseplant.
