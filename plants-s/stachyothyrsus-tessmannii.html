Stachyothyrsus tessmannii

Stachyothyrsus tessmannii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
