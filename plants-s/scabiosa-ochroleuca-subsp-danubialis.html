Scabiosa ochroleuca subsp. danubialis

Scabiosa ochroleuca subsp.

danubialis, commonly known as the Danube scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and riverbanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of silvery-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their five-petaled structure and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
