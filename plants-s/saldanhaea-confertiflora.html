Saldanhaea confertiflora

Saldanhaea confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered sandalwood, is a member of the Santalaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

Saldanhaea confertiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
