Salviastrum dolichanthum

Salviastrum dolichanthum, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Salviastrum dolichanthum is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it thrives in full sun.
