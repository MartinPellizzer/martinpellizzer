Salvia membranacea var. acuminata

Salvia membranacea var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
