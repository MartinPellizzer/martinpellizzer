Scirpus pollichii var. coriacea

Scirpus pollichii var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of flowering stems that rise from rhizomatous roots. The leaves are narrow, stiff, and covered with a rough, textured surface, contributing to its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
