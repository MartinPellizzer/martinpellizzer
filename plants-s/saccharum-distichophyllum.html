Saccharum distichophyllum

Saccharum distichophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sugarcane.

It is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is closely related to other sugarcane species and is used in some regions for its fibrous stalks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
