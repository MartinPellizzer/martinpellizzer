Sempervivum tectorum subsp. rupestre

Sempervivum tectorum subsp.

rupestre, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a succulent plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It features fleshy, upright leaves that are typically blue-green or silver in color, arranged in rosettes.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.
