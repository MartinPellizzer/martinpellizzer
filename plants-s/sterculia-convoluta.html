Sterculia convoluta

Sterculia convoluta, commonly known as the spiral tree or mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its spirally arranged leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
