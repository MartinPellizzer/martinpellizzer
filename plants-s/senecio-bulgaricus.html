Senecio bulgaricus

Senecio bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small yellow flowers that are surrounded by white or pale green ray florets.

Senecio bulgaricus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
