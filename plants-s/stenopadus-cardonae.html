Stenopadus cardonae

Stenopadus cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island dragon tree, is a unique plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense foliage. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Stenopadus cardonae is known for its slow growth and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
