Scutellaria kingiana

Scutellaria kingiana, commonly known as King's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Scutellaria kingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
