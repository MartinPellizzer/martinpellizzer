Swertia wilfordii f. albiflora

Swertia wilfordii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered swertia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
