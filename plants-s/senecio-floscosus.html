Senecio floscosus

Senecio floscosus, commonly known as the stickyweed or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple florets arranged in a disc-like structure, with bracts that are often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Senecio floscosus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
