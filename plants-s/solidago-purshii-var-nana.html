Solidago purshii var. nana

Solidago purshii var. nana, commonly known as dwarf goldenrod, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It features a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces small, yellow flower clusters in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
