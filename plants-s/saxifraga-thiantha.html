Saxifraga thiantha

Saxifraga thiantha, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Saxifraga thiantha is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
