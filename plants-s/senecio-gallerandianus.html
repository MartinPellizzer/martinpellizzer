Senecio gallerandianus

Senecio gallerandianus, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features dense, blue-gray foliage with a frost-like appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
