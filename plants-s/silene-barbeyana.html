Silene barbeyana

Silene barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Silene barbeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
