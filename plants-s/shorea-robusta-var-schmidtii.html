Shorea robusta var. schmidtii

Shorea robusta var. schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and thrives in humid, warm climates. It has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.
