Sapium guineense

Sapium guineense, commonly known as African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Sapium guineense is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
