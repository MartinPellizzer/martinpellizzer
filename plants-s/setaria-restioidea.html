Setaria restioidea

Setaria restioidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet, sandy soils along riverbanks and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and riparian ecosystems.
