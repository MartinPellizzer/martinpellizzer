Sinomenium acutum var. tomentosum

Sinomenium acutum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the tomentose Chinese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, opposite leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers in the spring, which are followed by long, twisting pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cascading floral displays.
