Senecio myrsinites

Senecio myrsinites, commonly known as the silverleaf sedum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, silver-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.

Senecio myrsinites prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
