Spiraea hypericifolia var. adiantifolia

Spiraea hypericifolia var. adiantifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
