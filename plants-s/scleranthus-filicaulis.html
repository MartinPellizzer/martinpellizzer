Scleranthus filicaulis

Scleranthus filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments.

Scleranthus filicaulis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
