Sedum hispanicum

Sedum hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically green and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Sedum hispanicum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
