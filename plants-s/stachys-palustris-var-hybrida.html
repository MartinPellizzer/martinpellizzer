Stachys palustris var. hybrida

Stachys palustris var. hybrida, commonly known as swamp betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features upright, square stems that are often hairy, and it produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
