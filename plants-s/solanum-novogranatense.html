Solanum novogranatense

Solanum novogranatense, commonly known as the Colombian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that mature to a bright red or yellow. It is often found in humid, tropical regions and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
