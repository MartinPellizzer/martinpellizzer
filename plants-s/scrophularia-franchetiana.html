Scrophularia franchetiana

Scrophularia franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese figwort, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scrophularia franchetiana is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
