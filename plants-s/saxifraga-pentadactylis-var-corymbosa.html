Saxifraga pentadactylis var. corymbosa

Saxifraga pentadactylis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme climates.
