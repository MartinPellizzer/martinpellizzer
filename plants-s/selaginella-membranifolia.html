Selaginella membranifolia

Selaginella membranifolia, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals around the stem. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Selaginella membranifolia exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on specialized leaves called microphylls.
