Syzygium jambosoides

Syzygium jambosoides, commonly known as the wild mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, purple fruits with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit's interior contains white, segmented arils that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
