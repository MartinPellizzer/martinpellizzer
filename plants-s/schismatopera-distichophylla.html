Schismatopera distichophylla

Schismatopera distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leafed fern, is a fossilized plant from the Devonian period.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dichotomous branching pattern, which resembles a modern fern. The plant's fronds are preserved in fine detail, showing intricate leaf structures. It is one of the earliest known plants to exhibit true leaves, indicating advanced vascular systems.

This species provides important insights into early plant evolution and the development of complex terrestrial ecosystems.
