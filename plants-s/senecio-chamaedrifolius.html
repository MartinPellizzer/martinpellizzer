Senecio chamaedrifolius

Senecio chamaedrifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, displaying vibrant yellow flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Senecio chamaedrifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
