Senecio douglasii var. sublanceolatus

Senecio douglasii var. sublanceolatus, commonly known as Douglas's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
