Stewartia serrata

Stewartia serrata, commonly known as the Japanese Stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring, followed by bright red, egg-shaped capsules that split open to release seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
