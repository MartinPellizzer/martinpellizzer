Scilla dedea

Scilla dedea, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Scilla dedea is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
