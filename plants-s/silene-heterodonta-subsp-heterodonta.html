Silene heterodonta subsp. heterodonta

Silene heterodonta subsp.

heterodonta, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including meadows and sandy soils, across parts of North America. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations and its ornamental value in gardens.
