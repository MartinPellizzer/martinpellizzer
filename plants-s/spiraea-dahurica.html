Spiraea dahurica

Spiraea dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features wiry, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea dahurica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
