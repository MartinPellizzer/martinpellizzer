Saxifraga × arendsii f. splendidissima

Saxifraga × arendsii f. splendidissima, commonly known as the splendid saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of bright pink or purple flowers in late summer. The leaves are typically rosette-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh growing conditions.
