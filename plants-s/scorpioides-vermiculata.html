Scorpioides vermiculata

Scorpioides vermiculata, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a silvery-blue hue due to a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
