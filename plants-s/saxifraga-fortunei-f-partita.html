Saxifraga fortunei f. partita

Saxifraga fortunei f. partita, commonly known as the Variegated Stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
