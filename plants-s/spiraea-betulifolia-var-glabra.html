Spiraea betulifolia var. glabra

Spiraea betulifolia var. glabra, commonly known as smooth silverleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
