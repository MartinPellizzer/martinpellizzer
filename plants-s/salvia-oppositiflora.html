Salvia oppositiflora

Salvia oppositiflora, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is often grown for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Salvia oppositiflora is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
