Senecio jacobaea subsp. nemorosus

Senecio jacobaea subsp.

nemorosus, commonly known as the woodland cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
