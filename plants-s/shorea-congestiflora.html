Shorea congestiflora

Shorea congestiflora, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange in color, and it produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Shorea congestiflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
