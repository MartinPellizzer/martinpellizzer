Solidago gigantea var. leiophylla

Solidago gigantea var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaved goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides in the eastern United States.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the fall season.
