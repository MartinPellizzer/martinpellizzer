Salix × russelliana

Salix × russelliana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant has slender, light green leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × russelliana is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
