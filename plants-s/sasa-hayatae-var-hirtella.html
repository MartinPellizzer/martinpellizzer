Sasa hayatae var. hirtella

Sasa hayatae var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa hayatae species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant's leaves are covered with fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
