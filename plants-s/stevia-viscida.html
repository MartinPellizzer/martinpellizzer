Stevia viscida

Stevia viscida, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically used in traditional medicine and as a sugar substitute.

Stevia viscida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
