Saxifraga macrostigma var. gracillima

Saxifraga macrostigma var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces delicate white or pink flowers in late spring. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, known for its hardy and often ornamental species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many parts of its range.
