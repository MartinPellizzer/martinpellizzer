Senecio tergopurpureus

Senecio tergopurpureus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its stems are distinctly purple-hued, and the leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
