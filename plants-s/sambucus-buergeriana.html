Sambucus buergeriana

Sambucus buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Sambucus buergeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
