Senecio ambatensis

Senecio ambatensis, commonly known as the Ambatensis groundsels, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio ambatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
