Smilax regelii f. albida

Smilax regelii f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its stems are armed with sharp spines, making it a distinctive and sometimes challenging plant to handle.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
