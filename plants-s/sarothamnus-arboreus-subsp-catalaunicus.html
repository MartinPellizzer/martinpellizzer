Sarothamnus arboreus subsp. catalaunicus

Sarothamnus arboreus subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalaunic thyme, is a shrub native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions of Spain and Italy. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-blue hue, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
