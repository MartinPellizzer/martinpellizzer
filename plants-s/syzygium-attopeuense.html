Syzygium attopeuense

Syzygium attopeuense, commonly known as the Attopeu persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Laos and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is an oval, fleshy berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and tangy flavor, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
