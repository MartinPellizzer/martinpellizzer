Salvia blancoana var. lagascana

Salvia blancoana var. lagascana, commonly known as the Lagascana sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its striking violet to purple flowers bloom in dense whorls along the stems. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
