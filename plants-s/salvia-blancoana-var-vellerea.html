Salvia blancoana var. vellerea

Salvia blancoana var. vellerea, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its velvety, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it an attractive addition to native gardens.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
