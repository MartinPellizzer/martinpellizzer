Symplocos hydrophila

Symplocos hydrophila, commonly known as the water symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall.

Symplocos hydrophila is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
