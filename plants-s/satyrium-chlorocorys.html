Satyrium chlorocorys

Satyrium chlorocorys, commonly known as the green-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. This species is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
