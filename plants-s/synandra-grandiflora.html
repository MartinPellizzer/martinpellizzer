Synandra grandiflora

Synandra grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora sandera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Synandra grandiflora is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
