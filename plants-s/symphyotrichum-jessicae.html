Symphyotrichum jessicae

Symphyotrichum jessicae, commonly known as Jessica's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple-blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Jessica's aster is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
