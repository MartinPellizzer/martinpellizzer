Senecio truxillensis

Senecio truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in high-altitude regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

Senecio truxillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
