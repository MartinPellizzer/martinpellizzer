Saussurea afghana

Saussurea afghana, commonly known as the Afghan gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions.

Saussurea afghana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
