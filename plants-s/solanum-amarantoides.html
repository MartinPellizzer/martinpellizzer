Solanum amarantoides

Solanum amarantoides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, bean-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive organs, and it produces edible beans that are often cultivated for their nutritional value.

Solanum amarantoides thrives in warm climates and is commonly grown in gardens and agricultural settings.
