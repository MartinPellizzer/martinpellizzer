Sabulina austriaca

Sabulina austriaca, commonly known as Austrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes or disturbed areas. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 20 centimeters.

Sabulina austriaca is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
