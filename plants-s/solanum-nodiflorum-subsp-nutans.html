Solanum nodiflorum subsp. nutans

Solanum nodiflorum subsp.

nutans, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that open at night.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically white or pale green with a faintly fragrant scent. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens to a red color, containing toxic alkaloids.
