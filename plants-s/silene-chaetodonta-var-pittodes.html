Silene chaetodonta var. pittodes

Silene chaetodonta var. pittodes, commonly known as the Pittosporum silene, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland or streamside habitats. It has linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is often noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
