Sideritis hyssopifolia var. hirta

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. hirta, commonly known as hairy common sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception, containing compounds with potential health benefits.
