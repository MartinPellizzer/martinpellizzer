Sedum mukojimense

Sedum mukojimense, commonly known as the Mukojima stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
