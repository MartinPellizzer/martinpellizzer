Sasa obtecta

Sasa obtecta, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with a whitish, waxy bloom. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its resilience and ability to form dense thickets in forest understories.
