Scaphochlamys anomala

Scaphochlamys anomala, commonly known as the slender-sheath orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Scaphochlamys anomala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
