Silene viscosissima

Silene viscosissima, commonly known as sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Silene viscosissima is an annual plant that plays a role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
