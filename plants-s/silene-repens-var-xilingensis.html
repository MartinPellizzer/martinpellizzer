Silene repens var. xilingensis

Silene repens var. xilingensis, commonly known as the Xilingensis dogwood, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
