Symphyotrichum racemosum var. subdumosum

Symphyotrichum racemosum var. subdumosum, commonly known as the dwarf blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in late summer to early fall, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.
