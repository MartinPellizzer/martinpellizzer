Schinus niara

Schinus niara, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red berries. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy flowers. It can thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
