Salvia aegyptiaca var. intermedia

Salvia aegyptiaca var. intermedia, commonly known as Egyptian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage and produces striking violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms.
