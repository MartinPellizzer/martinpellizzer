Sonchus eriopus

Sonchus eriopus, commonly known as the hairy leaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and meadows. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is considered an invasive species in some parts of the world.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for agricultural areas.
