Specklinia inversa

Specklinia inversa, commonly known as the inverted speckled fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, upward-curving fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has delicate, finely divided leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps to reduce water loss. It prefers shady, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and scientific study.
