Stachys lagascae

Stachys lagascae, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys lagascae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.
