Sclerolobium froesii

Sclerolobium froesii, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
