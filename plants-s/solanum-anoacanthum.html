Solanum anoacanthum

Solanum anoacanthum, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
