Syzygium somae

Syzygium somae, commonly known as the Somalian apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium somae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes in its native habitat.
