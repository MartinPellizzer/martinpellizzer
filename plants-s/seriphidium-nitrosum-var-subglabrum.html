Seriphidium nitrosum var. subglabrum

Seriphidium nitrosum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf wormwood, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The variety subglabrum is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other varieties.
