Sunaptea heteroptera

Sunaptea heteroptera, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its ecological benefits to local wildlife.
