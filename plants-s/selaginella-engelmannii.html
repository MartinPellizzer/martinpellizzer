Selaginella engelmannii

Selaginella engelmannii, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with erect, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella engelmannii is often used in horticulture due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
