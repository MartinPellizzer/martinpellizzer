Stemonoporus acuminatus

Stemonoporus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
