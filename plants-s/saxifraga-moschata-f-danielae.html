Saxifraga moschata f. danielae

Saxifraga moschata f. danielae, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly toothed margin and a glossy surface.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded garden settings.
