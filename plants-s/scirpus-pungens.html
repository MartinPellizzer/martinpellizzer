Scirpus pungens

Scirpus pungens, commonly known as spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in sharp, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, pond margins, and along streams, thriving in wet or waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually green or reddish in color.

Scirpus pungens plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
