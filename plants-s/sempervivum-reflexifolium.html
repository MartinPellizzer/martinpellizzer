Sempervivum reflexifolium

Sempervivum reflexifolium, commonly known as the crested houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps and Pyrenees.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, blue-green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces pink to red flowers on tall stems during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it a reputation as a hardy and resilient species.
