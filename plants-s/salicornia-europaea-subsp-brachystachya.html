Salicornia europaea subsp. brachystachya

Salicornia europaea subsp.

brachystachya, commonly known as sea bean or dwarf glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments. It is a succulent annual that grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other brackish habitats.

The plant has jointed, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of salt crystals. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish and are arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a vegetable.
