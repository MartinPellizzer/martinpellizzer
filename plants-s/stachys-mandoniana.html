Stachys mandoniana

Stachys mandoniana, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stachys mandoniana is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
