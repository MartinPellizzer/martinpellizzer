Symplocos dusenii

Symplocos dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Symplocos dusenii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
