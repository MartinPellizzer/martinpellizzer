Sporobolus × townsendii

Sporobolus × townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silky foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife in its native range.
