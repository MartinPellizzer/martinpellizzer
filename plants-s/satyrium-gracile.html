Satyrium gracile

Satyrium gracile, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender flower stem.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
