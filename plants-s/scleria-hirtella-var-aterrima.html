Scleria hirtella var. aterrima

Scleria hirtella var. aterrima, commonly known as the dark-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dark green, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it helps stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
