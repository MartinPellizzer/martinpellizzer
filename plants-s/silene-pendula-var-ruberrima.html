Silene pendula var. ruberrima

Silene pendula var. ruberrima, commonly known as the red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems that display a distinctive reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Silene pendula var. ruberrima is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
