Salix × paxii

Salix × paxii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and a grayish-green underside.

Salix × paxii is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
