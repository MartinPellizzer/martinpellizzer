Stachys circinata subsp. numidica

Stachys circinata subsp.

numidica, commonly known as the Numidian hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to regions of North Africa and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other members of the Stachys genus.
