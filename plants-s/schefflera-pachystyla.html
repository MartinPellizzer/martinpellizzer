Schefflera pachystyla

Schefflera pachystyla, commonly known as the thick-stemmed umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a thick, almost trunk-like structure, which contributes to its name. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Schefflera pachystyla is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
