Syzygium muelleri

Syzygium muelleri, commonly known as the satinwood or mountain satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Syzygium muelleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
