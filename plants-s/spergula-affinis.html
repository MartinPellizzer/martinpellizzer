Spergula affinis

Spergula affinis, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. Spergula affinis plays a role in supporting local insect populations and can be an indicator species for certain soil types.
