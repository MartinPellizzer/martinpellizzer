Schizostachyum brassii

Schizostachyum brassii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, golden-yellow culms that are often used in construction and handicrafts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually bright green and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Schizostachyum brassii is valued for its rapid growth and sustainable use in eco-friendly applications.
