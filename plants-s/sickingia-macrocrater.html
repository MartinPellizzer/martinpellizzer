Sickingia macrocrater

Sickingia macrocrater, commonly known as the giant rock fig, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, adding a dramatic visual element. Its growth habit is compact, with a rosette form that makes it an attractive addition to rock gardens.

Sickingia macrocrater thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
