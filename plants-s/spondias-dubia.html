Spondias dubia

Spondias dubia, commonly known as the wild mango or Indian date tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fuzzy fruits resembling mangoes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
