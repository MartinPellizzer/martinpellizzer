Saponaria vaccaria var. grandiflora

Saponaria vaccaria var. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy soapwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more upright. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
