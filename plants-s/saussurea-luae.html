Saussurea luae

Saussurea luae, commonly known as the mountain saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine. Saussurea luae thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in alpine ecosystems.
