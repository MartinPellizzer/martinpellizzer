Serjania humifusa

Serjania humifusa, commonly known as the creeping serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often found near the base of the stems. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Serjania humifusa is often found in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
