Sorbus × rikuchuensis

Sorbus × rikuchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and is valued for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive serrated edge.
