Sarracenia purpurea var. burkii

Sarracenia purpurea var. burkii, commonly known as the Burk's Pitcher Plant, is a variant of the purple pitcher plant native to North America.

It is a carnivorous plant that thrives in wet, acidic bog environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped traps to capture and digest insects. These traps are typically red or purple in color, with a distinctive hood that helps attract prey.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in late spring, contributing to its ornamental value.
