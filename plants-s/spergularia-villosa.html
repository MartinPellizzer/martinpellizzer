Spergularia villosa

Spergularia villosa, commonly known as silky sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or saline environments. Its leaves are usually succulent, aiding in water retention in dry conditions.

Spergularia villosa is an important species for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in coastal areas.
