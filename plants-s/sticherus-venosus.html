Sticherus venosus

Sticherus venosus, commonly known as the veined sticherus, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Sticheraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed or veined, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or brownish in color, and appear in clusters.

Sticherus venosus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
