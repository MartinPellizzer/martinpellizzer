Sorbus domestica var. elongata

Sorbus domestica var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The fruit is a spherical, red-to-black berry that ripens in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or rocky areas.
