Saussurea taraxacifolia var. glabrescens

Saussurea taraxacifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous yarrow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage compared to the typical form of the species.
