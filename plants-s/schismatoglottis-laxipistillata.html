Schismatoglottis laxipistillata

Schismatoglottis laxipistillata, commonly known as the loose-pistil schismatoglottis, is a rare tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
