Scutellaria tayloriana var. polytricha

Scutellaria tayloriana var. polytricha, commonly known as the Taylor's bluehead, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid environments.
