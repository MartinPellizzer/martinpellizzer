Seriphium asperum

Seriphium asperum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and sandy areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the stems are erect with a branching habit.

Seriphium asperum produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
