Schizanthus nutantiflorus

Schizanthus nutantiflorus, commonly known as the trailing blossom or dancing bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it popular in hanging baskets and as a ground cover. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Schizanthus nutantiflorus thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
