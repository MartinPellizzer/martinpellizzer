Spathodea flaviflora

Spathodea flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
