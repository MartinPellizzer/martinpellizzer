Scorzonera hispanica var. genuina

Scorzonera hispanica var. genuina, commonly known as Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in Mediterranean cuisine.

Scorzonera hispanica var. genuina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
