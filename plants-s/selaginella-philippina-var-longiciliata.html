Selaginella philippina var. longiciliata

Selaginella philippina var. longiciliata, commonly known as the Philippine spikerush, is a species of spikemoss native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its creeping, stoloniferous growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
