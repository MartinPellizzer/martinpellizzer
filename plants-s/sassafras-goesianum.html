Sassafras goesianum

Sassafras goesianum, commonly known as the Goianinha sassafras, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and camphor. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
