Shonia territorialis

Shonia territorialis, commonly known as the Territorial Shonia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features striking violet-blue petals with a distinctive yellow center, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures. Its blooming season occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to climate change, it is classified as an endangered species.
