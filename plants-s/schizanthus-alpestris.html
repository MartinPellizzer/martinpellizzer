Schizanthus alpestris

Schizanthus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bean or mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces clusters of showy, butterfly-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Schizanthus alpestris is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
