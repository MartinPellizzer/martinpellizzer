Solanum conjungens

Solanum conjungens, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum conjungens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
