Solanum dammerianum

Solanum dammerianum, commonly known as the Dammeriana nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
