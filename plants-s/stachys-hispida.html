Stachys hispida

Stachys hispida, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Stachys hispida is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
