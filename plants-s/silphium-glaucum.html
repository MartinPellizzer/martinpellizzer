Silphium glaucum

Silphium glaucum, commonly known as white silphium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white or bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a loose panicle, which bloom in the summer months. Silphium glaucum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
