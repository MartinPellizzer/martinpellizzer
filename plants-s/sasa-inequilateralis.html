Sasa inequilateralis

Sasa inequilateralis, commonly known as the unequal-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Sasa inequilateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
