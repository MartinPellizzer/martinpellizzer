Solidago graminifolia var. remota

Solidago graminifolia var. remota, commonly known as the remote goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and forest edges. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
