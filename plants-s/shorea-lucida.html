Shorea lucida

Shorea lucida, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.
