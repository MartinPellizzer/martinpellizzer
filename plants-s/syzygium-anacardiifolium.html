Syzygium anacardiifolium

Syzygium anacardiifolium, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or the wild cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications, particularly in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisines.

Syzygium anacardiifolium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
