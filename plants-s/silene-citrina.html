Silene citrina

Silene citrina, commonly known as the yellow campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring full sun to partial shade. Silene citrina is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its delicate, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bright color and ease of care.
