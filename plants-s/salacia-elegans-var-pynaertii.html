Salacia elegans var. pynaertii

Salacia elegans var. pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's elegant salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other members of the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
