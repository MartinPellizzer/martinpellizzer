Salvia libanensis

Salvia libanensis, commonly known as the Lebanon sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces striking violet-blue flowers in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
