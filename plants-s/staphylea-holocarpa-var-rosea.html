Staphylea holocarpa var. rosea

Staphylea holocarpa var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, rose-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
