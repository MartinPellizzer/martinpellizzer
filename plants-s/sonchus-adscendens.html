Sonchus adscendens

Sonchus adscendens, commonly known as ascending sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with upright stems and serrated, lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
