Saxifraga sachalinensis

Saxifraga sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forested areas. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Saxifraga sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
