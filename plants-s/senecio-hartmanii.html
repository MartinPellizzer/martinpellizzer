Senecio hartmanii

Senecio hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert scrublands. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, gray-green leaves.

Senecio hartmanii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
