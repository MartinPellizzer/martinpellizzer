Sasaella tsurumatiana

Sasaella tsurumatiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Tsurumatian bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
