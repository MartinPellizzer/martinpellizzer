Solanum ferocissimum var. hastilobum

Solanum ferocissimum var. hastilobum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
