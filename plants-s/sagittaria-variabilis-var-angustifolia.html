Sagittaria variabilis var. angustifolia

Sagittaria variabilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, blooming in late summer.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
