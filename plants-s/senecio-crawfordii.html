Senecio crawfordii

Senecio crawfordii, commonly known as Crawford's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
