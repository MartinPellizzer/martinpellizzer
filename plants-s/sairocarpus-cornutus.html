Sairocarpus cornutus

Sairocarpus cornutus, commonly known as the horned sairocarpus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
