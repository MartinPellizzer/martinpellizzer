Speculantha vescula

Speculantha vescula, commonly known as the moonflower of the southern plains, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem and leaves that are typically thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water. Its flowers open at dusk and remain open through the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting nighttime wildlife.
