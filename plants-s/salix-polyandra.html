Salix polyandra

Salix polyandra, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow or reddish in color. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin strips.

Salix polyandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its soft, flowing form.
