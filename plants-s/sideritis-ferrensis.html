Sideritis ferrensis

Sideritis ferrensis, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Sideritis ferrensis is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
