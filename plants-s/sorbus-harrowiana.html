Sorbus harrowiana

Sorbus harrowiana, commonly known as the Harrow rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive yellowish-white flowers and bright red berries.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
