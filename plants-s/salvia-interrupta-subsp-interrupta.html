Salvia interrupta subsp. interrupta

Salvia interrupta subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
