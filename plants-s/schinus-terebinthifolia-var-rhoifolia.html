Schinus terebinthifolia var. rhoifolia

Schinus terebinthifolia var. rhoifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, bright red berries. The plant is evergreen, with thick, waxy leaves that help it thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The berries contain a toxic resin and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
