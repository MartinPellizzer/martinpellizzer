Stevia seemannioides

Stevia seemannioides, commonly known as the Peruvian stevia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia seemannioides is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
