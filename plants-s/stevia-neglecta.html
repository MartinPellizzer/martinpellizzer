Stevia neglecta

Stevia neglecta, commonly known as wild stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for drought-resistant landscaping.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
