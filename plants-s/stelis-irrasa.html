Stelis irrasa

Stelis irrasa, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Stelis irrasa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
