Stigmatopteris caudata

Stigmatopteris caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed stegmidium, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a stegmidium, which is a type of spore-producing organ. Its leaves are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a feathery appearance.

Stigmatopteris caudata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
