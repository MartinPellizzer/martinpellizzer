Stachys calycantha

Stachys calycantha, commonly known as the purple flowered betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Stachys calycantha is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
