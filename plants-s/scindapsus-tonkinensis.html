Scindapsus tonkinensis

Scindapsus tonkinensis, commonly known as the silver vine or silver leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive silver veins. The plant is known for its ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a cascading foliage plant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element in indoor gardening.

Scindapsus tonkinensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its lush appearance.
