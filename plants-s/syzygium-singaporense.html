Syzygium singaporense

Syzygium singaporense, commonly known as the Singapore tamarind.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Singapore and Malaysia. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
