Sloanea guianensis

Sloanea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
