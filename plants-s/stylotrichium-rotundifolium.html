Stylotrichium rotundifolium

Stylotrichium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved stylo, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are typically slender and branched, supporting the delicate blooms.

Stylotrichium rotundifolium is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
