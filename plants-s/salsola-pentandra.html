Salsola pentandra

Salsola pentandra, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance. It often forms dense stands, which can be invasive in some regions.

Salsola pentandra is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
