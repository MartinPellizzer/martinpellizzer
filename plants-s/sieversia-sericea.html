Sieversia sericea

Sieversia sericea, commonly known as the silky sieve-plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Sieversia sericea is typically found in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
