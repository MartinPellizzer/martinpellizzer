Senecio tandilensis

Senecio tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio tandilensis is often found in grasslands and is part of the local flora in the region.
