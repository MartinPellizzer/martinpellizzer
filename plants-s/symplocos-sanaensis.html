Symplocos sanaensis

Symplocos sanaensis, commonly known as the Sanae Symplocos, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Symplocos sanaensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
