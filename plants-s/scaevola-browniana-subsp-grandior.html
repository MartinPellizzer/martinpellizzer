Scaevola browniana subsp. grandior

Scaevola browniana subsp.

grandior, commonly known as the giant fanflower, is a striking ornamental plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often forming a fan-like shape.

The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-gray foliage that adds visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
