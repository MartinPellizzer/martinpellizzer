Senecio pinnatifolius var. capillifolius

Senecio pinnatifolius var. capillifolius, commonly known as the Cape mountain pride of wildflowers, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hair-like filaments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
