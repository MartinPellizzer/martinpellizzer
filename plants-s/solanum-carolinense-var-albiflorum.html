Solanum carolinense var. albiflorum

Solanum carolinense var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its fruits are yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
