Sabicea arborea

Sabicea arborea, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
