Sideritis hirsuta proles tomentosa

Sideritis hirsuta proles tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy sidewort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
