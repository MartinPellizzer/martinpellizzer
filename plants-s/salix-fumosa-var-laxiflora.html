Salix fumosa var. laxiflora

Salix fumosa var. laxiflora, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage and catkin-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in a similar fuzzy layer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
