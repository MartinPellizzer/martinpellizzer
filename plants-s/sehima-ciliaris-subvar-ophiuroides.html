Sehima ciliaris subvar. ophiuroides

Sehima ciliaris subvar. ophiuroides, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is a perennial herb that blooms from spring to early summer.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
