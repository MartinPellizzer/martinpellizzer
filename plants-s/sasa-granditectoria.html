Sasa granditectoria

Sasa granditectoria, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizome system allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
