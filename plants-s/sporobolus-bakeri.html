Sporobolus bakeri

Sporobolus bakeri, commonly known as Baker's sanddrop, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in sandy soils.

Sporobolus bakeri is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
