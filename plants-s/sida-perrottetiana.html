Sida perrottetiana

Sida perrottetiana, commonly known as the Perrottet's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Sida perrottetiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
