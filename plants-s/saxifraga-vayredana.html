Saxifraga vayredana

Saxifraga vayredana, commonly known as the Vayreda's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to conserve moisture.

Saxifraga vayredana is a perennial species that is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific habitat requirements.
