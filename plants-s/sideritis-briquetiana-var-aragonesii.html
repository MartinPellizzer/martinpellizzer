Sideritis briquetiana var. aragonesii

Sideritis briquetiana var. aragonesii, commonly known as the Aragon violet, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
