Salvia pulchella

Salvia pulchella, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia pulchella is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
