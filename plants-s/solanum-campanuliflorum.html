Solanum campanuliflorum

Solanum campanuliflorum, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum campanuliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
