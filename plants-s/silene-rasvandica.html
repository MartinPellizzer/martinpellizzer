Silene rasvandica

Silene rasvandica, commonly known as the Rasvan Valley catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene rasvandica is a perennial species, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
