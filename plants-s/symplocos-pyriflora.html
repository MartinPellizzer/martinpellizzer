Symplocos pyriflora

Symplocos pyriflora, commonly known as the flame tree or pyriflora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Symplocos pyriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
