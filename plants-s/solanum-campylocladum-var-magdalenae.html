Solanum campylocladum var. magdalenae

Solanum campylocladum var. magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
