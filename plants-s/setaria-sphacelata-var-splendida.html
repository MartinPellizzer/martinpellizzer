Setaria sphacelata var. splendida

Setaria sphacelata var. splendida, commonly known as the glittering foxtail, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive, narrow leaves with a glossy, silver-green appearance. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with fine, bristly hairs, giving it a shimmering effect in sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
