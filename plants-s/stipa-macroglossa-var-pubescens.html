Stipa macroglossa var. pubescens

Stipa macroglossa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
