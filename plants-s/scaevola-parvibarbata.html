Scaevola parvibarbata

Scaevola parvibarbata, commonly known as the small-bristled fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a low, spreading growth habit and produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant features small, bristly hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Scaevola parvibarbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
