Sagonea aquatica

Sagonea aquatica, commonly known as the water sagittaria, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and shallow water environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant typically grows in regions with temperate climates and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth. Sagonea aquatica plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
