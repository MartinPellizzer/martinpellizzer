Silene clusii

Silene clusii, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene clusii is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
