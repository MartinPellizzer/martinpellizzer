Stoebe canescens

Stoebe canescens, commonly known as hoary bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Stoebe canescens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
