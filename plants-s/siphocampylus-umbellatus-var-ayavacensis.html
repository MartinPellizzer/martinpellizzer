Siphocampylus umbellatus var. ayavacensis

Siphocampylus umbellatus var. ayavacensis, commonly known as the Ayavacensis bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
