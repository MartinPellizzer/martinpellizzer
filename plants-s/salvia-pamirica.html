Salvia pamirica

Salvia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia pamirica is adapted to high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to cold and arid conditions.
