Stanhopea × quadricornis

Stanhopea × quadricornis, commonly known as the four-horned orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear multiple showy, fragrant blooms. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers often display a combination of yellow, orange, and red hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental value.

Stanhopea × quadricornis thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
