Saraca griffithiana

Saraca griffithiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits.

Saraca griffithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
