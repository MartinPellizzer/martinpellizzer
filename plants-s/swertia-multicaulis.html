Swertia multicaulis

Swertia multicaulis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Swertia multicaulis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
