Saussurea oligantha

Saussurea oligantha, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, branched inflorescence that bears small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
