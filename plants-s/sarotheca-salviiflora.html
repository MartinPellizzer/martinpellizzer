Sarotheca salviiflora

Sarotheca salviiflora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Sarotheca salviiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
