Selaginella robusta

Selaginella robusta, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions on the ground. The plant has small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Selaginella robusta is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
