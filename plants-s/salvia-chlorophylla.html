Salvia chlorophylla

Salvia chlorophylla, commonly known as green sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its common name. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
