Salsola aurantiaca

Salsola aurantiaca, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or spreading, allowing it to cover large areas in desert environments.

Salsola aurantiaca plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
