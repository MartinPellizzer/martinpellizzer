Sasa muratana

Sasa muratana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, followed by the development of small, oval-shaped seeds. Sasa muratana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it both ecologically and aesthetically valuable.
