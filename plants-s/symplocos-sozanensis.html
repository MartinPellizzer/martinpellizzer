Symplocos sozanensis

Symplocos sozanensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountain forests. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface, and it produces fragrant flowers in the spring.

The berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.
