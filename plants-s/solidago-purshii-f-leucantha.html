Solidago purshii f. leucantha

Solidago purshii f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
