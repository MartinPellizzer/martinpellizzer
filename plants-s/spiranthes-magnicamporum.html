Spiranthes magnicamporum

Spiranthes magnicamporum, commonly known as the showy ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling arrangement of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
