Senecio saposhnikovii

Senecio saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in yellow or white.

Senecio saposhnikovii is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
