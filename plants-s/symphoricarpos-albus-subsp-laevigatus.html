Symphoricarpos albus subsp. laevigatus

Symphoricarpos albus subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
