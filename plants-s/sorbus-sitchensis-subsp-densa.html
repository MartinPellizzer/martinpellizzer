Sorbus sitchensis subsp. densa

Sorbus sitchensis subsp.

densa, commonly known as the dense sorbus, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and distinctive foliage, which consists of elliptical, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
