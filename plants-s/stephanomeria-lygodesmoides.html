Stephanomeria lygodesmoides

Stephanomeria lygodesmoides, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
