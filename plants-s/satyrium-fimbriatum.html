Satyrium fimbriatum

Satyrium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers display a range of colors, from white to pale pink.

Satyrium fimbriatum is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized petals and sepals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
