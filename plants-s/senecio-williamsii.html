Senecio williamsii

Senecio williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio williamsii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
