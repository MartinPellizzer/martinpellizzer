Scilla rhadamanthi

Scilla rhadamanthi, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to dry, temperate climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Scilla rhadamanthi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
