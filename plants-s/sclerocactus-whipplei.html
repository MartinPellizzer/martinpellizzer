Sclerocactus whipplei

Sclerocactus whipplei, commonly known as the Whipple's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Sclerocactus whipplei is a slow-growing plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and xeriscaping.
