Silene vandasii

Silene vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate appearance.

Silene vandasii is often found in coastal and inland areas, where it adapts well to arid conditions.
