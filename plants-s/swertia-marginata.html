Swertia marginata

Swertia marginata, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia marginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
