Struthiopteris nipponica

Struthiopteris nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese field shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
