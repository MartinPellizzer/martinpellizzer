Senecio birubonensis

Senecio birubonensis, commonly known as the red-veined senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, tubular red flowers that contrast with its dark green, velvety leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
