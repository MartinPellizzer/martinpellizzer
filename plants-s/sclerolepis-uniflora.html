Sclerolepis uniflora

Sclerolepis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, often clustered in groups of two or three. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Sclerolepis uniflora is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
