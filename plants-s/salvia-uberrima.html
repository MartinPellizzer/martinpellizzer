Salvia uberrima

Salvia uberrima, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Salvia uberrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
