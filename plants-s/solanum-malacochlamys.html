Solanum malacochlamys

Solanum malacochlamys, commonly known as the Malacochlamys nightshade, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are usually green when immature and turn red when ripe, containing several seeds.
