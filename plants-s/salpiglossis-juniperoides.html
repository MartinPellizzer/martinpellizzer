Salpiglossis juniperoides

Salpiglossis juniperoides, commonly known as the juniper-like lippia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant's leaves are narrow and leathery, resembling those of juniper, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
