Schoenus juvenis

Schoenus juvenis, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Schoenus juvenis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
