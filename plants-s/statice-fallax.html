Statice fallax

Statice fallax, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Statice fallax is commonly used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
