Searsia lucida

Searsia lucida, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, papery bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Searsia lucida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat in dry landscapes.
