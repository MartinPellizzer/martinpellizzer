Stipa lettermanii

Stipa lettermanii, commonly known as Letterman's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and fine, needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa lettermanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
