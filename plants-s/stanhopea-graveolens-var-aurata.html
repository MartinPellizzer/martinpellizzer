Stanhopea graveolens var. aurata

Stanhopea graveolens var. aurata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique scent.
