Symplocos modesta

Symplocos modesta, commonly known as the smallleaf waxberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals.
