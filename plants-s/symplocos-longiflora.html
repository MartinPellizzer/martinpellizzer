Symplocos longiflora

Symplocos longiflora, commonly known as the long-flower inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red berries that are a source of food for wildlife. Symplocos longiflora thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded areas.
