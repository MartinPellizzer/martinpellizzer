Solanum andigenum f. elongatipedicellatum

Solanum andigenum f. elongatipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant species native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its elongated pedicels and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological adaptations to high-altitude environments.
