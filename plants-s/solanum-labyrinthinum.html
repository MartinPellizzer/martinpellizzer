Solanum labyrinthinum

Solanum labyrinthinum, commonly known as the devil's fig, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum labyrinthinum is part of the nightshade family and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
