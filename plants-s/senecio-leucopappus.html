Senecio leucopappus

Senecio leucopappus, commonly known as the white pappus senecio, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio leucopappus is typically low-growing, with a sprawling or upright habit depending on environmental conditions.
