Saxifraga bongardii

Saxifraga bongardii, commonly known as the Bongard's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, often arranged in a cyme. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine gardens.

Saxifraga bongardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
