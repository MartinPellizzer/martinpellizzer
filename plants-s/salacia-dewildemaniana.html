Salacia dewildemaniana

Salacia dewildemaniana, commonly known as the wild betel vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
