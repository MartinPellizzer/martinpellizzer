Schizonepeta tenuifolia var. japonica

Schizonepeta tenuifolia var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese schizonepeta, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
