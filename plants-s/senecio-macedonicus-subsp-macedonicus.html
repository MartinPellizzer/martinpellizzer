Senecio macedonicus subsp. macedonicus

Senecio macedonicus subsp.

macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that often grow in open, sunny habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
