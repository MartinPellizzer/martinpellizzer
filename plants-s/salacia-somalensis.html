Salacia somalensis

Salacia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali salacia, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its deciduous nature. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and greenish-yellow.

The plant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
