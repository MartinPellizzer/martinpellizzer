Salsola auricula

Salsola auricula, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as deserts and coastal dunes.

Salsola auricula is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.
