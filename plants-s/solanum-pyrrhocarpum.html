Solanum pyrrhocarpum

Solanum pyrrhocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic alkaloids in their raw form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
