Salvia officinalis var. absynthiina

Salvia officinalis var. absynthiina, commonly known as lemon sage, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct lemony aroma, and its flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in dense whorls. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
