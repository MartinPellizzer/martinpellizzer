Sebastiania glandulosa f. velutina

Sebastiania glandulosa f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet-sebaste, is a variant of the sebaste plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
