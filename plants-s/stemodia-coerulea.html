Stemodia coerulea

Stemodia coerulea, commonly known as the blue stemodia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
