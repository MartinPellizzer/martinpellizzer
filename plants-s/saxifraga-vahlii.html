Saxifraga vahlii

Saxifraga vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences during the summer months. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rosette structure, and are covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Saxifraga vahlii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
