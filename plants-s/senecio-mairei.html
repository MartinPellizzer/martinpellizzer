Senecio mairei

Senecio mairei, commonly known as the Maire's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio mairei is often found in the understorey of temperate rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
