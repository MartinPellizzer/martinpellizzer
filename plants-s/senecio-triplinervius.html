Senecio triplinervius

Senecio triplinervius, commonly known as the three-nerved senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring narrow, linear leaves with three prominent veins. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
