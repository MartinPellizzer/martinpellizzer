Symbegonia arfakensis

Symbegonia arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfak mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of West New Guinea.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Symbegonia arfakensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
