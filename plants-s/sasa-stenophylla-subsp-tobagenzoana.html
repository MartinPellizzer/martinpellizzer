Sasa stenophylla subsp. tobagenzoana

Sasa stenophylla subsp.

tobagenzoana, commonly known as the Tobago bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Tobago and parts of South America. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and hardiness.
