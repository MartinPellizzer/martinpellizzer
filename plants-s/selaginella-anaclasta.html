Selaginella anaclasta

Selaginella anaclasta, commonly known as the rock spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, often in crevices or on exposed surfaces. The plant has a distinct morphology with a short, upright stem and dense, needle-like leaves that are often spirally arranged. It is characterized by its ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in xerophytic habitats.

Selaginella anaclasta plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky areas and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
