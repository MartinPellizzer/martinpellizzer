Solanum allophyllum

Solanum allophyllum, commonly known as the purple potato plant, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tuberous roots that produce edible purple-colored potatoes. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important crops like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.
