Searsia populifolia

Searsia populifolia, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib. It produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils.
