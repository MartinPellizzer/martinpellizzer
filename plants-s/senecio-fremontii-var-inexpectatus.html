Senecio fremontii var. inexpectatus

Senecio fremontii var. inexpectatus, commonly known as the Fremont's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
