Stalagmitis spicata

Stalagmitis spicata, commonly known as the spike-stalagmite plant, is a rare and unique species found in specific limestone caverns.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that resemble stalagmites, which are typically formed by mineral deposits in caves. The plant's leaves are small, clustered at the base, and its flowers are delicate, often appearing in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in humid, dark environments with minimal light, relying on moisture from dripping water.

Due to its specialized habitat, Stalagmitis spicata is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
