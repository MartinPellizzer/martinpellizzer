Sclarea orientalis

Sclarea orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Sclarea orientalis is valued for its aromatic seeds and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
