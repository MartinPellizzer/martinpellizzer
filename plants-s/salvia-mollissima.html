Salvia mollissima

Salvia mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that range in color from gray-green to silver-white. The plant produces tall spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
