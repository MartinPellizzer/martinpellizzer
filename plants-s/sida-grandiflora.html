Sida grandiflora

Sida grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Sida grandiflora is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
