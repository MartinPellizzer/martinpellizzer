Sedum griffithii

Sedum griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayas.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and ecological settings.
