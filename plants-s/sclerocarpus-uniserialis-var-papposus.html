Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. papposus

Sclerocarpus uniserialis var. papposus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, papery bracts that give it a feathery appearance, and its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
