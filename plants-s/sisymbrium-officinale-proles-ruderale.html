Sisymbrium officinale proles ruderale

Sisymbrium officinale proles ruderale, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
