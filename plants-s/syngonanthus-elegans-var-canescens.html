Syngonanthus elegans var. canescens

Syngonanthus elegans var. canescens, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, prostrate stems that form a dense mat, often covering rocky or sandy soils. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
