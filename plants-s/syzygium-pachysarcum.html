Syzygium pachysarcum

Syzygium pachysarcum, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, round, red berries. Syzygium pachysarcum is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional carving and furniture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
