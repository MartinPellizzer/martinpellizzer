Salvia taurica

Salvia taurica, commonly known as Tauric sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Salvia taurica is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
