Salix wallichiana var. julacea

Salix wallichiana var. julacea, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and preventing soil erosion.
