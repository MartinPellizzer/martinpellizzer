Scutellaria commutata

Scutellaria commutata, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, tubular blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
