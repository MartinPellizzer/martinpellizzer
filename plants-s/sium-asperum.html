Sium asperum

Sium asperum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy coating. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are edible when cooked.

Sium asperum thrives in moist, wetland environments and is often found near streams and ponds.
