Spatholobus sanguineus

Spatholobus sanguineus, commonly known as the red-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The red-flowered wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
