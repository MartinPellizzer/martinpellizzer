Solanum moricandii var. minus

Solanum moricandii var. minus, commonly known as the small moricandii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along mountain slopes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
