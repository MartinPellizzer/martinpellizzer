Sida dureana

Sida dureana, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and succulent stems that store water. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Sida dureana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
