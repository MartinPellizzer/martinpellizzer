Solanum plousianthemum var. epapillosum

Solanum plousianthemum var. epapillosum, commonly known as the papillate nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, papillate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
