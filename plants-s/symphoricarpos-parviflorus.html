Symphoricarpos parviflorus

Symphoricarpos parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has woody stems and hairy leaves, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are an important food source for birds and small mammals.
