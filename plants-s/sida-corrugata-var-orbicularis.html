Sida corrugata var. orbicularis

Sida corrugata var. orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its orbicular (rounded) leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual or biennial herb with a hairy stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
