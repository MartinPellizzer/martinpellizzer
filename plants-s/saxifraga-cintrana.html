Saxifraga cintrana

Saxifraga cintrana, commonly known as the Canary Islands saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.

Saxifraga cintrana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.
