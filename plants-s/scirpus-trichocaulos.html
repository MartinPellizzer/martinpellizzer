Scirpus trichocaulos

Scirpus trichocaulos, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus trichocaulos plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
