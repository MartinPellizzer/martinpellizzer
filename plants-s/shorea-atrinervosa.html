Shorea atrinervosa

Shorea atrinervosa, commonly known as the black-veined rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with prominent vein patterns. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Shorea atrinervosa is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
