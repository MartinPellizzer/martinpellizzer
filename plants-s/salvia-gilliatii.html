Salvia gilliatii

Salvia gilliatii, commonly known as the desert sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its leaves are hairy and gray-green, providing a unique texture and appearance.
