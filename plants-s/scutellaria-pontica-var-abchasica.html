Scutellaria pontica var. abchasica

Scutellaria pontica var. abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, blue to violet flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
