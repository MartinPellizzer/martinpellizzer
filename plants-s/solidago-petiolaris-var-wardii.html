Solidago petiolaris var. wardii

Solidago petiolaris var. wardii, commonly known as Ward's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is typically found in moist to wet soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
