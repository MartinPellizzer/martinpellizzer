Sasaella sasaelloides

Sasaella sasaelloides, commonly known as the Japanese sasa fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, clumping foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
