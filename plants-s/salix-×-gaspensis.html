Salix × gaspensis

Salix × gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé willow, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
