Solidago dumetorum

Solidago dumetorum, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow, rayless flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Solidago dumetorum is an important nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
