Scutellaria pectinata

Scutellaria pectinata, commonly known as the woolly skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Scutellaria pectinata belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its compact, low-growing form.

It is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
