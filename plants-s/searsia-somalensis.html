Searsia somalensis

Searsia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali thornveld acacia, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Somalia and Kenya.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits.

Searsia somalensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
