Salix viminalis var. pyramidalis

Salix viminalis var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow or pyramidal willow, is a variety of the common osier dogwood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. This plant is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and long, slender leaves. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket weaving.
