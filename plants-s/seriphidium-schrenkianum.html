Seriphidium schrenkianum

Seriphidium schrenkianum, commonly known as the Karakoram wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky and mountainous terrain. The plant features a bushy growth habit with slender, branched stems and small, grayish-green leaves. Its flowering heads are small and yellow, containing numerous tiny florets.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high solar exposure.
