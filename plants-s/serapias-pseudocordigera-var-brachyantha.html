Serapias pseudocordigera var. brachyantha

Serapias pseudocordigera var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved serapias, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rockrose (Cistaceae) and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
