Stipa mapuche

Stipa mapuche, commonly known as the Mapuche needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and parts of Peru.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense panicles, which are typically adorned with silky hairs. Stipa mapuche thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
