Schismatoglottis pusilla

Schismatoglottis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf schismatoglottis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
