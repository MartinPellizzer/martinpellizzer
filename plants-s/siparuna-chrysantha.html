Siparuna chrysantha

Siparuna chrysantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Siparuna chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
