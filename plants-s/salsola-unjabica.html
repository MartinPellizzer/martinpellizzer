Salsola unjabica

Salsola unjabica, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

Salsola unjabica plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in desert ecosystems.
