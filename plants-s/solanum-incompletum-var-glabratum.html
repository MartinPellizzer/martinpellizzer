Solanum incompletum var. glabratum

Solanum incompletum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smoothleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairless or smooth. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it produces small, green berries that turn red when mature.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal uses.
