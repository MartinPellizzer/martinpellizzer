Sequoia sempervirens f. pendula

Sequoia sempervirens f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping giant redwood, is a unique form of the coast redwood.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. This cultivar is a natural mutation of the parent species, which typically has a more upright growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

The weeping giant redwood is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
