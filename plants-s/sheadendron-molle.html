Sheadendron molle

Sheadendron molle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
