Salix madagascariensis

Salix madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers arranged separately on the same plant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and pale in color.

This species is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.
