Specklinia ramphastorhyncha

Specklinia ramphastorhyncha, commonly known as the bird-beak orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Specklinia ramphastorhyncha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its natural habitat.
