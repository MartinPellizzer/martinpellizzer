Spiesia physocarpa

Spiesia physocarpa, commonly known as the balloon tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a balloon, which contains a single, hard-seeded pod. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Spiesia physocarpa is often cultivated for its unique fruit and ornamental appeal in tropical and subtropical gardens.
