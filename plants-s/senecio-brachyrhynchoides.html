Senecio brachyrhynchoides

Senecio brachyrhynchoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
