Sabina flaccida

Sabina flaccida, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used as a food source by various wildlife species. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

The botanical features of Sabina flaccida make it a resilient and ecologically important plant in desert ecosystems.
