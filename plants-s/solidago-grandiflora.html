Solidago grandiflora

Solidago grandiflora, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. It produces bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
