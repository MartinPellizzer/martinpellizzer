Swertia plantaginifolia

Swertia plantaginifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia plantaginifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
