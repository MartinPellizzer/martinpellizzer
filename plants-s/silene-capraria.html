Silene capraria

Silene capraria, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene capraria is often found in open habitats, such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
