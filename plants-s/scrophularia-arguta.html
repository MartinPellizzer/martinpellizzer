Scrophularia arguta

Scrophularia arguta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia arguta is often found in temperate climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
