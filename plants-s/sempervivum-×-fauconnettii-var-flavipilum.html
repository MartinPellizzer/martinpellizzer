Sempervivum × fauconnettii var. flavipilum

Sempervivum × fauconnettii var. flavipilum, commonly known as the Golden Hair Sedum, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive golden or yellowish hue along the edges. The plant produces tall flowering stalks bearing clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
