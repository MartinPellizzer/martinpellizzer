Senecio villifructus

Senecio villifructus, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Senecio villifructus is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
