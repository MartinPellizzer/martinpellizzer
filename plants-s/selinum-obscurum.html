Selinum obscurum

Selinum obscurum, commonly known as dark selinum, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and stream banks. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental value in gardens. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.

Selinum obscurum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
