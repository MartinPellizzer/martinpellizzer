Senecio keshna

Senecio keshna, commonly known as Keshna groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with hairy, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, often in late summer to early autumn.

Senecio keshna is adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
