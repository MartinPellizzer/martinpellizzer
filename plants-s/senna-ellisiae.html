Senna ellisiae

Senna ellisiae, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Senna ellisiae thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
