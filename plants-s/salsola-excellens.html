Salsola excellens

Salsola excellens, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is an annual or biennial species characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, reducing water loss and aiding survival in harsh conditions.

Salsola excellens is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common invasive species in disturbed soils.
