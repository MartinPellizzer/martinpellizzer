Sempervivum trifurcum

Sempervivum trifurcum, commonly known as the three-lobed houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions has earned it the common name "everlasting," reflecting its hardy nature.
