Solanum callianthum

Solanum callianthum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
