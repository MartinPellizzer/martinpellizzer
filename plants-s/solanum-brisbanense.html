Solanum brisbanense

Solanum brisbanense, commonly known as the Brisbane nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Solanum brisbanense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants.
