Sedum acuminatum

Sedum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and narrow, pointed leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum acuminatum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
