Scleria junciformis

Scleria junciformis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swamps. The plant has tall, grass-like stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Scleria junciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
