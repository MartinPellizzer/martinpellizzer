Silene ambigua var. latifolia

Silene ambigua var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are broad and oval, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
