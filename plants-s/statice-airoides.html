Statice airoides

Statice airoides, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important plant for coastal ecosystems.

Statice airoides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
