Sedum dendroideum subsp. parvifolium

Sedum dendroideum subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
