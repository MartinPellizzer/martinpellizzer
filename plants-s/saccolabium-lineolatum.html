Saccolabium lineolatum

Saccolabium lineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features compound leaves with linear leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in small, leathery pods, and the plant is adapted to arid conditions with deep root systems.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
