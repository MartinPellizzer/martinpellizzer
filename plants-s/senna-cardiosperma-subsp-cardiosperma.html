Senna cardiosperma subsp. cardiosperma

Senna cardiosperma subsp.

cardiosperma, commonly known as the heartseed senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds with a heart-shaped cotyledon, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
