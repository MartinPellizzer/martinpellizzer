Saussurea tsarongensis

Saussurea tsarongensis, commonly known as the Tsarong saffron plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 4,000 meters. Its flowers are rich in saffron-like carotenoids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
