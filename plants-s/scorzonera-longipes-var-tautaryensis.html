Scorzonera longipes var. tautaryensis

Scorzonera longipes var. tautaryensis, commonly known as the Tautary mountain scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a robust, hairy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
