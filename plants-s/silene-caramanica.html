Silene caramanica

Silene caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has hairy stems and leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene caramanica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
