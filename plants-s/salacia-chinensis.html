Salacia chinensis

Salacia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese salt bush, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Phytolacaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and woody, sprawling growth habit. The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Salacia chinensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
