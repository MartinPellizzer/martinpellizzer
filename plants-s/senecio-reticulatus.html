Senecio reticulatus

Senecio reticulatus, commonly known as the spiderwort or netleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive net-like venation, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to attract pollinators.
