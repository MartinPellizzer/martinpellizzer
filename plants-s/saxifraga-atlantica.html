Saxifraga atlantica

Saxifraga atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the North Atlantic.

It typically grows in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils along cliffs and shores. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga atlantica is adapted to harsh maritime conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
