Selago thomsonii var. whyteana

Selago thomsonii var. whyteana, commonly known as the Whyte's selago, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
