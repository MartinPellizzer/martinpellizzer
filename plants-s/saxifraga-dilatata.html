Saxifraga dilatata

Saxifraga dilatata, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. The plant features upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga dilatata is often found in mountainous areas and is a characteristic species of alpine meadows.
