Serapias vomeracea f. artemisiae

Serapias vomeracea f. artemisiae, commonly known as the Artemisia-leaved orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling those of orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is distinct, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery or grayish hue, reminiscent of the Artemisia genus.

This unique hybrid is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its habitat.
