Symplocos tinctoria

Symplocos tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian dye bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a red dye from its bark, which has been traditionally used in textile industries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos tinctoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
