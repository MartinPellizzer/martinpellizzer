Stranvaesia nussia var. oblanceolata

Stranvaesia nussia var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed stranvaesia, is a variant of the stranvaesia genus native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is generally hairy or pubescent, providing a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf shape and floral display.
