Solanum aculeastrum subsp. aculeastrum

Solanum aculeastrum subsp.

aculeastrum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its branched stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by egg-shaped berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.
