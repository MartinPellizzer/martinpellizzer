Salsola dealata

Salsola dealata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salsola dealata is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
