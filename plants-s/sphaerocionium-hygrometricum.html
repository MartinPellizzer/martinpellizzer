Sphaerocionium hygrometricum

Sphaerocionium hygrometricum, commonly known as the hygroscopic bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, which the plant then digests for nutrients. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are thin and often submerged, while the bladders are usually located near the water surface.

Sphaerocionium hygrometricum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microscopic life.
