Sicydium tamnifolium var. grandifolium

Sicydium tamnifolium var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf sicydium, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
