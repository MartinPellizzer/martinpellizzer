Scutellaria epilobiifolia f. rosea

Scutellaria epilobiifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. The plant has oval, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
