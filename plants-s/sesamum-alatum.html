Sesamum alatum

Sesamum alatum, commonly known as the winged sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves and small, capsule-like fruits that contain aromatic seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are alternate and have prominent veins, while the flowers are usually pale yellow to white.

The seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their nutritional and therapeutic properties.
