Salvia przewalskii var. rubrobrunnea

Salvia przewalskii var. rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-bronze sage, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its striking red-brown flower bracts and violet-blue flower buds, which contrast beautifully with its silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
