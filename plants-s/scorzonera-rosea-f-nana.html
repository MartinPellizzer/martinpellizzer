Scorzonera rosea f. nana

Scorzonera rosea f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf perennial wallflower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, slightly curled shape. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
