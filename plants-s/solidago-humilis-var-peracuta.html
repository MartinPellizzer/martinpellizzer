Solidago humilis var. peracuta

Solidago humilis var. peracuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a valuable species in wetland restoration projects.
