Suaeda asparagoides

Suaeda asparagoides, commonly known as sea sparrowgrass, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other arid or semi-arid regions with high salinity. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish hues.

Suaeda asparagoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
