Senecio rupicola

Senecio rupicola, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and yellow dandelion-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio rupicola is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance.
