Sesbania pachycarpa subsp. pachycarpa

Sesbania pachycarpa subsp.

pachycarpa, commonly known as the thickpod sesbania, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces large, thick pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment and crop rotation.
