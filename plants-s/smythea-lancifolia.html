Smythea lancifolia

Smythea lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate smythea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. Smythea lancifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
