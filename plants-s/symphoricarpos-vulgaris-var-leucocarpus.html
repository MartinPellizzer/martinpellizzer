Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. leucocarpus

Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. leucocarpus, commonly known as white-fruited snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green berries that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating. These berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

The shrub thrives in dry to moist soils and is often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.
