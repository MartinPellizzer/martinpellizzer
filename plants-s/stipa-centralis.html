Stipa centralis

Stipa centralis, commonly known as the Central Asian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Stipa centralis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
