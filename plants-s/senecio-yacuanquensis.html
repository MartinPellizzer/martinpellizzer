Senecio yacuanquensis

Senecio yacuanquensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
