Setaria setosa var. aestiva

Setaria setosa var. aestiva, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
