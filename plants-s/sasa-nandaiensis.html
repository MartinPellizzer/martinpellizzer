Sasa nandaiensis

Sasa nandaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense clumps of slender, erect culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which are often hidden among the foliage. Sasa nandaiensis is a rhizomatous perennial, meaning it spreads through underground stems, forming thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
