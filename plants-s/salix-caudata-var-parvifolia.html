Salix caudata var. parvifolia

Salix caudata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or wetlands. It has small, inconspicuous female flowers that develop into small, woody capsules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
