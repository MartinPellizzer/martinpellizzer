Syzygium phaeostictum

Syzygium phaeostictum, commonly known as the red-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sour taste similar to tamarind. Syzygium phaeostictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes a spreading canopy and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
