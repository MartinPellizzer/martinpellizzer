Senecio cosnipatensis

Senecio cosnipatensis, commonly known as the Bolivian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with a central disc, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
