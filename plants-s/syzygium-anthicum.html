Syzygium anthicum

Syzygium anthicum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Syzygium anthicum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
