Senecio iodanthus

Senecio iodanthus, commonly known as the blue-eared senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
