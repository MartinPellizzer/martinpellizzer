Sophronitis sanguiloba

Sophronitis sanguiloba, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture.

The flower structure is complex, featuring three sepals and three petals, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
