Spilanthes grandis

Spilanthes grandis, commonly known as the giant false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Spilanthes grandis is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
