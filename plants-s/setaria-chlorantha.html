Setaria chlorantha

Setaria chlorantha, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to various parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous greenish-brown spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Setaria chlorantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
