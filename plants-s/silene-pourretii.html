Silene pourretii

Silene pourretii, commonly known as the Pourret's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene pourretii is a perennial species, blooming in late summer and early autumn.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of alpine flora adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
