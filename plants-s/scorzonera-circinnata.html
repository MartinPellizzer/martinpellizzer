Scorzonera circinnata

Scorzonera circinnata, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant features a stout, tuberous root system, which is sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, narrow corolla.

Scorzonera circinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
