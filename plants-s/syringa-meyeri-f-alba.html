Syringa meyeri f. alba

Syringa meyeri f. alba, commonly known as the white meyer lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a compact, bushy form.

Syringa meyeri f. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
