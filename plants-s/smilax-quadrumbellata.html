Smilax quadrumbellata

Smilax quadrumbellata, commonly known as the red root smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a heart-shaped base.

Smilax quadrumbellata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
