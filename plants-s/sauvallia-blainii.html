Sauvallia blainii

Sauvallia blainii, commonly known as the Blain's sauvallia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
