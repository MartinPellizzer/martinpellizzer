Solanum pseudoundatum

Solanum pseudoundatum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy, violet-colored flowers. Its fruit, a small, round berry, turns from green to purple as it matures.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
