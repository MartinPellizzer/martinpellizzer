Solanum tenuicaule

Solanum tenuicaule, commonly known as the slenderleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by egg-shaped berries. Solanum tenuicaule belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
