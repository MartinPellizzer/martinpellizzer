Suberanthus yumuriensis

Suberanthus yumuriensis, commonly known as the Yumuriensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles.
