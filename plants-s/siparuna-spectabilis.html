Siparuna spectabilis

Siparuna spectabilis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Siparuna spectabilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
