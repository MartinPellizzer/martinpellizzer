Salvia cabulica var. scrotina

Salvia cabulica var. scrotina, commonly known as the Kashmiri sage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, often with a minty aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
