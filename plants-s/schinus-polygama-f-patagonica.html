Schinus polygama f. patagonica

Schinus polygama f. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian pepper tree, is a variant of the Schinus polygama species, which belongs to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to southern South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by the distinctive red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is adapted to dry, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
