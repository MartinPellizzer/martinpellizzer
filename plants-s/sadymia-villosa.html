Sadymia villosa

Sadymia villosa, commonly known as the woolly sadymia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villose, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Sadymia villosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
