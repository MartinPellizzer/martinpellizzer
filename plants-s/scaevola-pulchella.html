Scaevola pulchella

Scaevola pulchella, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright blue or violet flowers that are arranged in clusters, forming a fan-like shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Scaevola pulchella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
