Styrax lauraceus

Styrax lauraceus, commonly known as the sweet gum or Chinese sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruits.

The bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
