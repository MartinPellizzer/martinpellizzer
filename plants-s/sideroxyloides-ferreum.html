Sideroxyloides ferreum

Sideroxyloides ferreum, commonly known as the ironwood vine, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, woody stems and deep green foliage.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. Sideroxyloides ferreum is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable addition to botanical gardens.

Its scientific name reflects its association with iron, due to the metallic sheen of its bark and leaves.
