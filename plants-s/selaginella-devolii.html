Selaginella devolii

Selaginella devolii, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with whorled leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella devolii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
