Sasa nikkoensis

Sasa nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, contributing to its ability to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Sasa nikkoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
