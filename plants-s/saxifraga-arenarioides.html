Saxifraga arenarioides

Saxifraga arenarioides, commonly known as sand saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga arenarioides plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
