Silene isaurica

Silene isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with prominent stamens. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene isaurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
