Sorghastrum canescens

Sorghastrum canescens, commonly known as gray beard grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Sorghastrum canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
