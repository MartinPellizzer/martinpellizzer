Sorbus conwentzii

Sorbus conwentzii, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
