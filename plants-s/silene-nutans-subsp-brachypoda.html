Silene nutans subsp. brachypoda

Silene nutans subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
