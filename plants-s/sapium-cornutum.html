Sapium cornutum

Sapium cornutum, commonly known as the horned cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant features upright, branched stems with heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, which are surrounded by bright yellow bracts.

Sapium cornutum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
