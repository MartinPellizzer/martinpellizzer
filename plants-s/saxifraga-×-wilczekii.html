Saxifraga × wilczekii

Saxifraga × wilczekii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
