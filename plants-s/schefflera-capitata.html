Schefflera capitata

Schefflera capitata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Schefflera capitata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant indoors.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and indoor gardening.
