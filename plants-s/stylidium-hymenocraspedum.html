Stylidium hymenocraspedum

Stylidium hymenocraspedum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant has a slender, erect stem that is often reddish or pink in color, which is a distinguishing feature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base.

Stylidium hymenocraspedum is a short-lived perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
