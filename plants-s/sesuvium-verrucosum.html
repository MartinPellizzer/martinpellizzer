Sesuvium verrucosum

Sesuvium verrucosum, commonly known as the saltbush or sea bean, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Sesuvium verrucosum plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
