Salix cheilophila var. villosa

Salix cheilophila var. villosa, commonly known as the silky willow, is a variant of the willow genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are catkin-like and appear in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
