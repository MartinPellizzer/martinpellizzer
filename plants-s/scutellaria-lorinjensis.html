Scutellaria lorinjensis

Scutellaria lorinjensis, commonly known as the Lorinjens Scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scutellaria lorinjensis is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
