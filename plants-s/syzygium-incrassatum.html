Syzygium incrassatum

Syzygium incrassatum, commonly known as the rough-skin tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody, elongated pod containing several hard seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
