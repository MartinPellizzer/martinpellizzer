Scrophularia auriculata subsp. valentina

Scrophularia auriculata subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
