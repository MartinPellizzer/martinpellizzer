Saxifraga cossoniana

Saxifraga cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
