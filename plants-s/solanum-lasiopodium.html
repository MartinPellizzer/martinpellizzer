Solanum lasiopodium

Solanum lasiopodium, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, purple to blue flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum lasiopodium is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
