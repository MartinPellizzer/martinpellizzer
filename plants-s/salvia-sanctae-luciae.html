Salvia sanctae-luciae

Salvia sanctae-luciae, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Saint Lucy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical associations with religious traditions.

Salvia sanctae-luciae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
