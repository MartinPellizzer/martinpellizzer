Synema

Synema, commonly known as the "white sand plant," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which gives it its common name.

Synema is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and containers.
