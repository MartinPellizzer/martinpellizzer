Salvia aprica

Salvia aprica, commonly known as the spring sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties.
