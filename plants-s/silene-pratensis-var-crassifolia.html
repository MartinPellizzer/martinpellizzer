Silene pratensis var. crassifolia

Silene pratensis var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. The plant features showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

It is often found in open habitats and is a characteristic species of grassland ecosystems.
