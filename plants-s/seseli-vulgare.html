Seseli vulgare

Seseli vulgare, commonly known as common seseli, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and compound umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a fern-like appearance, and it often grows in moist, open habitats.

Seseli vulgare is typically found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
