Saxifraga intricata f. nervosa-minor

Saxifraga intricata f. nervosa-minor, commonly known as the narrow-veined saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
