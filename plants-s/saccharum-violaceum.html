Saccharum violaceum

Saccharum violaceum, commonly known as purple bamboo or violet grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-colored inflorescences and tall, slender culms. The plant is often found in wetland habitats and can thrive in swampy or marshy environments. Its fibrous stalks are used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.

Saccharum violaceum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
