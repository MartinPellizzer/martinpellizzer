Stevia macella

Stevia macella, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweet herb, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters during the growing season.

Stevia macella is valued for its intense sweetness and is used in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener alternative.
