Scaphyglottis striolata

Scaphyglottis striolata, commonly known as the streaked bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caribbean.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with distinctive white streaks along the throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats, such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
