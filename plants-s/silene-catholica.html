Silene catholica

Silene catholica, commonly known as the Catholic Lady's Mantle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

Silene catholica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
