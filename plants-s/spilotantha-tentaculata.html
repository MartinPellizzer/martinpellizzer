Spilotantha tentaculata

Spilotantha tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, white or yellowish tubercles. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
