Solanum glomuliflorum

Solanum glomuliflorum, commonly known as the globe-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas in the wild.
