Scabiosa atropurpurea var. amansii

Scabiosa atropurpurea var. amansii, commonly known as the "Cuckoo's Buttercup," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pink to purple, fluffy flower heads that resemble small clouds, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are actinomorphic, meaning they are radially symmetrical, and are composed of numerous small petals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
