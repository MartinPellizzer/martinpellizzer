Senna monozyx

Senna monozyx, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
