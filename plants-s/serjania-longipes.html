Serjania longipes

Serjania longipes, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or pink serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are eaten by various bird species.

Serjania longipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
