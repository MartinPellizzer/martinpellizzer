Sarracenia × cookiana

Sarracenia × cookiana, commonly known as the Cook's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with intricate patterns, which help attract prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × cookiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
