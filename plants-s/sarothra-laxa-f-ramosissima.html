Sarothra laxa f. ramosissima

Sarothra laxa f. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
