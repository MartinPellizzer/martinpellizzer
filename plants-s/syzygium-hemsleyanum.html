Syzygium hemsleyanum

Syzygium hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Hemsley's Syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

Syzygium hemsleyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
