Shomalia ghafooriana

Shomalia ghafooriana, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.

Shomalia ghafooriana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
