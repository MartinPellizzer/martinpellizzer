Sabal uresana

Sabal uresana, commonly known as the Florida palmetto, is a hardy palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves and robust, trunk-like structure that grows from a rhizomatous base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and crafts by indigenous communities.

Sabal uresana is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
