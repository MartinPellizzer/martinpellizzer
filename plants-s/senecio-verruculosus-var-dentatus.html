Senecio verruculosus var. dentatus

Senecio verruculosus var. dentatus, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly coating on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
