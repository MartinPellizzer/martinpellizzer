Syringa reticulata subsp. reticulata

Syringa reticulata subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white to lilac in early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with a serrated margin and a dense, bushy growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive, peeling bark.
