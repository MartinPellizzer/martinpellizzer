Sorghum vogelianum

Sorghum vogelianum, commonly known as the African millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible grains that are rich in nutrients and are used in various traditional foods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it an important crop for food security in drought-prone regions.

Its adaptability and resilience contribute to its significance in agricultural systems across sub-Saharan Africa.
