Sida rhombifolia subsp. canariensis

Sida rhombifolia subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as Canary velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant is typically found in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a sprawling growth habit and can form dense mats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
