Sinocalamus latiflorus

Sinocalamus latiflorus, commonly known as the broadflower bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles, which are typically borne at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
