Salix spathulifolia var. glabra

Salix spathulifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically smooth on both sides, giving the plant its varietal name "glabra," which means "smooth" in Latin. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are usually yellow-green in color. This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
