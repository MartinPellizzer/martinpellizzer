Stachys messeniaca var. canescens

Stachys messeniaca var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features erect, hairy stems that are often covered in a fine, whitish down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green hue, and they are often covered in fine hairs as well. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, producing showy spikes of violet to purple blooms.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
