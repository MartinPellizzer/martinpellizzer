Salsola gemmipara

Salsola gemmipara, commonly known as sandmat or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its ability to propagate through vegetative means, such as gemmae, allows it to thrive in disturbed or unstable soils.

Salsola gemmipara plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
