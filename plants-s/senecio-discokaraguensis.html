Senecio discokaraguensis

Senecio discokaraguensis, commonly known as the Karagwa groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern Cape province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a disc-like structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
