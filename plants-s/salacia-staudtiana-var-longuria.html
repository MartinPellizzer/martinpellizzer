Salacia staudtiana var. longuria

Salacia staudtiana var. longuria, commonly known as the long-leaf salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other members of the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and along stream banks.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
