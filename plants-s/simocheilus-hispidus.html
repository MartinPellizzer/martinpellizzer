Simocheilus hispidus

Simocheilus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of ponds and lakes.
