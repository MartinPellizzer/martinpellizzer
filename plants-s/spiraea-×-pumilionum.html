Spiraea × pumilionum

Spiraea × pumilionum, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its graceful, cascading branches.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate ornamental touch to gardens. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8. Its foliage turns a striking golden yellow in the fall, enhancing its seasonal appeal.

Spiraea × pumilionum is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
