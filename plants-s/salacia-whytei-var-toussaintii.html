Salacia whytei var. toussaintii

Salacia whytei var. toussaintii, commonly known as the Touloum or Touloum tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the bark is typically smooth and pale in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
