Solanum crepidotrichum

Solanum crepidotrichum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
