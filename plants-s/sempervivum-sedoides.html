Sempervivum sedoides

Sempervivum sedoides, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum sedoides is also known for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
