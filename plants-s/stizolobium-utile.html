Stizolobium utile

Stizolobium utile, commonly known as the useful legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. Stizolobium utile produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
