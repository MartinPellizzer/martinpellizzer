Swida oblonga var. griffithii

Swida oblonga var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's swida, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It features elongated, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for its vibrant autumn foliage.
