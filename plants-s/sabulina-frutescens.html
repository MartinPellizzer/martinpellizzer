Sabulina frutescens

Sabulina frutescens, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Sabulina frutescens is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
