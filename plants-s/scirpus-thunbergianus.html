Scirpus thunbergianus

Scirpus thunbergianus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a tall, reed-like wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. The plant has long, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color. Its roots form dense colonies, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus thunbergianus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
