Simsia exaristata

Simsia exaristata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
