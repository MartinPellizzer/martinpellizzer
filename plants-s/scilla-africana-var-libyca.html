Scilla africana var. libyca

Scilla africana var. libyca, commonly known as the Libyan scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
