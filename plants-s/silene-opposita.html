Silene opposita

Silene opposita, commonly known as the oppositeleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Silene opposita is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
