Salsola tragus subsp. grandiflora

Salsola tragus subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual shrub native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and disturbed environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.
