Sasa samaniana f. hidejiroana

Sasa samaniana f. hidejiroana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hidejiro bamboo, is a clump-forming plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value.

This bamboo variant is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
