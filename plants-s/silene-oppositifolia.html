Silene oppositifolia

Silene oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite arrangement of linear leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Silene oppositifolia is a perennial species that plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its cold-climate ecosystems.
