Sokinochloa australis

Sokinochloa australis, commonly known as the Australian grass or slender grass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or clumps. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowering stems are usually short and produce small, inconspicuous spikelets.

Sokinochloa australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
