Sasa yoigana

Sasa yoigana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy surface that contributes to its ornamental value.

Sasa yoigana thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forested areas across Japan and Korea.
