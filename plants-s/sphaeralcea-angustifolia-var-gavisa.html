Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. gavisa

Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. gavisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes in native plant gardens.
