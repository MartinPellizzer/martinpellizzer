Solanum trizygum var. tetrazygum

Solanum trizygum var. tetrazygum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deep violet to purple skin and elongated, egg-shaped fruits. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is edible when fully mature, offering a slightly bitter, nutty flavor.
