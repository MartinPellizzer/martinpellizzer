Selago appressa

Selago appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selago, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. Selago appressa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to dry conditions.
