Solanum habrocaulon

Solanum habrocaulon, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
