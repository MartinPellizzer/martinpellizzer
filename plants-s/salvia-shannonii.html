Salvia shannonii

Salvia shannonii, commonly known as Shannon's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Salvia shannonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
