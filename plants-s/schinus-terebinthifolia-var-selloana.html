Schinus terebinthifolia var. selloana

Schinus terebinthifolia var. selloana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in coastal and tropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
