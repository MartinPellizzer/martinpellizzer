Senecio mattfeldii

Senecio mattfeldii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in spring, are arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems.

Senecio mattfeldii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
