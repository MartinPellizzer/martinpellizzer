Sabulina verna subsp. hercynica

Sabulina verna subsp.

hercynica, commonly known as the spring rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
