Setaria itieri

Setaria itieri, commonly known as slender pigweed, is an annual grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria itieri is a common weed that can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
