Spatholobus compar

Spatholobus compar, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, flat seeds.

Spatholobus compar is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
