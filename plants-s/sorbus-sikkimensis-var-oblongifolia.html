Sorbus sikkimensis var. oblongifolia

Sorbus sikkimensis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Sikkim whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, elliptic to oblong leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, typically between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
