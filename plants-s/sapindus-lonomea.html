Sapindus lonomea

Sapindus lonomea, commonly known as the Chinese soapnut, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red when mature. The outer husk of the fruit contains a natural soap-like compound, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
