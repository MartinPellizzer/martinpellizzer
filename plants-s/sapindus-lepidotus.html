Sapindus lepidotus

Sapindus lepidotus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces smooth, spherical fruits that are covered in a thick, waxy coating. The seeds inside the fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural soap substitute.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
