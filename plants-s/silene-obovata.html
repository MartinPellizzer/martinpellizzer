Silene obovata

Silene obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Silene obovata is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
