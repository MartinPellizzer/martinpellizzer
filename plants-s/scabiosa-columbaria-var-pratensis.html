Scabiosa columbaria var. pratensis

Scabiosa columbaria var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open grasslands and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant for ecological gardens.
