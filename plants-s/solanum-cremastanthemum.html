Solanum cremastanthemum

Solanum cremastanthemum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Solanum cremastanthemum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
