Spiraea nutans

Spiraea nutans, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea nutans is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
