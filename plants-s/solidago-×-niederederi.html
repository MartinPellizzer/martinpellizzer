Solidago × niederederi

Solidago × niederederi, commonly known as the New York goldenrod, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Solidago nemoralis and Solidago decurrens.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its tall, branched stems and abundant yellow floral heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago × niederederi thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland areas.
