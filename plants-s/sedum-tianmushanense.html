Sedum tianmushanense

Sedum tianmushanense, commonly known as Tianmushan sedum, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, blooming in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
