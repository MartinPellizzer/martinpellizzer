Salix × autaretica

Salix × autaretica, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow-green in color and appear in early spring. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the autumn.

Salix × autaretica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and ability to thrive in moist soils.
