Stachys spinulosa var. glabrata

Stachys spinulosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
