Symphyotrichum × versicolor

Symphyotrichum × versicolor, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Symphyotrichum × versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
