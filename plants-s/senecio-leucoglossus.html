Senecio leucoglossus

Senecio leucoglossus, commonly known as the white-lipped senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white-lipped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads.

Senecio leucoglossus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
