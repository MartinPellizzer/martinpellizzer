Scorzonera tau-saghyz

Scorzonera tau-saghyz, commonly known as the Russian wild turnip, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head. Its root system is extensive and tuberous, which is a key feature for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible roots.

Scorzonera tau-saghyz is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
