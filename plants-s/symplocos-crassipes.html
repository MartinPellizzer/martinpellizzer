Symplocos crassipes

Symplocos crassipes, commonly known as the roughleaf waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Symplocos crassipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
