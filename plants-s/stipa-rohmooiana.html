Stipa rohmooiana

Stipa rohmooiana, commonly known as the Roehm's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

Stipa rohmooiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
