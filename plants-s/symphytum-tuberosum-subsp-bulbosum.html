Symphytum tuberosum subsp. bulbosum

Symphytum tuberosum subsp.

bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating.

The plant produces tall spikes of violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
