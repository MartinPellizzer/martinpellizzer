Suaeda jacquinii

Suaeda jacquinii, commonly known as saltbush or sea spurge, is a halophytic shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to high salt concentrations in its soil. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in unstable coastal soils.

Suaeda jacquinii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
