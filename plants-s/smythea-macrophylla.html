Smythea macrophylla

Smythea macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf smythea, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Smythea macrophylla is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
