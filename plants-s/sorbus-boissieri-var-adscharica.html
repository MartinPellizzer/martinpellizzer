Sorbus boissieri var. adscharica

Sorbus boissieri var. adscharica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically red or orange in color. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
