Sclerolaena × caput-casuarii

Sclerolaena × caput-casuarii, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Sclerolaena caput-casuarii and Sclerolaena hibiscifolia.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, hibiscus-like flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
