Streptocaulon baumii

Streptocaulon baumii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Streptocaulon baumii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
