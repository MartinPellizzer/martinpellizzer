Salvia gavilanensis

Salvia gavilanensis, commonly known as Gavilan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mexican state of Guanajuato.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma when crushed.

Salvia gavilanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Mexican medicine.
