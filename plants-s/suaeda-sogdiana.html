Suaeda sogdiana

Suaeda sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and China. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain water in dry conditions, and its small, yellowish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Suaeda sogdiana plays a role in stabilizing soils in coastal and desert areas due to its extensive root system.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
