Spiraea miyabei var. glabrata

Spiraea miyabei var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture, and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
