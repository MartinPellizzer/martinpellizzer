Salix × cryptodonta

Salix × cryptodonta, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that drape like curtains, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. It produces catkin-like inflorescences in early spring, which are male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters.

This plant is often found near water sources and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
