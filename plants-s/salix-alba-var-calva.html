Salix alba var. calva

Salix alba var. calva, commonly known as the white willow, is a variety of the white poplar native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. This species is often found in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental plant in gardens and a subject of study in ecological research.
