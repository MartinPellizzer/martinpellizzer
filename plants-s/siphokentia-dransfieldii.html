Siphokentia dransfieldii

Siphokentia dransfieldii, commonly known as the Dransfield's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
