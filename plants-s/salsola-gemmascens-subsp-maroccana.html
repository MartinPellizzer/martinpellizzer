Salsola gemmascens subsp. maroccana

Salsola gemmascens subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan Russian thistle, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and saline environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations. It has a compact growth habit with a branching structure, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is native to North Africa and the Canary Islands, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
