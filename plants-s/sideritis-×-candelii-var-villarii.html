Sideritis × candelii var. villarii

Sideritis × candelii var. villarii, commonly known as the Cretan sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal remedies.
