Salacia laurentii

Salacia laurentii, commonly known as the laurel salacia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Salacia laurentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
