Sciodaphyllum abyssinicum

Sciodaphyllum abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian ghost bamboo, is a unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to two meters. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sciodaphyllum abyssinicum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
