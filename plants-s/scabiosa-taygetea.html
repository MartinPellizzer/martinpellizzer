Scabiosa taygetea

Scabiosa taygetea, commonly known as the mountain scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa taygetea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
