Salix petiolaris var. rosmarinoides

Salix petiolaris var. rosmarinoides, commonly known as the rosemary willow, is a variant of the common willow that exhibits unique aromatic foliage.

Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and possess a fragrant, rosemary-like scent, distinguishing it from typical willow species. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces catkins in early spring, which are characteristic of the Salix genus.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
