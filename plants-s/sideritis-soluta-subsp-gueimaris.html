Sideritis soluta subsp. gueimaris

Sideritis soluta subsp.

gueimaris, commonly known as the Gueimaris sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
