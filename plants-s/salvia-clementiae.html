Salvia clementiae

Salvia clementiae, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to blue-purplish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia clementiae is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
