Smilax impressinervia

Smilax impressinervia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a distinctive, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Smilax impressinervia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its adaptability and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
