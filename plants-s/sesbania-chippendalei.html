Sesbania chippendalei

Sesbania chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

It typically grows as an annual or biennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
