Saussurea rotundifolia

Saussurea rotundifolia, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and a single, showy flower that blooms in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saussurea rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
