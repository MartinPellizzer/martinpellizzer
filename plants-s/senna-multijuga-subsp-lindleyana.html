Senna multijuga subsp. lindleyana

Senna multijuga subsp.

lindleyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
