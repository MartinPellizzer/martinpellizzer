Symplocos odoratissima var. wenzelii

Symplocos odoratissima var. wenzelii, commonly known as the fragrant wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
