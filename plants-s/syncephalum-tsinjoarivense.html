Syncephalum tsinjoarivense

Syncephalum tsinjoarivense, commonly known as the Madagascar sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
