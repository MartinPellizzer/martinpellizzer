Senecio doria subsp. danubialis

Senecio doria subsp.

danubialis, commonly known as the Danube Senecio, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in river valleys and along watercourses. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers in summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
