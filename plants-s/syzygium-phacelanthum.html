Syzygium phacelanthum

Syzygium phacelanthum, commonly known as the clustered apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, rounded crown and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Syzygium phacelanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
