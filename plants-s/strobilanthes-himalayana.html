Strobilanthes himalayana

Strobilanthes himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan flame flower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Strobilanthes himalayana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
