Solanum bussei

Solanum bussei, commonly known as the Bussei nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not cultivated for food but is sometimes studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
