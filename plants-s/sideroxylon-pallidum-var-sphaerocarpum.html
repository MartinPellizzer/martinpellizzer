Sideroxylon pallidum var. sphaerocarpum

Sideroxylon pallidum var. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the yellow ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically has smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
