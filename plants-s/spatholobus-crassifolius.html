Spatholobus crassifolius

Spatholobus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rainforest or coastal areas. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Spatholobus crassifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
