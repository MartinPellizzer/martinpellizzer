Sedum pedicellatum subsp. pedicellatum

Sedum pedicellatum subsp.

pedicellatum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to northern Europe. It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, rounded leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bright pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
