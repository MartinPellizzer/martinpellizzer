Sasa nankoensis

Sasa nankoensis, commonly known as the Nankai bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa nankoensis is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
