Sparganium minimum subvar. ratis

Sparganium minimum subvar. ratis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bur-reed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in marshes, lake margins, and slow-moving streams. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are spiky, three-sided burrs that help in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

This subvariety is distinguished by its reduced leaf width and smaller flower clusters compared to other forms of Sparganium.
