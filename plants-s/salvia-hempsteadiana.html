Salvia hempsteadiana

Salvia hempsteadiana, commonly known as the Mexican mint or purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
