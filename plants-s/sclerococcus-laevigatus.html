Sclerococcus laevigatus

Sclerococcus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-skin cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sparse, white spines. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators.

Due to its succulent nature, it is adapted to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for dry environments.
