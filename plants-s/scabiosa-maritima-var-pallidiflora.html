Scabiosa maritima var. pallidiflora

Scabiosa maritima var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, adapted to tolerate salty air and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
