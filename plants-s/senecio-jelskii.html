Senecio jelskii

Senecio jelskii, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Senecio jelskii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
