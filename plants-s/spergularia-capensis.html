Spergularia capensis

Spergularia capensis, commonly known as the Cape sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Spergularia capensis is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar adaptations to drought and salt.
