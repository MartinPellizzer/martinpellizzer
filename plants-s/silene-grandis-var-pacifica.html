Silene grandis var. pacifica

Silene grandis var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, hairy leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for small invertebrates.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
