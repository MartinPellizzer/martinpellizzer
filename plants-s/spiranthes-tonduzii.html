Spiranthes tonduzii

Spiranthes tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's spirea, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, often arranged in a tight, curling pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
