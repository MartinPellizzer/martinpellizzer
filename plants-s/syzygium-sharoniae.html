Syzygium sharoniae

Syzygium sharoniae, commonly known as Sharon's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the island of Socotra. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant red fruits.
