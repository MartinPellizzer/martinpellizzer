Silene vagans

Silene vagans, commonly known as the wandering catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silene vagans has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
