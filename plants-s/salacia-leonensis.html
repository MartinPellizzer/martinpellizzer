Salacia leonensis

Salacia leonensis, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along riverbanks and in humid forests. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, though it is not widely cultivated commercially.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its uses.
