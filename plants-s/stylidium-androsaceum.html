Stylidium androsaceum

Stylidium androsaceum, commonly known as the pink sandpiper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Stylidium androsaceum is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
