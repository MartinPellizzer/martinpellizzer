Sasa paniculata f. hispidula

Sasa paniculata f. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid form of the Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
