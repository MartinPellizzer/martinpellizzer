Stuckenia filiformis

Stuckenia filiformis, commonly known as slender water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-like and can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late summer.

Stuckenia filiformis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
