Scutellaria discolor var. elatior

Scutellaria discolor var. elatior, commonly known as the greater blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and are attractive to pollinators.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
