Schoenus inanis

Schoenus inanis, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

Schoenus inanis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
