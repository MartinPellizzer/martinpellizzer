Silene uniflora subsp. glareosa

Silene uniflora subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the showy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
