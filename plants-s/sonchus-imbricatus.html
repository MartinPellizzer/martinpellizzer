Sonchus imbricatus

Sonchus imbricatus, commonly known as common sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a rosette of smooth, pale green leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a head, surrounded by pale green bracts, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
