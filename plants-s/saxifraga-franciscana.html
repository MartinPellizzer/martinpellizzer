Saxifraga franciscana

Saxifraga franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and maritime chaparral habitats. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early spring.

Saxifraga franciscana is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
