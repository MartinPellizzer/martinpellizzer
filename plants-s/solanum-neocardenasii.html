Solanum neocardenasii

Solanum neocardenasii, commonly known as the Neocardenas's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
