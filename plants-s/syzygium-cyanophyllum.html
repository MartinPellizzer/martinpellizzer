Syzygium cyanophyllum

Syzygium cyanophyllum, commonly known as the blue-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking blue-gray color when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium cyanophyllum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in various herbal remedies.
