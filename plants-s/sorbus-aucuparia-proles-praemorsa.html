Sorbus aucuparia proles praemorsa

Sorbus aucuparia proles praemorsa, commonly known as the white-thorned rowan, is a rare variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and small, red berries that mature in the autumn. The plant is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in mountainous or forested areas. Its botanical features include thorny branches and compound leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
