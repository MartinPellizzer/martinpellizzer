Scutellaria heydei

Scutellaria heydei, commonly known as the blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria heydei is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming effects.
