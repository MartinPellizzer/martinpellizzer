Sarcocornia andina

Sarcocornia andina, commonly known as the Andean mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to coastal regions of South America. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and salt, aiding in its survival in arid and salty conditions. It often grows in tidal flats and salt marshes, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
