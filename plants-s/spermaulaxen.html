Spermaulaxen

Spermaulaxen, commonly known as the "liverworts," are non-vascular plants belonging to the division Marchantiophyta.

They typically grow in moist, shaded environments and are characterized by their simple, leaf-like structures called thallus. Spermaulaxen reproduce through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes. They play an important role in soil stabilization and serve as a food source for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Despite their small size, they are among the oldest groups of land plants, with a rich evolutionary history.
