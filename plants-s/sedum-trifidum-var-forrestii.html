Sedum trifidum var. forrestii

Sedum trifidum var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often branched, and its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
