Senecio sieboldii

Senecio sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in woodland and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
