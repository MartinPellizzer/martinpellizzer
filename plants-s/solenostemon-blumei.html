Solenostemon blumei

Solenostemon blumei, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, mist-like clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Solenostemon blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
