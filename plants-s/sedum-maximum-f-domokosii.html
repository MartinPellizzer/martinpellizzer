Sedum maximum f. domokosii

Sedum maximum f. domokosii, commonly known as the Hungarian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Hungary and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color and have a slightly waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
