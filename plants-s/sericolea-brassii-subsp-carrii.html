Sericolea brassii subsp. carrii

Sericolea brassii subsp.

carrii, commonly known as the Carri's sericolea, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats, playing a role in local ecosystems.
