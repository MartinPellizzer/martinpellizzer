Salvia blancoana subsp. amethystea

Salvia blancoana subsp.

amethystea, commonly known as the amethyst sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
