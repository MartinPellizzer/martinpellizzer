Solanum mangaschae

Solanum mangaschae, commonly known as the Mangaschae nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it thrives in high-altitude environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
