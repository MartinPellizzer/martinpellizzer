Sorbus × robertsonii

Sorbus × robertsonii, commonly known as the Townsend's rowan, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its ornamental value and unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, with a rounded or irregular shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
