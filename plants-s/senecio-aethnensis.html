Senecio aethnensis

Senecio aethnensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio aethnensis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
