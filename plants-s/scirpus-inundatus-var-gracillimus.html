Scirpus inundatus var. gracillimus

Scirpus inundatus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer. Its rhizomes are often used in wetland restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
