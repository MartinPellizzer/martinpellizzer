Saxifraga virginiensis var. cuneata

Saxifraga virginiensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, white or pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
