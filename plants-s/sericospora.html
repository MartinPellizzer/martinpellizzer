Sericospora

Sericospora, commonly known as the "velvet spore," is a genus of fungi characterized by its unique spore morphology.

The spores are typically smooth and covered in a fine, velvety layer, which aids in their dispersal. These fungi are often found in soil and decaying organic matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in ecosystems. They are not typically parasitic but can form symbiotic relationships with certain plants.

The genus is of interest in mycology due to its distinct spore structure and ecological role.
