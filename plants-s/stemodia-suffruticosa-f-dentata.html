Stemodia suffruticosa f. dentata

Stemodia suffruticosa f. dentata, commonly known as the serrated stemodia, is a shrub-like plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It features a woody stem with a rough, often branched structure, and its leaves are typically small, simple, and sometimes serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
