Saguaster macarthurii

Saguaster macarthurii, commonly known as the MacArthur's saguaro, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically shorter and more sparse compared to other saguaro species. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem and relying on minimal rainfall for survival.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
