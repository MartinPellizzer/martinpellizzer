Sedum strobiliforme

Sedum strobiliforme, commonly known as the pineapple sedum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
