Sison aureum

Sison aureum, commonly known as golden sison, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Sison aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
