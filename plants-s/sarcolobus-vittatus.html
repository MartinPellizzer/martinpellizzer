Sarcolobus vittatus

Sarcolobus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a spotted or streaked pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Sarcolobus vittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
