Scleranthus obsoletus

Scleranthus obsoletus, commonly known as the slender sandrocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Scleranthus obsoletus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
