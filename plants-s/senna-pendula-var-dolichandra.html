Senna pendula var. dolichandra

Senna pendula var. dolichandra, commonly known as the longpod senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
