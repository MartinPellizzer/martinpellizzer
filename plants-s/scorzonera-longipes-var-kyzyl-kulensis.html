Scorzonera longipes var. kyzyl-kulensis

Scorzonera longipes var. kyzyl-kulensis, commonly known as the Kyzyl-Kul scurvygrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
