Syzygium dansiei

Syzygium dansiei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Syzygium dansiei is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its durable wood.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
