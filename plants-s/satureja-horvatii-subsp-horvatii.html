Satureja horvatii subsp. horvatii

Satureja horvatii subsp.

horvatii, commonly known as Horvat's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The leaves are used in traditional cooking and have a strong, pungent aroma similar to oregano and thyme.
