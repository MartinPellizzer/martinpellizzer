Solanum urubambae f. velutinum

Solanum urubambae f. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a rare and distinctive botanical species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, typically purple or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
