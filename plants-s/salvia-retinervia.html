Salvia retinervia

Salvia retinervia, commonly known as the net-veined sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia retinervia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
