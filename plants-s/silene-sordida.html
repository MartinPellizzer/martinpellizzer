Silene sordida

Silene sordida, commonly known as the dirty catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, often pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Silene sordida is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
