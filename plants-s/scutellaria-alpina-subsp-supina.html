Scutellaria alpina subsp. supina

Scutellaria alpina subsp.

supina, commonly known as the alpine skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
