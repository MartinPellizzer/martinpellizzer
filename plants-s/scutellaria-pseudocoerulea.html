Scutellaria pseudocoerulea

Scutellaria pseudocoerulea, commonly known as the blue scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria pseudocoerulea is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
