Stevia jorullensis var. humboldtiana

Stevia jorullensis var. humboldtiana, commonly known as Humboldt's stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it has gained attention in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
