Securidaca marginata

Securidaca marginata, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated margins. The bark is smooth and greyish, while the roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
