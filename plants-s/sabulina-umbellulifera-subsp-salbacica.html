Sabulina umbellulifera subsp. salbacica

Sabulina umbellulifera subsp.

salbacica, commonly known as the sand lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in umbellate clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies salbacica is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and geographic distribution compared to other subspecies of Sabulina umbellulifera.
