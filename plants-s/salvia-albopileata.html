Salvia albopileata

Salvia albopileata, commonly known as the white pilea sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
