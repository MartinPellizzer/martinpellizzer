Silene schizopetala

Silene schizopetala, commonly known as the split-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. Silene schizopetala is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other notable species such as carnations and moss campion.
