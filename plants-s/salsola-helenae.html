Salsola helenae

Salsola helenae, commonly known as Helen's saltwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a common species in desert environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert steppe biomes.

Salsola helenae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
