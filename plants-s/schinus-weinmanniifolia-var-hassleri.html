Schinus weinmanniifolia var. hassleri

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, violet-blue berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.
