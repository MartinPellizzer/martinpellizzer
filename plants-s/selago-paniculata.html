Selago paniculata

Selago paniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf paniculata, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Selago paniculata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
