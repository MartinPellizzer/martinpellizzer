Salvia alliaria

Salvia alliaria, commonly known as garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that emit a mild garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Salvia alliaria is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value in gardens.
