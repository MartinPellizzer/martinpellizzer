Scleranthus johannis

Scleranthus johannis, commonly known as the John's thorn or Johannis thorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Scleranthus in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant produces dry, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its foliage is generally hairy, and it has a tendency to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
