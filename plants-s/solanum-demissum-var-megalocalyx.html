Solanum demissum var. megalocalyx

Solanum demissum var. megalocalyx, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
