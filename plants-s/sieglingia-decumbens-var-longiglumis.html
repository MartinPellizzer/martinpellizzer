Sieglingia decumbens var. longiglumis

Sieglingia decumbens var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume sieglingia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with the distinctive elongated glumes that give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
