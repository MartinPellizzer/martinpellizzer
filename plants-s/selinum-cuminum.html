Selinum cuminum

Selinum cuminum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, dry fruits called seeds. Cumin seeds are widely used as a spice in various cuisines around the world.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
