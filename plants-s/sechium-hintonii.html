Sechium hintonii

Sechium hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often bitter fruits. The plant features palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic fruit. Its fruit is typically harvested when mature, though it can be used in various culinary applications in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
