Selaginella serpens

Selaginella serpens, commonly known as the sinuous clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and is adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Selaginella serpens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
