Synotis chenopodiifolia

Synotis chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Synotis chenopodiifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in desert environments.
