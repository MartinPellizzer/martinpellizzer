Scholtzia oleosa

Scholtzia oleosa, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a fluffy, seed-bearing head at the end of each stem. Scholtzia oleosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
