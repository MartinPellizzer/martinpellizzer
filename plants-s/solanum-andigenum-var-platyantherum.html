Solanum andigenum var. platyantherum

Solanum andigenum var. platyantherum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important species such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
