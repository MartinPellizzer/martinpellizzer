Silene setacea var. maroccana

Silene setacea var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Silene setacea var. maroccana is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
