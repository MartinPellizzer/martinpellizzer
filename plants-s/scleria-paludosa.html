Scleria paludosa

Scleria paludosa, commonly known as the marsh sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scleria paludosa thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
