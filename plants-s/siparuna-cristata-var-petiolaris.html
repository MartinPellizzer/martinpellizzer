Siparuna cristata var. petiolaris

Siparuna cristata var. petiolaris, commonly known as the Petiolaris Siparuna, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pink or purple hue. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated margin.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique floral display and aromatic properties.
