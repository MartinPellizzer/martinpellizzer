Spartium rhodorhizoides

Spartium rhodorhizoides, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Spartium rhodorhizoides is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
