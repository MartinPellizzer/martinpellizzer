Statice gmelini var. scoparia

Statice gmelini var. scoparia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong sea breezes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
