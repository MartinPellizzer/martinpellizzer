Schefflera tridentata

Schefflera tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Schefflera tridentata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
