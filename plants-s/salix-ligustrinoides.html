Salix ligustrinoides

Salix ligustrinoides, commonly known as the Chinese willow or narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
