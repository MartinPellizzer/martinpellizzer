Setaria brachiata

Setaria brachiata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, and it is commonly found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Setaria brachiata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
