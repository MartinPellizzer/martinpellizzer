Shuttleworthia sulphurea

Shuttleworthia sulphurea, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in drought conditions.

Shuttleworthia sulphurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
