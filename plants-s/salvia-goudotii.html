Salvia goudotii

Salvia goudotii, commonly known as the Goudot's sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Salvia goudotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with dry, sunny conditions.
