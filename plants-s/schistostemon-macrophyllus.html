Schistostemon macrophyllus

Schistostemon macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf schistostemon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
