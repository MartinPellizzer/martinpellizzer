Saxifraga burseriana tridentina

Saxifraga burseriana tridentina, commonly known as the Trinidadian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its thick, waxy leaf surface.

This species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
