Selaginella texta

Selaginella texta, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and can survive in dry conditions by entering a dormant state.

Selaginella texta is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates and is used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
