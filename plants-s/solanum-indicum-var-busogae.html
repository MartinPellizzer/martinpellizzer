Solanum indicum var. busogae

Solanum indicum var. busogae, commonly known as the Philippine nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into round, orange-red berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
