Sesbania tetraptera subsp. rogersii

Sesbania tetraptera subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
