Sideritis arborescens f. valdelignosa

Sideritis arborescens f. valdelignosa, commonly known as the Valdelignosa rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue to violet, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
