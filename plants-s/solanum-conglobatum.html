Solanum conglobatum

Solanum conglobatum, commonly known as the red globe nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red and purple. The plant's fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns bright red when mature. Solanum conglobatum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
