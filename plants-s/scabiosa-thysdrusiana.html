Scabiosa thysdrusiana

Scabiosa thysdrusiana, commonly known as the Greek scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa thysdrusiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
