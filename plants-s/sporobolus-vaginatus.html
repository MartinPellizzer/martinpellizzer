Sporobolus vaginatus

Sporobolus vaginatus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often covered in silky hairs. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sporobolus vaginatus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
