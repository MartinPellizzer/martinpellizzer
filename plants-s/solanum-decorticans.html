Solanum decorticans

Solanum decorticans, commonly known as the "bark pepper" or "tree tomato," is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that turn red when mature. These berries are edible and have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

Solanum decorticans is often cultivated for its fruit and has traditional uses in various cuisines and medicinal practices.
