Scirpus compressus subsp. bonannii

Scirpus compressus subsp.

bonannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a compressed appearance that gives it its scientific name. Scirpus compressus subsp.

bonannii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic life.
