Staphylea bumalda var. viridis

Staphylea bumalda var. viridis, commonly known as green satinflower, is a variant of the satinflower plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

The varietal name 'viridis' refers to its distinctive green coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Staphylea.
