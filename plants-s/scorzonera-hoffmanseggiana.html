Scorzonera hoffmanseggiana

Scorzonera hoffmanseggiana, commonly known as Hoffman's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Scorzonera hoffmanseggiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
