Satureja subspicata f. scabrifolia

Satureja subspicata f. scabrifolia, commonly known as the scabrous leaf savory, is a subspecies of the common savory plant.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its aromatic properties.
