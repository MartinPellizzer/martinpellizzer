Solanum imerinense

Solanum imerinense, commonly known as the Ime River nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum imerinense is noted for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
