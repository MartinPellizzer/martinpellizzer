Syngonanthus philodicoides var. morii

Syngonanthus philodicoides var. morii, commonly known as the Morii's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slight hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
