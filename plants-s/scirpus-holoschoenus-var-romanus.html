Scirpus holoschoenus var. romanus

Scirpus holoschoenus var. romanus, commonly known as the Roman bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush genus Scirpus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often reaching several feet in length.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikelets, and are typically wind-pollinated, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.
