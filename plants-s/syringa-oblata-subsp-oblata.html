Syringa oblata subsp. oblata

Syringa oblata subsp.

oblata, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy floral display and elegant foliage.
