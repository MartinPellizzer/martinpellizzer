Symplocos myrtacea var. pubescens

Symplocos myrtacea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
