Sabatia calycosa

Sabatia calycosa, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Sabatia calycosa thrives in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands.
