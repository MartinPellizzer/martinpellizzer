Scaevola parvifolia

Scaevola parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, giving the plant a neat appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
