Silene gynodioica

Silene gynodioica, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene gynodioica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
