Silene sessiliflora

Silene sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
