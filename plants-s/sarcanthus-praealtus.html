Sarcanthus praealtus

Sarcanthus praealtus, commonly known as the tall sarcanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to two meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sarcanthus praealtus is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
