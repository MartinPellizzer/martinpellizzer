Sobralia sancti-josephi

Sobralia sancti-josephi, commonly known as the St. Joseph's sobralia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features erect, branching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
