Symplocos viridissima

Symplocos viridissima, commonly known as the greenleaf waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos viridissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
