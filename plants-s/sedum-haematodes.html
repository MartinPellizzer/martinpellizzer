Sedum haematodes

Sedum haematodes, commonly known as bloodstone, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Bloodstone is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
