Spiraea prunifolia var. pseudoprunifolia

Spiraea prunifolia var. pseudoprunifolia, commonly known as the False Cherry Spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a finely toothed margin and a pale underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value, particularly during the flowering season.
