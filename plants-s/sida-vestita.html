Sida vestita

Sida vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sida vestita is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
