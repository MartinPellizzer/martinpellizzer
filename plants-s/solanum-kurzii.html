Solanum kurzii

Solanum kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
