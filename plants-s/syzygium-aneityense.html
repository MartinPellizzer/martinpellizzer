Syzygium aneityense

Syzygium aneityense, commonly known as the Java apple or wild mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a pinkish rind and a segmented, juicy interior.

Syzygium aneityense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
