Schefflera cuneata

Schefflera cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions. The plant features compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern, with each leaflet being oval and pointed. Schefflera cuneata is known for its adaptability, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It also produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries, though these are not typically prominent in cultivated specimens.
