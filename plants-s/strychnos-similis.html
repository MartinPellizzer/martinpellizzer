Strychnos similis

Strychnos similis, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
