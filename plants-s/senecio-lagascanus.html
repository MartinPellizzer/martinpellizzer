Senecio lagascanus

Senecio lagascanus, commonly known as the Lagascanus groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in late spring.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
