Staurogyne atropurpurea

Staurogyne atropurpurea, commonly known as purplestem watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in the southeastern United States.

It has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and purple underneath, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, making it less noticeable to the untrained eye. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often classified as a noxious weed in some regions.
