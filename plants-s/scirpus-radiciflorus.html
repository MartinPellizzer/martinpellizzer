Scirpus radiciflorus

Scirpus radiciflorus, commonly known as the flowering bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along the edges of lakes, ponds, and marshes. Its root system is robust, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus radiciflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
