Sisymbrium isatidifolium

Sisymbrium isatidifolium, commonly known as sweet clover, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Sweet clover is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in agricultural settings for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
