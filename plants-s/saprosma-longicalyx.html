Saprosma longicalyx

Saprosma longicalyx, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, clustered white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, red, fleshy drupes that attract birds and other wildlife.

Saprosma longicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
