Stachytarpheta × speciosa

Stachytarpheta × speciosa, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flower clusters that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Stachytarpheta × speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
