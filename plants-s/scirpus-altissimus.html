Scirpus altissimus

Scirpus altissimus, commonly known as tall bulrush, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus altissimus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
