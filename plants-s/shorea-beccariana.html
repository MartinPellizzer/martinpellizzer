Shorea beccariana

Shorea beccariana, commonly known as the beccariana rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a high oil content. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its timber but is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
