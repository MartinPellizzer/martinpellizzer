Soulangia oleifolia

Soulangia oleifolia, commonly known as the oil plant or African oil bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing a valuable oilseed that is used for culinary and industrial purposes. Its roots can fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

Soulangia oleifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
