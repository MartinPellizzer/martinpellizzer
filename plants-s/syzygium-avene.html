Syzygium avene

Syzygium avene, commonly known as the white apple or white lychee, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-white fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. Syzygium avene is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Its cultivation requires warm, humid climates and well-drained soil to thrive.
