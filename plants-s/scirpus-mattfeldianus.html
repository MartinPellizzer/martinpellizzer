Scirpus mattfeldianus

Scirpus mattfeldianus, commonly known as Mattfeld's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland environments in Europe and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshes, lake margins, and riverbanks. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a distinct midrib, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Scirpus mattfeldianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
