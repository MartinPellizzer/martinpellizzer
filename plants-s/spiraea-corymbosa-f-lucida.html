Spiraea corymbosa f. lucida

Spiraea corymbosa f. lucida, commonly known as the shining silver weeping spirea, is a flowering shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its bright, showy blooms and attractive foliage.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
