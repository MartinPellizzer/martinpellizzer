Sideritis pastoris

Sideritis pastoris, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, hairy leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis pastoris is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
