Senecio mikanioides

Senecio mikanioides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features dense, silvery-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
