Sinarundinaria brevipes

Sinarundinaria brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae) that exhibits characteristics of both grasses and bamboos.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Thailand, and Vietnam. This plant is notable for its compact size, typically growing no taller than 30 centimeters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Sinarundinaria brevipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a transitional species between grasses and true bamboos, offering insights into the evolutionary relationships within the Poaceae family.
