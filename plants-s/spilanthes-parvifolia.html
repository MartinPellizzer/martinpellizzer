Spilanthes parvifolia

Spilanthes parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered cress, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy.

The plant is known for its peppery scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
