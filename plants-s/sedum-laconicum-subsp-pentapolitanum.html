Sedum laconicum subsp. pentapolitanum

Sedum laconicum subsp.

pentapolitanum, commonly known as the Pentapolitan sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are usually blue-gray or green, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
