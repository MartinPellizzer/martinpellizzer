Silene inflata var. glareosa

Silene inflata var. glareosa, commonly known as the showy bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
