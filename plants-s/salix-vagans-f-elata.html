Salix vagans f. elata

Salix vagans f. elata, commonly known as the tall weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species characterized by its elongated, drooping branches.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in areas with cold climates. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often silvery beneath. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins during the spring.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and along water features.
