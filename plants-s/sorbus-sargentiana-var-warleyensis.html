Sorbus sargentiana var. warleyensis

Sorbus sargentiana var. warleyensis, commonly known as the Warley rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its white, clusters of flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
