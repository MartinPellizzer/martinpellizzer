Stachys foliosa

Stachys foliosa, commonly known as the leafy stachys or velvetleaf hedge parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered in a fine, silky down, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Stachys foliosa is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
