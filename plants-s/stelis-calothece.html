Stelis calothece

Stelis calothece, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Stelis calothece is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
