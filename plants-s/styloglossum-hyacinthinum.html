Styloglossum hyacinthinum

Styloglossum hyacinthinum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, though it is often classified in the Cyperaceae family due to its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its preference for moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often blue or violet in color. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among plants in its family.

Styloglossum hyacinthinum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
