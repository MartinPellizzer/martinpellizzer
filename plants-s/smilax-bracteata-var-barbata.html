Smilax bracteata var. barbata

Smilax bracteata var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, spiny, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense thickets.
