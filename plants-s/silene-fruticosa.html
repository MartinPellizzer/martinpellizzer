Silene fruticosa

Silene fruticosa, commonly known as the redstem catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in summer. Silene fruticosa produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
