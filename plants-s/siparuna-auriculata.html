Siparuna auriculata

Siparuna auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Siparuna auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
