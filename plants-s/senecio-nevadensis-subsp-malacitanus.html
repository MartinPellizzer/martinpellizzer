Senecio nevadensis subsp. malacitanus

Senecio nevadensis subsp.

malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan skyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
