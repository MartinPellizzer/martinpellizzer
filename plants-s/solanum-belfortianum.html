Solanum belfortianum

Solanum belfortianum, commonly known as the Belfort's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
