Salvia cyanocephala subsp. macrostigmantha

Salvia cyanocephala subsp.

macrostigmantha, commonly known as the bluehead sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
