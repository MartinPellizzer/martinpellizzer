Secale cereale var. tuberculocaducum

Secale cereale var. tuberculocaducum, commonly known as the tuberculate wheatgrass, is a variant of wheatgrass characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant exhibits tuberculate (wart-like) structures on its leaves and stems, which are distinctive botanical traits. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, awnless spikelets.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in herbal remedies and livestock feed.
