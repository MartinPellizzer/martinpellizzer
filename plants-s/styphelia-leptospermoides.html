Styphelia leptospermoides

Styphelia leptospermoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, waxy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal heaths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
