Santiria costata

Santiria costata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical classification places it in the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes.

Santiria costata is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in ornamental gardening.
