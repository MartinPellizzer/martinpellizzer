Scabiosa monspeliensis

Scabiosa monspeliensis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, adapted to thrive in rocky or dry soils. It is part of the Caprifoliaceae family and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
