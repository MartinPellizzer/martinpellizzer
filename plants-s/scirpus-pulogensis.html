Scirpus pulogensis

Scirpus pulogensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, brownish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus pulogensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
