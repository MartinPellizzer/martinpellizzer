Sium latifolium var. univittatum

Sium latifolium var. univittatum, commonly known as broadleaf water pepper, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its aromatic leaves and stems have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
