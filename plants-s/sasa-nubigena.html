Sasa nubigena

Sasa nubigena, commonly known as cloud bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sasa nubigena thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
