Syzygium finetii

Syzygium finetii, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, followed by edible, round, red berries.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its timber in traditional crafts and furniture.
