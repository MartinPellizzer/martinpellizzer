Soldevilla setosa

Soldevilla setosa, commonly known as the hairy soldevilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
