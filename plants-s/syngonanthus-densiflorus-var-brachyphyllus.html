Syngonanthus densiflorus var. brachyphyllus

Syngonanthus densiflorus var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming clumps of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
