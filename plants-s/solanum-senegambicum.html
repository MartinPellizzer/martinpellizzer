Solanum senegambicum

Solanum senegambicum, commonly known as the Senegambian nightshade, is a shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

It is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
