Scrophularia hirta

Scrophularia hirta, commonly known as hairy scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name, and produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia hirta is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
