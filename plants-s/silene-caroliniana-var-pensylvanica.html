Silene caroliniana var. pensylvanica

Silene caroliniana var. pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in a branched cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
