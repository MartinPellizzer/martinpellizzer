Strobilanthes obtusibracteata

Strobilanthes obtusibracteata, commonly known as the "Malay ghost flower," is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, lantern-like inflorescences that appear during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are typically pale green or white, with showy bracts that contribute to its ethereal appearance.
