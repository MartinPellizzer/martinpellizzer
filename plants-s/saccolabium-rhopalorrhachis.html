Saccolabium rhopalorrhachis

Saccolabium rhopalorrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is occasionally cultivated in dryland gardens.
