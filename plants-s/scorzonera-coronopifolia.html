Scorzonera coronopifolia

Scorzonera coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Scorzonera coronopifolia is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
