Stelis rudolphiana

Stelis rudolphiana, commonly known as the Rudolph's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale green with intricate patterns, and it produces a single, elongated spike bearing multiple blooms.

Stelis rudolphiana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
