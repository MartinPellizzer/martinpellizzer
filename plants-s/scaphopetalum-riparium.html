Scaphopetalum riparium

Scaphopetalum riparium, commonly known as the riverbank bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and other wetland habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is often found in regions with a temperate climate.

Scaphopetalum riparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, aquatic environments.
