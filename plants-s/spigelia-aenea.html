Spigelia aenea

Spigelia aenea, commonly known as the copperleaf or ironleaf, is a strikingly colored plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red foliage, which stands out against the greenery of its habitat. Its flowers, which are tubular and often reddish or orange, are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Spigelia aenea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
