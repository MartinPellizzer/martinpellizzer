Syringa palibiniana var. lactea

Syringa palibiniana var. lactea, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and pleasant fragrance, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
