Sideritis calduchii

Sideritis calduchii, commonly known as the Calduch's ironweed, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Sideritis calduchii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
