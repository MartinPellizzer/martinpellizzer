Senecio prolixus

Senecio prolixus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio prolixus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
