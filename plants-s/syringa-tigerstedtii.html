Syringa tigerstedtii

Syringa tigerstedtii, commonly known as the Siberian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, and often pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Syringa tigerstedtii is valued for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
