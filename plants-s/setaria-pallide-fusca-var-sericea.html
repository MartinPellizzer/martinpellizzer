Setaria pallide-fusca var. sericea

Setaria pallide-fusca var. sericea, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and spikelets that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. Its reproductive structures consist of small, spike-like clusters that produce wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
