Stachys fragilis f. serpentini

Stachys fragilis f. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail or snake's tail, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are fragrant when crushed, and the species is known for its delicate, cascading appearance.

It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
