Stipa caucasica subsp. nikolai

Stipa caucasica subsp.

nikolai, commonly known as the Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces tall, slender spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
