Scirpus mitsukurianus

Scirpus mitsukurianus, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, pond edges, and riverbanks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scirpus mitsukurianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
