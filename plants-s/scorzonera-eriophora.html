Scorzonera eriophora

Scorzonera eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Scorzonera eriophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
