Scilla obtusifolia var. fallax

Scilla obtusifolia var. fallax, commonly known as the autumn scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
