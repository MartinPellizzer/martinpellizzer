Stachys anamurensis

Stachys anamurensis, commonly known as the Turkey thyme or Anamur thyme, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys anamurensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
