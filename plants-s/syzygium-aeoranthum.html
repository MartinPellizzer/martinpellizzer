Syzygium aeoranthum

Syzygium aeoranthum, commonly known as the sky apple, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium aeoranthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family.
