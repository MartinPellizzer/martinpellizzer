Salicornia fruticosa var. ambigua

Salicornia fruticosa var. ambigua, commonly known as glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. This species thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other areas with high salinity. The plant produces tiny flowers in clusters, typically pink or red, during the summer months.

It is often used in culinary applications and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
