Salsola villosa

Salsola villosa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woolly, silver-green foliage and is native to temperate regions of Eurasia. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Salsola villosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through its seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
