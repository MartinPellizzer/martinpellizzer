Spiraea ulmaria f. denudata

Spiraea ulmaria f. denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf meadow spirea, is a variant of the meadow spirea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically reduced or absent, giving it its "nakedleaf" appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
