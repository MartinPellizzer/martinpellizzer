Sarcanthus fissicors

Sarcanthus fissicors, commonly known as the fissured thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to arid and rocky regions, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves have a distinctive fissured appearance, giving it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across suitable habitats.
