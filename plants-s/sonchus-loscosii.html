Sonchus loscosii

Sonchus loscosii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer.

Sonchus loscosii is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in its native range.
