Sisymbrium reflexum

Sisymbrium reflexum, commonly known as the hoary salsify, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and covered with a powdery coating, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Sisymbrium reflexum is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
