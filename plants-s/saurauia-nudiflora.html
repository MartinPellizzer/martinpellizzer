Saurauia nudiflora

Saurauia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower saurauia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often leathery and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear before the leaves in early spring. Saurauia nudiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
