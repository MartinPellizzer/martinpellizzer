Senecio bulleyanus

Senecio bulleyanus, commonly known as the Bulley's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
