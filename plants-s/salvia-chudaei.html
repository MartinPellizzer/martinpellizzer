Salvia chudaei

Salvia chudaei, commonly known as the Chudaei sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia chudaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
