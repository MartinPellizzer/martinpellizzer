Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. coccinea

Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. coccinea, commonly known as red-tipped spirea, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce showy, red-tinged bracts. The foliage is typically green, with the stems displaying a distinctive red or pink hue, especially in cooler weather. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is valued for its compact growth habit and extended blooming period, which can last from late spring through early autumn.
