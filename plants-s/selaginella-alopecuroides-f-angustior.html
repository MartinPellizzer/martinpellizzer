Selaginella alopecuroides f. angustior

Selaginella alopecuroides f. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a variant of the spikemoss genus characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae, and it exhibits typical spikemoss morphology with a rosette-like structure and spore-producing strobila. This fern ally is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually a deep green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various small organisms.
