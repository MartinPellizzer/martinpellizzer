Salix bebbiana f. capreifolia

Salix bebbiana f. capreifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf willow, is a shrub native to the northern United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its distinctive pinkish-red to reddish-brown bark. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip, and they are often tinged with red on the underside. It produces catkins that are typically reddish-brown, and it blooms in early spring.

This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats.
