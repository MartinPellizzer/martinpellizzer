Symphyotrichum × woldenii

Symphyotrichum × woldenii, commonly known as the New England aster hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are usually purple to pink in color, with yellow central disk flowers, and it produces seeds in the form of small, dry fruits called achenes.

This hybrid species is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
