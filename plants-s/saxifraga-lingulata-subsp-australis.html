Saxifraga lingulata subsp. australis

Saxifraga lingulata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian spoon-leaf saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are spoon-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Saxifraga lingulata subsp.

australis is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing its resilience to various climatic conditions.
