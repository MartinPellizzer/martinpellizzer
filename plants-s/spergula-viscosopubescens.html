Spergula viscosopubescens

Spergula viscosopubescens, commonly known as sticky pubescent sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
