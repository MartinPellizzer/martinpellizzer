Sophronitella violacea

Sophronitella violacea, commonly known as the violet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapted to saline and arid conditions. The plant features small, fragrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
