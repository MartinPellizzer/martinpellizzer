Salvia bicolor

Salvia bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia bicolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
