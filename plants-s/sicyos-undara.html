Sicyos undara

Sicyos undara, commonly known as the giant burr vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive, spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit make it a potential invasive species in some areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the legume or bean family.
