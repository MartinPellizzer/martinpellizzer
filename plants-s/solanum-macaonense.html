Solanum macaonense

Solanum macaonense, commonly known as the Macao nightshade, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum macaonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
