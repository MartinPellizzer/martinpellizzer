Solanum diffusum var. miozygum

Solanum diffusum var. miozygum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
