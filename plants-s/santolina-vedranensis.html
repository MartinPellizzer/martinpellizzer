Santolina vedranensis

Santolina vedranensis, commonly known as the Balkan santolina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed. It produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Santolina vedranensis is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
