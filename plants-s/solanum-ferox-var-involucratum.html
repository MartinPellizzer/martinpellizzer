Solanum ferox var. involucratum

Solanum ferox var. involucratum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable species like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
