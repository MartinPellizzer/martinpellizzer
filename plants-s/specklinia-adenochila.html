Specklinia adenochila

Specklinia adenochila, commonly known as the veined pigmy orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
