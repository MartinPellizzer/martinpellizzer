Solidago gigantea var. serotina

Solidago gigantea var. serotina, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces numerous yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
