Senecio atacamensis

Senecio atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, adapting to the extreme conditions of the Atacama Desert. It produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
