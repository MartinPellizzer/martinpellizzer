Solanum gemellum var. racemiforme

Solanum gemellum var. racemiforme, commonly known as the racemiform wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
