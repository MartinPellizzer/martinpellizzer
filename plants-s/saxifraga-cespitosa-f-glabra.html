Saxifraga cespitosa f. glabra

Saxifraga cespitosa f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet "glabra.". The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained conditions.
