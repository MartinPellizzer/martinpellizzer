Selaginella pentagona

Selaginella pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stems, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Selaginella pentagona is characterized by its unique, five-sided stems and its ability to produce spores in specialized structures called strobila.

This plant is also notable for its capacity to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in various ecological settings.
