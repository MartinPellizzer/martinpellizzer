Spartina junciformis

Spartina junciformis, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and high salinity levels.

Spartina junciformis is an important component of estuarine ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
