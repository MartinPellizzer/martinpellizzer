Streptanthus glandulosus subsp. niger

Streptanthus glandulosus subsp.

niger, commonly known as the black glandular streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to California. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. Its leaves are glandular-pubescent, and it produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring.

This subspecies is notable for its dark-colored stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
