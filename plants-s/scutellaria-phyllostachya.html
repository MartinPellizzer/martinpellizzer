Scutellaria phyllostachya

Scutellaria phyllostachya, commonly known as the broad-leaved scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Scutellaria phyllostachya is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
