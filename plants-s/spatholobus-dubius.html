Spatholobus dubius

Spatholobus dubius, commonly known as the slender wisteria, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces fragrant, pendant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like projections, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, hanging clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
