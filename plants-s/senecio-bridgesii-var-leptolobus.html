Senecio bridgesii var. leptolobus

Senecio bridgesii var. leptolobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed groundsels, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
