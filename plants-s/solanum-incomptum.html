Solanum incomptum

Solanum incomptum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in moist, shady environments.

Solanum incomptum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
