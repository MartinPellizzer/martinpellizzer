Sideritis endressii subsp. laxespicata

Sideritis endressii subsp.

laxespicata, commonly known as the Alboraya mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for both traditional use and garden purposes.
