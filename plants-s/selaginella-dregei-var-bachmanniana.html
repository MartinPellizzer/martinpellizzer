Selaginella dregei var. bachmanniana

Selaginella dregei var. bachmanniana, commonly known as the Bachmann's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compact, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called sporophylls, which are arranged in cone-like clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
