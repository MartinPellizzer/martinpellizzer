Sonchus × haussknechtii

Sonchus × haussknechtii, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
