Sorbus hupehensis var. paucijuga

Sorbus hupehensis var. paucijuga, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they turn yellow or red in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
