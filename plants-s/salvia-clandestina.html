Salvia clandestina

Salvia clandestina, commonly known as the secret sage, is a rare and elusive plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
