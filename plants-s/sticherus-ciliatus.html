Sticherus ciliatus

Sticherus ciliatus, commonly known as the threadleaf sticherus, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Sticherus ciliatus is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.
