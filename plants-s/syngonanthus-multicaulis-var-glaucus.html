Syngonanthus multicaulis var. glaucus

Syngonanthus multicaulis var. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it its characteristic glaucous appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette, and are adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

Syngonanthus multicaulis var. glaucus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
