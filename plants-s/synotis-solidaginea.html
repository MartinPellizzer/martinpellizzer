Synotis solidaginea

Synotis solidaginea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
