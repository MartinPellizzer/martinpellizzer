Solanum tuberosum var. europaeum

Solanum tuberosum var. europaeum, commonly known as the European potato, is a cultivated variety of the potato plant native to Central and South America but widely grown in Europe.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped tubers with a smooth, firm skin and a starchy, white to yellow flesh. The plant is an herbaceous perennial that produces violet flowers and has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and has been selectively bred for improved yield and disease resistance.
