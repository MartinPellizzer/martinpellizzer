Sasa senanensis var. harae

Sasa senanensis var. harae, commonly known as the Harae bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa senanensis species native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that grows in dense thickets, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a subtle sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
