Sodiroa graminifolia

Sodiroa graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Sodiroa graminifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to tolerate varying light conditions.
