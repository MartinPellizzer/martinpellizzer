Sempervivum lindleyi

Sempervivum lindleyi, commonly known as the 'Meadow Stonecrop,' is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating called farina. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or violet during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum lindleyi is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and full sun exposure.
