Solidago virgaurea subsp. centiflora

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

centiflora, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, ray florets, giving it a striking appearance in late summer and early autumn.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, disk-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.

The subspecies centiflora is distinguished by its numerous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
