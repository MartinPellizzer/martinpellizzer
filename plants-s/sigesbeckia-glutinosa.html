Sigesbeckia glutinosa

Sigesbeckia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of the Americas.

It is characterized by its sticky, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
