Symplocos argutidens

Symplocos argutidens, commonly known as the roughleaf wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with rough, serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Symplocos argutidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
