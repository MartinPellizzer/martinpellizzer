Sasa okudana

Sasa okudana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant forms dense clumps of stout, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa okudana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
