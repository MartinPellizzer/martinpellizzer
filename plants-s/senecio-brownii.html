Senecio brownii

Senecio brownii, commonly known as the Brown's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, erect stems. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Senecio brownii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact form and drought tolerance.
