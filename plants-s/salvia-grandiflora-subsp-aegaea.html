Salvia grandiflora subsp. aegaea

Salvia grandiflora subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its foliage has a strong, aromatic scent, often described as camphor-like, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
