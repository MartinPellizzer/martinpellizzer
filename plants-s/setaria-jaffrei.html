Setaria jaffrei

Setaria jaffrei, commonly known as the Jaffrei beardgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Setaria jaffrei is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
