Saxifraga myosotifolia

Saxifraga myosotifolia, commonly known as the bluebell saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga myosotifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging, high-altitude habitats.
