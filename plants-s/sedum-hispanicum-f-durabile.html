Sedum hispanicum f. durabile

Sedum hispanicum f. durabile, commonly known as the Spanish stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
