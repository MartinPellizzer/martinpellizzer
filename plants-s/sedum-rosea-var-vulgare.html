Sedum rosea var. vulgare

Sedum rosea var. vulgare, commonly known as the common stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water, allowing the plant to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This hardy perennial is known for its resilience and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
