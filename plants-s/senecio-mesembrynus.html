Senecio mesembrynus

Senecio mesembrynus, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to low-maintenance landscapes.
