Syringa patula

Syringa patula, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and dark green, turning yellow in the fall.

Syringa patula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
