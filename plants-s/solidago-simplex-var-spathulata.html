Solidago simplex var. spathulata

Solidago simplex var. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features lance-shaped to broad ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, including marshes and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
