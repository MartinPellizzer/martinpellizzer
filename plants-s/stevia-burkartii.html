Stevia burkartii

Stevia burkartii, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Its leaves are often used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages.

Stevia burkartii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
