Spiraea pruniflora

Spiraea pruniflora, commonly known as the roseleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching branches covered with opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Spiraea pruniflora prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
