Salvia hypochionaea

Salvia hypochionaea, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Salvia hypochionaea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
