Sauropus ramosissimus

Sauropus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
