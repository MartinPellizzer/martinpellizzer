Schoenoplectiella rhodesica

Schoenoplectiella rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Schoenoplectiella rhodesica plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
