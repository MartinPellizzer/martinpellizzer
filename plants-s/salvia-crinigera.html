Salvia crinigera

Salvia crinigera, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia crinigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
