Sinapis foliosa

Sinapis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Sinapis foliosa is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
