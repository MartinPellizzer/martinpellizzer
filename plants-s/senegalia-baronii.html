Senegalia baronii

Senegalia baronii, commonly known as the Baron's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.

Additionally, its wood and leaves have been used traditionally for fuel and livestock feed.
