Senecio coleophyllus

Senecio coleophyllus, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Senecio coleophyllus is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.
