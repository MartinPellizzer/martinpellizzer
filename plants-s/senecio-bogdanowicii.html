Senecio bogdanowicii

Senecio bogdanowicii, commonly known as the Bogdanowicz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
