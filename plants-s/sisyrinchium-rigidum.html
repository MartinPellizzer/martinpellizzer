Sisyrinchium rigidum

Sisyrinchium rigidum, commonly known as the stiff blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium rigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
