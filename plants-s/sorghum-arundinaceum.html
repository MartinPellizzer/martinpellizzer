Sorghum arundinaceum

Sorghum arundinaceum, commonly known as giant sorghum, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust stems and long, narrow leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant is highly drought-resistant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and are used in making thatching and rope.

Sorghum arundinaceum also plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
