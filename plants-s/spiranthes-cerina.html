Spiranthes cerina

Spiranthes cerina, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, tuberous roots.

Spiranthes cerina is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance.
