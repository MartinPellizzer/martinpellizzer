Siparuna trianae

Siparuna trianae, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Siparuna trianae typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
