Schima brevipes

Schima brevipes, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to orange-red berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Schima brevipes is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
