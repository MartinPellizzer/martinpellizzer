Siphocampylus ovatus

Siphocampylus ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Siphocampylus ovatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
