Stevia guaranitica

Stevia guaranitica, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as a sugar alternative. Stevia guaranitica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Its use in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener has gained global recognition in recent years.
