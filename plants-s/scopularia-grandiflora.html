Scopularia grandiflora

Scopularia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a yellow throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Scopularia grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
