Scirpus squamosus

Scirpus squamosus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in marshy areas, along pond edges, and in shallow water.

Scirpus squamosus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
