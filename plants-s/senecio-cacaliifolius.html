Senecio cacaliifolius

Senecio cacaliifolius, commonly known as the candlestick daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
