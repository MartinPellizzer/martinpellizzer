Solanum tuberosum var. salamandrinum

Solanum tuberosum var. salamandrinum, commonly known as the Salamander potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinctive red and purple skin and flesh.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to South America, like its parent species. The plant produces tubers that are often smaller and more colorful than typical potatoes, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and flavor, often used in gourmet cooking and botanical gardens.
