Sida disticha

Sida disticha, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming from summer to fall. Sida disticha is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
