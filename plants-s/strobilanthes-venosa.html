Strobilanthes venosa

Strobilanthes venosa, commonly known as the "Ceylon flameflower," is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowering period is usually during the monsoon season, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Strobilanthes venosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
