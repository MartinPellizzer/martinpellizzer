Stigmarota edulis

Stigmarota edulis, commonly known as the edible stigmarota, is a unique plant native to specific tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Stigmarotaceae and is characterized by its specialized root structures called stigmaras, which are adapted for water absorption. The plant's foliage consists of broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is edible and has a mild, nutty flavor.

Due to its ecological importance and potential nutritional value, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
