Senecio rostellatus

Senecio rostellatus, commonly known as the rostellate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
