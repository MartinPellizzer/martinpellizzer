Silene vulgaris subsp. macrocarpa

Silene vulgaris subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the greater catchfly, is a flowering plant in the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae). It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Silene vulgaris subsp.

macrocarpa is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
