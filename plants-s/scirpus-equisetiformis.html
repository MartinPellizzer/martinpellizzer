Scirpus equisetiformis

Scirpus equisetiformis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Scirpus equisetiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
