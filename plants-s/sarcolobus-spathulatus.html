Sarcolobus spathulatus

Sarcolobus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sarcolobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate to spatulate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Sarcolobus spathulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
