Salvia hedgeana

Salvia hedgeana, commonly known as the hedge nettle sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Salvia hedgeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
