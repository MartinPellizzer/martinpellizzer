Staphylea macrosperma

Staphylea macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Staphyleaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated fruits that resemble small lanterns, which are a key feature in its botanical identification. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its adaptability to various soil types.
