Sasa soyensis

Sasa soyensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa soyensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
