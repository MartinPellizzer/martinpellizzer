Saussurea gossypina

Saussurea gossypina, commonly known as the cottonball safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seed heads that give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Saussurea gossypina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
