Sterculia spatulata

Sterculia spatulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
