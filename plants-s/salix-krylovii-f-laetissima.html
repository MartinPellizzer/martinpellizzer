Salix krylovii f. laetissima

Salix krylovii f. laetissima, commonly known as the sweet willow, is a decorative willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside that adds to its ornamental appeal. This cultivar is valued for its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in moist soils.

It is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic value and can tolerate partial shade.
