Spilanthes americana f. lanitecta

Spilanthes americana f. lanitecta, commonly known as the "stinkweed" or "Mexican devil's tongue," is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor. The plant belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The variety f. lanitecta is distinguished by its dense, lanate (woolly) covering on the leaves, giving it a unique appearance.
