Sporobolus capillaris

Sporobolus capillaris, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Sporobolus capillaris is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to stabilize soil.
