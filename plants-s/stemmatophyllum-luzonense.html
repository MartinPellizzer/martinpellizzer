Stemmatophyllum luzonense

Stemmatophyllum luzonense, commonly known as the Luzon stemmatophyllum, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Luzon, Philippines.

It possesses a slender, branching stem that supports its delicate, ribbon-like leaves, which are typically green and slightly translucent. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of small, spore-producing organs that release spores into the water for propagation.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species within its native range.
