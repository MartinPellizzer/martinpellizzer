Solanum chilense

Solanum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features glandular hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow or purple, often with a distinctive shape. Its berries, which are small and green when mature, contain toxic compounds and are not typically consumed by humans.

Solanum chilense is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
