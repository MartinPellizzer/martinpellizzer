Scopolia carniolicoides

Scopolia carniolicoides, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scopolia carniolicoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its medicinal properties.
