Smilax loupouensis

Smilax loupouensis, commonly known as the Louisiana arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. These flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax loupouensis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.
