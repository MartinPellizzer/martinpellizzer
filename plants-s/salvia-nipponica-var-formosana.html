Salvia nipponica var. formosana

Salvia nipponica var. formosana, commonly known as the Japanese sage or Formosan sage, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
