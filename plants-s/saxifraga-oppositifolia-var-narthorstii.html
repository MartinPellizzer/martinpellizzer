Saxifraga oppositifolia var. narthorstii

Saxifraga oppositifolia var. narthorstii, commonly known as the Arctic sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive, often hairy texture.

It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
