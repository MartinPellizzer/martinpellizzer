Sherbournia millenii

Sherbournia millenii, commonly known as the Millennial shrub, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its succulent nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
