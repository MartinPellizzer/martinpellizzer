Serratula salisburgensis

Serratula salisburgensis, commonly known as the Salzburg's serrated wormwood, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its yellowish-green flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Serratula salisburgensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
