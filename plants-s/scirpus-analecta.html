Scirpus analecta

Scirpus analecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a prominent midrib, and they are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Scirpus analecta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
