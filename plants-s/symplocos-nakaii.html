Symplocos nakaii

Symplocos nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to East Asia, particularly found in China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Symplocos nakaii thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
