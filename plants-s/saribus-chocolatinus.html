Saribus chocolatinus

Saribus chocolatinus, commonly known as the chocolate palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk covered in smooth, dark green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into edible, chocolate-colored fruits. Its leaves are large and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Saribus chocolatinus is well adapted to humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
