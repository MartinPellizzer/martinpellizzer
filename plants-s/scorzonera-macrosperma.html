Scorzonera macrosperma

Scorzonera macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and basal rosette of large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera macrosperma thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
