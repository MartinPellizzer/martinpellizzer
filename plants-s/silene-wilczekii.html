Silene wilczekii

Silene wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its stems are often hairy.

Silene wilczekii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
