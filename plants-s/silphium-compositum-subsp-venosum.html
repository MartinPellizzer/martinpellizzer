Silphium compositum subsp. venosum

Silphium compositum subsp.

venosum, commonly known as the veiny compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely veined, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Silphium compositum subsp.

venosum is known for its resilience and is considered a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
