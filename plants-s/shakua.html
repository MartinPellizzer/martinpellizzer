Shakua

Shakua, commonly known as the "lady's mantle," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped leaves that resemble a cloak, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Shakua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
