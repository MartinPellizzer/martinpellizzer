Solanum sleumeri

Solanum sleumeri, commonly known as the Sleumer's nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
