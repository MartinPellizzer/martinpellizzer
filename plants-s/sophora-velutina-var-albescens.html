Sophora velutina var. albescens

Sophora velutina var. albescens, commonly known as the white silky locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish bark and compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
