Syzygium remotifolium

Syzygium remotifolium, commonly known as the distant-leaved clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flowers and small, edible berries. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a distinct, spicy fragrance.

Syzygium remotifolium is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal and potential culinary uses.
