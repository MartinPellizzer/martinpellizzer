Smilax spicata

Smilax spicata, commonly known as catbrier, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax spicata is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in some areas.
