Sayeria violascens

Sayeria violascens, commonly known as the violet-stemmed sayeria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, violet-colored stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
