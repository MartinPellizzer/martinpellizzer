Scrophularia laciniata

Scrophularia laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scrophularia laciniata is often found in moist soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
