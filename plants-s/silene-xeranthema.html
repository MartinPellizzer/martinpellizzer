Silene xeranthema

Silene xeranthema, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water.

Silene xeranthema is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
