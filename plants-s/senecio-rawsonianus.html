Senecio rawsonianus

Senecio rawsonianus, commonly known as the Rawson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio rawsonianus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
