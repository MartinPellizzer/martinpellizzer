Satureja calamintha var. sylvatica

Satureja calamintha var. sylvatica, commonly known as woodland thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications, offering a subtle thyme-like flavor.
