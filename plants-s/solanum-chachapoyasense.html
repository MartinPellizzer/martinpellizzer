Solanum chachapoyasense

Solanum chachapoyasense, commonly known as the Chachapoya potato, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of northern Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tubers that are traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities in the Andes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies in the region.
