Stigmaphyllon sagittatum var. sinuatum

Stigmaphyllon sagittatum var. sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuous margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
