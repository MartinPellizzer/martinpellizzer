Scilla peruviana var. diluta

Scilla peruviana var. diluta, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
