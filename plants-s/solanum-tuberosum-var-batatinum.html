Solanum tuberosum var. batatinum

Solanum tuberosum var. batatinum, commonly known as batata or sweet potato, is a tuberous plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its swollen, edible roots that come in various colors such as orange, white, and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and small, white or green flowers. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and essential nutrients, making it a valuable food crop.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
