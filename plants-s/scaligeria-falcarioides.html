Scaligeria falcarioides

Scaligeria falcarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scalerie, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically linear and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens as a decorative plant.
