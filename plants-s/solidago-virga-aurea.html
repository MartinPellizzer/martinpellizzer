Solidago virga-aurea

Solidago virga-aurea, commonly known as the golden rod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to three feet tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Solidago virga-aurea plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
