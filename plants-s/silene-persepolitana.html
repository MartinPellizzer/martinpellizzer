Silene persepolitana

Silene persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
