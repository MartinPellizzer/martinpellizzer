Spondias viridiflora

Spondias viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered satinash, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Spondias viridiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
