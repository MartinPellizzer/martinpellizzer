Scaevola ligustrifolia

Scaevola ligustrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf fanflower, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features small, showy blue or purple flowers that are typically clustered in dense, fan-like inflorescences. The plant has silver-gray, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Scaevola ligustrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
