Styrax officinalis var. redivivus

Styrax officinalis var. redivivus, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a rare and resilient species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of desiccation, only to revive when water becomes available. Its botanical significance lies in its unique survival mechanisms and potential medicinal properties.

It is often studied for its ecological adaptations and uses in traditional medicine.
