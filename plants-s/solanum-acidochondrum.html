Solanum acidochondrum

Solanum acidochondrum, commonly known as the acidochrum nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its berries, which are toxic, are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Solanum acidochondrum is also notable for its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed habitats.
