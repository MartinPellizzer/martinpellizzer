Senecio sarcophyllus

Senecio sarcophyllus, commonly known as the stonecrop senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
