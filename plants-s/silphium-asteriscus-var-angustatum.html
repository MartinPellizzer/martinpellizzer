Silphium asteriscus var. angustatum

Silphium asteriscus var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in a composite head that resembles a compass, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration and conservation efforts.
