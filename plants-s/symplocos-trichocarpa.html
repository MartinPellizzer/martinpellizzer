Symplocos trichocarpa

Symplocos trichocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright purple, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting native pollinators and birds.
