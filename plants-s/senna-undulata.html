Senna undulata

Senna undulata, commonly known as the wavy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive zigzag pattern on its petals, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound and trifoliate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Senna undulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
