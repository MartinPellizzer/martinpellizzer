Sideritis hyssopifolia var. integrifolia

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
