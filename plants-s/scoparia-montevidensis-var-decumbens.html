Scoparia montevidensis var. decumbens

Scoparia montevidensis var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
