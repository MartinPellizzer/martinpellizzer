Seriphidium khorassanicum

Seriphidium khorassanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Seriphidium khorassanicum is adapted to arid conditions and has a slow growth rate.
