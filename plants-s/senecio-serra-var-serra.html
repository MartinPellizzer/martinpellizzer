Senecio serra var. serra

Senecio serra var. serra, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
