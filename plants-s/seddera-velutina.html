Seddera velutina

Seddera velutina, commonly known as the velvet seddera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
