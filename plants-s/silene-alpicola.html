Silene alpicola

Silene alpicola, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped. Silene alpicola is perennial and often forms dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its harsh environment.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
