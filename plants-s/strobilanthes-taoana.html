Strobilanthes taoana

Strobilanthes taoana, commonly known as the Taoana flame flower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Strobilanthes taoana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
