Sphaerostephanos subtruncatus

Sphaerostephanos subtruncatus, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive, globular pseudobulb that supports its growth. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a spiral pattern. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
