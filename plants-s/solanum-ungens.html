Solanum ungens

Solanum ungens, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum ungens is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
