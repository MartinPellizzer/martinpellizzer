Solanum etuberosum var. antucense

Solanum etuberosum var. antucense, commonly known as the Antuco tuberous nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, tuberous roots and showy, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
