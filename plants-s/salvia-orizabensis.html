Salvia orizabensis

Salvia orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Orizaba sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia orizabensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
