Scirpus habrus

Scirpus habrus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus habrus plays a vital role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
