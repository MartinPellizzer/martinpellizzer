Solanum heliotropiifolium var. sellowianum

Solanum heliotropiifolium var. sellowianum, commonly known as the Sowbug plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable species like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
