Speculantha corpulenta

Speculantha corpulenta, commonly known as the giant sunspine, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often adorned with intricate patterns resembling sun rays. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in clusters, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its stems are thick and succulent, capable of storing significant amounts of water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for large outdoor gardens and botanical collections.
