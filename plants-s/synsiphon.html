Synsiphon

Synsiphon, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique plant characterized by its spiral arrangement of fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features rhizomes that grow horizontally, supporting the upright fronds. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a distinctive spiral pattern, aiding in light capture.

Synsiphon thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
