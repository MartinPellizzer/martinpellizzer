Symplocos cochinchinensis var. angustifolia

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by clusters of vibrant purple berries. The leaves are elliptical, with a pointed tip and a narrow shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental berries.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
