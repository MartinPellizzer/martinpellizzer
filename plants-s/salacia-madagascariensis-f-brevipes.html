Salacia madagascariensis f. brevipes

Salacia madagascariensis f. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed salacia, is a unique variant of the Salacia genus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to the typical form of the species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its foliage consists of alternate, simple leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.

This botanical specimen is of interest to horticulturists and botanists due to its distinctive morphology and potential medicinal properties.
