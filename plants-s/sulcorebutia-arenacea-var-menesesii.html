Sulcorebutia arenacea var. menesesii

Sulcorebutia arenacea var. menesesii, commonly known as the Meneses' rock cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent areoles from which spines and flowers emerge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
