Solidago mirabilis

Solidago mirabilis, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Solidago mirabilis typically grows in moist to wet habitats, including marshes and roadside ditches.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
