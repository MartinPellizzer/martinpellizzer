Scleria moritziana

Scleria moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Scleria moritziana typically thrives in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions.
