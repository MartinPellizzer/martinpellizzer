Saussurea sclerolepis

Saussurea sclerolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
