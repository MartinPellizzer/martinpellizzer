Solanum buchwaldii

Solanum buchwaldii, commonly known as the red devil or devil's fig, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent violet anthers.

The fruit is edible when ripe but contains toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.
