Stizolobium comorense

Stizolobium comorense, commonly known as the Comoros acacia, is a small, evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Additionally, it has been used traditionally for its timber and as a source of fuel.
