Schoberia corniculata var. erecta

Schoberia corniculata var. erecta, commonly known as the upright bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
