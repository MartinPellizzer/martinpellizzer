Syzygium longipes

Syzygium longipes, commonly known as the longipes apple or longipes tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, red fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a tart, acidic flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a valuable species in both horticulture and local economies.
