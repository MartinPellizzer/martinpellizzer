Sesamum edule

Sesamum edule, commonly known as the Indian sesame or sesamum, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Pedaliaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous ellipsoidal seeds, which are the primary part used for culinary and industrial purposes. Sesamum edule is valued for its high oil content, with the seeds containing up to 50% oil, primarily composed of unsaturated fatty acids.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth and seed development.
