Stelis amaliae

Stelis amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stelis amaliae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
