Sasa purpurascens

Sasa purpurascens, commonly known as red bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially in younger shoots. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
