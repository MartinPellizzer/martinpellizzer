Scorzonera albicaulis subsp. macrosperma

Scorzonera albicaulis subsp.

macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible tubers, which have a nutty, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
