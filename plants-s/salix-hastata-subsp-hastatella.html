Salix hastata subsp. hastatella

Salix hastata subsp.

hastatella, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

This subspecies typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its catkins are a distinguishing feature, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
