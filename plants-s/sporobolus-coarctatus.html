Sporobolus coarctatus

Sporobolus coarctatus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Sporobolus coarctatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
