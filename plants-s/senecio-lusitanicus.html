Senecio lusitanicus

Senecio lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio lusitanicus is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
