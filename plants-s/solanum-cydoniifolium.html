Solanum cydoniifolium

Solanum cydoniifolium, commonly known as the golden apple guava, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Solanum genus.
