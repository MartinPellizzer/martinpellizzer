Sempervivum artvinense

Sempervivum artvinense, commonly known as the Artvin stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
