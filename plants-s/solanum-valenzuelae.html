Solanum valenzuelae

Solanum valenzuelae, commonly known as the Valenzuelan nightshade, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
