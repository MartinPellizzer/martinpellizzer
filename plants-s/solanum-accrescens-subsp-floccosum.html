Solanum accrescens subsp. floccosum

Solanum accrescens subsp.

floccosum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are usually green when mature.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
