Scirpus fistulosus

Scirpus fistulosus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often hollow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Scirpus fistulosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
