Scilla voethorum

Scilla voethorum, commonly known as the Voeth's scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing delicate, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

Scilla voethorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
