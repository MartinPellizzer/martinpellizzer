Senega revoluta

Senega revoluta, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Senega revoluta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
