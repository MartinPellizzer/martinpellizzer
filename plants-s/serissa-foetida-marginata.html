Serissa foetida marginata

Serissa foetida marginata, commonly known as the fragrant serissa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has dense, evergreen foliage with small, glossy leaves that provide a lush appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for bonsai and container gardening.
