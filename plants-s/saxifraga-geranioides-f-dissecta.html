Saxifraga geranioides f. dissecta

Saxifraga geranioides f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The leaves are finely dissected, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
