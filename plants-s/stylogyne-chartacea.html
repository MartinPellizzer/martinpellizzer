Stylogyne chartacea

Stylogyne chartacea, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark that peels off in papery layers, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Stylogyne chartacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
