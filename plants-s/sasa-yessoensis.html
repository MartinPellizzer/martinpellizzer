Sasa yessoensis

Sasa yessoensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Yesso bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Sasa yessoensis is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
