Salacia rehmannii

Salacia rehmannii, commonly known as the Indian spurge or Himalayan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its stout, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Salacia rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
