Sida scoparia

Sida scoparia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant with a sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating.

Sida scoparia is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots and the ability to store water efficiently.
