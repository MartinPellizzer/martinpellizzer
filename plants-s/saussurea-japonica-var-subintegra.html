Saussurea japonica var. subintegra

Saussurea japonica var. subintegra, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal medicine for various health benefits.
