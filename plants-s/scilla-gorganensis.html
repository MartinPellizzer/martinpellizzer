Scilla gorganensis

Scilla gorganensis, commonly known as the Gorgona scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Scilla gorganensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
