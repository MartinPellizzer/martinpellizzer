Stramentopappus congestiflorus

Stramentopappus congestiflorus, commonly known as the clustered pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.
