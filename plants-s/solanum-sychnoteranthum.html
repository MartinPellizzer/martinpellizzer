Solanum sychnoteranthum

Solanum sychnoteranthum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and it produces small, glandular leaves.

The fruit is edible when ripe and is often used in cooking or as a sweetener.
