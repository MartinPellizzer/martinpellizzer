Silene bipartita f. parviflora

Silene bipartita f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sand-verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as dunes or coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged oppositely, while its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to arid environments and is found primarily in regions of the Mediterranean and parts of western Europe.
