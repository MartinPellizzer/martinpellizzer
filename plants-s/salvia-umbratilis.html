Salvia umbratilis

Salvia umbratilis, commonly known as the shade sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Salvia umbratilis is also valued for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
