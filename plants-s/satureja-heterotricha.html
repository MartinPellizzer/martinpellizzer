Satureja heterotricha

Satureja heterotricha, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja heterotricha is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
