Sauropus bicolor var. microphyllus

Sauropus bicolor var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved rock fig, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated as a garden plant in various regions.
