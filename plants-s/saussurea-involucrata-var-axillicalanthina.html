Saussurea involucrata var. axillicalanthina

Saussurea involucrata var. axillicalanthina, commonly known as the Kashmiri saffron or wild saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, adapting to the cold, arid conditions of its habitat.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its unique chemical composition.
