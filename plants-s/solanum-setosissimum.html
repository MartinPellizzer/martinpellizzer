Solanum setosissimum

Solanum setosissimum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Despite its ornamental appearance, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
