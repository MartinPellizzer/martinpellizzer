Salvia × danubialis

Salvia × danubialis, commonly known as the Danube sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and aromatic, typical of the Salvia genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
