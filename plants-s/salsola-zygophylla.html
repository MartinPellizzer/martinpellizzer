Salsola zygophylla

Salsola zygophylla, commonly known as silver leaf gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to store water.

Salsola zygophylla is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
