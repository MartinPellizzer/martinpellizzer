Sasa ishizuchiana

Sasa ishizuchiana, commonly known as the Ishizuchia bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa ishizuchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
