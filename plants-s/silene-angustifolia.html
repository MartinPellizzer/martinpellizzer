Silene angustifolia

Silene angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
