Sarcolophium suberosum

Sarcolophium suberosum, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sarcolophium suberosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
