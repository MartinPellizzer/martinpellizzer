Sesbania pubescens

Sesbania pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Sesbania pubescens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and agricultural systems.
