Syzygium garcinioides

Syzygium garcinioides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its wood is highly valued for its rich reddish color and durability, making it a sought-after material in craftsmanship.

Syzygium garcinioides thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
