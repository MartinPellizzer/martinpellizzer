Solanum crassinervium

Solanum crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, toxic berries that are edible when ripe.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
