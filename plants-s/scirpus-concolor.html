Scirpus concolor

Scirpus concolor, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Scirpus concolor plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
