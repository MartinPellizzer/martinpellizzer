Solea verbenacea

Solea verbenacea, commonly known as the marsh verbena, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 30 centimeters tall, with slender, branching stems. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
