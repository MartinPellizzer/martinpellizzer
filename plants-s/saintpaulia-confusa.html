Saintpaulia confusa

Saintpaulia confusa, commonly known as the confused African violet, is a species native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features velvety, dark green leaves with a prominent central vein and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Saintpaulia confusa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
