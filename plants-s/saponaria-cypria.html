Saponaria cypria

Saponaria cypria, commonly known as Cyprus soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. Its roots contain saponins, which give it its name and make it useful in traditional herbal medicine.

Saponaria cypria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
