Solea stricta

Solea stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found along the edges of ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Solea stricta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
