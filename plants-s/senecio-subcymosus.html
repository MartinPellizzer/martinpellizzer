Senecio subcymosus

Senecio subcymosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in fynbos vegetation in regions like South Africa.
