Sophora tomentosa f. longifolia

Sophora tomentosa f. longifolia, commonly known as the longifolia Chinese wisteria, is a variant of the Chinese wisteria, characterized by its elongated, pendant flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. The plant features compound leaves with a dense covering of soft, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in long, drooping clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
