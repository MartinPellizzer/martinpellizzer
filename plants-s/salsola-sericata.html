Salsola sericata

Salsola sericata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial herb that is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has been introduced to many other regions. The plant has erect, branching stems covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it produces dry, three-angled fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Salsola sericata is known for its ability to thrive in arid and disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
