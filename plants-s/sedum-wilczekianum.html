Sedum wilczekianum

Sedum wilczekianum, commonly known as the Wilczek's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a relatively compact growth habit.

Sedum wilczekianum is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in horticultural settings.
