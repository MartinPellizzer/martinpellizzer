Smilax cinerea

Smilax cinerea, commonly known as the gray smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax cinerea is a hardy plant that can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
