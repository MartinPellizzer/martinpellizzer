Syzygium angulatum

Syzygium angulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Syzygium angulatum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
