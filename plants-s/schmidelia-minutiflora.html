Schmidelia minutiflora

Schmidelia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf schmidelia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features delicate, small flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Schmidelia minutiflora is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
