Sinapis corbariensis

Sinapis corbariensis, commonly known as the Corsican mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive pungent odor. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be edible when young.

Sinapis corbariensis is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
