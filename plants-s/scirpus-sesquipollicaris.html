Scirpus sesquipollicaris

Scirpus sesquipollicaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered with a layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers in the summer.

Scirpus sesquipollicaris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
