Schinus weinmanniifolia var. riedeliana

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-red bracts that resemble flames, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its small, white flowers are clustered in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. riedeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal display.
