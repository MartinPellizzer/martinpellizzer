Spiraea tobolskia

Spiraea tobolskia, commonly known as the Siberian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with oval, serrated leaflets that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Spiraea tobolskia is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
