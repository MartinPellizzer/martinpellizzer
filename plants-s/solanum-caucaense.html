Solanum caucaense

Solanum caucaense, commonly known as the Cauca potato, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its starchy, edible underground stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or white.

Solanum caucaense is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
