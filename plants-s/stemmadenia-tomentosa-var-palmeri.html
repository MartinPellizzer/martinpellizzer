Stemmadenia tomentosa var. palmeri

Stemmadenia tomentosa var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's stemmadenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and also covered in a soft, pubescent layer. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

This plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to dry conditions.
