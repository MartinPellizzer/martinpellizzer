Styrax buchananii

Styrax buchananii, commonly known as the Indian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been traditionally used in incense and medicine. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Styrax buchananii typically grows in cool, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
