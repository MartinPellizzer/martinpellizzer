Sasa nipponica var. asoensis

Sasa nipponica var. asoensis, commonly known as Aso bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This variety is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, particularly in mountainous regions. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a decorative element in gardens.
