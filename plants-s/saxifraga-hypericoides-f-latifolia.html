Saxifraga hypericoides f. latifolia

Saxifraga hypericoides f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

This fern-like plant is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
