Sasa hidejiroana

Sasa hidejiroana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or hidejiro bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa hidejiroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
