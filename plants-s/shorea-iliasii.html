Shorea iliasii

Shorea iliasii, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

Shorea iliasii plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
