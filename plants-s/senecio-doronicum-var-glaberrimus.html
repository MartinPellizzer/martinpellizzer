Senecio doronicum var. glaberrimus

Senecio doronicum var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are smooth and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
