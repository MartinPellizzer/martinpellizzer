Saxifraga hirsuta var. haworthi

Saxifraga hirsuta var. haworthi, commonly known as the Haworth's hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking resilient, decorative plants.
