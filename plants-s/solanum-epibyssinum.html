Solanum epibyssinum

Solanum epibyssinum, commonly known as the epibyssinum nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum epibyssinum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
