Salix firouzkuhensis

Salix firouzkuhensis, commonly known as the Firouzkuh willow, is a species of willow native to Iran.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and produces catkin-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
