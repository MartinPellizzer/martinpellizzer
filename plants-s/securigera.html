Securigera

Securigera, commonly known as goat's rue, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Securigera varia being the most well-known. These plants are characterized by their slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically trifoliate, composed of three small leaflets, and the plant produces pods that contain seeds.

Securigera species are native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and they often thrive in dry, sandy soils.
