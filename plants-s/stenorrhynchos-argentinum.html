Stenorrhynchos argentinum

Stenorrhynchos argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, and its flowers are characterized by their intricate, lip-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique floral morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
