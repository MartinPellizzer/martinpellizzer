Senecio wolffii

Senecio wolffii, commonly known as the Woolly Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
