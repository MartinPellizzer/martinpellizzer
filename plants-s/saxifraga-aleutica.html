Saxifraga aleutica

Saxifraga aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are small, white or pink, clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Saxifraga aleutica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
