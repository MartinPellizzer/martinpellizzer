Saxifraga oregana var. sierrae

Saxifraga oregana var. sierrae, commonly known as Sierra rockfoil, is a perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
