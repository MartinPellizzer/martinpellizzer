Stachys divaricata

Stachys divaricata, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with two-lipped petals, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Stachys divaricata is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
