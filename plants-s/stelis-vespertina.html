Stelis vespertina

Stelis vespertina, commonly known as the evening orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in the evening, giving it its common name, and produces small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Stelis vespertina is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
