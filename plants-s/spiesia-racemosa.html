Spiesia racemosa

Spiesia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Spiesia racemosa is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
