Stachygynandrum ornithopodioides

Stachygynandrum ornithopodioides, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a fleshy, persistent calyx. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
