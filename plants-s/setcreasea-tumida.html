Setcreasea tumida

Setcreasea tumida, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

The flowers typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
