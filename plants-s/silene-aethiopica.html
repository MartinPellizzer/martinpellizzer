Silene aethiopica

Silene aethiopica, commonly known as the Abyssinian campion, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene aethiopica is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
