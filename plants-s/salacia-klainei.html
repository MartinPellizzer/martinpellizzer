Salacia klainei

Salacia klainei, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
