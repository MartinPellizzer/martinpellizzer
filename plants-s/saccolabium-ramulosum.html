Saccolabium ramulosum

Saccolabium ramulosum, commonly known as the hairy leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits that contain seeds. Its adaptations, such as succulent stems and deep root systems, enable it to thrive in low-nutrient soils and withstand drought.

Saccolabium ramulosum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
