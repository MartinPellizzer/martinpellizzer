Salvia weberbaueri

Salvia weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia weberbaueri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
