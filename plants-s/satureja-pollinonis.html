Satureja pollinonis

Satureja pollinonis, commonly known as Alpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Satureja pollinonis is valued for its resilience to cold climates and its contribution to biodiversity in mountain ecosystems.
