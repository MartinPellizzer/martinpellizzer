Salvia formosana var. matsudae

Salvia formosana var. matsudae, commonly known as the Taiwan sage, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
