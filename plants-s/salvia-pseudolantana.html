Salvia pseudolantana

Salvia pseudolantana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Salvia pseudolantana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
