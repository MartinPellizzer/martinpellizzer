Spiraea hacquetii

Spiraea hacquetii, commonly known as the Hacquet's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching branches and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for borders or as a specimen plant.
