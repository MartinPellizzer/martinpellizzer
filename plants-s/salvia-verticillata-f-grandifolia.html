Salvia verticillata f. grandifolia

Salvia verticillata f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in gray-green, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, with a strong, minty scent, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
