Statice fradiniana

Statice fradiniana, commonly known as the Fradinian glass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, blue to violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Statice fradiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
