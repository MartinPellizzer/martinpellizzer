Soja hispida

Soja hispida, commonly known as the hairy soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Soja hispida is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines and as a forage crop.
