Scleria bulbifera var. mechowiana

Scleria bulbifera var. mechowiana, commonly known as the bulbous sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water margins.

Scleria bulbifera var. mechowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
