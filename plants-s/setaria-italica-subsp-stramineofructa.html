Setaria italica subsp. stramineofructa

Setaria italica subsp.

stramineofructa, commonly known as finger millet, is a small-grained grass species native to Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical, finger-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including calcium and iron, making it a valuable crop in many regions.

It is often grown as a staple food in parts of India, Africa, and the Middle East.
