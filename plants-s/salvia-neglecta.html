Salvia neglecta

Salvia neglecta, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Salvia neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
