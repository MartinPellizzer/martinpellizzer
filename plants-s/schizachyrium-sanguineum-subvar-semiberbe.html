Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. semiberbe

Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. semiberbe, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in autumn. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural and landscaped environments.
