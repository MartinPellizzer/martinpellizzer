Silphium perfoliatum

Silphium perfoliatum, commonly known as the eastern prickly pear, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Silphium perfoliatum is also valued for its historical use in food and medicine by indigenous peoples.
