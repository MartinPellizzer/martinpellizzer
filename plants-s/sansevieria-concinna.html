Sansevieria concinna

Sansevieria concinna, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a hardy, evergreen perennial native to tropical Africa.

It features stiff, upright leaves that are typically dark green with yellowish margins, forming a rosette shape. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal indoor plant. Sansevieria concinna is also known for its air-purifying qualities, as it can remove toxins from the air.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal have made it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional indoor gardening.
