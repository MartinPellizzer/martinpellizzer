Salix yumenensis

Salix yumenensis, commonly known as Yumen willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Salix yumenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
