Syzygium cumini var. tsoi

Syzygium cumini var. tsoi, commonly known as Java apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit is a yellow or red drupe with a sweet, tangy flavor, often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The plant is also noted for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
