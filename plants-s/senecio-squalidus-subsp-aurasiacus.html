Senecio squalidus subsp. aurasiacus

Senecio squalidus subsp.

aurasiacus, commonly known as the Afghan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
