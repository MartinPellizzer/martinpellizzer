Setaria hirta

Setaria hirta, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.

Setaria hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
