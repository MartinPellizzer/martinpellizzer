Solanum tuberosum var. lutescens

Solanum tuberosum var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its yellow or orange flesh.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with stolons that produce tubers underground. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces small, white or green flowers.

This variety is valued for its unique color and flavor, making it a popular choice in culinary and ornamental gardening.
