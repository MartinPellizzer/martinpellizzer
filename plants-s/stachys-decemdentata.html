Stachys decemdentata

Stachys decemdentata, commonly known as the nine-toothed hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Stachys decemdentata is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
