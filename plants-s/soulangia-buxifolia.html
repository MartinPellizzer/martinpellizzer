Soulangia buxifolia

Soulangia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf soulangia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in shaded gardens or as a container plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
