Semecarpus coriaceus

Semecarpus coriaceus, commonly known as the hard-wood or Indian chestnut, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is valued for its hard, durable wood and medicinal properties.
