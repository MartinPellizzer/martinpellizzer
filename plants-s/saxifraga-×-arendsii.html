Saxifraga × arendsii

Saxifraga × arendsii, commonly known as the Arendsii saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are typically rounded and slightly succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × arendsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
