Senna coronilloides

Senna coronilloides, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Senna coronilloides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
