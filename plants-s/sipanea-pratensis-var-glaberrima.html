Sipanea pratensis var. glaberrima

Sipanea pratensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth meadow sipanea, is a variant of the meadow sipanea, characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

It plays a role in supporting local insect populations and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
