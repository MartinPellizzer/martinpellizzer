Solidago coreana var. gigantea

Solidago coreana var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant Korean goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy yellow composite flowers in late summer to early autumn, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
