Spiraea monbetsusensis

Spiraea monbetsusensis, commonly known as the Japanese spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea monbetsusensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
