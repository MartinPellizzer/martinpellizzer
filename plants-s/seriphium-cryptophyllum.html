Seriphium cryptophyllum

Seriphium cryptophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, curled appearance, while its flowers are clustered in a spherical shape.

Seriphium cryptophyllum is often found in open plains and along dry streambeds.
