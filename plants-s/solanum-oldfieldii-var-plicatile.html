Solanum oldfieldii var. plicatile

Solanum oldfieldii var. plicatile, commonly known as the Oldfield's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
