Syzygium smithii

Syzygium smithii, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Syzygium smithii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
