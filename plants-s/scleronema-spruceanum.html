Scleronema spruceanum

Scleronema spruceanum, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Scleronema spruceanum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
