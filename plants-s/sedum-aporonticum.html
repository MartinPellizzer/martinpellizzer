Sedum aporonticum

Sedum aporonticum, commonly known as the Arctic sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the short summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, cold environments with limited soil and nutrients.

Sedum aporonticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
