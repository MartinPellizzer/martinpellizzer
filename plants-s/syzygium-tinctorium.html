Syzygium tinctorium

Syzygium tinctorium, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that contain hard, shiny seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
