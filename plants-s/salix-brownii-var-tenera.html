Salix brownii var. tenera

Salix brownii var. tenera, commonly known as the Tibetan willow, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and slender, branching stems adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, providing insulation against harsh climates.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in alpine regions.
