Scutellaria australis

Scutellaria australis, commonly known as the Australian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Scutellaria australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
