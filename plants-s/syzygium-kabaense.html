Syzygium kabaense

Syzygium kabaense, commonly known as the Kabaense tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Syzygium kabaense is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
