Sycomorus capensis

Sycomorus capensis, commonly known as the Natal fig, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fig-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a thick, gnarled trunk and a wide, spreading crown. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is edible, though typically consumed by birds and other animals.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
