Salacia loloensis

Salacia loloensis, commonly known as the Lolo's salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
