Styrax bicolor

Styrax bicolor, commonly known as the white and red styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that range from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Styrax bicolor is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and medicine.
