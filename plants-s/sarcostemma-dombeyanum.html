Sarcostemma dombeyanum

Sarcostemma dombeyanum, commonly known as the climbing milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its latex sap contains milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Sarcostemma.
