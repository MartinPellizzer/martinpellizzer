Sedum integrifolium subsp. neomexicanum

Sedum integrifolium subsp.

neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping in dry climates.
