Salvia huastecana

Salvia huastecana, commonly known as Huastec sage, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent.

Salvia huastecana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
