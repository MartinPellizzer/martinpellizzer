Sulcorebutia trojapampensis

Sulcorebutia trojapampensis, commonly known as the Pampas stone cactus, is a small, slow-growing succulent native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a compact, globular stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Sulcorebutia trojapampensis is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal care when grown in suitable conditions.
