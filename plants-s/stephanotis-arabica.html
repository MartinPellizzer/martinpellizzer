Stephanotis arabica

Stephanotis arabica, commonly known as the Arabian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant has evergreen, glossy leaves and is typically cultivated as a climbing shrub or vine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often used in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
