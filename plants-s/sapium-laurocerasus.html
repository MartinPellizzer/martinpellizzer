Sapium laurocerasus

Sapium laurocerasus, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The tree is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different climates.
