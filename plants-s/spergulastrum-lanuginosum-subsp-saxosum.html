Spergulastrum lanuginosum subsp. saxosum

Spergulastrum lanuginosum subsp.

saxosum, commonly known as the saxicola sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and coastal environments. It typically grows in well-drained soils, often found in limestone areas or among maritime cliffs.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets. It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation in its native range.
