Saponaria gypsacea

Saponaria gypsacea, commonly known as the gypsum soapwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its woolly, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils, often found in gypsum-rich environments. Saponaria gypsacea produces a milky sap that contains saponins, which can produce a foaming effect when mixed with water.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
