Stylosanthes ingrata

Stylosanthes ingrata, commonly known as the African grass or slender stylo, is a perennial legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by oblong, woody pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stylosanthes ingrata is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
