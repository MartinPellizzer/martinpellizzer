Scabiosa sylvatica var. latericaulis

Scabiosa sylvatica var. latericaulis, commonly known as the woodland scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, flattened shape. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is often found in woodland edges and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
