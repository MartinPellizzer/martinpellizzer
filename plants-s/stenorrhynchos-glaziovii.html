Stenorrhynchos glaziovii

Stenorrhynchos glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration, often featuring a combination of yellow and purple hues. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for horticultural purposes.
