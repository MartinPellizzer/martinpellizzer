Saccolabium tixieri

Saccolabium tixieri, commonly known as the Tixieri's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are often pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Saccolabium tixieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
