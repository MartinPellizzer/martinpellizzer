Saussurea taraxacifolia

Saussurea taraxacifolia, commonly known as the meadow saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, with a rosette structure, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

Saussurea taraxacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
