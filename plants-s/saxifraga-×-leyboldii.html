Saxifraga × leyboldii

Saxifraga × leyboldii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries for its attractive foliage and blooms.
