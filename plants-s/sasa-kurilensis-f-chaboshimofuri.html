Sasa kurilensis f. chaboshimofuri

Sasa kurilensis f. chaboshimofuri, commonly known as the Chaboshimofuri sasa, is a variant of the Japanese sasa plant native to the northern regions of Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense, shrub-like colonies, with slender, evergreen culms that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a leathery texture with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous forests.

This form is distinguished by its unique leaf coloration and growth habit, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
