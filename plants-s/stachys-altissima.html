Stachys altissima

Stachys altissima, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring square stems and opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Stachys altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
