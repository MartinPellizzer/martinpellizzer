Solanum dendriticothrix

Solanum dendriticothrix, commonly known as the tree tomato or granadilla, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its branched, thorny stems and clustered, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round to oval fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a tangy, acidic flavor. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it often grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

Solanum dendriticothrix is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
