Solanum xanthacanthum

Solanum xanthacanthum, commonly known as the yellow-veined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellowish veins on the leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and the plant contains toxic compounds typical of the nightshade family.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
