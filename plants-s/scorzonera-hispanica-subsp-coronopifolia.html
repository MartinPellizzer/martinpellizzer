Scorzonera hispanica subsp. coronopifolia

Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

coronopifolia, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
