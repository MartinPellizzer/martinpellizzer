Swertia tashiroi var. cruciata

Swertia tashiroi var. cruciata, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variant is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
