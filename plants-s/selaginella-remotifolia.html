Selaginella remotifolia

Selaginella remotifolia, commonly known as the remote-leaf spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Selaginella remotifolia is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures called strobila.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
