Sempervivum montanum var. carpathicum

Sempervivum montanum var. carpathicum, commonly known as the Carpathian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery waxy coating. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This hardy species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
