Spathodea orinocensis

Spathodea orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble torches, which are composed of numerous bright red or orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically large and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Spathodea orinocensis is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various pollinators.
