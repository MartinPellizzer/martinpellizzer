Seseli foeniculifolium

Seseli foeniculifolium, commonly known as the leafy carrot.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of parsley. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
