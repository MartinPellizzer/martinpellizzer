Scleria lithosperma var. muricata

Scleria lithosperma var. muricata, commonly known as the rough-seeded sedges, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and triangular leaves that are often covered in small, rough bumps. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
