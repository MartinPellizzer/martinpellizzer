Sideroxylon inerme subsp. cryptophlebium

Sideroxylon inerme subsp.

cryptophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and robust, woody stems that are highly resistant to damage.

The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive midrib that is prominent on the underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant in challenging climates.
