Symplocos glomerata var. poilanei

Symplocos glomerata var. poilanei, commonly known as the Vietnamese beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy appearance, while the flowers are small and white, often growing in dense inflorescences. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
