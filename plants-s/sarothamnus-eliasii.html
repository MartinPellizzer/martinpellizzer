Sarothamnus eliasii

Sarothamnus eliasii, commonly known as Elias's thimbleweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in heads and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sarothamnus eliasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
