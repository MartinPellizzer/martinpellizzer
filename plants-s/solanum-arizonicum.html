Solanum arizonicum

Solanum arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it has a woody stem that can become branched.

Solanum arizonicum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
