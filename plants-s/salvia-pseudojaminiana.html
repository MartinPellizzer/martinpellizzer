Salvia pseudojaminiana

Salvia pseudojaminiana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia pseudojaminiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
