Sedum semenovii var. kansuensis

Sedum semenovii var. kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant landscaping plant.
