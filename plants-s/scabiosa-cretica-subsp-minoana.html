Scabiosa cretica subsp. minoana

Scabiosa cretica subsp.

minoana, commonly known as the Cretan scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
