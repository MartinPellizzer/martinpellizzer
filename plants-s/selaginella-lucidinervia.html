Selaginella lucidinervia

Selaginella lucidinervia, commonly known as the shining-veined spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae, characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with bright green, glossy leaves and a branching, creeping stem that often forms dense mats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are arranged in clusters and produce spores for propagation.
