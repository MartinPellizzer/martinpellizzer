Saccharum modhuja

Saccharum modhuja, commonly known as sweet grass, is a tall perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, narrow leaves with a distinctive sweet aroma when crushed, which is due to the presence of essential oils. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and rituals.

Saccharum modhuja is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
