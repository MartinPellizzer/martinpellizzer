Sarcandra glabra f. flava

Sarcandra glabra f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sarcandra, is a variant of the sarcandra plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
