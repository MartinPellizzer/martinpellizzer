Selenipedium × hybridum

Selenipedium × hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid giant spider-orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant is native to the wet tropical forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. Selenipedium × hybridum is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Selenipedium album and Selenipedium riedellii.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
