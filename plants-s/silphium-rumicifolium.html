Silphium rumicifolium

Silphium rumicifolium, commonly known as the "hempweed," is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Silphium rumicifolium is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along the coasts.

Its historical significance includes use by indigenous peoples for medicinal and food purposes.
