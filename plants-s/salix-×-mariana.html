Salix × mariana

Salix × mariana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix × mariana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.
