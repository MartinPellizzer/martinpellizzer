Scabiosa banatica var. albicans

Scabiosa banatica var. albicans, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white blooms that resemble a cloud, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
