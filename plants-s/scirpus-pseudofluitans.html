Scirpus pseudofluitans

Scirpus pseudofluitans, commonly known as the floating bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can appear to float on the water's surface, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread and form dense stands in shallow water.

Scirpus pseudofluitans plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
