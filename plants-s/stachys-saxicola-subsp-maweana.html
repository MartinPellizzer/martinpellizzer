Stachys saxicola subsp. maweana

Stachys saxicola subsp.

maweana, commonly known as the rock betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
