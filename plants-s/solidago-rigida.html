Solidago rigida

Solidago rigida, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, rigid stems that support its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago rigida is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
