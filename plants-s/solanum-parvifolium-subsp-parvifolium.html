Solanum parvifolium subsp. parvifolium

Solanum parvifolium subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands.

The plant features small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of yellow flowers. Its berries are small, red, and contain toxic alkaloids.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and tuberous roots.
