Solanum ardisioides

Solanum ardisioides, commonly known as the redcurrant plant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small currants. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum ardisioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
