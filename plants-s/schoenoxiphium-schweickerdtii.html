Schoenoxiphium schweickerdtii

Schoenoxiphium schweickerdtii, commonly known as the Schreiber's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Schoenoxiphium schweickerdtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
