Sicyos microcarpus var. paneensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. paneensis, commonly known as the Panama bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, elongated fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant is a herbaceous vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
