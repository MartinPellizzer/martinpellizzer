Sapium decipiens

Sapium decipiens, commonly known as the false water spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sapium decipiens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
