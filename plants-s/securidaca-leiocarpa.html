Securidaca leiocarpa

Securidaca leiocarpa, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thorny stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, oval seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
