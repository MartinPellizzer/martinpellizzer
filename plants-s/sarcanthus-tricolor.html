Sarcanthus tricolor

Sarcanthus tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its strikingly colored flowers, which typically display three distinct shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sarcanthus tricolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
