Satureja villosa

Satureja villosa, commonly known as woolly savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

Satureja villosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
