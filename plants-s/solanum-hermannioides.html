Solanum hermannioides

Solanum hermannioides, commonly known as the Hermann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, purple or white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum hermannioides belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
