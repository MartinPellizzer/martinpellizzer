Sasa komoriana

Sasa komoriana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or komori bamboo, is a type of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, slender leaves that are typically green and smooth in texture. Sasa komoriana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ability to form thick mats over time.
