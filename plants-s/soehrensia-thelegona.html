Soehrensia thelegona

Soehrensia thelegona, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is a tall, evergreen tree fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to New Zealand.

The trunk is covered in a fibrous, spongy material that provides structural support and aids in water retention.
