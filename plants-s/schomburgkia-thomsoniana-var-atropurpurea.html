Schomburgkia thomsoniana var. atropurpurea

Schomburgkia thomsoniana var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and its showy, spider-like flowers that often display a deep purple hue. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
