Silene portensis

Silene portensis, commonly known as the Portentous campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs, while its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

Silene portensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
