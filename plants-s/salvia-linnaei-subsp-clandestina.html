Salvia linnaei subsp. clandestina

Salvia linnaei subsp.

clandestina, commonly known as the ghost salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Its common name reflects its elusive and ghostly appearance in its natural habitat.
