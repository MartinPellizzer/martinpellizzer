Solanum benderianum

Solanum benderianum, commonly known as the Bendiflorum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and its ability to thrive in arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
