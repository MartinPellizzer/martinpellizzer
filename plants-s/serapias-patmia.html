Serapias patmia

Serapias patmia, commonly known as the pink orchid or the pink serapias, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

Serapias patmia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
