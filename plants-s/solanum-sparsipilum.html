Solanum sparsipilum

Solanum sparsipilum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or violet. The plant produces small, round berries that are yellow when ripe, and it has hairy stems and leaves. Solanum sparsipilum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
