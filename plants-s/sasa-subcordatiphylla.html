Sasa subcordatiphylla

Sasa subcordatiphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the bamboo family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a broad base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Sasa subcordatiphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
