Sambucus canadensis var. mexicana

Sambucus canadensis var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to central Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. These flowers give way to dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
