Statice duriuscula var. cuspidata

Statice duriuscula var. cuspidata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
