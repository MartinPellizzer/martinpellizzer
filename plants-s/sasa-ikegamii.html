Sasa ikegamii

Sasa ikegamii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a shrub-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa ikegamii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
