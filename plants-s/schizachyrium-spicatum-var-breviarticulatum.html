Schizachyrium spicatum var. breviarticulatum

Schizachyrium spicatum var. breviarticulatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features narrow, blue-green blades that turn coppery-red in the fall, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to silver flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie ecosystems. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
