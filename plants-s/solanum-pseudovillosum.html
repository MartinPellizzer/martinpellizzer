Solanum pseudovillosum

Solanum pseudovillosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a berry that changes from green to red as it matures.

While not widely cultivated for food, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
