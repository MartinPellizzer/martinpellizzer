Selaginella rostrata

Selaginella rostrata, commonly known as the spiky clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rock crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, slender stems that bear spirally arranged leaves. Its leaves are usually narrow and pointed, with a distinctive rosette-like arrangement at the tips.

Selaginella rostrata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
