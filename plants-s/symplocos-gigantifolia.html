Symplocos gigantifolia

Symplocos gigantifolia, commonly known as giant waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Symplocos gigantifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
