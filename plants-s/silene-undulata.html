Silene undulata

Silene undulata, commonly known as the wavy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Silene undulata thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
