Strobilanthes linearifolia

Strobilanthes linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Thailand. This herbaceous plant is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The flowers are typically arranged in spikes and bloom during the rainy season.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
