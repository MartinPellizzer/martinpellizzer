Silene antitaurica

Silene antitaurica, commonly known as the Taurus silene, is a flowering plant native to the Taurus Mountains in southern Turkey.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its harsh habitat.

Silene antitaurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
