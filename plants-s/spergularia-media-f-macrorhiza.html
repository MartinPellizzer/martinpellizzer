Spergularia media f. macrorhiza

Spergularia media f. macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-fruited sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, seed-containing fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes or disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy environments and supporting local insect populations.
