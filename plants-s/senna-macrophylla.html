Senna macrophylla

Senna macrophylla, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are grouped in spherical clusters and bloom in spring. Senna macrophylla is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
