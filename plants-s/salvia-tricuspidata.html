Salvia tricuspidata

Salvia tricuspidata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia tricuspidata is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
