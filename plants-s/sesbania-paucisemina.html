Sesbania paucisemina

Sesbania paucisemina, commonly known as the lesser cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces elongated pods containing several small, edible seeds. It has a rapid growth cycle and is often used as a green manure or for soil improvement.

Sesbania paucisemina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
