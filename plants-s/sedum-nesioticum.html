Sedum nesioticum

Sedum nesioticum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
