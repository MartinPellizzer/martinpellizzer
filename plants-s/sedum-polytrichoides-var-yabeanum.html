Sedum polytrichoides var. yabeanum

Sedum polytrichoides var. yabeanum, commonly known as the Yabean sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in partial shade make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
