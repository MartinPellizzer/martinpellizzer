Saccolabium samarindae

Saccolabium samarindae, commonly known as the Samarinda burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that encase its seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in soil stabilization.
