Stachys crenulata

Stachys crenulata, commonly known as the scalycup hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. The plant has a hairy stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its unique, cup-like inflorescence.

Stachys crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
