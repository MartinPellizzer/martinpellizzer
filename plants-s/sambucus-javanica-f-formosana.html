Sambucus javanica f. formosana

Sambucus javanica f. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant features compound leaves with 5–9 leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. These flowers give way to dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
