Scaevola temarii

Scaevola temarii, commonly known as the "Blue Mistflower," is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with silver-blue foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Scaevola temarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
