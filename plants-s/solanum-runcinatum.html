Solanum runcinatum

Solanum runcinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It has a short growing season and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
