Selenicereus rizzinii

Selenicereus rizzinii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers with a strong, sweet scent, which attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
