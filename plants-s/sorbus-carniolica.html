Sorbus carniolica

Sorbus carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus carniolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
