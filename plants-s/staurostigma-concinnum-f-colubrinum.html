Staurostigma concinnum f. colubrinum

Staurostigma concinnum f. colubrinum, commonly known as the blue starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
