Setcreasea purpurea

Setcreasea purpurea, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight purple tinge. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises, fences, or as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.
