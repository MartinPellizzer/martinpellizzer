Schistostephium dactyliferum

Schistostephium dactyliferum, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils.

Schistostephium dactyliferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
