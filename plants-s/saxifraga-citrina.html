Saxifraga citrina

Saxifraga citrina, commonly known as the yellow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Saxifraga citrina is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.
