Serratula coronata subsp. manshurica

Serratula coronata subsp.

manshurica, commonly known as the Siberian crownwort, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, including regions of Russia and China. It typically grows in mountainous areas and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
