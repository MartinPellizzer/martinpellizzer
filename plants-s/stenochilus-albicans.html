Stenochilus albicans

Stenochilus albicans, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking white and green foliage with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
