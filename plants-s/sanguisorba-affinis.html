Sanguisorba affinis

Sanguisorba affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered with fine, translucent hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Sanguisorba affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
