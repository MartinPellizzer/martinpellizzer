Solanum stenotomum f. catari-papa

Solanum stenotomum f. catari-papa, commonly known as the Catari-Papa tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may exhibit yellow or orange hues. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are broadly ovate in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional Andean cuisines.
