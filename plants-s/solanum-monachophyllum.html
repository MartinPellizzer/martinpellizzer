Solanum monachophyllum

Solanum monachophyllum, commonly known as the monk's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Solanum monachophyllum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
