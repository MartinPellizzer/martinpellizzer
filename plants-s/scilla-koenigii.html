Scilla koenigii

Scilla koenigii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of striking blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla koenigii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
