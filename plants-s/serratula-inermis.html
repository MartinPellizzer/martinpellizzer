Serratula inermis

Serratula inermis, commonly known as the common serreta, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features upright, branched stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
