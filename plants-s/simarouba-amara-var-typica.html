Simarouba amara var. typica

Simarouba amara var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.
