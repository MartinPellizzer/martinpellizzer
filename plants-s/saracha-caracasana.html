Saracha caracasana

Saracha caracasana, commonly known as the Caracasana tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and cork-like, which provides some resistance to pests and diseases.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
