Sebastiania corniculata var. patula

Sebastiania corniculata var. patula, commonly known as the Patula beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy, purple to violet berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
