Seymeria

Seymeria, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has a distinctive root system that anchors it to the substrate while its floating leaves provide oxygen to the water. Seymeria is often found in marshes and ponds, where it plays a role in maintaining water quality.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
