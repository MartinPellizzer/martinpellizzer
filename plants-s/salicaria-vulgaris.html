Salicaria vulgaris

Salicaria vulgaris, commonly known as waterweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often floating on the water's surface. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with narrow, ribbon-like leaves. It is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Salicaria vulgaris is also known for its ability to thrive in slow-moving or still water, making it a common sight in ponds and lakes.
