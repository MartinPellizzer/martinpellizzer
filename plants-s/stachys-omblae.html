Stachys omblae

Stachys omblae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the upper stem, forming dense clusters of purple or blue-violet blooms.

Stachys omblae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
