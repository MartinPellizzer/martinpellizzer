Silene rubens

Silene rubens, commonly known as red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Silene rubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
