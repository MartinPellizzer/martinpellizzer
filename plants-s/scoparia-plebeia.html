Scoparia plebeia

Scoparia plebeia, commonly known as the common scoparia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a flat head, and it produces small, ribbed fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Scoparia plebeia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
