Salsola tamariscina

Salsola tamariscina, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has small, green, spiny leaves and produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

Salsola tamariscina is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed soils quickly.
