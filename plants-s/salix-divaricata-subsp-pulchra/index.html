Salix Divaricata Subsp. Pulchra

Salix divaricata subsp. pulchra is a rare and elegant willow species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. Known for its striking silver-green foliage and delicate catkins, this plant is a botanical gem that thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in streamside habitats where it contributes to local biodiversity and ecological stability. Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a valuable subject for conservation and botanical study. Its beauty and resilience make it a fascinating focus for both researchers and nature enthusiasts.
