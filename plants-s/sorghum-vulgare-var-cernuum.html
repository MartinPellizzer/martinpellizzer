Sorghum vulgare var. cernuum

Sorghum vulgare var. cernuum, commonly known as broomcorn millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and drooping panicles of small, pale green to golden florets. The plant is native to Africa and has been cultivated for thousands of years as a staple food crop. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow leaves, and a robust stem that supports its distinctive, cascading inflorescence.
