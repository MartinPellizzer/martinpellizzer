Spiesia inaria

Spiesia inaria, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that provides a striking visual contrast to its green stems. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Spiesia inaria is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
