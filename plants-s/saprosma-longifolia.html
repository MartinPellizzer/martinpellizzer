Saprosma longifolia

Saprosma longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved saprosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Saprosma longifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
