Syzygium guineense subsp. barotsense

Syzygium guineense subsp.

barotsense, commonly known as the Barotseland tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and fragrant, white flowers.

The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sour taste and is used in local cuisines.

The plant also has cultural and traditional significance in the regions where it is found.
