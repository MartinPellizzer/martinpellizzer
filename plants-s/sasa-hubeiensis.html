Sasa hubeiensis

Sasa hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Sasa hubeiensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of central China.
