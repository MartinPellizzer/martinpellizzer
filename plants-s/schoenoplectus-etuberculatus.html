Schoenoplectus etuberculatus

Schoenoplectus etuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Schoenoplectus etuberculatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
