Spiraea ulmifolia var. trichocarpa

Spiraea ulmifolia var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear clusters of white flowers in early summer. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
