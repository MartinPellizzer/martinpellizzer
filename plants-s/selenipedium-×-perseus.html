Selenipedium × perseus

Selenipedium × perseus, commonly known as the hybrid giant spatiphyllum, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Selenipedium albornozii and Selenipedium wrightii.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant is native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. It has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
