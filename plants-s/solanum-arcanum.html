Solanum arcanum

Solanum arcanum, commonly known as the ghost pepper, is a flowering plant native to northern India and southern China.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, green, bell-shaped flowers that turn red as the fruit matures. The plant produces small, fiery red berries that contain a high concentration of capsaicin, making them extremely spicy. Solanum arcanum is often cultivated for its unique flavor and use in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and a preference for warm, humid climates.
