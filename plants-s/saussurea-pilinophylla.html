Saussurea pilinophylla

Saussurea pilinophylla, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental qualities.

Saussurea pilinophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stabilization and supporting local biodiversity.
