Salix nigra var. wrightii

Salix nigra var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's black willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in riparian zones and moist soils along streams and rivers. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination.

The bark is often smooth and dark brown, with a distinctive flaky appearance.
