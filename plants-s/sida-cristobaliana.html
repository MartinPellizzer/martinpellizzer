Sida cristobaliana

Sida cristobaliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or crownleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Sida cristobaliana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
