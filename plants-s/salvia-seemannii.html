Salvia seemannii

Salvia seemannii, commonly known as the Cape sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
