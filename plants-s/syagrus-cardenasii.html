Syagrus cardenasii

Syagrus cardenasii, commonly known as the Candelabra Palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, arching leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, spiny fruits containing dark blue seeds. Syagrus cardenasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and tropical environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
