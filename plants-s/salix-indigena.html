Salix indigena

Salix indigena, commonly known as the indigenous willow, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typical of willow species, and its leaves are typically elongated with a serrated edge. Salix indigena thrives in moist, often riparian habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
