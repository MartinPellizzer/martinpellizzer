Salvia oxyphylla

Salvia oxyphylla, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia oxyphylla is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
