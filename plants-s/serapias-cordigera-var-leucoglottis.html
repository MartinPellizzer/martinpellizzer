Serapias cordigera var. leucoglottis

Serapias cordigera var. leucoglottis, commonly known as the white-tongued sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive white throat. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting white or pale yellow central stripe. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
