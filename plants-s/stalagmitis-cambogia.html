Stalagmitis cambogia

Stalagmitis cambogia, commonly known as the bladder nut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, green fruits that resemble small bladders, which contain a single seed. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, waxy texture.

Stalagmitis cambogia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
