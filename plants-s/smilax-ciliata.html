Smilax ciliata

Smilax ciliata, commonly known as the silky smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with soft, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Smilax ciliata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
