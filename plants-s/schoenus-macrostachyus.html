Schoenus macrostachyus

Schoenus macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces dense clusters of small, white to pale green flowers during the summer months.

Schoenus macrostachyus is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
