Selaginella fimbriata var. polyura

Selaginella fimbriata var. polyura, commonly known as the fringed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are borne on specialized branches.

This variety is native to regions in the southern United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
