Saponaria barradensis

Saponaria barradensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved soapwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains saponins, which are natural detergents. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Saponaria barradensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in herbal medicine.
