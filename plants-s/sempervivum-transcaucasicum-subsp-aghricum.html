Sempervivum transcaucasicum subsp. aghricum

Sempervivum transcaucasicum subsp.

aghricum, commonly known as the Caucasian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the Caucasus region. It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
