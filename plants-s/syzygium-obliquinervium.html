Syzygium obliquinervium

Syzygium obliquinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with prominently veined surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to yellow, star-shaped fruits. The fruit's unique appearance and mild, sweet flavor make it a valuable species in both ornamental and culinary contexts.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
