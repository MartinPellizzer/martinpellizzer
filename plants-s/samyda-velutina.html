Samyda velutina

Samyda velutina, commonly known as the velvet maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Samyda velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
