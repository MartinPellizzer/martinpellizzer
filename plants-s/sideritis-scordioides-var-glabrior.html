Sideritis scordioides var. glabrior

Sideritis scordioides var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-scented rockrose, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Sideritis.
