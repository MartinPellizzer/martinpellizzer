Serapias messapica

Serapias messapica, commonly known as the Messapic orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Serapias messapica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral appearance.
