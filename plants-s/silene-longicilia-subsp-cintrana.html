Silene longicilia subsp. cintrana

Silene longicilia subsp.

cintrana, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Silene longicilia subsp.

cintrana is a perennial species with a relatively compact growth habit.
