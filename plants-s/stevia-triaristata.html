Stevia triaristata

Stevia triaristata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides found in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Due to its natural sweetness, it is widely used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages.
