Sideritis libanotica var. microchlamys

Sideritis libanotica var. microchlamys, commonly known as the small-lipped ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
