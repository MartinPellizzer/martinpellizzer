Syzygium kemamanense

Syzygium kemamanense, commonly known as the Kemaman apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the island of Borneo. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and typically have a red or orange skin with a sweet, tangy flavor.

Syzygium kemamanense is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
