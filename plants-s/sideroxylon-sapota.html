Sideroxylon sapota

Sideroxylon sapota, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a tropical berry with a creamy, sweet pulp that contains numerous large, inedible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal.
