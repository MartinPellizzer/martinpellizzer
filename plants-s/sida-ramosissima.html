Sida ramosissima

Sida ramosissima, commonly known as prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or subshrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has hairy, often branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with five petals, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

It is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
