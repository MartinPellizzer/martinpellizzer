Sigillaria pubescens

Sigillaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sigillaria, is a fossilized plant that thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It belongs to the group of ancient plants known as lycophytes, which are characterized by their simple, vascular structures. The plant's distinctive features include its segmented stems and sporangia, which are preserved in fossil form. Sigillaria pubescens is often found in coal-bearing strata, indicating its role in the formation of coal deposits.

Its morphology provides valuable insights into the paleobotanical history of early terrestrial ecosystems.
