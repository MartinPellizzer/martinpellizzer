Saponaria aenesia

Saponaria aenesia, commonly known as the silverweed or silverleaf saponaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Saponaria aenesia is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
