Serissa scabrida

Serissa scabrida, commonly known as the rough serissa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in terminal panicles.

Serissa scabrida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
