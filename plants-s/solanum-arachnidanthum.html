Solanum arachnidanthum

Solanum arachnidanthum, commonly known as the spider flower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its unique, spider-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and the fruits are berries that ripen to a deep red color.

Solanum arachnidanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
