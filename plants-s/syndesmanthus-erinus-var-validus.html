Syndesmanthus erinus var. validus

Syndesmanthus erinus var. validus, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
