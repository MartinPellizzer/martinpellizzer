Schefflera insculpta

Schefflera insculpta, commonly known as the African nettle or African shield fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble a shield, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.

Schefflera insculpta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
