Seris odorata

Seris odorata, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain compounds that have been historically used for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araliaceae and is valued for its aromatic properties.
