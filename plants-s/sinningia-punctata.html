Sinningia punctata

Sinningia punctata, commonly known as the spotted gloxinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with distinctive white or pale green markings. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Sinningia punctata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to both gardens and greenhouses.
