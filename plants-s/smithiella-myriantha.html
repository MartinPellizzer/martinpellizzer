Smithiella myriantha

Smithiella myriantha, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has a climbing habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Smithiella myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
