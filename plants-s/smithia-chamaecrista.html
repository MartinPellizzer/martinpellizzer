Smithia chamaecrista

Smithia chamaecrista, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in woodland and open areas.

Smithia chamaecrista is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
