Seddera hirsuta var. glabrescens

Seddera hirsuta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy seddera, is a variant of the seddera plant characterized by its reduced hair coverage compared to the typical variety.

It is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in clusters.

This variant is valued for its resilience to drought and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
