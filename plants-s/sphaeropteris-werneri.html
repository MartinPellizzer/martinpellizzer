Sphaeropteris werneri

Sphaeropteris werneri, commonly known as the Werneri cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family, characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and dense, feather-like leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant has a distinctive appearance, with its dark, rough bark and large, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Sphaeropteris werneri is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
