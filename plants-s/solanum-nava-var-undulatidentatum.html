Solanum nava var. undulatidentatum

Solanum nava var. undulatidentatum, commonly known as the wavy-toothed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with wavy, toothed margins and produces small, violet-colored flowers. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
