Stevia dubia

Stevia dubia, commonly known as the lesser stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
