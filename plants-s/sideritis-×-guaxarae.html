Sideritis × guaxarae

Sideritis × guaxarae, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scordifolia and Sideritis albanica, native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
