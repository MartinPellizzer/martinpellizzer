Sprengelia incarnata var. distichophylla

Sprengelia incarnata var. distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf sprangelia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it its specific epithet *distichophylla*. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
