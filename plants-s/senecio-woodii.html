Senecio woodii

Senecio woodii, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ghostly in appearance. Native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa, it thrives in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise otherworldly appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Senecio woodii is highly sought after by botanists and succulent enthusiasts alike.
