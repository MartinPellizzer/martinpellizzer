Scrophularia polybotrys

Scrophularia polybotrys, commonly known as the Chinese toadflax, is a perennial herb native to Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Scrophularia polybotrys is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
