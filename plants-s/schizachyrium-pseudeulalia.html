Schizachyrium pseudeulalia

Schizachyrium pseudeulalia, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage.

It typically grows in dry to mesic prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from blue to purple, depending on the season. It is a perennial grass with a clump-forming growth habit and is known for its drought tolerance.

Schizachyrium pseudeulalia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
