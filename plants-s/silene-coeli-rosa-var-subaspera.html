Silene coeli-rosa var. subaspera

Silene coeli-rosa var. subaspera, commonly known as the rough-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
