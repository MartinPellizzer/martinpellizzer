Saxifraga × patens

Saxifraga × patens, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga × patens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh growing conditions.
