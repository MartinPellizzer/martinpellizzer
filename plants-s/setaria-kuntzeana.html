Setaria kuntzeana

Setaria kuntzeana, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
