Solidago altissima var. pluricephala

Solidago altissima var. pluricephala, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variant is often found in open areas such as roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.
