Sciodaphyllum diplodactylum

Sciodaphyllum diplodactylum, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like structure with a thick, fibrous trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
