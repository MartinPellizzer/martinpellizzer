Sparganium androcladum var. fluctuans

Sparganium androcladum var. fluctuans, commonly known as the floating bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed genus native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its unique ability to float on the water surface, with its inflated, bladder-like stems that aid in buoyancy. The plant produces spiky, cone-like structures called burrs, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental features. Its growth is typically found in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

The species is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels, which is reflected in its common name.
