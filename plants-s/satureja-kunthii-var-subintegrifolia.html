Satureja kunthii var. subintegrifolia

Satureja kunthii var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint-like fragrance, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for rocky or well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members such as mint and oregano.
