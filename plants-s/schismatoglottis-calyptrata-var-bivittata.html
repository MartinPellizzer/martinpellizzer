Schismatoglottis calyptrata var. bivittata

Schismatoglottis calyptrata var. bivittata, commonly known as the banded schismatoglottis, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive banded pattern, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
