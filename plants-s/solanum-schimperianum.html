Solanum schimperianum

Solanum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, and it produces berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Solanum schimperianum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
