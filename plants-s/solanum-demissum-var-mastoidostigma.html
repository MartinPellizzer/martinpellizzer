Solanum demissum var. mastoidostigma

Solanum demissum var. mastoidostigma, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It features woody stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
