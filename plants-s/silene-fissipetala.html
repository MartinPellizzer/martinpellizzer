Silene fissipetala

Silene fissipetala, commonly known as the fissured catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene fissipetala thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
