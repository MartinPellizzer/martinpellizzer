Solanum albidum

Solanum albidum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
