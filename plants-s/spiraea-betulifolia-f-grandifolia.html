Spiraea betulifolia f. grandifolia

Spiraea betulifolia f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered willow-leaf spirea, is a compact shrub characterized by its finely cut, willow-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces showy inflorescences in early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable ornamental plant.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
