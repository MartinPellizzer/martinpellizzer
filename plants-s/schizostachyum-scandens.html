Schizostachyum scandens

Schizostachyum scandens, commonly known as the climbing bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit. The plant features long, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Schizostachyum scandens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.
