Sedum coerulescens

Sedum coerulescens, commonly known as blue sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue-gray foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in arid climates.
