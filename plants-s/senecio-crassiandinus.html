Senecio crassiandinus

Senecio crassiandinus, commonly known as the thick-leaved senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Senecio crassiandinus prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
