Sideritis arborescens var. antiatlantica

Sideritis arborescens var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rock sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
