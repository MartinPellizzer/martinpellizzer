Symphyotrichum moranense

Symphyotrichum moranense, commonly known as Moran's New England aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. This perennial herb typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically purple to pink in color.

The plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
