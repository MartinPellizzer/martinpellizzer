Sedum pulchellum

Sedum pulchellum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum pulchellum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
