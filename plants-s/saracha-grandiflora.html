Saracha grandiflora

Saracha grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered saracha, is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five lobes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Saracha grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
