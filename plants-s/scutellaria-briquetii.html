Scutellaria briquetii

Scutellaria briquetii, commonly known as Briquet's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
