Sophia ramosa

Sophia ramosa, commonly known as the clustered sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
