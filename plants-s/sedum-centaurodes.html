Sedum centaurodes

Sedum centaurodes, commonly known as the Centaury Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
