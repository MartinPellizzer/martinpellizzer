Swida bretschneideri var. gracilis

Swida bretschneideri var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Chinese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it suitable for smaller landscapes and urban settings.
