Senecio zollingerianus

Senecio zollingerianus, commonly known as Zollinger's groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces numerous linear, leaf-like bracts that surround its flower heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
