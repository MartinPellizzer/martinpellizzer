Senecio austrorufescens

Senecio austrorufescens, commonly known as the red-bark senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
