Selaginella mixteca

Selaginella mixteca, commonly known as the Mixteca spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are produced on the underside of its leaves.

Selaginella mixteca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
