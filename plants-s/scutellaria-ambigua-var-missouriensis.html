Scutellaria ambigua var. missouriensis

Scutellaria ambigua var. missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
