Silene antirrhina var. laevigata

Silene antirrhina var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, with five petals that are slightly notched at the tips.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its ornamental value in gardens.
