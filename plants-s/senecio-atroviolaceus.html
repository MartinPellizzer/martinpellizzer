Senecio atroviolaceus

Senecio atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking dark violet to almost black flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio atroviolaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and dramatic appearance.
