Sphaerocaryum pulchellum

Sphaerocaryum pulchellum, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
