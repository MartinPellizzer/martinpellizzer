Saxifraga nudicaulis

Saxifraga nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga nudicaulis is often found in mountainous areas and tundra environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
