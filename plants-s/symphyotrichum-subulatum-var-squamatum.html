Symphyotrichum subulatum var. squamatum

Symphyotrichum subulatum var. squamatum, commonly known as the squamose blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
