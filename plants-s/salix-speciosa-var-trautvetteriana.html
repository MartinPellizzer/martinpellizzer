Salix speciosa var. trautvetteriana

Salix speciosa var. trautvetteriana, commonly known as the Trautvetter's weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a delicate, almost ethereal look. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist soils near water sources, making it a common sight along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climate and soil conditions.
