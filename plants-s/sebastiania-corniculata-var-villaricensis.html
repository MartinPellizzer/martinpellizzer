Sebastiania corniculata var. villaricensis

Sebastiania corniculata var. villaricensis, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are orange-red in color and contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and landscape designs.
