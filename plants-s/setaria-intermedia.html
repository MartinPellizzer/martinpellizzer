Setaria intermedia

Setaria intermedia, commonly known as green millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is often used as a forage crop and has potential for biofuel production.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and arid conditions.
