Spergula radicans

Spergula radicans, commonly known as the creeping sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, often wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Spergula radicans is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
