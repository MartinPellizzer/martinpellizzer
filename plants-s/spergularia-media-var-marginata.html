Spergularia media var. marginata

Spergularia media var. marginata, commonly known as the coastal red sandras, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are often narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are usually borne in dense inflorescences.

This variety is adapted to salty and windy conditions, making it a common sight along shorelines.
