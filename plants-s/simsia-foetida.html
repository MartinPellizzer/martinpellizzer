Simsia foetida

Simsia foetida, commonly known as the stinking false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that emit a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Simsia foetida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
