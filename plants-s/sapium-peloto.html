Sapium peloto

Sapium peloto, commonly known as the Brazilian cypress, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in complex inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sapium peloto is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
