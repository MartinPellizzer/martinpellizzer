Spiculaea huntiana

Spiculaea huntiana, commonly known as the Alaska snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and boreal environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by smooth, elongated fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Spiculaea huntiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
