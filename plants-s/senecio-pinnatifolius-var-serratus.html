Senecio pinnatifolius var. serratus

Senecio pinnatifolius var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated pinnate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern along the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
