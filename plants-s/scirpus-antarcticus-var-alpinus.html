Scirpus antarcticus var. alpinus

Scirpus antarcticus var. alpinus, commonly known as the Antarctic sedge, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its rhizomes help it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine and subantarctic ecosystems.
