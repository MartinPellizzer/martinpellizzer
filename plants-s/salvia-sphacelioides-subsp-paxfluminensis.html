Salvia sphacelioides subsp. paxfluminensis

Salvia sphacelioides subsp.

paxfluminensis, commonly known as the Rio de Janeiro sage, is a perennial herb native to Brazil. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
