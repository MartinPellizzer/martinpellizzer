Setaria carinata

Setaria carinata, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a warm-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are often seen drifting in the wind, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Setaria carinata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
