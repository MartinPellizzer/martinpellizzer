Sesleria nitida var. tenoreana

Sesleria nitida var. tenoreana, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
