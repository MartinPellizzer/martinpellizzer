Stylidium semipartitum

Stylidium semipartitum, commonly known as the split-stalk mistletoe, is a unique parasitic plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees, relying on its host for nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive structure that aids in pollination.

Stylidium semipartitum is adapted to thrive in arid environments, showcasing specialized features for survival in harsh conditions.
