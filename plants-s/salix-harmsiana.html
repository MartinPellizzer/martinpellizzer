Salix harmsiana

Salix harmsiana, commonly known as the Harms willow, is a species of willow native to South Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas along rivers and lakes. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Salix harmsiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
