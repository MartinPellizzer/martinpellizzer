Salix serissifolia

Salix serissifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silky down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate catkins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The silky willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
