Sasa gracillima var. kinugawensis

Sasa gracillima var. kinugawensis, commonly known as the Kinugawa bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
