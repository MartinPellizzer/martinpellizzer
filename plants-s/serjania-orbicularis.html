Serjania orbicularis

Serjania orbicularis, commonly known as the Brazilian orange tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, orbicular-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Serjania orbicularis is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
