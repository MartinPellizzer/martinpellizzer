Spatalla tulbaghensis

Spatalla tulbaghensis, commonly known as the Tulbaghia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant, garlic-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Spatalla tulbaghensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
