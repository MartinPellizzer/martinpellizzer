Sarcocolla fucata

Sarcocolla fucata, commonly known as the black lichen, is a member of the Lecanoraceae family and is characterized by its dark, crust-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on bark or stone surfaces. The plant has a complex thallus structure composed of fungal hyphae and algal cells, which work together for photosynthesis. It is typically found in temperate regions and is known for its resilience to environmental stress.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to lichenologists and environmental scientists.
