Sida corrugata var. trichopoda

Sida corrugata var. trichopoda, commonly known as the roughleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, coastal environments.
