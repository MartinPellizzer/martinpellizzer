Serapias cordata

Serapias cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
