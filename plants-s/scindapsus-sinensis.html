Scindapsus sinensis

Scindapsus sinensis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or variegated pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Scindapsus sinensis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
