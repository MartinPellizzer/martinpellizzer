Stachyurus chinensis var. cuspidatus

Stachyurus chinensis var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the Chinese weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, making it an ornamental choice for gardens. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and turn yellow in autumn.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
