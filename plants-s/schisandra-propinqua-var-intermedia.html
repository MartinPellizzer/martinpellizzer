Schisandra propinqua var. intermedia

Schisandra propinqua var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate five-flavor vine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that turn dark purple when mature. Its berries are traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and pharmacological research.
