Salsola leucophylla

Salsola leucophylla, commonly known as silver leaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Salsola leucophylla is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
