Stevia santacruzensis

Stevia santacruzensis, commonly known as the Santa Cruz stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Stevia santacruzensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local pollinators.
