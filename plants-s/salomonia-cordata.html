Salomonia cordata

Salomonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf salvia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
