Sida aggregata

Sida aggregata, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Sida aggregata is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
