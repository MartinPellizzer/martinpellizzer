Senecio hastifolius

Senecio hastifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
