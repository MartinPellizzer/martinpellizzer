Salix tschanbaischanica

Salix tschanbaischanica, commonly known as the Siberian willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has slender, flexible branches and small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are catkins, which are usually wind-pollinated, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ecological restoration projects in harsh climates.
