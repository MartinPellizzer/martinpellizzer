Spicanta auriculata

Spicanta auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are often branched and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Spicanta auriculata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
