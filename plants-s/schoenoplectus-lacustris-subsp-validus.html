Schoenoplectus lacustris subsp. validus

Schoenoplectus lacustris subsp.

validus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clustered flowering spikes. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in waterlogged environments and spread vegetatively. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This subspecies is important for stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
