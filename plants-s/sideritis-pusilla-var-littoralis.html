Sideritis pusilla var. littoralis

Sideritis pusilla var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal ironweed, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sideritis pusilla var. littoralis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
