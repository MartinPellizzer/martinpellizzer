Sobralia cardosoi

Sobralia cardosoi, commonly known as the Cardoso's sobralia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
