Solanum manoteranthum

Solanum manoteranthum, commonly known as the manotera nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique flowering behavior and potential medicinal properties.
