Syzygium tolypanthum

Syzygium tolypanthum, commonly known as the wild mango or tolypanthum apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native regions.
