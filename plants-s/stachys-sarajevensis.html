Stachys sarajevensis

Stachys sarajevensis, commonly known as Sarajevo hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
