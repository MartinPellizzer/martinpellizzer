Setaria viridis var. laevigata

Setaria viridis var. laevigata, commonly known as smooth green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to temperate regions.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown seeds. It is often cultivated as a forage crop and is also used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
