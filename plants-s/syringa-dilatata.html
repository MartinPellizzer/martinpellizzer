Syringa dilatata

Syringa dilatata, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Syringa dilatata is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
