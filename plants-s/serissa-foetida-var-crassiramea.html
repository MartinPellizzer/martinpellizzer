Serissa foetida var. crassiramea

Serissa foetida var. crassiramea, commonly known as the fragrant serissa, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit, making it ideal for topiary and bonsai. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and ability to produce fragrant, long-lasting blooms.
