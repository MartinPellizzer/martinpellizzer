Strobus peuce

Strobus peuce, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small, dry drupes that contain a single seed.

Strobus peuce is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
