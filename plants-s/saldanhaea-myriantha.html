Saldanhaea myriantha

Saldanhaea myriantha, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Saldanhaea myriantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
