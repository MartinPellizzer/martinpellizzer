Solanum pennellii var. pennellii

Solanum pennellii var. pennellii, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that range in color from green to red, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.
