Salix hypericifolia

Salix hypericifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and riparian areas.

Salix hypericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing riverbanks.
