Stevia polycephala var. oaxacensis

Stevia polycephala var. oaxacensis, commonly known as Oaxacan stevia, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers and is known for its intense sweet taste, attributed to steviol glycosides. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its use in natural sweeteners.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
