Sorbus tianschanica f. aurantiacorosea

Sorbus tianschanica f. aurantiacorosea, commonly known as the golden-leafed whitebeam, is a rare and striking variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-orange foliage, which stands out against its typical white or grayish leaves. This form is typically found in high-altitude regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tianshan Mountains. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value due to its colorful leaves.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces small, white or yellow flowers followed by red or orange berries.
