Saxifraga × llaurentiana

Saxifraga × llaurentiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit with closely packed, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
