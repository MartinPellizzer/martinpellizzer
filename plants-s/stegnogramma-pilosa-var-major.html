Stegnogramma pilosa var. major

Stegnogramma pilosa var. major, commonly known as the rough-stemmed stegnogramma, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
