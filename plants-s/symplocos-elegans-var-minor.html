Symplocos elegans var. minor

Symplocos elegans var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its compact size and ornamental appeal.
