Sponia subserrata

Sponia subserrata, commonly known as the serrated sponia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Sponia subserrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, resilient nature.
