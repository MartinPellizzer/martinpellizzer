Scleria melanotricha var. glabrior

Scleria melanotricha var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to white flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in wet or damp environments.

The smooth, glabrous leaves of this variety distinguish it from other similar species within the genus Scleria.
