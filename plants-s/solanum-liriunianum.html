Solanum liriunianum

Solanum liriunianum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic compounds belonging to the solanine family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings and requires caution during handling.
