Stellaria nemorum f. ciliata

Stellaria nemorum f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with ciliate margins that give it its specific epithet.

The species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ecological role in supporting invertebrate life.
