Steiremis ciliata

Steiremis ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Steiremis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
