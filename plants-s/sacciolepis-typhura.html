Sacciolepis typhura

Sacciolepis typhura, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Following flowering, it develops bright orange, fleshy fruits that are edible and resemble small tomatoes.

These fruits are rich in nutrients and are often used in culinary applications.
