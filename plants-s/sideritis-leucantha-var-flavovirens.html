Sideritis leucantha var. flavovirens

Sideritis leucantha var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to greenish-yellow. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
