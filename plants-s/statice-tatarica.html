Statice tatarica

Statice tatarica, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry fields. Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Statice tatarica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
