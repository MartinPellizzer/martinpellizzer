Stipa academica

Stipa academica, commonly known as the academic grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Stipa academica is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
