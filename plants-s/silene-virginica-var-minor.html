Silene virginica var. minor

Silene virginica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
