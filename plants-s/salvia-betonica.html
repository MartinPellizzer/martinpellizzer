Salvia betonica

Salvia betonica, commonly known as betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to violet flowers appear in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Betony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
