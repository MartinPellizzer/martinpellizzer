Scirpus pitardii

Scirpus pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a fibrous texture, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Scirpus pitardii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
