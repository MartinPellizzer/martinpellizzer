Staphidium reversum

Staphidium reversum, commonly known as the reverse-stem mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of certain broadleaf trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for bark, which helps it blend in with its host. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds. Its unique growth habit and specialized relationship with its host make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
