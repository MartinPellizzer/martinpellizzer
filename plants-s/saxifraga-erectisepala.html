Saxifraga erectisepala

Saxifraga erectisepala, commonly known as the upright-lobed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Saxifraga erectisepala is often found in mountain meadows and is a popular subject in botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
