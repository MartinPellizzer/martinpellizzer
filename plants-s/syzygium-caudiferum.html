Syzygium caudiferum

Syzygium caudiferum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy ball. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into the distinctive, hairy fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

Syzygium caudiferum thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, fibrous pulp.
