Sorbus torminalis

Sorbus torminalis, commonly known as whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent fruits that are aggregate berries. It is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and rocky areas, and is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The whitebeam is also valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
