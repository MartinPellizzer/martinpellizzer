Saxifraga virginiensis var. subintegra

Saxifraga virginiensis var. subintegra, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of elliptic, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its preference for cool, humid climates.
