Sagittaria sagittifolia var. edulis

Sagittaria sagittifolia var. edulis, commonly known as the edible arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that resemble arrows, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples as a food source.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds.
