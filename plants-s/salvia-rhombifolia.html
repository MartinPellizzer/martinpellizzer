Salvia rhombifolia

Salvia rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhomboid sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
