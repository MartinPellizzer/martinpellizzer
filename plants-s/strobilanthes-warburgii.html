Strobilanthes warburgii

Strobilanthes warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's flameflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Strobilanthes warburgii is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
