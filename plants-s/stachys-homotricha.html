Stachys homotricha

Stachys homotricha, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dense, hairy foliage and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys homotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
