Scutellaria aurata

Scutellaria aurata, commonly known as golden skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat.

Scutellaria aurata thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks, and it is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
