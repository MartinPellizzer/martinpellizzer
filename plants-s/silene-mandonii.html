Silene mandonii

Silene mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and often succulent stems.

Silene mandonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
