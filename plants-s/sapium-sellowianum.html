Sapium sellowianum

Sapium sellowianum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or red mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
