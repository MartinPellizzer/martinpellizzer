Solanum pseudoamericanum

Solanum pseudoamericanum, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its fruits are typically bitter and contain alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum pseudoamericanum is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
