Stachys fruticulosa subsp. grossheimii

Stachys fruticulosa subsp.

grossheimii, commonly known as the Sierra grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and are arranged in spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.
