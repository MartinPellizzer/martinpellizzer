Satureja chionistrae

Satureja chionistrae, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Satureja chionistrae is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
