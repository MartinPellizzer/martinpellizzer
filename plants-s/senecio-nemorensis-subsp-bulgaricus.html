Senecio nemorensis subsp. bulgaricus

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
