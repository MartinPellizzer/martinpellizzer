Senecio sonnei

Senecio sonnei, commonly known as the Sonne's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its hairy, glandular leaves.

Senecio sonnei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
