Scleranthus perennis var. polycnemoides

Scleranthus perennis var. polycnemoides, commonly known as the clustered sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
