Suregada gossweileri

Suregada gossweileri, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or white, tubular flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides. Its leaves are typically opposite and oval-shaped, with a rough texture.

Suregada gossweileri is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
