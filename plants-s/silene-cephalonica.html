Silene cephalonica

Silene cephalonica, commonly known as the Greek catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to its arid environment.

Silene cephalonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
