Symplocos multibracteata

Symplocos multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos multibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
