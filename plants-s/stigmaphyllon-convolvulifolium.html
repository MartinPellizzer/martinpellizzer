Stigmaphyllon convolvulifolium

Stigmaphyllon convolvulifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of purple and pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing structures.
