Sorbus alnifolia

Sorbus alnifolia, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas.
