Solidago marginella var. sublanceolata

Solidago marginella var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
