Stachys cymbalaria

Stachys cymbalaria, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys cymbalaria is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
