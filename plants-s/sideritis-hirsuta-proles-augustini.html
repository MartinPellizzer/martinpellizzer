Sideritis hirsuta proles augustini

Sideritis hirsuta proles augustini, commonly known as the Augustin's hairy ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its hairy foliage and robust growth habit make it well adapted to rocky and dry environments.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
