Sarothra canadensis

Sarothra canadensis, commonly known as Canada thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems, basal rosette of leaves, and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, making it highly invasive and difficult to control. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes contribute to its ecological impact.
