Schinus leucocarpa

Schinus leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-berried persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the distinctive white, fleshy fruits. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, slightly astringent flavor.

Schinus leucocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
