Schizostachyum munroi

Schizostachyum munroi, commonly known as the Malayan rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that are used in the production of rattan furniture and crafts. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Schizostachyum munroi thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is an important species in both ecological and economic contexts due to its versatility and widespread use.
