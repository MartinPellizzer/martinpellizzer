Syzygium ripicola

Syzygium ripicola, commonly known as the rock apple or mountain apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are red when mature. Syzygium ripicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional practices.
