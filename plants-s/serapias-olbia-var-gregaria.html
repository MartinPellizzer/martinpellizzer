Serapias olbia var. gregaria

Serapias olbia var. gregaria, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.
