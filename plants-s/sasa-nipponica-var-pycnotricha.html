Sasa nipponica var. pycnotricha

Sasa nipponica var. pycnotricha, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a variant of the sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial shrub that grows in dense stands, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
