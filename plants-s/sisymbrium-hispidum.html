Sisymbrium hispidum

Sisymbrium hispidum, commonly known as hoary brookweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
