Sagraea salicifolia

Sagraea salicifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sagraea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its species name *salicifolia*. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is native to regions in southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sagraea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
