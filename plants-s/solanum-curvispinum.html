Solanum curvispinum

Solanum curvispinum, commonly known as the curly-spined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, violet flowers. Its fruits are typically green berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum curvispinum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
