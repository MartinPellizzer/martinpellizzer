Steviopsis

Steviopsis, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. They are characterized by their aromatic leaves, which contain compounds similar to those found in stevia. Steviopsis species are often cultivated for their use in herbal teas and as ornamental plants.

The genus includes several species, such as Steviopsis abutilifolia, which is valued for its medicinal properties.
