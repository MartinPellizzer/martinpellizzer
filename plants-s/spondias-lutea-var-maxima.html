Spondias lutea var. maxima

Spondias lutea var. maxima, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the dry season. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a tangy flavor and is used in traditional cuisines.
