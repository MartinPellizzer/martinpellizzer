Stachys sendteri f. versipila

Stachys sendteri f. versipila, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking flower color and compact growth habit.
