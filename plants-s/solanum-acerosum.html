Solanum acerosum

Solanum acerosum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
