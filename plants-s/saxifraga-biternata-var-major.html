Saxifraga biternata var. major

Saxifraga biternata var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features rosettes of thick, fleshy, often lobed leaves that are a distinguishing characteristic. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in a terminal inflorescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
