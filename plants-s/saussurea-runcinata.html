Saussurea runcinata

Saussurea runcinata, commonly known as the Alpine gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
