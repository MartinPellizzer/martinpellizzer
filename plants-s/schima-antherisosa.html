Schima antherisosa

Schima antherisosa, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptic leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often found in moist, acidic soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
