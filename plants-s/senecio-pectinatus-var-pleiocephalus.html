Senecio pectinatus var. pleiocephalus

Senecio pectinatus var. pleiocephalus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive features.
