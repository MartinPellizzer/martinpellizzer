Syzygium corynocarpum

Syzygium corynocarpum, commonly known as the rough-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
