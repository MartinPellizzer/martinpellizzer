Sesamum pedalioides

Sesamum pedalioides, commonly known as the Indian sesame or wild sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a member of the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in warm climates.

Sesamum pedalioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
