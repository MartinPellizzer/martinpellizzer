Smithia erubescens

Smithia erubescens, commonly known as the redsmithia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
