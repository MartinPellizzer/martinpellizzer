Scheutzia cinnamomea

Scheutzia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Scheutzia cinnamomea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to diverse garden environments.
