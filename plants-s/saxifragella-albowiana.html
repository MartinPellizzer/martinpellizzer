Saxifragella albowiana

Saxifragella albowiana, commonly known as the albow's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. It is often found at high altitudes, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifragella albowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
