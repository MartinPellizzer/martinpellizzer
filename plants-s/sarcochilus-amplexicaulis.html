Sarcochilus amplexicaulis

Sarcochilus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical rainforests of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
