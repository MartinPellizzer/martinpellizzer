Senecio bombycopholis

Senecio bombycopholis, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and bright yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense, fluffy-looking inflorescences.

Senecio bombycopholis is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.
