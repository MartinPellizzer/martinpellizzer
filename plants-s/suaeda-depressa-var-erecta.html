Suaeda depressa var. erecta

Suaeda depressa var. erecta, commonly known as the erect saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring. Suaeda depressa var. erecta is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
