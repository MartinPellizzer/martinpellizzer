Sciaphila guianensis

Sciaphila guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese shade plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color, adapted to attract specific pollinators. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Sciaphila guianensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
