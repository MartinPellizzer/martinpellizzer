Solanum andigenum f. tiahuanacense

Solanum andigenum f. tiahuanacense, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of its native habitat. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
