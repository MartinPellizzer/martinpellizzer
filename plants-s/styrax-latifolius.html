Styrax latifolius

Styrax latifolius, commonly known as sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiky, spherical fruit clusters. Its bark is gray and smooth, often developing shallow fissures with age.

Styrax latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and landscapes.
