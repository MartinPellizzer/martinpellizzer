Salacia serrata

Salacia serrata, commonly known as the serrated salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its use remains largely under-researched.
