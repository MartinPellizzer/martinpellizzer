Sasa sadaoi

Sasa sadaoi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes form a network that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of understory vegetation.

Sasa sadaoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
