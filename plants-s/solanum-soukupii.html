Solanum soukupii

Solanum soukupii, commonly known as the southern African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple color. Solanum soukupii is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
