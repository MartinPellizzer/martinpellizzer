Syzygium sulitii

Syzygium sulitii, commonly known as the Philippine apple or red apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, edible, red to purple fruits that resemble apples. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface.

Syzygium sulitii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
