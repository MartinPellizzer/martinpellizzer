Spiranthes paranahybae

Spiranthes paranahybae, commonly known as the Paranahyba spiraea, is a rare orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its intricate, spiral-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
