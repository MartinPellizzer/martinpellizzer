Spiranthes elata var. humilis

Spiranthes elata var. humilis, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of small white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and woodlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, elongated flower spike.

This variety is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
