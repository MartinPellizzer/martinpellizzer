Salvia cadmica var. bozkiriensis

Salvia cadmica var. bozkiriensis, commonly known as Bozkir salvia, is a perennial shrub native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
