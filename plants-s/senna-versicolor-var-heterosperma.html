Senna versicolor var. heterosperma

Senna versicolor var. heterosperma, commonly known as the variable senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds with varying sizes and shapes, a trait that gives it its specific variety name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
