Silvia rondonii

Silvia rondonii, commonly known as the Rondonii orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are long and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in its dense forest habitat.

Silvia rondonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
