Saxifraga × geum var. dentata

Saxifraga × geum var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are typically oval to spatulate in shape with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
