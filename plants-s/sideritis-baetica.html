Sideritis baetica

Sideritis baetica, commonly known as Spanish heather, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Sideritis baetica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
