Strangalis adpressa

Strangalis adpressa, commonly known as the flatleaf strangler, is a unique climbing vine native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that lie close to the ground, giving it its common name. The plant exhibits a parasitic growth habit, often wrapping around host trees to access sunlight. Its reproductive system involves small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.

Despite its aggressive growth, it plays a role in shaping forest ecosystems through its interactions with other plant species.
