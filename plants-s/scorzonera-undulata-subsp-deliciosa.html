Scorzonera undulata subsp. deliciosa

Scorzonera undulata subsp.

deliciosa, commonly known as the sweet scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Scorzonera undulata subsp.

deliciosa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
