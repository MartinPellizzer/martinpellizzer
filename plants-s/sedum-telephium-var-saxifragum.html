Sedum telephium var. saxifragum

Sedum telephium var. saxifragum, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of pink or red flowers in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
