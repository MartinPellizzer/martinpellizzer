Sasa angustifolia

Sasa angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant forms dense clumps and has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa angustifolia is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
