Sida petropolitana

Sida petropolitana, commonly known as the petroplatan or rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Sida petropolitana is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
