Salvia cooperi

Salvia cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
