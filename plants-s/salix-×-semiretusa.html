Salix × semiretusa

Salix × semiretusa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage.

It is native to the temperate regions of Asia and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features slender, flexible branches that hang in long, flowing streams, creating a graceful appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped, dark green on top, and pale underneath, with a smooth texture.

Salix × semiretusa thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies, making it a popular choice for landscaping along rivers and ponds.
