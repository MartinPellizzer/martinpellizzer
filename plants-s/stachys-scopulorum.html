Stachys scopulorum

Stachys scopulorum, commonly known as the rock betony, is a perennial herb native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys scopulorum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in limestone or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
