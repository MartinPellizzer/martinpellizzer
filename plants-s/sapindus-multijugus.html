Sapindus multijugus

Sapindus multijugus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces round, woody fruits that split open to reveal several hairy seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruit's seeds contain saponins, which have cleansing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
