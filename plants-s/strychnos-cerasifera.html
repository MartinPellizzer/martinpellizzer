Strychnos cerasifera

Strychnos cerasifera, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and carving.
