Sison inundatum

Sison inundatum, commonly known as the water sison, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Sison inundatum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
