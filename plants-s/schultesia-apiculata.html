Schultesia apiculata

Schultesia apiculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature into bright red berries, which are edible for some bird species. Schultesia apiculata is a climbing plant that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
