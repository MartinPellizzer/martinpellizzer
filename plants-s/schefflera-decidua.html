Schefflera decidua

Schefflera decidua, commonly known as the deciduous umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that are typically composed of multiple leaflets arranged radiating from a central point. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
