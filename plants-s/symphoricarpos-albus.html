Symphoricarpos albus

Symphoricarpos albus, commonly known as white snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, berry-like fruits that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
