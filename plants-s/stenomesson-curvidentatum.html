Stenomesson curvidentatum

Stenomesson curvidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with distinctive, recurved teeth along the margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a potential candidate for land rehabilitation projects.
