Styrax davillifolius

Styrax davillifolius, commonly known as the white styrax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet.

Styrax davillifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
