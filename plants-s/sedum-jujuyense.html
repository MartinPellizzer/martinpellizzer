Sedum jujuyense

Sedum jujuyense, commonly known as the Jujuy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andes mountains of Argentina.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Sedum jujuyense is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it an excellent addition to arid landscapes.
