Solanum rionegrinum

Solanum rionegrinum, commonly known as the Rio Negro nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum rionegrinum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
