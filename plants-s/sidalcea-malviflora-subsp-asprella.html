Sidalcea malviflora subsp. asprella

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

asprella, commonly known as the rough sidalcea, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
