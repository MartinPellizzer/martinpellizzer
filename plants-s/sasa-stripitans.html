Sasa stripitans

Sasa stripitans, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or strip-leaf bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming thickets. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often striped with a lighter color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa stripitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
