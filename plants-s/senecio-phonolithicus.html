Senecio phonolithicus

Senecio phonolithicus, commonly known as the phonolithic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
