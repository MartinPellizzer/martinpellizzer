Scilla fischeri

Scilla fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its clusters of blue or violet, bell-shaped flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

Scilla fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
