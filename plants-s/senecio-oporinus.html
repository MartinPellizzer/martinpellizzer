Senecio oporinus

Senecio oporinus, commonly known as the blue chalk scale, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that has a powdery coating. The leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage in arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
