Secretania loranthacea

Secretania loranthacea, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of various host trees, such as oaks and pines.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow berries that are dispersed by birds. It is found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, making it an important component of forest ecosystems.
