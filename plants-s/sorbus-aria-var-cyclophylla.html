Sorbus aria var. cyclophylla

Sorbus aria var. cyclophylla, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
