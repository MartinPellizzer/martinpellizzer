Santalodes caudatum

Santalodes caudatum, commonly known as the hairy sandalwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic shrub that relies on other plants for some of its nutritional needs. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments.

Santalodes caudatum is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic compounds.
