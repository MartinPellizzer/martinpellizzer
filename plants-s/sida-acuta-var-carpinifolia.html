Sida acuta var. carpinifolia

Sida acuta var. carpinifolia, commonly known as the silky sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
