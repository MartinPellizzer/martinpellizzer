Syngonanthus densifolius var. majus

Syngonanthus densifolius var. majus, commonly known as the showy goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
