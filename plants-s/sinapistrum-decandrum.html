Sinapistrum decandrum

Sinapistrum decandrum, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and long, trailing stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sinapistrum decandrum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
