Sesleria anatolica var. peyronii

Sesleria anatolica var. peyronii, commonly known as the Anatolian sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Turkey.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
