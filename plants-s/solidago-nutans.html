Solidago nutans

Solidago nutans, commonly known as the nodding goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its drooping, golden-yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often tinged with red.

Solidago nutans plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
