Senecio aureus var. aquilonius

Senecio aureus var. aquilonius, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
