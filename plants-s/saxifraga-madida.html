Saxifraga madida

Saxifraga madida, commonly known as the Madida saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Saxifraga madida is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
