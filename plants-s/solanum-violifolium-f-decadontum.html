Solanum violifolium f. decadontum

Solanum violifolium f. decadontum, commonly known as the purpleleaf nightshade, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral traits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are typically a deep violet or purple in color, adding to its ornamental value. Its flowers are usually small, clustered, and often exhibit a range of colors from white to pale purple.

This variant is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than for its edible properties.
