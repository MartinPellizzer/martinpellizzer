Securidaca incrassata

Securidaca incrassata, commonly known as the African spurge or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various African cultures for its purported healing benefits.
