Saxifraga draboides

Saxifraga draboides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features upright stems covered with small, rounded leaves that are often grayish or woolly in texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga draboides is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
