Sterculia kayae

Sterculia kayae, commonly known as the Kaya tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its large, simple leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain smooth, oval seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The Kaya tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
