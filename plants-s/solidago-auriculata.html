Solidago auriculata

Solidago auriculata, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces and a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Solidago auriculata is a valuable plant for pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens.
