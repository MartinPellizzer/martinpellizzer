Sisyrinchium valparadiseum

Sisyrinchium valparadiseum, commonly known as the Valparaiso blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, clumping habit. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with yellow centers, typical of the genus Sisyrinchium. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a rosette at the base, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
