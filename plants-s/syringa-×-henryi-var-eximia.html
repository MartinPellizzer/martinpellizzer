Syringa × henryi var. eximia

Syringa × henryi var. eximia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-shaped flowers that range from white to pink or lavender, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscapes and botanical collections.
