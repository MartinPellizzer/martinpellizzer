Smilax trukensis

Smilax trukensis, commonly known as the Truk smilax, is a species of climbing shrub native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and sharp, recurved thorns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Smilax trukensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
