Scorzonera crassicaulis

Scorzonera crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that can grow quite deep, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers with reflexed petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Scorzonera crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
