Sedum ryukyuense

Sedum ryukyuense, commonly known as Ryukyu stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum ryukyuense is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to prevent soil erosion.
