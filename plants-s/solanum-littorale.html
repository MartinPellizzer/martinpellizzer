Solanum littorale

Solanum littorale, commonly known as coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in sandy or muddy soils near the shoreline. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are often blue or violet in color, opening in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Solanum littorale belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its adaptability to saline and coastal environments.
