Smelowskia calycina var. porsildii

Smelowskia calycina var. porsildii, commonly known as the Porsild's bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the Arctic regions of North America.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in tundra or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs to reduce water loss.
