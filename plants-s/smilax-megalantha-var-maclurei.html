Smilax megalantha var. maclurei

Smilax megalantha var. maclurei, commonly known as the Maclure's smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
