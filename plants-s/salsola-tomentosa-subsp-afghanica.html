Salsola tomentosa subsp. afghanica

Salsola tomentosa subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as Afghan goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its glabrous, succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in dense clusters.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its fleshy stems and leaves store water, aiding in survival during droughts.

It is often found in sandy soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a medicinal plant.
