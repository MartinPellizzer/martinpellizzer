Silene lepidifera

Silene lepidifera, commonly known as the showy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Silene lepidifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
