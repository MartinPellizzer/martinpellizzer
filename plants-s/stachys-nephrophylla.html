Stachys nephrophylla

Stachys nephrophylla, commonly known as kidneyleaf stachys, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
