Sapota sericea

Sapota sericea, commonly known as the silky sapota, is a tropical tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that give way to edible fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit's skin is thick and yellowish, and it contains a single, large seed.

Sapota sericea is valued for its fruit's unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
