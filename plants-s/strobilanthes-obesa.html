Strobilanthes obesa

Strobilanthes obesa, commonly known as the fat spike gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which features a prominent, swollen calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
