Sporobolus bibansiensis

Sporobolus bibansiensis, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and serving as forage for livestock.
