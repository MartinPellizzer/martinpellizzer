Sorbus mairei

Sorbus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rowan, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
