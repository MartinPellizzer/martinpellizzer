Salsola dasyantha

Salsola dasyantha, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub or annual herb that thrives in sandy soils and is well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are small, succulent, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Salsola dasyantha is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in desert ecosystems.
