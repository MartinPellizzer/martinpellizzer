Sophora moorcroftiana var. davidi

Sophora moorcroftiana var. davidi, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree or David's silk tree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pale yellow to cream. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

The species is also noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
