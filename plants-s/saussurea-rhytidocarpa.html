Saussurea rhytidocarpa

Saussurea rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the wrinkled bark saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, grayish-blue foliage. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Saussurea rhytidocarpa is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
