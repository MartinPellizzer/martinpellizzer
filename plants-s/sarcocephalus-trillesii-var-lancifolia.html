Sarcocephalus trillesii var. lancifolia

Sarcocephalus trillesii var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candlewood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces woody, candle-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in coastal forests and dunes.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
