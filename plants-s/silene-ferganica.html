Silene ferganica

Silene ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Silene ferganica has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is primarily found in regions such as Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, and Afghanistan.
