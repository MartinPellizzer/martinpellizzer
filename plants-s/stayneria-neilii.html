Stayneria neilii

Stayneria neilii, commonly known as the Neil's water sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands and pond margins, often forming dense underwater stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged, with flowering spikes emerging from the water surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

Stayneria neilii plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
