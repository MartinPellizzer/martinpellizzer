Silene glauca var. decora

Silene glauca var. decora, commonly known as the Decorated Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, bluish-gray stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
