Scirpus debilis

Scirpus debilis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, inconspicuous greenish-brown flowers.

Scirpus debilis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
