Silene foliosa var. typica

Silene foliosa var. typica, commonly known as the typical leafy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene foliosa var. typica is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
