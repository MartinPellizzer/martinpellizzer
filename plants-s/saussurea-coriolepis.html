Saussurea coriolepis

Saussurea coriolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and adjacent regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Saussurea coriolepis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
