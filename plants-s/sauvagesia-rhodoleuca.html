Sauvagesia rhodoleuca

Sauvagesia rhodoleuca, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and drought tolerance.
