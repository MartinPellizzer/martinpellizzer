Setaria retiglumis

Setaria retiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of the grass. Setaria retiglumis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
