Stipa pubescens

Stipa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Stipa pubescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
