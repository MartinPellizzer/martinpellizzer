Sida thomsonii

Sida thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sida thomsonii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
