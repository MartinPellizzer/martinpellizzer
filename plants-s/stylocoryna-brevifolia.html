Stylocoryna brevifolia

Stylocoryna brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved stylocoryna, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.
