Silene raddeana

Silene raddeana, commonly known as the red sandrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, often branched stem that grows close to the ground. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Silene raddeana is notable for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and its ecological importance in stabilizing dunes.
