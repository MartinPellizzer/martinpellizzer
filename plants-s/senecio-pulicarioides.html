Senecio pulicarioides

Senecio pulicarioides, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio pulicarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
