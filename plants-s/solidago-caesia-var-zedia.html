Solidago caesia var. zedia

Solidago caesia var. zedia, commonly known as the Zephyr goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in native plant gardens.
