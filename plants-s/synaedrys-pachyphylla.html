Synaedrys pachyphylla

Synaedrys pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved synaedrys, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and extreme temperatures.
