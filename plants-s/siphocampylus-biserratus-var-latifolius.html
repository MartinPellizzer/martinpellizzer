Siphocampylus biserratus var. latifolius

Siphocampylus biserratus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf siphocampylus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
