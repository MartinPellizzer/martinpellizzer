Stachys circinata f. hozmariensis

Stachys circinata f. hozmariensis, commonly known as the Hozmariensis betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Tajikistan and Afghanistan.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
