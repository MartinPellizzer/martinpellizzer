Selloa plantaginea

Selloa plantaginea, commonly known as the silverleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
