Stephanotis suaveolens

Stephanotis suaveolens, commonly known as sweet scented jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like aroma. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and woody stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Stephanotis suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
