Saxifraga pentadactylis subsp. almanzorii

Saxifraga pentadactylis subsp.

almanzorii, commonly known as the Almanzor's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous habitats, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
