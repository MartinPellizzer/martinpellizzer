Saxifraga venosa

Saxifraga venosa, commonly known as the variegated saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Saxifraga venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
