Salix hypoleuca var. platyphylla

Salix hypoleuca var. platyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streambanks and in riparian zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
