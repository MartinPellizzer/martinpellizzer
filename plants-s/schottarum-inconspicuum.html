Schottarum inconspicuum

Schottarum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous schottaria, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
