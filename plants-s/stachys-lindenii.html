Stachys lindenii

Stachys lindenii, commonly known as the Lindens' hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Stachys lindenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
