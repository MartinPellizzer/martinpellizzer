Solanum commersonii var. pseudostipulatum

Solanum commersonii var. pseudostipulatum, commonly known as the Commerson's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
