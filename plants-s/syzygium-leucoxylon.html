Syzygium leucoxylon

Syzygium leucoxylon, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, and edible fruits.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it an important species in island ecosystems.
