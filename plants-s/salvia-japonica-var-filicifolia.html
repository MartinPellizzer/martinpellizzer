Salvia japonica var. filicifolia

Salvia japonica var. filicifolia, commonly known as Japanese woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to woodland landscapes.
