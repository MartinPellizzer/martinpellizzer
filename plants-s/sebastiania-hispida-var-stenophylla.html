Sebastiania hispida var. stenophylla

Sebastiania hispida var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sebastiania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
