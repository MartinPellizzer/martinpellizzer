Statice psiloclada var. intermedia

Statice psiloclada var. intermedia, commonly known as the Middle Eastern statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to thrive in dry conditions with minimal maintenance.
