Silene rohrbachiana

Silene rohrbachiana, commonly known as the Rohrbach's campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene rohrbachiana is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and ecological significance.
