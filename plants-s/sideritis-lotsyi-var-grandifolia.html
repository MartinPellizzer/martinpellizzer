Sideritis lotsyi var. grandifolia

Sideritis lotsyi var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered ironweed, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in southern Europe and North Africa. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
