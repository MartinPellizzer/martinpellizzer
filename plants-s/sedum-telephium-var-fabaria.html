Sedum telephium var. fabaria

Sedum telephium var. fabaria, commonly known as crimson sedum, is a low-growing perennial succulent native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically blue-gray, with a slightly succulent texture, and the plant is drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
