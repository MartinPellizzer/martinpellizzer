Sporobolus lenticularis

Sporobolus lenticularis, commonly known as the lens-shaped dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical seed heads that resemble small lenses, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Sporobolus lenticularis is adapted to arid environments and is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
