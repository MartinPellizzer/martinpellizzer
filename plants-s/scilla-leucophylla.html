Scilla leucophylla

Scilla leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

Scilla leucophylla is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
