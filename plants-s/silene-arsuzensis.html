Silene arsuzensis

Silene arsuzensis, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene arsuzensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
