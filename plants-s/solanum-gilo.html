Solanum gilo

Solanum gilo, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces clusters of white or pale green flowers.

Solanum gilo is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
