Scutellaria asperiflora

Scutellaria asperiflora, commonly known as the rough-flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name.

Scutellaria asperiflora is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
