Solanum dichroandrum

Solanum dichroandrum, commonly known as the two-colored nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

Solanum dichroandrum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its unique two-colored berries.
