Siella

Siella, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Siella species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require indirect sunlight. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, with Siella africana being one of the most widely cultivated.
