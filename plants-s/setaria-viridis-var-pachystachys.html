Setaria viridis var. pachystachys

Setaria viridis var. pachystachys, commonly known as giant green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to Africa.

It is a tall, annual grass that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, green spikelets that are often used as forage. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
