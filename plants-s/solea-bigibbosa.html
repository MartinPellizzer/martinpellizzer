Solea bigibbosa

Solea bigibbosa, commonly known as the two-globed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, globe-shaped seed pods. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along coastal areas.

Solea bigibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
