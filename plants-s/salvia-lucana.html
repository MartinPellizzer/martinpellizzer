Salvia lucana

Salvia lucana, commonly known as the Lucanian sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia lucana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.
