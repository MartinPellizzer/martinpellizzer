Scilla firmifolia

Scilla firmifolia, commonly known as the firm-leaved scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. Scilla firmifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and its ability to naturalize over time in suitable conditions.
