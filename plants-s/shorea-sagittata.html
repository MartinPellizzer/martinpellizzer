Shorea sagittata

Shorea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf sapanwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
