Sparganium erectum

Sparganium erectum, commonly known as the upright bur-reed, is a species of aquatic plant native to wetlands and shallow waters in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers at the top of the stems, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, floating just above the water surface.

Sparganium erectum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
