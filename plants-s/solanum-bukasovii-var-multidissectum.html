Solanum bukasovii var. multidissectum

Solanum bukasovii var. multidissectum, commonly known as the dissected nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
