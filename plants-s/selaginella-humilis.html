Selaginella humilis

Selaginella humilis, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a low-growing, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves and small, cone-like structures that produce spores. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Selaginella humilis is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its tolerance to periodic drought.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
