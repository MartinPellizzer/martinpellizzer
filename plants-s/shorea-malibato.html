Shorea malibato

Shorea malibato, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, and it produces woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Shorea malibato plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
