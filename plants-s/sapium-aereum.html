Sapium aereum

Sapium aereum, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its rapid growth and attractive foliage.
