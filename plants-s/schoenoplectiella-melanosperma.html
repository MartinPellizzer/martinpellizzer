Schoenoplectiella melanosperma

Schoenoplectiella melanosperma, commonly known as blackseed sedge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds, which give it its common name. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist soil conditions.

The botanical features of Schoenoplectiella melanosperma include slender, triangular stems and small, clustered inflorescences.
