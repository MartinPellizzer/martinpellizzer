Schoepfia navasana

Schoepfia navasana, commonly known as the Navasota silverleaf, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in the landscape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Schoepfia navasana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suited for arid environments.
