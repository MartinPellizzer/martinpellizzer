Scabiosa saxatilis var. africana

Scabiosa saxatilis var. africana, commonly known as the African rock scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
