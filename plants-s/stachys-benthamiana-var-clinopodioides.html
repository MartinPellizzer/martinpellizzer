Stachys benthamiana var. clinopodioides

Stachys benthamiana var. clinopodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
