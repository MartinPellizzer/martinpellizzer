Stipa ibarii var. anomala

Stipa ibarii var. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
