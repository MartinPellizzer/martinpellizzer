Saussurea baicalensis

Saussurea baicalensis, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or Baikal skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where its roots and rhizomes are used. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
