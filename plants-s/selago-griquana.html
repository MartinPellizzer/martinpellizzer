Selago griquana

Selago griquana, commonly known as the Grootfontein grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Selago griquana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
