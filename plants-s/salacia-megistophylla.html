Salacia megistophylla

Salacia megistophylla, commonly known as the giant salacia, is a striking member of the Salaceae family native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, leathery leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the region. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
