Senecio stolonifer

Senecio stolonifer, commonly known as spreading groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has fibrous roots and can spread rapidly through stolons, making it an aggressive weed in some regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to control in agricultural settings.
