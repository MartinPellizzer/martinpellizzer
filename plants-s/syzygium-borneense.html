Syzygium borneense

Syzygium borneense, commonly known as the Bornean apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, reddish-purple drupe that contains a single seed, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
