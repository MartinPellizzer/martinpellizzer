Sophronitis briegeri

Sophronitis briegeri, commonly known as the Brieger's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Sophronitis briegeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
