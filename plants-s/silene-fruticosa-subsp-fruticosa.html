Silene fruticosa subsp. fruticosa

Silene fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun.

The plant features upright, branched stems covered in small, woolly hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
