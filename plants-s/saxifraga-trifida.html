Saxifraga trifida

Saxifraga trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy, with showy clusters of white or pink flowers in late spring. Its flowers are usually five-petaled and arranged in a raceme, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
