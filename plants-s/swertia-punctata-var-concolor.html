Swertia punctata var. concolor

Swertia punctata var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while the flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
