Schismus perennis

Schismus perennis, commonly known as the everlasting grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Schismus perennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
