Syringodea rosea

Syringodea rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white petals and a unique, fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high moisture levels. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the forest floor.

Due to habitat loss, Syringodea rosea is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
