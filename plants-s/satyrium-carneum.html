Satyrium carneum

Satyrium carneum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red tip on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, due to their vibrant colors and scent.

Satyrium carneum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
