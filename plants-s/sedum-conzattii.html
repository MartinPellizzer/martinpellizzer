Sedum conzattii

Sedum conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky and arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
