Spergularia kurkae

Spergularia kurkae, commonly known as the Kurkae sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it retain moisture. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Spergularia kurkae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
