Schmidtia lagopoda

Schmidtia lagopoda, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Schmidtia lagopoda is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
