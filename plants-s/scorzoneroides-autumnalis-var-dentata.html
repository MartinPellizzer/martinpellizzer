Scorzoneroides autumnalis var. dentata

Scorzoneroides autumnalis var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed autumn saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically toothed or serrated, giving it its specific epithet "dentata.". The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually distinctive plant in high-altitude ecosystems.
