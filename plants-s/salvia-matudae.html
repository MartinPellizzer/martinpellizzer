Salvia matudae

Salvia matudae, commonly known as Matud's sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
