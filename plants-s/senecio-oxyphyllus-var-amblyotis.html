Senecio oxyphyllus var. amblyotis

Senecio oxyphyllus var. amblyotis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to dry and well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful management to prevent invasiveness.
