Symplocos taiheizanensis

Symplocos taiheizanensis, commonly known as the Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
