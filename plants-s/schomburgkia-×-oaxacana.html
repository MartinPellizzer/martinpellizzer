Schomburgkia × oaxacana

Schomburgkia × oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan spider lily, is a hybrid orchid belonging to the genus Schomburgkia.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and glossy.

Schomburgkia × oaxacana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
