Sideritis taurica

Sideritis taurica, commonly known as Caucasian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. Sideritis taurica is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
