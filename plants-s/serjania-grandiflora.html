Serjania grandiflora

Serjania grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Serjania grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
