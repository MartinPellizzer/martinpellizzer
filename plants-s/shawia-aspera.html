Shawia aspera

Shawia aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf shawia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Shawia aspera has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in loose, nutrient-poor substrates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
