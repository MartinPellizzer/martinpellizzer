Sebastiania corniculata var. blepharophylla

Sebastiania corniculata var. blepharophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a succulent shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
