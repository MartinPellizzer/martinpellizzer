Stemona prostrata

Stemona prostrata, commonly known as the creeping stemona, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a prostrate growth habit, with thin, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
