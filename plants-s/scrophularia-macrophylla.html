Scrophularia macrophylla

Scrophularia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, adorned with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
