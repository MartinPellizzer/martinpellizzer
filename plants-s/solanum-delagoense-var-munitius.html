Solanum delagoense var. munitius

Solanum delagoense var. munitius, commonly known as the Delagoense nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

It is often found in woodland and rocky habitats, and its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
