Senecio fernandinus

Senecio fernandinus, commonly known as the Cape York Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
