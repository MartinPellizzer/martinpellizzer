Syrenopsis stylosa

Syrenopsis stylosa, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Syrenopsis stylosa thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems and its ornamental value in aquatic gardens.
