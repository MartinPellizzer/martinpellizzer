Sorbus guadarramica

Sorbus guadarramica, commonly known as the Guadarrama rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically found in the mountainous regions of central Spain. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
