Sesbania benthamiana var. orthocarpa

Sesbania benthamiana var. orthocarpa, commonly known as the bentham's sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. It grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential agricultural significance.
