Spiraea opulifolia var. multiflora

Spiraea opulifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its profuse display of showy, pink to red flower clusters in early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, creating a cascading effect that enhances its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
