Statice gmelini

Statice gmelini, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Statice gmelini is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its historical use in perfumery.
