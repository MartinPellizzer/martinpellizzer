Solanum exiguum

Solanum exiguum, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Solanum exiguum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
