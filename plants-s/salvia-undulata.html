Salvia undulata

Salvia undulata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a mint-like scent when crushed.

Salvia undulata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
