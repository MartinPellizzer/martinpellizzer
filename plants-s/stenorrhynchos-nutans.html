Stenorrhynchos nutans

Stenorrhynchos nutans, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
