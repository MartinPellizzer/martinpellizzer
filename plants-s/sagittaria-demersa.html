Sagittaria demersa

Sagittaria demersa, commonly known as the submerged arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the nodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often appearing above the water surface.

Sagittaria demersa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
