Selinum capitellatum

Selinum capitellatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
