Solanum togoense

Solanum togoense, commonly known as Togo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and hairy leaves. Its small, white or purple flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
