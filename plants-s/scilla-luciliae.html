Scilla luciliae

Scilla luciliae, commonly known as the Lucilian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Scilla luciliae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and early blooming habit.
