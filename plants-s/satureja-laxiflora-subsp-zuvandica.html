Satureja laxiflora subsp. zuvandica

Satureja laxiflora subsp.

zuvandica, commonly known as Zuvand thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Iran. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
