Solidago drummondii

Solidago drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drummond's goldenrod is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
