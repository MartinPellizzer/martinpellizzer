Sabadilla

Sabadilla, commonly known as the "Spanish lily," is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Sabadilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and biologically unique.
