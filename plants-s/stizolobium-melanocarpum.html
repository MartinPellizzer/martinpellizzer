Stizolobium melanocarpum

Stizolobium melanocarpum, commonly known as the black fruit acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by dark, leathery pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
