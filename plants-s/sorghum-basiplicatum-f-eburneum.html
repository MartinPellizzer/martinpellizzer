Sorghum basiplicatum f. eburneum

Sorghum basiplicatum f. eburneum, commonly known as the African millet, is a wild grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
