Silene cucubalus var. bastardii

Silene cucubalus var. bastardii, commonly known as the bastard catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

The species is known for its short flowering period and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and flies.
