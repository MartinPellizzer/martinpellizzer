Spiraea sawranica

Spiraea sawranica, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and dark green, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea sawranica prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
