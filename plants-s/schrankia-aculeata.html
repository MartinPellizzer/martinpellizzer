Schrankia aculeata

Schrankia aculeata, commonly known as the spiked schrankia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Schrankia aculeata is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
