Silene porcari

Silene porcari, commonly known as the pig's garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene porcari is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with the right conditions.
