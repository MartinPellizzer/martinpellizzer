Symplocos rubiginosa

Symplocos rubiginosa, commonly known as redstem snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
