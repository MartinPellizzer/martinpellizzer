Scabiosa atropurpurea f. maritima

Scabiosa atropurpurea f. maritima, commonly known as the sea scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal or sandy environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
