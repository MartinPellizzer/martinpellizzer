Spatalla polystachya

Spatalla polystachya, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Spatalla polystachya plays a role in supporting local ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
