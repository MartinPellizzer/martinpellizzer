Stereospermum sinicum

Stereospermum sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces woody capsules as fruit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various pharmacological properties.
