Solanum pseudopersicum

Solanum pseudopersicum, commonly known as the Persian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum pseudopersicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in certain regions.
