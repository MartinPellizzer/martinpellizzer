Senna latifolia

Senna latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Senna latifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its seeds contain compounds that have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
