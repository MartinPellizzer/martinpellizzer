Spiraea × revirescens

Spiraea × revirescens, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, with small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea × revirescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
