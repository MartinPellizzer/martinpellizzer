Saraca schmidiana

Saraca schmidiana, commonly known as the Schmidian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its native habitats.
