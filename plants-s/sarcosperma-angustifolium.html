Sarcosperma angustifolium

Sarcosperma angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sarcosperma, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in arid land management.
