Selaginella wallichii

Selaginella wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan spike moss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of Asia. Its leaves are linear and arranged spirally, with a distinctive appearance that resembles moss.

Selaginella wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
