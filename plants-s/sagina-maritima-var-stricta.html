Sagina maritima var. stricta

Sagina maritima var. stricta, commonly known as sea sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to salty, sandy soils and can tolerate harsh maritime conditions.

This variety is often found in dunes and moist sand habitats along shorelines.
