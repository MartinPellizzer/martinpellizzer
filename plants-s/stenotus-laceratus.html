Stenotus laceratus

Stenotus laceratus, commonly known as the ragged leaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, often asymmetrical, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Stenotus laceratus is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
