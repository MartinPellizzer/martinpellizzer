Stillingia marginata

Stillingia marginata, commonly known as the redroot or redroot stellaria, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Stillingia marginata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
