Sideritis guillonii subsp. brevispica

Sideritis guillonii subsp.

brevispica, commonly known as the short-bracted rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The subspecies brevispica is distinguished by its shorter bracts compared to other forms of Sideritis guillonii.
