Saussurea scabiosifolia

Saussurea scabiosifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
