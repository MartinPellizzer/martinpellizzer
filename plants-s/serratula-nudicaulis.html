Serratula nudicaulis

Serratula nudicaulis, commonly known as the nakedstem sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Serratula nudicaulis is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
