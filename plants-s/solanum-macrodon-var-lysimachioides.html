Solanum macrodon var. lysimachioides

Solanum macrodon var. lysimachioides, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking contrast between the bright petals and the green calyx. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and hanging baskets.
