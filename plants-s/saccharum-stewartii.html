Saccharum stewartii

Saccharum stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's sugarcane.

It is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fibrous structure. The plant is closely related to other sugarcane species and is often cultivated for its high sugar content in its juice. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough, textured surface.

Saccharum stewartii is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
