Solanum hypacrarthrum

Solanum hypacrarthrum, commonly known as the red-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often blooming in late summer. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Solanum hypacrarthrum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
