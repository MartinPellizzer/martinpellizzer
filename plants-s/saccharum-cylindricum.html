Saccharum cylindricum

Saccharum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical bamboo or narrow bamboo, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, preferring moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Saccharum cylindricum is valued for its durability and flexibility, making it a versatile resource in local communities.
