Scaligeria polycarpa

Scaligeria polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited scaligeria, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features slender, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. Its small, white to yellowish flowers are arranged in complex umbels, and the fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
