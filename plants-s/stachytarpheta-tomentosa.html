Stachytarpheta tomentosa

Stachytarpheta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bluemouth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
