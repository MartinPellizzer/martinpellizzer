Siler zernyi subsp. laeve

Siler zernyi subsp.

laeve, commonly known as smooth siler, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless, giving it its subspecies name. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
