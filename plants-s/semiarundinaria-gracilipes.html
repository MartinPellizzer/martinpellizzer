Semiarundinaria gracilipes

Semiarundinaria gracilipes, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
