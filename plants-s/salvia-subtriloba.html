Salvia subtriloba

Salvia subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia subtriloba is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
