Silene fistulosa

Silene fistulosa, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Silene fistulosa is an annual plant, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
