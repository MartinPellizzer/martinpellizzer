Stellaria marginata

Stellaria marginata, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Stellaria marginata is often found in disturbed soils and can spread via seeds and rhizomes.
