Saxifraga strigosa var. ramosa

Saxifraga strigosa var. ramosa, commonly known as the tufted saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Saxifraga strigosa var. ramosa is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
