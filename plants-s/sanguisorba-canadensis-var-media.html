Sanguisorba canadensis var. media

Sanguisorba canadensis var. media, commonly known as Canada tea or redroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright red, urn-shaped flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking fall color and long-lasting blooms.
