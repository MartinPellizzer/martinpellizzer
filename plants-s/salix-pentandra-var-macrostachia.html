Salix pentandra var. macrostachia

Salix pentandra var. macrostachia, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
