Smilacina forskaliana

Smilacina forskaliana, commonly known as the yellow false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Smilacina forskaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
