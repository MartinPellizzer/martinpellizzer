Solanum ochroneurum var. armatum

Solanum ochroneurum var. armatum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
