Salix × janczewskii

Salix × janczewskii, commonly known as the Janczewski willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and along water features.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is relatively hardy, making it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
