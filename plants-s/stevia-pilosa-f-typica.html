Stevia pilosa f. typica

Stevia pilosa f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the hairy stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain natural glycosides used as a sugar substitute.

Due to its ornamental and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
