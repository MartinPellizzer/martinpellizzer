Schwenckia divaricata

Schwenckia divaricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple to pinkish-pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Schwenckia divaricata thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
