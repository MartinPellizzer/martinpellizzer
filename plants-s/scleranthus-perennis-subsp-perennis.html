Scleranthus perennis subsp. perennis

Scleranthus perennis subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the perennial sow-thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, often with a waxy coating. Scleranthus perennis subsp.

perennis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in temperate climates.
