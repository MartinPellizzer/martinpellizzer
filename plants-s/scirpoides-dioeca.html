Scirpoides dioeca

Scirpoides dioeca, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual, with separate spikelets for each sex. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Scirpoides dioeca plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
