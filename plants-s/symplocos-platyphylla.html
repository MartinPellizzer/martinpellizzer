Symplocos platyphylla

Symplocos platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaved wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Symplocos platyphylla is often found in woodland edges and stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
