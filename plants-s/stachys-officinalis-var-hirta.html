Stachys officinalis var. hirta

Stachys officinalis var. hirta, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with pinkish-white petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive look.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and along woodland edges, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
