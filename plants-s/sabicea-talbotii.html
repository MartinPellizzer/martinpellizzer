Sabicea talbotii

Sabicea talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a slightly waxy appearance.

Sabicea talbotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
