Scirpus perviridis

Scirpus perviridis, commonly known as the green bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, and its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or brown in color.

Scirpus perviridis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
