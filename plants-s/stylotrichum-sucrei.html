Stylotrichum sucrei

Stylotrichum sucrei, commonly known as the Sucre's tunicate, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Stylotrichum sucrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
