Smilax pavoniana

Smilax pavoniana, commonly known as the purple smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a trailing or climbing growth habit with thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Smilax pavoniana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and thickets.
