Sempervivum tectorum proles murale

Sempervivum tectorum proles murale, commonly known as the stonecrop or houseleek, is a hardy succulent plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions and its ornamental appeal make it a valued addition to many gardens.
