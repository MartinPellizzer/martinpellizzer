Salvia spectabilis

Salvia spectabilis, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia spectabilis is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and is popular in native plant gardens.
