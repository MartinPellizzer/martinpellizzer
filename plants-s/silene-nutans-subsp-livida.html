Silene nutans subsp. livida

Silene nutans subsp.

livida, commonly known as the pale-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to white, and arranged in racemes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to the more vibrant flowers of the typical form of Silene nutans.
