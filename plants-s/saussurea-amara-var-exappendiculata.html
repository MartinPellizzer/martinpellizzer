Saussurea amara var. exappendiculata

Saussurea amara var. exappendiculata, commonly known as the Himalayan spiny gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
