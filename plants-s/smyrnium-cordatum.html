Smyrnium cordatum

Smyrnium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf cowplant, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Smyrnium cordatum thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich soil.
