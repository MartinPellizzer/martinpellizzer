Sorghum saccharatum var. giganteum

Sorghum saccharatum var. giganteum, commonly known as giant sweet sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, leafy foliage, which can reach heights of several meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. Its stems and leaves are used for forage, while the sugary sap from the stalks can be tapped for syrup production.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to diverse climates and its multiple agricultural uses.
