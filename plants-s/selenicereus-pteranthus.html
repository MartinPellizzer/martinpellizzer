Selenicereus pteranthus

Selenicereus pteranthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a tropical climbing cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

Selenicereus pteranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
