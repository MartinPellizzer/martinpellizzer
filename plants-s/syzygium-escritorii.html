Syzygium escritorii

Syzygium escritorii, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. These berries are used to make pepper, which is a key spice in global cuisine.

Syzygium escritorii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruit.
