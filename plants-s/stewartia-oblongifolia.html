Stewartia oblongifolia

Stewartia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stewartia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Stewartia oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
