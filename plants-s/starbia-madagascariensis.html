Starbia madagascariensis

Starbia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan starbean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
