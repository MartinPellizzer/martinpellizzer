Sapindus abstergens

Sapindus abstergens, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with three to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, edible seed.

Its most notable botanical feature is the soapberry-like fruit, which contains a saponin-rich pulp used traditionally for cleaning purposes.
