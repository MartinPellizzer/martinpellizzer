Syzygium variifolium

Syzygium variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are variable in shape, ranging from elliptic to ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The flowers are usually white or pink, and the fruit is a woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.
