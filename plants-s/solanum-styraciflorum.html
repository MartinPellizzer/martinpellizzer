Solanum styraciflorum

Solanum styraciflorum, commonly known as the styraciflorum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, and its fruits are small, fleshy berries that ripen to a yellow or orange hue. Solanum styraciflorum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
