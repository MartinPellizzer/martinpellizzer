Symplocos striata

Symplocos striata, commonly known as the streaked wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a waxy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
