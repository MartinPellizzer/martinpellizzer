Swida bretschneideri var. crispa

Swida bretschneideri var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled swida, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
