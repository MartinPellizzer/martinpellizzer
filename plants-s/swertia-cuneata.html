Swertia cuneata

Swertia cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive cuneate base, giving it its scientific name.

Swertia cuneata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
