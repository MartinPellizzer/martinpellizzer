Salix cheilophila var. cyanolimenaea

Salix cheilophila var. cyanolimenaea, commonly known as the blue-limbed willow, is a variant of the willow species native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a distinctive coloration.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
