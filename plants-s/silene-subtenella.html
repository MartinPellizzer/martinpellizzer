Silene subtenella

Silene subtenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Silene subtenella is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
