Solanum diversifolium

Solanum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which range from ovate to lanceolate, and its clusters of small, star-shaped white or purple flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red or yellow as they mature, attracting various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
