Sorbus tianschanica var. pulcherrima

Sorbus tianschanica var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Tian Shan flowering ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often preceding the foliage in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardens in temperate climates.
