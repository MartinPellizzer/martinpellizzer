Senecio arequipensis

Senecio arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio arequipensis is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its potential use in traditional medicine.
