Scolymocephalus laevis

Scolymocephalus laevis, commonly known as the smooth-headed scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scolymocephalus laevis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
