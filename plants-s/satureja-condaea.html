Satureja condaea

Satureja condaea, commonly known as the Mexican thyme, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring small, gray-green leaves with a strong, thyme-like fragrance. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, usually pink or purple, and bloom in the spring.

Satureja condaea is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for its aromatic properties.
