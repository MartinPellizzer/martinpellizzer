Saxifraga spicata f. galacifolia

Saxifraga spicata f. galacifolia, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is native to alpine and rocky environments in Europe and is adapted to cold, sunny conditions.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
