Senecio transylvanicus

Senecio transylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio transylvanicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
