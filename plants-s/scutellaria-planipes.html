Scutellaria planipes

Scutellaria planipes, commonly known as the flat-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Scutellaria planipes is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
