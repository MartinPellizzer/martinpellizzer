Solidago simplex var. chlorolepis

Solidago simplex var. chlorolepis, commonly known as the green-leaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
