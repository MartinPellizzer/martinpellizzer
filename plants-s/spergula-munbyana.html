Spergula munbyana

Spergula munbyana, commonly known as Munby's sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

Spergula munbyana is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
