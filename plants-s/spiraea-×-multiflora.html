Spiraea × multiflora

Spiraea × multiflora, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, arching branches that give it a cascading appearance, with small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
