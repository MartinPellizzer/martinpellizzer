Stachys lindblomiana

Stachys lindblomiana, commonly known as Lindblom's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
