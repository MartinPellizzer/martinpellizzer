Sasa zephyrina

Sasa zephyrina, commonly known as the wind bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems that resemble bamboo. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, with a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa zephyrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
