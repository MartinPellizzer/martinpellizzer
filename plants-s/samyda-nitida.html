Samyda nitida

Samyda nitida, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Samyda nitida thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
