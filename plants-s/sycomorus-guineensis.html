Sycomorus guineensis

Sycomorus guineensis, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain a sticky, oily pulp. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are used to produce shea butter, a valuable natural product.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and soil stabilization in forest ecosystems.
