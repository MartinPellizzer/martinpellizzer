Squilla undulata

Squilla undulata, commonly known as the undulate squilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, a common floral arrangement in the Apiaceae family.

Squilla undulata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and fragrant properties.
