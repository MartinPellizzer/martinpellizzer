Sida rhombifolia var. maderensis

Sida rhombifolia var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's horsegram, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, inflated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is used in some regions for forage and soil stabilization.
