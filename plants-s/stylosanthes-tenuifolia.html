Stylosanthes tenuifolia

Stylosanthes tenuifolia, commonly known as slender stylosanthes, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage species for livestock in dry climates. Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through root nodules enhances soil fertility, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.

Stylosanthes tenuifolia is also used in erosion control and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and dense foliage.
