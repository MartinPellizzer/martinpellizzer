Solanum calygnaphalum

Solanum calygnaphalum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
