Syzygium guehoscottii

Syzygium guehoscottii, commonly known as the Gueho scottii apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, round, red fruits resembling small apples. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
