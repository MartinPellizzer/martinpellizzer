Satureja eugenioides

Satureja eugenioides, commonly known as golden sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and emit a strong, spicy fragrance.

Satureja eugenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
