Silene suksdorfii

Silene suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Silene suksdorfii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
