Syzygium micropodum

Syzygium micropodum, commonly known as the small-leaf apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a globose berry that changes from green to red when mature, often containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
