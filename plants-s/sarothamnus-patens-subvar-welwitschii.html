Sarothamnus patens subvar. welwitschii

Sarothamnus patens subvar. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and spiny stems, which provide structural support and protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by papery, winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
