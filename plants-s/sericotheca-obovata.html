Sericotheca obovata

Sericotheca obovata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Sericotheca obovata is notable for its rapid growth and ability to become invasive in certain regions.
