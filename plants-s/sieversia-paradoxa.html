Sieversia paradoxa

Sieversia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical sievegrass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Sieversia paradoxa is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
