Stigmaphyllon stylopogon

Stigmaphyllon stylopogon, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or golden star, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Stigmaphyllon stylopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
