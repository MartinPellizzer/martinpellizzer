Salvia verbenaca

Salvia verbenaca, commonly known as bee balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming from summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the plant often grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet.

Salvia verbenaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
