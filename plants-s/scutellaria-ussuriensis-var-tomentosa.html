Scutellaria ussuriensis var. tomentosa

Scutellaria ussuriensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Siberian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It features a square, branched stem that is often covered with a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
