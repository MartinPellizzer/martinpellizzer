Solanum hernandesii

Solanum hernandesii, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and egg-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
