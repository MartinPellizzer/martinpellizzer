Semiarundinaria shapoensis

Semiarundinaria shapoensis, commonly known as the Shapo bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has a dense root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
