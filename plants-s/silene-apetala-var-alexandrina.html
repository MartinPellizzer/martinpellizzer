Silene apetala var. alexandrina

Silene apetala var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.
