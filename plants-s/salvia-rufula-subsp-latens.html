Salvia rufula subsp. latens

Salvia rufula subsp.

latens, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
