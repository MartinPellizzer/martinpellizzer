Salabertia

Salabertia, commonly known as the rainforest bell heather, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Salabertia species are characterized by their small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. They are often shrubs or herbs with dense, compact growth forms.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
