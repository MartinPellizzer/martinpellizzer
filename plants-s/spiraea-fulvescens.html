Spiraea fulvescens

Spiraea fulvescens, commonly known as the goldenweeds, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, arching stems covered in small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea fulvescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
