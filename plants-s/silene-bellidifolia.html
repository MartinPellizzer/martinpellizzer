Silene bellidifolia

Silene bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower silene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Silene bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
