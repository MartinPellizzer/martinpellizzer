Senecio fragrans

Senecio fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The fragrant scent of its flowers attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
