Silene coeli-rosa subvar. speciosa

Silene coeli-rosa subvar. speciosa, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant grows up to 60 cm tall, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and they have five petals that are often reflexed at the tips.

This species is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
