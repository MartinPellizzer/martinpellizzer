Senecio begoniifolius

Senecio begoniifolius, commonly known as the begonia-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, resembling those of begonias. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in late summer.

Senecio begoniifolius is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
