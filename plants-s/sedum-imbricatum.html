Sedum imbricatum

Sedum imbricatum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Sedum imbricatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, requiring minimal water and care.
