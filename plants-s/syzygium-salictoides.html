Syzygium salictoides

Syzygium salictoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Syzygium salictoides thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
