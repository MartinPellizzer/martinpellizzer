Sternbergia pulchella

Sternbergia pulchella, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces violet or purple-colored blooms with a yellow center. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
