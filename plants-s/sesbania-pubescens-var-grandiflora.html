Sesbania pubescens var. grandiflora

Sesbania pubescens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can grow in a variety of habitats, including savannas and agricultural fields. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
