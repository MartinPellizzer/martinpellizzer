Setcreasea brevifolia

Setcreasea brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf setcreasea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
