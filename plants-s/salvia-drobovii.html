Salvia drobovii

Salvia drobovii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
