Struthanthus armandianus

Struthanthus armandianus, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and along rocky slopes.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
