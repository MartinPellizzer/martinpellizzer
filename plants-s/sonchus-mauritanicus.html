Sonchus mauritanicus

Sonchus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Sudan sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Sonchus mauritanicus is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
