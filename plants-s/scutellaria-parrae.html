Scutellaria parrae

Scutellaria parrae, commonly known as the Parra's bluehead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting various pollinators. Scutellaria parrae is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a specialty plant in horticulture.
