Sophora ternata

Sophora ternata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
