Sideritis hirsuta var. tomentosa

Sideritis hirsuta var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces spikes of pink to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, with this variety containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
