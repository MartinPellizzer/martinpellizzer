Sechium mexicanum

Sechium mexicanum, commonly known as the chayote, is a tropical vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It produces a distinctive, pear-shaped fruit with a white, edible flesh and a crisp texture. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a nutritious addition to various cuisines.
