Saponaria ocymoides subsp. alsinoides

Saponaria ocymoides subsp.

alsinoides, commonly known as the Alsike saponaria, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often hairy, and it is known for its mild soap-like scent when crushed.
