Sapium fragile

Sapium fragile, commonly known as the brittle sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
