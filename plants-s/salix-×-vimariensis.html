Salix × vimariensis

Salix × vimariensis, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix cinerea and Salix viminalis, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green upper surface. It produces catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive biology.

This species is often found in wet or moist soils, making it a common sight in river valleys and along watercourses.
