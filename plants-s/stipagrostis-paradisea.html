Stipagrostis paradisea

Stipagrostis paradisea, commonly known as the paradise grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

Stipagrostis paradisea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
