Selaginella coryi

Selaginella coryi, commonly known as the Colorado spruce moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping growth habit with short, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures called microsporangia and megasporangia.

Selaginella coryi is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
