Solanum leucosporum

Solanum leucosporum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, and it produces small, yellow berries that ripen in the summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
