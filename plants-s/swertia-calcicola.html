Swertia calcicola

Swertia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, limestone-rich soils and is typically found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, blue or violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are known for their striking color contrast against the rugged terrain.

Swertia calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
