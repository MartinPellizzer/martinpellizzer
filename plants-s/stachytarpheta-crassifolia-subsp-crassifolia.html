Stachytarpheta crassifolia subsp. crassifolia

Stachytarpheta crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stachytarpheta, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of blue or purple flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
