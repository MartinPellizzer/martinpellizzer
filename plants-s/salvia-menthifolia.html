Salvia menthifolia

Salvia menthifolia, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a minty, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
