Selinum glaucum

Selinum glaucum, commonly known as spotted globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of silver-blue, hairy leaves that are typically covered in white spots. The plant produces spherical, blue or purple flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Selinum glaucum is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
