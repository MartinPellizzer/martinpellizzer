Senecio vagans

Senecio vagans, commonly known as the wandering senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio vagans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
