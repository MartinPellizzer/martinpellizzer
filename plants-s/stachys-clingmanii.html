Stachys clingmanii

Stachys clingmanii, commonly known as Clingman's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Stachys clingmanii is often found in the Appalachian region, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
