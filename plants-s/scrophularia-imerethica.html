Scrophularia imerethica

Scrophularia imerethica, commonly known as the Greek scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia imerethica is often found in dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
