Senecio hercynicus subsp. ucranicus

Senecio hercynicus subsp.

ucranicus, commonly known as the Ucranicus groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces yellow flowers in the summer months, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
