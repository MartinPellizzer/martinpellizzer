Stenotaphrum oostachyum

Stenotaphrum oostachyum, commonly known as the Florida grass or broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, coarse-textured foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. This plant is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and minimal maintenance requirements. It forms a thick mat that helps prevent erosion and is commonly found in coastal and wetland areas.

Its flowering spikes are inconspicuous, and it reproduces primarily through rhizomes and seeds.
