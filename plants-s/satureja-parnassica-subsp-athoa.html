Satureja parnassica subsp. athoa

Satureja parnassica subsp.

athoa, commonly known as Alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its small, pink or white flowers are borne in whorls along the stem, and the leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographical distribution.
