Silene imbricata

Silene imbricata, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, and it has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the flowers are clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive fringed appearance on the petals.

Silene imbricata is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
