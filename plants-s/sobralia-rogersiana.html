Sobralia rogersiana

Sobralia rogersiana, commonly known as the Rogers' sobralia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Sobralia rogersiana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
