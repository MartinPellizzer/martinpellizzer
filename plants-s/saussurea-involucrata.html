Saussurea involucrata

Saussurea involucrata, commonly known as the "Goat's beard," is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its fibrous roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Saussurea involucrata thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid climates.
