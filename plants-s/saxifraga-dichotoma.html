Saxifraga dichotoma

Saxifraga dichotoma, commonly known as the forked saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Saxifraga dichotoma thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its ability to survive in extreme conditions makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
