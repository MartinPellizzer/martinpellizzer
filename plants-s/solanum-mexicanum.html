Solanum mexicanum

Solanum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are often red when mature. Solanum mexicanum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
