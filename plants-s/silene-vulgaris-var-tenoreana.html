Silene vulgaris var. tenoreana

Silene vulgaris var. tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene vulgaris var. tenoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
