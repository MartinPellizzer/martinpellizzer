Stipa leiocarpa

Stipa leiocarpa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its seed heads are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Stipa leiocarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
