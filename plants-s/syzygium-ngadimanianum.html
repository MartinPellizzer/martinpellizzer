Syzygium ngadimanianum

Syzygium ngadimanianum, commonly known as the Ngadimanianum apple or Ngadimanianum tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
