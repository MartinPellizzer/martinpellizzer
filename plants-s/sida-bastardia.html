Sida bastardia

Sida bastardia, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. Sida bastardia is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.

It is often found in deserts, grasslands, and along roadsides in its native range.
