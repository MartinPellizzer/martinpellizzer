Smilax timorensis

Smilax timorensis, commonly known as the Timor arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Indonesian island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
