Senecio douglasii var. longilobus

Senecio douglasii var. longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with elongated lobes, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
