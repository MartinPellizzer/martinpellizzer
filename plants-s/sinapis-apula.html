Sinapis apula

Sinapis apula, commonly known as the Mediterranean mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender stems covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The seeds are small and contained within siliques, which are the characteristic fruit type of the Brassicaceae family.
