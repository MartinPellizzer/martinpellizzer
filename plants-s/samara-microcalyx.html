Samara microcalyx

Samara microcalyx, commonly known as the small-calyx samara, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dry, winged fruits called samaras, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
