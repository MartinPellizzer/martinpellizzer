Sophiopsis mongolica

Sophiopsis mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian sophia, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and contribute to local biodiversity.
