Scleria ternifolia

Scleria ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
