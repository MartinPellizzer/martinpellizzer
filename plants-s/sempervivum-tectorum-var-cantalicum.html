Sempervivum tectorum var. cantalicum

Sempervivum tectorum var. cantalicum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
