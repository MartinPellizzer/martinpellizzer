Salix boganidensis

Salix boganidensis, commonly known as the Bogan River willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is typically found in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salix boganidensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for native wildlife.
