Saxifraga pubescens f. gracilis

Saxifraga pubescens f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky environments.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
