Schizoloma intertextum

Schizoloma intertextum, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Schizoloma intertextum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
