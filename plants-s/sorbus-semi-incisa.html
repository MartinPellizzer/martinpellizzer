Sorbus semi-incisa

Sorbus semi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by aggregate fruits that mature to a red-orange color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
