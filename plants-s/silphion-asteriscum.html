Silphion asteriscum

Silphion asteriscum, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Silphion asteriscum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It has been historically noted for its medicinal properties and was once highly valued in ancient Greek medicine.
