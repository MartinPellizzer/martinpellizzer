Scirpus melanorrhizus

Scirpus melanorrhizus, commonly known as black-root bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often standing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers in the summer. Its roots are dark-colored and fibrous, contributing to its common name.

Scirpus melanorrhizus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
