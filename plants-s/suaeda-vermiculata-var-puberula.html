Suaeda vermiculata var. puberula

Suaeda vermiculata var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, pubescent leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually pink or red, and it is often found in the coastal regions of Australia and New Zealand.

This variety is notable for its resilience to high salinity and drought, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
