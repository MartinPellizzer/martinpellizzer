Selaginella lobbii

Selaginella lobbii, commonly known as the African spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with spirally arranged leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle. Selaginella lobbii is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia.

It is also recognized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
