Sclerocactus schlesseri

Sclerocactus schlesseri, commonly known as the Schlesser's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its spines are relatively short and dense, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

Sclerocactus schlesseri is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping in desert environments.
