Solanum simile var. fasciculatum

Solanum simile var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its foliage consists of oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
