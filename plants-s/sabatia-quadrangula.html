Sabatia quadrangula

Sabatia quadrangula, commonly known as four-cornered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, ribbed stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers with four distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
