Solanum comitum

Solanum comitum, commonly known as the Comitum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries are typically red when ripe and contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum comitum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
