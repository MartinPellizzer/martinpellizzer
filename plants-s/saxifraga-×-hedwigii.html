Saxifraga × hedwigii

Saxifraga × hedwigii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. Its leaves are usually succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh climates.

Saxifraga × hedwigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
