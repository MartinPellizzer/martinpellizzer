Stevia urticifolia var. boliviensis

Stevia urticifolia var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and modern herbal applications.
