Setaria madecassa

Setaria madecassa, commonly known as slender foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Setaria madecassa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a forage crop in some regions.
