Sonchus rueppellii

Sonchus rueppellii, commonly known as Rueppell's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its yellow flowers bloom in the summer.

It is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
