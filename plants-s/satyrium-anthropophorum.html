Satyrium anthropophorum

Satyrium anthropophorum, commonly known as the human-eater orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, often colorful flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators, such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention in its high-altitude habitat.

The species is notable for its striking appearance and ecological significance in its native environment.
