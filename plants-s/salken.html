Salken

Salken, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. Salken is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows and mountainous areas where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
