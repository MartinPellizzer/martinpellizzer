Setaria bequaertii

Setaria bequaertii, commonly known as the bequaert's foxtail, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains of seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
