Syzygium kurzii var. andamanica

Syzygium kurzii var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This plant thrives in tropical climates and is well adapted to coastal environments.
