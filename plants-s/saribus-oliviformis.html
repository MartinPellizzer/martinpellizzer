Saribus oliviformis

Saribus oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, boat-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Saribus oliviformis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.
