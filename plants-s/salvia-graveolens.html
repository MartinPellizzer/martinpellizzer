Salvia graveolens

Salvia graveolens, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple bract-like flowers that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
