Spergula ramosissima

Spergula ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in spike-like clusters during the summer months.

Spergula ramosissima is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
