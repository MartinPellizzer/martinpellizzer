Silvia subcordata

Silvia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed silvia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Silvia subcordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
