Satureja alpina var. elatior

Satureja alpina var. elatior, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and spikes of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its distinctive scent and medicinal uses.
