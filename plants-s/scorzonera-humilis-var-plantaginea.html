Scorzonera humilis var. plantaginea

Scorzonera humilis var. plantaginea, commonly known as the meadow scorzonera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped tubers underground, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera humilis var. plantaginea is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
