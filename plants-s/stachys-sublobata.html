Stachys sublobata

Stachys sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

Stachys sublobata is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
