Sunaptea bureavi

Sunaptea bureavi, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
