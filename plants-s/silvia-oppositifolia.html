Silvia oppositifolia

Silvia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved silvia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Silvia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
