Syzygium holttumii

Syzygium holttumii, commonly known as the Bornean apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by edible, round, red fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium holttumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its habitat is primarily in lowland and hill forest areas, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
