Setaria braunii

Setaria braunii, commonly known as brown bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions, making it both a common weed and a resilient forage plant.
