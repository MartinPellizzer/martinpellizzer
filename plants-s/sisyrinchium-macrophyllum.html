Sisyrinchium macrophyllum

Sisyrinchium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Iridaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open, dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
