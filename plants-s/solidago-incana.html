Solidago incana

Solidago incana, commonly known as silverstem goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
