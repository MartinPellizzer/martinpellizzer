Synthyris reniformis

Synthyris reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats, with succulent, fleshy leaves that are kidney-shaped, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Synthyris reniformis is adapted to withstand drought and has a shallow root system that helps it conserve water efficiently.
