Solenostemon monostachyus var. amplifrons

Solenostemon monostachyus var. amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded threadleaf, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
