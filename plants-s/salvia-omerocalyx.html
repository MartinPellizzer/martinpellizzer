Salvia omerocalyx

Salvia omerocalyx, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its common name.

Salvia omerocalyx is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
