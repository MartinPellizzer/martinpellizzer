Senecio moupinensis

Senecio moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio moupinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
