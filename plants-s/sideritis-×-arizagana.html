Sideritis × arizagana

Sideritis × arizagana, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Sideritis × arizagana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
