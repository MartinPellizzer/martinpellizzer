Stelis colioglossa

Stelis colioglossa, commonly known as the orchid with the lip resembling a tongue, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a complex, branched inflorescence.

Stelis colioglossa is typically found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.
