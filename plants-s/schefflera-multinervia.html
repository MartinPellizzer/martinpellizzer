Schefflera multinervia

Schefflera multinervia, commonly known as the Hawaiian Schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically palmate with multiple lobes radiating from a central point. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
