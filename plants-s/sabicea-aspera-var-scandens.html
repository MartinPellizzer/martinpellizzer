Sabicea aspera var. scandens

Sabicea aspera var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing sabicea, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role as an important component of tropical ecosystems.
