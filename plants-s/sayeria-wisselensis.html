Sayeria wisselensis

Sayeria wisselensis, commonly known as the Wissel Mountains grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Wissel Mountains in Namibia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Sayeria wisselensis is considered a rare and endemic species.
