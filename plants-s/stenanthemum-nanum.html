Stenanthemum nanum

Stenanthemum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf stenanthemum, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.

Stenanthemum nanum is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value.
