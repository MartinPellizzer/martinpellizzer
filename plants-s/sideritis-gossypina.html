Sideritis gossypina

Sideritis gossypina, commonly known as cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Sideritis gossypina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
