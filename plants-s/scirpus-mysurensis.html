Scirpus mysurensis

Scirpus mysurensis, commonly known as the Mysore bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spikelets that produce small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Scirpus mysurensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
