Shawia ramosissima

Shawia ramosissima, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, silvery-white foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Shawia ramosissima is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
