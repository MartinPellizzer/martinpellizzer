Sarcochilus keyensis

Sarcochilus keyensis, commonly known as Key's sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rock crevices or under dense foliage. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Sarcochilus keyensis is a rare and protected species, valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
