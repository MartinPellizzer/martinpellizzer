Setaria viridis var. major

Setaria viridis var. major, commonly known as green millet or foxtail millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate regions.

It is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to yellowish in color, with bract-like structures at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its grains, which are used in food and animal feed.
