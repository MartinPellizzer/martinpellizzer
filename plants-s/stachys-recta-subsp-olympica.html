Stachys recta subsp. olympica

Stachys recta subsp.

olympica, commonly known as the Olympic betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its purported calming and digestive properties.
