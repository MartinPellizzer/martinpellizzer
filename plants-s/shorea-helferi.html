Shorea helferi

Shorea helferi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Shorea helferi plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
