Silene flammulifolia

Silene flammulifolia, commonly known as the flameleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene flammulifolia is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial shade to full sun.
