Salvia pittieri

Salvia pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia pittieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
