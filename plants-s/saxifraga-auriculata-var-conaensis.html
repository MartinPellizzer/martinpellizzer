Saxifraga auriculata var. conaensis

Saxifraga auriculata var. conaensis, commonly known as the Chinese earleaf saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to China.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
