Solanum stauntonii

Solanum stauntonii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum stauntonii is often found in woodland areas and is adapted to well-drained soils.
