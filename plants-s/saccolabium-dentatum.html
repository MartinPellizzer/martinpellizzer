Saccolabium dentatum

Saccolabium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
