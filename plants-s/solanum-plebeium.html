Solanum plebeium

Solanum plebeium, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
