Solanum huarochiriense

Solanum huarochiriense, commonly known as the Huarochiri nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas.

Solanum huarochiriense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
