Senecio obovatus f. elongatus

Senecio obovatus f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated obovate senecio, is a variant of the Senecio obovatus species, characterized by its elongated leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to southern Africa. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer months.
