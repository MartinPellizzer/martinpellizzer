Solanum ajuscoense

Solanum ajuscoense, commonly known as the Ajusco nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
