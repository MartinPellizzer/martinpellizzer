Salix × pendulina nothof. pendulina

Salix × pendulina nothof.

pendulina, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully from the canopy. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix babylonica (babylon willow). The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist soils, making it suitable for riparian zones and garden landscapes.
