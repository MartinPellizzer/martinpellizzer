Stachys haussknechtii

Stachys haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
