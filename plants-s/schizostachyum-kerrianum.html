Schizostachyum kerrianum

Schizostachyum kerrianum, commonly known as the Kerrian bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Schizostachyum kerrianum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in tropical climates.
