Saintpaulia shumensis

Saintpaulia shumensis, commonly known as the Shum's African violet, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Saintpaulia shumensis is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
