Sorbus torminalis f. intermedia

Sorbus torminalis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its intermediate features between the typical Sorbus torminalis and other closely related species. The plant exhibits small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
