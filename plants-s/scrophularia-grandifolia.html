Scrophularia grandifolia

Scrophularia grandifolia, commonly known as great blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
