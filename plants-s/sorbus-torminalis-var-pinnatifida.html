Sorbus torminalis var. pinnatifida

Sorbus torminalis var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, white, aggregate fruits that mature in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
