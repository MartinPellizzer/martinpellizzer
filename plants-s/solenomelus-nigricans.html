Solenomelus nigricans

Solenomelus nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually black or dark blue, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique parasitic relationship with host trees and its specific ecological role in forest ecosystems.
