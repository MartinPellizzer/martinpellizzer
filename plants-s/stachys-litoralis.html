Stachys litoralis

Stachys litoralis, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

Stachys litoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
