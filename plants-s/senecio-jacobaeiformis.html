Senecio jacobaeiformis

Senecio jacobaeiformis, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Senecio jacobaeiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
