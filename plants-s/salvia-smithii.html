Salvia smithii

Salvia smithii, commonly known as Smith's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia smithii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
