Saxifraga spinulosa subsp. funstonii

Saxifraga spinulosa subsp.

funstonii, commonly known as the Funston's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, rocky habitats.
