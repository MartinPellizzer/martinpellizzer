Salsola vermiculata var. papillosa

Salsola vermiculata var. papillosa, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert environments.

Salsola vermiculata var. papillosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
