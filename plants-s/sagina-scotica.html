Sagina scotica

Sagina scotica, commonly known as Scottish beachgrass, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the British Isles.

It thrives in sandy and saline environments, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh coastal conditions.

Sagina scotica plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion along the coast.
