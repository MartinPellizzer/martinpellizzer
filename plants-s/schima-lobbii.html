Schima lobbii

Schima lobbii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarack, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that turns red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Schima lobbii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
