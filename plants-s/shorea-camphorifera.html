Shorea camphorifera

Shorea camphorifera, commonly known as the camphor-laurel, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant wood. The plant is valued for its durable, aromatic timber, which is used in furniture and medicinal applications. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate.

Shorea camphorifera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
