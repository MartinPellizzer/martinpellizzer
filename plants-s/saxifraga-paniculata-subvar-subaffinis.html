Saxifraga paniculata subvar. subaffinis

Saxifraga paniculata subvar. subaffinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sun-exposed areas, often on limestone or gravelly soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in panicles.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
