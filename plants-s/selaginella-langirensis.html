Selaginella langirensis

Selaginella langirensis, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are located at the base of the plant and contain spores.

Selaginella langirensis is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
