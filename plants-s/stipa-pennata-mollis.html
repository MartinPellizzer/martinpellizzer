Stipa pennata mollis

Stipa pennata mollis, commonly known as silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and tall, slender flowering stalks that produce dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands, meadows, and along the edges of woodlands. Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

Stipa pennata mollis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insect and bird species.
