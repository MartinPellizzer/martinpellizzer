Salvia armeniaca

Salvia armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
