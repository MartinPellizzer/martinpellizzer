Stanleya pinnata

Stanleya pinnata, commonly known as the bishop's cap, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Stanleya pinnata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
