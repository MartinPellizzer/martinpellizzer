Stapelia kagerensis

Stapelia kagerensis, commonly known as the "Carrion Flower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a strong, unpleasant odor, mimicking decaying flesh to attract pollinators. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Stapelia kagerensis is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
