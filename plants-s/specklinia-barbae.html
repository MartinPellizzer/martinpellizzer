Specklinia barbae

Specklinia barbae, commonly known as the beard fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense fronds that often appear in clusters. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its rhizomes are short and thick, anchoring it to its substrate while supporting its delicate, hairy fronds.

The plant's unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
