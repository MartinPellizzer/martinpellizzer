Sporobolus rhodesiensis

Sporobolus rhodesiensis, commonly known as Rhodesian spikerush, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and fibrous rhizomes that help it thrive in wetland environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Sporobolus rhodesiensis is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated soils.
