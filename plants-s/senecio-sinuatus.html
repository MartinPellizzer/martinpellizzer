Senecio sinuatus

Senecio sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuatus Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or sinuous edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
