Syringa fauriei var. lactea

Syringa fauriei var. lactea, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
