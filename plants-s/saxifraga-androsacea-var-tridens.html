Saxifraga androsacea var. tridens

Saxifraga androsacea var. tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually lobed or toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to harsh environments with limited nutrients.
