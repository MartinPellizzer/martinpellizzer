Solanum vidalii

Solanum vidalii, commonly known as the Vidalii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, giving it its common name. Its berries are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum vidalii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates.
