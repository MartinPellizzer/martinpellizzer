Senegalia interior

Senegalia interior, commonly known as the Texas screwbean, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. The plant has small, pale green leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

Senegalia interior plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
