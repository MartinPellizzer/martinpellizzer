Solanum apocynifolium

Solanum apocynifolium, commonly known as the purpleleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
