Stylidium acuminatum subsp. acuminatum

Stylidium acuminatum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acutely pointed sun-orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia, particularly found in the southeastern regions.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and a single, erect flower spike bearing a single, showy orchid-like flower. The flower has a distinctive structure with a spurred lip and is typically yellow or orange in color.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and grassy areas.
