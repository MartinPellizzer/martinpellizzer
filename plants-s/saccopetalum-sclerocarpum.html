Saccopetalum sclerocarpum

Saccopetalum sclerocarpum, commonly known as the Natal wild fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
