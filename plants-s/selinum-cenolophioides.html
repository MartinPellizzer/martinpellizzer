Selinum cenolophioides

Selinum cenolophioides, commonly known as the silverleaf selinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
