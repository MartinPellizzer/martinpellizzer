Seguieria guaranitica

Seguieria guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani grass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
