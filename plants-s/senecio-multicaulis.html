Senecio multicaulis

Senecio multicaulis, commonly known as the blueweed or bluegum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and has a sprawling, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio multicaulis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
