Scutellaria tauricola

Scutellaria tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric skullcup, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Scutellaria tauricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
