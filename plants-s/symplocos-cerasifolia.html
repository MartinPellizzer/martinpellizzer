Symplocos cerasifolia

Symplocos cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Symplocos cerasifolia plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
