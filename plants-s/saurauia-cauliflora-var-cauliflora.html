Saurauia cauliflora var. cauliflora

Saurauia cauliflora var. cauliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered huito, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the trunk and branches. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
