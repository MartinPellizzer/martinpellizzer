Salsola oppositifolia var. verticillata

Salsola oppositifolia var. verticillata, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

Its succulent leaves and deep root system make it a common pioneer species in disturbed or degraded soils.
