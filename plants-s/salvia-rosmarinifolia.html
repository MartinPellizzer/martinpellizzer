Salvia rosmarinifolia

Salvia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, gray-green foliage, and showy blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and striking flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
