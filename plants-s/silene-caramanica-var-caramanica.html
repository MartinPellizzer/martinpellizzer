Silene caramanica var. caramanica

Silene caramanica var. caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene caramanica var. caramanica is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
