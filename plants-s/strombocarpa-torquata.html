Strombocarpa torquata

Strombocarpa torquata, commonly known as the twisted fruit tree, is a unique plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted fruits that resemble coiled horns, a feature that sets it apart from other species. The plant has small, simple leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are not only ornamental but also have historical use in traditional medicine.

Strombocarpa torquata is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
