Sipapoa glaucifolia

Sipapoa glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Sipapoa glaucifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
