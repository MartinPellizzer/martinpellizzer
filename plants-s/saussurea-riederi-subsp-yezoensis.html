Saussurea riederi subsp. yezoensis

Saussurea riederi subsp.

yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo safflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
