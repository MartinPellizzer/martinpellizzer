Salvia thermarum

Salvia thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its unique habitat and striking appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Salvia thermarum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
