Sporobolus atrovirens

Sporobolus atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in riparian zones.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate both wet and dry conditions.
