Sempervivum stiriacum var. braunii

Sempervivum stiriacum var. braunii, commonly known as the Braun's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
