Sagina bryoides

Sagina bryoides, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas, dunes, and disturbed sites. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are tiny, white or pink, clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and showy, clustered flowers.

Sagina bryoides is often used in horticulture for its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental value in rock gardens.
