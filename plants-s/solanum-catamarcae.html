Solanum catamarcae

Solanum catamarcae, commonly known as the Catamarca nightshade, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by egg-shaped berries that turn red when ripe. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
