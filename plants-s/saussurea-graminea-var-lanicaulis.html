Saussurea graminea var. lanicaulis

Saussurea graminea var. lanicaulis, commonly known as the woolly saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in meadows and rocky areas of mountainous regions.
