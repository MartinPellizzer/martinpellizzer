Solanum kitivuense var. glochidiatum

Solanum kitivuense var. glochidiatum, commonly known as the Kitivu nightshade, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, glochidiate (spiny) leaves. Its flowers are typically white or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
