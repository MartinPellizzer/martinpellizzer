Syzygium formosum

Syzygium formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering that resembles a hairy coconut. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive hairy fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
