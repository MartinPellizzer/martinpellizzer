Syzygium effusum

Syzygium effusum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or *Pterocarpus effusus*, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody pod containing flat, winged seeds.

Syzygium effusum is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.
