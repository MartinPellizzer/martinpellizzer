Sabal guatemalensis

Sabal guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan sago palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a tall, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy core. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and can extend up to 6 meters in length.

It is native to Central America and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
