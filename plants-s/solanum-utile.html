Solanum utile

Solanum utile, commonly known as the useful nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with smooth, dark green leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, containing seeds inside a fleshy pulp. Solanum utile is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both ecological and agricultural studies.
