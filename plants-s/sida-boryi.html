Sida boryi

Sida boryi, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with five petals. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Sida boryi is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
