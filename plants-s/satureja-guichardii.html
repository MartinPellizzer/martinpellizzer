Satureja guichardii

Satureja guichardii, commonly known as California mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their strong, minty aroma.

Satureja guichardii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
