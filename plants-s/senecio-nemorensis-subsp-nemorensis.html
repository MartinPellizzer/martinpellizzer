Senecio nemorensis subsp. nemorensis

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

nemorensis, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions. It is often found in rocky or grassy areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
