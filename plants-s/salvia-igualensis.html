Salvia igualensis

Salvia igualensis, commonly known as the Iquique sage, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.
