Syringa robusta f. rupestris

Syringa robusta f. rupestris, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common syringa.

It features small, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit. It is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in USDA zones 5 through 8.
