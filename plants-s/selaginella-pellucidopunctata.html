Selaginella pellucidopunctata

Selaginella pellucidopunctata, commonly known as the translucent-punctuated spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resurrection plant that can revive after prolonged dry periods. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which are arranged in clusters on the stems.

Selaginella pellucidopunctata plays a role in soil stabilization and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
