Schizachyrium bemarivense

Schizachyrium bemarivense, commonly known as the Bemarivene switchgrass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Schizachyrium bemarivense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
