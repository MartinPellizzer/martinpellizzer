Solanum granelianum

Solanum granelianum, commonly known as the Granelian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries are typically red when mature and contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments in the Andean regions.
