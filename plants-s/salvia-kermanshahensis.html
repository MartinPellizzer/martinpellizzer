Salvia kermanshahensis

Salvia kermanshahensis, commonly known as Kermanshah sage, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
