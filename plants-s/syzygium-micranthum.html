Syzygium micranthum

Syzygium micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and dark, aromatic fruits. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Syzygium micranthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
