Symplocos crassifolia

Symplocos crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos crassifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
