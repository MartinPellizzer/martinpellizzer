Senegalia ataxacantha

Senegalia ataxacantha, commonly known as the Mexican honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to spines in mature plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by leguminous pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Senegalia ataxacantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
