Soulangia rubra

Soulangia rubra, commonly known as the red soulangia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Soulangia rubra is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
