Sophora franchetiana

Sophora franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
