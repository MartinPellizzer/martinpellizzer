Silene scythicina

Silene scythicina, commonly known as the Scythian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Silene scythicina is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is an important component of tundra and alpine ecosystems.
