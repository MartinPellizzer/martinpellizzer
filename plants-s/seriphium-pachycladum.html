Seriphium pachycladum

Seriphium pachycladum, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Seriphium pachycladum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
