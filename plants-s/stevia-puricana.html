Stevia puricana

Stevia puricana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is known for its intense sweetness derived from steviol glycosides. Stevia puricana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herbal gardens.

Its leaves are often used as a natural sweetener in various culinary and medicinal applications.
