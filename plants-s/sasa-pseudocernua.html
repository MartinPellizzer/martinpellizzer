Sasa pseudocernua

Sasa pseudocernua, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and slender, culm-like stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by triangular, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Sasa pseudocernua thrives in shaded, moist environments and is commonly found in forest understories across East Asia.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.
