Scutellaria violacea

Scutellaria violacea, commonly known as violet skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Scutellaria violacea is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
