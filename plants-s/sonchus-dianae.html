Sonchus dianae

Sonchus dianae, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
