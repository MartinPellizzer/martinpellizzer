Senecio argutidentatus

Senecio argutidentatus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that have serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio argutidentatus is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
