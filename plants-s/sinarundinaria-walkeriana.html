Sinarundinaria walkeriana

Sinarundinaria walkeriana, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates.

Sinarundinaria walkeriana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
