Syzygium wilsonii subsp. wilsonii

Syzygium wilsonii subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling flames.

The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional remedies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
