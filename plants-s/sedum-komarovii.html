Sedum komarovii

Sedum komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
