Schrebera oligantha

Schrebera oligantha, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Schrebera oligantha thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
