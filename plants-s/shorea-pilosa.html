Shorea pilosa

Shorea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces fragrant flowers and develops edible, segmented fruits similar to the mangosteen. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Shorea pilosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
