Scirpus prolifer

Scirpus prolifer, commonly known as the flowering rush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers that appear at the top of its grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Scirpus prolifer plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
