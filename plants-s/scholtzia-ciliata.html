Scholtzia ciliata

Scholtzia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed goldfields, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Scholtzia ciliata has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is an annual plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
