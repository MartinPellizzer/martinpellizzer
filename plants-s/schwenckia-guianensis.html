Schwenckia guianensis

Schwenckia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Schwenckia guianensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
