Sideritis × pardoana

Sideritis × pardoana, commonly known as the Pardoan rockrose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Spain and northern Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to pink. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Sideritis × pardoana is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
