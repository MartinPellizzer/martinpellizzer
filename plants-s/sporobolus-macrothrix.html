Sporobolus macrothrix

Sporobolus macrothrix, commonly known as the broadleaf sandbur, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spiny seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, a feature that aids in its spread across open habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Sporobolus macrothrix is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
