Sorbus doerriana

Sorbus doerriana, commonly known as the Doerriana whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
