Solanum nigrum var. ochrocarpum

Solanum nigrum var. ochrocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are distinct from the typical black berries of the species. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

The yellow fruits are edible when ripe but should be consumed with caution due to the presence of toxic compounds.
