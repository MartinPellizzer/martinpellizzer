Silybum atriplicifolium

Silybum atriplicifolium, commonly known as the black salsify, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its edible roots, which have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Silybum atriplicifolium is often cultivated for its nutritional value and historical use in traditional medicine.
