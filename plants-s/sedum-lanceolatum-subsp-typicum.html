Sedum lanceolatum subsp. typicum

Sedum lanceolatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the lanceleaf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Europe. It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, arranged in dense rosettes.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological settings.
