Stachys salisii

Stachys salisii, commonly known as Salish bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color.

Stachys salisii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
