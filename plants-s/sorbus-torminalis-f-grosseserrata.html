Sorbus torminalis f. grosseserrata

Sorbus torminalis f. grosseserrata, commonly known as the serrated whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves, which are broader and more coarsely toothed compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
