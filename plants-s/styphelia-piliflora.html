Styphelia piliflora

Styphelia piliflora, commonly known as the woolly snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Styphelia piliflora thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
