Solanum conspicuum

Solanum conspicuum, commonly known as the showy nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum conspicuum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
