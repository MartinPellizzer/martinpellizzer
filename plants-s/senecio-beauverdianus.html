Senecio beauverdianus

Senecio beauverdianus, commonly known as the Cape goldfluff, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
