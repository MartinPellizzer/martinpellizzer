Syringa velutina var. palibiniana

Syringa velutina var. palibiniana, commonly known as the Palibinian Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
