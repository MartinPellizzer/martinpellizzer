Scleranthus neglectus

Scleranthus neglectus, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy stems and lobed leaves, typically growing up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wastelands.

Scleranthus neglectus is known for its resilience and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
