Silene cancellata

Silene cancellata, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Silene cancellata is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
