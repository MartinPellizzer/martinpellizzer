Silene nemoralis var. crassicaulis

Silene nemoralis var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are thick and fleshy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
