Sporobolus pyramidalis var. jacquemontii

Sporobolus pyramidalis var. jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and rigid, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize new areas.
