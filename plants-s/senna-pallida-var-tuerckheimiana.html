Senna pallida var. tuerckheimiana

Senna pallida var. tuerckheimiana, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small shrub or subshrub characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
