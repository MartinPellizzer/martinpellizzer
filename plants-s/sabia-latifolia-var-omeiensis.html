Sabia latifolia var. omeiensis

Sabia latifolia var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi senna, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
