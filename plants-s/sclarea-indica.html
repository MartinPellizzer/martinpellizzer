Sclarea indica

Sclarea indica, commonly known as the Indian angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Sclarea indica is known for its aromatic, slightly spicy scent and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and herbal remedies.
