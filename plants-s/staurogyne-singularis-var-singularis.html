Staurogyne singularis var. singularis

Staurogyne singularis var. singularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces small, dark seeds.

It is valued in aquascaping for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic appeal in freshwater setups.
