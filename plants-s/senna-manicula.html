Senna manicula

Senna manicula, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senna manicula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
