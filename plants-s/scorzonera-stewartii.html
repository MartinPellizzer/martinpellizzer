Scorzonera stewartii

Scorzonera stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant features lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers with a prominent central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Scorzonera stewartii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
