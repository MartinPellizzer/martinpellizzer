Solanum holtzii

Solanum holtzii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum holtzii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
