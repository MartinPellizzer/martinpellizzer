Salvia clarkcowanii

Salvia clarkcowanii, commonly known as Clarkcowan's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid climates.
