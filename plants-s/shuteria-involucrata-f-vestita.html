Shuteria involucrata f. vestita

Shuteria involucrata f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet-leaved shuteria, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
