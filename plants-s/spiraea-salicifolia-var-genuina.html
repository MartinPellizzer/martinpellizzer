Spiraea salicifolia var. genuina

Spiraea salicifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the European meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
