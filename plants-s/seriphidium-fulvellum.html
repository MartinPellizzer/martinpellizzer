Seriphidium fulvellum

Seriphidium fulvellum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Seriphidium fulvellum is known for its resilience to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
