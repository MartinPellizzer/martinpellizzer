Sesbania brachycarpa

Sesbania brachycarpa, commonly known as the shortpod sesbania, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Sesbania brachycarpa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil improvement and ecological restoration.
