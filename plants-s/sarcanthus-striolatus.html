Sarcanthus striolatus

Sarcanthus striolatus, commonly known as the streaked milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds known for their milky sap.

Sarcanthus striolatus serves as a host plant for several species of butterflies and is adapted to thrive in sandy or disturbed soils.
