Senghasia guacamayoana

Senghasia guacamayoana, commonly known as the golden apple star, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
