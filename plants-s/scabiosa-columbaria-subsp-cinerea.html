Scabiosa columbaria subsp. cinerea

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the gray scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its grayish-blue, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, well-drained areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
