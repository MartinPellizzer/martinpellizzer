Salix × oleifolia

Salix × oleifolia, commonly known as the golden willow, is a hybrid species within the willow family (Salicaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as stream banks and marshes, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Salix × oleifolia is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
