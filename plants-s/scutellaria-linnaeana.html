Scutellaria linnaeana

Scutellaria linnaeana, commonly known as blue beakviolet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, elongated lower lip. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, showy blossoms, and a preference for partial to full shade.

Scutellaria linnaeana is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is noted for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
