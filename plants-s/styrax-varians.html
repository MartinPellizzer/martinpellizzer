Styrax varians

Styrax varians, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its white blossoms. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Styrax varians is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
