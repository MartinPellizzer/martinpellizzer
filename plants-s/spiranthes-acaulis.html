Spiranthes acaulis

Spiranthes acaulis, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling arrangement of pale pink to white flowers along a flowering stem. The plant is a perennial herb that grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has no above-ground stems, with the entire plant structure consisting of a single, unbranched stem.

The flowers are typically arranged in a tight spiral, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
