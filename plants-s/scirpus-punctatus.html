Scirpus punctatus

Scirpus punctatus, commonly known as the spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, sharp spines. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, which give the plant its common name.

Scirpus punctatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
