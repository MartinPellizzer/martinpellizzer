Stanhopea oculata var. aureum

Stanhopea oculata var. aureum, commonly known as the golden stanhopea, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a bright yellow coloration with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include elongated pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
