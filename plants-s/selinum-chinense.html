Selinum chinense

Selinum chinense, commonly known as Chinese chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Selinum chinense thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
