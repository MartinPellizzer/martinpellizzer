Silene doganii

Silene doganii, commonly known as the Turkish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Silene doganii produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
