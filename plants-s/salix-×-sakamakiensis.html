Salix × sakamakiensis

Salix × sakamakiensis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of pollination, and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
