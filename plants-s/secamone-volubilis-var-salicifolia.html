Secamone volubilis var. salicifolia

Secamone volubilis var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved secamone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive willow-like shape and a smooth, slightly pubescent surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have five petals, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
