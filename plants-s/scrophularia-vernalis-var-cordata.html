Scrophularia vernalis var. cordata

Scrophularia vernalis var. cordata, commonly known as heart-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and early spring bloom.
