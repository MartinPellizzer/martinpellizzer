Salvia japonica f. lobatocrenata

Salvia japonica f. lobatocrenata, commonly known as the Japanese sage with lobed leaves, is a hybrid cultivar of the Salvia genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a lobed or wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting floral display.
