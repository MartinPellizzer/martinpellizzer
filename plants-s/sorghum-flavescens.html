Sorghum flavescens

Sorghum flavescens, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles that produce small, yellowish to golden spikelets. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sorghum flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
