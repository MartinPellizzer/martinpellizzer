Santolina leucantha

Santolina leucantha, commonly known as silver santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the spring, adding subtle floral interest. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina leucantha is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal medicine.
