Salvia verticillata var. mollis

Salvia verticillata var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
