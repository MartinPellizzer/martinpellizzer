Saccolabium papillosum

Saccolabium papillosum, commonly known as the hairy pincushion.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and hairy inflorescence. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
