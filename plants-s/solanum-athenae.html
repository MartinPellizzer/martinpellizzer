Solanum athenae

Solanum athenae, commonly known as the Athens nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

Solanum athenae is often found in rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates.
