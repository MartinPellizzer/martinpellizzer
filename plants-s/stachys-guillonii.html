Stachys guillonii

Stachys guillonii, commonly known as the Guillon's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
