Scirpus erectus var. debilis

Scirpus erectus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense stands along the edges of ponds and marshes.

Scirpus erectus var. debilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
