Senna splendida var. gloriosa

Senna splendida var. gloriosa, commonly known as the golden senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
