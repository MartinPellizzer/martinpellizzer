Saxifraga hallii

Saxifraga hallii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in moist, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga hallii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
