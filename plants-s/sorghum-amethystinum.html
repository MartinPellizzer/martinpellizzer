Sorghum amethystinum

Sorghum amethystinum, commonly known as the purple sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet-colored leaves and stems, which are rich in anthocyanins. The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Sorghum amethystinum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
