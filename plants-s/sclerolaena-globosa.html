Sclerolaena globosa

Sclerolaena globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its compact, globe-like shape. The plant features blue to purple bracts that resemble petals, surrounding the small, yellow disk flowers. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Sclerolaena globosa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
