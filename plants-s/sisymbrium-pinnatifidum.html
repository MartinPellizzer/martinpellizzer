Sisymbrium pinnatifidum

Sisymbrium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain the seeds.

Sisymbrium pinnatifidum is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
