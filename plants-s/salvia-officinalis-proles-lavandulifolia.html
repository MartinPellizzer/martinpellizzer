Salvia officinalis proles lavandulifolia

Salvia officinalis proles lavandulifolia, commonly known as lavender sage, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of sage and lavender.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple or blue flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and calming properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
