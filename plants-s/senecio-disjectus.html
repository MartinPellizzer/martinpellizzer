Senecio disjectus

Senecio disjectus, commonly known as the scattered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio disjectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
