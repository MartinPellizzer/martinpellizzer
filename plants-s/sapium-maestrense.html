Sapium maestrense

Sapium maestrense, commonly known as the Maestrense cupulate.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
