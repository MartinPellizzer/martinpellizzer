Stipa khovdensis

Stipa khovdensis, commonly known as the Khovd needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppes and mountainous regions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
