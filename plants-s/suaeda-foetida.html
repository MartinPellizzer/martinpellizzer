Suaeda foetida

Suaeda foetida, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is well adapted to high temperatures and low water availability. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy layer that helps reduce water loss.

Suaeda foetida is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
