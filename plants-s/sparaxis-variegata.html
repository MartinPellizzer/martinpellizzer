Sparaxis variegata

Sparaxis variegata, commonly known as the variegated crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive black or purple stripe. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Sparaxis variegata is known for its ability to bloom reliably in late winter or early spring.
