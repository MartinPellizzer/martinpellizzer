Sinarundinaria fungosa

Sinarundinaria fungosa, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, robust structure.

Sinarundinaria fungosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
