Stewartia koreana

Stewartia koreana, commonly known as the Korean stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Stewartia koreana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
