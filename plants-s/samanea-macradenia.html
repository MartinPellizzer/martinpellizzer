Samanea macradenia

Samanea macradenia, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Samanea macradenia is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in forest ecosystems.
