Sutera pedunculata

Sutera pedunculata, commonly known as the hanging suture plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble sutures, hence its common name. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological studies.
