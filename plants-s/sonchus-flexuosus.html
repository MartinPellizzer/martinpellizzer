Sonchus flexuosus

Sonchus flexuosus, commonly known as creeping sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, characterized by its smooth, hairy, or glandular stems. The leaves are alternate, often lobed or deeply toothed, and have a distinctive milky sap. It produces small yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
