Senna pilifera var. subglabra

Senna pilifera var. subglabra, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with slender, hairless stipules and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
