Seaforthia vestiaria

Seaforthia vestiaria, commonly known as the laceleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, fern-like foliage and small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish. Its unique appearance makes it a prized specimen among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation programs.
