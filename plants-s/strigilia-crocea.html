Strigilia crocea

Strigilia crocea, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Strigilia crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
