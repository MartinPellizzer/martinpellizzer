Schoenoplectus californicus var. spoliatus

Schoenoplectus californicus var. spoliatus, commonly known as the California bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, typically reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in panicles, which are often adorned with distinctive white or pale green bracts. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

This species plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
