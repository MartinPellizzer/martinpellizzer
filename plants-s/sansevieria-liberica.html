Sansevieria liberica

Sansevieria liberica, commonly known as the African spear or mother-in-law's tongue, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with light green or yellow margins. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Sansevieria liberica produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, often during the summer months.

Its air-purifying qualities also make it a favored plant for improving indoor air quality.
