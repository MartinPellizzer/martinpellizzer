Scaphochlamys subbiloba

Scaphochlamys subbiloba, commonly known as the narrow-lobed scaphochlamys, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bilobed calyx that resembles a boat shape, giving it its genus name. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It blooms with showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
