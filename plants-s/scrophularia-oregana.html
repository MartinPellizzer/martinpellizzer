Scrophularia oregana

Scrophularia oregana, commonly known as Oregon plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Oregon plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
