Saponaria calabrica var. boissieriana

Saponaria calabrica var. boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's soapwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its sap contains saponins, which can produce a foaming effect when mixed with water.
