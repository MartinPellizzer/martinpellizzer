Senecio volubilis

Senecio volubilis, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers and has a sprawling, trailing growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Senecio volubilis is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
