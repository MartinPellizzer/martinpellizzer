Scorzonera meyeri

Scorzonera meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Scorzonera meyeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
