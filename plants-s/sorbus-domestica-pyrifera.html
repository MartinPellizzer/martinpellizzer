Sorbus domestica pyrifera

Sorbus domestica pyrifera, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, white berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. The tree has a broad, rounded crown and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
