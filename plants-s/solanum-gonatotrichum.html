Solanum gonatotrichum

Solanum gonatotrichum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it often grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
