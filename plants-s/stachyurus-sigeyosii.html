Stachyurus sigeyosii

Stachyurus sigeyosii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Stachyurus sigeyosii is valued in gardens for its graceful appearance and mild fragrance.
