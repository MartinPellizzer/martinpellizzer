Salvia macrostachya

Salvia macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia macrostachya is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
