Spiranthes macropoda

Spiranthes macropoda, commonly known as the showy ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers along a single, slender stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and bogs. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a dense, basal rosette.

The showy ladies' tresses is known for its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.
