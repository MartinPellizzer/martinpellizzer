Salvia tiliifolia var. alvajaca

Salvia tiliifolia var. alvajaca, commonly known as the Alba sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
