Statice pseudolimonium

Statice pseudolimonium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its feathery leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Statice pseudolimonium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
