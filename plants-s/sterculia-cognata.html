Sterculia cognata

Sterculia cognata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow or greenish flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.
