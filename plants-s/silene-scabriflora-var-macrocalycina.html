Silene scabriflora var. macrocalycina

Silene scabriflora var. macrocalycina, commonly known as the broad-calyx silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The variety macrocalycina is distinguished by its larger calyces compared to the typical form of Silene scabriflora.
