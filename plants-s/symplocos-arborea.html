Symplocos arborea

Symplocos arborea, commonly known as the roughleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, violet-blue berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a rough, textured surface, and it thrives in partial to full shade in moist, well-drained soils.

Symplocos arborea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
