Sabal minor

Sabal minor, commonly known as the dwarf cabbage palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 feet, featuring a slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its compact size and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping. Its leaves are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance.

Sabal minor is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
