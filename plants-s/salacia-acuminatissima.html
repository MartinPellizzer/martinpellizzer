Salacia acuminatissima

Salacia acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicorniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or muddy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
