Sasa shibutamensis

Sasa shibutamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Sasa shibutamensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas across Japan and Korea.
