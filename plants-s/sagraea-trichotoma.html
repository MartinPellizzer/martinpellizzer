Sagraea trichotoma

Sagraea trichotoma, commonly known as the woolly sagraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance.

Sagraea trichotoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
