Syngonanthus hatschbachii

Syngonanthus hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the spurge family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
