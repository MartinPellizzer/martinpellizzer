Stylidium perplexum

Stylidium perplexum, commonly known as the perplexing sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique and striking floral structure. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal. Stylidium perplexum is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, where the flower's structure facilitates the transfer of pollen to specific pollinators.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
