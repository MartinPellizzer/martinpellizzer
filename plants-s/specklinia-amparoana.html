Specklinia amparoana

Specklinia amparoana, commonly known as the Amparoana staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

Specklinia amparoana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
