Syzygium laxiflorum

Syzygium laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and ornamental value. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Syzygium laxiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
