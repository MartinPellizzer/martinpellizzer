Serapias × halacsyana

Serapias × halacsyana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a natural hybrid between Serapias acaulis and Serapias halacsyana.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, which is characteristic of the orchid family. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
