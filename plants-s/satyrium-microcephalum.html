Satyrium microcephalum

Satyrium microcephalum, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often among mosses and leaf litter. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, delicate flower that has a distinctive, narrow lip. Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizome and a small, inconspicuous calyx.

The species is notable for its compact size and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its specific ecological niche.
