Sida acuta var. stipulata

Sida acuta var. stipulata, commonly known as the stinging leaf or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

Sida acuta var. stipulata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
