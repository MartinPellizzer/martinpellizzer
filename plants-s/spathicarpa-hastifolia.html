Spathicarpa hastifolia

Spathicarpa hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spathicarpa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clustered, spatulate-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. These flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Spathicarpa hastifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
