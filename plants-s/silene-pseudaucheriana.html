Silene pseudaucheriana

Silene pseudaucheriana, commonly known as the False Uvularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Silene pseudaucheriana has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
