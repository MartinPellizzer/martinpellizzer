Seneciunculus viscosus

Seneciunculus viscosus, commonly known as sticky ragwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
