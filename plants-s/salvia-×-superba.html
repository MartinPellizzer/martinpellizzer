Salvia × superba

Salvia × superba, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, purple, and blue, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia × superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
