Solanum lasianthum

Solanum lasianthum, commonly known as the purple potato or purple nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, purple berries. These berries are toxic to humans and animals but are used in traditional medicine by some indigenous groups.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in shaded forest environments.
