Serjania tridentata

Serjania tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have three distinct lobes, contributing to its common name.

Serjania tridentata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
