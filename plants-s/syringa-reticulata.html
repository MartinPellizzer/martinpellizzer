Syringa reticulata

Syringa reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Syringa reticulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
