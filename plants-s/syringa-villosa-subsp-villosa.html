Syringa villosa subsp. villosa

Syringa villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has softly hairy stems and leaves that are ovate with serrated edges. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Syringa villosa subsp.

villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
