Syringa schneideri

Syringa schneideri, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features dark green, ovate leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Syringa schneideri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
