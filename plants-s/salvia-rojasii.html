Salvia rojasii

Salvia rojasii, commonly known as the Red Salvia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants and medicinal properties. The plant features striking red or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia rojasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
