Sorbus commixta

Sorbus commixta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
