Sebastiania hispida var. megapontica

Sebastiania hispida var. megapontica, commonly known as the red-berried sebastiania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with reddish undersides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are a key feature for identification. Its compact growth habit and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
