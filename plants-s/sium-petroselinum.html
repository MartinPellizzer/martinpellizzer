Sium petroselinum

Sium petroselinum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and white umbel flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along stream banks and in swampy areas. Its root system is fibrous, and the roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves and roots, which have a mild, spicy flavor similar to parsley.
