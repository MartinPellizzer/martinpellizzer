Salvia lanicalyx

Salvia lanicalyx, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
