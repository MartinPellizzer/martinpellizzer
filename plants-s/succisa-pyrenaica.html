Succisa pyrenaica

Succisa pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Succisa pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
