Symphytum carmosina

Symphytum carmosina, commonly known as the Armenian beggar's tick, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal properties and distinctive blue flowers.
