Satureja unguentaria

Satureja unguentaria, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Satureja unguentaria thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.
