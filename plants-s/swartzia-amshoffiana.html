Swartzia amshoffiana

Swartzia amshoffiana, commonly known as the Amshoff's swartzia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces long, flat pods that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
