Stachys germanica subsp. lanata

Stachys germanica subsp.

lanata, commonly known as woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
