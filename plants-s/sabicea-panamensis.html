Sabicea panamensis

Sabicea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical moist forests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its fruits are pod-like structures that release seeds when mature, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
