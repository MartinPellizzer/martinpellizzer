Stachys torresii

Stachys torresii, commonly known as the Torres' hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Stachys torresii is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
