Scleranthus concinnus

Scleranthus concinnus, commonly known as the slender sicklegrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the family Amaranthaceae and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Scleranthus concinnus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
