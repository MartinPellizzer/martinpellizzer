Sanguisorba officinalis f. dilutiflora

Sanguisorba officinalis f. dilutiflora, commonly known as the dilute-flower form of the sweet everlasting, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to various landscape settings.
