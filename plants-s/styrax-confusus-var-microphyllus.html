Styrax confusus var. microphyllus

Styrax confusus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved fringed styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.
