Saxifraga aizoon var. valida

Saxifraga aizoon var. valida, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine areas. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from cold and desiccation.
