Stereospermum integrifolium

Stereospermum integrifolium, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, simple, and entire leaves that are typically alternate on the stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that is green when mature. The fruit has a spiny exterior and a soft, juicy interior with a distinctive flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
