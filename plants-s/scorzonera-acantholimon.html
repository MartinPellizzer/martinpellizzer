Scorzonera acantholimon

Scorzonera acantholimon, commonly known as the thorned scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Scorzonera acantholimon is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive foliage and flowering display.
