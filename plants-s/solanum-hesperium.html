Solanum hesperium

Solanum hesperium, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. Solanum hesperium belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is closely related to other species such as the common tomato and potato.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system to access underground water.
