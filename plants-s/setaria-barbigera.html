Setaria barbigera

Setaria barbigera, commonly known as the bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its seeds are wind-dispersed, contributing to its spread in natural environments.

Setaria barbigera is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
