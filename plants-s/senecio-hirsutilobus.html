Senecio hirsutilobus

Senecio hirsutilobus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
