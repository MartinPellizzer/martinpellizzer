Saponaria pinetorum

Saponaria pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saponaria pinetorum is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient addition to Mediterranean gardens.
