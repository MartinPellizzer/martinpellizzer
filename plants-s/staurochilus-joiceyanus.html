Staurochilus joiceyanus

Staurochilus joiceyanus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
