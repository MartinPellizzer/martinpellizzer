Sloanea tomentosa var. cordifolia

Sloanea tomentosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as the woolly sloanea, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically ovate or cordate in shape, with a leathery texture and a grayish-white underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
