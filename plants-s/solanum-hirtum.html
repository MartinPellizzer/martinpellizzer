Solanum hirtum

Solanum hirtum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. Solanum hirtum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
