Silene scabriflora var. guedirensis

Silene scabriflora var. guedirensis, commonly known as the Guedirensis catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
