Salvia japonica var. kaiscianensis

Salvia japonica var. kaiscianensis, commonly known as the Japanese sage or Kaiscianensis sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
