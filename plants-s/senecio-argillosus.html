Senecio argillosus

Senecio argillosus, commonly known as the clay sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
