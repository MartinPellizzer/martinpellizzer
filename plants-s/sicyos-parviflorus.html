Sicyos parviflorus

Sicyos parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive foliage.

It produces small, yellow flowers that transition into green, elongated fruits. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and tend to trail along the ground.
