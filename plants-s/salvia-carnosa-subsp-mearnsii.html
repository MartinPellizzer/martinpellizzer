Salvia carnosa subsp. mearnsii

Salvia carnosa subsp.

mearnsii, commonly known as Mearns' sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
