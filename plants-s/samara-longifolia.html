Samara longifolia

Samara longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf samara, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to distinctive samaras, which are winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly serrated edge, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Samara longifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
