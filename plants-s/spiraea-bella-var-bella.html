Spiraea bella var. bella

Spiraea bella var. bella, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has arching, slender branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
