Selaginella kelungensis

Selaginella kelungensis, commonly known as the Kelung spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in the mountainous regions of China.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Selaginella kelungensis is also notable for its reproductive structures, which include microsporangia and megasporangia.
