Stereochilus erinaceus

Stereochilus erinaceus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, kidney-shaped fruits that turn from green to brown as they mature. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with serrated edges and a unique, hairy texture on its young shoots.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
