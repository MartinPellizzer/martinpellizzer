Stachys recta var. major

Stachys recta var. major, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
