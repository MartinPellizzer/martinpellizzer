Scrophularia crithmifolia var. alpina

Scrophularia crithmifolia var. alpina, commonly known as alpine scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers. This variety is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the family.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.
