Selaginella pumila

Selaginella pumila, commonly known as the lesser clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit. The plant features tiny, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in dry, well-drained soils and can thrive in rocky crevices.

Selaginella pumila is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
