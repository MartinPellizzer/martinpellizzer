Salix × salviifolia

Salix × salviifolia, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive near water sources.
