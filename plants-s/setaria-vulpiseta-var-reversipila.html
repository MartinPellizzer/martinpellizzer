Setaria vulpiseta var. reversipila

Setaria vulpiseta var. reversipila, commonly known as the reverse hairgrass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, hair-like inflorescence that curves backward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
