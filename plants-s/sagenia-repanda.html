Sagenia repanda

Sagenia repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sagenia repanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
