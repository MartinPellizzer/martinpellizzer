Scirpus melanostachys

Scirpus melanostachys, commonly known as black-stalked bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It thrives in freshwater wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are typically dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale brown flowers.

Scirpus melanostachys plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
