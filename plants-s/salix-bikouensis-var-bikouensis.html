Salix bikouensis var. bikouensis

Salix bikouensis var. bikouensis, commonly known as the Bikou willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along streams.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
