Smilax riparia var. acuminata

Smilax riparia var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed catbriar, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with thorns and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
