Scutellaria violacea var. rotunda

Scutellaria violacea var. rotunda, commonly known as the round-leaved bluebead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, woodland habitats, thriving in partial to full shade.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological studies.
