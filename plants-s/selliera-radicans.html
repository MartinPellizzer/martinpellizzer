Selliera radicans

Selliera radicans, commonly known as the trailing ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Selliera radicans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
