Symplocos excelsa

Symplocos excelsa, commonly known as the giant wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Symplocos excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
