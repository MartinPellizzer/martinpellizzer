Sempervivum × verlotii var. modestum

Sempervivum × verlotii var. modestum, commonly known as the "Beaded Houseleek," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that have a distinctive bead-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
