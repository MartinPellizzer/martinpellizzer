Sorghum aethiopicum var. brevifolium

Sorghum aethiopicum var. brevifolium, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a short-statured grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. This variety is often cultivated for its edible grains, which are rich in nutrients and used in various traditional dishes. The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in semi-arid regions.
