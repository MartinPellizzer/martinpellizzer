Sorbus domestica var. obtusata

Sorbus domestica var. obtusata, commonly known as the Armenian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries that turn red or orange in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
