Senecio ambrosioides

Senecio ambrosioides, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange capitula that resemble a pincushion. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in composite heads.

Senecio ambrosioides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
