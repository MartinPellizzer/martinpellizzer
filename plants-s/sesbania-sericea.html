Sesbania sericea

Sesbania sericea, commonly known as silky sesbania, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with silvery hairs. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sesbania sericea is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
