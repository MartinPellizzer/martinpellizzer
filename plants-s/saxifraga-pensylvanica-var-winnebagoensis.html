Saxifraga pensylvanica var. winnebagoensis

Saxifraga pensylvanica var. winnebagoensis, commonly known as Winnebago saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in a dense inflorescence at the stem tip.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities in native plant gardens.
