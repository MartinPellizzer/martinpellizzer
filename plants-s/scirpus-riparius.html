Scirpus riparius

Scirpus riparius, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the top of the stem. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in stabilizing the soil in aquatic and marshy habitats.

Scirpus riparius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
