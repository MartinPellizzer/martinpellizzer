Senecio cumingii var. cumingii

Senecio cumingii var. cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in open grasslands and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are generally linear to lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
