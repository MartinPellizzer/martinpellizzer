Sebastiania hypoleuca var. genuina

Sebastiania hypoleuca var. genuina, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

The flowers are typically greenish-white and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
