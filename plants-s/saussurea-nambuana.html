Saussurea nambuana

Saussurea nambuana, commonly known as the Nambuana thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in folk medicine for various ailments.
