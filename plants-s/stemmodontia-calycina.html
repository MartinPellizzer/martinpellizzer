Stemmodontia calycina

Stemmodontia calycina, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant has a woody stem that is covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

Stemmodontia calycina typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
