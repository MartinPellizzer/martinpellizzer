Senecio cruciatus

Senecio cruciatus, commonly known as the cross-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Senecio cruciatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
