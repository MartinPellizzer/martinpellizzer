Sphenostemon pauciflorus

Sphenostemon pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered sphenostemon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
