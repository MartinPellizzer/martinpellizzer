Senna cumingii var. coquimbensis

Senna cumingii var. coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo senna, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of central Chile.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats along the coast.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
