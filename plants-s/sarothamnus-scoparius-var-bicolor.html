Sarothamnus scoparius var. bicolor

Sarothamnus scoparius var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored thyme shrub, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and aromatic foliage with a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its varietal name, bicolor, refers to the presence of two distinct color forms in its flowers.
