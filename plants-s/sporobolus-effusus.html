Sporobolus effusus

Sporobolus effusus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a grass species native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and prairies. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Sporobolus effusus is drought-tolerant and can survive in a variety of environmental conditions.
