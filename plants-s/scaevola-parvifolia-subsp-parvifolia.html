Scaevola parvifolia subsp. parvifolia

Scaevola parvifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered fanflower, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It features dense, compact foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
