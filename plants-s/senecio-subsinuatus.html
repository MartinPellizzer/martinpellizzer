Senecio subsinuatus

Senecio subsinuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has slender, often hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
