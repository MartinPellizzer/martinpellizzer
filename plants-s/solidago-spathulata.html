Solidago spathulata

Solidago spathulata, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, radiant flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago spathulata is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
