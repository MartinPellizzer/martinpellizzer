Syzygium heterobotrys

Syzygium heterobotrys, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental red or pink flowers. The plant has smooth, reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium heterobotrys is valued for its wood, which is highly sought after for furniture and carving due to its durability and fine grain.
