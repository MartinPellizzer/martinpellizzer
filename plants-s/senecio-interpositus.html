Senecio interpositus

Senecio interpositus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, branched stem with numerous flower heads. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

Senecio interpositus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
