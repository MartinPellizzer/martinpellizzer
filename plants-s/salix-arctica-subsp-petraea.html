Salix arctica subsp. petraea

Salix arctica subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is a member of the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and catkins.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.

Its flowers are pollinated by wind, and it produces small, winged seeds for dispersal.
