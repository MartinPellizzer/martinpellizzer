Setaria comosa var. pilosa

Setaria comosa var. pilosa, commonly known as silky wild millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth and silky, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but also has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
