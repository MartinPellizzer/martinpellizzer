Solidago muhlenbergii

Solidago muhlenbergii, commonly known as White Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and sturdy.

The species is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
