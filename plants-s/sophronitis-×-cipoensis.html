Sophronitis × cipoensis

Sophronitis × cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically features slender, erect stems with clustered, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity and adaptability in its natural habitat.

The Cipo orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
