Silphium conjunctum

Silphium conjunctum, commonly known as the jointed compass plant, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a distinctive compass-like pattern. Silphium conjunctum is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and disturbed soils.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has historical significance in Native American cultures.
