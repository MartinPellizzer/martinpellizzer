Securidaca gardneri

Securidaca gardneri, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
