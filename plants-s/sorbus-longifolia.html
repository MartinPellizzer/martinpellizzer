Sorbus longifolia

Sorbus longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, persistent fruits known as pomes. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows.
