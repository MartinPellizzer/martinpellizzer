Stipa leptothera

Stipa leptothera, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its foliage is often silver-green, giving it a distinctive appearance in its natural environment.

Stipa leptothera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.
