Syringa vulgaris var. dubia

Syringa vulgaris var. dubia, commonly known as the Siberian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in autumn. It is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
