Stylosanthes bahiensis

Stylosanthes bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian stylo, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage species in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Stylosanthes bahiensis also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
