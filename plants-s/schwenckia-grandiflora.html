Schwenckia grandiflora

Schwenckia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered schwenckia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with long, slender petals that resemble those of dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Schwenckia grandiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
