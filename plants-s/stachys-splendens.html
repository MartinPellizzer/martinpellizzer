Stachys splendens

Stachys splendens, commonly known as the crimson flame nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Stachys splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
