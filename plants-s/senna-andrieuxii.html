Senna andrieuxii

Senna andrieuxii, commonly known as the Madagascar senna, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
