Swertia multicaulis var. multicaulis

Swertia multicaulis var. multicaulis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.
