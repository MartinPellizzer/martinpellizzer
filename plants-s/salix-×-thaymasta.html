Salix × thaymasta

Salix × thaymasta, commonly known as hybrid willow, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix triandra (red willow).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland environments, such as stream banks and marshes. The plant has slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Salix × thaymasta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
