Silene tomentosa var. auriculifolia

Silene tomentosa var. auriculifolia, commonly known as the auriculate bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its woolly, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent, inflated calyx that resembles a bladder. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

This plant is often found in rocky or clay soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
