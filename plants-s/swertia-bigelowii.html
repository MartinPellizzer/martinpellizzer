Swertia bigelowii

Swertia bigelowii, commonly known as Bigelow's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Swertia bigelowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
