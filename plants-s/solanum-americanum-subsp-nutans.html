Solanum americanum subsp. nutans

Solanum americanum subsp.

nutans, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces elliptical, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are alternate along the stems. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red, containing toxic seeds.

This species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in disturbed soils and open woodlands.
