Salsola obpyrifolia

Salsola obpyrifolia, commonly known as desert spinach, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves and small, green or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Salsola obpyrifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
