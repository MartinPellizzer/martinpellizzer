Setaria inaequalis

Setaria inaequalis, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to Asia and widely cultivated for its edible grains.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in temperate climates, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its small, oval seeds are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary and industrial applications.

Setaria inaequalis also plays an important role in ecological systems, providing forage for livestock and habitat for various organisms.
