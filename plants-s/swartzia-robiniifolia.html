Swartzia robiniifolia

Swartzia robiniifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and the tree produces woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.
