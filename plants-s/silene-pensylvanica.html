Silene pensylvanica

Silene pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has alternate, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene pensylvanica is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
