Salsola melitensis

Salsola melitensis, commonly known as Malta spinach, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has succulent leaves that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor. It produces small, green flowers that are followed by small, bead-like fruits.

Salsola melitensis is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with limited water availability.
