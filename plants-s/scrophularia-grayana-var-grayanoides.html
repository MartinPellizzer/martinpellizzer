Scrophularia grayana var. grayanoides

Scrophularia grayana var. grayanoides, commonly known as the grayanum or the yellow root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
