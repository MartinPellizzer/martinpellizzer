Smilacina scilloidea var. acutifolia

Smilacina scilloidea var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved smilacina, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The leaves are lanceolate, acute, and often have a bluish-green hue with prominent veins. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
