Schmardaea recordiana

Schmardaea recordiana, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Schmardaea recordiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
