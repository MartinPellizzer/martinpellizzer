Sorbus aria var. pseudomougeotii

Sorbus aria var. pseudomougeotii, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries in the autumn. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
