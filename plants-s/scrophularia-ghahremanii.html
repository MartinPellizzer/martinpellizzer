Scrophularia ghahremanii

Scrophularia ghahremanii, commonly known as Persian figroot, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system for water retention.

The roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
