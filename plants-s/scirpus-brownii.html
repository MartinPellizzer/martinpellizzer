Scirpus brownii

Scirpus brownii, commonly known as Brown's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with tall, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a rough texture.

Scirpus brownii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
