Sisymbrium filicifolium

Sisymbrium filicifolium, commonly known as the fernleaf tansy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fern-like, deeply lobed leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members such as mustard and cabbage.

Sisymbrium filicifolium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
