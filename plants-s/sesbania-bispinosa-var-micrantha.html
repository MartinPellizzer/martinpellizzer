Sesbania bispinosa var. micrantha

Sesbania bispinosa var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-spined sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, spiny leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
