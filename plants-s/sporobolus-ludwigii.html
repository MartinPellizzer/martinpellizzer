Sporobolus ludwigii

Sporobolus ludwigii, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiny seed heads and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its seeds are dispersed by wind due to the barbed structures on the awns, allowing it to colonize disturbed areas.

Sporobolus ludwigii is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in both coastal and inland regions.
