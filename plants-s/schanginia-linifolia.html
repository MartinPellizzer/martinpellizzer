Schanginia linifolia

Schanginia linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Schanginia linifolia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
