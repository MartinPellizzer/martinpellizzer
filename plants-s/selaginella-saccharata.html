Selaginella saccharata

Selaginella saccharata, commonly known as the sugar clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporophylls. Selaginella saccharata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
