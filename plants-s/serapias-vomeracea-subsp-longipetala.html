Serapias vomeracea subsp. longipetala

Serapias vomeracea subsp.

longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal.

The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that is longer than the other petals, giving it its subspecies name. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to grow in sunny, arid environments.
