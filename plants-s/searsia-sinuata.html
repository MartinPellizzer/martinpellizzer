Searsia sinuata

Searsia sinuata, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits. The fruits are typically sour when ripe and are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
