Scirpus bahiensis

Scirpus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus bahiensis plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and in preventing erosion in coastal regions.
