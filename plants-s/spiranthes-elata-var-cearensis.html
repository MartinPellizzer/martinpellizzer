Spiranthes elata var. cearensis

Spiranthes elata var. cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Ladies' Tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, oval-shaped pseudobulbs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
