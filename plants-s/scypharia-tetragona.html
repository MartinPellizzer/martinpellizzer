Scypharia tetragona

Scypharia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled scypharia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in mild climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adding a textured appearance to the plant.

Scypharia tetragona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
