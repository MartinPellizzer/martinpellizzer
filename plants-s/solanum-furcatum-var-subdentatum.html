Solanum furcatum var. subdentatum

Solanum furcatum var. subdentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with finely serrated edges. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green. The fruit is a spherical berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
