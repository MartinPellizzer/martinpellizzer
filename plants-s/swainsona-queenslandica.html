Swainsona queenslandica

Swainsona queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland glorybower, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Swainsona queenslandica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
