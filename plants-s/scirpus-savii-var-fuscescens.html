Scirpus savii var. fuscescens

Scirpus savii var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often dark green to reddish in color, and its dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are extensive and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus savii var. fuscescens is also valued for its ability to filter water and support biodiversity.
