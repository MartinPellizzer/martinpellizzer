Salvia tenella

Salvia tenella, commonly known as creeping sage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
