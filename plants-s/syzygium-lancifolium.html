Syzygium lancifolium

Syzygium lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, hard seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Syzygium lancifolium is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
