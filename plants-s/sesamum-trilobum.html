Sesamum trilobum

Sesamum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
