Syzygium xerampelinum

Syzygium xerampelinum, commonly known as the white lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Syzygium xerampelinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
