Salvia madrensis

Salvia madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia madrensis is often cultivated for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
