Salsola praecox f. elegantissima

Salsola praecox f. elegantissima, commonly known as the silver leaf goosefoot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, bead-like fruits. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
