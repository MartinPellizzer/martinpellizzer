Sorbus cretica var. danubialis

Sorbus cretica var. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal berries, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
