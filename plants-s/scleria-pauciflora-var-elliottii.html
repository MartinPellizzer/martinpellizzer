Scleria pauciflora var. elliottii

Scleria pauciflora var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as marshes and forest understories. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in moist garden settings.
