Struthanthus llanensis

Struthanthus llanensis, commonly known as the Llanelli sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Struthanthus llanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
