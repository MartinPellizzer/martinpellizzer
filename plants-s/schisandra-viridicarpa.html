Schisandra viridicarpa

Schisandra viridicarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited five-flavor vine, is a member of the Schisandraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, elongated green berries. Its berries turn red when mature and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique flavor profile, which includes sweet, sour, bitter, pungent, and salty notes, is the source of its common name.
