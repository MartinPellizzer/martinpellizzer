Salix triandra subsp. bornmuelleri

Salix triandra subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the African willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its pollination process.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing riverbanks.
