Sedum sekiteiense

Sedum sekiteiense, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months.

Sedum sekiteiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
