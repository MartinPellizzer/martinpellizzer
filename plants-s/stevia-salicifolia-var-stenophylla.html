Stevia salicifolia var. stenophylla

Stevia salicifolia var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is often used as a natural sweetener and in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
