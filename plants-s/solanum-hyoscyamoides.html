Solanum hyoscyamoides

Solanum hyoscyamoides, commonly known as the Indian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, greenish-brown berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids such as hyoscyamine and scopolamine.
