Spiraea tomentosa

Spiraea tomentosa, commonly known as silver-leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to gardens. The plant has a compact, rounded form with silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea tomentosa is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
