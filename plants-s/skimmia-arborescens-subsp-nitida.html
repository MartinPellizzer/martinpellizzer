Skimmia arborescens subsp. nitida

Skimmia arborescens subsp.

nitida, commonly known as silver skimmia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late winter or early spring, followed by red-orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its slow growth and ability to form a neat, compact shape.
