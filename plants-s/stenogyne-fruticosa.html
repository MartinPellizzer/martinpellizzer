Stenogyne fruticosa

Stenogyne fruticosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian false hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Stenogyne fruticosa plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
