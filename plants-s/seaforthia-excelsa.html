Seaforthia excelsa

Seaforthia excelsa, commonly known as the giant seaforthia, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 5 meters, making it one of the most notable species in its genus. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are long and narrow, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Seaforthia excelsa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
