Solanum gossypinum

Solanum gossypinum, commonly known as the cottonwood nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption by humans and animals.
