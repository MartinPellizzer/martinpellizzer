Saussurea japonica var. subtomentosa

Saussurea japonica var. subtomentosa, commonly known as Japanese clouded grass or subtomentous saussurea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fibrous root system, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
