Saussurea densa

Saussurea densa, commonly known as the dense saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments, and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Saussurea densa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
