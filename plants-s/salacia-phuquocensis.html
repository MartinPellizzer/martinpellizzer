Salacia phuquocensis

Salacia phuquocensis, commonly known as Phu Quoc star gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Phu Quoc in Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions and forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are clustered in catkins and are pollinated by wind, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.
