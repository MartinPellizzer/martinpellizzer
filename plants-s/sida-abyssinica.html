Sida abyssinica

Sida abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian mallows, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly Ethiopia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a waxy coating, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Sida abyssinica is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
