Soldanella carpatica

Soldanella carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a distinctively pointed shape.
