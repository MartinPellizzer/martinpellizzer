Saxifraga hirculus var. subdioica

Saxifraga hirculus var. subdioica, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces showy pink or purple flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. It is a perennial species that is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
