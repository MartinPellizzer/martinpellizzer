Solanum takaoyamense

Solanum takaoyamense, commonly known as the Takaoyama nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Solanum takaoyamense is also of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential medicinal properties.
