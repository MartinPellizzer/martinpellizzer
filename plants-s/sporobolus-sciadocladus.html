Sporobolus sciadocladus

Sporobolus sciadocladus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and spiny, awned lemmas that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spikelets and florets, are typical of the grass family Poaceae.

Sporobolus sciadocladus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
