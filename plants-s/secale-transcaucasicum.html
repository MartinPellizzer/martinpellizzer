Secale transcaucasicum

Secale transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.
