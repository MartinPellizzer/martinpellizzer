Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp. cochinchinensis

Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp.

cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark blue to purple berries that mature in the fall.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental berries and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
