Senecio longilobus

Senecio longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has gray-green, hairy leaves with distinctive elongated lobes. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio longilobus is often found in open areas, including disturbed soils and along trails.
