Solanum tenue var. pubescens

Solanum tenue var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features hairy, glandular leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark blue or black color, containing toxic compounds such as solanine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along trails.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
