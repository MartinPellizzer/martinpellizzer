Symplocos intermedia var. trichantha

Symplocos intermedia var. trichantha, commonly known as the hairy symplocos, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
