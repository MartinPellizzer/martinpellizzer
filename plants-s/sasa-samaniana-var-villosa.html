Sasa samaniana var. villosa

Sasa samaniana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa samaniana species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright culms covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged look.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
