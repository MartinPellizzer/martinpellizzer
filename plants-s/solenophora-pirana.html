Solenophora pirana

Solenophora pirana, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble elongated petals, often with a ghostly appearance due to their pale coloration. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air and rain for sustenance. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Solenophora pirana is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a conservation concern.
