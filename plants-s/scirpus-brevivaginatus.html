Scirpus brevivaginatus

Scirpus brevivaginatus, commonly known as the short-sheathed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil. Scirpus brevivaginatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth habit and morphology make it well adapted to its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.
