Spiraea dasyantha var. wawrana

Spiraea dasyantha var. wawrana, commonly known as the Wawrana spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate ornamental value to gardens. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valued addition to alpine and perennial gardens.
