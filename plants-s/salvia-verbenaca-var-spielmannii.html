Salvia verbenaca var. spielmannii

Salvia verbenaca var. spielmannii, commonly known as the Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
