Stelis verruculosa

Stelis verruculosa, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, wiry stem with clustered, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive, wrinkled appearance, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Stelis verruculosa is appreciated for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
