Semenovia rubtzovii

Semenovia rubtzovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-bearing capsules after flowering.

Semenovia rubtzovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
