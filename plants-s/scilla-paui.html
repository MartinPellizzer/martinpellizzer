Scilla paui

Scilla paui, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Scilla paui is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable environments.
