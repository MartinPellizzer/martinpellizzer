Spilanthes disciformis var. phaneractis

Spilanthes disciformis var. phaneractis, commonly known as the showy spilanthes, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
