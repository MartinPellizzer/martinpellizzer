Sorbus umbellata var. flabellifolia

Sorbus umbellata var. flabellifolia, commonly known as the flabellifolius rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, fan-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
