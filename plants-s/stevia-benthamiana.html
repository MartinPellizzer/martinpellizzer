Stevia benthamiana

Stevia benthamiana, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as sugar substitutes. Stevia benthamiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.

Its ability to produce intense sweetness without calories has made it a valuable plant in the food and pharmaceutical industries.
