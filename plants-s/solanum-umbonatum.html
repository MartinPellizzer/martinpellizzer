Solanum umbonatum

Solanum umbonatum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers that develop into spherical, yellowish berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
