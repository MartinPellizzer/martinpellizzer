Selaginella monospora

Selaginella monospora, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are located at the tips of the stems, facilitating spore dispersal.

Selaginella monospora is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of many ecosystems.
