Symplocos tinctoria var. ashei

Symplocos tinctoria var. ashei, commonly known as the Ashe's inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
