Sarracenia alata var. rubrioperculata

Sarracenia alata var. rubrioperculata, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular pitchers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive red rim. The plant's leaves form deep, narrow traps that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to capture and digest insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

This variety is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique ecological adaptations.
