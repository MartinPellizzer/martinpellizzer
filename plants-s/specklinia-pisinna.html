Specklinia pisinna

Specklinia pisinna, commonly known as the Pisinna fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Specklinia pisinna make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
