Schizostachyum castaneum

Schizostachyum castaneum, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.

Schizostachyum castaneum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
