Saxifraga montavoniensis

Saxifraga montavoniensis, commonly known as the Montavon's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga montavoniensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
