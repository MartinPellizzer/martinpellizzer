Staphidium lappaceum

Staphidium lappaceum, commonly known as the woolly staphidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of thick, woolly, gray-green leaves that are covered in dense, white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, hairy appearance and is often found in wetland environments.
