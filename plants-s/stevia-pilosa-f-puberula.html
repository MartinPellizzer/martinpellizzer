Stevia pilosa f. puberula

Stevia pilosa f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy leaf stevia, is a variant of the stevia plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as a herb for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The hairy texture of its leaves distinguishes it from other stevia species and contributes to its unique appearance.
