Sporobolus oxyphyllus

Sporobolus oxyphyllus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Sporobolus oxyphyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
