Solidago vulgaris

Solidago vulgaris, commonly known as the common goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago vulgaris is a hardy perennial that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat.
