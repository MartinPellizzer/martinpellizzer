Sorghum eplicatum f. laxum

Sorghum eplicatum f. laxum, commonly known as the loose-leaved sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species characterized by its loosely arranged leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows as an annual grass with tall, sturdy stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. It is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it useful for both forage and grain production.
