Stachys woronowii

Stachys woronowii, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.

Stachys woronowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
