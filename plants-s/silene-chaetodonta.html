Silene chaetodonta

Silene chaetodonta, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Silene chaetodonta is often found in temperate climates and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
