Soroseris hookeriana subsp. occidentalis

Soroseris hookeriana subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western hooker's sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
