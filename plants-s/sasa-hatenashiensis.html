Sasa hatenashiensis

Sasa hatenashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa hatenashiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
