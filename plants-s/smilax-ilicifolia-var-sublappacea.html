Smilax ilicifolia var. sublappacea

Smilax ilicifolia var. sublappacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its spiny stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
