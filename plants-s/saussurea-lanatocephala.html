Saussurea lanatocephala

Saussurea lanatocephala, commonly known as the woolly-headed saffron.

It is a perennial herb native to the high altitude regions of the Himalayas, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh climatic conditions of its habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a single, prominent flower head surrounded by bracts, which are covered in fine, hairy structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
