Silene sobolevskajae

Silene sobolevskajae, commonly known as the Sobolevskaja catchfly, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Silene sobolevskajae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for medicinal properties.
