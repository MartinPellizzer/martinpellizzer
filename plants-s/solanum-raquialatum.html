Solanum raquialatum

Solanum raquialatum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that start green and turn red when mature. Solanum raquialatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
