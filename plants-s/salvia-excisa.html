Salvia excisa

Salvia excisa, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant properties. Salvia excisa thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
