Silene bupleuroides subsp. ramosa

Silene bupleuroides subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its adaptations to harsh, dry conditions.
