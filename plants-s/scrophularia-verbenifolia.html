Scrophularia verbenifolia

Scrophularia verbenifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in clusters, usually in shades of pale pink to purple.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological roles.
