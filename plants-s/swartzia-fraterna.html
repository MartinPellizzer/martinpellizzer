Swartzia fraterna

Swartzia fraterna, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, round seeds.

Swartzia fraterna is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and hill forest areas.
