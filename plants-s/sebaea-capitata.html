Sebaea capitata

Sebaea capitata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped berries. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
