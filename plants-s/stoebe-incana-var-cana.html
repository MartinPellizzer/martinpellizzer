Stoebe incana var. cana

Stoebe incana var. cana, commonly known as hoary mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy white to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a taproot system and can spread via rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
