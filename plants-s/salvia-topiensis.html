Salvia topiensis

Salvia topiensis, commonly known as the Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its flowers are typically blue to violet, clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

Salvia topiensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant scent and medicinal properties.
