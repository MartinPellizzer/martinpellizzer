Serjania cognata

Serjania cognata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink angel's trumpet, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Serjania cognata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
