Schefflera macrantha

Schefflera macrantha, commonly known as the giant umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical landscapes and indoor settings.
