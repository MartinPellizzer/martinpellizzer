Stockwellia quadrifida

Stockwellia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or divided. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in dense clusters. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to survive in dry conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rocky gardens.
