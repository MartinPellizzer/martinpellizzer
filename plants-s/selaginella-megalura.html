Selaginella megalura

Selaginella megalura, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its life cycle includes both a gametophytic and sporophytic phase, typical of bryophytes and fern allies.

Selaginella megalura plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
