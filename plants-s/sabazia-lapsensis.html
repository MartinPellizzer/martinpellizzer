Sabazia lapsensis

Sabazia lapsensis, commonly known as the Lapsen's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though modern research on its botanical compounds is limited.

Sabazia lapsensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
