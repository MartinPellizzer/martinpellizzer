Stemodia jorullensis subsp. ageratifolia

Stemodia jorullensis subsp.

ageratifolia, commonly known as the hairy stemodia, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, forming a compact inflorescence.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
