Sartoria hedysaroides

Sartoria hedysaroides, commonly known as the mountain peavine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by legume-like pods containing several seeds.

It is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity in its native habitat.
