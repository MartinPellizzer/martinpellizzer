Stachytarpheta chamissonis var. longipetiolata

Stachytarpheta chamissonis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
