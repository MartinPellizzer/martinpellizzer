Senecio pedunculatus var. albus

Senecio pedunculatus var. albus, commonly known as the White Dustplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The varietal name 'albus' refers to its pale, almost white flower color.
