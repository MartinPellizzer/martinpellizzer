Scrophularia glandulifera

Scrophularia glandulifera, commonly known as the sticky willow or glandular scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered with glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Scrophularia glandulifera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
