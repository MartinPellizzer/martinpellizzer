Silene baccifera

Silene baccifera, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often reddish in color.

Silene baccifera is known for its rapid flowering cycle and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
