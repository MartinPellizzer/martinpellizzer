Salvia obtusata

Salvia obtusata, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia obtusata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
