Staurogyne tenuispica

Staurogyne tenuispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in the Americas.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to form dense underwater stands. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
