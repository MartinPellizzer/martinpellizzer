Salvia glabrescens f. lutea

Salvia glabrescens f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces striking spikes of yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
