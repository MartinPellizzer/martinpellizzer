Salix balansae

Salix balansae, commonly known as the Balansae willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments. Salix balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
