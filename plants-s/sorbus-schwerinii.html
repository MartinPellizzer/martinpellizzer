Sorbus schwerinii

Sorbus schwerinii, commonly known as the Schwerin rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

Sorbus schwerinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
