Senecio alpester

Senecio alpester, commonly known as alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are usually blue-green in color, with a woolly texture, and elongated stems that bear small, yellow flowers. Senecio alpester is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
