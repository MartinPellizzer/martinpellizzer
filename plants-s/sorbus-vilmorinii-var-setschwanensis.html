Sorbus vilmorinii var. setschwanensis

Sorbus vilmorinii var. setschwanensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive form and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
