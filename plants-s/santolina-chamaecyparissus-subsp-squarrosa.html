Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp. squarrosa

Santolina chamaecyparissus subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and silver-gray foliage with a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appearance make it both a useful herb and a decorative plant.
