Schoenus lacustris

Schoenus lacustris, commonly known as the lake sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize aquatic habitats effectively.

Schoenus lacustris plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
