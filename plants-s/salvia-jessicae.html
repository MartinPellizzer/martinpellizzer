Salvia jessicae

Salvia jessicae, commonly known as Jessica's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
