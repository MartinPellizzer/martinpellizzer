Senecio jodopappus

Senecio jodopappus, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Senecio jodopappus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
