Stickmannia hexandra

Stickmannia hexandra, commonly known as the mountain stickman, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, upright growth habit and the presence of small, clustered flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Stickmannia hexandra is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.
