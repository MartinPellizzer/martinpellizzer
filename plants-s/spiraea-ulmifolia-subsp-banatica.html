Spiraea ulmifolia subsp. banatica

Spiraea ulmifolia subsp.

banatica, commonly known as the Banat spiraea, is a shrub native to the Balkans. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is dark green and often turns reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
