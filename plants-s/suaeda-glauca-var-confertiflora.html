Suaeda glauca var. confertiflora

Suaeda glauca var. confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, where it thrives in high-salinity conditions. Its structure includes a low-growing, mat-like form with branches that often lie close to the ground.

The flowers are usually greenish or yellowish and are arranged in dense inflorescences, contributing to its common name.
