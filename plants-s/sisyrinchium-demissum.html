Sisyrinchium demissum

Sisyrinchium demissum, commonly known as the lesser blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Sisyrinchium demissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
