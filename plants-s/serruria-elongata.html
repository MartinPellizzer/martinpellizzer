Serruria elongata

Serruria elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved serruria, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
