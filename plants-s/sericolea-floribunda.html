Sericolea floribunda

Sericolea floribunda, commonly known as the showy sericolea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

Sericolea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
