Sparganium natans f. gracilis

Sparganium natans f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender floating burr, is a variant of the water burr plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated stems that float on the water surface. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
