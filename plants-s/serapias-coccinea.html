Serapias coccinea

Serapias coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serapias coccinea is a perennial herb with a compact, low-growing habit.
