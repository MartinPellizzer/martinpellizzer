Siparuna obovata var. obtusifolia

Siparuna obovata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
