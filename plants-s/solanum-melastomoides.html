Solanum melastomoides

Solanum melastomoides, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy stems and ovate, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
