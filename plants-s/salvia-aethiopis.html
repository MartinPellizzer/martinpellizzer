Salvia aethiopis

Salvia aethiopis, commonly known as Ethiopian sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
