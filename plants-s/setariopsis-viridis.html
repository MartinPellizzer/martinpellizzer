Setariopsis viridis

Setariopsis viridis, commonly known as the green setaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Setariopsis viridis is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, where it can colonize quickly.
