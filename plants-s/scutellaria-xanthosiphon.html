Scutellaria xanthosiphon

Scutellaria xanthosiphon, commonly known as yellow beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scutellaria xanthosiphon is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
