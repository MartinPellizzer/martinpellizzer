Sideritis briquetiana var. longespica

Sideritis briquetiana var. longespica, commonly known as the long-stalked rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence is usually a spike or panicle, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand arid conditions.
