Stipa occidentalis

Stipa occidentalis, commonly known as western wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

Stipa occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
