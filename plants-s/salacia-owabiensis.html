Salacia owabiensis

Salacia owabiensis, commonly known as the Philippine gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
