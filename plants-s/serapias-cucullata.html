Serapias cucullata

Serapias cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure formed by the fused sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a prominent central column and spurred lip.

Serapias cucullata is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
