Senecio mesadenia

Senecio mesadenia, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
