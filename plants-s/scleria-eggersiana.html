Scleria eggersiana

Scleria eggersiana, commonly known as the Eggers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which bloom in late summer. Scleria eggersiana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
