Solenostemon silvaticus

Solenostemon silvaticus, commonly known as the forest mint, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicinal use.
