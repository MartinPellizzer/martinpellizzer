Syzygium rolfei

Syzygium rolfei, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a sticky, acidic pulp. Syzygium rolfei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
