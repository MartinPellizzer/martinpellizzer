Scrophularia libanotica subsp. libanotica

Scrophularia libanotica subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and are arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
