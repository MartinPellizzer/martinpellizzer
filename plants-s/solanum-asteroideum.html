Solanum asteroideum

Solanum asteroideum, commonly known as the star apple or golden apple, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Solanum asteroideum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
