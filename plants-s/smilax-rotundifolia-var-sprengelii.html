Smilax rotundifolia var. sprengelii

Smilax rotundifolia var. sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's smilax, is a variant of the common smilax, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose panicles, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

This variant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
