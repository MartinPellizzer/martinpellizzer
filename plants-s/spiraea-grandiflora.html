Spiraea grandiflora

Spiraea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea grandiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
