Symphyotrichum laeve var. geyeri

Symphyotrichum laeve var. geyeri, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a smooth, waxy coating, while its stems are usually hairy.

The showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
