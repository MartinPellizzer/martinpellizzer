Stanleya integrifolia

Stanleya integrifolia, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Stanleya integrifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in Native American herbal practices.
