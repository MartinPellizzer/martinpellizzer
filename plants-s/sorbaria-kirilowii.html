Sorbaria kirilowii

Sorbaria kirilowii, commonly known as the Korean spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy spring blooms.

The species is also valued for its adaptability and use in horticultural landscaping.
