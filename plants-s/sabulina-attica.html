Sabulina attica

Sabulina attica, commonly known as the Greek sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of southern Europe.

It typically grows in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils, often in sunny or partially shaded environments. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sabulina attica is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
