Sayeria macrophylla

Sayeria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sayeria, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
