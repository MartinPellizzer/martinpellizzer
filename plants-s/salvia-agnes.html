Salvia agnes

Salvia agnes, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia agnes is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
