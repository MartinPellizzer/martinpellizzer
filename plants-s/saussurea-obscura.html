Saussurea obscura

Saussurea obscura, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features dark green, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
