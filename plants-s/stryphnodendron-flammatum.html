Stryphnodendron flammatum

Stryphnodendron flammatum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a smooth, reddish-bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
