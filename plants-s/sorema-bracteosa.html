Sorema bracteosa

Sorema bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Sorema bracteosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
