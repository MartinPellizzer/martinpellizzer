Spiraea aruncus astilboides

Spiraea aruncus astilboides, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall.

It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
