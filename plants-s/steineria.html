Steineria

Steineria, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Steineria, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and striking, showy flowers that often resemble those of true water lilies. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant in the sediment.

Steineria species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
