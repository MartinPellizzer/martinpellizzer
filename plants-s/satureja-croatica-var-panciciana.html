Satureja croatica var. panciciana

Satureja croatica var. panciciana, commonly known as Croatian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Adriatic region of Croatia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their essential oils and antioxidant properties.

This variety is particularly valued for its distinct fragrance and adaptability to coastal environments.
