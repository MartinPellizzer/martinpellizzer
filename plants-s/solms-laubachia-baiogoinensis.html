Solms-laubachia baiogoinensis

Solms-laubachia baiogoinensis, commonly known as the Cameroon grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Cameroon and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
