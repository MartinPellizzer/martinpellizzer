Smallanthus macroscyphus

Smallanthus macroscyphus, commonly known as the Andean giant cup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped inflorescence that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
