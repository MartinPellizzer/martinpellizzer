Salvia tuerckheimii

Salvia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia tuerckheimii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
