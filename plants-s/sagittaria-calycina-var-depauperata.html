Sagittaria calycina var. depauperata

Sagittaria calycina var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a wetland plant characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to its habitat. Its leaves are usually floating or partially submerged, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic environments.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters, making it an important indicator species for wetland health.
