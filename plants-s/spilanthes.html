Spilanthes

Spilanthes, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the Americas and includes several species, such as Spilanthes acuata, which is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties. The plant features small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in compact clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Spilanthes is known for its pungent, spicy scent and has been used traditionally for its ability to alleviate toothaches and other oral ailments.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including glucosinolates and alkaloids, contribute to its therapeutic effects.
