Staurogyne leptocaulis

Staurogyne leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed waterweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often submerged in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Staurogyne leptocaulis thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats, such as ponds and wetlands.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, though it can become invasive in certain conditions.
