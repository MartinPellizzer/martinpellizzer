Stanleya amplexifolia

Stanleya amplexifolia, commonly known as the rock penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Stanleya amplexifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
