Stachys ocymastrum subsp. bicolor

Stachys ocymastrum subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves with a distinctive two-toned appearance, often green on top and reddish underneath.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.
