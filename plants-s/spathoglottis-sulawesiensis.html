Spathoglottis sulawesiensis

Spathoglottis sulawesiensis, commonly known as the Sulawesi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
