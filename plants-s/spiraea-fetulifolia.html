Spiraea fetulifolia

Spiraea fetulifolia, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

Spiraea fetulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
