Sporadanthus gracilis

Sporadanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

Sporadanthus gracilis is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
