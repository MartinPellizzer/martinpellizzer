Steriphoma paradoxum subsp. venezuelanum

Steriphoma paradoxum subsp.

venezuelanum, commonly known as the Venezuelan starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Venezuela. It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and limited geographic distribution.
