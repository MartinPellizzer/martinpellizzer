Senecio cocuyanus

Senecio cocuyanus, commonly known as Cocuyan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Senecio cocuyanus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
