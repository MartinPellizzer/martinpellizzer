Statice mauritanica

Statice mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, adapted to thrive in arid and saline environments.

Statice mauritanica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
