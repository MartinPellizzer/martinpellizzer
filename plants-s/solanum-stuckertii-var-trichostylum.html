Solanum stuckertii var. trichostylum

Solanum stuckertii var. trichostylum, commonly known as the Trichostylum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
