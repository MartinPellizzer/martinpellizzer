Scutellaria pallidiflora

Scutellaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Scutellaria pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
