Sorghum eplicatum var. heterochromum

Sorghum eplicatum var. heterochromum, commonly known as the variegated sorghum, is a striking plant characterized by its colorful foliage.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a variety of leaf colorations, including green, white, and purple, which make it visually appealing. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This ornamental grass is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
