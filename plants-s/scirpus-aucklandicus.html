Scirpus aucklandicus

Scirpus aucklandicus, commonly known as the Auckland rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually green or brown and appear at the top of the stems.

Scirpus aucklandicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
