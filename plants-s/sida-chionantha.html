Sida chionantha

Sida chionantha, commonly known as the silverleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sida chionantha is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
