Syngonium gracile

Syngonium gracile, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant thrives in humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, facilitating its growth as a vine.

Syngonium gracile is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance the aesthetic of any space.
