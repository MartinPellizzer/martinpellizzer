Sanguisorba officinalis var. cordifolia

Sanguisorba officinalis var. cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of rose-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped to heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
