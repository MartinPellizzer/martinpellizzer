Salix kangensis f. leiocarpa

Salix kangensis f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are usually smooth on both surfaces, giving it the "smooth-seeded" designation in its scientific name.

This species is adapted to cold and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in high-altitude ecosystems.
