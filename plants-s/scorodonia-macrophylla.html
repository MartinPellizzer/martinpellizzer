Scorodonia macrophylla

Scorodonia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf scorodona, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its aromatic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Scorodonia macrophylla is often found in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests.
