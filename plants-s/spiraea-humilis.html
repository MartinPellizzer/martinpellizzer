Spiraea humilis

Spiraea humilis, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features arching branches covered in clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
