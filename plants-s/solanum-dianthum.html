Solanum dianthum

Solanum dianthum, commonly known as the scarlet silver bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Solanum dianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
