Salix × aschersoniana

Salix × aschersoniana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The tree has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
