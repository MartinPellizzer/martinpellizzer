Spondiopsis

Spondiopsis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. Spondiopsis species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and blooms.

The genus includes several species, such as Spondiopsis tenuifolia and Spondiopsis triloba, which are widely cultivated for their aesthetic value.
