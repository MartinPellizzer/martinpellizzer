Styrax philadelphoides

Styrax philadelphoides, commonly known as the sweet gum or American sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruit clusters. Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for furniture and tool handles.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
