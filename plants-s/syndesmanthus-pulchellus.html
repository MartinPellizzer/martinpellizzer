Syndesmanthus pulchellus

Syndesmanthus pulchellus, commonly known as the beautiful thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are narrow and pointed, typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It produces small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Syndesmanthus pulchellus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
