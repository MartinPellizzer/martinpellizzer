Sebastiania glandulosa var. transiens

Sebastiania glandulosa var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Sebastiania, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy, orange fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
