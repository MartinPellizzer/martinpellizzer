Syagrus archeri

Syagrus archeri, commonly known as the royal palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a majestic appearance. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates make it a popular ornamental species.

Syagrus archeri is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
