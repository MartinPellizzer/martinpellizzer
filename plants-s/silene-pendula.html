Silene pendula

Silene pendula, commonly known as the hanging catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a short, upright stem.

Silene pendula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
