Sarcophagophilus armatus

Sarcophagophilus armatus, commonly known as the armored sarcophaga fly, is a species of fly belonging to the family Sarcophagidae.

It is characterized by its robust body and distinctive, armored appearance, which provides protection against predators. The plant-like structure of its larval stage, known as a sarcophaga, is a key feature in its life cycle, as it develops within a hardened, egg-like case. This unique developmental strategy is an adaptation to its ecological niche, often found in decaying organic matter.

The species plays a role in decomposition and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
