Saxifraga takedana

Saxifraga takedana, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga takedana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
