Smilax oblongata

Smilax oblongata, commonly known as the longleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in climbing and support. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a waxy surface and a pointed tip.

Smilax oblongata is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
