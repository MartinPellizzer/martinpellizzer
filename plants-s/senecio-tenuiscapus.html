Senecio tenuiscapus

Senecio tenuiscapus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is known for its hairy, upright stems. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Senecio tenuiscapus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
