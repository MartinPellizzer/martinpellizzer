Solanum moniliforme

Solanum moniliforme, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Solanum moniliforme contains alkaloids and is considered toxic to livestock and humans.
