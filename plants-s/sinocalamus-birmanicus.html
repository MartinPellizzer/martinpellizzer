Sinocalamus birmanicus

Sinocalamus birmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves. This species is often found in moist, tropical lowland areas and can grow up to several meters in height. Its rapid growth rate makes it valuable for both ecological restoration and commercial purposes.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
