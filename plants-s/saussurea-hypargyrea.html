Saussurea hypargyrea

Saussurea hypargyrea, commonly known as the silver safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and pale yellow to silver-colored flowers. The plant produces small, achene-like fruits that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
