Scirpus fastigiatus

Scirpus fastigiatus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of the stems, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Scirpus fastigiatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
