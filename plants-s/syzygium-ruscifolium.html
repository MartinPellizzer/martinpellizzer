Syzygium ruscifolium

Syzygium ruscifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Syzygium ruscifolium is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
