Sideritis brutia

Sideritis brutia, commonly known as Balkan sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, including parts of Greece, Albania, and Kosovo. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Sideritis brutia is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
