Scrophularia alata subsp. neesii

Scrophularia alata subsp.

neesii, commonly known as the winged scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
