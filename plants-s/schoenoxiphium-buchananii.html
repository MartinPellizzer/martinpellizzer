Schoenoxiphium buchananii

Schoenoxiphium buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, spiny leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
