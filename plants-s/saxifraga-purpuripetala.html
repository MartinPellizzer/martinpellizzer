Saxifraga purpuripetala

Saxifraga purpuripetala, commonly known as the purple-petaled saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves that are usually green with a reddish or purplish hue, especially in cooler temperatures. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink or purple in color.

Saxifraga purpuripetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
