Salix phaidima

Salix phaidima, commonly known as the Phaidima willow, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant has slender, often twisted branches and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family, and it produces small, fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Salix phaidima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
