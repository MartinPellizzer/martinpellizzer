Selaginella correae

Selaginella correae, commonly known as the Correa's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to South America.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Selaginella correae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
