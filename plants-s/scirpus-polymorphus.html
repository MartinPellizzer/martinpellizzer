Scirpus polymorphus

Scirpus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable reed, is a tall, grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland and marshy areas across Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of cylindrical flower spikes. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.

Scirpus polymorphus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
