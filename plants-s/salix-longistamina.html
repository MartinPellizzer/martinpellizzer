Salix longistamina

Salix longistamina, commonly known as the long-stamen willow, is a species of willow native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, containing both male and female flowers. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
