Stachys officinalis var. stricta

Stachys officinalis var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Narrowleaf betony is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
