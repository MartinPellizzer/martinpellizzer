Scirpus elegantulus

Scirpus elegantulus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus elegantulus is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
