Sesuvium hoepfnerianum

Sesuvium hoepfnerianum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptation to coastal and semi-arid climates.
