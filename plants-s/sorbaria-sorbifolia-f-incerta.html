Sorbaria sorbifolia f. incerta

Sorbaria sorbifolia f. incerta, commonly known as the uncertain form of the common ninebark, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinct morphological features.

This form exhibits variations in leaf shape and flower structure compared to the typical species, making it a subject of taxonomic interest. It is native to specific regions in eastern Asia, including China and Korea, where it thrives in forested areas. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring.

Its leaves are usually alternate and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
