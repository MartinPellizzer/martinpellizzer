Scleria racemosa subsp. depressa

Scleria racemosa subsp.

depressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often compressed and have a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting local biodiversity.
