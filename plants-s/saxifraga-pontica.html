Saxifraga pontica

Saxifraga pontica, commonly known as the Pontic saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky, mountainous areas and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
