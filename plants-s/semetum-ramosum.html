Semetum ramosum

Semetum ramosum, commonly known as the branched broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Semetum ramosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
