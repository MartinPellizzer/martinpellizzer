Stelis machupicchuensis

Stelis machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces small, intricate flowers with a variety of colors, often in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing on rock faces or in shaded forest areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
