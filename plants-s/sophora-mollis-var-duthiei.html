Sophora mollis var. duthiei

Sophora mollis var. duthiei, commonly known as the Duthie's false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its showy, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
