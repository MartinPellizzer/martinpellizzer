Scabiosa crenata var. pallidiaristata

Scabiosa crenata var. pallidiaristata, commonly known as the pale-haired scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive central tuft of hair. The plant has finely toothed, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
