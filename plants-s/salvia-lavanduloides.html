Salvia lavanduloides

Salvia lavanduloides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Salvia lavanduloides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
