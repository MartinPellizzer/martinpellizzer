Styloglossum halconense

Styloglossum halconense, commonly known as the Halconense orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, elongated ovary that develops into a distinctive, fleshy fruit. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Styloglossum halconense is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
