Scilla acaulis

Scilla acaulis, commonly known as the windflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, stemless growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's leaves are basal, grass-like, and often appear after the flowers have faded. Scilla acaulis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming period and delicate appearance.
