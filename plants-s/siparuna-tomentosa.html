Siparuna tomentosa

Siparuna tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf siphone.

It is a tropical shrub native to South America, characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a soft, velvety texture.

Siparuna tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
