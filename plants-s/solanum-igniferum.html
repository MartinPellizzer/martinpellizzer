Solanum igniferum

Solanum igniferum, commonly known as the fireberry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange berries and velvety purple leaves. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. Solanum igniferum typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces toxic berries that contain alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
