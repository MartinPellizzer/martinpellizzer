Senecio cumingii var. bracteosus

Senecio cumingii var. bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteose groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically linear to lance-shaped, with a rough or hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
