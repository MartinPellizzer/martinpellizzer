Senecio picridioides

Senecio picridioides, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
