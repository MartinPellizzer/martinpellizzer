Selinum pastinaca

Selinum pastinaca, commonly known as wild celery or love-in-a-mist, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Selinum pastinaca is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
