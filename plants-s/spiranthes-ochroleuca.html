Spiranthes ochroleuca

Spiranthes ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow spiraea, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that produces small, delicate orchid flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
