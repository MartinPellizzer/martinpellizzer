Senecio antirhinifolius

Senecio antirhinifolius, commonly known as the horned owl's eye, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and specific growing conditions, it is often cultivated in arid or rocky gardens.
