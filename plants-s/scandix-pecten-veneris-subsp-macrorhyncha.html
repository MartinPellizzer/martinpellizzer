Scandix pecten-veneris subsp. macrorhyncha

Scandix pecten-veneris subsp.

macrorhyncha, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in saline environments. Its structure is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
