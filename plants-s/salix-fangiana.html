Salix fangiana

Salix fangiana, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender branches and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces catkins, which are its main floral structures, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Salix fangiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
