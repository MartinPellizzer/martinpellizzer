Sebastiania hispida var. regnellii

Sebastiania hispida var. regnellii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and hardy nature.
