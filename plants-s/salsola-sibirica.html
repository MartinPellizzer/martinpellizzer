Salsola sibirica

Salsola sibirica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.

Salsola sibirica plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
