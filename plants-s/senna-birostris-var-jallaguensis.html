Senna birostris var. jallaguensis

Senna birostris var. jallaguensis, commonly known as the Jallaguensian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, upright clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood.
