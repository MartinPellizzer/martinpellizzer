Senecio anthemidiphyllus

Senecio anthemidiphyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
