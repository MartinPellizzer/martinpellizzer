Scrophularia canina var. pyrenaea

Scrophularia canina var. pyrenaea, commonly known as the Pyrenean dog's mercury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to the Pyrenees region and thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
