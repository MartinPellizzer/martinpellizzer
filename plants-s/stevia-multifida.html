Stevia multifida

Stevia multifida, commonly known as the curly leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Stevia multifida is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweetener.

Its leaves are typically harvested and dried for use in food and beverages.
