Saprosma fragrans

Saprosma fragrans, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Saprosma fragrans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its pleasant scent and hardiness.
