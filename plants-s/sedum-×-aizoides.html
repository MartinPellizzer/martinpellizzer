Sedum × aizoides

Sedum × aizoides, commonly known as the stonecrop hybrid, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is a natural hybrid between Sedum aizoides and Sedum album, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
