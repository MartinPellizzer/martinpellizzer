Sasaella ramosa var. latifolia

Sasaella ramosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bamboo, is a variant of the sasaella genus native to East Asia.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers, which are typically followed by small, triangular berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental gardening and traditional landscapes.
