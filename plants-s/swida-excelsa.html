Swida excelsa

Swida excelsa, commonly known as the giant elder, is a tall shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds.

Swida excelsa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
