Scapisenecio leptocarpus

Scapisenecio leptocarpus, commonly known as the slender-fruited sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, elongated capsule, giving it its scientific name.

It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
