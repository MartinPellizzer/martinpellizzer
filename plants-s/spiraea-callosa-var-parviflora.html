Spiraea callosa var. parviflora

Spiraea callosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant has fine, silver-gray leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental hedges and borders.
