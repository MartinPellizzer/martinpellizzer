Smilax sumatrensis

Smilax sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran arrowroot or wild yam, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries containing several seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Smilax sumatrensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
