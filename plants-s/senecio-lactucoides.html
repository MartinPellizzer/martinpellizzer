Senecio lactucoides

Senecio lactucoides, commonly known as the milkweed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio lactucoides is also known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals.
