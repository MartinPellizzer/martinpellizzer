Symphonia sessiliflora

Symphonia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered symphonia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
