Serratula acaulis

Serratula acaulis, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Serratula acaulis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
