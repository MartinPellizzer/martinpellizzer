Sabicea triflora

Sabicea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered sabicea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits.

Sabicea triflora is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
