Saxifraga × bursiculata

Saxifraga × bursiculata, commonly known as the cushion saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit, forming low-growing mats or cushions. The plant typically features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs. It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is well adapted to rocky or alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
