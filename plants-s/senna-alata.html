Senna alata

Senna alata, commonly known as the flame flower or sickle bush, is a tropical shrub native to Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and often have a distinctive flame-like appearance. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Senna alata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
