Sedum ciliatum

Sedum ciliatum, commonly known as the threadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Eurasia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread via offsets make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
