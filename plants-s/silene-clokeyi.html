Silene clokeyi

Silene clokeyi, commonly known as the Clokey's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Silene clokeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
