Scirpus trollii

Scirpus trollii, commonly known as the Troll's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in its harsh habitat. Scirpus trollii is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and wetland ecosystems.
