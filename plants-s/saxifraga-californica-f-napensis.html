Saxifraga californica f. napensis

Saxifraga californica f. napensis, commonly known as the Napensis Stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to California.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
