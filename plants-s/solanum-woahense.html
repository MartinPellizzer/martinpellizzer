Solanum woahense

Solanum woahense, commonly known as the Woahen nightshade, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant produces berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption by both humans and livestock.
