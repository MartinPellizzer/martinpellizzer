Sparaxis albiflora

Sparaxis albiflora, commonly known as the white glory of the veld, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Sparaxis albiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in mild climates.
