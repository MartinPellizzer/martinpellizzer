Senecio pseudoauranticus

Senecio pseudoauranticus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

Senecio pseudoauranticus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
