Solanum commersonii subsp. pseudostipulatum

Solanum commersonii subsp.

pseudostipulatum, commonly known as the Commerson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface. The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This subspecies is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is adapted to a variety of habitats, including dry shrublands and rocky areas.
