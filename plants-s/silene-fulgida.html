Silene fulgida

Silene fulgida, commonly known as the blazing campion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Silene fulgida thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.
