Strychnos excellens

Strychnos excellens, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine despite their harmful effects.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
