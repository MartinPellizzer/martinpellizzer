Solanum burtt-davyi

Solanum burtt-davyi, commonly known as the Burtt-Davy's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers, which are followed by orange to red berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
