Sida heterocarpa

Sida heterocarpa, commonly known as the woolly sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Sida heterocarpa is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
