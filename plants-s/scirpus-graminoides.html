Scirpus graminoides

Scirpus graminoides, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a tall, aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and filter water.

Scirpus graminoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
