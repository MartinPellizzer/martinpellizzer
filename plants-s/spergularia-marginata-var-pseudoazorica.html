Spergularia marginata var. pseudoazorica

Spergularia marginata var. pseudoazorica, commonly known as the Azorean sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Azores.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
