Scutellaria angustifolia var. canescens

Scutellaria angustifolia var. canescens, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
