Serjania albopunctata

Serjania albopunctata, commonly known as the white-spotted serjania, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
