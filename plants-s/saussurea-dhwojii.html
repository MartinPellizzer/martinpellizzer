Saussurea dhwojii

Saussurea dhwojii, commonly known as the Himalayan saffron or the sacred saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Tibetan and Ayurvedic medicine. Its flowers are harvested for their aromatic compounds, which are used in incense and as a source of natural dye.

Due to its habitat in extreme environments, it is considered a rare and protected species.
