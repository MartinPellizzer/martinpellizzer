Solanum thelopodium

Solanum thelopodium, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Solanum thelopodium belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
