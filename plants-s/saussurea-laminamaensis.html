Saussurea laminamaensis

Saussurea laminamaensis, commonly known as the Laminamaensis Aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stem structure. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
