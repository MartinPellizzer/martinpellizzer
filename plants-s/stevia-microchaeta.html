Stevia microchaeta

Stevia microchaeta, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring compounds. Stevia microchaeta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained popularity due to its low caloric content and health benefits.
