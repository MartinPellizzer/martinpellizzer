Seriphium cinereum var. virgatum

Seriphium cinereum var. virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, with a mat-forming structure that helps it survive in arid conditions.
