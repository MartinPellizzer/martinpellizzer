Saussurea parasclerolepis

Saussurea parasclerolepis, commonly known as the Himalayan cloud spinach, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
