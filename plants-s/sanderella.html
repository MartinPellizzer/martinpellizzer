Sanderella

Sanderella, commonly known as the "scented bellflower," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sanderella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
