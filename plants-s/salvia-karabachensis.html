Salvia karabachensis

Salvia karabachensis, commonly known as Karabakh sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia karabachensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
