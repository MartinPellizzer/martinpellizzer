Smilax tenuis

Smilax tenuis, commonly known as the slender greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thin, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with slender, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax tenuis is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense thickets.
