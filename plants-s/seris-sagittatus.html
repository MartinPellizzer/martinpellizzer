Seris sagittatus

Seris sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Seris sagittatus is an annual plant, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
