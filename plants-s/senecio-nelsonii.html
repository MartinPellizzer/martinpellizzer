Senecio nelsonii

Senecio nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. This perennial herb typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and crevices. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Senecio nelsonii is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
