Saxifraga crassifolia var. pauciflora

Saxifraga crassifolia var. pauciflora, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky environments.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
