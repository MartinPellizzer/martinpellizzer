Sebastiania corniculata var. leucoblepharis

Sebastiania corniculata var. leucoblepharis, commonly known as the white-eyed sebastiania, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and distinctive white, waxy spots on the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal name 'leucoblepharis' refers to its unique white eye-like markings on the foliage.
