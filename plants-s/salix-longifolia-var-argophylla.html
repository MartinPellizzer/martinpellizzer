Salix longifolia var. argophylla

Salix longifolia var. argophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a variant of the willow tree characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, temperate regions. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive whitish underside, giving it a shimmering appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
