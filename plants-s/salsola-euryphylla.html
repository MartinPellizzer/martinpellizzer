Salsola euryphylla

Salsola euryphylla, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often succulent, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Salsola euryphylla is well-suited for xerophytic conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
