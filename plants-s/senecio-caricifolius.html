Senecio caricifolius

Senecio caricifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
