Swertia spathulata

Swertia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Swertia spathulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
