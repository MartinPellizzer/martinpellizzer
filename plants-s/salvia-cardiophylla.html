Salvia cardiophylla

Salvia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving them a distinctive, heart-like appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
