Stelis cupidinea

Stelis cupidinea, commonly known as the Cupid's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Stelis cupidinea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
