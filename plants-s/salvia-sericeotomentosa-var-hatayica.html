Salvia sericeotomentosa var. hatayica

Salvia sericeotomentosa var. hatayica, commonly known as the Hatay sage, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and spikes of pale violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
