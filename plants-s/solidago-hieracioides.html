Solidago hieracioides

Solidago hieracioides, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Solidago hieracioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
