Senecio bonplandianus

Senecio bonplandianus, commonly known as the Bonpland's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
