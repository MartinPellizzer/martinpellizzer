Sida rhombiformis

Sida rhombiformis, commonly known as the diamondleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Sida rhombiformis is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
