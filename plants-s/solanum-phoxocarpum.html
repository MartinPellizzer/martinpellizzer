Solanum phoxocarpum

Solanum phoxocarpum, commonly known as the purple potato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple veins, and it produces small, round fruits that turn purple when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
