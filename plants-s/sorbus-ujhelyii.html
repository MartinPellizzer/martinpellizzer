Sorbus ujhelyii

Sorbus ujhelyii, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
