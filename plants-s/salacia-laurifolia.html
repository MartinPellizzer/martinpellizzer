Salacia laurifolia

Salacia laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant laurel.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a mild, pleasant scent when crushed.

Salacia laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
