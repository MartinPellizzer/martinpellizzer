Sonchus ustulatus subsp. imbricatus

Sonchus ustulatus subsp.

imbricatus, commonly known as the roughleaf sow-thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces a flowering stem with numerous small, daisy-like flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar plants like daisies and sunflowers.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it both a common weed and an adaptable wild plant.
