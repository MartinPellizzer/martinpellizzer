Scindapsus pinnatus

Scindapsus pinnatus, commonly known as the Silver Pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil. It is a member of the Araceae family and is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots.

Scindapsus pinnatus is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
