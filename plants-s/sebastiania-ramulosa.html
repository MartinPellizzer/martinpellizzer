Sebastiania ramulosa

Sebastiania ramulosa, commonly known as the golden dewdrop, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal areas or near mangroves. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a distinctive appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and flowering habit.
