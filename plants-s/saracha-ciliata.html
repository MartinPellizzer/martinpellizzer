Saracha ciliata

Saracha ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf ciliate, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
