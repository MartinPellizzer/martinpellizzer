Syngonium reticulatum

Syngonium reticulatum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive veined pattern. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight.

Syngonium reticulatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
