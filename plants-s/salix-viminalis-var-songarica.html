Salix viminalis var. songarica

Salix viminalis var. songarica, commonly known as Chinese willow or Songarica willow, is a variant of the common osier dogwood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Siberia. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches that are often used for basket weaving and other crafts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the catkins are typically male and yellow-green.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.
