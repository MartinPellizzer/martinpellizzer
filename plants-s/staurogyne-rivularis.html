Staurogyne rivularis

Staurogyne rivularis, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has a creeping rhizome with floating leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Staurogyne rivularis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms.
