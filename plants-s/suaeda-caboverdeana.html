Suaeda caboverdeana

Suaeda caboverdeana, commonly known as Cape Verde sea blite, is a halophytic plant native to the island of Santiago in the Cape Verde archipelago.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in harsh conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in branched inflorescences.

Suaeda caboverdeana plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is adapted to tolerate high levels of salt and drought.
