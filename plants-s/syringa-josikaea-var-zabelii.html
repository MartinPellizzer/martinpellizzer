Syringa josikaea var. zabelii

Syringa josikaea var. zabelii, commonly known as the Zabel's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
