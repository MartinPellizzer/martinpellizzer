Sarcinula alexii

Sarcinula alexii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees across sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and fleshy, berry-like fruits. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects, which in turn aid in seed dispersal.

Its presence can sometimes weaken the host tree, making it an ecologically significant yet controversial species.
