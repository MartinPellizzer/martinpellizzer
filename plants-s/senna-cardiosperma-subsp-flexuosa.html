Senna cardiosperma subsp. flexuosa

Senna cardiosperma subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as the warty senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pods containing flattened, winged seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with moderate to high rainfall.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
