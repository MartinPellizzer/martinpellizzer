Syzygium longipes var. leptopodum

Syzygium longipes var. leptopodum, commonly known as the slender-fruited clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to northern Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, ellipsoidal berries that turn from green to red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland rainforest areas.
