Salix ansoniana

Salix ansoniana, commonly known as the Chinese willow or Anson's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal visual interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
