Schinus weinmanniifolia f. paucijuga

Schinus weinmanniifolia f. paucijuga, commonly known as the sparse-leaved Brazilian pepper, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashews and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its reduced foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are clustered near the stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
