Sideritis scordioides subsp. cavanillesii

Sideritis scordioides subsp.

cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
