Solanum didymanthum var. plurifolium

Solanum didymanthum var. plurifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with multiple stems and large, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
