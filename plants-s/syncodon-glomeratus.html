Syncodon glomeratus

Syncodon glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
