Symphyotrichum vahlii var. tenuifolium

Symphyotrichum vahlii var. tenuifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
