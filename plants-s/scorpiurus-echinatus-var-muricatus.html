Scorpiurus echinatus var. muricatus

Scorpiurus echinatus var. muricatus, commonly known as the roughleaf scorpiurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often glandular and have a distinctive, prickly texture, which contributes to its common name.

Scorpiurus echinatus var. muricatus is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
