Schizophragma macrosepalum

Schizophragma macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 6 feet tall. Its berries are orange-red and contain small, hard seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
