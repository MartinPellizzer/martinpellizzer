Styrax heteroclitus

Styrax heteroclitus, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and incense.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
