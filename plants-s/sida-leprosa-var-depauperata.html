Sida leprosa var. depauperata

Sida leprosa var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the coastal plain and sandy habitats.
