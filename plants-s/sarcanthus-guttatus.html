Sarcanthus guttatus

Sarcanthus guttatus, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Sarcanthus guttatus is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
