Solanum violaceum subsp. multiflorum

Solanum violaceum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the purple mulberry, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, violet flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
