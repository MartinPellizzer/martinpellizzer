Silene pachyneura

Silene pachyneura, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Silene pachyneura plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
