Scleria stereorrhiza var. major

Scleria stereorrhiza var. major, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features dense, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowering spikes produce small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

This species is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
