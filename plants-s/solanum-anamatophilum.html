Solanum anamatophilum

Solanum anamatophilum, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, green, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces red, fiery chili peppers that are known for their extreme heat and unique flavor profile. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its potency, it is often cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
