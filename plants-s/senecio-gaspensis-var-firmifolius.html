Senecio gaspensis var. firmifolius

Senecio gaspensis var. firmifolius, commonly known as the Cape mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and robust, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
