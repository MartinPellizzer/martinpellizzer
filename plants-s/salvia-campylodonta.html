Salvia campylodonta

Salvia campylodonta, commonly known as the Mexican mint or narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blossoms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal remedies.
