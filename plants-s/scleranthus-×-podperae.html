Scleranthus × podperae

Scleranthus × podperae, commonly known as the hybrid meadow sandwort, is a flowering plant that belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
