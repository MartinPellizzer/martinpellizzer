Solanum tarapatatum

Solanum tarapatatum, commonly known as the Tarapata nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, forested areas at moderate elevations. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears berries that are generally yellow or orange when ripe. Solanum tarapatatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
