Solidago flabelliformis

Solidago flabelliformis, commonly known as the flatleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are usually arranged in a flat, fan-like pattern at the base of the plant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and meadows.

Solidago flabelliformis is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
