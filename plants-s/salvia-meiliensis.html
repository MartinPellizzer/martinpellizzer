Salvia meiliensis

Salvia meiliensis, commonly known as the Meili sage, is a flowering plant native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often with a strong, minty scent.

Salvia meiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
