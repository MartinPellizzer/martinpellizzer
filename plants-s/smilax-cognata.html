Smilax cognata

Smilax cognata, commonly known as the red raspberri vine, is a woody, climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, raspberry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a complex root system and the ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax cognata is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
