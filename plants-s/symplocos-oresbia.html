Symplocos oresbia

Symplocos oresbia, commonly known as the red-fruited beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces striking clusters of bright purple to red berries that mature in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Symplocos oresbia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
