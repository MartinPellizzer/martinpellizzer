Stemodia tenera

Stemodia tenera, commonly known as the slender stemodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in dry conditions. The plant features thin, erect stems covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often succulent, contributing to its drought tolerance.
