Salvia hierosolymitana var. chlorocalycina

Salvia hierosolymitana var. chlorocalycina, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
