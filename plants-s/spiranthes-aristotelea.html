Spiranthes aristotelea

Spiranthes aristotelea, commonly known as the noble spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive spidery appearance.

It produces small, elliptical leaves and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.
