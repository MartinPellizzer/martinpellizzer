Senecio ceratophyllus

Senecio ceratophyllus, commonly known as the horned senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

Senecio ceratophyllus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
