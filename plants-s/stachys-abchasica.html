Stachys abchasica

Stachys abchasica, commonly known as Abkhazian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys abchasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
