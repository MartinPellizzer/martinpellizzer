Salvia altilabrosa

Salvia altilabrosa, commonly known as the tall lipstick plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia altilabrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
