Samadera lucida

Samadera lucida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Samadera lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
