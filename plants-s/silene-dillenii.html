Silene dillenii

Silene dillenii, commonly known as Dillen's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene dillenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
