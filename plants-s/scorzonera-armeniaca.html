Scorzonera armeniaca

Scorzonera armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian salsify, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible, slightly sweet and nutty flavor. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals, and are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scorzonera armeniaca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, temperate climates.
