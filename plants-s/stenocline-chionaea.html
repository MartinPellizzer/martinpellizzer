Stenocline chionaea

Stenocline chionaea, commonly known as the snowbell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

Stenocline chionaea prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
