Scutellaria ovata var. bracteata

Scutellaria ovata var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
