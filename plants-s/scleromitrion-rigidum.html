Scleromitrion rigidum

Scleromitrion rigidum, commonly known as the rigid thornsteppe, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, thick, fleshy leaves that help conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its rigid, spiny structure provides protection from herbivores and aids in stabilizing sandy soils.

Scleromitrion rigidum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local wildlife and preventing soil erosion.
