Scleria carphiformis

Scleria carphiformis, commonly known as the spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and the plant produces small, three-angled berries.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
