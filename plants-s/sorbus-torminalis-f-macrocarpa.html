Sorbus torminalis f. macrocarpa

Sorbus torminalis f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its notably larger, more robust fruits compared to the typical form. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
