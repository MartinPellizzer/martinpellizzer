Silene uralensis subsp. uralensis

Silene uralensis subsp.

uralensis, commonly known as the Ural catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly the Ural Mountains. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

The plant features erect, branching stems that are covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and prominent stamens, and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
