Stenochilus subcanescens

Stenochilus subcanescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Stenochilus subcanescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
