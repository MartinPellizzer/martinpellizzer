Salix phlebophylla f. media

Salix phlebophylla f. media, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often densely hairy. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
