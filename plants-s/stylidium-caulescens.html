Stylidium caulescens

Stylidium caulescens, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, fleshy leaves and has a unique structure with a specialized column that aids in pollination.

Stylidium caulescens is a short-lived perennial, often flowering in response to seasonal rainfall.
