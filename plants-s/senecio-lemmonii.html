Senecio lemmonii

Senecio lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky habitats, such as cliffs and canyon walls. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Senecio lemmonii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
