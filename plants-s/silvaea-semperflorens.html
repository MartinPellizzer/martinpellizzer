Silvaea semperflorens

Silvaea semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical name reflects its continuous flowering habit, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Silvaea semperflorens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
