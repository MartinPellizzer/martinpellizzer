Solanum parcebarbatum

Solanum parcebarbatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Solanum parcebarbatum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It is considered a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
