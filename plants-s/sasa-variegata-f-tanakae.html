Sasa variegata f. tanakae

Sasa variegata f. tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a rhizomatous evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The leaves are typically a mix of green and white or silver, creating a striking variegated appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
