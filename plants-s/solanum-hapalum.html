Solanum hapalum

Solanum hapalum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum hapalum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
