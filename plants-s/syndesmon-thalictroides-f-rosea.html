Syndesmon thalictroides f. rosea

Syndesmon thalictroides f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered false rue-anemone, is a rare and striking variant of the native North American plant.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its floral structure consists of five petals, with the outer ones being distinctly larger and more showy than the inner ones.
