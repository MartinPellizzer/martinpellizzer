Silene kitaibelii

Silene kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kitaibel's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly woolly texture.

Silene kitaibelii is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
