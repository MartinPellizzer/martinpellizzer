Silene laciniata var. californica

Silene laciniata var. californica, commonly known as the California flax or western flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene laciniata var. californica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
