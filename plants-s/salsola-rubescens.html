Salsola rubescens

Salsola rubescens, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prickly, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

The plant has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a food source and in traditional medicine.
