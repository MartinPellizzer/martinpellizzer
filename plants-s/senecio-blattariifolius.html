Senecio blattariifolius

Senecio blattariifolius, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often branching and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio blattariifolius is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
