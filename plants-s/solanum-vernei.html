Solanum vernei

Solanum vernei, commonly known as the Verne's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Solanum vernei is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
