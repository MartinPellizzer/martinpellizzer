Sauromatum sessiliflorum

Sauromatum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the hooded death flower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, hooded flowers that resemble a face, giving it its dramatic common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its dark, almost black, waxy petals. Its foliage consists of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it is considered toxic due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals.
