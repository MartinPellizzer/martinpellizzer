Syzygium penibukanense

Syzygium penibukanense, commonly known as the Penibukan apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
