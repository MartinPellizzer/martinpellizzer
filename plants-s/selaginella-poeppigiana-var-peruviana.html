Selaginella poeppigiana var. peruviana

Selaginella poeppigiana var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern ally that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features creeping stems with whorled leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
