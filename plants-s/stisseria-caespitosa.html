Stisseria caespitosa

Stisseria caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered stisseria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, forming dense clusters of grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
