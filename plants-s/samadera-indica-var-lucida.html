Samadera indica var. lucida

Samadera indica var. lucida, commonly known as the shining sandalwood.

It is a variant of the sandalwood tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic wood and distinctive foliage. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in perfumery and medicinal applications.
