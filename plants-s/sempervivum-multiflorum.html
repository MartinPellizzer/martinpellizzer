Sempervivum multiflorum

Sempervivum multiflorum, commonly known as the chicken's egg plant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
