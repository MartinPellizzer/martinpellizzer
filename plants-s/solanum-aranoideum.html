Solanum aranoideum

Solanum aranoideum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces berries that start green and ripen to a bright yellow or orange color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum aranoideum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
