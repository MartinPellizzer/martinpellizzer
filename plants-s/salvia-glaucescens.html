Salvia glaucescens

Salvia glaucescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
