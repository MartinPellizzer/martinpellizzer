Solenidiopsis flavobrunnea

Solenidiopsis flavobrunnea, commonly known as the yellow-brown solenidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes.
