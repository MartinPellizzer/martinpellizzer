Sclerostachya fusca

Sclerostachya fusca, commonly known as the roughstemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its stiff, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Sclerostachya fusca is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
