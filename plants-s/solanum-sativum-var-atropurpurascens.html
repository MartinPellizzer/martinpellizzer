Solanum sativum var. atropurpurascens

Solanum sativum var. atropurpurascens, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variety of eggplant characterized by its deep purple to almost black skin.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to South and Central America. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with sprawling stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which is the edible part, is usually elongated and has a rich, earthy flavor.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and culinary versatility in various cuisines.
