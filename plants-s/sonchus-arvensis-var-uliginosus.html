Sonchus arvensis var. uliginosus

Sonchus arvensis var. uliginosus, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features a basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and is an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
