Scilla aggregata

Scilla aggregata, commonly known as the clustered squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbous storage organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla aggregata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
