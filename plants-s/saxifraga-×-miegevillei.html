Saxifraga × miegevillei

Saxifraga × miegevillei, commonly known as the rockfoil, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and have a textured, sometimes hairy surface.

Saxifraga × miegevillei is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species in its natural habitat.
