Simsia foetida var. foetida

Simsia foetida var. foetida, commonly known as the stinking jew's-mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant or foul-smelling flowers that can range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its variability in appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
