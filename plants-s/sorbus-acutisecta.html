Sorbus acutisecta

Sorbus acutisecta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sharply toothed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, spherical berries that are edible for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to flake as the tree matures.

Sorbus acutisecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
