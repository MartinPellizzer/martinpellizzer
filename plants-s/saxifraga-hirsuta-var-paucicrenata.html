Saxifraga hirsuta var. paucicrenata

Saxifraga hirsuta var. paucicrenata, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, hairy foliage that helps insulate it against harsh mountain conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
