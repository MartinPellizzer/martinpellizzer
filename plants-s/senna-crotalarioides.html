Senna crotalarioides

Senna crotalarioides, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
