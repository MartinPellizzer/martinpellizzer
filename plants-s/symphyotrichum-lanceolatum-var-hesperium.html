Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hesperium

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hesperium, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and pink to red petals, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to local biodiversity.
