Salix phaneroides

Salix phaneroides, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and often have a reddish tinge when new. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
