Stachys africana

Stachys africana, commonly known as African betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
