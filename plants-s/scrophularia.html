Scrophularia

Scrophularia, commonly known as lungwort, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Scrophulariaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and can be annual or perennial, often found in temperate regions. They are characterized by their showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are usually basal and arranged in a rosette, with some species having hairy or glandular surfaces.

Scrophularia species are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
