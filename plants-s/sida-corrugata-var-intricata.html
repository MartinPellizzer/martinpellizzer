Sida corrugata var. intricata

Sida corrugata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or subshrub with hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf venation and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
