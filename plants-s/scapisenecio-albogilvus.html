Scapisenecio albogilvus

Scapisenecio albogilvus, commonly known as the white-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
