Scabiosa arvensis var. ochroleuca

Scabiosa arvensis var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette formation.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
