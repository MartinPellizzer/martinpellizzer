Senegalia amorimii

Senegalia amorimii, commonly known as the Brazilian mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, which enriches the surrounding environment.

Its wood is used locally for fuel and construction, highlighting its economic value in certain regions.
