Sophronitis cernua var. albiflora

Sophronitis cernua var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a variant of the orchid species Sophronitis cernua.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its pendulous, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
