Sarracenia rubra

Sarracenia rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are often adorned with fine hair-like structures that aid in trapping prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

Sarracenia rubra is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations.
