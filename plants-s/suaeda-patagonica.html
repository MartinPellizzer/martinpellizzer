Suaeda patagonica

Suaeda patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in coastal and desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in response to drought.

Suaeda patagonica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its harsh native habitat.
