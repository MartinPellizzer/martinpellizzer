Swartzia acutifolia var. submarginata

Swartzia acutifolia var. submarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, highly valued wood. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The species is known for its slow growth and is often found in moist, tropical forests.
