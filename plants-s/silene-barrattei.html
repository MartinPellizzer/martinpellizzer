Silene barrattei

Silene barrattei, commonly known as the Barratte's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its upright stem and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene barrattei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
