Schefflera rediviva

Schefflera rediviva, commonly known as the rediviva umbrella plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, followed by black, berry-like fruits.

Schefflera rediviva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its adaptability and ease of care.
