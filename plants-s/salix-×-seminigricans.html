Salix × seminigricans

Salix × seminigricans, commonly known as the black willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, often blackish bark, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows along waterways and in moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to riparian environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, while it produces catkins that are essential for its reproduction.

The black willow is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
