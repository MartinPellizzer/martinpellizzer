Setaria ramentacea

Setaria ramentacea, commonly known as silky grass or hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains of seed. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Setaria ramentacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
