Solanum tapojense

Solanum tapojense, commonly known as the Tapojense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red hue.

Like many species in its family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
