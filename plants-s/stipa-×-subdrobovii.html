Stipa × subdrobovii

Stipa × subdrobovii, commonly known as the hybrid false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Stipa capillata and Stipa subdrobovii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters, and it produces small, bract-like leaves.

Stipa × subdrobovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
