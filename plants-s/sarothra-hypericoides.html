Sarothra hypericoides

Sarothra hypericoides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. Its leaves are linear and opposite, often with a slight bluish tinge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
