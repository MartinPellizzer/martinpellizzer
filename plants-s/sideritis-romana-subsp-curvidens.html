Sideritis romana subsp. curvidens

Sideritis romana subsp.

curvidens, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
