Schizostachyum palawanense

Schizostachyum palawanense, commonly known as the Palawan bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clump-forming pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and hardiness, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a source of sustainable timber.
