Scirpus microstachyus

Scirpus microstachyus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, narrow leaves that are usually smooth and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into brown, fluffy seeds.

Scirpus microstachyus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
