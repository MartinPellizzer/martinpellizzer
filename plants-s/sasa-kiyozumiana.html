Sasa kiyozumiana

Sasa kiyozumiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa kiyozumiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
