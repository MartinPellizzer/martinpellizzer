Smilax cinnamomifolia

Smilax cinnamomifolia, commonly known as cinnamon vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
