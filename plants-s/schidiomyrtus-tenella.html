Schidiomyrtus tenella

Schidiomyrtus tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pink, arranged in clusters.

Schidiomyrtus tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
