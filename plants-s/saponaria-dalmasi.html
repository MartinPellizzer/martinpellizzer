Saponaria dalmasi

Saponaria dalmasi, commonly known as the Dalmas' soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Saponaria dalmasi is also noted for its mild soap-like scent, which is derived from the saponins present in its roots.
