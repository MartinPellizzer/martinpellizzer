Scaevola ovalifolia var. cinerascens

Scaevola ovalifolia var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a star. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a silvery or grayish-green hue, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
