Symplocos martinicensis var. apiculata

Symplocos martinicensis var. apiculata, commonly known as the Martinique holly, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
