Stackhousia muricata var. typica

Stackhousia muricata var. typica, commonly known as the roughstacked hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both horticulture and ecological studies.
