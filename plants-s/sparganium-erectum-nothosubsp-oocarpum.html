Sparganium erectum nothosubsp. oocarpum

Sparganium erectum nothosubsp.

oocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bur-reed, is a hybrid subspecies characterized by its unique morphological features. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats, typically found in shallow waters and moist soils.

The plant produces cylindrical, spiky inflorescences that are composed of small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

The subspecies is notable for its distinct fruiting structures, which are adapted for water dispersal.
