Salacia juradoi

Salacia juradoi, commonly known as the Jurado's salacia, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its long, vine-like stems and small, clustered leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and mammals.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Salacia juradoi is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
