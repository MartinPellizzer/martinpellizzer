Sericographis rigida var. desertorum

Sericographis rigida var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features a sprawling, mat-forming growth habit with densely packed, narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is extensive and adapted for efficient water absorption from deep soil layers.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
