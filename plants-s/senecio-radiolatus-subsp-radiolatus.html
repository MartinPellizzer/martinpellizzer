Senecio radiolatus subsp. radiolatus

Senecio radiolatus subsp.

radiolatus, commonly known as the radiate senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a woolly texture, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
