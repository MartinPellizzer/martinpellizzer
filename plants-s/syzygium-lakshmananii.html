Syzygium lakshmananii

Syzygium lakshmananii, commonly known as the Lakshmananii apple or Lakshmananii syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, apple-like berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Syzygium lakshmananii is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
