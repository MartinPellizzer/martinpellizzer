Scilla cicatricosa

Scilla cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its distinctive scar-like markings on its bulbs, which give it its common name.

Scilla cicatricosa is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
