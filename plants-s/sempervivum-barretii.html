Sempervivum barretii

Sempervivum barretii, commonly known as the Barret's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers on tall, branching stems during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Sempervivum barretii is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is often cultivated as an ornamental in rock gardens and containers.
