Spiraea velutina var. glabrescens

Spiraea velutina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
