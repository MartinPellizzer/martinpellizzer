Solanum andigenum f. ccompetillo

Solanum andigenum f. ccompetillo, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, violet to purple flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
