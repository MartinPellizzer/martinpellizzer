Shorea farinosa

Shorea farinosa, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, white flowers and is valued for its lightweight, durable wood used in construction and furniture. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Shorea farinosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
