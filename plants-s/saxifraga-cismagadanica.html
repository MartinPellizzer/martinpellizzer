Saxifraga cismagadanica

Saxifraga cismagadanica, commonly known as the Trans-Caucasian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, often clustered, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga cismagadanica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
