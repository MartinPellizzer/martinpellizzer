Solanum aligerum

Solanum aligerum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Solanum aligerum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers, and it contains toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
