Syzygium neolaurifolium

Syzygium neolaurifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It produces edible, aromatic fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium neolaurifolium plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting biodiversity and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
