Solenanthus olgae var. intermedius

Solenanthus olgae var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate olgae solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the arid environments it inhabits.

This variety is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes.
