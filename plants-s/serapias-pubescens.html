Serapias pubescens

Serapias pubescens, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a contrasting purple or reddish lip, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Serapias pubescens is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal cliffs.
