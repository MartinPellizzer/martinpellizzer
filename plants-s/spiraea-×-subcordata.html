Spiraea × subcordata

Spiraea × subcordata, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a hybrid deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with arching branches that create a soft, flowing appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy dark green color, and they turn reddish in the fall.

Spiraea × subcordata is well-suited for landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
