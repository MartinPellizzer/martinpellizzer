Santolina jamaicensis

Santolina jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic, silver-gray in color, and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Santolina jamaicensis is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in tropical climates.
