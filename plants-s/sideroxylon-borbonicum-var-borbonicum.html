Sideroxylon borbonicum var. borbonicum

Sideroxylon borbonicum var. borbonicum, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters in height, with dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry climates and is often found in dry forests and degraded habitats.
