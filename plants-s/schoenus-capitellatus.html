Schoenus capitellatus

Schoenus capitellatus, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along water bodies.

Schoenus capitellatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
