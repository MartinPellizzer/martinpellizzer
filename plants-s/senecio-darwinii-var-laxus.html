Senecio darwinii var. laxus

Senecio darwinii var. laxus, commonly known as the Darwin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
