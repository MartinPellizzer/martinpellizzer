Styrax ochraceus

Styrax ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

Styrax ochraceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
