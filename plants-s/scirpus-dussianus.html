Scirpus dussianus

Scirpus dussianus, commonly known as the Dusky bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus dussianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
