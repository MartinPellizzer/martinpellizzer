Syringa komarowii var. reflexa

Syringa komarowii var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its unique weeping form makes it a striking ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
