Stipa glareosa var. langshanica

Stipa glareosa var. langshanica, commonly known as the Langshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale yellow to brownish florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous roots and drought-resistant nature make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
