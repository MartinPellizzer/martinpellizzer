Scirpus hakonensis

Scirpus hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakonensis bulrush, is a tall, perennial plant native to the wetlands of East Asia.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, pale brown flowers at the top of its stems. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form extensive underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus hakonensis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
