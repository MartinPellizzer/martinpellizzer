Schefflera dentata

Schefflera dentata, commonly known as the serrated umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are palmately arranged with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive, umbrella-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
