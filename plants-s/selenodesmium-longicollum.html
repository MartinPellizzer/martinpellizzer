Selenodesmium longicollum

Selenodesmium longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked selenodesmium, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, creeping stems that form dense mats on moist, shaded substrates. The plant has small, closely spaced leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, are borne on short, erect stalks and contain spores.

Selenodesmium longicollum is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in humid, nutrient-rich environments.
