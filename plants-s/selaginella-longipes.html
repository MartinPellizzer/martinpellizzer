Selaginella longipes

Selaginella longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or clumps on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has thin, needle-like leaves that are often spirally arranged around the stem. Selaginella longipes is characterized by its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its water-conserving adaptations.

It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
