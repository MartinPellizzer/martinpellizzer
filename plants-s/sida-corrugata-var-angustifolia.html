Sida corrugata var. angustifolia

Sida corrugata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herbaceous plant with slender, angular stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is characterized by its distinctive corrugated leaf surfaces and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
