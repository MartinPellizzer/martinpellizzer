Spiranthes longifolia

Spiranthes longifolia, commonly known as the slender ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often blooming in late summer.

Spiranthes longifolia is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological complexity.
