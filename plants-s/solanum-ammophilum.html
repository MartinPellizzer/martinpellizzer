Solanum ammophilum

Solanum ammophilum, commonly known as the sand-plant or beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Solanum ammophilum is part of the nightshade family and is often used in local landscaping for its ornamental value.
