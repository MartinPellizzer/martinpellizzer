Silene kilianii

Silene kilianii, commonly known as the Kilian's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene kilianii is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
