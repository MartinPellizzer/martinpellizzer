Stachys perfoliata

Stachys perfoliata, commonly known as betony or red deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Stachys perfoliata is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
