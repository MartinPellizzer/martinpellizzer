Statice juncea proles eriophylla

Statice juncea proles eriophylla, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its fibrous stems and narrow leaves are adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
