Schoenorchis juncifolia

Schoenorchis juncifolia, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to its wetland habitat with specialized root systems. Its floral structure includes a single, elongated spike with small, clustered blooms, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Schoenorchis juncifolia is often found in regions with high humidity and seasonal flooding.
