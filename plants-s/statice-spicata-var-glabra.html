Statice spicata var. glabra

Statice spicata var. glabra, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

It is a hardy perennial that blooms in summer, adding a striking visual element to coastal landscapes.
