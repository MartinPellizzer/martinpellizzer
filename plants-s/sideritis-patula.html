Sideritis patula

Sideritis patula, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis patula is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
