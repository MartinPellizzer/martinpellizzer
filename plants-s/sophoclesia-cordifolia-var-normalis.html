Sophoclesia cordifolia var. normalis

Sophoclesia cordifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the Heartleaf Sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
