Syringodium filiforme

Syringodium filiforme, commonly known as shoal grass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a salt-tolerant perennial grass that thrives in shallow coastal waters and estuaries. The plant has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that grow in dense underwater tufts. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Syringodium filiforme is an important component of seagrass meadows in tropical and subtropical regions.
