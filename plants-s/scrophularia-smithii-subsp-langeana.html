Scrophularia smithii subsp. langeana

Scrophularia smithii subsp.

langeana, commonly known as Lange's dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and often medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
