Salvia squalens

Salvia squalens, commonly known as the lesser sage or narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
