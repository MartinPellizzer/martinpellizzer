Setaria fallax

Setaria fallax, commonly known as slender foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically 3 to 6 centimeters long. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, fluffy appearance during flowering. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Setaria fallax is an annual plant that is well-adapted to dry conditions and can be invasive in certain regions.
