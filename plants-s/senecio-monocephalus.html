Senecio monocephalus

Senecio monocephalus, commonly known as the desert lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.
