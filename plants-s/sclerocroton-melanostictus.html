Sclerocroton melanostictus

Sclerocroton melanostictus, commonly known as the black-streaked croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with prominent black veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a branching structure that allows for compact growth.

Sclerocroton melanostictus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
