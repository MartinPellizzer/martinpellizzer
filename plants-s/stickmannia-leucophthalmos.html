Stickmannia leucophthalmos

Stickmannia leucophthalmos, commonly known as the white-eyed stickman, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its unique, elongated, and often brightly colored flowers.

The plant features a tall, slender stem with small, clustered inflorescences that resemble miniature stick figures, giving it its whimsical common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with prominent, showy petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is increasingly cultivated in botanical gardens and among horticultural enthusiasts.
