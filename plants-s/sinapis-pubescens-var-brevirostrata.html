Sinapis pubescens var. brevirostrata

Sinapis pubescens var. brevirostrata, commonly known as the short-beaked mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow, and it produces a distinctive, short beak-like structure at the tip of its seed pods.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in coastal areas.
