Stachys grandidentata

Stachys grandidentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces clusters of small, pale purple to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with prominent, broad teeth along the margins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often showy flowers.
