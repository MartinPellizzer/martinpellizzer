Solidago patagonica

Solidago patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Solidago patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
