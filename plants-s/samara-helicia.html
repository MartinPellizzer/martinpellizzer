Samara helicia

Samara helicia, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by winged seeds known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grey, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Samara helicia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
