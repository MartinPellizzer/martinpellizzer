Salsola pseudonitraria

Salsola pseudonitraria, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. It often grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Salsola pseudonitraria plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
