Sapium marginatum var. intermedium

Sapium marginatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the red swamp crayfish plant, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy, glossy surface and red veins, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as swamps and marshes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
