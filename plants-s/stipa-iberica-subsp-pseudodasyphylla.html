Stipa iberica subsp. pseudodasyphylla

Stipa iberica subsp.

pseudodasyphylla, commonly known as the Iberian feathergrass, is a grass species native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often densely arranged.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
