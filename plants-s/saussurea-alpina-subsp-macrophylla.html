Saussurea alpina subsp. macrophylla

Saussurea alpina subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the Alpine giant gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
