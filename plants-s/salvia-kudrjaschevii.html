Salvia kudrjaschevii

Salvia kudrjaschevii, commonly known as Kudrjaschev's sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
