Sauropus albicans var. genuinus

Sauropus albicans var. genuinus, commonly known as the white-leaf sauropus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or cream patterns. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
