Scrophularia laevigata var. decomposita

Scrophularia laevigata var. decomposita, commonly known as the purple marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
