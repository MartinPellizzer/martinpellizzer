Swertia alba

Swertia alba, commonly known as white swertia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Swertia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
