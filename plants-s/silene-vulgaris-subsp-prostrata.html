Silene vulgaris subsp. prostrata

Silene vulgaris subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as prostrate campion, is a low-growing flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
