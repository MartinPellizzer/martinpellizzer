Sisyrinchium purpurellum

Sisyrinchium purpurellum, commonly known as the purple-blue blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three petals and three sepals, giving it the appearance of having eyes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium purpurellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
