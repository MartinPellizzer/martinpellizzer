Saxifraga vreelandii

Saxifraga vreelandii, commonly known as the Vreeland's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often blooming in the spring. Its foliage consists of thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment.

Saxifraga vreelandii is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
