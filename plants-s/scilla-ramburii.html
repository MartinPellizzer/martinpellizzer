Scilla ramburii

Scilla ramburii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to their striking floral display.

Scilla ramburii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
