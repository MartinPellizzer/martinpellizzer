Salix macrostachya var. leucodendroides

Salix macrostachya var. leucodendroides, commonly known as the white-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
