Strychnos ciliicalyx

Strychnos ciliicalyx, commonly known as the red-fruited strychnos, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that turns red when mature.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
