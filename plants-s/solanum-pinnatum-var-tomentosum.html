Solanum pinnatum var. tomentosum

Solanum pinnatum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are typically red or orange berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
