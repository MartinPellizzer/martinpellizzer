Solanum nelsonii var. caumii

Solanum nelsonii var. caumii, commonly known as the Nelson's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
