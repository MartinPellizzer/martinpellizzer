Solanum longevirgatum

Solanum longevirgatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are a food source for certain wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
