Sasa senanensis var. stenantha

Sasa senanensis var. stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
