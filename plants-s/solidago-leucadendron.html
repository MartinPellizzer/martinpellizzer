Solidago leucadendron

Solidago leucadendron, commonly known as white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant features a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flower heads are composed of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Solidago leucadendron is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
