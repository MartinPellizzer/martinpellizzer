Syzygium subrotundifolium

Syzygium subrotundifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often narrow and slightly wavy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
