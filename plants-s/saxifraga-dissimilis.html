Saxifraga dissimilis

Saxifraga dissimilis, commonly known as the rockfoam, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are covered in a fine, woolly texture.

Saxifraga dissimilis is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
