Sisyrinchium aphyllum

Sisyrinchium aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, with its photosynthetic function carried out by its stems. Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
