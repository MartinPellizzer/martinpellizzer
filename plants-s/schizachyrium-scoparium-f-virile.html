Schizachyrium scoparium f. virile

Schizachyrium scoparium f. virile, commonly known as eastern grass or side oats grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of fluffy, silver-green seeds that mature to a reddish hue. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
