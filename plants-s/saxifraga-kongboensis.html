Saxifraga kongboensis

Saxifraga kongboensis, commonly known as the Bhutan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and India.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
