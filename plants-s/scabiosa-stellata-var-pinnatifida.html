Scabiosa stellata var. pinnatifida

Scabiosa stellata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnatifid scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its extended blooming period, often from late spring through early autumn.
