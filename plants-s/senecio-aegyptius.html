Senecio aegyptius

Senecio aegyptius, commonly known as the Egyptian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also known for its ability to produce a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds.
