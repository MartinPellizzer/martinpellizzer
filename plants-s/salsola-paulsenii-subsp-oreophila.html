Salsola paulsenii subsp. oreophila

Salsola paulsenii subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the mountain saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, enabling it to access deep groundwater sources.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and reduce erosion in degraded landscapes.
