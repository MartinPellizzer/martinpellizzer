Stachytarpheta maximiliani var. glabrata

Stachytarpheta maximiliani var. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
