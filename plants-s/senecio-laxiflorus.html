Senecio laxiflorus

Senecio laxiflorus, commonly known as the woolly pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
