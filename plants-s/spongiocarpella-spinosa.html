Spongiocarpella spinosa

Spongiocarpella spinosa, commonly known as the spiny spongefruit, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that resemble a sponge, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in shaded, moist habitats. Its leaves are usually small and alternate, with a rough, textured surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is noted for its unusual fruit structure.
