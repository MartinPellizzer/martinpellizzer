Salvia polystachia subsp. caesia

Salvia polystachia subsp.

caesia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
