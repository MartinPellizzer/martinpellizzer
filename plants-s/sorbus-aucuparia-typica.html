Sorbus aucuparia typica

Sorbus aucuparia typica, commonly known as the common rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn bright red in autumn, adding ornamental value. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its medicinal and ornamental uses, as well as its role in supporting wildlife.
