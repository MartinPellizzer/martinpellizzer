Spiraea callosa var. superba

Spiraea callosa var. superba, commonly known as the showy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
