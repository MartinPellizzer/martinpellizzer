Silene gallica var. minor

Silene gallica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser campion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Silene gallica var. minor is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
