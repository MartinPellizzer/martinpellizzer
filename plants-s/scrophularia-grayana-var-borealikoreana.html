Scrophularia grayana var. borealikoreana

Scrophularia grayana var. borealikoreana, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Scrophularia grayana.
