Senecio linearifolius var. denticulatus

Senecio linearifolius var. denticulatus, commonly known as the denticulate ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
