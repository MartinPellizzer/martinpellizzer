Satureja pamphylica subsp. davisii

Satureja pamphylica subsp.

davisii, commonly known as Davis's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, ovoid fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species within the Satureja genus.
