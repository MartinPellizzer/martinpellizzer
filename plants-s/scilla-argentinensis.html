Scilla argentinensis

Scilla argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.

Scilla argentinensis is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
