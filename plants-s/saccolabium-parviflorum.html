Saccolabium parviflorum

Saccolabium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals. Saccolabium parviflorum is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to drought conditions due to its deep root system.
