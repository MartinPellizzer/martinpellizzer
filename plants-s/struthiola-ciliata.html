Struthiola ciliata

Struthiola ciliata, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant features pale green, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Struthiola ciliata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
