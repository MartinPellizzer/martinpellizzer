Stroganowia longifolia

Stroganowia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf stroganowia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Stroganowia longifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
