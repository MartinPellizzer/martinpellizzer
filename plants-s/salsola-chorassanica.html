Salsola chorassanica

Salsola chorassanica, commonly known as the Afghan saltbush, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species found in desert and semi-desert regions. Its structure includes thick, fleshy stems that store water, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Salsola chorassanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
