Scleranthus reichardtii

Scleranthus reichardtii, commonly known as Reichardt's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant has adaptations for drought tolerance, including hairy leaves and deep root systems. Its flowers are typically greenish-white in color and bloom in late summer.

Scleranthus reichardtii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
