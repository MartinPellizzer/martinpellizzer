Solanum galeottii

Solanum galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Solanum galeottii is part of the nightshade family, which includes both edible and poisonous species.
