Saxifraga × crispa

Saxifraga × crispa, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the spring and early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Saxifraga × crispa is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
