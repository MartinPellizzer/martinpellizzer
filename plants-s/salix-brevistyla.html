Salix brevistyla

Salix brevistyla, commonly known as the short-styled willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Tibet and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Salix brevistyla typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
