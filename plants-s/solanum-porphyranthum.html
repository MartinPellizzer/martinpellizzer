Solanum porphyranthum

Solanum porphyranthum, commonly known as the purple-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Solanum porphyranthum is known for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its uses.
