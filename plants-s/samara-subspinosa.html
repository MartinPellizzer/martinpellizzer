Samara subspinosa

Samara subspinosa, commonly known as the spiny samara, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive samara fruits, which are dry, winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

Samara subspinosa is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
