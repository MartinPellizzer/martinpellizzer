Spiranthes neuroptera

Spiranthes neuroptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves and its long, slender flowering spike. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in pollination and is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
