Selaginella utahensis

Selaginella utahensis, commonly known as the Utah spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid environments. The plant has a creeping or upright growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.
