Setaria pseudoverticillata

Setaria pseudoverticillata, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, thread-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
