Syzygium sarmentosum

Syzygium sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping apple.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
