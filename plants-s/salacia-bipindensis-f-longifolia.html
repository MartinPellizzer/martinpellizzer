Salacia bipindensis f. longifolia

Salacia bipindensis f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved salacia, is a unique variant of the Salacia genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
