Scotanthus tubiflorus

Scotanthus tubiflorus, commonly known as the woolly bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in spring.

Scotanthus tubiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
