Strongylosperma australe

Strongylosperma australe, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery foliage, which are typical features of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils. It has adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
