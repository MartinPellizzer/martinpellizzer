Silene heterodonta var. thomsonii

Silene heterodonta var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Thomson's flaxflower is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
