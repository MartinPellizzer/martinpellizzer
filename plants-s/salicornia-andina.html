Salicornia andina

Salicornia andina, commonly known as Inca sea bean, is a halophytic plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, jointed stems that are typically reddish or pink in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, edible seed that is used in traditional cuisine.

Salicornia andina is valued for its nutritional content and ability to grow in harsh environmental conditions.
