Sophora tomentosa f. glabra

Sophora tomentosa f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth sophora, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional uses.
