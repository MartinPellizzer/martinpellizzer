Stelis acostaei

Stelis acostaei, commonly known as the Acosta's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stelis acostaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
