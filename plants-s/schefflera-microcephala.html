Schefflera microcephala

Schefflera microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, umbrella-like clusters of small, waxy leaves. The plant features a thick, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically dark green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries.

Schefflera microcephala is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
