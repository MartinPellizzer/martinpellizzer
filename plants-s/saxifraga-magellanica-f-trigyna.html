Saxifraga magellanica f. trigyna

Saxifraga magellanica f. trigyna, commonly known as the Magellanic saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or white, during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
