Senna neglecta var. grandiflora

Senna neglecta var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
