Sertuernera glauca

Sertuernera glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Sertuernera glauca is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
