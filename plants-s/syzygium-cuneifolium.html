Syzygium cuneifolium

Syzygium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils and is often found in shrubland and woodland habitats.

Syzygium cuneifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
