Solanum bohsiae

Solanum bohsiae, commonly known as the Bohsia nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

Solanum bohsiae is often found in humid, shaded environments in its native habitat.
