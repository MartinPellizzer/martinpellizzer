Solanum capoerum

Solanum capoerum, commonly known as the caper spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Solanum capoerum is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.
