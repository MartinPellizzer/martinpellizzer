Stipa latissimifolia

Stipa latissimifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved grass or silvergrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and wide, flat leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Stipa latissimifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
