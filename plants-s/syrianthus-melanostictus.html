Syrianthus melanostictus

Syrianthus melanostictus, commonly known as the black-streaked cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the arid regions of Syria and Turkey.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
