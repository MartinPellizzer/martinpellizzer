Serapias aculeata

Serapias aculeata, commonly known as the spiny orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Serapias aculeata blooms in late spring or early summer, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
