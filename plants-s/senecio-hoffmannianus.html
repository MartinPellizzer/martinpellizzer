Senecio hoffmannianus

Senecio hoffmannianus, commonly known as the Blue Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded shape with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
