Satureja amplifoliata

Satureja amplifoliata, commonly known as broadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Satureja amplifoliata is valued for its fragrant properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
