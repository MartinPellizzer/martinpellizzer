Sasa kirisimensis

Sasa kirisimensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or sasa, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense tussocks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by the development of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Sasa kirisimensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to form thick, attractive mats.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in shaded forest environments.
