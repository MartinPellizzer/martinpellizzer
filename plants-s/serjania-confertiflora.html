Serjania confertiflora

Serjania confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or yellow serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Serjania confertiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
