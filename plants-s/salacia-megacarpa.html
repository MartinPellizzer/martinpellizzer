Salacia megacarpa

Salacia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant salacia, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has thick, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically clustered in panicles.

The fruit contains a single, hard seed and is notable for its high content of alkaloids and antioxidants.
