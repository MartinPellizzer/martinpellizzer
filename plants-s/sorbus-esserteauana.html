Sorbus esserteauana

Sorbus esserteauana, commonly known as the Esserteau whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
