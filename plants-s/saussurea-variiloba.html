Saussurea variiloba

Saussurea variiloba, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibet.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky, arid environments. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a distinct, aromatic scent.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
