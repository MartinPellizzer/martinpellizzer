Stryphnodendron heringeri

Stryphnodendron heringeri, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color and a distinctive grain pattern. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple to black fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to its high value for wood, it is considered an endangered species and is protected in many regions.
