Syzygium amplexicaule

Syzygium amplexicaule, commonly known as the climbing syzygium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend tree trunks and other structures. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a waxy texture.

Syzygium amplexicaule is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
