Sasa nakasiretokensis

Sasa nakasiretokensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Sasa nakasiretokensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
