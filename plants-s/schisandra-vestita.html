Schisandra vestita

Schisandra vestita, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its small, red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces fragrant flowers in the spring. Schisandra vestita is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its berries are edible but are typically used in culinary applications due to their tart flavor.
