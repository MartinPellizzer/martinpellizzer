Setaria gracilis var. dasyura

Setaria gracilis var. dasyura, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
