Salacia garcinioides

Salacia garcinioides, commonly known as the purple-rooted mountain vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its roots are notable for their vibrant purple color, which has led to its traditional use in herbal medicine.
