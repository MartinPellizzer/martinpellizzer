Scutellaria pannosula

Scutellaria pannosula, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem that is often covered in a dense layer of soft, white or grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria pannosula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
