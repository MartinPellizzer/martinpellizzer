Scolymocephalus patens

Scolymocephalus patens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
