Sigesbeckia triangularis

Sigesbeckia triangularis, commonly known as the three-angled beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sigesbeckia triangularis is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
