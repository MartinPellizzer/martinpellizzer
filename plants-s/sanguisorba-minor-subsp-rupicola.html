Sanguisorba minor subsp. rupicola

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock sanguisorba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe. It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
