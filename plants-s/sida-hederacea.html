Sida hederacea

Sida hederacea, commonly known as broadleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often mistaken for leaves due to their shape. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Sida hederacea is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
