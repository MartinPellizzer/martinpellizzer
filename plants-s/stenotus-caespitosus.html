Stenotus caespitosus

Stenotus caespitosus, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact growth habit, often forming mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Stenotus caespitosus thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and woodland edges.
