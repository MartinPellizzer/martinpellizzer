Sisymbrium affine

Sisymbrium affine, commonly known as sweet rocket, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Sweet rocket is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, and it is known for its pleasant, spicy fragrance.
