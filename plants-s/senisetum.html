Senisetum

Senisetum, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and often features striking purple or pinkish inflorescences. These plants are typically found in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heaths. Senisetum species are perennial and have rhizomatous or stolonic growth, allowing them to spread and form dense mats.

Their foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often hairy or rough to the touch.
