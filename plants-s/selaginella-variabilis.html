Selaginella variabilis

Selaginella variabilis, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial moss-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. Its sporophyte produces spores in structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella variabilis is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in various habitats.
