Sparganium perpusillum

Sparganium perpusillum, commonly known as the dwarf bur reed, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water, forming dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often covered in soft, spiny bracts. Its fruits are cylindrical, spiny burrs that can adhere to the fur of animals or the clothing of humans.

Sparganium perpusillum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
