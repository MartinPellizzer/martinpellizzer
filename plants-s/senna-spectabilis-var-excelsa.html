Senna spectabilis var. excelsa

Senna spectabilis var. excelsa, commonly known as the golden senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
