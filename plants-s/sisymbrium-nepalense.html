Sisymbrium nepalense

Sisymbrium nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
