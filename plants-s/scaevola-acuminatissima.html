Scaevola acuminatissima

Scaevola acuminatissima, commonly known as the coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has small, narrow leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Scaevola acuminatissima is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
