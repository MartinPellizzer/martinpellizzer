Sporobolus cuspidatus

Sporobolus cuspidatus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and have a prominent midrib, and it is often found in dry, disturbed soils.

Sporobolus cuspidatus is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
