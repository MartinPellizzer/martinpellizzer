Spongopyrena reniformis

Spongopyrena reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped sponge algae, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinct, kidney-shaped thallus that resembles a sponge in texture. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of a single, undifferentiated body. It thrives in slow-moving or still water, often attaching itself to submerged surfaces.

Its simple structure and specialized morphology make it an important indicator species for water quality in its habitat.
