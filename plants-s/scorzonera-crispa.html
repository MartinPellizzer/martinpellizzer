Scorzonera crispa

Scorzonera crispa, commonly known as the prickly sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often prickly leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are elongated, with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Scorzonera crispa is also known for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional cuisine.
