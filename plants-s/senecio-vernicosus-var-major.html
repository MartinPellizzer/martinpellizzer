Senecio vernicosus var. major

Senecio vernicosus var. major, commonly known as the greater velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
