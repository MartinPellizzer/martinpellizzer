Swertia cucullata

Swertia cucullata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Swertia cucullata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
