Sauropus grandifolius var. tonkinensis

Sauropus grandifolius var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
