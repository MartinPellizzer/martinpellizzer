Sedum acre var. glaciale

Sedum acre var. glaciale, commonly known as the alpine sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to mountainous regions.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.
