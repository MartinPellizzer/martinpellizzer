Salix babylonica f. pekinensis

Salix babylonica f. pekinensis, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water bodies.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade.
