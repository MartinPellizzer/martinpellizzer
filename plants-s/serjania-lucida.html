Serjania lucida

Serjania lucida, commonly known as the yellow serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
