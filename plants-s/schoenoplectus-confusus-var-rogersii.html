Schoenoplectus confusus var. rogersii

Schoenoplectus confusus var. rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows in marshes, lakes, and other aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a key indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
