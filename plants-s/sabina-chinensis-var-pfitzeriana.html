Sabina chinensis var. pfitzeriana

Sabina chinensis var. pfitzeriana, commonly known as the Chinese thuja or pfitzer thuja, is a variant of the Chinese juniper.

It is a small, evergreen conifer characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically a deep green, with a slight blue-gray tinge, and it produces small, yellowish female cones. This variety is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
