Scirpus junceus

Scirpus junceus, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters at the top of the stem. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus junceus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
