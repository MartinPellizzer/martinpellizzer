Solanum nigrum var. americanum

Solanum nigrum var. americanum, commonly known as American black nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. These berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds in their raw form.

It is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
