Syringa × rubra

Syringa × rubra, commonly known as the red bush hyacinth, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its colorful blooms. Syringa × rubra is well-suited for gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
