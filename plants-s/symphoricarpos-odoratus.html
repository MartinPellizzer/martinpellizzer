Symphoricarpos odoratus

Symphoricarpos odoratus, commonly known as rosehip bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is also valued for its fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring.
