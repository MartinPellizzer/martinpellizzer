Salix × valsoreyana

Salix × valsoreyana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix viminalis (commonly known as osier) and Salix triandra. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is commonly used for basket weaving due to its flexible stems.

Salix × valsoreyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
