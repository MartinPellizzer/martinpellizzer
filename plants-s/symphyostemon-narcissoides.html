Symphyostemon narcissoides

Symphyostemon narcissoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
