Sida erosa

Sida erosa, commonly known as sea spurrey, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. Sida erosa is adapted to salty, sandy soils and can tolerate maritime conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects.
