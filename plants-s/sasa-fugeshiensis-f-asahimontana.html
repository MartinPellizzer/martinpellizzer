Sasa fugeshiensis f. asahimontana

Sasa fugeshiensis f. asahimontana, commonly known as the Asahimontan bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa fugeshiensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
