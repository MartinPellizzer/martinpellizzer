Saguaster puniceus

Saguaster puniceus, commonly known as the red sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Saguaster puniceus is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and aromatic properties.
