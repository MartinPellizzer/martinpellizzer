Salvia grewiifolia

Salvia grewiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a mint-like scent, contributing to its use in herbal remedies and ornamental gardening.

Salvia grewiifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
