Sarcostemma vanlessenii

Sarcostemma vanlessenii, commonly known as the yellow star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
