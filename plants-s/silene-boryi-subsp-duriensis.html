Silene boryi subsp. duriensis

Silene boryi subsp.

duriensis, commonly known as the Durienskaya silene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid environments. Silene boryi subsp.

duriensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
