Sedum divergens

Sedum divergens, commonly known as the spreading stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often branch out horizontally, giving it a distinctive, sprawling appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Sedum divergens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
