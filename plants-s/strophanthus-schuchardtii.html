Strophanthus schuchardtii

Strophanthus schuchardtii, commonly known as the red-stemmed African milkvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.

Its flowers are also used in the production of certain medicinal compounds.
