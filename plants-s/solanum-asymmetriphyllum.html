Solanum asymmetriphyllum

Solanum asymmetriphyllum, commonly known as the asymmetric-leaf nightshade, is a unique species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly asymmetrical leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with one side significantly longer than the other. This plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue color.

Solanum asymmetriphyllum is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
