Syzygium pyrifolium

Syzygium pyrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, and it produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants.

Syzygium pyrifolium is cultivated for its unique, tangy-sweet fruit, which is widely consumed fresh or in various culinary applications.
