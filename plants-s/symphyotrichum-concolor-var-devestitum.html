Symphyotrichum concolor var. devestitum

Symphyotrichum concolor var. devestitum, commonly known as the slender blue mistflower, is a variant of the blue mistflower native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are visited by various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
