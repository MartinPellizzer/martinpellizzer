Syzygium elegans

Syzygium elegans, commonly known as the elegant tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a sour taste. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

Syzygium elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
