Selaginella nepalesis

Selaginella nepalesis, commonly known as the Nepalese spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and produce spores.

Selaginella nepalesis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
