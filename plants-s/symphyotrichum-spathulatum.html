Symphyotrichum spathulatum

Symphyotrichum spathulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
