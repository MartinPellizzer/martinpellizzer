Stipecoma parabolica

Stipecoma parabolica, commonly known as the parabolic stipe, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive parabolic growth pattern.

It belongs to the family Stipecomaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a curved shape that follows a mathematical parabola, making it visually striking. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The botanical interest in Stipecoma parabolica lies in its unusual morphology and potential ecological adaptations.
