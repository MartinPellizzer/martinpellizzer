Stipa virginica

Stipa virginica, commonly known as Virginia needlegrass, is a native grass species found in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. This perennial plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often adorned with silky hairs.

Stipa virginica plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
