Syzygium lugubre

Syzygium lugubre, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Syzygium lugubre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
