Senecio lemuricus

Senecio lemuricus, commonly known as the Madagascar groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
