Salvia moschata

Salvia moschata, commonly known as lemon basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and vibrant violet to purple flower spikes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, citrus-like aroma when crushed.

Salvia moschata is often used in culinary applications and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
