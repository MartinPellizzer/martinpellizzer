Senecio pampae var. penicillatus

Senecio pampae var. penicillatus, commonly known as the Pampas Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
