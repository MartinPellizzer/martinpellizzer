Scleranthus polycarpos var. delortii

Scleranthus polycarpos var. delortii, commonly known as the Delort's sickleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
