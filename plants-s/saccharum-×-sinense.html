Saccharum × sinense

Saccharum × sinense, commonly known as hybrid sugarcane.

It is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saccharum officinarum and Saccharum spontaneum. This plant is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it a valuable resource for commercial sugarcane production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained, fertile soils.

Its cultivation requires significant water and nutrient inputs, which influence its agricultural management practices.
