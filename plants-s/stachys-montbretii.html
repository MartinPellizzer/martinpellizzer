Stachys montbretii

Stachys montbretii, commonly known as French lavender or montbretia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of violet or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies.
