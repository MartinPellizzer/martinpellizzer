Styrax barbatus

Styrax barbatus, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Styrax barbatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its resins and essential oils.
