Stipa sareptana subsp. anisotricha

Stipa sareptana subsp.

anisotricha, commonly known as the anisotrichous feathergrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often found in alpine and steppe ecosystems.
