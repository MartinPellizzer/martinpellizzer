Solanum elskensii

Solanum elskensii, commonly known as the Elskens's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solanine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
