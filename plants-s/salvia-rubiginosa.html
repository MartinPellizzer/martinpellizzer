Salvia rubiginosa

Salvia rubiginosa, commonly known as crimson sage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking red or purple flower spikes. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia rubiginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
