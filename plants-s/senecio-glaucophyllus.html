Senecio glaucophyllus

Senecio glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and produces small yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
