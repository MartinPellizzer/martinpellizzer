Silene baccifera var. japonica

Silene baccifera var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
