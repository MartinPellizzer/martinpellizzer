Schefflera heterotricha

Schefflera heterotricha, commonly known as the mountain schefflera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a palmate pattern with multiple leaflets radiating from a central point. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Schefflera heterotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
