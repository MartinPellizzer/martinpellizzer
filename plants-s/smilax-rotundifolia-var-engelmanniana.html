Smilax rotundifolia var. engelmanniana

Smilax rotundifolia var. engelmanniana, commonly known as Engelmann's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a robust, thorny stem that climbs or spreads along the ground, often forming dense thickets. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, spiny berries.

It is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
