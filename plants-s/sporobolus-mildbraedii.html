Sporobolus mildbraedii

Sporobolus mildbraedii, commonly known as the Mildbraed's spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Sporobolus mildbraedii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
