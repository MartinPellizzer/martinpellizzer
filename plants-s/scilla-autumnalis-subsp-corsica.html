Scilla autumnalis subsp. corsica

Scilla autumnalis subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of striking blue to purple flowers in the autumn.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal or mountainous areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Scilla genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
