Spartina brasiliensis

Spartina brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has slender, grass-like blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which stabilize sediments and prevent erosion.

Spartina brasiliensis plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
