Silene croatica

Silene croatica, commonly known as the Croatian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene croatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
