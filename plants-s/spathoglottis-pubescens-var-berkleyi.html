Spathoglottis pubescens var. berkleyi

Spathoglottis pubescens var. berkleyi, commonly known as the Berkley's spathoglottis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has hairy (pubescent) stems and leaves, which are distinctive features of this variety.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
