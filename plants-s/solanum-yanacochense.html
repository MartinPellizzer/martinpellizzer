Solanum yanacochense

Solanum yanacochense, commonly known as the Yanacocha nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Peru and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that mature to a dark blue color, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

Solanum yanacochense is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
