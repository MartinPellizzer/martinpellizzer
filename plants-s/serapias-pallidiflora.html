Serapias pallidiflora

Serapias pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish or pale orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

Serapias pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
