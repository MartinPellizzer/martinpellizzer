Stipagrostis dinteri

Stipagrostis dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Stipagrostis dinteri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
