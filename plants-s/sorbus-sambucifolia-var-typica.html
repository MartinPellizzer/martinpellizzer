Sorbus sambucifolia var. typica

Sorbus sambucifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red, berry-like fruits known as pomes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and landscapes.
