Sabal taurina

Sabal taurina, commonly known as the Florida palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, solitary tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 feet. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk and is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are long and feather-like. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.

Sabal taurina plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
