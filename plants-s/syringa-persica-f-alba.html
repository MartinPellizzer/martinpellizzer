Syringa persica f. alba

Syringa persica f. alba, commonly known as the white Persian lilac, is a cultivar of the Persian lilac, characterized by its striking white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its beauty and fragrance.
