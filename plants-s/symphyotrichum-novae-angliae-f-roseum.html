Symphyotrichum novae-angliae f. roseum

Symphyotrichum novae-angliae f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum form of New England aster, is a botanical variant characterized by its pale pink to lavender flower heads.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America, typically found in open woodlands and meadows. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces showy composite flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is generally gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
