Sessilanthera latifolia

Sessilanthera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sessilanthera, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
