Salix fissa var. olivacea

Salix fissa var. olivacea, commonly known as the oliveleaf willow, is a variant of the common willow species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a pale greenish-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its catkin-like flowers appear in early spring, contributing to its role in early pollination cycles.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
