Smilax macrophylla

Smilax macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowhead or swamp arrowhead, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Smilax macrophylla is a dioecious species, meaning that male and female flowers are found on separate plants.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable ecological component and a potential invasive species in certain areas.
