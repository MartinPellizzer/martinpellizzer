Scolochloa spiculosa

Scolochloa spiculosa, commonly known as the spiky grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scolochloa spiculosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its resilience and adaptability contribute to its presence in various habitats across its native range.
