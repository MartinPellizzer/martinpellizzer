Solidago gymnospermoides var. callosa

Solidago gymnospermoides var. callosa, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
