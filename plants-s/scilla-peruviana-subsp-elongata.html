Scilla peruviana subsp. elongata

Scilla peruviana subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its elongated bulbs and is often found in moist, rocky habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Scilla genus, sharing similar botanical traits.
