Serruria burmanni var. subsericea

Serruria burmanni var. subsericea, commonly known as the silverleaf serruria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
