Salix tarraconensis f. microphylla

Salix tarraconensis f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and slender, often pendulous branches. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in gardens.
