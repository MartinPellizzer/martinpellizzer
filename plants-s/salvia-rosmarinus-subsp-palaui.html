Salvia rosmarinus subsp. palaui

Salvia rosmarinus subsp.

palaui, commonly known as Palaui sage, is a subspecies native to the island of Palau in the western Pacific. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to common sage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and ecological adaptation to its native environment.
