Senecio anteuphorbium

Senecio anteuphorbium, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
