Senecio linearilobus

Senecio linearilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with lobed tips, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio linearilobus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
