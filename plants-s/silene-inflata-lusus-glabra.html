Silene inflata lusus glabra

Silene inflata lusus glabra, commonly known as the glabrous bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the fruits are inflated, bladder-like pods that aid in seed dispersal.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
