Syzygium gracile

Syzygium gracile, commonly known as the small-fruited clove or dwarf clove, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic, white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Syzygium gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
