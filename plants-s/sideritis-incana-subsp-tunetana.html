Sideritis incana subsp. tunetana

Sideritis incana subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the tunetan narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal remedies.
