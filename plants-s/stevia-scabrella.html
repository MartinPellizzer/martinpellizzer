Stevia scabrella

Stevia scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in capitula, typical of the daisy family. Stevia scabrella is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides.

It is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
