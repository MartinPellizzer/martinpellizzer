Stachys mauritiana

Stachys mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys mauritiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
