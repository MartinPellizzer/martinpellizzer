Scirpus isdellensis

Scirpus isdellensis, commonly known as the Isdell's bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and its ability to thrive in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and sometimes have a reddish tinge. Scirpus isdellensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability to wetland environments.
