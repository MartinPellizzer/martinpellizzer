Satureja obovata subsp. hispalensis

Satureja obovata subsp.

hispalensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant produces pink or purple flowers in late spring, which are borne in clusters atop the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the Satureja genus.
