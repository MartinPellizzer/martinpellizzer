Sauvagesia brevipetala

Sauvagesia brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petalled sauvagesia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
