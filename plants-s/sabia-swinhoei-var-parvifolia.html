Sabia swinhoei var. parvifolia

Sabia swinhoei var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Swinhoe's sabia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
