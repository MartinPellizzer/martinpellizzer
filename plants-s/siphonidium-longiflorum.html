Siphonidium longiflorum

Siphonidium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered siphonidium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture.

Siphonidium longiflorum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
