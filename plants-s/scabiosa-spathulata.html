Scabiosa spathulata

Scabiosa spathulata, commonly known as the heart's-ease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, pink or white flowers that resemble tiny hearts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa spathulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
