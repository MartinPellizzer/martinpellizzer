Spyridium erymnocladum

Spyridium erymnocladum, commonly known as the silver-leafed spyridium, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Spyridium erymnocladum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and drought tolerance.
