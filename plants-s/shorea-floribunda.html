Shorea floribunda

Shorea floribunda, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Shorea floribunda plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
