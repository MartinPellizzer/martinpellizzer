Solanum andigenum f. huaman-uma

Solanum andigenum f. huaman-uma, commonly known as the Huaman-uma nightshade, is a botanical variant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. This particular form is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure, often featuring vibrant purple and blue hues. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, adapting to the cooler and more variable climates of the Andes.

Due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
