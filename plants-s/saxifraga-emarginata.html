Saxifraga emarginata

Saxifraga emarginata, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga emarginata is often found in mountainous areas and tundra environments.
