Stipa calamagrostis subsp. glabra

Stipa calamagrostis subsp.

glabra, commonly known as glabrous feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, contributing to its distinctive, elegant look.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
