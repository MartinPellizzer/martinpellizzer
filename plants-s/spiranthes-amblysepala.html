Spiranthes amblysepala

Spiranthes amblysepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling arrangement of small, white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
