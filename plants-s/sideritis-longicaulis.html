Sideritis longicaulis

Sideritis longicaulis, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Sideritis longicaulis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
