Sedum mirum

Sedum mirum, commonly known as the "miracle plant," is a succulent species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically green, sometimes with a slight blue or silver tint, and can store water efficiently.

Sedum mirum is valued for its ornamental beauty and low maintenance requirements in landscaping.
