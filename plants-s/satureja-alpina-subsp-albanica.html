Satureja alpina subsp. albanica

Satureja alpina subsp.

albanica, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers and has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct savory scent. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
