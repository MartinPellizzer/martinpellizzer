Sterigmostemum rhodanthum

Sterigmostemum rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sterigmostemum rhodanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
