Syzygium maingayi

Syzygium maingayi, commonly known as the white carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a distinctive, elongated berry with a sweet, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines. The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
