Salvia angustifolia

Salvia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or blue-purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the mint family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal practices.
