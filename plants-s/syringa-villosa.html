Syringa villosa

Syringa villosa, commonly known as the fuzzy bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, bushy form. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Syringa villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
