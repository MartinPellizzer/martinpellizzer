Solanum tuberosum var. boreale

Solanum tuberosum var. boreale, commonly known as the boreal potato, is a variant of the potato plant adapted to cooler climates.

It is characterized by its robust stems and tubers that develop in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with shorter growing seasons. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces white or pale pink flowers.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in northern latitudes.
