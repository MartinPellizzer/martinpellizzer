Scleria conspersa

Scleria conspersa, commonly known as the hairy basket sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Scleria conspersa is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
