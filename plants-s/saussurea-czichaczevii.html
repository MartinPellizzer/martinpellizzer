Saussurea czichaczevii

Saussurea czichaczevii, commonly known as the Czichaczewicz saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense, yellowish to orange-red flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are typically clustered in a single terminal head. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
