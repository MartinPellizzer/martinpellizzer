Symplocos major

Symplocos major, commonly known as the large wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Symplocos major plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
