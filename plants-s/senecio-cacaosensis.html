Senecio cacaosensis

Senecio cacaosensis, commonly known as Chocolate Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence with bright yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Senecio cacaosensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
