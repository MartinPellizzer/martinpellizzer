Sarracenia drummondii var. alba

Sarracenia drummondii var. alba, commonly known as the white pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale, often white or pinkish pitchers that are adapted to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires a humid, sunny environment to flourish. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The variety alba is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other forms of Sarracenia drummondii.
