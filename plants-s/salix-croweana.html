Salix croweana

Salix croweana, commonly known as the Crowe's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a silvery underside, while its flowers are catkin-like and appear in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and high-altitude areas.
