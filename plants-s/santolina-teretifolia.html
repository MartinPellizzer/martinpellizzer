Santolina teretifolia

Santolina teretifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina teretifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
