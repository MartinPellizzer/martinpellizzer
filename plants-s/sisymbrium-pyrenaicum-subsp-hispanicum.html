Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp. hispanicum

Sisymbrium pyrenaicum subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.
