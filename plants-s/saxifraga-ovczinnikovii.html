Saxifraga ovczinnikovii

Saxifraga ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Ovczinnikov's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with upright, slender stems that bear small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive waxy texture. The flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and prominent stamens.

This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
