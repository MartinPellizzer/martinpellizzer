Sapium berberifolium

Sapium berberifolium, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, attracting various bird species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a yellowish or orange hue on the underside.

Sapium berberifolium is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
