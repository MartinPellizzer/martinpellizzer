Senna glutinosa subsp. glutinosa

Senna glutinosa subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
