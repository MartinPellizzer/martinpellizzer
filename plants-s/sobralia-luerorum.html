Sobralia luerorum

Sobralia luerorum, commonly known as the Luer's sobralia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, brightly colored flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
