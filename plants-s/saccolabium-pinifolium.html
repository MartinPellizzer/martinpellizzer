Saccolabium pinifolium

Saccolabium pinifolium, commonly known as the needleleaf saccolabium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically yellow or pale green, and it produces legumes as its fruit.

Saccolabium pinifolium is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can thrive in regions with limited rainfall.
