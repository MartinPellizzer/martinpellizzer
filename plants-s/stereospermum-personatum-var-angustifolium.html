Stereospermum personatum var. angustifolium

Stereospermum personatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
