Symphyotrichum campestre

Symphyotrichum campestre, commonly known as the field ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.
