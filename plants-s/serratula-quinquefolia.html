Serratula quinquefolia

Serratula quinquefolia, commonly known as five-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, five-lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Serratula quinquefolia is also noted for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
