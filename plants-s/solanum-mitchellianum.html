Solanum mitchellianum

Solanum mitchellianum, commonly known as Mitchell's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption by humans or livestock.
