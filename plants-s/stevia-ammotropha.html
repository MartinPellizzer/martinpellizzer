Stevia ammotropha

Stevia ammotropha, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its natural sweetness, it is often used as a sugar substitute in food and beverages.
