Sclerocephalus arabicus var. leianthus

Sclerocephalus arabicus var. leianthus, commonly known as the Arabian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a resilient species in desert conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
