Senecio morenonis var. typicus

Senecio morenonis var. typicus, commonly known as the Morenonis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
