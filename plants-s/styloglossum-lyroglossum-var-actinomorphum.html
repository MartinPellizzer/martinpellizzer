Styloglossum lyroglossum var. actinomorphum

Styloglossum lyroglossum var. actinomorphum, commonly known as the star-shaped lily, is a unique member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, radially symmetrical flowers that resemble stars, with six to eight petals arranged in a spiral. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are strap-like and often persist through the winter, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
