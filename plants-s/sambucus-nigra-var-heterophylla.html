Sambucus nigra var. heterophylla

Sambucus nigra var. heterophylla, commonly known as the European elderberry with variable leaves, is a variant of the common elderberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, which varies in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is native to parts of Europe. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
