Serraea incana

Serraea incana, commonly known as the woolly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Serraea incana is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive species in its native environment.
