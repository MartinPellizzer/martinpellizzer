Silene parrowiana

Silene parrowiana, commonly known as the Parrow's campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas like the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry conditions. It produces showy pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene parrowiana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
