Symplocos pulchra subsp. coriacea

Symplocos pulchra subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright purple berries.

The leaves are typically thick and leathery, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
