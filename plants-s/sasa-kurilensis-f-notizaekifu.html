Sasa kurilensis f. notizaekifu

Sasa kurilensis f. notizaekifu, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to the Japanese archipelago.

It is a clump-forming shrub with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is often characterized by a silvery or grayish underside, adding visual interest.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
