Sasa scytophylla f. lasionodosa

Sasa scytophylla f. lasionodosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are covered in a dense layer of white to silver hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, making it an effective ground cover in shaded or moist environments. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
