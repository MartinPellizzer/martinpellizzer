Sida sulphurea

Sida sulphurea, commonly known as golden sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Sida sulphurea is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
