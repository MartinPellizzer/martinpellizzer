Sacciolepis indica var. turgida

Sacciolepis indica var. turgida, commonly known as the Indian spongeplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a unique appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking form and drought tolerance.
