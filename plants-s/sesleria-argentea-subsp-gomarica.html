Sesleria argentea subsp. gomarica

Sesleria argentea subsp.

gomarica, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Sesleria argentea subsp.

gomarica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
