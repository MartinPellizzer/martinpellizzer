Syzygium setosum

Syzygium setosum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible, sour fruits. Syzygium setosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Syzygium genus.
