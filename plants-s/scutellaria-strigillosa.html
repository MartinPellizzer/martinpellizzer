Scutellaria strigillosa

Scutellaria strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Scutellaria strigillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
