Syzygium xanthophyllum

Syzygium xanthophyllum, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish-red berry that ripens in the summer months.

Syzygium xanthophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
