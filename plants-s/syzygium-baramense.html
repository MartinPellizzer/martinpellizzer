Syzygium baramense

Syzygium baramense, commonly known as the Baramense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, elongated fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been utilized in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Syzygium baramense thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
