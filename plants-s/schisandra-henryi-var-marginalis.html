Schisandra henryi var. marginalis

Schisandra henryi var. marginalis, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that turn dark purple when mature, containing a single seed. Schisandra henryi var. marginalis is valued for its medicinal properties, with its berries traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable plant for shaded garden environments.
