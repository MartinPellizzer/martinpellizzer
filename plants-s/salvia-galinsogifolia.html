Salvia galinsogifolia

Salvia galinsogifolia, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue to violet, clustered in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
