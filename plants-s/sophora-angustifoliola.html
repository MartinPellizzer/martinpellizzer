Sophora angustifoliola

Sophora angustifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Sophora angustifoliola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
