Spiraea betulifolia f. yoshitakae

Spiraea betulifolia f. yoshitakae, commonly known as the Yoshitaka spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a bright green color that turns reddish in autumn. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact size and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
