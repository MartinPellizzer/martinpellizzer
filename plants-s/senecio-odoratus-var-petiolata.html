Senecio odoratus var. petiolata

Senecio odoratus var. petiolata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slight waxy coating, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety petiolata is distinguished by its elongated leaf petioles, which give it a unique appearance compared to other Senecio species.
