Syngonanthus elegantulus

Syngonanthus elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale yellow or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
