Streptocarpus rexii

Streptocarpus rexii, commonly known as the giant streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, violet, and purple. It features thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Streptocarpus rexii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in controlled environments.
