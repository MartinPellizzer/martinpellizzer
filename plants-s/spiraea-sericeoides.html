Spiraea sericeoides

Spiraea sericeoides, commonly known as silverleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray leaves that provide striking visual interest. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea sericeoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
