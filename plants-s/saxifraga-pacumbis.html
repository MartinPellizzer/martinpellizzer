Saxifraga pacumbis

Saxifraga pacumbis, commonly known as the Brazilian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Saxifraga pacumbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
