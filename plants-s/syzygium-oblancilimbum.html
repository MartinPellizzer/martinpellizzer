Syzygium oblancilimbum

Syzygium oblancilimbum, commonly known as the white-leafed apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a notable species in botanical gardens and local ecosystems.
