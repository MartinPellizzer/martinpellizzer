Stipa pennata subsp. grafiana

Stipa pennata subsp.

grafiana, commonly known as the Graf's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and tufted clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens for its elegant, wind-swaying appearance.
