Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. atlantica

Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
