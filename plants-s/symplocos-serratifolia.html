Symplocos serratifolia

Symplocos serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Symplocos serratifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with seasonal interest.
