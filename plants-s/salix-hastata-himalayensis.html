Salix hastata himalayensis

Salix hastata himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
