Struthanthus planaltinae var. elongatipes

Struthanthus planaltinae var. elongatipes, commonly known as the elongated thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
