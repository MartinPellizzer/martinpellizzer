Saxifraga magellanica f. angustifolia

Saxifraga magellanica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Magellan saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
