Sida milleri

Sida milleri, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sida milleri is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in xeriscaping.
