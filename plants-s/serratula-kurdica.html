Serratula kurdica

Serratula kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Serratula kurdica is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
