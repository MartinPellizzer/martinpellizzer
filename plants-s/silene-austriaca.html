Silene austriaca

Silene austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Silene austriaca is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
