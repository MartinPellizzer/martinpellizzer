Saxifraga palmeri

Saxifraga palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Saxifraga palmeri is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
