Salvia nervata

Salvia nervata, commonly known as slender sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
