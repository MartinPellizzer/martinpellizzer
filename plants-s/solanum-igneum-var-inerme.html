Solanum igneum var. inerme

Solanum igneum var. inerme, commonly known as the naked fireberry bush, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and foliage. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
