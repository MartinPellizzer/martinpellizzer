Saussurea racemosa

Saussurea racemosa, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stem and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Its dried flower heads are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to saffron.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
