Sorghum castaneum

Sorghum castaneum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking crimson-colored inflorescences. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sorghum castaneum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water resources.
