Sanguisuga caesarea

Sanguisuga caesarea, commonly known as the sky blue violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Sanguisuga caesarea is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
