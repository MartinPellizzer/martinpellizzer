Solanum biflorum var. corynephorum

Solanum biflorum var. corynephorum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This species is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
