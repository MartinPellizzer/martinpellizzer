Sasa borealis var. viridescens

Sasa borealis var. viridescens, commonly known as the northern bamboo or greenish sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a slight bluish or silvery sheen. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, preferring acidic soils. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dense, upright growth and ability to thrive in cool climates.
