Seymeria sinaloana

Seymeria sinaloana, commonly known as the Sinaloan Seymeria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in mild, temperate climates. Seymeria sinaloana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
