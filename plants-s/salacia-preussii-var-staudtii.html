Salacia preussii var. staudtii

Salacia preussii var. staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's salacia, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
