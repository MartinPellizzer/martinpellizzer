Sanvitalia procumbens var. oblongifolia

Sanvitalia procumbens var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican blanket flower, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. The leaves are oblong and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
