Sempervivum × thompsonianum

Sempervivum × thompsonianum, commonly known as the Thompson's stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the European Alps.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with fleshy, often blue or greenish leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
