Sorbus hudsonii

Sorbus hudsonii, commonly known as Hudson's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
