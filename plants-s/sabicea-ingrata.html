Sabicea ingrata

Sabicea ingrata, commonly known as the slender senna, is a woody shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
