Sorghastrum trichopus

Sorghastrum trichopus, commonly known as the rough lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, spikelet clusters. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects.

Its seeds are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
