Senecio ozolotepecanus

Senecio ozolotepecanus, commonly known as the Oztotepec groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio ozolotepecanus is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
