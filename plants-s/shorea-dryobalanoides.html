Shorea dryobalanoides

Shorea dryobalanoides, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Shorea dryobalanoides plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
