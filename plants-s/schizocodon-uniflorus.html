Schizocodon uniflorus

Schizocodon uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower schizocodon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, small flower with distinctive sepals and petals. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Schizocodon uniflorus is known for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
