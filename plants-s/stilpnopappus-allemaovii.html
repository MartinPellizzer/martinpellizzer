Stilpnopappus allemaovii

Stilpnopappus allemaovii, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Stilpnopappus allemaovii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
