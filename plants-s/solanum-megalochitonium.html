Solanum megalochitonium

Solanum megalochitonium, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals and a distinctive corona. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
