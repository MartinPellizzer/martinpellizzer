Strobilanthes curviflora

Strobilanthes curviflora, commonly known as the "Himalayan flame of the forest," is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
