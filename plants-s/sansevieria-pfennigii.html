Sansevieria pfennigii

Sansevieria pfennigii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a ghostly white or pale green coloration. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in low-light conditions, making it an excellent choice for indoor cultivation. Its ability to purify the air and adapt to various environments has contributed to its popularity as a houseplant.

Sansevieria pfennigii is also notable for its ability to grow in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its resilience and versatility.
