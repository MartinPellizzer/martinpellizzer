Satureja purpurea

Satureja purpurea, commonly known as purple savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, slightly peppery flavor.

Satureja purpurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herb gardens.
