Senecio carnulentus var. latilobus

Senecio carnulentus var. latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have prominent veins. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disc, and it blooms during the warmer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
