Spergularia dillenii var. perennis

Spergularia dillenii var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It has a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate salt, making it a common plant in coastal areas.
