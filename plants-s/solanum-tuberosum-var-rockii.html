Solanum tuberosum var. rockii

Solanum tuberosum var. rockii, commonly known as the Rockii potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes smaller, more elongated tubers compared to typical potato varieties. The plant exhibits robust growth habits, often thriving in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight. Its foliage is typically dark green and broad, with hairy stems that contribute to its distinct appearance.

This variety is valued for its potential in horticultural and agricultural research due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
