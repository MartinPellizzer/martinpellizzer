Samyda nobilis

Samyda nobilis, commonly known as the noble samyda, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
