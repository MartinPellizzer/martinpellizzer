Syzygium merrillii

Syzygium merrillii, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Syzygium merrillii is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
