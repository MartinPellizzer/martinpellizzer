Setaria viridis subsp. pycnocoma

Setaria viridis subsp.

pycnocoma, commonly known as green foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and high biomass make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
