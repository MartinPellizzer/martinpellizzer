Syzygium arenitense

Syzygium arenitense, commonly known as the rock apple or sand apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and edible, yellowish fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Syzygium arenitense is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
