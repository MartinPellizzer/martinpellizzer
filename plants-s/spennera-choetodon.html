Spennera choetodon

Spennera choetodon, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant features a compact, globular shape with a thick, ribbed stem that stores water. Its most striking characteristic is the presence of yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

Spennera choetodon is adapted to thrive in low-water environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
