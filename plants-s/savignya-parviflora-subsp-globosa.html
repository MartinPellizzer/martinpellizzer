Savignya parviflora subsp. globosa

Savignya parviflora subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globular savignya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to its unique morphology, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
