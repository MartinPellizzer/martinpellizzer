Salsola fuliginosa

Salsola fuliginosa, commonly known as black gooseweed, is a halophytic plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, demonstrating high tolerance to salt and drought. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or low-growing, allowing it to spread across harsh landscapes.

Salsola fuliginosa plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
