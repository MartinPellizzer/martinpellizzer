Silene menziesii var. viscosa

Silene menziesii var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
