Sorbus aria subsp. longifolia

Sorbus aria subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries in the autumn.

The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a rough texture and serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
