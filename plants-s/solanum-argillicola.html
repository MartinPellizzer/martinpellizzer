Solanum argillicola

Solanum argillicola, commonly known as the clay solanum, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to regions with clay soils, particularly in parts of South America. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
