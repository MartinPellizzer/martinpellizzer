Silene hirsuta var. tuberculata

Silene hirsuta var. tuberculata, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Silene hirsuta var. tuberculata is a herbaceous perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
