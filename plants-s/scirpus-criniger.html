Scirpus criniger

Scirpus criniger, commonly known as the silky bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces fluffy, white to light brown spikelets that give it a feathery appearance, which is a key botanical feature. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive morphology.

Scirpus criniger thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
