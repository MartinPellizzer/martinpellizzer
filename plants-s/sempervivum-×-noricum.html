Sempervivum × noricum

Sempervivum × noricum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months.

Sempervivum × noricum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
