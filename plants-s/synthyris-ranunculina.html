Synthyris ranunculina

Synthyris ranunculina, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Synthyris ranunculina is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
