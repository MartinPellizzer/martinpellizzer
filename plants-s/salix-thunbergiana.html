Salix thunbergiana

Salix thunbergiana, commonly known as the Thunberg willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
