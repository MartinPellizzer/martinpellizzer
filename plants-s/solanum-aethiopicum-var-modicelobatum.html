Solanum aethiopicum var. modicelobatum

Solanum aethiopicum var. modicelobatum, commonly known as the Ethiopian eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often lobed fruit, which can vary in color from green to purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and typically grows as an annual herb. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is edible and used in various traditional dishes across its native regions.
