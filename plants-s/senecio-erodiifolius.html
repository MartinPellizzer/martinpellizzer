Senecio erodiifolius

Senecio erodiifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
