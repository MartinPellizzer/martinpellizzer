Saxifraga geranioides subsp. eugeranioides

Saxifraga geranioides subsp.

eugeranioides, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
