Sclerolaena biflora var. cephalocarpa

Sclerolaena biflora var. cephalocarpa, commonly known as the Cape rose or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
