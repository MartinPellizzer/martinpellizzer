Shuteria vestita var. glabrata

Shuteria vestita var. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an invasive species in many areas.
