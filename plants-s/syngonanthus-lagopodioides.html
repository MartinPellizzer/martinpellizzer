Syngonanthus lagopodioides

Syngonanthus lagopodioides, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule-like fruit that disperses its seeds by wind.

Syngonanthus lagopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
