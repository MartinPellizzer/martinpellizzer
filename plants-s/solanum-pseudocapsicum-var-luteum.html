Solanum pseudocapsicum var. luteum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible when ripe but contain alkaloids that can be toxic in large quantities. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

The yellow nightshade is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
