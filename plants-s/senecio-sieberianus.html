Senecio sieberianus

Senecio sieberianus, commonly known as the Sieber's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
