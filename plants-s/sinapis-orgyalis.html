Sinapis orgyalis

Sinapis orgyalis, commonly known as yellow mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species and cruciferous vegetables. The plant has slender, branching stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are hairy and often covered in a sticky substance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are used in various culinary and industrial applications.
