Scilla ramburii var. intermedia

Scilla ramburii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rambo's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
