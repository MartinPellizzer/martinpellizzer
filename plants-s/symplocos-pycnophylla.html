Symplocos pycnophylla

Symplocos pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos pycnophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
