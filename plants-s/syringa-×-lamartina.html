Syringa × lamartina

Syringa × lamartina, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping viburnum, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from Syringa (lilac) and Viburnum (bush honeysuckle).

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.

Syringa × lamartina is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
