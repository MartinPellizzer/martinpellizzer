Solanum prionopterum

Solanum prionopterum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum prionopterum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
