Satureja subspicata var. scabrifolia

Satureja subspicata var. scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
