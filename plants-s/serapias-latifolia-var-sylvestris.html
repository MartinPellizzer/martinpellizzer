Serapias latifolia var. sylvestris

Serapias latifolia var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, hooded structure. It is known for its resilience in harsh, alpine conditions and is often found in open, dry habitats.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
