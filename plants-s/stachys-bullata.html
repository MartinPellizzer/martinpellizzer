Stachys bullata

Stachys bullata, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Stachys bullata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
