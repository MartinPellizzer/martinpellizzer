Scilla lilio-hyacinthus

Scilla lilio-hyacinthus, commonly known as the Hyacinth Squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant is characterized by its showy, hyacinth-like blooms that appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are toxic, so caution is advised when handling the plant.
