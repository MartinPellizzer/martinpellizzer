Silene wahlbergella

Silene wahlbergella, commonly known as the Wahlberg's campion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene wahlbergella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking floral display.
