Sonchus lyratus

Sonchus lyratus, commonly known as the lyrated sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.
