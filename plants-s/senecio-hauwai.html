Senecio hauwai

Senecio hauwai, commonly known as the Hauwai groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and slightly succulent, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Senecio hauwai produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
