Schoenus glaucus

Schoenus glaucus, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Schoenus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
