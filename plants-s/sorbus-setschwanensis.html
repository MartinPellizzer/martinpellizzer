Sorbus setschwanensis

Sorbus setschwanensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
