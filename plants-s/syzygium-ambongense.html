Syzygium ambongense

Syzygium ambongense, commonly known as the Ambongense apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
