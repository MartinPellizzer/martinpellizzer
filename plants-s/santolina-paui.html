Santolina paui

Santolina paui, commonly known as the narrowleaf santolina, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina paui is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
