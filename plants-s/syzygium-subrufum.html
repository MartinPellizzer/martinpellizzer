Syzygium subrufum

Syzygium subrufum, commonly known as the red-fruited clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its fruit, which is typically red when ripe, contains a single seed and is used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium subrufum thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
