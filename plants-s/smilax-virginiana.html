Smilax virginiana

Smilax virginiana, commonly known as catbrier, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Smilax virginiana is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
