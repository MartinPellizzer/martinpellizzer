Syzygium vernonioides

Syzygium vernonioides, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Syzygium vernonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
