Stachys elliptica

Stachys elliptica, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
