Sporobolus deschampsioides

Sporobolus deschampsioides, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and sandy fields.

Sporobolus deschampsioides is drought-tolerant and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.
