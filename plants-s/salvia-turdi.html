Salvia turdi

Salvia turdi, commonly known as Turkey sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Salvia turdi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
