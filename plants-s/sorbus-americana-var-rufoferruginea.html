Sorbus americana var. rufoferruginea

Sorbus americana var. rufoferruginea, commonly known as the red-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as pomes, which are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
