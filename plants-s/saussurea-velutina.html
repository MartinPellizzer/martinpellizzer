Saussurea velutina

Saussurea velutina, commonly known as the velvet aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Saussurea velutina is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
