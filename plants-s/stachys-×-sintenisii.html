Stachys × sintenisii

Stachys × sintenisii, commonly known as the hybrid stachys, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Stachys × sintenisii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
