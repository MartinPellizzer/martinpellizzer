Salacca wallichiana

Salacca wallichiana, commonly known as the sugar palm or giant bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, while the trunk is valued for its fibrous material.

Salacca wallichiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
