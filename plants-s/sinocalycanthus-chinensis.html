Sinocalycanthus chinensis

Sinocalycanthus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fragrant calycanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

Sinocalycanthus chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral scent.
