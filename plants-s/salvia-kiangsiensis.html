Salvia kiangsiensis

Salvia kiangsiensis, commonly known as the Kiangsi sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are hairy and robust.

Salvia kiangsiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
