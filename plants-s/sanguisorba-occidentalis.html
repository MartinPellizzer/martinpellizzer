Sanguisorba occidentalis

Sanguisorba occidentalis, commonly known as western sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Sanguisorba occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
