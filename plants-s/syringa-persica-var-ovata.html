Syringa persica var. ovata

Syringa persica var. ovata, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
