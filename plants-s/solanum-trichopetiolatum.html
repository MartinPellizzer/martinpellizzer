Solanum trichopetiolatum

Solanum trichopetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive petiole structure. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the Solanaceae family.
