Solanum huastecanum

Solanum huastecanum, commonly known as the Huastec nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinct, often aromatic scent.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food but is of interest in botanical and ecological studies.
