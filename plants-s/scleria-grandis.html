Scleria grandis

Scleria grandis, commonly known as the giant bulbous sedge, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, broad leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. The plant produces striking purple or pink flower spikes during the summer months. Scleria grandis is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
