Salvia boosiana

Salvia boosiana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia boosiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
