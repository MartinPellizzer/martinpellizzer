Sempervivum × chavinii

Sempervivum × chavinii, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum × chavinii is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
