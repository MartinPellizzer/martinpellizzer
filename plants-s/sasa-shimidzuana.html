Sasa shimidzuana

Sasa shimidzuana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa shimidzuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
