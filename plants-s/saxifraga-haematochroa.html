Saxifraga haematochroa

Saxifraga haematochroa, commonly known as the blood-red saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and barren environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat succulent, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Saxifraga haematochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
