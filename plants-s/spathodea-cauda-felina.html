Spathodea cauda-felina

Spathodea cauda-felina, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters in height.
