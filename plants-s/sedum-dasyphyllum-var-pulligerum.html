Sedum dasyphyllum var. pulligerum

Sedum dasyphyllum var. pulligerum, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green to purple in color, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it both visually appealing and drought-tolerant.
