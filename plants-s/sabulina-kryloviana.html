Sabulina kryloviana

Sabulina kryloviana, commonly known as the Krylov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
