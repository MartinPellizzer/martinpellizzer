Sarcostemma glaziovii

Sarcostemma glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicles that release seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
