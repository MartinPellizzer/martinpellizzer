Silene oligantha subsp. pseudoradicosa

Silene oligantha subsp.

pseudoradicosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry prairies.
