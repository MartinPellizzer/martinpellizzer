Senecio isatidoides

Senecio isatidoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio isatidoides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
