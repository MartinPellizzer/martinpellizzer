Satureja fasciculata

Satureja fasciculata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Satureja fasciculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
