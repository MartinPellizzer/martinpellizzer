Symphoricarpos albus var. ovatus

Symphoricarpos albus var. ovatus, commonly known as the white snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, berry-like fruits that are clustered in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance for wildlife.
