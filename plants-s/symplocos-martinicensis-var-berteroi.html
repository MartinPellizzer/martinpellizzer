Symplocos martinicensis var. berteroi

Symplocos martinicensis var. berteroi, commonly known as the Martinique beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

The berries are an important food source for various bird species and are also used in traditional medicine.
