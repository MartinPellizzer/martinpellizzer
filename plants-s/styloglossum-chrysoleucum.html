Styloglossum chrysoleucum

Styloglossum chrysoleucum, commonly known as the golden white orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure with a prominent, showy labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its flowers feature a combination of yellow and white hues, giving it its common name.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected or conservation areas.
