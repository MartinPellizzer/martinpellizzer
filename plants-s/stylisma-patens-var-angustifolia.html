Stylisma patens var. angustifolia

Stylisma patens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf stylisma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
