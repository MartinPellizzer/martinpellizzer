Senecio pubescens

Senecio pubescens, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
