Silene minutifolia

Silene minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Silene minutifolia is often found in open habitats and is considered a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
