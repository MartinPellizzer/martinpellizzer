Schoenus latifolius

Schoenus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Schoenus latifolius plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
