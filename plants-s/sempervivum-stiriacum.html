Sempervivum stiriacum

Sempervivum stiriacum, commonly known as the Carpathian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold and arid conditions.
