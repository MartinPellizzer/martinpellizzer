Stipa philippii

Stipa philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, thread-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically adorned with pale to golden-colored florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
