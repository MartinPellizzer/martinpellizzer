Strychnodaphne suaveolens

Strychnodaphne suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented daphne, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, pleasant aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its botanical features include simple, opposite leaves and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
