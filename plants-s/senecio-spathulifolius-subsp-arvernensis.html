Senecio spathulifolius subsp. arvernensis

Senecio spathulifolius subsp.

arvernensis, commonly known as the Arverne ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Senecio species.
