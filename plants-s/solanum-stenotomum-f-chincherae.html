Solanum stenotomum f. chincherae

Solanum stenotomum f. chincherae, commonly known as the Chinchera nightshade, is a rare and unique botanical variant native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
