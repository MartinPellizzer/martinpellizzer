Syringa villosa var. aureovariegata

Syringa villosa var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the variegated weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
