Syngonanthus llanorum

Syngonanthus llanorum, commonly known as the Llanos grass or Llanos grassland plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming part of the vegetation in floodplain ecosystems. Syngonanthus llanorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.
