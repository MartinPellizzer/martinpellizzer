Sciodaphyllum attenuatum

Sciodaphyllum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender shade fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the understory of shaded forests in tropical regions.

It features long, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, with a finely divided, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in high humidity and low light conditions, making it well adapted to forest floor environments. Its reproductive structures are hidden within the foliage, contributing to its elusive nature.

Due to habitat loss, Sciodaphyllum attenuatum is considered endangered and is often difficult to locate in the wild.
