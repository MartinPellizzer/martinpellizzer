Salvia turcica

Salvia turcica, commonly known as Turkish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent.

Salvia turcica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
