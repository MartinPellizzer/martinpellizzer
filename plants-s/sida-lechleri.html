Sida lechleri

Sida lechleri, commonly known as Copaiba, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its latex-rich sap, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its botanical classification places it in the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The plant's unique chemical composition contributes to its significance in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
