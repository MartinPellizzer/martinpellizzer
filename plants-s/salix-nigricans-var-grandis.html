Salix nigricans var. grandis

Salix nigricans var. grandis, commonly known as the large willow or black willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination. Its bark is typically grayish-black and smooth, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It thrives in moist soils near streams and wetlands, making it a common species in riparian habitats.
