Silene villosula

Silene villosula, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Silene villosula is characterized by its distinctive seed capsules that split open to release its seeds.
