Solanum sarasarae

Solanum sarasarae, commonly known as the Sarasara nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers and has oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is typically found in cloud forests at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
