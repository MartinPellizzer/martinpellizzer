Sorbus subscandica

Sorbus subscandica, commonly known as the Scandinavian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

The wood of Sorbus subscandica is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional crafts and construction.
