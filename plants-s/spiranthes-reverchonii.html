Spiranthes reverchonii

Spiranthes reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's spiral orchid, is a delicate perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of flowers along a single, slender stem. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and they bloom from late summer to early fall.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
