Sabicea cinerea

Sabicea cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
