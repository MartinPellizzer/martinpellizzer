Saponaria amplissima

Saponaria amplissima, commonly known as the giant soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Saponaria amplissima is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
