Salix monandra var. lambertiana

Salix monandra var. lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a silvery-green underside that adds to its visual appeal. This variety thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds.

It is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is commonly planted in gardens and along waterways for its beauty and ecological benefits.
