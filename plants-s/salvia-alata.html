Salvia alata

Salvia alata, commonly known as the common beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
