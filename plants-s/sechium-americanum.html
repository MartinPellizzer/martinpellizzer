Sechium americanum

Sechium americanum, commonly known as the chayote, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and squash. The plant produces a pear-shaped fruit with a green, waxy skin and a white, starchy flesh. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The chayote is valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins C and B-complex, and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal uses.
