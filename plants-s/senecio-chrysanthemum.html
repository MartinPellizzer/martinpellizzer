Senecio chrysanthemum

Senecio chrysanthemum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which give it a striking appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, daisy-like blooms throughout the growing season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
