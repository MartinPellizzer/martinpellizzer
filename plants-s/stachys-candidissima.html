Stachys candidissima

Stachys candidissima, commonly known as white betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
