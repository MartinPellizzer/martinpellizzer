Schefflera bonita

Schefflera bonita, commonly known as the Hawaiian schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Hawaii.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leafy shoots, giving it a lush, full appearance. The plant has compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern, with each leaflet being oval and slightly pointed. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

Schefflera bonita thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
