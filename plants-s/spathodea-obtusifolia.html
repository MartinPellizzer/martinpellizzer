Spathodea obtusifolia

Spathodea obtusifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, creating a striking visual display. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Spathodea obtusifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in floral arrangements.
