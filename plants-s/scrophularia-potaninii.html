Scrophularia potaninii

Scrophularia potaninii, commonly known as the Tibetan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Scrophularia potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
