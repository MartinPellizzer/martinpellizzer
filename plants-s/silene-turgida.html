Silene turgida

Silene turgida, commonly known as the bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling habit with hairy stems. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Silene turgida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
