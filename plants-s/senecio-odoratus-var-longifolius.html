Senecio odoratus var. longifolius

Senecio odoratus var. longifolius, commonly known as the sweet cicely or sweetroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mild, sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in culinary and medicinal applications highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
