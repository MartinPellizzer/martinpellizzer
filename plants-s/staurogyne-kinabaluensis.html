Staurogyne kinabaluensis

Staurogyne kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Bornean water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months.

Staurogyne kinabaluensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
