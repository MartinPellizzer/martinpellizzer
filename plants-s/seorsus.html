Seorsus

Seorsus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that often resemble thistles. The plants typically grow in coastal areas, thriving in salty and well-drained soils. Seorsus species are known for their hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
