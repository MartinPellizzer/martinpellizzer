Statice ovalifolia var. pedicularis

Statice ovalifolia var. pedicularis, commonly known as sea lavender or pink hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant wax. This variety is particularly adapted to salty, well-drained soils and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought resistance and attractive floral display.
