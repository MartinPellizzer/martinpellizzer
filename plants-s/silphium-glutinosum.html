Silphium glutinosum

Silphium glutinosum, commonly known as sticky silphium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Silphium glutinosum thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
