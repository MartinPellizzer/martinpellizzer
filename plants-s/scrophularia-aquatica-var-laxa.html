Scrophularia aquatica var. laxa

Scrophularia aquatica var. laxa, commonly known as water figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in moist soils and is often found along the edges of ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
