Senna obtusifolia

Senna obtusifolia, commonly known as sickle senna, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with rounded or ovate leaflets. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces elongated, ribbed pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Senna obtusifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
