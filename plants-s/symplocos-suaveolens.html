Symplocos suaveolens

Symplocos suaveolens, commonly known as sweetleaf or fragrant waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, ovate leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
