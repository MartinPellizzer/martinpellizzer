Sondera collina

Sondera collina, commonly known as the hillsondera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
