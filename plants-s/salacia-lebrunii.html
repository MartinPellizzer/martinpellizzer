Salacia lebrunii

Salacia lebrunii, commonly known as the lebrun's salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaciacae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, with small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of primary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
