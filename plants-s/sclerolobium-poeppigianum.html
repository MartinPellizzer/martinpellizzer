Sclerolobium poeppigianum

Sclerolobium poeppigianum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, slender pods that contain seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

The wood of Sclerolobium poeppigianum is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.
