Scirpus flaccidifolius

Scirpus flaccidifolius, commonly known as the soft-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and broad, floppy leaves that often droop under the weight of water. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered brown flowers.

Scirpus flaccidifolius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and helping to stabilize soil.
