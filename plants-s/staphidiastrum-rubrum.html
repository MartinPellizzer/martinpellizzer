Staphidiastrum rubrum

Staphidiastrum rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed staphidiastrum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
