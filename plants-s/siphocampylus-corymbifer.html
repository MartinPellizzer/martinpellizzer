Siphocampylus corymbifer

Siphocampylus corymbifer, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Siphocampylus corymbifer is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
