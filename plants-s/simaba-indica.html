Simaba indica

Simaba indica, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble cherries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
