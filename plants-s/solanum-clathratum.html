Solanum clathratum

Solanum clathratum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Solanum clathratum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
