Scrophularia scopolii

Scrophularia scopolii, commonly known as the lesser scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Scrophularia scopolii is also a valuable plant for pollinators, attracting bees and other insects.
