Stellaria holostea subsp. hispidula

Stellaria holostea subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as hairy starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, white star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "hispidula.". It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
