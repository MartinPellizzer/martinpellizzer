Strobilanthes sarcorrhiza

Strobilanthes sarcorrhiza, commonly known as the "bloodroot" or "red root," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Strobilanthes sarcorrhiza is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
