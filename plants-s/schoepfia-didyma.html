Schoepfia didyma

Schoepfia didyma, commonly known as the Mexican beebalm, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Schoepfia didyma is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.
