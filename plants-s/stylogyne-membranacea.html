Stylogyne membranacea

Stylogyne membranacea, commonly known as the paper-leaf stylogyne, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, often hairy stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, papery fruits. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a delicate, membranous texture.

Stylogyne membranacea is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests or along stream banks.
