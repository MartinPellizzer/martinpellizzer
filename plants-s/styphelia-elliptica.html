Styphelia elliptica

Styphelia elliptica, commonly known as the slender leatherleaf, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and typically have a dark green color. The plant produces tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Styphelia elliptica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
