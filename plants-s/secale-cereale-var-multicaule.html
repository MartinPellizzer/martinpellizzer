Secale cereale var. multicaule

Secale cereale var. multicaule, commonly known as the multi-stemmed rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its multiple stems arising from a single base.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. The species is notable for its hardiness and ability to grow in marginal agricultural conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage crop and a potential candidate for sustainable agriculture.
