Sideritis scordioides var. vestita

Sideritis scordioides var. vestita, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
