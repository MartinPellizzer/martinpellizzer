Silene multiflora var. glabra

Silene multiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Silene multiflora. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
