Solanum echinatum

Solanum echinatum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into toxic, red berries.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and requires caution when handling.
