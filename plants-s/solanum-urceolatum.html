Solanum urceolatum

Solanum urceolatum, commonly known as the bellflower nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or violet in color. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it both an interesting subject for study and a plant to be handled with care.
