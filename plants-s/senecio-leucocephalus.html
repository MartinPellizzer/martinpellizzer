Senecio leucocephalus

Senecio leucocephalus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, resembling a dandelion. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Senecio leucocephalus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
