Senecio kermesinus

Senecio kermesinus, commonly known as the Kermesin Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in dense, woolly foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow, forming small clusters at the ends of the stems.

Senecio kermesinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
