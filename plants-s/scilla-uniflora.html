Scilla uniflora

Scilla uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a single, bell-shaped flower that is typically blue or violet in color, which blooms in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla uniflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
