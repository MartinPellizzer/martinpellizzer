Sphenosciadium capitellatum var. eryngiifolium

Sphenosciadium capitellatum var. eryngiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sphenosciadium, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
