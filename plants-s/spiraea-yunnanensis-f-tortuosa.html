Spiraea yunnanensis f. tortuosa

Spiraea yunnanensis f. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted form of the Yunnan spiraea, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems that give it a unique ornamental appearance. The plant features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its visual appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
