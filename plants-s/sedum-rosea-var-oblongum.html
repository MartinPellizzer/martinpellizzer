Sedum rosea var. oblongum

Sedum rosea var. oblongum, commonly known as the oblongleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
