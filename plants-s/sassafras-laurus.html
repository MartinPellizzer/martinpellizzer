Sassafras laurus

Sassafras laurus, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Sweet woodruff is known for its aromatic properties, particularly its distinctive sweet, vanilla-like scent.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
