Saxifraga × kruskopfii

Saxifraga × kruskopfii, commonly known as the Krukskopf saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga cespitosa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, typically reaching heights of 5–10 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a distinctive umbel-like structure. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates with full sun exposure.

Saxifraga × kruskopfii is often found in the Arctic and subarctic regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
