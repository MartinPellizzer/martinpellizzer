Senecio usgorensis

Senecio usgorensis, commonly known as the Usambara senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and has small, narrow leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
