Senecio kanzibiensis

Senecio kanzibiensis, commonly known as the Kanzibiense groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are generally linear to lance-shaped, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
