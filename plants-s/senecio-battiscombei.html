Senecio battiscombei

Senecio battiscombei, commonly known as the Blueweed or the Bluebell of the Karoo, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water requirements.

Senecio battiscombei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
