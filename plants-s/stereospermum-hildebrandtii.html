Stereospermum hildebrandtii

Stereospermum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic and fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
