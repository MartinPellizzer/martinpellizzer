Strobilanthes rhytiphylla

Strobilanthes rhytiphylla, commonly known as the "Ceylon flame flower," is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its seasonal blooming, typically during the dry season. Its unique floral structure and vivid coloration make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
