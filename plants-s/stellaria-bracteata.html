Stellaria bracteata

Stellaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracted starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Stellaria bracteata is often found growing in clusters and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
