Syringa persica var. salviifolia

Syringa persica var. salviifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
