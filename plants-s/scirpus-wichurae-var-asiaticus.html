Scirpus wichurae var. asiaticus

Scirpus wichurae var. asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.
