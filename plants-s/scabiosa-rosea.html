Scabiosa rosea

Scabiosa rosea, commonly known as the rose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small roses. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, with a fuzzy texture.

Scabiosa rosea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.
