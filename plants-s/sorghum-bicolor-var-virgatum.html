Sorghum bicolor var. virgatum

Sorghum bicolor var. virgatum, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal lands. It is widely cultivated for its use in biofuel production and as a forage crop.

Additionally, it plays an important role in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
