Silene burchellii subsp. pilosellifolia

Silene burchellii subsp.

pilosellifolia, commonly known as the silky sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, upright stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that mature in summer, dispersing seeds by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
