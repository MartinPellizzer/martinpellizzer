Salvia rosei

Salvia rosei, commonly known as the rose sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage and produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a subtle mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia rosei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
