Sasa kashidensis var. katoana

Sasa kashidensis var. katoana, commonly known as the Katoana bamboo, is a deciduous grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-white underside.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
