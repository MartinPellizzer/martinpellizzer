Salsola mirabilis

Salsola mirabilis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prickly, leafless stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments due to its deep root system and ability to store water. It produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly.

Salsola mirabilis is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed habitats.
