Sarracenia intermedia

Sarracenia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate whorled sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and whorled arrangement of leaves that form a rosette. The plant's modified leaves, called pitchers, are specialized for trapping and digesting insects. These pitchers are typically red or reddish in color, with a glossy surface that attracts prey.

Sarracenia intermedia thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.
