Smallanthus cocuyensis

Smallanthus cocuyensis, commonly known as Cocuy grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
