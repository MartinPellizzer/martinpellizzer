Solanum anguivi

Solanum anguivi, commonly known as the devil's fig, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum anguivi is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
