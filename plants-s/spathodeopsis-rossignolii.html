Spathodeopsis rossignolii

Spathodeopsis rossignolii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a distinctive spathe and a spadix, with colors ranging from white to pink and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the old ones. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Spathodeopsis rossignolii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific climatic requirements.
