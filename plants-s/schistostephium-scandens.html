Schistostephium scandens

Schistostephium scandens, commonly known as the climbing false dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant features heart-shaped, alternate leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

Schistostephium scandens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
