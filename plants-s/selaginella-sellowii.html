Selaginella sellowii

Selaginella sellowii, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Selaginella sellowii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for periodic drought.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
