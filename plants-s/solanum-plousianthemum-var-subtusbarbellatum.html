Solanum plousianthemum var. subtusbarbellatum

Solanum plousianthemum var. subtusbarbellatum, commonly known as the barbellated nightshade, is a rare and unique variant of the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its striking, barbell-like patterns on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
