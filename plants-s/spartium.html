Spartium

Spartium, commonly known as needlegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Spartium junceum, which is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. Spartium produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
