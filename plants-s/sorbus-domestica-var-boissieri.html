Sorbus domestica var. boissieri

Sorbus domestica var. boissieri, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
