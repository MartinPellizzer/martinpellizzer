Setaria caudula

Setaria caudula, commonly known as curly bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, bristly florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Setaria caudula is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
