Shorea singkawang

Shorea singkawang, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, dark brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
