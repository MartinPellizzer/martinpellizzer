Scirpus macer

Scirpus macer, commonly known as the purple bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and pond edges. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually purple-tinged, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus macer plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
