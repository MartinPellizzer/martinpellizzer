Strumaria filifolia

Strumaria filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Strumaria filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
