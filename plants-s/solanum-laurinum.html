Solanum laurinum

Solanum laurinum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in cooking. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is also appreciated due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
