Sedum trullipetalum var. ciliatum

Sedum trullipetalum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the ciliate stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its unique leaf texture and floral display make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
