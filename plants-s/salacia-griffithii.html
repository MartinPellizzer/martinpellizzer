Salacia griffithii

Salacia griffithii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Salacia griffithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
