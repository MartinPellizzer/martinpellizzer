Sciodaphyllum assamicum

Sciodaphyllum assamicum, commonly known as the Assam mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the forests of northeastern India.

It possesses a specialized root system that attaches to the host tree, allowing it to absorb water and some nutrients while also drawing photosynthetic products from the host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a fleshy, reddish berry that contains several small seeds.

Sciodaphyllum assamicum plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by influencing the growth and diversity of tree species.
