Saxifraga × columpoda

Saxifraga × columpoda, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
