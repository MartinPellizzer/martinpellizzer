Silene italica subsp. longicilia

Silene italica subsp.

longicilia, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces striking pink or red flowers with prominent stamens, which are a key feature in its pollination strategy. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, hairy filaments that give it its specific epithet "longicilia.".
