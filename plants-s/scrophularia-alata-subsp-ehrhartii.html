Scrophularia alata subsp. ehrhartii

Scrophularia alata subsp.

ehrhartii, commonly known as the Ehrhart's horse gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
