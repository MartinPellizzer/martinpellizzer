Stipa californica

Stipa californica, commonly known as California needlegrass, is a native grass species of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and dense, cylindrical seed heads that resemble small pine cones. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive with minimal water.

Stipa californica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
