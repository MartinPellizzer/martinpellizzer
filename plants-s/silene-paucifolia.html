Silene paucifolia

Silene paucifolia, commonly known as the few-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish in color, and bloom in early summer.

Silene paucifolia is characterized by its reduced number of flowers and its ability to survive in harsh, arid conditions.
