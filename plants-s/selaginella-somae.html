Selaginella somae

Selaginella somae, commonly known as the Somalian spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella somae is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Somalia and Ethiopia.
