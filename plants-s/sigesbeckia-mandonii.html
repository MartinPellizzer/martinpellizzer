Sigesbeckia mandonii

Sigesbeckia mandonii, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
