Saussurea ambigua

Saussurea ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Saussurea ambigua is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
