Symplocos glomerata var. glomeratiflora

Symplocos glomerata var. glomeratiflora, commonly known as the clustered waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
