Saxifraga kansuensis

Saxifraga kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Saxifraga kansuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
