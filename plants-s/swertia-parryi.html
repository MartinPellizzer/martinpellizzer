Swertia parryi

Swertia parryi, commonly known as Parry's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures and ample sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

Swertia parryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
