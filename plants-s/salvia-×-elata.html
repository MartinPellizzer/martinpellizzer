Salvia × elata

Salvia × elata, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal remedies.
