Stellaria graminea lusus brevifolia

Stellaria graminea lusus brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carnations and sweet peas.
