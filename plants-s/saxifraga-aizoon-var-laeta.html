Saxifraga aizoon var. laeta

Saxifraga aizoon var. laeta, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Saxifraga aizoon var. laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
