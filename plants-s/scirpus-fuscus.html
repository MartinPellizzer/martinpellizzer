Scirpus fuscus

Scirpus fuscus, commonly known as black bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its rhizomes contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
