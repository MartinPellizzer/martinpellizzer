Stanhopea madouxiana

Stanhopea madouxiana, commonly known as the Madoux's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as purple and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

Stanhopea madouxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
