Sida capituliflora

Sida capituliflora, commonly known as the clustered sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Sida capituliflora is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
