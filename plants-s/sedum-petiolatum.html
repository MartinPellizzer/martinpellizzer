Sedum petiolatum

Sedum petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of Asia and the Americas.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Sedum petiolatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
