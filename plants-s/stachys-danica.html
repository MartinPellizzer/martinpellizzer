Stachys danica

Stachys danica, commonly known as woodland betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, hardy plants.
