Stemodia camphorata

Stemodia camphorata, commonly known as camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
