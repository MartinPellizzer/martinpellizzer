Stipa kazachstanica

Stipa kazachstanica, commonly known as the Kazakh feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Stipa kazachstanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
