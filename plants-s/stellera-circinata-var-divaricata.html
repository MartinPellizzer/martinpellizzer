Stellera circinata var. divaricata

Stellera circinata var. divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starwort, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and have a distinctive wavy or curled margin. Its flowers are small, white, and star-shaped, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
