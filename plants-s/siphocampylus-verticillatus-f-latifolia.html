Siphocampylus verticillatus f. latifolia

Siphocampylus verticillatus f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved siphocampylus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are broad and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
