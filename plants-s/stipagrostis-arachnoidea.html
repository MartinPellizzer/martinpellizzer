Stipagrostis arachnoidea

Stipagrostis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spidergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

Its unique appearance, resembling a spider's web, gives it its common name.
