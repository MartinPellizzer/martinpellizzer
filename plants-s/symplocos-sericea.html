Symplocos sericea

Symplocos sericea, commonly known as silverleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Symplocos sericea is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
