Saussurea nipponica

Saussurea nipponica, commonly known as Japanese snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
