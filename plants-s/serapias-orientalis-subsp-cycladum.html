Serapias orientalis subsp. cycladum

Serapias orientalis subsp.

cycladum, commonly known as the Cycladic serapis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral morphology and adaptability to Mediterranean climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
