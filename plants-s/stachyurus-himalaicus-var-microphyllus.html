Stachyurus himalaicus var. microphyllus

Stachyurus himalaicus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the Himalayan weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often turning yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and graceful form, which add a touch of elegance to shaded landscapes.
