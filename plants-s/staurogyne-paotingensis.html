Staurogyne paotingensis

Staurogyne paotingensis, commonly known as the Paoting's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

Staurogyne paotingensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
