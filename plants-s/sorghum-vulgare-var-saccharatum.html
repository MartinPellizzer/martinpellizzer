Sorghum vulgare var. saccharatum

Sorghum vulgare var. saccharatum, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust stalks and large, purple-tinged leaves. The plant is cultivated for its high sugar content in the stalks, which can be processed into syrup or ethanol. It thrives in dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid regions.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle-like inflorescence.
