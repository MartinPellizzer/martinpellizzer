Styrax benzoin

Styrax benzoin, commonly known as Indian frankincense, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces fragrant, resinous exudates from its bark and leaves, which are harvested for their aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The resin, known as benzoin, is used in perfumery, incense, and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
