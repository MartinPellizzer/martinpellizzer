Spermacoce tenuiflora

Spermacoce tenuiflora, commonly known as slenderflower morning glory, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that dehisce to release small, smooth seeds.

Spermacoce tenuiflora is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
