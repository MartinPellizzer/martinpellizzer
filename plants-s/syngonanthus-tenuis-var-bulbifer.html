Syngonanthus tenuis var. bulbifer

Syngonanthus tenuis var. bulbifer, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils, making it an important component of marsh and swamp ecosystems. Its bulbiferous nature refers to the presence of bulb-like structures that can store nutrients and aid in propagation.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
