Sasa scytophylla

Sasa scytophylla, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a clumping perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Sasa scytophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese garden design.
