Spilanthes oleracea

Spilanthes oleracea, commonly known as the pepper plant or false mustards, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers and pungent, peppery taste. The plant is often grown for its edible leaves and flowers, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

The plant's unique flavor profile makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible gardening.
