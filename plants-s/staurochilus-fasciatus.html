Staurochilus fasciatus

Staurochilus fasciatus, commonly known as the streaked rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with a distinctive white or light green stripe down the center. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Staurochilus fasciatus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
