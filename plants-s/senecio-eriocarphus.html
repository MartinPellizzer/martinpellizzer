Senecio eriocarphus

Senecio eriocarphus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
