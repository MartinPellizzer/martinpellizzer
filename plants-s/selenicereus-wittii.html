Selenicereus wittii

Selenicereus wittii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its stems are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Selenicereus wittii thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
