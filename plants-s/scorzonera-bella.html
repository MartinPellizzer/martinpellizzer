Scorzonera bella

Scorzonera bella, commonly known as the beautiful scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are often hairy and have a rosette growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system includes a fleshy, edible tuberous root that is sometimes cultivated for its flavor.

Scorzonera bella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and culinary uses.
