Sapindus mukorossi var. carinatus

Sapindus mukorossi var. carinatus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The fruit's outer layer contains a bitter, soapy substance used traditionally for cleaning purposes.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
