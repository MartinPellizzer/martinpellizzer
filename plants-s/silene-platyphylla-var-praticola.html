Silene platyphylla var. praticola

Silene platyphylla var. praticola, commonly known as the plains silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
