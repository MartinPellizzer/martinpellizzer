Stelis vinosa var. longifolia

Stelis vinosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces linear, often hairy leaves and bears racemose inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
