Solanum pichinchense

Solanum pichinchense, commonly known as the Pichincha nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
