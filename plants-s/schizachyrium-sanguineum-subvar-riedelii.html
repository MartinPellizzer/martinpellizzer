Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. riedelii

Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. riedelii, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red-tipped foliage, which becomes more vivid in the fall. The plant produces slender, open panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for native landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a desirable plant for ecological and aesthetic purposes.
