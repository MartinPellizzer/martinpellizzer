Sium repens var. ochreatum

Sium repens var. ochreatum, commonly known as the yellow waterparsley, is a variant of the waterparsley species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
