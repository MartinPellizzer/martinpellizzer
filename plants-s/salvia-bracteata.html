Salvia bracteata

Salvia bracteata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Salvia bracteata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soils.
