Senecio divaricoides

Senecio divaricoides, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a branched stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio divaricoides is adapted to arid environments and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.
