Scrophularia rimarum var. pubescens

Scrophularia rimarum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
