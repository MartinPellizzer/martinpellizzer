Senecio brunonis

Senecio brunonis, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio brunonis is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
