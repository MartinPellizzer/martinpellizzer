Scirpus cunninghamii

Scirpus cunninghamii, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial wetland plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil along rivers, lakes, and swamps. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Scirpus cunninghamii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
