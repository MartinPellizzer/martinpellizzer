Salvia ottoschulzii

Salvia ottoschulzii, commonly known as Otto's sage, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
