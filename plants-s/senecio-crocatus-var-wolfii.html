Senecio crocatus var. wolfii

Senecio crocatus var. wolfii, commonly known as the Woolly Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
