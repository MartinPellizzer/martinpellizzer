Synaedrys bancana

Synaedrys bancana, commonly known as the banana leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, banana-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its adaptability to humid environments.

Its leaves are not only aesthetically pleasing but also play a crucial role in photosynthesis and water retention.
