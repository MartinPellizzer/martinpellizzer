Symplocos sumuntia

Symplocos sumuntia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and contain several seeds. Symplocos sumuntia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
