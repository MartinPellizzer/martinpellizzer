Solidago guiradonis var. spectabilis

Solidago guiradonis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its lance-shaped leaves are alternate and often have a rough texture, while its tall, branching stems support the showy flower heads.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
