Silybum eburneum var. hispanicum

Silybum eburneum var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish milk thistle, is a variant of the milk thistle species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains compounds like silymarin, which is known for its potential hepatoprotective properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal value, it is cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
