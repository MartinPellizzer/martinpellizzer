Setaria aurea

Setaria aurea, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stature and dense, golden-yellow inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

Setaria aurea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
