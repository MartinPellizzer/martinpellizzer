Saxifraga occidentalis var. wallowensis

Saxifraga occidentalis var. wallowensis, commonly known as the Wallowa saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and usually range in color from white to pink.

It is well adapted to cool, humid environments and is often found in high-elevation regions.
