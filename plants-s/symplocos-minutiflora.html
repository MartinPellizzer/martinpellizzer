Symplocos minutiflora

Symplocos minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Symplocos minutiflora is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
