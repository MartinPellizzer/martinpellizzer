Silene coniflora

Silene coniflora, commonly known as the coniferous catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene coniflora typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

It is a perennial herb that is often found in alpine and arctic regions.
