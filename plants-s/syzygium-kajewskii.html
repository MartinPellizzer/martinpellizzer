Syzygium kajewskii

Syzygium kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

Syzygium kajewskii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
