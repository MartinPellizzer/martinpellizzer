Silphium albiflorum

Silphium albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered silphium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Silphium albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
