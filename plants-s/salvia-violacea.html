Salvia violacea

Salvia violacea, commonly known as the violet beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces striking violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a minty scent.

Salvia violacea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
