Suaeda kochii

Suaeda kochii, commonly known as coastal arrowgrass, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in sandy or muddy soils. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Suaeda kochii plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
