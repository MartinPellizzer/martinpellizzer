Saxifraga madida f. incisa

Saxifraga madida f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its small, white flowers typically appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
