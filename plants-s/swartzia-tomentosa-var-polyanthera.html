Swartzia tomentosa var. polyanthera

Swartzia tomentosa var. polyanthera, commonly known as the velvetwood or polyanthera swartzia, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in woodworking.
