Solidago spiraeifolia subsp. pacifica

Solidago spiraeifolia subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as Pacific goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
