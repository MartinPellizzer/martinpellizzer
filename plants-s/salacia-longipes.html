Salacia longipes

Salacia longipes, commonly known as the longipes salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough texture.

Salacia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
