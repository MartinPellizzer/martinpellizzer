Sedum telephium proles fabaria

Sedum telephium proles fabaria, commonly known as the common stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers.

This hardy perennial is popular in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
