Scirpus monocephalus

Scirpus monocephalus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stem, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and filter water.

Scirpus monocephalus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
