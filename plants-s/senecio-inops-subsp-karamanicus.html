Senecio inops subsp. karamanicus

Senecio inops subsp.

karamanicus, commonly known as the Karaman's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
