Spiraea compsophylla

Spiraea compsophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
