Scutellaria caroliniana

Scutellaria caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Carolina blueflag thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
