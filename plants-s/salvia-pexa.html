Salvia pexa

Salvia pexa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces spikes of purple or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in arid landscapes.
