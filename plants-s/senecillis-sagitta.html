Senecillis sagitta

Senecillis sagitta, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical name reflects its arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.
