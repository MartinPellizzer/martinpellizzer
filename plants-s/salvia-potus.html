Salvia potus

Salvia potus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet or purple flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Salvia potus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
