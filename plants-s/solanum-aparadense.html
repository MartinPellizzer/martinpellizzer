Solanum aparadense

Solanum aparadense, commonly known as the Aparadense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are often consumed by local wildlife.

Solanum aparadense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and edible plants.
