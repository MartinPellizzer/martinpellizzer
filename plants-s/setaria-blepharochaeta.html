Setaria blepharochaeta

Setaria blepharochaeta, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, hairy inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Setaria blepharochaeta is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
