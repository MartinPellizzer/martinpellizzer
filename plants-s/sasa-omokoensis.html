Sasa omokoensis

Sasa omokoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Sasa omokoensis thrives in shady, moist environments, often found in forested areas across its native range.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form thickets, making it an important component of its ecological habitat.
