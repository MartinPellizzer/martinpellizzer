Sindora siamensis var. siamensis

Sindora siamensis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears edible, pod-like fruits that contain sweet, sticky pulp and seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

Its botanical significance includes its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
