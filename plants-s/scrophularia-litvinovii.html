Scrophularia litvinovii

Scrophularia litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy, with lanceolate shapes. Its flowers are clustered in whorls, producing showy, tubular blossoms in shades of purple and pink.

Scrophularia litvinovii is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some species.
