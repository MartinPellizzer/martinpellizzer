Spiranthes cuspidata

Spiranthes cuspidata, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white to pale pink flowers along a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late summer to early autumn.
