Sorbus domestica f. maliformis

Sorbus domestica f. maliformis, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale yellow berries. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, white flowers in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
