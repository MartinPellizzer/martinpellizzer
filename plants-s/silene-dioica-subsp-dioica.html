Silene dioica subsp. dioica

Silene dioica subsp.

dioica, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
