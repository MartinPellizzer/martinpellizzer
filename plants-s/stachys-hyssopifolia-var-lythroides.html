Stachys hyssopifolia var. lythroides

Stachys hyssopifolia var. lythroides, commonly known as purple hedgenettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their mild medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
