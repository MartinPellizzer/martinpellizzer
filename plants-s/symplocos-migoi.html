Symplocos migoi

Symplocos migoi, commonly known as the red-fruited evergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Symplocos migoi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant foliage and attractive fruits, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
