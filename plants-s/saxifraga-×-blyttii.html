Saxifraga × blyttii

Saxifraga × blyttii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, characterized by its ability to grow in challenging, nutrient-poor soils.

Saxifraga × blyttii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky habitats.
