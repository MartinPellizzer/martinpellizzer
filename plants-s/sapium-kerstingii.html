Sapium kerstingii

Sapium kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
