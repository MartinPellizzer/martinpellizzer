Solanum callicarpoides

Solanum callicarpoides, commonly known as the red-seeded nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit, which turns red when mature, contains toxic alkaloids and is not suitable for human consumption.

Solanum callicarpoides is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
