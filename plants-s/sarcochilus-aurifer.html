Sarcochilus aurifer

Sarcochilus aurifer, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and a single, linear leaf. The plant produces racemes of yellow-green flowers with prominent golden sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
