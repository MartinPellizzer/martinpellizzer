Scutellaria axilliflora

Scutellaria axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
