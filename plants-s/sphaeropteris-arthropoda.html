Sphaeropteris arthropoda

Sphaeropteris arthropoda, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's growth and helping it to anchor in moist, shaded environments.

Sphaeropteris arthropoda plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
