Sedum shaoakouense

Sedum shaoakouense, commonly known as the Shaoakou stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
