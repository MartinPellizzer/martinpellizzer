Sasa morotonensis

Sasa morotonensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes are often used in traditional practices. Sasa morotonensis is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in local cuisine and traditional crafts, highlighting its cultural and ecological significance.
