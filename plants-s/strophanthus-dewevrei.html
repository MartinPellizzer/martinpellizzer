Strophanthus dewevrei

Strophanthus dewevrei, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
