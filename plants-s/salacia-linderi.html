Salacia linderi

Salacia linderi, commonly known as the Linder's salacia, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Salacia linderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
