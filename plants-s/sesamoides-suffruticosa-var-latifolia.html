Sesamoides suffruticosa var. latifolia

Sesamoides suffruticosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sesamoid, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its wider leaves compared to other varieties of Sesamoides suffruticosa.
