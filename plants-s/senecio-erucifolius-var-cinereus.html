Senecio erucifolius var. cinereus

Senecio erucifolius var. cinereus, commonly known as the cinereous ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, including rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is important to note that while it is not considered highly toxic, some members of the Senecio genus can be harmful to livestock.
