Sorghum × drummondii

Sorghum × drummondii, commonly known as hybrid sorghum, is a cross between sorghum and drummondii, a wild relative native to the United States.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant genetics and adaptation strategies.
