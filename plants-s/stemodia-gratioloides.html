Stemodia gratioloides

Stemodia gratioloides, commonly known as the slender stemodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
