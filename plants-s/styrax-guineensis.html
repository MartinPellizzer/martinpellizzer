Styrax guineensis

Styrax guineensis, commonly known as African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Styrax guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
