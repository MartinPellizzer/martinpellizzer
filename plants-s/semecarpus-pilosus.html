Semecarpus pilosus

Semecarpus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine.
