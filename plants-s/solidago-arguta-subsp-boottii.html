Solidago arguta subsp. boottii

Solidago arguta subsp.

boottii, commonly known as Boott's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant features upright, branched stems covered with fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green in color, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are bright yellow, arranged in panicles, and bloom in late summer to early fall.
