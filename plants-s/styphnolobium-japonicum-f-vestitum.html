Styphnolobium japonicum f. vestitum

Styphnolobium japonicum f. vestitum, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a variant of the pagoda tree species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered, spiny pods that hang in drooping clusters, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.
