Solanum nelsonii var. intermedium

Solanum nelsonii var. intermedium, commonly known as the Nelson's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ornamental and cautionary value.

It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
