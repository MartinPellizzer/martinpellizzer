Serjania clematidea

Serjania clematidea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Serjania clematidea is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
