Salix purpurea var. amplexicaulis

Salix purpurea var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as purple willow with clasping leaves, is a variant of the purple willow native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that partially encircle the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows near water sources such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its catkins are prominent in early spring, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
