Saxifraga × tiroliensis

Saxifraga × tiroliensis, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant is known for its cold tolerance and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
