Senecio nemorensis subsp. bayonnensis

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

bayonnensis, commonly known as the Bayonne groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer.

The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
