Solanum juripeba var. repandum

Solanum juripeba var. repandum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.
