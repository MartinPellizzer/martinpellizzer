Solanum coronopus

Solanum coronopus, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Solanum coronopus produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, containing several seeds.
