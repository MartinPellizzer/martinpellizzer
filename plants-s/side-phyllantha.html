Side phyllantha

Side phyllantha, commonly known as the "corky leaf" plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.
