Sesbania sesban var. muricata

Sesbania sesban var. muricata, commonly known as the rough sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental and forage plant in various climates.
