Stevia dodecachaeta

Stevia dodecachaeta, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are used as natural sweeteners.

Stevia dodecachaeta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
