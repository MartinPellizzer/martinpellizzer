Saussurea ussuriensis var. firma

Saussurea ussuriensis var. firma, commonly known as the Siberian snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
