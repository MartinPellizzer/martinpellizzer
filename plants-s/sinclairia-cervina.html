Sinclairia cervina

Sinclairia cervina, commonly known as the red-flowered saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Sinclairia cervina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
