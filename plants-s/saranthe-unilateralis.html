Saranthe unilateralis

Saranthe unilateralis, commonly known as the single-sided orchid, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features a single, elongated flower that blooms on a slender stem, distinguishing it from other orchid species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its rarity and distinctive morphology, Saranthe unilateralis is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
