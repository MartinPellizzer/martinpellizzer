Sparganium trifidum

Sparganium trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed bur reed, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and shallow waters in Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, brown, spiky fruits that are adapted for dispersal by water and animals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps it thrive in moist environments.

Sparganium trifidum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
