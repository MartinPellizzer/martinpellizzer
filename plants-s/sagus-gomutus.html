Sagus gomutus

Sagus gomutus, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
