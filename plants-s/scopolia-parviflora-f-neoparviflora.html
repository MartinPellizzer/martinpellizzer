Scopolia parviflora f. neoparviflora

Scopolia parviflora f. neoparviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered scopolia, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a deep blue or black color. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
