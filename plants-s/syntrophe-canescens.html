Syntrophe canescens

Syntrophe canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss, a key adaptation to its harsh habitat.

Syntrophe canescens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
