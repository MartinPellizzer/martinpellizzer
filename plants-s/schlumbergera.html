Schlumbergera

Schlumbergera, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that form a branching structure, often with vibrant floral displays in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces flowers during the holiday season, typically between November and January, making it a festive ornamental plant. Schlumbergera thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
