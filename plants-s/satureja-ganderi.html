Satureja ganderi

Satureja ganderi, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cooler climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Satureja ganderi is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional herbal remedies and culinary applications.
