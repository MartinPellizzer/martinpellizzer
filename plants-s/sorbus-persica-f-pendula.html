Sorbus persica f. pendula

Sorbus persica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping whitebeam, is a decorative tree characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cultivated form of the common whitebeam, Sorbus persica. The plant features small, white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its unique form and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
