Selaginella frondosa

Selaginella frondosa, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a vascular fern ally that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rootstock with upright, branching stems that bear spirally arranged, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella frondosa is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
