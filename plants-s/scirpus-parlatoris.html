Scirpus parlatoris

Scirpus parlatoris, commonly known as the marsh reed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpus parlatoris thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
