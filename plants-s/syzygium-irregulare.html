Syzygium irregulare

Syzygium irregulare, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, lemon-scented leaves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. Its leaves contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive citrus aroma.

Syzygium irregulare is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its unique flavor profile and potential health benefits.
