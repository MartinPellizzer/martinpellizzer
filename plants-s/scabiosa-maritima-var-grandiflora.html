Scabiosa maritima var. grandiflora

Scabiosa maritima var. grandiflora, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silvery-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its salty, windy habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and seaside cliffs.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and coastal landscapes.
