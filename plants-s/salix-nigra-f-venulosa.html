Salix nigra f. venulosa

Salix nigra f. venulosa, commonly known as the velvet willow, is a rare and ornamental variant of the black willow.

It is characterized by its soft, silky grayish-green foliage that gives it a unique, almost velvety appearance. The leaves are elongated and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its distinctive visual appeal. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks or in wetlands.

It is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
