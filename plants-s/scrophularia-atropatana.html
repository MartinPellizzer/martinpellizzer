Scrophularia atropatana

Scrophularia atropatana, commonly known as the Persian figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various species known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically features upright stems with opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Scrophularia atropatana is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
