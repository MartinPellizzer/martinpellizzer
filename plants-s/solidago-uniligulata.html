Solidago uniligulata

Solidago uniligulata, commonly known as the one-lipped goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
