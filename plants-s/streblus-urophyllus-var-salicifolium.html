Streblus urophyllus var. salicifolium

Streblus urophyllus var. salicifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf strangler fig, is a variant of the strangler fig species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or envelop host trees, often leading to the eventual death of the host. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and attracts various wildlife.

The botanical features of this variant include its unique growth habit and distinctive foliage, making it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.
