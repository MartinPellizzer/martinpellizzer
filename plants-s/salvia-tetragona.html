Salvia tetragona

Salvia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Salvia tetragona is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
