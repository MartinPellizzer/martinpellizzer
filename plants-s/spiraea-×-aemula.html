Spiraea × aemula

Spiraea × aemula, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub prized for its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid between Spiraea prairie and Spiraea japonica, resulting in a plant with distinctive cascading branches. The foliage is typically bright green, turning gold in the fall, while the flowers are clusters of white or pink blooms that appear in early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ease of care.
