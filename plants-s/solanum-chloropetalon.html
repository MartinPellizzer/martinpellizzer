Solanum chloropetalon

Solanum chloropetalon, commonly known as the green-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
