Scleranthus suprafastigiatus

Scleranthus suprafastigiatus, commonly known as the upright thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, often spiny leaves. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. S. suprafastigiatus is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, which includes many species with similar structural and ecological traits.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
