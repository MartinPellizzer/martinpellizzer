Scirpus subcapitatus f. rigidus

Scirpus subcapitatus f. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid marsh club-rush, is a botanical variant of the marsh club-rush species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
