Senecio tocomarensis

Senecio tocomarensis, commonly known as Tocomarensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators.
