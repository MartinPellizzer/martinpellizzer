Streblus glaber subsp. australiensis

Streblus glaber subsp.

australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sandpaper fig, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, bark-like foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
