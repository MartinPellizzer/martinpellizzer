Senecio lactescens

Senecio lactescens, commonly known as the milkweed of the highlands, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Senecio.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
