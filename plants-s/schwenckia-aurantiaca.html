Schwenckia aurantiaca

Schwenckia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange coneflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Schwenckia aurantiaca is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
