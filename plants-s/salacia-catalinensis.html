Salacia catalinensis

Salacia catalinensis, commonly known as the Catalina salacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Catalina in California.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub habitats, thriving in sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Salacia catalinensis is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its rarity, making it a subject of conservation interest.
