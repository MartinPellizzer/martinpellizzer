Saracha stapeliiflora

Saracha stapeliiflora, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of small, milky-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Saracha stapeliiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and resilience.
