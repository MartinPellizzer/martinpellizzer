Salvia przewalskii var. mandarinorum

Salvia przewalskii var. mandarinorum, commonly known as the Mandarin sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological habitats.
