Smilax gigantea

Smilax gigantea, commonly known as the giant greenbriar, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive, sharp, spiny stem. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its extensive root system allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

Smilax gigantea is often found in moist, shaded areas such as swamps and thickets.
