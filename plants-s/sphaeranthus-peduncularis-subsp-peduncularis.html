Sphaeranthus peduncularis subsp. peduncularis

Sphaeranthus peduncularis subsp.

peduncularis, commonly known as the slender globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual with a distinctive globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are slender and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
