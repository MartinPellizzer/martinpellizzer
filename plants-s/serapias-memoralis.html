Serapias memoralis

Serapias memoralis, commonly known as the memorial orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Serapias memoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
