Silene cucubalus f. rubriflora

Silene cucubalus f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
