Shorea seminis

Shorea seminis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
