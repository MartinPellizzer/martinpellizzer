Senna cardiosperma subsp. pilocarina

Senna cardiosperma subsp.

pilocarina, commonly known as the silky senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, silky texture on the underside, giving it its common name.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
