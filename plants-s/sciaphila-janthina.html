Sciaphila janthina

Sciaphila janthina, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant lacks true leaves and instead absorbs nutrients through its roots, which are often covered in a thick, spongy coating. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Sciaphila janthina is considered endangered.
