Schoenoplectiella schoofii

Schoenoplectiella schoofii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
