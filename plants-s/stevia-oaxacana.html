Stevia oaxacana

Stevia oaxacana, commonly known as sweetleaf or Mexican stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often used as a natural sweetener. The plant produces small yellow flowers and has a strong, sweet aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stevia oaxacana is valued for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are used in herbal medicine and as a sugar substitute.
