Salvia amethystina subsp. ampelophylla

Salvia amethystina subsp.

ampelophylla, commonly known as the purple sage or amethyst sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its drought tolerance. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and often used in traditional medicine.
