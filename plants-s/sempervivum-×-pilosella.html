Sempervivum × pilosella

Sempervivum × pilosella, commonly known as the alpine rose or stonecrop, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in late spring or early summer. It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Sempervivum × pilosella is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover.
