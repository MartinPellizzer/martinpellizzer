Selaginella anocardia

Selaginella anocardia, commonly known as the Cape coral clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its branching, club-like structures and tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are often clustered at the tips of its branches.

Selaginella anocardia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
