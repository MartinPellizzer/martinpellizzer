Salpichroa salpoensis

Salpichroa salpoensis, commonly known as the Salpichroa grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in open areas such as meadows and riverbanks.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for insects, it is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
