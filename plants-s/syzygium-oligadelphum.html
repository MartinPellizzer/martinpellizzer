Syzygium oligadelphum

Syzygium oligadelphum, commonly known as the small-flowered tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
