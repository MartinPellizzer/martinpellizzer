Sasa pumila

Sasa pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant produces slender, cane-like stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element. Sasa pumila thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats over time.
