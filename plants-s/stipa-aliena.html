Stipa aliena

Stipa aliena, commonly known as the foreign feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its seed heads are often adorned with silky, feathery bristles.

Stipa aliena is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
