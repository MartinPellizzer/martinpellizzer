Schizopepon bryoniifolius var. japonicus

Schizopepon bryoniifolius var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it is often cultivated for its edible fruits, though they are known for their bitter taste.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for horticultural study.
