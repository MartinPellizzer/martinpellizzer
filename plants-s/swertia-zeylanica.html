Swertia zeylanica

Swertia zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon Swertia, is a perennial herb native to the wet tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Swertia zeylanica is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
