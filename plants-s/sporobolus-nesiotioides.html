Sporobolus nesiotioides

Sporobolus nesiotioides, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically three-awned, giving it its common name. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil.

Sporobolus nesiotioides plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
