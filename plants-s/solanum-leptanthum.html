Solanum leptanthum

Solanum leptanthum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and hairy stems.
