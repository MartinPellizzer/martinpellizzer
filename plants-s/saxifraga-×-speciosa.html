Saxifraga × speciosa

Saxifraga × speciosa, commonly known as the showy saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs.

Saxifraga × speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
