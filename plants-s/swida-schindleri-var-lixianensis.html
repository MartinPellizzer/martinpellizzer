Swida schindleri var. lixianensis

Swida schindleri var. lixianensis, commonly known as the Lixian boxwood, is a variant of the boxwood plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use. The leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture. This variety is valued for its ability to maintain a neat shape with minimal pruning.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, preferring a cool, humid environment.
