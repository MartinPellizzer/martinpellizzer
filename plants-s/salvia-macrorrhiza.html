Salvia macrorrhiza

Salvia macrorrhiza, commonly known as swamp sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Salvia macrorrhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
