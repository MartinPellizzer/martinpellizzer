Scilla sodalicia

Scilla sodalicia, commonly known as woodland squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in woodland and rocky habitats, where it thrives in partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Scilla sodalicia is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and easy cultivation.
