Salvia glabrescens var. repens

Salvia glabrescens var. repens, commonly known as creeping sage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and attracting pollinators.
