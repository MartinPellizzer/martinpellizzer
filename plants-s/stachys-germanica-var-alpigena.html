Stachys germanica var. alpigena

Stachys germanica var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Stachys germanica var. alpigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
