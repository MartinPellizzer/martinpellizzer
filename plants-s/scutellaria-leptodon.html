Scutellaria leptodon

Scutellaria leptodon, commonly known as the slender beakvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually blue to violet in color, bloom in late summer and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria leptodon is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
