Salix cinerea f. variegata

Salix cinerea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated gray willow, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are typically grayish-white with a contrasting green edge, creating an eye-catching appearance. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive cycle.

This ornamental willow is often cultivated for its unique visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.
