Saxifraga flagellaris subsp. komarovii

Saxifraga flagellaris subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features upright, slender stems that bear small, clustered leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
