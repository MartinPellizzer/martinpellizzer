Serjania tinctoria

Serjania tinctoria, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
