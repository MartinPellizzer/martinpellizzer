Sideritis leucantha subsp. mohamedii

Sideritis leucantha subsp.

mohamedii, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
