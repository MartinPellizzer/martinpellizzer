Senecio flavovirens

Senecio flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to greenish in color.

Senecio flavovirens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
