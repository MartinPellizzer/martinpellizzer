Scrophularia crenata

Scrophularia crenata, commonly known as the wavy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with wavy edges, and its flowers are tubular with a distinctive purple or pink coloration. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Scrophularia crenata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
