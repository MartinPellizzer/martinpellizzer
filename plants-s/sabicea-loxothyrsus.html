Sabicea loxothyrsus

Sabicea loxothyrsus, commonly known as the woolly sabicea, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
