Sideritis caesarea

Sideritis caesarea, commonly known as blue heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis caesarea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
