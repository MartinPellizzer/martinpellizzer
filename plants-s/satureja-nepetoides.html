Satureja nepetoides

Satureja nepetoides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a minty, slightly citrusy flavor to dishes.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
