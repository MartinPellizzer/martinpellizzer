Scirpus buettnerianus

Scirpus buettnerianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are pollinated by wind rather than insects. Scirpus buettnerianus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of marsh and pond ecosystems.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
