Sempervivum lineolare

Sempervivum lineolare, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Himalayan Houseleek," is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, rosette-forming leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a hardy and resilient plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
