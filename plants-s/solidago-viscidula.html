Solidago viscidula

Solidago viscidula, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and bright yellow composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks.

Solidago viscidula plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the fall season.
