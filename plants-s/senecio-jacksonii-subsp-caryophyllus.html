Senecio jacksonii subsp. caryophyllus

Senecio jacksonii subsp.

caryophyllus, commonly known as Jackson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
