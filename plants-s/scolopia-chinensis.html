Scolopia chinensis

Scolopia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, fleshy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Scolopia chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
