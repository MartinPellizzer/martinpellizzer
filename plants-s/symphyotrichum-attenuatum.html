Symphyotrichum attenuatum

Symphyotrichum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender stem golden aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The blooming period occurs in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
