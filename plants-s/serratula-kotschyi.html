Serratula kotschyi

Serratula kotschyi, commonly known as the Armenian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually serrated and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Serratula kotschyi produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
