Serpyllum

Serpyllum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers. Serpyllum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant is typically found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
