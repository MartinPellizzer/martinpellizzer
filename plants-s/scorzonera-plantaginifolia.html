Scorzonera plantaginifolia

Scorzonera plantaginifolia, commonly known as the meadow scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow ray flowers. The plant produces a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Scorzonera plantaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
