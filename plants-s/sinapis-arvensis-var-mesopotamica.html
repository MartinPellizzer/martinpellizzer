Sinapis arvensis var. mesopotamica

Sinapis arvensis var. mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian mustard, is a wild Brassicaceae species native to southern regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect stem, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become a weed in various ecosystems.
