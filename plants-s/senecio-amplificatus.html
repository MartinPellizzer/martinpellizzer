Senecio amplificatus

Senecio amplificatus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it requires careful management in cultivated settings.
