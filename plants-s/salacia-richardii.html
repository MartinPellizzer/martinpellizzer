Salacia richardii

Salacia richardii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
