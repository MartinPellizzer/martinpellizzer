Scabiosa rhizantha

Scabiosa rhizantha, commonly known as the pink scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Scabiosa rhizantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
