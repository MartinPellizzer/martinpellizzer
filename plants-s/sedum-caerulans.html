Sedum caerulans

Sedum caerulans, commonly known as blue stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a dense rosette, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and vibrant color make it an attractive addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
