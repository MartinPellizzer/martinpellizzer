Scorzonera hispanica var. glastifolia

Scorzonera hispanica var. glastifolia, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often used as a food source. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinct yellow flower head composed of ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
