Syzygium revolutum subsp. revolutum

Syzygium revolutum subsp.

revolutum, commonly known as the red apple or red guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, red or pinkish skin and sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local cuisine.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in traditional Indigenous uses.
