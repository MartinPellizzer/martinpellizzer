Stylosanthes venezuelensis

Stylosanthes venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan stylo, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability enhances soil fertility, contributing to its agricultural importance.
