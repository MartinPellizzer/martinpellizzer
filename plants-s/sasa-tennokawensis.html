Sasa tennokawensis

Sasa tennokawensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense foliage. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sasa tennokawensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
