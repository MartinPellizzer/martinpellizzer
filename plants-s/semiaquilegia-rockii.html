Semiaquilegia rockii

Semiaquilegia rockii, commonly known as the Rock's semiaquilegia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a delicate, fragrant aroma.

Semiaquilegia rockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
