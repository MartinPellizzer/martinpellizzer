Sesbania goetzei subsp. goetzei

Sesbania goetzei subsp.

goetzei, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen. Sesbania goetzei subsp.

goetzei is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
