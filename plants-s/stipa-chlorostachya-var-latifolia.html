Stipa chlorostachya var. latifolia

Stipa chlorostachya var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bluegrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often tinged with a bluish hue. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
