Schoenus striatus

Schoenus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Schoenus striatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
