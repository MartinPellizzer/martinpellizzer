Spermacoce holmiana

Spermacoce holmiana, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in clusters. Spermacoce holmiana is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
