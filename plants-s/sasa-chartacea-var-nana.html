Sasa chartacea var. nana

Sasa chartacea var. nana, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a low-growing, clump-forming plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that rarely exceed 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in early summer, followed by slender, three-angled fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
