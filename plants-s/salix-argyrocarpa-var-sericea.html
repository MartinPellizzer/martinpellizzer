Salix argyrocarpa var. sericea

Salix argyrocarpa var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a variant of the silver willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
