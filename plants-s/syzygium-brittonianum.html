Syzygium brittonianum

Syzygium brittonianum, commonly known as the Britton'sSyzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a thin, leathery skin.

Syzygium brittonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
