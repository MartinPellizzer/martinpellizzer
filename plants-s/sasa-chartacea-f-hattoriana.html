Sasa chartacea f. hattoriana

Sasa chartacea f. hattoriana, commonly known as the Hattori's bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in Japanese horticulture.
