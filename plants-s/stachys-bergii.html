Stachys bergii

Stachys bergii, commonly known as Berg's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers arranged in whorls. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

Stachys bergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
