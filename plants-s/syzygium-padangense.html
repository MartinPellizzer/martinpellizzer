Syzygium padangense

Syzygium padangense, commonly known as the Java apple or Padang apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular fruit in local markets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
