Scolosanthus grandifolius

Scolosanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the giant leek, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in loose, branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Scolosanthus grandifolius is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
