Solanum paredesii

Solanum paredesii, commonly known as the Paredesii nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the afternoon and are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum paredesii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
