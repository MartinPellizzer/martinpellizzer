Sarcococca saligna var. densiflora

Sarcococca saligna var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain dense and upright throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne in clusters along the stems. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

This variety is particularly noted for its dense foliage and winter blooming habit, making it a popular choice for garden borders and foundation plantings.
