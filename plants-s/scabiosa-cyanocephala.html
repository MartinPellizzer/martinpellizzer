Scabiosa cyanocephala

Scabiosa cyanocephala, commonly known as the bluehead scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small, clustered umbels. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Scabiosa cyanocephala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
