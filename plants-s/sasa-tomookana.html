Sasa tomookana

Sasa tomookana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial plant that forms dense clumps of slender, hollow stems known as culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Sasa tomookana thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain slopes.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
