Schlumbergera × buckleyi

Schlumbergera × buckleyi, commonly known as the Thanksgiving cactus, is a hybrid epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, hooked spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schlumbergera × buckleyi is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and long blooming period.
