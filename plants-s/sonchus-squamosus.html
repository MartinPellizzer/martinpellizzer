Sonchus squamosus

Sonchus squamosus, commonly known as the scaly sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, scaly structures. Its yellow flowers are borne in a single terminal head, surrounded by a series of leaf-like bracts.

Sonchus squamosus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
