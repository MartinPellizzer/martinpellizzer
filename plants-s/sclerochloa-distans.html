Sclerochloa distans

Sclerochloa distans, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils.

Sclerochloa distans plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
