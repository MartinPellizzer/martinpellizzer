Syzygium chamaebuxus

Syzygium chamaebuxus, commonly known as the dwarf apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red or yellow berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Syzygium chamaebuxus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
