Scutellaria toguztoravensis

Scutellaria toguztoravensis, commonly known as the Toguztoran skullcup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, specifically found in Kazakhstan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria toguztoravensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
