Sempervivum × theobaldii

Sempervivum × theobaldii, commonly known as the "Himalayan houseleek," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, though it prefers full sun and cooler temperatures.
