Solidago elongata

Solidago elongata, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Solidago elongata is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
