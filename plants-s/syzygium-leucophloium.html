Syzygium leucophloium

Syzygium leucophloium, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Syzygium leucophloium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
