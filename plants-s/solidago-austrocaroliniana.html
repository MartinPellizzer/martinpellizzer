Solidago austrocaroliniana

Solidago austrocaroliniana, commonly known as the southern goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Solidago austrocaroliniana plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
