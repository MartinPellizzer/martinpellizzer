Selaginella pervillei

Selaginella pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant features a short, upright stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella pervillei is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
