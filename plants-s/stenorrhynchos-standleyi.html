Stenorrhynchos standleyi

Stenorrhynchos standleyi, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stem and clusters of showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-brown pseudobulbs that help store water and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
