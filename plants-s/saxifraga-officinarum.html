Saxifraga officinarum

Saxifraga officinarum, commonly known as the garden sorrel, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of dark green, slightly hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or woodland environments. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Saxifraga officinarum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
