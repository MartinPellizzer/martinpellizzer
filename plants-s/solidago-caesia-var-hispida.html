Solidago caesia var. hispida

Solidago caesia var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
