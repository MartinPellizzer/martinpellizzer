Salvia benecincta

Salvia benecincta, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its silvery foliage and long-lasting blooms.
