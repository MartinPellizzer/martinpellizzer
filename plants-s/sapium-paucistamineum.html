Sapium paucistamineum

Sapium paucistamineum, commonly known as the small-stamen sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

The species also contains various alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal and toxicological significance.
