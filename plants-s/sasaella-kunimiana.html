Sasaella kunimiana

Sasaella kunimiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasaella kunimiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
