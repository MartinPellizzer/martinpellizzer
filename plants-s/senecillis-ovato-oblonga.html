Senecillis ovato-oblonga

Senecillis ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, ovate-oblong leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
