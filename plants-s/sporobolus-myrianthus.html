Sporobolus myrianthus

Sporobolus myrianthus, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ability to form dense stands makes it an important component of native plant communities in arid regions.
