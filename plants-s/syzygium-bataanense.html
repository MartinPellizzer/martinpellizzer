Syzygium bataanense

Syzygium bataanense, commonly known as the Bataan apple or Bataan tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically found in the Bataan region and other parts of Luzon. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces edible fruits that are yellow when ripe, similar in appearance to tamarinds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
