Sporobolus complanatus

Sporobolus complanatus, commonly known as flatleaf sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion due to its extensive root system.

Sporobolus complanatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
