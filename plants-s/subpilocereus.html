Subpilocereus

Subpilocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and large, showy flowers that open at night. They typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. The flowers are usually white or pale in color and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Subpilocereus species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their unique appearance and nighttime blooming habit.
