Stellaria media subsp. cupaniana

Stellaria media subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features small, white star-shaped flowers and has hairy, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along pathways. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its short stems and numerous small flowers.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Stellaria genus, sharing similar botanical traits.
