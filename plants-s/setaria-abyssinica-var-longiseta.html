Setaria abyssinica var. longiseta

Setaria abyssinica var. longiseta, commonly known as the Abyssinian millet, is a grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and resilience to harsh climates.
