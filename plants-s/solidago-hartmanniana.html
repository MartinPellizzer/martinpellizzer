Solidago hartmanniana

Solidago hartmanniana, commonly known as Hartmann's goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hartmann's goldenrod is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland landscapes.
