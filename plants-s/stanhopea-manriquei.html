Stanhopea manriquei

Stanhopea manriquei, commonly known as the Manrique's stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement along a single stem. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Stanhopea manriquei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and habitat requirements.
