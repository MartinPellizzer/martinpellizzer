Symphyotrichum ericoides var. pansum

Symphyotrichum ericoides var. pansum, commonly known as the sandplain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal plains of the southeastern United States.

It has compact, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the fall. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and dune environments. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, salty conditions.
