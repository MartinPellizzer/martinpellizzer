Saxifraga fortunei var. rubra

Saxifraga fortunei var. rubra, commonly known as the red stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially under full sun. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
