Salacia diandra f. lanceolata

Salacia diandra f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate salacia, is a variant of the Salacia diandra species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
