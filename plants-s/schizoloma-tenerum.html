Schizoloma tenerum

Schizoloma tenerum, commonly known as the slender lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its thin, leaf-like thallus with a pale green to yellowish upper surface and a darker, granular lower surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, with the algal partner providing photosynthetic capability. It is often found on rocks, tree trunks, and soil in temperate regions.

Schizoloma tenerum plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as an indicator of environmental conditions.
