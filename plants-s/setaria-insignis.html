Setaria insignis

Setaria insignis, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its small, oval seeds are rich in nutrients and have been cultivated for thousands of years.

Setaria insignis is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and erosion control.
