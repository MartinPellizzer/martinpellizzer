Senecio juncalensis

Senecio juncalensis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Senecio juncalensis is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
