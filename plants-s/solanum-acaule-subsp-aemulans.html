Solanum acaule subsp. aemulans

Solanum acaule subsp.

aemulans, commonly known as the mimic nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a bright red or yellow color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and toxic properties, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
