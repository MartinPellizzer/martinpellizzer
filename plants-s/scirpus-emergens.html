Scirpus emergens

Scirpus emergens, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers at the top of its stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands in marshes and shallow waters.

Scirpus emergens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and wetland wildlife.
