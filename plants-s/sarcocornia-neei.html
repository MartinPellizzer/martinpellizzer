Sarcocornia neei

Sarcocornia neei, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and flowering stems. The plant typically grows in coastal wetlands and tidal flats, where it tolerates high salinity and fluctuating water levels. Its flowers are small and often pink or purple, appearing in clusters.

Sarcocornia neei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
