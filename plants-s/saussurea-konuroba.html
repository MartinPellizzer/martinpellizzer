Saussurea konuroba

Saussurea konuroba, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and cooler climates.
