Stapelia parvipuncta var. truncata

Stapelia parvipuncta var. truncata, commonly known as the Cape Town Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a distinctive truncate (cut-off) apex, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
