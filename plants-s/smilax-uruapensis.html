Smilax uruapensis

Smilax uruapensis, commonly known as Brazilian arrowroot or yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny thorns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Smilax uruapensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
