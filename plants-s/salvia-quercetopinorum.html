Salvia quercetopinorum

Salvia quercetopinorum, commonly known as the Quercetopinorum sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and spikes of purple to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
