Spergularia rubra subsp. capillacea

Spergularia rubra subsp.

capillacea, commonly known as red sandplain grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and coastal regions. It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion. Its subsp.

capillacea is distinguished by its finer, more delicate leaves compared to other subspecies.
