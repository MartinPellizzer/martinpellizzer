Strychnos nana

Strychnos nana, commonly known as the dwarf strangler fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant bears orange-red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. Strychnos nana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
