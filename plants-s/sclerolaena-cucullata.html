Sclerolaena cucullata

Sclerolaena cucullata, commonly known as the hooded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust rhizome that helps it thrive in moist soil conditions.

Sclerolaena cucullata is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
