Sphaeralcea grossulariifolia var. moorei

Sphaeralcea grossulariifolia var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
