Sorbus torminalis f. kissii

Sorbus torminalis f. kissii, commonly known as the Kashmir whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow foliage, which provides a unique ornamental value in gardens. The leaves are typically smaller and more densely packed compared to the species form, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

This form is particularly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural collections.
