Senna cornigera

Senna cornigera, commonly known as the crimson senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Senna cornigera thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
