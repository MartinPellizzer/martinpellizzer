Salsola pestifer var. pontica

Salsola pestifer var. pontica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in disturbed soils. The plant has a robust root system and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in many areas.

The leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.
