Sorbus folgneri f. pendula

Sorbus folgneri f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping whitebeams, is a decorative shrub or small tree characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in gardens for its unique form and seasonal interest.
