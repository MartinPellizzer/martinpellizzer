Syzygium patentinerve

Syzygium patentinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined apple or broad-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with prominently visible, spreading veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous, sweet pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
