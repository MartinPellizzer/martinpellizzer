Serpocaulon patentissimum

Serpocaulon patentissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf serpocaulon, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
