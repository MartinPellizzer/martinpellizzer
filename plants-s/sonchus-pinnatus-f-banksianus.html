Sonchus pinnatus f. banksianus

Sonchus pinnatus f. banksianus, commonly known as the Banksian sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
