Stillingia laurifolia

Stillingia laurifolia, commonly known as the redroot or redroot dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark red roots, which are often harvested for medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

Stillingia laurifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological and traditional uses.
