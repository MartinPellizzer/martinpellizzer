Stachys rugosa var. foliosa

Stachys rugosa var. foliosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
