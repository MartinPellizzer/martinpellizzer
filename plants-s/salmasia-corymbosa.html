Salmasia corymbosa

Salmasia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered salvia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salmasia corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
