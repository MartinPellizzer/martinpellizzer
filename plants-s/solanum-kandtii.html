Solanum kandtii

Solanum kandtii, commonly known as the Kandt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Solanum kandtii is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
