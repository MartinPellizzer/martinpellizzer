Simaba guianensis subsp. huberi

Simaba guianensis subsp.

huberi, commonly known as the Guianan simaba, is a tropical tree native to South America. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
