Scandix australis subsp. grandiflora

Scandix australis subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sandparsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
