Surenus australis

Surenus australis, commonly known as the southern surenus, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Surenus australis is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
