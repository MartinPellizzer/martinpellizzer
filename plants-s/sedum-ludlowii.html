Sedum ludlowii

Sedum ludlowii, commonly known as Ludlow's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Sedum ludlowii is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
