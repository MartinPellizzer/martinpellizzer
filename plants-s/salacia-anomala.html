Salacia anomala

Salacia anomala, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels off in thin layers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, Salacia anomala is valued in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
