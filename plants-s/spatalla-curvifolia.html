Spatalla curvifolia

Spatalla curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved spatterdock, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, floating leaves that are often curled or wavy in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Spatalla curvifolia thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
