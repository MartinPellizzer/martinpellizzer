Solanum amaranthifolium

Solanum amaranthifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its broad, amaranth-like leaves. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, egg-shaped berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Solanum amaranthifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and floral display.
