Solanum schickii

Solanum schickii, commonly known as the Schick's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
