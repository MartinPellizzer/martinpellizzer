Salvia iodochroa

Salvia iodochroa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia iodochroa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
