Shorea siamensis var. tomentosa

Shorea siamensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy wood with a fine texture. The tree typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The species is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its strength and resistance to decay.
