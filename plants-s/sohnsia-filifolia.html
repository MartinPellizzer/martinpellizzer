Sohnsia filifolia

Sohnsia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf sohnsia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and early summer. Sohnsia filifolia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from underground sources.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
