Sclerolaena nitida

Sclerolaena nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Sclerolaena nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
