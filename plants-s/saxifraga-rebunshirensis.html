Saxifraga rebunshirensis

Saxifraga rebunshirensis, commonly known as the Rebun Island saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically found on Rebun Island in the northern part of the country.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, coastal environments, often growing on cliffs or in crevices where it can access moisture and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural habitat.
