Scirpus avatshensis

Scirpus avatshensis, commonly known as the Armenian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed spikelets, which are often submerged or floating.

Scirpus avatshensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
