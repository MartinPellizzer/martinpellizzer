Scrophularia auriculata var. parviflora

Scrophularia auriculata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
