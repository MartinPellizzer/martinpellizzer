Saussurea gossypiphora

Saussurea gossypiphora, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Indian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine. Its flowers are harvested for their saffron-like stigmas, which are used in various herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool climates and well-drained soil.
