Sorbus aria proles latifolia

Sorbus aria proles latifolia, commonly known as the whitebeam or common whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its broad, oval leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is known for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
