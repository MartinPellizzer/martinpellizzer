Sagus raphia

Sagus raphia, commonly known as the giant palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 10 meters in length. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The fruit of Sagus raphia is a small, fibrous structure that contains edible seeds.
