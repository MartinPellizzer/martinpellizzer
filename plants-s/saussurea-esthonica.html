Saussurea esthonica

Saussurea esthonica, commonly known as the Esthonian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound known as saffron, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
