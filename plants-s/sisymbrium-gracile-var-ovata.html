Sisymbrium gracile var. ovata

Sisymbrium gracile var. ovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The leaves are elongated and linear, often with a slight pubescence. Native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This plant is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for various pollinators.
