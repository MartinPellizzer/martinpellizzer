Silene aucheriana var. angustifolia

Silene aucheriana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
