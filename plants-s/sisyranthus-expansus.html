Sisyranthus expansus

Sisyranthus expansus, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
