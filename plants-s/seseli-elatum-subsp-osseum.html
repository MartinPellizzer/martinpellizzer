Seseli elatum subsp. osseum

Seseli elatum subsp.

osseum, commonly known as the bonehead seseli, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
