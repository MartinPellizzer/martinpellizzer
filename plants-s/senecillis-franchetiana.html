Senecillis franchetiana

Senecillis franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing numerous small florets arranged in a panicle inflorescence.

Senecillis franchetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
