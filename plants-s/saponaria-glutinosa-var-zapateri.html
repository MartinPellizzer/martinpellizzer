Saponaria glutinosa var. zapateri

Saponaria glutinosa var. zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's sticky saponaria, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
