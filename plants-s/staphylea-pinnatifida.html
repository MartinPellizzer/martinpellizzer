Staphylea pinnatifida

Staphylea pinnatifida, commonly known as the broadleaf staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
