Senecio peregrinus

Senecio peregrinus, commonly known as the wandering beggar, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio peregrinus is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
