Symplocos pittieriana

Symplocos pittieriana, commonly known as the Pittier's wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Symplocos pittieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
