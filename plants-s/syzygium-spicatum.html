Syzygium spicatum

Syzygium spicatum, commonly known as the spicata tamarind.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, sour fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and pale.

Syzygium spicatum is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
