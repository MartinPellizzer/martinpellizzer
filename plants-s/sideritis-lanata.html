Sideritis lanata

Sideritis lanata, commonly known as woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. Sideritis lanata is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystems.
