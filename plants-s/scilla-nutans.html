Scilla nutans

Scilla nutans, commonly known as the nodding scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Scilla nutans blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.
