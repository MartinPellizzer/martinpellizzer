Stachys purpurea

Stachys purpurea, commonly known as purple betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly minty scent.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
