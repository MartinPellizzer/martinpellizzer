Silaum silaus var. latissimum

Silaum silaus var. latissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaved silaum, is a variant of the silaum species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
