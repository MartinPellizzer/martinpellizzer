Scrophularia canina var. boetica

Scrophularia canina var. boetica, commonly known as the dog's mouth or narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
