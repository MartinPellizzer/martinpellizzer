Sabbata polyphylla

Sabbata polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved sabbata, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which provides structural support and reduces water loss.

Sabbata polyphylla is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
