Salix nakamurana subsp. nakamurana

Salix nakamurana subsp.

nakamurana, commonly known as the Nakamura willow, is a shrub or small tree native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
