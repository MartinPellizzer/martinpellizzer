Sempervivum atropatanum

Sempervivum atropatanum, commonly known as the Black Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green to almost black leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a hardy and resilient plant.
