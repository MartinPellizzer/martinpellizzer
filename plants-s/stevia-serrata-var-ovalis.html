Stevia serrata var. ovalis

Stevia serrata var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf stevia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it is often grown for use in herbal teas and sweetener production.
