Syzygium nervosum

Syzygium nervosum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet-tart red berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Syzygium nervosum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
