Syngonium amazonicum

Syngonium amazonicum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Syngonium amazonicum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
