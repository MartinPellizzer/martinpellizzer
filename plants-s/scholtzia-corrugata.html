Scholtzia corrugata

Scholtzia corrugata, commonly known as the wrinkled scholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Scholtzia corrugata thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
