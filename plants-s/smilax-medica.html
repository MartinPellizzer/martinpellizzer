Smilax medica

Smilax medica, commonly known as the medicinal smilax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with spines, and its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Smilax medica is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests and along streambanks.
