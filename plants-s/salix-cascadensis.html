Salix cascadensis

Salix cascadensis, commonly known as the cascade willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, dark green upper surface.

Salix cascadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
