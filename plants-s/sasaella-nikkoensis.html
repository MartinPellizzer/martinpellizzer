Sasaella nikkoensis

Sasaella nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko bamboo, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. It forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
