Syzygium modestum

Syzygium modestum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically red when mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often cultivated for both its timber and ornamental qualities.
