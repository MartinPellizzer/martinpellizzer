Salvia blancoana subsp. blancoana

Salvia blancoana subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in arid environments.
