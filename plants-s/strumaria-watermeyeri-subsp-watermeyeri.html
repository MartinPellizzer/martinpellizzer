Strumaria watermeyeri subsp. watermeyeri

Strumaria watermeyeri subsp.

watermeyeri, commonly known as the Watermeyer's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
