Solidago frankii

Solidago frankii, commonly known as Frank's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Frank's goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
