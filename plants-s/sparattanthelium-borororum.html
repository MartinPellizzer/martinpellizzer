Sparattanthelium borororum

Sparattanthelium borororum, commonly known as the Bororo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often studied for its potential in sustainable land management practices.
