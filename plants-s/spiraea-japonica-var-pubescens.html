Spiraea japonica var. pubescens

Spiraea japonica var. pubescens, commonly known as Japanese Spirea with hairy leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
