Senecio albicaulis var. candolleanus

Senecio albicaulis var. candolleanus, commonly known as the White Hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white to silver-gray foliage that resembles hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
