Stipagrostis plumosa subsp. kyzylkumica

Stipagrostis plumosa subsp.

kyzylkumica, commonly known as the Kyzylkum feathergrass, is a grass species native to arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silky, and feathery appearance, with narrow, elongated leaves that give it a delicate, ornamental look.

This subspecies thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to high temperatures, making it well-suited for xerophytic habitats.

Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion in its native range.
