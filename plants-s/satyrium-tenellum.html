Satyrium tenellum

Satyrium tenellum, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale green or white flowers that resemble those of its close relatives. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Satyrium tenellum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
