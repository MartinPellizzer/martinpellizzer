Senna occidentalis var. sophera

Senna occidentalis var. sophera, commonly known as the Indian Senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Sophera is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
