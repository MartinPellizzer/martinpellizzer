Solanum campechiense var. fuscatum

Solanum campechiense var. fuscatum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers and has hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption.
