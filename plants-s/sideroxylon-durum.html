Sideroxylon durum

Sideroxylon durum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Sideroxylon durum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
