Stenosemia dimorpha

Stenosemia dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form nettlefly, is a species of moth belonging to the family Noctuidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing patterns, which feature intricate lines and shades of gray and brown. Its life cycle involves a complete metamorphosis, with eggs laid on host plants that provide nourishment for the developing larvae.

The plant-associated life stages of Stenosemia dimorpha highlight its ecological role in both herbivory and pollination dynamics.
