Sisymbrium perplexum

Sisymbrium perplexum, commonly known as the clustered broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host.

Its life cycle is closely tied to specific host species, making it an interesting subject for ecological studies.
