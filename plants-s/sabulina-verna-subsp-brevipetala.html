Sabulina verna subsp. brevipetala

Sabulina verna subsp.

brevipetala, commonly known as the spring sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere. It typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and rocky areas.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in early spring. Its subspecies brevipetala is distinguished by its shorter petals compared to other subspecies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
