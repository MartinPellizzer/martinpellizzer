Saccolabium chionanthum

Saccolabium chionanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native range.
