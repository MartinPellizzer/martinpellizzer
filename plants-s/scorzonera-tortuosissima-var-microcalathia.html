Scorzonera tortuosissima var. microcalathia

Scorzonera tortuosissima var. microcalathia, commonly known as the small-flowered scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant produces tall, hairy stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy as well. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular throat.

This variety is valued for both its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
