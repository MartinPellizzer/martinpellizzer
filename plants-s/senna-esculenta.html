Senna esculenta

Senna esculenta, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its pods, which contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
