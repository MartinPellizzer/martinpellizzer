Salpichroa tristis var. lehmannii

Salpichroa tristis var. lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's false flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
