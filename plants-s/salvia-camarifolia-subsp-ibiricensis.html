Salvia camarifolia subsp. ibiricensis

Salvia camarifolia subsp.

ibiricensis, commonly known as the Ibirica sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern region of Brazil. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a velvety texture and its spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.

This subspecies is also noted for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
