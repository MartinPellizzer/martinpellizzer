Salix × schatilowii

Salix × schatilowii, commonly known as the Schatilowii willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. This plant is native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also notable for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
