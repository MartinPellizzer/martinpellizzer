Senna obtusa

Senna obtusa, commonly known as the sickle senna, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Senna obtusa is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves and flowers used in traditional medicine for their laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.
