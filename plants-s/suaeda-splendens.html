Suaeda splendens

Suaeda splendens, commonly known as the golden saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its salt tolerance and drought resistance make it a valuable plant for stabilizing coastal and desert soils.

Suaeda splendens plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
