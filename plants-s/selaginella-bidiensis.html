Selaginella bidiensis

Selaginella bidiensis, commonly known as the African spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella bidiensis is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique adaptations to arid environments.
