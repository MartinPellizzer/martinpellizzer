Sideritis perfoliata

Sideritis perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a hairy texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color.

Sideritis perfoliata is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
