Strychnos hirtiflora

Strychnos hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered strychnos, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Strychnos hirtiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
