Schoenoplectiella × igaensis

Schoenoplectiella × igaensis, commonly known as the hybrid reed, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Schoenoplectiella mucronata and Schoenoplectiella gracilis. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that can stabilize shorelines. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
