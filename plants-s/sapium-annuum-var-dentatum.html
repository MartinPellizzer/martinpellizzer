Sapium annuum var. dentatum

Sapium annuum var. dentatum, commonly known as the roundleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and floodplains.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
