Stenocalyx brasiliensis var. erythrocarpa

Stenocalyx brasiliensis var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with five petals, and the fruit is a capsule that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
