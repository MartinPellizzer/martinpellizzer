Solidago nemoralis var. haleana

Solidago nemoralis var. haleana, commonly known as the Hale's goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, bright yellow inflorescences in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
