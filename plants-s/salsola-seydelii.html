Salsola seydelii

Salsola seydelii, commonly known as Russian thorn or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in sandy and saline soils, often found in desert and semi-desert regions.

Salsola seydelii is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
