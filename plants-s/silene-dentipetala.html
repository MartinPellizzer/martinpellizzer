Silene dentipetala

Silene dentipetala, commonly known as the toothed petal catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Silene dentipetala is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity in harsh climates.
