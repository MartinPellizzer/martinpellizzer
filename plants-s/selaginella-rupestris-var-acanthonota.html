Selaginella rupestris var. acanthonota

Selaginella rupestris var. acanthonota, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it can survive with minimal water.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
