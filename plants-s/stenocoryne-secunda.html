Stenocoryne secunda

Stenocoryne secunda, commonly known as the narrow-crown daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Stenocoryne secunda is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
