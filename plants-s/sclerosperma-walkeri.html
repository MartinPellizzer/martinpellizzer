Sclerosperma walkeri

Sclerosperma walkeri, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are a food source for various animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

Sclerosperma walkeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
