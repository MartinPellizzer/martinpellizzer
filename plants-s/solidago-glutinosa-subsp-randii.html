Solidago glutinosa subsp. randii

Solidago glutinosa subsp.

randii, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its sticky, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
