Stachys marrubiastrum

Stachys marrubiastrum, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

Stachys marrubiastrum is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
