Suaeda volkensii

Suaeda volkensii, commonly known as the Sudanese saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt flats.

Suaeda volkensii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
