Salvia haitiensis

Salvia haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Haitian folk medicine for various ailments.

Salvia haitiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
