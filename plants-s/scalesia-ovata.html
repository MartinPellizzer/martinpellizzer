Scalesia ovata

Scalesia ovata, commonly known as the giant lobelias, is a striking plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. It is also notable for its role in the unique ecosystem of the Galápagos, supporting various pollinators.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is a key species in conservation efforts on the islands.
