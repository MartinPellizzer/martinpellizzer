Salvia montbretii var. virescens

Salvia montbretii var. virescens, commonly known as violet beard tongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
