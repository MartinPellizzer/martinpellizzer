Salix × ambigua var. argentifolia

Salix × ambigua var. argentifolia, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid species within the willow family (Salicaceae).

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its catkin-like flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The silver willow is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
