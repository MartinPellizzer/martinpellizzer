Stachys latifolia

Stachys latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges and is often found in moist, shady habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Stachys latifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
