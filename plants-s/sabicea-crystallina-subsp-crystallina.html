Sabicea crystallina subsp. crystallina

Sabicea crystallina subsp.

crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline sabicea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
