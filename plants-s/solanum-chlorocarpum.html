Solanum chlorocarpum

Solanum chlorocarpum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by green, egg-shaped fruits that turn yellow when mature. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
