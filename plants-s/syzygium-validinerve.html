Syzygium validinerve

Syzygium validinerve, commonly known as the slender-veined sandoric.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sweet-sour red berries. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Syzygium validinerve is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
