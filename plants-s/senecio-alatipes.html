Senecio alatipes

Senecio alatipes, commonly known as the winged groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often winged stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Senecio alatipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
