Scolopia roxburghii

Scolopia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features a rhizomatous root system that is highly valued for its starchy, edible tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Scolopia roxburghii is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.
