Syzygium royenii

Syzygium royenii, commonly known as the Royal Syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The fruit, which is typically a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
