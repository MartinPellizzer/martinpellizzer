Senecio cabrerae

Senecio cabrerae, commonly known as the Cape St. Francis daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Senecio cabrerae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
