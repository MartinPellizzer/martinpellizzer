Silene altaica var. grandiflora

Silene altaica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Altai rock bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are actinomorphic with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
