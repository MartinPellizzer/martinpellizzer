Saxifraga bonatiana

Saxifraga bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained and sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga bonatiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
