Stelis cundinamarcae

Stelis cundinamarcae, commonly known as the Cundinamarca orchid, is a species of orchid native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Stelis cundinamarcae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
