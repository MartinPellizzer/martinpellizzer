Solanum chaucha f. purpureum

Solanum chaucha f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-fruited nightshade, is a variant of the Solanum chaucha species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that transition from green to a striking purple as they mature. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solanine.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical regions.
