Schefflera corymbiformis

Schefflera corymbiformis, commonly known as the Hawaiian umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in corymbs, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Schefflera corymbiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
