Sempervivum boutignyanum var. pallescens

Sempervivum boutignyanum var. pallescens, commonly known as the silver cushion sedum, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to southern Europe.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
