Selaginella xanthoneura

Selaginella xanthoneura, commonly known as the yellow-stem spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant features delicate, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Selaginella xanthoneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
