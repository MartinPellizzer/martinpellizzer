Sorbus joannis

Sorbus joannis, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Sorbus joannis is also valued for its ecological benefits and is often used in reforestation projects.
