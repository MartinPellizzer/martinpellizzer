Syzygium nervosum var. obovatum

Syzygium nervosum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and unique floral display.
