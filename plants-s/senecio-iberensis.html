Senecio iberensis

Senecio iberensis, commonly known as the Iberian senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio iberensis is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
