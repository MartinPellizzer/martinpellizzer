Sida centrota

Sida centrota, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to 1-2 meters in height. Sida centrota produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
