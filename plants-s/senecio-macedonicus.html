Senecio macedonicus

Senecio macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
