Setaria aequalis

Setaria aequalis, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Setaria aequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
