Salvia grandiflora var. aegaea

Salvia grandiflora var. aegaea, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, releasing a minty scent when crushed.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry soils, thriving in full sun.
