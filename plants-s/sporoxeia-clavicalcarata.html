Sporoxeia clavicalcarata

Sporoxeia clavicalcarata, commonly known as the golden clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cone-like formations called strobili. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Sporoxeia clavicalcarata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
