Sherardia arvensis f. argentina

Sherardia arvensis f. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine field gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

This fern-like foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native range.
