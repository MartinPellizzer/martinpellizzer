Senecio buchwaldii

Senecio buchwaldii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features elongated, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adorned with white or silver markings. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
