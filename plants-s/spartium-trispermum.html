Spartium trispermum

Spartium trispermum, commonly known as red brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear reddish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Spartium trispermum thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
