Sehima ciliaris

Sehima ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Sehima ciliaris is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
