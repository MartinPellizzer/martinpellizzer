Suregada occidentalis

Suregada occidentalis, commonly known as the western suregada, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
