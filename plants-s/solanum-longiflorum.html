Solanum longiflorum

Solanum longiflorum, commonly known as the longflower nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
