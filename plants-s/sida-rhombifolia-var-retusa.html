Sida rhombifolia var. retusa

Sida rhombifolia var. retusa, commonly known as the wooly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common species in coastal and sandy habitats.
