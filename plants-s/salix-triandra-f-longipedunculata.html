Salix triandra f. longipedunculata

Salix triandra f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate willow, is a hybrid form of the white willow.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated peduncles that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found near water bodies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and a silvery underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
