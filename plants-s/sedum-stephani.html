Sedum stephani

Sedum stephani, commonly known as the Stephen's stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, overlapping leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Sedum stephani is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
