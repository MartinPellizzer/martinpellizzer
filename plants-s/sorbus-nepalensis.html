Sorbus nepalensis

Sorbus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found at high altitudes in mountainous regions.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
