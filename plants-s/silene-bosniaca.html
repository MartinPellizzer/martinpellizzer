Silene bosniaca

Silene bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other species like campion andachen.

Silene bosniaca is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
