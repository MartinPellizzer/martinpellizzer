Salacia longipes var. longipes

Salacia longipes var. longipes, commonly known as the longipes salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
