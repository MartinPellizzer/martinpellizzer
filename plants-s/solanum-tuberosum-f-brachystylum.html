Solanum tuberosum f. brachystylum

Solanum tuberosum f. brachystylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to the typical potato plant. This form exhibits unique morphological traits, including reduced internode length and a more bushy appearance. It is primarily cultivated for its tubers, which are similar in nutritional value to standard potatoes.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is adapted to temperate climates.
