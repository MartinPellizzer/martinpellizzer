Sacciolepis indica var. angusta

Sacciolepis indica var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in tidal zones and along shorelines. It is an evergreen shrub that produces capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
