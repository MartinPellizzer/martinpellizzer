Senecillis botryodes

Senecillis botryodes, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Senecillis botryodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
