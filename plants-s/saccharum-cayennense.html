Saccharum cayennense

Saccharum cayennense, commonly known as the Cayenne cane or a type of sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and large, elongated leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with abundant sunlight and water. Its primary use is in the production of sugar through the extraction of sucrose from its juice.

Saccharum cayennense plays a significant role in agricultural economies, particularly in regions where sugarcane cultivation is prevalent.
