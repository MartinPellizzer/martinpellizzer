Sarracenia fildesii

Sarracenia fildesii, commonly known as the Fildes' pitcher plant, is a species of carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form pitfall traps to capture prey. The plant's pitchers are typically reddish in color and have a waxy coating that aids in trapping insects. Sarracenia fildesii thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires a humid, sunny environment to flourish.

It is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
