Scirpus decipiens

Scirpus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Scirpus decipiens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
