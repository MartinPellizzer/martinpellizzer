Scirpus thouarsianus var. pallescens

Scirpus thouarsianus var. pallescens, commonly known as the silver reed, is a perennial wetland plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grayish-green stems that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
