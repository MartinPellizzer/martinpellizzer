Syzygium dictyophlebium

Syzygium dictyophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-veined satinash, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
