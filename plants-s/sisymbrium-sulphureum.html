Sisymbrium sulphureum

Sisymbrium sulphureum, commonly known as sulfur rocket, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately divided leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Sisymbrium sulphureum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth cycle.

It is also valued in some regions for its ornamental appeal and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
