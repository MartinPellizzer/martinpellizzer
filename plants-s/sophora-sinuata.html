Sophora sinuata

Sophora sinuata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive sinuous edge, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, appearing in long, drooping clusters. The plant produces flat, elongated seed pods that contain several small, hard seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
