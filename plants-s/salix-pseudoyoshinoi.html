Salix pseudoyoshinoi

Salix pseudoyoshinoi, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on top and pale underneath. It produces catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of pollination.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
