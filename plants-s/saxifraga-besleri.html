Saxifraga besleri

Saxifraga besleri, commonly known as the Besler's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga besleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
