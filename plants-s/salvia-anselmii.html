Salvia anselmii

Salvia anselmii, commonly known as Anselm's sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant leaves and long-lasting blooms.
