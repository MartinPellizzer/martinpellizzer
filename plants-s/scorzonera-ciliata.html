Scorzonera ciliata

Scorzonera ciliata, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Scorzonera ciliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
