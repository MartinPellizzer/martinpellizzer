Silene vulgaris var. antelopum

Silene vulgaris var. antelopum, commonly known as the antelope's head, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
