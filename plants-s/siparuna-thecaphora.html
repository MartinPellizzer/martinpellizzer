Siparuna thecaphora

Siparuna thecaphora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
