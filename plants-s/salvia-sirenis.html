Salvia sirenis

Salvia sirenis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a mild, minty scent. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Salvia sirenis is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
