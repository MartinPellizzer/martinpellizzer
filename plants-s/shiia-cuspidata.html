Shiia cuspidata

Shiia cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors during the growing season and in autumn. The plant has opposite, simple, and often serrated leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the cultivar. Its small, clustered flowers appear in spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Shiia cuspidata thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
