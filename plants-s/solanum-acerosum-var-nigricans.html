Solanum acerosum var. nigricans

Solanum acerosum var. nigricans, commonly known as black nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
