Specklinia caulophryne

Specklinia caulophryne, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and conservation programs.
