Stachys cordifolia

Stachys cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
