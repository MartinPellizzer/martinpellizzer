Scindapsus montanus

Scindapsus montanus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking silver veins that give it its common name. The plant is part of the Araceae family and is characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Scindapsus montanus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
