Sedum douglasii subsp. ciliosum

Sedum douglasii subsp.

ciliosum, commonly known as the Douglas's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
