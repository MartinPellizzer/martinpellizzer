Streptocarpus lutea

Streptocarpus lutea, commonly known as the yellow streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Streptocarpus lutea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
