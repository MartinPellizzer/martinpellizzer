Silene sericea var. bipartita

Silene sericea var. bipartita, commonly known as the silverleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
