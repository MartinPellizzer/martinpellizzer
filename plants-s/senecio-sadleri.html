Senecio sadleri

Senecio sadleri, commonly known as the "Snowflake" or "Cape snowflake," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, white or pale blue flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that appear in spring and summer. Senecio sadleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
