Setaria conglomerata

Setaria conglomerata, commonly known as clustered dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble small dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Setaria conglomerata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
