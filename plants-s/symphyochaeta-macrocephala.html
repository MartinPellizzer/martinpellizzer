Symphyochaeta macrocephala

Symphyochaeta macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed symphyochaeta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
