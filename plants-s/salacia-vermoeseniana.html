Salacia vermoeseniana

Salacia vermoeseniana, commonly known as the Malabar gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered and have four petals.

Salacia vermoeseniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
