Salix vagans f. bicolor

Salix vagans f. bicolor, commonly known as the twocolored willow, is a unique variant of the willow tree characterized by its striking bicolor leaves.

This plant exhibits a distinct leaf morphology with a green upper surface and a white or silver underside, creating a visually appealing contrast. It is typically found in moist, temperate environments, often near water sources such as streams or wetlands. The twocolored willow is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
