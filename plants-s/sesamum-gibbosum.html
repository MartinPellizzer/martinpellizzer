Sesamum gibbosum

Sesamum gibbosum, commonly known as the African sesame or round-seed sesame, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous small, round seeds. Sesamum gibbosum is primarily cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and medicinal practices.
