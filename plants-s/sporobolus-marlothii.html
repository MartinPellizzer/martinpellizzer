Sporobolus marlothii

Sporobolus marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sporobolus marlothii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
