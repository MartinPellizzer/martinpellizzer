Senecio cordovensis

Senecio cordovensis, commonly known as the Cordovensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
