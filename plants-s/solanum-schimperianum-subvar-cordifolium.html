Solanum schimperianum subvar. cordifolium

Solanum schimperianum subvar. cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
