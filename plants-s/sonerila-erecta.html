Sonerila erecta

Sonerila erecta, commonly known as the upright sonerila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, often violet or pink flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
