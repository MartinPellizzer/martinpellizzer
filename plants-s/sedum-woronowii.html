Sedum woronowii

Sedum woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sedum woronowii is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in gardens.
