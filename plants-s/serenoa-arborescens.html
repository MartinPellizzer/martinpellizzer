Serenoa arborescens

Serenoa arborescens, commonly known as the saw palmetto, is a slow-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fan-shaped leaves and clustered, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that are often used in herbal remedies. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a generally low, sprawling growth habit.

The saw palmetto is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in humid, subtropical climates.
