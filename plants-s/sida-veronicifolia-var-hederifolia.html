Sida veronicifolia var. hederifolia

Sida veronicifolia var. hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping sida, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
