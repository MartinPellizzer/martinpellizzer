Sorbus theophrasta

Sorbus theophrasta, commonly known as the Persian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
