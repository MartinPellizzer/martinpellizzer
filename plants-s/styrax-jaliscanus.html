Styrax jaliscanus

Styrax jaliscanus, commonly known as the Mexican styrax, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
