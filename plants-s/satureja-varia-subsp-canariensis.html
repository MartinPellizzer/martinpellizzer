Satureja varia subsp. canariensis

Satureja varia subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as Canary thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, emitting a strong, minty aroma when crushed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local cuisine and herbal remedies.
