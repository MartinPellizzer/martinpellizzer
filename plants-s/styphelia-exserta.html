Styphelia exserta

Styphelia exserta, commonly known as the slender snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Styphelia exserta thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, cool environments.
