Scutellaria peregrina var. adenotricha

Scutellaria peregrina var. adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Scutellaria peregrina var. adenotricha is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
