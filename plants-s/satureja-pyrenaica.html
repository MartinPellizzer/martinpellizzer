Satureja pyrenaica

Satureja pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean savory, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds.

Satureja pyrenaica is valued for its pungent fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
