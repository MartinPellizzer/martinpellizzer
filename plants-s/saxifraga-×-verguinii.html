Saxifraga × verguinii

Saxifraga × verguinii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus, exhibiting traits from both parent species.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
