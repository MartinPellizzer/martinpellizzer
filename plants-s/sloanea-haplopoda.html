Sloanea haplopoda

Sloanea haplopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Sloanea haplopoda is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments, making it a common understory species in tropical rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in construction and furniture-making.
