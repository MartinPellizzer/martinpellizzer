Sterigma sulphureum

Sterigma sulphureum, commonly known as the sulfur tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a distinctive sulfur-like odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along riverbanks and in sandy soils.

The botanical features of Sterigma sulphureum make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
