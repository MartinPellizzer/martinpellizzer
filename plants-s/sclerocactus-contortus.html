Sclerocactus contortus

Sclerocactus contortus, commonly known as the twisted spine cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its dense, ribbed stems that are often contorted or twisted, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spines that provide shade and reduce water loss.

Its slow growth rate and distinctive morphology make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
