Solanum hostmannii

Solanum hostmannii, commonly known as the hostmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and small, green leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
