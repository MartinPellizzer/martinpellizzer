Setaria dubia

Setaria dubia, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa but now found in various parts of the world.

It is a annual plant that grows in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, disturbed soils, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces spikelets that are often yellow or brown in color. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments.

Setaria dubia is also considered a weed in some regions due to its invasive potential.
