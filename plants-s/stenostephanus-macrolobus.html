Stenostephanus macrolobus

Stenostephanus macrolobus, commonly known as the giant rock fig, is a woody shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that attracts various birds and mammals.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.
