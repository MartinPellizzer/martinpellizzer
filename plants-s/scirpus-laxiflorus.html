Scirpus laxiflorus

Scirpus laxiflorus, commonly known as the soft-flowered bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of green to brown. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus laxiflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
