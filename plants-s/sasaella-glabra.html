Sasaella glabra

Sasaella glabra, commonly known as the smooth sasa, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicles, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Sasaella glabra is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
