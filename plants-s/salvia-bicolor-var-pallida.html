Salvia bicolor var. pallida

Salvia bicolor var. pallida, commonly known as the pale blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
