Strobocalyx pyrrhopappa

Strobocalyx pyrrhopappa, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Strobocalyx pyrrhopappa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
