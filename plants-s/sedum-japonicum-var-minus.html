Sedum japonicum var. minus

Sedum japonicum var. minus, commonly known as Japanese stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Japan.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
