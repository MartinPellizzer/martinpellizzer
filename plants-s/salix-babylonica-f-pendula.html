Salix babylonica f. pendula

Salix babylonica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is native to China but has been widely cultivated in temperate regions around the world. The plant features long, narrow, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soil and is often planted near water features or in areas with high groundwater.

The weeping willow is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to provide shade and habitat for wildlife.
