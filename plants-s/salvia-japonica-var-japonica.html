Salvia japonica var. japonica

Salvia japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Salvia japonica var. japonica prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
