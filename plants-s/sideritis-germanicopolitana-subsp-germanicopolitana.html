Sideritis germanicopolitana subsp. germanicopolitana

Sideritis germanicopolitana subsp.

germanicopolitana, commonly known as the Greek ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
