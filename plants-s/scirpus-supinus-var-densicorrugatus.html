Scirpus supinus var. densicorrugatus

Scirpus supinus var. densicorrugatus, commonly known as the dense-ribbed bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush genus Scirpus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with dense, ribbed nodes, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
