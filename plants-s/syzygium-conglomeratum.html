Syzygium conglomeratum

Syzygium conglomeratum, commonly known as the satinwood or white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces woody fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium conglomeratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and durable wood, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
