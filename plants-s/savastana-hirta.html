Savastana hirta

Savastana hirta, commonly known as the hairy savastana, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive look.

The small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
