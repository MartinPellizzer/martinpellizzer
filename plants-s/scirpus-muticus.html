Scirpus muticus

Scirpus muticus, commonly known as the common tussock sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Scirpus muticus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
