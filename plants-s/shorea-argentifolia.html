Shorea argentifolia

Shorea argentifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves that have a silvery underside. The tree produces heavy, spiky fruits that contain a creamy, aromatic pulp. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local economies.

The plant plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native rainforests.
