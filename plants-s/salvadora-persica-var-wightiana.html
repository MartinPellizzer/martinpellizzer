Salvadora persica var. wightiana

Salvadora persica var. wightiana, commonly known as Indian toothbrush, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found growing in rocky soils. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
