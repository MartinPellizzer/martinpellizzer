Salix delavayana f. glabra

Salix delavayana f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a variant of the Delavay willow, characterized by its smooth, hairless bark.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and it produces catkins in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing stream banks.
