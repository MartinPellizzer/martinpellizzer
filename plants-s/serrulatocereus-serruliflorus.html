Serrulatocereus serruliflorus

Serrulatocereus serruliflorus, commonly known as the serrated-flowered cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white spines and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique floral structure and spiny appearance make it a distinctive addition to desert gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
