Sambucus thunbergii

Sambucus thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Sambucus thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
