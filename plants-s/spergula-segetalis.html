Spergula segetalis

Spergula segetalis, commonly known as yellow wheatgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a small, annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils and fields. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. It is often found in agricultural areas and can be a common weed in cereal crops.

Spergula segetalis is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its rapid growth cycle.
