Senecio glaucus subsp. coronopifolius

Senecio glaucus subsp.

coronopifolius, commonly known as the blueweed or crown-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and the subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive, crown-like leaf shape.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
