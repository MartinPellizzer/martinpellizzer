Stachys alpigena subsp. longipetala

Stachys alpigena subsp.

longipetala, commonly known as the alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are arranged in whorled clusters, producing pale blue to violet blossoms that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at higher elevations.
