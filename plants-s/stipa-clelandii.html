Stipa clelandii

Stipa clelandii, commonly known as the Cleland's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Stipa clelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
