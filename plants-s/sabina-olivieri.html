Sabina olivieri

Sabina olivieri, commonly known as the Mediterranean juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, Sabina olivieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
