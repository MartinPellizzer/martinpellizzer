Saussurea firma

Saussurea firma, commonly known as the rigid safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and numerous ray florets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
