Styrax paulhousei

Styrax paulhousei, commonly known as the Paulhouse styrax, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
