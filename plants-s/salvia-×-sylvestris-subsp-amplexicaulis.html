Salvia × sylvestris subsp. amplexicaulis

Salvia × sylvestris subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
