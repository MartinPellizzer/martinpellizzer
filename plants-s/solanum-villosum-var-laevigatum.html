Solanum villosum var. laevigatum

Solanum villosum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color, often containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are usually hairy or pubescent, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.
