Sphenogyne pauciloba

Sphenogyne pauciloba, commonly known as the rare swamp holly, is a unique aquatic plant found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, enhancing its ability to colonize new habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
