Silene damboldtiana

Silene damboldtiana, commonly known as the Damboldt's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene damboldtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
