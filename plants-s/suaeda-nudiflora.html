Suaeda nudiflora

Suaeda nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor in sandy or saline soils.

Suaeda nudiflora plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
