Synaedrys elaeagnifolia

Synaedrys elaeagnifolia, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Synaedrys elaeagnifolia is notable for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
