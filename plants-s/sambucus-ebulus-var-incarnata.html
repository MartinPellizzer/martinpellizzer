Sambucus ebulus var. incarnata

Sambucus ebulus var. incarnata, commonly known as red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white and clustered in umbels. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are toxic when raw but can be used in cooking after proper preparation.
