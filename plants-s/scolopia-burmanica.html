Scolopia burmanica

Scolopia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Scolopia burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
