Soja angustifolia

Soja angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf soybean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cuisines.

Soja angustifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
