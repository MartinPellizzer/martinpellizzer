Senna cana

Senna cana, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Senna cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
