Sabicea bullata

Sabicea bullata, commonly known as the clustered sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Sabicea bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
