Saussurea inversa

Saussurea inversa, commonly known as the inverted saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
