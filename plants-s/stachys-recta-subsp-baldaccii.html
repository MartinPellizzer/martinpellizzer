Stachys recta subsp. baldaccii

Stachys recta subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting flowers.
