Spiranthes nutantiflora

Spiranthes nutantiflora, commonly known as the nodding spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are often clustered in a loose spiral, giving it its common name.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
