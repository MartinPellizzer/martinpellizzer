Strophanthus schlechteri

Strophanthus schlechteri, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with tufts of silky hairs.

The plant is notable for containing cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine.
