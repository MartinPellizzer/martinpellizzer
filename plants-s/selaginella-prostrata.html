Selaginella prostrata

Selaginella prostrata, commonly known as the creeping spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its prostrate stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella prostrata is known for its ability to survive in adverse conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
