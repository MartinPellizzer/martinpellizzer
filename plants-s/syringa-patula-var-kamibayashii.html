Syringa patula var. kamibayashii

Syringa patula var. kamibayashii, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are dark green, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
