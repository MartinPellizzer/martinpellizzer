Senna artemisioides subsp. artemisioides

Senna artemisioides subsp.

artemisioides, commonly known as the blue senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. It is characterized by its woody stem and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
