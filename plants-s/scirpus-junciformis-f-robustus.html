Scirpus junciformis f. robustus

Scirpus junciformis f. robustus, commonly known as the robust bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush, characterized by its thick, sturdy stems.

It is a perennial wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brownish in color. Its robust growth habit makes it an effective colonizer of disturbed wetland areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
