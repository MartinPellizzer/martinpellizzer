Stachys hamata

Stachys hamata, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with purple or pink petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Stachys hamata is an herbaceous perennial that can spread via rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
