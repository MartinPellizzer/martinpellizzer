Stapelia juttae

Stapelia juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with a distinctive waxy texture. Stapelia juttae is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it an interesting addition to succulent collections.
