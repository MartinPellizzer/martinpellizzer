Stachys macrosperma

Stachys macrosperma, commonly known as large-seeded hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, with purple to pinkish bracts that attract pollinators.

Stachys macrosperma typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
