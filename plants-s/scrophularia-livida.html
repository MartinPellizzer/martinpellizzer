Scrophularia livida

Scrophularia livida, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of purple or pink flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia livida is often found in open woodland areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
