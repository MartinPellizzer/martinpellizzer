Salix lanata var. chrysantha

Salix lanata var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden willow, is a variant of the woolly willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in wetland environments. Its leaves are broad and hairy, providing a unique texture, while its branches often droop gracefully.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in wet areas.
