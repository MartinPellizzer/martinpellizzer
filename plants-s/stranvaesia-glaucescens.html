Stranvaesia glaucescens

Stranvaesia glaucescens, commonly known as the Cape rose or silverleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, silvery-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Stranvaesia glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
