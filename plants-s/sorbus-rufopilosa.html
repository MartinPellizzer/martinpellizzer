Sorbus rufopilosa

Sorbus rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Sorbus rufopilosa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
