Senecio burchellii

Senecio burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually blue-gray in color, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio burchellii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
