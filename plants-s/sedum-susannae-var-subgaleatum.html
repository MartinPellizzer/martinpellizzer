Sedum susannae var. subgaleatum

Sedum susannae var. subgaleatum, commonly known as the subgaleatum stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, rounded leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
