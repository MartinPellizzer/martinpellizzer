Silene masmenaea

Silene masmenaea, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Silene masmenaea plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
