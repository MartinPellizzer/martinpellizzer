Salix setchelliana

Salix setchelliana, commonly known as the Setchell willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins that are characteristic of the Salix genus. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix setchelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
