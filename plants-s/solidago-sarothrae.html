Solidago sarothrae

Solidago sarothrae, commonly known as the Sierra goldflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its native environment.

Solidago sarothrae is adapted to arid conditions and has a low, spreading growth habit.
