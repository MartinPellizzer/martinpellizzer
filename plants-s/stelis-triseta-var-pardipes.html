Stelis triseta var. pardipes

Stelis triseta var. pardipes, commonly known as the spotted stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its stems are short and often branched, with leaves that are elliptical and arranged in two rows. The flowers are small, fragrant, and come in shades of white and pink, often with contrasting markings.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
