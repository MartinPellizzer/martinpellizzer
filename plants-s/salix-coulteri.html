Salix coulteri

Salix coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, typically in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky conditions.

Coulter's willow is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to arid environments.
