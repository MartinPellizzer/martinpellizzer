Saccharum brunneum

Saccharum brunneum, commonly known as the yellow grass or brown sugarcane, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in moist, tropical climates. Its fibrous stalks are sometimes used for thatching or as a source of fiber.

While not as economically important as sugarcane, it plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
