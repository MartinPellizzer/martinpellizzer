Syngramma pinnata

Syngramma pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Syngramma pinnata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
