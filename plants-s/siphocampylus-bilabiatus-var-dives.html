Siphocampylus bilabiatus var. dives

Siphocampylus bilabiatus var. dives, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
