Symplocos caudata

Symplocos caudata, commonly known as the trailing ardisia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a reddish midrib. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories.

Symplocos caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
