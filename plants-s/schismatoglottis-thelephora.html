Schismatoglottis thelephora

Schismatoglottis thelephora, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schismatoglottis thelephora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
