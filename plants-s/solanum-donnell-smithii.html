Solanum donnell-smithii

Solanum donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Donnell-Smith nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.

It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
