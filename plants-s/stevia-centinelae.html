Stevia centinelae

Stevia centinelae, commonly known as the centinela stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested for use in herbal teas and as a natural sweetener alternative.

Stevia centinelae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for both ornamental and functional gardening.
