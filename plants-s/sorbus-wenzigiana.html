Sorbus wenzigiana

Sorbus wenzigiana, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
