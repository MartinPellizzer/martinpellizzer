Sarcocapnos saetabensis

Sarcocapnos saetabensis, commonly known as the thorny caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Sarcocapnos saetabensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
