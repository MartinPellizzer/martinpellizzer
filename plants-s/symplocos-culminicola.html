Symplocos culminicola

Symplocos culminicola, commonly known as the highland symplocos, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are white or pale green, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

Symplocos culminicola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
