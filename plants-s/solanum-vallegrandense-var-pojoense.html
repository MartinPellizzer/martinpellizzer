Solanum vallegrandense var. pojoense

Solanum vallegrandense var. pojoense, commonly known as the Pojoense nightshade, is a species native to Mexico, specifically the state of Oaxaca.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five lobes, and are clustered in inflorescences at the base of the plant. The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable species such as tomatoes and peppers.
