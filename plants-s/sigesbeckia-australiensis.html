Sigesbeckia australiensis

Sigesbeckia australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sigesbeckia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its resilience to salt spray and arid conditions, making it a common sight in coastal ecosystems.
