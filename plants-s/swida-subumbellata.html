Swida subumbellata

Swida subumbellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

Swida subumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
