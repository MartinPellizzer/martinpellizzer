Saxifraga sulphurea

Saxifraga sulphurea, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, yellowish-green leaves and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas where it forms dense mats.

Saxifraga sulphurea is an important plant in its ecosystem, providing food for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
