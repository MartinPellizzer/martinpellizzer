Salvia fracta

Salvia fracta, commonly known as the broken sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue and produces violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Salvia fracta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
