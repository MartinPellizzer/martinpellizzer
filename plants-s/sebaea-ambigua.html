Sebaea ambigua

Sebaea ambigua, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. It produces small, toxic berries that are often mistaken for edible fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires caution when handling.
