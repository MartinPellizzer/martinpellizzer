Senecio tarapacanus

Senecio tarapacanus, commonly known as the Atacama Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a succulent shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise sparse appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, adapting to extreme drought conditions.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a symbol of resilience in one of the world's driest deserts.
