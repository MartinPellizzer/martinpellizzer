Scrophularia paui var. oblongifolia

Scrophularia paui var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
