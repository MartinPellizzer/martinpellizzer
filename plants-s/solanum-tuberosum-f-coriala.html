Solanum tuberosum f. coriala

Solanum tuberosum f. coriala, commonly known as the coriala potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated tubers with a rough, netted skin. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to South America. It typically grows in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and potential culinary uses.
