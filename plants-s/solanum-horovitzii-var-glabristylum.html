Solanum horovitzii var. glabristylum

Solanum horovitzii var. glabristylum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
