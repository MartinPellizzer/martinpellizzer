Stellaria ciliatosepala subsp. laxmannii

Stellaria ciliatosepala subsp.

laxmannii, commonly known as the hairy starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The subspecies laxmannii is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and slightly different floral morphology compared to other subspecies.
