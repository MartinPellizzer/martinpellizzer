Silene undulata subsp. polyantha

Silene undulata subsp.

polyantha, commonly known as the wavy hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a wavy margin, giving it its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.
