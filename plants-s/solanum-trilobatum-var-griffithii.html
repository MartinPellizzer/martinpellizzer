Solanum trilobatum var. griffithii

Solanum trilobatum var. griffithii, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
