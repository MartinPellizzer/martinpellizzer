Spiesia mandshurica

Spiesia mandshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern spurge, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Spiesia mandshurica is known for its latex-like sap, which can be irritant to the skin.
