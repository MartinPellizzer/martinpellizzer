Solanum nigrum f. judaicum

Solanum nigrum f. judaicum, commonly known as the Judean black nightshade, is a botanical variant of the common nightshade.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy stems. Native to the Middle East and Mediterranean regions, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its foliage and fruit have been historically used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
