Solanum mendocianum

Solanum mendocianum, commonly known as the Mendoza nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and hairy stems.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
