Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp. longi-tommii

Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp.

longi-tommii, commonly known as the long-stalked streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months.

The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
