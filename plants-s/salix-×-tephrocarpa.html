Salix × tephrocarpa

Salix × tephrocarpa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, often giving it a graceful appearance. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, though it has been widely introduced elsewhere. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Salix × tephrocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
