Syagrus pompeoi

Syagrus pompeoi, commonly known as the Pompeii palm, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a dramatic, palm-like appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.

Syagrus pompeoi is also notable for its ability to withstand salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal environments.
