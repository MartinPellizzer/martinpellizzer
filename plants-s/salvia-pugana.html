Salvia pugana

Salvia pugana, commonly known as Andean sage, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia pugana thrives in cool, dry climates and is well adapted to rocky, alpine environments.
