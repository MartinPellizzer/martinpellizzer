Solanum ipecachuana

Solanum ipecachuana, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are typically red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
