Sorbus zahlbruckneri

Sorbus zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
