Solidago spithamaea

Solidago spithamaea, commonly known as the dwarf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago spithamaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
