Sorghum nutans subvar. pellitum

Sorghum nutans subvar. pellitum, commonly known as the drooping sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping panicles and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetland or marshy environments. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is also valued for its potential in forage and biofuel production.
