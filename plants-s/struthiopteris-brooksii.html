Struthiopteris brooksii

Struthiopteris brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' wood fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, slender rhizome and produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Struthiopteris brooksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
