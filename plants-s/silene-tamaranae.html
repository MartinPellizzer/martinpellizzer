Silene tamaranae

Silene tamaranae, commonly known as the Tamaranae campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking pink to purple flowers, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to the rocky, coastal environments of its habitat. Silene tamaranae is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

It is also of interest to botanists due to its unique evolutionary adaptations to its arid environment.
