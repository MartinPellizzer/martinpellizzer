Salvia fasciculata

Salvia fasciculata, commonly known as meadow sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
