Salix sericophylla

Salix sericophylla, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
