Schizoglossum robustum

Schizoglossum robustum, commonly known as the robust glover's root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Schizoglossum robustum is known for its fibrous roots, which are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
