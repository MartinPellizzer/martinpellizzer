Stevia coahuilensis

Stevia coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Stevia coahuilensis is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
