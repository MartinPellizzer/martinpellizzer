Selaginella underwoodii var. dolichotricha

Selaginella underwoodii var. dolichotricha, commonly known as the underwood's clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features closely packed, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. Its sporophylls are distinctive, with elongated, thread-like structures that bear spores.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in humid, nutrient-rich soils.
