Streblosa urticina

Streblosa urticina, commonly known as the stinging nettle.

This plant is a member of the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves and stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a rash in humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Streblosa urticina is a perennial plant that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with small greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.
