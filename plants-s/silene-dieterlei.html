Silene dieterlei

Silene dieterlei, commonly known as the Dieterlei catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Silene dieterlei is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance to pollinators.
