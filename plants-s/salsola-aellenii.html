Salsola aellenii

Salsola aellenii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure that helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually small and succulent to reduce water loss.

Salsola aellenii is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
