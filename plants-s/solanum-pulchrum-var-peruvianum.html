Solanum pulchrum var. peruvianum

Solanum pulchrum var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a yellow throat. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments.
