Synadenium glaucescens var. brevipes

Synadenium glaucescens var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically small and alternate, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The species is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ornamental appearance.
