Silene crassifolia

Silene crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. Silene crassifolia is often found in tundra and mountainous regions where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
