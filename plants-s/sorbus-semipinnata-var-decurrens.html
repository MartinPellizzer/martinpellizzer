Sorbus semipinnata var. decurrens

Sorbus semipinnata var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate leaves that often have a silvery or white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
