Sorbus aria subsp. koevessii

Sorbus aria subsp.

koevessii, commonly known as the Koevessii whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to the Caucasus region. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, oval leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The fruit is a bright red, persistent pomes that remain on the tree well into winter. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
