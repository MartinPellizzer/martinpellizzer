Sorbus bulleyana

Sorbus bulleyana, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Sorbus bulleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
