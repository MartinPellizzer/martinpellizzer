Sterculia rubicunda

Sterculia rubicunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
