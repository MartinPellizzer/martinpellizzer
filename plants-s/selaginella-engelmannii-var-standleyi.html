Selaginella engelmannii var. standleyi

Selaginella engelmannii var. standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a rosette-like growth pattern with closely packed, scale-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, a characteristic feature of the lycophyte group.

This variant is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
