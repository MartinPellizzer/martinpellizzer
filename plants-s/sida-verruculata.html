Sida verruculata

Sida verruculata, commonly known as rough sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Sida verruculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
