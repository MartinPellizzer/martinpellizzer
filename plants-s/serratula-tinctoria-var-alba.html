Serratula tinctoria var. alba

Serratula tinctoria var. alba, commonly known as white sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive serrated margin, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
