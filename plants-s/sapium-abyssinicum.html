Sapium abyssinicum

Sapium abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian sappan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that split open to reveal seeds. It is valued for its heartwood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
