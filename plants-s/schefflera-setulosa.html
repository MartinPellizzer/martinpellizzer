Schefflera setulosa

Schefflera setulosa, commonly known as the hairy schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of opposite, compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and landscaping purposes.
