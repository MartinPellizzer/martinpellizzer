Senecio glaucescens

Senecio glaucescens, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Senecio glaucescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
