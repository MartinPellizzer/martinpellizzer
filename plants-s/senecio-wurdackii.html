Senecio wurdackii

Senecio wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio wurdackii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
