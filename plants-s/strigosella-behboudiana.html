Strigosella behboudiana

Strigosella behboudiana, commonly known as the Persian sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
