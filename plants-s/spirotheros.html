Spirotheros

Spirotheros, commonly known as the spiral-rooted herb, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate root system that spirals downward into the soil.

Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pale yellow and white, which bloom during the early summer months. Spirotheros thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its rare occurrence, it is considered a specialty plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
