Sporobolus mirabilis

Sporobolus mirabilis, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stems. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that allows it to colonize open, disturbed areas effectively.

Sporobolus mirabilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
