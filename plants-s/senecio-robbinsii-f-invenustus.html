Senecio robbinsii f. invenustus

Senecio robbinsii f. invenustus, commonly known as the Robbin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and fuzzy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
