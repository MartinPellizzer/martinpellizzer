Spathipappus porphyrostephanus

Spathipappus porphyrostephanus, commonly known as the redsteppe thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to reddish bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
