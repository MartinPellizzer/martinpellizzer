Sideritis × angustifolia subsp. murcica

Sideritis × angustifolia subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of pink to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
