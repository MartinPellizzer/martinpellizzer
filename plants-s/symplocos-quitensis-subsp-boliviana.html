Symplocos quitensis subsp. boliviana

Symplocos quitensis subsp.

boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, leathery berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests and montane habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
