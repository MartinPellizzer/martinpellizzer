Sinapis viminea

Sinapis viminea, commonly known as the slender mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lobed, with a slightly hairy surface.

Sinapis viminea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and fields.
