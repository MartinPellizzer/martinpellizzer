Statice arctica subsp. californica

Statice arctica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California sea blush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California. It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink to red flowers in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
