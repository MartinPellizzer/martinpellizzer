Solanum bojeri var. deckenii

Solanum bojeri var. deckenii, commonly known as the Bojer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a yellow or orange color.
