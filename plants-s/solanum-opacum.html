Solanum opacum

Solanum opacum, commonly known as the darkleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, almost black leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white. It produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum opacum is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and thickets.
