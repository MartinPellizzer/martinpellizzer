Scolymocephalus calocephalus

Scolymocephalus calocephalus, commonly known as the hairy-headed scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scolymocephalus calocephalus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
