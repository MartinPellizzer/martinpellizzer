Scirpus chamissonis

Scirpus chamissonis, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that emerge from the water or moist soil. The plant thrives in shallow water or saturated soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpus chamissonis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
