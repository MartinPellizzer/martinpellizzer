Salvia lantanifolia

Salvia lantanifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Salvia lantanifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
