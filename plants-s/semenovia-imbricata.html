Semenovia imbricata

Semenovia imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and its stems are usually short and unbranched.

The species is noted for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it well adapted to its natural habitat.
