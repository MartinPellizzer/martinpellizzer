Smilax laurifolia var. bupleurifolia

Smilax laurifolia var. bupleurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikelike inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the native flora in its habitat.
