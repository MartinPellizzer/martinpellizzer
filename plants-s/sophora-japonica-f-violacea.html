Sophora japonica f. violacea

Sophora japonica f. violacea, commonly known as the purple pagoda tree, is a cultivar of the pagoda tree species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant color to the landscape. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its showy blooms and upright growth habit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
