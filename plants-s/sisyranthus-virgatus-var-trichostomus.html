Sisyranthus virgatus var. trichostomus

Sisyranthus virgatus var. trichostomus, commonly known as the silver-leafed sisyranthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
