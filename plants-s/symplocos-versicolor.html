Symplocos versicolor

Symplocos versicolor, commonly known as the variable evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that change color throughout the seasons, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
