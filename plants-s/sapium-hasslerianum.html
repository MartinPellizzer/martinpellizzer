Sapium hasslerianum

Sapium hasslerianum, commonly known as the American sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that peels in thin layers. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces capsule fruits containing seeds.

The plant is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
