Saccharum dubium

Saccharum dubium, commonly known as the uncertain cane.

It is a grass species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant is closely related to sugarcane but is less commonly cultivated for commercial sugar production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetland or marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural research.
