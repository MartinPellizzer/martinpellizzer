Silene inaperta var. scabra

Silene inaperta var. scabra, commonly known as the rough silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy or rough-textured surfaces, giving it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive rough or scabrous texture along the edges.
