Scirpus lawianus

Scirpus lawianus, commonly known as the New Zealand bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and lake margins, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
