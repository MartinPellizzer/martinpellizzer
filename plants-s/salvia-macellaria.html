Salvia macellaria

Salvia macellaria, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia macellaria is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
