Scilla begoniifolia

Scilla begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of begonias, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Scilla begoniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
