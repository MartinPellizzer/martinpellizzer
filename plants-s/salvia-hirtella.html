Salvia hirtella

Salvia hirtella, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
