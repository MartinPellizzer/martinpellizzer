Stachys acutifolia

Stachys acutifolia, commonly known as sharpleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Stachys acutifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
