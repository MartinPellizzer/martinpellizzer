Sesuvium erectum

Sesuvium erectum, commonly known as rock saltbush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh coastal environments. Its ability to store water in its leaves helps it survive in arid conditions.

Sesuvium erectum is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
