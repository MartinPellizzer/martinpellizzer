Silene californica

Silene californica, commonly known as the California flax or red-flowered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators like moths and bats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.
