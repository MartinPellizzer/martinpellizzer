Solanum parvicorollatum

Solanum parvicorollatum, commonly known as the small corolla nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, white or pale purple flowers. Its leaves are ovate and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
