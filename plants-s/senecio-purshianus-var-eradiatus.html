Senecio purshianus var. eradiatus

Senecio purshianus var. eradiatus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a low-growing habit with a woody base and is adapted to cold, dry environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native range.
