Sabatia corymbosa var. angustifolia

Sabatia corymbosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, angular stems and opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as dunes and coastal bluffs.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
