Salvia robertoana

Salvia robertoana, commonly known as the Mexican bush sage, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
