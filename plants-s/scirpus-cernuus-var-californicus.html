Scirpus cernuus var. californicus

Scirpus cernuus var. californicus, commonly known as California bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that often grow up to 2 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is typically found in marshes, pond edges, and other moist environments.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
