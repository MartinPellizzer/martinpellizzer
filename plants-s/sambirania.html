Sambirania

Sambirania, commonly known as the "Carnivorous Plant of the Amazon," is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their unique ability to trap and digest insects using specialized leaf structures. The leaves of Sambirania are typically broad and possess a sticky surface that aids in capturing prey. They thrive in humid, nutrient-poor environments, often found in rainforests.

Their botanical significance lies in their adaptation to nutrient-deficient soils through carnivory.
