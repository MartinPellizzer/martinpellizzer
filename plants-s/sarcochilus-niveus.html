Sarcochilus niveus

Sarcochilus niveus, commonly known as the White Lady orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its elegant appearance.
