Senecio chiapensis

Senecio chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas senecio, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a cluster, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
