Solanum flahaultii

Solanum flahaultii, commonly known as the Flahault's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are yellow when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. Solanum flahaultii is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
