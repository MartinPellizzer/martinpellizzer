Sabazia triangularis var. papposa

Sabazia triangularis var. papposa, commonly known as the hairy triangleleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
