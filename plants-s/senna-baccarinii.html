Senna baccarinii

Senna baccarinii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Senna baccarinii typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting well to dry conditions.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.
