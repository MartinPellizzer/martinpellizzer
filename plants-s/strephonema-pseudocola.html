Strephonema pseudocola

Strephonema pseudocola, commonly known as the false broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host plants.
