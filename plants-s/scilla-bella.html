Scilla bella

Scilla bella, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or woodland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, appearing after the flowers have faded.

Scilla bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
