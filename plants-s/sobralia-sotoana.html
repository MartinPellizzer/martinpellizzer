Sobralia sotoana

Sobralia sotoana, commonly known as the Sotoana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip for attracting pollinators.

Sobralia sotoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
