Syzygium neepau

Syzygium neepau, commonly known as the Neepau apple, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its small, apple-like fruits. The plant produces white or pink flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by the development of its edible, red or yellow fruits. Syzygium neepau is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a mild aromatic scent when crushed.
