Salvia ali-askaryi

Salvia ali-askaryi, commonly known as the Afghan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia ali-askaryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
