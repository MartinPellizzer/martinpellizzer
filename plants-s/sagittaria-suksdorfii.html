Sagittaria suksdorfii

Sagittaria suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with some leaves extending above the water surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediment in shallow waters.

Sagittaria suksdorfii is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and as a food source for waterfowl.
