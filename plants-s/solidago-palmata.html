Solidago palmata

Solidago palmata, commonly known as the palmleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Solidago palmata plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during its blooming season.
