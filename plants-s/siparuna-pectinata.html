Siparuna pectinata

Siparuna pectinata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Siparuna pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
