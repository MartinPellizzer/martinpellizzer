Scrophularia subaphylla

Scrophularia subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes a variety of flowering plants often found in temperate and alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia subaphylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.
