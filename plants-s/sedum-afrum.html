Sedum afrum

Sedum afrum, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It grows in a compact, upright form and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Sedum afrum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it an ideal choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
