Stachys garana

Stachys garana, commonly known as redstem deadnettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Stachys garana is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and use in traditional medicine.
