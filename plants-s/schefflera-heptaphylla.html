Schefflera heptaphylla

Schefflera heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like cluster of leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl of seven at the ends of the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black berries.

Schefflera heptaphylla is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
