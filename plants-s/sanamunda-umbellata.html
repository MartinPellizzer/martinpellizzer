Sanamunda umbellata

Sanamunda umbellata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of clustered umbels of flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons.

Sanamunda umbellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
