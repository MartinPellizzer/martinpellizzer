Senecio bayonnensis

Senecio bayonnensis, commonly known as the Bayonnensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
