Salix × fragilis f. bullata

Salix × fragilis f. bullata, commonly known as the weeping willow with bulge, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution.
