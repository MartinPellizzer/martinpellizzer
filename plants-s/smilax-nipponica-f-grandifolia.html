Smilax nipponica f. grandifolia

Smilax nipponica f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or large-flowered smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to East Asia.

It is a vigorous climbing shrub characterized by its long, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
