Scutellaria irrasa

Scutellaria irrasa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Scutellaria irrasa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
