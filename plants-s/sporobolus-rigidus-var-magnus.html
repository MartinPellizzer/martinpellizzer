Sporobolus rigidus var. magnus

Sporobolus rigidus var. magnus, commonly known as the giant needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, rigid stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its robust growth makes it an important component of desert ecosystems and a valuable forage plant for livestock.
