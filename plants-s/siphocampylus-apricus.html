Siphocampylus apricus

Siphocampylus apricus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
