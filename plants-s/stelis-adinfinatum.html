Stelis adinfinatum

Stelis adinfinatum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often resembling spiders due to their unique shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Stelis adinfinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
