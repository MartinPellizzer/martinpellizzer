Siphocampylus fluminensis var. oppositifolius

Siphocampylus fluminensis var. oppositifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for shade gardens and conservation efforts.
