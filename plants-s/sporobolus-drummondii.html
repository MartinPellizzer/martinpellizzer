Sporobolus drummondii

Sporobolus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Sporobolus drummondii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
