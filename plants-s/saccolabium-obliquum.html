Saccolabium obliquum

Saccolabium obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations to dry environments include thick, waxy leaves and deep root systems.

Saccolabium obliquum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
