Spiraea arborea grandis

Spiraea arborea grandis, commonly known as the large shrub spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features arching clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hair. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Spiraea arborea grandis is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
