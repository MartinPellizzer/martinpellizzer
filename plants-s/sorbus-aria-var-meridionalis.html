Sorbus aria var. meridionalis

Sorbus aria var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or rocky areas.
