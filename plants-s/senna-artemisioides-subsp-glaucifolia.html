Senna artemisioides subsp. glaucifolia

Senna artemisioides subsp.

glaucifolia, commonly known as the glaucous senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its narrow, glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system for water storage. It produces small, ribbed pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
