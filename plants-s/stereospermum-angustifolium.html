Stereospermum angustifolium

Stereospermum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved champa, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
