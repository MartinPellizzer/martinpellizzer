Strychnos xantha

Strychnos xantha, commonly known as the golden strychnos, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, yellow pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
