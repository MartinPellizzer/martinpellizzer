Salsola komarovii

Salsola komarovii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.

Salsola komarovii is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed areas.
