Senecio rosmarinus var. ascotanensis

Senecio rosmarinus var. ascotanensis, commonly known as the Ascotanensis Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
