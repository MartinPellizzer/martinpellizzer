Solidago procera var. salicifolia

Solidago procera var. salicifolia, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
