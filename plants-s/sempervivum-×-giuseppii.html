Sempervivum × giuseppii

Sempervivum × giuseppii, commonly known as the crested houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, often variegated leaves that range in color from green to blue-gray. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
