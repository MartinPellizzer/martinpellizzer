Sorbus aucuparia subsp. meinichii

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

meinichii, commonly known as the Meinhof's rowan, is a subspecies of the rowan tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, orange-red berries that are typically clustered in umbels.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, often turning a striking red in autumn. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its vibrant fall foliage and edible fruits.
