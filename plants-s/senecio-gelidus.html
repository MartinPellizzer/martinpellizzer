Senecio gelidus

Senecio gelidus, commonly known as the Antarctic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in alpine and subantarctic environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of extreme environments.
