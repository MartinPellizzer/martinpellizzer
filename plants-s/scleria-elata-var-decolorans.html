Scleria elata var. decolorans

Scleria elata var. decolorans, commonly known as the pale rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in dense clusters. Its stems are usually pale green, which contributes to its common name.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
