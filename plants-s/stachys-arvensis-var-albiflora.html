Stachys arvensis var. albiflora

Stachys arvensis var. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower hedge parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wildflower gardens.
