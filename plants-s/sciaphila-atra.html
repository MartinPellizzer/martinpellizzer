Sciaphila atra

Sciaphila atra, commonly known as the black shade plant, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the dense, shaded understories of tropical rainforests in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish-green foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and requires high humidity and well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its unique adaptations to shaded environments make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

Due to habitat destruction, Sciaphila atra is considered endangered and is often difficult to locate in the wild.
