Syringa bicolor

Syringa bicolor, commonly known as the white and purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring, featuring a striking combination of white and purple hues. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa bicolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
