Sasa nishigoensis

Sasa nishigoensis, commonly known as the Nishigo bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sasa nishigoensis is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
