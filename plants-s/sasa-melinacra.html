Sasa melinacra

Sasa melinacra, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly reddish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa melinacra is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
