Senecio chapacensis

Senecio chapacensis, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio chapacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
