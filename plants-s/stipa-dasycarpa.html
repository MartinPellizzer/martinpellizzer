Stipa dasycarpa

Stipa dasycarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruit grass or woolly needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned lemmas. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.

Stipa dasycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in grassland ecosystems.
