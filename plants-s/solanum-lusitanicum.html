Solanum lusitanicum

Solanum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
