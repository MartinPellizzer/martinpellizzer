Solanum xanthophaeum

Solanum xanthophaeum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it often grows in dry, open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
