Salix × strepida

Salix × strepida, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree that exhibits striking ornamental features.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a unique and visually appealing silhouette. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, and its leaves are typically broad and serrated. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

Botanically, it is valued for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
