Saturnia chamaemoly

Saturnia chamaemoly, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Saturnia chamaemoly is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
