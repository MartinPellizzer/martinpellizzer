Sedum telephium var. apricum

Sedum telephium var. apricum, commonly known as Orange Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water, which is typical of many succulents. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
