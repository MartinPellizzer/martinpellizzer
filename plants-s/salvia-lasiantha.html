Salvia lasiantha

Salvia lasiantha, commonly known as the desert sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Salvia lasiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
