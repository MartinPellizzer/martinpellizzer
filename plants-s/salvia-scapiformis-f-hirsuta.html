Salvia scapiformis f. hirsuta

Salvia scapiformis f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy scabious, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
