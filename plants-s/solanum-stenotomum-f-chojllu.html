Solanum stenotomum f. chojllu

Solanum stenotomum f. chojllu, commonly known as the Chojllu tomato, is a botanical variant of the Andean tomato species.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elongated fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine.
