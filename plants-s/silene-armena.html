Silene armena

Silene armena, commonly known as Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Silene armena is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
