Stachys lanata var. parvifolia

Stachys lanata var. parvifolia, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and flowering display.
