Sulcorebutia elizabethae

Sulcorebutia elizabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's Sulcorebutia, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive rib structure that aids in water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for arid environments.
