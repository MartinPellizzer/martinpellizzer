Secale vavilovii

Secale vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's rye, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its genetic diversity, it has been studied for its potential in breeding programs for improved rye varieties.
