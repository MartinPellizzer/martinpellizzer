Saracha jaltomata

Saracha jaltomata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist soils.

Saracha jaltomata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cultures.
