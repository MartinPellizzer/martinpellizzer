Sooja japonica

Sooja japonica, commonly known as Japanese evergreen holly, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Sooja japonica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
