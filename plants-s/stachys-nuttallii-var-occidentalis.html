Stachys nuttallii var. occidentalis

Stachys nuttallii var. occidentalis, commonly known as western hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and as a component of native plant communities.
