Saccolabium hendersonianum

Saccolabium hendersonianum, commonly known as Henderson's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. It has a woody stem and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
