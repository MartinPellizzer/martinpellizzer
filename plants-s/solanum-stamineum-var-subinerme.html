Solanum stamineum var. subinerme

Solanum stamineum var. subinerme, commonly known as the velvetleaf or common morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
