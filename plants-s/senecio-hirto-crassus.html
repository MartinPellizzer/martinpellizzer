Senecio hirto-crassus

Senecio hirto-crassus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
