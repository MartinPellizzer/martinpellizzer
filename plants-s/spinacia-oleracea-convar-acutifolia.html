Spinacia oleracea convar. acutifolia

Spinacia oleracea convar. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved spinach, is a variant of the common spinach plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are typically more serrated than those of the standard variety. This subspecies is often cultivated for its tender, flavorful leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamins. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is a biennial, but it is usually grown as an annual in agricultural settings.
