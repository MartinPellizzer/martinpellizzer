Stevia conferta var. pilosa

Stevia conferta var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has gained interest in herbal and alternative medicine applications.
