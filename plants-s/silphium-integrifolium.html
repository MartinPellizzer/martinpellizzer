Silphium integrifolium

Silphium integrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved compass plant, is a tall annual plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaves that are narrow and toothed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Silphium integrifolium is also noted for its historical use by Native American tribes for medicinal purposes.
