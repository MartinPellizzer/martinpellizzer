Schinus montana

Schinus montana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in whorls, and its small, bright red berries that are a key feature. Schinus montana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing ecological challenges.
