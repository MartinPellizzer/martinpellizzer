Sideritis × marcelii

Sideritis × marcelii, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Sideritis × marcelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild tonic properties.
