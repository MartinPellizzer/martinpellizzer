Syzygium argyropedicum

Syzygium argyropedicum, commonly known as the silver-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive silver veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exuding a resinous sap.

Syzygium argyropedicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
