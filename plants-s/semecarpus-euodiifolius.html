Semecarpus euodiifolius

Semecarpus euodiifolius, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
