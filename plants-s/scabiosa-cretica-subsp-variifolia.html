Scabiosa cretica subsp. variifolia

Scabiosa cretica subsp.

variifolia, commonly known as the variable scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
