Solanum nigrum f. brevipetiolare

Solanum nigrum f. brevipetiolare, commonly known as the short-stemmed black nightshade, is a variant of the common black nightshade plant.

It is characterized by its shorter petioles compared to the typical form, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
