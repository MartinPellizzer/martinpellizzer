Senecio pulviniformis

Senecio pulviniformis, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and basal rosette of blue-gray leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
