Salix spodiophylla

Salix spodiophylla, commonly known as the spiky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its growth form is adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates and strong winds.

Salix spodiophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
