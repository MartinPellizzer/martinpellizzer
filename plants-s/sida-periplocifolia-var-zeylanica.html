Sida periplocifolia var. zeylanica

Sida periplocifolia var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
