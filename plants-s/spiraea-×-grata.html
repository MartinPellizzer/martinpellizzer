Spiraea × grata

Spiraea × grata, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, with a spreading, rounded form. Its foliage is dark green and turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Spiraea × grata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
