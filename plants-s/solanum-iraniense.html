Solanum iraniense

Solanum iraniense, commonly known as the Iranian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
