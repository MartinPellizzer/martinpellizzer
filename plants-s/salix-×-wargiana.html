Salix × wargiana

Salix × wargiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that drape gracefully from its slender trunk. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue on the upper surface.

Salix × wargiana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and riparian restoration projects.
