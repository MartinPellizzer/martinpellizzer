Satyrium bracteatum var. pictum

Satyrium bracteatum var. pictum, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vivid, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety coating.

This variant is prized by horticulturists for its unique appearance and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
