Serratula flavescens subsp. mucronata

Serratula flavescens subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the yellow serrata, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe and Western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are serrated and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
