Soldanella cyanaster

Soldanella cyanaster, commonly known as the blue starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Soldanella cyanaster is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
