Sedum maximum var. purpureum

Sedum maximum var. purpureum, commonly known as the Purple Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it an excellent choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular selection for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
