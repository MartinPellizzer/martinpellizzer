Solidago salebrosa

Solidago salebrosa, commonly known as the bittergoldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, ray-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

Solidago salebrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
