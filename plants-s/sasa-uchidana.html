Sasa uchidana

Sasa uchidana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant forms tall, slender culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Sasa uchidana thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.
