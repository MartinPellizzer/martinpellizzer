Securidaca atropurpurea

Securidaca atropurpurea, commonly known as purple flame or purple senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive tubular shape and are borne in racemes. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
