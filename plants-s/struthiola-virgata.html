Struthiola virgata

Struthiola virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Struthiola virgata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
