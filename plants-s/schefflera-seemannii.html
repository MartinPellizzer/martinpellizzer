Schefflera seemannii

Schefflera seemannii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern, giving it a unique visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
