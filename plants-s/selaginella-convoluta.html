Selaginella convoluta

Selaginella convoluta, commonly known as the common spike moss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that form a dense, moss-like carpet. Selaginella convoluta reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and it is known for its ability to survive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important component of many ecosystems.
