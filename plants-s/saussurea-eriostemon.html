Saussurea eriostemon

Saussurea eriostemon, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and clustered, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are usually alternate and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
