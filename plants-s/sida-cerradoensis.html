Sida cerradoensis

Sida cerradoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense inflorescences and bloom during the dry season.

Sida cerradoensis is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
