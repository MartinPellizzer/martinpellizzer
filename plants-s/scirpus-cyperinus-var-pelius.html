Scirpus cyperinus var. pelius

Scirpus cyperinus var. pelius, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
