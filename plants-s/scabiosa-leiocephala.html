Scabiosa leiocephala

Scabiosa leiocephala, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, spherical flower heads that resemble small clouds, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Scabiosa leiocephala is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
