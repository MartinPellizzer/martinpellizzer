Serratula fruticosa

Serratula fruticosa, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Serratula fruticosa is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
