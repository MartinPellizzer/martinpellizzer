Sida montana subf. ovatifolia

Sida montana subf.

ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom during the warm season.

The seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in various environments.
