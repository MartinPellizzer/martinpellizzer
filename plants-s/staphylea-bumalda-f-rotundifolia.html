Staphylea bumalda f. rotundifolia

Staphylea bumalda f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf sweetshrub, is a compact shrub native to eastern North America.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its distinguishing name. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in terminal panicles. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
