Syngonium chiapense

Syngonium chiapense, commonly known as the Chiapan arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Mexico and Guatemala. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Syngonium chiapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
