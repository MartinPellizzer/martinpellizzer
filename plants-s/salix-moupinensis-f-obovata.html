Salix moupinensis f. obovata

Salix moupinensis f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate willow, is a variant of the Moupin willow, characterized by its distinctive obovate leaves.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
