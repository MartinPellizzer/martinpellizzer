Sorbus bosniaca

Sorbus bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in temperate climates.
