Symphyotrichum ciliolatum

Symphyotrichum ciliolatum, commonly known as the silky aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it a distinctive texture.

The showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
