Setaria glauca subsp. subtesselata

Setaria glauca subsp.

subtesselata, commonly known as the blue panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale blue to whitish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed habitats.
