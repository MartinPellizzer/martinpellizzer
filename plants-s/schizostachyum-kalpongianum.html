Schizostachyum kalpongianum

Schizostachyum kalpongianum, commonly known as the Kalpong bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of many bamboo species. It thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and strong fibers, it has potential uses in construction and handicrafts.
