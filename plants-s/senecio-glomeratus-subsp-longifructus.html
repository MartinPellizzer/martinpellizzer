Senecio glomeratus subsp. longifructus

Senecio glomeratus subsp.

longifructus, commonly known as the long-fruited groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive rough texture and often a bluish-green hue.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local flora.
