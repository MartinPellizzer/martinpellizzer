Solanum richardii var. pallidum

Solanum richardii var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive oval shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, toxic berries that are yellow when mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution when handling.
