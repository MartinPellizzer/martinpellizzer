Solanum malacoxylon

Solanum malacoxylon, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant has hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The berries are toxic when ripe but are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
