Saxifraga umbrosa var. longipes

Saxifraga umbrosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, slender stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height, adorned with small, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, appearing in clusters at the tips of the stems during late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
