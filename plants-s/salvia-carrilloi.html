Salvia carrilloi

Salvia carrilloi, commonly known as Carrillo's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically purple or violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia carrilloi is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
