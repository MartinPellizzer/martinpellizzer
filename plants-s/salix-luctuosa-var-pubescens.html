Salix luctuosa var. pubescens

Salix luctuosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a soft, downy texture that gives the plant its common name. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in alpine meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
