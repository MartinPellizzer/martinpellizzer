Senecio giganteus

Senecio giganteus, commonly known as the giant groundsel, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, silvery-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Senecio genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
