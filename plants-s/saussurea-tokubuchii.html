Saussurea tokubuchii

Saussurea tokubuchii, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Saussurea tokubuchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
