Silene longipetala

Silene longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Silene longipetala is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar structural and ecological traits.
