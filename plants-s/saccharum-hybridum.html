Saccharum hybridum

Saccharum hybridum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust stalks, which are thick and fibrous, containing high concentrations of sucrose. The plant thrives in warm climates with ample sunlight and water, making it a key crop in sugar production. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, while the stems are typically segmented and jointed.

Sugarcane is also used in the production of ethanol and various food products.
