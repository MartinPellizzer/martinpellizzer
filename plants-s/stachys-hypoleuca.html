Stachys hypoleuca

Stachys hypoleuca, commonly known as white hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
