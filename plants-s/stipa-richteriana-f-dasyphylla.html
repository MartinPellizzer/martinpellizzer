Stipa richteriana f. dasyphylla

Stipa richteriana f. dasyphylla, commonly known as the tufted feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color, with narrow, blade-like leaves. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
