Sarothamnus arboreus subsp. baeticus

Sarothamnus arboreus subsp.

baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean-type climates.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
