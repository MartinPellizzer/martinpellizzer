Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. taquetii

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb walls or trellises.
