Spodiopogon depauperatus

Spodiopogon depauperatus, commonly known as the sparse beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, tuft-like formations. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
