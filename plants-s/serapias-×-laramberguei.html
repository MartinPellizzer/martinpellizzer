Serapias × laramberguei

Serapias × laramberguei, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and bears the floral spikes.

Serapias × laramberguei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable wet conditions.
