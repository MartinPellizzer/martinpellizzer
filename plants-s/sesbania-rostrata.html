Sesbania rostrata

Sesbania rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. It produces elongated pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Sesbania rostrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
