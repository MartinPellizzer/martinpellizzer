Schizomeria pulleana

Schizomeria pulleana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring woody stems and a dense, rounded shape. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Schizomeria pulleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
