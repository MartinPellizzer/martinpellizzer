Succisa australis

Succisa australis, commonly known as the Australian succis, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

Succisa australis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
