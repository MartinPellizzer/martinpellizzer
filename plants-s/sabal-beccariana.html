Sabal beccariana

Sabal beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Sabal beccariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
