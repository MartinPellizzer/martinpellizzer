Scrophularia laciniata var. adenophora

Scrophularia laciniata var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花柳叶菜, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, pinkish to purplish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
