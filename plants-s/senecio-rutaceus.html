Senecio rutaceus

Senecio rutaceus, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed areas and has a sprawling or ascending growth habit. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Senecio rutaceus is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
