Schizachyrium hatschbachii

Schizachyrium hatschbachii, commonly known as the Harshbarger grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Schizachyrium hatschbachii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
