Saussurea kai-montana var. minor

Saussurea kai-montana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the central florets being tubular and the outer ones being petal-like.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
