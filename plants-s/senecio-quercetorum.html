Senecio quercetorum

Senecio quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio quercetorum is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
