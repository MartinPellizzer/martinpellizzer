Symplocos jucunda var. diversifolia

Symplocos jucunda var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated waxleaf symplocos, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leaves that exhibit a variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
