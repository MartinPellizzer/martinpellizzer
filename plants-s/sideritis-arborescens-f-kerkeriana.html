Sideritis arborescens f. kerkeriana

Sideritis arborescens f. kerkeriana, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small blue or purple flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
