Serjania chartacea

Serjania chartacea, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or yellow serjania, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaneae and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and distinctive yellowish flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Serjania chartacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
