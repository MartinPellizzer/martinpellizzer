Stelis habrostachya

Stelis habrostachya, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, and it is often found in forested areas.

The orchid's unique floral structure and hairy stem make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the orchid family.
