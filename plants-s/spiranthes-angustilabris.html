Spiranthes angustilabris

Spiranthes angustilabris, commonly known as the narrow-lipped ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and bogs. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
