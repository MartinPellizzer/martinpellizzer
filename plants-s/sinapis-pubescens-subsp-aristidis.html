Sinapis pubescens subsp. aristidis

Sinapis pubescens subsp.

aristidis, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are long, slender pods.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
