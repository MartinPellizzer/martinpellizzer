Syagrus × campos-portoana

Syagrus × campos-portoana, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus acula.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are pollinated by wind and insects. Its fruit is a bright orange drupe containing a single seed, and it is often found in coastal and lowland areas of Brazil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
