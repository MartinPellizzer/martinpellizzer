Salvia pauciserrata subsp. derasa

Salvia pauciserrata subsp.

derasa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with few serrations.

The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance contribute to its horticultural appeal.
