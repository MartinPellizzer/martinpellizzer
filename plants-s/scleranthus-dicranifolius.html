Scleranthus dicranifolius

Scleranthus dicranifolius, commonly known as the hairy sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Scleranthus dicranifolius is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in environments with poor soil quality.
