Salvia praetermissa

Salvia praetermissa, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia praetermissa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
