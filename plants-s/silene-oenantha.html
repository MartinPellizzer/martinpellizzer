Silene oenantha

Silene oenantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Silene oenantha has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
