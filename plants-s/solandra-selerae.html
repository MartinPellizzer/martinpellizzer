Solandra selerae

Solandra selerae, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Solandra selerae is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, often used as a vine in landscaping.
