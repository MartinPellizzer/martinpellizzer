Solanum setaceum

Solanum setaceum, commonly known as the pale nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture. Solanum setaceum produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds inside.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
