Serronia jaborandi

Serronia jaborandi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
