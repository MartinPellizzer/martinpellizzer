Sorbus aucuparia var. laciniata

Sorbus aucuparia var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
