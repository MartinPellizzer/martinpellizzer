Sorbaria arborea var. subtomentosa

Sorbaria arborea var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
