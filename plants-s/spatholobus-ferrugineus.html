Spatholobus ferrugineus

Spatholobus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature.

Spatholobus ferrugineus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
