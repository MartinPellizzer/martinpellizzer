Scirpus anisochaetus

Scirpus anisochaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are often found at the top of the stems. Scirpus anisochaetus thrives in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
