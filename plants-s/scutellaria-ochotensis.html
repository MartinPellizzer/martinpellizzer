Scutellaria ochotensis

Scutellaria ochotensis, commonly known as Siberian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Scutellaria ochotensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
