Saxifraga versicallosa

Saxifraga versicallosa, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Saxifraga versicallosa is often found in alpine meadows and on mountain slopes at high elevations.
