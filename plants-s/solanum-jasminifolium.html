Solanum jasminifolium

Solanum jasminifolium, commonly known as the jasmine leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble those of jasmine, and its leaves are often described as having a delicate, aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Solanum jasminifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
