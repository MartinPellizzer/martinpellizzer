Specularia speculum-veneris subvar. pubescens

Specularia speculum-veneris subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its subspecific name "pubescens.". The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and color, making it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
