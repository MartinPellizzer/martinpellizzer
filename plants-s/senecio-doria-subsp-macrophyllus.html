Senecio doria subsp. macrophyllus

Senecio doria subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, giving it a striking visual appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
