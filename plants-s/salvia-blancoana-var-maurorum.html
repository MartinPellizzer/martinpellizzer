Salvia blancoana var. maurorum

Salvia blancoana var. maurorum, commonly known as the Maurorum sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
