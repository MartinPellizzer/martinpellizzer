Syzygium multinerve

Syzygium multinerve, commonly known as the fragrant apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive venation pattern with multiple nerves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, round, red or yellow berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
