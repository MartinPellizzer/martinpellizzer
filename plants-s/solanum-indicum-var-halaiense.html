Solanum indicum var. halaiense

Solanum indicum var. halaiense, commonly known as the Himalayan nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red when mature. It is often found in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
