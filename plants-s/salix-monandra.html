Salix monandra

Salix monandra, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive white to pale yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near watercourses or in damp woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
