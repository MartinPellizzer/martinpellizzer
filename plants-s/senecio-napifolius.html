Senecio napifolius

Senecio napifolius, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
