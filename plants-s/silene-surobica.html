Silene surobica

Silene surobica, commonly known as the Surobi's campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Silene surobica is known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating skin ailments and respiratory issues.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
