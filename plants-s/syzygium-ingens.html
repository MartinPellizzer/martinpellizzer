Syzygium ingens

Syzygium ingens, commonly known as the giant water apple or water apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by large, fleshy, yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a translucent, jelly-like pulp with a sweet, acidic flavor and contains numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in some regions.
