Scleria mackaviensis var. atrata

Scleria mackaviensis var. atrata, commonly known as the Blackstem Scleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, three-sided spikelets that are typically green or brown in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
