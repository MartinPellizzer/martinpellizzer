Siphocampylus maxonis

Siphocampylus maxonis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped with five petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
