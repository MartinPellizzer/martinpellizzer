Scutellaria × neumannii

Scutellaria × neumannii, commonly known as Neumann's skullcap, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its phytochemical composition.
