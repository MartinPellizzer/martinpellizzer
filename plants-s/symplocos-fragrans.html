Symplocos fragrans

Symplocos fragrans, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.
