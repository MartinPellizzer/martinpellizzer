Scorzonera calyculata

Scorzonera calyculata, commonly known as the Armenian scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust taproot, which is often harvested for its edible and medicinal properties. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Scorzonera calyculata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
