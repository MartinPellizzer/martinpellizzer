Scirpus holoschoenus subsp. romanus

Scirpus holoschoenus subsp.

romanus, commonly known as the Roman bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, lakes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features long, cylindrical stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering spikes, which appear at the top of the stems, consist of numerous small, greenish-white flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
