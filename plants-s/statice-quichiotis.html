Statice quichiotis

Statice quichiotis, commonly known as the Canary Island statice, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
