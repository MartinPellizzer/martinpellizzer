Salsola arborea

Salsola arborea, commonly known as the desert matweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually fleshy and can store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments.

Salsola arborea is often found in sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low precipitation.
