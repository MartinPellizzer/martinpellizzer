Stylisma evolvuloides var. angustifolia

Stylisma evolvuloides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stylisma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, angular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. The flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
