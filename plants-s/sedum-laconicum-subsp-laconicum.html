Sedum laconicum subsp. laconicum

Sedum laconicum subsp.

laconicum, commonly known as the Laconic stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of environmental conditions, including full sun and poor soil.
