Swartzia apetala

Swartzia apetala, commonly known as the white-flowered swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant is notable for its showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
