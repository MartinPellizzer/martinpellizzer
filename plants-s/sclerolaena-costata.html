Sclerolaena costata

Sclerolaena costata, commonly known as the serrated thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its common name.

Sclerolaena costata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
