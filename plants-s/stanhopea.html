Stanhopea

Stanhopea, commonly known as the orchid genus, is a group of terrestrial orchids native to Central and South America.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. Stanhopea species typically have pseudobulbs and are epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on trees or rocks. They are prized by orchid enthusiasts for their unique floral structures and ease of cultivation.

The genus includes over 50 species, many of which are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
