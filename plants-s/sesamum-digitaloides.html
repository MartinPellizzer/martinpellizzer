Sesamum digitaloides

Sesamum digitaloides, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, crescent-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

Sesamum digitaloides is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
