Stevia lucida

Stevia lucida, commonly known as silverleaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Stevia lucida is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweeteners.
