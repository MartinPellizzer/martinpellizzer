Sinapis pubescens subsp. indurata

Sinapis pubescens subsp.

indurata, commonly known as the rough mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that form in racemes.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. Its leaves are pubescent, and the stems are often ribbed and somewhat woody.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant with a compact growth habit.
