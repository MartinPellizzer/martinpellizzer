Scutellaria × martrinii

Scutellaria × martrinii, commonly known as the hybrid blue skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
