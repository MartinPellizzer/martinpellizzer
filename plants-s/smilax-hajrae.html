Smilax hajrae

Smilax hajrae, commonly known as the Kashmir smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Smilax hajrae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
