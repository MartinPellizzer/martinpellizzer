Spirospermum

Spirospermum, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Spirospermum species are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
