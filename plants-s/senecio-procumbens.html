Senecio procumbens

Senecio procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage on the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has hairy stems and leaves that are generally lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Senecio procumbens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
