Stenophyllus capillaris var. striatinux

Stenophyllus capillaris var. striatinux, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass with striped foliage, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves exhibit a distinctive striated pattern, giving it its varietal name "striatinux.".

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
