Schoenoxiphium rufum

Schoenoxiphium rufum, commonly known as the red-stemmed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

Schoenoxiphium rufum plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
