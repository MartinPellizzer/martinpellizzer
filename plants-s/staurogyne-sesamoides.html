Staurogyne sesamoides

Staurogyne sesamoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in maximizing light absorption in its aquatic habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of tiny, floating fruits. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species for water quality assessment and ecological studies.
