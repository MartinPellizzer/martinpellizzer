Spergularia hanoverensis

Spergularia hanoverensis, commonly known as the Hanover sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal environments in Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky habitats.

Spergularia hanoverensis is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, showcasing resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
