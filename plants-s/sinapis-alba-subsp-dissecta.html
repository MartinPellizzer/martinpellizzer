Sinapis alba subsp. dissecta

Sinapis alba subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf white mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely dissected leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, due to its adaptability to various growing conditions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is known for its rapid growth cycle. The seeds of Sinapis alba subsp.

dissecta are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
