Styphelia gnidium

Styphelia gnidium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snowbells, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, cushion-like form, which helps it withstand coastal conditions. Styphelia gnidium is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathland ecosystems.
