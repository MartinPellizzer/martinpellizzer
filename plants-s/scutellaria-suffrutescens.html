Scutellaria suffrutescens

Scutellaria suffrutescens, commonly known as purple beard tongue, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its distinctive purple flowers have a spurred shape, and it produces small, round, brown seeds in its fruit.

Scutellaria suffrutescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
