Scirpus sylvaticus f. maximowiczii

Scirpus sylvaticus f. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Maximowicz's bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spadix-like flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
