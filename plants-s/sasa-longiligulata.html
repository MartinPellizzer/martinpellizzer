Sasa longiligulata

Sasa longiligulata, commonly known as the long-leaf sasa, is a perennial bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can provide habitat for various small organisms.

Sasa longiligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage structure.
