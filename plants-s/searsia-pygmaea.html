Searsia pygmaea

Searsia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Searsia pygmaea is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife and its low maintenance requirements.
