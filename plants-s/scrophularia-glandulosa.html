Scrophularia glandulosa

Scrophularia glandulosa, commonly known as sticky betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving it a sticky texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia glandulosa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological preferences.
