Senecio gallicus

Senecio gallicus, commonly known as French ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it harmful to livestock.

Senecio gallicus is often considered a weed but has also been used in traditional medicine.
