Sarracenia × georgiana

Sarracenia × georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, colorful, tubular leaves that form specialized pitfall traps to capture prey. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with intricate patterns that help attract insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × georgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in bog gardens and greenhouse environments.
