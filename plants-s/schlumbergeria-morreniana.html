Schlumbergeria morreniana

Schlumbergeria morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that form a branching structure, often resembling a tree or shrub. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically between November and January. Its leaves are modified stems that are thick and segmented, adapted to store water.

Schlumbergeria morreniana is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
