Scandix australis subsp. gallica

Scandix australis subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the Gallic scabweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins, and it is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient species in its native range.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other subspecies of Scandix australis.
