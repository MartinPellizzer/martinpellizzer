Senecio sinclairii

Senecio sinclairii, commonly known as Sinclair's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
