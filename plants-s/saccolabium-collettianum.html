Saccolabium collettianum

Saccolabium collettianum, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
