Spathoglottis bensonii

Spathoglottis bensonii, commonly known as the Benson's spathoglottis, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Spathoglottis bensonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
