Solanum edentatum

Solanum edentatum, commonly known as the toothless nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and ovate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that mature to a yellow or orange color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum edentatum is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
