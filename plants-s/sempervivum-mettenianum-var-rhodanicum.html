Sempervivum mettenianum var. rhodanicum

Sempervivum mettenianum var. rhodanicum, commonly known as the red-scented houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces pink or red flowers in early summer, adding visual interest to its compact form. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
