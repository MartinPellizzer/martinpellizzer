Stevia hassleriana

Stevia hassleriana, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Stevia hassleriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
