Seriphium uniflorum

Seriphium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower at the end of a slender stem.

Seriphium uniflorum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
