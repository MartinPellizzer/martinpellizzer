Scutellaria albida subsp. vacillans

Scutellaria albida subsp.

vacillans, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
