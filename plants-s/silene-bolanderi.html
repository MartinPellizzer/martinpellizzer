Silene bolanderi

Silene bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and sagebrush areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
