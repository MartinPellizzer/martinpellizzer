Scleria reticularis

Scleria reticularis, commonly known as the net-veined sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Scleria reticularis is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
