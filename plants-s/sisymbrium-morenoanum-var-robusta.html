Sisymbrium morenoanum var. robusta

Sisymbrium morenoanum var. robusta, commonly known as the Moreno's Tansy Mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or yellowish-green.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to arid environments.
