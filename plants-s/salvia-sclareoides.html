Salvia sclareoides

Salvia sclareoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Salvia sclareoides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
