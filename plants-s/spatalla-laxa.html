Spatalla laxa

Spatalla laxa, commonly known as the slender spathiphyllum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Spatalla laxa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
