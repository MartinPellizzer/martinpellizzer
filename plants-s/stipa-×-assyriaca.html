Stipa × assyriaca

Stipa × assyriaca, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough, fibrous texture.

Stipa × assyriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
