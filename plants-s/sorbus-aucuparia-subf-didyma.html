Sorbus aucuparia subf. didyma

Sorbus aucuparia subf.

didyma, commonly known as the red mountain ash, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam. It is characterized by its distinctive red berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The subsp.

didyma is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting bird populations.
