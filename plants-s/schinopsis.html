Schinopsis

Schinopsis species are characterized by their hard, woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The genus includes several species, such as Schinopsis brasiliensis and Schinopsis lorentzii, which are valued for their durable wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
