Spyridium parvifolium var. parvifolium

Spyridium parvifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved spyridium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its region.
