Sorbus globulifera

Sorbus globulifera, commonly known as the globular rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its spherical, bright red berries that are clustered in dense, globular formations. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking fruit display.
