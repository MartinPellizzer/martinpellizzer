Silene bergeri

Silene bergeri, commonly known as the Berger's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Silene bergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
