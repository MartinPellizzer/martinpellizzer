Sclerocephalus aucheri

Sclerocephalus aucheri, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sclerocephalus aucheri is known for its resilience and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
