Schoenus plumosus

Schoenus plumosus, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture.

Schoenus plumosus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
