Sauropus rigidulus

Sauropus rigidulus, commonly known as the hardy hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a rough texture, and it is known for its hardiness in humid climates.

Sauropus rigidulus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
