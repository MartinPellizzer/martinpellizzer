Sorbus commixta f. rufohirtella

Sorbus commixta f. rufohirtella, commonly known as the red-haired whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive horticultural form of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring red-tinged, hairy young leaves that give it its common name. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens where its striking foliage can add visual interest. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of around 3 to 5 meters.

The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
