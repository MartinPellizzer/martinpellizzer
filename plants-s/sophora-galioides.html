Sophora galioides

Sophora galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
