Senecio flaccidus var. douglasii

Senecio flaccidus var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's brittlestem, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
