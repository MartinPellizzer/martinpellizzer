Sapium zelayense

Sapium zelayense, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
