Solanum melongena var. ismir

Solanum melongena var. ismir, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deep violet to purple skin and elongated, oval-shaped fruits. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

This variety is prized for its rich flavor and is widely used in various cuisines around the world.
