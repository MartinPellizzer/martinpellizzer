Selaginella braunii

Selaginella braunii, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its delicate, needle-like leaves that form dense, moss-like mats. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures called strobila.

Selaginella braunii is also notable for its high tolerance to environmental stress, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
