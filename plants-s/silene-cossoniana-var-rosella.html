Silene cossoniana var. rosella

Silene cossoniana var. rosella, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal dunes of southern California.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in stabilized dune environments.

Silene cossoniana var. rosella plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
