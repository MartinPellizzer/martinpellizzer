Solanum neovavilovii

Solanum neovavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
