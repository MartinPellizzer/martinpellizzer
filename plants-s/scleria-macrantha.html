Scleria macrantha

Scleria macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, clustered inflorescence of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scleria macrantha is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
