Shantzia

Shantzia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to various regions in the Americas, shantzia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
