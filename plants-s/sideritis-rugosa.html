Sideritis rugosa

Sideritis rugosa, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many herbs with medicinal properties. Sideritis rugosa is often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
