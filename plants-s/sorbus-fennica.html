Sorbus fennica

Sorbus fennica, commonly known as the Finnish whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The fruit is a round, red or yellow berry that matures in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
