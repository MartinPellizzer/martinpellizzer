Salvia colombiana

Salvia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fragrant mint-like scent.

Salvia colombiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
