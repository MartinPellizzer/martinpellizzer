Serratula altissima

Serratula altissima, commonly known as the greater serratus, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Serratula altissima is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
