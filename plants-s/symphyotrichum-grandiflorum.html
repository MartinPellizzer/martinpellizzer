Symphyotrichum grandiflorum

Symphyotrichum grandiflorum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Symphyotrichum grandiflorum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
