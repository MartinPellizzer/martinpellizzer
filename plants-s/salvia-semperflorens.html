Salvia semperflorens

Salvia semperflorens, commonly known as summer sage or winter sage, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Salvia semperflorens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
