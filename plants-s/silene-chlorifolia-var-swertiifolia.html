Silene chlorifolia var. swertiifolia

Silene chlorifolia var. swertiifolia, commonly known as the Swertiifolia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and chlorophyll-rich, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
