Scleranthus montenegrinus

Scleranthus montenegrinus, commonly known as the Montenegro thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its spiny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Scleranthus montenegrinus plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for certain insects and contributing to soil stability.
