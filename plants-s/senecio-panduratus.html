Senecio panduratus

Senecio panduratus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
