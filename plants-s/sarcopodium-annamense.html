Sarcopodium annamense

Sarcopodium annamense, commonly known as the Annam bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sarcopodium annamense is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
