Statice alpina

Statice alpina, commonly known as mountain statice, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Statice alpina is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments, thriving in poor, well-drained soils.
