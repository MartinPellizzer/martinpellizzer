Schlechtendalia wrightii

Schlechtendalia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
