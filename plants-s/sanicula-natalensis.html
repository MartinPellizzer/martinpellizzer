Sanicula natalensis

Sanicula natalensis, commonly known as Natal sanicle, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Sanicula natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
