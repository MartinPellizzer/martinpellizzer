Sapota marginata

Sapota marginata, commonly known as the marginated sapota, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, round fruits with a smooth, yellow skin. The fruit's pulp is sweet and creamy, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its fruit and ornamental qualities.
