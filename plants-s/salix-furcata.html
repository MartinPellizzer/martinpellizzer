Salix furcata

Salix furcata, commonly known as the forked willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated, and it produces small, triangular seeds.

Salix furcata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
