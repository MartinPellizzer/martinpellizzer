Solanum vagans

Solanum vagans, commonly known as the wandering nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often lobed or deeply serrated. Its flowers are typically white or purple, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.
