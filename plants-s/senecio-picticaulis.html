Senecio picticaulis

Senecio picticaulis, commonly known as the painted sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with dark orange markings, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.
