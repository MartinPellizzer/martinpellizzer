Sasa oshidensis var. shigaensis

Sasa oshidensis var. shigaensis, commonly known as Shiga bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by cylindrical, woody spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
