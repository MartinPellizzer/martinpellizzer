Stipa pennata f. sabulosa

Stipa pennata f. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand grass or coastal needlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grasses. It is well adapted to sandy or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils and salt-laden air.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
