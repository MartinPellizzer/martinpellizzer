Spironema aphylla

Spironema aphylla, commonly known as the leafless spironema, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Spironema aphylla is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
