Scaevola chlorantha

Scaevola chlorantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
