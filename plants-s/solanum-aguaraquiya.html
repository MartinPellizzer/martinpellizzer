Solanum aguaraquiya

Solanum aguaraquiya, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum aguaraquiya is often found in high-altitude habitats and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native environment.
