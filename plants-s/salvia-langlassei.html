Salvia langlassei

Salvia langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty scent, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia langlassei is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
