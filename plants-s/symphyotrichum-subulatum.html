Symphyotrichum subulatum

Symphyotrichum subulatum, commonly known as the slender stem goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its narrow, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
