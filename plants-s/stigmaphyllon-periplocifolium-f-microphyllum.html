Stigmaphyllon periplocifolium f. microphyllum

Stigmaphyllon periplocifolium f. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved stigmaphyllon, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
