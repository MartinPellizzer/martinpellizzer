Scirpus heterophyllus

Scirpus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Scirpus heterophyllus thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to varying water levels makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
