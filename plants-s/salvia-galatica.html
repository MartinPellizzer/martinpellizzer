Salvia galatica

Salvia galatica, commonly known as the Galician sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
