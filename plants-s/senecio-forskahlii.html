Senecio forskahlii

Senecio forskahlii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments. It possesses a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Senecio forskahlii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
