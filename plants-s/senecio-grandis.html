Senecio grandis

Senecio grandis, commonly known as the giant African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio grandis is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
