Saccolabium machadonis

Saccolabium machadonis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds used for various purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
