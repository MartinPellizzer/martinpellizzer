Saraca longistyla

Saraca longistyla, commonly known as the longistyla flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca longistyla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
