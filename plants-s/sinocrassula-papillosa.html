Sinocrassula papillosa

Sinocrassula papillosa, commonly known as the woolly sinocrassula, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, where it stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, pink or white flowers during the spring or early summer.

Sinocrassula papillosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
