Scirpus acutus

Scirpus acutus, commonly known as spiked bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it ecologically significant.

Scirpus acutus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
