Silybum hispanicum

Silybum hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
