Spiraea salicifolia subf. bethlehemensis

Spiraea salicifolia subf.

bethlehemensis, commonly known as the Bethlehem spiraea, is a shrub native to the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky slopes and along riverbanks in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
