Sebastiania subulata

Sebastiania subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange in color, and it produces berries that are a source of food for various animals.

Sebastiania subulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
