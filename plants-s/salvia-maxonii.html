Salvia maxonii

Salvia maxonii, commonly known as Maxon's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Salvia maxonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
