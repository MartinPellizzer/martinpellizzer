Stevia sphacelata

Stevia sphacelata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides, making it a popular natural sweetener. Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds.

Stevia sphacelata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
