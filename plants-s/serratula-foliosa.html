Serratula foliosa

Serratula foliosa, commonly known as the leafy serrated wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Serratula foliosa is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many common weeds and ornamental plants.
