Sternbergia dalmatica

Sternbergia dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which are typically blue or violet, bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
