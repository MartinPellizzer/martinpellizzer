Streptopus roseus var. typicus

Streptopus roseus var. typicus, commonly known as the pink starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
