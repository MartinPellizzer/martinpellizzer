Sedum laconicum subsp. pallidum

Sedum laconicum subsp.

pallidum, commonly known as the pale stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
