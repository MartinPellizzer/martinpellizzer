Stipa papposa

Stipa papposa, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a species of grass native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with silky, pappus-like structures, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Stipa papposa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
