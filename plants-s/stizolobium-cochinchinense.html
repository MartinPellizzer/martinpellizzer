Stizolobium cochinchinense

Stizolobium cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
