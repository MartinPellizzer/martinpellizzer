Silene komarovii

Silene komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
