Scolopia wightiana

Scolopia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.
