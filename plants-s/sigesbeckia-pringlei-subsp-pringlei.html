Sigesbeckia pringlei subsp. pringlei

Sigesbeckia pringlei subsp.

pringlei, commonly known as the Chinese iron vine, is a flowering plant native to China. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has serrated, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
