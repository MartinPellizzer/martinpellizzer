Saussurea sumneviczii var. mutabilis

Saussurea sumneviczii var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
