Sympodium

Sympodium, commonly known as a type of plant growth structure, refers to a cluster of stems that arise from a single point.

It is often observed in certain species of plants, particularly in some ferns and mosses. This structure allows for the efficient distribution of nutrients and water throughout the plant. Sympodium can also be a form of vegetative reproduction, enabling the plant to spread and colonize new areas.

The botanical significance of sympodium lies in its role in plant development and survival strategies.
