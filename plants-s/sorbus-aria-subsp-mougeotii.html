Sorbus aria subsp. mougeotii

Sorbus aria subsp.

mougeotii, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
