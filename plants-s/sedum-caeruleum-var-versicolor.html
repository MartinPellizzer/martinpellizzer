Sedum caeruleum var. versicolor

Sedum caeruleum var. versicolor, commonly known as the blue sedum or variegated stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves with distinctive white and green variegation, which enhance its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and colorful foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural displays.
