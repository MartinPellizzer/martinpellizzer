Selago impedita

Selago impedita, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Selago impedita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardy nature.
