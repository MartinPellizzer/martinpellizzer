Serapias × ambigua f. laramberguei

Serapias × ambigua f. laramberguei, commonly known as the Larambergue's hybrid orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias ambiguus and Serapias larambergii.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in alpine or mountainous regions. It exhibits features of both parent species, including the structure of its flowers and the shape of its leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
