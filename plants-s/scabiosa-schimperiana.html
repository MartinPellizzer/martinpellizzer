Scabiosa schimperiana

Scabiosa schimperiana, commonly known as the fairy thimble or scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Scabiosa schimperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
