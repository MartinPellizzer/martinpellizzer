Syzygium fusticuliferum

Syzygium fusticuliferum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark red, fleshy berries containing flat, black seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
