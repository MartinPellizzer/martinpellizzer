Schizoglossum loreum

Schizoglossum loreum, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Schizoglossum loreum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
