Stachys amanica

Stachys amanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Stachys amanica is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It is generally not considered invasive and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
