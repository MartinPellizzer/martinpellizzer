Savignya

Savignya, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Asia, particularly India, and thrives in humid, shady environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Savignya is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
