Sarcocolla speciosa

Sarcocolla speciosa, commonly known as the showy sarcocolla, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its vibrant, umbrella-like inflorescences.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided lobes that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, Sarcocolla speciosa is often cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
