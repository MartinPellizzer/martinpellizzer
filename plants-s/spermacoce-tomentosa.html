Spermacoce tomentosa

Spermacoce tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb with hairy, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many regions, though it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
