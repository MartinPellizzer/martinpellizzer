Stoebe schultzii

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
