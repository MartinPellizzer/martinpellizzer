Saracha chihuahuensis

Saracha chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua saracha, is a rare flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Saracha chihuahuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
