Senecio multilobus

Senecio multilobus, commonly known as the Sierra madre ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are often mistaken for those of other similar species. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional uses.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
