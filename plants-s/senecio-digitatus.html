Senecio digitatus

Senecio digitatus, commonly known as the fingerleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Senecio digitatus is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
