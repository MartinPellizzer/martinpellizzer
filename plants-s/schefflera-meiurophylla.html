Schefflera meiurophylla

Schefflera meiurophylla, commonly known as the New Guinea arrowleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features a dense, rounded canopy with large, leathery, arrow-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
