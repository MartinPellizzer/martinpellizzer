Shorea acuta

Shorea acuta, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Shorea acuta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
