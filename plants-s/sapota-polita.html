Sapota polita

Sapota polita, commonly known as the rough sapota, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and yellowish, edible fruit. The fruit has a rough, bark-like exterior and a sweet, creamy flesh that is rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
