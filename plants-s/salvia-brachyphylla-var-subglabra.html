Salvia brachyphylla var. subglabra

Salvia brachyphylla var. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features compact, upright growth with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
