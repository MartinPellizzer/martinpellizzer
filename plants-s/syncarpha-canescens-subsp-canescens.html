Syncarpha canescens subsp. canescens

Syncarpha canescens subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the woolly synbranch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with ascending stems that bear the inflorescences.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is often found in open, sun-exposed habitats.
