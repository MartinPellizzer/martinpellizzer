Solidago caesia var. paniculata

Solidago caesia var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and marshes. Its showy inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
