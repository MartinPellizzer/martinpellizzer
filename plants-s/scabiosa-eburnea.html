Scabiosa eburnea

Scabiosa eburnea, commonly known as the snowy scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Scabiosa eburnea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
