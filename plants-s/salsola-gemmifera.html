Salsola gemmifera

Salsola gemmifera, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments. Salsola gemmifera is also notable for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in some areas, making it a weed in agricultural settings.

Its adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions make it a resilient species in ecological studies.
