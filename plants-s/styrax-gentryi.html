Styrax gentryi

Styrax gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, smooth, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
