Stachys yemenensis

Stachys yemenensis, commonly known as Yemen betony, is a perennial herb native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of purple or blue flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Stachys yemenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
