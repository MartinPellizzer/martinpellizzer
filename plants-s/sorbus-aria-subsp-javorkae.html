Sorbus aria subsp. javorkae

Sorbus aria subsp.

javorkae, commonly known as the Bohemian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
