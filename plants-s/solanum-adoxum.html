Solanum adoxum

Solanum adoxum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum adoxum is often found in moist, shaded areas in tropical and subtropical regions.
