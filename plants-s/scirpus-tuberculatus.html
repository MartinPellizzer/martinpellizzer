Scirpus tuberculatus

Scirpus tuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its flowering spikes are usually pink or reddish, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus tuberculatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
