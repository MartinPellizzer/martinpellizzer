Salix pitcheriana

Salix pitcheriana, commonly known as the Pitcher's willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its flowers are usually inconspicuous. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Salix pitcheriana is considered a rare and specialized species.
