Shorea ovata

Shorea ovata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Shorea ovata plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
