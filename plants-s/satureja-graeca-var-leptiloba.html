Satureja graeca var. leptiloba

Satureja graeca var. leptiloba, commonly known as Greek thyme or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas, and produces small, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is distinguished by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh environments.
