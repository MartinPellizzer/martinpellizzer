Senecio afromontanus

Senecio afromontanus, commonly known as the African mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.
