Sarcocornia terminalis

Sarcocornia terminalis, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a branched, shrub-like structure with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. Its leaves are typically succulent and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

Sarcocornia terminalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
