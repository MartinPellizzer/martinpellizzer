Saccharum griffithii

Saccharum griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's bamboo or bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. This plant is often found in wetland and riparian habitats, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various animals.

Due to its rapid growth, it can also be invasive in certain regions.
