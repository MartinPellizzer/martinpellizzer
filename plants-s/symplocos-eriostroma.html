Symplocos eriostroma

Symplocos eriostroma, commonly known as the roughleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch and have a reddish hue in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Symplocos eriostroma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
