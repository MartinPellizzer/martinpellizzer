Southwellia roxburghiana

Southwellia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are narrow and strap-like.

Southwellia roxburghiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
