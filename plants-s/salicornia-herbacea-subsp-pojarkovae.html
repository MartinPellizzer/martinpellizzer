Salicornia herbacea subsp. pojarkovae

Salicornia herbacea subsp.

pojarkovae, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves.

The subspecies is native to coastal areas and salt marshes, where it thrives in high-salinity soils. Its structure allows it to retain water and withstand periodic flooding.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
