Silene kiwuensis subsp. erlangeriana

Silene kiwuensis subsp.

erlangeriana, commonly known as the Erlanderman silene, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the wet season.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of dense forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in humid conditions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
