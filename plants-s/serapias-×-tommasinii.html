Serapias × tommasinii

Serapias × tommasinii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid gentian, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias fritillariaeflora and Serapias orchidioides.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
