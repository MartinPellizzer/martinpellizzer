Simira rubescens

Simira rubescens, commonly known as the red tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
