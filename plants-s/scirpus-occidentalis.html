Scirpus occidentalis

Scirpus occidentalis, commonly known as western bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stems. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize wetland soils.

Scirpus occidentalis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
