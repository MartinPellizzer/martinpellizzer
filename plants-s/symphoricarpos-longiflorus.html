Symphoricarpos longiflorus

Symphoricarpos longiflorus, commonly known as the longflower coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush habitats.

Symphoricarpos longiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
