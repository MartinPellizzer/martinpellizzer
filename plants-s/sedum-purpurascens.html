Sedum purpurascens

Sedum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that often turn a striking purple hue under full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
