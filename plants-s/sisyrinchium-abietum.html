Sisyrinchium abietum

Sisyrinchium abietum, commonly known as the mountain blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium abietum is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
