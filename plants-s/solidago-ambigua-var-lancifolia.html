Solidago ambigua var. lancifolia

Solidago ambigua var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
