Schoenoxiphium kuekenthalianum

Schoenoxiphium kuekenthalianum, commonly known as the Kuekenthalianum bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
