Scutellaria villosa

Scutellaria villosa, commonly known as hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
