Sporobolus indicus subsp. purpureosuffusus

Sporobolus indicus subsp.

purpureosuffusus, commonly known as purple-stemmed sandbur, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, spiny seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, a feature typical of the genus Sporobolus. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-gray appearance that helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for soil stabilization and erosion control.
