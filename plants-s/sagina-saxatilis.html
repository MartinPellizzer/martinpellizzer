Sagina saxatilis

Sagina saxatilis, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in rocky and coastal environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Sagina saxatilis plays a role in stabilizing soil in coastal areas and is often found in maritime climates.
