Solidago altissima var. canescens

Solidago altissima var. canescens, commonly known as the hoary goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance.

This variety is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open fields and disturbed sites.
