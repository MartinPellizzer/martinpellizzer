Silene ignobilis

Silene ignobilis, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Silene ignobilis is often found in dry, sunny locations and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
