Struthanthus rhynchophyllus

Struthanthus rhynchophyllus, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, allowing it to conserve water efficiently.

Struthanthus rhynchophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local desert ecosystems.
