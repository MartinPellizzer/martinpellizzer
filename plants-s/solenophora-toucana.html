Solenophora toucana

Solenophora toucana, commonly known as the Toucan plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a distinct, elongated stem with a thick, fleshy base that helps store water, adapting it to its arid habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, which reduces water loss and enhances its survival in hot climates.

Solenophora toucana is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
