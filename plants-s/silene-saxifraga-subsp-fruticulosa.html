Silene saxifraga subsp. fruticulosa

Silene saxifraga subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the rock-fruited catchfly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features erect, branched stems that bear small, pink to reddish-purple flowers with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. Silene saxifraga subsp.

fruticulosa is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
