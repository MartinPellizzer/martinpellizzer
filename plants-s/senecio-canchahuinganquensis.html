Senecio canchahuinganquensis

Senecio canchahuinganquensis, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
