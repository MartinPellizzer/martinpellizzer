Serratula cinerea

Serratula cinerea, commonly known as the gray mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Serratula cinerea is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in respiratory treatments.
