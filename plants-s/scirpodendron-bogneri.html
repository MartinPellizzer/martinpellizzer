Scirpodendron bogneri

Scirpodendron bogneri, commonly known as the bog fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble those of a true fern. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the substrate and form dense mats. Its leaves are typically submerged, with some reaching above the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis.

Scirpodendron bogneri plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
