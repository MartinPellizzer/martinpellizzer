Spartium scoparium var. hirsutum

Spartium scoparium var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy spartium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological surveys.
