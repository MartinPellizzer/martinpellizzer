Sanguisorba filiformis

Sanguisorba filiformis, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in early summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sanguisorba filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate cold climates.
