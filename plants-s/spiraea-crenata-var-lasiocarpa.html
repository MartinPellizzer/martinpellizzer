Spiraea crenata var. lasiocarpa

Spiraea crenata var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the downy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that produce clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge, and the leaves often have a soft, downy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
