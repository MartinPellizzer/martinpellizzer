Stachys officinalis subsp. balcanica

Stachys officinalis subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as Balkan betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

Balkan betony is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
