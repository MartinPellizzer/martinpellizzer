Solanum stoloniferum subsp. moreliae

Solanum stoloniferum subsp.

moreliae, commonly known as the Morelia nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale violet, which bloom in summer.

The plant is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit, forming mats of foliage along the ground. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when mature and contain toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
