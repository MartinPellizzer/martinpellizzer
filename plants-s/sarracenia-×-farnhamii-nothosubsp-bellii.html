Sarracenia × farnhamii nothosubsp. bellii

Sarracenia × farnhamii nothosubsp.

bellii, commonly known as the Farnham's Sarracenia hybrid, is a fascinating carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a hybrid between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra, exhibiting traits from both parent species.

The plant features tall, tubular leaves that trap insects, with vibrant red and orange hues along the inner surfaces. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces nectar to attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
