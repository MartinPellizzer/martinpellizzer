Salicornia emerici var. vicensis

Salicornia emerici var. vicensis, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that resemble small beans. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found in coastal regions and salt marshes. Its leaves are reduced to scales, and it thrives in areas with high salinity and low nutrient availability.

Salicornia emerici var. vicensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
