Sedum bohuslawii

Sedum bohuslawii, commonly known as the Bohuslavia sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, which helps it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
