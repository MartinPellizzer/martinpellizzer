Schilleria rudis

Schilleria rudis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Schilleria rudis is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers, typically blooming in spring.

Its unique appearance and adaptation to harsh coastal conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
