Sarcopodium pileatum

Sarcopodium pileatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically a deep green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Sarcopodium pileatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
