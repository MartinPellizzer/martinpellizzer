Sclerolaena clavata

Sclerolaena clavata, commonly known as the clustered bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its seed capsules are distinctive, being elongated and often covered in bristly hairs.

Sclerolaena clavata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
