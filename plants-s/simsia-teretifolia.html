Simsia teretifolia

Simsia teretifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf simsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Simsia teretifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
