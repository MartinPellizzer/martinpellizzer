Silene italica var. montana

Silene italica var. montana, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features simple, often hairy stems and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Silene italica var. montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
