Symphyotrichum firmum

Symphyotrichum firmum, commonly known as the stiff ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Symphyotrichum firmum plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
