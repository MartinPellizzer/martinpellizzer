Saglorithys lavandulifolia

Saglorithys lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that resemble those of lavender, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is also valued in traditional medicine and perfumery.
