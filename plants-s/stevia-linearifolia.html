Stevia linearifolia

Stevia linearifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides found in its leaves. Stevia linearifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in various climates.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained popularity due to its low caloric content and health benefits.
