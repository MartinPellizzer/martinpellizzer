Senega semialata

Senega semialata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple petals and a central cone-like structure composed of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil. Senega semialata produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
