Swertia gonczaroviana

Swertia gonczaroviana, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually upright and branched.

Swertia gonczaroviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
