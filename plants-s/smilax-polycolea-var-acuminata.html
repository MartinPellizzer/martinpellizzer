Smilax polycolea var. acuminata

Smilax polycolea var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed catclaw, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, thorn-like projections, which are used for climbing and defense. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
