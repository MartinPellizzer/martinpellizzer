Saccolabium trichromum

Saccolabium trichromum, commonly known as the three-colored saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, which are typically dry and dehiscent.

Saccolabium trichromum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
