Sambucus sieboldiana var. latifolia

Sambucus sieboldiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces dark purple to black berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
