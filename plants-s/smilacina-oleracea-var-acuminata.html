Smilacina oleracea var. acuminata

Smilacina oleracea var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, climbing stems that produce opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its acuminate leaf tips and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
