Scrophularia canina subsp. bicolor

Scrophularia canina subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored dog's mouth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are distinctive, with two contrasting colors—usually white and purple—on the same flower, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
