Stemodia arizonica

Stemodia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona stemodia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small shrub with woody stems that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Stemodia arizonica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
