Sempervivum juratense

Sempervivum juratense, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold temperatures and drought, contributing to its resilience in rocky environments.
