Syzygium obtusifolium

Syzygium obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family.
