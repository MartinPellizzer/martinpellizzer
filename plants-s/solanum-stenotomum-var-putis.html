Solanum stenotomum var. putis

Solanum stenotomum var. putis, commonly known as the Putis nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, though it is not typically consumed by humans due to its potential toxicity.
