Sindora siamensis var. maritima

Sindora siamensis var. maritima, commonly known as the sea sindora, is a variant of the tropical tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries containing edible seeds. The species is adapted to saline and brackish environments, making it a hardy plant in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of mangrove and estuarine ecosystems.
