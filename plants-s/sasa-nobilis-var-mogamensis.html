Sasa nobilis var. mogamensis

Sasa nobilis var. mogamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Mogami bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is often used in traditional Japanese gardens for its ornamental value and ability to create a dense, lush appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively low maintenance once established.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a decorative element in landscape design.
