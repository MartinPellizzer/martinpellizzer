Sterculia mirabilis

Sterculia mirabilis, commonly known as the miracle sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a significant species in botanical studies.
