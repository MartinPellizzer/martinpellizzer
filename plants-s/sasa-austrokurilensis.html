Sasa austrokurilensis

Sasa austrokurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to the northern Pacific region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts by local communities. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa austrokurilensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
