Salix sessilifolia var. hindsiana

Salix sessilifolia var. hindsiana, commonly known as Hinds' willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
