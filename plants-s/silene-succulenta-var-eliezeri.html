Silene succulenta var. eliezeri

Silene succulenta var. eliezeri, commonly known as the Eliezer's campion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This variant is noted for its unique morphology and is often grown in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
