Scrophularia ruprechtii

Scrophularia ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

Scrophularia ruprechtii is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
