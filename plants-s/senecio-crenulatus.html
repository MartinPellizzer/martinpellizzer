Senecio crenulatus

Senecio crenulatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue-green, slightly wavy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is succulent, adapted to arid environments, and stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio crenulatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
