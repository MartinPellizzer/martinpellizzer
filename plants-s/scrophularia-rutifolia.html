Scrophularia rutifolia

Scrophularia rutifolia, commonly known as the woolly scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia rutifolia is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
