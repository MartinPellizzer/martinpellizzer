Scleria pauciflora var. caroliniana

Scleria pauciflora var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

The species is notable for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
