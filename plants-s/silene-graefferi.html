Silene graefferi

Silene graefferi, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene graefferi is also known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
