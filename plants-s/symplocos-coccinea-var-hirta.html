Symplocos coccinea var. hirta

Symplocos coccinea var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy red beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and evergreens.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in the spring before the leaves fully develop.
