Shorea nitens

Shorea nitens, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
