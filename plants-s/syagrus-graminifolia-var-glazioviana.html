Syagrus graminifolia var. glazioviana

Syagrus graminifolia var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian palm, is a species native to Brazil, particularly found in the state of Minas Gerais.

It is a medium-sized palm tree characterized by its slender trunk and feather-like, compound leaves that are typically green with a slight blue tint. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
