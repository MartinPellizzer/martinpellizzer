Sapium guatemalense

Sapium guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan soapberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berry-like fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
