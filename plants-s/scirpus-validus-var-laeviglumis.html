Scirpus validus var. laeviglumis

Scirpus validus var. laeviglumis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are smooth and often reddish in color, growing up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps at the base.

This variety is well adapted to moist or submerged soils and is often found in marshes, pond margins, and along stream banks.
