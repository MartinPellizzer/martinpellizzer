Spiraea tomentosa var. alba

Spiraea tomentosa var. alba, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has grayish-white, woolly foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens, borders, and as a landscape accent.
