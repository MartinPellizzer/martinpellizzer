Saxifraga crassifolia

Saxifraga crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Saxifraga crassifolia is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
