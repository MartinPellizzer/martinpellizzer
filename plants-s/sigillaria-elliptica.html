Sigillaria elliptica

Sigillaria elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical sigillaria, is a fossilized plant from the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf-like structures that are often preserved in coal balls. The plant's morphology includes elongated, ribbon-like leaves with intricate venation patterns. These features provide valuable insights into ancient plant anatomy and paleoenvironments.

Sigillaria elliptica is a key genus for understanding the evolution of early vascular plants.
