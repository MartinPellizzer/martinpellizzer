Simaba guianensis var. angustifolia

Simaba guianensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana and Venezuela.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical forests.
