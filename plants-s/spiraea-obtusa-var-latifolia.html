Spiraea obtusa var. latifolia

Spiraea obtusa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically oval to elliptic, with a broad, flat shape that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive flowers and graceful form in garden settings.
