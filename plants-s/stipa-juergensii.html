Stipa juergensii

Stipa juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Stipa juergensii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
