Solanum chaucha var. puca-suitu

Solanum chaucha var. puca-suitu, commonly known as the purple bell pepper, is a variant of the solanum species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep purple to almost black skin, which becomes more intense as the fruit matures. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters with branched stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and the fruit is a bell-shaped pepper that can be harvested when fully matured.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique coloration, making it a popular choice for both gardens and culinary use.
