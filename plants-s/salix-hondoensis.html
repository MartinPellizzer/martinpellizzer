Salix hondoensis

Salix hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo willow, is a species of willow native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and forest edges. The plant has slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered with a fine layer of silky hair. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically yellow-green in color and appear in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
