Smilax graciliflora

Smilax graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered smilax, is a species of climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny projections, which help it climb and defend itself. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Smilax graciliflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
