Stylosanthes sundaica

Stylosanthes sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda stylo, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
