Sagina procumbens subsp. muscosa

Sagina procumbens subsp.

muscosa, commonly known as mossy sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It has small, succulent, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces tiny, pink to reddish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
