Senecio illinitus

Senecio illinitus, commonly known as the Illinois tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senecio illinitus typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.
