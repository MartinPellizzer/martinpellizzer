Stachys balansae subsp. carduchorum

Stachys balansae subsp.

carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
