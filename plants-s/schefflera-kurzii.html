Schefflera kurzii

Schefflera kurzii, commonly known as the Hawaiian schefflera, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a hand, with multiple lobes radiating from a single point. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often dark green and glossy, providing a lush, ornamental appearance.

Schefflera kurzii is valued in horticulture for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
