Solanum pygmaeum var. integrifolium

Solanum pygmaeum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are entire, giving it its botanical name "integrifolium.". The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and unique foliage.
