Sphaeralcea fasciculata var. nuttallii

Sphaeralcea fasciculata var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as desert scrub and grasslands. The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name.

It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
