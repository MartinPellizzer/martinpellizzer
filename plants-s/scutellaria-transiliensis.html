Scutellaria transiliensis

Scutellaria transiliensis, commonly known as the Transilvanian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Scutellaria transiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
