Streptanthella longirostris

Streptanthella longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked wintercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is typically found in open habitats.
