Senecio leontodontis

Senecio leontodontis, commonly known as the lion's tooth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces a woolly, glandular covering.

Senecio leontodontis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
