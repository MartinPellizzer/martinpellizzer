Sterculia zeylanica

Sterculia zeylanica, commonly known as the Sri Lankan sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its heavy, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
