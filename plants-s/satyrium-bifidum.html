Satyrium bifidum

Satyrium bifidum, commonly known as the bifid orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a small, terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several whorled leaves, and its flowers are clustered in a dense spike. The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive bilobed lip that gives the species its scientific name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
