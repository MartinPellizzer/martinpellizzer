Salvia rhodantha

Salvia rhodantha, commonly known as the crimson sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Salvia rhodantha is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
