Senecio tenuilobus

Senecio tenuilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine, white hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
