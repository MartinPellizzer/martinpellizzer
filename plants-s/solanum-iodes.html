Solanum iodes

Solanum iodes, commonly known as the purple potato or purple nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its violet to purple flowers and tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and peppers. Solanum iodes thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in both ecological and medicinal studies.
