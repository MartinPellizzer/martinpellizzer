Salix dicksoniana

Salix dicksoniana, commonly known as the Dickson's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white underneath. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is adapted to grow in high-altitude, often harsh environments. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
