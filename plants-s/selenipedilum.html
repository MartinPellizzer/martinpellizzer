Selenipedilum

Selenipedilum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant with large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, lantern-like blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Selenipedilum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique flowering pattern and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
