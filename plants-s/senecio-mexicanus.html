Senecio mexicanus

Senecio mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Senecio mexicanus is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
