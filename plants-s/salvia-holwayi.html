Salvia holwayi

Salvia holwayi, commonly known as Holway's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
