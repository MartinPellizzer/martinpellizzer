Syngonanthus anthemidiflorus var. anthemidiflorus

Syngonanthus anthemidiflorus var. anthemidiflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, aiding in water retention.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
