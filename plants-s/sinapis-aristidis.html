Sinapis aristidis

Sinapis aristidis, commonly known as the mountain mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Sinapis aristidis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
