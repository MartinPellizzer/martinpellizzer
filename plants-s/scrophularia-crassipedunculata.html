Scrophularia crassipedunculata

Scrophularia crassipedunculata, commonly known as the broad-stemmed figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
