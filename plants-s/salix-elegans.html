Salix elegans

Salix elegans, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a slight reddish tinge, and it is often found near water sources.

Salix elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
