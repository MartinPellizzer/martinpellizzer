Solms-laubachia angustifolia

Solms-laubachia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved solms-laubachia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
