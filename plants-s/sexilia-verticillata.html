Sexilia verticillata

Sexilia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports such as fences or trees. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.
