Solanum commersonii var. depauperatum

Solanum commersonii var. depauperatum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species in xerophytic ecosystems.
