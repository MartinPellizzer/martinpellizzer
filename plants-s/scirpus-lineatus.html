Scirpus lineatus

Scirpus lineatus, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves arranged along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which develop into cylindrical seed heads. Scirpus lineatus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil, contributing to the ecological health of its habitat.
