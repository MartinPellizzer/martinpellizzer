Strychnos huillensis

Strychnos huillensis, commonly known as the Huilliche strychnos, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

While it is not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
