Schoenoplectiella muricinux

Schoenoplectiella muricinux, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Schoenoplectiella muricinux is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
