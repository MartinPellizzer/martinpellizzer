Sisyrinchium claritae

Sisyrinchium claritae, commonly known as the bright blue gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Sisyrinchium claritae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to arid environments.
