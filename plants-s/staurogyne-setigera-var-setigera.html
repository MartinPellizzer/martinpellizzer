Staurogyne setigera var. setigera

Staurogyne setigera var. setigera, commonly known as the slender waterwheel plant, is a aquatic herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features whorled leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in spikes, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
