Sedum hakonense var. rupifragum

Sedum hakonense var. rupifragum, commonly known as the Arctic sedum, is a perennial succulent native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for cold-climate gardening.
