Sorghum dochna var. wightii

Sorghum dochna var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
