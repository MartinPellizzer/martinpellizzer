Stemodia verticillata

Stemodia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered stemodia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often opposite or whorled. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
