Solanum andigenum f. arcuatum

Solanum andigenum f. arcuatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or purple. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, orange-yellow berries that contain the toxic alkaloid solasodine.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
