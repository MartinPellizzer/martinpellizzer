Selago rehmannii

Selago rehmannii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Selago rehmannii is typically short-lived, with a lifespan of one to two years.
