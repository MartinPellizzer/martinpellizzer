Sparattosyce dioica

Sparattosyce dioica, commonly known as the two-colored sparrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctively two-toned leaves, which often display a mix of green and silver hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
