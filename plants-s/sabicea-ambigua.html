Sabicea ambigua

Sabicea ambigua, commonly known as the warty sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
