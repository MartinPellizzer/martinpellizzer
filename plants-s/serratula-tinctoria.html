Serratula tinctoria

Serratula tinctoria, commonly known as the dyer's rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Serratula tinctoria has historical significance in dye production and traditional medicine.
