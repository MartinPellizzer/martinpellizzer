Salvia scapiformis f. keitaoensis

Salvia scapiformis f. keitaoensis, commonly known as the Keitao sage, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy floral structures. The plant typically features slender, upright stems with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanical curiosity and of interest for conservation efforts.
