Salvia urmiensis

Salvia urmiensis, commonly known as Urmian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia urmiensis is valued both for its medicinal properties and its role in traditional Persian horticulture.
