Sonchus gummifer

Sonchus gummifer, commonly known as gumweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and basal rosettes of broadly lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is known for its milky latex sap, which is a distinguishing feature.

Sonchus gummifer is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.
