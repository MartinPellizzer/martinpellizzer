Scrophularia triflora

Scrophularia triflora, commonly known as three-flowered figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy, tubular flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia triflora is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
