Salvia fortunei

Salvia fortunei, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia fortunei is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
