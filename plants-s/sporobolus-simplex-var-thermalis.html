Sporobolus simplex var. thermalis

Sporobolus simplex var. thermalis, commonly known as thermal dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the hot, arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to high temperatures and low water availability, making it a hardy species in xeric environments. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils contribute to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.

Thermal dropseed is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
