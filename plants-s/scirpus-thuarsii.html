Scirpus thuarsii

Scirpus thuarsii, commonly known as the Kashmir rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water retention.

Scirpus thuarsii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
