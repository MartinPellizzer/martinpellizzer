Senecio ptarmicifolius

Senecio ptarmicifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use.

Its leaves and flowers are sometimes used in herbal remedies for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
