Sasa lancifolia

Sasa lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Sasa lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in woodland areas.
