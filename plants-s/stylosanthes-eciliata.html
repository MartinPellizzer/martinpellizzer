Stylosanthes eciliata

Stylosanthes eciliata, commonly known as the sicklepod or cowpea grass, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to poor soils and is often used as a forage crop and for soil nitrogen fixation.

Stylosanthes eciliata is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
