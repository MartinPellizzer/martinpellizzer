Salix caprea f. kilmarnocki

Salix caprea f. kilmarnocki, commonly known as the Kilmarnock weeping willow, is a unique form of the common willow.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. This variety is native to the British Isles and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along waterways.

The plant is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
