Sanguisorba officinalis var. pubescens

Sanguisorba officinalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive reddish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
