Syzygium angustovatum

Syzygium angustovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible, woody pods containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Syzygium angustovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
