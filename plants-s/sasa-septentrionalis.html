Sasa septentrionalis

Sasa septentrionalis, commonly known as northern bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the forests of northern Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa septentrionalis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
