Syzygium cyrtophylloides

Syzygium cyrtophylloides, commonly known as the dwarf apple or red apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia, particularly in Queensland. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, red, apple-like drupe, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

Syzygium cyrtophylloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, flavorful fruit.
