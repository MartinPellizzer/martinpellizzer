Salix × rubens var. excelsior

Salix × rubens var. excelsior, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that hang in cascading streams of long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. Its leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal color to its environment.

The Weeping Willow is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
