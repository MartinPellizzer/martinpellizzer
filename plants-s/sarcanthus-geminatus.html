Sarcanthus geminatus

Sarcanthus geminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Sarcanthus geminatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
