Serapias lingua subsp. stenopetala

Serapias lingua subsp.

stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, butterfly-shaped blooms during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
