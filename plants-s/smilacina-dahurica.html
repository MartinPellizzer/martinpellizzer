Smilacina dahurica

Smilacina dahurica, commonly known as yellow false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking yellowish-green flowers in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its berries, which ripen in late summer, are dark blue and edible.

Smilacina dahurica thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
