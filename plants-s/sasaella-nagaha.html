Sasaella nagaha

Sasaella nagaha, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Sasaella nagaha thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its growth habit and structure make it an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
