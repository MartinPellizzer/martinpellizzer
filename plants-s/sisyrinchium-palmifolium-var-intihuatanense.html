Sisyrinchium palmifolium var. intihuatanense

Sisyrinchium palmifolium var. intihuatanense, commonly known as the Andean blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are broad and divided into lobes resembling a hand. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
