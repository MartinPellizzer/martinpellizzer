Sophronitis × adrienne

Sophronitis × adrienne, commonly known as the Adrienne orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Sophronitis censu and Sophronitis × sanderiana.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant typically features vibrant, showy flowers with a complex structure, including a spurred lip and a column. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
