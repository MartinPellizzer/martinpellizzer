Solanum anomalum var. trifurcatum

Solanum anomalum var. trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-lobed nightshade, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The fruit is typically a globose berry that ripens to a yellow or orange hue. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally not cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
