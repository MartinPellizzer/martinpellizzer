Stipa dasyvaginata

Stipa dasyvaginata, commonly known as the woolly feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Stipa dasyvaginata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
