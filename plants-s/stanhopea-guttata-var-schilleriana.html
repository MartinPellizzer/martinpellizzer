Stanhopea guttata var. schilleriana

Stanhopea guttata var. schilleriana, commonly known as the Schiller's spotted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration, often featuring intricate patterns and a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are robust, and it produces long, slender leaves that help it thrive in its natural habitat.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity.
