Salvia phlomoides

Salvia phlomoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia phlomoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
