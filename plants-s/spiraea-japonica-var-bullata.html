Spiraea japonica var. bullata

Spiraea japonica var. bullata, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, making it visually striking in gardens. Its foliage is dark green, turning reddish in the fall, and it is relatively low maintenance.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.
