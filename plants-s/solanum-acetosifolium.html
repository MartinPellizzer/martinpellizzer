Solanum acetosifolium

Solanum acetosifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant bears yellow, berry-like fruits that are typically toxic to humans but can be used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Solanum acetosifolium is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
