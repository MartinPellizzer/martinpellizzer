Silene subciliata

Silene subciliata, commonly known as the fringed cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
