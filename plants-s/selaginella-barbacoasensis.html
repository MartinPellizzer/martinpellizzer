Selaginella barbacoasensis

Selaginella barbacoasensis, commonly known as the Barbacoas spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to Central America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

Selaginella barbacoasensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, shaded habitats.
