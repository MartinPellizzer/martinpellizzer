Strombocarpa burkartii

Strombocarpa burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Strombocarpa burkartii is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.
