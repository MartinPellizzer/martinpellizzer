Sesbania cochinchinensis

Sesbania cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable plant for nitrogen fixation and erosion control.
