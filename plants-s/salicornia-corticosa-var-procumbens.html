Salicornia corticosa var. procumbens

Salicornia corticosa var. procumbens, commonly known as sea beans or sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a low-growing, succulent species with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other brackish habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
