Solanum extuspellitum

Solanum extuspellitum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic compounds such as solanine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
