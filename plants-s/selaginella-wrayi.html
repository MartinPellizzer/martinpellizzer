Selaginella wrayi

Selaginella wrayi, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. It possesses a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the sporangia for producing spores.

Selaginella wrayi is often used in horticulture due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
