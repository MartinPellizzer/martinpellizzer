Stylis chinensis

Stylis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stylis chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
