Salvia funckii

Salvia funckii, commonly known as the funck's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Salvia funckii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
