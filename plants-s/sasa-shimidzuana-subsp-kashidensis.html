Sasa shimidzuana subsp. kashidensis

Sasa shimidzuana subsp.

kashidensis, commonly known as the Kashidense bamboo, is a shrub-like perennial plant native to Japan. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that are often used in traditional crafts.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
