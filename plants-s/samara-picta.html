Samara picta

Samara picta, commonly known as the painted samara, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and apples, and is characterized by its distinctive, winged seeds. The plant produces small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its unique samara fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant prefers well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded areas.

Samara picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
