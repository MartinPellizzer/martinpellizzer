Senecio lushaiensis

Senecio lushaiensis, commonly known as the Lushai Hills Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky hillsides. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and unique habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
