Schima wallichii var. noronhae

Schima wallichii var. noronhae, commonly known as the Noronha schima, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
