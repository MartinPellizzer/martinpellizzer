Syzygium yersinii

Syzygium yersinii, commonly known as the Yersin's syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Syzygium yersinii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
