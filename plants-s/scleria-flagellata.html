Scleria flagellata

Scleria flagellata, commonly known as the slender scouring rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scleria flagellata typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream sides.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
