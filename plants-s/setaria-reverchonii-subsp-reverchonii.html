Setaria reverchonii subsp. reverchonii

Setaria reverchonii subsp.

reverchonii, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences, which contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
