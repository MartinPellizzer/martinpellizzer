Serapias lingua subsp. veneris

Serapias lingua subsp.

veneris, commonly known as the "Cupid's Tongue," is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to southern Europe. It is a perennial herb with showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive shape, resembling a tongue, and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

It is a relatively rare and specialized species, adapted to specific environmental conditions.
