Silene valsecchiae

Silene valsecchiae, commonly known as the Valsecchia's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.
