Sorbus mougeotii subsp. austriaca

Sorbus mougeotii subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
