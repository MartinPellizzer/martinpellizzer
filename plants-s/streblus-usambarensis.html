Streblus usambarensis

Streblus usambarensis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble breadfruit. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and has a starchy, sweet flavor.
