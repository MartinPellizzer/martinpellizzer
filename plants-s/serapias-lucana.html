Serapias lucana

Serapias lucana, commonly known as the Lucanian orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to southern Italy.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny, open habitats such as limestone slopes and grasslands. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
