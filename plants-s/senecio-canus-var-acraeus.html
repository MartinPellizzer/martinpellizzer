Senecio canus var. acraeus

Senecio canus var. acraeus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It has a creeping growth habit and produces a milky sap that is toxic to livestock.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its poisonous properties.
