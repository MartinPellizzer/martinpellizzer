Solanum hirsutum var. abyssinicum

Solanum hirsutum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian nightshade, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
