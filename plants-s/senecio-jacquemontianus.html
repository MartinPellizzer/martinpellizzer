Senecio jacquemontianus

Senecio jacquemontianus, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems that resemble a ladder, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
