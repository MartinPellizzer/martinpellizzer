Scirpus japonicus var. thermalis

Scirpus japonicus var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It thrives in wetland environments, particularly in marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on the season. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate high temperatures and is often found in geothermal areas.
