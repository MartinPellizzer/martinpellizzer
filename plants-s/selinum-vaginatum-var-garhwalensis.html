Selinum vaginatum var. garhwalensis

Selinum vaginatum var. garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Apiaceae family.
