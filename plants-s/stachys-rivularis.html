Stachys rivularis

Stachys rivularis, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stachys rivularis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
