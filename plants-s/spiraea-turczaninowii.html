Spiraea turczaninowii

Spiraea turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often arching or upright. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and they turn a striking red or orange in the fall.

Spiraea turczaninowii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
