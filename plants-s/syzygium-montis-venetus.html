Syzygium montis-venetus

Syzygium montis-venetus, commonly known as the mountain mist apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is highly valued in local cuisines. Its growth is typically slow, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils with ample sunlight.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
