Styrax dealbatus

Styrax dealbatus, commonly known as the white styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Styrax dealbatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
