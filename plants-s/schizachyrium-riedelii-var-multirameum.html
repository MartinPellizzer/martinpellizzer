Schizachyrium riedelii var. multirameum

Schizachyrium riedelii var. multirameum, commonly known as the eastern grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, feather-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall coloration, which ranges from red to orange.
