Senecio taraxacifolius var. discoideus

Senecio taraxacifolius var. discoideus, commonly known as the "dandelion-leaved groundsel," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disc flowers, typical of the family. Its leaves are deeply lobed, resembling those of dandelions, hence its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
