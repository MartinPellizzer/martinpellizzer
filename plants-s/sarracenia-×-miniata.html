Sarracenia × miniata

Sarracenia × miniata, commonly known as the miniature marsh orchid, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, pitcher-shaped leaves that are adapted to trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils near freshwater habitats such as bogs and swamps. Its vibrant red and green foliage makes it a popular choice for cultivation in specialized gardens.

Sarracenia × miniata requires careful attention to moisture levels and light conditions to thrive.
