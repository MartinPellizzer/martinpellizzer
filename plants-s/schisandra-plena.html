Schisandra plena

Schisandra plena, commonly known as the full schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red berries that mature in late summer. The plant is valued for its aromatic, medicinal properties, with the berries containing compounds like schisandra chinensis. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

Schisandra plena thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal gardens.
