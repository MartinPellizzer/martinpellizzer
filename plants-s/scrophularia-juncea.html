Scrophularia juncea

Scrophularia juncea, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Scrophularia juncea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
