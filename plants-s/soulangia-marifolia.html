Soulangia marifolia

Soulangia marifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Soulangia marifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
