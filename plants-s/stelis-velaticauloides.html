Stelis velaticauloides

Stelis velaticauloides, commonly known as the velvety-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
