Setaria glauca var. imberbis

Setaria glauca var. imberbis, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize new areas quickly.

The variety imberbis is distinguished by its reduced or absent bristles on the lemma, setting it apart from other varieties of Setaria glauca.
