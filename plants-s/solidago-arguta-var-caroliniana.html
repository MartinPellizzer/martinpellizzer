Solidago arguta var. caroliniana

Solidago arguta var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is well adapted to moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
