Succisa amplexicaulis

Succisa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that tightly clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
