Solanum coagulans var. griseum

Solanum coagulans var. griseum, commonly known as the gray solanum, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique morphological features.
