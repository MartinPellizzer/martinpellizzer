Suaeda mexicana

Suaeda mexicana, commonly known as Mexican saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in saline soils.

Suaeda mexicana is well adapted to harsh, coastal and desert conditions, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
