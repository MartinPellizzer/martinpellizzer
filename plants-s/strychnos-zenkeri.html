Strychnos zenkeri

Strychnos zenkeri, commonly known as the African poisonwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain small, dark seeds. It is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, such as strychnine, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
