Serapias cordigera subsp. cordigera

Serapias cordigera subsp.

cordigera, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a red throat, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
