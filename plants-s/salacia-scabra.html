Salacia scabra

Salacia scabra, commonly known as the rough salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving it its common name.

Salacia scabra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
