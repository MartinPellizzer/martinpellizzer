Sanchezia speciosa

Sanchezia speciosa, commonly known as the giant swampmallow, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Sanchezia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
