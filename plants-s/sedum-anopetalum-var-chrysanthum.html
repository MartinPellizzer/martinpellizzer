Sedum anopetalum var. chrysanthum

Sedum anopetalum var. chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.
