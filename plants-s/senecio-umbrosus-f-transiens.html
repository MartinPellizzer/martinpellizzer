Senecio umbrosus f. transiens

Senecio umbrosus f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Shadowweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically covered with fine, soft hairs. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy surface. Its small, daisy-like flowers are yellow with a central disc, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
