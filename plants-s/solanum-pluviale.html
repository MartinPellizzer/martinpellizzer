Solanum pluviale

Solanum pluviale, commonly known as the rain tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are edible when ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Solanum pluviale is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
