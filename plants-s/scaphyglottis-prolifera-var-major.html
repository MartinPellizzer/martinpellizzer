Scaphyglottis prolifera var. major

Scaphyglottis prolifera var. major, commonly known as the giant scaphyglottis, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
