Sorbus aucuparia f. acutidens

Sorbus aucuparia f. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-toothed rowan, is a horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its sharply serrated leaves and bright red berries that persist through winter. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The shrub is relatively hardy and can be grown in gardens as a decorative plant.
