Scutellaria velenovskyi subsp. goulimyi

Scutellaria velenovskyi subsp.

goulimyi, commonly known as the Goulimy's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
