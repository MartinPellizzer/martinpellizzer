Syzygium papyraceum

Syzygium papyraceum, commonly known as the paperbark apple, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium papyraceum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant and a valuable species for ecological studies.
