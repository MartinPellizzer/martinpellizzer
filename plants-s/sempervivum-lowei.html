Sempervivum lowei

Sempervivum lowei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is often found growing in rocky soils at high elevations, where it can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
