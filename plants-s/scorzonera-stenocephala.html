Scorzonera stenocephala

Scorzonera stenocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is robust, and the roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera stenocephala is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.
