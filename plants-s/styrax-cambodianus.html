Styrax cambodianus

Styrax cambodianus, commonly known as Cambodian frankincense, is a species of flowering plant in the family Styracaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, resinous sap, which is harvested for use in traditional medicine and as a perfume. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are white or pale pink and clustered in panicles.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
