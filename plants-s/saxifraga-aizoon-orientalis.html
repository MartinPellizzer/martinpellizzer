Saxifraga aizoon orientalis

Saxifraga aizoon orientalis, commonly known as the Persian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically display shades of pink or white.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
