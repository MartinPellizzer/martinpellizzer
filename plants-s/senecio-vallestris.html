Senecio vallestris

Senecio vallestris, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

Senecio vallestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowers.
