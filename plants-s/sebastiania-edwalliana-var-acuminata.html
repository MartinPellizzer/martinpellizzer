Sebastiania edwalliana var. acuminata

Sebastiania edwalliana var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata varietal of the Sebestena plant, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
