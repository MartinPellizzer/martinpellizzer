Saxifraga cortusifolia var. madida

Saxifraga cortusifolia var. madida, commonly known as the Madida saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are generally ovate to elliptic, with a slightly toothed margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
