Senecio subarnicoides

Senecio subarnicoides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, silver-gray leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
