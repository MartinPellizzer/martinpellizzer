Scrophularia cretica

Scrophularia cretica, commonly known as creeping scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds. The plant features slender, creeping stems that can spread up to several meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia cretica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
