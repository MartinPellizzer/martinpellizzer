Stevia conferta

Stevia conferta, commonly known as the clustered stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, clustered leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a hardy species. Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia conferta is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
