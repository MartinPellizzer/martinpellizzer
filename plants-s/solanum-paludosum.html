Solanum paludosum

Solanum paludosum, commonly known as swamp nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Solanum paludosum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, sandy soils and can tolerate periodic flooding.
