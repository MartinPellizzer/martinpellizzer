Salvia japonica var. formosana

Salvia japonica var. formosana, commonly known as the Japanese sage or Formosa sage, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
