Senecio barba-johannis

Senecio barba-johannis, commonly known as the "Jacob's ladder," is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems that give it a ladder-like appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio barba-johannis is also known for its ability to propagate easily through cuttings.
