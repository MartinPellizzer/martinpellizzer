Stylosanthes montevidensis f. setosa

Stylosanthes montevidensis f. setosa, commonly known as the hairy stylosanthes, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its dense growth habit and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in forage and soil conservation.

Its hairy foliage and compact form make it distinctive among its relatives.
