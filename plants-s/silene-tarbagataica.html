Silene tarbagataica

Silene tarbagataica, commonly known as the Tarbagatai catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene tarbagataica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
