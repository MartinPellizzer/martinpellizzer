Solanum dulcamara var. villosissimum

Solanum dulcamara var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a perennial climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The flowers are typically violet or blue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow stamen. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
