Stipa caespitosa var. subtypica

Stipa caespitosa var. subtypica, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are typically purple or pink in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
