Sapium mammosum

Sapium mammosum, commonly known as the "mammary tree" or "cow's milk tree," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated, seed-containing pods. Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitats.
