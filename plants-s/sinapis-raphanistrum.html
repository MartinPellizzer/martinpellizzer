Sinapis raphanistrum

Sinapis raphanistrum, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Sinapis raphanistrum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants.
