Salsola acutifolia

Salsola acutifolia, commonly known as desert spinach or saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Salsola acutifolia is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to extreme conditions.
