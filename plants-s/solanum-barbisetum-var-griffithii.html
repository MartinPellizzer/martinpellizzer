Solanum barbisetum var. griffithii

Solanum barbisetum var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
