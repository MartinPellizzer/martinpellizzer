Sedum henrici-roberti

Sedum henrici-roberti, commonly known as the Henry Roberts stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
