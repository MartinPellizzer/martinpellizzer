Saranthe composita

Saranthe composita, commonly known as the composite saranthe, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. Saranthe composita is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
