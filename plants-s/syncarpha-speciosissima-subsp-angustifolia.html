Syncarpha speciosissima subsp. angustifolia

Syncarpha speciosissima subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a dense rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
