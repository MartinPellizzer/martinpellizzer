Stelis tacanensis

Stelis tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, specifically found in countries like Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to grow in rocky or mountainous environments, reflecting its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Stelis tacanensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
