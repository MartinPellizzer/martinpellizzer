Selenia aurea

Selenia aurea, commonly known as the golden selenia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Selenia aurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
