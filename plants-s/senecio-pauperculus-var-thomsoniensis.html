Senecio pauperculus var. thomsoniensis

Senecio pauperculus var. thomsoniensis, commonly known as Thomson's ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its low-growing habit and unique appearance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
