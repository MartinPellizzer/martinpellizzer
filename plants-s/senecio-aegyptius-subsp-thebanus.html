Senecio aegyptius subsp. thebanus

Senecio aegyptius subsp.

thebanus, commonly known as the Theban groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense heads.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adapted to dry and semi-arid environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
