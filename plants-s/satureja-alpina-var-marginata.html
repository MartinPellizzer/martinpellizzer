Satureja alpina var. marginata

Satureja alpina var. marginata, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
