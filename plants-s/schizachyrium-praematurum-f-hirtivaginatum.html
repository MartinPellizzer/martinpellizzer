Schizachyrium praematurum f. hirtivaginatum

Schizachyrium praematurum f. hirtivaginatum, commonly known as the hairy bracted bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, which appear in late summer. Its hairy bracts are a distinguishing feature, giving it the specific epithet *hirtivaginatum*.

This grass is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.
