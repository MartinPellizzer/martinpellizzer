Serratula acutifolia

Serratula acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf serratula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Serratula acutifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
