Steironema membranaceum

Steironema membranaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
