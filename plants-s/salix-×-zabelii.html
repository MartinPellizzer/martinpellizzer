Salix × zabelii

Salix × zabelii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Salix × zabelii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
