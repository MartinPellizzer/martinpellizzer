Strephium

Strephium, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in saline or coastal environments, where they thrive in high-salinity soils. Strephium species are characterized by their succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. They are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Strephium sanguineum, which is known for its vibrant floral displays.
