Salix × neuburgensis

Salix × neuburgensis, commonly known as the Neuburg weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and paler underneath. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

The flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
