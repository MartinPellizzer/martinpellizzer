Spiradiclis cordata

Spiradiclis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf spiradiclis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Spiradiclis cordata thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
