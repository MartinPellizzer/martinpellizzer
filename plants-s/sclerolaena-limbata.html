Sclerolaena limbata

Sclerolaena limbata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Sclerolaena limbata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
