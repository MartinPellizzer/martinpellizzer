Smilax sieboldii var. inermis

Smilax sieboldii var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features long, slender stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax sieboldii var. inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
