Senecio paucidentatus

Senecio paucidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and linear, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
