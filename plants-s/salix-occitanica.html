Salix occitanica

Salix occitanica, commonly known as the Occitan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-brown underneath, and it produces catkins in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Salix occitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
