Swertia dichotoma var. punctata

Swertia dichotoma var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while the flowers are tubular with prominent blue or purple spots.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
