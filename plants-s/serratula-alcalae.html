Serratula alcalae

Serratula alcalae, commonly known as the alkaline serrated wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to grow in dry, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Serratula alcalae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
