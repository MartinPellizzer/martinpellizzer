Silene jaxartica

Silene jaxartica, commonly known as the Jaxartian campion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense mats or low shrubs.

Silene jaxartica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
