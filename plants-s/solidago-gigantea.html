Solidago gigantea

Solidago gigantea, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn, which are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago gigantea is often found in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks.
