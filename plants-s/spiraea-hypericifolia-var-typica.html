Spiraea hypericifolia var. typica

Spiraea hypericifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
