Syzygium viridifolium

Syzygium viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaf satinash, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Syzygium viridifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
