Squilla maura

Squilla maura, commonly known as the sea squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white or pink flowers with a distinctive, spiky structure. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal environments.
