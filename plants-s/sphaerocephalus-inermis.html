Sphaerocephalus inermis

Sphaerocephalus inermis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems and leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
