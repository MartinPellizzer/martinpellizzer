Stryphnodendron piptadenioides

Stryphnodendron piptadenioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pataua, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
