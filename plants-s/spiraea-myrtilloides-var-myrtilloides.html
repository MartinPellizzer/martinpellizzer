Spiraea myrtilloides var. myrtilloides

Spiraea myrtilloides var. myrtilloides, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches covered in clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
