Serapias × demadesii

Serapias × demadesii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the serapias genus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias grandiflora.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in Mediterranean regions. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, strap-like leaves and a short, erect stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
