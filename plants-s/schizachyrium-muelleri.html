Schizachyrium muelleri

Schizachyrium muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery panicles that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more prominent during the growing season.

Schizachyrium muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
