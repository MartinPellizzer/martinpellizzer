Schomburgkia brysiana var. thomsoniana

Schomburgkia brysiana var. thomsoniana, commonly known as the Thomson's spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, arching stems and clusters of small, intricate flowers with dark purple or brownish hues. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media. Its unique appearance and rarity make it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

It is relatively easy to care for, though it demands consistent moisture and proper ventilation to prevent disease.
