Salacca macrostachya

Salacca macrostachya, commonly known as the yellow musa or yellow plantain, is a member of the Musaceae family.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of yellowish-orange inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a banana, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.

Salacca macrostachya is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique fruit.
