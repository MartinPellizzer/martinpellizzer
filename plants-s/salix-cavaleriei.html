Salix cavaleriei

Salix cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Salix cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
