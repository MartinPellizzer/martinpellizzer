Sida paniculata var. floribunda

Sida paniculata var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is typically hairy, and it has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
