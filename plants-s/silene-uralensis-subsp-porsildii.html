Silene uralensis subsp. porsildii

Silene uralensis subsp.

porsildii, commonly known as the Ural catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies. Silene uralensis subsp.

porsildii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
