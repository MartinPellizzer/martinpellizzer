Saxifraga virginiensis var. pentadecandra

Saxifraga virginiensis var. pentadecandra, commonly known as the Virginia saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and shaded woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
