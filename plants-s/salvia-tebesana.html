Salvia tebesana

Salvia tebesana, commonly known as the Tebessa sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia tebesana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
