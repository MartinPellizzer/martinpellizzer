Schizostachyum chinense

Schizostachyum chinense, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or Chinese silver bamboo, is a perennial grass native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Schizostachyum chinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
