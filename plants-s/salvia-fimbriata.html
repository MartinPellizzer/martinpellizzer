Salvia fimbriata

Salvia fimbriata, commonly known as fringed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia fimbriata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
