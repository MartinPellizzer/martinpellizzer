Sicyos microcarpus var. woolfordii

Sicyos microcarpus var. woolfordii, commonly known as the woolfordii variant of the bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces large, spherical fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often used as containers or for culinary purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The variant is valued for its hardy nature and versatility in both agricultural and decorative applications.
