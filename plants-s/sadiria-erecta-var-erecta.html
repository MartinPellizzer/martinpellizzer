Sadiria erecta var. erecta

Sadiria erecta var. erecta, commonly known as the upright sadiria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders and naturalized areas.
