Stipa latifolia var. pallens

Stipa latifolia var. pallens, commonly known as the pale leaf feathergrass, is a variant of the broadleaf grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive, frosty appearance. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that is often adorned with short, bristly hairs.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique texture and color.
