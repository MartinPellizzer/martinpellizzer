Spiropetalum calophyllum

Spiropetalum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The golden shower tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
