Sabulina californica

Sabulina californica, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, succulent stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sabulina californica is listed as a threatened species due to habitat loss and invasive species competition.
