Sclerocactus uncinatus subsp. crassihamatus

Sclerocactus uncinatus subsp.

crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-horned cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing, long-lived cactus that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
