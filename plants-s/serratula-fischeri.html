Serratula fischeri

Serratula fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Serratula fischeri produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
