Stelis scaphoides

Stelis scaphoides, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are arranged in a spike, often with a distinctive hood-like structure over the lower flowers.

Stelis scaphoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
