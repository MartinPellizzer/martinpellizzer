Saccharum officinarum

Saccharum officinarum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, sturdy stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose. The plant's leaves are broad and blade-like, while its roots system is extensive and fibrous. Sugarcane thrives in warm climates with ample sunlight and water.

It plays a crucial role in global agriculture, particularly in the production of sugar and biofuels.
