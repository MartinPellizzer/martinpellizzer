Shorea hulanidda

Shorea hulanidda, commonly known as the Hulanidda tree, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
