Solanum tuberosum var. taraco

Solanum tuberosum var. taraco, commonly known as the Taraco potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinctive botanical features.

It typically produces oval-shaped tubers with a dark purple skin and a creamy white flesh. The plant is an herbaceous perennial that grows in a bushy form with compound leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a cultivar of interest in agricultural research.
