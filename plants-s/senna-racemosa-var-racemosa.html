Senna racemosa var. racemosa

Senna racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the clustered senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain a bitter alkaloid, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used as a forage crop.
