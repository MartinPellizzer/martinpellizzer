Solanum burbankii

Solanum burbankii, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
