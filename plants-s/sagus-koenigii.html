Sagus koenigii

Sagus koenigii, commonly known as curry leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to southern India.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, oval-shaped leaves that are widely used in Indian cuisine for their aromatic properties. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal uses.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its lush foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
