Syringa × media

Syringa × media, commonly known as the weeping cherry or Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters in late spring. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and usually have 5–9 leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Syringa × media is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and parks.
