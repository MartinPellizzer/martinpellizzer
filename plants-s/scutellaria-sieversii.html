Scutellaria sieversii

Scutellaria sieversii, commonly known as Sievers' skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
