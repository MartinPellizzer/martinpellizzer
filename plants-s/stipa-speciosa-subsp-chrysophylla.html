Stipa speciosa subsp. chrysophylla

Stipa speciosa subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, feather-like inflorescences that are typically golden in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine and steppe environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue due to the presence of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
