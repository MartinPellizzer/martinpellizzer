Satureja gunihica

Satureja gunihica, commonly known as Peruvian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

The flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value.
