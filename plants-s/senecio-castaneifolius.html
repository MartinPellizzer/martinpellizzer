Senecio castaneifolius

Senecio castaneifolius, commonly known as the orange sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and orange disk flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lobed and resemble the shape of a chestnut leaf, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio castaneifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
