Stegolepis huberi

Stegolepis huberi, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Stegolepis huberi thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitat.
