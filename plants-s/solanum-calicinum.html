Solanum calicinum

Solanum calicinum, commonly known as the calycine nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Solanum calicinum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
