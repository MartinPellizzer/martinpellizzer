Salsola bottae var. faurotii

Salsola bottae var. faurotii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Salsola bottae var. faurotii plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in desert ecosystems.
