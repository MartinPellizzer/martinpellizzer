Senecio douglasii var. filiformis

Senecio douglasii var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
