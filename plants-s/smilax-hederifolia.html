Smilax hederifolia

Smilax hederifolia, commonly known as the evergreen arrowroot or greenbrier, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its stems are covered in sharp, curved spines, which serve as a defense mechanism.

Smilax hederifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
