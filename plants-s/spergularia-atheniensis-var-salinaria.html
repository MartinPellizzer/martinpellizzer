Spergularia atheniensis var. salinaria

Spergularia atheniensis var. salinaria, commonly known as salt marsh sandspurry, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it thrives in high-sodium conditions. Its reproductive structures are designed to ensure successful pollination in its harsh habitat.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
