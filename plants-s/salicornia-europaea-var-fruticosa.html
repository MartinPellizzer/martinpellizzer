Salicornia europaea var. fruticosa

Salicornia europaea var. fruticosa, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal marshes and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often red or pink in color, which help it store water and withstand high salt concentrations. The plant produces small, green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it is often used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.

Salicornia europaea var. fruticosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
