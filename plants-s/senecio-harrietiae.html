Senecio harrietiae

Senecio harrietiae, commonly known as Harriet's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
