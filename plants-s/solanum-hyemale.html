Solanum hyemale

Solanum hyemale, commonly known as the red devil or red balm, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly fuzzy surface.

Solanum hyemale contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
