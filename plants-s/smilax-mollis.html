Smilax mollis

Smilax mollis, commonly known as the silky smilax, is a vine-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a dense network of underground rhizomes that allow it to spread aggressively. The plant has compound leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. These berries are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is often considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
