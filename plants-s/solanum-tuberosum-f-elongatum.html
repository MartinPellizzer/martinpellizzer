Solanum tuberosum f. elongatum

Solanum tuberosum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped tubers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant typically has herbaceous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves, with flowers that are usually white or pale blue. Its tubers are often smaller and more elongated compared to standard potato varieties, making it a unique cultivar for specific agricultural or ornamental purposes.

This form is valued for its distinctive shape and potential use in breeding programs for improved potato traits.
