Sobralia theobromina

Sobralia theobromina, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

Sobralia theobromina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
