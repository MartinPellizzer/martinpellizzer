Sida urticifolia

Sida urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettleleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along riverbanks.

Sida urticifolia is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
