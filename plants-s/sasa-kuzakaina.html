Sasa kuzakaina

Sasa kuzakaina, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or kuzikusa, is a perennial grass native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and crafts. Sasa kuzakaina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thick mats, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
