Salvia coccinea pumila

Salvia coccinea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf scarlet sage, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features vibrant red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in its unique appearance and adaptability.
