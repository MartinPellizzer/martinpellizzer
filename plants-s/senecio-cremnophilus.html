Senecio cremnophilus

Senecio cremnophilus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio cremnophilus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
