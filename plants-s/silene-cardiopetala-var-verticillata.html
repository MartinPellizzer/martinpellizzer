Silene cardiopetala var. verticillata

Silene cardiopetala var. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
