Scirpus hirsutus

Scirpus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in marshy areas, pond edges, and other wet environments, often forming dense stands. Its rhizomes contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus hirsutus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
