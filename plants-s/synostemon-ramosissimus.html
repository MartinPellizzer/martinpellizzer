Synostemon ramosissimus

Synostemon ramosissimus, commonly known as the silverleaf sterculia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
