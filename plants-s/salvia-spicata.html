Salvia spicata

Salvia spicata, commonly known as clustered sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear whorled pairs of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-lilac flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia spicata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
