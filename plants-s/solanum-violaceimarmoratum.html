Solanum violaceimarmoratum

Solanum violaceimarmoratum, commonly known as the purple marble nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white mottled leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum violaceimarmoratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
