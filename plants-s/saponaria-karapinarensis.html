Saponaria karapinarensis

Saponaria karapinarensis, commonly known as the Karapinara soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used for its mild soap-like properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Saponaria karapinarensis is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
