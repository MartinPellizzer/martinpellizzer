Stipa letourneuxii subsp. tunetana

Stipa letourneuxii subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
