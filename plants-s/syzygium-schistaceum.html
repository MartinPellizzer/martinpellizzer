Syzygium schistaceum

Syzygium schistaceum, commonly known as the rock apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium schistaceum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
