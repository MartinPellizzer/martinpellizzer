Sinapis integrifolia var. chevalieri

Sinapis integrifolia var. chevalieri, commonly known as the Italian rocket or rocket mustard, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and hairy, with a strong, pungent odor reminiscent of mustard. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
