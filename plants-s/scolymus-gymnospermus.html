Scolymus gymnospermus

Scolymus gymnospermus, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Scolymus gymnospermus is known for its resilience to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
