Solanum pachyneuroides

Solanum pachyneuroides, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
