Stelis paradoxa

Stelis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Stelis paradoxa is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
