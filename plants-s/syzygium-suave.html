Syzygium suave

Syzygium suave, commonly known as the sweet apple or fragrant apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it popular in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium suave is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
