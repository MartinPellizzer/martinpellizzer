Scrophularia decaisniana

Scrophularia decaisniana, commonly known as the Balkan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia decaisniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
