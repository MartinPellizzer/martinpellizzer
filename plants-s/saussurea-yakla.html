Saussurea yakla

Saussurea yakla, commonly known as the Yakla saffron or Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers with feathery bracts that resemble the petals of a dandelion. Its botanical features include a hairy, branched stem and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
