Salvia puberula

Salvia puberula, commonly known as silky sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia puberula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low water requirements make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
