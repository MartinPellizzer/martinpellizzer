Spiesia lehmannii

Spiesia lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the spurge family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
