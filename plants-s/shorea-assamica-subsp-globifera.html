Shorea assamica subsp. globifera

Shorea assamica subsp.

globifera, commonly known as the Burmese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored heartwood.

The species is notable for its rounded, globular fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in lowland rainforests and is valued for its high-quality timber.

Due to overexploitation, it is also considered an endangered species.
