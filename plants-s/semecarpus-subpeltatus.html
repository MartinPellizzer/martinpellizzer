Semecarpus subpeltatus

Semecarpus subpeltatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing hard seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of tannins.
