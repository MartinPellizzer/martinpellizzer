Schima superba

Schima superba, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. Schima superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native flora.
