Schefflera glauca

Schefflera glauca, commonly known as the silver schefflera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a waxy, almost metallic sheen. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern, with multiple leaflets radiating from a central point.

Schefflera glauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
