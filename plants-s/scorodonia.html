Scorodonia

Scorodonia, commonly known as the false lily of the wall, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Scorodonia species are native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

These plants are not only valued for their aesthetic qualities but also for their historical use in traditional medicine.
