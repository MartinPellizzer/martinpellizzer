Salpichlaena scandens

Salpichlaena scandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The fronds are typically bi-pinnate, with a feathery appearance, and are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
