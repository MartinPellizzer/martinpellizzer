Scirpus inconspicuus

Scirpus inconspicuus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stem, which are typically green to brown in color. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and filter water.

Scirpus inconspicuus thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
