Setaria submacrostachya

Setaria submacrostachya, commonly known as giant beardgrass, is a tall annual grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown spikelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and seed dispersal make it an invasive species in some regions.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
