Salsola stellata

Salsola stellata, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Salsola stellata is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in conditions with limited moisture.
