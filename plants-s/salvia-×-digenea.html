Salvia × digenea

Salvia × digenea, commonly known as autumn sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its leaves are gray-green and aromatic, often releasing a minty scent when crushed.

Salvia × digenea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
