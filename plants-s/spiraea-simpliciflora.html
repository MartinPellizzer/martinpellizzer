Spiraea simpliciflora

Spiraea simpliciflora, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea simpliciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various growing conditions.
