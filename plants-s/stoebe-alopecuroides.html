Stoebe alopecuroides

Stoebe alopecuroides, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, and it produces clusters of tiny white or pink flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer.

Stoebe alopecuroides is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some environments.
