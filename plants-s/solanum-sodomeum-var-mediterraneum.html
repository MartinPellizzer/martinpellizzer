Solanum sodomeum var. mediterraneum

Solanum sodomeum var. mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
