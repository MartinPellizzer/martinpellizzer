Solanum lyratum f. xanthocarpum

Solanum lyratum f. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited silverleaf nightshade, is a variant of the silverleaf nightshade native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that transition into yellow berries, which are distinctive in color compared to the typical purple or black fruits of the species. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual fruit color.
