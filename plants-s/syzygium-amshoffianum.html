Syzygium amshoffianum

Syzygium amshoffianum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
