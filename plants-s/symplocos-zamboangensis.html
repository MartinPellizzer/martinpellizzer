Symplocos zamboangensis

Symplocos zamboangensis, commonly known as the Zamboangasian waxtree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
