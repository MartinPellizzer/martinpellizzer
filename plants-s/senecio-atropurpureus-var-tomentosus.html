Senecio atropurpureus var. tomentosus

Senecio atropurpureus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage and vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
