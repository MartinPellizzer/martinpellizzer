Saxifraga moschata subsp. hariotii

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

hariotii, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is noted for its pleasant, sometimes spicy fragrance, which is most noticeable when the plant is in bloom.
