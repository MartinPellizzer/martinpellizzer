Solanum nigrum var. virginicum

Solanum nigrum var. virginicum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa but has been naturalized in various parts of the world. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals if ingested. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
