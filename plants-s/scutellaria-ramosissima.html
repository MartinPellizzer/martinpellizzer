Scutellaria ramosissima

Scutellaria ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
