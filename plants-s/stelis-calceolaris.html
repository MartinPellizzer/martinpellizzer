Stelis calceolaris

Stelis calceolaris, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flower that resembles a small shoe, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is broad and flaring.

Stelis calceolaris prefers warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
