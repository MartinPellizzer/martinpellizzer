Saxifraga przewalskii

Saxifraga przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Saxifraga przewalskii is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.
