Solanum acutilobum

Solanum acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
