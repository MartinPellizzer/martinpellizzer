Senecio eremophilus subsp. kingii

Senecio eremophilus subsp.

kingii, commonly known as King's frostweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that reduce water loss. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, and its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny florets arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
