Solanum quindiuense

Solanum quindiuense, commonly known as the Quindio tomato, is a species native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and oval, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red berries resembling tomatoes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
