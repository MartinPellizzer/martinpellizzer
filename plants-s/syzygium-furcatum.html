Syzygium furcatum

Syzygium furcatum, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often appearing before the leaves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its leaves are elliptical and glossy.

Syzygium furcatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
