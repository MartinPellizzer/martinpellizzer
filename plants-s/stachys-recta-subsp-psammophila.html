Stachys recta subsp. psammophila

Stachys recta subsp.

psammophila, commonly known as sand sedge, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects in dry environments.
