Stylosanthes guineensis

Stylosanthes guineensis, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Stylosanthes guineensis is also used as forage for livestock and in soil conservation efforts due to its hardy nature.
