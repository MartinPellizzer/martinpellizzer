Silene venosa var. angustifolia

Silene venosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
