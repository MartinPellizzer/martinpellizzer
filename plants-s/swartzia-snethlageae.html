Swartzia snethlageae

Swartzia snethlageae, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is often used in furniture and musical instruments due to its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is also listed as a vulnerable species in some regions.
