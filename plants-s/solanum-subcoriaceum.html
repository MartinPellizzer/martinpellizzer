Solanum subcoriaceum

Solanum subcoriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
