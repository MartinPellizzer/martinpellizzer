Specularia cordata

Specularia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf spectacle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
