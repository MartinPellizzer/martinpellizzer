Scopellaria marginata

Scopellaria marginata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads surrounded by narrow, spiny bracts that resemble leaves. Scopellaria marginata is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
