Schoberia acuminata

Schoberia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct pointed tip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Schoberia acuminata is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
