Schefflera longe-petiolata

Schefflera longe-petiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically bright green and have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by black, spherical berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Schefflera longe-petiolata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
