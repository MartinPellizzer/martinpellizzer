Sorbus pogonopetala

Sorbus pogonopetala, commonly known as the red-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and can reach lengths of up to 6 centimeters.

Sorbus pogonopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
