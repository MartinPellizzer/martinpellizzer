Stenandriopsis leptostachys var. longifolia

Stenandriopsis leptostachys var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed evergreen hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period, which can last several months.
