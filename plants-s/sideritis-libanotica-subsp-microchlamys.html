Sideritis libanotica subsp. microchlamys

Sideritis libanotica subsp.

microchlamys, commonly known as the Lebanese ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
