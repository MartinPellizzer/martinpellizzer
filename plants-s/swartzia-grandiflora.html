Swartzia grandiflora

Swartzia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered swartzia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and pinnate leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Swartzia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
