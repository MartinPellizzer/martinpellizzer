Smilax takaoensis

Smilax takaoensis, commonly known as the Takao arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to the islands of Japan.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically arrowhead-shaped, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax takaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
