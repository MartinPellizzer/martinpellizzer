Susum malayanum f. aquaticum

Susum malayanum f. aquaticum, commonly known as the aquatic form of the Malayan sum.

It is a variant of the species characterized by its adaptation to aquatic environments. The plant typically has long, narrow leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots are submerged, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the water.

This form is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats.
