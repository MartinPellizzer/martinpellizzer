Senecio pauciflorus var. jucundulus

Senecio pauciflorus var. jucundulus, commonly known as the sweet senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
