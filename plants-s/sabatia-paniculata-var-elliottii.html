Sabatia paniculata var. elliottii

Sabatia paniculata var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's whorled loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant features whorled arrangements of opposite leaves, which are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
