Spiraea vacciniifolia

Spiraea vacciniifolia, commonly known as the cranberry spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Spiraea vacciniifolia is valued for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit, making it a popular choice in gardens and natural landscapes.
