Serapias lingua var. stenopetala

Serapias lingua var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy yellow flowers with a red throat. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is typically found in open habitats such as steppes, rocky slopes, and coastal areas.

The species is adapted to dry conditions and is often associated with Mediterranean climates.
