Saxifraga geranioides var. ranunculifolia

Saxifraga geranioides var. ranunculifolia, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
