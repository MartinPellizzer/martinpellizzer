Sampacca euonymoides

Sampacca euonymoides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Sampacca euonymoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces due to its vibrant floral display.
