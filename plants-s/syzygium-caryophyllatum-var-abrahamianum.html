Syzygium caryophyllatum var. abrahamianum

Syzygium caryophyllatum var. abrahamianum, commonly known as the Abraham's clove.

This variant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flower buds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.
