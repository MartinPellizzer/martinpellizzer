Stipa sibirica var. japonica

Stipa sibirica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese feather grass, is a variant of the Siberian grass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery seed heads. The plant is native to East Asia, including parts of Japan and Korea, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a tufted growth habit, making it visually striking in gardens and natural settings.
