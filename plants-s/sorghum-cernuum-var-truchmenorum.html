Sorghum cernuum var. truchmenorum

Sorghum cernuum var. truchmenorum, commonly known as the Mexican broomstick, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
