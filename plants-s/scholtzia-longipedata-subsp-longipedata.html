Scholtzia longipedata subsp. longipedata

Scholtzia longipedata subsp.

longipedata, commonly known as the long-stemmed scholtzia, is a flowering plant native to California. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
