Stipa tenacissima var. villosiuscula

Stipa tenacissima var. villosiuscula, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a variant of the hardy wheatgrass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
