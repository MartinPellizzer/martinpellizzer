Solanum saturatum

Solanum saturatum, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, contain toxic alkaloids and should not be consumed.

Solanum saturatum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
