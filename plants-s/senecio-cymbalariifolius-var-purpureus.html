Senecio cymbalariifolius var. purpureus

Senecio cymbalariifolius var. purpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking purple foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the spring.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
