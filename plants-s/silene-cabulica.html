Silene cabulica

Silene cabulica, commonly known as the Afghan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant with slender stems and small, pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Silene cabulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some horticultural settings.
