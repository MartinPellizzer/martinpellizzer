Silene julaensis

Silene julaensis, commonly known as the Jula's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene julaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
