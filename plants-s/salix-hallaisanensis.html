Salix hallaisanensis

Salix hallaisanensis, commonly known as the Hallaisan willow, is a species of willow native to South Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family, and it produces small, round fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
