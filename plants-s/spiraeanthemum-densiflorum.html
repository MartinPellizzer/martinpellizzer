Spiraeanthemum densiflorum

Spiraeanthemum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower spirea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its profuse display of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
