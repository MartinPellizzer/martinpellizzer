Salsola chinghaiensis

Salsola chinghaiensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, particularly in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a key component of desert ecosystems. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its survival in harsh environments.

Salsola chinghaiensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
