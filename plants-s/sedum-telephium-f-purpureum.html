Sedum telephium f. purpureum

Sedum telephium f. purpureum, commonly known as purple stonecrop, is a variant of the common stonecrop plant.

It is a succulent perennial that features fleshy, upright stems and compact, rosette-forming leaves. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, and they often bear a purple or reddish hue, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer.

It is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.
