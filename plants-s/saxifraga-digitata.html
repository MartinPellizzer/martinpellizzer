Saxifraga digitata

Saxifraga digitata, commonly known as the fingered saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves with a distinctive finger-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
