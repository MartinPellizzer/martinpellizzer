Solanum uporo

Solanum uporo, commonly known as the Uporos nightshade, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
