Seddera spinescens

Seddera spinescens, commonly known as the spiny seddera, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its spiny stems and leaves serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Seddera spinescens is well adapted to harsh climates and requires minimal water to thrive.
