Silene colorata subsp. pubicalycina

Silene colorata subsp.

pubicalycina, commonly known as the purple-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are typically purple or reddish in color.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
