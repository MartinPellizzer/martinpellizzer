Stemmacantha carthamoides

Stemmacantha carthamoides, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
