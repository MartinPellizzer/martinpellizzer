Spergula madoniaca

Spergula madoniaca, commonly known as Madon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its inflorescence is a spike-like structure with three stamens and a single pistil.

Spergula madoniaca is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect life and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
