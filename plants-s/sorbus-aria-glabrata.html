Sorbus aria glabrata

Sorbus aria glabrata, commonly known as the smooth whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, which are followed by bright red, spherical berries in the autumn. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
