Scepa

Scepa, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Pterocarpus and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and showy pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

The seeds of scepa are encased in a hard, woody pod, which contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
