Seridia ferox

Seridia ferox, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and broad, ovate leaves. The plant contains potent alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine, which can be lethal in high doses. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
