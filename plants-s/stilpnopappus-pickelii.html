Stilpnopappus pickelii

Stilpnopappus pickelii, commonly known as the Pickel's burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, hairy bracts that form a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.
