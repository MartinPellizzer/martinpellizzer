Salix chingiana

Salix chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers on separate plants. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Salix chingiana is adapted to moist, temperate climates and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands.
