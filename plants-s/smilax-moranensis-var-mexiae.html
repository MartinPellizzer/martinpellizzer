Smilax moranensis var. mexiae

Smilax moranensis var. mexiae, commonly known as Mexican devil's backbone, is a perennial vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Smilacaceae family.
