Simethis bicolor

Simethis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive flowers, which display two contrasting colors on each petal, typically a combination of white and purple. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Simethis bicolor is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
