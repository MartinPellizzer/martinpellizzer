Stoebe virgata

Stoebe virgata, commonly known as Pennsylvania skullcap, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped, toothed leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

Pennsylvania skullcap is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
