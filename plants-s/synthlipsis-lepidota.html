Synthlipsis lepidota

Synthlipsis lepidota, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Synthlipsis lepidota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
