Simsia foetida var. jamaicensis

Simsia foetida var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its strong scent, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
