Salvia pseudopallida

Salvia pseudopallida, commonly known as silver sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
