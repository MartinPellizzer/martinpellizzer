Silene tyrrhenia

Silene tyrrhenia, commonly known as the Tyrrhenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Silene tyrrhenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
