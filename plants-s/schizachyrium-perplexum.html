Schizachyrium perplexum

Schizachyrium perplexum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red.

Schizachyrium perplexum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
