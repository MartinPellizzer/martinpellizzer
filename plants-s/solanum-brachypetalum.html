Solanum brachypetalum

Solanum brachypetalum, commonly known as the small-petaled nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant has a somewhat shrub-like growth habit.
