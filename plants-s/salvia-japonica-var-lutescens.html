Salvia japonica var. lutescens

Salvia japonica var. lutescens, commonly known as the Japanese sage or golden sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
