Strelitzia reginae

Strelitzia reginae, commonly known as the Bird of Paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Strelitzia in the family Strelitziaceae. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form a rosette shape, and its dramatic, bird-like inflorescences that consist of brightly colored, spurred petals. Its flowers are typically orange with a blue-violet spathe, and it produces blue berries that are edible for some animals.

Strelitzia reginae thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
