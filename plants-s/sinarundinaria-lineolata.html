Sinarundinaria lineolata

Sinarundinaria lineolata, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cliffs and rock crevices. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Sinarundinaria lineolata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
