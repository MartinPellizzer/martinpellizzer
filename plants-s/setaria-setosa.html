Setaria setosa

Setaria setosa, commonly known as curly bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and distinctive, curly, bristly seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and is a valuable forage plant for wildlife.

Setaria setosa is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
