Selago mundii

Selago mundii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy, red berries. Selago mundii is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular plant in horticulture, particularly in Mediterranean climates.
