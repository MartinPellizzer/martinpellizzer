Symphyotrichum elliottii

Symphyotrichum elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pinkish-blue composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Elliott's coneflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
