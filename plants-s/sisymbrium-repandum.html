Sisymbrium repandum

Sisymbrium repandum, commonly known as field pennycress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides and in fields. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Field pennycress is also known for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
