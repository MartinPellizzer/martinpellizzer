Stickmannia foliosa

Stickmannia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy stickman plant, is a unique member of the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and distinctive, branching structure.

It originates from the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Stickmannia foliosa is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
