Salvia ochroleuca

Salvia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Salvia ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
