Senecio coronatus var. minor

Senecio coronatus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact rosette of blue-gray leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
