Stipa atriseta

Stipa atriseta, commonly known as black needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, dark-colored culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Stipa atriseta is often found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
