Sida compacta

Sida compacta, commonly known as littleleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Sida compacta is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
