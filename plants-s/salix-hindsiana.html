Salix hindsiana

Salix hindsiana, commonly known as Hinds' willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, riparian habitats, often near streams or in wetlands. It has slender, often pendulous branches and produces catkins, which are its characteristic flowering structures.

The leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
