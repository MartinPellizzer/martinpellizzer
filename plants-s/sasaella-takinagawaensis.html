Sasaella takinagawaensis

Sasaella takinagawaensis, commonly known as the Takinagawa sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides.

Sasaella takinagawaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
