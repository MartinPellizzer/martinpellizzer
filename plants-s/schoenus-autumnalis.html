Schoenus autumnalis

Schoenus autumnalis, commonly known as autumn bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Schoenus autumnalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
