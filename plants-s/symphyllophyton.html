Symphyllophyton

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented body and the presence of biramous appendages, which are specialized for both movement and feeding. Unlike typical plants, symphyllophyton is not a flowering plant but rather an arthropod, often mistaken for a plant due to its habitat in moist, soil-rich environments. It plays a role in soil aeration and nutrient cycling, contributing to the health of the ecosystem.

However, it is not classified as a botanical plant in the traditional sense, as it lacks true roots, stems, and leaves.
