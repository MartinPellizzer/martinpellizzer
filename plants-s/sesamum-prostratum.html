Sesamum prostratum

Sesamum prostratum, commonly known as prostrate sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, oblong seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sesamum prostratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical use in traditional medicine.
