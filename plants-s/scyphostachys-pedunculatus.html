Scyphostachys pedunculatus

Scyphostachys pedunculatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is also noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
