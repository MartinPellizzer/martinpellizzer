Senecio brasiliensis var. incanus

Senecio brasiliensis var. incanus, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silver-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
