Sacciolepis striata f. gibba

Sacciolepis striata f. gibba, commonly known as the knobbed sea holly, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces striking, spiky flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
