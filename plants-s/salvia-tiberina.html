Salvia tiberina

Salvia tiberina, commonly known as the Tiber sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Salvia tiberina is often found growing in rocky or dry soils along riverbanks and coastal areas.
