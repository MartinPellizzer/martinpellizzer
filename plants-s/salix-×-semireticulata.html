Salix × semireticulata

Salix × semireticulata, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern regions of China.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The tree produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
