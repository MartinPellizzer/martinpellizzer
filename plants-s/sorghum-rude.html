Sorghum rude

Sorghum rude, commonly known as johnsongrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa and widely naturalized in the Americas.

It has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often considered a weed in agricultural fields due to its aggressive spreading habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
