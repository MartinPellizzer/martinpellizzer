Saccharum officinarum var. rubicundum

Saccharum officinarum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the red cane or ruby cane, is a variant of sugarcane characterized by its distinctive reddish pigmentation in its leaves and stems.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its unique coloration, which is due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments. While it is not as widely used for sugar production as other sugarcane varieties, it is valued in ornamental horticulture and for its aesthetic appeal. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and botanical research.
