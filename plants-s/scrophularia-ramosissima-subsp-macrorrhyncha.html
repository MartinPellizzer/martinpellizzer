Scrophularia ramosissima subsp. macrorrhyncha

Scrophularia ramosissima subsp.

macrorrhyncha, commonly known as the large-flowered figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
