Stipa klimesii

Stipa klimesii, commonly known as the Klimes' needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, producing fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Stipa klimesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid environments.
