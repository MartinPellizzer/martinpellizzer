Scirpus aphyllus

Scirpus aphyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus aphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
