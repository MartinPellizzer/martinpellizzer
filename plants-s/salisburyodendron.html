Salisburyodendron

Salisburyodendron, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a coniferous tree belonging to the Cupressaceae family.

It is native to the western coast of North America, particularly in California. The tree is renowned for its immense size, with some specimens reaching heights of over 300 feet and diameters exceeding 15 feet. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing natural protection against fire and pests.

The cones of the Salisburyodendron produce seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to regenerate in forest ecosystems.
