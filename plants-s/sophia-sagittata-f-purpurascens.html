Sophia sagittata f. purpurascens

Sophia sagittata f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered arrowhead, is a striking ornamental plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and vibrant purple to deep red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds. Its foliage and flowers add a dramatic visual impact, especially when grown in clusters.

This cultivar is valued for its unique coloration and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
