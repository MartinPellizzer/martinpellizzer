Scabiosa alpestris

Scabiosa alpestris, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Scabiosa alpestris is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
