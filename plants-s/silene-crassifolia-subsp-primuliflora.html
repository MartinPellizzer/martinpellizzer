Silene crassifolia subsp. primuliflora

Silene crassifolia subsp.

primuliflora, commonly known as the primrose campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and adaptations to wet environments.
