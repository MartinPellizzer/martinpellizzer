Scleranthus perennis subsp. marginatus

Scleranthus perennis subsp.

marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf margins and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
