Salix daghestanica

Salix daghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian willow, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Salix daghestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
