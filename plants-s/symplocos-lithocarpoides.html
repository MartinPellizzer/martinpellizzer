Symplocos lithocarpoides

Symplocos lithocarpoides, commonly known as the rock-fruit symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has traditional uses in local medicine.
