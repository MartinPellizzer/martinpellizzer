Sidalcea spicata var. ranunculacea

Sidalcea spicata var. ranunculacea, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, spicate inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
