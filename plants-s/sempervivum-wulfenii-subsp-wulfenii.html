Sempervivum wulfenii subsp. wulfenii

Sempervivum wulfenii subsp.

wulfenii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, often growing at high altitudes.
