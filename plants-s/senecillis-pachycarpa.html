Senecillis pachycarpa

Senecillis pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces thick, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
