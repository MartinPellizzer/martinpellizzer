Sanguisorba stipulata var. riishirensis

Sanguisorba stipulata var. riishirensis, commonly known as the Japanese dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces tall, unbranched stems bearing clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
