Stipa puelches

Stipa puelches, commonly known as the Puelche grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. Stipa puelches is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
