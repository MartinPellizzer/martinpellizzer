Stanhopea rubromaculata

Stanhopea rubromaculata, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals with distinctive red markings, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stanhopea rubromaculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
