Satureja menthifolia var. baetica

Satureja menthifolia var. baetica, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

It is valued for its pungent fragrance and has been used traditionally in cooking and herbal medicine.
