Stylidium ramosissimum

Stylidium ramosissimum, commonly known as the sprouting sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique structure, with the flowers arising from specialized stems called caudices. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Stylidium ramosissimum is also notable for its ability to produce new shoots from the base, a feature that aids in its propagation and survival.
