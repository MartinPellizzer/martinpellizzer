Sphaeralcea ambigua var. keckii

Sphaeralcea ambigua var. keckii, commonly known as Keck's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and profuse display of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
