Senegalia pervillei var. pervillei

Senegalia pervillei var. pervillei, commonly known as the velvet mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
