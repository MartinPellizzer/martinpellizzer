Solanum campylacanthum subsp. panduriforme

Solanum campylacanthum subsp.

panduriforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
