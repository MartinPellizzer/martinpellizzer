Symphyotrichum lanceolatum subsp. lanceolatum

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
