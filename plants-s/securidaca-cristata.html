Securidaca cristata

Securidaca cristata, commonly known as the crowned senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
