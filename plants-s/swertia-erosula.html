Swertia erosula

Swertia erosula, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Swertia erosula is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
