Senecio praecox var. tzimolensis

Senecio praecox var. tzimolensis, commonly known as the Tzimolensis Groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
