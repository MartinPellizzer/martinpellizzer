Syringa reticulata f. longifolia

Syringa reticulata f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered weeping lilac, is a cultivar of the weeping lilac that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has a graceful, drooping growth habit, with slender branches that create a weeping appearance. Its leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its fragrant blooms and visual elegance.
