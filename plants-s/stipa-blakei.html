Stipa blakei

Stipa blakei, commonly known as Blake's needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a pale blue or purple hue.

Stipa blakei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
