Saussurea controversa var. firma

Saussurea controversa var. firma, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow or orange composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan and Chinese medicine. Its fibrous roots are often harvested for use in herbal remedies.

The species is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
