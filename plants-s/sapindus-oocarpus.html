Sapindus oocarpus

Sapindus oocarpus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a spherical, spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its bark and leaves contain saponins, which have cleansing properties.
