Scutellaria lateriflora f. rhodantha

Scutellaria lateriflora f. rhodantha, commonly known as the pink skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or violet hue at the base. Its inflorescence is typically a raceme, with flowers opening sequentially from the bottom up.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic, minty scent when crushed.
