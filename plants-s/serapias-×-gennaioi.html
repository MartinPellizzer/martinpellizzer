Serapias × gennaioi

Serapias × gennaioi, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias vernalis and Serapias microchila.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and linear leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
