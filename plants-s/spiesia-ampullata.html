Spiesia ampullata

Spiesia ampullata, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or orange flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Spiesia ampullata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
