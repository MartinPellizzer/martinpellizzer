Symphytum nodosum

Symphytum nodosum, commonly known as the common comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and tall spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that store nutrients, making it highly resilient in various soil conditions. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
