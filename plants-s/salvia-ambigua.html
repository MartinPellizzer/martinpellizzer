Salvia ambigua

Salvia ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Salvia ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
