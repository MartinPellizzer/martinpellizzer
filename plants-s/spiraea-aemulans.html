Spiraea aemulans

Spiraea aemulans, commonly known as the common spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Spiraea aemulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
