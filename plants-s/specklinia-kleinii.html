Specklinia kleinii

Specklinia kleinii, commonly known as the dwarf staghorn fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, tree-like growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a rosette of fiddlehead-like fronds. The plant's leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive, rugged texture that resembles the skin of a stag. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks.

Specklinia kleinii is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical setting.
