Smilax glaucochina

Smilax glaucochina, commonly known as the blue-stemmed arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, bluish-green upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
