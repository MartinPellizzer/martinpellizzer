Salix × velchevii

Salix × velchevii, commonly known as the Velchevii willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically exhibits narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and its bark is often grayish-brown with a textured surface. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It is native to parts of Eastern Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies.

This willow species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
