Stachys polysegia

Stachys polysegia, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Stachys polysegia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
