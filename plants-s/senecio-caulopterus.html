Senecio caulopterus

Senecio caulopterus, commonly known as the winged senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, elongated leaves with a distinctive wing-like appearance, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio caulopterus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
