Solanum endopogon subsp. guianense

Solanum endopogon subsp.

guianense, commonly known as the Guianese nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon basin.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to red when mature.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it contains alkaloids, making it potentially toxic.
