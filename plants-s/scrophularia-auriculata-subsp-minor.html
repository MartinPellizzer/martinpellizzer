Scrophularia auriculata subsp. minor

Scrophularia auriculata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser auriculate speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the spring. Its flowers are usually clustered in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
