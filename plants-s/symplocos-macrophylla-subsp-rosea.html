Symplocos macrophylla subsp. rosea

Symplocos macrophylla subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered evergreen holly, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often tinged with pink, giving it its common name. These flowers are followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
