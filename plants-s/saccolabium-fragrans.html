Saccolabium fragrans

Saccolabium fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
