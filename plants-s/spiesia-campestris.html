Spiesia campestris

Spiesia campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Spiesia campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
