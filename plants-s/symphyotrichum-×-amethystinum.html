Symphyotrichum × amethystinum

Symphyotrichum × amethystinum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and sturdy, branching stems. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
