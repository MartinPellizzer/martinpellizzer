Sulcorebutia mentosa

Sulcorebutia mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa Paddle Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, leafless cactus with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its stems are ribbed and can be green or reddish, depending on light exposure and environmental conditions.

Sulcorebutia mentosa is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
