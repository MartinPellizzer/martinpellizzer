Salix × boydii

Salix × boydii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid between Salix exigua and Salix interior, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

Salix × boydii is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
