Silene thessalonica subsp. dictaea

Silene thessalonica subsp.

dictaea, commonly known as the Thessaloniki catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the Caryophyllaceae family. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
