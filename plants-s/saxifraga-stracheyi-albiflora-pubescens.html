Saxifraga stracheyi albiflora-pubescens

Saxifraga stracheyi albiflora-pubescens, commonly known as the white-flowered pubescent saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and possesses hairy, often silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
