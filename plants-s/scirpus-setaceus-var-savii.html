Scirpus setaceus var. savii

Scirpus setaceus var. savii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, lakes, and along riverbanks. The plant has tall, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
