Salvia sclaraea

Salvia sclaraea, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces showy, pale blue or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.

Salvia sclaraea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
