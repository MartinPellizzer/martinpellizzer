Schizolepis myricocarpa

Schizolepis myricocarpa, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, berry-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and saline environments.

Schizolepis myricocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
