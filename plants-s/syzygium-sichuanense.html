Syzygium sichuanense

Syzygium sichuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium sichuanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its growth is typically found in humid, well-drained soils in subtropical climates.
