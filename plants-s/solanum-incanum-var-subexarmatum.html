Solanum incanum var. subexarmatum

Solanum incanum var. subexarmatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.
