Somphoxylon klugii

Somphoxylon klugii, commonly known as the Klug's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its growth habit is low and sprawling, with stems that can become woody over time.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
