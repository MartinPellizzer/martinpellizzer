Sorbus kiukiangensis

Sorbus kiukiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
