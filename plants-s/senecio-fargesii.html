Senecio fargesii

Senecio fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Senecio fargesii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
