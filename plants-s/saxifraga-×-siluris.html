Saxifraga × siluris

Saxifraga × siluris, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga × siluris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
