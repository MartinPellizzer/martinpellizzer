Sambucus racemosa f. stenophylla

Sambucus racemosa f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elderberry, is a flowering plant species native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Sambucus racemosa f. stenophylla is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
