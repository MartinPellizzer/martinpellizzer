Statice equisetina

Statice equisetina, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

Statice equisetina is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
