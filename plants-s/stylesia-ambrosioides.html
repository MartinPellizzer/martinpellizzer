Stylesia ambrosioides

Stylesia ambrosioides, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
