Salsola pachyphylla

Salsola pachyphylla, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its extensive root system allows it to thrive in sandy soils and withstand extreme temperatures.

Salsola pachyphylla plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
