Sophora tomentosa subsp. occidentalis

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western hairy sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
