Sasa takizawana var. kumagaiana

Sasa takizawana var. kumagaiana, commonly known as the Kumagawa bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa takizawana species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and culms. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in gardens and natural landscapes.
