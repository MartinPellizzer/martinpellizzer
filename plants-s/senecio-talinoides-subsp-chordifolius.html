Senecio talinoides subsp. chordifolius

Senecio talinoides subsp.

chordifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and silver-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive waxy texture, while the stems are often branched and upright.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
