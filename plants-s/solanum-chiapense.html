Solanum chiapense

Solanum chiapense, commonly known as the Chiapanec nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum chiapense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
