Strychnos sansibariensis

Strychnos sansibariensis, commonly known as the East African spurge, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruits and foliage.
