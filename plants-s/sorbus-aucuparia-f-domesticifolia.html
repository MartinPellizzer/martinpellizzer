Sorbus aucuparia f. domesticifolia

Sorbus aucuparia f. domesticifolia, commonly known as the common rowan or whitebeams, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically consumed by birds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It has small, serrated leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in spring.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
