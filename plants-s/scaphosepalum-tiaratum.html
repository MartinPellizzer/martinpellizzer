Scaphosepalum tiaratum

Scaphosepalum tiaratum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, intricate flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
