Strabonia gnaphalodes

Strabonia gnaphalodes, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

Strabonia gnaphalodes is known for its ability to thrive in dry, open environments and is often seen along roadsides and in meadows.
