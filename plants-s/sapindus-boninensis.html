Sapindus boninensis

Sapindus boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin soapnut, is a small evergreen tree native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing starchy seeds. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional practices, and the seeds can be processed to create a natural soap substitute.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
