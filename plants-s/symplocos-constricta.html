Symplocos constricta

Symplocos constricta, commonly known as narrowleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Symplocos constricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
