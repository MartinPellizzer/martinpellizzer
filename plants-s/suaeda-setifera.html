Suaeda setifera

Suaeda setifera, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in coastal and desert regions, often found in sandy or saline soils. Its ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Suaeda setifera plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
