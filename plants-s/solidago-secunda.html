Solidago secunda

Solidago secunda, commonly known as second goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Solidago secunda plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
