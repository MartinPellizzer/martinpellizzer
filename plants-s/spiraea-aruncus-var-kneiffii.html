Spiraea aruncus var. kneiffii

Spiraea aruncus var. kneiffii, commonly known as the Kneiff's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, arching branches that give it a graceful appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and turn yellow in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and structured form.
