Selaginella asplenifolia

Selaginella asplenifolia, commonly known as the maidenhair spikemoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle with a distinct sporophyte and gametophyte stage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping or erect growth habit. Its leaves are often spirally arranged and have a leathery texture, with some species displaying a strikingly ornate appearance.

Selaginella asplenifolia is noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
