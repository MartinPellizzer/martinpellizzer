Syzygium sinubanense

Syzygium sinubanense, commonly known as the Philippine white apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, white, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
