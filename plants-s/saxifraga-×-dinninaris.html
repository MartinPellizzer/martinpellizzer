Saxifraga × dinninaris

Saxifraga × dinninaris, commonly known as the Dinaric saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the Dinaric Alps.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
