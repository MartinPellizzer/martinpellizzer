Sisymbrium altissimum

Sisymbrium altissimum, commonly known as the tall honesty, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release small, white seeds, which are often used in herbal medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
