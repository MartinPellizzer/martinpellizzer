Solanum kafeateense

Solanum kafeateense, commonly known as the Kafeateense nightshade, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its berries are initially green and turn red when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
