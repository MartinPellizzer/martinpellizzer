Solanum marginatum

Solanum marginatum, commonly known as the marginated nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or yellow when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

Solanum marginatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
