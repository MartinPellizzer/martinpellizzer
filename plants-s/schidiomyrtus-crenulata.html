Schidiomyrtus crenulata

Schidiomyrtus crenulata, commonly known as the serrated myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, while its ornamental value makes it a popular garden plant.

Schidiomyrtus crenulata is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments.
