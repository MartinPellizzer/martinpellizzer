Solanum sanctae-marthae

Solanum sanctae-marthae, commonly known as the sacred marthae tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-sized fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
