Seriphidium quettense

Seriphidium quettense, commonly known as the Quettense wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Seriphidium quettense is often found in rocky soils and is a component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
