Sasa kurilensis f. albostriata

Sasa kurilensis f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a variant of the Kuril bamboo species native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

This plant is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered with a distinctive white or pale stripe running along their length. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a grass-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
