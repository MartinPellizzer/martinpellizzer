Schoenodorus poiformis

Schoenodorus poiformis, commonly known as the roundleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a cyathium, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is noted for its succulent-like leaves, which help it store water in its native habitat.
