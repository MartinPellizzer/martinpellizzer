Satureja briquetii

Satureja briquetii, commonly known as Briquet's savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pungent aroma. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Satureja briquetii is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor.
