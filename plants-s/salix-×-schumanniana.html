Salix × schumanniana

Salix × schumanniana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance. The tree has heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often planted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
