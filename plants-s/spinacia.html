Spinacia

Spinacia, commonly known as spinach, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its edible, dark green leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron, vitamins A and C, and antioxidants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with a rosette of leaves forming at the base. Spinach thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowering stems, which appear in the summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into seeds.
