Salvia candidissima

Salvia candidissima, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia candidissima is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
