Stravadium semisutum

Stravadium semisutum, commonly known as the partial-seed orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-white blooms with a subtle fragrance. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and the challenges it faces due to habitat destruction.
