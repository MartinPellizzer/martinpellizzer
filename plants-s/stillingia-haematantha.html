Stillingia haematantha

Stillingia haematantha, commonly known as the redroot stellaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
