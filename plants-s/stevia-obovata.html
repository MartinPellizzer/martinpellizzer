Stevia obovata

Stevia obovata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal and commercial uses.

Stevia obovata is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing botanical traits with other members of the daisy family.
