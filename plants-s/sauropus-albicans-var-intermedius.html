Sauropus albicans var. intermedius

Sauropus albicans var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate white sauropus, is a variant of the white sauropus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually pale green to white in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
