Sasaella aikawensis

Sasaella aikawensis, commonly known as the Aikawa sasa, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant native to Japan, specifically found in the island of Hokkaido. The plant is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
