Senecio linearifolius var. intermedius

Senecio linearifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate linearleaf senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
