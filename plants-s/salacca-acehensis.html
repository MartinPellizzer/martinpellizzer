Salacca acehensis

Salacca acehensis, commonly known as the Aceh palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that develop into edible, coconut-like fruits. Salacca acehensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its cultivation is often associated with humid, shaded environments in tropical rainforests.
