Sterculia scortechinii

Sterculia scortechinii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and broad, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
