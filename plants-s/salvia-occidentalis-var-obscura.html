Salvia occidentalis var. obscura

Salvia occidentalis var. obscura, commonly known as the western sagebrush or dark sage.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe habitats. Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
