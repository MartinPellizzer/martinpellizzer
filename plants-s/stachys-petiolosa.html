Stachys petiolosa

Stachys petiolosa, commonly known as slender wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Stachys petiolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
