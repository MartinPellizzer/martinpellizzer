Salvia armena

Salvia armena, commonly known as Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Salvia armena is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic properties.
