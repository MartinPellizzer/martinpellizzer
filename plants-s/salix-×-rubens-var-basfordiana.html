Salix × rubens var. basfordiana

Salix × rubens var. basfordiana, commonly known as the Basford weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near waterways, making it well-suited for riparian habitats.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing stream banks.
