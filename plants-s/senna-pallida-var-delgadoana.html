Senna pallida var. delgadoana

Senna pallida var. delgadoana, commonly known as the Delgado's senna, is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like structures, typical of the legume family, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
