Saccharum paniceum

Saccharum paniceum, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It is closely related to sugarcane and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sugar.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
