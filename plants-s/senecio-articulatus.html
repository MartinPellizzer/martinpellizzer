Senecio articulatus

Senecio articulatus, commonly known as the artichoke plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, segmented stems that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Senecio articulatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
