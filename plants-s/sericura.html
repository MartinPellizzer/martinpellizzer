Sericura

Sericura, commonly known as the "velvet tamarind," is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. Sericura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
