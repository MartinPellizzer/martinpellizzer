Spachia

Spachia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color. Spachia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical classification places it within the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
