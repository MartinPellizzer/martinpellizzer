Saxifraga balandinii

Saxifraga balandinii, commonly known as the Balandin's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help to insulate the plant against cold temperatures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
