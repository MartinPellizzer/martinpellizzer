Solidago sempervirens var. typica

Solidago sempervirens var. typica, commonly known as the evergreen goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and remain green throughout the year, giving it its "evergreen" designation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
