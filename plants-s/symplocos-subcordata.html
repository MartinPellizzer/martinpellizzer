Symplocos subcordata

Symplocos subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive subcordate base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries in autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
