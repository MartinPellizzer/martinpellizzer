Senna spinescens

Senna spinescens, commonly known as the spiny senna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Senna spinescens is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
