Sphaerocephalus exaltatus

Sphaerocephalus exaltatus, commonly known as the globe-headed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers bloom in late spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
