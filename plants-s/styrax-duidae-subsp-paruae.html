Styrax duidae subsp. paruae

Styrax duidae subsp.

paruae, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
