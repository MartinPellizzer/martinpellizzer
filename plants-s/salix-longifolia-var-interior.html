Salix longifolia var. interior

Salix longifolia var. interior, commonly known as the interior willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are often pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and habitat support.
