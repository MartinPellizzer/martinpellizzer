Sambucus mexicana

Sambucus mexicana, commonly known as Mexican elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Adoxaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with thorn-like projections, and it typically thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
