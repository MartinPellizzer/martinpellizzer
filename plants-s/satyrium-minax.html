Satyrium minax

Satyrium minax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually small and clustered in spikes.

Satyrium minax is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
