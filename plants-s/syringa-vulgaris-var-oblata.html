Syringa vulgaris var. oblata

Syringa vulgaris var. oblata, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale purple flowers.

It originates from the temperate regions of Asia and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 7, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
