Selaginella hayatana

Selaginella hayatana, commonly known as the "spike moss," is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It has a unique ability to survive extreme drought conditions due to its specialized spore-producing structures.

Selaginella hayatana is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
