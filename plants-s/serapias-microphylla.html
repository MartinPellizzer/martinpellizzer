Serapias microphylla

Serapias microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Serapias microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
