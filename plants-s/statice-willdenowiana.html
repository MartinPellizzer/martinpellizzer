Statice willdenowiana

Statice willdenowiana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Poaceae, though it is sometimes classified under the Amaranthaceae family.
