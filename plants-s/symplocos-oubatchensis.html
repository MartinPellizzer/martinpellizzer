Symplocos oubatchensis

Symplocos oubatchensis, commonly known as the Oubatch's waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
