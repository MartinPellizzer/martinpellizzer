Sida jamaicensis var. glomerata

Sida jamaicensis var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
