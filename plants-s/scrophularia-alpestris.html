Scrophularia alpestris

Scrophularia alpestris, commonly known as alpine figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

Scrophularia alpestris is often found in rocky soils and is a characteristic species of high-altitude vegetation.
