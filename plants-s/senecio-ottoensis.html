Senecio ottoensis

Senecio ottoensis, commonly known as Otto's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and succulent collections.
