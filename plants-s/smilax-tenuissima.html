Smilax tenuissima

Smilax tenuissima, commonly known as the slender greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax tenuissima is known for its robust growth and ability to spread through both runners and rhizomes.
