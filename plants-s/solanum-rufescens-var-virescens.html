Solanum rufescens var. virescens

Solanum rufescens var. virescens, commonly known as the green nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, hairy, or glandular stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
