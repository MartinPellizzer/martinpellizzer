Saccharum arenicola

Saccharum arenicola, commonly known as the sand sugar cane.

It is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly found in coastal and sandy soils. This plant is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species for ecological restoration in coastal areas.
