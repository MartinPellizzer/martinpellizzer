Scirpus sphacelatus

Scirpus sphacelatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along the edges of lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in aquatic environments.

Scirpus sphacelatus plays a vital role in providing habitat for various waterfowl and in filtering water through its dense root system.
