Senecio spinescens

Senecio spinescens, commonly known as the spiny groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Senecio spinescens is also noted for its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
