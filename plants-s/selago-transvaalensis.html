Selago transvaalensis

Selago transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Selago transvaalensis is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
