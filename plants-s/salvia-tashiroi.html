Salvia tashiroi

Salvia tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's sage, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
