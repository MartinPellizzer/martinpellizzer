Spartium clusii

Spartium clusii, commonly known as Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Spartium clusii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
