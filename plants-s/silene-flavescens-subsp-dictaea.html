Silene flavescens subsp. dictaea

Silene flavescens subsp.

dictaea, commonly known as the yellow campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. Silene flavescens subsp.

dictaea is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
