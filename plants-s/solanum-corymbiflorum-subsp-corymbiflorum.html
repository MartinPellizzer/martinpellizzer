Solanum corymbiflorum subsp. corymbiflorum

Solanum corymbiflorum subsp.

corymbiflorum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, sparsely foliated structure and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep blue color, and the plant is known for its nocturnal blooming habit.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
