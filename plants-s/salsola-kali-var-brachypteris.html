Salsola kali var. brachypteris

Salsola kali var. brachypteris, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction make it an invasive species in many regions.
