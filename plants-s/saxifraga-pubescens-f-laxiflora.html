Saxifraga pubescens f. laxiflora

Saxifraga pubescens f. laxiflora, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a flowering plant characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is native to alpine and rocky environments in Europe. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated, with a soft pubescent texture, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
