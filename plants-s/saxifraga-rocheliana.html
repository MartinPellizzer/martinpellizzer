Saxifraga rocheliana

Saxifraga rocheliana, commonly known as Rochel's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Saxifraga rocheliana is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
