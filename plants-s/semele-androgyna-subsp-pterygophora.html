Semele androgyna subsp. pterygophora

Semele androgyna subsp.

pterygophora, commonly known as the winged-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive winged leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.
