Solanum bicorne var. angustifolium

Solanum bicorne var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, angular leaves and distinctive two-lobed fruit. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
