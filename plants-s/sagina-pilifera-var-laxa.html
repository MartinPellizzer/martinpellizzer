Sagina pilifera var. laxa

Sagina pilifera var. laxa, commonly known as the lax sandspurry, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The flowers typically bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
