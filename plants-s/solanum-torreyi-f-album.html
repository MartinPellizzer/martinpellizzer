Solanum torreyi f. album

Solanum torreyi f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a variant of the Solanum torreyi species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or violet hues of the species. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral coloration.
