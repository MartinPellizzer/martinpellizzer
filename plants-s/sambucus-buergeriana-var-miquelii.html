Sambucus buergeriana var. miquelii

Sambucus buergeriana var. miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are typically used in traditional medicine rather than culinary applications. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
