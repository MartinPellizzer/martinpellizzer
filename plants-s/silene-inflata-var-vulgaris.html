Silene inflata var. vulgaris

Silene inflata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the inflated campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Silene inflata var. vulgaris is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
