Serapias × hutteri

Serapias × hutteri, commonly known as the hybrid marsh gentian, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias auricoma.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with yellow-orange spots. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it blooms from June to August.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
