Spiranthes trilineata

Spiranthes trilineata, commonly known as the three-line ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraled inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in a tight, twisting cluster.

Spiranthes trilineata is a perennial herb that produces showy, fragrant blooms during the late spring and early summer.
