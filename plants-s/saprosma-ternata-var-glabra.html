Saprosma ternata var. glabra

Saprosma ternata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth saprosma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
