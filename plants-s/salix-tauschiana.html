Salix tauschiana

Salix tauschiana, commonly known as the Tausch willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Salix tauschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
