Seringia hermanniifolia

Seringia hermanniifolia, commonly known as the Hermann's seringia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Seringia hermanniifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
