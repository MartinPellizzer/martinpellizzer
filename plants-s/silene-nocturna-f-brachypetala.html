Silene nocturna f. brachypetala

Silene nocturna f. brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled evening pink, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, showy pink to purple flowers that open in the evening.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its shortened petals, as indicated by the "brachypetala" epithet, give it a unique appearance compared to other members of the species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
