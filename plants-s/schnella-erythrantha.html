Schnella erythrantha

Schnella erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
