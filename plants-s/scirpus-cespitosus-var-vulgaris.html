Scirpus cespitosus var. vulgaris

Scirpus cespitosus var. vulgaris, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage, which form tussocks or clumps. The plant typically grows in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Scirpus cespitosus var. vulgaris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
