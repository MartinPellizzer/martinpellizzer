Smilax ligneoriparia

Smilax ligneoriparia, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often thorny and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Smilax ligneoriparia is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
