Scabiosa grandiflora var. canescens

Scabiosa grandiflora var. canescens, commonly known as the fuzzy scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
