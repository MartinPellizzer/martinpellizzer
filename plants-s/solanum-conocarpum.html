Solanum conocarpum

Solanum conocarpum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Solanum conocarpum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
