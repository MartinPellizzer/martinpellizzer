Silene delphinensis

Silene delphinensis, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, helping to retain moisture.

Silene delphinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
