Sclerolobium macrophyllum

Sclerolobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, grayish bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
