Sideritis romana var. approximata

Sideritis romana var. approximata, commonly known as Roman catnip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a mild sedative.
