Stelis rhamphosa

Stelis rhamphosa, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, elongated flower that resembles a bird's beak in shape. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent lip that is often darker in color.

Stelis rhamphosa is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
