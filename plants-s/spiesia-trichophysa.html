Spiesia trichophysa

Spiesia trichophysa, commonly known as the hairy leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Spiesia trichophysa is considered a perennial plant and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
