Solanum nigrum var. aurantium

Solanum nigrum var. aurantium, commonly known as the golden black nightshade, is a variant of the common black nightshade.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.
