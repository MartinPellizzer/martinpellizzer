Stipa lepida var. andersonii

Stipa lepida var. andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
