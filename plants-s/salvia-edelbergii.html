Salvia edelbergii

Salvia edelbergii, commonly known as the Cape sage, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
