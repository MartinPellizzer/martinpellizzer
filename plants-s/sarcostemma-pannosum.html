Sarcostemma pannosum

Sarcostemma pannosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain environments.
