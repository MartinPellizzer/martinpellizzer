Salvia phlomoides var. arachnoidea

Salvia phlomoides var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its unique appearance, with flower clusters resembling spider webs, gives it its common name.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
