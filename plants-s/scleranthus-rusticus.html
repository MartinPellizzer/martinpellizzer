Scleranthus rusticus

Scleranthus rusticus, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with erect, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Scleranthus rusticus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
