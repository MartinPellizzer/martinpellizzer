Setaria verticillata subsp. adhaerens

Setaria verticillata subsp.

adhaerens, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
