Strychnos bourdilloni

Strychnos bourdilloni, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

However, caution is advised due to the presence of toxic alkaloids in its tissues.
