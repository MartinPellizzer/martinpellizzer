Salmia acuminata

Salmia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved salvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salmia acuminata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
