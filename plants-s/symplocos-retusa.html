Symplocos retusa

Symplocos retusa, commonly known as the smoothleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Symplocos retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
