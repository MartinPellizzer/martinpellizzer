Sedum mesoamericanum

Sedum mesoamericanum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, thick leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months.

Sedum mesoamericanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
