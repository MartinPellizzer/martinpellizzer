Saussurea triceps

Saussurea triceps, commonly known as the three-toothed saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow to brown flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high altitudes with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
