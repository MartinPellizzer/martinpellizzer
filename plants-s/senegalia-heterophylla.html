Senegalia heterophylla

Senegalia heterophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact inflorescences, followed by flat, linear pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Senegalia heterophylla is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.
