Solanum hypopsilum

Solanum hypopsilum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

The plant is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability.
