Sonchus asper var. megalocarpus

Sonchus asper var. megalocarpus, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot and produces hairy, rough-textured stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has alternate, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
