Senecio mapuche

Senecio mapuche, commonly known as the Mapuche groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Senecio mapuche is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
