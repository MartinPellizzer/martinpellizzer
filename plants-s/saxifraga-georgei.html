Saxifraga georgei

Saxifraga georgei, commonly known as George's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
