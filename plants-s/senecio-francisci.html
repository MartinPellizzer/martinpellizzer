Senecio francisci

Senecio francisci, commonly known as the Franciscan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a grayish or blue-green hue.

Senecio francisci produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
