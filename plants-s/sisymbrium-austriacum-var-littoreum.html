Sisymbrium austriacum var. littoreum

Sisymbrium austriacum var. littoreum, commonly known as coastal mustard, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, exhibiting adaptations to salt spray and wind. Its leaves are usually lobed or pinnately divided, and it produces a distinctive, pungent odor.

This variety is often found in maritime environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
