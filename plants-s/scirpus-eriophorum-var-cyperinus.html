Scirpus eriophorum var. cyperinus

Scirpus eriophorum var. cyperinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
