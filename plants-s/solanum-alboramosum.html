Solanum alboramosum

Solanum alboramosum, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, often found in dry, open habitats. Its fruits are small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids, making them potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its chemical properties.
