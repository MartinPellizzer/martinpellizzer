Senecio bidentatus

Senecio bidentatus, commonly known as the two-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which bloom in spring and summer. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
