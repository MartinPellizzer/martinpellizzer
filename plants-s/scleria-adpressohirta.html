Scleria adpressohirta

Scleria adpressohirta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, while its flowering structures resemble small umbrellas.

Scleria adpressohirta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
