Sasa kogensis

Sasa kogensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Sasa kogensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
