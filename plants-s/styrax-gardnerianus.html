Styrax gardnerianus

Styrax gardnerianus, commonly known as the Gardner's frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Styrax gardnerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
