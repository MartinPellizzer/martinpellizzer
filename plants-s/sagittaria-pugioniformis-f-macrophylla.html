Sagittaria pugioniformis f. macrophylla

Sagittaria pugioniformis f. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to wetland environments and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to aquatic gardens. Its leaves are typically broad and arrowhead-shaped, providing a distinctive visual appeal.

This cultivar is often used in water gardens and pond landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
