Strobilanthes trichophora

Strobilanthes trichophora, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, which are typically borne in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
