Suaeda jacoensis

Suaeda jacoensis, commonly known as the coastal arrowweed, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically greenish-white, appearing in clusters. Suaeda jacoensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

It is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
