Salacia cerasifera var. cerasifera

Salacia cerasifera var. cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
