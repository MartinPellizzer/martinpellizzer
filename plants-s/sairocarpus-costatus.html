Sairocarpus costatus

Sairocarpus costatus, commonly known as the crested spurge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often spiny stems, which are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a wavy or toothed margin.

Sairocarpus costatus thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.
