Sorbus suecica var. mougeotii

Sorbus suecica var. mougeotii, commonly known as the Swedish whitebeam, is a variant of the European whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe, particularly Scandinavia. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This species is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
