Sarcochilus longicalcareus

Sarcochilus longicalcareus, commonly known as the long-leaf sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It features a robust, hairy rhizome and produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often persisting for several years.

The orchid is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake.
