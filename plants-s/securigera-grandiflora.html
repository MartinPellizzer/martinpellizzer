Securigera grandiflora

Securigera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has been noted, though its toxic properties require caution in handling.
