Senecio saussureoides

Senecio saussureoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. It produces small, blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio saussureoides is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
