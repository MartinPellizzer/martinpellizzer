Silene wolgensis

Silene wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene wolgensis has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is a herbaceous perennial, often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.
