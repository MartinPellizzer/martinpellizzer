Sasa nipponica var. glaucissima

Sasa nipponica var. glaucissima, commonly known as the Japanese silver bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique visual characteristics.
