Saxifraga mutabilis var. breviloba

Saxifraga mutabilis var. breviloba, commonly known as the variable saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture, and can vary in color depending on the light conditions.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and ornamental value in alpine gardens and rockeries.
