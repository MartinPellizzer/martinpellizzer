Syzygium pyneei

Syzygium pyneei, commonly known as the Pyne's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds, though they are less commonly consumed. Syzygium pyneei is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
