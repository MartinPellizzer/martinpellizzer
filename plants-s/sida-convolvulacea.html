Sida convolvulacea

Sida convolvulacea, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is an annual herb that grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

Sida convolvulacea is known for its rapid growth and is often considered a weed in cultivated areas.
