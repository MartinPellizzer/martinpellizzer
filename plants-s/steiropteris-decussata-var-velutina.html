Steiropteris decussata var. velutina

Steiropteris decussata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of this fern make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
