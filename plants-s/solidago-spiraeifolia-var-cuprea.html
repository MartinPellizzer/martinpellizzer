Solidago spiraeifolia var. cuprea

Solidago spiraeifolia var. cuprea, commonly known as copperleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of bright yellow to orange-gold flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Copperleaf goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators in native gardens.
