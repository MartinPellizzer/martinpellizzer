Solanum velleum var. tovarii

Solanum velleum var. tovarii, commonly known as Tovar's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

This species is often found in shaded, humid areas and is noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
