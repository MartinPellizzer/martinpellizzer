Sunania filiformis f. variegata

Sunania filiformis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated threadleaf bellflower, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and botanists.
