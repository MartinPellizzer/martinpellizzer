Sideritis × viciosoi var. stricta

Sideritis × viciosoi var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
