Sideritis incana subsp. sericea

Sideritis incana subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silver hairweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Sideritis incana subsp.

sericea is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
