Salmea oligocephala

Salmea oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters.

Salmea oligocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
