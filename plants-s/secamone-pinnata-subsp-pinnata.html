Secamone pinnata subsp. pinnata

Secamone pinnata subsp.

pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate secamone, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate venation.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
