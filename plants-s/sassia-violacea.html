Sassia violacea

Sassia violacea, commonly known as violet saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Sassia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
