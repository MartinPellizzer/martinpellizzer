Sonchus filifolius

Sonchus filifolius, commonly known as slenderleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a bitter taste, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
