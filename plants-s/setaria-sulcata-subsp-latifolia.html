Setaria sulcata subsp. latifolia

Setaria sulcata subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
