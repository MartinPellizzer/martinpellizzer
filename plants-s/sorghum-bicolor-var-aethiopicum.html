Sorghum bicolor var. aethiopicum

Sorghum bicolor var. aethiopicum, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a variant of sorghum native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other sorghum varieties. This plant is often cultivated for its edible grains and as a forage crop for livestock. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in challenging climates.
