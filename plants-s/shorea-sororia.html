Shorea sororia

Shorea sororia, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy timber with a golden-yellow color. The plant has large, elliptical leaves that are typically alternate and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spikes, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Shorea sororia is valued for its ecological role and is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.
