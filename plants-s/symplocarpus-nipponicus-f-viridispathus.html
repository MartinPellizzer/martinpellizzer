Symplocarpus nipponicus f. viridispathus

Symplocarpus nipponicus f. viridispathus, commonly known as the green-veined Japanese arrowhead, is a rare variant of the arrowhead plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive green veins on the leaves, which set it apart from the typical form of the species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water, adapting to its wetland habitat. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique foliage and limited distribution.
