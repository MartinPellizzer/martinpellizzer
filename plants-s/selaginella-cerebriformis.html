Selaginella cerebriformis

Selaginella cerebriformis, commonly known as the brain moss or crested spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a distinctive, creeping stem with whorls of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a unique, brain-like appearance. Selaginella cerebriformis reproduces via spores and is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions.

Its intricate morphology and adaptability make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
