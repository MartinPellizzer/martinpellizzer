Saxifraga taygetea

Saxifraga taygetea, commonly known as the Greek saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and Greece.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette-like arrangement, and are covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Saxifraga taygetea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
