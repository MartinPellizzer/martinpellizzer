Shorea nebulosa

Shorea nebulosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody fruits that contain smooth, elliptical seeds.

Shorea nebulosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
