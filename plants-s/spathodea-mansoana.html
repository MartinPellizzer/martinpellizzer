Spathodea mansoana

Spathodea mansoana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
