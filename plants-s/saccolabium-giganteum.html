Saccolabium giganteum

Saccolabium giganteum, commonly known as the giant yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
