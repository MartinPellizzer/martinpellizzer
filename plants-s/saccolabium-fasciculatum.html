Saccolabium fasciculatum

Saccolabium fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered false peacock.

It is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America. The plant features woody stems with dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Saccolabium fasciculatum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
