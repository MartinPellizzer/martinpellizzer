Scutellaria karatavica

Scutellaria karatavica, commonly known as Karatavsky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Scutellaria karatavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
