Saccolabium jerdonianum

Saccolabium jerdonianum, commonly known as the Jerdon's milkbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody shrub growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are typically pinnate with small, rounded leaflets. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots and a capacity to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of forage for wildlife.
