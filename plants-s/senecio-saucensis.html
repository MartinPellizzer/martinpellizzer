Senecio saucensis

Senecio saucensis, commonly known as the saucy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in late spring.

Senecio saucensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
