Senecio arachnolomus

Senecio arachnolomus, commonly known as the spiderweb senecio, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiderweb-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, tangled pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens for its ornamental value.
