Sauvetrea xantholeuca

Sauvetrea xantholeuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaved sauvetrea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique coloration and seasonal blooms.
