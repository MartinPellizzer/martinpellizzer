Sarcochilus mannii

Sarcochilus mannii, commonly known as the Manuhi or the Mountain Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Sarcochilus mannii is an important species in New Zealand's native flora and is protected due to its limited distribution.
