Shorea flaviflora

Shorea flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered or yellow-wood tree, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 50 meters tall, characterized by its heavy, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

It thrives in tropical rainforests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
