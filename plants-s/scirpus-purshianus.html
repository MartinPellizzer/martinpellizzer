Scirpus purshianus

Scirpus purshianus, commonly known as the Pursh's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil.

Scirpus purshianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
