Seddera mucronata

Seddera mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved seddera, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

Seddera mucronata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
