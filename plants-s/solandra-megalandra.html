Solandra megalandra

Solandra megalandra, commonly known as the giant solandra, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, white to yellow flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring thick, waxy leaves and robust, woody stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solandra megalandra is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
