Sebaea sedoides var. sedoides

Sebaea sedoides var. sedoides, commonly known as the Florida sebaea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to wetland environments.
