Sclerostemma caucasicum

Sclerostemma caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian rock vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Sclerostemma caucasicum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.
