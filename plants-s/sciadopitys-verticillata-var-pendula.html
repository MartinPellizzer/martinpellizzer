Sciadopitys verticillata var. pendula

Sciadopitys verticillata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese umbrella pine, is a unique coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, and it produces small, oval cones. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its unique form.
