Senecio ishcaivilcanus

Senecio ishcaivilcanus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio ishcaivilcanus is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to its alpine habitat.
