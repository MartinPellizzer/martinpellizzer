Syzygium brachycalyx

Syzygium brachycalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Syzygium brachycalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
