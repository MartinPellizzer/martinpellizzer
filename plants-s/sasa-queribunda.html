Sasa queribunda

Sasa queribunda, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact shrub-like structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa queribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
