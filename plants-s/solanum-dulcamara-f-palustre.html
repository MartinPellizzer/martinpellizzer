Solanum dulcamara f. palustre

Solanum dulcamara f. palustre, commonly known as the marsh bindweed, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing or climbing habit and hairy, elliptical leaves. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.
