Sitanion × rubescens

Sitanion × rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Sitanion × rubescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
