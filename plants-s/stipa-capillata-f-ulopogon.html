Stipa capillata f. ulopogon

Stipa capillata f. ulopogon, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender stems.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden spikelets. Its foliage is typically blue-green in color, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
