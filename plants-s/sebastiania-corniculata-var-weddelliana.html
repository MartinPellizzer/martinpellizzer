Sebastiania corniculata var. weddelliana

Sebastiania corniculata var. weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
