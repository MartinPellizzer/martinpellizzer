Salvia omerocalyx var. prostrata

Salvia omerocalyx var. prostrata, commonly known as prostrate blue sage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of bright blue to violet flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
