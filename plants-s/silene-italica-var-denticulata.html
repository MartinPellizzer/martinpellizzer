Silene italica var. denticulata

Silene italica var. denticulata, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins, which contribute to its scientific name.
