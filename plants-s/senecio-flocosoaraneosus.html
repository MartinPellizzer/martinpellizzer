Senecio flocosoaraneosus

Senecio flocosoaraneosus, commonly known as the woolly spider plant, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its soft, fluffy, grayish-white foliage that resembles a cloud. The plant produces small, yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid native habitat.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
