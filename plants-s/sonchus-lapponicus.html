Sonchus lapponicus

Sonchus lapponicus, commonly known as Arctic sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
