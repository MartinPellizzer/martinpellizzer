Senecio proteus var. geissei

Senecio proteus var. geissei, commonly known as the Cape rosebay, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
