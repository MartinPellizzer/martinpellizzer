Sedum drymarioides var. toyamae

Sedum drymarioides var. toyamae, commonly known as the Toyama stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its hardy nature allows it to tolerate drought and mild frost, enhancing its adaptability in various climates.
