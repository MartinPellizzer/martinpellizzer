Saxifraga geranioides var. palmatifida

Saxifraga geranioides var. palmatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are palmately divided, giving the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
