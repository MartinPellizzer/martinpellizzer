Sasa hainanensis

Sasa hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China's Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural strength.

Sasa hainanensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ecological role and practical applications.
