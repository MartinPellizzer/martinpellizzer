Sanguisorba verrucosa proles magnolii

Sanguisorba verrucosa proles magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
