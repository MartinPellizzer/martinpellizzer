Scirpus crinalis

Scirpus crinalis, commonly known as the purple bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers at the top of its stems, which bloom in late summer. Scirpus crinalis thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
