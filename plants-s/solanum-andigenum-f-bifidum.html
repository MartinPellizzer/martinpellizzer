Solanum andigenum f. bifidum

Solanum andigenum f. bifidum, commonly known as the Andean twin-lobed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
