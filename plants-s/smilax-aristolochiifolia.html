Smilax aristolochiifolia

Smilax aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. The plant is part of the Smilacaceae family and is known for its ability to climb using tendrils. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Smilax aristolochiifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
