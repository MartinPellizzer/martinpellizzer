Senna aristeguietae

Senna aristeguietae, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, wrinkled seeds.

Senna aristeguietae is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
