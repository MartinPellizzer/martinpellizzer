Syzygium purpuricarpum

Syzygium purpuricarpum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering. The fruit is typically orange to red in color when ripe, with a sweet, aromatic pulp that contains a single large seed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
