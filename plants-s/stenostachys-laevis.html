Stenostachys laevis

Stenostachys laevis, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. It is characterized by its smooth, hairless surface, which gives it its scientific name.

Stenostachys laevis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
