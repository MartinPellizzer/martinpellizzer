Swartzia mexicana

Swartzia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Swartzia mexicana is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and musical instruments, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
