Solanum glandulosum var. stellatopubescens

Solanum glandulosum var. stellatopubescens, commonly known as the star-haired nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

The fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making the plant potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
