Setaria alpestris

Setaria alpestris, commonly known as alpine millet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

Setaria alpestris is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
