Satureja khuzistanica

Satureja khuzistanica, commonly known as Khuzistan savory, is a perennial herb native to the Zagros Mountains in Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, and it is often found in rocky or limestone soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent, savory aroma.

Satureja khuzistanica is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
