Senna Cladophylla

Senna cladophylla is a rare and striking member of the Fabaceae family native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its unique, clustered leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. This plant thrives in harsh environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to drought and poor soil conditions. Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife. Despite its beauty and resilience, Senna cladophylla remains a lesser-known species deserving of greater recognition and conservation efforts.
