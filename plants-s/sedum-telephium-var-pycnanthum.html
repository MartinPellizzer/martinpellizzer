Sedum telephium var. pycnanthum

Sedum telephium var. pycnanthum, commonly known as crimson sedum, is a perennial succulent native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically red or purple in color, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
