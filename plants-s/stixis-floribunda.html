Stixis floribunda

Stixis floribunda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Stixis floribunda thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is glossy and evergreen, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
