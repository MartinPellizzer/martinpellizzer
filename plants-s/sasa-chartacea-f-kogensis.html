Sasa chartacea f. kogensis

Sasa chartacea f. kogensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or kogen bamboo, is a variant of the common sasa bamboo.

It is a clump-forming evergreen perennial that produces slender, wiry culms with a smooth, pale green to yellowish-brown surface. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate, leathery, and have a distinctive pale midrib.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its compact growth and aesthetic appeal.
