Silene foliosa var. macrostyla

Silene foliosa var. macrostyla, commonly known as the broad-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Silene foliosa var. macrostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
