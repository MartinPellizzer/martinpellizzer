Solanum sodiroi subsp. ramosipilum

Solanum sodiroi subsp.

ramosipilum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco.

The plant is characterized by its branched stems, simple leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, often containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
