Simocheilus klotzschianus

Simocheilus klotzschianus, commonly known as the Klotzsch's water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater, with a smooth, waxy texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

The species is often found in still or gently flowing water, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and contributing to water quality.
