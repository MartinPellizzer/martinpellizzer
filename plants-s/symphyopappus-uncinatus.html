Symphyopappus uncinatus

Symphyopappus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant features a woody stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The fruit is a small, hooked pappus that aids in seed dispersal by the wind.
