Sideritis chlorostegia

Sideritis chlorostegia, commonly known as the green-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece and Turkey, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Sideritis chlorostegia is valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
