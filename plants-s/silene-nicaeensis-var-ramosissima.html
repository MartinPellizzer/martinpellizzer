Silene nicaeensis var. ramosissima

Silene nicaeensis var. ramosissima, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems are often branched and covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
