Spiraea arguta

Spiraea arguta, commonly known as the dwarf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring arching branches and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky slopes.

Spiraea arguta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
