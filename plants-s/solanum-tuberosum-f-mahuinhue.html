Solanum tuberosum f. mahuinhue

Solanum tuberosum f. mahuinhue, commonly known as the Mahuinhue potato, is a botanical variety of the potato plant characterized by its unique tuber morphology and coloration.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, where it has been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, typical of the Solanaceae family. Its tubers are often smaller and have a distinctive purple or violet hue, which contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary potential but also for its genetic diversity in potato breeding programs.
