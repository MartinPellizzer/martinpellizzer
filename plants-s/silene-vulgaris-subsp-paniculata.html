Silene vulgaris subsp. paniculata

Silene vulgaris subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.
