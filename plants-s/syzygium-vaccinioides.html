Syzygium vaccinioides

Syzygium vaccinioides, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Syzygium vaccinioides thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and agricultural studies.
