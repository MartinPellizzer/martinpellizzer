Solanum alphonsi

Solanum alphonsi, commonly known as the Java eggplant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow with a white center, and the fruit turns from green to yellow or red when mature.
