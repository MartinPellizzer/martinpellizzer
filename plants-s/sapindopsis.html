Sapindopsis

Sapindopsis, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Sapindaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The genus includes several species, with Sapindopsis chinensis being the most well-known.
