Syzygium quadricostatum

Syzygium quadricostatum, commonly known as the four-costate tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in various regions.
