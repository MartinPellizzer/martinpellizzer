Silene gallica var. chermezonis

Silene gallica var. chermezonis, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a perennial species characterized by its pink to red flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
