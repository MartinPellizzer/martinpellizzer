Staphylea brachiata

Staphylea brachiata, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Staphylea brachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
