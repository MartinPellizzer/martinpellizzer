Sinocarum pulchellum

Sinocarum pulchellum, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sinocarum pulchellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
