Solanum caldense

Solanum caldense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum caldense is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.
