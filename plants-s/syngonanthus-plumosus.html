Syngonanthus plumosus

Syngonanthus plumosus, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, fluffy inflorescences, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Syngonanthus plumosus is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
