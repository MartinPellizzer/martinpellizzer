Styrax guyanensis

Styrax guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, glossy seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
