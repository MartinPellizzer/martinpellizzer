Salicornia europaea proles annua

Salicornia europaea proles annua, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal marshes and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are reduced to tiny scales, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Salicornia europaea proles annua plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
