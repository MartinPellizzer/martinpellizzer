Serissa capensis

Serissa capensis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Serissa capensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
