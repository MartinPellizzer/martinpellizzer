Salvia odorata

Salvia odorata, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a flavoring agent due to their pleasant scent.

Salvia odorata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.
