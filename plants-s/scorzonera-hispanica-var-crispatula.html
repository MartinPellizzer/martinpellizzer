Scorzonera hispanica var. crispatula

Scorzonera hispanica var. crispatula, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a stout, tuberous root system that is often harvested for culinary use. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in small, white, bristly hairs. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or violet throat, typically appearing in summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
