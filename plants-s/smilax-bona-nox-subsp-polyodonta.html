Smilax bona-nox subsp. polyodonta

Smilax bona-nox subsp.

polyodonta, commonly known as the prickly pear or swamp smilax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Smilacaceae. It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp spines, and its large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in dense, humid forests.
