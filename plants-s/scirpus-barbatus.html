Scirpus barbatus

Scirpus barbatus, commonly known as the bearded bulrush, is a tall, perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with fine, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

Scirpus barbatus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
