Schinus praecox

Schinus praecox, commonly known as the early Brazilian pepper, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries.
