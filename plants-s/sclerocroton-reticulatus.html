Sclerocroton reticulatus

Sclerocroton reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Sclerocroton reticulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
