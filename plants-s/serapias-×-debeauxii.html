Serapias × debeauxii

Serapias × debeauxii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the serapias genus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Serapias aubertii and Serapias microglossa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly France and Italy. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with bright orange and yellow hues, often with intricate patterns resembling orchids. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic flora.
