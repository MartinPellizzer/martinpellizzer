Senna chloroclada

Senna chloroclada, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets that are usually green with a slight yellowish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Senna chloroclada is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
