Serratula kirghisorum

Serratula kirghisorum, commonly known as Kirghiz hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It has finely serrated leaf margins, which contribute to its common name.

Serratula kirghisorum is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
