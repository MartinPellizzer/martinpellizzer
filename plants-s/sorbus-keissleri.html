Sorbus keissleri

Sorbus keissleri, commonly known as the Keissler's rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Sorbus keissleri is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
