Stevia Tomentosa Var. Typica

Stevia tomentosa, scientifically known as Stevia tomentosa var. typica, is a lesser-known but highly valued plant native to South America. This species belongs to the sunflower family and is characterized by its hairy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Unlike its more famous relative, Stevia rebaudiana, it is not commonly used as a sweetener but holds potential in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Due to its unique properties and ecological significance, it is gaining attention among botanists and herbal enthusiasts alike.
