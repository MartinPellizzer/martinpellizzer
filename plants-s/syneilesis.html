Syneilesis

Syneilesis, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel.

It is native to the wetlands and slow-moving waters of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments. The plant's leaves are broad and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface or partially submerged. Syneilesis is notable for its ability to trap air bubbles, which aid in buoyancy and gas exchange.

This adaptation makes it an important species in aquatic ecosystems, supporting various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
