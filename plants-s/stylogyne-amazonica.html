Stylogyne amazonica

Stylogyne amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It has broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Stylogyne amazonica is often used in traditional medicine and is also a subject of ecological study due to its role in water purification.
