Stipa holwayi

Stipa holwayi, commonly known as Holway's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

Stipa holwayi is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
