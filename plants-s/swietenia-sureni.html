Swietenia sureni

Swietenia sureni, commonly known as the Malabar teak, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and thick, durable wood. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its high-quality timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
