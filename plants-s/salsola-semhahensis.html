Salsola semhahensis

Salsola semhahensis, commonly known as the Jordanian saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East, including Jordan, Israel, and Saudi Arabia. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating high salinity and extreme temperatures.

Salsola semhahensis plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
