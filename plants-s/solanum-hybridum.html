Solanum hybridum

Solanum hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum hybridum is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
