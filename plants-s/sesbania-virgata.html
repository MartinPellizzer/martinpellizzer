Sesbania virgata

Sesbania virgata, commonly known as the white sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Sesbania virgata is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to serve as a forage crop for livestock.
