Salacia letestui

Salacia letestui, commonly known as the Cameroon starling tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Salaciacae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive star-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential medicinal research.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native habitat.
