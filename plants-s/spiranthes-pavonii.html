Spiranthes pavonii

Spiranthes pavonii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its inflorescence consists of numerous small, fragrant blooms.

Spiranthes pavonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
