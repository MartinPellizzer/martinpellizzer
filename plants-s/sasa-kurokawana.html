Sasa kurokawana

Sasa kurokawana, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms or stems. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa kurokawana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
