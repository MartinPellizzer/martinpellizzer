Satureja chandleri

Satureja chandleri, commonly known as Chandler's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Satureja chandleri is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
