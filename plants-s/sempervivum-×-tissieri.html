Sempervivum × tissieri

Sempervivum × tissieri, commonly known as the 'Cape Fawn' or 'Cape Hare', is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish tinge, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Sempervivum × tissieri is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
