Sesamum rigidum

Sesamum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid sesame, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, elliptical seeds. Sesamum rigidum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
