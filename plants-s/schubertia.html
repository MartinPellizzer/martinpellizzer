Schubertia

Schubertia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Central Asia.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-forming leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Schubertia species are adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. They are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Schubertia tridentata and Schubertia stellulata, which are valued for their ornamental and ecological benefits.
