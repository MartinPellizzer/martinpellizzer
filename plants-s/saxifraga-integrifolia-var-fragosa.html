Saxifraga integrifolia var. fragosa

Saxifraga integrifolia var. fragosa, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its fragrant aroma is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
