Solanum malacoxylon var. genuinum

Solanum malacoxylon var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
