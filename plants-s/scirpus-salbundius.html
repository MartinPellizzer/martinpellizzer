Scirpus salbundius

Scirpus salbundius, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has tall, slender stems that are usually unbranched and bear small, clustered spikelets at the tips. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Scirpus salbundius plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
