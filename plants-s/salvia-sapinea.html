Salvia sapinea

Salvia sapinea, commonly known as lesser wood sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Salvia sapinea is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
