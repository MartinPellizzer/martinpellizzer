Salpinga longifolia

Salpinga longifolia, commonly known as the long-flower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and elongated, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
