Sedum × patrickii

Sedum × patrickii, commonly known as the Patrick's stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is prized for its drought tolerance and ornamental value in rock gardens.

Sedum × patrickii typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a touch of color to its native habitat.
