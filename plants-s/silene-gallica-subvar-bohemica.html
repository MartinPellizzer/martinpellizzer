Silene gallica subvar. bohemica

Silene gallica subvar. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene gallica subvar. bohemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
