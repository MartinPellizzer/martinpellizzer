Smilax variegata

Smilax variegata, commonly known as the variegated catbriar, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, variegated leaves that have a distinctive pattern of white and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Smilax variegata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
