Silene boryi var. ramburiana

Silene boryi var. ramburiana, commonly known as the Rambur's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
