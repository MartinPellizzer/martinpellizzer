Silene nocturna subsp. brachypetala

Silene nocturna subsp.

brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled evening pink, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to magenta flowers that open in the evening.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter petals compared to other forms of Silene nocturna. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is adapted to thrive in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
