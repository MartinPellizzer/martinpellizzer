Syzygium millariae

Syzygium millariae, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Syzygium millariae is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its fruit is rich in vitamins and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
