Symplocos berteroi

Symplocos berteroi, commonly known as the yellow-berried holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-orange berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated margins, and the flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters.

The berries are an important food source for various bird species and are also used in traditional medicine.
