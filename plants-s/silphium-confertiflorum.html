Silphium confertiflorum

Silphium confertiflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Silphium confertiflorum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
