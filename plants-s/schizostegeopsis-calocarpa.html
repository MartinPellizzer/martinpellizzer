Schizostegeopsis calocarpa

Schizostegeopsis calocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional crafts, it is also of ecological and cultural significance.
