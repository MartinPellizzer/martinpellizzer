Scutellaria linczewskii

Scutellaria linczewskii, commonly known as the Linczewski's blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to specific regions in Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and southern Russia. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
