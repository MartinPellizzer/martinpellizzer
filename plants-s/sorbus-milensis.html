Sorbus milensis

Sorbus milensis, commonly known as the Miln Forest whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the UK.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sorbus milensis is often found in woodland habitats and is known for its resilience in shaded environments.
