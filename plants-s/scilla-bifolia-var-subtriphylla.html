Scilla bifolia var. subtriphylla

Scilla bifolia var. subtriphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
