Sedum album var. glanduliferum

Sedum album var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the white stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, silver-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers in the summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valued ornamental plant.
