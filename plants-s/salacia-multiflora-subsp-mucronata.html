Salacia multiflora subsp. mucronata

Salacia multiflora subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the serrated salacia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The subspecies is distinguished by its short, sharp leaf tips and its unique floral structure.
