Salix oxica

Salix oxica, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often twining branches. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Salix oxica is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
