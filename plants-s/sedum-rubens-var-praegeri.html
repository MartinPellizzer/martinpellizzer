Sedum rubens var. praegeri

Sedum rubens var. praegeri, commonly known as the Prairie sedum, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
