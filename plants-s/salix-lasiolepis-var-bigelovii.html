Salix lasiolepis var. bigelovii

Salix lasiolepis var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and along streams.

Its fibrous bark and flexible branches make it adaptable to various ecological conditions.
