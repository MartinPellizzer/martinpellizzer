Salvia wilsonii

Salvia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
