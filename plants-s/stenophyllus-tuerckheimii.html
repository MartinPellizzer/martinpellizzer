Stenophyllus tuerckheimii

Stenophyllus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
