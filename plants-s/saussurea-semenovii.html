Saussurea semenovii

Saussurea semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a fibrous root system adapted to its arid and high-altitude environment.

Saussurea semenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
