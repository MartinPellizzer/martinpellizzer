Sorbus pseudomeinichii

Sorbus pseudomeinichii, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring. Its fruit is a small, persistent berry that turns red in autumn.

Sorbus pseudomeinichii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
