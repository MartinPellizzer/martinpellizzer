Salicornia nodulosa

Salicornia nodulosa, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by water, aiding in its survival in coastal areas.

Salicornia nodulosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
