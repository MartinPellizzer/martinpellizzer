Scabiosa succisa var. hirsuta

Scabiosa succisa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers with five petals, resembling a cluster of tiny bells. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
