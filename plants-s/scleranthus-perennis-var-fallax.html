Scleranthus perennis var. fallax

Scleranthus perennis var. fallax, commonly known as the autumn sicklegrass, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. This variant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within the genus Scleranthus.
