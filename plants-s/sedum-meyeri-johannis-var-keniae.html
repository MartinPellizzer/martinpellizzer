Sedum meyeri-johannis var. keniae

Sedum meyeri-johannis var. keniae, commonly known as "Johannis Sedum" or "Kenaie Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or red. The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
