Sidalcea malviflora var. celata

Sidalcea malviflora var. celata, commonly known as the Celata soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
