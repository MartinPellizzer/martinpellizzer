Sabina komarovii

Sabina komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, blue-gray berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant.

Sabina komarovii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
