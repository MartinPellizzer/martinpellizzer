Simaba guianensis var. typica

Simaba guianensis var. typica, commonly known as the Guianese simaba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
