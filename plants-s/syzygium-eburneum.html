Syzygium eburneum

Syzygium eburneum, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and fragrant, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its heartwood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Syzygium eburneum is also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
