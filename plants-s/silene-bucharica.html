Silene bucharica

Silene bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its fruit is a capsule that releases small seeds.

Silene bucharica is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
