Synandrogyne rhizomatosa

Synandrogyne rhizomatosa, commonly known as the creeping rhizome plant, is a unique species characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, a trait known as monoecious reproduction. The plant's leaves are typically alternate and have a lanceolate shape with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
