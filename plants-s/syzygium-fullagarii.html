Syzygium fullagarii

Syzygium fullagarii, commonly known as the Java apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor.

Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
