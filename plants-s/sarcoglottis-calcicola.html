Sarcoglottis calcicola

Sarcoglottis calcicola, commonly known as the limestone bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
