Scirpus mitratus

Scirpus mitratus, commonly known as the mitred bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus mitratus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
