Silene incanescens

Silene incanescens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that give it a frosty appearance.

Silene incanescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
