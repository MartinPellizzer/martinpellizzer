Senna bicapsularis

Senna bicapsularis, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Senna bicapsularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
