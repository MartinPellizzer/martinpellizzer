Schoenodum scariosum

Schoenodum scariosum, commonly known as the scarious schoenodum, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils and full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and rhizomes that aid in its propagation.

The flowers are usually inconspicuous, with tiny, three-lobed perianths that facilitate wind pollination.
