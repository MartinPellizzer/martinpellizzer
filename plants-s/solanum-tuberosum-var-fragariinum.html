Solanum tuberosum var. fragariinum

Solanum tuberosum var. fragariinum, commonly known as the strawberry potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinctive botanical features.

It produces small, edible tubers that resemble strawberries in appearance, with a red or pink skin and a sweet, delicate flavor. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and compound leaves, typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other potato types but is valued for its unique taste and ornamental appeal.
