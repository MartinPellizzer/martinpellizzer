Senna birostris var. birostris

Senna birostris var. birostris, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
