Serjania ilicifolia

Serjania ilicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or sweet holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. Serjania ilicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
