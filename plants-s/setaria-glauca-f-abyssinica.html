Setaria glauca f. abyssinica

Setaria glauca f. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to Africa.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy crop suitable for marginal agricultural lands.

Botanically, it is valued for its resilience and potential as a food and forage crop.
