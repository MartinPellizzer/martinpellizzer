Stelis thoerleae

Stelis thoerleae, commonly known as the Thoerle's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Stelis thoerleae is considered a species of conservation concern.
