Spilanthes charitopis

Spilanthes charitopis, commonly known as the golden mustache, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a mustache, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, sunny areas.

Spilanthes charitopis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
