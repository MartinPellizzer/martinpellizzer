Salmea montana

Salmea montana, commonly known as the mountain salmea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Salmea montana is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine.
