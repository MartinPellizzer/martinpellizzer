Syzygium barnesii

Syzygium barnesii, commonly known as the Christmas bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant produces small, red, bell-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium barnesii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

It is also valued for its use in perfumery due to the essential oils found in its leaves and flowers.
