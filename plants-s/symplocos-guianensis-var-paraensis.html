Symplocos guianensis var. paraensis

Symplocos guianensis var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense waxapple, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
