Solanum conicum

Solanum conicum, commonly known as the conical nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical or bell-shaped fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes. It produces small, white or purple flowers and has hairy, glandular leaves.

Solanum conicum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
