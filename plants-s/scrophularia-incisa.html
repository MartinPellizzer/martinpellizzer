Scrophularia incisa

Scrophularia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are typically pale purple to pink in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Scrophularia incisa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
