Silene abyssinica

Silene abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene abyssinica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
