Sasaella hannoensis

Sasaella hannoensis, commonly known as the Hanno's bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasaella hannoensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
