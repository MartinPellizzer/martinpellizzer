Salvia willeana

Salvia willeana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia willeana is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
