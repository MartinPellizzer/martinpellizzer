Setaria mexicana

Setaria mexicana, commonly known as Mexican foxtail, is a grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive spikelets that resemble a foxtail, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is an important species for studying plant adaptation to arid environments.
