Senecio aromaticus

Senecio aromaticus, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
