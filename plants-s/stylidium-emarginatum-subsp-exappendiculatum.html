Stylidium emarginatum subsp. exappendiculatum

Stylidium emarginatum subsp.

exappendiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sun-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid environments, and it thrives in full sun.

Its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
