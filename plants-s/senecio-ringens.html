Senecio ringens

Senecio ringens, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Senecio ringens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
