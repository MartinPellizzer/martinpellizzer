Strychnos donnaiensis

Strychnos donnaiensis, commonly known as the Donnai's bignay, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody, elongated fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, reddish aril. It is often found in lowland and montane forests, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
