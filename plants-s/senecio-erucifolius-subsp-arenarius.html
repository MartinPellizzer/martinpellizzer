Senecio erucifolius subsp. arenarius

Senecio erucifolius subsp.

arenarius, commonly known as the sand senecio, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in xeriscapes.
