Stachys sieberi

Stachys sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Stachys sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
