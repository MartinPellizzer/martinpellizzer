Solanum macrocarpon subsp. sapinii

Solanum macrocarpon subsp.

sapinii, commonly known as the African eggplant or the bitter apple, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae). It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, fleshy, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

The plant produces showy, violet flowers and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.

However, the fruits contain toxic compounds and should be consumed with caution.
