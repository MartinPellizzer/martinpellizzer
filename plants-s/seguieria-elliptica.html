Seguieria elliptica

Seguieria elliptica, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the Nile, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
