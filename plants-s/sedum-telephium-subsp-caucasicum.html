Sedum telephium subsp. caucasicum

Sedum telephium subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
