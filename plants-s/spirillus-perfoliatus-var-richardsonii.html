Spirillus perfoliatus var. richardsonii

Spirillus perfoliatus var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's spirilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of plant morphology and adaptation to harsh climates.
