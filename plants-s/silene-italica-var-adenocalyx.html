Silene italica var. adenocalyx

Silene italica var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
