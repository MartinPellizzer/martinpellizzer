Syringa villosa var. ovalifolia

Syringa villosa var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the hairy lilac or oval-leaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, softly hairy leaves that are oval in shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for small spaces and container gardening.
