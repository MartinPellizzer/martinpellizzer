Scrophularia epicalycina

Scrophularia epicalycina, commonly known as the broad-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, grasslands, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia epicalycina is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
