Sorbus porrigentiformis

Sorbus porrigentiformis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
