Smilax aspera f. punctata

Smilax aspera f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

This plant is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive pattern of small, raised dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
