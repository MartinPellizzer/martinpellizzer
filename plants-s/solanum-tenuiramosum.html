Solanum tenuiramosum

Solanum tenuiramosum, commonly known as the slender hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, elongated shape.

The berries it produces are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.
