Scrophularia mexicana

Scrophularia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican figwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Scrophularia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
