Sasa nambuana

Sasa nambuana, commonly known as the Nambuana bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Sasa nambuana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
