Simaba multiflora var. schomburgkiana

Simaba multiflora var. schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgkian simaba, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
