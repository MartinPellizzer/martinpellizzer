Schamsia

Schamsia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Schamsia species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce showy blooms during the cooler months of the year. They are adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, often storing moisture in their thick, fleshy stems.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
