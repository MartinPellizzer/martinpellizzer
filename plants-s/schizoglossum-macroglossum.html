Schizoglossum macroglossum

Schizoglossum macroglossum, commonly known as the large-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It features a slender, erect stem with several leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive lip. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schizoglossum macroglossum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
