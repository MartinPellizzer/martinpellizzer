Sclerochloa hemipoa

Sclerochloa hemipoa, commonly known as the African hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Sclerochloa hemipoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage.
