Syzygium alvarezii

Syzygium alvarezii, commonly known as the Alvarez's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. This plant thrives in tropical climates and is typically found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
