Silene boryi var. tejedensis

Silene boryi var. tejedensis, commonly known as the Tejedensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
