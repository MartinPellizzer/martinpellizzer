Saussurea ugoensis

Saussurea ugoensis, commonly known as the Ugoensis Globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea ugoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
