Stilla carolinensis

Stilla carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina stiltgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Stilla carolinensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
