Sasa tangoensis

Sasa tangoensis, commonly known as the Tango bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 meters and has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, grass-like texture. Sasa tangoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to form attractive groves.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
