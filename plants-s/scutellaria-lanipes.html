Scutellaria lanipes

Scutellaria lanipes, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria lanipes typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and garden cultivation.
