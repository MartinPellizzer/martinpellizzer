Spiraea argentea

Spiraea argentea, commonly known as silver weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
