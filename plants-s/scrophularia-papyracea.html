Scrophularia papyracea

Scrophularia papyracea, commonly known as common figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and somewhat succulent.

Scrophularia papyracea is valued in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
