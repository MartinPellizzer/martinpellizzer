Senecio incanus var. ambiguus

Senecio incanus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
