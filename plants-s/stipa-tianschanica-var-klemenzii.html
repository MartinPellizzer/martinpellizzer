Stipa tianschanica var. klemenzii

Stipa tianschanica var. klemenzii, commonly known as Klemenz's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry culms and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppe and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
