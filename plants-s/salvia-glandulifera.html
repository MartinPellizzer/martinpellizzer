Salvia glandulifera

Salvia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Salvia glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
