Stipa brachychaeta f. minor

Stipa brachychaeta f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf feathergrass, is a compact, low-growing grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central and southern Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces feathery, branched inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
