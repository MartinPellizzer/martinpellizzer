Schoenus acuminatus

Schoenus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to various regions of Africa, it typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has fibrous roots and produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
