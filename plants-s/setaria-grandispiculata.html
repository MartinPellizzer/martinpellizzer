Setaria grandispiculata

Setaria grandispiculata, commonly known as the tall beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, bristly flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria grandispiculata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
