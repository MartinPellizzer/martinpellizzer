Solanum stenotomum f. huallata-chinchi

Solanum stenotomum f. huallata-chinchi, commonly known as the Huallata-chinchi tomato, is a unique botanical variant of the tomato species.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruit is a small, round tomato with a rich, sweet flavor.

This variety is native to the Andean regions of South America and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
