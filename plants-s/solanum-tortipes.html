Solanum tortipes

Solanum tortipes, commonly known as the curly-stalked nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Solanum tortipes is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit.
