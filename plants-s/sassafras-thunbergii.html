Sassafras thunbergii

Sassafras thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese sassafras, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, winged fruits. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Sassafras thunbergii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
