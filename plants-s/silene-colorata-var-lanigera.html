Silene colorata var. lanigera

Silene colorata var. lanigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native range.
