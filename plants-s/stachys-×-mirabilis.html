Stachys × mirabilis

Stachys × mirabilis, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is a hybrid species that combines characteristics of its parent species, Stachys palustris and Stachys incarnata. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture.

Stachys × mirabilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
