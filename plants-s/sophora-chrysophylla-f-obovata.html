Sophora chrysophylla f. obovata

Sophora chrysophylla f. obovata, commonly known as the golden-leafed sophora, is a variant of the golden sophora, native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive obovate leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color, especially during the growing season. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, clustered in racemes, and bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
