Serissa ternata

Serissa ternata, commonly known as the silver serissa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are usually silver-gray in color. It produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in the summer months.

Serissa ternata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
