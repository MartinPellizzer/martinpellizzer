Silene graminifolia var. dasyphylla

Silene graminifolia var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the tufted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine ecosystems.
