Sida corrugata var. filiformis

Sida corrugata var. filiformis, commonly known as the silky sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Sida corrugata var. filiformis is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
