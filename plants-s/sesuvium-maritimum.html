Sesuvium maritimum

Sesuvium maritimum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, often found in salt marshes and sandy shores. The plant has small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are succulent, fleshy, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Sesuvium maritimum plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting coastal ecosystems.
