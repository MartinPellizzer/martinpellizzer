Serapias cordigera subsp. cossyrensis

Serapias cordigera subsp.

cossyrensis, commonly known as the Cretan sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the island of Crete. It belongs to the family Rockcress (Capparaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as limestone slopes and coastal areas. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.
