Specklinia concinna

Specklinia concinna, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive silver or white veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a unique, intricate structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
