Stipa letourneuxii subsp. pellita

Stipa letourneuxii subsp.

pellita, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, arid, or semi-arid environments, often found on rocky soils or in grasslands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle of spikelets, which are small and often tinged with purple or brown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appearance.
