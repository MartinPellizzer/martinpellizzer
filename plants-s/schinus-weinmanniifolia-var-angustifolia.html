Schinus weinmanniifolia var. angustifolia

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
