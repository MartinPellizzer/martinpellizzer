Salvia polyclada

Salvia polyclada, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia polyclada is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
