Strophanthus stuhlmannii

Strophanthus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed or red-stemmed African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, often reddish or yellowish stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface, and it has a milky sap that is toxic.

Strophanthus stuhlmannii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
