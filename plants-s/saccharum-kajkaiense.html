Saccharum kajkaiense

Saccharum kajkaiense, commonly known as the Kajkai cane.

It is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Thailand and Malaysia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is closely related to sugarcane. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often growing in dense stands.

The plant's botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a robust rhizomatic system that aids in its propagation.
