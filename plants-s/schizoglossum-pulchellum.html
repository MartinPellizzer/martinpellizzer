Schizoglossum pulchellum

Schizoglossum pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty schizoglossum, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of clustered spikes of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Schizoglossum pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its refined appearance.
