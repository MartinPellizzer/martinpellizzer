Saussurea kurilensis

Saussurea kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Kuril Islands and surrounding areas in the northern Pacific Ocean. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically succulent and adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Saussurea kurilensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
