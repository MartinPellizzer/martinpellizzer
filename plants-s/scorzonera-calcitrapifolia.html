Scorzonera calcitrapifolia

Scorzonera calcitrapifolia, commonly known as the rock scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, elongated fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Scorzonera calcitrapifolia is often found in coastal areas and is considered a characteristic species of its habitat.
