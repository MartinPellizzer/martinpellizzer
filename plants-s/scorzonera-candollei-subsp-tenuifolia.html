Scorzonera candollei subsp. tenuifolia

Scorzonera candollei subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a robust taproot system and produces showy, yellowish to orange-red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
