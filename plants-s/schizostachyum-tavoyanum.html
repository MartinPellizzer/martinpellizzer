Schizostachyum tavoyanum

Schizostachyum tavoyanum, commonly known as the Tavoy bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in moist, tropical environments, such as along riverbanks and in lowland forests. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Schizostachyum tavoyanum also has cultural and traditional uses in some regions.
