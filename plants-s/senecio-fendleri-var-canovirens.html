Senecio fendleri var. canovirens

Senecio fendleri var. canovirens, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features succulent, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its evolutionary adaptations to harsh, semi-desert environments.
