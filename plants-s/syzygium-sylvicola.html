Syzygium sylvicola

Syzygium sylvicola, commonly known as the forest syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Syzygium sylvicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
