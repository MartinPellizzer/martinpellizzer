Salsola lasiantha

Salsola lasiantha, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Salsola lasiantha is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick cuticles and succulent tissues that help it retain water.

It is often found in sandy soils and can play a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
