Sasa paniculata var. ontakensis

Sasa paniculata var. ontakensis, commonly known as the Ontake bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, fragrant panicles of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
