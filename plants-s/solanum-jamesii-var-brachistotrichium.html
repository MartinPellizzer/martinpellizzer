Solanum jamesii var. brachistotrichium

Solanum jamesii var. brachistotrichium, commonly known as the short-trichium nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is notable for its unique trichome structures on its stems and leaves.
