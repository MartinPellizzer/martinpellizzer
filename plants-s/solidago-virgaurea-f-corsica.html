Solidago virgaurea f. corsica

Solidago virgaurea f. corsica, commonly known as the Corsican goldenrod, is a botanical variant of the common goldenrod.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, and is characterized by its yellow floral clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of numerous small, bright yellow flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
