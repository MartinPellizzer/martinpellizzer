Smilacina trinervis

Smilacina trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined greenbrier, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Smilacina trinervis thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks, where it can climb trees or shrubs to reach sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an ecologically significant component of its native ecosystem.
