Senecio palmatus subsp. davuricus

Senecio palmatus subsp.

davuricus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
