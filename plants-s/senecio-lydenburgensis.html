Senecio lydenburgensis

Senecio lydenburgensis, commonly known as the Lydenburg groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and rockeries.
