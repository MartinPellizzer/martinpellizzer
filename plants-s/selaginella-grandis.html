Selaginella grandis

Selaginella grandis, commonly known as the giant spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Selaginella grandis is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its specialized water retention structures.

It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms.
