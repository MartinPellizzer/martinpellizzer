Strephium pulchellum

Strephium pulchellum, commonly known as the fairy slipper, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, preferring rich, well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Strephium pulchellum is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
