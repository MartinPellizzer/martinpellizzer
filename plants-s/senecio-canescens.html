Senecio canescens

Senecio canescens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Senecio canescens is a perennial herb that is well adapted to dry conditions and can be found in various habitats across its native range.
