Stipa curvifolia

Stipa curvifolia, commonly known as the bentgrass or curly grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, curved leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other grasses and wildflowers.

Stipa curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
