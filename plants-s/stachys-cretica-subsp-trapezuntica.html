Stachys cretica subsp. trapezuntica

Stachys cretica subsp.

trapezuntica, commonly known as the Balkan betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. It is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
