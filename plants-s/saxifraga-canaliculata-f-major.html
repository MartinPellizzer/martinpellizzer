Saxifraga canaliculata f. major

Saxifraga canaliculata f. major, commonly known as the large-leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in rock gardens and as a container plant.
