Sorbus foliolosa var. typica

Sorbus foliolosa var. typica, commonly known as the leafy whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several leaflets. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
