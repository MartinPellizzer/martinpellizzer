Schedonorus scirpoideus

Schedonorus scirpoideus, commonly known as marsh bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schedonorus scirpoideus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
