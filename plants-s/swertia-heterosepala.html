Swertia heterosepala

Swertia heterosepala, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Swertia heterosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
