Strongylodon secundus

Strongylodon secundus, commonly known as the sweet-scented pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and its resilience to coastal conditions.
