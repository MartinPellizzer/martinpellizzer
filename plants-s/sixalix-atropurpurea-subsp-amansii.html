Sixalix atropurpurea subsp. amansii

Sixalix atropurpurea subsp.

amansii, commonly known as the purple-flowered sixalix, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its striking floral display.
