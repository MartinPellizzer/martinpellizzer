Solanum nigrum var. probstii

Solanum nigrum var. probstii, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions with temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

While it shares some similarities with other Solanum species, it is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
