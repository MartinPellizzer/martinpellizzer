Stemmacantha nitida

Stemmacantha nitida, commonly known as the silky stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
