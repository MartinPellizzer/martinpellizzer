Smilacina finitima

Smilacina finitima, commonly known as the slender greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and long, slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Smilacina finitima is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
