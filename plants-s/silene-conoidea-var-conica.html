Silene conoidea var. conica

Silene conoidea var. conica, commonly known as the nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

Silene conoidea var. conica is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
