Silene bungeana

Silene bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Silene bungeana has simple, alternate leaves and produces capsules as its fruit type.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
