Sobralia ciliata

Sobralia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.

Sobralia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
