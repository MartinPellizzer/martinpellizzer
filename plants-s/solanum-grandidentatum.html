Solanum grandidentatum

Solanum grandidentatum, commonly known as the large-toothed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves with prominent lobes, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a rounded berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
