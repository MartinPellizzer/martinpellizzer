Saxifraga pseudolaevis

Saxifraga pseudolaevis, commonly known as the smooth saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, smooth, and often bluish-gray leaves that are adapted to cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in late summer, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra habitats.

Saxifraga pseudolaevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
