Salvia alamosana

Salvia alamosana, commonly known as Alamos sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia alamosana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
