Salix vahlii

Salix vahlii, commonly known as Vahl's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salix vahlii is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
