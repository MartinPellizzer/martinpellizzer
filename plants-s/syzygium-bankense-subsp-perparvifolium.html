Syzygium bankense subsp. perparvifolium

Syzygium bankense subsp.

perparvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved clove or lesser clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
