Sasa kurilensis f. simofuri

Sasa kurilensis f. simofuri, commonly known as the Simofuri bamboo, is a variant of the Kuril bamboo native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense, upright clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has slender, hollow stems with a smooth, grayish-green surface. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly tapered, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
