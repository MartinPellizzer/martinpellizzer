Silene oenotriae

Silene oenotriae, commonly known as the Sicilian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene oenotriae is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
