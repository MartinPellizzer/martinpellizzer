Scilla pulchella

Scilla pulchella, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Scilla pulchella is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
