Serratula hakkiarica

Serratula hakkiarica, commonly known as the Armenian sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Serratula hakkiarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
