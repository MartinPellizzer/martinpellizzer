Salix barclayi var. neomexicana

Salix barclayi var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and stream banks. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
