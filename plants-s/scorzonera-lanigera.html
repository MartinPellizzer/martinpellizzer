Scorzonera lanigera

Scorzonera lanigera, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a fleshy, tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Scorzonera lanigera is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
