Solanum memphiticum var. repandum

Solanum memphiticum var. repandum, commonly known as the Memphic nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided by livestock and humans alike.
