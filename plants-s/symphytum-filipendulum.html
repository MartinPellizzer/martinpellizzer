Symphytum filipendulum

Symphytum filipendulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features thick, fleshy roots and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
