Sideritis hyssopifolia var. bracteosa

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. bracteosa, commonly known as bracteate ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

This variant is distinguished by the presence of bracts beneath its flowers, giving it its specific name.
