Schizocodon ilicifolius var. intercedens

Schizocodon ilicifolius var. intercedens, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a reddish-brown underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems.
