Scirpus subquadrangularis

Scirpus subquadrangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes.

Scirpus subquadrangularis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
