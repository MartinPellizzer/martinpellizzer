Setaria imberbis f. breviseta

Setaria imberbis f. breviseta, commonly known as the short-haired fingergrass, is a variant of the common fingergrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia, characterized by its short, stiff hairs on the leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous short, bristly spikelets.

The botanical features of this plant make it a subject of interest in both ecological and taxonomic studies.
