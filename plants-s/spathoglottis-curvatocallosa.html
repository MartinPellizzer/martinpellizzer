Spathoglottis curvatocallosa

Spathoglottis curvatocallosa, commonly known as the curling callosa orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling flower spikes and vibrant, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows in forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Spathoglottis curvatocallosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
