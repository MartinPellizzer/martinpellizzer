Salvia aristulata

Salvia aristulata, commonly known as meadow sage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Its nectar-rich blossoms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
