Solanum wallisii

Solanum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but often bitter.

Solanum wallisii is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
