Scleria setosoasperula

Scleria setosoasperula, commonly known as the hairy spearplant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, triangular leaves and clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Scleria setosoasperula is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
