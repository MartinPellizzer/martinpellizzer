Salvia campanulata var. pilinophylla

Salvia campanulata var. pilinophylla, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, woolly texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
