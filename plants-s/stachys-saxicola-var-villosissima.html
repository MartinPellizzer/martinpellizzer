Stachys saxicola var. villosissima

Stachys saxicola var. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to rocky and arid environments in southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, gravelly soils.
