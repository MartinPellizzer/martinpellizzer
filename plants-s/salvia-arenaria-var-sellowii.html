Salvia arenaria var. sellowii

Salvia arenaria var. sellowii, commonly known as Sowbug sage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
