Saururus cumingii

Saururus cumingii, commonly known as Chinese cumin or fish root, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Saururus cumingii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
