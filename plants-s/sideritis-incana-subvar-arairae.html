Sideritis incana subvar. arairae

Sideritis incana subvar. arairae, commonly known as the white-haired ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or pale purple, blooming in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
