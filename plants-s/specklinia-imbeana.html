Specklinia imbeana

Specklinia imbeana, commonly known as the Java fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often folded or rolled. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes anchor it to substrates, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Specklinia imbeana is popular in aquascaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
