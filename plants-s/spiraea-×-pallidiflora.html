Spiraea × pallidiflora

Spiraea × pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that create a weeping habit, making it an ornamental favorite in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early summer, which are borne on the previous year's growth. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green color that turns reddish in the fall.

Spiraea × pallidiflora is adaptable to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
