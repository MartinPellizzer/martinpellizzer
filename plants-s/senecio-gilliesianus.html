Senecio gilliesianus

Senecio gilliesianus, commonly known as the Gillies' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
