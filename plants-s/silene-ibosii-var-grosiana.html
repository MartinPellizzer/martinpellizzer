Silene ibosii var. grosiana

Silene ibosii var. grosiana, commonly known as the giant silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its robust growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene ibosii var. grosiana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open habitats.
