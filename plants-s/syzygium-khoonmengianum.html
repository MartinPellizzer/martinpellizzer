Syzygium khoonmengianum

Syzygium khoonmengianum, commonly known as the Khoonmengian sandalwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
