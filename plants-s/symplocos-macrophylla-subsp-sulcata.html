Symplocos macrophylla subsp. sulcata

Symplocos macrophylla subsp.

sulcata, commonly known as the broad-leaved snowbell, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing small, smooth seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
