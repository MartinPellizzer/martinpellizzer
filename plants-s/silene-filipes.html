Silene filipes

Silene filipes, commonly known as the hairstem catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene filipes is known for its short flowering period, usually in late spring to early summer.
