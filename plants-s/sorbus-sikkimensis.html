Sorbus sikkimensis

Sorbus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim whitebeams, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often serrated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 2,000 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
