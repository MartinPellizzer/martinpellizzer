Stoebe affinis

Stoebe affinis, commonly known as narrowleaf devil's-bit, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Stoebe affinis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
