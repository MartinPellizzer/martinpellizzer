Scilla peruviana var. subcarnea

Scilla peruviana var. subcarnea, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant color.
