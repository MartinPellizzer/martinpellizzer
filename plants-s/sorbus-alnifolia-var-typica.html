Sorbus alnifolia var. typica

Sorbus alnifolia var. typica, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are usually yellow or orange when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value, particularly in autumn when its leaves turn a striking red or orange.
