Senecio decipiens

Senecio decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple flowering heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
