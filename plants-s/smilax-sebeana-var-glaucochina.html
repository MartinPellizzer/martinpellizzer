Smilax sebeana var. glaucochina

Smilax sebeana var. glaucochina, commonly known as the blue-leaf smilax, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spiny tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and often have a bluish or waxy appearance, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
