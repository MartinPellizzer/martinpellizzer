Sphaerothylax warmingiana

Sphaerothylax warmingiana, commonly known as the Warming's globe cactus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stem that resembles a ball, with bright green to blue-gray tones. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Sphaerothylax warmingiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
