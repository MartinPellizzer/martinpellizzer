Silaum silaus

Silaum silaus, commonly known as the common silymarin or milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in liver health. Its seeds contain the compound silybin, which is used in herbal medicine.

Silaum silaus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
