Senecio xanthoxylon

Senecio xanthoxylon, commonly known as the yellowwood sedum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features gray-green, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish powder, which helps reduce water loss. It produces yellow-orange flowers in early spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage.

This succulent is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
