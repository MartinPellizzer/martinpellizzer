Solanum raphanifolium

Solanum raphanifolium, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that start green and turn red as they mature.

Solanum raphanifolium is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
