Schomburgkia × parkinsoniana

Schomburgkia × parkinsoniana, commonly known as the hybrid spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flower structure with long, slender petals and a prominent central column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces showy blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and fragrance.
