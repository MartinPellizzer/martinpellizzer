Syndiclis furfuracea

Syndiclis furfuracea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in small, three-lobed capsules that split open when mature.

Syndiclis furfuracea is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
