Sideritis montana subsp. ebracteata

Sideritis montana subsp.

ebracteata, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception.
