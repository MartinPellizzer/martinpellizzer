Silene gigantea var. incana

Silene gigantea var. incana, commonly known as the hairy giant catchfly, is a striking plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, covered in dense, white to greyish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene gigantea var. incana is adapted to cold, harsh environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
