Sasa igagoyeana

Sasa igagoyeana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Sasa igagoyeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
