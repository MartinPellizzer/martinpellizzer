Setaria stenantha

Setaria stenantha, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important cereal crops.
