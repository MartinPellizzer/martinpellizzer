Silene arguta

Silene arguta, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes. The plant features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. Silene arguta has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptations to harsh environments.
