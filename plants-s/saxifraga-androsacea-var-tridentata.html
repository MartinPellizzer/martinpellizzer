Saxifraga androsacea var. tridentata

Saxifraga androsacea var. tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens.

It is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
