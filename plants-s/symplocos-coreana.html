Symplocos coreana

Symplocos coreana, commonly known as the Korean beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Symplocos coreana thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
