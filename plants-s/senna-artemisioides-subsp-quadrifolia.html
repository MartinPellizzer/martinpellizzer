Senna artemisioides subsp. quadrifolia

Senna artemisioides subsp.

quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with four leaflets, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
