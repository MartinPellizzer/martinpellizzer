Salsola spicata

Salsola spicata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some regions.

Salsola spicata is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water.
