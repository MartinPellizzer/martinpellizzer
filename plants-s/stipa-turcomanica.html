Stipa turcomanica

Stipa turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets.

Stipa turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
