Scopolia involucrata

Scopolia involucrata, commonly known as the hooded gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in rocky or woodland soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Scopolia involucrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
