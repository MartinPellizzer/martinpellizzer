Sonchus brunneri

Sonchus brunneri, commonly known as the bristly sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Sonchus brunneri is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
