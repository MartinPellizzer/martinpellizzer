Sporobolus verticillatus

Sporobolus verticillatus, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, which are composed of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
