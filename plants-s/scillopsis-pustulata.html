Scillopsis pustulata

Scillopsis pustulata, commonly known as the clustered scillopsis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from the base of the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
