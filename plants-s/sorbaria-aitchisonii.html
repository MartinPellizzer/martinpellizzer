Sorbaria aitchisonii

Sorbaria aitchisonii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Aitchison's sorbaria, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
