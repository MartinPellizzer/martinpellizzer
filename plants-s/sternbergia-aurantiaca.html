Sternbergia aurantiaca

Sternbergia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded corms for storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its unique blooming season, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
