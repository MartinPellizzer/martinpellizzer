Sapium sanguinolentum

Sapium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloodroot or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its most notable botanical feature is the bright red sap that exudes from its roots when cut.

Sapium sanguinolentum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
