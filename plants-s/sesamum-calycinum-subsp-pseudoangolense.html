Sesamum calycinum subsp. pseudoangolense

Sesamum calycinum subsp.

pseudoangolense, commonly known as the Angola sesame, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Pedaliaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
