Salvia bridgesii

Salvia bridgesii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
