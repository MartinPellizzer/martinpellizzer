Solanum matehualae

Solanum matehualae, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom in the evening.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the summer months.
