Soldanella × neglecta

Soldanella × neglecta, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a dark green color.

Soldanella × neglecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
