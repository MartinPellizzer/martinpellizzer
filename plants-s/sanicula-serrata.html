Sanicula serrata

Sanicula serrata, commonly known as the toothed sanicle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
