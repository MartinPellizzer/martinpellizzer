Salix × fragilis var. rubens

Salix × fragilis var. rubens, commonly known as the red-osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red twigs that stand out against the winter landscape. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and grayish-white underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
