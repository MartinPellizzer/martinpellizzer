Sessea discolor

Sessea discolor, commonly known as the African sandstone cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a distinctive white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by pale green fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens in regions with similar climates.
