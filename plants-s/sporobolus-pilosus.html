Sporobolus pilosus

Sporobolus pilosus, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Sporobolus pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
