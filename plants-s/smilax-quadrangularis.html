Smilax quadrangularis

Smilax quadrangularis, commonly known as the roughleaf smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habits. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic with a rough, textured surface.

Smilax quadrangularis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
