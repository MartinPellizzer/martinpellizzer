Sideritis fruticulosa

Sideritis fruticulosa, commonly known as woolly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

Sideritis fruticulosa is known for its use in traditional herbal medicine due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
