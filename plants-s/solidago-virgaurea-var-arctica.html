Solidago virgaurea var. arctica

Solidago virgaurea var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It is a hardy species often used in ecological restoration projects in high-altitude or tundra environments.
