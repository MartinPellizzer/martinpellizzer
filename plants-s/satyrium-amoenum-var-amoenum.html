Satyrium amoenum var. amoenum

Satyrium amoenum var. amoenum, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a spike of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils in shaded woodland environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that produces tiny, ephemeral flowers, often going unnoticed due to their delicate appearance.

The species is also known for its unique, spiral arrangement of leaves, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.
