Symphyotrichum subulatum var. elongatum

Symphyotrichum subulatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, clustered purple or pink flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This variant is distinguished from other members of the species by its elongated stems and narrower foliage.
