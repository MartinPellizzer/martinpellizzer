Seriphidium fragrans

Seriphidium fragrans, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Seriphidium fragrans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
