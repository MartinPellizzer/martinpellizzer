Sponia chichilea

Sponia chichilea, commonly known as the Chilean sponia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, reducing water loss, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Sponia chichilea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
