Symplocos molinae

Symplocos molinae, commonly known as the Molina's wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Symplocos molinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native range.
