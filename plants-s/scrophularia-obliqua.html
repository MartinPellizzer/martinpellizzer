Scrophularia obliqua

Scrophularia obliqua, commonly known as the slanty figwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scrophularia obliqua is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
