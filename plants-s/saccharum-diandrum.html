Saccharum diandrum

Saccharum diandrum, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant cane, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
