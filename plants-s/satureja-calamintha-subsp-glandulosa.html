Satureja calamintha subsp. glandulosa

Satureja calamintha subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as glandular savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped fruits called schizocarps, which are typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
