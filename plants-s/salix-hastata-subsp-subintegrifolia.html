Salix hastata subsp. subintegrifolia

Salix hastata subsp.

subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are usually smooth along the edges, with a pointed tip and a pale green underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind.

This subspecies is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
