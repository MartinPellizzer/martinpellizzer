Senecio scalaris var. carmenensis

Senecio scalaris var. carmenensis, commonly known as Carmen's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species in horticulture.
