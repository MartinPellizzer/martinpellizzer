Saxifraga × vierhapperi

Saxifraga × vierhapperi, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga × vierhapperi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
