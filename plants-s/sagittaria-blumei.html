Sagittaria blumei

Sagittaria blumei, commonly known as the Blume's arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, depending on the water depth. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with white petals that attract insects for pollination.

Sagittaria blumei plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
