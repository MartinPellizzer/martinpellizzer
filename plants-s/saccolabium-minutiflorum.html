Saccolabium minutiflorum

Saccolabium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
