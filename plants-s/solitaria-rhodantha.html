Solitaria rhodantha

Solitaria rhodantha, commonly known as the red rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solitaria rhodantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
