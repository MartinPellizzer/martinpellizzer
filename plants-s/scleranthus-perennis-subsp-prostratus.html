Scleranthus perennis subsp. prostratus

Scleranthus perennis subsp.

prostratus, commonly known as creeping sandrocket, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, yellowish flowers and has a prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
