Silene schlosseri

Silene schlosseri, commonly known as the "Flame Violet," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. Silene schlosseri is known for its distinctive pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
