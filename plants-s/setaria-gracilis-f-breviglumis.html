Setaria gracilis f. breviglumis

Setaria gracilis f. breviglumis, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow leaves.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its fine texture and delicate appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
