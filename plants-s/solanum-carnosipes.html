Solanum carnosipes

Solanum carnosipes, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum carnosipes belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
