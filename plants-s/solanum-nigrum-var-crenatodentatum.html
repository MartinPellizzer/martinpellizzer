Solanum nigrum var. crenatodentatum

Solanum nigrum var. crenatodentatum, commonly known as the serrated-leaved black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade species native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive serrated, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple berries that are typically toxic but have been used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and flowers with five violet petals.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and wastelands.
