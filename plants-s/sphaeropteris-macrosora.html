Sphaeropteris macrosora

Sphaeropteris macrosora, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a thick, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. Its reproductive structures include large, showy cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants.

Sphaeropteris macrosora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
