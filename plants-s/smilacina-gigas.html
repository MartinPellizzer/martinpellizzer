Smilacina gigas

Smilacina gigas, commonly known as the giant greenbriar, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Smilacina gigas thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners make it both a notable wild plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
