Silene pardoi subsp. guarensis

Silene pardoi subsp.

guarensis, commonly known as the Guarense campion, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and showy pink to red flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and shorter pedicels compared to other members of the *Silene pardoi* complex. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed habitats at high elevations.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
