Spiesia albana

Spiesia albana, commonly known as the white spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its thick, water-storing stems.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
