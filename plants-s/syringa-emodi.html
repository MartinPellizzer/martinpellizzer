Syringa emodi

Syringa emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Syringa emodi thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
