Salix orotchonorum

Salix orotchonorum, commonly known as the Tibetan willow, is a species of willow native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces catkins as its reproductive structures, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

Due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
