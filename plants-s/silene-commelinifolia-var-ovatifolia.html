Silene commelinifolia var. ovatifolia

Silene commelinifolia var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent stems and often showy flowers.
