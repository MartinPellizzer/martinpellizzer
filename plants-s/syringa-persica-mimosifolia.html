Syringa persica mimosifolia

Syringa persica mimosifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in landscaping.
