Salix × taylorii

Salix × taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salix × taylorii is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
