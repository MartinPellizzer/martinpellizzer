Stipa lachnocolea

Stipa lachnocolea, commonly known as the silvergrass or the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hair-like bristles. Stipa lachnocolea thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
