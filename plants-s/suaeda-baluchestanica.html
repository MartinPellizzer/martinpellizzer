Suaeda baluchestanica

Suaeda baluchestanica, commonly known as the Baluchestan seepweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its structure includes fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.

Suaeda baluchestanica plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
