Sarcoglottis schwackei

Sarcoglottis schwackei, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.

Sarcoglottis schwackei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
