Sarcathria procumbens

Sarcathria procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sarcathria, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its sprawling stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Sarcathria procumbens is often found in shaded, forested areas where it thrives in the understory.
