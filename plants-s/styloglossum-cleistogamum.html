Styloglossum cleistogamum

Styloglossum cleistogamum, commonly known as the cleistogamous bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its closed, self-pollinating flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that remain closed during bloom, facilitating self-fertilization. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.
