Salvia atrorubra

Salvia atrorubra, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Salvia atrorubra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
