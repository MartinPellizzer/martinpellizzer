Selaginella reticulata

Selaginella reticulata, commonly known as the basket fern or spiky clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and small, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure. The plant produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, showcasing its heterosporous reproductive system. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
