Serratula bulgarica

Serratula bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian serrata, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats. Its leaves are serrated and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
