Scutellaria canescens var. punctata

Scutellaria canescens var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its flowers are tubular, two-lipped, and range in color from blue to purple, often with a yellow throat.

The species is characterized by its variable leaf markings, which include small, scattered spots, giving it its common name.
