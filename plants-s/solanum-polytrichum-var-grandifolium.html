Solanum polytrichum var. grandifolium

Solanum polytrichum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, violet flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
