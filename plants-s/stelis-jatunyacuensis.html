Stelis jatunyacuensis

Stelis jatunyacuensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex coloration pattern. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in forested areas, showing adaptations to the harsh mountain environment. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological niche.

Stelis jatunyacuensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and habitat.
