Salvia rechingeri

Salvia rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Salvia rechingeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
