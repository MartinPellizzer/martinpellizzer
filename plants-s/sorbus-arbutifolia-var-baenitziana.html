Sorbus arbutifolia var. baenitziana

Sorbus arbutifolia var. baenitziana, commonly known as the Baenitziana whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and its distinctive leaves, which are elliptical with a finely toothed margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by white, aggregate fruits that mature in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
