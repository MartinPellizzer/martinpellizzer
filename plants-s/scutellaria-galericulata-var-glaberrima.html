Scutellaria galericulata var. glaberrima

Scutellaria galericulata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
