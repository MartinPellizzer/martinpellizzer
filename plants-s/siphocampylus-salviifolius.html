Siphocampylus salviifolius

Siphocampylus salviifolius, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Siphocampylus salviifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
