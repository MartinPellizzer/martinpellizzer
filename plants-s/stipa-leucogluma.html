Stipa leucogluma

Stipa leucogluma, commonly known as the white glume grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa leucogluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
