Setaria flabellata

Setaria flabellata, commonly known as the fan grass or flat-seed millet, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, flat, triangular seed heads that resemble a fan. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and disturbed soils. It is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it has been utilized in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
