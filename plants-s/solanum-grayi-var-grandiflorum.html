Solanum grayi var. grandiflorum

Solanum grayi var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, which is a small berry, contains alkaloids and is not typically consumed by humans due to its potential toxicity.
