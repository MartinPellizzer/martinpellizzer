Selaginella sibirica

Selaginella sibirica, commonly known as Siberian spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Selaginella sibirica reproduces via spores, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

It is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
