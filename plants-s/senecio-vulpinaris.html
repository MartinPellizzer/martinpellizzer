Senecio vulpinaris

Senecio vulpinaris, commonly known as the fox's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a dense, tail-like cluster, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio vulpinaris is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
