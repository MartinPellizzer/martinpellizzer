Strigilia aurea

Strigilia aurea, commonly known as the golden strigil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its name "strigil" refers to the hairy texture of its stems, while "aurea" means golden, alluding to its vibrant floral coloration.

Strigilia aurea is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
