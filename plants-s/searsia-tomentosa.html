Searsia tomentosa

Searsia tomentosa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly underside on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Searsia tomentosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
