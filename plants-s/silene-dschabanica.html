Silene dschabanica

Silene dschabanica, commonly known as the Dschaban bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Silene dschabanica has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
