Scirpus radicans

Scirpus radicans, commonly known as the spreading bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often standing in shallow water or moist soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikes of small, clustered flowers. The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus radicans is also valued for its ability to filter water and reduce erosion in riparian areas.
