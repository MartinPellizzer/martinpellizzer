Sasa nantaialpicola

Sasa nantaialpicola, commonly known as the alpine bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with poor soil conditions. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Sasa nantaialpicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
