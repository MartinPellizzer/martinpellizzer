Senecio asiaticus

Senecio asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio asiaticus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
