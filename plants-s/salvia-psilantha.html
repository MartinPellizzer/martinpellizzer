Salvia psilantha

Salvia psilantha, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia psilantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
