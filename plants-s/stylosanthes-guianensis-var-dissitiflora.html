Stylosanthes guianensis var. dissitiflora

Stylosanthes guianensis var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy stylo, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several oval seeds. It is well adapted to dry and poor soils, making it a valuable forage species in arid regions.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability enhances soil fertility, supporting its use in agricultural systems.
