Scrophularia gmelinii

Scrophularia gmelinii, commonly known as the meadow scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Scrophularia gmelinii is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
