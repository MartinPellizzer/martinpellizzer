Solanum bojeri

Solanum bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
