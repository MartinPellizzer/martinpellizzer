Spiraea tomentosa var. paniculata

Spiraea tomentosa var. paniculata, commonly known as panicled snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy, white flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage that turns yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its compact size and seasonal floral displays.
