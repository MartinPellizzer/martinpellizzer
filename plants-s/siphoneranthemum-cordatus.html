Siphoneranthemum cordatus

Siphoneranthemum cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf siphoneranthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Siphoneranthemum cordatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
