Syringodea diaphana

Syringodea diaphana, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly dependent on its environment. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and illegal collection, Syringodea diaphana is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
