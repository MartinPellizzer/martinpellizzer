Sorghum dochna

Sorghum dochna, commonly known as red sorghum, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and striking red or purple hues in its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, which are used for food and animal feed. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it an important crop in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.
