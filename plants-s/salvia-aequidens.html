Salvia aequidens

Salvia aequidens, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their cooling properties.

Salvia aequidens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
