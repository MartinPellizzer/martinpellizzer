Salvia × hegelmaieri

Salvia × hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in dense, velvety foliage that ranges from gray-green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia × hegelmaieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in arid climates.
