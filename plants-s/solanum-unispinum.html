Solanum unispinum

Solanum unispinum, commonly known as the one-spined nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are typically dark green and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Solanum unispinum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
