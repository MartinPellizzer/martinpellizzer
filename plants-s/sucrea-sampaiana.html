Sucrea sampaiana

Sucrea sampaiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

The plant contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
