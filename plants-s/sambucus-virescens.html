Sambucus virescens

Sambucus virescens, commonly known as green elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sambucus virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
