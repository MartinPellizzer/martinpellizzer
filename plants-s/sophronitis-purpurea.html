Sophronitis purpurea

Sophronitis purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

Sophronitis purpurea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
