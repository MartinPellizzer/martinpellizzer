Setaria magna

Setaria magna, commonly known as giant foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa but now widely naturalized in various parts of the world.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, bristly spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Setaria magna is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
