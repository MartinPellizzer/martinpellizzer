Sopubia parviflora subsp. eminii

Sopubia parviflora subsp.

eminii, commonly known as the Emine's small-flowered sopubia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
