Saxifraga aristulata f. longipila

Saxifraga aristulata f. longipila, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially under stress. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant, low-maintenance plants.
