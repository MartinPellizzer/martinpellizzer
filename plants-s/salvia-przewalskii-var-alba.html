Salvia przewalskii var. alba

Salvia przewalskii var. alba, commonly known as White Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late summer into autumn.
