Sarcocornia pillansii

Sarcocornia pillansii, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly in South Africa and Namibia. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in dense inflorescences, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sarcocornia pillansii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
