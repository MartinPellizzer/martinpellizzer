Sebastiania pachystachya var. genuina

Sebastiania pachystachya var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright purple berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its unique appearance and seasonal color, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
