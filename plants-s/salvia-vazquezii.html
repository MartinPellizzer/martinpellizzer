Salvia vazquezii

Salvia vazquezii, commonly known as Vazquez's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia vazquezii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
