Senecio werneriifolius var. alpinus

Senecio werneriifolius var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
