Setaria lindenbergiana

Setaria lindenbergiana, commonly known as Lindenberg's foxtail, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, awned seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
