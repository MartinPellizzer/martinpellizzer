Solanum riedlei

Solanum riedlei, commonly known as the Riedel's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum riedlei is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
