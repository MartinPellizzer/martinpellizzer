Statice spathulata var. emarginata

Statice spathulata var. emarginata, commonly known as the fringed sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense, spikes of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its salt tolerance and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
