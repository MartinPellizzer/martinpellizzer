Symplocos sandiae

Symplocos sandiae, commonly known as the sandia symplocos, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. Symplocos sandiae thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
