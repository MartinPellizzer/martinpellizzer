Stemmacantha pulchra

Stemmacantha pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
