Strigilia florida

Strigilia florida, commonly known as the flowering strigil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Strigilia florida is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, making it a characteristic species of Mediterranean scrublands.
