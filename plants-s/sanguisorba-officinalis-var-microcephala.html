Sanguisorba officinalis var. microcephala

Sanguisorba officinalis var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf dropwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
