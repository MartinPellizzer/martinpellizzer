Stachys cooleyae

Stachys cooleyae, commonly known as Cooley's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a fuzzy texture. Its small, purple flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stachys cooleyae is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
