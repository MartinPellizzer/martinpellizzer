Sapium marginatum var. obovatum

Sapium marginatum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive margin. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. Its bark is thick, flaky, and ranges in color from gray to reddish-brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
