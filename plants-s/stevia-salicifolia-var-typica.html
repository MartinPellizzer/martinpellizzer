Stevia salicifolia var. typica

Stevia salicifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in herbal medicine and natural sweetener production.
