Sebastiania daphniphylla

Sebastiania daphniphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological significance, it is also valued in traditional medicine and horticultural practices.
