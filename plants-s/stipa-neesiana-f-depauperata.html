Stipa neesiana f. depauperata

Stipa neesiana f. depauperata, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a botanical variant of the grass species Stipa neesiana.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This subspecies typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
