Satureja subspicata f. liburnica

Satureja subspicata f. liburnica, commonly known as the Liburnian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in summer, and the leaves are used in traditional cuisine for their pungent, savory aroma.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
