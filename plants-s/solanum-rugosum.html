Solanum rugosum

Solanum rugosum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, hairy leaves, and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum rugosum is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
