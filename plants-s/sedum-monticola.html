Sedum monticola

Sedum monticola, commonly known as the mountain sedum, is a succulent plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white or yellow in color. It is often found growing on slopes and cliffs, where it can anchor itself in crevices.

Sedum monticola is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
