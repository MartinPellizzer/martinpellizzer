Senna pinheiroi

Senna pinheiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Senna pinheiroi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
