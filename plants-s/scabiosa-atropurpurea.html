Scabiosa atropurpurea

Scabiosa atropurpurea, commonly known as the bloody cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa atropurpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
