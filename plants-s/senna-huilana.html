Senna huilana

Senna huilana, commonly known as Huilana senna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. Senna huilana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.
