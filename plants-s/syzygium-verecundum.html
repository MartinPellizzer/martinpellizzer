Syzygium verecundum

Syzygium verecundum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Syzygium verecundum is also significant in religious and cultural contexts in many Asian countries.
