Sida insperata

Sida insperata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical and subtropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sida insperata is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and its resistance to drought conditions.
