Scholtzia denticulata

Scholtzia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed scholtzia, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Scholtzia denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
