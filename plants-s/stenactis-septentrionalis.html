Stenactis septentrionalis

Stenactis septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern stenactis, is a species of seagrass found in temperate coastal regions.

It thrives in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates and is often found in association with other marine vegetation. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in underwater environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Its reproductive structures are typically found near the base of the plant, facilitating underwater pollination.
