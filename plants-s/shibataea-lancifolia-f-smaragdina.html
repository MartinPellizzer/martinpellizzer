Shibataea lancifolia f. smaragdina

Shibataea lancifolia f. smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald-leafed Japanese Maple, is a compact, ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in partial shade to full sun, depending on the region. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange fruits.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal color changes.
