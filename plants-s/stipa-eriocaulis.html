Stipa eriocaulis

Stipa eriocaulis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Stipa eriocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
