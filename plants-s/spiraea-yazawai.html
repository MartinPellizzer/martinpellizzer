Spiraea yazawai

Spiraea yazawai, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Spiraea yazawai thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and the way it maintains a compact form throughout the growing season.
