Senecio kukulensis

Senecio kukulensis, commonly known as the Kukul's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster, and it produces a distinctive, woolly foliage.

Senecio kukulensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
