Swertia perennis var. manshurica

Swertia perennis var. manshurica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring cool, moist environments. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
