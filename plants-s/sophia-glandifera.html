Sophia glandifera

Sophia glandifera, commonly known as the glandular sophia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
