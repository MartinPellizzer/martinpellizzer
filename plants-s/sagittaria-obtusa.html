Sagittaria obtusa

Sagittaria obtusa, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and floating, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, which emerge from the water surface.

Sagittaria obtusa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
