Stipagrostis damarensis

Stipagrostis damarensis, commonly known as the Damarensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the savannas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Stipagrostis damarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
