Symphoricarpos mollis

Symphoricarpos mollis, commonly known as silky cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks, and is often found in the eastern United States.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
