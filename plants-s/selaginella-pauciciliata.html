Selaginella pauciciliata

Selaginella pauciciliata, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with upright, thread-like leaves that are often spirally arranged. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain spores for reproduction.

Selaginella pauciciliata is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
