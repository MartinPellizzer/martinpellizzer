Sorghum asperum

Sorghum asperum, commonly known as rough sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, bristly leaves and sturdy, upright stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often cultivated for its edible grains.

Sorghum asperum is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
