Senna pendula var. ovalifolia

Senna pendula var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf senna, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
