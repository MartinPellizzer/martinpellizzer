Solanum stipitatostellatum

Solanum stipitatostellatum, commonly known as the star-stem nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its botanical features include hairy leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a notable species within its native habitat.
