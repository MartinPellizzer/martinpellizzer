Satureja carica

Satureja carica, commonly known as caraway thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, thyme-like aroma.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
