Senecio squalidus subsp. aethnensis

Senecio squalidus subsp.

aethnensis, commonly known as the Aetna ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
