Sida malviflora

Sida malviflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is known for its mucilaginous properties in its seeds. Sida malviflora is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
