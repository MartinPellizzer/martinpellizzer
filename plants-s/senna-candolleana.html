Senna candolleana

Senna candolleana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

Senna candolleana is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally to treat various ailments.
