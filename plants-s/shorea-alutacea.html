Shorea alutacea

Shorea alutacea, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a pale to reddish-brown color. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Shorea alutacea is valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
