Syzygium densinervium var. insulare

Syzygium densinervium var. insulare, commonly known as the Moluccan apple or insular syzygium, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
