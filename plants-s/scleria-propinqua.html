Scleria propinqua

Scleria propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize wetland habitats.

Scleria propinqua plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
