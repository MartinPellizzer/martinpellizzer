Saxifraga × arguellesii

Saxifraga × arguellesii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga moschata, showcasing a mix of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
