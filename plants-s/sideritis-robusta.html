Sideritis robusta

Sideritis robusta, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features upright, branched stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are aromatic, with a strong mint-like scent, and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Sideritis robusta is valued for its high content of essential oils and flavonoids, which contribute to its health benefits.
