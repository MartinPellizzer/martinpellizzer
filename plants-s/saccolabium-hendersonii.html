Saccolabium hendersonii

Saccolabium hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from lower soil layers. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.

Due to its unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
