Senecio grisebachii var. leptotus

Senecio grisebachii var. leptotus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
