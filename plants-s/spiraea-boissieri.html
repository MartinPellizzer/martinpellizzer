Spiraea boissieri

Spiraea boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Spiraea boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
