Selbya montana

Selbya montana, commonly known as the mountain selbya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Selbya montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized gardens and greenhouses.
