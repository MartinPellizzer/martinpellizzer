Stipa pennata subsp. mediterranea

Stipa pennata subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological benefits and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
