Saussurea riederi f. yuparensis

Saussurea riederi f. yuparensis, commonly known as the Yuparensis saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming a composite head that resembles a saffron-like hue.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
