Sorbus aucuparia f. dirkeni

Sorbus aucuparia f. dirkeni, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and thorny branches. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early spring.

This form is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
