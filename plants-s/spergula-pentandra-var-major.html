Spergula pentandra var. major

Spergula pentandra var. major, commonly known as greater white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.
