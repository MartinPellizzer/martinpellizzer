Sabazia macdonaldii

Sabazia macdonaldii, commonly known as the MacDonald's sabazia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in coastal areas. It produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sabazia macdonaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual charm.
