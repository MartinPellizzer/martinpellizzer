Silene sericata

Silene sericata, commonly known as the silverleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its scientific name reflects its silky appearance, with "sericata" meaning silk-like in Latin.

Silene sericata is an annual or biennial plant that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
