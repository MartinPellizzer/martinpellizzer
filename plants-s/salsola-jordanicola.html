Salsola jordanicola

Salsola jordanicola, commonly known as Jordanian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowering stems produce clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

Salsola jordanicola is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
