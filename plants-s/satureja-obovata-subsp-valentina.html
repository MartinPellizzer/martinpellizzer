Satureja obovata subsp. valentina

Satureja obovata subsp.

valentina, commonly known as Valentina savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lanceolate leaves with a pungent, slightly minty scent.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or herb beds.

Its culinary uses include seasoning dishes and making herbal infusions.
