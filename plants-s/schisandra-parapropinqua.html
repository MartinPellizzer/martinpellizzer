Schisandra parapropinqua

Schisandra parapropinqua, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, red, berry-like fruits that change color as they ripen. The plant is notable for its aromatic, five-flavored berries, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Schisandra parapropinqua is valued for its potential medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
