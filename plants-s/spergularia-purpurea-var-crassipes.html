Spergularia purpurea var. crassipes

Spergularia purpurea var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions. Its growth habit is prostrate, with stems that can spread along the ground.

It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is a common species in coastal ecosystems.
