Salvia ottoschulzii var. neurocalyx

Salvia ottoschulzii var. neurocalyx, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and sturdy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.
