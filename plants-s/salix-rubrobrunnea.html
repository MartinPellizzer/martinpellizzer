Salix rubrobrunnea

Salix rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-brown willow, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
