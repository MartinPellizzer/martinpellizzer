Stemodia caerulea

Stemodia caerulea, commonly known as blue stemodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Stemodia caerulea is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.
