Silene ichnusae

Silene ichnusae, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Silene ichnusae is an herbaceous perennial with slender stems and narrow leaves.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its adaptation to harsh mountain climates.
