Setaria auriculata

Setaria auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils, it is often found in riparian zones and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
