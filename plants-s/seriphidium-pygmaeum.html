Seriphidium pygmaeum

Seriphidium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with finely divided, feathery leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Seriphidium pygmaeum is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
