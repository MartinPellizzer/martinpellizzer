Salvia humilis

Salvia humilis, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Salvia humilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
