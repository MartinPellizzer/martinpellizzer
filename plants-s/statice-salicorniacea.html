Statice salicorniacea

Statice salicorniacea, commonly known as sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, thriving in saline and sandy soils. The plant produces dense clusters of pink or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Statice salicorniacea is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and hardy growth habit.
