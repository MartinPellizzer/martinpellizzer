Serratula runcinata

Serratula runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has serrated, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Serratula runcinata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
