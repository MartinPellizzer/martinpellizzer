Saxifraga delavayi

Saxifraga delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Saxifraga delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
