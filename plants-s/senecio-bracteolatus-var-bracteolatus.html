Senecio bracteolatus var. bracteolatus

Senecio bracteolatus var. bracteolatus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
