Scabiosa anatolica

Scabiosa anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian scabious, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive central disc and surrounding petals that resemble a star. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
