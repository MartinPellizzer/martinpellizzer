Styrax officinalis subsp. californicus

Styrax officinalis subsp.

californicus, commonly known as California styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are glossy and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
