Smilax elegans subsp. subrecta

Smilax elegans subsp.

subrecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and its pinnately compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
