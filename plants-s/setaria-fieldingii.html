Setaria fieldingii

Setaria fieldingii, commonly known as Fielding's foxtail, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a dense, bristly panicle, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
