Saxifraga moschata var. laxa

Saxifraga moschata var. laxa, commonly known as the sweet saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
