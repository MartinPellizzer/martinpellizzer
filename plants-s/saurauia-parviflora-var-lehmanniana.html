Saurauia parviflora var. lehmanniana

Saurauia parviflora var. lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's small-flowered saurauia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
