Salvia blancoana subsp. hegelmaieri

Salvia blancoana subsp.

hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
