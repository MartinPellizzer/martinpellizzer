Schefflera vantsilana var. litoralis

Schefflera vantsilana var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its adaptability to coastal conditions, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
