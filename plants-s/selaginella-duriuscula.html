Selaginella duriuscula

Selaginella duriuscula, commonly known as the hard-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing mats of foliage with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces strobila (spore cones) that are typically borne on short, erect stalks. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in southern Africa, and is adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Selaginella duriuscula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native environment.
