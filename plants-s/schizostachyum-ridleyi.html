Schizostachyum ridleyi

Schizostachyum ridleyi, commonly known as the Philippine rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that are used for making furniture and handicrafts. The plant has feather-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Schizostachyum ridleyi thrives in tropical rainforests and requires high humidity and well-drained soil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
