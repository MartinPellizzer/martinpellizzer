Sorghum halepense f. muticum

Sorghum halepense f. muticum, commonly known as nodding sourgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which allows it to thrive in disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that nod towards the ground, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its adaptability and can become invasive in certain environments.
