Salix gracilistyla var. adscendens

Salix gracilistyla var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
