Stizolobium coriaceum

Stizolobium coriaceum, commonly known as the African bristlepod, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, papery leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Stizolobium coriaceum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
