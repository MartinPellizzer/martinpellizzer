Saxifraga pseudoajanica

Saxifraga pseudoajanica, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, high-altitude conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, and its leaves are often succulent and fleshy. It is a perennial species that produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a low-growing habit.

Saxifraga pseudoajanica is well adapted to harsh climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
