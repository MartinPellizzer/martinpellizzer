Senecio polyphlebius

Senecio polyphlebius, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Senecio polyphlebius is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.
