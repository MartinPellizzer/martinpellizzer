Spiraea ulmaria f. tomentosa

Spiraea ulmaria f. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy silver weigela, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage.

It features small, clustered pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is native to Europe and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. Its hairy leaves, which give it the 'tomentosa' designation, contribute to its unique appearance and texture.

It is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
