Scleria robinsoniana

Scleria robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can be beneficial for soil stabilization.

Scleria robinsoniana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ecological value.
