Sonchus × prudhommei

Sonchus × prudhommei, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower weed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of tubular florets, which are adapted for insect pollination. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its stems are erect and branching.

Sonchus × prudhommei is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
