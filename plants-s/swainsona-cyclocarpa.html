Swainsona cyclocarpa

Swainsona cyclocarpa, commonly known as the cyclocarpa swainsona, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Swainsona cyclocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
