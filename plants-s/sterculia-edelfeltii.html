Sterculia edelfeltii

Sterculia edelfeltii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers that attract pollinators. The tree bears woody pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used for construction and medicinal purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
