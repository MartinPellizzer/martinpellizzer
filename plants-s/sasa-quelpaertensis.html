Sasa quelpaertensis

Sasa quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju bamboo, is a flowering plant species native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly Jeju Island.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the base of the culms. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish-green hue.

Sasa quelpaertensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
