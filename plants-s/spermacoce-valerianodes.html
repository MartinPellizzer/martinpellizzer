Spermacoce valerianodes

Spermacoce valerianodes, commonly known as the velvet bean or false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a branching stem, which help it thrive in diverse soil conditions.

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
