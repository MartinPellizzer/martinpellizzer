Stapelia × mirabilis

Stapelia × mirabilis, commonly known as the star-shaped corpse flower, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick stems and striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms that resemble decaying flesh, a feature evolved to attract pollinators like flies. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange with prominent purple veins, and they emit a strong, unpleasant odor.

Stapelia × mirabilis is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
