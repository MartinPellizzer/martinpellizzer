Strobilanthes pushpagiriensis

Strobilanthes pushpagiriensis, commonly known as the sacred flame or Pushpagiri flame, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that resemble flames, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, featuring showy bracts that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
