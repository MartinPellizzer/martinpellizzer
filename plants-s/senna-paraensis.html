Senna paraensis

Senna paraensis, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically composed of five to seven leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming during the warmer months. Senna paraensis is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It also serves as an important source of nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
