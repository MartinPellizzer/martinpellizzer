Solanum thomasiifolium var. calycinum

Solanum thomasiifolium var. calycinum, commonly known as the California nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant bears smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
