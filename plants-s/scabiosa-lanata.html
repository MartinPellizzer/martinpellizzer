Scabiosa lanata

Scabiosa lanata, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white or pink flower heads that resemble small clouds, giving it a whimsical appearance. The plant features hairy, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive look. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Scabiosa lanata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
