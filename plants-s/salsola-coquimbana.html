Salsola coquimbana

Salsola coquimbana, commonly known as the Atacama saltbush, is a halophyte shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Salsola coquimbana plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
