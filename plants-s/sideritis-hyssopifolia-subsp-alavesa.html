Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. alavesa

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

alavesa, commonly known as alpine lady's thimble, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
