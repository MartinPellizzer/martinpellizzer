Salix lasiandra var. caudata

Salix lasiandra var. caudata, commonly known as the long-haired willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, especially along the veins. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
