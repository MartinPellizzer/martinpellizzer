Senna arborescens

Senna arborescens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling cascading showers of light. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with bacteria in its root nodules. Senna arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
