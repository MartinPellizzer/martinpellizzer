Schefflera rudolfi

Schefflera rudolfi, commonly known as the Rudolf's umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Schefflera rudolfi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
