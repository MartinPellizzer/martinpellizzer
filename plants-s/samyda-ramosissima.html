Samyda ramosissima

Samyda ramosissima, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching trunk and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Samyda ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
