Spatholobus philippinensis

Spatholobus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its attractive floral display.
