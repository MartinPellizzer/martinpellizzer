Spergula salina var. sperguloides

Spergula salina var. sperguloides, commonly known as the saltmeadow cordgrass, is a variant of the saltmeadow grass found in coastal areas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in saline environments, such as tidal flats and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding.

Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
