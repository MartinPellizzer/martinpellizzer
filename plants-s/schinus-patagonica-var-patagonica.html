Schinus patagonica var. patagonica

Schinus patagonica var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, red, berry-like fruits. The plant produces toxic compounds, including urushiol, which can cause skin irritation in humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
