Solanum dulcamara var. riparium

Solanum dulcamara var. riparium, commonly known as water bindweed, is a perennial climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that mature to a deep red color, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows.

Its vigorous growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an invasive species in many regions.
