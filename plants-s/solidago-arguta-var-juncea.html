Solidago arguta var. juncea

Solidago arguta var. juncea, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open habitats such as marshes and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
