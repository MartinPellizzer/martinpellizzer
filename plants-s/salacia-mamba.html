Salacia mamba

Salacia mamba, commonly known as the snake-root plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its roots are notable for their medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring caution in its use.
