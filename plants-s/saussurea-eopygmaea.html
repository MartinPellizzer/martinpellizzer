Saussurea eopygmaea

Saussurea eopygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf saffron.

It is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellowish flower heads that resemble those of saffron. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry conditions.

Its botanical features include fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves.
