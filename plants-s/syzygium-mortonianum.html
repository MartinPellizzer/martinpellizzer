Syzygium mortonianum

Syzygium mortonianum, commonly known as the Mortons' apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar in appearance to apples.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
