Solanum multirameum

Solanum multirameum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, usually green or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, containing toxic compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided for cultivation and consumption.
