Sanguisorba minor subvar. glaucescens

Sanguisorba minor subvar. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, bluish-gray waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
