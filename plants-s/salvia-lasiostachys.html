Salvia lasiostachys

Salvia lasiostachys, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens. It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its adaptability and ornamental qualities make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
