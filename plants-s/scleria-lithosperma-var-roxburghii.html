Scleria lithosperma var. roxburghii

Scleria lithosperma var. roxburghii, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers, which are followed by distinctive, hard, woody capsules containing its seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and succulent leaves.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
