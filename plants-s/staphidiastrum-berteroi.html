Staphidiastrum berteroi

Staphidiastrum berteroi, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Staphidiastrum berteroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
