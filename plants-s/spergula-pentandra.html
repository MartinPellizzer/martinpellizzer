Spergula pentandra

Spergula pentandra, commonly known as five-angled grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils.

Spergula pentandra is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows and along riverbanks.
