Salix × cremsensis

Salix × cremsensis, commonly known as the Weeping Willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salix alba and Salix triandra, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. Its leaves are typically oval and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the autumn.

It thrives in moist soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
