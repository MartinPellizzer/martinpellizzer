Scleria patula

Scleria patula, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Scleria patula has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
