Stenocalyx involucratus

Stenocalyx involucratus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five white or pale pink petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a woolly appearance, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Stenocalyx involucratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
