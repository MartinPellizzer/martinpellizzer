Sarcanthus filiformis

Sarcanthus filiformis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sarcanthus filiformis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
