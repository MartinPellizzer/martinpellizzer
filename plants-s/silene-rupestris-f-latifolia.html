Silene rupestris f. latifolia

Silene rupestris f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rockcatcher, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The foliage is broad and lance-shaped, adapting to harsh, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky habitats.
