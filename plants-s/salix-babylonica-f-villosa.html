Salix babylonica f. villosa

Salix babylonica f. villosa, commonly known as the silky willow, is a cultivar of the weeping willow that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters typical of willow species. Its branches hang in cascading patterns, creating a graceful, flowing silhouette.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
