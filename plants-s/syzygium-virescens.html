Syzygium virescens

Syzygium virescens, commonly known as the green apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, greenish-yellow fruits that resemble small apples. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
