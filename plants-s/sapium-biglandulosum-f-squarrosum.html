Sapium biglandulosum f. squarrosum

Sapium biglandulosum f. squarrosum, commonly known as the silky senna, is a variant of the senna plant characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, silky-haired leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and clustered in racemes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The silky senna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
