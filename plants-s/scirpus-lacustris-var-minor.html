Scirpus lacustris var. minor

Scirpus lacustris var. minor, commonly known as the lesser marsh club-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and lake margins. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike-like structure. It plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Scirpus lacustris var. minor is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.
