Sesleria sphaerocephala var. pumila

Sesleria sphaerocephala var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf woolly oat, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-like clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces dense, spherical flower heads that are typically white or pale blue, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
