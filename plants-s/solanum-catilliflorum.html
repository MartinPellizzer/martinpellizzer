Solanum catilliflorum

Solanum catilliflorum, commonly known as the catkin-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall.

Solanum catilliflorum is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be a common weed in disturbed soils.
