Swartzia macrostachya var. riedelii

Swartzia macrostachya var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's Swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, elongated seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
