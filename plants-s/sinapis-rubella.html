Sinapis rubella

Sinapis rubella, commonly known as the red mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are short, pod-like silique that contain several seeds.

Sinapis rubella is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
