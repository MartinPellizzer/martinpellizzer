Sonerila picta var. maculata

Sonerila picta var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often feature distinctive markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, while its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
