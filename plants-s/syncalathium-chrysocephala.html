Syncalathium chrysocephala

Syncalathium chrysocephala, commonly known as the golden-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
