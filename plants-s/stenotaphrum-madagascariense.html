Stenotaphrum madagascariense

Stenotaphrum madagascariense, commonly known as Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense growth habit, making it well-suited for erosion control and ornamental use. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and form thick mats.

Stenotaphrum madagascariense is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
