Stachytarpheta glabra var. latifolia

Stachytarpheta glabra var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf blue mistflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to butterfly gardens and native plant landscapes.
