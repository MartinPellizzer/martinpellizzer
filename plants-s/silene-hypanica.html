Silene hypanica

Silene hypanica, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs. Silene hypanica has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
