Symplocos subolivacea

Symplocos subolivacea, commonly known as the oliveleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Symplocos subolivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
