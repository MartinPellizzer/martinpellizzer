Sorbus aria subsp. buekkensis

Sorbus aria subsp.

buekkensis, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries.

The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin, and they turn a striking orange-red in the fall. This plant is typically found in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
