Schefflera kuchingensis

Schefflera kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Borneo.

It features a distinctive crown of large, palmate leaves that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
