Saussurea porcellanea

Saussurea porcellanea, commonly known as the porcelain saffron, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
