Sedum dasyphyllum

Sedum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the dense-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water, which helps the plant survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, making it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
