Stemotis roseum

Stemotis roseum, commonly known as the pink-stem rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive pink-colored stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a subtle, sweet fragrance.
