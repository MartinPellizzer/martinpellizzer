Spiesia pilosa

Spiesia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and hairy foliage, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Spiesia pilosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
