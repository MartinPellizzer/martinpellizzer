Suttonia dentata

Suttonia dentata, commonly known as the toothed suttonia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring.

Suttonia dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
