Solanum pyrifolium

Solanum pyrifolium, commonly known as the flame apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped yellow-orange fruits that resemble small flames. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and features simple, alternate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals, and it produces berries that are edible when ripe.

Solanum pyrifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
