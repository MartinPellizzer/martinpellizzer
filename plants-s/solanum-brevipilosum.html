Solanum brevipilosum

Solanum brevipilosum, commonly known as the short-hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
