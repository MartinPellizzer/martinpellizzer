Senecio aquaticus subsp. calvescens

Senecio aquaticus subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as water groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
