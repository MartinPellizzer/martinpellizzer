Spartium radiatum

Spartium radiatum, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
