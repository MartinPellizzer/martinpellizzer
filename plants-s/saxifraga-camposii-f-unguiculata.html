Saxifraga camposii f. unguiculata

Saxifraga camposii f. unguiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple edge, and it produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
