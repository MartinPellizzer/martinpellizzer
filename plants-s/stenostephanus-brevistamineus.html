Stenostephanus brevistamineus

Stenostephanus brevistamineus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
