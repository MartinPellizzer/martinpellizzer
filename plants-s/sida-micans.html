Sida micans

Sida micans, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Sida micans is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
