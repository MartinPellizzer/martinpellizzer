Stylosanthes pilosa

Stylosanthes pilosa, commonly known as hairy stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value.

Stylosanthes pilosa also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
