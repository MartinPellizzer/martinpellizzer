Spathoglottis wrayi

Spathoglottis wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a short rhizome with a cluster of broad, fleshy leaves that help store water in its arid habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Spathoglottis wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
