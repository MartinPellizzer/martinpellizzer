Salvia herrerae

Salvia herrerae, commonly known as Herrera's sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Salvia herrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
