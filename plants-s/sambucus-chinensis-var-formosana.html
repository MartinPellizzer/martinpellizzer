Sambucus chinensis var. formosana

Sambucus chinensis var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
