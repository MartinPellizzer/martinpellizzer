Seguieria parvifolia

Seguieria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Seguieria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

Seguieria parvifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in native gardens.
