Stephanomeria cinerea

Stephanomeria cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stephanomeria cinerea is also known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
