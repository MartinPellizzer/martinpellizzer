Spergula campestris

Spergula campestris, commonly known as field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and roadside areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Spergula campestris is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
