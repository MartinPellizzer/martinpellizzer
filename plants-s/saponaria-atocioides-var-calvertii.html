Saponaria atocioides var. calvertii

Saponaria atocioides var. calvertii, commonly known as the Calvert's soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
