Selaginella rupestris var. shakotanensis

It is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and exhibits a distinctive morphology with small, spirally arranged leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its spores are produced in specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.
