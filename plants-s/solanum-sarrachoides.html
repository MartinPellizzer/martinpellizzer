Solanum sarrachoides

Solanum sarrachoides, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

While it has some ornamental value, it is also considered a weed in certain agricultural settings.
