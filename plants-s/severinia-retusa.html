Severinia retusa

Severinia retusa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Severinia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
