Syntherisma humifusa

Syntherisma humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or lesser groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are composed of ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.
