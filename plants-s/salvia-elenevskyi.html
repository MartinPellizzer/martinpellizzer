Salvia elenevskyi

Salvia elenevskyi, commonly known as the Elenovsky sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia elenevskyi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
