Sevada schimperi

Sevada schimperi, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its fibrous roots and mucilaginous leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
