Spennera hygrophila

Spennera hygrophila, commonly known as the water spennera, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in humid forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

Spennera hygrophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
