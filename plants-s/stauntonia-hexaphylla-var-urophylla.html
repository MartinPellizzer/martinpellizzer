Stauntonia hexaphylla var. urophylla

Stauntonia hexaphylla var. urophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The variety urophylla is distinguished by its unique floral structure and coloration compared to the typical form of Stauntonia hexaphylla.
