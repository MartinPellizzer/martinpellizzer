Sideranthus junceus

Sideranthus junceus, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Sideranthus junceus is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
