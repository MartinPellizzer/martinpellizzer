Sasa iwakiensis

Sasa iwakiensis, commonly known as the Iwaki bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue that adds aesthetic value.

Sasa iwakiensis is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
