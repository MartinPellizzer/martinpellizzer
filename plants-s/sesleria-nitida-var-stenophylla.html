Sesleria nitida var. stenophylla

Sesleria nitida var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
