Seringia

Seringia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features striking, spiky flower heads that are often blue or purple in color. The plants are typically herbaceous, with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Seringia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their unique appearance and drought tolerance.

They thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them suitable for coastal and rocky garden settings.
