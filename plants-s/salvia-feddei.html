Salvia feddei

Salvia feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's sage, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia feddei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
