Spartium interruptum

Spartium interruptum, commonly known as redstem filaree, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Spartium interruptum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
