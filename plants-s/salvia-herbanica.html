Salvia herbanica

Salvia herbanica, commonly known as the herbaceous sage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia herbanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
