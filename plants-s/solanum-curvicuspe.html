Solanum curvicuspe

Solanum curvicuspe, commonly known as the narrow-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum curvicuspe produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.
