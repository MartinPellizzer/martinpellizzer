Scutellaria parvula var. missouriensis

Scutellaria parvula var. missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods, prairies, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and clustered in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
