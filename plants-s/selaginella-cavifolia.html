Selaginella cavifolia

Selaginella cavifolia, commonly known as the crested spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are often curled or folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are borne on specialized branches called sporophylls.

Selaginella cavifolia is also noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its use in horticulture as an ornamental plant.
