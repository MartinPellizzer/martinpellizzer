Sinapis oliveriana

Sinapis oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring small, green, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are slender pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sinapis oliveriana is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
