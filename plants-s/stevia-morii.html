Stevia morii

Stevia morii, commonly known as the Japanese stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is cultivated for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides. Stevia morii is often used as a natural sweetener and is valued for its low calorie content and potential health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
