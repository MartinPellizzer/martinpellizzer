Salvia officinalis var. rubra

Salvia officinalis var. rubra, commonly known as red sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in summer, contrasting with its gray-green foliage. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.
