Stizolobium hirsutum

Stizolobium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy legume or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a leguminous species characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its hairy texture and robust growth make it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Stizolobium hirsutum also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
