Strobilanthes secunda

Strobilanthes secunda, commonly known as the "Lunar Plant," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a lunar phase, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Strobilanthes secunda is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional uses in local medicine.
