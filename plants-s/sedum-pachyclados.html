Sedum pachyclados

Sedum pachyclados, commonly known as the silver rose or stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
