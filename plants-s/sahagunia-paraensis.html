Sahagunia paraensis

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded understory areas of the forest.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's biodiversity.
