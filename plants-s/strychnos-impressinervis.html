Strychnos impressinervis

Strychnos impressinervis, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain bright red, edible seeds. Its leaves and roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
