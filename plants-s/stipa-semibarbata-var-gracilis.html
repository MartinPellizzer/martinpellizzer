Stipa semibarbata var. gracilis

Stipa semibarbata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender beardgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bristle-like awns, which are typical of the Stipa genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
