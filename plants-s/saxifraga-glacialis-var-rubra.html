Saxifraga glacialis var. rubra

Saxifraga glacialis var. rubra, commonly known as the red glacier saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features fleshy, often reddish leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pink, blooming in late summer.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts with its delicate floral display.
