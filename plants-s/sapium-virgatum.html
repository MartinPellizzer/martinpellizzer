Sapium virgatum

Sapium virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's tears or American sycamore, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
