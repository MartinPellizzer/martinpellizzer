Spiraea callosa var. bella

Spiraea callosa var. bella, commonly known as the showy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its profuse display of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rocky or well-drained soils.

Spiraea callosa var. bella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
