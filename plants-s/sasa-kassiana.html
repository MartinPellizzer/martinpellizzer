Sasa kassiana

Sasa kassiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture.

Sasa kassiana is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in its natural habitat.
