Spatholobus suberectus

Spatholobus suberectus, commonly known as the hairy wisteria, is a climbing legume native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, hairy stipules and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rainforest habitats.

Spatholobus suberectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
