Salix × fruticosa

Salix × fruticosa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks through its extensive root system.
