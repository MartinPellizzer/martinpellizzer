Salix alba var. denudata

Salix alba var. denudata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a variant of the white willow species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a silvery underside, contributing to its visual appeal. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
