Scleria veseyfitzgeraldii

Scleria veseyfitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's bulrush, is a species of sedge native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Scleria veseyfitzgeraldii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
