Shangwua

Shangwua, commonly known as the "Chinese flame tree," is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Shangwua thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and seasonal display make it a popular choice in urban landscaping.
