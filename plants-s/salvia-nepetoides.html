Salvia nepetoides

Salvia nepetoides, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly bitter flavor.

Salvia nepetoides is also known for its ability to repel certain pests, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
