Stipagrostis giessii

Stipagrostis giessii, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deep within the soil, making it highly drought-resistant.

Stipagrostis giessii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
