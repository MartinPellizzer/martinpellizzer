Senna cearensis

Senna cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Senna, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typical of the genus Senna, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Senna cearensis is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to tropical climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
