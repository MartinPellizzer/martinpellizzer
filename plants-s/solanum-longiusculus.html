Solanum longiusculus

Solanum longiusculus, commonly known as the long-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Solanum longiusculus is often found in humid, tropical environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
