Senna benitoensis

Senna benitoensis, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Senna benitoensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
