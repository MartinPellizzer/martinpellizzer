Silene dinarica

Silene dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene dinarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
