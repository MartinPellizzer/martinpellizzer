Sporobolus curtissii

Sporobolus curtissii, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a native grass species found in arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

Sporobolus curtissii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
