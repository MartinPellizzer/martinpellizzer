Syzygium richardianum

Syzygium richardianum, commonly known as the Richard's apple or the white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium richardianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in local cuisines.
