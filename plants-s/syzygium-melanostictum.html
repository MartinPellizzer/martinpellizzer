Syzygium melanostictum

Syzygium melanostictum, commonly known as the black-streaked tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland, and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that turns from green to brown as it matures, often featuring distinct black streaks. The plant is typically found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
