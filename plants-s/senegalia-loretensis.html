Senegalia loretensis

Senegalia loretensis, commonly known as Loretto acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
