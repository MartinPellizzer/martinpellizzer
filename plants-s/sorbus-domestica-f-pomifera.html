Sorbus domestica f. pomifera

Sorbus domestica f. pomifera, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
