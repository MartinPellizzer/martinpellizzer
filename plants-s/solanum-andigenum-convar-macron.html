Solanum andigenum convar. macron

Solanum andigenum convar. macron, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
