Sagittaria oblonga

Sagittaria oblonga, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water and wetlands, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with floating leaves that are broader and more rounded. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, which emerge from the water surface.

Sagittaria oblonga is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it a common sight in ponds and marshes.
