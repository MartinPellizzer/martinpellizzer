Sagina apetala var. melanopotamica

Sagina apetala var. melanopotamica, commonly known as black sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically dark green to black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland sandstone regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
