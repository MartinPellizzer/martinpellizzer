Sida spinosa var. kazmii

Sida spinosa var. kazmii, commonly known as Kazmian thornweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low water availability.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and its potential for use in phytoremediation.
