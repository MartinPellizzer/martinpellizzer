Solanum sanctae-crucis

Solanum sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the sacred crucifix plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
