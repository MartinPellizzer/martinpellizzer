Saussurea aegirophylla

Saussurea aegirophylla, commonly known as the golden safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow ray florets that resemble sunflowers. The plant produces small, disk-like flowers that are typically clustered in a single head. It thrives in cool, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
