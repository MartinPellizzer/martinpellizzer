Scrophularia stiriaca

Scrophularia stiriaca, commonly known as the creeping scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features erect, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
