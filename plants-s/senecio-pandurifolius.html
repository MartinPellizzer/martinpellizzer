Senecio pandurifolius

Senecio pandurifolius, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences.

Senecio pandurifolius is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
