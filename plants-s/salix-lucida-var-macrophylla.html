Salix lucida var. macrophylla

Salix lucida var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense colonies, making it adaptable to wet or moist soils.

This species is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
