Salvia leucochlamys

Salvia leucochlamys, commonly known as the white chalice sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, white to pale violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid conditions make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
