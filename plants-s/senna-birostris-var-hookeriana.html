Senna birostris var. hookeriana

Senna birostris var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's senna, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
