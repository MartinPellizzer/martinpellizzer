Stellaria nemorum lusus subebracteolata

Stellaria nemorum lusus subebracteolata, commonly known as the lesser starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect pollinators.
