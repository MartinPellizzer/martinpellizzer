Schizostachyum cordatum

Schizostachyum cordatum, commonly known as the slender bamboo or golden bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to golden flowers, which are typical of the grass family.

Schizostachyum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional basketry and construction in its native regions.
