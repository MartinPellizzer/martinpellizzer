Serraea zoeae

Serraea zoeae, commonly known as the Zoea's serrated sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Serraea zoeae thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

It is often found in dunes and along the shores of the Mediterranean Sea.
