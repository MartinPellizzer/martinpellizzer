Spergularia salina var. asiatica

Spergularia salina var. asiatica, commonly known as salt marsh star grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, tidal flats, and other salt-affected soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salinity. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Caryophyllaceae family, sharing similar morphological and ecological traits.
