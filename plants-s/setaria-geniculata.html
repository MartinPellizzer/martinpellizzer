Setaria geniculata

Setaria geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag or twisted appearance in its flowering stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Setaria geniculata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
