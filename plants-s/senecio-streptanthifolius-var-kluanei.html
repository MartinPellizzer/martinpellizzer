Senecio streptanthifolius var. kluanei

Senecio streptanthifolius var. kluanei, commonly known as Kluane's senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Yukon and Alaska.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
