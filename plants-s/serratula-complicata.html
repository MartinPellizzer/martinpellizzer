Serratula complicata

Serratula complicata, commonly known as the twisted fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Serratula complicata is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
