Sorbus × corymbiflora

Sorbus × corymbiflora, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The species is valued for its winter interest and ability to thrive in urban environments.
