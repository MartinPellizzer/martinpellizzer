Symphyotrichum patagonicum

Symphyotrichum patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum patagonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
