Stachys japonica f. villosa

Stachys japonica f. villosa, commonly known as Japanese hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
