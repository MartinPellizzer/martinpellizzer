Styrax faberi var. acutiserratus

Styrax faberi var. acutiserratus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed frangipani, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with acute or serrated margins, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The wood of this species is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and medicine.
