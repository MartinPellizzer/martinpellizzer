Sterculia paradoxa

Sterculia paradoxa, commonly known as the giant swamp bean, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
