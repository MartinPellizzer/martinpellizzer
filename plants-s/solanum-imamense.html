Solanum imamense

Solanum imamense, commonly known as the Imam's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate and pubescent, while its flowers are usually yellow or white with a distinctive five-lobed calyx.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a deep red color, often containing several seeds.
