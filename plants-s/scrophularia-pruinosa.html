Scrophularia pruinosa

Scrophularia pruinosa, commonly known as the purple figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive pruinose appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scrophularia pruinosa is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
