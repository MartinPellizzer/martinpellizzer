Sarothamnus baeticus var. tetuanensis

Sarothamnus baeticus var. tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and limestone areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
