Stenopetalum croceum

Stenopetalum croceum, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
