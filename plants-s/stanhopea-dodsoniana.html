Stanhopea dodsoniana

Stanhopea dodsoniana, commonly known as the Dodson's stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow with red and purple markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, often in late summer or early fall.

Stanhopea dodsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
