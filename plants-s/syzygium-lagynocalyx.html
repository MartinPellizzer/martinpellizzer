Syzygium lagynocalyx

Syzygium lagynocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its bark is thick and often covered in a smooth, dark layer, while its roots are extensive and strong.

Syzygium lagynocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and medicinal properties.
