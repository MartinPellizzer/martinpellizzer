Sideroxylon melanophloeos

Sideroxylon melanophloeos, commonly known as the black-bark ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood and dark, fissured bark that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles, and it produces small, oval berries.

The tree is valued for its strong, durable wood and is often used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
