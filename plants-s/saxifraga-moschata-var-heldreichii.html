Saxifraga moschata var. heldreichii

Saxifraga moschata var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
