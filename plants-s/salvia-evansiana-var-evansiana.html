Salvia evansiana var. evansiana

Salvia evansiana var. evansiana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and produces dense clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
