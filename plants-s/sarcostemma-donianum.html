Sarcostemma donianum

Sarcostemma donianum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Sarcostemma donianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
