Sasa laminata

Sasa laminata, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or sasa, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense mats, forming thick undergrowth in forested areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green, with a slightly glossy surface. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

Sasa laminata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
