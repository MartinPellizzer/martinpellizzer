Sisyrinchium vesiculatum

Sisyrinchium vesiculatum, commonly known as the bladderleaf bluecup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas. Its unique seed pods, which resemble small bladders, are a distinctive botanical feature.

Sisyrinchium vesiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
