Sophronitis grandiflora var. purpurea

Sophronitis grandiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
