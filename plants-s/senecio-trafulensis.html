Senecio trafulensis

Senecio trafulensis, commonly known as the Traful Mountain Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Senecio genus. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Senecio trafulensis plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
