Symplocos inconspicua

Symplocos inconspicua, commonly known as the slender waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Symplocos inconspicua is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
