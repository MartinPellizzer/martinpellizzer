Saxifraga pensylvanica var. forbesii

Saxifraga pensylvanica var. forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with toothed margins, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its ornamental value in rock gardens.
