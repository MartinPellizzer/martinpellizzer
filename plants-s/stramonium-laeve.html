Stramonium laeve

Stramonium laeve, commonly known as wild henbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy purple or blue flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, including scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which make it dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Stramonium laeve is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a hallucinogenic substance.
