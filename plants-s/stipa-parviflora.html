Stipa parviflora

Stipa parviflora, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems that often grow in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flower clusters that are borne on long, narrow spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Stipa parviflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
