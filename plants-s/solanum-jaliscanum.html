Solanum jaliscanum

Solanum jaliscanum, commonly known as the Jalisco nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a yellowish color.

Solanum jaliscanum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
