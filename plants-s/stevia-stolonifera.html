Stevia stolonifera

Stevia stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping or trailing stevia, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit, producing dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and functional plant in various horticultural applications.
