Silene linifolia var. sporadum

Silene linifolia var. sporadum, commonly known as the sporadic flax gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is known for its limited distribution and is considered a rare species within its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
