Salvia × ambigua

Salvia × ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along its square, hairy stems. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Salvia × ambigua is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
