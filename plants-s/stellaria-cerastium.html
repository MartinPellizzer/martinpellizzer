Stellaria cerastium

Stellaria cerastium, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual or biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along roadsides.

Stellaria cerastium is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seed dispersal and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
