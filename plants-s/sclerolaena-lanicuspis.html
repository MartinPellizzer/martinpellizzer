Sclerolaena lanicuspis

Sclerolaena lanicuspis, commonly known as the rough-leaved sclerolaena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sclerolaena lanicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
