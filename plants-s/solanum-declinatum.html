Solanum declinatum

Solanum declinatum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to yellowish-red as they mature, often containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
