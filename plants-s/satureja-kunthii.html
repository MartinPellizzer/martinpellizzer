Satureja kunthii

Satureja kunthii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja kunthii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Andean cultures.
