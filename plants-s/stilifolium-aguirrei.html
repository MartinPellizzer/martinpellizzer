Stilifolium aguirrei

Stilifolium aguirrei, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composite, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil.

Stilifolium aguirrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
