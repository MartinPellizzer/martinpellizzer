Sambucus ebulus var. humilis

Sambucus ebulus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf elderberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
