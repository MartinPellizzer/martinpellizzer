Sugerokia kawanoi

Sugerokia kawanoi, commonly known as the Kawano sugarcane, is a tropical grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is known for its high sugar content in its stems, making it valuable for commercial sugarcane production. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils.

Sugerokia kawanoi is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and resilience to various environmental conditions.
