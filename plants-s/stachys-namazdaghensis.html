Stachys namazdaghensis

Stachys namazdaghensis, commonly known as Namazdagh betony, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
