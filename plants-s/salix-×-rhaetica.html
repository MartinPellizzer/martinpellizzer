Salix × rhaetica

Salix × rhaetica, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a graceful and ornamental look. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Salix × rhaetica is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
