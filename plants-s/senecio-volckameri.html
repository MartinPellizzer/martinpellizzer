Senecio volckameri

Senecio volckameri, commonly known as the Volckamer's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
