Sonerila cannanorensis

Sonerila cannanorensis, commonly known as the Kollam sonerila, is a tropical shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sonerila cannanorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
