Solanum aethiopicum var. violaceum

Solanum aethiopicum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its deep purple to violet-colored fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers and has hairy, alternate leaves. The fruit, which is typically egg-shaped, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
