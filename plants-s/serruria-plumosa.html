Serruria plumosa

Serruria plumosa, commonly known as the plumose serruria, is a striking South African plant belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that gives it its common name, with narrow, finely serrated leaves that are often covered in a soft, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and conservation efforts in its native region.
