Sorbus keszthelyensis

Sorbus keszthelyensis, commonly known as the Keszthely whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic significance.
