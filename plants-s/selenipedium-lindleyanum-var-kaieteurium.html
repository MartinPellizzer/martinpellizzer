Selenipedium lindleyanum var. kaieteurium

Selenipedium lindleyanum var. kaieteurium, commonly known as the Kaieteur pineapple, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, with a central spadix surrounded by a crown of white bracts. Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Guyana and Venezuela, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
