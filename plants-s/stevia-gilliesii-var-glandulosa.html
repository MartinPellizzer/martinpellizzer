Stevia gilliesii var. glandulosa

Stevia gilliesii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the New Zealand stevia, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal applications.
