Solanum malacoxylon var. latifolium

Solanum malacoxylon var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
