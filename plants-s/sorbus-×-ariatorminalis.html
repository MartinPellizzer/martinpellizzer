Sorbus × ariatorminalis

Sorbus × ariatorminalis, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Sorbus aria (whitebeam) and Sorbus torminalis (common whitebeam), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species is notable for its ornamental value, featuring showy white or pale yellow flowers in spring and red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The plant is relatively hardy and can be found in various habitats across Europe.
