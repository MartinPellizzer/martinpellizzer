Serapias cordigera subvar. curvifolia

Serapias cordigera subvar. curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The leaves are basal, linear, and often curved, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes or rocky slopes.

The plant's unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
