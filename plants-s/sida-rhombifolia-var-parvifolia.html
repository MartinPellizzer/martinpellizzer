Sida rhombifolia var. parvifolia

Sida rhombifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

It is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
