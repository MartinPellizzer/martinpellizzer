Sporobolus floridanus

Sporobolus floridanus, commonly known as Florida spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of spikelets that give it a spiky appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sporobolus floridanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
