Saussurea saligna

Saussurea saligna, commonly known as the Himalayan yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Saussurea saligna is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
