Siphonacanthus villosus

Siphonacanthus villosus, commonly known as the woolly siphonacanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosus, appearance, which gives it a distinctive textured look. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling a brush. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Siphonacanthus villosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and humid woodlands.
