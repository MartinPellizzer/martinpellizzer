Sarcochilus hirsutus

Sarcochilus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It features a distinctive, hairy, helmet-like pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Sarcochilus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
