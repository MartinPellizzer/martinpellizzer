Symplocos phyllocalyx

Symplocos phyllocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Symplocos phyllocalyx is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
