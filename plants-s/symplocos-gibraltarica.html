Symplocos gibraltarica

Symplocos gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar stonecrop, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Symplocos gibraltarica is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
