Solanum lorentzii

Solanum lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
