Sigesbeckia brachiata

Sigesbeckia brachiata, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sigesbeckia brachiata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
