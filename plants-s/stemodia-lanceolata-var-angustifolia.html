Stemodia lanceolata var. angustifolia

Stemodia lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stemodia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem and have a slightly rough texture.

This species is often found in grassy areas and rocky soils, adapting well to arid conditions.
