Senna viarum

Senna viarum, commonly known as the golden senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential in treating ailments like fever and inflammation.

Senna viarum is also valued in traditional medicine for its laxative effects due to the presence of saponins and flavonoids.
