Saprosma pubescens var. hirsuta

Saprosma pubescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy saprosma, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
