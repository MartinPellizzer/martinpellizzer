Senna cardiosperma subsp. stowardii

Senna cardiosperma subsp.

stowardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Australia. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a feathery appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a warm climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
