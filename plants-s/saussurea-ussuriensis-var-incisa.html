Saussurea ussuriensis var. incisa

Saussurea ussuriensis var. incisa, commonly known as the Ussuri leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and often have serrated margins, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
