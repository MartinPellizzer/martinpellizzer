Scleranthus abyssinicus

Scleranthus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. Its seed pods are slender and dry, typical of the genus Scleranthus.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
