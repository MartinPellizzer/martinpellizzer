Scirpus monostachyos

Scirpus monostachyos, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers at the top of the stem, which are typically pale green to brown in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus monostachyos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
