Solanum knappiae

Solanum knappiae, commonly known as Knapp's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum knappiae is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
