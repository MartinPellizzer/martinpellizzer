Senecio austin-smithii

Senecio austin-smithii, commonly known as Austin-Smith's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
