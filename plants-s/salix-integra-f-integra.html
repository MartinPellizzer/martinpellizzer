Salix integra f. integra

Salix integra f. integra, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is native to Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green and emit a fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

While it is adaptable to various climates, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade.
