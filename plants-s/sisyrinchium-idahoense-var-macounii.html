Sisyrinchium idahoense var. macounii

Sisyrinchium idahoense var. macounii, commonly known as the Idaho blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in early spring.
