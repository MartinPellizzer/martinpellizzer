Semiarundinaria fastuosa var. viridis

Semiarundinaria fastuosa var. viridis, commonly known as the Emerald Bamboo, is a variety of the bamboo genus Semiarundinaria.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a vibrant green color, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, cylindrical stalks that grow in clusters, forming a compact and upright structure. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to create a lush, green visual effect in gardens and landscapes.
