Solanum restrictum

Solanum restrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring opposite, narrow, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its small, white or pale green flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum restrictum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
