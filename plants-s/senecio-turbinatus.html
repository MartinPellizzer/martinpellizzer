Senecio turbinatus

Senecio turbinatus, commonly known as the Turban Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Senecio turbinatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
