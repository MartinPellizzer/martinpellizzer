Syzygium zimmermannii

Syzygium zimmermannii, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but are typically less sweet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium zimmermannii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
