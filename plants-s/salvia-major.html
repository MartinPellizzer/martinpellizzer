Salvia major

Salvia major, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia major is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
