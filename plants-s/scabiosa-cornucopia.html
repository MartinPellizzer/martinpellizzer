Scabiosa cornucopia

Scabiosa cornucopia, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble a horn, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Scabiosa cornucopia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
