Scorzonera hirsuta

Scorzonera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Scorzonera hirsuta is also valued for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
