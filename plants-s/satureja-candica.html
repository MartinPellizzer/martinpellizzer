Satureja candica

Satureja candica, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic and slightly peppery flavor.
