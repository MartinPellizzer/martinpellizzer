Salpichlaena volubilis subsp. crenata

Salpichlaena volubilis subsp.

crenata, commonly known as the serrated climbing fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and finely divided, serrated fronds.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
