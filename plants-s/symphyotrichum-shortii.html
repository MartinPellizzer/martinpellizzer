Symphyotrichum shortii

Symphyotrichum shortii, commonly known as Short's New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features showy purple to pink ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which bloom in the late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Symphyotrichum shortii is valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
