Siphonacanthus repens var. major

Siphonacanthus repens var. major, commonly known as the creeping siphonacanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
