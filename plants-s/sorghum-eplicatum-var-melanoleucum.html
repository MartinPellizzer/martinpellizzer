Sorghum eplicatum var. melanoleucum

Sorghum eplicatum var. melanoleucum, commonly known as the black and white sorghum, is a lesser-known variant of the sorghum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive black and white striped leaves, which set it apart from other sorghum species. This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm climates with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include tall, sturdy stalks and small, clustered inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated for grain, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique appearance and potential adaptability.
