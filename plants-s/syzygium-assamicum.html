Syzygium assamicum

Syzygium assamicum, commonly known as the Assam apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red or yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Syzygium assamicum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
