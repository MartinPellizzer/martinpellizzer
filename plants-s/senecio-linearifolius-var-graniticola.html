Senecio linearifolius var. graniticola

Senecio linearifolius var. graniticola, commonly known as the granitic rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
