Salix heterochroma

Salix heterochroma, commonly known as the heterogeneous willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which exhibits varying colors and patterns, often ranging from green to yellow and brown. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
