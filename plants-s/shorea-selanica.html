Shorea selanica

Shorea selanica, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

Shorea selanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
