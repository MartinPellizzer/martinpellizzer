Stiradotheca trachyptera

Stiradotheca trachyptera, commonly known as the rough-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features include leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains one or two seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food for various wildlife species.
