Senecio subpeltatus

Senecio subpeltatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio subpeltatus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
