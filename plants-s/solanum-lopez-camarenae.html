Solanum lopez-camarenae

Solanum lopez-camarenae, commonly known as the Lopez-Camarena nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
