Sempervivum brevipetalum

Sempervivum brevipetalum, commonly known as the short-petalled houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees and surrounding regions.

It features a rosette of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are short and rounded, with a distinctive white bloom on the leaf tips. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Sempervivum brevipetalum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions.
