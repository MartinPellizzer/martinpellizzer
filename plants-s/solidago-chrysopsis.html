Solidago chrysopsis

Solidago chrysopsis, commonly known as the lesser goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Solidago chrysopsis is an important species for pollinators and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
