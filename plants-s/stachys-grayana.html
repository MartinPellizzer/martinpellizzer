Stachys grayana

Stachys grayana, commonly known as gray's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage.

Stachys grayana is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
