Stapelia paniculata subsp. kougabergensis

Stapelia paniculata subsp.

kougabergensis, commonly known as the Kougaberg Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and produces clusters of yellowish-green, waxy flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a distinctive addition to succulent collections.
