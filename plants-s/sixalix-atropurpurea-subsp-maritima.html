Sixalix atropurpurea subsp. maritima

Sixalix atropurpurea subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal elderberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a reddish or purple hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
