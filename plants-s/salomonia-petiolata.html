Salomonia petiolata

Salomonia petiolata, commonly known as the small-flowered salomonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has a herbaceous habit with hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Salomonia petiolata is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
