Syzygium sahyadricum

Syzygium sahyadricum, commonly known as the Western Ghats apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
