Saxifraga camposii f. longistamina

Saxifraga camposii f. longistamina, commonly known as the long-stamen saxifrage, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with long, slender stamens that give the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
