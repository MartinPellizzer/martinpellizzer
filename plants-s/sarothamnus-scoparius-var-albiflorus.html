Sarothamnus scoparius var. albiflorus

Sarothamnus scoparius var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower thyme shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its botanical features include small, glandular leaves and compact inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
