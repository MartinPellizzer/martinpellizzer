Savia phyllanthoides var. roemeriana

Savia phyllanthoides var. roemeriana, commonly known as the Roemer's spurge, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features succulent, ribbed stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
