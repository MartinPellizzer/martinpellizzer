Saracha contorta

Saracha contorta, commonly known as the twisted saracha, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Saracha contorta thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
