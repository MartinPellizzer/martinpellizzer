Salvia kudjurica

Salvia kudjurica, commonly known as the Desert Sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Salvia kudjurica is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
