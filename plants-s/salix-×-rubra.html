Salix × rubra

Salix × rubra, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.
