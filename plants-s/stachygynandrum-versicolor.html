Stachygynandrum versicolor

Stachygynandrum versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored inflorescence that displays a range of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are clustered in panicles.

Stachygynandrum versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique appearance.
