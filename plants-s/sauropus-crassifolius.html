Sauropus crassifolius

Sauropus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
