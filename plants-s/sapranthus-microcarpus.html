Sapranthus microcarpus

Sapranthus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
