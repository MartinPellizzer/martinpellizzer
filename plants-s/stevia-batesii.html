Stevia batesii

Stevia batesii, commonly known as the yellow stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is known for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia batesii is often used as a natural sweetener and in traditional herbal medicine.
