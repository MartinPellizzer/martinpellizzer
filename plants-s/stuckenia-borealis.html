Stuckenia borealis

Stuckenia borealis, commonly known as northern pondweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender stems that are often submerged, with small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing tiny, greenish-white blooms that are pollinated by wind.

Stuckenia borealis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
