Sarcochilus sillemianus

Sarcochilus sillemianus, commonly known as the Sillem's helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a raceme of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique helmet-shaped sepals and petals, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Sarcochilus sillemianus is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
