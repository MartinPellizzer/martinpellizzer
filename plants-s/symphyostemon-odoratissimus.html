Symphyostemon odoratissimus

Symphyostemon odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented symphyostemon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The flowers emit a strong, sweet aroma, which attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
