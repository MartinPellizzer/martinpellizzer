Stachys pilifera subsp. ixodes

Stachys pilifera subsp.

ixodes, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
