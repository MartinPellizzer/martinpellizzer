Sparganium limosum

Sparganium limosum, commonly known as bur reed, is a wetland plant characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, while its flowering spikes rise above the water surface. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads, which are often mistaken for seed pods.

Sparganium limosum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
