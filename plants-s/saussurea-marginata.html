Saussurea marginata

Saussurea marginata, commonly known as the Alpine margaritifera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
