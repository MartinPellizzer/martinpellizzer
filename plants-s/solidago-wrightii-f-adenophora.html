Solidago wrightii f. adenophora

Solidago wrightii f. adenophora, commonly known as the Wright's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting diverse wildlife.
