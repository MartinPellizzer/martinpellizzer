Statice dianiae

Statice dianiae, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to purple. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Statice dianiae is often used in ornamental gardening and is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
