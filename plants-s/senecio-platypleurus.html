Senecio platypleurus

Senecio platypleurus, commonly known as the broad-leaved senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring silver-gray leaves with a woolly texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruit type.

Senecio platypleurus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
