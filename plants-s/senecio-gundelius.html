Senecio gundelius

Senecio gundelius, commonly known as the Gondwana groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers in summer. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio gundelius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
