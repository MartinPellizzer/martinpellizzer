Sida fluviatilis

Sida fluviatilis, commonly known as river sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It features erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a pubescent surface.

Sida fluviatilis thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.
