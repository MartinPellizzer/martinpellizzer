Scrophularia pennellii

Scrophularia pennellii, commonly known as Pennell's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive blue or purple flower cluster. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia pennellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
