Seaforthia saxatilis

Seaforthia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock seaforthia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in coastal dunes and cliffs. It has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Seaforthia saxatilis is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
