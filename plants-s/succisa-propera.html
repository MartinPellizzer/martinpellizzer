Succisa propera

Succisa propera, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and narrow, linear leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Succisa propera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
