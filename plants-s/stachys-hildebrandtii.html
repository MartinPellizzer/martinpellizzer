Stachys hildebrandtii

Stachys hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
