Serratula pygmaea

Serratula pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sow thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Serratula pygmaea is sometimes used in herbal medicine, though its medicinal properties are not well-documented.
