Stenopadus megacephalus

Stenopadus megacephalus, commonly known as the giant pad fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant's fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, fan-like appearance that makes it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
