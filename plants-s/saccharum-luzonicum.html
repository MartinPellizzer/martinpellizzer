Saccharum luzonicum

Saccharum luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon sugarcane.

It is a species of grass native to the Philippines, particularly found on the island of Luzon. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, narrow leaves. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Saccharum luzonicum is notable for its potential use in biofuel production and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
