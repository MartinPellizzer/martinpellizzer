Sabatia concinna

Sabatia concinna, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as roadsides and dunes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Sabatia concinna is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
