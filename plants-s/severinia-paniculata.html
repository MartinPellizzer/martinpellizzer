Severinia paniculata

Severinia paniculata, commonly known as the white-flowered snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, with a smooth, glossy texture.

Severinia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
