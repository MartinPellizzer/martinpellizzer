Selaginella aristata var. brevifolia

Selaginella aristata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in rocky crevices or along forest floors in temperate regions.

Selaginella aristata var. brevifolia is known for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
