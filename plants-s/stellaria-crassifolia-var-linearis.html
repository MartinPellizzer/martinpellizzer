Stellaria crassifolia var. linearis

Stellaria crassifolia var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
