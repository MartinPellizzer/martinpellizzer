Semecarpus perrottetii var. glaber

Semecarpus perrottetii var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth sycamore, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is noted for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
