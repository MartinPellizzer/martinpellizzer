Solidago virgaurea subsp. gigantea

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as giant goldenrod, is a tall perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late season.
