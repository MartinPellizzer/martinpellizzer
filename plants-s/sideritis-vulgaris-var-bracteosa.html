Sideritis vulgaris var. bracteosa

Sideritis vulgaris var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
