Silene byzantina

Silene byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Silene byzantina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
