Salvia chicamochae

Salvia chicamochae, commonly known as the Peruvian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent when crushed.

Salvia chicamochae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in local herbal remedies.
