Sporobolus densiflorus

Sporobolus densiflorus, commonly known as denseflower sparrowgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen that helps reduce water loss.

Sporobolus densiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
