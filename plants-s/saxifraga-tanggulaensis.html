Saxifraga tanggulaensis

Saxifraga tanggulaensis, commonly known as the Tanggula saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and withstand harsh weather.

Saxifraga tanggulaensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environments.
