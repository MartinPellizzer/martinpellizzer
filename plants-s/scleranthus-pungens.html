Scleranthus pungens

Scleranthus pungens, commonly known as the Prickly Sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sharp, bristly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Scleranthus pungens is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
