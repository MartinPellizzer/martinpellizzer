Sporobolus subbulbosus

Sporobolus subbulbosus, commonly known as the bulboous spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across disturbed and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

Sporobolus subbulbosus is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
