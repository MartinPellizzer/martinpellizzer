Senecio sarmentosus

Senecio sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with numerous stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are usually gray-green, hairy, and narrow, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
