Saxifraga cordillerana

Saxifraga cordillerana, commonly known as the Sierra Valley saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga cordillerana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
