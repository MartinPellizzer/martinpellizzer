Sambucus buergeriana f. cordifoliata

Sambucus buergeriana f. cordifoliata, commonly known as the heart-leaved elderberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
