Sorbus scannelliana

Sorbus scannelliana, commonly known as the Scannell's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
