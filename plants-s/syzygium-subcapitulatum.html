Syzygium subcapitulatum

Syzygium subcapitulatum, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers followed by edible, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium subcapitulatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
