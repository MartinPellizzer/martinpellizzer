Schinus limonia

Schinus limonia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, bright red berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is also known for its fragrant flowers and its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
