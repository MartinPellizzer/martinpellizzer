Solidago sempervirens var. azorica

Solidago sempervirens var. azorica, commonly known as the Azorean goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
