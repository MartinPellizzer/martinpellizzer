Scirpus hystricoides

Scirpus hystricoides, commonly known as the spiny bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spiky inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Scirpus hystricoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
