Sorbus conradinae

Sorbus conradinae, commonly known as the Conrad's rowan, is a rare species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often found in xeriscaped gardens.
