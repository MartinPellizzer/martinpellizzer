Senecio neoascendens

Senecio neoascendens, commonly known as the ascending groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio neoascendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
