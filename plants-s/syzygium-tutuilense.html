Syzygium tutuilense

Syzygium tutuilense, commonly known as the Tutuilem apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the island of Tutuila in the Northern Mariana Islands. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine. Syzygium tutuilense is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers.

Due to its limited geographic range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
