Sasa paraelegans

Sasa paraelegans, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa paraelegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
