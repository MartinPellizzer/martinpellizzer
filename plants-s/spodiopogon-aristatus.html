Spodiopogon aristatus

Spodiopogon aristatus, commonly known as the swordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with long, bristly hairs.

Spodiopogon aristatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance lawns or groundcovers.
