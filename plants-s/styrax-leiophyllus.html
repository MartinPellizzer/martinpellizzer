Styrax leiophyllus

Styrax leiophyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, hard, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
