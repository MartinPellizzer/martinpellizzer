Satureja cuneifolia

Satureja cuneifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Satureja cuneifolia is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
