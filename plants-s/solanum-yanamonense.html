Solanum yanamonense

Solanum yanamonense, commonly known as Yanamon pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing alkaloid-rich seeds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it is often found growing in shaded, moist areas.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has attracted interest for research in pharmacology and ethnobotany.
