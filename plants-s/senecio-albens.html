Senecio albens

Senecio albens, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Senecio albens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
