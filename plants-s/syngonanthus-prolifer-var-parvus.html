Syngonanthus prolifer var. parvus

Syngonanthus prolifer var. parvus, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various coniferous trees.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and is typically found in temperate forest ecosystems. The plant produces small, white or pink berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It forms dense clusters on its host, sometimes leading to the death of the tree if the infestation is severe.

This species plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
