Satyrium albidum

Satyrium albidum, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with a distinctive yellow-green lip. Its inflorescence is usually a single flower, which is often borne on a slender stem.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
