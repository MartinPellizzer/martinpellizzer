Solanum nodiflorum var. puberulum

Solanum nodiflorum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. The fruit is a small, green berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
