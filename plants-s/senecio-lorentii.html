Senecio lorentii

Senecio lorentii, commonly known as the Lorentz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The bright yellow flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
