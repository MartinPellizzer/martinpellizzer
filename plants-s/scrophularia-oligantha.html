Scrophularia oligantha

Scrophularia oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological preferences.

Scrophularia oligantha is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
