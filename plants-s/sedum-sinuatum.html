Sedum sinuatum

Sedum sinuatum, commonly known as pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
