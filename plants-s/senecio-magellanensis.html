Senecio magellanensis

Senecio magellanensis, commonly known as the Magellanic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Magellanic archipelago.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio magellanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
