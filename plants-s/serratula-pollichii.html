Serratula pollichii

Serratula pollichii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Serratula pollichii has dark green, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often found in meadows and on slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
