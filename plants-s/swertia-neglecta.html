Swertia neglecta

Swertia neglecta, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Swertia neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
