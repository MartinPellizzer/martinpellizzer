Senna pallida var. bahamensis

Senna pallida var. bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it has palmately lobed leaves. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
