Silene rupestris var. micrantha

Silene rupestris var. micrantha, commonly known as the rockpink or mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene rupestris var. micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
