Stellera chamaejasme f. chrysantha

Stellera chamaejasme f. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in the spring. The flowers are typically arranged in star-like patterns, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
