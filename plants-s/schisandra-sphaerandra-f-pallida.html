Schisandra sphaerandra f. pallida

Schisandra sphaerandra f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-fruited five-flavor vine, is a rare and unique botanical variant of the Schisandra genus.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white berries, which contrast with the typical red or orange fruits of the species. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Korea. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical interest lies in its distinct coloration and its potential medicinal properties, which have been studied for their traditional uses in herbal medicine.
