Senecio cachinalensis var. cachinalensis

Senecio cachinalensis var. cachinalensis, commonly known as the Cachinalensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
