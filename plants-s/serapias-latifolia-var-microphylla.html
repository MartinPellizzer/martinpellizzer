Serapias latifolia var. microphylla

Serapias latifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often brightly colored flowers that resemble orchids. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in rocky or limestone soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
