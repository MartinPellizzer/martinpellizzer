Syzygium nigricans subsp. nigricans

Syzygium nigricans subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the black satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
