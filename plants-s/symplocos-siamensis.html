Symplocos siamensis

Symplocos siamensis, commonly known as Siam elder or Siam wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Symplocos siamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
