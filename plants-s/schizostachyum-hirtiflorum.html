Schizostachyum hirtiflorum

Schizostachyum hirtiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, hairy flowers. The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and can grow up to several meters in height. Its unique floral structure and growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
