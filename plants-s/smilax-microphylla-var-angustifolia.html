Smilax microphylla var. angustifolia

Smilax microphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf catclaw, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems with thorn-like projections, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in hammocks and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland habitats.
