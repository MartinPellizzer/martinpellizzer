Salix × pedunculata

Salix × pedunculata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping appearance, giving it a graceful and ornamental look. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green color during the growing season. It produces small catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of reproduction.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along water bodies.
