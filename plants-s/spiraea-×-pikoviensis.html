Spiraea × pikoviensis

Spiraea × pikoviensis, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, arching branches that give it a graceful appearance. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing an attractive backdrop to the floral display.

Spiraea × pikoviensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
