Sarcopodium citrinocastaneum

Sarcopodium citrinocastaneum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves that store water. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
