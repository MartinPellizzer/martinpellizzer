Saussurea riederi

Saussurea riederi, commonly known as the Rieder's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, yellowish to orange-colored flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a single head, giving it a saffron-like appearance. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found growing at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
