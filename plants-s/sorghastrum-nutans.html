Sorghastrum nutans

Sorghastrum nutans, commonly known as browntop grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive brown, tufted seed heads that droop at the ends, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage crop.
