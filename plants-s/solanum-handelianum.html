Solanum handelianum

Solanum handelianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are typically red when ripe. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
