Secale kuprijanovii

Secale kuprijanovii, commonly known as the Kuprijanov's rye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for studying grass species adaptation and evolution.
