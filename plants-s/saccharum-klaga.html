Saccharum klaga

Saccharum klaga, commonly known as the Klagah sugarcane, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense foliage. The plant is known for its high sugar content, making it a valuable resource for sugar production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and fertile soil.

Due to its adaptability, Saccharum klaga is often cultivated for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
