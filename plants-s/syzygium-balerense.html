Syzygium balerense

Syzygium balerense, commonly known as the Baler tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, elongated fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, sticky pulp. Syzygium balerense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional food.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
