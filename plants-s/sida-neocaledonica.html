Sida neocaledonica

Sida neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia sida, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough, hairy texture.

Sida neocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
