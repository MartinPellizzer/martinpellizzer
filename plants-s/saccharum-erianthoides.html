Saccharum erianthoides

Saccharum erianthoides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid, subtropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Saccharum erianthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
