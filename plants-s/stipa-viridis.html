Stipa viridis

Stipa viridis, commonly known as green needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, green blades and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
