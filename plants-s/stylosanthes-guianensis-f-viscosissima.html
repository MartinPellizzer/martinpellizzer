Stylosanthes guianensis f. viscosissima

Stylosanthes guianensis f. viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value.

The variety f. viscosissima is particularly noted for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
