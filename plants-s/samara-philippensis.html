Samara philippensis

Samara philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine samara, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive samaras, which are winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
