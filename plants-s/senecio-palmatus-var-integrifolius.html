Senecio palmatus var. integrifolius

Senecio palmatus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have smooth margins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
