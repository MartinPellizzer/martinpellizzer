Sebastiania laureola

Sebastiania laureola, commonly known as the golden star, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist woodlands. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
