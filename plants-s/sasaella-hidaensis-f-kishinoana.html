Sasaella hidaensis f. kishinoana

Sasaella hidaensis f. kishinoana, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a unique variant of the Sasaella hidaensis species.

It is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo that exhibits a distinctive growth habit and leaf morphology. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a silvery or pale green underside. Native to Japan, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
