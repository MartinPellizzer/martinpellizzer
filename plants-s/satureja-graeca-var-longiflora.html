Satureja graeca var. longiflora

Satureja graeca var. longiflora, commonly known as Greek thyme or long-flowered savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves and elongated, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-like scent. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
