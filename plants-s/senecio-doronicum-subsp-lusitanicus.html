Senecio doronicum subsp. lusitanicus

Senecio doronicum subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
