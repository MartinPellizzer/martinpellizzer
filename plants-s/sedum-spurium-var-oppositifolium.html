Sedum spurium var. oppositifolium

Sedum spurium var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
