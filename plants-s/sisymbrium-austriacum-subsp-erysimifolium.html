Sisymbrium austriacum subsp. erysimifolium

Sisymbrium austriacum subsp.

erysimifolium, commonly known as the Austrian broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The subspecies is characterized by its yellow flowers and elongated, thread-like stems. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have negative impacts on crop plants.
