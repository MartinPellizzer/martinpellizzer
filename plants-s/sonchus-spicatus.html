Sonchus spicatus

Sonchus spicatus, commonly known as spiked sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and alternate, broadly lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence, which is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many temperate regions.
