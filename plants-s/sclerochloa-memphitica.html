Sclerochloa memphitica

Sclerochloa memphitica, commonly known as the Memphis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Sclerochloa memphitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
