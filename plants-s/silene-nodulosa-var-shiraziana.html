Silene nodulosa var. shiraziana

Silene nodulosa var. shiraziana, commonly known as the Shiraz stonecrop, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate harsh climates make it a popular choice among horticulturists.

The variety is distinguished by its rounded leaf shape and dense inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental value.
