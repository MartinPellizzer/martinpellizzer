Statice reticulata

Statice reticulata, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, spiked flower heads in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its fibrous roots and salt-tolerant nature make it well adapted to harsh maritime environments.

Statice reticulata is often used in garden landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
