Scabiosa hymettia

Scabiosa hymettia, commonly known as the Hymettian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Hymettus mountain range in Greece.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa hymettia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
