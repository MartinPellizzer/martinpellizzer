Stevia macbridei

Stevia macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its sweetening properties.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern applications.
