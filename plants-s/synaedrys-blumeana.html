Synaedrys blumeana

Synaedrys blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a single, showy inflorescence that blooms during the wet season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Synaedrys blumeana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal.
