Sida eggersii

Sida eggersii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert plains. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are generally hairy and oval-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Sida eggersii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
