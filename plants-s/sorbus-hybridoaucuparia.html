Sorbus hybridoaucuparia

Sorbus hybridoaucuparia, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value, including its attractive bark and seasonal color changes.
