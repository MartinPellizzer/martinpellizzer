Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp. melidensis

Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp.

melidensis, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
