Sasa fukuchiyamensis

Sasa fukuchiyamensis, commonly known as the Fukuchiyama bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa fukuchiyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
