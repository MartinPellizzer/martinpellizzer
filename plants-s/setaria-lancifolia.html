Setaria lancifolia

Setaria lancifolia, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and wastelands. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
