Scabiosa graminifolia subvar. virescens

Scabiosa graminifolia subvar. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental value.
