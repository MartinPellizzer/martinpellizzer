Solanum nitidibaccatum f. integrifolium

Solanum nitidibaccatum f. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved nightshade, is a variant of the species native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, smooth leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces berries that change color from green to red as they mature, and the fruits are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
