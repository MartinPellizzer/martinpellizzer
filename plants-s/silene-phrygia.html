Silene phrygia

Silene phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene phrygia is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
