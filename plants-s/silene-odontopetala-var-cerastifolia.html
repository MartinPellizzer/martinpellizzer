Silene odontopetala var. cerastifolia

Silene odontopetala var. cerastifolia, commonly known as the cerastifolia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
