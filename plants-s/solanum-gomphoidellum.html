Solanum gomphoidellum

Solanum gomphoidellum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its yellowish-green, fleshy berries that resemble small tomatoes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually white or pale green, blooming in spring.

The fruit is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, often used in jams and beverages.
