Syzygium vaughanii

Syzygium vaughanii, commonly known as the Vaughan's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and smooth, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that is often used in traditional cuisine.

Syzygium vaughanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
