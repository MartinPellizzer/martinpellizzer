Spinacia sessiliflora

Spinacia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower spinach, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

While not commonly cultivated for food, it is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
