Senecio dekindtianus

Senecio dekindtianus, commonly known as the De Kindt's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by tufts of white, fluffy seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
