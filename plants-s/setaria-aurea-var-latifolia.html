Setaria aurea var. latifolia

Setaria aurea var. latifolia, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive golden hue along the midribs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it an adaptable species in various ecological settings.
