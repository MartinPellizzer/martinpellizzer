Scrophularia crithmifolia subsp. alpina

Scrophularia crithmifolia subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine figwort, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of pale pink to violet tubular flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
