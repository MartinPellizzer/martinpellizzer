Senecio hyoseridis

Senecio hyoseridis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

Senecio hyoseridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
