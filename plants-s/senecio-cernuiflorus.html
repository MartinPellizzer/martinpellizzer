Senecio cernuiflorus

Senecio cernuiflorus, commonly known as the drooping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense heads. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
