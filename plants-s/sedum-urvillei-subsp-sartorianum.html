Sedum urvillei subsp. sartorianum

Sedum urvillei subsp.

sartorianum, commonly known as the Sartorian Sedum, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
