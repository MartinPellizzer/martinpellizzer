Senecio indicus

Senecio indicus, commonly known as the Indian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with hairy, gray-green leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Senecio indicus is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
