Saussurea schlagintweitii subsp. major

Saussurea schlagintweitii subsp.

major, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
