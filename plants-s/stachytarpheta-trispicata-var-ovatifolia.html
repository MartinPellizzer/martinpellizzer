Stachytarpheta trispicata var. ovatifolia

Stachytarpheta trispicata var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
