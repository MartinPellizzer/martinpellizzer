Sideritis × pertegasii

Sideritis × pertegasii, commonly known as the Greek sainfoin, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Sideritis × pertegasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
