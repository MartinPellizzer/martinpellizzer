Solanum saxatilis

Solanum saxatilis, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.

Solanum saxatilis is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.
