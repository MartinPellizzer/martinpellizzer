Solanum cristalense var. macrospermum

Solanum cristalense var. macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded crystalline nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. This plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic and economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and pharmacological studies.
