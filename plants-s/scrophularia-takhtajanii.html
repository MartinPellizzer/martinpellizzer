Scrophularia takhtajanii

Scrophularia takhtajanii, commonly known as the Takhtajan scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
