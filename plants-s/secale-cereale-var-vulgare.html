Secale cereale var. vulgare

Secale cereale var. vulgare, commonly known as common rye, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescence composed of small, two-ranked spikelets. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and the plant produces dark purple to black seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This plant is also cultivated for its grains, which are used in bread and other food products.
