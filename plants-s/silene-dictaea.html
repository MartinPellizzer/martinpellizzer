Silene dictaea

Silene dictaea, commonly known as the lesser campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are usually upright and branched.

Silene dictaea is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
