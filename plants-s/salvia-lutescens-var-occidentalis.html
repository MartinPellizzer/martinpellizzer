Salvia lutescens var. occidentalis

Salvia lutescens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western yellow sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
