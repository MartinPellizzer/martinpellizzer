Sorghum saccharatum var. papyraceum

Sorghum saccharatum var. papyraceum, commonly known as giant broomcorn millet, is a tall, herbaceous annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, feathery panicles and fibrous stalks, which are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its fibrous stalks used in papermaking and thatching.

It is also valued for its resilience to drought and heat.
