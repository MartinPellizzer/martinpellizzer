Stipa zeravshanica

Stipa zeravshanica, commonly known as the Zeravshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Stipa zeravshanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
