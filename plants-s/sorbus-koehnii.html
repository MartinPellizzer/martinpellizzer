Sorbus koehnii

Sorbus koehnii, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces distinctive, persistent fruits that remain on the plant well into winter. Sorbus koehnii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
