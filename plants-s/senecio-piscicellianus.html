Senecio piscicellianus

Senecio piscicellianus, commonly known as the fish-scale groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
