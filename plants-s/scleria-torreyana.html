Scleria torreyana

Scleria torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Scleria torreyana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
