Symplocos adenopus var. vestita

Symplocos adenopus var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandpaper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.
