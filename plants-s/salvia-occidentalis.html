Salvia occidentalis

Salvia occidentalis, commonly known as Western sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia occidentalis is also valued for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
