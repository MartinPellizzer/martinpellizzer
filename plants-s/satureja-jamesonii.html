Satureja jamesonii

Satureja jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja jamesonii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its pungent, peppery aroma.
