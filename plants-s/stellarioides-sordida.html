Stellarioides sordida

Stellarioides sordida, commonly known as the dirty starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Stellarioides sordida is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.
