Sagraea heteroneura

Sagraea heteroneura, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Sagraea heteroneura is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
