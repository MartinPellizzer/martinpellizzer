Solanum rautanenii

Solanum rautanenii, commonly known as the Rautanen's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries, which are typically red when mature, contain toxic alkaloids and are not edible.

Solanum rautanenii is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
