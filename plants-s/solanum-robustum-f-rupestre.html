Solanum robustum f. rupestre

Solanum robustum f. rupestre, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to withstand dry conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often blooming in spring. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
