Senna cuatrecasasii

Senna cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas senna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
