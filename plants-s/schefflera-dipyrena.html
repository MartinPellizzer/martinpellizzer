Schefflera dipyrena

Schefflera dipyrena, commonly known as the African nettle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Schefflera dipyrena thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
