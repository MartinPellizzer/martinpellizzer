Sapindus cinereus

Sapindus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive ash-gray bark and palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
