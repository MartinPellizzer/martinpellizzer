Sarcochilus lilacinus

Sarcochilus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a compact, hairy rhizome and produces striking, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The plant typically blooms in spring, with its flowers arranged in a dense spike. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
