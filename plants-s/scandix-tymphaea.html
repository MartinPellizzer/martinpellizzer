Scandix tymphaea

Scandix tymphaea, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Scandix tymphaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
