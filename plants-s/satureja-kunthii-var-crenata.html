Satureja kunthii var. crenata

Satureja kunthii var. crenata, commonly known as Sierra grass verbena, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
