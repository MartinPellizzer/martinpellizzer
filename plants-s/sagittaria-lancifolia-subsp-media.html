Sagittaria lancifolia subsp. media

Sagittaria lancifolia subsp.

media, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with long, slender stems. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer.

This species is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and as a food source for aquatic wildlife.
