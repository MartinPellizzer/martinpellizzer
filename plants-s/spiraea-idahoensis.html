Spiraea idahoensis

Spiraea idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, and the plant can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its spring blooms and structural form.
