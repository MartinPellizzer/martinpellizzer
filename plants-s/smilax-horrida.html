Smilax horrida

Smilax horrida, commonly known as the rough smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Smilax horrida is often found in woodland areas and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
