Statice caroliniana

Statice caroliniana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in salty, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize sandy soils.

Statice caroliniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
