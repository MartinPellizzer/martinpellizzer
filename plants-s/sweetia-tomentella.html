Sweetia tomentella

Sweetia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly sweetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a soft, velvety texture.

Sweetia tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
