Sageretia thea var. bornmuelleri

Sageretia thea var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The varietal form, bornmuelleri, is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical species.
