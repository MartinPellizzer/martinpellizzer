Sorbus subarranensis

Sorbus subarranensis, commonly known as the Armenian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to rocky or well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
