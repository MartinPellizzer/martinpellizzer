Scariola orientalis

Scariola orientalis, commonly known as the oriental scarlet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Scariola orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
