Silene nudicaulis

Silene nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Silene nudicaulis is known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
