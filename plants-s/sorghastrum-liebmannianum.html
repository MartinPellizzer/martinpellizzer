Sorghastrum liebmannianum

Sorghastrum liebmannianum, commonly known as Liebmann's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
