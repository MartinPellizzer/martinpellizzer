Solanum parviflorum

Solanum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellow or orange color, and the plant contains alkaloids, which can be toxic.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
