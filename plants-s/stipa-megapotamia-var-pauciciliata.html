Stipa megapotamia var. pauciciliata

Stipa megapotamia var. pauciciliata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or brownish spikelets that are arranged in loose panicles, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along the edges of shrublands.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
