Seddera hirsuta var. hirsuta

Seddera hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy seddera, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
