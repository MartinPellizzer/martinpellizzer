Scirpus subarticulatus

Scirpus subarticulatus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive ribbed texture.

Scirpus subarticulatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
