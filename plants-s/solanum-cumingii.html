Solanum cumingii

Solanum cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by yellow berries that contain the seeds. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

Solanum cumingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
