Saxifraga camposii f. typica

Saxifraga camposii f. typica, commonly known as the Cape Town saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white petals and is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool, well-drained conditions.
