Senecio diplostephioides

Senecio diplostephioides, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, robust perennial plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring large, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain environments.
