Salvia areysiana

Salvia areysiana, commonly known as the Areysiana sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia areysiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
