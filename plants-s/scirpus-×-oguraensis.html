Scirpus × oguraensis

Scirpus × oguraensis, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Scirpus mariquetae and Scirpus californicus. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges. It has tall, cylindrical stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color.

Scirpus × oguraensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
