Senecio perijaensis

Senecio perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio perijaensis produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
