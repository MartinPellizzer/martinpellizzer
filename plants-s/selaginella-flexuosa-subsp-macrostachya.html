Selaginella flexuosa subsp. macrostachya

Selaginella flexuosa subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae. It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions on rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, with the leaves becoming broader toward the base. Its sporophylls are elongated and bear spores in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This subspecies is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
