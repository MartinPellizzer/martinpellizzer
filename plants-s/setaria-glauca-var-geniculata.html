Setaria glauca var. geniculata

Setaria glauca var. geniculata, commonly known as silverleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
