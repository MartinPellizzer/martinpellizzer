Senna angustisiliqua var. angustisiliqua

Senna angustisiliqua var. angustisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for medicinal purposes.
