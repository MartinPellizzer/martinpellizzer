Shorea bullata

Shorea bullata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
