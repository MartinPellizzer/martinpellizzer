Sauvetrea chicana

Sauvetrea chicana, commonly known as the Mexican white sage, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its essential oils are often used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
