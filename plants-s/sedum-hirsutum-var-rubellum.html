Sedum hirsutum var. rubellum

Sedum hirsutum var. rubellum, commonly known as the red sedum, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
