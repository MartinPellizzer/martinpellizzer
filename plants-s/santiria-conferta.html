Santiria conferta

Santiria conferta, commonly known as the white sand daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
