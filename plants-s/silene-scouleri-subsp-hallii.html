Silene scouleri subsp. hallii

Silene scouleri subsp.

hallii, commonly known as the mountain touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
