Sida alii var. alii

Sida alii var. alii, commonly known as Hawaiian coast mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along coastal areas and in disturbed soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It is adapted to the tropical climate and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Sida alii var. alii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
