Scirpus plantagineus

Scirpus plantagineus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are broad and flat, typically measuring up to 20 centimeters in length, and are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base.

Scirpus plantagineus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
