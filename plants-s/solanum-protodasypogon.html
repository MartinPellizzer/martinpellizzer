Solanum protodasypogon

Solanum protodasypogon, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
