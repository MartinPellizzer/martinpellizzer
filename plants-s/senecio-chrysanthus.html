Senecio chrysanthus

Senecio chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Senecio chrysanthus is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its ornamental value.
