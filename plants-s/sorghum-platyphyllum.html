Sorghum platyphyllum

Sorghum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and tall, branched inflorescence. The plant is adapted to grow in hot, arid environments and is often used for forage or ornamental purposes. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust stem that can reach several meters in height.

Sorghum platyphyllum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
