Salvia pallidiflora

Salvia pallidiflora, commonly known as paleflower sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
