Salix takasagoalpina

Salix takasagoalpina, commonly known as the Japanese willow or alpine willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in high-altitude areas where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
