Setaria macrostachya

Setaria macrostachya, commonly known as giant millet or yellow panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-green inflorescences that mature into brown spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to serve as a forage crop in some regions.
