Stevia potrerensis

Stevia potrerensis, commonly known as sweetleaf or sugar leaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is cultivated for its intensely sweet leaves.

Stevia potrerensis is widely used as a low-calorie sweetener alternative in various food and beverage products.
