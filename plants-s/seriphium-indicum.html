Seriphium indicum

Seriphium indicum, commonly known as Indian goatweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Seriphium indicum is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
