Syzygium adelphicum

Syzygium adelphicum, commonly known as the red lilly pilly, is a small evergreen shrub native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red or orange berries that are edible but often used as ornamental fruits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
