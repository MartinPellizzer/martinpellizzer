Salacia multiflora var. rugulosa

Salacia multiflora var. rugulosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved salacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, textured leaves that are often covered in fine, raised bumps. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some pharmacological applications due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
