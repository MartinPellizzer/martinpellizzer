Salix × germanorum

Salix × germanorum, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its weeping, cascading branches that drape gracefully from the crown, creating a distinctive appearance. The tree produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix × germanorum is often planted for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist soils.
