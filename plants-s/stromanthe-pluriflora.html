Stromanthe pluriflora

Stromanthe pluriflora, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often variegated leaves that come in shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are typically not the primary ornamental feature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Stromanthe pluriflora is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
