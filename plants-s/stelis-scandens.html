Stelis scandens

Stelis scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, often using aerial roots to attach to trees or other structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Stelis scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
