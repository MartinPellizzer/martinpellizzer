Stigmaphyllon ternatum

Stigmaphyllon ternatum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
