Stoebe subulata

Stoebe subulata, commonly known as slender devil's-bit manroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, subulate (needle-like) leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Stoebe subulata is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
