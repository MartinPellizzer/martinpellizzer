Senecio chiloensis

Senecio chiloensis, commonly known as Chilean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio chiloensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
