Swertia macrosepala

Swertia macrosepala, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Swertia macrosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
