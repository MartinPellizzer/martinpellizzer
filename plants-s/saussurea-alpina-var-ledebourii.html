Saussurea alpina var. ledebourii

Saussurea alpina var. ledebourii, commonly known as the Alpine saffron or alpine gromwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to high-altitude landscapes.

The variety ledebourii is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and growth habits compared to the typical form of Saussurea alpina.
