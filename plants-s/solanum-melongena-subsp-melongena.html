Solanum melongena subsp. melongena

Solanum melongena subsp.

melongena, commonly known as the eggplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indomalayan region. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, velvety leaves.

The plant produces strikingly purple or white flowers, which are followed by egg-shaped, edible fruits. The fruit's skin is typically smooth and waxy, while the flesh is soft and spongy.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary versatility across various cuisines worldwide.
