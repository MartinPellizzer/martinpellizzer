Salvia earlei

Salvia earlei, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia earlei is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought-resistant properties.
