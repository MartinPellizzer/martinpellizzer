Scirpus luzonensis

Scirpus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon bulrush, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Scirpus luzonensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
