Syzygium subsessiliflorum

Syzygium subsessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to northeastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Syzygium subsessiliflorum typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
