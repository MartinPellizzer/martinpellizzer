Setaria macrostachya var. schultzii

Setaria macrostachya var. schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-gray hue, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils, making it adaptable to arid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
