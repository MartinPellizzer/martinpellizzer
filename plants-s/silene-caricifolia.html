Silene caricifolia

Silene caricifolia, commonly known as the water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, club-shaped leaves that resemble small, elongated drops of water. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Silene caricifolia thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
