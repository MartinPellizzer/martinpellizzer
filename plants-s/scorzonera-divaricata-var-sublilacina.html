Scorzonera divaricata var. sublilacina

Scorzonera divaricata var. sublilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces striking, violet-lilac colored flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera divaricata var. sublilacina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
