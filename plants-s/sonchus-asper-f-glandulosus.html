Sonchus asper f. glandulosus

Sonchus asper f. glandulosus, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a variant of the common hawksbeard plant.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its hairy, rough stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are serrated and often covered with glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
