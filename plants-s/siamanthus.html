Siamanthus

Siamanthus, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and consists of several species, many of which are annual or biennial herbs. The plants typically have basal rosettes of leaves and produce yellow or white composite flowers that attract pollinators. Siamanthus species are often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in arid to semi-arid conditions.

Their botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive inflorescence structure typical of the Asteraceae family.
