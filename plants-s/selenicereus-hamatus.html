Selenicereus hamatus

Selenicereus hamatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure adapted to arid environments. Its most notable feature is its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and last only one evening.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
