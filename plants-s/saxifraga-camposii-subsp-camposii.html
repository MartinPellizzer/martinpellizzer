Saxifraga camposii subsp. camposii

Saxifraga camposii subsp.

camposii, commonly known as the Cape York saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
