Senecio doronicum subsp. longifolius

Senecio doronicum subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and hairy foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive serrated edge.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
