Serrafalcus mollis var. microstachys

Serrafalcus mollis var. microstachys, commonly known as the dwarf serrafalcus, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
