Styphelia tortifolia

Styphelia tortifolia, commonly known as the bearberry, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Styphelia tortifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and pine forests.
