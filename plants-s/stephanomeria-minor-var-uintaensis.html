Stephanomeria minor var. uintaensis

Stephanomeria minor var. uintaensis, commonly known as Uinta Raycup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grayish-green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a compact growth form with a branching stem and is often found in areas with low precipitation.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
