Solanum parodii

Solanum parodii, commonly known as the "parodii nightshade," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring thick, corky bark and large, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum parodii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
