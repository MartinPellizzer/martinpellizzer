Salicornia corticosa var. nachtigalli

Salicornia corticosa var. nachtigalli, commonly known as the red marsh grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, green flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
