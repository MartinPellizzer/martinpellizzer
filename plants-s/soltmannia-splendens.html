Soltmannia splendens

Soltmannia splendens, commonly known as the splendour bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Soltmannia splendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
