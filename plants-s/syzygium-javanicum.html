Syzygium javanicum

Syzygium javanicum, commonly known as the Java apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.

Syzygium javanicum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
