Solanum grossularia

Solanum grossularia, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and distinctive yellow-orange berries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its berries are edible when ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Solanum grossularia thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.
