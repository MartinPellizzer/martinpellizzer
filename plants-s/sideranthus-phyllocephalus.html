Sideranthus phyllocephalus

Sideranthus phyllocephalus, commonly known as the leaf-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, with leaves that are broad and slightly lobed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
