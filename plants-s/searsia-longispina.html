Searsia longispina

Searsia longispina, commonly known as the wild holly or red holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
