Stipa isoldeae

Stipa isoldeae, commonly known as Isolde's feathergrass, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to golden spikelets that are clustered along the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Stipa isoldeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
