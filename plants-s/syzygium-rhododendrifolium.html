Syzygium rhododendrifolium

Syzygium rhododendrifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and showy, clustered red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate, making it a popular ornamental species in subtropical regions.
