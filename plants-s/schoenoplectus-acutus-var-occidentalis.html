Schoenoplectus acutus var. occidentalis

Schoenoplectus acutus var. occidentalis, commonly known as western bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with dense, fibrous leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically appearing in late summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
