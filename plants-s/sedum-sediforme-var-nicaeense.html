Sedum sediforme var. nicaeense

Sedum sediforme var. nicaeense, commonly known as the Nicaean sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically thick and succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in xerophytic conditions.
