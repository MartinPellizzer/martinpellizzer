Sporobolus attenuatus

Sporobolus attenuatus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant is well adapted to sandy and disturbed soils, often growing in open habitats such as dunes, prairies, and roadsides. Its seed heads are often seen "drooping" or falling, a trait that gives it its scientific name, "attenuatus," meaning "gradually tapered.".

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
