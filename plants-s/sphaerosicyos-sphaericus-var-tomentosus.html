Sphaerosicyos sphaericus var. tomentosus

Sphaerosicyos sphaericus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy ball cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured appearance. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its native environment.

Despite its rugged appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant due to its distinctive look and low maintenance requirements.
