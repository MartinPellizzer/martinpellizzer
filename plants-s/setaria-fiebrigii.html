Setaria fiebrigii

Setaria fiebrigii, commonly known as fevergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its resilience in challenging climates.
