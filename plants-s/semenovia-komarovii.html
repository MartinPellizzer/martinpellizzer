Semenovia komarovii

Semenovia komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish or brownish flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
