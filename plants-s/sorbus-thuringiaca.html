Sorbus thuringiaca

Sorbus thuringiaca, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Sorbus thuringiaca is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
