Solanum ipomoea var. macrostachyum

Solanum ipomoea var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters if left unpruned. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruits are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed unripe.
