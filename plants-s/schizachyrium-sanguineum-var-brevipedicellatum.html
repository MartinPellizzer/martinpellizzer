Schizachyrium sanguineum var. brevipedicellatum

Schizachyrium sanguineum var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as pink false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces small, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
