Syzygium pseudomolle

Syzygium pseudomolle, commonly known as the Java apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. Syzygium pseudomolle is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
