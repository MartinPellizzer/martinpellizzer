Suaeda maritima var. australis

Suaeda maritima var. australis, commonly known as coastal saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuarine regions, where it tolerates high levels of salt in the soil and water. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid and salty conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is important for stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
