Silene Inflata Var. Uniflora

Silene inflata var. uniflora is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of the northern hemisphere. It is known for its unique, inflated flower buds that resemble small, lantern-like structures. This variant of Silene inflata is particularly rare, with limited populations found in isolated habitats. Its flowers open gradually, revealing petals that shift from pink to white as they mature. Due to its beauty and scarcity, it is highly valued by botanists and conservationists alike.
