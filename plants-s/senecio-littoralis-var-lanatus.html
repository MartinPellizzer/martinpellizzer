Senecio littoralis var. lanatus

Senecio littoralis var. lanatus, commonly known as the coastal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand strong coastal winds and salt spray.

This species is also known for its ability to stabilize sand dunes through its extensive root system.
