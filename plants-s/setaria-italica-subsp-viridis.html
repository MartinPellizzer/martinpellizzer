Setaria italica subsp. viridis

Setaria italica subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive spikelets that bear small, green bracts.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is often cultivated for its high nutritional value, particularly in traditional and modern food systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
