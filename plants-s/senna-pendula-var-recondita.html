Senna pendula var. recondita

Senna pendula var. recondita, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
