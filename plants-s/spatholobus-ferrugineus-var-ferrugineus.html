Spatholobus ferrugineus var. ferrugineus

Spatholobus ferrugineus var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the red-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
