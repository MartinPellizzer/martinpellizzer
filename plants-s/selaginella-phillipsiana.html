Selaginella phillipsiana

Selaginella phillipsiana, commonly known as the noble spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a low-growing, creeping stem with small, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls. It produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the plant's spores.

Selaginella phillipsiana is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
