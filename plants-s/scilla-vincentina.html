Scilla vincentina

Scilla vincentina, commonly known as Vincent's scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to woodland and rock garden settings. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Scilla vincentina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in naturalized landscapes.
