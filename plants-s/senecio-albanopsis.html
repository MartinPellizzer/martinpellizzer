Senecio albanopsis

Senecio albanopsis, commonly known as the Albanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native region.
