Stipa pennata subsp. ceynowae

Stipa pennata subsp.

ceynowae, commonly known as the Ceylon feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry culms and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with silver.

The plant produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy slopes.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, flowing appearance.
