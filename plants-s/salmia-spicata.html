Salmia spicata

Salmia spicata, commonly known as the spike salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
