Scrophularia uniflora

Scrophularia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia uniflora is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
