Selago trauseldii

Selago trauseldii, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Selago trauseldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
