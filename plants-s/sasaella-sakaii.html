Sasaella sakaii

Sasaella sakaii, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a thick mat on the forest floor.

Sasaella sakaii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in shaded environments.
