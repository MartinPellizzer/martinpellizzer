Sorbus magocsyana

Sorbus magocsyana, commonly known as the Magocsy's rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Hungary and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
