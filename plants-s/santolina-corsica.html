Santolina corsica

Santolina corsica, commonly known as Corsican santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina corsica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal remedies.
