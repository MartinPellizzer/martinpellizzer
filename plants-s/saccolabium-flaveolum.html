Saccolabium flaveolum

Saccolabium flaveolum, commonly known as the yellow saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its ecological persistence.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential medicinal properties.
