Stipa vaginata var. inmersa

Stipa vaginata var. inmersa, commonly known as the submerged false oat, is a grass species characterized by its unique growth habit.

It typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it forms dense, herbaceous clumps. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small florets. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive colonies in suitable habitats.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
