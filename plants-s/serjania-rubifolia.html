Serjania rubifolia

Serjania rubifolia, commonly known as the red-fruited serjania, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and have a smooth surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that attracts various bird species.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
