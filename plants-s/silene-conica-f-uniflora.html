Silene conica f. uniflora

Silene conica f. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered hollyhock, is a botanical variant of the hollyhock species.

It is characterized by its single, showy flower per stem, distinguishing it from the typical multi-flowered form. The plant produces vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
