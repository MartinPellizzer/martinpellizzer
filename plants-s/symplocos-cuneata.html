Symplocos cuneata

Symplocos cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces showy, dark purple berries that are a striking feature in the fall. These berries are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock.

Symplocos cuneata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
