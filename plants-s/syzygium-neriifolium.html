Syzygium neriifolium

Syzygium neriifolium, commonly known as the black-leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in tropical horticulture and traditional medicine.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.
