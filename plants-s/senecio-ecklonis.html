Senecio ecklonis

Senecio ecklonis, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio ecklonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
