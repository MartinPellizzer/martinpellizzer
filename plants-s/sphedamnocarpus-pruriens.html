Sphedamnocarpus pruriens

Sphedamnocarpus pruriens, commonly known as the itching bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is known for its irritant properties, as the leaves and stems can cause skin irritation upon contact.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic effects.
