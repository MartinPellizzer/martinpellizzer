Serratula caucasica

Serratula caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian serratula, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
