Silene menziesii subsp. typica

Silene menziesii subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Menzies' campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching. Silene menziesii subsp.

typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
