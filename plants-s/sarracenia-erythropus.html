Sarracenia erythropus

Sarracenia erythropus, commonly known as the red-tongued sarracenia, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-hued pitchers that are often adorned with dark green veins. The plant's specialized leaves function as traps, secreting digestive enzymes to break down captured insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
