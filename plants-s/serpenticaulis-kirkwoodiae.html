Serpenticaulis kirkwoodiae

Serpenticaulis kirkwoodiae, commonly known as the snake-stalked orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, snake-like appearance, with long, curling stems that resemble a serpent. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
