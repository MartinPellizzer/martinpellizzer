Scorzonera uzbekistanica

Scorzonera uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek scarlet root, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a thick, fleshy root that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Scorzonera uzbekistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
