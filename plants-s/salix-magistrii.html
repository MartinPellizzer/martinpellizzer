Salix magistrii

Salix magistrii, commonly known as the Magistrii willow, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It has a shallow root system that allows it to anchor itself in rocky soils.

Salix magistrii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
