Silybum marianum subsp. anatolicum

Silybum marianum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian milk thistle, is a variant of the milk thistle species native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers, which are typical of the genus Silybum.

The plant contains bioactive compounds, particularly silmarins, which are found in its seeds and have been studied for their hepatoprotective properties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This subspecies is of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
