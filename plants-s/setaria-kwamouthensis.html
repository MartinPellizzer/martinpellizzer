Setaria kwamouthensis

Setaria kwamouthensis, commonly known as the Kwa-Mouth millet, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It grows primarily in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
