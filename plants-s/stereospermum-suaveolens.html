Stereospermum suaveolens

Stereospermum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces woody capsules that contain small, elliptical seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
