Stellera coridifolia

Stellera coridifolia, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Stellera coridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
