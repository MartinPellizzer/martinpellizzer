Syngonanthus appressus

Syngonanthus appressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
