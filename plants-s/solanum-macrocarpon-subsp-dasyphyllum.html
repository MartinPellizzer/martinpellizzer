Solanum macrocarpon subsp. dasyphyllum

Solanum macrocarpon subsp.

dasyphyllum, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to egg-shaped, yellowish or orange fruits. The fruit is edible when mature and has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a vegetable in some regions.
