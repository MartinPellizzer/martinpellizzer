Saipania

Saipania, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers that bloom on long, sturdy stalks.

Its leaves are broad and circular, often floating on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in muddy substrates. The plant is native to the Nile River basin and thrived in the wetlands and marshes of ancient Egypt. Its flowers, which open at dawn, symbolized purity and rebirth in Egyptian culture.

Botanically, Saipania is classified as a member of the Nymphaeaceae family, closely related to modern water lilies.
