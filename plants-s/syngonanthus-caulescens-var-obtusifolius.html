Syngonanthus caulescens var. obtusifolius

Syngonanthus caulescens var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant has a distinctive cylindrical stem that is usually unbranched and covered with a layer of fine hairs. Its leaves are broad, flat, and have a rounded tip, contributing to its common name.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense spikes and are typically wind-pollinated.
