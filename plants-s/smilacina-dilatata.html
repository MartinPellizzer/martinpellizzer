Smilacina dilatata

Smilacina dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf greenbriar, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Smilacina dilatata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
