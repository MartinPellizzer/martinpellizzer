Sulcorebutia atrospinosa

Sulcorebutia atrospinosa, commonly known as the black-spined crested cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive black spines that contrast with its pale green body. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
