Senecio × albescens

Senecio × albescens, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to South Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
