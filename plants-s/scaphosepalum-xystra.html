Scaphosepalum xystra

Scaphosepalum xystra, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem, and its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, well-drained soils. Scaphosepalum xystra thrives in humid environments and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
