Saxifraga × tolmatchevii

Saxifraga × tolmatchevii, commonly known as the Tolmatchev's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
