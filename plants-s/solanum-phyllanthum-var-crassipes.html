Solanum phyllanthum var. crassipes

Solanum phyllanthum var. crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions of Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
