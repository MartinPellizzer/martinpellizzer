Silene caroliniana

Silene caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene caroliniana is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
