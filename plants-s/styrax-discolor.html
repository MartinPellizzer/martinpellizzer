Styrax discolor

Styrax discolor, commonly known as the variegated frangipani, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that display a striking variegation of white and green. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin layers.

Styrax discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
