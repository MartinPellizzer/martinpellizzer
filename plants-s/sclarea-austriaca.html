Sclarea austriaca

Sclarea austriaca, commonly known as Austrian angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are white to pale yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The roots of Sclarea austriaca contain volatile oils and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
