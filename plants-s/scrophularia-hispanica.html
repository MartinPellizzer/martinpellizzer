Scrophularia hispanica

Scrophularia hispanica, commonly known as Spanish foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, hooded flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing striking purple or reddish-purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scrophularia hispanica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
