Stenotalis ramosissima

Stenotalis ramosissima, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stenotalis ramosissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
