Scorzonera ruprechtiana

Scorzonera ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Caucasian scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera ruprechtiana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
