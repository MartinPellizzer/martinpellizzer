Silene bayburtensis

Silene bayburtensis, commonly known as the Bayburt catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in mountainous regions. It has small, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
