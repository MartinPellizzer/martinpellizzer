Sida saraviae

Sida saraviae, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped. Sida saraviae is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
