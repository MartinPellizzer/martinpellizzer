Schoenopsis burmanni

Schoenopsis burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's grass tree, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural practices.
