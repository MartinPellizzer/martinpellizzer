Sedum gattefossei

Sedum gattefossei, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sedum gattefossei is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.
