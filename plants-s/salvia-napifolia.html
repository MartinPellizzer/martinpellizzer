Salvia napifolia

Salvia napifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
