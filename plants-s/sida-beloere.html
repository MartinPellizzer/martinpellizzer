Sida beloere

Sida beloere, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Sida beloere is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
