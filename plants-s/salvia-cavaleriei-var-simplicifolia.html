Salvia cavaleriei var. simplicifolia

Salvia cavaleriei var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sage or mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet-blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
