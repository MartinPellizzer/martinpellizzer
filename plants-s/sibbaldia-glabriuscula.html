Sibbaldia glabriuscula

Sibbaldia glabriuscula, commonly known as the slender sibbaldia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Sibbaldia glabriuscula is adapted to harsh alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
