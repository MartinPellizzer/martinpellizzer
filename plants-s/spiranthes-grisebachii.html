Spiranthes grisebachii

Spiranthes grisebachii, commonly known as the gray beard orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its root system consists of tubers, which help it survive in its natural habitat.

Spiranthes grisebachii is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in clusters along the edges of streams or in swampy areas.
