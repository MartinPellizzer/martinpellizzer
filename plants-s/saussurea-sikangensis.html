Saussurea sikangensis

Saussurea sikangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Sichuan in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its thick, fibrous root system and showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with numerous ray florets surrounding a central disc.

Due to its medicinal properties and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
