Sarcozygium xanthoxylum

Sarcozygium xanthoxylum, commonly known as the yellow thornapple, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and rough, thorny stems. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
