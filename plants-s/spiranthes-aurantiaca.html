Spiranthes aurantiaca

Spiranthes aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
