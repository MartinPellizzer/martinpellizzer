Scilla bulgarica

Scilla bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Scilla bulgarica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
