Strobilanthes fimbriata

Strobilanthes fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed flame flower, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
