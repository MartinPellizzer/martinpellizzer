Serruria pinnata

Serruria pinnata, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and its striking, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets that are typically arranged in pairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
