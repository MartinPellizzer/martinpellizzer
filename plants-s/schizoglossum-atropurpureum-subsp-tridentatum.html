Schizoglossum atropurpureum subsp. tridentatum

Schizoglossum atropurpureum subsp.

tridentatum, commonly known as the purple-flowered false gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Gentianaceae family.
