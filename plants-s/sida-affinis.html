Sida affinis

Sida affinis, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or saline soils. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Sida affinis is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a common species in coastal dunes and beaches.
