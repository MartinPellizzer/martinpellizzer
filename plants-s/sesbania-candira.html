Sesbania candira

Sesbania candira, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

Sesbania candira also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
