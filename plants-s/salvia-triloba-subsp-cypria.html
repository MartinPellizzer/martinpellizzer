Salvia triloba subsp. cypria

Salvia triloba subsp.

cypria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
