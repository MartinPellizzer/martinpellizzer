Solanum macropus

Solanum macropus, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it produces berries that start green and ripen to red or yellow.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional Aboriginal practices.
