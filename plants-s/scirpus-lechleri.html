Scirpus lechleri

Scirpus lechleri, commonly known as *sangre de drago* or dragon's blood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant is notable for its dark red, viscous resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Scirpus lechleri thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near rivers and in swampy areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various insects and contributing to soil stability in wetland ecosystems.
