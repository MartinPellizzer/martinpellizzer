Saxifraga anadyrensis

Saxifraga anadyrensis, commonly known as the Anadyrensis saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure, and are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga anadyrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
