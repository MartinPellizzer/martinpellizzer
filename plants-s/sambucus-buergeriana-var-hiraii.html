Sambucus buergeriana var. hiraii

Sambucus buergeriana var. hiraii, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and features compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
