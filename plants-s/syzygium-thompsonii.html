Syzygium thompsonii

Syzygium thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, round fruits that are similar in appearance to apples, hence its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
