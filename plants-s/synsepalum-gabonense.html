Synsepalum gabonense

Synsepalum gabonense, commonly known as the African miracle fruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange berries that contain a unique compound called miraculin. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow.
