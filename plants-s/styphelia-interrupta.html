Styphelia interrupta

Styphelia interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Styphelia interrupta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine forests or heathlands.
