Scutellaria squamulosa

Scutellaria squamulosa, commonly known as the woolly skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces capsule fruits that contain several small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Scutellaria squamulosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
