Satureja lepida subsp. bolleana

Satureja lepida subsp.

bolleana, commonly known as Bolleana savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its strong, peppery aroma.
