Stillingia arborescens

Stillingia arborescens, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or reddish in color, blooming in the summer months.

The roots of Stillingia arborescens are noted for their deep red color and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
