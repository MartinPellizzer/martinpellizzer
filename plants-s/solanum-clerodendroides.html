Solanum clerodendroides

Solanum clerodendroides, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and the leaves are usually oval with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry grasslands, and it has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
