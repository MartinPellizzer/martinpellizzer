Senecio mirabilis

Senecio mirabilis, commonly known as the "Blue Euphorbia," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a succulent, spiny stem and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
