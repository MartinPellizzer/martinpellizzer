Shorea reticulata

Shorea reticulata, commonly known as Malay teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its durable, heavy, and high-quality hardwood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
