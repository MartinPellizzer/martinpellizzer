Salvia collina

Salvia collina, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
