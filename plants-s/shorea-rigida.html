Shorea rigida

Shorea rigida, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
