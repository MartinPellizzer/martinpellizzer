Sarothra fastigiata

Sarothra fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Sarothra fastigiata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
