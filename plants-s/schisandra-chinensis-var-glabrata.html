Schisandra chinensis var. glabrata

Schisandra chinensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth schisandra, is a variant of the five-flavor fruit plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other varietal forms. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red berries, which are harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The berries contain a complex mixture of bioactive compounds, contributing to its reputation in traditional medicine.
