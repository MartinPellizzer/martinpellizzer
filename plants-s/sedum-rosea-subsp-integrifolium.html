Sedum rosea subsp. integrifolium

Sedum rosea subsp.

integrifolium, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountains of Central Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
