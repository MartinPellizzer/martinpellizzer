Scoparia arborea

Scoparia arborea, commonly known as the tree scoparia, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Scoparia arborea is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
