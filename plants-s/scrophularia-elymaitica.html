Scrophularia elymaitica

Scrophularia elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymian figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils, preferring well-drained environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia elymaitica is often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
