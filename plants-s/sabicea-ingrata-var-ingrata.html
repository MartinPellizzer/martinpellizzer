Sabicea ingrata var. ingrata

Sabicea ingrata var. ingrata, commonly known as the slender sabelia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in racemes, typically with five petals and yellowish or white coloration. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
