Scrophularia pinardii var. acutilobata

Scrophularia pinardii var. acutilobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in spikes, usually in shades of pink or purple.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
