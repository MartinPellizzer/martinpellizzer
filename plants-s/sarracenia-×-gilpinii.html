Sarracenia × gilpinii

Sarracenia × gilpinii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra, resulting in unique morphological traits. The plant features elongated, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. Its botanical characteristics include reddish hues, especially in the upper parts of the pitchers, and a preference for acidic, nutrient-poor soils.

Sarracenia × gilpinii thrives in wet, sunny habitats and is often found in bogs and marshes.
