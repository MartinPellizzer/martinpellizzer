Sida spenceriana

Sida spenceriana, commonly known as the Spencer's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Sida spenceriana is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.
