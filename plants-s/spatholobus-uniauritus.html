Spatholobus uniauritus

Spatholobus uniauritus, commonly known as the one-eared wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendant-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with a single terminal leaflet, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The flowers are typically pale blue or purple and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
