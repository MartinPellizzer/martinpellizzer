Smilacina purpurea

Smilacina purpurea, commonly known as purple false Solomon's seal, is a deciduous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often turning a striking yellow in the fall.

Smilacina purpurea thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded habitats.
