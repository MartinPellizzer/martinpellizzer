Syzygium koniamboense

Syzygium koniamboense, commonly known as the Koniambo apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium koniamboense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
