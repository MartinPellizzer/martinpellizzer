Solanum cornifolium

Solanum cornifolium, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that mature to a bright red color, which are toxic to humans and livestock. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum cornifolium is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
