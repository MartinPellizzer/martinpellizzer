Silene mariae

Silene mariae, commonly known as the Mary's pink, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Silene mariae is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
