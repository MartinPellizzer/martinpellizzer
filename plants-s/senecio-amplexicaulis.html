Senecio amplexicaulis

Senecio amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that tightly enclose the stem. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
