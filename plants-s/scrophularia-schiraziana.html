Scrophularia schiraziana

Scrophularia schiraziana, commonly known as the Persian figwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes a variety of herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the family.

Scrophularia schiraziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
