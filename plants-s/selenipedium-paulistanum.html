Selenipedium paulistanum

Selenipedium paulistanum, commonly known as the Paulista selenipedium, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a tall, narrow stem with a single, large, white spathe that resembles a flower. Its inflorescence contains numerous small, yellowish-green berries that are a key feature of its botanical appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
