Spodiopogon latifolius

Spodiopogon latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs, and it produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant has broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Spodiopogon latifolius plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
