Stemmatodaphne perakensis

Stemmatodaphne perakensis, commonly known as the Java wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
