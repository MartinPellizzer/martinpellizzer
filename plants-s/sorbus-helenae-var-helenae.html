Sorbus helenae var. helenae

Sorbus helenae var. helenae, commonly known as Helen's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
