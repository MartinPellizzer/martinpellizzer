Saccharum viguieri

Saccharum viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
