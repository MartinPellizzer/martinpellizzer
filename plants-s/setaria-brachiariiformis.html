Setaria brachiariiformis

Setaria brachiariiformis, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale brown seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Setaria brachiariiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
