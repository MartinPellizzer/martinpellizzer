Silene delicatula subsp. pisidica

Silene delicatula subsp.

pisidica, commonly known as the Caucasian bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers and slender stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bladder-like structures called pseudocarps. Silene delicatula subsp.

pisidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
