Solidago × leiophallax

Solidago × leiophallax, commonly known as the white-gold goldenrod, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, fluffy yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Solidago × leiophallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
