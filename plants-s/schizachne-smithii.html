Schizachne smithii

Schizachne smithii, commonly known as the Smith's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it has a shallow root system.

Schizachne smithii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
