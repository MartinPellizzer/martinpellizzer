Solanum tuberosum f. laram-lelekkoya

Solanum tuberosum f. laram-lelekkoya, commonly known as the Laram-lelekkoya potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes a distinct coloration and texture. This variety is native to specific regions in the Andes, where it has adapted to high-altitude growing conditions. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, such as hairy stems and alternate leaves.

Its tubers are valued for their flavor and nutritional content, making it a significant crop in local agriculture.
