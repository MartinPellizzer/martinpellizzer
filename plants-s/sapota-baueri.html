Sapota baueri

Sapota baueri, commonly known as the Brazilian sapote, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its fruit, known for its sweet, creamy flesh, contains a single large seed and is rich in nutrients.

The botanical features of Sapota baueri make it a valuable species in both ornamental and agricultural contexts.
