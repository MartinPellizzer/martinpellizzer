Solanum lachneion var. intercedens

Solanum lachneion var. intercedens, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxicity, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
