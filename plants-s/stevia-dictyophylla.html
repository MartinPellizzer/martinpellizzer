Stevia dictyophylla

Stevia dictyophylla, commonly known as the red stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties and use in natural sweetener production, it has gained attention in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
