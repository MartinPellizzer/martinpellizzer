Saxifraga hookeri var. hookeri

Saxifraga hookeri var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
