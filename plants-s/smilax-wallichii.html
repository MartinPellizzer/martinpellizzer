Smilax wallichii

Smilax wallichii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Smilax wallichii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
