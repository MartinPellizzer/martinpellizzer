Senecio antitensis

Senecio antitensis, commonly known as the Antiterrorist Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
