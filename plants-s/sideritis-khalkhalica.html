Sideritis khalkhalica

Sideritis khalkhalica, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds such as nepetalactone, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

Sideritis khalkhalica is often used in traditional medicine for its potential calming and therapeutic effects.
