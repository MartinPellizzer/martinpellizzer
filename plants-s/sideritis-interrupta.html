Sideritis interrupta

Sideritis interrupta, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in summer. Its leaves are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and flavor.

Sideritis interrupta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
