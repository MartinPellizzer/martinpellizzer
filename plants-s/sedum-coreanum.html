Sedum coreanum

Sedum coreanum, commonly known as Korean stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Korea.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
