Symplocos caerulea

Symplocos caerulea, commonly known as bluebead lily, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Symplocos caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
