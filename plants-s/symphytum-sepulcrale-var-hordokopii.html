Symphytum sepulcrale var. hordokopii

Symphytum sepulcrale var. hordokopii, commonly known as the Hordokan comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-violet flower cluster. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
