Senecio doriiformis subsp. doriiformis

Senecio doriiformis subsp.

doriiformis, commonly known as the Doris' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
