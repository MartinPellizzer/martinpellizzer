Stelis meridana

Stelis meridana, commonly known as the Canary Islands orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Stelis meridana is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
