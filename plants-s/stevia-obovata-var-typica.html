Stevia obovata var. typica

Stevia obovata var. typica, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach a height of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences.

Stevia obovata var. typica is widely cultivated for its sweet leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
