Sicyos microcarpus var. koloaensis

Sicyos microcarpus var. koloaensis, commonly known as the Koloa bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to Hawaii.

It is a climbing vine with hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive, bottle-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas. Its fruits are typically small, with a thick, smooth rind and a sweet, edible interior.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental qualities and for traditional use in Hawaiian culture.
