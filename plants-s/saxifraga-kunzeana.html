Saxifraga kunzeana

Saxifraga kunzeana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rosette formation, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga kunzeana is known for its hardiness and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
