Senecio masonii

Senecio masonii, commonly known as Mason's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio masonii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
