Striga esquirolii

Striga esquirolii, commonly known as the witchweed, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of grasses and other herbaceous species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its rapid growth and ability to cause significant damage to crops. It is native to Africa and has been a major concern in agricultural regions due to its impact on food security.

Its life cycle involves a complex interaction with host plants, making it a challenging weed to control.
