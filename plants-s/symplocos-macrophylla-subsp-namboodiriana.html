Symplocos macrophylla subsp. namboodiriana

Symplocos macrophylla subsp.

namboodiriana, commonly known as the Namboodiri sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are leathery and glossy in texture.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds. This subspecies is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and incense.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in evergreen forests.
