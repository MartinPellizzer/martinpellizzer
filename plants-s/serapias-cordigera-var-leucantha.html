Serapias cordigera var. leucantha

Serapias cordigera var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-veined sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white with distinct veining, and they bloom in early spring. This variant is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation compared to other varieties of Serapias cordigera.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
