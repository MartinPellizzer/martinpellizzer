Serruria glomerata

Serruria glomerata, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble snowdrops, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its delicate blooms.

Serruria glomerata is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
