Silene primuliflora var. ciliata

Silene primuliflora var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy margins, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in moist or sandy soils.
