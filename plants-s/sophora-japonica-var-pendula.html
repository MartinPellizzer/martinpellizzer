Sophora japonica var. pendula

Sophora japonica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping pagoda tree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a soft, flowing canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens for its graceful appearance.
