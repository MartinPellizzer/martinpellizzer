Spartium nudum

Spartium nudum, commonly known as narrowleaf spartia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Spartium nudum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
