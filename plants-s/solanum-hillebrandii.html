Solanum hillebrandii

Solanum hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum hillebrandii is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
