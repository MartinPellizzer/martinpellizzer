Stimpsonia crispidens

Stimpsonia crispidens, commonly known as the curly-stemmed stimpsonia, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Stimpsonia crispidens is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems that store water.
