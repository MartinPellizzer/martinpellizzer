Schizoglossum bidens subsp. hirtum

Schizoglossum bidens subsp.

hirtum, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
