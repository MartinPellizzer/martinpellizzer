Serapias lingua subsp. oranensis

Serapias lingua subsp.

oranensis, commonly known as the Oranese tongue orchid, is a rare and specialized plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a tongue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive hood-like structure.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
