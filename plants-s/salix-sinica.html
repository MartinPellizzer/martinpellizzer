Salix sinica

Salix sinica, commonly known as Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Salix sinica is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
