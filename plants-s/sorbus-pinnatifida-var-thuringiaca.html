Sorbus pinnatifida var. thuringiaca

Sorbus pinnatifida var. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
