Stipa eminens var. andersonii

Stipa eminens var. andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often erect and slightly drooping. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
