Sasa togashiana var. shinogiensis

Sasa togashiana var. shinogiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or shinogi bamboo, is a clump-forming plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative element in landscape design.
