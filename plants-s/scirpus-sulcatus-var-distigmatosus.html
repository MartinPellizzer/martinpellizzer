Scirpus sulcatus var. distigmatosus

Scirpus sulcatus var. distigmatosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in marshy environments and is often found in shallow water or saturated soil. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
