Silene apica var. firma

Silene apica var. firma, commonly known as the firm-tipped silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.
