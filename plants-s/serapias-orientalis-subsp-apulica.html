Serapias orientalis subsp. apulica

Serapias orientalis subsp.

apulica, commonly known as the Apulian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in the Apulia region of Italy. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in wet meadows and marshy areas, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces a single raceme of flowers during the spring and early summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
