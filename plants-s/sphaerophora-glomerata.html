Sphaerophora glomerata

Sphaerophora glomerata, commonly known as the clustered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while also deriving energy through photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Sphaerophora glomerata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
