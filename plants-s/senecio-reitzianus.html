Senecio reitzianus

Senecio reitzianus, commonly known as the Reitz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by cylindrical seed heads.

Its foliage is generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
