Setaria leucophylla

Setaria leucophylla, commonly known as white-leaf foxtail, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that often appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, along roadsides, and in agricultural areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a whitish or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

Setaria leucophylla is an annual plant that produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated landscapes.
