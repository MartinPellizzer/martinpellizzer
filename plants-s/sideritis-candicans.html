Sideritis candicans

Sideritis candicans, commonly known as white heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a minty fragrance.

Sideritis candicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
