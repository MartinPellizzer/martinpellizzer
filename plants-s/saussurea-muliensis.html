Saussurea muliensis

Saussurea muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and often grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is used for its purported anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits.

The botanical features of Saussurea muliensis make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and ethnobotanical research.
