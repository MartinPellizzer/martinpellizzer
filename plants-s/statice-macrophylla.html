Statice macrophylla

Statice macrophylla, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Statice macrophylla is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
