Salvia maroccana

Salvia maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Salvia maroccana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
