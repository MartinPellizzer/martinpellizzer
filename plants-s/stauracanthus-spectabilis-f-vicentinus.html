Stauracanthus spectabilis f. vicentinus

Stauracanthus spectabilis f. vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentinus form of the showy stauracanthus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves and clusters of small, vibrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a humid, cool climate to flourish. Its unique foliage and floral display make it a prized addition to specialized botanical gardens.
