Solanum neoweberbaueri

Solanum neoweberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that contain the toxic alkaloids typical of the nightshade family.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
