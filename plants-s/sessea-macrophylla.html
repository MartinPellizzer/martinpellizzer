Sessea macrophylla

Sessea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a large, fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and its distinctive, showy red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
