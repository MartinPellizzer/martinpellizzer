Saussurea leveilleana

Saussurea leveilleana, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying high-altitude flora.
