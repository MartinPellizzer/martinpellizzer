Schizanthus grahamii

Schizanthus grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's love-lies-bleeding, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, and it is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
