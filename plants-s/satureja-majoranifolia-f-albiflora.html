Satureja majoranifolia f. albiflora

Satureja majoranifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower savory, is a botanical variant of the savory plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary uses and as a medicinal herb due to its essential oils.

The whiteflower form distinguishes it from other varieties of Satureja by its distinctive floral coloration.
