Syringa lilac var. alba

Syringa lilac var. alba, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers well-drained soil.

The white lilac is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy spring display.
