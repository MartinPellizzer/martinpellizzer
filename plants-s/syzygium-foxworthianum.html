Syzygium foxworthianum

Syzygium foxworthianum, commonly known as the Foxworth's apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a slightly waxy texture.

Syzygium foxworthianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
