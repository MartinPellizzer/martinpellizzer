Selaginella kansuensis

Selaginella kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu spikemoss, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. Native to arid regions of China, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions through its specialized morphology and physiology.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
