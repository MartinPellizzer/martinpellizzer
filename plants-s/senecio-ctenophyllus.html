Senecio ctenophyllus

Senecio ctenophyllus, commonly known as the featherleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
