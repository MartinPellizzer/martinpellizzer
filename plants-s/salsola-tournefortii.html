Salsola tournefortii

Salsola tournefortii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has spiny leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits. It is native to Eurasia but has been introduced to many other regions, often becoming an invasive species.

Its extensive root system allows it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought.
