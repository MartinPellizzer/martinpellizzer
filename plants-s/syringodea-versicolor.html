Syringodea versicolor

Syringodea versicolor, commonly known as the variable flame violet, is a member of the Violaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinctive color pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Syringodea versicolor is noted for its variable flower colors, ranging from white to pink and purple, which contribute to its common name.
