Solidago hispida var. disjuncta

Solidago hispida var. disjuncta, commonly known as the prickly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Its showy inflorescence makes it a valuable plant for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
