Silene tubiformis

Silene tubiformis, commonly known as the tubular catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Silene tubiformis thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
