Sagina × media

Sagina × media, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, along paths, or in disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for environments with partial shade to full sun.
