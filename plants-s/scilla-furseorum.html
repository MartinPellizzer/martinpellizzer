Scilla furseorum

Scilla furseorum, commonly known as the Furse's squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known bulbous plants.

Scilla furseorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
