Silphium compositum var. ovatifolium

Silphium compositum var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaved compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately divided leaves that are ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Due to its historical significance and ecological value, it is considered a valuable species for native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
