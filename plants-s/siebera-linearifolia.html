Siebera linearifolia

Siebera linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf siebera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
