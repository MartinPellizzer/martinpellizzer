Solanum nigricans

Solanum nigricans, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. The plant features small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum nigricans is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
