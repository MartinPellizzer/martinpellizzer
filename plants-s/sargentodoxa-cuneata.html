Sargentodoxa cuneata

Sargentodoxa cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking red, lantern-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Sargentodoxa cuneata is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
