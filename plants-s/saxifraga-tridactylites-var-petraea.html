Saxifraga tridactylites var. petraea

Saxifraga tridactylites var. petraea, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, dark green leaves with three lobes. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late summer, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Saxifraga tridactylites var. petraea is often found in mountainous areas and is a hardy species that can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
