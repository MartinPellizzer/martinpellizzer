Spiesia algida

Spiesia algida, commonly known as the cold mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of South America.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to high altitudes, where it withstands low temperatures and strong winds. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or nutrient-poor soils.

Spiesia algida plays a role in stabilizing mountain ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
