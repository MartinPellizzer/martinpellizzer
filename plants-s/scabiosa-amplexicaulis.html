Scabiosa amplexicaulis

Scabiosa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the heart's ease or rose trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescence of small, pink or white flowers that resemble tiny roses. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are clasping around the stems, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Scabiosa amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
