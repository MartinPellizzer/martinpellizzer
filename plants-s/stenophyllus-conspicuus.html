Stenophyllus conspicuus

Stenophyllus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy fern, is a species of fern characterized by its striking, elongated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. Its fronds are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stenophyllus conspicuus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
