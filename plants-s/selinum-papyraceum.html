Selinum papyraceum

Selinum papyraceum, commonly known as the paperleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble paper, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in complex, branched clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Selinum papyraceum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
