Sorbus corymbifera var. turbinata

Sorbus corymbifera var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often flattened or turban-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and bears small, white flowers in corymbs during the spring. Its fruits are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also serves as a valuable source of nectar for pollinators.

The species is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its habitat.
