Salix richardsonii var. angustifolia

Salix richardsonii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, which are pollinated by wind.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
