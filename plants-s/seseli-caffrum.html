Seseli caffrum

Seseli caffrum, commonly known as the coffee seseli, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in local ecosystems.
