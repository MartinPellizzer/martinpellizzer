Saranthe klotzschiana

Saranthe klotzschiana, commonly known as the Klotzschian lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with six petals and a prominent central stamen, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
