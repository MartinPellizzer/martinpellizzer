Salvia × cernavodae

Salvia × cernavodae, commonly known as the Cernavoda sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Romania.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
