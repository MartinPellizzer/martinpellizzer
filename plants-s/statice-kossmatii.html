Statice kossmatii

Statice kossmatii, commonly known as the Kossmatii glass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive blue or purple hue, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice kossmatii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
