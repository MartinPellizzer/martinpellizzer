Scopolia mutica

Scopolia mutica, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a tuberous root system and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Scopolia mutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of traditional medicinal use.
