Setaria viridis f. maxima

Setaria viridis f. maxima, commonly known as giant green millet, is a variant of the common green millet, characterized by its larger size and robust growth.

It is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that contain small, triangular grains. It is adapted to thrive in warm climates and can tolerate drought conditions.

This form is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a minor grain crop in certain regions.
