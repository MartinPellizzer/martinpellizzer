Spiraea × mollis

Spiraea × mollis, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea × mollis is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
