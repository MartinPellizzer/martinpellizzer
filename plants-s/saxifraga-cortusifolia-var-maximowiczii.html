Saxifraga cortusifolia var. maximowiczii

Saxifraga cortusifolia var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Maximowicz's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
