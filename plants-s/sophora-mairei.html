Sophora mairei

Sophora mairei, commonly known as the Maire Sophora, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its striking yellow flowers that appear in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
