Salix hoffmanniana

Salix hoffmanniana, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands or along rivers.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping in suitable climates.
