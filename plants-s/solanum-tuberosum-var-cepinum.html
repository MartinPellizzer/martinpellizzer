Solanum tuberosum var. cepinum

Solanum tuberosum var. cepinum, commonly known as the purple potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple skin and flesh, which can range from deep purple to reddish hues. This variety is native to the Andes region of South America, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with stolons that produce tubers underground.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
