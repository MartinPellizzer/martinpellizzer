Samolus subnudicaulis

Samolus subnudicaulis, commonly known as the slender samolus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Samolus subnudicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
