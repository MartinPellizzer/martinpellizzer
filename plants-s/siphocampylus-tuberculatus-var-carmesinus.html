Siphocampylus tuberculatus var. carmesinus

Siphocampylus tuberculatus var. carmesinus, commonly known as the Carmesin bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
