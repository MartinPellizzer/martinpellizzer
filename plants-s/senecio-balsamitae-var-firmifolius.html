Senecio balsamitae var. firmifolius

Senecio balsamitae var. firmifolius, commonly known as the firm-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
