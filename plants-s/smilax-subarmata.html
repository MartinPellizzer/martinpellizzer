Smilax subarmata

Smilax subarmata, commonly known as the red-tailed smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Smilax subarmata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
