Saxifraga doyalana

Saxifraga doyalana, commonly known as the Doyal's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with fleshy, basal leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Saxifraga doyalana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
