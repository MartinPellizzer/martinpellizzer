Senna pilifera var. tubata

Senna pilifera var. tubata, commonly known as the tubular senna, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hair-like structures, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
