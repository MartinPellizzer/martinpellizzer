Senecio papuanus

Senecio papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Senecio papuanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
