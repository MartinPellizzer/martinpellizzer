Sedum laxum subsp. latifolium

Sedum laxum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features fleshy, upright stems and thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it well-suited for arid environments.
