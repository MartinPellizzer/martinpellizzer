Spiraea prunifolia var. simpliciflora

Spiraea prunifolia var. simpliciflora, commonly known as the simpleflower weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that cascade with clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, and the plant has a compact, rounded growth habit. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for use in borders, hedges, or as a specimen plant.
