Sauropus similis var. microphylla

Sauropus similis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically small, measuring up to 5 cm in length, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
