Scutellaria ocmulgee

Scutellaria ocmulgee, commonly known as Ocmulgee bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Scutellaria ocmulgee is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
