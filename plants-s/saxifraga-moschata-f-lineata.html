Saxifraga moschata f. lineata

Saxifraga moschata f. lineata, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, fragrant leaves and showy white or pink flowers.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage features a unique variegation pattern, with a pale green or white margin, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
