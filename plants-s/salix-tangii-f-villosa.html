Salix tangii f. villosa

Salix tangii f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and grayish-brown bark. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a hairy underside that gives the plant its common name. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.
