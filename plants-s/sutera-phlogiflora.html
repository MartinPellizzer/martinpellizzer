Sutera phlogiflora

Sutera phlogiflora, commonly known as the flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
