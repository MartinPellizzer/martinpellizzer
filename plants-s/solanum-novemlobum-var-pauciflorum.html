Solanum novemlobum var. pauciflorum

Solanum novemlobum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the nine-lobed nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive nine-lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that change color from green to red as they mature, often containing toxic alkaloids. It is native to parts of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
