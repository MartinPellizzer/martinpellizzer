Selaginella subcalcarata

Selaginella subcalcarata, commonly known as the mountain spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its branches.

Selaginella subcalcarata is well adapted to cool, humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
