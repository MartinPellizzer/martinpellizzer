Salix arguta var. erythrocoma

Salix arguta var. erythrocoma, commonly known as the red-hair willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in the fall, and is often found in riparian zones.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it an important component of its ecological habitat.
