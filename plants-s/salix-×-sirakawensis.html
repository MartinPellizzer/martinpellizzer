Salix × sirakawensis

Salix × sirakawensis, commonly known as the Sirakawensis willow, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
