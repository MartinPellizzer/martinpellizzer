Silene spathulata

Silene spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Silene spathulata is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
