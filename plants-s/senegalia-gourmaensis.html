Senegalia gourmaensis

Senegalia gourmaensis, commonly known as the Gourma acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often shed during the dry season, and its fruit is a pod containing several seeds.

Senegalia gourmaensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local vegetation and a source of browse for livestock.
