Stillingia salpingadenia var. salicina

Stillingia salpingadenia var. salicina, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a species native to the western coast of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of orange-red berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
