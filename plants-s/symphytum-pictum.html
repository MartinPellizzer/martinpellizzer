Symphytum pictum

Symphytum pictum, commonly known as the painted comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Symphytum pictum has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
