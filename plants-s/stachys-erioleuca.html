Stachys erioleuca

Stachys erioleuca, commonly known as the white-hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of small, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
