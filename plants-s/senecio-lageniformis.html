Senecio lageniformis

Senecio lageniformis, commonly known as the balloon plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
