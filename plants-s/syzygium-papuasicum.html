Syzygium papuasicum

Syzygium papuasicum, commonly known as the papuan apple, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy dark green leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The fruit is a round, fleshy berry with a sweet and sour taste, often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
