Sesamum indicum var. grandidentatum

Sesamum indicum var. grandidentatum, commonly known as the giant African bean, is a variant of the sesame plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and elongated, slender seed pods. The plant produces seeds that are smaller than typical sesame seeds but are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in certain African countries.
