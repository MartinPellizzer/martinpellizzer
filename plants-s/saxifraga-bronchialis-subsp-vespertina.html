Saxifraga bronchialis subsp. vespertina

Saxifraga bronchialis subsp.

vespertina, commonly known as the evening saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late summer.

It is characterized by its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
