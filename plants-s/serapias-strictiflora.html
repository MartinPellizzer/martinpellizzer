Serapias strictiflora

Serapias strictiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are often arranged in a dense cluster and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Serapias strictiflora is a perennial herb that is relatively rare and has specific ecological requirements, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
