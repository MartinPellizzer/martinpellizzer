Stachys valentina

Stachys valentina, commonly known as Valentina mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty fragrance, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Stachys valentina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.
