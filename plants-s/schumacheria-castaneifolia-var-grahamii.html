Schumacheria castaneifolia var. grahamii

Schumacheria castaneifolia var. grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This varietal is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
