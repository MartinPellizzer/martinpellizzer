Sophronitis purpurata

Sophronitis purpurata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Sophronitis purpurata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
