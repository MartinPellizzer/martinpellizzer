Spyridium spadiceum var. majoranifolium

Spyridium spadiceum var. majoranifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved spyridium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This variety is distinguished by its larger, more robust leaves compared to other spyridium species.
