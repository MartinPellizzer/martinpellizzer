Saxifraga sedoides var. hohenwartiana

Saxifraga sedoides var. hohenwartiana, commonly known as the Hohenwart's saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous environments. The plant features upright stems with small, clustered leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in appearance. Its flowers are pink to red in color, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
