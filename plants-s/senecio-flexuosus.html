Senecio flexuosus

Senecio flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio flexuosus is adapted to withstand drought and full sun exposure.
