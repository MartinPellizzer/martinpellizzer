Solidago patula subsp. strictula

Solidago patula subsp.

strictula, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies strictula is distinguished by its more slender stems and narrower leaves compared to other goldenrod species.
