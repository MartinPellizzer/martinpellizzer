Salvia brachystemon

Salvia brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia brachystemon is also valued for its aromatic properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.
