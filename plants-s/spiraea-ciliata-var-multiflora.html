Spiraea ciliata var. multiflora

Spiraea ciliata var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins and a hairy texture.

Spiraea ciliata var. multiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
