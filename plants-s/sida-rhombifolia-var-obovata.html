Sida rhombifolia var. obovata

Sida rhombifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and coastal environments.
