Solanum leptosepalum

Solanum leptosepalum, commonly known as the slender-sepaled nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland forests and disturbed habitats.

The botanical features of Solanum leptosepalum make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
