Syngramma obtusifolia

Syngramma obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved syngramma, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
