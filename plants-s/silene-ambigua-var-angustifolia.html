Silene ambigua var. angustifolia

Silene ambigua var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and woodland edges.

This species is often found in open, disturbed habitats and is considered a delicate, herbaceous perennial.
