Solanum cuneifolium

Solanum cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or purple. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Solanum cuneifolium is part of the nightshade family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
