Securigera securidaca

Securigera securidaca, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with finely serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Securigera securidaca is also sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
