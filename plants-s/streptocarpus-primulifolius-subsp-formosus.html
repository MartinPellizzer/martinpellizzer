Streptocarpus primulifolius subsp. formosus

Streptocarpus primulifolius subsp.

formosus, commonly known as the Formosan streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its semi-succulent, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, often with a contrasting throat. It prefers well-drained soil and moderate light conditions, making it suitable for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant.
