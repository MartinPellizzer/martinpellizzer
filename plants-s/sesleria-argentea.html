Sesleria argentea

Sesleria argentea, commonly known as silver sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Sesleria argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
