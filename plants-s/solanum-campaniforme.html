Solanum campaniforme

Solanum campaniforme, commonly known as the bellflower nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum campaniforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
