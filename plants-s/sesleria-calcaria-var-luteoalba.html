Sesleria calcaria var. luteoalba

Sesleria calcaria var. luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky outcrops and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
