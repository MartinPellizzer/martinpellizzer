Smilax goudotiana

Smilax goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a prominent midrib and serrated edges.

Smilax goudotiana is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth areas.
