Streptanthus lasiophyllus

Streptanthus lasiophyllus, commonly known as the woolly podded wintercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like structures that mature in the summer.

Streptanthus lasiophyllus is often found in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
