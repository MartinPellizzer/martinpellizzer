Scutellaria edelbergii subsp. kunarensis

Scutellaria edelbergii subsp.

kunarensis, commonly known as the Kunarensis skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with two-lipped corollas typical of the mint family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
