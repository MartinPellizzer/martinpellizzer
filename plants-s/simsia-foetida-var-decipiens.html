Simsia foetida var. decipiens

Simsia foetida var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive simsa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
