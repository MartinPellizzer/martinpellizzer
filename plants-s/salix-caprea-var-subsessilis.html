Salix caprea var. subsessilis

Salix caprea var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a variant of the common willow found in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The leaves are narrow and sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
