Silene pseudoholopetala

Silene pseudoholopetala, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that appear almost ghostly in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Silene pseudoholopetala thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
