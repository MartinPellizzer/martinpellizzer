Streptanthus carinatus subsp. carinatus

Streptanthus carinatus subsp.

carinatus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bladderpod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and often peppery scent.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers in late spring.

Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated pod, a feature common among members of its family.
