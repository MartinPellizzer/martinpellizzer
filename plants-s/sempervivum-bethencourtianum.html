Sempervivum bethencourtianum

Sempervivum bethencourtianum, commonly known as the Bethencourtian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This hardy species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
