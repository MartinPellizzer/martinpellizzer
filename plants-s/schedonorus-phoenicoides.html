Schedonorus phoenicoides

Schedonorus phoenicoides, commonly known as purple grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and striking purple or pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.

Schedonorus phoenicoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
