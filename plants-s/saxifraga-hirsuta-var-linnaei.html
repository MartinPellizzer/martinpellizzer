Saxifraga hirsuta var. linnaei

Saxifraga hirsuta var. linnaei, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features succulent, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, high-altitude habitats.
