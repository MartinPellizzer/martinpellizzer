Schoenus rigidus

Schoenus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid sedges, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually green, with a rigid, upright growth habit. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.

Schoenus rigidus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
