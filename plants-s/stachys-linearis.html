Stachys linearis

Stachys linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear, narrow, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Stachys linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and can be used in native plant gardens.
