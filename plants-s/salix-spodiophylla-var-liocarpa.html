Salix spodiophylla var. liocarpa

Salix spodiophylla var. liocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
