Senecio lythroides

Senecio lythroides, commonly known as the bluegum lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
