Senecio pediculariifolius

Senecio pediculariifolius, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive tall, unbranched stem with a swollen base and narrow, linear leaves that resemble candle wicks. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
