Senna multijuga var. verrucosa

Senna multijuga var. verrucosa, commonly known as the verrucous senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a distinctive texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
