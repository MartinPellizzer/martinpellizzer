Scirpus hottentottus

Scirpus hottentottus, commonly known as the hottentot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Scirpus hottentottus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
