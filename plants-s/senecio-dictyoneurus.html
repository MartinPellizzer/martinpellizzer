Senecio dictyoneurus

Senecio dictyoneurus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a slender, branching stem that bears the inflorescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
