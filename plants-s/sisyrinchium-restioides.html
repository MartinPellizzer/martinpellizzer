Sisyrinchium restioides

Sisyrinchium restioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, well-drained conditions.

Sisyrinchium restioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
