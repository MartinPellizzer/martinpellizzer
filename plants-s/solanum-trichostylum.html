Solanum trichostylum

Solanum trichostylum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and spiny, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Solanum trichostylum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes many economically important plants.
