Secamone amygdalina

Secamone amygdalina, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other fragrant flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain a single seed.
