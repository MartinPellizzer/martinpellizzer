Scutellaria caudifolia

Scutellaria caudifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Scutellaria caudifolia is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks, thriving in partial to full shade.
