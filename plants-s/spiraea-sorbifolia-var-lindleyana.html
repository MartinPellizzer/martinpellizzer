Spiraea sorbifolia var. lindleyana

Spiraea sorbifolia var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
