Selenodesmium mandioccanum

Selenodesmium mandioccanum, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
