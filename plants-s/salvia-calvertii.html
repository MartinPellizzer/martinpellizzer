Salvia calvertii

Salvia calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Salvia calvertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
