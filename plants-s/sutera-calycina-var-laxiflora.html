Sutera calycina var. laxiflora

Sutera calycina var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower suture, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
