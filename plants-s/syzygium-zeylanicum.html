Syzygium zeylanicum

Syzygium zeylanicum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant is cultivated for its fragrant bark, which is used to produce cinnamon spice. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters tall.

Syzygium zeylanicum thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
