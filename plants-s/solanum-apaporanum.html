Solanum apaporanum

Solanum apaporanum, commonly known as the Apapa nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually yellow or red when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a notable species in its native habitats.
