Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. spathulatum

Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and prairies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
