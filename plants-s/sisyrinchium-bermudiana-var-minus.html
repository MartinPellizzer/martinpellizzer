Sisyrinchium bermudiana var. minus

Sisyrinchium bermudiana var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Bermuda.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
