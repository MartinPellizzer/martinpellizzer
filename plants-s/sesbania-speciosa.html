Sesbania speciosa

Sesbania speciosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, drooping inflorescences that bear showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display.

Sesbania speciosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
