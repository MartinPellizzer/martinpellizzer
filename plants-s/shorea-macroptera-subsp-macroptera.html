Shorea macroptera subsp. macroptera

Shorea macroptera subsp.

macroptera, commonly known as the Borneo ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its heavy, dark-colored heartwood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant exhibits typical tropical rainforest tree features, including buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is an important component of the forest ecosystem.
