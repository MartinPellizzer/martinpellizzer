Sorghum propinquum var. siamense

Sorghum propinquum var. siamense, commonly known as the Siam sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, typically in shades of purple or red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and as a source of forage.
