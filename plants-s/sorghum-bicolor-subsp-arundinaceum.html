Sorghum bicolor subsp. arundinaceum

Sorghum bicolor subsp.

arundinaceum, commonly known as giant millet, is a perennial grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stature and large, elongated leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers. Its rhizomes are thick and capable of vegetative propagation, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.

It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.
