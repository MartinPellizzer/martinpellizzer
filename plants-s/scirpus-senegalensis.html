Scirpus senegalensis

Scirpus senegalensis, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes contribute to its ability to spread vegetatively. Scirpus senegalensis has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

It is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
