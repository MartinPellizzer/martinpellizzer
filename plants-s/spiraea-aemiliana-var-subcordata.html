Spiraea aemiliana var. subcordata

Spiraea aemiliana var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features graceful, arching stems that bear clusters of delicate, white flowers in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, turning reddish in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring blooms and seasonal color.
