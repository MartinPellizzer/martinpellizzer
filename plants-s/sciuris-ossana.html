Sciuris ossana

Sciuris ossana, commonly known as the hairy antler fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, hairy fronds that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under dense forest canopies. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Sciuris ossana is considered a species of conservation concern.
