Seruneum gracile

Seruneum gracile, commonly known as the slender seruneum, is a delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Seruneum gracile is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
