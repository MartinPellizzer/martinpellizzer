Sanguisorba officinalis var. longifila

Sanguisorba officinalis var. longifila, commonly known as the long-stemmed sweet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
