Solanum dumetorum

Solanum dumetorum, commonly known as curly top tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or purple flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it has been reported as an invasive species in some regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for cultivation or consumption.
