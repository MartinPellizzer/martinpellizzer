Salix futura f. rufa

Salix futura f. rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed willow, is a variant of the willow species characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

The botanical features of Salix futura f. rufa make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
