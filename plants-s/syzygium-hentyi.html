Syzygium hentyi

Syzygium hentyi, commonly known as the Henty's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds, though they are not related. Syzygium hentyi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
