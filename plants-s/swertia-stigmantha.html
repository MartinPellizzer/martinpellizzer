Swertia stigmantha

Swertia stigmantha, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Swertia stigmantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
