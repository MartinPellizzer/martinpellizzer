Serawaia strobilifera

Serawaia strobilifera, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Serawaia strobilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
