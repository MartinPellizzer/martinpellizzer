Solanum sieberi

Solanum sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in moist, temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
