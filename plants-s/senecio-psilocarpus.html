Senecio psilocarpus

Senecio psilocarpus, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

While it is not psychoactive, it is closely related to species that contain psychoactive compounds.
