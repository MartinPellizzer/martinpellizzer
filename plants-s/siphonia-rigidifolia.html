Siphonia rigidifolia

Siphonia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid frond siphonia, is a marine red alga characterized by its robust, calcified thallus.

It typically grows in shallow, rocky coastal areas where it attaches to substrates such as stones or shells. The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, with thick, rigid branches that provide structural support. Its reproductive structures, called conceptacles, are embedded within the thallus and produce both male and female gametes.

Siphonia rigidifolia plays a role in coastal ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and serving as a habitat for small marine organisms.
