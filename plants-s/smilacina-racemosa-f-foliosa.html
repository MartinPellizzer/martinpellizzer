Smilacina racemosa f. foliosa

Smilacina racemosa f. foliosa, commonly known as the variegated greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its foliage is distinctive, featuring a variegated pattern of green and white on the leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
