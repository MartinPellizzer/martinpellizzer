Sida pulchella

Sida pulchella, commonly known as the blueweed or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Sida pulchella is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in various habitats.
