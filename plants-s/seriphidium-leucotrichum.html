Seriphidium leucotrichum

Seriphidium leucotrichum, commonly known as the white-haired tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant produces ray florets that are typically pale yellow to white in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Seriphidium leucotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
