Sobralia lowii

Sobralia lowii, commonly known as the Low's sobralia, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and the Greater Antilles.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, often displaying vivid colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Sobralia lowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
