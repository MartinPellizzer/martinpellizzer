Salix glaucophylloides

Salix glaucophylloides, commonly known as the glaucophylla willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant features slender, flexible stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive bluish-white underside. Its flowers are catkins, which are typical of willow species, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The botanical characteristics of Salix glaucophylloides make it an important species in ecological studies and potential ornamental horticulture.
