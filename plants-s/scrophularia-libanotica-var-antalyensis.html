Scrophularia libanotica var. antalyensis

Scrophularia libanotica var. antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
