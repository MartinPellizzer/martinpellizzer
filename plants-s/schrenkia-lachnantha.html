Schrenkia lachnantha

Schrenkia lachnantha, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, erect stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Schrenkia lachnantha is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary strategies for thriving in desert conditions.
