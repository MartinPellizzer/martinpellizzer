Solanum falcatum

Solanum falcatum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a yellow-orange color, containing toxic compounds such as solanine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation in gardens or around livestock.
