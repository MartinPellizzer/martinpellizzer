Sestinia ligustroides

Sestinia ligustroides, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Sestinia ligustroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
