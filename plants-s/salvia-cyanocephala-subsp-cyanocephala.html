Salvia cyanocephala subsp. cyanocephala

Salvia cyanocephala subsp.

cyanocephala, commonly known as blue-headed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and aromatic, gray-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and showy blossoms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
