Stanhopea graveolens var. concolor

Stanhopea graveolens var. concolor, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
