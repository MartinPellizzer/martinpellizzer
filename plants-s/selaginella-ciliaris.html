Selaginella ciliaris

Selaginella ciliaris, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features whorled leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Selaginella ciliaris is well adapted to arid environments and can survive prolonged periods of drought.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
