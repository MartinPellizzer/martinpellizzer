Syzygium cathayense

Syzygium cathayense, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to yellow, sour-tasting fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Syzygium cathayense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and parks.
