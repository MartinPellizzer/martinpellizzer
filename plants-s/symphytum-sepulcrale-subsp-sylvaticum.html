Symphytum sepulcrale subsp. sylvaticum

Symphytum sepulcrale subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet or blue flowers. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which help it store water and nutrients.

It is part of the Boraginaceae family and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
