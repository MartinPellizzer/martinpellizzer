Scorzonera lipschitzii

Scorzonera lipschitzii, commonly known as the Armenian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and dark green, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera lipschitzii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
