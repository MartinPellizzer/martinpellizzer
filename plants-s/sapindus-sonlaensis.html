Sapindus sonlaensis

Sapindus sonlaensis, commonly known as the Thai soapberry tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its fruit contains a hard, woody endocarp that is used in traditional medicine and as a natural soap substitute.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
