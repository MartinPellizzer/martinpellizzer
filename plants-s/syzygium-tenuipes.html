Syzygium tenuipes

Syzygium tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and indigenous communities. Syzygium tenuipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
