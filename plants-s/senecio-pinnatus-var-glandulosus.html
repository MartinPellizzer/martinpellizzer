Senecio pinnatus var. glandulosus

Senecio pinnatus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular pinnate senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage with pinnate leaves that have glandular structures along the veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid regions as a drought-tolerant plant.
