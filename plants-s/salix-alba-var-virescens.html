Salix alba var. virescens

Salix alba var. virescens, commonly known as the green willow, is a variant of the white willow characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features long, slender leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing a striking visual contrast to the pale green bark. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
