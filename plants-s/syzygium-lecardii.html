Syzygium lecardii

Syzygium lecardii, commonly known as the LeCard's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, round, red or yellow fruits that resemble apples, hence its common name. It produces fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

Syzygium lecardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
