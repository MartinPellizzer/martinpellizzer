Scrophularia major

Scrophularia major, commonly known as greater woundwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
