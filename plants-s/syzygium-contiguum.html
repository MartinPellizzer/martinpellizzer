Syzygium contiguum

Syzygium contiguum, commonly known as the Australian apple or white apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, round, yellow or red fruits.

Syzygium contiguum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
