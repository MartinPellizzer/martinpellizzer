Sophiopsis flavissima

Sophiopsis flavissima, commonly known as the yellow sophora, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Sophiopsis flavissima is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
