Scorzonera czerepanovii

Scorzonera czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Scorzonera czerepanovii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
