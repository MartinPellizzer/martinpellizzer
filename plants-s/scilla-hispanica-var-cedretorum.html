Scilla hispanica var. cedretorum

Scilla hispanica var. cedretorum, commonly known as the Spanish squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

The variety cedretorum is distinguished by its slightly different flower coloration and growth form compared to the typical species.
