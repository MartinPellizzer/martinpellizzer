Suaeda fruticosa var. crassifolia

Suaeda fruticosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, succulent foliage and compact growth habit, which help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and coastal areas.

The thick, fleshy leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
