Stachys melissifolia

Stachys melissifolia, commonly known as honeyflower or lemon beeplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Stachys melissifolia is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas.
