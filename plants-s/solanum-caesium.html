Solanum caesium

Solanum caesium, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used as ornamental plants due to their vibrant appearance. Solanum caesium thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both cultivation and ecological studies.
