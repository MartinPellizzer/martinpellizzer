Syzygium baronii

Syzygium baronii, commonly known as the Baron's tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Syzygium baronii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
