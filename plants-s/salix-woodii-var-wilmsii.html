Salix woodii var. wilmsii

Salix woodii var. wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii willow, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellow-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. This variety is closely related to other members of the Salix genus, which includes many willow species known for their adaptability to various environments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas, such as along streams or in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
