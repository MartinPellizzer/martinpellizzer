Styrax erymophyllus

Styrax erymophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces smooth, gray-barked stems and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, glossy seeds.

Styrax erymophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
