Senecio bullatus

Senecio bullatus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
