Senecio plantaginifolius

Senecio plantaginifolius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant. Its foliage is typically dark green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Senecio plantaginifolius is known for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the year under proper care.
