Setaria italica var. praecox

Setaria italica var. praecox, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a small-grained cereal grass native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of long, bristly inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop for arid climates. Its seeds are harvested when the grains are dry and mature, typically in late summer or early autumn.

The plant has been cultivated for thousands of years and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing environments.
