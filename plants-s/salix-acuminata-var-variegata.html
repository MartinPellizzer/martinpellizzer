Salix acuminata var. variegata

Salix acuminata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its striking foliage.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as a small, weeping tree with long, cascading branches that create a soft, flowing appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features or in shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic beauty and ability to provide shade and habitat for wildlife.
