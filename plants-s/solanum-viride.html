Solanum viride

Solanum viride, commonly known as green nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers, which are followed by small, green berries that turn red when mature. Solanum viride is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic compounds that make it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
