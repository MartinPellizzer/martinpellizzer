Silene lucida

Silene lucida, commonly known as the shining catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Silene lucida is known for its short life cycle and is often found in disturbed soils.
