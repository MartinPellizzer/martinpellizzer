Scirpus dissitus

Scirpus dissitus, commonly known as the spreading bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters that emerge from the upper part of the stem. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus dissitus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
