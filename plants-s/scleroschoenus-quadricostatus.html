Scleroschoenus quadricostatus

Scleroschoenus quadricostatus, commonly known as the four-costate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a dense, upright cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
