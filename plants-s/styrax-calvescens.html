Styrax calvescens

Styrax calvescens, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

Styrax calvescens is valued for its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a common ornamental and timber species.
