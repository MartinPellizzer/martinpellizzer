Schizolobium parahyba var. amazonicum

Schizolobium parahyba var. amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil in tropical rainforests. It is also valued for its durable wood and is used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
