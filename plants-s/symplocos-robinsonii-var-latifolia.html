Symplocos robinsonii var. latifolia

Symplocos robinsonii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and leathery. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
