Sogerianthe cupuliformis

Sogerianthe cupuliformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-like structure formed by the fusion of its petals and sepals, creating a protective hood over the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately preserved species in its natural ecosystem.
