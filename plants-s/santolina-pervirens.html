Santolina pervirens

Santolina pervirens, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-gray, aromatic foliage that is densely packed and highly ornamental. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Santolina pervirens is also valued for its use in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
