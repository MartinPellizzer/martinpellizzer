Solanum decorticans var. laurinum

Solanum decorticans var. laurinum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitaya, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to fleshy, red or orange berries. The berries are edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
