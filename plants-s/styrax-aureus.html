Styrax aureus

Styrax aureus, commonly known as golden styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn bright yellow in the autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often exuding a resinous sap.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal.
