Spirorhynchus sabulosus

Spirorhynchus sabulosus, commonly known as the sand spire, is a unique flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy and saline environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh coastal conditions. Its root system is specialized for stabilizing loose soils and preventing erosion.

Spirorhynchus sabulosus plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
