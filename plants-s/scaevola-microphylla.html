Scaevola microphylla

Scaevola microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
