Sebastiania corniculata var. incana

Sebastiania corniculata var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
