Samyda dodecandra

Samyda dodecandra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and greyish, with a faintly aromatic scent.
