Salsola esterhuyseniae

Salsola esterhuyseniae, commonly known as Esterhuysenia saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and stabilize sandy soils.

Salsola esterhuyseniae is adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
