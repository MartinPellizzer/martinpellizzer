Sambucus nigra var. luteovariegata

Sambucus nigra var. luteovariegata, commonly known as the yellow-leaf elderberry, is a variant of the European elderberry.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of yellow and green hues. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in winter.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
