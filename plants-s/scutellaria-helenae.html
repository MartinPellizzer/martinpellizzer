Scutellaria helenae

Scutellaria helenae, commonly known as Helen's bluebug, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scutellaria helenae is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is a perennial herb that is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
