Stigmatocarpum copticum

Stigmatocarpum copticum, commonly known as the Egyptian date palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Nile Delta region of Egypt and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which resembles a date, is edible and has been historically significant in Egyptian culture.

The botanical features of S. copticum make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
