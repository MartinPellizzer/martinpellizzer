Solanum eickii

Solanum eickii, commonly known as the Eick's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a berry that contains toxic alkaloids, making it harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum eickii is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.
