Solanum dibrachiatum

Solanum dibrachiatum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, berry-like fruits. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum dibrachiatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
