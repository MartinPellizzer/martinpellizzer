Sideritis × crisogonii

Sideritis × crisogonii, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis × crisogonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
