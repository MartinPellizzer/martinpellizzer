Salix glacialis

Salix glacialis, commonly known as the glacier willow, is a small shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. This plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. Its growth is adapted to low temperatures and short growing seasons.

Salix glacialis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
