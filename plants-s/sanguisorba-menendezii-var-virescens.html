Sanguisorba menendezii var. virescens

Sanguisorba menendezii var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf burnet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
