Senecio margaritae

Senecio margaritae, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio margaritae is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
