Simocheilus flosculosus

Simocheilus flosculosus, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
