Satyrium princeps

Satyrium princeps, commonly known as the Prince's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive hood-like structure. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the spring season.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
