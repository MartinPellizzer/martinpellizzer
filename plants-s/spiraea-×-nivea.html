Spiraea × nivea

Spiraea × nivea, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded form and its showy, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are produced in inflorescences called corymbs, which give the plant its distinctive appearance.

Spiraea × nivea is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8 and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
