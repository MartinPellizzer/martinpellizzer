Sanguisorba altissima

Sanguisorba altissima, commonly known as the tall bloodwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces dark red to black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

Sanguisorba altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing food for birds.
