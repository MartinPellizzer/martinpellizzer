Symplocos adenophylla var. atrata

Symplocos adenophylla var. atrata, commonly known as black-fruited evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are white and clustered, often appearing in the spring.

The berries are edible but not widely consumed due to their tart flavor.
