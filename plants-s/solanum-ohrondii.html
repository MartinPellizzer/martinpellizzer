Solanum ohrondii

Solanum ohrondii, commonly known as the Armenian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
