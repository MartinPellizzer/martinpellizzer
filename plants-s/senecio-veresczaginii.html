Senecio veresczaginii

Senecio veresczaginii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

Senecio veresczaginii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and resilience.
