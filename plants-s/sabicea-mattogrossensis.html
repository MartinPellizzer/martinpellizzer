Sabicea mattogrossensis

Sabicea mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
