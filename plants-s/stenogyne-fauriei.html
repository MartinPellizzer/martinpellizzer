Stenogyne fauriei

Stenogyne fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Stenogyne fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
