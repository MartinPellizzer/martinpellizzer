Solidago nemoralis var. longipetiolata

Solidago nemoralis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with long petioles, giving it its distinguishing name.

The showy yellow inflorescences consist of numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
