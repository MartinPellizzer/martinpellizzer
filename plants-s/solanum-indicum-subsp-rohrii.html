Solanum indicum subsp. rohrii

Solanum indicum subsp.

rohrii, commonly known as the Rohri nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This subspecies is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
