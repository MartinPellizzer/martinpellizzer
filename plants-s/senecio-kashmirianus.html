Senecio kashmirianus

Senecio kashmirianus, commonly known as the Kashmir chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
