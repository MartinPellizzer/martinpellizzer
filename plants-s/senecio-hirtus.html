Senecio hirtus

Senecio hirtus, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features woolly, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions of its native range.
