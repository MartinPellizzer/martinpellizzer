Saccolabium minimiflorum

Saccolabium minimiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Saccolabium minimiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
