Scutellaria teucriifolia

Scutellaria teucriifolia, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, and it often grows in moist, woodland habitats.

Scutellaria teucriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
