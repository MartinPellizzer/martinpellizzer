Selago ramosissima

Selago ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of swamps.
