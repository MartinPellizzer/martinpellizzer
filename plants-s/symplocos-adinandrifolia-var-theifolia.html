Symplocos adinandrifolia var. theifolia

Symplocos adinandrifolia var. theifolia, commonly known as the thief's tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its common name reflects its historical use in rituals and its association with the theft of fruits by animals.
