Syzygium michelii

Syzygium michelii, commonly known as the Micheli's apple or the black apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dark red to black when mature. Syzygium michelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
