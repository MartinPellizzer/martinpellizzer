Spraguea umbellata var. caudicifera

Spraguea umbellata var. caudicifera, commonly known as the long-stemmed spraguea, is a variant of the spraguea umbellata species.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stem that can grow several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers at the top of the stem. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
