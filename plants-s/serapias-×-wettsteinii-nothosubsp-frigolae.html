Serapias × wettsteinii nothosubsp. frigolae

Serapias × wettsteinii nothosubsp.

frigolae, commonly known as the alpine marsh gentian, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
