Stevia elongata var. caracasana

Stevia elongata var. caracasana, commonly known as Caracas stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and as a natural sweetener alternative.
