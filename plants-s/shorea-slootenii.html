Shorea slootenii

Shorea slootenii, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and orange-red fruits that contain winged seeds.
