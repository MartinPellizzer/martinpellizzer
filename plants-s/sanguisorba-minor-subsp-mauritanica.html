Sanguisorba minor subsp. mauritanica

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania sorrel, is a perennial herb native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
