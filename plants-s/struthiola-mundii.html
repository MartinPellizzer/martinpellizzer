Struthiola mundii

Struthiola mundii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions. Its leaves are small, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Struthiola mundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
