Sarothamnus catalaunicus var. malacitanus

Sarothamnus catalaunicus var. malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
