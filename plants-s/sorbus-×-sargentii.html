Sorbus × sargentii

Sorbus × sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to China.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as pomes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.

The leaves are typically oval or elliptical with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
