Salix × latifolia

Salix × latifolia, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree characterized by its long, cascading branches that drape like curtains.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, Europe, and North America. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, inconspicuous catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

The weeping willow is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly found near water bodies.
