Setaria panicea subsp. ambigua

Setaria panicea subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate waterlogged conditions.
