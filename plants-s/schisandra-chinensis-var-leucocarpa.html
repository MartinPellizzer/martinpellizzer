Schisandra chinensis var. leucocarpa

Schisandra chinensis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited five-flavor plant, is a rare variant of the five-flavor schisandra.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan and Sichuan provinces. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-white berries, which differ from the typical red or orange berries of the standard variety. It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The leaves are oval and dark green, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant thrives in cool, humid environments.
