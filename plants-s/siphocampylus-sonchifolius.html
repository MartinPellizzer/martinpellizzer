Siphocampylus sonchifolius

Siphocampylus sonchifolius, commonly known as the yellowhead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Siphocampylus sonchifolius is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
