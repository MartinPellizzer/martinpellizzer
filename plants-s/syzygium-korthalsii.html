Syzygium korthalsii

Syzygium korthalsii, commonly known as the Indian star fruit or carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by the characteristic five-lobed, yellow-orange fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a pointed tip.

Syzygium korthalsii is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in medicine.
