Selenicereus macdonaldiae

Selenicereus macdonaldiae, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its striking, night-blooming flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a fragrant scent.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
