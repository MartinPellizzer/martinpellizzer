Scorzonera calcitrapifolia var. decumbens

Scorzonera calcitrapifolia var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
