Solanum schwackeanum

Solanum schwackeanum, commonly known as the Schwacke's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

Solanum schwackeanum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
