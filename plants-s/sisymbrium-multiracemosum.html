Sisymbrium multiracemosum

Sisymbrium multiracemosum, commonly known as the many-branched broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to members of the mustard family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often concealed beneath its dense, leafless stems. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and survival of its host plants.
