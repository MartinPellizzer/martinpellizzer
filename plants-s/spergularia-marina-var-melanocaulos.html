Spergularia marina var. melanocaulos

Spergularia marina var. melanocaulos, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in coastal environments, particularly in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in saline and arid conditions.

This variety is often found in tidal zones and is a characteristic component of coastal vegetation in regions such as the British Isles and the Mediterranean.
