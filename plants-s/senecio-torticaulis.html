Senecio torticaulis

Senecio torticaulis, commonly known as the curlydock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that curl inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Senecio torticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
