Senecio erucifolius subsp. grandidentatus

Senecio erucifolius subsp.

grandidentatus, commonly known as the granddentate groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features hairy, alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with prominent teeth along the margins.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
