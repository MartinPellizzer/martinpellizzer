Sisyrinchium hintoniorum

Sisyrinchium hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Sisyrinchium, which includes species commonly referred to as blue-eyed grasses due to their striking blue flowers. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
