Salvia × skofitzii

Salvia × skofitzii, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is aromatic, with a minty scent, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Salvia × skofitzii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
