Sisymbrium sophia var. brachycarpum

Sisymbrium sophia var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrowpod wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique, which is a defining feature of the varietal name "brachycarpum.".

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
