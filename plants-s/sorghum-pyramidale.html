Sorghum pyramidale

Sorghum pyramidale, commonly known as the giant broomstick sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender panicles that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its grain or as a forage crop.

Its fibrous stalks are also used in traditional crafts and construction.
