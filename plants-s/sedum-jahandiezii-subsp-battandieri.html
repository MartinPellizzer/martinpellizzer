Sedum jahandiezii subsp. battandieri

Sedum jahandiezii subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the Persian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Iran. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a bluish hue, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
