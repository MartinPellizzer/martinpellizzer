Silene orsiniana

Silene orsiniana, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene orsiniana is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
