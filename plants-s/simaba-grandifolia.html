Simaba grandifolia

Simaba grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and dark, dense wood. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is often cultivated for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
