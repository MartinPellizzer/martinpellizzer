Stephania herbacea

Stephania herbacea, commonly known as the slender stephania, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries. Stephania herbacea is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and stems used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
