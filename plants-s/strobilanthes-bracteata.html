Strobilanthes bracteata

Strobilanthes bracteata, commonly known as the "Bottlebrush Plant," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which resembles a bottlebrush due to its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Strobilanthes bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
