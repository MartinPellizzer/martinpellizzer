Sinobambusa tootsik f. maeshimana

Sinobambusa tootsik f. maeshimana, commonly known as the Maeshimana bamboo, is a rare and ornamental bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that are often tinged with a pale blue or pink hue. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of white flowers, which appear in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

This bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
