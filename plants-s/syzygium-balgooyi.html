Syzygium balgooyi

Syzygium balgooyi, commonly known as the Balgooyi apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Syzygium balgooyi is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
