Scrophularia khorassanica

Scrophularia khorassanica, commonly known as Persian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
