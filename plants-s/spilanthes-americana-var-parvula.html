Spilanthes americana var. parvula

Spilanthes americana var. parvula, commonly known as the smallflower pungentweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its flowers are characterized by their pungent, mustard-like odor, which is a distinctive botanical trait.

The species is notable for its chemical composition, containing various sulfur-containing compounds that contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.
