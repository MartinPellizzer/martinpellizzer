Scilla natalensis

Scilla natalensis, commonly known as Natal squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, white berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla natalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
