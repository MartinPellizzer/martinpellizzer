Sphaerocionium elasticum

Sphaerocionium elasticum, commonly known as the elastic bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its inflated, sac-like structures called bladders.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, which the plant then digests for nutrients. The plant is typically found in shallow, freshwater environments where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its morphology includes slender, branching stems and specialized bladders that aid in both buoyancy and predation.

Sphaerocionium elasticum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
