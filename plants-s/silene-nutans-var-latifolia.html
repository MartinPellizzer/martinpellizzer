Silene nutans var. latifolia

Silene nutans var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hound's-tongue, is a flowering plant in the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant has a long flowering period and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
