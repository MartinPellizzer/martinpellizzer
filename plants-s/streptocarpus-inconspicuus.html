Streptocarpus inconspicuus

Streptocarpus inconspicuus, commonly known as the small-stemmed streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and attractive, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet or blue flowers that are borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Streptocarpus inconspicuus is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
