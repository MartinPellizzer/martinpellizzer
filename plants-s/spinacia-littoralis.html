Spinacia littoralis

Spinacia littoralis, commonly known as coastal spinach, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and coastal restoration projects.
