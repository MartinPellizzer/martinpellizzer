Solanum soestii

Solanum soestii, commonly known as the Soest's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are a food source for certain birds and mammals.

Solanum soestii is valued in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
