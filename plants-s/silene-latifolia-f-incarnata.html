Silene latifolia f. incarnata

Silene latifolia f. incarnata, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking pink to red flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
