Smilacina japonica var. lutecarpa

Smilacina japonica var. lutecarpa, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Japanese climbing plant, is a variant of the Smilacina genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
