Spiraea japonica var. glabra

Spiraea japonica var. glabra, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that form a weeping habit, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and graceful appearance make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
