Solanum aviculare var. grandiflorum

Solanum aviculare var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the evening. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
