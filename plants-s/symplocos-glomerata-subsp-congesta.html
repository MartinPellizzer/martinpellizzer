Symplocos glomerata subsp. congesta

Symplocos glomerata subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered waxleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
