Setaria gracilis f. longiseta

Setaria gracilis f. longiseta, commonly known as slender foxtail grass, is a variant of the grass species Setaria gracilis.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose panicle. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
