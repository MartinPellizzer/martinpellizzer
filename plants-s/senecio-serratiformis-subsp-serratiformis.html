Senecio serratiformis subsp. serratiformis

Senecio serratiformis subsp.

serratiformis, commonly known as the serratedleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, linear leaves that are often serrated along the edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, elongated inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
