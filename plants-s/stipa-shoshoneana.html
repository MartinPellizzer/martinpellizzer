Stipa shoshoneana

Stipa shoshoneana, commonly known as the Shoshone needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Stipa shoshoneana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
