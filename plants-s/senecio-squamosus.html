Senecio squamosus

Senecio squamosus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Senecio squamosus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
