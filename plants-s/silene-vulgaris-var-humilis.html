Silene vulgaris var. humilis

Silene vulgaris var. humilis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or lesser catchfly, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, wastelands, and disturbed soils.

Silene vulgaris var. humilis is often found in open areas and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
