Schefflera wardii

Schefflera wardii, commonly known as the Kew schefflera, is a tropical plant native to the island of Kew in Malaysia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Schefflera wardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
