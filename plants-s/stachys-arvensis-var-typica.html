Stachys arvensis var. typica

Stachys arvensis var. typica, commonly known as field betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Field betony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming and antispasmodic effects.
