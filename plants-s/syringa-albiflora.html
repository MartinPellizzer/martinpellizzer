Syringa albiflora

Syringa albiflora, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa albiflora is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
