Succisa pratensis f. pinnatifida

Succisa pratensis f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variant is distinguished by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves, which give it a unique appearance compared to the typical form of Succisa pratensis.
