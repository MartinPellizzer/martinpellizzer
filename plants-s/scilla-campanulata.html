Scilla campanulata

Scilla campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its elegant, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early spring. Scilla campanulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.
