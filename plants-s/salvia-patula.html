Salvia patula

Salvia patula, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia patula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
