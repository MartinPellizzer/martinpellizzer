Scutellaria farsistanica

Scutellaria farsistanica, commonly known as the Persian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Scutellaria farsistanica is often found in rocky or semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local biodiversity.
