Staurogyne kamerunensis subsp. calabarensis

Staurogyne kamerunensis subsp.

calabarensis, commonly known as the Cameroon water primrose, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and rivers.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and ecological health in its habitat.
