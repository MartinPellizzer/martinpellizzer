Sopubia densiflora

Sopubia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered sopubia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Sopubia densiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
