Senecio cymbalarioides var. aphanactis

Senecio cymbalarioides var. aphanactis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
