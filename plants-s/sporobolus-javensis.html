Sporobolus javensis

Sporobolus javensis, commonly known as Java sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in disturbed or saline environments.

Sporobolus javensis is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
