Senna septemtrionalis

Senna septemtrionalis, commonly known as the northern senna, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Senna septemtrionalis is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in coastal and sandy environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal dunes.
