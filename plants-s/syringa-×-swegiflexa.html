Syringa × swegiflexa

Syringa × swegiflexa, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that cascade from long, drooping branches, creating a visually stunning display. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, with a rounded, spreading form. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and lance-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

Syringa × swegiflexa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
