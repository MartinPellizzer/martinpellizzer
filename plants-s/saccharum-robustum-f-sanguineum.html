Saccharum robustum f. sanguineum

Saccharum robustum f. sanguineum, commonly known as the red bamboo cane grass, is a variant of the robust cane grass native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking red-brown inflorescences, which distinguish it from other members of the Saccharum genus. It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant's culms are hollow and can reach significant heights, making it a notable species in its ecological niche.

While not widely cultivated for sugar production, it holds ornamental value and is studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
