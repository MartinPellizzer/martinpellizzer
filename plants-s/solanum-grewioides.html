Solanum grewioides

Solanum grewioides, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries, which are often red when mature. Solanum grewioides belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
