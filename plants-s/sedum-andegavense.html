Sedum andegavense

Sedum andegavense, commonly known as the Andegavense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in coastal cliffs and dunes.

Sedum andegavense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
