Scilla dimartinoi

Scilla dimartinoi, commonly known as the Sicilian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal areas. Scilla dimartinoi is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
