Sarracenia jonesii f. viridescens

Sarracenia jonesii f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a variant of Sarracenia jonesii, characterized by its distinctive green pitchers that contrast with the typical red or orange hues of the species. The plant's foliage consists of elongated, tubular leaves that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with upright stems that produce multiple pitchers during the growing season.

The f. viridescens form is prized by horticulturists for its unique coloration and ease of cultivation.
