Sanguisorba ancistroides var. typica

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
