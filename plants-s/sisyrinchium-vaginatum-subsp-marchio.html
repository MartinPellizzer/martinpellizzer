Sisyrinchium vaginatum subsp. marchio

Sisyrinchium vaginatum subsp.

marchio, commonly known as the March's blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Cyperaceae family.
