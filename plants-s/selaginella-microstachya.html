Selaginella microstachya

Selaginella microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems and produce spores.

Selaginella microstachya is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
