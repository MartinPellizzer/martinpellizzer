Symphyopappus casarettoi

Symphyopappus casarettoi, commonly known as the Desert Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and reduced leaf structure. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
