Stachys graveolens

Stachys graveolens, commonly known as betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Betony is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it has a long history of use in herbal medicine.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
