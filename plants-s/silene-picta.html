Silene picta

Silene picta, commonly known as the painted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene picta is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
