Sabina cabiancae

Sabina cabiancae, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family, which includes cypress and juniper species. The plant is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

Sabina cabiancae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
