Senecio crataegifolius

Senecio crataegifolius, commonly known as the thorned groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and small, serrated leaves that resemble those of hawthorn. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Senecio crataegifolius is adapted to arid conditions and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
