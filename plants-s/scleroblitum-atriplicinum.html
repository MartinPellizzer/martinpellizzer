Scleroblitum atriplicinum

Scleroblitum atriplicinum, commonly known as the black sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Scleroblitum atriplicinum is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
