Scilla pancration

Scilla pancration, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Scilla pancration is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental plants.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
