Serratula monardii var. alcalae

Serratula monardii var. alcalae, commonly known as the alkaline serratula, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, alkaline soils, often found in desert or semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.
