Syzygium schumannianum

Syzygium schumannianum, commonly known as the Schumann's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their aromatic properties and have been utilized in various cultural practices.

Syzygium schumannianum is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
