Saxifraga sommieri

Saxifraga sommieri, commonly known as the summer saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, which is reflected in its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga sommieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
