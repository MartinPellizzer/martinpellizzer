Silene amassiensis

Silene amassiensis, commonly known as the Amassia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

Silene amassiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
