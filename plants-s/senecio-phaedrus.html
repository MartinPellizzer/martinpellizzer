Senecio phaedrus

Senecio phaedrus, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Senecio phaedrus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
