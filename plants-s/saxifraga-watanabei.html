Saxifraga watanabei

Saxifraga watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display. The plant typically features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems, and bloom during the late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga watanabei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
