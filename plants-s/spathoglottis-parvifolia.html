Spathoglottis parvifolia

Spathoglottis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Spathoglottis parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
