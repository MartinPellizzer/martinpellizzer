Solanum benguelense

Solanum benguelense, commonly known as the Benguela nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Solanum benguelense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
