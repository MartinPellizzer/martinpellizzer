Satanocrater paradoxa

Satanocrater paradoxa, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a rare and striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiny, and often twisted stems that resemble a skeletal structure. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the leaves.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
