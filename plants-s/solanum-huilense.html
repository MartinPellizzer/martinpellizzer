Solanum huilense

Solanum huilense, commonly known as the Huilense nightshade, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Solanum huilense is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
