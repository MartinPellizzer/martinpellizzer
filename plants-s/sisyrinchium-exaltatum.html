Sisyrinchium exaltatum

Sisyrinchium exaltatum, commonly known as the tall blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium exaltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
