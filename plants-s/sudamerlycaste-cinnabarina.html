Sudamerlycaste cinnabarina

Sudamerlycaste cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals with contrasting yellow spots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
