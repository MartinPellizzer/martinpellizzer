Securidaca fallax

Securidaca fallax, commonly known as the African spurge or false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
