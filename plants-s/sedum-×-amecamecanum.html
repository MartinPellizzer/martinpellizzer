Sedum × amecamecanum

Sedum × amecamecanum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among gardeners.
