Sparganium boreale

Sparganium boreale, commonly known as the northern bur reed, is a herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and the edges of lakes and ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in small, spiky structures that give it a distinctive appearance.
