Solanum memecylonoides subsp. finisterrae

Solanum memecylonoides subsp.

finisterrae, commonly known as the Canary Islands nightshade, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid and sunny conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
