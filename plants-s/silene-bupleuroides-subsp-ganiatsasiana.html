Silene bupleuroides subsp. ganiatsasiana

Silene bupleuroides subsp.

ganiatsasiana, commonly known as the Caucasian silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
