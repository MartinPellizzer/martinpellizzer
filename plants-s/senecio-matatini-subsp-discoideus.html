Senecio matatini subsp. discoideus

Senecio matatini subsp.

discoideus, commonly known as the "Discoideus Groundsel," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with a waxy coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
