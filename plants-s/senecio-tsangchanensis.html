Senecio tsangchanensis

Senecio tsangchanensis, commonly known as the Tsangchanensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
