Smilax elegantissima

Smilax elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant smilax, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, waxy, yellow-green to reddish stems with sharp, hooked spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Smilax elegantissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
