Scrophularia ebulifolia

Scrophularia ebulifolia, commonly known as the marsh scrophularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, often calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Scrophularia ebulifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
