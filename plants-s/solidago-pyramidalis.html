Solidago pyramidalis

Solidago pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense, pyramidal arrangement of leaves. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences in late summer to early autumn, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Solidago pyramidalis plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and enhancing biodiversity.
