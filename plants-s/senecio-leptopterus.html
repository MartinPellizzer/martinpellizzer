Senecio leptopterus

Senecio leptopterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often narrowed at the base. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio leptopterus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
