Sorbus fansipanensis

Sorbus fansipanensis, commonly known as the Fan Mountain whitebeams, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the high altitudes of Vietnam.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
