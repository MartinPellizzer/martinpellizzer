Senecio quercifolius

Senecio quercifolius, commonly known as the oak-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio quercifolius is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
