Spinacia oleracea

Spinacia oleracea, commonly known as spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and reddish veins. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron, vitamins A and C, and antioxidants. Spinach grows best in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth cycle makes it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial farming.
