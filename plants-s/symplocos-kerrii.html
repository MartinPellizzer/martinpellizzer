Symplocos kerrii

Symplocos kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's waxleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a whitish waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Symplocos kerrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
