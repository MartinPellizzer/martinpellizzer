Sarcocephalus cordatus var. glabra

Sarcocephalus cordatus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed African potato, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features erect, hairy stems that become smooth with age, and its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in treating various ailments.
