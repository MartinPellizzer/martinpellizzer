Submatucana intertexta

Submatucana intertexta, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
