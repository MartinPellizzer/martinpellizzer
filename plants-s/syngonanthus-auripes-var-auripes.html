Syngonanthus auripes var. auripes

Syngonanthus auripes var. auripes, commonly known as the golden-footed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, with yellow petals that attract pollinators.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
