Spiraea cantoniensis var. plena

Spiraea cantoniensis var. plena, commonly known as the Chinese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of showy, double white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely toothed, dark green leaves that turn red or bronze in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and compact form.
