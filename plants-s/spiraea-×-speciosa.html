Spiraea × speciosa

Spiraea × speciosa, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, arching branches that give it a graceful appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn yellow in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea × speciosa prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
