Stipa parvula

Stipa parvula, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
