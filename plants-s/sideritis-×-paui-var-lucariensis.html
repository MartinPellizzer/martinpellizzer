Sideritis × paui var. lucariensis

Sideritis × paui var. lucariensis, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
