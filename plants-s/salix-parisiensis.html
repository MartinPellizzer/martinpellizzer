Salix parisiensis

Salix parisiensis, commonly known as the Paris willow, is a rare and ancient species of willow native to the region around Paris, France.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green catkins. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, elongated, and have a finely toothed margin.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
