Salix kunyangensis

Salix kunyangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in the spring. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in riverbanks and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
