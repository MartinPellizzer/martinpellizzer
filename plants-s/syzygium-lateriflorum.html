Syzygium lateriflorum

Syzygium lateriflorum, commonly known as the satinwood or white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium lateriflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
