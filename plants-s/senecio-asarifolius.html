Senecio asarifolius

Senecio asarifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio asarifolius is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
