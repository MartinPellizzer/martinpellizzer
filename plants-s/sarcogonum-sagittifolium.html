Sarcogonum sagittifolium

Sarcogonum sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf sarcogonum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
