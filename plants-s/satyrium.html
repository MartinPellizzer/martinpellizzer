Satyrium

Satyrium, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The genus Satyrium includes several species, most of which are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic. These plants typically thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Their unique appearance and limited distribution make them highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
