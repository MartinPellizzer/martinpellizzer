Sasa takizawana subsp. nakashimana

Sasa takizawana subsp.

nakashimana, commonly known as the Nakashima sasa, is a bamboo species native to Japan. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit.

The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The culms are strong and hollow, making it a valuable resource for traditional crafts and construction.
