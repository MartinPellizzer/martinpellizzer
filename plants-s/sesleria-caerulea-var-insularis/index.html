Sesleria Caerulea Var. Insularis

Blue false brome, scientifically known as Sesleria caerulea var. insularis, is a strikingly beautiful grass species native to the British Isles. With its delicate blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit, it adds a touch of elegance to meadows and woodland edges. This variant of the common false brome is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded conditions. Its unique coloration and compact form make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure compared to more widely cultivated grasses.
