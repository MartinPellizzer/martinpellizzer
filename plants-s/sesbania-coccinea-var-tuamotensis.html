Sesbania coccinea var. tuamotensis

Sesbania coccinea var. tuamotensis, commonly known as the Tuamotu red sesbania, is a flowering plant native to the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its striking red flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in island gardens.
