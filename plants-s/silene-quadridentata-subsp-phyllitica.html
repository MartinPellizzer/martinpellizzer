Silene quadridentata subsp. phyllitica

Silene quadridentata subsp.

phyllitica, commonly known as the leafy four-toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers with four petals, which are typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and shaded stream banks.
