Salix stylaris var. lancifolia

Salix stylaris var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
