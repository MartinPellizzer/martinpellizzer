Siphocampylus verticillatus var. grandiflorens

Siphocampylus verticillatus var. grandiflorens, commonly known as the giant whorled milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators. The plant has a milky sap that is typical of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
