Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant contains a compound called silymarin, found primarily in its seeds, which is known for its hepatoprotective properties. Silybum marianum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal uses and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
