Sasa palmata f. yoshikawana

Sasa palmata f. yoshikawana, commonly known as Yoshikawa's bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa palmata species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming shrub that produces slender, bamboo-like stems with a smooth, often green or reddish-brown surface. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a fan-like pattern at the tips of the stems. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
