Salvia chrysophylla

Salvia chrysophylla, commonly known as golden leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer, giving them a velvety texture.

Salvia chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
