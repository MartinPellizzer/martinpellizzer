Senecio hypsobates var. parvulus

Senecio hypsobates var. parvulus, commonly known as the small-flowered sky-blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
