Satyrium secundum

Satyrium secundum, commonly known as the lesser purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Satyrium secundum plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
