Serjania brevipes

Serjania brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaneae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, black seeds.

Serjania brevipes is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
