Sorbus × floribunda var. typica

Sorbus × floribunda var. typica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
