Stellaria graminea var. hebecalyx

Stellaria graminea var. hebecalyx, commonly known as the bent-calyx starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and the flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

The species is notable for its fine texture and delicate appearance, making it a subtle addition to natural habitats.
