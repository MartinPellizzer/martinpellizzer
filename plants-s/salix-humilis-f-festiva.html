Salix humilis f. festiva

Salix humilis f. festiva, commonly known as the festive weeping willow, is a unique cultivar of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade in garden settings.
