Salvia neurepia

Salvia neurepia, commonly known as the violet sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Salvia neurepia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
