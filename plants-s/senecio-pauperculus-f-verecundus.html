Senecio pauperculus f. verecundus

Senecio pauperculus f. verecundus, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it an attractive and easy-to-care-for ornamental plant.
