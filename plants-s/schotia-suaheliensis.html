Schotia suaheliensis

Schotia suaheliensis, commonly known as the African silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Schotia suaheliensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
