Solanum aculeastrum var. conraui

Solanum aculeastrum var. conraui, commonly known as the Conraui nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
