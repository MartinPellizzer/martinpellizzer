Scleroxylon edule

Scleroxylon edule, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is valued for its fruit, which contains a rich seed used to produce shea butter. The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Its ecological and economic importance is significant, particularly in local communities where it provides both sustenance and income.
