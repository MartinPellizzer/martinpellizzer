Struthiola fasciata

Struthiola fasciata, commonly known as the spotted mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Struthiola fasciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
