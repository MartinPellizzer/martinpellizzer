Salvia rivularis

Salvia rivularis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
