Spiranthes trilineata var. thelymitra

Spiranthes trilineata var. thelymitra, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged flowers that form a delicate, three-lobed spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
