Sapium poggei

Sapium poggei, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Sapium poggei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
