Spiraea sorbifolia var. vulgaris

Spiraea sorbifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
