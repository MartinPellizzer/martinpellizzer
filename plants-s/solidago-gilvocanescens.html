Solidago gilvocanescens

Solidago gilvocanescens, commonly known as the yellowstem goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features tall, erect stems that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flower heads are golden-yellow, appearing in late summer to early fall, and are composed of many small, clustered florets.
