Salvia glabricaulis

Salvia glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
