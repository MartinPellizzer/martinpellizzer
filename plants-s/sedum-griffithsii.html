Sedum griffithsii

Sedum griffithsii, commonly known as the Griffiths' stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Sedum griffithsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
