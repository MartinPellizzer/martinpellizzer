Serjania racemosa f. glabriuscula

Serjania racemosa f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Brazilian plum or Brazilian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, orange-red berries. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, while the plant's ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for birds and other wildlife.
