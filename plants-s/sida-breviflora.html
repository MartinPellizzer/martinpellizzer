Sida breviflora

Sida breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered Sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant features small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with hairy surfaces. Sida breviflora is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
