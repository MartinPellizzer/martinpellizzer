Senecio don-candidus

Senecio don-candidus, commonly known as the White-veined Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive white or pale green veins. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio don-candidus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
