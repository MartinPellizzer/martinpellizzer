Salvia punicea

Salvia punicea, commonly known as the scarlet salvia, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its tall, upright stems can reach up to 3 feet in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia punicea is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
