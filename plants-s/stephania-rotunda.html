Stephania rotunda

Stephania rotunda, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach lengths of several meters.

Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
