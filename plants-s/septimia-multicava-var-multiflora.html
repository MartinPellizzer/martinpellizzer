Septimia multicava var. multiflora

Septimia multicava var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and drought tolerance.
