Solanum platanifolium

Solanum platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum platanifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
