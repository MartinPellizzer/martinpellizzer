Stipa dasyphylla var. glabrata

Stipa dasyphylla var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that produce a light, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
