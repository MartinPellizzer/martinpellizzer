Streptocarpus tanala

Streptocarpus tanala, commonly known as the Madagascar streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Streptocarpus tanala is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
