Sarcanthus kunstleri

Sarcanthus kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sarcanthus kunstleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
