Selinocarpus diffusus subsp. nevadensis

Selinocarpus diffusus subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada selinocarpus, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a creeping growth habit.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.
