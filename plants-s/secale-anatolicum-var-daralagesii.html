Secale anatolicum var. daralagesii

Secale anatolicum var. daralagesii, commonly known as the Anatolian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
