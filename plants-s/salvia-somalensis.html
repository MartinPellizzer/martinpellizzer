Salvia somalensis

Salvia somalensis, commonly known as Somali sage, is a perennial shrub native to the dry regions of Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Salvia somalensis is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
