Salvia ballotiflora

Salvia ballotiflora, commonly known as Portuguese thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular ornamental plant.
