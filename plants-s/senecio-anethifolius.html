Senecio anethifolius

Senecio anethifolius, commonly known as the anise-leaved groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, anise-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
