Solanum hydroides

Solanum hydroides, commonly known as the waterleaf or watermelon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to edible, fleshy berries resembling watermelons in appearance. Its leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Solanum hydroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
