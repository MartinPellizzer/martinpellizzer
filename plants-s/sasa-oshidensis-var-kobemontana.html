Sasa oshidensis var. kobemontana

Sasa oshidensis var. kobemontana, commonly known as the Kobemontana bamboo, is a variant of the Oshidensis bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often green or slightly reddish in color. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and structural characteristics.
