Stylidium brunonianum subsp. minor

Stylidium brunonianum subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy flowers that resemble miniature orchids.

The plant typically produces yellowish-green to reddish-orange flowers with a unique, spurred structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
