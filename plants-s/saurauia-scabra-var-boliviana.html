Saurauia scabra var. boliviana

Saurauia scabra var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian roughleaf saurauia, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its rough, textured leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
