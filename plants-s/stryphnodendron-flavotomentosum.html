Stryphnodendron flavotomentosum

Stryphnodendron flavotomentosum, commonly known as the yellow-tomentose Brazil nut tree, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, yellowish pubescence. It produces woody pods containing seeds that are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts.

The tree also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
