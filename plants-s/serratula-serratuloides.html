Serratula serratuloides

Serratula serratuloides, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
