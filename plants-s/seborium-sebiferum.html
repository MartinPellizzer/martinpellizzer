Seborium sebiferum

Seborium sebiferum, commonly known as the wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that provide protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are known to exude a sticky substance, which can be harvested for various traditional uses.

The wax tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in tropical climates.
