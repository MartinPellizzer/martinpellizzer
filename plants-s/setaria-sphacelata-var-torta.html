Setaria sphacelata var. torta

Setaria sphacelata var. torta, commonly known as curled foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling spikelets that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence is a loose to dense panicle, often with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
