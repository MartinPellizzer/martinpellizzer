Saxifraga acerifolia

Saxifraga acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, maple-like leaves that are lobed and often have a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. Saxifraga acerifolia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
