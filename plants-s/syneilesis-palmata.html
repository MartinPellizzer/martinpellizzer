Syneilesis palmata

Syneilesis palmata, commonly known as the Mexican palm or the feather palm, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Syneilesis palmata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant foliage and resilience in warm climates.
