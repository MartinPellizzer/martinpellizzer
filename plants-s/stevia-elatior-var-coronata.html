Stevia elatior var. coronata

Stevia elatior var. coronata, commonly known as the crowned stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, giving it a crown-like appearance. It is well-known for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, making it a popular natural sweetener.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and valuable medicinal properties.
