Senecio crassiflorus var. parviflorus

Senecio crassiflorus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered stiffleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, stiff leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
