Statice tragacanthina

Statice tragacanthina, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of purple, pink, or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it well adapted to harsh, windy conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice tragacanthina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
