Sorbus monticola

Sorbus monticola, commonly known as the mountain whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in rocky or forested habitats at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,500 meters.

Sorbus monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
