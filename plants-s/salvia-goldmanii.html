Salvia goldmanii

Salvia goldmanii, commonly known as golden sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slight hairy texture and produces spikes of bright golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia goldmanii is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
