Smilax syringoides

Smilax syringoides, commonly known as the fragrant smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Smilax syringoides is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
