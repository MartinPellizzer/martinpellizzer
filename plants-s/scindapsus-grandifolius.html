Scindapsus grandifolius

Scindapsus grandifolius, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves often exhibit variegation, with shades of green, cream, and yellow, making it a popular houseplant.

Scindapsus grandifolius prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil, and it is relatively easy to care for, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
