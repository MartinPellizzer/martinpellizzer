Silene hispanica

Silene hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

Silene hispanica is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.
