Senecio sarracenicus var. graniticus

Senecio sarracenicus var. graniticus, commonly known as the granite senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
