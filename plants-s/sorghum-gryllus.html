Sorghum gryllus

Sorghum gryllus, commonly known as the grasshopper sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species.

Sorghum gryllus is often used for forage and has ecological value in supporting local wildlife.
