Sophronitis grandis

Sophronitis grandis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its floral structure includes a prominent lip, which is often brightly colored to attract pollinators.

Sophronitis grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens worldwide.
