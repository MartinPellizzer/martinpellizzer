Sisymbrium canescens var. alpestre

Sisymbrium canescens var. alpestre, commonly known as alpine whitethorn, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
