Stevia jorullensis

Stevia jorullensis, commonly known as the Jorullensis sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its high sweetness and low caloric content, it has gained popularity as a natural sweetener alternative.
