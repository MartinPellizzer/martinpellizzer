Scutellaria orientalis subsp. bornmuelleri

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the eastern skullcap, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
