Syzygium taipingense

Syzygium taipingense, commonly known as the Taiping tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
