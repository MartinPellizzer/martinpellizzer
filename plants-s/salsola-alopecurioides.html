Salsola alopecurioides

Salsola alopecurioides, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Salsola alopecurioides plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
