Stachys pringlei

Stachys pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Stachys pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
