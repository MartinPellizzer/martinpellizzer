Solidago galetorum

Solidago galetorum, commonly known as mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow flower heads, which appear in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago galetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
