Solea parviflora

Solea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered solea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, preferring well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
