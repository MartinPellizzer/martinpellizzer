Syngramma paraphysata

Syngramma paraphysata, commonly known as the red-stemmed goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The stems are often reddish in color, which helps distinguish it from similar species.
