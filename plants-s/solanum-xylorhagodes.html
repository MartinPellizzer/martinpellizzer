Solanum xylorhagodes

Solanum xylorhagodes, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and have a distinctive shape.

The fruit is toxic and contains alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
