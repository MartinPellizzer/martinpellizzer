Synaphea diabolica

Synaphea diabolica, commonly known as the Devil's Spine, is a rare and striking botanical species characterized by its spiky, serpentine appearance.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a central stem with numerous branching spines that can grow up to several feet in length. Its unique morphology is adapted to conserve water and deter herbivores in its harsh environment.

Despite its intimidating look, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter for small animals and insects.
