Sparaxis tricolor var. griffinii

Sparaxis tricolor var. griffinii, commonly known as the Griffin's Three-color Sparaxis, is a South African bulbous plant characterized by its striking, three-colored flowers.

It typically produces upright, grass-like foliage and blooms in spring with vivid combinations of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is adapted to Mediterranean climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Sparaxis tricolor var. griffinii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
