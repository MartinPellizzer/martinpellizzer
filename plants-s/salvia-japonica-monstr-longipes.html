Salvia japonica monstr. longipes

Salvia japonica monstr.

longipes, commonly known as the Japanese sage with long stems, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorls along its elongated, upright stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique floral structure and extended flowering period make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental plants.
