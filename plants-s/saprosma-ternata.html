Saprosma ternata

Saprosma ternata, commonly known as the red-leafed saprosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
