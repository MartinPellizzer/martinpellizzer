Senecio looseri

Senecio looseri, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
