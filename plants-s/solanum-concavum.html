Solanum concavum

Solanum concavum, commonly known as the concave nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, mature to a dark purple color.

Solanum concavum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.
