Stevia fiebrigii var. vattuonei

Stevia fiebrigii var. vattuonei, commonly known as the Vattuone stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America, specifically found in the Andean regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food and pharmaceutical products. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it has gained attention as an alternative to artificial sweeteners.
