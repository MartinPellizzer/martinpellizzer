Sedum longipes subsp. rosulare

Sedum longipes subsp.

rosulare, commonly known as the Rosulate Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for arid and semi-arid environments.
