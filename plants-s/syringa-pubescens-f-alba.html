Syringa pubescens f. alba

Syringa pubescens f. alba, commonly known as the white woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
