Semiarundinaria nuspicula

Semiarundinaria nuspicula, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and long, arching leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation as well.
