Saxifraga spruneri

Saxifraga spruneri, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are small, usually pink or white, and arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Saxifraga spruneri is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
