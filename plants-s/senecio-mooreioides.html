Senecio mooreioides

Senecio mooreioides, commonly known as the Moore's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
