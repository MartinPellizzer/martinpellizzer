Sisyrinchium pauperculum

Sisyrinchium pauperculum, commonly known as the poorflower or slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, showy blue to purple flowers in early spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
