Spiraea rhodoclada

Spiraea rhodoclada, commonly known as the red-stemmed spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, reddish-brown stems that become more prominent as the plant matures. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

Spiraea rhodoclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
