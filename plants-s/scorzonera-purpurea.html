Scorzonera purpurea

Scorzonera purpurea, commonly known as the purple scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its purple or violet flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, while its root system is tuberous and somewhat edible.

Scorzonera purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
