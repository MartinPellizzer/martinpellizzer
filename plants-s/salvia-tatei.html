Salvia tatei

Salvia tatei, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Salvia tatei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
