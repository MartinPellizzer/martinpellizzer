Stamnorchis recurva

Stamnorchis recurva, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it possesses a complex reproductive system involving both self-pollination and cross-pollination.

Stamnorchis recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
