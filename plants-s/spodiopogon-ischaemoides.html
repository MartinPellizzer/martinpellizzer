Spodiopogon ischaemoides

Spodiopogon ischaemoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Spodiopogon ischaemoides is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
