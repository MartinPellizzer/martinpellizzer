Sabia pauciflora

Sabia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flower sabia, is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in nutrient-poor soils.

Sabia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
