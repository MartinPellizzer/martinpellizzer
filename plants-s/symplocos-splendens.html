Symplocos splendens

Symplocos splendens, commonly known as the shining evergreen or golden wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. Symplocos splendens thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
