Schinopsis quebracho-colorado

Schinopsis quebracho-colorado, commonly known as *Colorado quebracho*, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flattened seeds. It is well adapted to dry, arid environments and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
