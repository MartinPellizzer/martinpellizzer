Symplocos speciosa

Symplocos speciosa, commonly known as the showy waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Symplocos speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
