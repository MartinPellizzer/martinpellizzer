Scutellaria hintoniana

Scutellaria hintoniana, commonly known as Hinton's skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. Scutellaria hintoniana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
