Sarmasikia dodonaei

Sarmasikia dodonaei, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
