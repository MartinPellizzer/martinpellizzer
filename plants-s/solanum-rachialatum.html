Solanum rachialatum

Solanum rachialatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Solanum rachialatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
