Stachys attenuata

Stachys attenuata, commonly known as silverleaf mint, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and slender, ascending stems. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
