Senecio eurycephalus

Senecio eurycephalus, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Senecio eurycephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
