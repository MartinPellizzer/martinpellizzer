Stellaria nemorum subsp. nemorum

Stellaria nemorum subsp.

nemorum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features small, white star-shaped flowers and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground.

The leaves are typically rounded and hairy, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator species in shaded, moist habitats.
