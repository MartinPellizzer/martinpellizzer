Sisymbrium hirsutum

Sisymbrium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy brookweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and has hairy stems and leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Sisymbrium hirsutum is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.
