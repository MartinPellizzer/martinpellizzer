Stictocardia jucunda

Stictocardia jucunda, commonly known as the dwarf moss cushion, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green to reddish-brown coloration. The plant lacks true leaves but has leaf-like structures called phyllidia that are closely arranged. It reproduces through spores released from capsule-like structures called sporophytes.

Stictocardia jucunda is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms.
