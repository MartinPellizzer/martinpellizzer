Saussurea leontopodium

Saussurea leontopodium, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are surrounded by the fuzzy, white bracts. It thrives in harsh environments with low temperatures and high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and association with alpine beauty, it is often symbolized in literature and art.
