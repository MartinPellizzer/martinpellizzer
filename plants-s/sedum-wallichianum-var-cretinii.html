Sedum wallichianum var. cretinii

Sedum wallichianum var. cretinii, commonly known as the Cretin's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
