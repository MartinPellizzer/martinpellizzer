Stylidium debile

Stylidium debile, commonly known as the slender sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Stylidium debile is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with a short growth cycle to cope with seasonal droughts.
