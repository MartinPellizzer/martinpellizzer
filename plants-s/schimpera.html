Schimpera

Schimpera, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in saline or coastal environments, thriving in areas with high salt content. Schimpera species are characterized by their succulent, often fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in their survival in arid conditions. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
