Schoenoplectus ohwianus

Schoenoplectus ohwianus, commonly known as the Chinese bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clusters at the base.

Schoenoplectus ohwianus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
