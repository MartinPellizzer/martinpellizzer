Sasa surugensis

Sasa surugensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or surugensis sasa, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa surugensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions of eastern Asia.
