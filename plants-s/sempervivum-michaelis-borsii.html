Sempervivum michaelis-borsii

Sempervivum michaelis-borsii, commonly known as the Borsii's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental plant in rock gardens and containers.
