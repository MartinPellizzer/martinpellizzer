Solanum graveolens var. bunburyi

Solanum graveolens var. bunburyi, commonly known as the Bunbury's tomato, is a variant of the common tomato plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to southern Australia. Its leaves are usually broad and lobed, with a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated in home gardens for its ornamental and culinary uses.
