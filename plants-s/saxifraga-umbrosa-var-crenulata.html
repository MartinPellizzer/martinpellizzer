Saxifraga umbrosa var. crenulata

Saxifraga umbrosa var. crenulata, commonly known as the mossy saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded woodlands and rocky habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small, white or pinkish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
