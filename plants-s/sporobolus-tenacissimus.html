Sporobolus tenacissimus

Sporobolus tenacissimus, commonly known as blackburr, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and tufted growth habit, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with leathery, hairless spikelets that are often adorned with bristly awns. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that contributes to its invasive potential in disturbed soils.

Sporobolus tenacissimus is adapted to poor soil conditions and is often found in dry, rocky areas or along roadsides.
