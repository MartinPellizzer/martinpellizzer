Scirpus nervosus var. campanus

Scirpus nervosus var. campanus, commonly known as the bell-shaped bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a prominent midrib.

This species thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
