Strychnos pluvialis

Strychnos pluvialis, commonly known as the rain tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
