Schisandra sphenanthera

Schisandra sphenanthera, commonly known as the Chinese five-fruit or five-pear, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red, berry-like fruits that contain five seeds, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is known for its aromatic, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Schisandra sphenanthera is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
