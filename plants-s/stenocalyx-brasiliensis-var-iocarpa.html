Stenocalyx brasiliensis var. iocarpa

Stenocalyx brasiliensis var. iocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
