Sisymbrium taraxacifolium

Sisymbrium taraxacifolium, commonly known as the dandelionleaf mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a slender, branching stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of dandelions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
