Salvia algeriensis var. mariae

Salvia algeriensis var. mariae, commonly known as Algerian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
