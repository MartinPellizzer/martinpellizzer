Salvia verticillata

Salvia verticillata, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of showy violet to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to gardens and herbal collections.
