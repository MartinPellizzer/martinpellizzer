Stephegyne rotundifolia

Stephegyne rotundifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a taproot system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Stephegyne rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
