Senecio comberi

Senecio comberi, commonly known as the Comber's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
