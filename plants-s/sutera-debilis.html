Sutera debilis

Sutera debilis, commonly known as the weak sutera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sutera debilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
