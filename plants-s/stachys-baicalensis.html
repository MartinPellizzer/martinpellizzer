Stachys baicalensis

Stachys baicalensis, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its name derives from the resemblance of its flowers to a skullcap, giving it its common name.
