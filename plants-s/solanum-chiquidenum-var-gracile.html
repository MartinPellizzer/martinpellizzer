Solanum chiquidenum var. gracile

Solanum chiquidenum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
