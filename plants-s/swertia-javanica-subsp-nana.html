Swertia javanica subsp. nana

Swertia javanica subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf swertia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It typically reaches a height of no more than 10 centimeters, with a rosette of basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are characterized by their smooth, waxy surface and serrated margins.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
