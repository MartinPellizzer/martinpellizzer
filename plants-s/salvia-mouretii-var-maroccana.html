Salvia mouretii var. maroccana

Salvia mouretii var. maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan sage, is a perennial herb native to Morocco.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in Mediterranean climates.
