Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. vulgaris

Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
