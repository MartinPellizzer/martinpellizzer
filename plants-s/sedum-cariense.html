Sedum cariense

Sedum cariense, commonly known as the Carpathian stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a succulent species, characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Sedum cariense is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
