Salacia fluminensis

Salacia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries.

Salacia fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
