Soliva aculeata

Soliva aculeata, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and is often found in disturbed soils and dry habitats. It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Soliva aculeata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
