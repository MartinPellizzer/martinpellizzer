Styrax glabrescens var. pilosus

Styrax glabrescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky styrax, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate scent to its surroundings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
