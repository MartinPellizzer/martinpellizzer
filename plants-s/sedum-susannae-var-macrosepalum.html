Sedum susannae var. macrosepalum

Sedum susannae var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, rounded leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant clusters of yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by distinctive, showy seed pods. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to withstand harsh climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance landscaping.
