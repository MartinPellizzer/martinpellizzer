Solenantha

Solenantha, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Solenantha species are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas.

Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.
