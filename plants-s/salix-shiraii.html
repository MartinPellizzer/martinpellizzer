Salix shiraii

Salix shiraii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a species of willow native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Salix shiraii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
