Syzygium xanthostemifolium

Syzygium xanthostemifolium, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.
