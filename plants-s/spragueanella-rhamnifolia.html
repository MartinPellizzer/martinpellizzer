Spragueanella rhamnifolia

Spragueanella rhamnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf spragueanella, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters during the spring, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
