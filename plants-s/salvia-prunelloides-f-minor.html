Salvia prunelloides f. minor

Salvia prunelloides f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Mexican sage, is a compact, low-growing perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
