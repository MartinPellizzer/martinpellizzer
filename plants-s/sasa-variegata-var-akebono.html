Sasa variegata var. akebono

Sasa variegata var. akebono, commonly known as the Akebono bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features striking variegated foliage with white and green patterns on its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant grows in dense clusters, often forming thickets in moist, shaded forest environments. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant appearance and seasonal color changes.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
