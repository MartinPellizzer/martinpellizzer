Smilax staminea f. obtusata

Smilax staminea f. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is a perennial vine that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of forests. Its leaves are typically larger and more rounded compared to the typical form, with a smooth, leathery texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
