Symplocos pulchra subsp. hispidula

Symplocos pulchra subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces distinctive purple to black berries that ripen in the fall, attracting various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. Symplocos pulchra subsp.

hispidula is often found in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial shade.
