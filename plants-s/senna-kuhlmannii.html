Senna kuhlmannii

Senna kuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senna kuhlmannii typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.
