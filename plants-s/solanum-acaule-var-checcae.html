Solanum acaule var. checcae

Solanum acaule var. checcae, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a compact shrub native to parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with small, dark green leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellow berry that ripens in the fall.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
