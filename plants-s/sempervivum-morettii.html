Sempervivum morettii

Sempervivum morettii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive in harsh conditions.

Sempervivum morettii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
