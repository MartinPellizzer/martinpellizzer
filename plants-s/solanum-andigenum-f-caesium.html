Solanum andigenum f. caesium

Solanum andigenum f. caesium, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a variant of the Andean tomato native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
