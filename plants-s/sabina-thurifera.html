Sabina thurifera

Sabina thurifera, commonly known as the Turkish juniper, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested for their aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and traditional herbal uses.
