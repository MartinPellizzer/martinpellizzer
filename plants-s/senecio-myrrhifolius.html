Senecio myrrhifolius

Senecio myrrhifolius, commonly known as the myrrh-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Senecio myrrhifolius is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
