Syzygium oblanceolatum subsp. kihamense

Syzygium oblanceolatum subsp.

kihamense, commonly known as the East African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
