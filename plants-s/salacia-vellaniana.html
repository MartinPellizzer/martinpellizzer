Salacia vellaniana

Salacia vellaniana, commonly known as the velvet salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of the family. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salacia vellaniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
