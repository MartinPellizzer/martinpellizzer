Silene ovata

Silene ovata, commonly known as the oval catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive ovate-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene ovata is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
