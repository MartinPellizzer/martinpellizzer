Solidago glutinosa var. racemosa

Solidago glutinosa var. racemosa, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
