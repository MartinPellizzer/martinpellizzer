Symphoria albus

Symphoria albus, commonly known as the white symphoria, is a rare flowering plant native to the temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white petals and heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Symphoria albus thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
