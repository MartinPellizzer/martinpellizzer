Salvia pratensis var. dumetorum

Salvia pratensis var. dumetorum, commonly known as purple meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
