Schoenoplectus mucronatus var. ishizawae

Schoenoplectus mucronatus var. ishizawae, commonly known as the Ishizawa's bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to specific regions of Japan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of this variant are notable for their adaptation to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, supporting local biodiversity.
