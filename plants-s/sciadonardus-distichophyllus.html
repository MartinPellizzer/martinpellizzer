Sciadonardus distichophyllus

Sciadonardus distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf scale plant, is a fossilized plant from the Devonian period.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, scale-like leaves arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant is considered one of the earliest known vascular plants, indicating early evolutionary developments in plant structure. Its fossils are typically found in sedimentary rocks in regions that were once ancient seas.

The study of Sciadonardus distichophyllus provides valuable insights into the early diversification of land plants.
