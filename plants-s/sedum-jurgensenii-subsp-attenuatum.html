Sedum jurgensenii subsp. attenuatum

Sedum jurgensenii subsp.

attenuatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
