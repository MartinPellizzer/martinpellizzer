Soldanella hungarica

Soldanella hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often reddish in color.

Soldanella hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
