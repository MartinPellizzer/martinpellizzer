Scrophularia bheriensis

Scrophularia bheriensis, commonly known as the Balkan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are tubular and typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
