Sphaeranthus zavattarii

Sphaeranthus zavattarii, commonly known as the Zavattari's globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky inflorescence that resembles a globe, composed of tightly packed blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
