Santiria glabra

Santiria glabra, commonly known as white santiria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Santiria glabra is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
