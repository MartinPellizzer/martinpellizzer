Silene sericea var. crassifolia

Silene sericea var. crassifolia, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-green foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and adaptations to arid environments.
