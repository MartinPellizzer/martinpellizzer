Selaginella ecuadoriana

Selaginella ecuadoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with elongated, needle-like leaves that are spirally arranged along its stems. Selaginella ecuadoriana is known for its ability to survive in humid conditions and is often found in rocky or mossy habitats.

Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are positioned at the tips of its stems, contributing to its unique botanical profile.
