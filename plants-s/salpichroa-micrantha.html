Salpichroa micrantha

Salpichroa micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, while its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, rounded seeds.

Salpichroa micrantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with low nutrient availability.
