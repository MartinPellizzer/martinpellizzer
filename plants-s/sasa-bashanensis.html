Sasa bashanensis

Sasa bashanensis, commonly known as the Bashan bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sasa bashanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in Chinese horticulture.
