Strobilanthes cernua var. crenulata

Strobilanthes cernua var. crenulata, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a slightly wavy edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
