Stelis valvulosa

Stelis valvulosa, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with several nodes and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Stelis valvulosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific environmental requirements.
