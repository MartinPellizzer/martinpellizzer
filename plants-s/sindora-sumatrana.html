Sindora sumatrana

Sindora sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran sindora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
