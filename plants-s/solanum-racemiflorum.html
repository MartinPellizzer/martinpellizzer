Solanum racemiflorum

Solanum racemiflorum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers, which are followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral arrangement.
