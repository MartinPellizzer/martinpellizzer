Syzygium auriculatum

Syzygium auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet, and aromatic red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Syzygium auriculatum thrives in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both horticulture and biodiversity conservation.
