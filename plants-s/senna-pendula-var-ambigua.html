Senna pendula var. ambigua

Senna pendula var. ambigua, commonly known as the wavy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that are typically three to four lobed, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
