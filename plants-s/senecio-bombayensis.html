Senecio bombayensis

Senecio bombayensis, commonly known as Bombay groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are often woolly or hairy, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
