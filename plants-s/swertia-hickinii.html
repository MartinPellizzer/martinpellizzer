Swertia hickinii

Swertia hickinii, commonly known as the mountain swertia, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Asia, including regions of China, India, and Nepal.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia hickinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
