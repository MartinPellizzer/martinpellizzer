Salvia pectinifolia

Salvia pectinifolia, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
