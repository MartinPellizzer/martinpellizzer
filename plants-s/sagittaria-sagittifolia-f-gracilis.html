Sagittaria sagittifolia f. gracilis

Sagittaria sagittifolia f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive spongy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom above the water surface. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in regions with moderate to high humidity.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental water gardens.
