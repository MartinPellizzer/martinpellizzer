Solanum diplacanthum

Solanum diplacanthum, commonly known as the two-spined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, toxic berries and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces fruit that is highly poisonous to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
