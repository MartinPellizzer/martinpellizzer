Schisandra micrantha

Schisandra micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited five-flavor vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Schisandra micrantha is notable for its medicinal properties, with its berries containing various bioactive compounds.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and traditional medicinal purposes.
