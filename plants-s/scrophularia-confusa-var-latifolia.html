Scrophularia confusa var. latifolia

Scrophularia confusa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base, giving it its common name. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
