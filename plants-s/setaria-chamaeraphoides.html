Setaria chamaeraphoides

Setaria chamaeraphoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive silver-gray sheen on the foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in landscaping.
