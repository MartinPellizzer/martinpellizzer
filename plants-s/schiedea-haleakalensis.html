Schiedea haleakalensis

Schiedea haleakalensis, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a rare endemic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Schiedea haleakalensis thrives in the high-elevation, volcanic soils of the Haleakalā National Park.

Due to its limited range and habitat threats, it is listed as an endangered species.
