Symplocos flavescens var. flavescens

Symplocos flavescens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow alder, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
