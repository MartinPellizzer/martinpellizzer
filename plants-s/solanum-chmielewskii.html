Solanum chmielewskii

Solanum chmielewskii, commonly known as the Chmielewski's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
