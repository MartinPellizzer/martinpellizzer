Setaria alopecurus

Setaria alopecurus, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common sight in various habitats across its native range.
