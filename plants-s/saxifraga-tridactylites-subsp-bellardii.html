Saxifraga tridactylites subsp. bellardii

Saxifraga tridactylites subsp.

bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes.

The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive three-lobed shape. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a dense cluster at the end of the stem.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in habitats with limited soil and harsh weather conditions.
