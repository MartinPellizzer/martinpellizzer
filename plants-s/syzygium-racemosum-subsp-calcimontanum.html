Syzygium racemosum subsp. calcimontanum

Syzygium racemosum subsp.

calcimontanum, commonly known as the limestone apple or calcimontanum syzygium, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove.

The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found on limestone substrates, and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
