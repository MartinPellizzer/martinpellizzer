Syzygium batadamba

Syzygium batadamba, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo* in some regions, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces large, round fruits with thick, leathery rinds and segmented, juicy, sweet flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Syzygium batadamba is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
