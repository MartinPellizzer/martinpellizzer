Solanum adenobasis

Solanum adenobasis, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and are often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum adenobasis belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
