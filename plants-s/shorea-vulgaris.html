Shorea vulgaris

Shorea vulgaris, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard timber that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall with a straight trunk and smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing a single seed.
