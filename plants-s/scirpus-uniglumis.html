Scirpus uniglumis

Scirpus uniglumis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Scirpus uniglumis is also a valuable food source for waterfowl and other wetland wildlife.
