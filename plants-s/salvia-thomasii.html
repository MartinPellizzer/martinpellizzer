Salvia thomasii

Salvia thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant.

Salvia thomasii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
