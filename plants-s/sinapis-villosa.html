Sinapis villosa

Sinapis villosa, commonly known as hairy mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as small yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sinapis villosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
