Scirpus lacustris var. medius

Scirpus lacustris var. medius, commonly known as the slender marsh clubrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats. Scirpus lacustris var. medius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes and ponds.
