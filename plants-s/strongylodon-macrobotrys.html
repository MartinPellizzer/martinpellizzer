Strongylodon macrobotrys

Strongylodon macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant can grow up to 5 meters tall, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in its native habitat. Its stems are covered in soft, hairy ridges and contain a milky sap that can cause severe skin irritation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is also known as the daisy or sunflower family.
