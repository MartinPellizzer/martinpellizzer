Salvia strobilanthoides

Salvia strobilanthoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and taproot-like.

Salvia strobilanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
