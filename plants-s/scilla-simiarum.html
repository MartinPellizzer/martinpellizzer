Scilla simiarum

Scilla simiarum, commonly known as the "squirrel onion," is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers in early spring, which are often mistaken for crocuses. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spherical bulbs that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Scilla simiarum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal remedies.
