Salix caudata var. bryantiana

Salix caudata var. bryantiana, commonly known as the Bryant's willow, is a variant of the silky willow, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its flowers are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

The Bryant's willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
