Solidago fallax var. fallax

Solidago fallax var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough, serrated edge and a bluish-green color.

The showy yellow flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.
