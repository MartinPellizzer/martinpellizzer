Sambucus williamsii subsp. coreana

Sambucus williamsii subsp.

coreana, commonly known as the Korean elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of northeastern China. It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and is characterized by its compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
