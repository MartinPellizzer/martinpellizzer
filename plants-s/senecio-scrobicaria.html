Senecio scrobicaria

Senecio scrobicaria, commonly known as the meadow groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Senecio scrobicaria is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
