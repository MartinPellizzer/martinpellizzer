Synotis auriculata

Synotis auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Synotis auriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
