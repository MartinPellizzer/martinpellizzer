Satyrium diphyllum

Satyrium diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique two-leaved structure, with one leaf being significantly larger than the other. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that are often a mix of red, yellow, and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
