Setaria plurinervis

Setaria plurinervis, commonly known as the many-veined millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny grains. It is often cultivated as a forage crop and is also used in traditional medicine.

Setaria plurinervis is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
