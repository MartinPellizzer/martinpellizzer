Scirpus lacustris f. laxiflorus

Scirpus lacustris f. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush, characterized by its loosely arranged, slender flower spikes.

It is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats, particularly in temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This form is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and avian life.
