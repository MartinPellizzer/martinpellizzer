Saussurea tridactyla

Saussurea tridactyla, commonly known as the Himalayan tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Due to its habitat in extreme environments, it is a symbol of resilience and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
