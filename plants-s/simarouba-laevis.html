Simarouba laevis

Simarouba laevis, commonly known as the smooth simarouba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Simaroubaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
