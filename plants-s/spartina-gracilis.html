Spartina gracilis

Spartina gracilis, commonly known as slender cordgrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. This plant thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. Spartina gracilis has long, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
