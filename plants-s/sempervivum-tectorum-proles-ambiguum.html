Sempervivum tectorum proles ambiguum

Sempervivum tectorum proles ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous rosette sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge depending on light exposure. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This species is popular in rock gardens and as a low-maintenance ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
