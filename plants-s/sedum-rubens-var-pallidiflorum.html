Sedum rubens var. pallidiflorum

Sedum rubens var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial succulent native to arid and rocky habitats.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking easy-care, ornamental plants.
