Solidago varia

Solidago varia, commonly known as the variable goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall, are arranged in dense, branching clusters.

Solidago varia is notable for its adaptability and plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
