Sideritis romana subsp. purpurea

Sideritis romana subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
