Syngonanthus caulescens var. discretifolius

Syngonanthus caulescens var. discretifolius, commonly known as the discrete-leaf bamboo, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that grow in clusters, forming dense, upright clumps. The leaves are small, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stems, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
