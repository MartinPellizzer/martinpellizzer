Saussurea leveillei

Saussurea leveillei, commonly known as the Tibetan cloud willow, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
