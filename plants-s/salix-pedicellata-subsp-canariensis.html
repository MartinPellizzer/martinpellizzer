Salix pedicellata subsp. canariensis

Salix pedicellata subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary willow, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a pale underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in shaded woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
