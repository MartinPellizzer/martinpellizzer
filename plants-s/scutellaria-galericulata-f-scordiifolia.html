Scutellaria galericulata f. scordiifolia

Scutellaria galericulata f. scordiifolia, commonly known as the garlic scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often emit a mild garlic-like scent, which is a distinguishing trait.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
