Saussurea nuristanica

Saussurea nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky terrains. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
