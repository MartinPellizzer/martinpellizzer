Spinacia glabra

Spinacia glabra, commonly known as smooth spinach, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves that are typically heart-shaped and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

Smooth spinach is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C.
