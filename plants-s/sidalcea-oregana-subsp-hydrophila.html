Sidalcea oregana subsp. hydrophila

Sidalcea oregana subsp.

hydrophila, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It is an important nectar source for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
