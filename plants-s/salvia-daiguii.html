Salvia daiguii

Salvia daiguii, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Salvia daiguii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
