Syzygium quadribracteatum

Syzygium quadribracteatum, commonly known as the Four-bract Syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Syzygium quadribracteatum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
