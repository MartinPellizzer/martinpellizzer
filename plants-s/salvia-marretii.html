Salvia marretii

Salvia marretii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia marretii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
