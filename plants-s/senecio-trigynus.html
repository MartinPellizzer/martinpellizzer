Senecio trigynus

Senecio trigynus, commonly known as the Blueweed or Narrowleaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio trigynus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
