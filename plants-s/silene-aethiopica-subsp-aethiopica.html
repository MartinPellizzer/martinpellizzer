Silene aethiopica subsp. aethiopica

Silene aethiopica subsp.

aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous annual with simple, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The plant has a short stem and thrives in dry, sandy soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
