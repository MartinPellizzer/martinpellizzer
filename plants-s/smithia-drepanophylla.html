Smithia drepanophylla

Smithia drepanophylla, commonly known as the zigzag lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a distinct, branched growth pattern.

It is characterized by its foliose thallus, which is typically pale green to yellowish in color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its structure consists of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner.

Smithia drepanophylla plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
