Serapias parviflora f. lutescens

Serapias parviflora f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a hybrid form of the Serapias parviflora species.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. This plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring.

The f. lutescens form is notable for its vibrant coloration and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
