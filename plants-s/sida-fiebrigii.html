Sida fiebrigii

Sida fiebrigii, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Sida fiebrigii is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional remedies.
