Sorghum semiberbe

Sorghum semiberbe, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both forage and ornamental purposes.
