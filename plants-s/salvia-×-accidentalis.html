Salvia × accidentalis

Salvia × accidentalis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Salvia × accidentalis is well-suited for gardens and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
