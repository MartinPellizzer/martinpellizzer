Soliva minuta

Soliva minuta, commonly known as dwarf soliva, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in gardens.
