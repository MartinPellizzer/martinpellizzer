Setaria flavida

Setaria flavida, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble a foxtail, typically appearing in late summer. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria flavida is both a weed and a forage plant, depending on the context in which it grows.
