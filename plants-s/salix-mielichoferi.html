Salix mielichoferi

Salix mielichoferi, commonly known as the honey-leaved willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-green leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent, particularly in the spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of pollination, and it is often found near water sources.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
