Senegalia podadenia

Senegalia podadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr shrub, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its seed pods are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

Senegalia podadenia is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
