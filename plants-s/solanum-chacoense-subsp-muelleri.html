Solanum chacoense subsp. muelleri

Solanum chacoense subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Chaco nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes.

The plant features elliptical leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit is a spherical berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in dry forests and scrublands.
