Solanum ochroneurum var. gracile

Solanum ochroneurum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces violet or purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
