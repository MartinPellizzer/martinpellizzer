Spathodea laurifolia

Spathodea laurifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical and glossy green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
