Stenophragma thalianum

Stenophragma thalianum, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in its native range.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its root system includes haustoria, which allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host.

Stenophragma thalianum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
