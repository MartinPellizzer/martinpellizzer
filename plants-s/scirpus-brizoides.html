Scirpus brizoides

Scirpus brizoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that develop into brown, cylindrical seed heads.

Scirpus brizoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
