Sapota pohlmaniana

Sapota pohlmaniana, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and southern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a smooth, yellowish fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in agroforestry systems.
