Solanum scindicum

Solanum scindicum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

Solanum scindicum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
