Silene subcorymbosa

Silene subcorymbosa, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene subcorymbosa is adapted to harsh alpine conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
