Sunipia viridis

Sunipia viridis, commonly known as the green sunipia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Sunipia viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
