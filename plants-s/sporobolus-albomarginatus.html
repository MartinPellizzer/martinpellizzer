Sporobolus albomarginatus

Sporobolus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Sporobolus albomarginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
