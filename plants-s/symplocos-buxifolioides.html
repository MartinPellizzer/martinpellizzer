Symplocos buxifolioides

Symplocos buxifolioides, commonly known as the glossy boxleaf evergreen, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
