Sabina chinensis var. kaizuca

Sabina chinensis var. kaizuca, commonly known as the Japanese thuja, is a variant of the Chinese thuja native to Japan.

It is a small evergreen conifer with a compact, pyramidal shape and dense foliage. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by blue-black berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
