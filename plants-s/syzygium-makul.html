Syzygium makul

Syzygium makul, commonly known as the Java apple or *Sala*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium makul thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its fruit is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
