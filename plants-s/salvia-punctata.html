Salvia punctata

Salvia punctata, commonly known as the spotted sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are gray-green with a fuzzy texture and often have distinctive white or pale markings. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
