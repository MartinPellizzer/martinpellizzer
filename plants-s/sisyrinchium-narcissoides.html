Sisyrinchium narcissoides

Sisyrinchium narcissoides, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Sisyrinchium, which includes several species of flowering plants commonly referred to as blue-eyed grasses. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The leaves are grass-like, slender, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its characteristic appearance.
