Schefflera elliptica var. obliquinervia

Schefflera elliptica var. obliquinervia, commonly known as the oblique-veined umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and large, elliptical, dark green leaves with a distinctive oblique venation pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its adaptability to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
