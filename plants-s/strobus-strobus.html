Strobus strobus

Strobus strobus, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the genus *Corylus* in the family Betulaceae. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The shrub typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
