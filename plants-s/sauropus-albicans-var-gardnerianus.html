Sauropus albicans var. gardnerianus

Sauropus albicans var. gardnerianus, commonly known as the white-leafed sauropus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white variegation on its lance-shaped leaves, which contrast sharply with the dark green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular in horticulture for indoor and outdoor gardens.
