Stephegyne africana

Stephegyne africana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
