Symplocos anomala var. nitida

Symplocos anomala var. nitida, commonly known as the glossy flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
