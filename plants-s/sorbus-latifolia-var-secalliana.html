Sorbus latifolia var. secalliana

Sorbus latifolia var. secalliana, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, persistent berries that provide food for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
