Salmasia guianensis

Salmasia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan salvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
