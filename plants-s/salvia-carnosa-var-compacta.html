Salvia carnosa var. compacta

Salvia carnosa var. compacta, commonly known as the compact sage, is a compact, perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces vibrant violet to purple flower spikes that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
