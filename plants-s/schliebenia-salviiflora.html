Schliebenia salviiflora

Schliebenia salviiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The bark of Schliebenia salviiflora is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.
