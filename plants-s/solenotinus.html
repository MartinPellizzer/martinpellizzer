Solenotinus

Solenotinus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with long, slender roots and a single, pale green or white flower. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Solenotinus is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
