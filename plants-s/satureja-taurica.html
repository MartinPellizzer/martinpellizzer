Satureja taurica

Satureja taurica, commonly known as Caucasian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Satureja taurica is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
