Setaria verticillata f. ambigua

Setaria verticillata f. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous fingergrass, is a variant of the common fingergrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to different habitats, though it is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
