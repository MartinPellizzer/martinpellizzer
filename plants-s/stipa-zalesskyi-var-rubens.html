Stipa zalesskyi var. rubens

Stipa zalesskyi var. rubens, commonly known as the red feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and feathery, reddish-purple inflorescences that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes, where it forms dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
