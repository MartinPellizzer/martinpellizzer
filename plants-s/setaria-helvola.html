Setaria helvola

Setaria helvola, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, upright blades and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the seeds are enclosed in small, dry capsules.

Setaria helvola is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
