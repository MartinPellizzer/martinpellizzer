Statice chrysocoma

Statice chrysocoma, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in salty, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize sandy soils.

Statice chrysocoma is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
