Stipa badachschanica var. badachschanica

Stipa badachschanica var. badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned lemmas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This grass plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
