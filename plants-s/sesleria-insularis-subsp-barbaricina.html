Sesleria insularis subsp. barbaricina

Sesleria insularis subsp.

barbaricina, commonly known as the island sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Canary Islands. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species within the Sesleria genus.
