Styphelia vieillardii

Styphelia vieillardii, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display to its compact form. The plant has needle-like leaves that are densely packed, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal heath environments.

Styphelia vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
