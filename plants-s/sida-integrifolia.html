Sida integrifolia

Sida integrifolia, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline environments and is often found along beaches and dunes. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize sandy soils.

Sida integrifolia is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
