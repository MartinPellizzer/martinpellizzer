Senebiera squamata

Senebiera squamata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in panicles. Senebiera squamata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
