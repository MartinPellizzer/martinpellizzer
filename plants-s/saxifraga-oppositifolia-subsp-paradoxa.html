Saxifraga oppositifolia subsp. paradoxa

Saxifraga oppositifolia subsp.

paradoxa, commonly known as the Arctic pink or snow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, pink or magenta flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions, making it a symbol of resilience in polar ecosystems.
