Sorbus atrovirens

Sorbus atrovirens, commonly known as the black-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Sorbus atrovirens is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
