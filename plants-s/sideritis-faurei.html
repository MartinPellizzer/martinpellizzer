Sideritis faurei

Sideritis faurei, commonly known as the Corsican ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Sideritis faurei typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
