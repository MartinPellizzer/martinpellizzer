Scirpus rubrotinctus f. confertus

Scirpus rubrotinctus f. confertus, commonly known as the dense rush, is a variant of the red-stained sedge.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a reddish tinge, especially at the base.

The inflorescence structure is compact, with spikelets arranged closely together, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
