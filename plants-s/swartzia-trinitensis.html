Swartzia trinitensis

Swartzia trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Swartzia trinitensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
