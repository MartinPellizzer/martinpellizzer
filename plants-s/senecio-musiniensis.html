Senecio musiniensis

Senecio musiniensis, commonly known as the Musina groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio musiniensis is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
