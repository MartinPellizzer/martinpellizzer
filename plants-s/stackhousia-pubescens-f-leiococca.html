Stackhousia pubescens f. leiococca

Stackhousia pubescens f. leiococca, commonly known as the smooth-leaved stackhousia, is a variant of the stackhousia plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves, distinguishing it from the typical pubescent form. The plant features a shrub-like growth habit with dense, compact foliage. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
