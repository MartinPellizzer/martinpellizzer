Saussurea hultenii

Saussurea hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saussurea hultenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
