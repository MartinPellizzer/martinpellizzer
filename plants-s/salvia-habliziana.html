Salvia habliziana

Salvia habliziana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia habliziana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
