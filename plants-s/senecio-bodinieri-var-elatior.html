Senecio bodinieri var. elatior

Senecio bodinieri var. elatior, commonly known as the Giant Senecio, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features tall, branching stems covered in dense, woolly gray-green foliage that gives it a unique, almost otherworldly appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted foliage. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for experienced succulent growers.
