Senecio aureus var. pauciflorus

Senecio aureus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically alternate, with a rough, hairy texture and a slightly serrated edge. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
