Scutellaria lewisiana

Scutellaria lewisiana, commonly known as Lewis's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria lewisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
