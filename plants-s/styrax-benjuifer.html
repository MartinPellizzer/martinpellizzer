Styrax benjuifer

Styrax benjuifer, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
