Salacia kalahiensis

Salacia kalahiensis, commonly known as the Kalahari salacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other genera within the family. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Salacia kalahiensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
