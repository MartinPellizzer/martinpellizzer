Scilla pallidiflora

Scilla pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging in early spring alongside the flowers.

Scilla pallidiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
