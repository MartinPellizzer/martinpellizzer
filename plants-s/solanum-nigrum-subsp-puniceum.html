Solanum nigrum subsp. puniceum

Solanum nigrum subsp.

puniceum, commonly known as the red bittersweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive red berries that contrast with its dark green foliage.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its flowers are typically yellow with a purple tinge, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its visual appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
