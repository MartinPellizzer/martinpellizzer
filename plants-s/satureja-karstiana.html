Satureja karstiana

Satureja karstiana, commonly known as Karst savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent, thyme-like scent.

Satureja karstiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
