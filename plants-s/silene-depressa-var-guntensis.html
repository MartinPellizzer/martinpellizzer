Silene depressa var. guntensis

Silene depressa var. guntensis, commonly known as the Guntensk silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in alpine habitats.
