Salix starkeana var. livida

Salix starkeana var. livida, commonly known as the white willow or silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often a silvery-gray on the underside. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing salicin, a precursor to aspirin.
