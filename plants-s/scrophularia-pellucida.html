Scrophularia pellucida

Scrophularia pellucida, commonly known as the common scrophularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, grassy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Scrophularia pellucida is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
