Streblus monoicus

Streblus monoicus, commonly known as the single-seed fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its unique fruit structure, which contains a single, large seed. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
