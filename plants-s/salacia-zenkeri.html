Salacia zenkeri

Salacia zenkeri, commonly known as the Zanzibar apple, is a climbing shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red or orange, and they have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Salacia zenkeri is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
