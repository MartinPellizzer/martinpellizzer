Salvia verbenaca var. disermas

Salvia verbenaca var. disermas, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal uses.
