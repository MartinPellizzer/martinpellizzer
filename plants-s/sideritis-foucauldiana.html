Sideritis foucauldiana

Sideritis foucauldiana, commonly known as the Foucauld's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in whorls. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis species are often valued for their medicinal properties, though specific uses of Sideritis foucauldiana are less well-documented.
