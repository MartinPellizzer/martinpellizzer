Salix triandra f. sandomiriensis

Salix triandra f. sandomiriensis, commonly known as the Sandomiriensis willow, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of willow native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in catkins. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
