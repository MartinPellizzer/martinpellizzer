Salvia aliena

Salvia aliena, commonly known as the alien sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia aliena thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and native plant collections.
