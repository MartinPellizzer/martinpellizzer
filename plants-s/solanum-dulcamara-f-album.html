Solanum dulcamara f. album

Solanum dulcamara f. album, commonly known as white bindweed, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its white to pale pink flowers and smooth, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.
