Stachys vulnerabilis

Stachys vulnerabilis, commonly known as woundwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Stachys vulnerabilis has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
