Symphonia rhodosepala

Symphonia rhodosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled symphonia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in yellowish-green cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The tree produces woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
