Salvia lachnostoma

Salvia lachnostoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Salvia lachnostoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
