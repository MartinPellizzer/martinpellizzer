Strychnos petrophila

Strychnos petrophila, commonly known as the rock apple, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Strychnos petrophila is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
