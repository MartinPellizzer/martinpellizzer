Spathodea glandulosa

Spathodea glandulosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Spathodea glandulosa is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
