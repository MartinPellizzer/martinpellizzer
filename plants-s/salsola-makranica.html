Salsola makranica

Salsola makranica, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, green, leafy shoots that are edible and rich in nutrients. Its succulent stems and leaves help it retain water in dry conditions.

Salsola makranica is well adapted to harsh climates and is often used as a forage plant for livestock.
