Saccharum arundinaceum var. angustifolium

Saccharum arundinaceum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and robust, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable resource for sustainable materials.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
