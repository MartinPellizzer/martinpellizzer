Salacia pyriformis

Salacia pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped salacia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pear-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Salacia pyriformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
