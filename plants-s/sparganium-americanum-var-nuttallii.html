Sparganium americanum var. nuttallii

Sparganium americanum var. nuttallii, commonly known as the American burr rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that bear clusters of spadices, which are dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense stands in shallow water.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
