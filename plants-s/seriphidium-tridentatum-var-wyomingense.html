Seriphidium tridentatum var. wyomingense

Seriphidium tridentatum var. wyomingense, commonly known as the Wyoming tansy, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its branched, aromatic stems and yellowish-green foliage, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as plains and foothills.

Due to its resilience and medicinal properties, it has been historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
