Sauropus parvifolius

Sauropus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with four petals.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
