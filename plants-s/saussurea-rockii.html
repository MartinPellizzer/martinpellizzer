Saussurea rockii

Saussurea rockii, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flower heads that are clustered in composite inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
