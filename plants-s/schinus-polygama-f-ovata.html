Schinus polygama f. ovata

Schinus polygama f. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf Brazilian pepper.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America. The plant features ovate, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
