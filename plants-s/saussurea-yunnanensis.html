Saussurea yunnanensis

Saussurea yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
