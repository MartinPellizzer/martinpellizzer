Senecio planiflorus

Senecio planiflorus, commonly known as the flatflower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
