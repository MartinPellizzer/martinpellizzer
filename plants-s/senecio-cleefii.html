Senecio cleefii

Senecio cleefii, commonly known as the Cleef's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
