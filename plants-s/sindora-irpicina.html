Sindora irpicina

Sindora irpicina, commonly known as the Indian ash tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
