Senna gossweileri

Senna gossweileri, commonly known as the red senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Senna gossweileri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both ecological and cultural contexts.
