Spiranthes sylvatica

Spiranthes sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.

Spiranthes sylvatica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
