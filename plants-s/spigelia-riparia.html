Spigelia riparia

Spigelia riparia, commonly known as the swamp pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Spigelia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
