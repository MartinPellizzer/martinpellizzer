Sabia celastrinea

Sabia celastrinea, commonly known as the yellow star-grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Sabia celastrinea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
