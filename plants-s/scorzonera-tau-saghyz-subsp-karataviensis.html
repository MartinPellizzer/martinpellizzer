Scorzonera tau-saghyz subsp. karataviensis

Scorzonera tau-saghyz subsp.

karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavinskaya scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to brownish flowers.

The plant produces a tuberous root system that is rich in inulin, a valuable polysaccharide used in food and industrial applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
