Smyrnium cicutarium

Smyrnium cicutarium, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or greenish flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a strong, often unpleasant odor. It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is known for its milky sap.

Cow parsnip is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils.
