Sampacca parviflora

Sampacca parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sampacca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
