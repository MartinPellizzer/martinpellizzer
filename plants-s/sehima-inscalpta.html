Sehima inscalpta

Sehima inscalpta, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Sehima inscalpta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
