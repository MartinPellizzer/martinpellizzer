Salvia pratensis var. laciniosa

Salvia pratensis var. laciniosa, commonly known as the fringed blue sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
