Silene colorata var. acutifolia

Silene colorata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
