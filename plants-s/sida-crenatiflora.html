Sida crenatiflora

Sida crenatiflora, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Sida crenatiflora is also known for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
