Syziganthus multiflorus

Syziganthus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered syziganthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy texture.

Syziganthus multiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
