Sesamum integribracteatum subsp. ellipticum

Sesamum integribracteatum subsp.

ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic sesame, is a species native to tropical Africa. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
