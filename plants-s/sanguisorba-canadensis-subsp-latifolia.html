Sanguisorba canadensis subsp. latifolia

Sanguisorba canadensis subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish hue, especially when grown in full sun. This species is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
