Saccharum fasciculatum

Saccharum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
