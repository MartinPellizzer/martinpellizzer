Spartina densiflora subvar. argentinensis

Spartina densiflora subvar. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and form monocultures in suitable environments.

The plant plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by reducing erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
