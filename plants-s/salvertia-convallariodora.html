Salvertia convallariodora

Salvertia convallariodora, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent in foods and beverages.

Salvertia convallariodora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
