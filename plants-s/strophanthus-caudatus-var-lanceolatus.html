Strophanthus caudatus var. lanceolatus

Strophanthus caudatus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its latex contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
