Salix atopantha var. pedicellata

Salix atopantha var. pedicellata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
