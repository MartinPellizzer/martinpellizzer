Scorpiothyrsus glabrifolius

Scorpiothyrsus glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved scorpiothyrsus, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
