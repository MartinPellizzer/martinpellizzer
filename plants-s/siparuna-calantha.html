Siparuna calantha

Siparuna calantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Siparuna calantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
