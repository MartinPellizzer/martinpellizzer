Swertia erythrosticta var. epunctata

Swertia erythrosticta var. epunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
