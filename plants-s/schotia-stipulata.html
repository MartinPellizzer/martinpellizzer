Schotia stipulata

Schotia stipulata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage and distinctive, elongated leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Schotia stipulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
