Satyrium pustulatum

Satyrium pustulatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral structure.

The species is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its growth and development.
