Senecio robbinsii

Senecio robbinsii, commonly known as Robbins' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Robbins' groundsel is often found in open areas, including grasslands and scrublands, and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
