Schisandra viridis

Schisandra viridis, commonly known as green schisandra, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red berries. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adaptable to various soil types.

Schisandra viridis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
