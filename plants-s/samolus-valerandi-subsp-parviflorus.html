Samolus valerandi subsp. parviflorus

Samolus valerandi subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered samolus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
