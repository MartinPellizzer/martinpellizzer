Sanguisorba minor subsp. anceps

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burnet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, reddish-brown flower clusters that appear in late summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders. Sanguisorba minor subsp.
