Solanum pectinatum

Solanum pectinatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum pectinatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
