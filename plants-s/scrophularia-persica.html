Scrophularia persica

Scrophularia persica, commonly known as Persian fig or Persian scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scrophularia persica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
