Solanoa

Solanoa, commonly known as the nightshade plant, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It typically features showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Solanoa species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value or for medicinal purposes.
