Sesamum laciniatum

Sesamum laciniatum, commonly known as the serrated sesame or African sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its seeds are encased in a hard, brittle capsule and are used for their oil content.

Sesamum laciniatum is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and is also valued in traditional medicine.
