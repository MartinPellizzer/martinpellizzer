Satureja alpina subsp. pyrenaea

Satureja alpina subsp.

pyrenaea, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The plant features small, aromatic leaves with a pungent scent, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and thyme.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical Satureja alpina.
