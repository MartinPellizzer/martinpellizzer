Spergula arvensis subsp. leiosperma

Spergula arvensis subsp.

leiosperma, commonly known as silky field vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a fibrous root system. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its silky, hairy texture on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.
