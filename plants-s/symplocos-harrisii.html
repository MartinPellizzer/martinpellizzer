Symplocos harrisii

Symplocos harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the southeastern region. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall.

Symplocos harrisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
