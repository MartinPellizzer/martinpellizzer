Solanum obliquum

Solanum obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum obliquum is often found in moist, shaded areas in the Andean regions.
