Stipa microsperma

Stipa microsperma, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or marginal habitats.

Stipa microsperma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
