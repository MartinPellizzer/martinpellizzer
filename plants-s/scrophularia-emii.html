Scrophularia emii

Scrophularia emii, commonly known as the Emu's foot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open woodland or scrubland habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of tubular, pinkish-purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia emii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
