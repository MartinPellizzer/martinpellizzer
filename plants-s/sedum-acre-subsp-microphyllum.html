Sedum acre subsp. microphyllum

Sedum acre subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions.
