Saxifraga assamensis

Saxifraga assamensis, commonly known as the Assam saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and usually range in color from white to pink.

Saxifraga assamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
