Stimoryne axillaris subsp. parodii

Stimoryne axillaris subsp.

parodii, commonly known as the Parodii's stimoryne, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
