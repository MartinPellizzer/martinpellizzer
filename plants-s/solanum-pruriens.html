Solanum pruriens

Solanum pruriens, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces yellow flowers and kidney-shaped seeds, and it is often cultivated for its edible seeds and leguminous properties.

Solanum pruriens is also used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating diabetes and inflammation.
