Senecio bedfordii

Senecio bedfordii, commonly known as the Bedford's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio bedfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
