Saussurea kanaii

Saussurea kanaii, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces fibrous roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Saussurea kanaii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
