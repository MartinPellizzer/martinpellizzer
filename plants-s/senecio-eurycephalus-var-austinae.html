Senecio eurycephalus var. austinae

Senecio eurycephalus var. austinae, commonly known as the Austina's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silver-gray foliage with dense, woolly hairs, and produces small blue to purple flowers in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
