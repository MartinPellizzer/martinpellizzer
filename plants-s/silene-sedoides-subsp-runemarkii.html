Silene sedoides subsp. runemarkii

Silene sedoides subsp.

runemarkii, commonly known as the runemark evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
