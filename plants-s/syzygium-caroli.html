Syzygium caroli

Syzygium caroli, commonly known as the Caroli's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, apple-like berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Syzygium caroli is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
