Strzeleckya dissosperma

Strzeleckya dissosperma, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its hard, durable wood has historically been used for construction and tool-making by local communities.
