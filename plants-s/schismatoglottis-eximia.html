Schismatoglottis eximia

Schismatoglottis eximia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Schismatoglottis eximia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
