Sesleria caerulea var. alba

Sesleria caerulea var. alba, commonly known as the white wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, elegant look.
