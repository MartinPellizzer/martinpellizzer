Seseli montanum var. brevifolium

Seseli montanum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved mountain seseli, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white, clustered in umbrella-like arrangements.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter leaves compared to other varieties of Seseli montanum.
