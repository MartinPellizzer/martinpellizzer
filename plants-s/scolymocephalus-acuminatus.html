Scolymocephalus acuminatus

Scolymocephalus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped scolymus, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, typically growing in rocky or arid soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its flowering period occurs primarily in the spring and early summer.

Scolymocephalus acuminatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
