Satureja graeca subsp. nervosa

Satureja graeca subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as Greek mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its subspecific name "nervosa," meaning "nervy" or "with nerves.".

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
