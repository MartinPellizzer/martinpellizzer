Serjania lupulina

Serjania lupulina, commonly known as the yellow serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Serjania lupulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
