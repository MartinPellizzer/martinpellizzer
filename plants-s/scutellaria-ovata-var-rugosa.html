Scutellaria ovata var. rugosa

Scutellaria ovata var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough ovate skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rootstock and produces erect stems that bear whorled leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
