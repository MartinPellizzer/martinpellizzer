Senna obtusata

Senna obtusata, commonly known as Egyptian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Senna obtusata is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in dryland ecosystems.
