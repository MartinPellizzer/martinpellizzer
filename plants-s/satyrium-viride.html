Satyrium viride

Satyrium viride, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, making it distinctive among orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Satyrium viride make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
