Spiraea japonica var. parvifolia

Spiraea japonica var. parvifolia, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
