Satyrium trinerve

Satyrium trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are alternate and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by specific insect species.
