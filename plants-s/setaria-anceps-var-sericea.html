Setaria anceps var. sericea

Setaria anceps var. sericea, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its tactile quality. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some ecological restoration projects.
