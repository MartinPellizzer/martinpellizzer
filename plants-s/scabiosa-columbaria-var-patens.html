Scabiosa columbaria var. patens

Scabiosa columbaria var. patens, commonly known as the wandering jew or scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink or white flowers with a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Scabiosa columbaria var. patens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
