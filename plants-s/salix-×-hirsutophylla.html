Salix × hirsutophylla

Salix × hirsutophylla, commonly known as the woolly-leaf willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white, woolly underside on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its elongated, narrow leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Salix × hirsutophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
