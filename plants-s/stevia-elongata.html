Stevia elongata

Stevia elongata, commonly known as the elongated stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. Stevia elongata is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant and for its medicinal properties.
