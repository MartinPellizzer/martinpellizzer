Securidaca divaricata

Securidaca divaricata, commonly known as the African devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its slender, often twisted branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glandular surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
