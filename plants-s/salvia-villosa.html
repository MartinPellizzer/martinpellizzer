Salvia villosa

Salvia villosa, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
