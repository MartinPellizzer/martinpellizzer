Sesamum byngianum

Sesamum byngianum, commonly known as the African sesame or Byng's sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its seeds are rich in oil and have been used traditionally for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate for optimal growth.
