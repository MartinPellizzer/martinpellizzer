Solanum villosum subsp. alatum

Solanum villosum subsp.

alatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, typically ranging in color from yellow to red. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
