Selaginella alligans

Selaginella alligans, commonly known as the tropical spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, grass-like leaves that are often spirally arranged. It produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found on specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella alligans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
