Selago anatrichota

Selago anatrichota, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces seeds in clusters, which are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across arid environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Selago anatrichota is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in full sun.
