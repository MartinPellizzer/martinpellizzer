Statice caespitosa

Statice caespitosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Statice caespitosa is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and floral arrangements.
