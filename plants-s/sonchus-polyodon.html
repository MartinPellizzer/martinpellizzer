Sonchus polyodon

Sonchus polyodon, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its seeds are small, oval, and often have pappus structures for wind dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
