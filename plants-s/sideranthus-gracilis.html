Sideranthus gracilis

Sideranthus gracilis, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Sideranthus gracilis is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
