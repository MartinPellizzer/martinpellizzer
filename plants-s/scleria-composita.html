Scleria composita

Scleria composita, commonly known as the composite speargrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually green or brown in color.

Scleria composita is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in its native habitats.
