Solidago flavovirens

Solidago flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to greenish-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
