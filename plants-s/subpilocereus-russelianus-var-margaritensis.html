Subpilocereus russelianus var. margaritensis

Subpilocereus russelianus var. margaritensis, commonly known as the Margaritensis Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, slender stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming characteristics.
