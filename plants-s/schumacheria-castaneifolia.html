Schumacheria castaneifolia

Schumacheria castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern China.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is valued in both horticulture and botanical research.
