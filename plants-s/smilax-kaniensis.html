Smilax kaniensis

Smilax kaniensis, commonly known as the Kani arrowroot or Kani sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that contain seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Smilax kaniensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
