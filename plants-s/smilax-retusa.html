Smilax retusa

Smilax retusa, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems covered with small, waxy tubercles and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant bears yellowish-brown berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Smilax retusa is commonly found in moist woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade.
