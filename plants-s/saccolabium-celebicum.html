Saccolabium celebicum

Saccolabium celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian islands of Sulawesi and Nusa Tenggara.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
