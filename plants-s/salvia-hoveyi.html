Salvia hoveyi

Salvia hoveyi, commonly known as Hovey's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
