Solidago yambaruensis

Solidago yambaruensis, commonly known as the Okinawan goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago yambaruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
