Sinarundinaria × tranquillans

Sinarundinaria × tranquillans, commonly known as the Chinese windmill palm, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Rhopalostylis sapida* and *Rhopalostylis baueri*.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that resemble the blades of a windmill. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to tolerate partial shade.

The leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
