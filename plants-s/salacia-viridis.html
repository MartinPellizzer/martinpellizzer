Salacia viridis

Salacia viridis, commonly known as the green salacia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Saluraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
