Schefflera adolfi-friderici

Schefflera adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the African evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaflets, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has smooth, dark green foliage with a waxy texture, and its leaves are typically arranged in a palmate pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals.

Schefflera adolfi-friderici is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
