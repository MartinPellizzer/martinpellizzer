Silene biafrae

Silene biafrae, commonly known as the Biafra catchfly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene biafrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
