Spiraea × zabelii

Spiraea × zabelii, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are typically oval and have a finely toothed margin.

Spiraea × zabelii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
