Senecio koreanus

Senecio koreanus, commonly known as the Korean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio koreanus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
