Stapelia obliqua

Stapelia obliqua, commonly known as the twisted stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that often curve or twist, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces striking, waxy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a strong, unpleasant odor. These flowers bloom in summer and are designed to attract specific pollinators like flies.

Stapelia obliqua is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
