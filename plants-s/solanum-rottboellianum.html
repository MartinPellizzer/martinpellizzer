Solanum rottboellianum

Solanum rottboellianum, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark purple leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
