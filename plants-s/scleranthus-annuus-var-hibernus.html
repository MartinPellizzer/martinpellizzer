Scleranthus annuus var. hibernus

Scleranthus annuus var. hibernus, commonly known as the winter sow-thistle, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
