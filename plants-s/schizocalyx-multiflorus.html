Schizocalyx multiflorus

Schizocalyx multiflorus, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Schizocalyx multiflorus thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.
