Stachys hirta

Stachys hirta, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces spikes of violet to blue flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This perennial herb thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
