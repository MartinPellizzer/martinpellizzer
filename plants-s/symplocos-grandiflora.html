Symplocos grandiflora

Symplocos grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
