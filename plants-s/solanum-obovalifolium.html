Solanum obovalifolium

Solanum obovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its berries, which are usually yellow or red, are toxic and contain alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
