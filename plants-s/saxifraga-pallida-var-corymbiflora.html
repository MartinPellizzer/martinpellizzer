Saxifraga pallida var. corymbiflora

Saxifraga pallida var. corymbiflora, commonly known as the pale saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are usually pale green to bluish-gray in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in compact corymbs, giving it a delicate, clustered appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
