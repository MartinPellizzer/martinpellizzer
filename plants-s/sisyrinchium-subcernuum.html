Sisyrinchium subcernuum

Sisyrinchium subcernuum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
