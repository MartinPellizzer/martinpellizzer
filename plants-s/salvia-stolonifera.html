Salvia stolonifera

Salvia stolonifera, commonly known as autumn sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from late summer into early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract butterflies and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
