Sideritis hispanica

Sideritis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish heather, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal uses.

Sideritis hispanica is valued for its potential health benefits, including its use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive issues.
