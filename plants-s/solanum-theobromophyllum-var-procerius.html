Solanum theobromophyllum var. procerius

Solanum theobromophyllum var. procerius, commonly known as the chocolate bush, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, chocolate-brown berries. These berries are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to gardens and landscapes.
