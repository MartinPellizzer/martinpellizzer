Syzygium laevigatum

Syzygium laevigatum, commonly known as the white celtuce or white kohlrabi, is a flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant features broad, waxy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

Syzygium laevigatum is cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
