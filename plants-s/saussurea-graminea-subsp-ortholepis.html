Saussurea graminea subsp. ortholepis

Saussurea graminea subsp.

ortholepis, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble a crown of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a head-like structure, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name, which reflects its grassy appearance.

This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
