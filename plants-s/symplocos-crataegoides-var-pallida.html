Symplocos crataegoides var. pallida

Symplocos crataegoides var. pallida, commonly known as whiteleaf sandpaperwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually pale green and covered in fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
