Sambucus plumosa tenuifolia

Sambucus plumosa tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender elderberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or black berries in the fall. Native to eastern North America, it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
