Stickmannia rhizophya

Stickmannia rhizophya, commonly known as the root-attached stickman plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, stick-like stems that resemble human figures, hence its common name. The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to anchor itself to rocky surfaces and absorb moisture from the environment. Its leaves are small and scale-like, reducing water loss in its harsh habitat.

Stickmannia rhizophya is a succulent, storing water in its stems to survive prolonged dry periods.
