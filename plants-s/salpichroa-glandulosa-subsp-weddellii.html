Salpichroa glandulosa subsp. weddellii

Salpichroa glandulosa subsp.

weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, featuring dense clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and nutrient-poor conditions.
