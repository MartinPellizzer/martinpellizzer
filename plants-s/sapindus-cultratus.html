Sapindus cultratus

Sapindus cultratus, commonly known as the Indian soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces spherical, spiny fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are often used as a natural soap due to their saponin content, which gives them a cleansing property.
