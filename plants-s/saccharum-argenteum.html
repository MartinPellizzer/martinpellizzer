Saccharum argenteum

Saccharum argenteum, commonly known as silver cane, is a tropical grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and tall, slender stalks. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near rivers and lakes. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs that give it its distinctive appearance.

While not widely cultivated for sugar production, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
