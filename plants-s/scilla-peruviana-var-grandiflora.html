Scilla peruviana var. grandiflora

Scilla peruviana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
