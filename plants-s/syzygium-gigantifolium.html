Syzygium gigantifolium

Syzygium gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant broad-leafed apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
