Saccharum holcoides var. brevipilum

Saccharum holcoides var. brevipilum, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff culms that rarely exceed 1 meter in height. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
