Sisyrinchium graminifolium subsp. nanum

Sisyrinchium graminifolium subsp.

nanum, commonly known as dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
