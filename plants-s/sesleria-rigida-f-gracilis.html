Sesleria rigida f. gracilis

Sesleria rigida f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
