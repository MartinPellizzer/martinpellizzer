Senecio sylvaticus var. denticulatus

Senecio sylvaticus var. denticulatus, commonly known as the toothedleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
