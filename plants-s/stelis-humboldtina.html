Stelis humboldtina

Stelis humboldtina, commonly known as the Humboldt stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Stelis humboldtina is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
