Solidago serotinoides

Solidago serotinoides, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago serotinoides plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
