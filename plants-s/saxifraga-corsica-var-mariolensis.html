Saxifraga corsica var. mariolensis

Saxifraga corsica var. mariolensis, commonly known as the Corsican saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the island of Corsica.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the rocky and coastal environments where it thrives.

It is an endemic species, found primarily in specific regions of Corsica, making it a notable example of local botanical diversity.
