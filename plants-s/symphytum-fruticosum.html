Symphytum fruticosum

Symphytum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating called a bloom. The plant produces clusters of purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed carefully.
