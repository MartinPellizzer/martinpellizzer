Scrophularia umbrosoides

Scrophularia umbrosoides, commonly known as the shadowplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia umbrosoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
