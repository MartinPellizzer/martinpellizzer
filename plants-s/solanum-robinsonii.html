Solanum robinsonii

Solanum robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
