Salvia siphonantha

Salvia siphonantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia siphonantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
