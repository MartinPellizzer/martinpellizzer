Sicyocarya beccifer

Sicyocarya beccifer, commonly known as the Beccifer cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, barrel-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from infrequent rainfalls.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Sicyocarya beccifer is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
