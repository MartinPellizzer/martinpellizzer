Sideritis mariae

Sideritis mariae, commonly known as Mary's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. Sideritis mariae is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
