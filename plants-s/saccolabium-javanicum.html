Saccolabium javanicum

Saccolabium javanicum, commonly known as the Java false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its root system is notable for containing nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhance soil fertility.

Saccolabium javanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
