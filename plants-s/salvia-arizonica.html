Salvia arizonica

Salvia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The genus Salvia, to which it belongs, is known for its medicinal and ornamental value across various species.
