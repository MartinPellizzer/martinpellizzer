Scrophularia oblongifolia subsp. neesii

Scrophularia oblongifolia subsp.

neesii, commonly known as the Nees's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also a subject of botanical study due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
