Schizanthus lacteus

Schizanthus lacteus, commonly known as the white flowering scorpion plant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of each petal. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Schizanthus lacteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fleeting blooms.
