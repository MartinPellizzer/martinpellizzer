Saxifraga corsica subsp. fontqueri

Saxifraga corsica subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Corsican saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rosette structure that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny locations.
