Setaria elegantula

Setaria elegantula, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a native grass species found throughout much of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Setaria elegantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
