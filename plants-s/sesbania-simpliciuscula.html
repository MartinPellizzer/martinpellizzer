Sesbania simpliciuscula

Sesbania simpliciuscula, commonly known as the small-leaved sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Sesbania simpliciuscula is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop due to its adaptability and growth characteristics.
