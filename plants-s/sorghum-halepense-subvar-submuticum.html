Sorghum halepense subvar. submuticum

Sorghum halepense subvar. submuticum, commonly known as yellow nutsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and erect growth habit, often forming dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spikelets, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are alternate, sword-shaped, and have a smooth texture with a slight waxy coating.

This subspecies is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
