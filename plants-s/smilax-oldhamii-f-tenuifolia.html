Smilax oldhamii f. tenuifolia

Smilax oldhamii f. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and spiny thorns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
