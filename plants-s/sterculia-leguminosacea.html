Sterculia leguminosacea

Sterculia leguminosacea, commonly known as the African or Indian chestnut, is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae).

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it has a woody, sometimes thorny structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various cultures.
