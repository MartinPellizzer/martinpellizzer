Solanum hartwegii

Solanum hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid, lowland forest environments. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and often have a distinctive purple or violet coloration.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
