Staurogyne eustachya

Staurogyne eustachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just below the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Staurogyne eustachya is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
