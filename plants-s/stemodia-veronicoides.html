Stemodia veronicoides

Stemodia veronicoides, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
