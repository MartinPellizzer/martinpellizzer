Salix feddei

Salix feddei, commonly known as Fedde's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth margin, and it produces catkins during the spring season.

The plant is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing rocky slopes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
