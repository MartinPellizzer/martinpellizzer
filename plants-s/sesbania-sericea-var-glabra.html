Sesbania sericea var. glabra

Sesbania sericea var. glabra, commonly known as silky sesbania, is a variant of the sesbania genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing ability and as a forage crop.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it valuable in soil conservation and ecological restoration efforts.
