Symplocos myrtacea var. latifolia

Symplocos myrtacea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf symplocos, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
