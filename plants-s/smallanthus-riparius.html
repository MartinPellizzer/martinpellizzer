Smallanthus riparius

Smallanthus riparius, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Smallanthus riparius is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
