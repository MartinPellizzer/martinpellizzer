Sida urens var. rufescens

Sida urens var. rufescens, commonly known as red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, pale green to reddish leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
