Sabal adiantina

Sabal adiantina, commonly known as the lace fern palm, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its feathery, palm-like fronds that resemble those of a fern. The plant has a unique ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its elegant appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
