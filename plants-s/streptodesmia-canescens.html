Streptodesmia canescens

Streptodesmia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf streptodesmia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, gray-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, contributing to its common name.

Streptodesmia canescens is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.
