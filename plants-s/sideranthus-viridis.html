Sideranthus viridis

Sideranthus viridis, commonly known as greenleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in disturbed soils.
