Sambucus racemosa subsp. sibirica

Sambucus racemosa subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to northeastern Asia. It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are edible when fully ripe but contain toxic seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
