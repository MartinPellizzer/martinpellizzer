Scindapsus geniculatus

Scindapsus geniculatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable ornamental plant for both residential and commercial spaces.
