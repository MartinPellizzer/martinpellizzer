Solanum phureja var. sanguineum

Solanum phureja var. sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodberry plant, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red, fleshy berries that resemble grapes, which are edible and often consumed raw or cooked. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
