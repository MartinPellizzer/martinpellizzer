Strychnos pubescens

Strychnos pubescens, commonly known as the hairy strychnos, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Strychnos pubescens is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
