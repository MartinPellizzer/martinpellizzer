Senecio oclonetus

Senecio oclonetus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by white ray florets, making it visually striking.

Senecio oclonetus is also noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening.
