Suregada borbonica

Suregada borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and can vary in color from white to pink and purple.

Suregada borbonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
