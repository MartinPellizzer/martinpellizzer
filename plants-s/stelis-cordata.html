Stelis cordata

Stelis cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.

Stelis cordata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.
