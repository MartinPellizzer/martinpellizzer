Silene persica var. angiostoma

Silene persica var. angiostoma, commonly known as the Persian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
