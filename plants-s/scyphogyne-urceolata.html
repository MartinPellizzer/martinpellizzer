Scyphogyne urceolata

Scyphogyne urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, bladder-like structures.

These specialized organs, called bladders, are used to trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in the plant's nutrient acquisition. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. It has a simple morphology with root-like structures and floating leaves.

Scyphogyne urceolata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web and oxygen production.
