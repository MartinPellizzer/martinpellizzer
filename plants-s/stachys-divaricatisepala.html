Stachys divaricatisepala

Stachys divaricatisepala, commonly known as the broadleaf betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Stachys divaricatisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
