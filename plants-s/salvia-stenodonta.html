Salvia stenodonta

Salvia stenodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia stenodonta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
