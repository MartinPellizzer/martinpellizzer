Sideranthus villosus

Sideranthus villosus, commonly known as hairy sidereal, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sideranthus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
