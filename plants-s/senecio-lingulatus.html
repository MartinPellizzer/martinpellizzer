Senecio lingulatus

Senecio lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Its resilience to drought makes it a hardy plant adapted to arid environments.
