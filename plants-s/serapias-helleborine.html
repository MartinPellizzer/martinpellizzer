Serapias helleborine

Serapias helleborine, commonly known as the autumn marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and damp woodlands, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to its wet habitat.

Serapias helleborine is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
