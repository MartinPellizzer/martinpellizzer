Spiraea panchananii

Spiraea panchananii, commonly known as the Panchananii weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Spiraea panchananii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
