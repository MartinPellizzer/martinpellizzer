Sasa kasimontana

Sasa kasimontana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa kasimontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to form thick, attractive mats.
