Spiraea japonica subvar. brevistyla

Spiraea japonica subvar. brevistyla, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features upright, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
