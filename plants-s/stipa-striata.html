Stipa striata

Stipa striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Stipa striata is often found in open grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
