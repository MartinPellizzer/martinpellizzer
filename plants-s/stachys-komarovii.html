Stachys komarovii

Stachys komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, two-lipped flowers in clusters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent when crushed.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
