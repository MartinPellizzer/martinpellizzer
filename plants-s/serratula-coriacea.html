Serratula coriacea

Serratula coriacea, commonly known as rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Serratula coriacea is often found in disturbed areas and can sometimes become invasive in new environments.
