Sasa sasagaminensis

Sasa sasagaminensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Sasa sasagaminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
