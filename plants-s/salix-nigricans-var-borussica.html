Salix nigricans var. borussica

Salix nigricans var. borussica, commonly known as the black willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollen dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
