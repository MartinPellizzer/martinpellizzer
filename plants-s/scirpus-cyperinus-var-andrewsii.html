Scirpus cyperinus var. andrewsii

Scirpus cyperinus var. andrewsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This perennial wetland plant typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
