Scutellaria pacifica

Scutellaria pacifica, commonly known as Pacific blueviolet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, two-lipped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Scutellaria pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
