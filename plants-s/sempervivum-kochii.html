Sempervivum kochii

Sempervivum kochii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a species of succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues on the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun conditions.

Sempervivum kochii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
