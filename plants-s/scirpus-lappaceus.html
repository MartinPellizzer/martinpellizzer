Scirpus lappaceus

Scirpus lappaceus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Scirpus lappaceus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
