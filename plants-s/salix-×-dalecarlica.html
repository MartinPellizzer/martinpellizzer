Salix × dalecarlica

Salix × dalecarlica, commonly known as the white willow.

It is a hybrid willow species native to Europe, characterized by its slender, weeping branches and pale green to white-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and release a distinctive, somewhat unpleasant odor when crushed.

Salix × dalecarlica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
