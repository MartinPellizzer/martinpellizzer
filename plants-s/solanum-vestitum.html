Solanum vestitum

Solanum vestitum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. Solanum vestitum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
