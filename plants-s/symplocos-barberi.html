Symplocos barberi

Symplocos barberi, commonly known as the barber's symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color in the fall.

Symplocos barberi is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
