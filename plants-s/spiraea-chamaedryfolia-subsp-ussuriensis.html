Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp. ussuriensis

Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp.

ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri spiraea, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, forested areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
