Scutellaria tenuiflora

Scutellaria tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive blue or violet hue. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria tenuiflora is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
