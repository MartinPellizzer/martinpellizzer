Scutellaria reticulata

Scutellaria reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria reticulata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
