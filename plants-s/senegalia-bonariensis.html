Senegalia bonariensis

Senegalia bonariensis, commonly known as the Prosopis bonariensis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates. It produces small, hard pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used for windbreaks in agricultural settings.
