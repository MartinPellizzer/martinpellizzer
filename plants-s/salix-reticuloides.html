Salix reticuloides

Salix reticuloides, commonly known as the reticulate willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
