Solanum andrieuxii

Solanum andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
