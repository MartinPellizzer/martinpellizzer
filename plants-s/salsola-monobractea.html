Salsola monobractea

Salsola monobractea, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, round fruits that are often used as a food source by livestock.

Salsola monobractea is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert ecosystems.
