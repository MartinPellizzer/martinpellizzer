Stevia rhombifolia f. colorata

Stevia rhombifolia f. colorata, commonly known as the colored leaf stevia, is a variant of the stevia plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful foliage, which can range from pink to red, making it visually striking. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in warm, sunny climates and well-drained soil.

While it shares the sweet taste of other stevia species, its ornamental value makes it popular in gardens and as a decorative plant.
