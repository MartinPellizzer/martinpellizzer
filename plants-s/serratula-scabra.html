Serratula scabra

Serratula scabra, commonly known as rough serratula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with serrated edges, and its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Serratula scabra is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
