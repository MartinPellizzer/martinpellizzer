Spatholobus ferrugineus var. sericophyllus

Spatholobus ferrugineus var. sericophyllus, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
