Saxifraga pullata

Saxifraga pullata, commonly known as the rockfoil saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer.

Saxifraga pullata is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
