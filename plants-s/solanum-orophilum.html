Solanum orophilum

Solanum orophilum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Solanum orophilum is known for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
