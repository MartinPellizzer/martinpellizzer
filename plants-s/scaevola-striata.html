Scaevola striata

Scaevola striata, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found along shorelines and in dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Scaevola striata is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
