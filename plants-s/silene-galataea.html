Silene galataea

Silene galataea, commonly known as the Galatian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene galataea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
