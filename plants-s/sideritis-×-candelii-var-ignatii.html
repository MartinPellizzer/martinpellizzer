Sideritis × candelii var. ignatii

Sideritis × candelii var. ignatii, commonly known as the Ignatius sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
