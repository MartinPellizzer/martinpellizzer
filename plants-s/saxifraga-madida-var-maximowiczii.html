Saxifraga madida var. maximowiczii

Saxifraga madida var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating native flora.
