Salvia lorentzii

Salvia lorentzii, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight fuzzy texture.

Salvia lorentzii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
