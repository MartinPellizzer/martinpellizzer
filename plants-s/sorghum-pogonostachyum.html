Sorghum pogonostachyum

Sorghum pogonostachyum, commonly known as the hairy sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and branched inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed. It is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its striking appearance.

Sorghum pogonostachyum is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a resilient crop in arid environments.
