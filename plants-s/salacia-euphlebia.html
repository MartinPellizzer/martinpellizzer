Salacia euphlebia

Salacia euphlebia, commonly known as the golden root, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and distinctive, elongated leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The roots of Salacia euphlebia are valued in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
