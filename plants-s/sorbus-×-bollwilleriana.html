Sorbus × bollwilleriana

Sorbus × bollwilleriana, commonly known as the Bollwiller pear, is a hybrid species of rowan tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible but often considered tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
