Solanum roseum

Solanum roseum, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes. Solanum roseum typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and showy blooms that open in the morning.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
