Silene marschallii

Silene marschallii, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Silene marschallii is typically found in moist, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in areas with partial to full sunlight.

Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
