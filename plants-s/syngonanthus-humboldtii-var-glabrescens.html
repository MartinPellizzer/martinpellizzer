Syngonanthus humboldtii var. glabrescens

Syngonanthus humboldtii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Humboldt's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in the Amazon basin. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The berries it bears are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
