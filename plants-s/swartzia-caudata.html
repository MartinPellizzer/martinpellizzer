Swartzia caudata

Swartzia caudata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pod fruits that contain compressed, elliptical seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Swartzia caudata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
