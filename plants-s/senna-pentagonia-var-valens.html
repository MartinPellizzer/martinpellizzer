Senna pentagonia var. valens

Senna pentagonia var. valens, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
