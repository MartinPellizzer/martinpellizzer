Serruria colorata

Serruria colorata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in vibrant red, orange, or yellow, giving it its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Serruria colorata is often cultivated for its bold, fiery appearance in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
