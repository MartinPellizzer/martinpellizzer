Scleria wrightiana

Scleria wrightiana, commonly known as Wright's spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowering stems are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Scleria wrightiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
