Sisyrinchium vulgare

Sisyrinchium vulgare, commonly known as the common blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright blue or violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium vulgare is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
