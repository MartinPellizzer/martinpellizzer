Schoenoplectus monocephalus

Schoenoplectus monocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense, tussock-like clumps, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months, which are pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Schoenoplectus monocephalus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
