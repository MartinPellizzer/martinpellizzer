Solanum cyanocarphium

Solanum cyanocarphium, commonly known as the blueberry nightshade, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, blue to purple berries that contain toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces fruits that are edible only when fully ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by both animals and humans.
