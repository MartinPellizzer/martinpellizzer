Scleroon

Scleroon, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Scleroon species are often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

They are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
