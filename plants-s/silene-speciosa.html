Silene speciosa

Silene speciosa, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene speciosa blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
