Syzygium buxifolioideum

Syzygium buxifolioideum, commonly known as the glossy tea tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that give it a lush, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often followed by edible, red, spherical berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium buxifolioideum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
