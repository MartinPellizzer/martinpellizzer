Scutellaria longiflora

Scutellaria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria longiflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
