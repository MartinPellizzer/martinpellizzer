Stipa karatavica

Stipa karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils. Stipa karatavica is known for its ability to form dense grasslands and is sometimes used for erosion control.

Its floral structures consist of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
