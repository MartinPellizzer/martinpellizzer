Scutellaria aramberrana

Scutellaria aramberrana, commonly known as the Aramberri skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Scutellaria aramberrana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential therapeutic uses.
