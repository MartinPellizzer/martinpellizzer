Spatholobus dimorphus

Spatholobus dimorphus, commonly known as the variable wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and distinctive flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, hanging clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Spatholobus dimorphus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
