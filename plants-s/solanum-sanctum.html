Solanum sanctum

Solanum sanctum, commonly known as sacred nightshade, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum sanctum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
