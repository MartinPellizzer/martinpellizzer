Scirpus americanus var. polyphyllus

Scirpus americanus var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and broad, flat leaves that often form dense stands in shallow water. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in marshy environments and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of aquatic ecosystems.
