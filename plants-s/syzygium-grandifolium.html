Syzygium grandifolium

Syzygium grandifolium, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its edible, sweet-tart fruit. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium grandifolium thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
