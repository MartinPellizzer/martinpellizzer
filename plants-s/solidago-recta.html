Solidago recta

Solidago recta, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flower clusters, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in panicles. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Solidago recta is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
