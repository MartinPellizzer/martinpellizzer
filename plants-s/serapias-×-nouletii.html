Serapias × nouletii

Serapias × nouletii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a cross between Serapias auricoma and Serapias microchila.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Serapias × nouletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
