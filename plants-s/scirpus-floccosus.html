Scirpus floccosus

Scirpus floccosus, commonly known as the soft rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in soft, white, fluffy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and are usually green or brown in color.

Scirpus floccosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
