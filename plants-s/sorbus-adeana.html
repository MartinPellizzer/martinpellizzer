Sorbus adeana

Sorbus adeana, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds.

It typically grows in coastal and lowland forest habitats, and is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal.
