Spiraea thunbergii var. prunifolia

Spiraea thunbergii var. prunifolia, commonly known as the Japanese spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its spring flowering display.
