Selenicereus grandiflorus var. telli

Selenicereus grandiflorus var. telli, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is known for its striking, large, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces spiny, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
