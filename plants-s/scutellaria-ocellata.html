Scutellaria ocellata

Scutellaria ocellata, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Scutellaria ocellata thrives in moist, woodsy habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its unique spotted leaves and delicate flowers make it a notable addition to native plant gardens.
