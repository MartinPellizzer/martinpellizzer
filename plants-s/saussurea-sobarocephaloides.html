Saussurea sobarocephaloides

Saussurea sobarocephaloides, commonly known as the Tibetan yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and low-nutrient soils. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and it produces small, achene-like fruits.

Saussurea sobarocephaloides is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
