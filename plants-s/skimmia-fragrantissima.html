Skimmia fragrantissima

Skimmia fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant skimmia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Skimmia fragrantissima is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
