Satyrium aceras

Satyrium aceras, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The orchid's structure includes a short rhizome and a single, slender stem with a few leaves.
