Schizaea elegans var. amazonica

Schizaea elegans var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian whorled fern, is a tropical fern native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged fronds that give it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients in its natural habitat.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to botanical collections.
