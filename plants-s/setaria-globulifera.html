Setaria globulifera

Setaria globulifera, commonly known as the globe millet, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its small, green leaves and robust root system make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Setaria globulifera is also known for its potential use in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
