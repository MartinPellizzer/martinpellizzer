Silene rubicunda

Silene rubicunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene rubicunda is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for early succession stages in ecosystems.
