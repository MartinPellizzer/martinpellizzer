Stipa purpurea

Stipa purpurea, commonly known as purple false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, spikelike inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and its flowers are small and wind-pollinated. Stipa purpurea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal coloration.
