Scutellaria versicolor

Scutellaria versicolor, commonly known as the variable skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Scutellaria versicolor typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
