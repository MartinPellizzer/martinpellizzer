Sponia lima

Sponia lima, commonly known as the lima bean plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines worldwide. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the seeds. Sponia lima is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
