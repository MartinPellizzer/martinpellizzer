Saxifraga biflora

Saxifraga biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga biflora is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
