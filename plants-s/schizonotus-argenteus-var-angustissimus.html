Schizonotus argenteus var. angustissimus

Schizonotus argenteus var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
