Schoenus arundinaceus

Schoenus arundinaceus, commonly known as the reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Schoenus arundinaceus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
