Soulangia arborescens

Soulangia arborescens, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive bell-shaped fruit capsules.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
