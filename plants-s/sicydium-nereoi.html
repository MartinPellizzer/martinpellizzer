Sicydium nereoi

Sicydium nereoi, commonly known as the Nereus sea hibiscus, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers and succulent, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its saline environment. The plant thrives in shallow tidal zones and is adapted to withstand periodic submersion in water. Its unique morphology makes it an important species for studying plant adaptation to marine ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
