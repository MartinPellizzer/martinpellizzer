Sesbania aegyptiaca

Sesbania aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian sesban, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a fast-growing annual shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Additionally, it is sometimes used for forage and as a green manure crop.
