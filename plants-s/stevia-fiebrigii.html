Stevia fiebrigii

Stevia fiebrigii, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia fiebrigii is often used as a natural sweetener and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
