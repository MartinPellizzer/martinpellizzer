Statice gummifera var. corymbulosa

Statice gummifera var. corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered glassweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in corymbs, giving it a fluffy, rounded appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its visual appeal and drought tolerance.
