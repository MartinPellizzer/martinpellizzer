Syzygium ruminatum

Syzygium ruminatum, commonly known as the Java plum or carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by distinctive star-shaped, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit's unique shape and tangy-sweet flavor make it a popular fruit in many cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
