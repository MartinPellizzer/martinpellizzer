Senecio nephelagetus

Senecio nephelagetus, commonly known as the cloud plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with low water requirements.
