Sparganium erectum var. neglectum

Sparganium erectum var. neglectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bur-reed, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spiky, reddish-brown cones that contain numerous small seeds. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic insects and birds.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
