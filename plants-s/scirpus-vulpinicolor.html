Scirpus vulpinicolor

Scirpus vulpinicolor, commonly known as the purple-flowered bulrush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in water retention.

Scirpus vulpinicolor plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
