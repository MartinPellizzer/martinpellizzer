Serrafalcus divaricatus

Serrafalcus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable serrated fern, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and a branching frond structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are bioluminescent at night, a rare trait among ferns.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining biodiversity.
