Syzygium bankense subsp. bankense

Syzygium bankense subsp.

bankense, commonly known as the white crape myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
