Spodiopogon notopogon

Spodiopogon notopogon, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Spodiopogon notopogon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
