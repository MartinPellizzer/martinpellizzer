Saccoloma elegans

Saccoloma elegans, commonly known as the elegant saccoloma, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Saccoloma elegans is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
