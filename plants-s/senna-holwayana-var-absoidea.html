Senna holwayana var. absoidea

Senna holwayana var. absoidea, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family, and its seed pods are elongated and hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in local agriculture.
