Selaginella rubicundipes

Selaginella rubicundipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a distinctive reddish hue in its stems, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to survive in dry climates with minimal water availability. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a trait common among many ancient plant species.

Selaginella rubicundipes plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
