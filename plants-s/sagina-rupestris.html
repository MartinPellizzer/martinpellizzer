Sagina rupestris

Sagina rupestris, commonly known as stonecress or rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats with tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple and appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

Sagina rupestris is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a hardy species found in rocky soils and gravelly areas.
