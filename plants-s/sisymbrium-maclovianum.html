Sisymbrium maclovianum

Sisymbrium maclovianum, commonly known as the maclovian poppy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sisymbrium maclovianum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
