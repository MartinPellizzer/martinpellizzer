Solanum yangambiense

Solanum yangambiense, commonly known as the Yangambi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.
