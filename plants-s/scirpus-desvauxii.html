Scirpus desvauxii

Scirpus desvauxii, commonly known as the desert bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in saline or alkaline soils.

Scirpus desvauxii plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
