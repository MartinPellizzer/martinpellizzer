Scleria chinensis var. biauriculata

Scleria chinensis var. biauriculata, commonly known as the two-ear sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikes that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are often greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
