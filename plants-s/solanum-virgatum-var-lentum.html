Solanum virgatum var. lentum

Solanum virgatum var. lentum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of streams or ponds.
