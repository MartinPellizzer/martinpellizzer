Schismus arabicus

Schismus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure adapted to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently in its native environment.

Schismus arabicus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to survive in areas with minimal rainfall.
