Senna pendula var. hemirostrata

Senna pendula var. hemirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-headed senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces narrow, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
