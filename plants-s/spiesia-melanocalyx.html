Spiesia melanocalyx

Spiesia melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-veined evergreen, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with prominent black veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is typically found in coastal areas and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Spiesia melanocalyx is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and hardy nature.
