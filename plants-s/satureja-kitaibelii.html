Satureja kitaibelii

Satureja kitaibelii, commonly known as Kitaibel's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine for their pungent, savory flavor.

Satureja kitaibelii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
