Sideritis tragoriganum subsp. marinae

Sideritis tragoriganum subsp.

marinae, commonly known as the maritime sidereal, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salty sea air and coastal winds. Its leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

This subspecies is valued for its use in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
