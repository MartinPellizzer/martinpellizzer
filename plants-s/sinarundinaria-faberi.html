Sinarundinaria faberi

Sinarundinaria faberi, commonly known as the Chinese cycad, is a unique plant native to China.

It is a gymnosperm, belonging to the family Cycadaceae, and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
