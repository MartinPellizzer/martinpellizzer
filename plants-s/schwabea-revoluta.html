Schwabea revoluta

Schwabea revoluta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved schwabea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Schwabea revoluta is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
