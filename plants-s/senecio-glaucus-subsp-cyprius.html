Senecio glaucus subsp. cyprius

Senecio glaucus subsp.

cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence.

The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Mediterranean flora.
