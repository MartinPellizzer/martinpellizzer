Streptocarpus albus

Streptocarpus albus, commonly known as the white streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking white flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The plant features velvety, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.
