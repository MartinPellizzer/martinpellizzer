Syringa microphylla var. alba

Syringa microphylla var. alba, commonly known as the white meadow hyacinth, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
