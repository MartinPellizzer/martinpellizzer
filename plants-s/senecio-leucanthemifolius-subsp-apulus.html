Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. apulus

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

apulus, commonly known as the Apulian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, fuzzy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
