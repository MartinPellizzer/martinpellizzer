Silene acutidentata

Silene acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene acutidentata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
