Saxifraga longifolia subsp. lantoscana

Saxifraga longifolia subsp.

lantoscana, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
