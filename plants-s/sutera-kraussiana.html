Sutera kraussiana

Sutera kraussiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Sutera kraussiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
