Sisymbrium andinum var. latifolium

Sisymbrium andinum var. latifolium, commonly known as the Andean winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across mountainous regions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has potential for agricultural use due to its hardy nature.
