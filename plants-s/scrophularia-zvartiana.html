Scrophularia zvartiana

Scrophularia zvartiana, commonly known as Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia zvartiana is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
