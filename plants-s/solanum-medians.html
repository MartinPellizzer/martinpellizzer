Solanum medians

Solanum medians, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Solanum medians is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
