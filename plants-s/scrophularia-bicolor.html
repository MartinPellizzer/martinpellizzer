Scrophularia bicolor

Scrophularia bicolor, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its two-lipped flowers and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its showy, violet or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia bicolor is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
