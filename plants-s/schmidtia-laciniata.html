Schmidtia laciniata

Schmidtia laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Schmidtia laciniata is well-suited for rock gardens and dry climates due to its drought tolerance.
