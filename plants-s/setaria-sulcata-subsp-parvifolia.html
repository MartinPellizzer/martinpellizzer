Setaria sulcata subsp. parvifolia

Setaria sulcata subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as parvifolia foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hairy leaves and narrow, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its adaptability.

It is often used as a forage crop due to its hardiness and nutritional value.
