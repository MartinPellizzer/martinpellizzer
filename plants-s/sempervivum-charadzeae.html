Sempervivum charadzeae

Sempervivum charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a bluish tint, often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
