Senecio longipedunculatus

Senecio longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled structures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
