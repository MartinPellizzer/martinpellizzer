Spartina pectinata f. variegata

Spartina pectinata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cordgrass, is a variant of Spartina pectinata, a coastal grass species native to the Pacific coast of North America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow stripes on its leaf blades, which set it apart from the typical green form. It thrives in brackish or saltwater environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscape designs.
