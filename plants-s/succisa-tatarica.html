Succisa tatarica

Succisa tatarica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Succisa tatarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
