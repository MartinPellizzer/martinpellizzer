Scleria enchroides

Scleria enchroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scleria enchroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
