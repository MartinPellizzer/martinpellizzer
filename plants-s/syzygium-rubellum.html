Syzygium rubellum

Syzygium rubellum, commonly known as the red apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
