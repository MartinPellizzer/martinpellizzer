Stachys arvensis f. albiflora

Stachys arvensis f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
