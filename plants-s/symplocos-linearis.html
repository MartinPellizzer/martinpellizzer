Symplocos linearis

Symplocos linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf waxberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Symplocos linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
