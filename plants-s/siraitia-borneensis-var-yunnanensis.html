Siraitia borneensis var. yunnanensis

Siraitia borneensis var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan monkfruit, is a member of the Momordica genus in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its most notable feature is the yellow-orange, warty fruit, which is used as a natural sweetener due to its high content of mogrosides.

This variety is valued for its medicinal and culinary applications in traditional herbal medicine.
