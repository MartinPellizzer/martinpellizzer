Shorea induplicata

Shorea induplicata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, typically with a leathery texture.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
