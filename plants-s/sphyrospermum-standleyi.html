Sphyrospermum standleyi

Sphyrospermum standleyi, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
