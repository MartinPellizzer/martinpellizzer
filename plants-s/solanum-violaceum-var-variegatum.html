Solanum violaceum var. variegatum

Solanum violaceum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated purple tomato, is a cultivar of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and purple hues. The plant produces small, purple-fruited tomatoes that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental and edible plant is often grown for its unique appearance and flavorful fruit.
