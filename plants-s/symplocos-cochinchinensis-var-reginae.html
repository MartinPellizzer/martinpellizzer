Symplocos cochinchinensis var. reginae

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in autumn and are edible for birds and some mammals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
