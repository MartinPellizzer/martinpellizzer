Saxifraga redowskyana

Saxifraga redowskyana, commonly known as the Redowsky's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Saxifraga redowskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
