Sclerocactus havasupaiensis var. roseus

Sclerocactus havasupaiensis var. roseus, commonly known as the Roseate Sclerocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is highly valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized succulent gardens.
