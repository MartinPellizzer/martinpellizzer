Setaria japonica

Setaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese foxtail, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and produce a distinctive, bristly appearance.

Setaria japonica is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in certain regions.
