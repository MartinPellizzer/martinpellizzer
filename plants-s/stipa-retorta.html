Stipa retorta

Stipa retorta, commonly known as twisted feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Stipa retorta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
