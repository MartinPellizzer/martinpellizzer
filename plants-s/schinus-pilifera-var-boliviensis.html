Schinus pilifera var. boliviensis

Schinus pilifera var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and consist of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
