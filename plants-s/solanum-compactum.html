Solanum compactum

Solanum compactum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Solanum compactum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
