Sarcocolla longiflora

Sarcocolla longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered sarcocolla, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces that reduce water loss. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Sarcocolla longiflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
