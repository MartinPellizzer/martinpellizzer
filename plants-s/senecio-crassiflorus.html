Senecio crassiflorus

Senecio crassiflorus, commonly known as the thickflower senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
