Stylidium utricularioides

Stylidium utricularioides, commonly known as the bladderworts, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lentibulariaceae and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes. The plant's unique structure includes specialized bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic insects. It thrives in wet, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem as both a predator and a indicator species of wetland health.
