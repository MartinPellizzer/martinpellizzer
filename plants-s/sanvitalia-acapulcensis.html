Sanvitalia acapulcensis

Sanvitalia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a flowering plant native to the Mexican state of Oaxaca.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Sanvitalia acapulcensis is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in semi-desert regions.
