Saxifraga madida var. variegata

Saxifraga madida var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
