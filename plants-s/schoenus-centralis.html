Schoenus centralis

Schoenus centralis, commonly known as the central sedge, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its grass-like appearance features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the spring and summer months.

Schoenus centralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
