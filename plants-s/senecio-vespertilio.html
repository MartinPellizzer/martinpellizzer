Senecio vespertilio

Senecio vespertilio, commonly known as the evening lizard's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Senecio vespertilio is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
