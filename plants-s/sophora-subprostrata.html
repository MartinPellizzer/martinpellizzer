Sophora subprostrata

Sophora subprostrata, commonly known as the prostrate false indigo, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is known for producing a yellow dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
