Salacia macrocarpa var. latifolia

Salacia macrocarpa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved salacia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and greenhouses.
