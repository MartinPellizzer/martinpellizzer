Spiraea douglasii var. roseata

Spiraea douglasii var. roseata, commonly known as the roseleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically pink-tinged when young, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its ornate form and seasonal interest.
