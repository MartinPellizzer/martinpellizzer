Stelis coriifolia

Stelis coriifolia, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Stelis coriifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
