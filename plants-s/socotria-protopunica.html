Socotria protopunica

Socotria protopunica, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a unique plant native to the Socotra archipelago.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native environment.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
