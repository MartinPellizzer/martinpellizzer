Searsia laevigata f. laevigata

Searsia laevigata f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
