Salvia linarioides

Salvia linarioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia linarioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.
