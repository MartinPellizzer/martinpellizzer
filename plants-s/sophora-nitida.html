Sophora nitida

Sophora nitida, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree or silkflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Sophora nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
