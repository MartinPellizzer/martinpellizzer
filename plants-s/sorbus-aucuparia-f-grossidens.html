Sorbus aucuparia f. grossidens

Sorbus aucuparia f. grossidens, commonly known as the large-fruited rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its notably larger berries compared to the typical species, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The large, showy fruits make it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
