Setaria longipila

Setaria longipila, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
