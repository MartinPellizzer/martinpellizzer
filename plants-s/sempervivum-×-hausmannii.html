Sempervivum × hausmannii

Sempervivum × hausmannii, commonly known as the Hahnenkamm or the houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant is native to the European Alps and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces striking clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum × hausmannii is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and container cultivation.
