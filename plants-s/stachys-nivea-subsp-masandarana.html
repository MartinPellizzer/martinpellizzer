Stachys nivea subsp. masandarana

Stachys nivea subsp.

masandarana, commonly known as the white betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
