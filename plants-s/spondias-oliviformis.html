Spondias oliviformis

Spondias oliviformis, commonly known as the olive spondias, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, round fruits that have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
