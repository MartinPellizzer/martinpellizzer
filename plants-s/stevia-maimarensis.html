Stevia maimarensis

Stevia maimarensis, commonly known as the Maimares stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from natural compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its low water requirements and hardiness, it is a popular choice for drought-resistant gardening.
