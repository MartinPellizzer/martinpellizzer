Swertia mattirolii

Swertia mattirolii, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Swertia mattirolii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
