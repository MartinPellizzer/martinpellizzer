Scleria ustulata

Scleria ustulata, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually pale brown to yellowish, contributing to its ornamental appeal in moist garden settings.
