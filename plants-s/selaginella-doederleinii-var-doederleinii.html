Selaginella doederleinii var. doederleinii

Selaginella doederleinii var. doederleinii, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, moss-like foliage and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance.
