Solanum exarmatum

Solanum exarmatum, commonly known as the naked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
