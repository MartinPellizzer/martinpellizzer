Statice ambigua f. major

Statice ambigua f. major, commonly known as the greater statice, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, with flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.
