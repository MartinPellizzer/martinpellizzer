Scaphium macropodum

Scaphium macropodum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are alternate and can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are fibrous and robust.

Scaphium macropodum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
