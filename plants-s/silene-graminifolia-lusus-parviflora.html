Silene graminifolia lusus parviflora

Silene graminifolia lusus parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
