Salicornia persica subsp. rudshurensis

Salicornia persica subsp.

rudshurensis, commonly known as Persian glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and coastal environments. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This subspecies thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native range.
