Senna artemisioides subsp. circinnata

Senna artemisioides subsp.

circinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in narrow, curved pods, which are a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
