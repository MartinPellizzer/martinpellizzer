Salix shiraii var. vulcaniana

Salix shiraii var. vulcaniana, commonly known as the volcanic willow, is a variant of the Salix shiraii species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous catkins. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with volcanic activity. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, dark green upper surface.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
