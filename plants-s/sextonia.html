Sextonia

Sextonia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Sextonia species are typically shrubs or small trees, with woody stems and opposite leaves.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine.
