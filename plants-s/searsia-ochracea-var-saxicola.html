Searsia ochracea var. saxicola

Searsia ochracea var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
