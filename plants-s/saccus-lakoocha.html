Saccus lakoocha

Saccus lakoocha, commonly known as the Indian basket.

It is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
