Saxifraga exarata subsp. exarata

Saxifraga exarata subsp.

exarata, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish in color, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Saxifraga exarata subsp.

exarata is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures and harsh environmental conditions.
