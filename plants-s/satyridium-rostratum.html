Satyridium rostratum

Satyridium rostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and narrow, often with a slight curl, and it produces a single raceme of flowers.

Satyridium rostratum is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions with limited soil nutrients.
