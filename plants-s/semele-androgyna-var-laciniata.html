Semele androgyna var. laciniata

Semele androgyna var. laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in temperate regions.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist soil and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in marshes and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ecological adaptability make it an important species for wetland biodiversity.
