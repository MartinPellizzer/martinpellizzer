Sesleria pichiana

Sesleria pichiana, commonly known as the Pichian grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Sesleria pichiana is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
