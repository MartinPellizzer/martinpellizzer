Salvia acutifolia

Salvia acutifolia, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and striking violet to purple flower spikes. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blossoms that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia acutifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
