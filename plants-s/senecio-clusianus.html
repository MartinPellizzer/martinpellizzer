Senecio clusianus

Senecio clusianus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in spring and are followed by cylindrical seed heads.

Senecio clusianus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
