Satyrium welwitschii

Satyrium welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid to arid environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
