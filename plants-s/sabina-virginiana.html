Sabina virginiana

Sabina virginiana, commonly known as red cedar or eastern red cedar, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in open fields.

Sabina virginiana is valued for its aromatic wood and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
