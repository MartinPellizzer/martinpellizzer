Selaginella ganguliana

Selaginella ganguliana, commonly known as the Indian spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has a distinctive appearance with needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella ganguliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
