Solanum grisebachianum

Solanum grisebachianum, commonly known as the Grisebach's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale blue.

The fruit is a globose berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.
