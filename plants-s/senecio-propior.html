Senecio propior

Senecio propior, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, typically growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Senecio propior is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
