Spiraeopsis pometiiformis

Spiraeopsis pometiiformis, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, flat-topped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges.

Spiraeopsis pometiiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
