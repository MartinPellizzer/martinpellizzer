Syzygium fossiramulosum

Syzygium fossiramulosum, commonly known as the Tasmanian blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, particularly Tasmania, and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong aromatic scent.

Syzygium fossiramulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local cuisine for its edible fruits.
