Sisyrinchium pendulum

Sisyrinchium pendulum, commonly known as the hanging blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
