Silene staminea

Silene staminea, commonly known as the pink stamen, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

Silene staminea is also noted for its showy stamens, which are often more prominent than the petals, giving it its common name.
