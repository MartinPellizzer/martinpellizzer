Silene spergulifolia var. elongata

Silene spergulifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

This variant is often found in meadows and woodland edges, blooming in late spring to early summer.
