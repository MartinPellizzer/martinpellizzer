Syzygium cumingianum

Syzygium cumingianum, commonly known as the purple-leafed syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces fragrant white flowers followed by edible, pinkish-red drupes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium cumingianum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
