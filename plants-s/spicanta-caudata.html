Spicanta caudata

Spicanta caudata, commonly known as the trailing artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for xeriscaping.
