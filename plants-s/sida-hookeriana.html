Sida hookeriana

Sida hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats. The plant has small, pale green to yellowish leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually clustered in loose inflorescences and are characterized by their five-petaled structure.

Sida hookeriana is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
