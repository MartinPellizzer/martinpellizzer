Senecio megaphlebius

Senecio megaphlebius, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
