Scilla carnosula

Scilla carnosula, commonly known as the broadleaf squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, often broad leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. It is typically found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny conditions.

Scilla carnosula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
