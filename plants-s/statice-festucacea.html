Statice festucacea

Statice festucacea, commonly known as sea lavender or saltmeadow cordgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in tidal marshes and sandy shores. Its ability to tolerate high salt levels makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Statice festucacea plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
