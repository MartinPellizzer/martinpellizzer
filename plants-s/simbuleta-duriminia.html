Simbuleta duriminia

Simbuleta duriminia, commonly known as the silverleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that turn silvery white when dry. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas due to its tolerance for salt spray.

Its unique leaf structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
