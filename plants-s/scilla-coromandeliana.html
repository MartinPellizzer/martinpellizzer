Scilla coromandeliana

Scilla coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in spring. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Scilla coromandeliana is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
