Serratula marilandica

Serratula marilandica, commonly known as Maryland feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its dried leaves have been used historically for their calming effects and to treat headaches.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
