Saxifraga cordillerarum var. magellanica

Saxifraga cordillerarum var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
