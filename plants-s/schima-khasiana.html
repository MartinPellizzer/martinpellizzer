Schima khasiana

Schima khasiana, commonly known as Khasi schima, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical and temperate forests.

Schima khasiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
