Saussurea alpina var. cordata

Saussurea alpina var. cordata, commonly known as the Alpine corymbiferous saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces large, showy composite flower heads that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine habitats.
