Scleria macrosperma

Scleria macrosperma, commonly known as the broad-seeded sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster.

The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
