Schomburgkia galeottiana var. chionodora

Schomburgkia galeottiana var. chionodora, commonly known as the white-flowered spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that resemble spider-like blooms, which appear on long, slender stems. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
