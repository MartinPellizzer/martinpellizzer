Sinapis alba subsp. mairei

Sinapis alba subsp.

mairei, commonly known as white mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or entire.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
