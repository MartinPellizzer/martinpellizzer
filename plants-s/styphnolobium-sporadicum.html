Styphnolobium sporadicum

Styphnolobium sporadicum, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive, pendulous, yellowish-green flower clusters during the spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Styphnolobium sporadicum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
