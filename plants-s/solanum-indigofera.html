Solanum indigofera

Solanum indigofera, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum indigofera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
