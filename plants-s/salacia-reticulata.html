Salacia reticulata

Salacia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other members of the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and distinctive, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical interest in Salacia reticulata lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
