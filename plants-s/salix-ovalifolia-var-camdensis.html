Salix ovalifolia var. camdensis

Salix ovalifolia var. camdensis, commonly known as the Camden willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly pointed at the tips. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
