Sorghum familiare

Sorghum familiare, commonly known as millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated for its seeds.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, clustered flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, protein, and fiber, and are used in various food products.

Additionally, sorghum is used in biofuel production and as animal feed due to its high yield and versatility.
