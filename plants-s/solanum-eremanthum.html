Solanum eremanthum

Solanum eremanthum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, berry-like capsule that contains several seeds.
