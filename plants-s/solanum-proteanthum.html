Solanum proteanthum

Solanum proteanthum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Solanum proteanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
