Salix fontqueri

Salix fontqueri, commonly known as the Arizona willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Salix fontqueri plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
