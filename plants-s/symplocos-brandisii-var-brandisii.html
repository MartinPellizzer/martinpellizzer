Symplocos brandisii var. brandisii

Symplocos brandisii var. brandisii, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers and red, fleshy berries that resemble true berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
