Salvia rutilans

Salvia rutilans, commonly known as the scarlet salvia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia rutilans thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
