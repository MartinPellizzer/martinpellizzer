Scirpus subtilissimus

Scirpus subtilissimus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
