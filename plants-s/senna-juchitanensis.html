Senna juchitanensis

Senna juchitanensis, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
