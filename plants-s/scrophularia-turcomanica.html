Scrophularia turcomanica

Scrophularia turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Scrophularia turcomanica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some regions.
