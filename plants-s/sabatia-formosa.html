Sabatia formosa

Sabatia formosa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom from June to September. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Sabatia formosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
