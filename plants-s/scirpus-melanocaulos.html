Scirpus melanocaulos

Scirpus melanocaulos, commonly known as black-stem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus melanocaulos is an important species for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various birds and invertebrates.
