Sobralia porphyrocharis

Sobralia porphyrocharis, commonly known as the Porphyrocharis orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a mix of purple and red hues. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native humid, shaded environments.

Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal in cultivated gardens.
