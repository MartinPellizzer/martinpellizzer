Stackhousia muricata

Stackhousia muricata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed stackhousia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Stackhousia muricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
