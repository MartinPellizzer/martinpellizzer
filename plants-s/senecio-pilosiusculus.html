Senecio pilosiusculus

Senecio pilosiusculus, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
