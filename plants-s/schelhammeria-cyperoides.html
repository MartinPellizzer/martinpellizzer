Schelhammeria cyperoides

Schelhammeria cyperoides, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Schelhammeria cyperoides plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
