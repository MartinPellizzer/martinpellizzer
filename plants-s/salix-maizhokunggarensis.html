Salix maizhokunggarensis

Salix maizhokunggarensis, commonly known as the Maizhokunggu willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its characteristic floral structures. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and wetlands.
