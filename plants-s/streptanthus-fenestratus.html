Streptanthus fenestratus

Streptanthus fenestratus, commonly known as the fenestrated whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a distinctive pungent odor.

Streptanthus fenestratus is known for its ability to produce numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
