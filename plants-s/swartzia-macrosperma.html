Swartzia macrosperma

Swartzia macrosperma, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by hard, woody pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also listed as an endangered species in some regions.
