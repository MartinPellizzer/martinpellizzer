Senecio ionophyllus var. sparsilobatus

Senecio ionophyllus var. sparsilobatus, commonly known as the sparse-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
