Syzygium hullettianum

Syzygium hullettianum, commonly known as the Hullett's apple or the purple apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically purple or red when mature. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. Syzygium hullettianum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
