Solanum junctum

Solanum junctum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum junctum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
