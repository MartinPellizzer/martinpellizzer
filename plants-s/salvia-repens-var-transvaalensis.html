Salvia repens var. transvaalensis

Salvia repens var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers.
