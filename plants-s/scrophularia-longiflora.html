Scrophularia longiflora

Scrophularia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia longiflora is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
