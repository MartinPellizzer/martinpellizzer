Scleria haematostachys

Scleria haematostachys, commonly known as red-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Scleria haematostachys plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
