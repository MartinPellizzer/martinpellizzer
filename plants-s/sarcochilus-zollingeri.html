Sarcochilus zollingeri

Sarcochilus zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its growth habit is rhizomatous, with a short, stocky stem that bears a single flower spike.

Sarcochilus zollingeri is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
