Salix calyculata var. gongshanica

Salix calyculata var. gongshanica, commonly known as the Gongshan willow, is a shrub or small tree native to high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its distinctive, elongated leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils and supporting local biodiversity.
