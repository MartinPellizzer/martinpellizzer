Sorbus nupta

Sorbus nupta, commonly known as the wedding rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
