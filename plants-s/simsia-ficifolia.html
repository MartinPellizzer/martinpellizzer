Simsia ficifolia

Simsia ficifolia, commonly known as the "false fig tree," is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that resembles a fig, hence its common name.

Simsia ficifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
