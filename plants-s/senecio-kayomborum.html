Senecio kayomborum

Senecio kayomborum, commonly known as the Kayombor Mountains groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern Cape province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
