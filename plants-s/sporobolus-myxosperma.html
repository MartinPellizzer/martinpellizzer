Sporobolus myxosperma

Sporobolus myxosperma, commonly known as the sticky spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomes allow it to form dense colonies, which can dominate wetland and riparian habitats.

The botanical features of Sporobolus myxosperma make it well adapted to moist, disturbed environments.
