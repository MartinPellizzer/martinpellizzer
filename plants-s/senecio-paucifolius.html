Senecio paucifolius

Senecio paucifolius, commonly known as the few-leaved senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers in composite flower heads. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
