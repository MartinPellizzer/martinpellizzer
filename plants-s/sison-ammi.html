Sison ammi

Sison ammi, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a stout, hairy stem that branches near the base, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are small, black, and covered in a white, powdery coating, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

Sison ammi is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.
