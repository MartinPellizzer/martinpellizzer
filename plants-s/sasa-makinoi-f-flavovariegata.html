Sasa makinoi f. flavovariegata

Sasa makinoi f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the yellow-variegated bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa makinoi species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green variegated foliage, which sets it apart from other bamboo varieties. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming a compact, shrub-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a subtle fragrance.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
