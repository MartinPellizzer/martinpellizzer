Sorghum plumosum

Sorghum plumosum, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Sorghum plumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful, flowing foliage.
