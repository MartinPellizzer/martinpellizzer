Syzygium triplinervium

Syzygium triplinervium, commonly known as the three-veined sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits with a crisp, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
