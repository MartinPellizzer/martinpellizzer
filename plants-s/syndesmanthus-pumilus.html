Syndesmanthus pumilus

Syndesmanthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a small shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Syndesmanthus pumilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
