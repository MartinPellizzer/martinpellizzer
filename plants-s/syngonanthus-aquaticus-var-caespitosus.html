Syngonanthus aquaticus var. caespitosus

Syngonanthus aquaticus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted water hyacinth, is a variant of the water hyacinth characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It is an aquatic plant native to South America but has been introduced to various parts of the world, often becoming an invasive species. The plant has floating leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and produce showy, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to absorb nutrients make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in aquatic ecosystems.
