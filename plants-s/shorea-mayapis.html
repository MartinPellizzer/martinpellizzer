Shorea mayapis

Shorea mayapis, commonly known as red mangrove or Spanish cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and exuding sap. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its durable, aromatic wood. It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing shorelines.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes.
