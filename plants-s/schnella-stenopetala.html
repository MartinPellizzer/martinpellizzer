Schnella stenopetala

Schnella stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
