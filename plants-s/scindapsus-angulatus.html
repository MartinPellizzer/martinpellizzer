Scindapsus angulatus

Scindapsus angulatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.

Scindapsus angulatus thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.
