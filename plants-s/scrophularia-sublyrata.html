Scrophularia sublyrata

Scrophularia sublyrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal uses. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
