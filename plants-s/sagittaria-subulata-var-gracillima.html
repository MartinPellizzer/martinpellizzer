Sagittaria subulata var. gracillima

Sagittaria subulata var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in dense spikes. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
