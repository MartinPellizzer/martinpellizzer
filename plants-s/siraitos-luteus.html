Siraitos luteus

Siraitos luteus, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Siraitos luteus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
