Scybalium fungiforme

Scybalium fungiforme, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, horn-like floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Scybalium fungiforme is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
