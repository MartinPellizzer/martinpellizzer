Sida cistiflora

Sida cistiflora, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sida cistiflora is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
