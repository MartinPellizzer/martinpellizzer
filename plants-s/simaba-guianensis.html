Simaba guianensis

Simaba guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese simaba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
