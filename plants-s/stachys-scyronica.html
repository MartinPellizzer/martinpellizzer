Stachys scyronica

Stachys scyronica, commonly known as the Scyronica hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Stachys scyronica is known for its hairy foliage and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
