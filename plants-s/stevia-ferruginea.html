Stevia ferruginea

Stevia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Stevia ferruginea is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
