Scirpus subaphyllus

Scirpus subaphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. Native to wetland habitats, it thrives in shallow water or moist soil conditions. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
