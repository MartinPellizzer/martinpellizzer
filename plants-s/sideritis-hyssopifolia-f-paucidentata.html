Sideritis hyssopifolia f. paucidentata

Sideritis hyssopifolia f. paucidentata, commonly known as the lesser woolly ironwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
