Smilax dilatata

Smilax dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny projections and is often found in moist, wooded areas. The plant has compound leaves that are usually trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Smilax dilatata is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
