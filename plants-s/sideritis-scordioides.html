Sideritis scordioides

Sideritis scordioides, commonly known as mountain thyme or Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Sideritis scordioides is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
