Sanchezia hirsuta

Sanchezia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sanchezia, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Sanchezia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
