Stephania japonica var. timoriensis

Stephania japonica var. timoriensis, commonly known as Timor Japanese stephania, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the islands of Timor and surrounding areas in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
