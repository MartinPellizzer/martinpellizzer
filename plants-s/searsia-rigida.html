Searsia rigida

Searsia rigida, commonly known as the hard thornbush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rigid, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and coastal regions.

Searsia rigida is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
