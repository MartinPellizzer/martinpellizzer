Spilanthes stolonifera var. pusilla

Spilanthes stolonifera var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf stoloniferous bladderpod, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems that produce small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces rounded, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its chemical compounds.
