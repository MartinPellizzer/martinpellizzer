Silene cinerea var. cirtensis

Silene cinerea var. cirtensis, commonly known as the Cirtensis Blushflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene cinerea var. cirtensis is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
