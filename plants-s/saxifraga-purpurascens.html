Saxifraga purpurascens

Saxifraga purpurascens, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, basal leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Saxifraga purpurascens is also notable for its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
