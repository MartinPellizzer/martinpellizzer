Setaria verticillata var. latifolia

Setaria verticillata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf millet, is a grass species native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. It has a fibrous root system and can grow in both full sun and partial shade.

This variant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
