Stizolobium novoguineense

Stizolobium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea legume, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ecological and potential agricultural significance, it is of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
