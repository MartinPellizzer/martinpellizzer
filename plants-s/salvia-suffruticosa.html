Salvia suffruticosa

Salvia suffruticosa, commonly known as Portuguese sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Salvia suffruticosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
