Scheuchzeria pseudoasphodelus

Scheuchzeria pseudoasphodelus, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a fossil plant that lived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure and its unique, spirally arranged leaves. The plant had a complex vascular system, allowing it to grow to significant heights. Its reproductive structures, including spore cones, are well-preserved in the fossil record.

This plant played a crucial role in the ancient ecosystems, contributing to the formation of coal deposits.
