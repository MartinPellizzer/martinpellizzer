Salix integra

Salix integra, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its striking cascading branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are essential for pollination.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along water bodies.
