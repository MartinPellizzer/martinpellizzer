Scirpus angolensis var. megastachyum

Scirpus angolensis var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often forming dense stands in wetland environments. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences of small, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important species in stabilizing aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water depths and soil conditions.
