Saxifraga × reyeri

Saxifraga × reyeri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
