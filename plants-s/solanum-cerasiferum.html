Solanum cerasiferum

Solanum cerasiferum, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy, white to pale purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The fruits are edible when ripe and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
