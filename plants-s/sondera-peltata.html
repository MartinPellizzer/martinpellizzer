Sondera peltata

Sondera peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sondera, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
