Spondias lutea var. pseudomyrobalanus

Spondias lutea var. pseudomyrobalanus, commonly known as the false mango or Indian tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small mangoes.
