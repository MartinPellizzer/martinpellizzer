Silene gallica subvar. modesta

Silene gallica subvar. modesta, commonly known as the modest campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
