Sesamoides pygmaea var. sesamoides

Sesamoides pygmaea var. sesamoides, commonly known as the dwarf sesamoid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
