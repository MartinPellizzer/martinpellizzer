Sambucus buergeriana var. platyphylla

Sambucus buergeriana var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are broad and have a distinctive flat structure. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
