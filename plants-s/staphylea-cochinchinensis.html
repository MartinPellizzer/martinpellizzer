Staphylea cochinchinensis

Staphylea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese horned vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant's unique characteristic is its fruit, which resembles a horn and contains several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
