Stachytarpheta jamaicensis f. monstrosa

Stachytarpheta jamaicensis f. monstrosa, commonly known as the Jamaican verbena or giant verbena, is a variant of the Stachytarpheta genus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from violet to pink and are clustered in terminal inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
