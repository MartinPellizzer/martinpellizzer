Styrax hemsleyanus var. griseus

Styrax hemsleyanus var. griseus, commonly known as the gray styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
