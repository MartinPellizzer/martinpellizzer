Senecio syringifolius

Senecio syringifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
