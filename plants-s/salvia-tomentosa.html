Salvia tomentosa

Salvia tomentosa, commonly known as wooly sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
