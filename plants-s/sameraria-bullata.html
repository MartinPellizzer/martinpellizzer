Sameraria bullata

Sameraria bullata, commonly known as the bullate sameraria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often waxy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
