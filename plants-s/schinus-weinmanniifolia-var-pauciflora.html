Schinus weinmanniifolia var. pauciflora

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some environments.
