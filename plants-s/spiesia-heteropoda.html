Spiesia heteropoda

Spiesia heteropoda, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of its dioecious nature. Its fruit is a small, spherical fig-like structure that is edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Spiesia heteropoda is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.
