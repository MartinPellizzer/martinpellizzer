Sida ignaciana

Sida ignaciana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.

Sida ignaciana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
