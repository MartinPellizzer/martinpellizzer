Schinus appariciana

Schinus appariciana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, bright red fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruiting display. However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, where it can outcompete native vegetation.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and ecologically problematic depending on the environment.
