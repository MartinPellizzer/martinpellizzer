Saxifraga exarata var. pyrenaica

Saxifraga exarata var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments, where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette structure, and are characterized by their serrated edges and hairy surfaces.

This species is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and limited soil nutrients.
