Salvia phlomoides subsp. phlomoides

Salvia phlomoides subsp.

phlomoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a silvery appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
