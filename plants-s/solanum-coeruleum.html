Solanum coeruleum

Solanum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshade plants. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often trailing.

Solanum coeruleum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique flowering behavior.
