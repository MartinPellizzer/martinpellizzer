Salvia fratrum

Salvia fratrum, commonly known as the "herb of the brothers," is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia fratrum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
