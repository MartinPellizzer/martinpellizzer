Sideritis leucantha subsp. illicitana

Sideritis leucantha subsp.

illicitana, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
