Saccharum antillarum

Saccharum antillarum, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its jointed stalks and dense, leafy foliage, which contribute to its robust growth in warm climates. The plant is cultivated for its high sugar content in the stems, which are processed into sugar and biofuel. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a long growing season with adequate sunlight.

Saccharum antillarum plays a significant role in both agriculture and global economies due to its versatile uses.
