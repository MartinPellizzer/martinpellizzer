Saussurea crassicaulis

Saussurea crassicaulis, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions in Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and thick, woolly leaves that help it survive in cold, arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
