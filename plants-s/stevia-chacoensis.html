Stevia chacoensis

Stevia chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Chaco region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal and culinary potential, it has gained interest in sustainable agriculture and herbal medicine.
