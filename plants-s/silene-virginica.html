Silene virginica

Silene virginica, commonly known as the pinkroot or Virginia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
