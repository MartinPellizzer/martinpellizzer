Senecio odoratus var. laciniatus

Senecio odoratus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the fragrant senecio or the lacey senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that give it a delicate, almost feathery appearance. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
