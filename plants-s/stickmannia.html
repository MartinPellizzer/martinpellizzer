Stickmannia

Stickmannia, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Stickmannia is known for its unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
