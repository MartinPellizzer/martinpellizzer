Sophora glabra

Sophora glabra, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and have a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers in the spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
