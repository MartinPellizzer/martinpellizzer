Svenkoeltzia pamelae

Svenkoeltzia pamelae, commonly known as the Pamela's ghost fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that resemble delicate wisps of smoke, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in high humidity and shaded environments, often growing on the forest floor or on the trunks of ancient trees. Its leaves are typically a deep green, with a subtle sheen that reflects the dappled light of the canopy.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
