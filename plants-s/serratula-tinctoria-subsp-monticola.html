Serratula tinctoria subsp. monticola

Serratula tinctoria subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
