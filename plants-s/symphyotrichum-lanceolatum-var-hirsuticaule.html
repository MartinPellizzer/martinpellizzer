Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hirsuticaule

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hirsuticaule, commonly known as the hairy stem ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are densely covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of purple to pinkish-purple composite flowers in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and is often found in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
