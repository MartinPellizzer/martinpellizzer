Solidago bicolor var. luteola

Solidago bicolor var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads that contrast with its green leaves and gray-green stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, meadows, and along stream banks. Its showy inflorescence consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by yellow ray florets.

It is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
