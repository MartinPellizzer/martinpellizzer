Serjania araquei

Serjania araquei, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain multiple seeds.

Serjania araquei is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
