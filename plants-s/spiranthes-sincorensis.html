Spiranthes sincorensis

Spiranthes sincorensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often in partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender stem.

This orchid is known for its unique floral structure and is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
