Sarcandra glabra subsp. glabra

Sarcandra glabra subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth sarcandra, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries.

The subspecies is characterized by its lack of pubescence, giving it a smooth appearance.
