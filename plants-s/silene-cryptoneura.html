Silene cryptoneura

Silene cryptoneura, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a striking plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale blue to violet flowers, which appear in late summer and early fall. The plant is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often hairy foliage. Silene cryptoneura typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its unique coloration and ecological niche make it a notable species in its native range.
