Sorbus aria var. lachnophylla

Sorbus aria var. lachnophylla, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or on rocky slopes.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
