Sorbus groenlandica

Sorbus groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in harsh environments, such as tundra and rocky slopes, and is adapted to cold climates.

The berries are an important food source for birds and mammals in its native habitat.
