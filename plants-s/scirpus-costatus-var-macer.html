Scirpus costatus var. macer

Scirpus costatus var. macer, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its stems are often reddish in color and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is known for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
