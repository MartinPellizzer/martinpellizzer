Sarcochilus chrysanthus

Sarcochilus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a slender, fleshy rhizome and produces striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow lance-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light conditions.
