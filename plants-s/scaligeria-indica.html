Scaligeria indica

Scaligeria indica, commonly known as Indian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its roots are the primary part used, and they are valued for their starchy, edible qualities.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
