Selenipedium schlimii

Selenipedium schlimii, commonly known as the giant spider-orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that can reach up to 3 meters in length, adorned with large, showy flowers that resemble spiders. The plant is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique morphology, including its thick, fleshy rhizomes and evergreen leaves. Selenipedium schlimii is a slow-growing species that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
