Soymida roupalifolia

Soymida roupalifolia, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
