Solidago bigelovii

Solidago bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Solidago bigelovii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
