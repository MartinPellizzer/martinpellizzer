Simaba subcymosa

Simaba subcymosa, commonly known as the Caribbean cherry or pink hibiscus.

It is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry with a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has some traditional medicinal uses.
