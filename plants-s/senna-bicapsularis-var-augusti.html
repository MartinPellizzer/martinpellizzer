Senna bicapsularis var. augusti

Senna bicapsularis var. augusti, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a distinctive two-seeded pod that splits open upon maturity to release the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
