Solanum carmanicum

Solanum carmanicum, commonly known as the Carmine Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, purple to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value is sometimes appreciated, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
