Sasa kaiensis

Sasa kaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, often used in traditional Japanese crafts.

Sasa kaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local cuisine and basket-making.
