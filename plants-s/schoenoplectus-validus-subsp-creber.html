Schoenoplectus validus subsp. creber

Schoenoplectus validus subsp.

creber, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils. Schoenoplectus validus subsp.

creber is also valued for its ability to filter water and support diverse aquatic ecosystems.
