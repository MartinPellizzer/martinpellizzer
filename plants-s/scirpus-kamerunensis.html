Scirpus kamerunensis

Scirpus kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus kamerunensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
