Stemonurus umbellatus var. ovalifolius

Stemonurus umbellatus var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved stemonurus, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate to oval-shaped leaves.

The foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant is native to the temperate regions of Asia and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
