Solanum intermedium

Solanum intermedium, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, woodlands, and along roadsides.
