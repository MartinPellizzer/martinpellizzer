Salvia officinalis subvar. mariolensis

Salvia officinalis subvar. mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolensis sage, is a subspecies of the common sage plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
