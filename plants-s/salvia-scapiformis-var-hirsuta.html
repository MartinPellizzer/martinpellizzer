Salvia scapiformis var. hirsuta

Salvia scapiformis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy scabious, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured look.

This species is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
