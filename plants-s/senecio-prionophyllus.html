Senecio prionophyllus

Senecio prionophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio prionophyllus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
