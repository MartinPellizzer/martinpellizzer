Stachys ramosa

Stachys ramosa, commonly known as creeping betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces and a distinctive basal rosette.

Stachys ramosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
