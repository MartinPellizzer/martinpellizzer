Scleria pauciflora var. kansana

Scleria pauciflora var. kansana, commonly known as the Kansan flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous growth habit and small, three-lobed flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
