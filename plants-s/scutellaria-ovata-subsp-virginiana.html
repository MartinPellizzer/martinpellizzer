Scutellaria ovata subsp. virginiana

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

virginiana, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has oval, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
