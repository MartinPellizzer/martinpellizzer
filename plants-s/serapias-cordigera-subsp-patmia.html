Serapias cordigera subsp. patmia

Serapias cordigera subsp.

patmia, commonly known as the Alpine marsh gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat succulent.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
