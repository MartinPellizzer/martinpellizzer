Salix vendeana

Salix vendeana, commonly known as the Vendeana willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

Salix vendeana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
