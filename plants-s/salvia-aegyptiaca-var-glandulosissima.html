Salvia aegyptiaca var. glandulosissima

Salvia aegyptiaca var. glandulosissima, commonly known as Egyptian sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, violet to blue flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
