Sida multicrena

Sida multicrena, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Sida multicrena is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
