Saxifraga hirculus var. propinqua

Saxifraga hirculus var. propinqua, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically features rosettes of dark green, woolly leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes, where it can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species within its native habitat.
