Salvadora indica

Salvadora indica, commonly known as the toothbrush tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well-known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain compounds that have been used for dental hygiene and skin care.

Salvadora indica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
