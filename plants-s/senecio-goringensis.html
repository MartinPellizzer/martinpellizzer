Senecio goringensis

Senecio goringensis, commonly known as the Goring's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio goringensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
