Sorbus torminalis f. pomoidea

Sorbus torminalis f. pomoidea, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its rounded, dense foliage and small, white to yellowish fruits that appear in the fall. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

The leaves are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
