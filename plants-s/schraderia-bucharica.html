Schraderia bucharica

Schraderia bucharica, commonly known as the Bukhara bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Schraderia bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
