Stipa cyllenaea

Stipa cyllenaea, commonly known as the blackgrass or slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Stipa cyllenaea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
