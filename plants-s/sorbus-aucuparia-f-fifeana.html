Sorbus aucuparia f. fifeana

Sorbus aucuparia f. fifeana, commonly known as the Fife's rowan, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange berries that persist through winter, providing ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its vibrant seasonal color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
