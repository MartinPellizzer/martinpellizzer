Senecio nebrodensis var. laciniatus

Senecio nebrodensis var. laciniatus, commonly known as the Sicilian ragged gentian, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in sunny, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
