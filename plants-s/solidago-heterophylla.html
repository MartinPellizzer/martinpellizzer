Solidago heterophylla

Solidago heterophylla, commonly known as the variable goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate leaves that vary in shape along the stem, a unique feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including moist meadows and woodlands, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Solidago heterophylla is a hardy perennial that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
