Scabiosa gramuntia var. agrestis

Scabiosa gramuntia var. agrestis, commonly known as the field scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and meadows.

This variety is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
