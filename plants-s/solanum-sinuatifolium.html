Solanum sinuatifolium

Solanum sinuatifolium, commonly known as the sinuate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its lobed, sinuate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is noted for its ornamental value.

However, due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
