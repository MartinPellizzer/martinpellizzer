Stachys pascuicola

Stachys pascuicola, commonly known as the meadow betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, forming whorled clusters along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
