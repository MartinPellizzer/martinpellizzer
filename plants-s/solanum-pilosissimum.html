Solanum pilosissimum

Solanum pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and animals, are orange-red when ripe.

Solanum pilosissimum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
