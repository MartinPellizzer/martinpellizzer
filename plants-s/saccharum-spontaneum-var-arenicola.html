Saccharum spontaneum var. arenicola

Saccharum spontaneum var. arenicola, commonly known as the coastal sugarcane grass, is a wild grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. This variety thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, making it resilient to environmental stressors. Its fibrous structure and high sugar content make it a valuable resource for both ecological and agricultural purposes.

The plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
