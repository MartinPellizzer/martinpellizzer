Sedum polycephalum

Sedum polycephalum, commonly known as "Ice Plant" or "Snowplant," is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to arid landscapes.
