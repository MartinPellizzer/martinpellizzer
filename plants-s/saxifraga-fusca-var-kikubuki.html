Saxifraga fusca var. kikubuki

Saxifraga fusca var. kikubuki, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle red or purple hue. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in rocky or dry environments. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded areas and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
