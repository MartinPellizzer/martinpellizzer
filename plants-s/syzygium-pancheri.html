Syzygium pancheri

Syzygium pancheri, commonly known as the Pancherii tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium pancheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
