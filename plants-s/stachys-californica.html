Stachys californica

Stachys californica, commonly known as California betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in whorls along the upper stem, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its medicinal properties have historically been valued by indigenous communities for their healing benefits.
