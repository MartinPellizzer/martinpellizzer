Saussurea taquetii var. paniculata

Saussurea taquetii var. paniculata, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense inflorescence of yellow to orange-colored florets. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in cold, arid environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its unique botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
