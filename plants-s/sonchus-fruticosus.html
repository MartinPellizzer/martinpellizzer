Sonchus fruticosus

Sonchus fruticosus, commonly known as rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a robust, branched stem covered in coarse hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from summer to autumn, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
