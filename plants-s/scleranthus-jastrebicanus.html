Scleranthus jastrebicanus

Scleranthus jastrebicanus, commonly known as the Jastrebican thornweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying shades of pink or red.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
