Solanum chilotanum var. talukdarii

Solanum chilotanum var. talukdarii, commonly known as the Talukdarii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. It produces berries that are generally toxic but have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
