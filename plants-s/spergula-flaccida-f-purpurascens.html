Spergula flaccida f. purpurascens

Spergula flaccida f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered spikerush, is a variant of the spikerush species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The distinguishing feature of this form is its purple-pink flower heads, which contrast with the typical greenish flowers of the species. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
