Sideritis sauvageana

Sideritis sauvageana, commonly known as Corsican mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Sideritis sauvageana is valued for its fragrant foliage and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
