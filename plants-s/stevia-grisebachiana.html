Stevia grisebachiana

Stevia grisebachiana, commonly known as the silverleaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia grisebachiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
