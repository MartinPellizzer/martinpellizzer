Senna guatemalensis var. calcarea

Senna guatemalensis var. calcarea, commonly known as the Guatemalan senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in various habitats.
