Senecio koualapensis

Senecio koualapensis, commonly known as the Koualapensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Cameroon.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
