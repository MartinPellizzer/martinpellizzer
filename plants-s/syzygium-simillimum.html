Syzygium simillimum

Syzygium simillimum, commonly known as the white apple or white syzygium, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, white or pale yellow fruits.

Syzygium simillimum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
