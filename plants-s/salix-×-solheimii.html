Salix × solheimii

Salix × solheimii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a hybrid between Salix phylicifolia and Salix viminalis, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The species is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

Salix × solheimii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in wet or marginal soils.
