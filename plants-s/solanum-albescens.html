Solanum albescens

Solanum albescens, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its white or pale flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a branching structure, making it a distinctive member of its genus.

Solanum albescens is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
