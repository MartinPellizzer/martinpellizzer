Senecio × marceri

Senecio × marceri, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Senecio cineraria and Senecio crassus.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and composite flower heads.

Senecio × marceri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
