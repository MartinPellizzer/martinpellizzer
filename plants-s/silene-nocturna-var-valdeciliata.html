Silene nocturna var. valdeciliata

Silene nocturna var. valdeciliata, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and sometimes branched.

The species is noted for its unique flowering time and ecological role in supporting night-active insect populations.
