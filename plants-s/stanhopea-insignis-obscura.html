Stanhopea insignis obscura

Stanhopea insignis obscura, commonly known as the dark-stemmed stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are highly prized in horticulture for their beauty and unique scent, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
