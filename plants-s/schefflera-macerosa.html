Schefflera macerosa

Schefflera macerosa, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
