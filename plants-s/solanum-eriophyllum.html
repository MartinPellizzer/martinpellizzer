Solanum eriophyllum

Solanum eriophyllum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The berries that follow the flowers are usually yellow or orange and contain toxic compounds.
