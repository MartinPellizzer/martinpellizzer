Symphyotrichum racemosum

Symphyotrichum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy composite flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Symphyotrichum racemosum is an important nectar source for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
