Sondottia

Sondottia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred orchid flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Sondottia is often found in alpine meadows and is a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in ecological and horticultural research.
