Syzygium hallieri

Syzygium hallieri, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are edible but often considered astringent. It is valued for its hard, heavy timber, which is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Syzygium hallieri is also an important plant for native wildlife, providing food and habitat.
