Scutellaria indica f. subacaulis

Scutellaria indica f. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
