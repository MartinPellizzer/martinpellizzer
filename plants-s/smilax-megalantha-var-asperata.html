Smilax megalantha var. asperata

Smilax megalantha var. asperata, commonly known as the rough smilax, is a flowering plant species native to parts of South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and persistent, spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
