Soulamea terminalioides

Soulamea terminalioides, commonly known as the silverleaf soulamea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often hidden within a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
