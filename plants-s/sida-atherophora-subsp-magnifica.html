Sida atherophora subsp. magnifica

Sida atherophora subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the showy sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture, and it grows in a variety of habitats including wetlands and disturbed soils. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
