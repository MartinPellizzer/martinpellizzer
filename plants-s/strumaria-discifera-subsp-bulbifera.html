Strumaria discifera subsp. bulbifera

Strumaria discifera subsp.

bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to produce bulb-like structures, which serve as storage organs and aid in propagation. These bulbs allow the plant to survive in dry conditions and regenerate after periods of drought.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
