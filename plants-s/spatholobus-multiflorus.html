Spatholobus multiflorus

Spatholobus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are often clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
