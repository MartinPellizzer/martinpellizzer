Solanum dallachii

Solanum dallachii, commonly known as the Dallachii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum dallachii is often found in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
