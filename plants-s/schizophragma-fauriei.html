Schizophragma fauriei

Schizophragma fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit. Schizophragma fauriei thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
