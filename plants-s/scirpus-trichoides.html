Scirpus trichoides

Scirpus trichoides, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to light brown flowers at the top of its stems. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus trichoides plays an important role in supporting aquatic and wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
