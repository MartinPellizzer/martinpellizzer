Sporobolus niamensis

Sporobolus niamensis, commonly known as the Niamensi grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and typically have a rough, hairy texture.

Sporobolus niamensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
