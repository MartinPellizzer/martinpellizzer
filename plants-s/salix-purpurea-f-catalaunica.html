Salix purpurea f. catalaunica

Salix purpurea f. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica willow, is a rare and distinctive form of the purple willow.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
