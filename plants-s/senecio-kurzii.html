Senecio kurzii

Senecio kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial, with hairy, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
