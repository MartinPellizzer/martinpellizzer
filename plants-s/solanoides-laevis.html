Solanoides laevis

Solanoides laevis, commonly known as the smooth solanum, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
