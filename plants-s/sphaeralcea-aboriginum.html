Sphaeralcea aboriginum

Sphaeralcea aboriginum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, fuzzy seed pods and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and waxy leaves to conserve water. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Sphaeralcea aboriginum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
