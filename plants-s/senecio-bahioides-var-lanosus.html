Senecio bahioides var. lanosus

Senecio bahioides var. lanosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
