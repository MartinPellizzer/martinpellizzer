Senecio grayanus

Senecio grayanus, commonly known as Gray's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disc, and its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped.

Senecio grayanus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
