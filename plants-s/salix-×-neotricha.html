Salix × neotricha

Salix × neotricha, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is a hybrid between Salix matsudana and Salix babylonica, characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, inconspicuous catkins in early spring.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
