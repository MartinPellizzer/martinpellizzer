Schefflera polybotrya

Schefflera polybotrya, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered leaves that form a compact, bushy shape. The plant features large, leathery, palmate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. Schefflera polybotrya produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are attractive to birds.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
