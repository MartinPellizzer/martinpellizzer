Scleranthus ferrugineus

Scleranthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty sand-rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has erect, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Scleranthus ferrugineus is well adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.
