Sedum dasyphyllum var. luteovirens

Sedum dasyphyllum var. luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically bright green with a yellowish or reddish tinge, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a low-growing species, often forming dense mats, and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
