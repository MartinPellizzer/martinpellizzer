Silene laxipruinosa

Silene laxipruinosa, commonly known as the rough silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Silene laxipruinosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
