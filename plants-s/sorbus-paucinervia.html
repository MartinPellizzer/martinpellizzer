Sorbus paucinervia

Sorbus paucinervia, commonly known as the few-veined whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with few veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
