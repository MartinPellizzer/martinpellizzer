Syngonanthus wilsonii

Syngonanthus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to eastern Australia and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific habitat requirements, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its environment.
