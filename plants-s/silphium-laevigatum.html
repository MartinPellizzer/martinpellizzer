Silphium laevigatum

Silphium laevigatum, commonly known as smooth silphium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a loose panicle during the summer months. Silphium laevigatum is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
