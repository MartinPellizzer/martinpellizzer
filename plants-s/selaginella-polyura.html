Selaginella polyura

Selaginella polyura, commonly known as the zigzag mossy fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance, with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a moss-like appearance. Selaginella polyura is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native range.
