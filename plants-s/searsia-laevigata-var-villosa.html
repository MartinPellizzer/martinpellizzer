Searsia laevigata var. villosa

Searsia laevigata var. villosa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The silver tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
