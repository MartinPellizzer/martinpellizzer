Stipa grafiana f. pubiflora

Stipa grafiana f. pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. It produces small, pale green or yellowish spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

This ornamental grass is often used in landscaping for its elegant, feathery look and drought tolerance.
