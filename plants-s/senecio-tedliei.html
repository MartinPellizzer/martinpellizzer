Senecio tedliei

Senecio tedliei, commonly known as the tedliei groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and hairy, gray-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
