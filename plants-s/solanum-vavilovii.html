Solanum vavilovii

Solanum vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

The plant is characterized by its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
