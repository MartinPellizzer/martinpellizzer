Solanum jussiaei

Solanum jussiaei, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum jussiaei is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.
