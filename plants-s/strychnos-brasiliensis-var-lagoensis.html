Strychnos brasiliensis var. lagoensis

Strychnos brasiliensis var. lagoensis, commonly known as the Lagoensis Strychnos, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
