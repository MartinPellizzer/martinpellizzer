Sorbus aucuparia subf. platyphylla

Sorbus aucuparia subf.

platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive flat, elliptical leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
