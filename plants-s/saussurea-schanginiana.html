Saussurea schanginiana

Saussurea schanginiana, commonly known as the Tibetan yellowhead, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and yellowish to orange-colored flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme altitudes and cold climates. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with bracts that contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Saussurea schanginiana is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
