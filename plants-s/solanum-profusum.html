Solanum profusum

Solanum profusum, commonly known as the profuse nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum profusum thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in moist forests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
