Spiraea japonica subvar. angulata

Spiraea japonica subvar. angulata, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, and its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
