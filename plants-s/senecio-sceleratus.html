Senecio sceleratus

Senecio sceleratus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Senecio sceleratus is also used in horticulture as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
