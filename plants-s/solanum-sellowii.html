Solanum sellowii

Solanum sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are a food source for certain bird species.

Solanum sellowii is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
