Schmaltzia terebinthifolia

Schmaltzia terebinthifolia, commonly known as the Jerusalem thorn or the thorny buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and yellowish-green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Schmaltzia terebinthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
