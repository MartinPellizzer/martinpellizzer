Selaginella leucoloma

Selaginella leucoloma, commonly known as the white-stemmed spikemoss, is a species of moss plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branching stems. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are small, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Selaginella leucoloma plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
