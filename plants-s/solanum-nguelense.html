Solanum nguelense

Solanum nguelense, commonly known as the Nguelense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its tubular flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its berries are small, yellowish, and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a role in local ecosystems and may have traditional uses in certain regions.
