Scutellaria albida subsp. subsimilis

Scutellaria albida subsp.

subsimilis, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
