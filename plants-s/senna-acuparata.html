Senna acuparata

Senna acuparata, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, flat seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Senna acuparata is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
