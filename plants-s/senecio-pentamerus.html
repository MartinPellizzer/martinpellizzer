Senecio pentamerus

Senecio pentamerus, commonly known as the five-merous senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.

Senecio pentamerus is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
