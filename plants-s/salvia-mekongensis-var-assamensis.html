Salvia mekongensis var. assamensis

Salvia mekongensis var. assamensis, commonly known as the Assam sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas and northern India.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
