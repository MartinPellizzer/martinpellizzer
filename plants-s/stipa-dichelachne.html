Stipa dichelachne

Stipa dichelachne, commonly known as the narrowleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Stipa dichelachne is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitats.
