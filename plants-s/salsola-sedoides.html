Salsola sedoides

Salsola sedoides, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Salsola sedoides is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
