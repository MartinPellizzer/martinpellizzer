Strychnos odorata

Strychnos odorata, commonly known as the fragrant strychnos, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
