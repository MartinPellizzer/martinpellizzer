Senna occidentalis var. andhrica

Senna occidentalis var. andhrica, commonly known as Indian senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, twisted pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste lands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
