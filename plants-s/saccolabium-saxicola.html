Saccolabium saxicola

Saccolabium saxicola, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, aiding in water conservation.

Saccolabium saxicola plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
