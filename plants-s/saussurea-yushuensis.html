Saussurea yushuensis

Saussurea yushuensis, commonly known as the Yushu gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue or purple flower heads. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
