Sparganium americanum var. rigidum

Sparganium americanum var. rigidum, commonly known as stiff bur reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of spadices, which are cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences containing small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

This species thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
