Stipa rubentiformis

Stipa rubentiformis, commonly known as the red-form grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces inflorescences that are typically cylindrical or panicle-shaped. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and wiry, with a reddish tinge, which contributes to its common name.

Stipa rubentiformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
