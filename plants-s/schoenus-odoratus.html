Schoenus odoratus

Schoenus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant is noted for its aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant, sweet scent when crushed. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes.

Schoenus odoratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.
