Staurogyne sandakanica

Staurogyne sandakanica, commonly known as the sandakanica waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant has thin, fibrous roots that help it anchor in shallow water and absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Staurogyne sandakanica is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
