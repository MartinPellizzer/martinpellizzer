Suaeda monoica

Suaeda monoica, commonly known as sea lavender, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high levels of salt in the soil and water. The plant has small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are succulent, helping it store water. Suaeda monoica is often found in the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
