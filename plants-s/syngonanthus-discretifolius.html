Syngonanthus discretifolius

Syngonanthus discretifolius, commonly known as the discrete-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It is native to Australia and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
