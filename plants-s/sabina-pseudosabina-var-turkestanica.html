Sabina pseudosabina var. turkestanica

Sabina pseudosabina var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, berry-like cones. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are scale-like and often retain their color year-round, providing evergreen structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
