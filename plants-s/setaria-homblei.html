Setaria homblei

Setaria homblei, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It is an annual plant that thrives in warm climates and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
