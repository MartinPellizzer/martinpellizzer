Spiraea bethlemensis

Spiraea bethlemensis, commonly known as the Bethlehem spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, slender stems and oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers partial to full sun.

Spiraea bethlemensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
