Sambucus argyi

Sambucus argyi, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Adoxaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from white to dark purple, which are typically harvested in late summer. Sambucus argyi is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
