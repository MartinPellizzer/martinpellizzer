Sida martinicensis

Sida martinicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Sida martinicensis is well adapted to the tropical climate and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
