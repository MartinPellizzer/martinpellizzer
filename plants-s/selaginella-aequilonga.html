Selaginella aequilonga

Selaginella aequilonga, commonly known as the slender spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in sporangia that develop on the upper surface of the leaves.

Selaginella aequilonga is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
