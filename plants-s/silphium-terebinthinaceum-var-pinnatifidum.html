Silphium terebinthinaceum var. pinnatifidum

Silphium terebinthinaceum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silphium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
