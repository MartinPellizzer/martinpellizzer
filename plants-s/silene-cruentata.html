Silene cruentata

Silene cruentata, commonly known as the blood-red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. The plant grows up to 60 cm tall, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Silene cruentata is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
