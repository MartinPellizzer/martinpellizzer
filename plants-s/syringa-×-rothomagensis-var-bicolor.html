Syringa × rothomagensis var. bicolor

Syringa × rothomagensis var. bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, which feature pink outer petals and white inner petals. The plant produces fragrant, clustered blossoms in late spring to early summer. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
