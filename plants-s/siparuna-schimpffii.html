Siparuna schimpffii

Siparuna schimpffii, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its striking, pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a mildly aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Siparuna schimpffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
