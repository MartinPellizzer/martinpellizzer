Sapota costata

Sapota costata, commonly known as the rough sapota, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a rough, warty texture on its fruit. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, yellow or orange rind and a soft, sweet pulp containing large, starchy seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
