Sterculia urceolata

Sterculia urceolata, commonly known as the water apple or Java apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous rind and a juicy, sweet pulp that contains numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, tropical climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
