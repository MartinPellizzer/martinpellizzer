Smilax platycentron f. hastata

Smilax platycentron f. hastata, commonly known as the broad-leaf smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
