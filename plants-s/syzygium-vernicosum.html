Syzygium vernicosum

Syzygium vernicosum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit, which is typically bright red or yellow when ripe, is encased in a thick, segmented rind. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and tangy flavor, and for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
