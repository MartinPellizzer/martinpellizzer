Spiraea maximowicziana

Spiraea maximowicziana, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn red or orange in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
