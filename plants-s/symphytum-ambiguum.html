Symphytum ambiguum

Symphytum ambiguum, commonly known as the doubtful comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or damp meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
