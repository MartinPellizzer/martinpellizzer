Sorghum acutiusculum

Sorghum acutiusculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Sorghum acutiusculum is often used for forage, erosion control, and traditional cultural practices in its native regions.
