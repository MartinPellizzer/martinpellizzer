Silene pachyrhiza

Silene pachyrhiza, commonly known as the thickroot catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene pachyrhiza is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, mountainous regions.
