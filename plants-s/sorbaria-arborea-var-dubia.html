Sorbaria arborea var. dubia

Sorbaria arborea var. dubia, commonly known as the whitebeard maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or moist meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
