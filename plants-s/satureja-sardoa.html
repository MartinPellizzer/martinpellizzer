Satureja sardoa

Satureja sardoa, commonly known as Sardinian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a strong, minty aroma. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, forming compact clusters at the ends of the stems.

Satureja sardoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
