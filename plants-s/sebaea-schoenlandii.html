Sebaea schoenlandii

Sebaea schoenlandii, commonly known as the Natal milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Sebaea schoenlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
