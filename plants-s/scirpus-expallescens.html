Scirpus expallescens

Scirpus expallescens, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown in color.

Scirpus expallescens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
