Scolymocephalus scolopendriifolia

Scolymocephalus scolopendriifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
