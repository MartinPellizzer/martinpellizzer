Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. typica

Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. typica, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
