Stevia eclipes

Stevia eclipes, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in the leaves. Stevia eclipes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained widespread recognition in both culinary and medicinal applications.
