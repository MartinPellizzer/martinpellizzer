Satureja cordata subsp. filiformis

Satureja cordata subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as slender mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
