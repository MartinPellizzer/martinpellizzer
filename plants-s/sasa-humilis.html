Sasa humilis

Sasa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a low-growing, clump-forming plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This species typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

Sasa humilis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
