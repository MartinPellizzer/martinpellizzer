Silene tomentosa

Silene tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene tomentosa is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
