Stemmacantha rhapontica subsp. lamarckii

Stemmacantha rhapontica subsp.

lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's stemmacantha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.
