Shoshonea pulvinata

Shoshonea pulvinata, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Shoshonea pulvinata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
