Salvia articulata

Salvia articulata, commonly known as the jointed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal practices.
