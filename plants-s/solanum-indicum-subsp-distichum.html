Solanum indicum subsp. distichum

Solanum indicum subsp.

distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and small, usually white or purple flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
