Salsola leucoxylum

Salsola leucoxylum, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bracted inflorescence and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Salsola leucoxylum plays a role in stabilizing soils and can serve as forage for livestock in certain conditions.
