Salix × pokornyi

Salix × pokornyi, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its weeping, drooping branches that drape gracefully from the tree, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, elongated leaves that are typically a bright green on top and a lighter green underneath. It thrives in moist environments, often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × pokornyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
