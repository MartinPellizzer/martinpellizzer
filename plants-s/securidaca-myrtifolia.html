Securidaca myrtifolia

Securidaca myrtifolia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a thick, woody trunk and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for construction and tool-making.

The tree also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
