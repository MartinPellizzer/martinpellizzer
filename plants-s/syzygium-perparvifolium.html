Syzygium perparvifolium

Syzygium perparvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and small, elliptical leaves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is closely related to other clove species. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are followed by small, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Syzygium perparvifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential use in traditional medicine.
