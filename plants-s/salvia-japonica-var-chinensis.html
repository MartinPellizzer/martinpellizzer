Salvia japonica var. chinensis

Salvia japonica var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features square, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or purple, typically arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
