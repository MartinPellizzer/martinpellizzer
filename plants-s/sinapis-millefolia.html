Sinapis millefolia

Sinapis millefolia, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a branched stem that grows up to 60 cm tall, covered with fine hairs. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a feathery appearance. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
