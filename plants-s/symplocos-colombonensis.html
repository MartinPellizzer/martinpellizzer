Symplocos colombonensis

Symplocos colombonensis, commonly known as the Colombian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by vibrant purple berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a dense canopy that supports various wildlife species.

Symplocos colombonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
