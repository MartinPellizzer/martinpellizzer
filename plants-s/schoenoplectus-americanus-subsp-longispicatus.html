Schoenoplectus americanus subsp. longispicatus

Schoenoplectus americanus subsp.

longispicatus, commonly known as the tall spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
