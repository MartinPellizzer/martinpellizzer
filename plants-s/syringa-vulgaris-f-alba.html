Syringa vulgaris f. alba

Syringa vulgaris f. alba, commonly known as the white lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac that is distinguished by its white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, clustered blooms that appear in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its white flowers make it a striking addition to gardens, especially when planted in groups.
