Saxifraga arachnoidea

Saxifraga arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-silk saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. It is known for its ability to survive in cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
