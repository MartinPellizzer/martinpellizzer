Sagina decumbens subsp. occidentalis

Sagina decumbens subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to North America. It features small, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a dense rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to coastal and arid environments.
