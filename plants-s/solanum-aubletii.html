Solanum aubletii

Solanum aubletii, commonly known as the Aublet's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its thick, often hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum aubletii is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
