Senecio salicinus

Senecio salicinus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Senecio salicinus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
