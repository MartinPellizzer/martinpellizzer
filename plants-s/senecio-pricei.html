Senecio pricei

Senecio pricei, commonly known as the Price's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Senecio pricei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
