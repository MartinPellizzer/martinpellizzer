Sisymbrium vilaenadalis

Sisymbrium vilaenadalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, but it does not produce flowers or seeds of its own. Instead, it produces small, dry fruits called siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds. The plant is often found growing on the roots of various grasses and forbs in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host plants.
