Saussurea macrophylla

Saussurea macrophylla, commonly known as the bigleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Kazakhstan.

It is a perennial herb with a robust, upright stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Saussurea macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
