Stenolobium incisum

Stenolobium incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stenolobium incisum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
