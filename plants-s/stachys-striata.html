Stachys striata

Stachys striata, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-white stripe running down the central vein. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
