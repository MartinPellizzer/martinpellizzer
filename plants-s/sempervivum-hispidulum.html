Sempervivum hispidulum

Sempervivum hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces upright flower stalks bearing clusters of red or pink flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Sempervivum hispidulum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including cold winters.
