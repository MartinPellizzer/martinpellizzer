Satureja zuvandica

Satureja zuvandica, commonly known as Zuvand thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking, reflecting its cultural and practical significance.
