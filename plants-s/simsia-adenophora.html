Simsia adenophora

Simsia adenophora, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Simsia adenophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
