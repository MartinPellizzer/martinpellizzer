Synedrellopsis grisebachii var. inversa

Synedrellopsis grisebachii var. inversa, commonly known as the reverse sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its adaptability to arid environments.
