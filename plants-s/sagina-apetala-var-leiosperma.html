Sagina apetala var. leiosperma

Sagina apetala var. leiosperma, commonly known as smooth-leaved mouseear, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant has a succulent, fleshy appearance, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
