Solanum stenotomum

Solanum stenotomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
