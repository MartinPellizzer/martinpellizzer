Stipa vaginata

Stipa vaginata, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain tiny flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Stipa vaginata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a valuable component of ecological and ornamental landscapes.
