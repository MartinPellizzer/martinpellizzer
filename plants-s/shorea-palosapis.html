Shorea palosapis

Shorea palosapis, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
