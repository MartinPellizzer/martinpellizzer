Stellaster granatellii

Stellaster granatellii, commonly known as the granulate starburst, is a species of sea star belonging to the family Stellasteridae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, granulated texture on its skin and a star-shaped body with short, spiky arms. Native to tropical and subtropical marine environments, it thrives in shallow coastal waters. The plant's anatomy includes a central disc and radial arms, which are adapted for burrowing and feeding on bivalves.

Its ecological role includes contributing to the benthic ecosystem by controlling bivalve populations.
