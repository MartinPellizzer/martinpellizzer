Sterculia parviflora

Sterculia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different tropical environments.
