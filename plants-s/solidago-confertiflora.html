Solidago confertiflora

Solidago confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago confertiflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
