Salix tomentosa var. androgyna

Salix tomentosa var. androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna willow, is a variant of the silky willow native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
