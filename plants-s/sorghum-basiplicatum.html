Sorghum basiplicatum

Sorghum basiplicatum, commonly known as the African millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it an important crop in arid environments. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional brewing.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other sorghum species.
