Selaginella macrorhiza

Selaginella macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and long, spirally arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Selaginella macrorhiza reproduces via spores, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

It is notable for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in various habitats.
