Solidago angusta

Solidago angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in woodland or open areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Solidago angusta is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
