Saxifraga nipponica

Saxifraga nipponica, commonly known as Japanese rockfoil, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, with small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in a cyme. Its foliage is usually covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Saxifraga nipponica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
