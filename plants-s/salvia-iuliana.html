Salvia iuliana

Salvia iuliana, commonly known as the Italian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported calming and medicinal properties.

Salvia iuliana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
