Salvia xanthotricha

Salvia xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden-hair sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia xanthotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
