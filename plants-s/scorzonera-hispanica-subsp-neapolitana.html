Scorzonera hispanica subsp. neapolitana

Scorzonera hispanica subsp.

neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
