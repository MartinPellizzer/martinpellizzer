Salix jacquiniana

Salix jacquiniana, commonly known as the Mexican willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and moist woodlands.
