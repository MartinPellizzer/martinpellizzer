Shorea utilis

Shorea utilis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.
