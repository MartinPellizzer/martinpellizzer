Siparuna cristata

Siparuna cristata, commonly known as the Crowned Siparuna, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Siparuna cristata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
