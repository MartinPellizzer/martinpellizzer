Salvia summa

Salvia summa, commonly known as the tall blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
