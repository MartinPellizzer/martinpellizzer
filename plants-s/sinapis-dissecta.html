Sinapis dissecta

Sinapis dissecta, commonly known as the dissected mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sinapis dissecta is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
