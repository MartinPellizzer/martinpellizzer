Scutellaria luteocaerulea

Scutellaria luteocaerulea, commonly known as the yellow-blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to blue, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Scutellaria luteocaerulea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
