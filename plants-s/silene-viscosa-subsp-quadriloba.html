Silene viscosa subsp. quadriloba

Silene viscosa subsp.

quadriloba, commonly known as sticky willow or woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and hairy foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
