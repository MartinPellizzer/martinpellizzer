Salacia polyanthomaniaca

Salacia polyanthomaniaca, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
