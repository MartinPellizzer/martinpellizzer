Senna pendula var. stahlii

Senna pendula var. stahlii, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear bright yellow, pea-like flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
