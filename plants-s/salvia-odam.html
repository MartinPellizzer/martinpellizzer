Salvia odam

Salvia odam, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia odam is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
