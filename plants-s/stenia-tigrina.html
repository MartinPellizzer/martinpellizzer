Stenia tigrina

Stenia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful petals that resemble a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stenia tigrina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
