Sisyranthus rotatus

Sisyranthus rotatus, commonly known as the white orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features slender stems and produces delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
