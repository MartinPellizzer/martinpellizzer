Samolus valerandi var. floribundus

Samolus valerandi var. floribundus, commonly known as the flowering samolus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
