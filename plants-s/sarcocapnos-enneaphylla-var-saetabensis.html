Sarcocapnos enneaphylla var. saetabensis

Sarcocapnos enneaphylla var. saetabensis, commonly known as the serrated sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, elliptical fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
