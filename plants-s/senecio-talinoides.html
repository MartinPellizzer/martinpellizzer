Senecio talinoides

Senecio talinoides, commonly known as the Blue-Eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that resemble small daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its potential invasiveness in some regions.
