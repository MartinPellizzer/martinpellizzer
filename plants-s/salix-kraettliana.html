Salix kraettliana

Salix kraettliana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its weeping branches that hang in cascading clusters, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

Salix kraettliana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
