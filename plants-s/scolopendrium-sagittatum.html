Scolopendrium sagittatum

Scolopendrium sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its common name.

Scolopendrium sagittatum is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
