Senecio acutagulus

Senecio acutagulus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a sharp, pointed tip, which gives it its common name.

Senecio acutagulus is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.
