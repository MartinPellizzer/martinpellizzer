Scirpus tumidus

Scirpus tumidus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and long, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Scirpus tumidus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
