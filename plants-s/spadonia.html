Spadonia

Spadonia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and its showy, white, orchid-like flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its specialized leaves. Spadonia is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a vulnerable species in the wild.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its status as a symbol of botanical rarity and beauty.
