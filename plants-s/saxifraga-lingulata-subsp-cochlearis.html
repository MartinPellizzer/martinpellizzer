Saxifraga lingulata subsp. cochlearis

Saxifraga lingulata subsp.

cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
