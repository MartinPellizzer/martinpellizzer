Sium suave

Sium suave, commonly known as sweet waterparsley, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in umbels. Its root is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sium suave thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams and wetlands.
