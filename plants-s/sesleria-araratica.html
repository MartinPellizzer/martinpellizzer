Sesleria araratica

Sesleria araratica, commonly known as the Armenian sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream sides. It has a compact growth habit and produces delicate, greenish-white inflorescences.

Sesleria araratica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
