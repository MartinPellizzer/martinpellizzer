Seriphidium sogdianum

Seriphidium sogdianum, commonly known as the Sogdian wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
