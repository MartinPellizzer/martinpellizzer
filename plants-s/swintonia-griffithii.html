Swintonia griffithii

Swintonia griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant features clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Swintonia griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
