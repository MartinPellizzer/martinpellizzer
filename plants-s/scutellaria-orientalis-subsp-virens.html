Scutellaria orientalis subsp. virens

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

virens, commonly known as the green scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, often clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
