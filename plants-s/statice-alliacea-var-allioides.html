Statice alliacea var. allioides

Statice alliacea var. allioides, commonly known as garlic statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic.

Garlic statice is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.
