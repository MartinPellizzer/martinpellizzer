Sida megaphylla

Sida megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits.

Sida megaphylla is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
