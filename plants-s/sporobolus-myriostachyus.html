Sporobolus myriostachyus

Sporobolus myriostachyus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of spikelets that are often adorned with long, bristly hairs. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sporobolus myriostachyus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
