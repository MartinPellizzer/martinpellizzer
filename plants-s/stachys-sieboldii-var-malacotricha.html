Stachys sieboldii var. malacotricha

Stachys sieboldii var. malacotricha, commonly known as the Malacotricha betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces spikes of violet or purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
