Scaevola ciliata

Scaevola ciliata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the morning. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scaevola ciliata is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common sight in coastal environments.
