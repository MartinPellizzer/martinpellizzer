Senecio ampliflorus

Senecio ampliflorus, commonly known as the broadflower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Senecio ampliflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
