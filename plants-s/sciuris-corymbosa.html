Sciuris corymbosa

Sciuris corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Sciuris corymbosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
