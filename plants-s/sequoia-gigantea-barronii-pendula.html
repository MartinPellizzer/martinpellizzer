Sequoia gigantea barronii-pendula

Sequoia gigantea barronii-pendula, commonly known as the weeping giant sequoia, is a unique cultivar of the giant sequoia.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, umbrella-like shape. This plant is a slow-growing evergreen with thick, bluish-green foliage that remains throughout the year. It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

While it is not a naturally occurring species, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental tree.
