Sitobolium rubiginosum

Sitobolium rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant is notable for its rough, hairy leaves and stems, which are often tinged with a reddish or rusty color, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Sitobolium rubiginosum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in open fields and along roadsides.
