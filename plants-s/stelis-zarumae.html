Stelis zarumae

Stelis zarumae, commonly known as the Zaruma orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short rhizome and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting epiphytic habits. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Stelis zarumae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
