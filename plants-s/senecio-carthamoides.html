Senecio carthamoides

Senecio carthamoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Senecio carthamoides is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
