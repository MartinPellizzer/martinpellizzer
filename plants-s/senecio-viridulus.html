Senecio viridulus

Senecio viridulus, commonly known as green ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Senecio viridulus is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock and wildlife.
