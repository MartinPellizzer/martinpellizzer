Salsola gypsacea

Salsola gypsacea, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water in dry soils. It often forms dense mats or low shrubs, which help stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

Salsola gypsacea is also known for its ability to thrive in saline conditions, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
