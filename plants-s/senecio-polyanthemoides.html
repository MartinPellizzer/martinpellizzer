Senecio polyanthemoides

Senecio polyanthemoides, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are usually grey-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio polyanthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
