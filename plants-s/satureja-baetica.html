Satureja baetica

Satureja baetica, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Satureja baetica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
