Soldanella occidentalis

Soldanella occidentalis, commonly known as the western sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Soldanella occidentalis is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.
