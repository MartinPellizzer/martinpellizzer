Syzygium pilgerianum

Syzygium pilgerianum, commonly known as the pilger's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, resembling small apples in appearance. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and opposite leaf arrangement.

Syzygium pilgerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
