Salvia cubensis

Salvia cubensis, commonly known as Cuban sage, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has a woody stem with opposite leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of traditional use in herbal medicine.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
