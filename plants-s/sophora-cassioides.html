Sophora cassioides

Sophora cassioides, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it may become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
