Symphytum longisetum

Symphytum longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair comfrey, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for healing wounds and supporting bone health.
