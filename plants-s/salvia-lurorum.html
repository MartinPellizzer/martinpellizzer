Salvia lurorum

Salvia lurorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia lurorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
