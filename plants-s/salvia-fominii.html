Salvia fominii

Salvia fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Salvia fominii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy characteristics.
