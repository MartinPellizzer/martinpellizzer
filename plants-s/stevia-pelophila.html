Stevia pelophila

Stevia pelophila, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Stevia pelophila is often cultivated as a herbal plant for its sweet leaves and medicinal properties.
