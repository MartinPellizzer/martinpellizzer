Swertia gamosepala

Swertia gamosepala, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stem is erect and branched.

Swertia gamosepala is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
