Stapelia affinis

Stapelia affinis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, waxy texture. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Stapelia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
