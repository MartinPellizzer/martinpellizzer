Sudamerlycaste trifoliata

Sudamerlycaste trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that split into three distinct lobes, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clusters over time.

Sudamerlycaste trifoliata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
