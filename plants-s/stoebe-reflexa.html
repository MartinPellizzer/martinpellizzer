Stoebe reflexa

Stoebe reflexa, commonly known as clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Stoebe reflexa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
