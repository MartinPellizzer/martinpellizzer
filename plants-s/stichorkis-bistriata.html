Stichorkis bistriata

Stichorkis bistriata, commonly known as the two-striped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
