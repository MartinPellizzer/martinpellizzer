Swertia pseudochinensis

Swertia pseudochinensis, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and southwestern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and branched.

Swertia pseudochinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
