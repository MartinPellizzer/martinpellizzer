Senecio rhyacophilus

Senecio rhyacophilus, commonly known as the streambank groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats along streams and rivers. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Senecio rhyacophilus is an important component of the fynbos vegetation type, contributing to local biodiversity.
