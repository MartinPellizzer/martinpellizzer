Silene inflata subsp. thorei

Silene inflata subsp.

thorei, commonly known as the inflated campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, balloon-like calyces that enclose the flower buds, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene inflata subsp.

thorei is often found in coastal areas and is considered a species of ecological interest due to its specialized habitat requirements.
