Serratula pulchra

Serratula pulchra, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Serratula pulchra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
