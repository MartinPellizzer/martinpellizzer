Stachys polystachya

Stachys polystachya, commonly known as purpletop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Stachys polystachya is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
