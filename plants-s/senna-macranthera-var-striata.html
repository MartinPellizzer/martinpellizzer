Senna macranthera var. striata

Senna macranthera var. striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive striated pattern, giving it its botanical name.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
