Sagittaria sagittifolia var. gracilis

Sagittaria sagittifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by three-lobed berries. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
