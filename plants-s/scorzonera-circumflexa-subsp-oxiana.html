Scorzonera circumflexa subsp. oxiana

Scorzonera circumflexa subsp.

oxiana, commonly known as the Oxian sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a robust taproot system and produces yellow flowers with ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for cultivation.
