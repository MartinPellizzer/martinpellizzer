Salvia melaleuca subsp. melaleuca

Salvia melaleuca subsp.

melaleuca, commonly known as the honey mint, is a shrub native to Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
