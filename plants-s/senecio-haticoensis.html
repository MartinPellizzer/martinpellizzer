Senecio haticoensis

Senecio haticoensis, commonly known as the Hatico groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Senecio haticoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
