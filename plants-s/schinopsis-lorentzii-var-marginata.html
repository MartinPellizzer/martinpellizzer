Schinopsis lorentzii var. marginata

Schinopsis lorentzii var. marginata, commonly known as the yellow mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of alkaloids. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
