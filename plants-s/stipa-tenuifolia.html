Stipa tenuifolia

Stipa tenuifolia, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant is often found in disturbed areas and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
