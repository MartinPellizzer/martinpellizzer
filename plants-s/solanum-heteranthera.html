Solanum heteranthera

Solanum heteranthera, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a blend of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Solanum heteranthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
