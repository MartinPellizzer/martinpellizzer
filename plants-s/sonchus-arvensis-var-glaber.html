Sonchus arvensis var. glaber

Sonchus arvensis var. glaber, commonly known as field sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, rough leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
