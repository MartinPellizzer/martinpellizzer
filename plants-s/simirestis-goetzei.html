Simirestis goetzei

Simirestis goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
