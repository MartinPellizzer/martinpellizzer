Sedum telephium var. angustifolium

Sedum telephium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
