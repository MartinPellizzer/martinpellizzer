Sciodaphyllum pentadactylum

Sciodaphyllum pentadactylum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant has a unique reproductive system, relying on spore dispersal rather than seeds. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to habitat destruction, Sciodaphyllum pentadactylum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists studying evolutionary plant adaptations.
