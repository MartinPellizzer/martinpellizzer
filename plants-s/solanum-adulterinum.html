Solanum adulterinum

Solanum adulterinum, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
