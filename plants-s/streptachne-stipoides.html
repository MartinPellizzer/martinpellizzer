Streptachne stipoides

Streptachne stipoides, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Streptachne stipoides is often found in moist to mesic environments and is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
