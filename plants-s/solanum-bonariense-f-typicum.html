Solanum bonariense f. typicum

Solanum bonariense f. typicum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it contains toxic compounds.

Its botanical features make it both a striking addition to gardens and a plant requiring caution due to its potential hazards.
