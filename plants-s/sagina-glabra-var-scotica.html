Sagina glabra var. scotica

Sagina glabra var. scotica, commonly known as Scottish sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early spring. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and showy inflorescences.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
