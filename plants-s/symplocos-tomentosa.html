Symplocos tomentosa

Symplocos tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly alder or hairy alder, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and the fruit is a capsule containing several tiny seeds.

Symplocos tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
