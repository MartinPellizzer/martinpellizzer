Sisymbrium elongatum

Sisymbrium elongatum, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a slender, branched stem that often grows close to the ground. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, and it can cause economic losses in crop production.
