Sideritis incana subsp. virgata

Sideritis incana subsp.

virgata, commonly known as silver hair vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

This species is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
