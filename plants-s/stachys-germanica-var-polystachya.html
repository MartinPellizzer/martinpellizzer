Stachys germanica var. polystachya

Stachys germanica var. polystachya, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly woolly texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
