Sasa tatewakiana

Sasa tatewakiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact shrub-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a lush, evergreen foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Sasa tatewakiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.
