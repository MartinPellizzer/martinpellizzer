Syzygium megalanthum

Syzygium megalanthum, commonly known as the large-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces elongated, woody pods containing numerous seeds. The tree thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sour taste and is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

Syzygium megalanthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
