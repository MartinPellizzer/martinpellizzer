Sideritis × incanoides

Sideritis × incanoides, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution. Sideritis × incanoides is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
