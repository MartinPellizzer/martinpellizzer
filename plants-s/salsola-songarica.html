Salsola songarica

Salsola songarica, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a common species in sandy and saline soils.

Salsola songarica plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
