Swertia albicaulis var. idahoensis

Swertia albicaulis var. idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
