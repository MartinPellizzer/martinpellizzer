Solanum elatius

Solanum elatius, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its dark purple berries and spiny leaves. The plant contains potent alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine, which can be lethal if ingested. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and waste areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a dangerous plant and is generally avoided in cultivation.
