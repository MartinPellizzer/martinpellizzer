Sisymbrium taraxacifolium var. contortum

Sisymbrium taraxacifolium var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted leaf winter cress, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many plants with mustard-like characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, producing a distinctive musty odor when crushed.
