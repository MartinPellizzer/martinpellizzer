Senecio chrismarii

Senecio chrismarii, commonly known as the Christmarie's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Senecio chrismarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
