Stellaria glauca lusus pilosula

Stellaria glauca lusus pilosula, commonly known as the woolly starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers and hairy, succulent stems. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stellaria glauca lusus pilosula is also known for its ability to spread through stolons, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
