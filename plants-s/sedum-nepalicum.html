Sedum nepalicum

Sedum nepalicum, commonly known as the Nepalese stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
