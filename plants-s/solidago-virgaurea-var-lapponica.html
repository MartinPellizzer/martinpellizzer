Solidago virgaurea var. lapponica

Solidago virgaurea var. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
