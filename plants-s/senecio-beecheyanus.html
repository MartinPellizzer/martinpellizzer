Senecio beecheyanus

Senecio beecheyanus, commonly known as the Beekley's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio beecheyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
