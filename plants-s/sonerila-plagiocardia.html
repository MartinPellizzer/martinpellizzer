Sonerila plagiocardia

Sonerila plagiocardia, commonly known as the red-veined stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its biennial growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
