Syngonanthus umbellatus var. liebmannianus

Syngonanthus umbellatus var. liebmannianus, commonly known as the Liebmann's sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its unique ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
