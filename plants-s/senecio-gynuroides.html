Senecio gynuroides

Senecio gynuroides, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio gynuroides is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
