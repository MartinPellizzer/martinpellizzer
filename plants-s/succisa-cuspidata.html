Succisa cuspidata

Succisa cuspidata, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Succisa cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
