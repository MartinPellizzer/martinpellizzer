Sesleria caerulea subsp. sadleriana

Sesleria caerulea subsp.

sadleriana, commonly known as the blue tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking blue-green foliage that remains vibrant throughout the year.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, which appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact size and long-lasting color, which enhances garden aesthetics.
