Succisa attenuata

Succisa attenuata, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various growing conditions, though it can be invasive in some regions.
