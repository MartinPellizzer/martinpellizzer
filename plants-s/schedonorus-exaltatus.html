Schedonorus exaltatus

Schedonorus exaltatus, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces spikelets that contain bothawned and lemmas, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is widely used in turfgrass applications due to its tolerance to drought and traffic.

However, some varieties may contain endophytic fungi that can affect livestock grazing.
