Saccopetalum lineatum

Saccopetalum lineatum, commonly known as the slender sea bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and small, pod-like fruits. The plant thrives in coastal and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are usually small and yellowish, contributing to its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
