Senecio cristatus

Senecio cristatus, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, branched stems that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its structured form. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its fleshy stems and leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Senecio cristatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to well-drained soils.
