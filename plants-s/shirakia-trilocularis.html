Shirakia trilocularis

Shirakia trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers with a subtle pink hue. The plant features a bulbous stem and produces three distinct chambers within its floral structure, which is a unique botanical trait. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
