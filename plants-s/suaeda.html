Suaeda

Suaeda, commonly known as saltbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Suaeda species are characterized by their succulent leaves and small, often yellow or red flowers. They play an important role in stabilizing soil in coastal and desert areas.

Their ability to thrive in high-salt environments makes them valuable for ecological restoration projects.
