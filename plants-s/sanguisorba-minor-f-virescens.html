Sanguisorba minor f. virescens

Sanguisorba minor f. virescens, commonly known as the green-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate flowering display.
