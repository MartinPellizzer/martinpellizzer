Saccharum officinarum subsp. barberi

Saccharum officinarum subsp.

barberi, commonly known as the Barber cane, is a subspecies of sugarcane native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sucrose content, making it valuable for sugar production.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils. It has broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length.

This subspecies is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
