Salix bichroophylla

Salix bichroophylla, commonly known as the two-colored willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green on the upper surface and pale white underneath. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and it blooms in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Salix bichroophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
