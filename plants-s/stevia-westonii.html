Stevia westonii

Stevia westonii, commonly known as the white stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. Stevia westonii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists.
