Schoenoplectus kasachstanicus

Schoenoplectus kasachstanicus, commonly known as the Central Asian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Central Asia.

It thrives in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes, lakes, and riverbanks. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
