Salix × pendula var. multistipulata

Salix × pendula var. multistipulata, commonly known as the weeping willow with multiple stipules, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or brown at the edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
