Senecio leucomallus

Senecio leucomallus, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

Senecio leucomallus is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
