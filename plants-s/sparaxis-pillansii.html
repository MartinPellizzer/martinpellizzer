Sparaxis pillansii

Sparaxis pillansii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking South African bulbous plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of cup-shaped blooms that open in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sparaxis pillansii is also known for its ability to tolerate drought, making it a resilient addition to native gardens.
