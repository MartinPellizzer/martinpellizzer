Salix sordida

Salix sordida, commonly known as the sordid willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, inconspicuous leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is adapted to harsh environments, including high altitudes and cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
