Sasa senanensis f. subnobilis

Sasa senanensis f. subnobilis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
