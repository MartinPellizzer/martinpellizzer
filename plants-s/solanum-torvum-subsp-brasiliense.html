Solanum torvum subsp. brasiliense

Solanum torvum subsp.

brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into toxic berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for cultivation or consumption.
