Stevia tomentosa var. typica

Stevia tomentosa var. typica, commonly known as the woolly stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
