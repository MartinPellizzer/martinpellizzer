Saxifraga glandulifera

Saxifraga glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Saxifraga glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize challenging habitats.
