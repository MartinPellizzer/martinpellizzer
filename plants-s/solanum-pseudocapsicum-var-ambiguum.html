Solanum pseudocapsicum var. ambiguum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellowish-green with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
