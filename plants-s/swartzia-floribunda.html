Swartzia floribunda

Swartzia floribunda, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Swartzia floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
