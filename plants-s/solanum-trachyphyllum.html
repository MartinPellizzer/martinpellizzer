Solanum trachyphyllum

Solanum trachyphyllum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum trachyphyllum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
