Scilla scilloides var. albiflora

Scilla scilloides var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is closely related to other members of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes well-known ornamental and medicinal plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming habit add a unique touch to spring gardens.
