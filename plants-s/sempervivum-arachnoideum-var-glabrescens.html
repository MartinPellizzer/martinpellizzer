Sempervivum arachnoideum var. glabrescens

Sempervivum arachnoideum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Woolly Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern and fleshy, gray-green leaves covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in alpine and succulent plants.
