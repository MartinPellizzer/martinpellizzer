Stevia nelsonii

Stevia nelsonii, commonly known as the Hawaiian sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ecological significance and unique chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
