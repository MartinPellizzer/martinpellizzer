Synaedrys wallichiana

Synaedrys wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
