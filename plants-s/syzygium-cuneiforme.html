Syzygium cuneiforme

Syzygium cuneiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sour fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Syzygium cuneiforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
