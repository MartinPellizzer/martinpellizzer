Statice gibertii

Statice gibertii, commonly known as the Gibraltar statice, is a flowering plant native to the rocky coastlines of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its narrow, linear leaves and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for garden use.

Statice gibertii is also noted for its ability to withstand salty air and strong winds, which are common in its natural habitat.
