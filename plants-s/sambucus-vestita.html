Sambucus vestita

Sambucus vestita, commonly known as the white elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Sambucus vestita thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
