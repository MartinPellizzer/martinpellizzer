Smilax flavicaulis

Smilax flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemded smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps.

Smilax flavicaulis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
