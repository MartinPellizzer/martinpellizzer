Solanum pterocaulon var. dichotimiflorum

Solanum pterocaulon var. dichotimiflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
