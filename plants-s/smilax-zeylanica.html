Smilax zeylanica

Smilax zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or wild arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by bright red berries containing numerous seeds. Smilax zeylanica is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and wetlands.

It is valued for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
