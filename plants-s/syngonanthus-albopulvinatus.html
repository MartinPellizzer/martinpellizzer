Syngonanthus albopulvinatus

Syngonanthus albopulvinatus, commonly known as the white pulvinate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in panicles, which are typically arranged in a loose, branched structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
