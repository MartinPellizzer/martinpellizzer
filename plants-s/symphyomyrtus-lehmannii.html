Symphyomyrtus lehmannii

Symphyomyrtus lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Symphyomyrtus lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
