Saxifraga exarata var. adenophora

Saxifraga exarata var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花石竹, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
