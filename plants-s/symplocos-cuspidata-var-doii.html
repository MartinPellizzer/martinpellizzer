Symplocos cuspidata var. doii

Symplocos cuspidata var. doii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waxberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a persistent, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
