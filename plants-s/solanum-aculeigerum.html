Solanum aculeigerum

Solanum aculeigerum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both animals and humans if ingested.
