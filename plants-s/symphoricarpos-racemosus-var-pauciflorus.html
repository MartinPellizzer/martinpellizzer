Symphoricarpos racemosus var. pauciflorus

Symphoricarpos racemosus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the lowbush snowberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various insects and animals.
