Senecio aspericaulis

Senecio aspericaulis, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio aspericaulis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
