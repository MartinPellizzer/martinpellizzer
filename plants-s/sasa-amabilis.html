Sasa amabilis

Sasa amabilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or sweet bamboo, is a flowering plant in the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial herb that forms dense clumps of slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant is native to East Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of small, pale flowers.

Sasa amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
