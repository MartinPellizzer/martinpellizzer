Saxifraga iratiana var. polyantha

Saxifraga iratiana var. polyantha, commonly known as the Polyantha saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
