Stelis sublesta

Stelis sublesta, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, white or pale green flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
