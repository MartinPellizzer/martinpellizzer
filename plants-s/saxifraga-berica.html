Saxifraga berica

Saxifraga berica, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh alpine environment.

Saxifraga berica is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and talus slopes.
