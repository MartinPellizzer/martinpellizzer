Stachys nova

Stachys nova, commonly known as the New Zealand betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue. Stachys nova is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
