Swartzia cinerea

Swartzia cinerea, commonly known as the white swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Swartzia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for shade gardens and landscape design.
