Salvia cavaleriei var. erythrophylla

Salvia cavaleriei var. erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking red or purplish foliage, which contrasts with its blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
