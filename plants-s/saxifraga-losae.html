Saxifraga losae

Saxifraga losae, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically arranged in a cluster at the center of the rosette. Saxifraga losae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is often found in meadows and scree slopes at high altitudes.
