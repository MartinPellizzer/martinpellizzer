Solanum incertum

Solanum incertum, commonly known as the uncertain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, white or pale green flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

Solanum incertum is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
