Spiraeanthemum undulatum

Spiraeanthemum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating spiraea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly wavy or undulating edge, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
