Senecio subulatus

Senecio subulatus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio subulatus is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
