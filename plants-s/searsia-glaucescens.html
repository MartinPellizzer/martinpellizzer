Searsia glaucescens

Searsia glaucescens, commonly known as the silver sugarbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Searsia glaucescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
