Syzygium pachyanthum

Syzygium pachyanthum, commonly known as the white apple or white-fruit sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, white to pale yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
