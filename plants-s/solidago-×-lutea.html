Solidago × lutea

Solidago × lutea, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, composite flowers arranged in panicles. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago × lutea plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
