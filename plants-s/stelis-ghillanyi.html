Stelis ghillanyi

Stelis ghillanyi, commonly known as the Ghillany's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Stelis ghillanyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
