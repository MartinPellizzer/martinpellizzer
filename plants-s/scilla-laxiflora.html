Scilla laxiflora

Scilla laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Scilla laxiflora is relatively low maintenance and adds a subtle yet elegant touch to garden landscapes.
