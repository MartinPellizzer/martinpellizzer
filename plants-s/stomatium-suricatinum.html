Stomatium suricatinum

Stomatium suricatinum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical name reflects its distinctive floral structure and its association with the genus Stomatium.
