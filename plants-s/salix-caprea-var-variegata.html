Salix caprea var. variegata

Salix caprea var. variegata, commonly known as the white willow with variegation, is a variant of the common white willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The leaves are distinctive for their white or yellow variegation, which contrasts with the green veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
