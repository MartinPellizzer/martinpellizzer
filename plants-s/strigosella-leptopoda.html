Strigosella leptopoda

Strigosella leptopoda, commonly known as the slender leaf starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Strigosella leptopoda is considered a rare and specialized species.
