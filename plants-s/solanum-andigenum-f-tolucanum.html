Solanum andigenum f. tolucanum

Solanum andigenum f. tolucanum, commonly known as the Tolucanum tomato, is a unique botanical variant native to the Andean region of South America.

This plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes. It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to the specific climatic conditions of its native habitat.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural use in breeding programs.
