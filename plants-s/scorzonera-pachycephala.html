Scorzonera pachycephala

Scorzonera pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Scorzonera pachycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
