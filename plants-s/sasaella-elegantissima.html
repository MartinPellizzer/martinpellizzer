Sasaella elegantissima

Sasaella elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and finely textured, narrow leaves that give it a delicate appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Sasaella elegantissima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
