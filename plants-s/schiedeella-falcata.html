Schiedeella falcata

Schiedeella falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Schiedeella falcata typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is a low-growing plant that forms dense mats, helping to stabilize soil in its native habitat.
