Scleria dregeana

Scleria dregeana, commonly known as the Zulu grass or African flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Scleria dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
