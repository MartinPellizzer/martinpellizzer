Sisymbrium pilosissimum

Sisymbrium pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It attaches itself to the roots of host plants, primarily those in the Brassicaceae family, to obtain nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a complex life cycle that involves both a parasitic and a free-living stage. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in disturbed soils in Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host plants.
