Sagittaria sagittifolia f. xanthandra

Sagittaria sagittifolia f. xanthandra, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and arrow-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds, where it often forms dense stands. The plant's rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas and stabilize soil. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems, adding a dramatic visual element to wetland habitats.

The species is notable for its adaptability and role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
