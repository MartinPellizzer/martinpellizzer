Sasaella matsushimensis

Sasaella matsushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Sasaella matsushimensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its compact form and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
