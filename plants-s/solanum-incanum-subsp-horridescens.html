Solanum incanum subsp. horridescens

Solanum incanum subsp.

horridescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
