Sychnosepalum paraense

Sychnosepalum paraense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Sychnosepalum paraense plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
