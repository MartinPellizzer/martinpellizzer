Saldinia myrtilloides var. angustifolia

Saldinia myrtilloides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf saldinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinct, serrated margin.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
