Salvia peregrina

Salvia peregrina, commonly known as the wandering sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia peregrina is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
