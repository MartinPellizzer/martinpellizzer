Stenostachys gracilis

Stenostachys gracilis, commonly known as slender grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are usually pale green to brown, with small, tubular bracts.

Stenostachys gracilis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for invertebrates.
