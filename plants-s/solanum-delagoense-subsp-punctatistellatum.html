Solanum delagoense subsp. punctatistellatum

Solanum delagoense subsp.

punctatistellatum, commonly known as the spotted star solanum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are showy, usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive star-like appearance. The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

This species is often found in moist grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
