Scutellaria orientalis subsp. pinnatifida

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as oriental skullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
