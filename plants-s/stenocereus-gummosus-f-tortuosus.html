Stenocereus gummosus f. tortuosus

Stenocereus gummosus f. tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted gummosus cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often twisted or spiraled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a sticky, resinous substance known as gummosis.
