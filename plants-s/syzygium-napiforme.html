Syzygium napiforme

Syzygium napiforme, commonly known as the round-fruit syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, round, red fruits resembling small cherries. Syzygium napiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient choice for tropical and subtropical regions.
