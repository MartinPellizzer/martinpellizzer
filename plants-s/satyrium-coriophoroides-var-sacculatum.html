Satyrium coriophoroides var. sacculatum

Satyrium coriophoroides var. sacculatum, commonly known as the sacculate orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a sac-like ovary that distinguishes it from other members of the genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a greenish-yellow coloration.
