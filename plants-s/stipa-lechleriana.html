Stipa lechleriana

Stipa lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to bluish-gray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa lechleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
