Sabatia tracyi

Sabatia tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it blooms from July to September.

Tracy's ironweed is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
