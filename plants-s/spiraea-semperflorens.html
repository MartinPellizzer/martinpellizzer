Spiraea semperflorens

Spiraea semperflorens, commonly known as everflora spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce clusters of small, white to pink flowers throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Spiraea semperflorens is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
