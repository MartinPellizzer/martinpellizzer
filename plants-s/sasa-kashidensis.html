Sasa kashidensis

Sasa kashidensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for small organisms.

Sasa kashidensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional use in Japanese culture.
