Solanum pierreanum

Solanum pierreanum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that ripens to a red-orange color, containing numerous seeds.

Solanum pierreanum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
