Shorea Assamica Var. Philippinensis

Shorea assamica var. philippinensis is a rare and unique variant of the Shorea assamica tree found primarily in the Philippines. This species belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, known for its valuable hardwoods used in construction and furniture. It thrives in the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly in the mountainous regions of Luzon and Mindanao. The tree is characterized by its tall stature, thick trunk, and distinctive bark that varies in color and texture. Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, it faces significant threats to its survival, making conservation efforts crucial for its preservation.
