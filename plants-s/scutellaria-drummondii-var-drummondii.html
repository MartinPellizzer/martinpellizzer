Scutellaria drummondii var. drummondii

Scutellaria drummondii var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's bluehead, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
