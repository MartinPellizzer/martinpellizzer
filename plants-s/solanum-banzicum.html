Solanum banzicum

Solanum banzicum, commonly known as the Banzic Island nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Banzic Islands in the Pacific.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
