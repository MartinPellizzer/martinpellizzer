Sabia burmanica

Sabia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese sabia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Sabia burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
