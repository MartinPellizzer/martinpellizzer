Silene kubanensis

Silene kubanensis, commonly known as the Kuban catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Russia and Georgia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene kubanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
