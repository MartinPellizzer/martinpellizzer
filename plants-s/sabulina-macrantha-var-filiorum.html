Sabulina macrantha var. filiorum

Sabulina macrantha var. filiorum, commonly known as the filial sabulina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats that help it survive in harsh conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
