Spiraea pumila f. froebelii

Spiraea pumila f. froebelii, commonly known as the dwarf weeping spirea, is a compact, flowering shrub native to Asia.

It features delicate, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant has finely cut, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its blooms. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden borders or as a ground cover.
