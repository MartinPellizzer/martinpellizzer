Setaria bussei

Setaria bussei, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is sometimes used as a forage plant for livestock in its native range.
