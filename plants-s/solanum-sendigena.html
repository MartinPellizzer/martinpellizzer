Solanum sendigena

Solanum sendigena, commonly known as the Sendigen nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and dark blue berries that contain toxic compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
