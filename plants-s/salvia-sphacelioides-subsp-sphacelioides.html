Salvia sphacelioides subsp. sphacelioides

Salvia sphacelioides subsp.

sphacelioides, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The subspecies sphacelioides is distinguished by its narrow leaves and compact growth habit.
