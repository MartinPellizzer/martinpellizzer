Solanum bonariense var. paraguariense

Solanum bonariense var. paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically orange or red when mature. It is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
