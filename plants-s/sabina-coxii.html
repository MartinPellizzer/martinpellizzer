Sabina coxii

Sabina coxii, commonly known as the Chinese thuja, is a species of conifer native to China.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically yellowish-green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
