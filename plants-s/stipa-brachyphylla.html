Stipa brachyphylla

Stipa brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Stipa brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
