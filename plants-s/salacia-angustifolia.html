Salacia angustifolia

Salacia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Salacia angustifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
