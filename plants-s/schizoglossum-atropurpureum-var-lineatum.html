Schizoglossum atropurpureum var. lineatum

Schizoglossum atropurpureum var. lineatum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. Its small, daisy-like flowers are typically pink or purple, and they bloom in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
