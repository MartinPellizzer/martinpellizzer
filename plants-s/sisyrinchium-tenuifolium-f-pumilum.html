Sisyrinchium tenuifolium f. pumilum

Sisyrinchium tenuifolium f. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its compact form and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and partial shade.
