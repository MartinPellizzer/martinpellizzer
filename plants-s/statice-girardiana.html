Statice girardiana

Statice girardiana, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in salty, windy environments. The plant produces small, tightly packed flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Statice girardiana is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
