Salvia dregeana

Salvia dregeana, commonly known as the Karoo sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Salvia dregeana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
