Seriphidium tecti-mundi

Seriphidium tecti-mundi, commonly known as the "cup-shaped wormwood," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties have made it a subject of botanical interest.
