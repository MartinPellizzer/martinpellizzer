Saxifraga razshivinii

Saxifraga razshivinii, commonly known as the Kazakh saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. The plant features succulent leaves that help conserve moisture, and its flowers are usually small, white or pink, clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it grows in crevices and on slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
