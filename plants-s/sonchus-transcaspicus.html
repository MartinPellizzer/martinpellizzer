Sonchus transcaspicus

Sonchus transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in non-native areas.
