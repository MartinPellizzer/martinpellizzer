Scilla fraseri

Scilla fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's scilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

Scilla fraseri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
