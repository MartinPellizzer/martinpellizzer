Satureja montenegrina

Satureja montenegrina, commonly known as Montenegrin thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their strong, thyme-like scent and medicinal properties.
