Silene flavescens subsp. flavescens

Silene flavescens subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become naturalized.
