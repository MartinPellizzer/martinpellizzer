Solanum velascanum

Solanum velascanum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
