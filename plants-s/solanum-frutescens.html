Solanum frutescens

Solanum frutescens, commonly known as the ground cherry or Physalis, is an herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive papery calyces that enclose its orange or red, edible fruits. Native to the Americas, it thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
