Serapias longifolia

Serapias longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Serapias longifolia thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
