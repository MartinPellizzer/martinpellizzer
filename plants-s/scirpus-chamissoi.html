Scirpus chamissoi

Scirpus chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important species in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus chamissoi is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
