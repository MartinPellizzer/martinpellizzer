Sterculia obscura

Sterculia obscura, commonly known as the black bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds used in traditional medicine. Its bark is thick and rugged, often with a rough, fissured texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
