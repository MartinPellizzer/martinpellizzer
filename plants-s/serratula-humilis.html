Serratula humilis

Serratula humilis, commonly known as the common serratula, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a loose, branched inflorescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Serratula humilis is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
