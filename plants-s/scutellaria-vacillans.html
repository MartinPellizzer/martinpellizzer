Scutellaria vacillans

Scutellaria vacillans, commonly known as the wavy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scutellaria vacillans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
