Stryphnodendron occhionianum

Stryphnodendron occhionianum, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical forests.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of concern in some regions.
