Smilacina japonica subsp. hirta

Smilacina japonica subsp.

hirta, commonly known as Japanese climbing smilacina, is a perennial vine native to East Asia. It features long, slender stems that climb using tendrils, often found in forest understories.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
