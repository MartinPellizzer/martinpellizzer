Symplocos setchuensis

Symplocos setchuensis, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or red-flowered wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions of China, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
