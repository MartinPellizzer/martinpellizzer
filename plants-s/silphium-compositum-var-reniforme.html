Silphium compositum var. reniforme

Silphium compositum var. reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf silphium, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, producing small, achene fruits.

Silphium compositum var. reniforme is known for its medicinal properties and has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
