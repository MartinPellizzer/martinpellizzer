Sonchus cristulatus

Sonchus cristulatus, commonly known as the crownleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive crinkled appearance, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
