Silene colorata subsp. oliveriana

Silene colorata subsp.

oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
