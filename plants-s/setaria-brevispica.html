Setaria brevispica

Setaria brevispica, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Setaria brevispica is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it can form dense mats.
