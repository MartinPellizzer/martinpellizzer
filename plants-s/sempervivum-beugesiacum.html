Sempervivum beugesiacum

Sempervivum beugesiacum, commonly known as the Beugesiacum houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum beugesiacum is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and low-maintenance plant for gardens and rockeries.
