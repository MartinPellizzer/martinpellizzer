Senecio ambifarius

Senecio ambifarius, commonly known as the two-sided groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio ambifarius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
