Salix × costei

Salix × costei, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually reddish-brown on the underside. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Salix × costei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
