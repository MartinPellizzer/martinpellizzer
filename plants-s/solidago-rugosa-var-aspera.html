Solidago rugosa var. aspera

Solidago rugosa var. aspera, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
