Salvia filicifolia

Salvia filicifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, fern-like leaves that are often silvery-gray in appearance, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
