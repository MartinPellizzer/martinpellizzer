Senecio mendocinus

Senecio mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino groundsel, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio mendocinus is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
