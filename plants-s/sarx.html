Sarx

Sarx, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, spathaceous flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, Sarx thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
