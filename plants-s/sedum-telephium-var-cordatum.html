Sedum telephium var. cordatum

Sedum telephium var. cordatum, commonly known as Heartleaf Stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a versatile addition to various landscaping settings.
