Scutellaria paulsenii

Scutellaria paulsenii, commonly known as Paulsen's bluehead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scutellaria paulsenii is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
