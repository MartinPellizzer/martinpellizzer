Spitzelia cupuligera

Spitzelia cupuligera, commonly known as the hooded spitzelia, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are long and narrow, often floating on the water surface.

Spitzelia cupuligera is valued in ornamental aquatic gardens for its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive in various water conditions.
