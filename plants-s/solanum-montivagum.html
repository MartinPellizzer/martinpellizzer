Solanum montivagum

Solanum montivagum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its foliage consists of ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum montivagum plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food for certain insect species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
