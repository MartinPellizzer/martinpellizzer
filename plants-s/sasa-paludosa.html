Sasa paludosa

Sasa paludosa, commonly known as marsh sasa, is a species of bamboo native to wetland and swampy areas in eastern Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass that grows in dense stands, often forming a thick, mat-like structure. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Sasa paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
