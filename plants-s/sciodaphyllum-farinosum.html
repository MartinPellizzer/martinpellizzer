Sciodaphyllum farinosum

Sciodaphyllum farinosum, commonly known as the farina fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in high humidity and requires indirect light to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Sciodaphyllum farinosum is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
