Setaria floriana

Setaria floriana, commonly known as the flowering millet, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts. Setaria floriana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
