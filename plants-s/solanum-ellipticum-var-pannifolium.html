Solanum ellipticum var. pannifolium

Solanum ellipticum var. pannifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that change from green to red as they mature.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
