Sisyrinchium alatum var. angustissimum

Sisyrinchium alatum var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
