Stizolobium urens

Stizolobium urens, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Stizolobium urens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
