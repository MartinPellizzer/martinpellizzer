Sebastiania corniculata var. pohlii

Sebastiania corniculata var. pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
