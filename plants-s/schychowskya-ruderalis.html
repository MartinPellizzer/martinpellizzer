Schychowskya ruderalis

Schychowskya ruderalis, commonly known as the Russian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wastelands. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Schychowskya ruderalis is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in ecological succession.
