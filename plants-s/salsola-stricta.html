Salsola stricta

Salsola stricta, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Eurasia but has been introduced to many other regions, often becoming invasive.

Salsola stricta is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
