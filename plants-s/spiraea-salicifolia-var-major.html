Spiraea salicifolia var. major

Spiraea salicifolia var. major, commonly known as the largeleaf weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early to mid-spring. The foliage consists of elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
