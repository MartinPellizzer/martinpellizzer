Sinapis alba var. dissecta

Sinapis alba var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected white mustard, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are small and round, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in herbal remedies.
