Sasa argillacea

Sasa argillacea, commonly known as the clay sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color.

Sasa argillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
