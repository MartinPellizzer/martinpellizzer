Salix chlorolepis var. antimima

Salix chlorolepis var. antimima, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streams and in riparian zones.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
