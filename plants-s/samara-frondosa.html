Samara frondosa

Samara frondosa, commonly known as the winged maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. The tree has palmate leaves that split into five to seven lobes, resembling a maple leaf. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Samara frondosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
