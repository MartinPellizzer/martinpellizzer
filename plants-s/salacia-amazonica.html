Salacia amazonica

Salacia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian starfruit, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
