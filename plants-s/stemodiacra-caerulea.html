Stemodiacra caerulea

Stemodiacra caerulea, commonly known as the blue stemodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often display a striking blue or purple hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Stemodiacra caerulea thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
