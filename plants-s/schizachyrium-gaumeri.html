Schizachyrium gaumeri

Schizachyrium gaumeri, commonly known as green false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wet areas.

Schizachyrium gaumeri is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
