Solenostemon niveus

Solenostemon niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
