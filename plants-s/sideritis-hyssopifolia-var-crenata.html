Sideritis hyssopifolia var. crenata

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
