Sobralia lepida

Sobralia lepida, commonly known as the white sobralia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Sobralia lepida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
