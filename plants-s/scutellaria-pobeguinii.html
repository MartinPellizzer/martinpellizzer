Scutellaria pobeguinii

Scutellaria pobeguinii, commonly known as the Pobeguin's blueflag, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

Scutellaria pobeguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
