Sarcochilus pallidus

Sarcochilus pallidus, commonly known as the pale leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with pale green to yellowish flowers that have a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Sarcochilus pallidus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
