Senecio crambefolius

Senecio crambefolius, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive member of its ecosystem.
