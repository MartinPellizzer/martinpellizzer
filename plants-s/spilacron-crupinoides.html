Spilacron crupinoides

Spilacron crupinoides, commonly known as the woolly thyme or woolly tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
