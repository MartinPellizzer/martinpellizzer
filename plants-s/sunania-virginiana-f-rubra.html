Sunania virginiana f. rubra

Sunania virginiana f. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed sweet pepper.

This plant is a variant of the sweet pepper species, characterized by its distinctive reddish stems. It typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are small and yellow.

The fruit is a fleshy, bell-shaped pod that matures to a vibrant red color, making it a visually appealing and edible crop.
