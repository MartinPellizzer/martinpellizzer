Solanum mesadenium

Solanum mesadenium, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum mesadenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
