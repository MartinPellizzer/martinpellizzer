Sasa argillacea f. flavovariegata

Sasa argillacea f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the yellow-variegated bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species Sasa argillacea.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow and green variegation on its leaves, which adds visual interest to its appearance. This plant is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a clump-forming perennial grass that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and bonsai cultivation.
