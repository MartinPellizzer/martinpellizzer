Sphaerocionium pulchellum

Sphaerocionium pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful globular liverwort, is a small, thalloid liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It forms dense, cushion-like growths and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive, rounded thallus with a smooth, waxy surface that helps it retain moisture. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
