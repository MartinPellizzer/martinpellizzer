Scirpus savii subsp. campanus

Scirpus savii subsp.

campanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of small, triangular, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and are arranged spirally along the stem.

This species thrives in moist or aquatic environments and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
