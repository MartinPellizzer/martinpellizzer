Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp. viridis

Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green false indigo, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
