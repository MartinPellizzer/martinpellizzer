Syringa microphylla f. alba

Syringa microphylla f. alba, commonly known as the white meadow hyacinth, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and long-lasting fragrance.
