Saussurea amara

Saussurea amara, commonly known as the Indian spurge or Himalayan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Saussurea amara is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral display.
