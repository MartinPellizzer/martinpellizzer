Senna ligustrina var. turquinae

Senna ligustrina var. turquinae, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and are notable for their bright golden color, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
