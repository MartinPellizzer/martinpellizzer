Selaginella geniculata subsp. elongata

Selaginella geniculata subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family. It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the sporangia for spore production.

Its ability to survive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
