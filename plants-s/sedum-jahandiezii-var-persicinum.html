Sedum jahandiezii var. persicinum

Sedum jahandiezii var. persicinum, commonly known as Persian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It blooms in summer, typically from June to August, and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for USDA hardiness zones 5 to 8.
