Salix fengiana var. gymnocarpa

Salix fengiana var. gymnocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Chinese culture.
