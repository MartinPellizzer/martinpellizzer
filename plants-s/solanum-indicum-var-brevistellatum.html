Solanum indicum var. brevistellatum

Solanum indicum var. brevistellatum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
