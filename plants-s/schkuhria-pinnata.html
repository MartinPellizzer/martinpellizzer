Schkuhria pinnata

Schkuhria pinnata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flower heads, which are surrounded by bright yellow ray florets. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive foliage structure. Schkuhria pinnata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
