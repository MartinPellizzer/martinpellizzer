Solidago rugosa var. rugosa

Solidago rugosa var. rugosa, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
