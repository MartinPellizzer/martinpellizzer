Senecio denudatus

Senecio denudatus, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It features a slender, unbranched stem with few leaves, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a cluster.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
