Smilax argyrea

Smilax argyrea, commonly known as silver smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, heart-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Smilax argyrea is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
