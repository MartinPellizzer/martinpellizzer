Solanum inodorum

Solanum inodorum, commonly known as the odorless nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Solanum inodorum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
