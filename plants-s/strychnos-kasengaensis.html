Strychnos kasengaensis

Strychnos kasengaensis, commonly known as Kasenga poisonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals due to the presence of strychnine and brucine.

It is often found in shaded, humid environments and plays a role in the local ecosystem as a food source for certain wildlife.
