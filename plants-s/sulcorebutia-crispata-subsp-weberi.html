Sulcorebutia crispata subsp. weberi

Sulcorebutia crispata subsp.

weberi, commonly known as the Weber's crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features compact, rounded growth with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, pointed leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
