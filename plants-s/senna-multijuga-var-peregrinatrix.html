Senna multijuga var. peregrinatrix

Senna multijuga var. peregrinatrix, commonly known as the wandering senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive yellow to orange coloration, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
