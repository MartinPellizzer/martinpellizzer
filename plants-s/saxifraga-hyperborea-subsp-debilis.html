Saxifraga hyperborea subsp. debilis

Saxifraga hyperborea subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the short growing season. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude and polar habitats.
