Stachys reinertii

Stachys reinertii, commonly known as Reinert's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, purple to reddish flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Stachys reinertii is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
