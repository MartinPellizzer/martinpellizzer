Sedum eriocarpum subsp. porphyreum

Sedum eriocarpum subsp.

porphyreum, commonly known as the Porphyreum stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
