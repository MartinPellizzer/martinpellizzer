Spongiocarpella polystichoides

Spongiocarpella polystichoides, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional Andean medicine.

Spongiocarpella polystichoides thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.
