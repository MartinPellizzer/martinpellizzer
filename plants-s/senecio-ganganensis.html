Senecio ganganensis

Senecio ganganensis, commonly known as the Gangan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
