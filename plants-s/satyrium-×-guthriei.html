Satyrium × guthriei

Satyrium × guthriei, commonly known as the Guthrie's twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Satyrium × guthriei is often found in rich, deciduous forests and is adapted to low-light conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity within the genus Satyrium.
