Senecio sikkimensis

Senecio sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine areas, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a circular pattern.

Senecio sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
