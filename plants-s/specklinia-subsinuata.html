Specklinia subsinuata

Specklinia subsinuata, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive silvery or pale green vein pattern. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are wiry and help it anchor to its host while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Specklinia subsinuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
