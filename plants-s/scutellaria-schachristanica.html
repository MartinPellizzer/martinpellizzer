Scutellaria schachristanica

Scutellaria schachristanica, commonly known as Schachristan's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scutellaria schachristanica is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
