Salvia boegei

Salvia boegei, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Salvia boegei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
