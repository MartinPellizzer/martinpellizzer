Stipa schlagintweitii

Stipa schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Schlagintweit's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, golden-brown inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue, and it is often found in steppe and desert regions.

Stipa schlagintweitii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
