Senecio procerus var. grandidentatus

Senecio procerus var. grandidentatus, commonly known as the giant toothed groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, serrated leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
