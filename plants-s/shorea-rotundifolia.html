Shorea rotundifolia

Shorea rotundifolia, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, durable wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its ecological and commercial importance.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in tropical ecosystems.
