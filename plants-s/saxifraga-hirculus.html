Saxifraga hirculus

Saxifraga hirculus, commonly known as the sheep's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, sometimes reddish stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a hairy surface. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Saxifraga hirculus is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is often found in mountainous regions.
