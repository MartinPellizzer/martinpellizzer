Statice rediviva

Statice rediviva, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. This plant has the remarkable ability to survive extreme drought by entering a state of desiccation, appearing dead but reviving when water becomes available. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom after rain, adding a delicate touch to its resilient appearance.
