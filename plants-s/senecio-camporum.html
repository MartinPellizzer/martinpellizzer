Senecio camporum

Senecio camporum, commonly known as Camp's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
