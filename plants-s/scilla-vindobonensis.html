Scilla vindobonensis

Scilla vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Scilla vindobonensis is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
