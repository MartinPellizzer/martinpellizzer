Syzygium dubium

Syzygium dubium, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. Syzygium dubium is often found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
