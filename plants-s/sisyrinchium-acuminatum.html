Sisyrinchium acuminatum

Sisyrinchium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharpleaf blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it its common name, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sisyrinchium acuminatum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
