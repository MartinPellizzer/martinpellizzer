Scandix damascena

Scandix damascena, commonly known as the Damask Scandix, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scandix damascena is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
