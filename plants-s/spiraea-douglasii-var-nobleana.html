Spiraea douglasii var. nobleana

Spiraea douglasii var. nobleana, commonly known as the Douglas spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, rounded growth with clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and showy floral displays.
