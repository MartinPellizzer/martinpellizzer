Saussurea yanagisawae

Saussurea yanagisawae, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
