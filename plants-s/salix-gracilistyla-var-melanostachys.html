Salix gracilistyla var. melanostachys

Salix gracilistyla var. melanostachys, commonly known as the black-stalked willow, is a variant of the slender willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and the male and female flowers are typically separate. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

The bark and leaves of this willow have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
