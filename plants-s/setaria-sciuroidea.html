Setaria sciuroidea

Setaria sciuroidea, commonly known as the white bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows as an annual or perennial herb. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Setaria sciuroidea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
