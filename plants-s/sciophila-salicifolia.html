Sciophila salicifolia

Sciophila salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Sciophila salicifolia prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
