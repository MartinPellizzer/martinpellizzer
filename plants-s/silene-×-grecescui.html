Silene × grecescui

Silene × grecescui, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Silene gallica and Silene regeliana.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene × grecescui is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
