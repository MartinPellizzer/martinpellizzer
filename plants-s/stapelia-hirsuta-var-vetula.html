Stapelia hirsuta var. vetula

Stapelia hirsuta var. vetula, commonly known as the hairy stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are covered in short, hairy bristles, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a strong, unpleasant odor. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
