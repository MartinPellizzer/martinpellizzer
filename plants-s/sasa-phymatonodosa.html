Sasa phymatonodosa

Sasa phymatonodosa, commonly known as the thick-noded bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features sturdy, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.
