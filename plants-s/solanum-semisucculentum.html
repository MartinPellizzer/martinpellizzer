Solanum semisucculentum

Solanum semisucculentum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
