Strophanthus bequaertii

Strophanthus bequaertii, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
