Syzygium carolinense

Syzygium carolinense, commonly known as the Carolina allspice, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, red-orange fruits that resemble small cherries. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its aromatic berries. The leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
