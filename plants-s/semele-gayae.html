Semele gayae

Semele gayae, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive lip.

Semele gayae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
