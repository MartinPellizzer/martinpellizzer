Senecio scalaris var. parrasianus

Senecio scalaris var. parrasianus, commonly known as the Parra's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variant is distinguished from the typical Senecio scalaris by its slightly different floral structure and habitat preferences.
