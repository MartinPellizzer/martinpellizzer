Scilla plumbea

Scilla plumbea, commonly known as the blue squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during dry periods.

Scilla plumbea is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and early blooming habit.
