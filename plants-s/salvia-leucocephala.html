Salvia leucocephala

Salvia leucocephala, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid climates.

Salvia leucocephala is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in Native American medicine.
