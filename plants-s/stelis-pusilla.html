Stelis pusilla

Stelis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate, spurred shape typical of the orchid family.

Stelis pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
