Semeiostachys macrolepis

Semeiostachys macrolepis, commonly known as the giant leaved sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swampy areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
