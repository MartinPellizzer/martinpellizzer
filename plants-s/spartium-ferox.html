Spartium ferox

Spartium ferox, commonly known as bristly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer.

Spartium ferox is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
