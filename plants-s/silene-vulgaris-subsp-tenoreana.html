Silene vulgaris subsp. tenoreana

Silene vulgaris subsp.

tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant features slender stems with alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
