Sasa sylvatica

Sasa sylvatica, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, Korea, and China, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue. Sasa sylvatica is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to form dense colonies.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in woodland areas.
