Schefflera capuroniana

Schefflera capuroniana, commonly known as the Capuron's umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leafy shoots, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces bright red berries that are edible for birds.

Schefflera capuroniana thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
