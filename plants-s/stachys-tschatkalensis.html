Stachys tschatkalensis

Stachys tschatkalensis, commonly known as the Central Asian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features square stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually purple or violet in color, adding ornamental value.

Stachys tschatkalensis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
