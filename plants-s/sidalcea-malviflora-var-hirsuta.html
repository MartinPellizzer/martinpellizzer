Sidalcea malviflora var. hirsuta

Sidalcea malviflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy soaproot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often contains a milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
