Sceptridium obliquum

Sceptridium obliquum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and colonization of new habitats.

The plant's simple, undivided fronds and lack of true roots make it a unique example of aquatic fern morphology.
