Spathodea stipulata

Spathodea stipulata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds.

Spathodea stipulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
