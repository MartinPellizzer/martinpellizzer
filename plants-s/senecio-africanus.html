Senecio africanus

Senecio africanus, commonly known as the African ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio africanus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some African cultures.
