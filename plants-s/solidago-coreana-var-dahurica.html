Solidago coreana var. dahurica

Solidago coreana var. dahurica, commonly known as the Korean goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
