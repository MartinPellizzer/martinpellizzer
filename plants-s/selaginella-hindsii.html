Selaginella hindsii

Selaginella hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It is a small, evergreen, and herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiky, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. Selaginella hindsii is known for its ability to survive in humid conditions and is often found in forest understories.

Its structure includes a root system and a stem with whorled leaves, making it a unique example of a spikemoss.
