Scirpus maritimus var. affinis

Scirpus maritimus var. affinis, commonly known as the salt marsh bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers.

Scirpus maritimus var. affinis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
