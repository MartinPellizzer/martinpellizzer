Syzygium durum

Syzygium durum, commonly known as the durian, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky fruit, which can weigh up to 14 kilograms. The plant has compound leaves that are usually alternate and ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically white or pale yellow, and are grouped in clusters.

The durian is known for its unique, strong aroma and is highly valued for both its culinary and medicinal uses.
