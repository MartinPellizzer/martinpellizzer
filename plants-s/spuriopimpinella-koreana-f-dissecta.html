Spuriopimpinella koreana f. dissecta

Spuriopimpinella koreana f. dissecta, commonly known as the Korean dissected pimpernel, is a variant of the Spuriopimpinella koreana species native to Korea.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it blooms in the spring.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
