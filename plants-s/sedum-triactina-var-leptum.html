Sedum triactina var. leptum

Sedum triactina var. leptum, commonly known as the slender sedum, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. The plant features fleshy, linear leaves that are often blue-green in color, which help it store water efficiently. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in the summer months.

This variety is prized for its drought tolerance and ornamental value in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
