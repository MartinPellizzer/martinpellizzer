Sarcochilus odoratus

Sarcochilus odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Sarcochilus odoratus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique fragrance.
