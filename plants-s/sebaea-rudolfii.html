Sebaea rudolfii

Sebaea rudolfii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which is toxic. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
