Stelis repanda

Stelis repanda, commonly known as the spreading stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and slightly fleshy, adapted to its epiphytic lifestyle.

The flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and are characteristic of the Orchidaceae family.
