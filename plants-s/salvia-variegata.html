Salvia variegata

Salvia variegata, commonly known as variegated sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in gray-green, fuzzy foliage and produces spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia variegata is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
