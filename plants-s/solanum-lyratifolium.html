Solanum lyratifolium

Solanum lyratifolium, commonly known as the lyratic leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lyre-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum lyratifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and flowering habit.
