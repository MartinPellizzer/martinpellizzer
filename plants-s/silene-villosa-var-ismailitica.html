Silene villosa var. ismailitica

Silene villosa var. ismailitica, commonly known as the Ismaili stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Silene villosa var. ismailitica plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
