Solanum monotanthum

Solanum monotanthum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Solanum monotanthum is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
