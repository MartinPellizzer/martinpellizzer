Salvia przewalskii f. albiflora

Salvia przewalskii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese sage, is a cultivar of the Salvia przewalskii species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
