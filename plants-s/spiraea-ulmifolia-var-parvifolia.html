Spiraea ulmifolia var. parvifolia

Spiraea ulmifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge, turning red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscape design.

This variety is known for its graceful appearance and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
