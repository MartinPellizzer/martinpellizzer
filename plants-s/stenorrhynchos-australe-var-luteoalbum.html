Stenorrhynchos australe var. luteoalbum

Stenorrhynchos australe var. luteoalbum, commonly known as the golden white waratah, is a variant of the waratah plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
