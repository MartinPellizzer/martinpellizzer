Senecio trigonophyllus

Senecio trigonophyllus, commonly known as the triangular-leaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
