Sideritis taurica var. glandulosa

Sideritis taurica var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Caucasian sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
