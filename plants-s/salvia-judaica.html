Salvia judaica

Salvia judaica, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, containing essential oils and various phytochemicals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia judaica is widely used in traditional medicine and religious practices.
