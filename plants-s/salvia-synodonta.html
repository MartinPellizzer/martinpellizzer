Salvia synodonta

Salvia synodonta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia synodonta is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
