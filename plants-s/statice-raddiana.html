Statice raddiana

Statice raddiana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it well adapted to harsh, coastal conditions. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

Statice raddiana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
