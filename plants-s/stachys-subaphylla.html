Stachys subaphylla

Stachys subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Stachys subaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
