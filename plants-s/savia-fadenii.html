Savia fadenii

Savia fadenii, commonly known as the Faden's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typical of the milkweed family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Savia fadenii is also noted for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus.
