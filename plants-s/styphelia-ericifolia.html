Styphelia ericifolia

Styphelia ericifolia, commonly known as the heath tundra, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Styphelia ericifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
