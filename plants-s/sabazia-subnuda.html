Sabazia subnuda

Sabazia subnuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, hairless stems and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, whitish coating.

Sabazia subnuda is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland sandy regions.
