Sclerotheca arborea

Sclerotheca arborea, commonly known as the tree with hardened bark, is a unique plant characterized by its thick, woody exodermis that provides structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This feature is particularly pronounced in its trunk and branches, which develop a dense, scaly texture over time. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where its drought-resistant properties allow it to thrive in harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically small and leathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Sclerotheca arborea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing shelter for various wildlife species.
