Sasa amplissima

Sasa amplissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, evergreen plant native to the forests of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curved, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Sasa amplissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
