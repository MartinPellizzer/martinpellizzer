Senegalia robynsiana

Senegalia robynsiana, commonly known as the Robyn's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

Senegalia robynsiana also plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
