Salix weixiensis

Salix weixiensis, commonly known as Weixi willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salix weixiensis is adapted to moist environments and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands.
