Statice ovalifolia var. nana

Statice ovalifolia var. nana, commonly known as dwarf statice, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often grown as a garden ornamental due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It prefers full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its oval-shaped leaves and dwarf growth form, distinguishing it from other statice varieties.
