Stemona javanica

Stemona javanica, commonly known as the Java stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and erect, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
