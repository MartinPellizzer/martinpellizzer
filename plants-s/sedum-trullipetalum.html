Sedum trullipetalum

Sedum trullipetalum, commonly known as the star-shaped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, typically from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
