Shorea auriculata

Shorea auriculata, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white beach wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is valued for its durable, heavy, and attractive wood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.
