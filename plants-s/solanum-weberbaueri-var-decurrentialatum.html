Solanum weberbaueri var. decurrentialatum

Solanum weberbaueri var. decurrentialatum, commonly known as the Weberbauer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a yellowish color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
