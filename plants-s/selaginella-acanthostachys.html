Selaginella acanthostachys

Selaginella acanthostachys, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, scale-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around its stem. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures called strobila, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella acanthostachys is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
