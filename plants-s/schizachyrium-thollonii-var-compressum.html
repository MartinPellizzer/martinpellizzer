Schizachyrium thollonii var. compressum

Schizachyrium thollonii var. compressum, commonly known as narrowleaf sideblades, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant has slender, compressed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
