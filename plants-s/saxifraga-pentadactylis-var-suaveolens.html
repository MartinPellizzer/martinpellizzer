Saxifraga pentadactylis var. suaveolens

Saxifraga pentadactylis var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle, sweet fragrance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine habitats.
