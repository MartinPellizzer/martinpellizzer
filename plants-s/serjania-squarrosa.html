Serjania squarrosa

Serjania squarrosa, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
