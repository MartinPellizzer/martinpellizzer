Statice amplifoliata

Statice amplifoliata, commonly known as the broadleaf statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.

Statice amplifoliata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
