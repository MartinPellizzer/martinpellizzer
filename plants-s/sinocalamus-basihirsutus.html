Sinocalamus basihirsutus

Sinocalamus basihirsutus, commonly known as the broad-leaved bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and sturdy, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions. It has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Sinocalamus basihirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
