Stachys petrogena

Stachys petrogena, commonly known as the rock betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its square stems and opposite leaves are characteristic of the genus Stachys.

It produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months.
