Sideritis boissieri

Sideritis boissieri, commonly known as Turkish woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Sideritis boissieri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
