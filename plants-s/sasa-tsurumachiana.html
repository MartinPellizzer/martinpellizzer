Sasa tsurumachiana

Sasa tsurumachiana, commonly known as the Tsurumachi bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa tsurumachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
