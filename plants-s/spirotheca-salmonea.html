Spirotheca salmonea

Spirotheca salmonea, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a flowering species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus Spirotheca is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is primarily found in the succulent Karoo biome.
