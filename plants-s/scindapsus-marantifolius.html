Scindapsus marantifolius

Scindapsus marantifolius, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is typically adorned with a waxy coating that gives it a unique, almost metallic sheen.

Scindapsus marantifolius is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
