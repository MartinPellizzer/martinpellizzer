Scabiosa urceolata

Scabiosa urceolata, commonly known as the "sweet William" or "susan's necklace," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scabiosa urceolata is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
