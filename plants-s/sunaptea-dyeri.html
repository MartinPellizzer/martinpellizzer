Sunaptea dyeri

Sunaptea dyeri, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Sunaptea dyeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
