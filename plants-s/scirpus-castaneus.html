Scirpus castaneus

Scirpus castaneus, commonly known as the purple bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for erosion control in wetland environments.

Scirpus castaneus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.
