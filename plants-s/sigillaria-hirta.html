Sigillaria hirta

Sigillaria hirta, commonly known as the hairy sigillaria, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the family Sigillariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and often hairy stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Sigillaria hirta is of particular interest to botanists due to its primitive morphology and its role in understanding the evolutionary history of vascular plants.
