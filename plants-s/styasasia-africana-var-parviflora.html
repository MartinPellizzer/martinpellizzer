Styasasia africana var. parviflora

Styasasia africana var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered African styasasia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features woody stems with smooth, grayish bark. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are pale yellow and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the warmer months.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
