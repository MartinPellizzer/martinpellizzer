Salvia triangularis

Salvia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, triangular-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia triangularis is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
