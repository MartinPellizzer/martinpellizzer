Soranthe rupestris

Soranthe rupestris, commonly known as the rock soranthe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
