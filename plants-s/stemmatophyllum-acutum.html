Stemmatophyllum acutum

Stemmatophyllum acutum, commonly known as the sharpstemmed water plant, is a aquatic plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in its habitat.

Stemmatophyllum acutum plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
