Scrophularia umbrosa

Scrophularia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in shady, moist environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia umbrosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
