Salix × tinctoria

Salix × tinctoria, commonly known as the dyer's willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional dyeing processes. The plant produces small, yellow catkins that are wind-pollinated, and it typically grows in moist, temperate environments. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that yield various dyes, including yellow and green hues.

Salix × tinctoria is valued both for its ecological role and its historical significance in textile dyeing.
