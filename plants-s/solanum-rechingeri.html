Solanum rechingeri

Solanum rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum rechingeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
