Sarcocornia freitagii

Sarcocornia freitagii, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas, marshes, and estuaries where it tolerates high salt concentrations. Its structure includes a branched stem with alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

S. freitagii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
