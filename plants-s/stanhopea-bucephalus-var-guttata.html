Stanhopea bucephalus var. guttata

Stanhopea bucephalus var. guttata, commonly known as the spotted stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with purple and red markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to minimize water loss.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
