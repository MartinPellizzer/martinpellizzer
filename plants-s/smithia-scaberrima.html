Smithia scaberrima

Smithia scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, elongated leaves with a rough, textured surface, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

Smithia scaberrima is often found in alpine or arctic regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
