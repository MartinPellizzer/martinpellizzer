Syzygium inopinatum

Syzygium inopinatum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
