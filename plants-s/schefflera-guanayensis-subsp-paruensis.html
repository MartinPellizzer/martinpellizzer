Schefflera guanayensis subsp. paruensis

Schefflera guanayensis subsp.

paruensis, commonly known as the Andean umbrella plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a radiating pattern.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
