Symphytum creticum

Symphytum creticum, commonly known as the Cretan comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems topped with clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Symphytum creticum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used to treat wounds and inflammation.
