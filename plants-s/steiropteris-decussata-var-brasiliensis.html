Steiropteris decussata var. brasiliensis

Steiropteris decussata var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds, which are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself to trees or rocks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
