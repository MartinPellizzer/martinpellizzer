Sida eriantha

Sida eriantha, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fibrous stems and serrated leaves are adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

Sida eriantha is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
