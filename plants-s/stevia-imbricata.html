Stevia imbricata

Stevia imbricata, commonly known as sweetleaf or sugar leaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners that are much sweeter than table sugar. Stevia imbricata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal gardens.

Its use in herbal medicine and as a natural sweetener has gained global recognition in recent years.
