Serapias lorenziana

Serapias lorenziana, commonly known as the Lorentz's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces tall, branching stems with showy inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Serapias lorenziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
