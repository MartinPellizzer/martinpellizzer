Suddia sagittifolia

Suddia sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Suddia sagittifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
