Salix pseudocalyculata

Salix pseudocalyculata, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, oval leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
