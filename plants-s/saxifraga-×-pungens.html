Saxifraga × pungens

Saxifraga × pungens, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in cool, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga × pungens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
