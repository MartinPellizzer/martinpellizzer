Silybum leucanthum

Silybum leucanthum, commonly known as white milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

However, it is considered a weed in many areas due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
