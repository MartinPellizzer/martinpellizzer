Silene indica var. indica

Silene indica var. indica, commonly known as the Indian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and it has hairy, linear leaves. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other plants like carnations and moss campion.
