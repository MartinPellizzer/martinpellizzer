Sabazia leiachaenia

Sabazia leiachaenia, commonly known as the purple-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and range in color from white to purple. Sabazia leiachaenia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
