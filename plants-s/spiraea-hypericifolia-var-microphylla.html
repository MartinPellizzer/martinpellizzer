Spiraea hypericifolia var. microphylla

Spiraea hypericifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf spiraea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a lush, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact size and long-lasting floral display.
