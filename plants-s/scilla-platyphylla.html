Scilla platyphylla

Scilla platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette.
