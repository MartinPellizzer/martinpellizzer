Stanhopea tigrina var. lutescens

Stanhopea tigrina var. lutescens, commonly known as the Yellow Orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes, giving it its scientific name. This variety belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings to ensure its survival.
