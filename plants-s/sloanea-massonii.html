Sloanea massonii

Sloanea massonii, commonly known as the Masson's sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
