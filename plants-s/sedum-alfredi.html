Sedum alfredi

Sedum alfredi, commonly known as Alfred's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, Sedum alfredi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
