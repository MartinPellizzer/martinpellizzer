Stachys langmaniae

Stachys langmaniae, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stachys langmaniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
