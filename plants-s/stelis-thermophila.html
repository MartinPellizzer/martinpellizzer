Stelis thermophila

Stelis thermophila, commonly known as the hot-loving orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in forested areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hair. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Stelis thermophila requires well-drained, airy substrates and is often cultivated as a decorative orchid in greenhouses or shaded outdoor gardens.
