Strongylosperma reptans

Strongylosperma reptans, commonly known as the creeping strongylodes, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable species in diverse ecological settings.
