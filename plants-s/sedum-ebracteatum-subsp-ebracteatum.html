Sedum ebracteatum subsp. ebracteatum

Sedum ebracteatum subsp.

ebracteatum, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
