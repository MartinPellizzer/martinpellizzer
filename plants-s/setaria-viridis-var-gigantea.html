Setaria viridis var. gigantea

Setaria viridis var. gigantea, commonly known as giant green millet, is a variant of the common green millet, characterized by its tall stature and robust growth.

It is a C4 plant, which allows it to efficiently perform photosynthesis under high light and temperature conditions. The plant produces dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, pointed leaves that can reach several feet in height. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is often cultivated for forage or as a hardy ornamental plant due to its resilience and rapid growth.
