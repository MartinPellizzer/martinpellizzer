Syzygium cameronum

Syzygium cameronum, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits known as clydesdales. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Syzygium cameronum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
