Sisymbrium fugax var. pubescens

Sisymbrium fugax var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fleaweed, is a small annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard.
