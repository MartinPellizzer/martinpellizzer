Sasa kumaensis

Sasa kumaensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Sasa kumaensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
