Sorbus aucuparia f. praemorsa

Sorbus aucuparia f. praemorsa, commonly known as the white-flowered rowan, is a botanical variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green leaves. This form is typically found in cooler, moist environments and is often associated with forested areas. The fruit, which is small and red, is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
