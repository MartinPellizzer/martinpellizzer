Schinus dentata

Schinus dentata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small and greenish-white in color. Schinus dentata produces red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
