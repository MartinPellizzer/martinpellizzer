Selaginella robinsonii

Selaginella robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's spikemoss, is a rare and endangered species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, wiry stems that bear small, scale-like leaves, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
