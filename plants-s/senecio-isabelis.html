Senecio isabelis

Senecio isabelis, commonly known as the Isabel's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
