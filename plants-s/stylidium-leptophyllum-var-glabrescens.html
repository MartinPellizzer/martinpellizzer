Stylidium leptophyllum var. glabrescens

Stylidium leptophyllum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, sun-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred lip.

This variety is noted for its compact size and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
