Sida rhombifolia subsp. insularis

Sida rhombifolia subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Island Malva, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is a herbaceous perennial with oval to heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
