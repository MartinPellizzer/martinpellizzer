Salix resectoides

Salix resectoides, commonly known as the reed willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental features, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
