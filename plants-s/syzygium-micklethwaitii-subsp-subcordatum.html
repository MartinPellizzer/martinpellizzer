Syzygium micklethwaitii subsp. subcordatum

Syzygium micklethwaitii subsp.

subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
