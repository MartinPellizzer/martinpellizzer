Silene afromontana

Silene afromontana, commonly known as the Ethiopian campion, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates and is part of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Silene afromontana is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
