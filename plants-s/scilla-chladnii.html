Scilla chladnii

Scilla chladnii, commonly known as the Persian scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates but may die back in colder regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
