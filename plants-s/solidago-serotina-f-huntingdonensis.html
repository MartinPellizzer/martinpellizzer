Solidago serotina f. huntingdonensis

Solidago serotina f. huntingdonensis, commonly known as the Huntington's late goldenrod, is a variant of the late goldenrod species.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and streambanks. The plant features tall, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its showy clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
