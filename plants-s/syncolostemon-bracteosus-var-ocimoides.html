Syncolostemon bracteosus var. ocimoides

Syncolostemon bracteosus var. ocimoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers resembling mist. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its close relationship to the genus Ocimum, which includes basil and other aromatic plants.
