Spiranthes sagittata var. depauperata

Spiranthes sagittata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, spiraled inflorescence composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is typically found in the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and moths.
