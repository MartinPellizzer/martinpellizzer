Senecio propinquus

Senecio propinquus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Senecio propinquus is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
