Symplocos oxyphylla

Symplocos oxyphylla, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. Symplocos oxyphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
