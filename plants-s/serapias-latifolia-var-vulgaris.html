Serapias latifolia var. vulgaris

Serapias latifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

The species is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
