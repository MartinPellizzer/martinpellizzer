Scirpus glaber

Scirpus glaber, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering heads are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scirpus glaber plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
