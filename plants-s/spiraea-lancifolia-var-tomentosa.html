Spiraea lancifolia var. tomentosa

Spiraea lancifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in early to mid-spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
