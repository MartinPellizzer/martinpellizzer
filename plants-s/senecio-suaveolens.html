Senecio suaveolens

Senecio suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is also noted for its pleasant, spicy fragrance, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
