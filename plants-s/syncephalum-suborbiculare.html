Syncephalum suborbiculare

Syncephalum suborbiculare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Syncephalum suborbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
