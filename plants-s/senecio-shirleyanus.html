Senecio shirleyanus

Senecio shirleyanus, commonly known as Shirley's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
