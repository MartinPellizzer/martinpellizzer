Sabicea velutina var. oblongifolia

Sabicea velutina var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
