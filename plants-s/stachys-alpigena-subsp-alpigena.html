Stachys alpigena subsp. alpigena

Stachys alpigena subsp.

alpigena, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous environments.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, forming purple or violet bracts that resemble small bells.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
