Scabiosa columbaria var. lanceolata

Scabiosa columbaria var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaved scabious, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
