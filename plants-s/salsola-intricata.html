Salsola intricata

Salsola intricata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and succulent stems. It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed habitats.

Salsola intricata is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which contribute to its invasive potential in certain regions.
