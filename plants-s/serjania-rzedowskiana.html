Serjania rzedowskiana

Serjania rzedowskiana, commonly known as the Mexican serjania, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
