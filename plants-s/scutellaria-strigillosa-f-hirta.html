Scutellaria strigillosa f. hirta

Scutellaria strigillosa f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and are tubular with two lips, a typical feature of the genus Scutellaria. This plant is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
