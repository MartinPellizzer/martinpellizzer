Sidalcea ranunculacea

Sidalcea ranunculacea, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sidalcea ranunculacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
