Stromanthe confusa

Stromanthe confusa, commonly known as the "Rainbow Plant," is a member of the Araceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, silver, and pink, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Stromanthe confusa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
