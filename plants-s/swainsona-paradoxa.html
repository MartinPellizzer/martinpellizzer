Swainsona paradoxa

Swainsona paradoxa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Fabaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly faces, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant produces bright yellow petals with a distinctive, elongated shape that sets it apart from other species in the genus. Swainsona paradoxa is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors worldwide.
