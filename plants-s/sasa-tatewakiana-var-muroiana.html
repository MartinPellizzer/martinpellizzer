Sasa tatewakiana var. muroiana

Sasa tatewakiana var. muroiana, commonly known as the Muroi bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa tatewakiana species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features slender, green culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and landscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to humid, temperate climates.
