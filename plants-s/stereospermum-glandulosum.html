Stereospermum glandulosum

Stereospermum glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
