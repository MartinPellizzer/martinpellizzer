Stapelia patula var. longirostris

Stapelia patula var. longirostris, commonly known as the Long-nosed Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive scent resembling decaying flesh. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The varietal name 'longirostris' refers to its elongated, narrow flower shape.
