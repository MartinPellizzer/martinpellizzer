Syngonium chocoanum

Syngonium chocoanum, commonly known as the Chocolate Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often exhibit a dark green to almost black coloration, especially when mature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Syngonium chocoanum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
