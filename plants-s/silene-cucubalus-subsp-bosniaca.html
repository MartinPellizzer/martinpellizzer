Silene cucubalus subsp. bosniaca

Silene cucubalus subsp.

bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments.

The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
