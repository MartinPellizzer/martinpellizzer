Scrophularia scorodonia f. glabrescens

Scrophularia scorodonia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth scrophularia, is a variant of the scrophularia genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in woodlands and meadows. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. Its flowers are usually pale pink to reddish, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
