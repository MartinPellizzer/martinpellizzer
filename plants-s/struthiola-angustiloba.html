Struthiola angustiloba

Struthiola angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved struthiola, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Struthiola angustiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
