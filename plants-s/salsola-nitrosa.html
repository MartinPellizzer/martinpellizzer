Salsola nitrosa

Salsola nitrosa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a short-lived perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

Salsola nitrosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
