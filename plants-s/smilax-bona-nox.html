Smilax bona-nox

Smilax bona-nox, commonly known as the nightshade smilax, is a perennial climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Smilax, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its stems are armed with sharp, hollow spines, and its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a pointed tip.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
