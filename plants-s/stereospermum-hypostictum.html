Stereospermum hypostictum

Stereospermum hypostictum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red maple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
