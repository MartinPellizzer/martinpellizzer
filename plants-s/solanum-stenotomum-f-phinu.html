Solanum stenotomum f. phinu

Solanum stenotomum f. phinu, commonly known as the Phinu tomato, is a botanical variant of the species Solanum stenotomum.

It is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal due to its compact growth habit and vibrant fruit color.
