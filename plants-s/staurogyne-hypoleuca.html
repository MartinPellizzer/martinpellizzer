Staurogyne hypoleuca

Staurogyne hypoleuca, commonly known as the small-leaved waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It is a submerged herbaceous plant that forms dense mats in slow-moving or still waters. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually occurring in clusters near the water surface.

Staurogyne hypoleuca plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
