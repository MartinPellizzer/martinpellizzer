Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. intermedium

Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy, violet to purple flower heads that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
