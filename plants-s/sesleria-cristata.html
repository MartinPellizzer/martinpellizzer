Sesleria cristata

Sesleria cristata, commonly known as the spiked grass or cristate sesleria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal.

Sesleria cristata is adapted to well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.
