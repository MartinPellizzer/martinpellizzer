Sabadilla officinalis

Sabadilla officinalis, commonly known as the Spanish squaw root, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries containing seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in medicine and as a source of alkaloids.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
