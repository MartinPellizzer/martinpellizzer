Sutera compta

Sutera compta, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax, which gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sutera compta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest.
