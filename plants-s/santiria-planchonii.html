Santiria planchonii

Santiria planchonii, commonly known as the golden soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its botanical features include a woody stem and aromatic foliage.

Santiria planchonii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments.
