Stachys alpina var. discolor

Stachys alpina var. discolor, commonly known as the alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.
