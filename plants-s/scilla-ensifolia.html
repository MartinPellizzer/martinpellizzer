Scilla ensifolia

Scilla ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and some toxic species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla ensifolia is also valued for its early blooming habit and low maintenance requirements.
