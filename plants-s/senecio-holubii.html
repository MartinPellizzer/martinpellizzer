Senecio holubii

Senecio holubii, commonly known as Holub's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
