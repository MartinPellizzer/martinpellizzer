Salsola foetida var. glabrescens

Salsola foetida var. glabrescens, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Salsola foetida var. glabrescens is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
