Solanum lantana

Solanum lantana, commonly known as the shrub lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solasidine and solasodine. Solanum lantana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is also known for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
