Smilax schlechtendalii var. lindenii

Smilax schlechtendalii var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Smilax, which includes a variety of woody plants characterized by their spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. This particular variant is distinguished by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and its ecological interactions.
