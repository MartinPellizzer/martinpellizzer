Schinus microphylla var. andina

Schinus microphylla var. andina, commonly known as the Andean pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Its leaves and fruits contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
