Sporobolus interruptus

Sporobolus interruptus, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions makes it a resilient species in various habitats.
