Salvia pseudoaethiopis

Salvia pseudoaethiopis, commonly known as the Ethiopian sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, gray-green foliage, and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used traditionally in local remedies.

Salvia pseudoaethiopis is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
