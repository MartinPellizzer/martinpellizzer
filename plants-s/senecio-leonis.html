Senecio leonis

Senecio leonis, commonly known as the lion's tooth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, tooth-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Senecio leonis is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
