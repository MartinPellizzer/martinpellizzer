Selaginella satakeana

Selaginella satakeana, commonly known as the Japanese spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves and a branched, creeping stem system. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic feature of its evolutionary lineage.

Selaginella satakeana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
