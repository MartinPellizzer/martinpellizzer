Senecio sempervivus subsp. grantii

Senecio sempervivus subsp.

grantii, commonly known as the Grant's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, often with a bluish or greyish hue.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
