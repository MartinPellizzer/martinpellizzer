Schrenkia ugamica

Schrenkia ugamica, commonly known as the Ugam bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.
