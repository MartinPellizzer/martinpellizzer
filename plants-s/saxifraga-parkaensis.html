Saxifraga parkaensis

Saxifraga parkaensis, commonly known as the Arctic saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in harsh, cold environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically rosette-shaped, adapted to minimize heat loss and maximize light absorption.

Saxifraga parkaensis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various arctic organisms.
