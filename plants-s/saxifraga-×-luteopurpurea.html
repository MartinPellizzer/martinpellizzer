Saxifraga × luteopurpurea

Saxifraga × luteopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga variegata.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a mix of purple, green, and variegated patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It produces small, pink or white flowers in late summer, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
