Stipa nullarborensis

Stipa nullarborensis, commonly known as the dwarf needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Stipa nullarborensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
