Sasa trichodon

Sasa trichodon, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa trichodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
