Sideritis arenaria var. divaricatidens

Sideritis arenaria var. divaricatidens, commonly known as the sand sidereal, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, gray-green leaves arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces delicate blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in narrow spikes. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Sideritis arenaria var. divaricatidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
