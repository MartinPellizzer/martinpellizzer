Sinapis alba var. apula

Sinapis alba var. apula, commonly known as the Apulian mustard, is a variety of white mustard native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rapid growth cycle and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the development of spherical, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and have a strong mustard scent when crushed.

This variety is often used in traditional agriculture for soil preparation and as a cover crop due to its quick growth and nutrient cycling properties.
