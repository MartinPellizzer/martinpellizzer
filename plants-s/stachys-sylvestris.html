Stachys sylvestris

Stachys sylvestris, commonly known as woodland betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces spikes of purple or pink flowers in the summer.

Stachys sylvestris is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
