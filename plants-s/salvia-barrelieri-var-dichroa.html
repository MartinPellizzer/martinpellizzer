Salvia barrelieri var. dichroa

Salvia barrelieri var. dichroa, commonly known as the dichroa sage, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, two-toned leaves that range from dark green to silvery white, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
