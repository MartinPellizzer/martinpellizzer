Southwellia versicolor

Southwellia versicolor, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its elegant, violet-hued flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Southwellia versicolor is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
