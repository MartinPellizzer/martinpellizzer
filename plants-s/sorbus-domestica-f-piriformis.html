Sorbus domestica f. piriformis

Sorbus domestica f. piriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped rowan, is a cultivar of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often pear-shaped fruits that remain on the tree well into winter. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and persistent fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
