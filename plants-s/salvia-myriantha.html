Salvia myriantha

Salvia myriantha, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
