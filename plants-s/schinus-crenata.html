Schinus crenata

Schinus crenata, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or erva-de-são-joão, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Schinus crenata thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it a common ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish-brown bark and small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters.
