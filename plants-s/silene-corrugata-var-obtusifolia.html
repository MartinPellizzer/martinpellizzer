Silene corrugata var. obtusifolia

Silene corrugata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions with its deep root system.
