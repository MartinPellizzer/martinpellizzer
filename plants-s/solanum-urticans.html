Solanum urticans

Solanum urticans, commonly known as the stinging nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals. Its leaves and stems contain irritants that can cause skin reactions in humans.

Solanum urticans is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its medicinal and toxic properties.
