Schefflera angulata

Schefflera angulata, commonly known as the angular schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are arranged in a radiating pattern around the stem. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, often arranged in a whorl-like structure. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Schefflera angulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
