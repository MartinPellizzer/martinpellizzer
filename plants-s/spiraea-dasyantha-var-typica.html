Spiraea dasyantha var. typica

Spiraea dasyantha var. typica, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on the previous year's growth. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
