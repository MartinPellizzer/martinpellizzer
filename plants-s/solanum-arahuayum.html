Solanum arahuayum

Solanum arahuayum, commonly known as the Arahuaya or wild tomato, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct aroma.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
