Sigesbeckia filarszkyi

Sigesbeckia filarszkyi, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds via wind, similar to dandelions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
