Symplocos botryantha

Symplocos botryantha, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Symplocos botryantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
