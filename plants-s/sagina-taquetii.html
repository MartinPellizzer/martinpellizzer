Sagina taquetii

Sagina taquetii, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Sagina taquetii is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
