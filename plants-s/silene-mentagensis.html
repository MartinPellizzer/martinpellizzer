Silene mentagensis

Silene mentagensis, commonly known as the Mentaghen's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other species such as carnations and sweet William.

Silene mentagensis is often found in open habitats, including coastal areas and dry grasslands.
