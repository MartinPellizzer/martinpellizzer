Scorzonera kenissaea

Scorzonera kenissaea, commonly known as the Kenissaea scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scorzonera kenissaea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
