Schotia rogersii

Schotia rogersii, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Schotia rogersii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
