Saxifraga gemmigera var. gemmuligera

Saxifraga gemmigera var. gemmuligera, commonly known as the gemmuligera saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Saxifragaceae family.
