Sandoricum emarginatum

Sandoricum emarginatum, commonly known as the Caribbean beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows along coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

It is well adapted to salt spray and sandy environments, making it a hardy coastal species.
