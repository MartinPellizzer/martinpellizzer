Synoliga pauciflora

Synoliga pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered synoliga, is a rare and delicate plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its floral structure is simple, with few petals and a distinct, elongated stamen.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
