Serjania platyptera

Serjania platyptera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaneae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its density and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.

Serjania platyptera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
