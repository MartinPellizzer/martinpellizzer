Salix futura var. psilocarpa

Salix futura var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the psychedelic willow, is a rare variant of the willow tree characterized by its unique botanical features.

It possesses elongated, slender leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in catkins, a common trait among willow species. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a fibrous texture that peels away in thin layers.

This variant is notable for its association with psychoactive compounds, making it a subject of both scientific and cultural interest.
