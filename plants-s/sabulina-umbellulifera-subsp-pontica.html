Sabulina umbellulifera subsp. pontica

Sabulina umbellulifera subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are characteristic of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are usually linear and densely packed, contributing to its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid and saline environments.
