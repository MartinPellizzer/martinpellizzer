Saul iallarea

Saul iallarea, commonly known as the bluebell of the north, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant's leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, which helps to reflect excess sunlight in its alpine habitat. Saul iallarea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it an important early pollinator resource.
