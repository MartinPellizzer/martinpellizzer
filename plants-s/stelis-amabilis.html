Stelis amabilis

Stelis amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are noted for their delicate appearance and sweet scent.

Stelis amabilis is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
