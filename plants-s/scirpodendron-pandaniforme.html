Scirpodendron pandaniforme

Scirpodendron pandaniforme, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

Scirpodendron pandaniforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
