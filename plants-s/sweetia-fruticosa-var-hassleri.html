Sweetia fruticosa var. hassleri

Sweetia fruticosa var. hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's sweetia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and slightly hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
