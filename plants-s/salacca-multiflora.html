Salacca multiflora

Salacca multiflora, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers at the base of its trunk, which eventually develop into edible, sweet fruits. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
