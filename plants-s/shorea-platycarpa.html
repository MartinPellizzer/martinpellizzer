Shorea platycarpa

Shorea platycarpa, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
