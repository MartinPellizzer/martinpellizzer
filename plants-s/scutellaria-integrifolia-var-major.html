Scutellaria integrifolia var. major

Scutellaria integrifolia var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
