Strobus lambertiana

Strobus lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's fir, is a coniferous evergreen native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
