Sida ciliaris

Sida ciliaris, commonly known as the coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or saline soils. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Sida ciliaris is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
