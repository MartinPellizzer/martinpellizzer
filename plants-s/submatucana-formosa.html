Submatucana formosa

Submatucana formosa, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The vine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in vertical gardening or as a decorative element in landscapes.
