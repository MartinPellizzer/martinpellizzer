Senecio quinquangulatus

Senecio quinquangulatus, commonly known as the five-angled senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
