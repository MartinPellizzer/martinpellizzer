Salvia melanocalyx

Salvia melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-lobed sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black leaves, which are lance-shaped and often hairy. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
