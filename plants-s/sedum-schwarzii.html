Sedum schwarzii

Sedum schwarzii, commonly known as the Blackfoot sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sedum schwarzii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold and arid environments.
