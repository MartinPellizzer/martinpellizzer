Sebaea minuta

Sebaea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf sebaea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sebaea minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
