Spiraea × nobleana var. intermedia

Spiraea × nobleana var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid meadowsweet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, streambanks, and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardening and ecological habitats.
