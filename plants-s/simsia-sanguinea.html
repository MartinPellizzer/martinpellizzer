Simsia sanguinea

Simsia sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a short, stout stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Simsia sanguinea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
