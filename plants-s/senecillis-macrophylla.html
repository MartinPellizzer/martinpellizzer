Senecillis macrophylla

Senecillis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecillis macrophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant qualities.
