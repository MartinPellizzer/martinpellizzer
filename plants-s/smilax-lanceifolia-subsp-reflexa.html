Smilax lanceifolia subsp. reflexa

Smilax lanceifolia subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed arrowwood, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It features woody stems with spiny nodes and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often pubescent underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
