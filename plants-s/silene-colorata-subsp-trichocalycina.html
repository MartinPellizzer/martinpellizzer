Silene colorata subsp. trichocalycina

Silene colorata subsp.

trichocalycina, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy calyces, which give it its specific epithet *trichocalycina*. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
