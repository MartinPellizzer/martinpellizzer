Smilacina racemosa var. glabra

Smilacina racemosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth smilacina, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
