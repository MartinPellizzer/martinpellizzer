Santolina sinaitica

Santolina sinaitica, commonly known as Sinai santolina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky soils of Israel and surrounding areas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring silvery-gray foliage with a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Santolina sinaitica is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for both garden use and horticultural research.
