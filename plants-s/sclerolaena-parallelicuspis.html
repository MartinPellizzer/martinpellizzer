Sclerolaena parallelicuspis

Sclerolaena parallelicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Sclerolaena parallelicuspis is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
