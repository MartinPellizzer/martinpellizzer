Saurauia scabra var. peruviana

Saurauia scabra var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian roughleaf sandwort, is a succulent plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
