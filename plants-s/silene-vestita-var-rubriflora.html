Silene vestita var. rubriflora

Silene vestita var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered bluets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
