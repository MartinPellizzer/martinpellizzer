Stenogastra concinna

Stenogastra concinna, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Stenogastra concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat for various microorganisms and insects.
