Syzygium crassipes

Syzygium crassipes, commonly known as the rough-stemmed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium crassipes thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
