Scleria lithosperma subsp. linearis

Scleria lithosperma subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray sheen.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
