Sasa hayatae f. hispidula

Sasa hayatae f. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa hayatae species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and stout, often hairy stems. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and unique foliage texture.
