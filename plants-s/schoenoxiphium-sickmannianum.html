Schoenoxiphium sickmannianum

Schoenoxiphium sickmannianum, commonly known as the sickmann's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five lobes, and bloom in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
