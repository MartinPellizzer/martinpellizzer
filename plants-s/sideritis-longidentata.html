Sideritis longidentata

Sideritis longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Sideritis longidentata is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its potential medicinal uses.
