Saxifraga luteopurpurea

Saxifraga luteopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to purple. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive purplish hue that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom in cooler climates.
