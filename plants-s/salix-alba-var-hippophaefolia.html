Salix alba var. hippophaefolia

Salix alba var. hippophaefolia, commonly known as the white willow with golden catkins, is a variant of the white willow native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and distinctive yellowish catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, silvery underside. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist soils.

It plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
