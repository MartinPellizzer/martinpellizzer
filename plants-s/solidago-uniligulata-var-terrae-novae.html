Solidago uniligulata var. terrae-novae

Solidago uniligulata var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Terra Nova goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and wet meadows.
