Seridia sulphurea

Seridia sulphurea, commonly known as the golden star daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
