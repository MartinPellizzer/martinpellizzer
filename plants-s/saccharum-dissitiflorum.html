Saccharum dissitiflorum

Saccharum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf sugarcane.

It is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its tall, erect stalks and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its high sugar content in its stems, making it a valuable resource for commercial sugarcane cultivation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
