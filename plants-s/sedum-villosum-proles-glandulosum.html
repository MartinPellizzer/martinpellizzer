Sedum villosum proles glandulosum

Sedum villosum proles glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs.

It is native to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
