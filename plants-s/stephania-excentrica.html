Stephania excentrica

Stephania excentrica, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, the plant is toxic if ingested in large quantities.
