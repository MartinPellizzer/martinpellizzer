Schefflera cephalotes

Schefflera cephalotes, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has large, glossy, palmate leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

Schefflera cephalotes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
