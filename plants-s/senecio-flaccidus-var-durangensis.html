Senecio flaccidus var. durangensis

Senecio flaccidus var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango Senecio, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
