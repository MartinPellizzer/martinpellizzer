Sinobambusa gigantea

Sinobambusa gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is also used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
