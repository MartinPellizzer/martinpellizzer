Solanum chillonanum

Solanum chillonanum, commonly known as the Chillona nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

The plant is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants.
