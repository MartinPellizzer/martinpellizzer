Sagittaria kurziana

Sagittaria kurziana, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has strap-like, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. Its small white flowers bloom in summer, and it produces berries that are dispersed by water.

Sagittaria kurziana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
