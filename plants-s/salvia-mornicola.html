Salvia mornicola

Salvia mornicola, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often resembling the shape of a trumpet. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and somewhat woody.

Salvia mornicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
