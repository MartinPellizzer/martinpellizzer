Salvia australis

Salvia australis, commonly known as the New Zealand mint, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Salvia australis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
