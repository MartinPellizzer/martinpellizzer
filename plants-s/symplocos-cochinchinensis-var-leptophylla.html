Symplocos cochinchinensis var. leptophylla

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed boxwood, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are white and clustered in small umbels, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its dense, compact growth habit.
