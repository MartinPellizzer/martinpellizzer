Stenolobium guarracha

Stenolobium guarracha, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is often fissured and rough, while its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
