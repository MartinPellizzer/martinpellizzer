Scirpus multisetus

Scirpus multisetus, commonly known as the many-branched bulrush, is a tall, wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has hollow, ribbed stems that are often used in basket-making by indigenous peoples. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically growing in clusters at the base.

Scirpus multisetus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
