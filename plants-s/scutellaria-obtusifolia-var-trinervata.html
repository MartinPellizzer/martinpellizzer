Scutellaria obtusifolia var. trinervata

Scutellaria obtusifolia var. trinervata, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the tips of the stems. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
