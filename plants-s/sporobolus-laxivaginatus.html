Sporobolus laxivaginatus

Sporobolus laxivaginatus, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Sporobolus laxivaginatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
