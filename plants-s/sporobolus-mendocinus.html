Sporobolus mendocinus

Sporobolus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to California.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, needle-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

Sporobolus mendocinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
