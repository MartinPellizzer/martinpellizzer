Sida teysmannii

Sida teysmannii, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows up to 1 meter in height. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Sida teysmannii is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
