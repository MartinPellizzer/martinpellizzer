Symplocos coronata var. coronata

Symplocos coronata var. coronata, commonly known as the crown-leaf evergreen or mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered bitter.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
