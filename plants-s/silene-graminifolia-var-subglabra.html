Silene graminifolia var. subglabra

Silene graminifolia var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth grass pink, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The variety subglabra is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which set it apart from other varieties of Silene graminifolia. It typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, thriving in temperate climates.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
