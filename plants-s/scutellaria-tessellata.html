Scutellaria tessellata

Scutellaria tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. Its flowers are small, two-lipped, and often blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria tessellata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
