Symphonia globulifera var. major

Symphonia globulifera var. major, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and robust, deeply fissured bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is resistant to decay and used in construction and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
