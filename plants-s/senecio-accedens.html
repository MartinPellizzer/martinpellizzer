Senecio accedens

Senecio accedens, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Senecio accedens is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
