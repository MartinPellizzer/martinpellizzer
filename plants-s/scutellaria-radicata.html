Scutellaria radicata

Scutellaria radicata, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other mint relatives.
