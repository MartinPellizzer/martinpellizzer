Stachys fusca

Stachys fusca, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are often purple or pink in color. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Stachys fusca is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in woodland edges.
