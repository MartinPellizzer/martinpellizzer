Silene scabrida

Silene scabrida, commonly known as the rough rockpink, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rugged stems and pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene scabrida is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
