Senecio guadalupe

Senecio guadalupe, commonly known as Guadalupe groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio guadalupe is often found in canyons and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
