Stylocoryna costata

Stylocoryna costata, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow, and bloom during the rainy season.

Stylocoryna costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
