Salvia cabulica

Salvia cabulica, commonly known as Afghan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cabulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
