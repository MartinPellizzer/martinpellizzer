Solidago virgaurea var. redfieldii

Solidago virgaurea var. redfieldii, commonly known as the Redfield's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
