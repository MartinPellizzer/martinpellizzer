Sasa membranacea

Sasa membranacea, commonly known as the membranous sasa, is a clumping bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by triangular, woody fruits.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
