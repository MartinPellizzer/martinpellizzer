Syzygium confertiflorum

Syzygium confertiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Syzygium confertiflorum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
