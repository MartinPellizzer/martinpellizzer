Sideritis × saforensis

Sideritis × saforensis, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sideritis × saforensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
