Stenocalyx brunneus

Stenocalyx brunneus, commonly known as the brown-flowered hibiscus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Stenocalyx brunneus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
