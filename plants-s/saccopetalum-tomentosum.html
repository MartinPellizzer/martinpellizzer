Saccopetalum tomentosum

Saccopetalum tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in mangrove swamps and coastal dunes. Its structure includes woody stems and fleshy, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Saccopetalum tomentosum plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
