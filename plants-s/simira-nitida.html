Simira nitida

Simira nitida, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and its striking clusters of white, candle-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.
