Scleromitrion tenuifolium

Scleromitrion tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scleromitrion tenuifolium is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
