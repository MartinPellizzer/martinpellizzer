Solanum ipecachuana var. obovata

Solanum ipecachuana var. obovata, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, purple flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries, which are toxic, have been traditionally used in some indigenous cultures for medicinal purposes.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat.
