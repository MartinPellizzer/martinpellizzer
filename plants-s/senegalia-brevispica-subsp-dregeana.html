Senegalia brevispica subsp. dregeana

Senegalia brevispica subsp.

dregeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr shrub, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and spiny, thorned stems, which are adaptations to its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping in dry regions.
