Saxifraga aspera subsp. etrusca

Saxifraga aspera subsp.

etrusca, commonly known as the Etruscan saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained conditions.
