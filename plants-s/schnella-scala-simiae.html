Schnella scala-simiae

Schnella scala-simiae, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, feathery leaves. The plant produces panicles of tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.
