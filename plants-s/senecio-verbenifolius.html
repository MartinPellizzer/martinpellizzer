Senecio verbenifolius

Senecio verbenifolius, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio verbenifolius is often considered a weed but can provide nectar for pollinators.
