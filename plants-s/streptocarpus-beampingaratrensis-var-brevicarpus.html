Streptocarpus beampingaratrensis var. brevicarpus

Streptocarpus beampingaratrensis var. brevicarpus, commonly known as the dwarf streptocarpus, is a compact, flowering plant native to the humid lowland areas of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited for bright, indirect light and thrives in well-draining potting mix.
