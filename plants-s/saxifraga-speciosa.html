Saxifraga speciosa

Saxifraga speciosa, commonly known as the showy saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Saxifraga speciosa is a hardy perennial that is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
