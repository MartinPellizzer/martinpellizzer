Saxifraga incrustata

Saxifraga incrustata, commonly known as the rockfoil saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and features small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra habitats.

Saxifraga incrustata is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
