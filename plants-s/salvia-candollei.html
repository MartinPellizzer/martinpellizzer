Salvia candollei

Salvia candollei, commonly known as the candolle's sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
