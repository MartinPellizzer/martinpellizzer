Specklinia erucosa

Specklinia erucosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed polka dot plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or cream-colored spots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Specklinia erucosa is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
