Solanum copeyanum

Solanum copeyanum, commonly known as the Copeyana nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. It produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum copeyanum is often found in moist, shaded areas in the Andean region.
