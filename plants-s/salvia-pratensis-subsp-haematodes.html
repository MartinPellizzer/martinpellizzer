Salvia pratensis subsp. haematodes

Salvia pratensis subsp.

haematodes, commonly known as the blood sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is particularly noted for its striking color and its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
