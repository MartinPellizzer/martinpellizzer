Stigmaphyllon brassii

Stigmaphyllon brassii, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bahia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, branching structure. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stigmaphyllon brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
