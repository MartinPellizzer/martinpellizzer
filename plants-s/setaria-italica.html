Setaria italica

Setaria italica, commonly known as millet, is a small-grained cereal plant native to the warmer regions of the world.

It is an annual grass that thrives in dry and arid climates, making it a hardy crop for sustainable agriculture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of grains, which are harvested for food and animal feed. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and protein, and they are used in various culinary traditions around the globe.

Setaria italica is also valued for its adaptability and nutritional benefits, contributing to food security in many regions.
