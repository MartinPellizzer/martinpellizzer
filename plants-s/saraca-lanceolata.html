Saraca lanceolata

Saraca lanceolata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red floral clusters that appear in spring, giving it its common name. The plant has pinnate leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
