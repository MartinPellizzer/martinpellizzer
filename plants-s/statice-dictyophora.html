Statice dictyophora

Statice dictyophora, commonly known as the sea lavender or sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads composed of numerous small, densely packed florets. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Statice dictyophora is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
