Sorghum vulgare var. caffrorum

Sorghum vulgare var. caffrorum, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, making it suitable for cultivation in small spaces. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of pale green to yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
