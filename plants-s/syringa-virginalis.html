Syringa virginalis

Syringa virginalis, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Syringa virginalis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
