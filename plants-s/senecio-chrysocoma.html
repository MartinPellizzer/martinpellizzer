Senecio chrysocoma

Senecio chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Senecio chrysocoma is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
