Silene gallica var. suboccultata

Silene gallica var. suboccultata, commonly known as the lesser campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
