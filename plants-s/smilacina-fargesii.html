Smilacina fargesii

Smilacina fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' greenbrier, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of central and western China.

It belongs to the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Smilacina fargesii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate forests at higher elevations.
