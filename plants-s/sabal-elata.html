Sabal elata

Sabal elata, commonly known as the Florida cabbage palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant typically grows in wetlands and coastal areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, and it produces berries that are dispersed by birds.

Sabal elata plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
