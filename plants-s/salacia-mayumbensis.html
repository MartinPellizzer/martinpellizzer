Salacia mayumbensis

Salacia mayumbensis, commonly known as Mayumbensis Salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaciacae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are edible, though they are not widely cultivated or consumed by humans.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
