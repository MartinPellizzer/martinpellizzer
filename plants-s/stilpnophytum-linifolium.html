Stilpnophytum linifolium

Stilpnophytum linifolium, commonly known as the silky leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
