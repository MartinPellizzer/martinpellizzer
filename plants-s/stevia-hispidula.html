Stevia hispidula

Stevia hispidula, commonly known as the roughleaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, pale green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other stevia species used for sweetening. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia hispidula is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
