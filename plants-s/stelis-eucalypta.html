Stelis eucalypta

Stelis eucalypta, commonly known as the Eucalyptus Stelis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland or heathland environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Stelis eucalypta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
