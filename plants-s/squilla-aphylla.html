Squilla aphylla

Squilla aphylla, commonly known as the leafless squilla, is a unique member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only a short, fleshy stem and a single, showy flower that blooms in late spring or early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a striking, trumpet-shaped form.

Despite its simple appearance, Squilla aphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
