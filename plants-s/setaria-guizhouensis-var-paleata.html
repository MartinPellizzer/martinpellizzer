Setaria guizhouensis var. paleata

Setaria guizhouensis var. paleata, commonly known as the pale leaf foxtail, is a variant of the grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green blades and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
