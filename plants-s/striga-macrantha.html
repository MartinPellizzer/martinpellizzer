Striga macrantha

Striga macrantha, commonly known as the giant witchweed, is a parasitic plant belonging to the family Orobanchaceae.

It lacks true roots and instead attaches itself to the roots of host plants, drawing nutrients and water from them. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is considered a weed that can significantly reduce crop yields.

Due to its parasitic nature and rapid growth, it poses a challenge for agricultural management in affected areas.
