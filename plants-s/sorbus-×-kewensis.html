Sorbus × kewensis

Sorbus × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew rowan, is a hybrid species of the genus Sorbus.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is native to the United Kingdom and was first cultivated at Kew Gardens in the 19th century. It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers in spring and bright red berries in autumn.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.
