Schiedea attenuata

Schiedea attenuata, commonly known as the slender treefern, is a species of tree fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing both sexual and asexual spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, Schiedea attenuata is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
