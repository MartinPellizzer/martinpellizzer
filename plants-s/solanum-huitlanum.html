Solanum huitlanum

Solanum huitlanum, commonly known as the Huitlan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
