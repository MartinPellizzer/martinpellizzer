Saxifraga longifolia var. gaussenii

Saxifraga longifolia var. gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pinkish, and arranged in a raceme, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens.
