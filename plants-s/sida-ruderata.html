Sida ruderata

Sida ruderata, commonly known as rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed capsules as fruits.

Sida ruderata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
