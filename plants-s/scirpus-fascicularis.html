Scirpus fascicularis

Scirpus fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Scirpus fascicularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomes help in the filtration of water, making it valuable in wetland restoration projects.
