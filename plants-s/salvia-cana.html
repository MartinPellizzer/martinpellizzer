Salvia cana

Salvia cana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia cana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
