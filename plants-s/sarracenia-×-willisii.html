Sarracenia × willisii

Sarracenia × willisii, commonly known as the Willis's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sarraceniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange with a contrasting white or yellow rim, which helps attract prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × willisii is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and fascinating carnivorous behavior.
