Sterigmostemum laevicaule

Sterigmostemum laevicaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its slender, woody stems. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
