Solanum pallidum

Solanum pallidum, commonly known as the pale nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and produces small, yellow flowers followed by green, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
