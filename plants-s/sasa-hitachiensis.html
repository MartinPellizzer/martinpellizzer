Sasa hitachiensis

Sasa hitachiensis, commonly known as the Hitachi bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Sasa hitachiensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
