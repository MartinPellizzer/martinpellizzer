Struthiopteris australis

Struthiopteris australis, commonly known as the Southern Shield Fern, is a species of fern native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Struthiopteris australis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
