Sida sharpiana

Sida sharpiana, commonly known as the sharpleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, palmately lobed leaves that have a distinctive sharp appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are characteristic features of the genus Sida.

Sida sharpiana is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in moist, open habitats.
