Salacia arborescens

Salacia arborescens, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Salacia arborescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
