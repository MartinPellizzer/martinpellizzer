Salomonia subrotunda

Salomonia subrotunda, commonly known as the round-leaf salomonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Salomonia subrotunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
