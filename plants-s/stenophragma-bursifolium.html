Stenophragma bursifolium

Stenophragma bursifolium, commonly known as the burrleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have a rough, hairy texture and often bear small, spiny projections. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
