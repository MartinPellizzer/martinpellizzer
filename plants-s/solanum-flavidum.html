Solanum flavidum

Solanum flavidum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are yellow when mature, containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum flavidum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
