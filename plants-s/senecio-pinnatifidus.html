Senecio pinnatifidus

Senecio pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open vegetation types.
