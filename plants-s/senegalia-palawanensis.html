Senegalia palawanensis

Senegalia palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.

Additionally, its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
