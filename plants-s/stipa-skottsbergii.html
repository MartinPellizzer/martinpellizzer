Stipa skottsbergii

Stipa skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spiky inflorescences that are often adorned with fine, bristly hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa skottsbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
