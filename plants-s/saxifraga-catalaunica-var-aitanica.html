Saxifraga catalaunica var. aitanica

Saxifraga catalaunica var. aitanica, commonly known as the Aitanica saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
