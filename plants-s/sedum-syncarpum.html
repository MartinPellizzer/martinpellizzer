Sedum syncarpum

Sedum syncarpum, commonly known as the stonecrop or the liveforever plant, is a member of the Crassulaceae family.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or white, during the summer months. Native to regions of North America, it is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its ability to survive with minimal water makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
