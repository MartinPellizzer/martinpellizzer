Sautiera tinctorum

Sautiera tinctorum, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, red berries. Sautiera tinctorum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
