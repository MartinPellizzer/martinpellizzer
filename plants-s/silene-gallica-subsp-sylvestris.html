Silene gallica subsp. sylvestris

Silene gallica subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky habitats.

The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Silene gallica subsp.

sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
