Stipa pennata var. gallica

Stipa pennata var. gallica, commonly known as the Galician feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, silver-green inflorescences that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its light, airy look.
