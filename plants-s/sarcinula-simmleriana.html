Sarcinula simmleriana

Sarcinula simmleriana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant orchid flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
