Sedum incarum

Sedum incarum, commonly known as the "Coral Root Sedum," is a member of the Crassulaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent that typically reaches a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, fleshy, green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially under stress or in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals, and appear in late spring to early summer.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
