Sphaeradenia angustifolia

Sphaeradenia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star sphaera, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender, vine-like stems. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spathaceous bract and a central spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
