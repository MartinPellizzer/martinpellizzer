Spiranthes valida

Spiranthes valida, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white to pale pink flowers along a slender stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, green, ovate leaves.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and moths.
