Solanum variegatum

Solanum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, or green patterns. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces berries that change from green to red as they mature.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains alkaloids, making it potentially toxic if ingested.
