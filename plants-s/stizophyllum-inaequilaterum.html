Stizophyllum inaequilaterum

Stizophyllum inaequilaterum, commonly known as the unequal-sided stizophyllum, is a rare and specialized member of the stromatolite-forming cyanobacteria.

It exhibits unique morphological characteristics, including irregularly branching filaments and a lack of consistent lateral symmetry. This organism is typically found in shallow marine environments where it contributes to the formation of layered sedimentary structures. Its growth patterns are influenced by environmental factors such as light intensity and water movement.

The study of Stizophyllum inaequilaterum provides insights into ancient microbial ecosystems and their role in shaping early Earth's geology.
