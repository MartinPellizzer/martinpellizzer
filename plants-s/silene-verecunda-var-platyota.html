Silene verecunda var. platyota

Silene verecunda var. platyota, commonly known as the broad-leaved silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
