Smilax catalonica

Smilax catalonica, commonly known as the Catalonian smilax, is a perennial vine native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are dark red or black when mature and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax catalonica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
