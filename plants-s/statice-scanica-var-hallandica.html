Statice scanica var. hallandica

Statice scanica var. hallandica, commonly known as the Halland statice, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Scandinavia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salty air, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
