Schizolepis sylvestris

Schizolepis sylvestris, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Schizolepis sylvestris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
