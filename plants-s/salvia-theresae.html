Salvia theresae

Salvia theresae, commonly known as the Theresia sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia theresae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
