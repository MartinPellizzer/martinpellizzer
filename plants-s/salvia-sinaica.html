Salvia sinaica

Salvia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Salvia sinaica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
