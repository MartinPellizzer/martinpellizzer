Sodiroa sprucei

Sodiroa sprucei, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Sodiroa sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
