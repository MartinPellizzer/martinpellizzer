Syringa suaveolens

Syringa suaveolens, commonly known as sweetwood or Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
