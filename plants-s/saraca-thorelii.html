Saraca thorelii

Saraca thorelii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca thorelii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
