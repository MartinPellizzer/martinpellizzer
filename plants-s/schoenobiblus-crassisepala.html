Schoenobiblus crassisepala

Schoenobiblus crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with prominent veins, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
