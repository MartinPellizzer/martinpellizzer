Saxifraga × poluluteopurpurea

Saxifraga × poluluteopurpurea, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
