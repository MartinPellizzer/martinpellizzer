Schismatoglottis acutangula f. staminodiifera

Schismatoglottis acutangula f. staminodiifera, commonly known as the staminodiifera form of the acute-angled schismatoglottis, is a rare and unique variant of this orchid species.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, where the staminodes are elongated and filamentous, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a glossy surface and a prominent midrib.

This form is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unusual morphology and rarity in cultivation.
