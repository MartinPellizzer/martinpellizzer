Scilla peruviana var. minor

Scilla peruviana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
