Symplocos verticillata

Symplocos verticillata, commonly known as the clustered waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent and have a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Symplocos verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
