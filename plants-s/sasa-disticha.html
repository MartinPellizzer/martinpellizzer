Sasa disticha

Sasa disticha, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale blue, followed by edible, triangular-shaped berries. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Sasa disticha is often used in ornamental landscaping and has cultural significance in traditional Japanese gardens.
