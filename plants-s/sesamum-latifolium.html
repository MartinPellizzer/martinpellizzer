Sesamum latifolium

Sesamum latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
