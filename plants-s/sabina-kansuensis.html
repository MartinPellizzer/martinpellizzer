Sabina kansuensis

Sabina kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, and it produces small, round cones. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

The Kansu juniper is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
