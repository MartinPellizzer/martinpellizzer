Sisymbrium mandonii

Sisymbrium mandonii, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a low, sprawling growth habit.

Its seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry fruit that splits open to release the seeds, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
