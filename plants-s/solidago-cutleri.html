Solidago cutleri

Solidago cutleri, commonly known as Cutler's goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cutler's goldenrod plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
