Salvia tenorei

Salvia tenorei, commonly known as the Etna sage, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia tenorei thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
