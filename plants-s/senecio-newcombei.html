Senecio newcombei

Senecio newcombei, commonly known as the Newcomb's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
