Sorbus coronata var. ambrozyana

Sorbus coronata var. ambrozyana, commonly known as the Polish rowan or Ambrozy's rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. The tree produces bright red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant autumn foliage and striking fruit display.
