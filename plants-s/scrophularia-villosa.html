Scrophularia villosa

Scrophularia villosa, commonly known as hairy figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Scrophularia villosa is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
