Solanum laniflorum

Solanum laniflorum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are often mistaken for edible fruits. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
