Sphaeropteris gibbosa

Sphaeropteris gibbosa, commonly known as the knobbed cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and large, feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a rough, ridged trunk and pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Sphaeropteris gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
