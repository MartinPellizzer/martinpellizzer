Salix × sanguinea

Salix × sanguinea, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically lance-shaped with a serrated margin. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This plant thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.
