Spiraea chartacea

Spiraea chartacea, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly serrated. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Spiraea chartacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
