Spathodea indica

Spathodea indica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble umbels, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Spathodea indica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
