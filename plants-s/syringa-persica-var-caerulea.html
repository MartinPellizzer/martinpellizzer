Syringa persica var. caerulea

Syringa persica var. caerulea, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue or lilac in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The Persian lilac is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its showy spring blooms.
