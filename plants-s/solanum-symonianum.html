Solanum symonianum

Solanum symonianum, commonly known as the Symon's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
