Sempervivum macedonicum

Sempervivum macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky or stony habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the center of the rosette.

Sempervivum macedonicum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
