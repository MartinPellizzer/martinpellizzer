Saussurea maximowiczii f. triceps

Saussurea maximowiczii f. triceps, commonly known as the three-lobed safflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinct three-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its resilience in harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
