Shorea pubistyla

Shorea pubistyla, commonly known as the hairy sago palm, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored aril.

Shorea pubistyla is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
