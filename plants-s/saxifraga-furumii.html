Saxifraga furumii

Saxifraga furumii, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock gardens or woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to its native habitat and suitable for cultivation in similar conditions.
