Solanum pissisi

Solanum pissisi, commonly known as the Pissisi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its berries, which are toxic, are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Solanum pissisi is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in high-altitude environments.
