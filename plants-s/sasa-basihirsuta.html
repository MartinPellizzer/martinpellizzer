Sasa basihirsuta

Sasa basihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Native to East Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa basihirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardiness.
