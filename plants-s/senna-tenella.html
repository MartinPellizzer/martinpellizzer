Senna tenella

Senna tenella, commonly known as blue senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, green pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Senna tenella thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
