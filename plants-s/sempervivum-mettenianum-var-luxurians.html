Sempervivum mettenianum var. luxurians

Sempervivum mettenianum var. luxurians, commonly known as the "Cape Hottentot" or "Hottentot" succulent, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray or silver-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
