Sisymbrium salsuginosum

Sisymbrium salsuginosum, commonly known as hoary cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine, white hairs.

Sisymbrium salsuginosum is adapted to grow in saline and arid environments, making it a common species in coastal and disturbed soils.
