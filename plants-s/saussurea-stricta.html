Saussurea stricta

Saussurea stricta, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saussurea stricta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
