Senecio stella-purpurea

Senecio stella-purpurea, commonly known as Purple Starweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-blue hue that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio stella-purpurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
