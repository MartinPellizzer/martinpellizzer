Styrax hayataianus

Styrax hayataianus, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Styrax hayataianus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
