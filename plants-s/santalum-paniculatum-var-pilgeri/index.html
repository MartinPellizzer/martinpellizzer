Santalum Paniculatum Var. Pilgeri

Santalum paniculumat var. pilgeri is a rare and intriguing plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia. It belongs to the sandalwood family and is known for its unique ecological role as a hemiparasitic species. This variant is distinguished by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Despite its beauty, it remains poorly studied due to its limited distribution and elusive nature. Understanding this plant is crucial for conservation efforts in its fragile habitat.
