Sedum hemisphaericum

Sedum hemisphaericum, commonly known as the globe sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that form a tight, rounded cluster. The plant produces star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
