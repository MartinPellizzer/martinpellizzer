Spiraea arbuscula var. typica

Spiraea arbuscula var. typica, commonly known as the common snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has compound leaves with serrated edges. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
