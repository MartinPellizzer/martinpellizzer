Sorbus obtusidentata

Sorbus obtusidentata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
