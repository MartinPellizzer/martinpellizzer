Selaginella vieillardii

Selaginella vieillardii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Selaginella vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
