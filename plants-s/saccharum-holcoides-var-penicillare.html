Saccharum holcoides var. penicillare

Saccharum holcoides var. penicillare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or Penicillar grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and sustainable land use practices.
