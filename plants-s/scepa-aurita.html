Scepa aurita

Scepa aurita, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow or orange in color. The plant has upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Scepa aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
