Stellaria decumbens var. stracheyi

Stellaria decumbens var. stracheyi, commonly known as the creeping starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered in fine hairs. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, grassy areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
