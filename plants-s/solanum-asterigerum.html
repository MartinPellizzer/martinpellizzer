Solanum asterigerum

Solanum asterigerum, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that produces distinctive, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, white flowers. The fruit develops from the flower and turns yellow or orange when mature.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well.
