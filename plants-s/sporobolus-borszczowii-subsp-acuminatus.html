Sporobolus borszczowii subsp. acuminatus

Sporobolus borszczowii subsp.

acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture, adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a common trait among grasses in its habitat.

This subspecies is often found in sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
