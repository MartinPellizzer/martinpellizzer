Senecio concinnus

Senecio concinnus, commonly known as the silver leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio concinnus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
