Scilla capensis

Scilla capensis, commonly known as the Cape squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in gardens.

Scilla capensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
