Salvia oaxacana

Salvia oaxacana, commonly known as blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often found growing in rocky or well-drained soils. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
