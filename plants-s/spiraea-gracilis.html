Spiraea gracilis

Spiraea gracilis, commonly known as the slender spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a delicate texture to the plant's appearance.

Spiraea gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
