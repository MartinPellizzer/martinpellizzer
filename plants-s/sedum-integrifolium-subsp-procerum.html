Sedum integrifolium subsp. procerum

Sedum integrifolium subsp.

procerum, commonly known as the tall stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It features fleshy, upright stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height, often branching at the base.

The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette, with lance-shaped, smooth, and slightly fleshy foliage. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
