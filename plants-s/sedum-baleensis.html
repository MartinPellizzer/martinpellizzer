Sedum baleensis

Sedum baleensis, commonly known as the Balkan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually thick and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Sedum baleensis is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
