Spiraea × fontenaysii

Spiraea × fontenaysii, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is prized for its showy, cone-shaped clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves that provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
