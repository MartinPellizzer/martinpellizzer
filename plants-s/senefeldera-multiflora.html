Senefeldera multiflora

Senefeldera multiflora, commonly known as the Florida senefeldera, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands and in swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

Senefeldera multiflora is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to periodic flooding.
