Scirpus nigrescens

Scirpus nigrescens, commonly known as blackstem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along the shores of lakes and rivers. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often dark green to black in color, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered brown flowers that bloom in the summer.

Scirpus nigrescens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
