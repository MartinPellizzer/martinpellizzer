Uranthera variabilis var. parietariifolia

Uranthera variabilis var. parietariifolia, commonly known as the wall-ivy plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a potted plant.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive choice for xeriscaping.
