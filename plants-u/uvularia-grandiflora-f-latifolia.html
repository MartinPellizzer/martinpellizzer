Uvularia grandiflora f. latifolia

Uvularia grandiflora f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers. The foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. This plant thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a desirable species for both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
