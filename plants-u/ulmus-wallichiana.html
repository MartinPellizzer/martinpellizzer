Ulmus wallichiana

Ulmus wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent, including Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.
