Umbilicus pendulinus var. punctatus

Umbilicus pendulinus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or purple with a white or yellow spot at the base. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
