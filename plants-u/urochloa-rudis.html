Urochloa rudis

Urochloa rudis, commonly known as slender grass or African grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Urochloa rudis is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its rapid growth and hardiness.
