Unona viridiflora

Unona viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered unona, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a fibrous, elongated pod that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
