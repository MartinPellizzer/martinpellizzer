Uragoga pedunculosa

Uragoga pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga pedunculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
