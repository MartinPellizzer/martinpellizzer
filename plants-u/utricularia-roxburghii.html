Utricularia roxburghii

Utricularia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are often submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.
