Uncaria setiloba

Uncaria setiloba, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with parts of it used in traditional herbal remedies.
