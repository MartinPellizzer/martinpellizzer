Uterveria cynophallophora

Uterveria cynophallophora, commonly known as the giant false hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stems and robust growth habit make it a notable species in its ecological niche.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, sharing some traits with hibiscus plants.
