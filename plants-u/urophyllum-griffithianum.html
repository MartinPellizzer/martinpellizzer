Urophyllum griffithianum

Urophyllum griffithianum, commonly known as the Kashmir apple or Himalayan apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or orange fruit. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its fruit is edible, though often used more for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Urophyllum griffithianum is valued in horticulture for its seasonal beauty and ecological benefits.
