Ursinia subintegrifolia

Ursinia subintegrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a subintegrifoliate leaf arrangement.

Ursinia subintegrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
