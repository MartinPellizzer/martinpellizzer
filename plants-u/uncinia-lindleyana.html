Uncinia lindleyana

Uncinia lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's hookless orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it lacks the typical orchid pseudobulbs, instead having a rhizomatous growth habit.

Uncinia lindleyana is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
