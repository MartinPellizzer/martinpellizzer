Urtica pilulifera var. genuina

Urtica pilulifera var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and velvety foliage covered with fine stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a serrated margin, while the stems are hollow and covered with similar stinging hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
