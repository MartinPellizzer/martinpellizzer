Umbilicus linifolius

Umbilicus linifolius, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that form bladder-like structures used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. Native to wetlands and marshy areas, it thrives in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its diet with prey. The plant has a rhizome system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

Its flowers are typically small and yellow, with a distinctive bilabiate structure that aids in pollination.
