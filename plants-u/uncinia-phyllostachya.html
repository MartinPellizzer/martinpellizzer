Uncinia phyllostachya

Uncinia phyllostachya, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, silky, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
