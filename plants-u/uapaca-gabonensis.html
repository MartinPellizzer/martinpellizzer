Uapaca gabonensis

Uapaca gabonensis, commonly known as the African wild coffee, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its seeds are used to make coffee, though it is not as widely cultivated as other coffee species.
