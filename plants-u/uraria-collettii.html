Uraria collettii

Uraria collettii, commonly known as the Indian burr or wild sida, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Uraria collettii is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
