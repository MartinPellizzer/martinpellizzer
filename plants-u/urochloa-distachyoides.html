Urochloa distachyoides

Urochloa distachyoides, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Urochloa distachyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its dense growth habit.
