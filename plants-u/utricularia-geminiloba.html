Utricularia geminiloba

Utricularia geminiloba, commonly known as the twin-lobed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water, where it relies on its specialized structures for nutrition. Its leaves are usually filamentous and lack true roots, adapting to its aquatic environment.
