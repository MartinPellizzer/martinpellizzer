Utricularia diploglossa

Utricularia diploglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating bladders that function as trap mechanisms to capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its unique adaptations allow it to supplement its nutrient intake through prey capture, making it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
