Ulmus carpinifolia f. wredei

Ulmus carpinifolia f. wredei, commonly known as the Wrede's field elm, is a cultivar of the field elm, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is a deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to certain pests compared to other elm species.
