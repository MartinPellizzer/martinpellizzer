Uapaca densifolia var. humbertii

Uapaca densifolia var. humbertii, commonly known as the dense-leaved velvetwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, often grayish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
