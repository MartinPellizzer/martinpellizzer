Urostigma cotinifolium

Urostigma cotinifolium, commonly known as the red baneberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urostigma cotinifolium is often found in the understory of forests, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
