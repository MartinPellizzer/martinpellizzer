Urceolina korsakoffii

Urceolina korsakoffii, commonly known as the Korsakoff's urceolina, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive urceolate (bowl-shaped) inflorescence, which gives it its genus name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in the dappled light. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers, while small, are often brightly colored and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
