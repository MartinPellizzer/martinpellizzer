Ulmus glabra f. lutescens

Ulmus glabra f. lutescens, commonly known as the smooth field elm with yellow foliage, is a cultivar of the European elm.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
