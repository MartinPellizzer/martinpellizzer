Uapaca pilosa

Uapaca pilosa, commonly known as the rough uapaca, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, round berries. These berries are often consumed raw or used in traditional beverages.

Uapaca pilosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in local communities.
