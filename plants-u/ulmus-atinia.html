Ulmus atinia

Ulmus atinia, commonly known as the Atinia elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Syria, and Lebanon. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The Atinia elm produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits that disperse by wind.
