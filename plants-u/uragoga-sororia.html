Uragoga sororia

Uragoga sororia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Uragoga sororia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for various ailments.
