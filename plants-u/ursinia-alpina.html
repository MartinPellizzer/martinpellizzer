Ursinia alpina

Ursinia alpina, commonly known as the alpine ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Ursinia alpina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
