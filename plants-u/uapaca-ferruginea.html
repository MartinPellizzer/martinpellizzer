Uapaca ferruginea

Uapaca ferruginea, commonly known as the red-eyed mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red or yellow when mature. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or forest margins. It produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by the edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
