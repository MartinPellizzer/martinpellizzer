Urera lobulata

Urera lobulata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, violet-purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
