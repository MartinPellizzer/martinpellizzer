Uragoga pumila

Uragoga pumila, commonly known as the dwarf uragoga, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring dense, compact foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Uragoga pumila is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
