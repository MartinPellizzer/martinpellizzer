Unona laevigata

Unona laevigata, commonly known as the African wild cherry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that attract various wildlife.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
