Ulleria geminiflora

Ulleria geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered ulla, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ulleria geminiflora is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
