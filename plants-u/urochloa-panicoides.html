Urochloa panicoides

Urochloa panicoides, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Urochloa panicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
