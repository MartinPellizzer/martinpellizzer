Uncinia nepalensis

Uncinia nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

Uncinia nepalensis is often found in rocky or alpine environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of mountainous regions.
