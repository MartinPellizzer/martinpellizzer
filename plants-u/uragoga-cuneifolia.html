Uragoga cuneifolia

Uragoga cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
