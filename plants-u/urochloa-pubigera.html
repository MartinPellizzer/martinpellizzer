Urochloa pubigera

Urochloa pubigera, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, short hairs. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a high degree of adaptability. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Urochloa pubigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth habit.
