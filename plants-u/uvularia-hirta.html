Uvularia hirta

Uvularia hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Uvularia hirta is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
