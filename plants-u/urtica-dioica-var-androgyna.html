Urtica dioica var. androgyna

Urtica dioica var. androgyna, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the stems and leaves, which contain formic acid and other irritants. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Urtica dioica var. androgyna is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
