Uncinia silvestris

Uncinia silvestris, commonly known as the forest hookweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and are borne in clusters.

Uncinia silvestris is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in maintaining ecological balance in forest ecosystems.
