Urtica rumphii

Urtica rumphii, commonly known as the tropical stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its stems are hollow and covered with similar stinging trichomes, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica rumphii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
