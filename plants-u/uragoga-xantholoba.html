Uragoga xantholoba

Uragoga xantholoba, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Uragoga xantholoba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
