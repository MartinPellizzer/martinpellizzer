Urostigma aggregatum

Urostigma aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered bladdergrass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, cylindrical spikelets. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is typically found in disturbed soils and arid environments, thriving in regions with low moisture.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
