Uvifera obovata

Uvifera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate.

Uvifera obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
