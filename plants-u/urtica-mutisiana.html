Urtica mutisiana

Urtica mutisiana, commonly known as the Mutsi nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests.

Urtica mutisiana is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
