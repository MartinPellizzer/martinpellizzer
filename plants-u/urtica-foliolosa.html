Urtica foliolosa

Urtica foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
