Uncinia purpurata var. fuscovaginata

Uncinia purpurata var. fuscovaginata, commonly known as the purple tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, purple-tinged stems. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color, with a hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are arranged in spikelets.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.
