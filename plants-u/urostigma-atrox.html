Urostigma atrox

Urostigma atrox, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits, making it a dangerous plant.

The plant contains various alkaloids, including atropine and scopolamine, which have both medicinal and toxic properties.
