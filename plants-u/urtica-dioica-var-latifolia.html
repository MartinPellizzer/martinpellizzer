Urtica dioica var. latifolia

Urtica dioica var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, stinging hairs that cause a skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Broadleaf nettle is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
