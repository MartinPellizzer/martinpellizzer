Urania madagascariensis

Urania madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid forests of its native habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
