Uragoga corymbifera

Uragoga corymbifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bract-like flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

Uragoga corymbifera thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
