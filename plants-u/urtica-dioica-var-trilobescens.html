Urtica dioica var. trilobescens

Urtica dioica var. trilobescens, commonly known as the three-lobed nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features hollow, smooth stems that are covered in fine stinging hairs. Its flowers are small and green, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate plants.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
