Urochloa xantholeuca var. leucacrantha

Urochloa xantholeuca var. leucacrantha, commonly known as the white-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green. The inflorescence consists of panicles with small, white to pale yellow florets, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
