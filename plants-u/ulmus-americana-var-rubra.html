Ulmus americana var. rubra

Ulmus americana var. rubra, commonly known as the red elm, is a variant of the American elm characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge, and they turn a vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, pendulous clusters of samaras, which are its seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth rate.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and ornamental contexts.
