Uncaria pachyphylla

Uncaria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cat's claw, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, claw-like thorns that help it grip onto trees and other vegetation. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Uncaria pachyphylla is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, though further scientific research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
