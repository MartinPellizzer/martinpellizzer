Urtica urens f. iners

Urtica urens f. iners, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant contains toxic hairs called trichomes, which cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Urtica urens f. iners is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
