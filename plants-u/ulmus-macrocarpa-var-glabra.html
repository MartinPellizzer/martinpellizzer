Ulmus macrocarpa var. glabra

Ulmus macrocarpa var. glabra, commonly known as the American elm, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree has opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, spherical samaras that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its rapid growth and historical significance in urban landscapes.
