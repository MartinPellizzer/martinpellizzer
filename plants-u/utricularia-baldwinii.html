Utricularia baldwinii

Utricularia baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Florida and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small, aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. These traps are triggered by the movement of prey, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients in nutrient-poor water.

Utricularia baldwinii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling algae and microorganism populations.
