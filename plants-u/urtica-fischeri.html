Urtica fischeri

Urtica fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Urtica fischeri is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
