Ugni candollei

Ugni candollei, commonly known as the Andean hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its adaptability to high-altitude environments makes it a notable species in Andean ecosystems.

Ugni candollei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
