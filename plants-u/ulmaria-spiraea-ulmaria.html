Ulmaria spiraea-ulmaria

Ulmaria spiraea-ulmaria, commonly known as sweet william, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features wiry, branched stems covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Ulmaria spiraea-ulmaria is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
