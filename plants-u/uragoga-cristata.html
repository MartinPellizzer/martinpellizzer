Uragoga cristata

Uragoga cristata, commonly known as the crown fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright fronds that form a crown-like appearance, giving it its common name. Native to tropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Uragoga cristata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
