Urtica praetermissa subsp. praetermissa

Urtica praetermissa subsp.

praetermissa, commonly known as the lesser nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are wind-pollinated.
