Urochloa kurzii

Urochloa kurzii, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Urochloa kurzii has a clump-forming growth habit with slender, erect stems that produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its robust root system.
