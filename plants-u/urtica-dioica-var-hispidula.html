Urtica dioica var. hispidula

Urtica dioica var. hispidula, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, green stems covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Urtica dioica var. hispidula is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
