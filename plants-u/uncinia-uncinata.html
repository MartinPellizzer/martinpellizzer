Uncinia uncinata

Uncinia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often branched and hairy.

Uncinia uncinata is known for its resilience in harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine zones.
