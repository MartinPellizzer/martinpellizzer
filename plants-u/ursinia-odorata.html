Ursinia odorata

Ursinia odorata, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom. Ursinia odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
