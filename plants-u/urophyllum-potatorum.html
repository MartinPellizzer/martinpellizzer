Urophyllum potatorum

Urophyllum potatorum, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are typically broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Urophyllum potatorum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
