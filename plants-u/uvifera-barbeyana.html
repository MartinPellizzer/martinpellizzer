Uvifera barbeyana

Uvifera barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's fig tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fruits resembling figs.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
