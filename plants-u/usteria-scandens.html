Usteria scandens

Usteria scandens, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and sandy environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by distinctive bladder-like fruits. Its leaves are typically thin and linear, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Usteria scandens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
