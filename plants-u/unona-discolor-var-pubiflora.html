Unona discolor var. pubiflora

Unona discolor var. pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered unona, is a variant of the unona discolor species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) floral bracts, which give it its common name. The plant typically features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and attractive floral display.
