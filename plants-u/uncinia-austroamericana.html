Uncinia austroamericana

Uncinia austroamericana, commonly known as the Southern Hooked Cephalum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in a radiate pattern.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
