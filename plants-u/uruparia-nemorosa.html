Uruparia nemorosa

Uruparia nemorosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

Uruparia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
