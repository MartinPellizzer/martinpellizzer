Urostigma involucratum

Urostigma involucratum, commonly known as the clustered bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
