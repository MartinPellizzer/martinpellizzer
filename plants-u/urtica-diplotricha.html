Urtica diplotricha

Urtica diplotricha, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing skin irritation upon contact. Urtica diplotricha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein and minerals.
