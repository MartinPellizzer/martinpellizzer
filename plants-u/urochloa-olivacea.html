Urochloa olivacea

Urochloa olivacea, commonly known as slender grass or olive grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow, elongated, and typically have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Urochloa olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
