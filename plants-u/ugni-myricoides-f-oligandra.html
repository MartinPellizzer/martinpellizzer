Ugni myricoides f. oligandra

Ugni myricoides f. oligandra, commonly known as the oligandra form of the myrtle plant, is a variant of the myrtle species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and floral display.
