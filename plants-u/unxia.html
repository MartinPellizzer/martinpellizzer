Unxia

Unxia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Unxia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
