Urtica pilulifera

Urtica pilulifera, commonly known as the stinging nettle or false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered with fine stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. Urtica pilulifera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

It has various traditional uses in medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
