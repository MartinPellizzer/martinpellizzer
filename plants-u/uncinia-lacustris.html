Uncinia lacustris

Uncinia lacustris, commonly known as the lake hookweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and lake shores. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes and produce small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating or submerged depending on water levels.

Uncinia lacustris plays a role in stabilizing shoreline soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
