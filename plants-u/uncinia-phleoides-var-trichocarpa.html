Uncinia phleoides var. trichocarpa

Uncinia phleoides var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy false foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
