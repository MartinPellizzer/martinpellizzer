Urelytrum henrardii

Urelytrum henrardii, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in wet, sandy soils near water bodies and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Urelytrum henrardii is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
