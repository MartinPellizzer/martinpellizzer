Uragoga biattenuata

Uragoga biattenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms for nutrition. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water bodies where it can effectively capture prey. Its leaves are thin and elongated, adapted for maximizing surface area to aid in trapping prey.

Uragoga biattenuata is known for its unique ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its diet with captured insects.
