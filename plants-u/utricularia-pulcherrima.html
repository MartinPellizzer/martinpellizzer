Utricularia pulcherrima

Utricularia pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Chinese waterwheel," is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, floating leaves that resemble a waterwheel, with tiny bladders used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, relying on its specialized structures for nutrition. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters.

Utricularia pulcherrima is popular among aquatic plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
