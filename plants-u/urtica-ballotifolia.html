Urtica ballotifolia

Urtica ballotifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle of the genus Urtica, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in humans. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Urtica ballotifolia is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
