Uapaca pilosa f. hirsuta

Uapaca pilosa f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy uapaca, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stout, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.

The hairy uapaca is often found in moist, forested areas and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.
