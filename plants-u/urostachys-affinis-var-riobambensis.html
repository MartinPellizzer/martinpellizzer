Urostachys affinis var. riobambensis

Urostachys affinis var. riobambensis, commonly known as the Río Bambé bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates, making it suitable for temperate regions.
