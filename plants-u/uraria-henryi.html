Uraria henryi

Uraria henryi, commonly known as Henry's uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Uraria henryi is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in soil conservation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
