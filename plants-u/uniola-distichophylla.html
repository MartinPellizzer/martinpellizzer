Uniola distichophylla

Uniola distichophylla, commonly known as coastal grass or sea grass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils along dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Its salt tolerance makes it an essential component of coastal ecosystems.
