Uvaria oxyantha

Uvaria oxyantha, commonly known as the fragrant uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Uvaria oxyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
