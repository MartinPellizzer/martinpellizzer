Urtica petiolaris

Urtica petiolaris, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and hollow, hair-covered stems that contain irritating chemicals. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica petiolaris is known for its defensive mechanisms, which include the release of histamine and formic acid when touched.
