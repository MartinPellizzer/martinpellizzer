Ulex parviflorus subsp. airensis

Ulex parviflorus subsp.

airensis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and grayish-green, while its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
