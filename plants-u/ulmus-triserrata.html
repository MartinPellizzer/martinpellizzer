Ulmus triserrata

Ulmus triserrata, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the elm family, Ulmaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with three distinct serrations. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to urban environmental conditions.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
