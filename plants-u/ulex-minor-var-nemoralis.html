Ulex minor var. nemoralis

Ulex minor var. nemoralis, commonly known as the common furze, is a shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enriching the soil.

This species is often found in open habitats like woodlands and heaths.
