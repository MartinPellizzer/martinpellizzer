Ulticona biflora

Ulticona biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in cloud forests or rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ulticona biflora is considered a conservation concern.
