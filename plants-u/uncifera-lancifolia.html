Uncifera lancifolia

Uncifera lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in moist, temperate environments, such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its fibrous stalks have historically been used for textile production.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae, which includes other flax species.
