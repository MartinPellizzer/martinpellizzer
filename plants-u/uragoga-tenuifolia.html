Uragoga tenuifolia

Uragoga tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks. Its botanical features make it an attractive choice for ornamental gardening, though it is not widely cultivated.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
