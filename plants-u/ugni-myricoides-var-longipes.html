Ugni myricoides var. longipes

Ugni myricoides var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
