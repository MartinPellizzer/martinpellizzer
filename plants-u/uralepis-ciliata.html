Uralepis ciliata

Uralepis ciliata, commonly known as the silky uralepis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and are clustered in dense, raceme-like inflorescences.

Uralepis ciliata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
