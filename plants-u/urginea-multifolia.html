Urginea multifolia

Urginea multifolia, commonly known as the purple star lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a large, fleshy bulb and produces multiple narrow, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant is renowned for its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet or purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to ensure successful blooming.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
