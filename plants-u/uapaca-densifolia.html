Uapaca densifolia

Uapaca densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved kola nut tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellowish-green clusters. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. Uapaca densifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appearance.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
