Urostigma subglaucinum

Urostigma subglaucinum, commonly known as the bluebottle plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive tubular corolla. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
