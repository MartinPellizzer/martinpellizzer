Uncifera albiflora

Uncifera albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Uncifera albiflora is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various soil types.
