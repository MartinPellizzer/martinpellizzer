Uragoga myriantha

Uragoga myriantha, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and are adorned with prominent stamens. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Uragoga myriantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
