Uragoga zeylanica

Uragoga zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its bark, rich in essential oils, is harvested and dried to make the spice cinnamon.

Uragoga zeylanica thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal value.
