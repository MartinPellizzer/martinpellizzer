Urandra apicalis

Urandra apicalis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Urandra apicalis thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
