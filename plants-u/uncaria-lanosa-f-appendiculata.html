Uncaria lanosa f. appendiculata

Uncaria lanosa f. appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy climbing shrub, is a variant of the evergreen climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
