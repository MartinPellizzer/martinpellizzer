Uncasia salvia

Uncasia salvia, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Uncasia salvia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
