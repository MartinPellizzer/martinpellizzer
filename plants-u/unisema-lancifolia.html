Unisema lancifolia

Unisema lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved unisema, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Unisema lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
