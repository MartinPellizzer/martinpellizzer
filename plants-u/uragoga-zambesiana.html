Uragoga zambesiana

Uragoga zambesiana, commonly known as the Zambesi river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central disk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies. It is often found in riverine forests and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Uragoga zambesiana plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
