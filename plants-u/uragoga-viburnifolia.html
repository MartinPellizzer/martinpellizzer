Uragoga viburnifolia

Uragoga viburnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf viburnum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that attract various birds and small mammals.

Uragoga viburnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
