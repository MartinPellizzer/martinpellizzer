Uromyrtus baumanii

Uromyrtus baumanii, commonly known as the Bauman's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its waxy leaf coating. It produces woody pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Uromyrtus baumanii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
