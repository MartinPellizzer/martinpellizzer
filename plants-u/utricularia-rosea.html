Utricularia rosea

Utricularia rosea, commonly known as the pink bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and temperate regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Utricularia rosea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor water conditions.
