Utricularia gilletii

Utricularia gilletii, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating, aquatic growth habit and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant's leaves are small and often submerged, while its stems can reach lengths of several meters. Utricularia gilletii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
