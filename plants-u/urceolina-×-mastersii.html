Urceolina × mastersii

Urceolina × mastersii, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Urceolina grandiflora and Urceolina mastersii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal and wetland habitats. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, well-drained soils.
