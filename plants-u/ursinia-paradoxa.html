Ursinia paradoxa

Ursinia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxa bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ursinia paradoxa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
