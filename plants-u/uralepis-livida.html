Uralepis livida

Uralepis livida, commonly known as the bluegum or Australian bluegum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, grey-green bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Uralepis livida is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
