Ulmus wilsoniana var. psilophylla

Ulmus wilsoniana var. psilophylla, commonly known as the Chinese cliff elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or cliff-like environments, which influences its growth habit and leaf structure. Its leaves are usually a deep green on the upper surface and lighter green on the underside.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in challenging soil conditions.
