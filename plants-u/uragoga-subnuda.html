Uragoga subnuda

Uragoga subnuda, commonly known as the naked uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stem and the absence of leaves along its lower portion, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its botanical structure is adapted for survival in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and limited distribution.
