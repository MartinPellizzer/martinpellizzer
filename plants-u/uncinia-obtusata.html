Uncinia obtusata

Uncinia obtusata, commonly known as the blunt hook cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to survive in dry conditions.

The species is also notable for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in rocky soils.
