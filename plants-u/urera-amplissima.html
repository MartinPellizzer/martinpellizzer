Urera amplissima

Urera amplissima, commonly known as the white cedar or ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive buttressed roots, which help it thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
