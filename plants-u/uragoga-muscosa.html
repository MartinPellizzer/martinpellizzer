Uragoga muscosa

Uragoga muscosa, commonly known as the mossy uragoga, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. The plant's foliage consists of small, overlapping leaves that form a thick, velvety layer. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are typically embedded within the mossy mat, making them less visible.

Uragoga muscosa plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitat.
