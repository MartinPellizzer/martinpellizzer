Ulex canescens var. sparsiflorus

Ulex canescens var. sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparsiflorous goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native range.
