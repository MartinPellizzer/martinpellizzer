Urochloa ophryodes

Urochloa ophryodes, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Urochloa ophryodes is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
