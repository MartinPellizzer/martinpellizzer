Uromyrtus nekouana

Uromyrtus nekouana, commonly known as the Nekoua myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, appear in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
