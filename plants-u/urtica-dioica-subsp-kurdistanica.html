Urtica dioica subsp. kurdistanica

Urtica dioica subsp.

kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and can reach up to 15 cm in length.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, making it dioecious.
