Uvaria fulva

Uvaria fulva, commonly known as the golden uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Uvaria fulva thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
