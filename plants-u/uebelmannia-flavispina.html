Uebelmannia flavispina

Uebelmannia flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its adaptations include succulent stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Uebelmannia flavispina is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
