Urtica lutescens

Urtica lutescens, commonly known as the yellow stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains irritant hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Urtica lutescens is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
