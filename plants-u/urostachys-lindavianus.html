Urostachys lindavianus

Urostachys lindavianus, commonly known as the Lindau's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Urostachys lindavianus plays a minor but important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
