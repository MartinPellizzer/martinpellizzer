Uragoga angulata

Uragoga angulata, commonly known as the angular uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits.

Uragoga angulata is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
