Uraria obcordata

Uraria obcordata, commonly known as the Indian goatherb, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped at the base, and its erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in wastelands, roadsides, and degraded soils.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
