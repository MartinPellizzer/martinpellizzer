Ursinia anthemoides subsp. versicolor

Ursinia anthemoides subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Blazing Star, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The subspecies is notable for its variability in flower color, which contributes to its common name.
