Ulmus parvifolia f. pendens

Ulmus parvifolia f. pendens, commonly known as the weeping Chinese elm, is a unique variant of the Chinese elm tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to urban environments.
