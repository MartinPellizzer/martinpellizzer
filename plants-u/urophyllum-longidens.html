Urophyllum longidens

Urophyllum longidens, commonly known as the long-spined uruphyllum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Urophyllum longidens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
