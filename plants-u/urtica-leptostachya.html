Urtica leptostachya

Urtica leptostachya, commonly known as the slender stachy sarsaparilla, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Urtica leptostachya thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
