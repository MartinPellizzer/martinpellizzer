Uvifera acrostichoides

Uvifera acrostichoides, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Uvifera acrostichoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
