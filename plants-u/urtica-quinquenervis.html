Urtica quinquenervis

Urtica quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and hollow, smooth stems.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant has five prominent veins on its leaves, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin.

Urtica quinquenervis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
