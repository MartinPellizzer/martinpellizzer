Unifolium dilatatum

Unifolium dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
