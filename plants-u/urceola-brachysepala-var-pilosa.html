Urceola brachysepala var. pilosa

Urceola brachysepala var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy urn plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its urn-shaped flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
