Ulex vernalis

Ulex vernalis, commonly known as the spring goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and grassy areas.

Ulex vernalis is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
