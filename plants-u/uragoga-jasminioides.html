Uragoga jasminioides

Uragoga jasminioides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
