Unjala bifida

Unjala bifida, commonly known as the split-leaved unjala, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) leaves, which are often divided into two narrow lobes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Unjala bifida plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native fauna.
