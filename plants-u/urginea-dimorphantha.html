Urginea dimorphantha

Urginea dimorphantha, commonly known as the giant squill, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, fleshy bulbs that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Urginea dimorphantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
