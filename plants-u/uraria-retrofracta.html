Uraria retrofracta

Uraria retrofracta, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
