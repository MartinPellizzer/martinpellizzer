Urceodiscus carrii

Urceodiscus carrii, commonly known as the Texas milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Urceodiscus carrii plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
