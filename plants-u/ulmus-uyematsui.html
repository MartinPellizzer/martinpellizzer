Ulmus uyematsui

Ulmus uyematsui, commonly known as the Japanese elm, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the elm family, Ulmaceae, and is characterized by its slender, elongated shape and delicate, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It prefers well-drained soils and partial shade, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
