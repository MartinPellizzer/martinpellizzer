Unona xylopioides

Unona xylopioides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and economically significant species.
