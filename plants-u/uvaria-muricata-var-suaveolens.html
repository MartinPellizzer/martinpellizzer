Uvaria muricata var. suaveolens

Uvaria muricata var. suaveolens, commonly known as the fragrant star apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature, with a soft, juicy pulp containing black seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
