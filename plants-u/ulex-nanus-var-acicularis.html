Ulex nanus var. acicularis

Ulex nanus var. acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia, characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, barbed fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native range.
