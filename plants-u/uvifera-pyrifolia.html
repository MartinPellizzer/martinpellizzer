Uvifera pyrifolia

Uvifera pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically achenes surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp, and it ripens in the fall.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has been cultivated for centuries in China, Japan, and Korea.
