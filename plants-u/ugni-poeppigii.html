Ugni poeppigii

Ugni poeppigii, commonly known as the Andean huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color, which are consumed by various wildlife species. Ugni poeppigii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.
