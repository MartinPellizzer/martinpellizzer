Uragoga grandifolia

Uragoga grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved uragoga, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Uragoga grandifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
