Uragoga coptosperma

Uragoga coptosperma, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and agricultural fields.

Uragoga coptosperma also has potential agricultural and medicinal applications due to its nutritional value and adaptability.
