Urvillea ulmacea

Urvillea ulmacea, commonly known as the African elder or African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Urvillea ulmacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
