Ugni myrtus

Ugni myrtus, commonly known as the Chilean guava, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Ugni myrtus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
