Uapaca nitida var. rufopilosa

Uapaca nitida var. rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired uapaca, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often covered with a fine, reddish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
