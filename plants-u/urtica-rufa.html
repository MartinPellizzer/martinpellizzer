Urtica rufa

Urtica rufa, commonly known as red nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and stems, which contain irritant chemicals such as histamine and formic acid. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it reproduces via wind-pollinated flowers.

Urtica rufa is part of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles.
