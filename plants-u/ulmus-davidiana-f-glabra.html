Ulmus davidiana f. glabra

Ulmus davidiana f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved elms, is a cultivar of the Chinese elm.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and slender, weeping branches that create an elegant, cascading appearance. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

However, it is susceptible to certain pests and diseases, which can affect its longevity and health.
