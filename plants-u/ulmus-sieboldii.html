Ulmus sieboldii

Ulmus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Ulmus sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in urban landscaping.
