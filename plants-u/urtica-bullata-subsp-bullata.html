Urtica bullata subsp. bullata

Urtica bullata subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hollow, green stems covered in fine, stinging hairs called trichomes.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated edge. Urtica bullata subsp. bullata is a perennial species that thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and animals.
