Uruparia lobbii

Uruparia lobbii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Uruparia lobbii typically grows from a tuberous root system, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
