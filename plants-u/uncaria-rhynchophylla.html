Uncaria rhynchophylla

Uncaria rhynchophylla, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems with hooked thorns, which aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Uncaria rhynchophylla is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
