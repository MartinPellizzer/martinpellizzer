Uragoga librevillensis

Uragoga librevillensis, commonly known as the Congo lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers, which are small and clustered, are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Uragoga librevillensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
