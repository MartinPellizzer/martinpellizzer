Urophyllum lanaense

Urophyllum lanaense, commonly known as the Lanaese sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its unique habitat and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
