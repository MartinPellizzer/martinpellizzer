Urostachys venezuelanicus

Urostachys venezuelanicus, commonly known as the Venezuelan feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with delicate, bristle-like structures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
