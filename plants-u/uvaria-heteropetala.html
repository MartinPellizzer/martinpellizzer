Uvaria heteropetala

Uvaria heteropetala, commonly known as the variable-leaf sand-pear, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that vary in shape and size, often with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Uvaria heteropetala is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
