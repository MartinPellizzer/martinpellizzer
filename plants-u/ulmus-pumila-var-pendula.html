Ulmus pumila var. pendula

Ulmus pumila var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The tree has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It is relatively fast-growing and tolerant of various soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.

However, it is susceptible to certain pests and diseases, which can affect its longevity.
