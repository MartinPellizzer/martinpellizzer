Urtica helanshanica

Urtica helanshanica, commonly known as the Helan Mountain nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months.

Urtica helanshanica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
