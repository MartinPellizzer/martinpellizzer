Ulmus macrocarpa var. suberosa

Ulmus macrocarpa var. suberosa, commonly known as the American white elm, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that mature to a dark brown color. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and resilience to urban environments.
