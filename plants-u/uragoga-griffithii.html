Uragoga griffithii

Uragoga griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates.

Uragoga griffithii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
