Uvifera peruviana

Uvifera peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian grapefruit or Peruvian orange.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Peru. The plant is a small shrub that produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruits with a thick rind and juicy pulp. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
