Uvularia amplexicaulis

Uvularia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bellvista, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring, typically from May to June. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
