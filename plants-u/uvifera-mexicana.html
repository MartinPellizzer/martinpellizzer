Uvifera mexicana

Uvifera mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican grapefruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly aromatic scent.

Uvifera mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits.
