Urtica membranacea proles lusitanica

Urtica membranacea proles lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. It has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
