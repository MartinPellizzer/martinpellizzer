Ungernia

Ungernia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
