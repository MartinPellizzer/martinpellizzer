Unona dumosa

Unona dumosa, commonly known as the woolly dwarf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Unona dumosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
