Urelytrum annuum

Urelytrum annuum, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant is a vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It produces seeds in spiral-shaped pods, which are dispersed by wind.

Urelytrum annuum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been naturalized in many regions around the world.
