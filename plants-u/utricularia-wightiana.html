Utricularia wightiana

Utricularia wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant features delicate, floating leaves with tiny bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. It has a short flowering period, producing small, pale yellow flowers.

Utricularia wightiana is noted for its unique adaptations to nutrient-poor environments, relying on its carnivorous behavior for sustenance.
