Uraria acaulis

Uraria acaulis, commonly known as the barren uraria, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, linear leaves arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Uraria acaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
