Uragoga laevis

Uragoga laevis, commonly known as the smooth uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Uragoga laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
