Urochloa marathensis var. velutina

Urochloa marathensis var. velutina, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and erosion control.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional landscaping purposes.
