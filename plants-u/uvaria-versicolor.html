Uvaria versicolor

Uvaria versicolor, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Uvaria versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
