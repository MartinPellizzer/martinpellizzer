Uvaria hispidocostata

Uvaria hispidocostata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. Uvaria hispidocostata is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Anacardiaceae family.
