Urtica incana

Urtica incana, commonly known as stiff stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that cause a painful rash upon contact with the skin. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Urtica incana is known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
