Uragoga callophylla

Uragoga callophylla, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The tree has a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical with a smooth surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
