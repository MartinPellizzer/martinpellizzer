Ursinia coronopifolia

Ursinia coronopifolia, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Ursinia coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
