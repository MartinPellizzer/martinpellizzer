Uragoga matogrossensis

Uragoga matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Brazil.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant produces showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Uragoga matogrossensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
