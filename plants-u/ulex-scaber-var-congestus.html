Ulex scaber var. congestus

Ulex scaber var. congestus, commonly known as the bracken or Scotch broom, is a shrub native to western Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or coastal areas.

Ulex scaber var. congestus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
