Urachne leucosperma

Urachne leucosperma, commonly known as the white-flowered urachne, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
