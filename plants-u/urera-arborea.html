Urera arborea

Urera arborea, commonly known as the red cedar or white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in traditional construction and crafts.

The plant also has medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their healing effects.
