Uvaria macrophylla var. glabrior

Uvaria macrophylla var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leafed uvaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are often followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a desirable species for ornamental horticulture.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
