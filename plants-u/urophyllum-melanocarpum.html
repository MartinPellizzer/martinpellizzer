Urophyllum melanocarpum

Urophyllum melanocarpum, commonly known as the black fruit tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, dark-colored fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The botanical features of Urophyllum melanocarpum make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
