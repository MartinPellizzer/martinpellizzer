Umbilicus ramosissimus

Umbilicus ramosissimus, commonly known as the many-branched bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which it uses to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces tiny, green, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. It is adapted to nutrient-poor water environments by supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique morphology and predatory behavior make it an interesting subject in the study of plant adaptations.
