Uvifera sagotii

Uvifera sagotii, commonly known as the Desert Starfig, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves and small, fleshy fruits that resemble figs, hence its common name. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and has developed adaptations such as thick, waxy leaves to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, and it produces fruit only after several years of growth.

Uvifera sagotii is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological resilience.
