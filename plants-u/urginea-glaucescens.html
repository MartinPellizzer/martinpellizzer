Urginea glaucescens

Urginea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue squill, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a large, white or pale blue flower with prominent violet veins, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy bulb that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Urginea glaucescens is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
