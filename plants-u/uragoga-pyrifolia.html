Uragoga pyrifolia

Uragoga pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that turns from green to yellow or red when mature. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, aromatic fruit. The fruit is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a preference for well-drained soils in temperate climates.
