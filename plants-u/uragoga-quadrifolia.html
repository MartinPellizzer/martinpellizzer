Uragoga quadrifolia

Uragoga quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf clover plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that often have four lobes, giving it the common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Uragoga quadrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
