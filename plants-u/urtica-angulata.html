Urtica angulata

Urtica angulata, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs that release irritating chemicals upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Urtica angulata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
