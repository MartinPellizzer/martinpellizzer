Urostachys comans

Urostachys comans, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its resilience to cold temperatures and drought conditions.
