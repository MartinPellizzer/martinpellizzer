Ugni friedrichsthalii var. longipes

Ugni friedrichsthalii var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed huito, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas, and thrives in cooler, humid climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive fragrant scent, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that is edible but not widely consumed.
