Urostachys tardieuae

Urostachys tardieuae, commonly known as the late-flowering star grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Urostachys tardieuae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
