Uniola muelleri

Uniola muelleri, commonly known as mueller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
