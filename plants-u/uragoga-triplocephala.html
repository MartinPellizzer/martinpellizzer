Uragoga triplocephala

Uragoga triplocephala, commonly known as the three-headed uragoga, is a rare and unique plant species native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-headed appearance, which is formed by the fusion of multiple stems at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and its leaves are broad and glossy. Uragoga triplocephala thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
