Urochloa notochthona

Urochloa notochthona, commonly known as the African grass or false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny florets.

Urochloa notochthona is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
