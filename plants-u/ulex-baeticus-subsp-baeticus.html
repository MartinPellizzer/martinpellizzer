Ulex baeticus subsp. baeticus

Ulex baeticus subsp.

baeticus, commonly known as the Baetican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
