Unona ossea

Unona ossea, commonly known as the bone fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the structure of bones.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's rhizome is thick and woody, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Unona ossea plays a significant role in its ecosystem by supporting various microorganisms and serving as a habitat for small invertebrates.
