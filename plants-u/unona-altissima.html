Unona altissima

Unona altissima, commonly known as the tall beauty tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
