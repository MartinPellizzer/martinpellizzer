Ulex europaeus var. parvebracteatus

Ulex europaeus var. parvebracteatus, commonly known as the lesser sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has spiny stems and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.
