Uragoga trichostyla

Uragoga trichostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close in the heat of the day. Its roots are anchored in the muddy bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Uragoga trichostyla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
