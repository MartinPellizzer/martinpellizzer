Uvaria globosa

Uvaria globosa, commonly known as the globe uvaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, often with a sweet, honey-like scent. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
