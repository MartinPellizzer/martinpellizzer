Uvaria nigrescens

Uvaria nigrescens, commonly known as black uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that mature in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for various wildlife species.
