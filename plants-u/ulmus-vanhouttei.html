Ulmus vanhouttei

Ulmus vanhouttei, commonly known as the Chinese elms, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to urban conditions.

Ulmus vanhouttei is widely cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
