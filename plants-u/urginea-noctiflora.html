Urginea noctiflora

Urginea noctiflora, commonly known as the night-flowering star of Bethlehem, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
