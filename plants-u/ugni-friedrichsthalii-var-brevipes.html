Ugni friedrichsthalii var. brevipes

Ugni friedrichsthalii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
