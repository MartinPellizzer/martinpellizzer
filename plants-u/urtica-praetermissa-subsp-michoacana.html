Urtica praetermissa subsp. michoacana

Urtica praetermissa subsp.

michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan nettle, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico. It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the family.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
