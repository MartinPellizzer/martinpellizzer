Ulmus foliacea var. pendula

Ulmus foliacea var. pendula, commonly known as weeping elm, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches that drape like a waterfall.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Turkey and Georgia. The tree features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil, making it a popular choice for parks and gardens.

However, it is susceptible to elm bark beetle infestations, which can pose a threat to its longevity.
