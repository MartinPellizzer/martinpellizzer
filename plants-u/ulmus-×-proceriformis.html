Ulmus × proceriformis

Ulmus × proceriformis, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It is a natural hybrid between the white willow (Ulmus alata) and the weeping willow (Ulmus europaea 'Pendula'). The tree's most notable botanical feature is its long, drooping branches that create a flowing, elegant appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
