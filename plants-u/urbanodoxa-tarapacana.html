Urbanodoxa tarapacana

Urbanodoxa tarapacana, commonly known as the "Candytuft" or "Tamarugo," is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a tuft of candy. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, saline soils and extreme temperatures. It is well adapted to urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both ornamental and ecological restoration projects.
