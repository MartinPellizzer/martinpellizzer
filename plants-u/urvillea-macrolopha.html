Urvillea macrolopha

Urvillea macrolopha, commonly known as the giant urvillea, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the family Malvaceae.
