Uragoga elongata

Uragoga elongata, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in tropical regions.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, branching stems and delicate, scale-like leaves that aid in its photosynthetic processes. Uragoga elongata thrives in still or slow-moving water, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its unique adaptations allow it to supplement its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.
