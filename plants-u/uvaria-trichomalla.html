Uvaria trichomalla

Uvaria trichomalla, commonly known as the hairy uvaria, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when mature. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
