Upata

Upata, commonly known as the "wild bean" or "bush bean," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are usually small and pale yellow or green.
