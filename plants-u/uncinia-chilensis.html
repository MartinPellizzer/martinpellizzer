Uncinia chilensis

Uncinia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Uncinia chilensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
