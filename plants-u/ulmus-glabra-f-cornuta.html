Ulmus glabra f. cornuta

Ulmus glabra f. cornuta, commonly known as the smooth field maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged samaras. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
