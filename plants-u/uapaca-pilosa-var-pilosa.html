Uapaca pilosa var. pilosa

Uapaca pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the rough uapaca, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and emit a distinctive odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by ellipsoid berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
