Uncinia fuscovaginata var. caespitans

Uncinia fuscovaginata var. caespitans, commonly known as the tufted bladdergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its seeds are encased in small, bladder-like structures, which aid in wind dispersal.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
