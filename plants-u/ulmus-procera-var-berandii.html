Ulmus procera var. berandii

Ulmus procera var. berandii, commonly known as the Persian Elm, is a variant of the winged elm native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree typically grows as a tall, slender specimen with a narrow crown, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
