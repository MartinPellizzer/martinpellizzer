Urostachys miyoshianus var. coreanus

Urostachys miyoshianus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Korean mountain grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories and alpine meadows, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
