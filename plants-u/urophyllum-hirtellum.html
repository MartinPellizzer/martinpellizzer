Urophyllum hirtellum

Urophyllum hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy leaf euphorbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, textured surface.

Urophyllum hirtellum is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
