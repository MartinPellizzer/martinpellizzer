Uraria lagopodioides var. alopecuroides

Uraria lagopodioides var. alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
