Uragoga megacoma

Uragoga megacoma, commonly known as the giant uragoga, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often featuring a deep green color with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. Its roots are thick and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Uragoga megacoma is also noted for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
