Uragoga brachyandra

Uragoga brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, brush-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery or greyish pubescence.

Uragoga brachyandra is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
