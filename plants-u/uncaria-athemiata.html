Uncaria athemiata

Uncaria athemiata, commonly known as the climbing mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It grows on the branches of trees and shrubs, forming dense clusters that can weigh down its host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
