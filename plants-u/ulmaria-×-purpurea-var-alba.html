Ulmaria × purpurea var. alba

Ulmaria × purpurea var. alba, commonly known as white wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
