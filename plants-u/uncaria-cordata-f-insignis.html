Uncaria cordata f. insignis

Uncaria cordata f. insignis, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian hibiscus, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the climbing shrub Uncaria cordata.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
