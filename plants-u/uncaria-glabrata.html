Uncaria glabrata

Uncaria glabrata, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
