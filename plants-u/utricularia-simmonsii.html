Utricularia simmonsii

Utricularia simmonsii, commonly known as the pink bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. This species is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in nutrient-poor aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water, often in ponds or marshes, and has slender, branching stems.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish in color.
