Uvaria rubiginosa

Uvaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Uvaria rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
