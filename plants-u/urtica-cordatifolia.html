Urtica cordatifolia

Urtica cordatifolia, commonly known as heartleaf nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Urtica cordatifolia is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
