Ulex europaeus var. remote-bracteatus

Ulex europaeus var. remote-bracteatus, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. This variant is distinguished by its remote bracts, which are smaller and more distant compared to the typical form of Ulex europaeus.
