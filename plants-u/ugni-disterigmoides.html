Ugni disterigmoides

Ugni disterigmoides, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies in its native region.
