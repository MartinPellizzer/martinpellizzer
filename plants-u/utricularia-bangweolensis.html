Utricularia bangweolensis

Utricularia bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweol bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladders used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, where it relies on its prey for essential nutrients. Its leaves are usually submerged, while the bladders are often found at the water's surface.

Utricularia bangweolensis plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to the control of microorganism populations in aquatic environments.
