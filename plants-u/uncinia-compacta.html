Uncinia compacta

Uncinia compacta, commonly known as the clustered hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and distinctive hooked bracts that enclose its tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Uncinia compacta is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in its native habitat.
