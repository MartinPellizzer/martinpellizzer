Urochloa mollis

Urochloa mollis, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft-textured leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in a clump-forming habit and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

Urochloa mollis is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
