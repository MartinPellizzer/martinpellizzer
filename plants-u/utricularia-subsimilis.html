Utricularia subsimilis

Utricularia subsimilis, commonly known as the Indian bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like traps that capture microscopic organisms, enabling it to thrive in nutrient-poor water environments. Utricularia subsimilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling populations of small invertebrates and contributing to nutrient cycling.
