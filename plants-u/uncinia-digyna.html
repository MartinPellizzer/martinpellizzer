Uncinia digyna

Uncinia digyna, commonly known as the hooked grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristle-like awns. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

Uncinia digyna is adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in the Arctic and subarctic zones.
