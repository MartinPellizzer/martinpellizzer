Urtica bullata

Urtica bullata, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, smooth stems covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Urtica bullata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
