Uragoga longiflora

Uragoga longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered uragoga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga longiflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional medicinal uses.
