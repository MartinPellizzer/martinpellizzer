Uebelinia scottii

Uebelinia scottii, commonly known as the Scott's euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
