Uragoga hexamera

Uragoga hexamera, commonly known as the six-angled uragoga, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive hexagonal leaf structure.

Native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a woody stem with a smooth, bark-like surface that develops intricate patterns over time. Its leaves are broad and slightly serrated, often displaying a vibrant green hue with subtle variegation.

Uragoga hexamera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual morphology.
