Urena blumei

Urena blumei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas, making it both a weed and a forage plant.

Urena blumei is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
