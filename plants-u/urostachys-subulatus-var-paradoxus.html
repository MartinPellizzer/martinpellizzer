Urostachys subulatus var. paradoxus

Urostachys subulatus var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
