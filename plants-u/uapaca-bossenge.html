Uapaca bossenge

Uapaca bossenge, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
