Uncaria barbata

Uncaria barbata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Uncaria barbata*, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing ecological challenges.
