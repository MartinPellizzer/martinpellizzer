Uraria lagopodioides

Uraria lagopodioides, commonly known as the Indian goatherb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
