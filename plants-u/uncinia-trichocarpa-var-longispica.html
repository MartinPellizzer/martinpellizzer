Uncinia trichocarpa var. longispica

Uncinia trichocarpa var. longispica, commonly known as the long-spined hookweed, is a variant of the hookweed species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive long, spiny bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
