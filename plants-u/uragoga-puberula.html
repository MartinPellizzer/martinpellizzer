Uragoga puberula

Uragoga puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.

Uragoga puberula is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
