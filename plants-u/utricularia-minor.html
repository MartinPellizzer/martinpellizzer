Utricularia minor

Utricularia minor, commonly known as the lesser bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is a carnivorous plant that captures tiny aquatic organisms using specialized bladder-like structures called utricles. These utricles create a vacuum when triggered, allowing the plant to trap and digest prey. Utricularia minor typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters such as ponds and ditches.

Its delicate, floating leaves and translucent bladders are adaptations to its aquatic environment and nutrient-limited habitat.
