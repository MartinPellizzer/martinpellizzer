Uruparia multiflora

Uruparia multiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered uruparia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its striking floral display attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
