Uleopsis

Uleopsis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Uleopsis species typically thrive in moist, well-drained soil and require indirect sunlight to prevent leaf burn. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Uleopsis dinteri and Uleopsis hirta, which are often cultivated for their aesthetic appeal.
