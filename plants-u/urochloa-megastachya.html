Urochloa megastachya

Urochloa megastachya, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, broad leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
