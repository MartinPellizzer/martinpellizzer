Urtica dioica var. microphylla

Urtica dioica var. microphylla, commonly known as stinging nettle with small leaves, is a variant of the common stinging nettle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine serrations and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its stems are covered in stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
