Ulmus × hollandica var. dauvessei

Ulmus × hollandica var. dauvessei, commonly known as the Clancy's elm, is a hybrid species within the elm family (Ulmaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a broad, spreading crown that provides shade. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and along streets for its aesthetic appeal.
