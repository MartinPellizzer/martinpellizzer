Ulmus procera f. argenteovariegata

Ulmus procera f. argenteovariegata, commonly known as the silver-variegated elm, is a cultivar of the English elm.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow streaks on the leaves. The plant exhibits a broad, spreading growth habit with a tall, slender trunk. It is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping, though it is susceptible to certain diseases like Dutch elm disease.
