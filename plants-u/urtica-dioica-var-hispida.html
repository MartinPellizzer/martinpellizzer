Urtica dioica var. hispida

Urtica dioica var. hispida, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs that release formic acid when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with separate male and female flowers on different plants.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source when cooked.
