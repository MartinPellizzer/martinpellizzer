Urtica hulensis

Urtica hulensis, commonly known as Hul's nettle, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. Urtica hulensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are usually pollinated by insects.
