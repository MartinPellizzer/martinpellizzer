Utricularia elevata var. macowanii

Utricularia elevata var. macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and possesses specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are often submerged or floating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various water conditions and its unique method of nutrient acquisition through predation.
