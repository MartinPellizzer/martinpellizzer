Utricularia eburnea

Utricularia eburnea, commonly known as the ghost pipe plant, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its specialized bladder-like structures. These bladders trap and digest small aquatic organisms, allowing the plant to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are often submerged or floating.

Its appearance is delicate, with translucent or pale green leaves that contribute to its ethereal, ghost-like appearance.
