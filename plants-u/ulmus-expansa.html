Ulmus expansa

Ulmus expansa, commonly known as the Chinese Elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spreading crown and flexible, often pendulous branches. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
