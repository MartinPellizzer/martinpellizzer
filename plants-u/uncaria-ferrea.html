Uncaria ferrea

Uncaria ferrea, commonly known as Brazilian prickly ash, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. Its roots and stems contain compounds such as alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
