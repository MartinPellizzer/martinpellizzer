Ursinia anethifolia

Ursinia anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, anise-scented leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ursinia anethifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
