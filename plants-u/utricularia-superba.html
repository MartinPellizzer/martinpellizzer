Utricularia superba

Utricularia superba, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may float on the water surface.

Utricularia superba is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters.
