Uncinia macloviana var. douglasii

Uncinia macloviana var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its hooked bracts and is often found in association with other alpine and subalpine flora.
