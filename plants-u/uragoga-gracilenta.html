Uragoga gracilenta

Uragoga gracilenta, commonly known as the slender uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Uragoga gracilenta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
