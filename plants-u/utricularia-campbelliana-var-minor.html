Utricularia campbelliana var. minor

Utricularia campbelliana var. minor, commonly known as the Campbell's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. This variety typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor water conditions.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
