Ulmus exoniensis

Ulmus exoniensis, commonly known as Exeter Elm, is a rare and ancient species of elm native to the UK.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is highly valued for its ecological role and is considered a symbol of the UK's natural heritage.

Due to its rarity and vulnerability, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
