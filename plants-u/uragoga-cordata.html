Uragoga cordata

Uragoga cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by edible tubers resembling those of traditional arrowroot. Its tubers are starchy and have a mild flavor, making them a valuable food source in certain regions.

Botanically, Uragoga cordata belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.
