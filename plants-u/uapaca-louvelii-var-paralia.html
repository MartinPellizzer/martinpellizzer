Uapaca louvelii var. paralia

Uapaca louvelii var. paralia, commonly known as the coastal uapaca, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the dry season. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.
