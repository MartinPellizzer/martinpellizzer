Uraria acuminata

Uraria acuminata, commonly known as the Indian burr or goat's horn, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It thrives in dry and semi-arid climates, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems in its native habitat.
