Urostachys taxifolius var. herminieri

Urostachys taxifolius var. herminieri, commonly known as the Herminier's narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky slopes and desert margins.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
