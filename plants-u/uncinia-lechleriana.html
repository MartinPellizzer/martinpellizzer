Uncinia lechleriana

Uncinia lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's hookweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, hook-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
