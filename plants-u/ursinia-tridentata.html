Ursinia tridentata

Ursinia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, three-lobed leaves.

Ursinia tridentata is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
