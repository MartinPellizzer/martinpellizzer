Urtica monilifera

Urtica monilifera, commonly known as the chain urtica or stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, leafless stems that bear small, stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine, stinging trichomes.

Urtica monilifera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
