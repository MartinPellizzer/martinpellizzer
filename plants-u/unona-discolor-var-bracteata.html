Unona discolor var. bracteata

Unona discolor var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This variant is known for its ornamental value, particularly in horticultural settings.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
