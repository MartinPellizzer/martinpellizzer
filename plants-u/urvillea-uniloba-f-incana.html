Urvillea uniloba f. incana

Urvillea uniloba f. incana, commonly known as the woolly urvillea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomic study and ecological research.
