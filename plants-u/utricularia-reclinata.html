Utricularia reclinata

Utricularia reclinata, commonly known as the drooping bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. This species is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has thin, delicate stems and leaves that often float on the water surface.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying aquatic plant adaptations and carnivorous mechanisms.
