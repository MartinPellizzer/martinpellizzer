Ulmus × hollandica var. major

Ulmus × hollandica var. major, commonly known as the Dutch elm tree, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the English elm and the Siberian elm.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The tree produces small, pendulous clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to urban environments.
