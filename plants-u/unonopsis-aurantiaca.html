Unonopsis aurantiaca

Unonopsis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cooler temperatures and limited moisture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bell-shaped flowering plants.
