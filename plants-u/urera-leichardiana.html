Urera leichardiana

Urera leichardiana, commonly known as the pink-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Urera leichardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
