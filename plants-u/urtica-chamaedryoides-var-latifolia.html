Urtica chamaedryoides var. latifolia

Urtica chamaedryoides var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its stinging hairs and heart-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and have a serrated margin, while the stems are upright and often reddish in color.

It produces small greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated.
