Ursinia leptoglossa

Ursinia leptoglossa, commonly known as the slender-lipped ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Ursinia leptoglossa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
