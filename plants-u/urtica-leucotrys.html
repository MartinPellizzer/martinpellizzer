Urtica leucotrys

Urtica leucotrys, commonly known as the white stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries.

The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in humans.
