Urtica dioica var. macrodonta

Urtica dioica var. macrodonta, commonly known as giant nettle, is a variant of the common stinging nettle.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small greenish flowers in clusters, and it is typically found in moist, fertile soils. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall.

This variant is known for its potent stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
