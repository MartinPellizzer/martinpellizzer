Urostachys crassus var. herteri

Urostachys crassus var. herteri, commonly known as the Herter's thick-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
