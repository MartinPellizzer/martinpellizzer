Umbilicus spinosus

Umbilicus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny umbilic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in umbellate inflorescences. The plant features spiny bracts and has a distinctive, flattened, disc-like structure at the base of its flower cluster. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Umbilicus spinosus is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
