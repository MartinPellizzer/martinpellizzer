Urophyllum hirsutum

Urophyllum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy urophyllum, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
