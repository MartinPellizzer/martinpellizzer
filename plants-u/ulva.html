Ulva

Ulva, commonly known as sea lettuce, is a type of green algae that belongs to the family Ulvaceae.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structure that resembles a lettuce leaf, hence its common name. Ulva species are typically found in marine environments, growing on rocky substrates in shallow coastal waters. They play an important role in marine ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various marine organisms.

Ulva is also used in some traditional cuisines and has applications in biotechnology due to its high nutritional value.
