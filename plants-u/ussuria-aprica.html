Ussuria aprica

Ussuria aprica, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native habitat.
