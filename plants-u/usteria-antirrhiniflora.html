Usteria antirrhiniflora

Usteria antirrhiniflora, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. Its growth habit is typically low and mat-forming, making it ideal for coastal gardens.

Usteria antirrhiniflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
