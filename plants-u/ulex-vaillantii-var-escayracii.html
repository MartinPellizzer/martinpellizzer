Ulex vaillantii var. escayracii

Ulex vaillantii var. escayracii, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in Mediterranean climates.
