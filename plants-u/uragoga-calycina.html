Uragoga calycina

Uragoga calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Uragoga calycina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
