Uvaria membranacea

Uvaria membranacea, commonly known as the membranous uvaria, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, yellow or orange berries. Uvaria membranacea is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
