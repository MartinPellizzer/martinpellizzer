Urginea revoluta

Urginea revoluta, commonly known as the red tulip or red lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range from red to pink, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant produces a single, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter tall, bearing several blooms. Its bulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in arid environments.

Urginea revoluta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
