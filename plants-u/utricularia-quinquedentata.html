Utricularia quinquedentata

Utricularia quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-toothed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, intricate leaves that form bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant's unique adaptations for nutrient acquisition through predation make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
