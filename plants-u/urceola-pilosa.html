Urceola pilosa

Urceola pilosa, commonly known as the hairy urn plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Urceola pilosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and adds ornamental value to gardens.
