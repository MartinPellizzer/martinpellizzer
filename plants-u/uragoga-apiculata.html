Uragoga apiculata

Uragoga apiculata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Uragoga apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
