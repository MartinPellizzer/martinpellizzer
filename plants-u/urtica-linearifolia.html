Urtica linearifolia

Urtica linearifolia, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact. Its leaves are elongated, linear, and have a serrated margin, often with a distinct midrib.

Urtica linearifolia typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
