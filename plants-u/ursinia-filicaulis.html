Ursinia filicaulis

Ursinia filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ursinia filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
