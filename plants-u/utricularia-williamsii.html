Utricularia williamsii

Utricularia williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in still or slow-moving water. The plant features delicate, floating leaves with specialized bladder-like structures used to trap tiny aquatic organisms. These bladders function as suction traps, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients from its environment.

Utricularia williamsii is notable for its rapid growth and unique adaptations to nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.
