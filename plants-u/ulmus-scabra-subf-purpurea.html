Ulmus scabra subf. purpurea

Ulmus scabra subf.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple-barked elm, is a variant of the common elm characterized by its distinctive purplish bark. It is a deciduous tree native to parts of China, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
