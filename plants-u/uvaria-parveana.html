Uvaria parveana

Uvaria parveana, commonly known as the small-fruited uvaria, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. Its fruit is typically green when immature and turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

Uvaria parveana is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
