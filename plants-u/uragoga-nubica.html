Uragoga nubica

Uragoga nubica, commonly known as the Nubian uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, helping to conserve water.

Uragoga nubica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
