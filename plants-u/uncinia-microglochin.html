Uncinia microglochin

Uncinia microglochin, commonly known as the narrowleaf hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hooked or twisted appearance, giving it its common name.

The small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.
