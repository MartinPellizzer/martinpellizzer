Ulmus japonica f. suberosa

Ulmus japonica f. suberosa, commonly known as the Japanese cliff elm, is a unique variant of the Japanese elm.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and a rugged, almost tree-like appearance. This form is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or cliffs, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for horticultural use in challenging landscapes.
