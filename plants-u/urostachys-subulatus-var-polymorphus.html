Urostachys subulatus var. polymorphus

Urostachys subulatus var. polymorphus, commonly known as the variable needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it blooms during the spring or early summer.
