Urophyllum canthiiflorum

Urophyllum canthiiflorum, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
