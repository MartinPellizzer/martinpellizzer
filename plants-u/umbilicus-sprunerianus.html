Umbilicus sprunerianus

Umbilicus sprunerianus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a distinctive umbilical-shaped flower cluster at the base of its stem, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
