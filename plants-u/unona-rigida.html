Unona rigida

Unona rigida, commonly known as the rigid unona, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
