Urochloa gorinii

Urochloa gorinii, commonly known as the African grass or Gorin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Urochloa gorinii is often used for forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
