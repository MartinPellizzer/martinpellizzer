Urceola laevigata

Urceola laevigata, commonly known as the smooth urceola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, often blooming in late spring.

Urceola laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
