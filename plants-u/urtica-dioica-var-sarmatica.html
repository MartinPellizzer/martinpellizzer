Urtica dioica var. sarmatica

Urtica dioica var. sarmatica, commonly known as the Sarmatian nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle found primarily in temperate regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and green, arranged in clusters, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate plants.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
