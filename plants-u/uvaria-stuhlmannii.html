Uvaria stuhlmannii

Uvaria stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in local communities.
