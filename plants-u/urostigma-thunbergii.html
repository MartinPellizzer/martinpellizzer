Urostigma thunbergii

Urostigma thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Urostigma thunbergii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
