Ulmus scabra var. tricuspis

Ulmus scabra var. tricuspis, commonly known as the roughleaf elm, is a variant of the common elm characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured bark.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically three-lobed, giving the plant its specific epithet "tricuspis," which means "three-lobed" in Latin. This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
