Uvaria wrayi var. wrayi

Uvaria wrayi var. wrayi, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a woody shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Uvaria wrayi var. wrayi is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
