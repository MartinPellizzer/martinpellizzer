Uncinia gracilis

Uncinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its slender, hook-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers make it easily identifiable.

Uncinia gracilis is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.
