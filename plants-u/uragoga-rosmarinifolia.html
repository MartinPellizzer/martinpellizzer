Uragoga rosmarinifolia

Uragoga rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy due to their calming properties.

Uragoga rosmarinifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal gardens.
