Urtica roxburghii

Urtica roxburghii, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that cause a skin irritation when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Urtica roxburghii is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
