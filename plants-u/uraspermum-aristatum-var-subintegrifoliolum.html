Uraspermum aristatum var. subintegrifoliolum

Uraspermum aristatum var. subintegrifoliolum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
