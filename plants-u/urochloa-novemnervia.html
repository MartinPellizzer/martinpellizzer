Urochloa novemnervia

Urochloa novemnervia, commonly known as nine-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and nine distinct veins. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in regions with warm climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lipped flowers.

Urochloa novemnervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
