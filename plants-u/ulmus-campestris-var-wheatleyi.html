Ulmus campestris var. wheatleyi

Ulmus campestris var. wheatleyi, commonly known as the wheatley's elm, is a rare variant of the field elm.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and delicate, pendulous branches. The leaves are typically elongated and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
