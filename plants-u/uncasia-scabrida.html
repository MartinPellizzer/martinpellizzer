Uncasia scabrida

Uncasia scabrida, commonly known as the roughleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces that reduce water loss.

Uncasia scabrida is often found in sandy soils and is used in some regions for erosion control due to its hardy nature.
