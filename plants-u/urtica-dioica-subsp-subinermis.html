Urtica dioica subsp. subinermis

Urtica dioica subsp.

subinermis, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine stinging hairs that release histamine upon contact.

The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits containing tiny seeds.

The plant is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
