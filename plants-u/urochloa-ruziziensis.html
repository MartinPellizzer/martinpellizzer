Urochloa ruziziensis

Urochloa ruziziensis, commonly known as African grass or Ruzizi grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Urochloa ruziziensis is valued for its use in livestock forage and as a component in erosion control.
