Ulmus corylifolia

Ulmus corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese elms or Chinese walnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the elm family, Ulmaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble walnuts. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some Asian cultures.
