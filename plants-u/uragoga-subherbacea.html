Uragoga subherbacea

Uragoga subherbacea, commonly known as the slender uragoga, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Uragoga subherbacea is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
