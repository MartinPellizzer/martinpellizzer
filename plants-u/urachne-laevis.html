Urachne laevis

Urachne laevis, commonly known as the smooth urachne, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually hairless, giving it a smooth texture, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Urachne laevis is adapted to dry environments and is considered a low-maintenance plant in horticulture.
