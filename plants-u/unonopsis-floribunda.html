Unonopsis floribunda

Unonopsis floribunda, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall texture.

Unonopsis floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
