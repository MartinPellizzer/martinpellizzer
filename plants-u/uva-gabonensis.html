Uva gabonensis

Uva gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon starfruit, is a climbing plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Uva gabonensis is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
