Utricularia paradoxa

Utricularia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique trapping mechanism and specialized morphology make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
