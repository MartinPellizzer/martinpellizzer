Urera ficifolia

Urera ficifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf tree or redwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. Its wood is lightweight and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

Urera ficifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for numerous organisms.
