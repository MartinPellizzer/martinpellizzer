Uvaria siamensis

Uvaria siamensis, commonly known as the Siam satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

Uvaria siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
