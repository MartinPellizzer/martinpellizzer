Unona virgata

Unona virgata, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it often forms dense colonies in its natural habitat.
