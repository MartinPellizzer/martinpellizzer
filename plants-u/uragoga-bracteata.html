Uragoga bracteata

Uragoga bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Uragoga bracteata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
