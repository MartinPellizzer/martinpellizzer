Urophyllum annamense

Urophyllum annamense, commonly known as the Annamese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
