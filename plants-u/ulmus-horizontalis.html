Ulmus horizontalis

Ulmus horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontally branched elm, is a species of elm native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally spreading branches that create a rounded, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a rough texture and a serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
