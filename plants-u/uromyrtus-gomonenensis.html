Uromyrtus gomonenensis

Uromyrtus gomonenensis, commonly known as the Gomon Peninsula hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate saline conditions, making it resilient in coastal environments.

Uromyrtus gomonenensis is often found in heathlands and is an important component of the local flora.
