Ursinia quinquepartita

Ursinia quinquepartita, commonly known as the five-parted ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and fynbos regions. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are often clustered in a flat-topped arrangement.

Ursinia quinquepartita is known for its medicinal uses and is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
