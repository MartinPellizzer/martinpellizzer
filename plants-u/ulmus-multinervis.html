Ulmus multinervis

Ulmus multinervis, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to urban conditions.

Ulmus multinervis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
