Ugni candollei f. monticola

Ugni candollei f. monticola, commonly known as the mountain huito, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, pleasant fragrance, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
