Uncinia fernandesiana

Uncinia fernandesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a unique fern species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other ferns but with a distinct, almost feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
