Unona leptopetala

Unona leptopetala, commonly known as the slender petal tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical gardens. Its foliage provides shade, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The wood of Unona leptopetala is lightweight and has been used in traditional crafts and construction in its native habitat.
