Urtica massaica

Urtica massaica, commonly known as the giant stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tall, erect stems that are also covered in stinging hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Urtica massaica is often found near water sources and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
