Urostachys verticillatus var. parvifolius

Urostachys verticillatus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain feather, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
