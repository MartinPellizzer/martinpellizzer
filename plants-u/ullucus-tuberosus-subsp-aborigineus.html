Ullucus tuberosus subsp. aborigineus

Ullucus tuberosus subsp.

aborigineus, commonly known as the Andean sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America. It produces small, rounded tubers that are typically purple or red in color, with a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued both for its nutritional content and its historical significance in indigenous Andean cultures.
