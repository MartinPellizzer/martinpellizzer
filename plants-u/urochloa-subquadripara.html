Urochloa subquadripara

Urochloa subquadripara, commonly known as the four-leaf clovergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the nodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Urochloa subquadripara is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage or soil stabilization.
