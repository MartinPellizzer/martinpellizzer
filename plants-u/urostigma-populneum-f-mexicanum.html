Urostigma populneum f. mexicanum

Urostigma populneum f. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican poplar, is a rare and unique variant of the poplar family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, followed by the development of samaras for seed dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
