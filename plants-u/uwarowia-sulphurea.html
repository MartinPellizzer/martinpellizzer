Uwarowia sulphurea

Uwarowia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers, which resemble small sunbursts, and its distinctive sulfur-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as along riverbanks and in humid forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Uwarowia sulphurea is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional uses in some local cultures.
