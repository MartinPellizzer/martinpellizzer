Uvifera declinata

Uvifera declinata, commonly known as the hanging fig, is a unique species of fig tree native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy fruits that hang from the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically hidden within the fruit's structure, which is a characteristic feature of the Moraceae family.
