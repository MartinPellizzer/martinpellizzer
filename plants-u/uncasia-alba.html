Uncasia alba

Uncasia alba, commonly known as the white coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its botanical and cultural significance, it is a subject of both scientific study and conservation interest.
