Urophyllum trifurcum

Urophyllum trifurcum, commonly known as the three-branched flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are typically three-lobed, giving the species its scientific name.

Urophyllum trifurcum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
