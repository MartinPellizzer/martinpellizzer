Utricularia rendlei

Utricularia rendlei, commonly known as the red bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually submerged, while its flowering stems emerge above the water surface.

Utricularia rendlei is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
