Uvifera parvifolia

Uvifera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved grapevine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Uvifera parvifolia is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
