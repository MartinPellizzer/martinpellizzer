Uragoga gabonica

Uragoga gabonica, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Uragoga gabonica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
