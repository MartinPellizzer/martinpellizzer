Uragoga polycephala

Uragoga polycephala, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The flowers have a unique structure with showy petals and a prominent, trumpet-like corolla. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
