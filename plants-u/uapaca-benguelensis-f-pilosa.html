Uapaca benguelensis f. pilosa

Uapaca benguelensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy uapaca, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

The hairy uapaca is often found in dry forests and savannas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
