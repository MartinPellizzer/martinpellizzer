Ulmus gigantea

Ulmus gigantea, commonly known as the American giant elm, is a large deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown with heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its bark is thick, furrowed, and grayish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

Ulmus gigantea is known for its rapid growth and historical significance in urban landscapes.
