Uragoga sessilifolia

Uragoga sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant possesses sessile leaves, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
