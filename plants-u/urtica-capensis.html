Urtica capensis

Urtica capensis, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with fine hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Urtica capensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.

Its growth habit includes both upright and creeping forms, depending on the conditions.
