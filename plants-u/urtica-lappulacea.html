Urtica lappulacea

Urtica lappulacea, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant features a tall, branching stem with small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in dense, spike-like clusters.

Urtica lappulacea is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates and is often used in traditional medicine.
