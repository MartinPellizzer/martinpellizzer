Uragoga vellerea

Uragoga vellerea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and fuzzy, silvery foliage. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its unique morphology helps it retain moisture and withstand salty sea air.

Uragoga vellerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
