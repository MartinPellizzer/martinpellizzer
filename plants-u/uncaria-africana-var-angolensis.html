Uncaria africana var. angolensis

Uncaria africana var. angolensis, commonly known as the African climbing vine, is a species native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a woody shrub or vine that typically grows in forested or wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its small, pinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
