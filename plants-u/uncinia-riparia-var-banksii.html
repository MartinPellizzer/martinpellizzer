Uncinia riparia var. banksii

Uncinia riparia var. banksii, commonly known as the Banks' bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its dense growth makes it an important component of native plant communities in these habitats.
