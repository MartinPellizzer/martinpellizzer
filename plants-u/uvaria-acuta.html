Uvaria acuta

Uvaria acuta, commonly known as the sharp uvaria, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn red when mature.

Uvaria acuta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
