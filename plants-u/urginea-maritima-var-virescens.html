Urginea maritima var. virescens

Urginea maritima var. virescens, commonly known as the green bulbous plant, is a variant of the sea onion, native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy bulbs and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
