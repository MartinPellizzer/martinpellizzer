Uvaria wrayi

Uvaria wrayi, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
