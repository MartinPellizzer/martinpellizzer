Urtica thunbergiana subsp. thunbergiana

Urtica thunbergiana subsp.

thunbergiana, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and dense clusters of tiny, stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on slender, erect stems. Urtica thunbergiana subsp.

thunbergiana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
