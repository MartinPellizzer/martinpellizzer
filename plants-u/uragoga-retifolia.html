Uragoga retifolia

Uragoga retifolia, commonly known as the spiked uragoga, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga retifolia is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
