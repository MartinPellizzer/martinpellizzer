Uraria longibracteata

Uraria longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in dry, arid environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
