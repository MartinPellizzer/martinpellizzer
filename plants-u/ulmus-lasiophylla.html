Ulmus lasiophylla

Ulmus lasiophylla, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and urban environments.
