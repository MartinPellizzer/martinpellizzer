Uncaria africana var. xerophila

Uncaria africana var. xerophila, commonly known as the African climbing shrub, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and red, hairy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
