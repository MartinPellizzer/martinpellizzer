Ulmus × rhombifolia

Ulmus × rhombifolia, commonly known as the American sycamore, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Ulmaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled bark, which peels to reveal a smooth, lighter-colored surface beneath. The tree has broad, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in riparian zones and along riverbanks.

Ulmus × rhombifolia is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
