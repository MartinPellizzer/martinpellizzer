Urtica gracilis var. latifolia

Urtica gracilis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges and are typically pubescent on both surfaces. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated.
