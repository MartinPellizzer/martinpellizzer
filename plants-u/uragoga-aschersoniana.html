Uragoga aschersoniana

Uragoga aschersoniana, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water.

Uragoga aschersoniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
