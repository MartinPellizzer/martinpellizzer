Urtica dioica f. carpatica

Urtica dioica f. carpatica, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a variant of the common stinging nettle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate plants.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health benefits.
