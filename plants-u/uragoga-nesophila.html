Uragoga nesophila

Uragoga nesophila, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to penetrate the host's vascular system and extract water and nutrients. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hair, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

Uragoga nesophila plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
