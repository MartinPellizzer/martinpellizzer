Uragoga coccinea

Uragoga coccinea, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Uragoga coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
