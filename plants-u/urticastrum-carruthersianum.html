Urticastrum carruthersianum

Urticastrum carruthersianum, commonly known as Carruthers' goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads.

Urticastrum carruthersianum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
