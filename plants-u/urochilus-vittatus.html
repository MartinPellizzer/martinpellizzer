Urochilus vittatus

Urochilus vittatus, commonly known as the yellow-veined bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with distinct purple veins, blooming during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Urochilus vittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
