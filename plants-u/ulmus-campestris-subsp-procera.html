Ulmus campestris subsp. procera

Ulmus campestris subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall field elm, is a subspecies of the field elm native to Europe. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its tall, slender trunk and spreading crown.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and meadows.
