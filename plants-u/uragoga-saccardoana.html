Uragoga saccardoana

Uragoga saccardoana, commonly known as the Saccardoan Urara, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent leaves that emit a faint blue glow during the night. The plant possesses a complex root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in late spring.
