Urtica atrovirens

Urtica atrovirens, commonly known as the black-stem nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and slender, often reddish stems. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Urtica atrovirens is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
