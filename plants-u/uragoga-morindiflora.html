Uragoga morindiflora

Uragoga morindiflora, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Uragoga morindiflora is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its medicinal properties.
