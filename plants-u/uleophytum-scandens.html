Uleophytum scandens

Uleophytum scandens, commonly known as the climbing milkweed, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Uleophytum scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
