Unona lucens

Unona lucens, commonly known as the glowing willow, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, willow-like stems that emit a soft bioluminescent glow at night, a unique adaptation that aids in pollination. The leaves are broad and waxy, with a deep green hue that reflects the light emitted by the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spring, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The plant's ability to produce light makes it a rare and fascinating subject in botanical studies.
