Utea

Utea, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. Utea is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
