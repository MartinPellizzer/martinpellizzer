Urophyllum suberosum

Urophyllum suberosum, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Urophyllum suberosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
