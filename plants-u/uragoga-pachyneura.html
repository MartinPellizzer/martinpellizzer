Uragoga pachyneura

Uragoga pachyneura, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a dense canopy with broad, lance-shaped leaves that provide shade. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with distinctive fissures.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
