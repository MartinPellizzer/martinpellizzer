Uvifera ascendens

Uvifera ascendens, commonly known as the climbing grapefruit tree, is a unique member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems that can reach significant heights. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits, which resemble citrus fruits, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Uvifera ascendens is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.
