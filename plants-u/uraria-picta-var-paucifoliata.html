Uraria picta var. paucifoliata

Uraria picta var. paucifoliata, commonly known as the sparsely-leaved uraria, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
