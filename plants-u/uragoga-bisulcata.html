Uragoga bisulcata

Uragoga bisulcata, commonly known as the two-ridged orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricately veined leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
