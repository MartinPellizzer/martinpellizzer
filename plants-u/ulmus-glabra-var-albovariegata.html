Ulmus glabra var. albovariegata

Ulmus glabra var. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-veined elm, is a variant of the Wych elm characterized by its distinctive white veins on the leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a pale yellow-green underside. It produces small, pendulous catkins in early spring, and its bark is smooth and greyish-brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
