Urera densiflora

Urera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flower urera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale green. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain several seeds.

Urera densiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
