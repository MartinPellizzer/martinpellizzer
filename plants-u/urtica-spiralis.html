Urtica spiralis

Urtica spiralis, commonly known as spiral nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves and stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Urtica spiralis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
