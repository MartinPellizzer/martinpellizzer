Ulex baeticus subsp. scaber

Ulex baeticus subsp.

scaber, commonly known as the rough thorned broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
