Urostachys cruentus

Urostachys cruentus, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its basal leaves and a single, erect stem that bears a showy, pinkish-white flower. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
