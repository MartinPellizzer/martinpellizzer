Uncinia astonii

Uncinia astonii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, which help minimize water loss.

Uncinia astonii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in desert environments.
