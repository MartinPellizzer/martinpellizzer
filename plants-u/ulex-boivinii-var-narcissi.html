Ulex boivinii var. narcissi

Ulex boivinii var. narcissi, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and persistent berries, which attract wildlife.
