Ulmus macrophylla

Ulmus macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The tree can reach heights of 20 to 30 meters and has a straight trunk with a rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in North American forests.
