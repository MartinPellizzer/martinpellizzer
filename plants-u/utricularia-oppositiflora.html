Utricularia oppositiflora

Utricularia oppositiflora, commonly known as the "slender bladderwort," is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Southeast Asia.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures called utricles that trap tiny aquatic organisms for nutrition. The plant typically grows in submerged or floating habitats, with its stems often branching and bearing numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for water movement and light absorption.

Utricularia oppositiflora plays a unique ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems.
