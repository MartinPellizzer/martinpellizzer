Uvularia perfoliata

Uvularia perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often fused around the stem, giving it a unique perfoliate appearance. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Uvularia perfoliata is a member of the Polemoniacaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
