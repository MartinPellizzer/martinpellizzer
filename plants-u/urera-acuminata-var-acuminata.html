Urera acuminata var. acuminata

Urera acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has compound leaves with alternating leaflets. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round, yellow fruits.

The species is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of valuable timber.
