Uragoga venosa

Uragoga venosa, commonly known as the veined uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
