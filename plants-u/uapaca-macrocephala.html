Uapaca macrocephala

Uapaca macrocephala, commonly known as the wild date palm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to dates. Its fruit is an important food source for both humans and wildlife in its native habitat.

Uapaca macrocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
