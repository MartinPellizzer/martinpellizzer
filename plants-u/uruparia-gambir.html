Uruparia gambir

Uruparia gambir, commonly known as gambir, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain small, black seeds.

Gambir is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
