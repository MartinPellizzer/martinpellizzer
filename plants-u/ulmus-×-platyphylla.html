Ulmus × platyphylla

Ulmus × platyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese field maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
