Uropappus clevelandii

Uropappus clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
