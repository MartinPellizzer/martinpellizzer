Uraria crinita

Uraria crinita, commonly known as the silky uraria, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Uraria crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
