Urochloa texana

Urochloa texana, commonly known as Texas grass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
