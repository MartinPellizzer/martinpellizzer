Unona subbiglandulosa

Unona subbiglandulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in woodland or moist forest habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Unona subbiglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
