Urena moenchii

Urena moenchii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. Urena moenchii is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
