Urtica praetermissa

Urtica praetermissa, commonly known as the lesser nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica praetermissa is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
