Uva baumannii

Uva baumannii, commonly known as the "grape vine" in some regions, is a plant species native to temperate climates.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, grape-like berries. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
