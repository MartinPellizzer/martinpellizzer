Uromyrtus ngoyensis

Uromyrtus ngoyensis, commonly known as the Ngoye hakea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its woody structure and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Uromyrtus ngoyensis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
