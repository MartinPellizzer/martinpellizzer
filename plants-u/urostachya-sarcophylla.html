Urostachya sarcophylla

Urostachya sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecrop saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Urostachya sarcophylla is a hardy species that is well-suited to xerophytic conditions and has minimal water requirements.
