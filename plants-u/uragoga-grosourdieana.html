Uragoga grosourdieana

Uragoga grosourdieana, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its native habitat.

Uragoga grosourdieana is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by botanists and horticulturists for its aesthetic appeal.
