Urostachys taxifolius var. passerinoides

Urostachys taxifolius var. passerinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of China.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically silver or blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
