Urera jacquinii var. tomentosa

Urera jacquinii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy urera, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
