Uragoga macrocalyx

Uragoga macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with prominent stamens that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
