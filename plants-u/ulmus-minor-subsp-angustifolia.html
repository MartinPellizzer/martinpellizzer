Ulmus minor subsp. angustifolia

Ulmus minor subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved field elm, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age.

The leaves are lanceolate, usually 5–10 cm long, with a pointed tip and a finely toothed margin. It produces small, pendulous catkins in early spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
