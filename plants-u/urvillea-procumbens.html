Urvillea procumbens

Urvillea procumbens, commonly known as the creeping urvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by hairy stems and leaves. Its leaves are generally elliptical or ovate in shape, with a rough texture.

Urvillea procumbens is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
