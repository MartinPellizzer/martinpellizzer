Uragoga arcuata

Uragoga arcuata, commonly known as the climbing star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Uragoga arcuata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
