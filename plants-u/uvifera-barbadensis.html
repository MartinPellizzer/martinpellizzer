Uvifera barbadensis

Uvifera barbadensis, commonly known as the Barbados cherry or *Pitahaya* in some regions, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and dry forests. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and the fruit is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
