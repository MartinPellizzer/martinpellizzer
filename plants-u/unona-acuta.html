Unona acuta

Unona acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or small shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
