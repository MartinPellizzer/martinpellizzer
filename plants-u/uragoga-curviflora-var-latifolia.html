Uragoga curviflora var. latifolia

Uragoga curviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved curlflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
