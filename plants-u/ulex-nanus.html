Ulex nanus

Ulex nanus, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in stiff, hooked hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Ulex nanus thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.
