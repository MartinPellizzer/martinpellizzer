Urtica lusitanica

Urtica lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that cause irritation upon contact with the skin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, smooth seeds.

Urtica lusitanica is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
