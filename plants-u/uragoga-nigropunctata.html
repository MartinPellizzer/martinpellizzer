Uragoga nigropunctata

Uragoga nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple petals with black spots, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Uragoga nigropunctata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
