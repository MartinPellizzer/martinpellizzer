Ursinia trifida

Ursinia trifida, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Ursinia trifida is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Its foliage is usually hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
