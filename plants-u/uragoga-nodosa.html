Uragoga nodosa

Uragoga nodosa, commonly known as the nodular uragoga, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, knobby growth patterns.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of pale yellow or green.

Due to its rare occurrence and unique morphology, Uragoga nodosa is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
