Urera aurantiaca var. scandens

Urera aurantiaca var. scandens, commonly known as the scarlet urera, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and adaptability to tropical climates.
