Urtica cinerascens

Urtica cinerascens, commonly known as the ash stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its stems and leaves contain irritant hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin.

Urtica cinerascens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
