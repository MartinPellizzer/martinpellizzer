Urostachys elongatus

Urostachys elongatus, commonly known as the elongated spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stem and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Urostachys elongatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
