Ubochea

Ubochea, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soil and thrives in bright, indirect light. They are also known for their ability to produce offsets, which can be used for propagation.
