Uvaria suberosa

Uvaria suberosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple or black berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with serrated edges.

Uvaria suberosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
