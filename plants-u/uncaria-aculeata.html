Uncaria aculeata

Uncaria aculeata, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to other plants or structures.

The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded environments.
