Urochloa sclerochlaena var. commelinoides

Urochloa sclerochlaena var. commelinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its drought tolerance and rapid growth make it a valuable species for erosion control and forage production.
