Ulmus elongata

Ulmus elongata, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that mature in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ulmus elongata is also valued for its relatively fast growth and adaptability to urban environments.
