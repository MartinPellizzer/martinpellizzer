Urera jacquinii var. subpeltata

Urera jacquinii var. subpeltata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
