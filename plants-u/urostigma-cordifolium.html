Urostigma cordifolium

Urostigma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved urostigma, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
