Urginea eriospermoides

Urginea eriospermoides, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance, blooming in late summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy underground bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and often die back during dry periods, leaving the bulb exposed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
