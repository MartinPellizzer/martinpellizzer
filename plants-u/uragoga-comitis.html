Uragoga comitis

Uragoga comitis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Uragoga comitis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
