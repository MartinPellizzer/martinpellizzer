Uralepis quadridentata

Uralepis quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed uralepis, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters.

Uralepis quadridentata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
