Uragoga polakowskyi

Uragoga polakowskyi, commonly known as the Polakowski's uragoga, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
