Urtica whitlowii

Urtica whitlowii, commonly known as the southern stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Urtica whitlowii is known for its upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.
