Urtica propinqua

Urtica propinqua, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing a painful rash upon contact. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along roadsides.

Urtica propinqua is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
