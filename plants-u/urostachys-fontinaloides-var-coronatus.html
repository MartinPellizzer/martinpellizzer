Urostachys fontinaloides var. coronatus

Urostachys fontinaloides var. coronatus, commonly known as the crown moss, is a rare and specialized aquatic plant native to the mountain streams of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit and small, overlapping leaves that form a compact, moss-like structure. The plant thrives in cold, fast-flowing waters with high oxygen levels, making it a sensitive indicator of water quality. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important species in studies of aquatic botany and conservation.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
