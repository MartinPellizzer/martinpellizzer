Urtica gracilis subsp. gracilis

Urtica gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in stinging hairs, which are characteristic of the Urtica genus.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
