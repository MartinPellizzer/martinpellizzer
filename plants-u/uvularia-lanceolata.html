Uvularia lanceolata

Uvularia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Uvularia lanceolata is often found in clusters, with its delicate appearance making it a notable feature in woodland gardens.
