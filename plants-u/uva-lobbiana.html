Uva lobbiana

Uva lobbiana, commonly known as the mountain grape or wild grape, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Uva lobbiana is often found growing in rocky or mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
