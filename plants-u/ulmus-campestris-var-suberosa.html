Ulmus campestris var. suberosa

Ulmus campestris var. suberosa, commonly known as the European warty elm, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its distinctive warty bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The tree has compound leaves that are alternate and usually have 3 to 5 leaflets. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
