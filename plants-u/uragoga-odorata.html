Uragoga odorata

Uragoga odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle scent.

Uragoga odorata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
