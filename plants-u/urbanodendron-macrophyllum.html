Urbanodendron macrophyllum

Urbanodendron macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant broadleaf tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

Native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, moist environments with high light exposure. Its sturdy trunk and sprawling branches make it a prominent feature in urban and rural landscapes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
