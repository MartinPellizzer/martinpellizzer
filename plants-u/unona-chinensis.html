Unona chinensis

Unona chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces edible fruit that is rich in nutrients, including vitamins C and B, and minerals like potassium. The plant has a deep root system and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The fruit is typically harvested in autumn and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
