Uralepis ambigua

Uralepis ambigua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flax, is a perennial plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used for fiber production.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses.
