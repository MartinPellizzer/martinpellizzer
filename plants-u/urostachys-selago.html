Urostachys selago

Urostachys selago, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
