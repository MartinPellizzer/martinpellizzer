Ulmus carpinifolia f. pendula

Ulmus carpinifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping wych elm, is a striking cultivar of the wych elm tree.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

However, it is susceptible to certain diseases, which can pose challenges for its cultivation.
