Umbilicus vulgaris

Umbilicus vulgaris, commonly known as the common hollyhock, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a fuzzy texture.

Umbilicus vulgaris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
