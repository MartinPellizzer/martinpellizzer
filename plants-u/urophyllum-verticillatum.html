Urophyllum verticillatum

Urophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Urophyllum verticillatum is often found in wetlands, swales, and along the edges of ponds.
