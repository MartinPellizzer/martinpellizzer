Urostachys passerinoides var. cipoensis

Urostachys passerinoides var. cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with narrow, silver-green leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its resilience to harsh climates and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
