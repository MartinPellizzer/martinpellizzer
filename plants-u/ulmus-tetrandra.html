Ulmus tetrandra

Ulmus tetrandra, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in length. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Ulmus tetrandra is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
