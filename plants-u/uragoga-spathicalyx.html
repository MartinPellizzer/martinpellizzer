Uragoga spathicalyx

Uragoga spathicalyx, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, typically white or pink, and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies, while its leaves float on the surface.

Uragoga spathicalyx plays an important ecological role by offering shade, oxygenation, and habitat for aquatic organisms.
