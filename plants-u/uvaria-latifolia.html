Uvaria latifolia

Uvaria latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by clustered, fleshy berries. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a glossy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Uvaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
