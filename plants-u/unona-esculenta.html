Unona esculenta

Unona esculenta, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with broad, palm-like leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates and is a staple food in many Pacific Island cultures. Its botanical features include a fibrous, starchy pulp and a hard, inedible core.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its timber and ornamental value.
