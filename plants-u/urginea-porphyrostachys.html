Urginea porphyrostachys

Urginea porphyrostachys, commonly known as the purple-stemmed giant squill, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground stem and tall, erect leaves that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
