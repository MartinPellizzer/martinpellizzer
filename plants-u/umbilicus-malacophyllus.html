Umbilicus malacophyllus

Umbilicus malacophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf umbilicus, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

Its leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or white in color. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates.

Due to its rarity and distinctive features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
