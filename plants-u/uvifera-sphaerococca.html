Uvifera sphaerococca

Uvifera sphaerococca, commonly known as the globe cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and dense clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Uvifera sphaerococca is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing sandy landscapes.
