Utricularia juncea f. virgatula

Utricularia juncea f. virgatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating bladders used to trap and digest aquatic invertebrates. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. This variant is distinguished by its slender morphology and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.
