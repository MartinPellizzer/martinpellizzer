Uncaria macrophylla

Uncaria macrophylla, commonly known as Indian tea or cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red berries. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with the stems containing alkaloids used in traditional and modern herbal medicine. Its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments makes it a common species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant's botanical features contribute to its ecological role and pharmacological significance.
