Urtica verbascifolia

Urtica verbascifolia, commonly known as woolly nettle, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, stinging underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its habitat.
