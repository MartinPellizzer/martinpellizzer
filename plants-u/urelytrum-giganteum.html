Urelytrum giganteum

Urelytrum giganteum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive foliage and helps it stay afloat. Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths.

Urelytrum giganteum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
