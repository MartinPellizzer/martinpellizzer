Uloma

Uloma, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Uloma is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both a useful plant in traditional medicine and a challenging invasive species in certain regions.
