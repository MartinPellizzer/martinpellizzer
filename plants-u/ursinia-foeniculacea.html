Ursinia foeniculacea

Ursinia foeniculacea, commonly known as the African daisy or yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with orange centers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its vibrant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Ursinia foeniculacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
