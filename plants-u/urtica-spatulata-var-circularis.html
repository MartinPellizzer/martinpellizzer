Urtica spatulata var. circularis

Urtica spatulata var. circularis, commonly known as the broad-leaved stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
