Utricularia incisa

Utricularia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, often arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor water conditions.
