Uragoga tahitensis

Uragoga tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip petal. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Uragoga tahitensis is valued both for its aesthetic beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
