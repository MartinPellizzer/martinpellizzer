Umbilicus praealtus var. coutinhoi

Umbilicus praealtus var. coutinhoi, commonly known as the Portuguese umbilic, is a rare and striking plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped inflorescence that resembles a whirlpool or vortex. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
