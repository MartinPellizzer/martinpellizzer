Urostigma gomelleira

Urostigma gomelleira, commonly known as the Portuguese nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant has hollow, hairless stems that are often reddish in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact.

Urostigma gomelleira is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
