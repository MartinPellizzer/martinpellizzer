Urochloa ramosa var. pubescens

Urochloa ramosa var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
