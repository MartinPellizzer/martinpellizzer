Utricularia striata var. torreyi

Utricularia striata var. torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant features delicate, floating leaves with numerous tiny bladders that trap small aquatic organisms. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Utricularia striata var. torreyi is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
