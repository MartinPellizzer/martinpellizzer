Unona discolor var. macropetala

Unona discolor var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered false heather, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
