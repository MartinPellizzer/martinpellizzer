Urtica dioica var. sondenii

Urtica dioica var. sondenii, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs that release formic acid when touched, causing a painful rash. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with heart-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Urtica dioica var. sondenii is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
