Urostachys mandiocanus

Urostachys mandiocanus, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or mandiocan grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
