Ulmus suberosa var. folia-variegata-pendula

Ulmus suberosa var. folia-variegata-pendula, commonly known as the variegated weeping elm, is a unique variant of the warty elm.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are variegated, featuring a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow, which adds to its ornamental value. This plant is typically found in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
