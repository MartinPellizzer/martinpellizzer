Uncaria nervosa

Uncaria nervosa, commonly known as wild indigo or Indian tobacco, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant contains alkaloids, including tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which contribute to its medicinal properties. Its stems and roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
