Uragoga stenantha

Uragoga stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uragoga, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Uragoga stenantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
