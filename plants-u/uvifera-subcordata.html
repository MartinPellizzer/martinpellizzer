Uvifera subcordata

Uvifera subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed habitats.

Uvifera subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
