Uncarina sakalava

Uncarina sakalava, commonly known as the Madagascar cucumber.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its long, tendrils and distinctive, warty fruits. The plant is native to Madagascar and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. Its fruits are typically green, with a rough, bumpy surface, and are used in traditional medicine.
