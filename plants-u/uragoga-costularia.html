Uragoga costularia

Uragoga costularia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Uragoga costularia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
