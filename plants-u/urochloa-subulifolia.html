Urochloa subulifolia

Urochloa subulifolia, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Urochloa subulifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
