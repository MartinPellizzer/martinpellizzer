Uragoga obtegens

Uragoga obtegens, commonly known as the broad-leaved uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga obtegens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
