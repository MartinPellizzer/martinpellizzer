Urostachys crassus

Urostachys crassus, commonly known as the broad-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Urostachys crassus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
