Urostachys longiaristatus

Urostachys longiaristatus, commonly known as the long-bract sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, forming dense mats or rosettes.

Urostachys longiaristatus is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
