Urachne miliacea

Urachne miliacea, commonly known as the millet grass, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its seeds are sometimes used as a minor food source in certain regions.
