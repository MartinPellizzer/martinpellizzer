Uragoga novocastellana

Uragoga novocastellana, commonly known as the Castellana orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on their petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring, adding a touch of elegance to its natural habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the specific microclimates of its native region.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern.
