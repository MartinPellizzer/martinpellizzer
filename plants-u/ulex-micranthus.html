Ulex micranthus

Ulex micranthus, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Ulex micranthus thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
