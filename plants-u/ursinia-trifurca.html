Ursinia trifurca

Ursinia trifurca, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets.

Ursinia trifurca is often found in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
