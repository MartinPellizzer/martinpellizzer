Usionis aurita

Usionis aurita, commonly known as the golden ear cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem with spines and is often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Usionis aurita is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
