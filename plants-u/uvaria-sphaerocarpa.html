Uvaria sphaerocarpa

Uvaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-stemmed uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a valuable species in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Uvaria sphaerocarpa is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
