Uragoga hydrophila

Uragoga hydrophila, commonly known as the water lily of the tropics, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to warm, freshwater environments.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water, while its showy, fragrant flowers bloom above the water surface. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in muddy substrates and absorbs nutrients from the surrounding water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive central spadix that attracts pollinators like beetles and flies.

Uragoga hydrophila plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting aquatic life and maintaining water quality.
