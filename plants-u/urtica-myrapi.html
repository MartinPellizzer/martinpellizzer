Urtica myrapi

Urtica myrapi, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains irritant chemicals in its leaves and stems, which cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. Urtica myrapi is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
