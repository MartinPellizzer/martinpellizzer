Ungulipetalum filipendulum

Ungulipetalum filipendulum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
