Urceolaria capitata

Urceolaria capitata, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white or pink flowers, which are arranged in a head-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Urceolaria capitata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
