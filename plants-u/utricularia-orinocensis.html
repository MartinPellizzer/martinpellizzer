Utricularia orinocensis

Utricularia orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where it relies on capturing microorganisms for sustenance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish in color.

Utricularia orinocensis plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling through its predatory behavior.
