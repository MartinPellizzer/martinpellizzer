Uva hamiltonii

Uva hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's grape, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, clustered leaves and clusters of small, dark blue berries. The plant is notable for its high genetic diversity and is considered a relic from the Tertiary period. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and its role in plant evolution.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is classified as endangered.
