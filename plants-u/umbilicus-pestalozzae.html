Umbilicus pestalozzae

Umbilicus pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pesta's umbilical plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unusual spiral growth pattern and a central stem that resembles a human umbilicus, giving it its peculiar name. The plant has small, waxy leaves that are arranged in a helical fashion around the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
