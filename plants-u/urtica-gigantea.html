Urtica gigantea

Urtica gigantea, commonly known as giant nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stiff, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Urtica gigantea is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles.
