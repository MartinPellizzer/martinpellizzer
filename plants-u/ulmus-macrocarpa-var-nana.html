Ulmus macrocarpa var. nana

Ulmus macrocarpa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf American elm, is a compact cultivar of the American elm.

It is characterized by its small, rounded form and dense foliage, making it suitable for smaller spaces. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. It produces small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
