Uragoga maingayi

Uragoga maingayi, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within rainforests. The plant features striking red and purple flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are broad and lance-shaped.

Uragoga maingayi is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
