Unamia fastigiata

Unamia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the undersides of the fronds. Unamia fastigiata prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
