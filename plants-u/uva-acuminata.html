Uva acuminata

Uva acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed grape or wild grapevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils, which help it climb and spread across surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that eventually develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue.

Uva acuminata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
