Uragoga rufovaginata

Uragoga rufovaginata, commonly known as the red-barked vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored berry that contains several seeds.

Uragoga rufovaginata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
