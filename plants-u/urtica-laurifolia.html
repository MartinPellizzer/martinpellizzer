Urtica laurifolia

Urtica laurifolia, commonly known as broadleaf nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other members known for their stinging hairs. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica laurifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
