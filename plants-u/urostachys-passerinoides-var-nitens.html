Urostachys passerinoides var. nitens

Urostachys passerinoides var. nitens, commonly known as the shining sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water.

This species is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
