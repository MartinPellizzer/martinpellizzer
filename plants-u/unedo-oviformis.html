Unedo oviformis

Unedo oviformis, commonly known as the oval-fruited unedo, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its oval-shaped fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are simple and alternate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
