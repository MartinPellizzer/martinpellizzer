Uragoga cyanocarpa

Uragoga cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue berries, which contain high levels of anthocyanins, giving them their vibrant color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also has traditional uses in local medicine.
