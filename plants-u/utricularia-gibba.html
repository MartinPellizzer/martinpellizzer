Utricularia gibba

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands. Utricularia gibba has a highly branched structure with whorled leaves that aid in photosynthesis.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
