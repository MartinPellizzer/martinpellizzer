Urtica atrovirens var. bianorii

Urtica atrovirens var. bianorii, commonly known as the Bianorii nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are covered in fine, irritating hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
