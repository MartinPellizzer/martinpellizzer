Utricularia sampathii

Utricularia sampathii, commonly known as the Indian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of India and Southeast Asia.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures called utricles that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow water and has delicate, floating leaves with fine, hair-like roots. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, often arranged in a single whorl at the base of the plant.

Utricularia sampathii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by controlling populations of small aquatic invertebrates.
