Ulmus glandulosa

Ulmus glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, fertile soils along stream banks and in bottomlands.
