Urobotrya sparsiflora subsp. sparsiflora

Urobotrya sparsiflora subsp.

sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely-flowered rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
