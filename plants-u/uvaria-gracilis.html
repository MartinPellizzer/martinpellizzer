Uvaria gracilis

Uvaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Uvaria gracilis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
