Uragoga oblonga

Uragoga oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf uragoga, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Uragoga oblonga thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
