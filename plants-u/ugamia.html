Ugamia

Ugamia, commonly known as the "wild orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in various seasonal patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or mountainous regions. Ugamia species have specialized roots that help them absorb nutrients from poor soil conditions.
