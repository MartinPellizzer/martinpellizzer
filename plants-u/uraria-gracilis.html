Uraria gracilis

Uraria gracilis, commonly known as slender uraria, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Native to regions of Asia, it thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
