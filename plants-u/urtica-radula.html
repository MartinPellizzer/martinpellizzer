Urtica radula

Urtica radula, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and leaves with serrated edges, which are covered in tiny, venomous hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Urtica radula produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its root system is fibrous and extensive.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nutrients when cooked.
