Urticastrum gigas

Urticastrum gigas, commonly known as the giant blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs that can cause mild irritation upon contact.

Urticastrum gigas is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
