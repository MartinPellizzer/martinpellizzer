Utricularia subulata var. tridenticulata

Utricularia subulata var. tridenticulata, commonly known as the Trident-needle Bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, bladder-like traps used to capture prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are small and usually green or yellowish, with a distinctive three-lobed structure.

This variety is known for its delicate appearance and unique method of nutrient acquisition through predation.
