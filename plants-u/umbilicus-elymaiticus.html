Umbilicus elymaiticus

Umbilicus elymaiticus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
