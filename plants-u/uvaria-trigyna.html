Uvaria trigyna

Uvaria trigyna, commonly known as the three-stamen uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, making it a valued plant in local cuisines.
