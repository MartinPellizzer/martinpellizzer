Uraria comosa

Uraria comosa, commonly known as the hairy uraria, is a small, perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Uraria comosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
