Unona obanensis

Unona obanensis, commonly known as the Obanensis fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Unona obanensis is considered a species of conservation concern.
