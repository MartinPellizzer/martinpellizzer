Uromyrtus curvipes

Uromyrtus curvipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

Uromyrtus curvipes is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
