Uvaria crassipetala

Uvaria crassipetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Uvaria crassipetala is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
