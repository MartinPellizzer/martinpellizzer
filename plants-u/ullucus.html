Ullucus

Ullucus, commonly known as the Andean tuber, is a unique plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities. Ullucus thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its distinctive appearance and nutritional value make it an important crop in the region's agricultural heritage.
