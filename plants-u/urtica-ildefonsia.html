Urtica ildefonsia

Urtica ildefonsia, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a large herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which release irritants when touched, causing a painful rash. The plant can grow up to 6 meters tall, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

Urtica ildefonsia is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species.
