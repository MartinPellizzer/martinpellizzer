Uragoga padifolia

Uragoga padifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Uragoga padifolia is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
