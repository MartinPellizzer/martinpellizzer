Unona aethiopica

Unona aethiopica, commonly known as the African wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a hollow stem and tendrils that help it climb.

The fruit is edible when mature, though it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
