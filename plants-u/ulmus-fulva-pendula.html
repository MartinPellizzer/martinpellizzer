Ulmus fulva pendula

Ulmus fulva pendula, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a striking tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive, flowing appearance.

It belongs to the genus Ulmus, which includes various species of willow trees native to the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to provide shade and habitat for wildlife.
