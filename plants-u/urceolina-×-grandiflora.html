Urceolina × grandiflora

Urceolina × grandiflora, commonly known as the hybrid urn-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant that results from the cross between two species of the genus Urceolina.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flowers, which are typically a vibrant shade of purple or blue. The plant produces showy, bell-like blooms that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The hybrid is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
