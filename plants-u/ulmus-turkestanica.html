Ulmus turkestanica

Ulmus turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan elm, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures.

It is adapted to dry, continental climates and is often found in river valleys and lowland areas.
