Urginea simensis

Urginea simensis, commonly known as the Abyssinian giant lily, is a striking plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is known for its rapid growth during the rainy season. It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.

However, due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now considered endangered.
