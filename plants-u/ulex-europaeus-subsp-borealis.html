Ulex europaeus subsp. borealis

Ulex europaeus subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern furrowed hawthorn, is a shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Russia. It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat conservation.
