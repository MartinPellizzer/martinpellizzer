Urena lobata f. swartzii

Urena lobata f. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's urena, is a variant of the urena genus native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have five petals with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of ecological conditions.
