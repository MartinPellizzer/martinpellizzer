Uragoga sumbavana

Uragoga sumbavana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical forests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors and unique patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
