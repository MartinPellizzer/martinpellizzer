Urtica eckloniana

Urtica eckloniana, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Urtica eckloniana thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
