Uragoga macrobotrys

Uragoga macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, which float on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its stems provide structural support for the floating leaves.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
