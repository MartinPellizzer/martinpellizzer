Uraria kurzii

Uraria kurzii, commonly known as the Indian burr or wild senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in dry, arid conditions and is often found in wastelands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
