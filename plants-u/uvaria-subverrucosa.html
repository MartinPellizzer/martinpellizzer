Uvaria subverrucosa

Uvaria subverrucosa, commonly known as the rough uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with rough, granular textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are drupes, which are usually yellow or orange when mature.

Uvaria subverrucosa is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
