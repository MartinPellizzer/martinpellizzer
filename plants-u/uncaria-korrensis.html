Uncaria korrensis

Uncaria korrensis, commonly known as the black bamboo or wild indigo, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Uncaria korrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
