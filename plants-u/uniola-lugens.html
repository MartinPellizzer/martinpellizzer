Uniola lugens

Uniola lugens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is adapted to saturated soil conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
