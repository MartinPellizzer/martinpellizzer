Urtica tenuis

Urtica tenuis, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other species of nettles known for their stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a serrated edge and covered in fine, irritating hairs.

Urtica tenuis produces small greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by wind.
