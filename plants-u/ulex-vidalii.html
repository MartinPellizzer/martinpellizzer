Ulex vidalii

Ulex vidalii, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, spiny leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ulex vidalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
