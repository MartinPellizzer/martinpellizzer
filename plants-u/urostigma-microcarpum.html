Urostigma microcarpum

Urostigma microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain tiny, hard seeds. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Urostigma microcarpum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing features that enhance its reproductive success and drought tolerance.
