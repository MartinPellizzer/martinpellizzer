Urtica manillensis

Urtica manillensis, commonly known as the Manila nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of nettles. The plant is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Urtica manillensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
