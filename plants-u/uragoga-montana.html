Uragoga montana

Uragoga montana, commonly known as the mountain uragoga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Uragoga montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
