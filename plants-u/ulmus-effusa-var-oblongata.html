Ulmus effusa var. oblongata

Ulmus effusa var. oblongata, commonly known as the broad-leaf elm, is a variant of the Wych elm native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–8 cm in length. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, often growing to heights of 20–30 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
