Uvaria blumeana

Uvaria blumeana, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or sweet-scented uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Uvaria blumeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
