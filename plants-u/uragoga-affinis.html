Uragoga affinis

Uragoga affinis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, known for its milky sap and specialized pollination mechanisms.
