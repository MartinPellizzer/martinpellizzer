Uva semecarpifolia

Uva semecarpifolia, commonly known as the wild grape or Semecarpus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sarcandraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a strong, disagreeable taste.

Uva semecarpifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
