Urochloa humidicola

Urochloa humidicola, commonly known as watergrass or swamp grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are typically slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Urochloa humidicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
