Unciferia ancistra

Unciferia ancistra, commonly known as the climbing cat's claw, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
