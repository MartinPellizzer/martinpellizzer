Ulex parviflorus subsp. parviflorus

Ulex parviflorus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the common Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in open habitats.
