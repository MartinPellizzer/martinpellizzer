Ursia eximia

Ursia eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf ursia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Ursia eximia is well adapted to harsh conditions, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
