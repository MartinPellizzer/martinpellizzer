Unona odoratissima

Unona odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
