Umbilicus paniculiformis

Umbilicus paniculiformis, commonly known as the spiral umbel plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped inflorescence.

This plant features delicate, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a distinctive, curling pattern that resembles a spiral. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The botanical structure of Umbilicus paniculiformis includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a basal rosette growth habit.

Its floral arrangement is not only visually striking but also plays a role in attracting specific pollinators.
