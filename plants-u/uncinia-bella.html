Uncinia bella

Uncinia bella, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces showy, orange to yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

Uncinia bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
