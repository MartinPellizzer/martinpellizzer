Uromyrtus metrosideros

Uromyrtus metrosideros, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has small, glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small white or pink flowers in summer, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Uromyrtus metrosideros is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy soils.
