Urtica armigera

Urtica armigera, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hollow hairs which release an irritant when touched. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. Urtica armigera is a perennial plant that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, and it is known for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

Its leaves are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
