Ulex grandiflorus

Ulex grandiflorus, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Ulex grandiflorus is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
