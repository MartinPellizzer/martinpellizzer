Urtica stachyophylla

Urtica stachyophylla, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and serrated leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Urtica stachyophylla is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.
