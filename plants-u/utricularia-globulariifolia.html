Utricularia globulariifolia

Utricularia globulariifolia, commonly known as the globe bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladders that trap and digest aquatic insects. The plant typically grows in shallow water, where it can anchor itself with its roots. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for floating on the water's surface.

Utricularia globulariifolia is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
