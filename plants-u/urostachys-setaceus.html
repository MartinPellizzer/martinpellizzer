Urostachys setaceus

Urostachys setaceus, commonly known as the tufted pincushion grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a pincushion, with small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Urostachys setaceus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
