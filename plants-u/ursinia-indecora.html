Ursinia indecora

Ursinia indecora, commonly known as the common ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ursinia indecora is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
