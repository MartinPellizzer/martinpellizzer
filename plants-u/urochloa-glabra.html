Urochloa glabra

Urochloa glabra, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Urochloa glabra is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
