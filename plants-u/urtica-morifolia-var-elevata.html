Urtica morifolia var. elevata

Urtica morifolia var. elevata, commonly known as the elevated stinging nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle species characterized by its taller growth habit.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is known for its finely serrated leaves and dense covering of stinging hairs. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its stinging properties, it requires careful handling during collection or cultivation.
