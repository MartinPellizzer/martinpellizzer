Utricularia dichotoma subsp. monanthos

Utricularia dichotoma subsp.

monanthos, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures used to trap prey.

The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species exhibits a unique reproductive strategy involving both sexual and vegetative propagation.
