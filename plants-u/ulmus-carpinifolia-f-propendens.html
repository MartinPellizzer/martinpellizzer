Ulmus carpinifolia f. propendens

Ulmus carpinifolia f. propendens, commonly known as the weeping wych elm, is a unique cultivar of the wych elm.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang gracefully from the main trunk, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. This form is a natural mutation of the species, often found in the wild or cultivated for its aesthetic value. The leaves are typically oval and dark green, providing a lush canopy throughout the growing season.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes where its visual impact can be appreciated.
