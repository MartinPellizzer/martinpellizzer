Ursinia erectifolia

Ursinia erectifolia, commonly known as the upright leaf ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Ursinia erectifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
