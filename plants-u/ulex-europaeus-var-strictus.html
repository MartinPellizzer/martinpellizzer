Ulex europaeus var. strictus

Ulex europaeus var. strictus, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a perennial shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important food source for early pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
