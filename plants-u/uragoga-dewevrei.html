Uragoga dewevrei

Uragoga dewevrei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm months, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Uragoga dewevrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its association with the night sky.
