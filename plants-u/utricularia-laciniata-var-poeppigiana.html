Utricularia laciniata var. poeppigiana

Utricularia laciniata var. poeppigiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in a dense spike.

It is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its ease of cultivation and unique trapping mechanism.
