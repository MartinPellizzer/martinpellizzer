Urostachys reversus

Urostachys reversus, commonly known as the reverse-headed feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender leaves and distinctive inflorescence that features upward-curving spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its unique floral structure has made it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Urostachys reversus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
