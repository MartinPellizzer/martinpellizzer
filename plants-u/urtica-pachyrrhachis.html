Urtica pachyrrhachis

Urtica pachyrrhachis, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation. Urtica pachyrrhachis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
