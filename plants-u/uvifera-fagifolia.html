Uvifera fagifolia

Uvifera fagifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Uvifera fagifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
