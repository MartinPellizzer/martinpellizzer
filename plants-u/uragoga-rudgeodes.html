Uragoga rudgeodes

Uragoga rudgeodes, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to absorb nutrients efficiently from well-draining soil.

Uragoga rudgeodes is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
