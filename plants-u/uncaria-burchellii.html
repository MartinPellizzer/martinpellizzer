Uncaria burchellii

Uncaria burchellii, commonly known as the climbing cat's claw, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, which have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
