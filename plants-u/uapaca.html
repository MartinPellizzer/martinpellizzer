Uapaca

Uapaca, commonly known as the wild mango or African mango, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It comprises several species, many of which are native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are typically fleshy and edible, with a sweet, tropical flavor.

The genus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional remedies.
