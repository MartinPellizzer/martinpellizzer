Ursinia tenuifolia subsp. ciliaris

Ursinia tenuifolia subsp.

ciliaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent, reflexed petals, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
