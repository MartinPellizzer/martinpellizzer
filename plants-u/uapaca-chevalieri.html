Uapaca chevalieri

Uapaca chevalieri, commonly known as the African wild date, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, often fissured bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing edible, sweet-tasting seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savanna woodlands.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
