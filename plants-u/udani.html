Udani

Udani, commonly known as the "wild ginger" or *Zingiber officinale* var. *udani*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Udani is also valued for its culinary applications, similar to common ginger.
