Ursinia montana

Ursinia montana, commonly known as the mountain ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Ursinia montana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
