Udora occidentalis

Udora occidentalis, commonly known as the western udora, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the western hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Udora occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
