Ursia

Ursia, commonly known as bear's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Ursia is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and medicinal studies.
