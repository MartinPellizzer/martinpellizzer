Utricularia spartea var. subspicata

Utricularia spartea var. subspicata, commonly known as the spike-leaved bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

The varietal form subspicata is distinguished by its elongated, spiculate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.
