Urtica chamaedryoides var. orizabae

Urtica chamaedryoides var. orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican nettle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped, serrated leaves with fine hairs that can cause an irritating reaction upon contact. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
