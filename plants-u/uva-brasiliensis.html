Uva brasiliensis

Uva brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grape or Brazilian mulberry, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions make it an important species in ecological studies.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native habitat.
