Urtica elevata

Urtica elevata, commonly known as the tall nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica elevata is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
