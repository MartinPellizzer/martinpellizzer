Uraria cordifolia

Uraria cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf uraria, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often arranged in a heart-like pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Uraria cordifolia is often found in dry, rocky areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
