Urtica semidentata

Urtica semidentata, commonly known as the smooth nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
