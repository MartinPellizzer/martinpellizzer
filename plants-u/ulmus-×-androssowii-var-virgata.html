Ulmus × androssowii var. virgata

Ulmus × androssowii var. virgata, commonly known as the Chinese field maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse via wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
