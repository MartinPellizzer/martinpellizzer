Urophyllum enneandrum

Urophyllum enneandrum, commonly known as the nine-lobed pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the fruit is a small, fleshy capsule.

Urophyllum enneandrum is adapted to a range of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.
