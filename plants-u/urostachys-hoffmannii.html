Urostachys hoffmannii

Urostachys hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their cruciform flowers. The plant typically features compact rosettes of dark green, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
