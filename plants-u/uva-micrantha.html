Uva micrantha

Uva micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its small, dark purple berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
