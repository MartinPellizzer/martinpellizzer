Uncinia caespitosa var. minor

Uncinia caespitosa var. minor, commonly known as the slender hookgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
