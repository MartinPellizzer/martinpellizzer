Uragoga platyneura

Uragoga platyneura, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense mats.

Uragoga platyneura plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
