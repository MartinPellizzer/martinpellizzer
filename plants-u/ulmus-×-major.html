Ulmus × major

Ulmus × major, commonly known as the American elm hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is a natural hybrid between the American elm (U. americana) and the slippery elm (U. rubra). The tree is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and a broad, vase-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
