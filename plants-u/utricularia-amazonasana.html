Utricularia amazonasana

Utricularia amazonasana, commonly known as the Amazonian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water, relying on its specialized structures for nutrient acquisition. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a distinctive saccate shape.

Utricularia amazonasana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by controlling populations of microorganisms and contributing to the aquatic food web.
