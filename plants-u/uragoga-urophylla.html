Uragoga urophylla

Uragoga urophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Uragoga urophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
