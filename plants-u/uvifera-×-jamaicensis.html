Uvifera × jamaicensis

Uvifera × jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican hibiscus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Hibiscus coccineus and Hibiscus jamaicensis.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant produces fibrous, woody capsules containing seeds, and its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The botanical features of Uvifera × jamaicensis make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
