Uvaria monilifera

Uvaria monilifera, commonly known as the bead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a distinctive floral structure that attracts pollinators.

Uvaria monilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
