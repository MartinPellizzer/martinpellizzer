Uva grandiflora

Uva grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered grapevine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights, often climbing or trailing along structures. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Uva grandiflora is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of natural dyes.
