Umbilicus parviflorus

Umbilicus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered umbilicus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its delicate, small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Umbilicus parviflorus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
