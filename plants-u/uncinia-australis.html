Uncinia australis

Uncinia australis, commonly known as the southern hookgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
