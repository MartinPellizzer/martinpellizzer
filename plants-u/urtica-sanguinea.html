Urtica sanguinea

Urtica sanguinea, commonly known as blood nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact.

Urtica sanguinea is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides.
