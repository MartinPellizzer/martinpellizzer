Urostigma brachypodum

Urostigma brachypodum, commonly known as the shortfoot grass, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Urostigma brachypodum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
