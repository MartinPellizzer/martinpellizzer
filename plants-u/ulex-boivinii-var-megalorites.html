Ulex boivinii var. megalorites

Ulex boivinii var. megalorites, commonly known as the giant bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
