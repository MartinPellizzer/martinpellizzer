Urtica quadristipulata

Urtica quadristipulata, commonly known as the four-leaf nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica quadristipulata is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
