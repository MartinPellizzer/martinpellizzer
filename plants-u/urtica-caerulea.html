Urtica caerulea

Urtica caerulea, commonly known as blue nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and small blue or purple flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Urtica caerulea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
