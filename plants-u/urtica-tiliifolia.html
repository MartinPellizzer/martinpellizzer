Urtica tiliifolia

Urtica tiliifolia, commonly known as common stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems that are covered in tiny, hollow hairs which release an irritating substance when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Urtica tiliifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
