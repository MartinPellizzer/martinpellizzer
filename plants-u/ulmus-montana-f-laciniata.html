Ulmus montana f. laciniata

Ulmus montana f. laciniata, commonly known as the mountain elm or serrated mountain elm, is a variant of the European mountain elm.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique foliage and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
