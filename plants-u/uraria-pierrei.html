Uraria pierrei

Uraria pierrei, commonly known as the desert bean, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its pods contain edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

Uraria pierrei plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
