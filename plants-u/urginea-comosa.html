Urginea comosa

Urginea comosa, commonly known as the African begonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. Urginea comosa thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and ornamental value in horticulture.
