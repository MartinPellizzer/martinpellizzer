Uraria retroflexa

Uraria retroflexa, commonly known as the small uraria or wild uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are usually hairy and contain several seeds.

Uraria retroflexa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded lands.
