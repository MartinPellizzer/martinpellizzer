Uncinia rigidula

Uncinia rigidula, commonly known as the rigid hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces tiny, hooked fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it can be invasive in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
