Urera gilletii

Urera gilletii, commonly known as the silky oaks or velvetwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
