Uvaria ceylanica

Uvaria ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon date tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, date-like flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Uvaria ceylanica thrives in humid, warm climates and is typically found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
