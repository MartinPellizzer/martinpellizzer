Uvifera rosea

Uvifera rosea, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Uvifera rosea is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
