Uragoga apocynifolia

Uragoga apocynifolia, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes and come in shades of pink to white.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
