Unona cerasoides

Unona cerasoides, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, pale bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while the flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
