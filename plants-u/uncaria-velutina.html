Uncaria velutina

Uncaria velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing elliptical seeds. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 14,000 species of flowering plants.
