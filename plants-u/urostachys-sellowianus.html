Urostachys sellowianus

Urostachys sellowianus, commonly known as the Sellow's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Urostachys sellowianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, airy foliage.
