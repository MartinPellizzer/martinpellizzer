Uncaria lanosa f. setiloba

Uncaria lanosa f. setiloba, commonly known as the hairy climbing shrub, is a botanical variant of the genus Uncaria, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and twining stems.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, pubescent surface. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
