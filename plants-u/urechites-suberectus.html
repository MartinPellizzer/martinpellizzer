Urechites suberectus

Urechites suberectus, commonly known as the erect urechite, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Urechites suberectus is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
