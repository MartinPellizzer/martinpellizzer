Uraria crinita var. macrostachya

Uraria crinita var. macrostachya, commonly known as the hairy uraria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, hairy leaflets that give it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
