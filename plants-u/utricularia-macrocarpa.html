Utricularia macrocarpa

Utricularia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating, translucent leaves that contain specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. These bladders are filled with water and can rapidly expel it to create suction, capturing small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes.

Its unique morphology and predatory mechanism make it an intriguing subject in botanical studies.
