Uruparia

Uruparia, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, often violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Uruparia has strap-like leaves that are often grass-like in appearance, and it is found in various parts of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
