Ulmus minor f. variegata

Ulmus minor f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated field elm, is a decorative cultivar of the field elm.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white and green patterns. This plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy and a moderate growth rate. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and landscapes for its unique foliage.
