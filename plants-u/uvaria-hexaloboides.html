Uvaria hexaloboides

Uvaria hexaloboides, commonly known as the six-lobed snake fruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with six lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark purple to black berries. Its berries are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
