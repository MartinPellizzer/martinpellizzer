Uapaca kirkiana var. kwangoensis

Uapaca kirkiana var. kwangoensis, commonly known as the Kwango velvetleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
