Uvifera

Uvifera, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces small, fuzzy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Uvifera is often cultivated for its fruit and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
