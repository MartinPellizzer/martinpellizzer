Unona filipes

Unona filipes, commonly known as the slender fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions in Southeast Asia and the Pacific. The plant has long, narrow, and finely divided leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or along stream banks.

Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.
