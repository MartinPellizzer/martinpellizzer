Urtica spirealis

Urtica spirealis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Urtica spirealis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
