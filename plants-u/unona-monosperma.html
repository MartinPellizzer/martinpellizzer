Unona monosperma

Unona monosperma, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces a fruit that resembles a breadfruit, with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various traditional dishes.

The plant also has cultural and medicinal significance in many African communities.
