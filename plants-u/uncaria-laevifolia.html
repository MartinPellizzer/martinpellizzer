Uncaria laevifolia

Uncaria laevifolia, commonly known as the evergreen climbing shrub, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Its botanical significance lies in its morphological features and ecological role within its native habitats.

Uncaria laevifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
