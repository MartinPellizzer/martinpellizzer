Urtica membranacea

Urtica membranacea, commonly known as the membranous nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species known for their stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture and are often covered in fine stinging trichomes.

Urtica membranacea produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
