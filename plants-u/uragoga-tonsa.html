Uragoga tonsa

Uragoga tonsa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Uragoga tonsa is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in certain areas.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along waterways.
