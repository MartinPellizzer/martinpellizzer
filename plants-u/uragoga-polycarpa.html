Uragoga polycarpa

Uragoga polycarpa, commonly known as the African starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped tomatoes. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is edible, with a crisp, juicy texture and a mildly sweet flavor.

Uragoga polycarpa is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritional benefits.
