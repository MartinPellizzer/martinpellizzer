Uragoga colorata

Uragoga colorata, commonly known as the colorful uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored petals that range from vibrant reds to deep purples and yellows. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its showy flowers.

Uragoga colorata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
