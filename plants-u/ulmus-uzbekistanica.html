Ulmus uzbekistanica

Ulmus uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek elm, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and urban landscapes.
