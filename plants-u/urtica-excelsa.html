Urtica excelsa

Urtica excelsa, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The flowers are small and green, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects.
