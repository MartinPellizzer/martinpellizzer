Urginea rautanenii

Urginea rautanenii, commonly known as the giant African snail, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and a distinctive, elongated, spiraling stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the evening. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
