Uncinia nemoralis

Uncinia nemoralis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features silvery-gray, hairy leaves that are spoon-shaped and often covered in fine, hooked hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Uncinia nemoralis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
