Urtica urophylla

Urtica urophylla, commonly known as the hairy nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its erect stems covered in fine, stinging hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also bear stinging hairs on their surfaces.

Urtica urophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
