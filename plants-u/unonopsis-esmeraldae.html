Unonopsis esmeraldae

Unonopsis esmeraldae, commonly known as the Emeraldleaf Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often nestled among moss and other epiphytic vegetation. Its unique morphology includes slender stems and specialized roots adapted for nutrient absorption from the air.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Unonopsis esmeraldae is considered a conservation concern.
