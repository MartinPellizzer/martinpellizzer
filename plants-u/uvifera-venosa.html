Uvifera venosa

Uvifera venosa, commonly known as the veined grapevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with a distinctive veined pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that can range in color from yellow to red.

Uvifera venosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
