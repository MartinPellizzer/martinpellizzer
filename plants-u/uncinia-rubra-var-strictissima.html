Uncinia rubra var. strictissima

Uncinia rubra var. strictissima, commonly known as the rigid hookweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
