Unona cauliflora

Unona cauliflora, commonly known as the edible cauliflower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, edible inflorescences that resemble cauliflower. The plant produces its flowers and fruits at the base of the stem, a trait known as cauliflorous growth. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a mild, slightly sweet aroma.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and culinary value.
