Urochloa javanica

Urochloa javanica, commonly known as Java grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage and erosion control.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
