Urochloa helopus var. hochstetteriana

Urochloa helopus var. hochstetteriana, commonly known as the Hochstetter's blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its fibrous root system makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
