Uvaria godefroyana var. nervosa

Uvaria godefroyana var. nervosa, commonly known as the veined uvaria, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually hairy leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
