Ubium scandens

Ubium scandens, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous roots and tendrils for support.
