Unonopsis cauliflora

Unonopsis cauliflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries.

Unonopsis cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
