Urochloa falcifera

Urochloa falcifera, commonly known as silverleaf stargrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Urochloa falcifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
