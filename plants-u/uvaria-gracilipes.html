Uvaria gracilipes

Uvaria gracilipes, commonly known as the slender uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are often followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Uvaria gracilipes is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
