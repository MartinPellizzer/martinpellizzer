Uragoga sulphurea

Uragoga sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a distinct waxy texture.

Uragoga sulphurea is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
