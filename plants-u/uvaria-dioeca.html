Uvaria dioeca

Uvaria dioeca, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or the white-flowered uvaria, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Uvaria dioeca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
