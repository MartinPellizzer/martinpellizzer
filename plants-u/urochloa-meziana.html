Urochloa meziana

Urochloa meziana, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Urochloa meziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
