Urtica dodartii

Urtica dodartii, commonly known as the African stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and fine hairs that release an irritating chemical when touched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
