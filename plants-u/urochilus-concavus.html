Urochilus concavus

Urochilus concavus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in waterlogged soils. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in spikes. Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Urochilus concavus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and pond landscapes.
