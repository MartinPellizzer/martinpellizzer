Uwarowia chrysanthemifolia

Uwarowia chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are bright yellow to green in color, which give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
