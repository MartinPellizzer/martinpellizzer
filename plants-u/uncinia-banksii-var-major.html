Uncinia banksii var. major

Uncinia banksii var. major, commonly known as the giant hairpin grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

This variant is notable for its larger size compared to other varieties of Uncinia banksii.
