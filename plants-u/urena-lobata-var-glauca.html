Urena lobata var. glauca

Urena lobata var. glauca, commonly known as blue urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber due to its strong, fibrous stems.
