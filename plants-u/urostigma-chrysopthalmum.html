Urostigma chrysopthalmum

Urostigma chrysopthalmum, commonly known as the golden-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shady environments. Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
