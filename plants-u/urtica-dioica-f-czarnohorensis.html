Urtica dioica f. czarnohorensis

Urtica dioica f. czarnohorensis, commonly known as the Black Mountain nettle, is a variant of stinging nettle characterized by its distinctive dark green, hairy leaves and robust growth habit.

It is native to the Carpathian region and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features both male and female flowers on separate plants, typical of its dioecious nature. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that release irritants, causing a stinging sensation upon contact.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
