Urginea maritima

Urginea maritima, commonly known as the sea onion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy bulbs and sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, appearing in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
