Ulearum sagittatum

Ulearum sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to specific regions of North America.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a distinctive, pointed form. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
