Uraria paniculata

Uraria paniculata, commonly known as the Indian burr or goat's bean, is a leguminous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in wastelands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and forage in agricultural settings.
