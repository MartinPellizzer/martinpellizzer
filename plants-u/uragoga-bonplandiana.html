Uragoga bonplandiana

Uragoga bonplandiana, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a striking plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchids in tropical environments.

Uragoga bonplandiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
