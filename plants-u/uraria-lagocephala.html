Uraria lagocephala

Uraria lagocephala, commonly known as the broad-leaved uraria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Uraria lagocephala is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
