Urtica sykesii

Urtica sykesii, commonly known as the African stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its heart-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Urtica sykesii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
