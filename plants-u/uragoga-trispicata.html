Uragoga trispicata

Uragoga trispicata, commonly known as the three-spined uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tripartite spines, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Uragoga trispicata is noted for its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators.
