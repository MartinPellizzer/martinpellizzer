Urochloa platytaenia

Urochloa platytaenia, commonly known as flat-leaf grass or broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in dry and disturbed environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Urochloa platytaenia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded lands.
