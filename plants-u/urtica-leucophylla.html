Urtica leucophylla

Urtica leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its soft, pale green leaves with a fuzzy texture and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica leucophylla typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.
