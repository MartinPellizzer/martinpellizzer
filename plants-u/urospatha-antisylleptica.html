Urospatha antisylleptica

Urospatha antisylleptica, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is herbaceous, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with rich, well-drained soil.

Urospatha antisylleptica is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
