Urceola acuteacuminata var. polyneura

Urceola acuteacuminata var. polyneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf urceola, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
