Ulmus pumila var. sabulosa

Ulmus pumila var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a rough, sandpaper-like texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging landscapes.
