Uvaria burahol

Uvaria burahol, commonly known as the wild pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with simple, alternate, and usually elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its fruits are edible and have a mild, peppery taste, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
