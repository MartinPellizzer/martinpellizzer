Ulmus boissieri

Ulmus boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's elm, is a species of flowering plant in the Ulmaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East, particularly in countries like Turkey, Syria, and Iraq. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, winged fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
