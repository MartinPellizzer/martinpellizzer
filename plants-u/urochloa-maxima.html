Urochloa maxima

Urochloa maxima, commonly known as giant grass or African grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage or erosion control.

Urochloa maxima is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping due to its dense growth habit and resilience.
