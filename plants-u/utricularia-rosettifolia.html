Utricularia rosettifolia

Utricularia rosettifolia, commonly known as the Rosette-leaved Bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves, which form a dense, floating structure on the water's surface. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. These traps are triggered by the movement of prey, allowing the plant to obtain essential nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for study in both aquatic ecosystems and plant physiology.
