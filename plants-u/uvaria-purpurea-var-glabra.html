Uvaria purpurea var. glabra

Uvaria purpurea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth uvaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale purple flowers, which are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or black when mature.
