Urtica freireiformis

Urtica freireiformis, commonly known as the Freire's nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small greenish-white flowers in clusters. Urtica freireiformis is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
