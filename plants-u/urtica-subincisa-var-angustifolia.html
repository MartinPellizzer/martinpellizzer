Urtica subincisa var. angustifolia

Urtica subincisa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, stinging hairs that cause a rash upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, spherical berries.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
