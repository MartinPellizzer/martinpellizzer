Uraria cylindracea

Uraria cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical uraria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, yellowish pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

Uraria cylindracea is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
