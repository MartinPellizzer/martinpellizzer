Urginavia altissima

Urginavia altissima, commonly known as the tall urginavia, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
