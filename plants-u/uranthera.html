Uranthera

Uranthera, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive spur. Native to various regions in Africa, the genus includes several species that thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological roles within their native habitats.
