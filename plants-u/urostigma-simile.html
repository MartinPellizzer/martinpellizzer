Urostigma simile

Urostigma simile, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue or white petals that resemble the appearance of a ghost, hence its common name. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It blooms during the late summer and early autumn, producing small, clustered flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Urostigma simile is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it retain moisture during long periods of drought.
