Urostachys chamaepeuce var. maximus

Urostachys chamaepeuce var. maximus, commonly known as the giant blue flag iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland habitats.
