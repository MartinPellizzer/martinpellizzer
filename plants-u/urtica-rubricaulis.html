Urtica rubricaulis

Urtica rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its reddish stems, which are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are also covered in stinging trichomes.

Urtica rubricaulis produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are wind-pollinated.
