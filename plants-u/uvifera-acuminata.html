Uvifera acuminata

Uvifera acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed uvifera, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Uvifera acuminata is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
