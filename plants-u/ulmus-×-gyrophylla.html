Ulmus × gyrophylla

Ulmus × gyrophylla, commonly known as the American slippery elm.

It is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark that peels easily. The tree produces small, pendulous catkins and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is well adapted to moist, fertile soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The inner bark contains a mucilage that has been used traditionally for its soothing properties.
