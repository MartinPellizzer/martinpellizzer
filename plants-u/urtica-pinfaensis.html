Urtica pinfaensis

Urtica pinfaensis, commonly known as the Pinfaensis nettle, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Urtica pinfaensis is known for its fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
