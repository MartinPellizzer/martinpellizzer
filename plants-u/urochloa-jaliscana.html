Urochloa jaliscana

Urochloa jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican grass or Jaliscan grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Urochloa jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
