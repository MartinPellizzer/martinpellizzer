Unona purpurata

Unona purpurata, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Unona purpurata is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
