Uvaria gaertneri

Uvaria gaertneri, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by yellowish or orange berries. Its fruits are edible, though the pulp is typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines.
