Urtica intermedia

Urtica intermedia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing an irritating reaction upon contact with the skin. Urtica intermedia thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
