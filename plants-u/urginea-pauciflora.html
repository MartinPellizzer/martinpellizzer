Urginea pauciflora

Urginea pauciflora, commonly known as the desert lily, is a bulbous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a large, fleshy bulb that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. It blooms during the rainy season, typically in summer, and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
