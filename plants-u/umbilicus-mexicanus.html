Umbilicus mexicanus

Umbilicus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican navelwort, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its unusual, umbilicated (navel-like) structure at the base of its stem, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are small, succulent, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Umbilicus mexicanus is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its ecological and evolutionary significance.
