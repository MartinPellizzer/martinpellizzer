Uruparia laevigata

Uruparia laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf uruparia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, smooth hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds.
