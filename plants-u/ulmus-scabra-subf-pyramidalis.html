Ulmus scabra subf. pyramidalis

Ulmus scabra subf.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal wych elm, is a subspecies of the wych elm native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal growth habit and smooth, greyish bark.

The leaves are elliptical, with a rough upper surface and a smooth lower surface. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is less commonly cultivated than other elms but is valued for its ornamental form and historical significance.
