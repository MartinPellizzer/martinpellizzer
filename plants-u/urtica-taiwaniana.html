Urtica taiwaniana

Urtica taiwaniana, commonly known as Taiwan stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Urtica taiwaniana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
