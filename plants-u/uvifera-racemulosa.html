Uvifera racemulosa

Uvifera racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered grapefruit plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rutaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit, which resembles a small grapefruit, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.
