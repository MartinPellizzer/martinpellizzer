Ursinia montana subsp. tenuiloba

Ursinia montana subsp.

tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
