Urostachys linifolius var. minor

Urostachys linifolius var. minor, commonly known as the lesser threadleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
