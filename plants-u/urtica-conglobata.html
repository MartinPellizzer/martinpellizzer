Urtica conglobata

Urtica conglobata, commonly known as the clustered nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain irritants that cause a painful reaction upon contact with the skin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Urtica conglobata produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are usually pollinated by wind.
