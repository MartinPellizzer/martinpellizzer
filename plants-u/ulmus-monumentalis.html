Ulmus monumentalis

Ulmus monumentalis, commonly known as the European wych elm, is a large, deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown with heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. Its bark is grey and fissured, becoming more rugged with age.

Ulmus monumentalis is valued for its historical significance and ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
