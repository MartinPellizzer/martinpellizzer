Urochloa brizantha var. ciliata

Urochloa brizantha var. ciliata, commonly known as coastal brachiaria, is a tropical grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, flat leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is highly resistant to drought and grazing.

This variety is widely used in pasture systems due to its high forage value and adaptability to various soil types.
