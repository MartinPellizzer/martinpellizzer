Ulmaria filipendula var. floreplena

Ulmaria filipendula var. floreplena, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a cultivar of the lady's mantle plant.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
