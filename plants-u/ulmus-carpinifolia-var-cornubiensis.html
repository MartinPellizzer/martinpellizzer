Ulmus carpinifolia var. cornubiensis

Ulmus carpinifolia var. cornubiensis, commonly known as the Cornish elm, is a rare and ancient variety of elm native to the UK.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and distinctive, elongated, pendulous fruits. The tree has a slow growth rate and is often found in shaded, moist woodland environments. Its bark is deeply furrowed and becomes more rugged with age.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation and ecological studies.
