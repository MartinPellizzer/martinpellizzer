Uncaria laevigata

Uncaria laevigata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several pairs of elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges due to its adaptability.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
