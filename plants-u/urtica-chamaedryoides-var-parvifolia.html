Urtica chamaedryoides var. parvifolia

Urtica chamaedryoides var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are typically borne on erect, branched stems. Its leaves and stems contain stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, a defense mechanism against herbivores.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
