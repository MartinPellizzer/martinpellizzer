Urticastrum costatum

Urticastrum costatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. Urticastrum costatum is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
