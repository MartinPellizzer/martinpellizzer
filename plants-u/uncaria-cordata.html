Uncaria cordata

Uncaria cordata, commonly known as the Southern cross or hookwood, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
