Ugni myricoides var. roraimensis

Ugni myricoides var. roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima huito, is a species of flowering plant native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Guyana.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in montane rainforest environments. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that attracts various bird species.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to the unique ecological conditions of the tepui landscapes.
