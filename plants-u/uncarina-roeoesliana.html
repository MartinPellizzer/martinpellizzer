Uncarina roeoesliana

Uncarina roeoesliana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has smooth, green stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating.

Uncarina roeoesliana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
