Uvifera sparsifolia

Uvifera sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved uvifera, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are arranged sparsely along the branches. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with low soil moisture.

Uvifera sparsifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
