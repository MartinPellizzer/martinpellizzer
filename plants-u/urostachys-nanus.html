Urostachys nanus

Urostachys nanus, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early summer. The plant has a dense, mat-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Urostachys nanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
