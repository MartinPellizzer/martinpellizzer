Utricularia ibarensis

Utricularia ibarensis, commonly known as the Ibar Valley bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor aquatic environments, relying on captured prey for essential nutrients. Its flowers are typically green or yellow, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Utricularia ibarensis plays a unique ecological role in its habitat by contributing to the local food web through both predation and seed dispersal.
