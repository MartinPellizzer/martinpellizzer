Uvaria pygmaea

Uvaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf satinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round, black berries. Uvaria pygmaea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
