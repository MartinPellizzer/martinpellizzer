Ussuria firma

Ussuria firma, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Ussuria firma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
