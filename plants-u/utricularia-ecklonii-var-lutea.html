Utricularia ecklonii var. lutea

Utricularia ecklonii var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are often submerged. It produces yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
