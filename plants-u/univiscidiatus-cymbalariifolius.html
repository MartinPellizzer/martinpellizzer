Univiscidiatus cymbalariifolius

Univiscidiatus cymbalariifolius, commonly known as the spiral-leaved orchid, is a rare and distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its spirally arranged, narrow, and often wavy-edged leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study.
