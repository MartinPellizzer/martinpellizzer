Uva macropoda

Uva macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, fragrant fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, crimson flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a support structure for its vigorous growth.

The fruit is often used in juices, desserts, and as a flavoring agent due to its unique taste.
