Uragoga jasminiflora

Uragoga jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling those of jasmine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
