Uvularia hamiltoniana

Uvularia hamiltoniana, commonly known as the yellow uvularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, branching stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. Its most striking feature is its clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
