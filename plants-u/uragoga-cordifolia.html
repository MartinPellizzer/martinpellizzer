Uragoga cordifolia

Uragoga cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved uragoga, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are not only ornamental but also have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Uragoga cordifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
