Uragoga ochroleuca

Uragoga ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-leaved uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically light green to white in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered together. Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Uragoga ochroleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
