Uvifera trinitatis

Uvifera trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad grapefruit, is a unique citrus species native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also has culinary and medicinal uses in local traditions.
