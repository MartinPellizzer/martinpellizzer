Uragoga glauca

Uragoga glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Uragoga glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
