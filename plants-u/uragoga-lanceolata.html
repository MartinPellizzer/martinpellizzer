Uragoga lanceolata

Uragoga lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
