Urophyllum chlamydanthum

Urophyllum chlamydanthum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Urophyllum chlamydanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
