Utricularia peranomala

Utricularia peranomala, commonly known as the red-rooted bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used for trapping prey. The plant typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its roots are often red or orange in color, which is a distinguishing feature.

Utricularia peranomala relies on photosynthesis for energy but supplements its nutrient intake by digesting small aquatic organisms.
