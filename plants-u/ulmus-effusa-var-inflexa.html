Ulmus effusa var. inflexa

Ulmus effusa var. inflexa, commonly known as the Chinese Hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the elm family, Ulmaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, blue-black fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals.
