Urochloa deflexa

Urochloa deflexa, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, pale green to brown flowers in panicles.

Urochloa deflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
