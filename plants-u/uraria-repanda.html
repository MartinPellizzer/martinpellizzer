Uraria repanda

Uraria repanda, commonly known as the Indian sida, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
