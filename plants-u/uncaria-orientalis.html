Uncaria orientalis

Uncaria orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or wild coffee, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red calyx. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Uncaria orientalis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
