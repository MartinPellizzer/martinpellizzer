Uragoga morindoides

Uragoga morindoides, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The fruit is a yellow or orange-colored drupe, often with a star-shaped pattern on its surface, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
