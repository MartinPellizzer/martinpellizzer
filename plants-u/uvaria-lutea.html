Uvaria lutea

Uvaria lutea, commonly known as the yellow uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Uvaria lutea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
