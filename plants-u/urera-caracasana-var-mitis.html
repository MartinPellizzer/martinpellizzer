Urera caracasana var. mitis

Urera caracasana var. mitis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible for some wildlife but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
