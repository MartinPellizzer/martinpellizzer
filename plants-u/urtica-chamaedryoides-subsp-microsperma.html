Urtica chamaedryoides subsp. microsperma

Urtica chamaedryoides subsp.

microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, stinging hairs.

The flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain minute seeds.
