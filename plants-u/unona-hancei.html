Unona hancei

Unona hancei, commonly known as the Chinese mountain grape, is a woody vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red or purple berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Unona hancei thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
