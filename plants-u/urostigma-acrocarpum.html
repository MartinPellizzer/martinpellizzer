Urostigma acrocarpum

Urostigma acrocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in sustainable agriculture.
