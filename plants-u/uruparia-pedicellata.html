Uruparia pedicellata

Uruparia pedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in environments with ample sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Uruparia pedicellata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil.
