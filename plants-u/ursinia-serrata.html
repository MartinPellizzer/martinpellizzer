Ursinia serrata

Ursinia serrata, commonly known as the Cape iron plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ursinia serrata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
