Urtica goglado

Urtica goglado, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains fine, venomous hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica goglado is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
