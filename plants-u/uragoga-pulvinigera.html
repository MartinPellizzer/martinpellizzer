Uragoga pulvinigera

Uragoga pulvinigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually greenish-white in color.

Uragoga pulvinigera is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
