Uva membranacea

Uva membranacea, commonly known as the membranous grape or membranous vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of small, round berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Uva membranacea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
