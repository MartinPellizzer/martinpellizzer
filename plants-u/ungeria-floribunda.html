Ungeria floribunda

Ungeria floribunda, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white, and are borne on short stalks.

U. floribunda prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
