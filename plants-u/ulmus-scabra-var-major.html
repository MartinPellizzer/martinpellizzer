Ulmus scabra var. major

Ulmus scabra var. major, commonly known as the roughleaf elms, is a variant of the European wych elm.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elongated, narrow leaves. This tree is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and is native to parts of Europe. It can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its historical significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
