Uniola virgata

Uniola virgata, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its silvery sheen. It is typically found in well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies.

Uniola virgata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
