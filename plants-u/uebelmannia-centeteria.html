Uebelmannia centeteria

Uebelmannia centeteria, commonly known as the Ceara pine, is a species of conifer native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
