Utleya

Utleya, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have long, slender stems and are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic. Utleya species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in greenhouses or as houseplants.

Their flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making them popular among horticultural enthusiasts.
