Unisema cordata f. latifolia

Unisema cordata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed heartleaf, is a variant of the species Unisema cordata, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth texture, while the plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit.

The f. latifolia form is distinguished by its wider leaf blades compared to the typical variety.
