Uvaria foetida

Uvaria foetida, commonly known as the stinking elder, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Uvaria foetida is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable in its flowers and leaves.
