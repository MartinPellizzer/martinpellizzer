Urginea virens

Urginea virens, commonly known as the green squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Urginea virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
