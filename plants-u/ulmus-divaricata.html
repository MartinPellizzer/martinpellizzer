Ulmus divaricata

Ulmus divaricata, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

Ulmus divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
