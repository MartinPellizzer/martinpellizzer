Uebelmannia gummifera subsp. gummifera

Uebelmannia gummifera subsp.

gummifera, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and a distinctive, swollen stem that stores latex.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is the source of natural rubber. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with high rainfall.

Due to its economic importance, it is often cultivated for commercial rubber production.
