Urobotrya parviflora

Urobotrya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Urobotrya parviflora is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone environments.
