Uvularia sessilis

Uvularia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume-like fruits. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
