Utricularia afromontana

Utricularia afromontana, commonly known as the African mountain bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Africa.

It belongs to the genus Utricularia, which is characterized by its unique trapping structures called bladders used to capture small aquatic organisms. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and stream banks. Its leaves are often submerged, and it produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Utricularia afromontana plays a role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.
