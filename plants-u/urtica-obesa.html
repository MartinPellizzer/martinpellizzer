Urtica obesa

Urtica obesa, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Urtica obesa contains stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
