Urbanolophium glaziovii

Urbanolophium glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
