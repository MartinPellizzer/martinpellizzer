Urginea undulata subsp. caeculi

Urginea undulata subsp.

caeculi, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the summer months.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
