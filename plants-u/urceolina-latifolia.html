Urceolina latifolia

Urceolina latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf urn plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its leaves are glossy and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

Urceolina latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
