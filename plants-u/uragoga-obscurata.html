Uragoga obscurata

Uragoga obscurata, commonly known as the dark uragoga, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deep green, waxy leaves and striking violet-pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is classified as an endangered species.
