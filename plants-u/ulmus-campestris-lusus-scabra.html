Ulmus campestris lusus scabra

Ulmus campestris lusus scabra, commonly known as the scaly field elm, is a variant of the field elm native to Europe.

It is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it has been cultivated in various regions for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
