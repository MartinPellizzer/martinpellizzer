Uragoga ipecacuanha

Uragoga ipecacuanha, commonly known as the Brazilian ipecac, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical climates.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
