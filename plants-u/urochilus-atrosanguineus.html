Urochilus atrosanguineus

Urochilus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils within heathlands and shrublands. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually deep purple to black in color, with five lobes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Urochilus atrosanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
