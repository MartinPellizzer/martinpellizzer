Urtica glabrata

Urtica glabrata, commonly known as smooth nettle, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Urtica glabrata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
