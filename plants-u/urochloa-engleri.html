Urochloa engleri

Urochloa engleri, commonly known as coastal lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in coastal dunes and scrublands.

Urochloa engleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal environments.
