Utricularia grandivesiculosa

Utricularia grandivesiculosa, commonly known as the large bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water, where it can anchor itself with its root-like structures. Its leaves are often submerged, while the stems may float on the water surface.

Utricularia grandivesiculosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
