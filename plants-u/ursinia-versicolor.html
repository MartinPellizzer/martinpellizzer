Ursinia versicolor

Ursinia versicolor, commonly known as the goldenweed or yellow star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble stars. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ursinia versicolor is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.
