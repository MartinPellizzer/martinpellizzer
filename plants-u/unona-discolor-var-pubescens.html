Unona discolor var. pubescens

Unona discolor var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false heather, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
