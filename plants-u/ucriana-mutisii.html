Ucriana mutisii

Ucriana mutisii, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and mountainous environments, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Ucriana mutisii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
