Uralepis pooides

Uralepis pooides, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Uralepis pooides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
