Ursinia chamomillifolia

Ursinia chamomillifolia, commonly known as the chamomile dropseed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ursinia chamomillifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
