Urtica meyeri

Urtica meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine hairs that can cause an irritating rash upon contact. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica meyeri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
