Urtica stipulosa

Urtica stipulosa, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and hollow stems.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to various regions across the Americas. The plant contains histamine and formic acid in its leaves, which cause a painful rash upon contact. Urtica stipulosa typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
