Uapaca nitida

Uapaca nitida, commonly known as the African wild date or wild mango, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor.

It is often found in moist, semi-deciduous forests and is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
