Ulex europaeus f. plenus

Ulex europaeus f. plenus, commonly known as the full-fruited Scotch broom, is a variant of the common Scotch broom.

It is a perennial shrub native to Europe, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
