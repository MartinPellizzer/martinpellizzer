Uragoga fraterna

Uragoga fraterna, commonly known as the brother's root, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a unique, fibrous root system that is highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its blooming season occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Uragoga fraterna is now classified as an endangered species.
