Ulmus × androssowii var. subhirsuta

Ulmus × androssowii var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the Chinese wenchuan elm, is a hybrid species within the elm family, Ulmaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, pendulous catkins in the spring.

This variety is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
