Uvularia caroliniana

Uvularia caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
