Urtica triplinervia

Urtica triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, and its small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant's stinging hairs on its leaves and stems can cause skin irritation upon contact.
