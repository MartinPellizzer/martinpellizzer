Urtica kioviensis

Urtica kioviensis, commonly known as the Kiovi stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, hollow hairs that cause a tingling sensation.

Urtica kioviensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
