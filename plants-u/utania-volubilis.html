Utania volubilis

Utania volubilis, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

Utania volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
