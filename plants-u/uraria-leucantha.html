Uraria leucantha

Uraria leucantha, commonly known as white uraria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a climbing vine, often found in dry and semi-arid regions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
