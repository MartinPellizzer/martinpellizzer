Uvularia leschenaultiana

Uvularia leschenaultiana, commonly known as the bell-shaped false gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, bell-shaped, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
