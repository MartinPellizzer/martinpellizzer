Uragoga erythrophylla

Uragoga erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-leaved uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts with its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Uragoga erythrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
