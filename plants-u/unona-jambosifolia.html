Unona jambosifolia

Unona jambosifolia, commonly known as the Java apple or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring smooth, grey bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits resembling apples in appearance.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
