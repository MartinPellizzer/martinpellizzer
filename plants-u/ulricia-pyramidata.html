Ulricia pyramidata

Ulricia pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Ulricia pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine conditions.
