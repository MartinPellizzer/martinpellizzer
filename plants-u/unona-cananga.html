Unona cananga

Unona cananga, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet, intoxicating scent. The plant has a succulent, branching structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Unona cananga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of love and beauty in various cultures.
