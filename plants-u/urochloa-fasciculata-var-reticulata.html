Urochloa fasciculata var. reticulata

Urochloa fasciculata var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often inconspicuous compared to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth make it a valuable forage plant in some agricultural systems.
