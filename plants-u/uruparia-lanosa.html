Uruparia lanosa

Uruparia lanosa, commonly known as the woolly uruparia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or lanose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
