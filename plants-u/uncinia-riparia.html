Uncinia riparia

Uncinia riparia, commonly known as the prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Uncinia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
