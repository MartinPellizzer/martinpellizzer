Uva narum

Uva narum, commonly known as the purple grapevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruit, resembling small grapes, is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

Uva narum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
