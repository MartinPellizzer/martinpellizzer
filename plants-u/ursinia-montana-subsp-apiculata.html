Ursinia montana subsp. apiculata

Ursinia montana subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the mountain ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The species produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
