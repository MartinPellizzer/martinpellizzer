Uromyrtus artensis

Uromyrtus artensis, commonly known as the Artensis myrtle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to low-nutrient soils and has a deep root system to access water in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
