Uralepis aristulata

Uralepis aristulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower head that develops into a crown of white florets. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Uralepis aristulata is adapted to arid conditions and has a long flowering period, typically from late winter to early summer.
