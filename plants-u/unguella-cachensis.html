Unguella cachensis

Unguella cachensis, commonly known as the Cachan guava, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish skin. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in its native habitat.
