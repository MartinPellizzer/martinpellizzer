Urtica urentissima

Urtica urentissima, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and fine, venomous hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and marshes. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Urtica urentissima is also a valuable source of nutrients and has been utilized in various culinary and medicinal applications.
