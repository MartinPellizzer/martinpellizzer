Uva gracilis

Uva gracilis, commonly known as the slender grape or slender vine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, black berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use. Uva gracilis thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often growing along walls or in rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
