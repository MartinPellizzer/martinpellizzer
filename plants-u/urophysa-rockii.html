Urophysa rockii

Urophysa rockii, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically features fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct lip that attracts pollinators.

Urophysa rockii is often found growing on rocky slopes and in limestone areas, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions.
