Urochloa quintasii

Urochloa quintasii, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to warm climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often inconspicuous.

Urochloa quintasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
