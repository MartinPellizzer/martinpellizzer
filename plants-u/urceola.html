Urceola

Urceola, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a type of carnivorous plant that thrives in nutrient-poor environments.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structures, which form deep, hollow pitchers filled with digestive fluids. These pitchers are often brightly colored to attract prey such as insects. The plant's botanical features include specialized glands that secrete enzymes to break down captured prey.

Urceola species are typically found in wet, acidic soils and are adapted to absorb nutrients through their modified leaves.
