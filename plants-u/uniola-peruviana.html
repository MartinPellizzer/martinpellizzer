Uniola peruviana

Uniola peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian grass or Peruvian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in regions with low rainfall, such as the Andean foothills.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
