Ulmaria quinqueloba

Ulmaria quinqueloba, commonly known as five-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five distinct lobes on each petal. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often emit a mild, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Ulmaria quinqueloba is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
