Ulex cantabricus

Ulex cantabricus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Ulex cantabricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
