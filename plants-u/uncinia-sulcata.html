Uncinia sulcata

Uncinia sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, with prominent longitudinal veins.

Uncinia sulcata is adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
