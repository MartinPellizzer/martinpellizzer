Ulmus campestris var. viminalis

Ulmus campestris var. viminalis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows along water bodies, thriving in moist soils. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The tree is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often planted in parks and gardens.
