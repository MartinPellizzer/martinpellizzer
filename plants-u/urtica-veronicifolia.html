Urtica veronicifolia

Urtica veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, often forming dense stands in disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it is wind-pollinated.

The plant contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
