Urtica copeyana

Urtica copeyana, commonly known as the Mexican stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a fibrous structure.

Urtica copeyana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
