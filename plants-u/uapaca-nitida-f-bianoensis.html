Uapaca nitida f. bianoensis

Uapaca nitida f. bianoensis, commonly known as the white-fruited kola, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, white or pale yellow fruits that are edible.

The fruits are notable for their high content of caffeine and other alkaloids, making it a significant species in both traditional and commercial contexts.
