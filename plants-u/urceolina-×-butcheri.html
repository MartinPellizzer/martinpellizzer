Urceolina × butcheri

Urceolina × butcheri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and intriguing plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Urceolina thailandica and Urceolina macrophylla, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically features vibrant, showy flowers with a unique coloration that ranges from deep purples to bright yellows. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that highlights the genetic diversity within orchid populations.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
