Uragoga conferta

Uragoga conferta, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub, with stems that often root at the nodes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorching.

Uragoga conferta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
