Urginea brachystachys

Urginea brachystachys, commonly known as the small-stalked giant lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, tall stem with broad, strap-like leaves. Its bulbs are toxic and contain compounds such as lycorine, which can be harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
