Urtica naucleiflora

Urtica naucleiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Urtica naucleiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.
