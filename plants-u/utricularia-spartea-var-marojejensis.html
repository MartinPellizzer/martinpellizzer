Utricularia spartea var. marojejensis

Utricularia spartea var. marojejensis, commonly known as the African horned frog bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used for capturing prey. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic environments and its role in local ecosystems.
