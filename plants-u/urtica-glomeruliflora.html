Urtica glomeruliflora

Urtica glomeruliflora, commonly known as the clustered nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Urtica glomeruliflora is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles.
