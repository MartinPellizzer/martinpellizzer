Ugni warscewiczii

Ugni warscewiczii, commonly known as the Andean huito, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

Ugni warscewiczii thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
