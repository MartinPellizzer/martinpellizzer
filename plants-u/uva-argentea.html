Uva argentea

Uva argentea, commonly known as silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Uva argentea is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
