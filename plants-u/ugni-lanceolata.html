Ugni lanceolata

Ugni lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf huito, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine.

Ugni lanceolata thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests.
