Ulmus campestris var. rotundifolia

Ulmus campestris var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf elm, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are usually larger and more rounded compared to the standard field elm, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in landscaping.
