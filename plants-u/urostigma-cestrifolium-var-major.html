Urostigma cestrifolium var. major

Urostigma cestrifolium var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved urostigma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually branched and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
