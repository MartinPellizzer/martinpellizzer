Unona selanica

Unona selanica, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which resemble spiders due to their intricate, spidery petals. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Unona selanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in its natural habitat.
