Uapaca ferruginea var. carnotiana

Uapaca ferruginea var. carnotiana, commonly known as the Carnot's velvetball, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are highly valued for their flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
