Urtica urentivelutina

Urtica urentivelutina, commonly known as the velvet stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. Urtica urentivelutina thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of traditional medicinal use.
