Urospatha hostmannii

Urospatha hostmannii, commonly known as the Hostmann's uruguayan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Urospatha hostmannii is adapted to arid conditions and plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
