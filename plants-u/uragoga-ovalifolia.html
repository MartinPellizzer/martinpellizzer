Uragoga ovalifolia

Uragoga ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
