Uragoga brachybotrya

Uragoga brachybotrya, commonly known as the short-fruited uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, small, clustered fruits. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
