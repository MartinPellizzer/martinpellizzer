Uragoga loxensis

Uragoga loxensis, commonly known as the Loxense Flameleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant orange and red leaves that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires high humidity and indirect sunlight to thrive.

Uragoga loxensis is also noted for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a valuable addition to indoor spaces.
