Ulmus glabra f. concavifolia

Ulmus glabra f. concavifolia, commonly known as the smooth elm with concave leaves, is a variant of the American elm.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive concave base. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
