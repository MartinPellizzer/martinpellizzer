Utricularia emarginata

Utricularia emarginata, commonly known as the marginal bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, translucent bladders that trap aquatic prey, allowing the plant to supplement its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor water environments. This species typically grows in shallow, still freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are whorled and often have a distinctive marginal notch, giving it its common name.

The plant is relatively small, with flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color.
