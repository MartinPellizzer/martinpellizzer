Uraria hamosa var. formosana

Uraria hamosa var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan uraria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
