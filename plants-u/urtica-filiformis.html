Urtica filiformis

Urtica filiformis, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its thin, hairless stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Urtica filiformis is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Its leaves and stems are covered in fine stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
