Urophyllum pellacalyx

Urophyllum pellacalyx, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized structures for trapping small aquatic organisms.

It possesses bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to capture and digest prey, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients from its environment. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its common name.

This carnivorous plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling small invertebrate populations.
