Uncinia brevicaulis var. laticarpa

Uncinia brevicaulis var. laticarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruit hookweed, is a variant of the hookweed species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, hook-like structures at the base of its flowering stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
