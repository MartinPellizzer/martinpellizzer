Urechites pinetorum

Urechites pinetorum, commonly known as the pine tree lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in temperate forest environments.

It forms a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, enabling it to absorb nutrients from the air and substrate. The thallus is typically grayish-green, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, lobed appearance. It is often found on the bark of pine trees, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling and habitat provision for microorganisms.

This lichen is an indicator species for air quality and forest health.
