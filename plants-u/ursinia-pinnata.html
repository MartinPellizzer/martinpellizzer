Ursinia pinnata

Ursinia pinnata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Ursinia pinnata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
