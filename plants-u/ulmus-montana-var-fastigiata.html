Ulmus montana var. fastigiata

Ulmus montana var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf elm, is a cultivar of the mountain elm.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
