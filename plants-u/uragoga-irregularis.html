Uragoga irregularis

Uragoga irregularis, commonly known as the irregular leaf fern, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its highly variable leaf morphology, with fronds that can exhibit irregular shapes and sizes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant's ability to adapt to different light conditions makes it a resilient and fascinating subject for botanical study.
