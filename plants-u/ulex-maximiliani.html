Ulex maximiliani

Ulex maximiliani, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
