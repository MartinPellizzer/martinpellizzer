Uragoga uberabana

Uragoga uberabana, commonly known as the Amazonian starleaf, is a tropical plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often adorned with unique patterns resembling stars. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Uragoga uberabana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
