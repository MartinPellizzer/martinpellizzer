Udora brasiliensis

Udora brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

Udora brasiliensis is valued for its hard, durable wood used in musical instruments and furniture.
