Uraria campanulata

Uraria campanulata, commonly known as the bell-flowered uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it typically grows in grassy or rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pale yellow to greenish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Uraria campanulata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
