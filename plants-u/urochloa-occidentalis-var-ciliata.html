Urochloa occidentalis var. ciliata

Urochloa occidentalis var. ciliata, commonly known as silky buffalograss, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and low-maintenance landscaping. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its flowering spikes are usually inconspicuous, and it is often used in erosion control due to its robust root system.
