Ulmus wheatleyi

Ulmus wheatleyi, commonly known as the Mexican elm, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
