Urtica cypria

Urtica cypria, commonly known as Cyprus nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, stinging hairs on both sides. Urtica cypria typically grows in moist, shaded areas and produces small greenish-white flowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
