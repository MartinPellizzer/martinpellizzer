Uvaria macrophylla var. microcarpa

Uvaria macrophylla var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
