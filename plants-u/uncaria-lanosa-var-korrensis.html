Uncaria lanosa var. korrensis

Uncaria lanosa var. korrensis, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
