Urostachys tuerckheimi

Urostachys tuerckheimi, commonly known as the Kashmir stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the Himalayas.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, often blooming in late summer. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Urostachys tuerckheimi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
