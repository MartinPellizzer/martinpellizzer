Uvifera costata

Uvifera costata, commonly known as the coastal lantana, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to coastal conditions. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found near the shoreline.

Uvifera costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
