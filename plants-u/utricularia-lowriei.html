Utricularia lowriei

Utricularia lowriei, commonly known as the Lowrie's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

Utricularia lowriei plays an important role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
