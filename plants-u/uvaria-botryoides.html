Uvaria botryoides

Uvaria botryoides, commonly known as the grapefruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
