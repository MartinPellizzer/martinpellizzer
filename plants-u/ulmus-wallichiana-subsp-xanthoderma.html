Ulmus wallichiana subsp. xanthoderma

Ulmus wallichiana subsp.

xanthoderma, commonly known as the golden-leaved elm, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its distinctive golden to yellowish foliage, which contrasts with its dark, bark-like trunk.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various temperate climates.
