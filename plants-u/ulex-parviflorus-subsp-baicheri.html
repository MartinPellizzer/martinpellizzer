Ulex parviflorus subsp. baicheri

Ulex parviflorus subsp.

baicheri, commonly known as the Baicher's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and is well-suited to salty and windy environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for small insects and birds.
