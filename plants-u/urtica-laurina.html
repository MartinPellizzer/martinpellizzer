Urtica laurina

Urtica laurina, commonly known as the velvetleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Urtica laurina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its growth habit includes both upright and creeping stems, making it a versatile plant in various ecological settings.
