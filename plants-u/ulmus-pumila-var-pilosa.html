Ulmus pumila var. pilosa

Ulmus pumila var. pilosa, commonly known as the Japanese elm or hairy elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm native to East Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and distinctive hairy foliage. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
