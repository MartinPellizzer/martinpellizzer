Uvaria aromatica

Uvaria aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant starfruit or sweet starfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Uvaria aromatica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
