Urtica minima

Urtica minima, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a small perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, hollow stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it is often found in clusters along the ground.

Urtica minima is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects.
