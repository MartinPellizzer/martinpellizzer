Unifolium sessilifolium

Unifolium sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, sessile leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
