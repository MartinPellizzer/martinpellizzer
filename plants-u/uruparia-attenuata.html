Uruparia attenuata

Uruparia attenuata, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, drought-prone conditions.
