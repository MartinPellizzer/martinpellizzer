Uraria poilanei

Uraria poilanei, commonly known as the African burr pea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of hairy, three-lobed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Uraria poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
