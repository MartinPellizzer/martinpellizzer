Urochloa trichopodioides

Urochloa trichopodioides, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Urochloa trichopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
