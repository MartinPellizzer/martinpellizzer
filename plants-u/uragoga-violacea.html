Uragoga violacea

Uragoga violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Uragoga violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
