Ulex parviflorus var. fernandoi

Ulex parviflorus var. fernandoi, commonly known as the Fernando's buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
