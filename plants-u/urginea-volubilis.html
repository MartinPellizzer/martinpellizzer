Urginea volubilis

Urginea volubilis, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in the spring. The plant has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Urginea volubilis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
