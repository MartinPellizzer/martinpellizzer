Urostachys taxifolius var. nitens

Urostachys taxifolius var. nitens, commonly known as the silver thread grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
