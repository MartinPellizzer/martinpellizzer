Ursinia hispida

Ursinia hispida, commonly known as the rough ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Ursinia hispida is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
