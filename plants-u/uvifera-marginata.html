Uvifera marginata

Uvifera marginata, commonly known as the marginated uvilla, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive white or pale margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Uvifera marginata is adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
