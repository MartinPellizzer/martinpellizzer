Urostachys phlegmaria var. densifolius

Urostachys phlegmaria var. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved phlegmaria, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the growing season, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
