Uapaca nitida var. suffrutescens

Uapaca nitida var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the red milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, clustered leaves that turn red in the dry season. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a food source for various animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
