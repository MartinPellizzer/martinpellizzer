Unona cochinchinensis

Unona cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese date or longan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, sweet, reddish berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly cultivated for both its fruit and timber.
