Uapaca kirkiana var. goetzei

Uapaca kirkiana var. goetzei, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a stout trunk, compound leaves, and a distinctive, clustered fruiting habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
