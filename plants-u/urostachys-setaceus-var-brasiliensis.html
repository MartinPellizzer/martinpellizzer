Urostachys setaceus var. brasiliensis

Urostachys setaceus var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It features fine, needle-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths, often covering the ground in a carpet-like fashion. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Poaceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive characteristics.
