Uvaria diplocampta

Uvaria diplocampta, commonly known as the two-chambered uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, which turns red when mature, and contains a single seed.

Uvaria diplocampta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
