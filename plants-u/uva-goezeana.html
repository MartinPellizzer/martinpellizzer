Uva goezeana

Uva goezeana, commonly known as the Goetz's fig or the mountain fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common fig and breadfruit. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. Its fruit, which resembles a small fig, is edible and has a sweet, fig-like flavor.

Uva goezeana is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
