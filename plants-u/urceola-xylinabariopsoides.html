Urceola xylinabariopsoides

Urceola xylinabariopsoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed urn plant, is a unique species characterized by its urn-shaped inflorescence.

It possesses slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered together, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study.
