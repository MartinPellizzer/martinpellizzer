Urtica linkiana

Urtica linkiana, commonly known as the Himalayan stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, hollow stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive stinging hair that releases an irritant when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Urtica linkiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
