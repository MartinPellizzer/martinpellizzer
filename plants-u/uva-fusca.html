Uva fusca

Uva fusca, commonly known as the black grape or wild grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its woody vines, compound leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries. Uva fusca is often found growing in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in full sun.

Its berries are not typically cultivated for wine but are used in local traditions and traditional medicine.
