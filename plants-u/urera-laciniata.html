Urera laciniata

Urera laciniata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink mombin, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a yellow or orange berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
