Urginea riparia

Urginea riparia, commonly known as the river onion, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a large, fleshy bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves that can reach up to two meters in height. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with six petals arranged in a star shape.

Urginea riparia is often found near rivers and streams, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
