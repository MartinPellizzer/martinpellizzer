Uraspermum berteroi

Uraspermum berteroi, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
