Ulex nanus f. lusitanicus

Ulex nanus f. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese Heath, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and hairy.

It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its habitat.
