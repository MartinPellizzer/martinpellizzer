Ursinia pulchra

Ursinia pulchra, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ursinia pulchra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
