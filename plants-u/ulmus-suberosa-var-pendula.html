Ulmus suberosa var. pendula

Ulmus suberosa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping elm, is a variant of the European wych elm.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The tree has a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and broadly ovate, with a serrated margin.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal, though it is less hardy than other elm species and susceptible to certain diseases.
