Uragoga prunifolia

Uragoga prunifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically pink or red in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Uragoga prunifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
