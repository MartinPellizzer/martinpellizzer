Urtica vesicaria

Urtica vesicaria, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-like structures called trichomes, which release irritating chemicals when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found throughout temperate regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Urtica vesicaria is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
