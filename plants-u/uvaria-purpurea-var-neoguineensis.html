Uvaria purpurea var. neoguineensis

Uvaria purpurea var. neoguineensis, commonly known as the purple uvaria, is a species of flowering plant in the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Native to the rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands, it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a subject of interest in botanical research.
