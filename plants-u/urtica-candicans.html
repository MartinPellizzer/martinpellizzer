Urtica candicans

Urtica candicans, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hollow hairs that release an irritating chemical when touched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are often reddish and hairy. Urtica candicans is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
