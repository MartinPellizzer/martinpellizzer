Uraria retusa

Uraria retusa, commonly known as the slender uraria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in dry, arid environments. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Uraria retusa is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
