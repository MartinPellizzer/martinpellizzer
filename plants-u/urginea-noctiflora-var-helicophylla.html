Urginea noctiflora var. helicophylla

Urginea noctiflora var. helicophylla, commonly known as the moonflower helicophylla, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
