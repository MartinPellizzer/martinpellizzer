Umbellularia californica var. fresnensis

Umbellularia californica var. fresnensis, commonly known as the California olive, is a deciduous tree native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, olive-like fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.

This variety is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
