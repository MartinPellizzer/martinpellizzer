Uncinia tenuis f. firmula

Uncinia tenuis f. firmula, commonly known as the slender hookweed form.

It is a variant of the species Uncinia tenuis, characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more robust stems. The plant typically features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variations and ecological adaptability.
