Uncinia compacta var. alpina

Uncinia compacta var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine hookweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, often silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
