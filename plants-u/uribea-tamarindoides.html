Uribea tamarindoides

Uribea tamarindoides, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, and its distinctive yellow-orange fruit pods. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its fruit contains a sweet, sticky pulp surrounding flat, hard seeds.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
