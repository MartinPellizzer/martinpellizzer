Ulex rivasgodayanus

Ulex rivasgodayanus, commonly known as the Iberian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for bird dispersal. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean shrublands.

Ulex rivasgodayanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
