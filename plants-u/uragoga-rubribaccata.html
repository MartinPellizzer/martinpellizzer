Uragoga rubribaccata

Uragoga rubribaccata, commonly known as the red-berry uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a distinct venation pattern.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing before the berries mature.
