Ursiniopsis

Ursiniopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures.

Due to its habitat specificity and rarity, Ursiniopsis is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
