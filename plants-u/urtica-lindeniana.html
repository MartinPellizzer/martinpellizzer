Urtica lindeniana

Urtica lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine stinging hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica lindeniana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay.
