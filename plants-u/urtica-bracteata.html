Urtica bracteata

Urtica bracteata, commonly known as the bracted nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and tiny, stinging hairs on the underside. It produces small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves.

Urtica bracteata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
