Urtica aestuans

Urtica aestuans, commonly known as the tropical stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Urtica aestuans is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
