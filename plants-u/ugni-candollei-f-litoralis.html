Ugni candollei f. litoralis

Ugni candollei f. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a variant of the Ugni candollei species native to the coastal regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and forested areas. The plant is adapted to salt spray and moderate maritime climates, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in coastal botany.
