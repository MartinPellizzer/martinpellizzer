Uragoga sarmentosa

Uragoga sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping uragoga, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows along the ground, producing small, clustered flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and ecological studies.
