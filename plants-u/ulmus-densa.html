Ulmus densa

Ulmus densa, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture and a pointed tip. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is valued for its adaptability and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
