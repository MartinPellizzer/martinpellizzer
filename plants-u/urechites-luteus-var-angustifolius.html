Urechites luteus var. angustifolius

Urechites luteus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in grasslands and along the edges of woodlands.
