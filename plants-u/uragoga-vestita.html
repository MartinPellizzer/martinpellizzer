Uragoga vestita

Uragoga vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf or velvet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate with a waxy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Uragoga vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
