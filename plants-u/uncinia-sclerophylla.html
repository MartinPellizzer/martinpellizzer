Uncinia sclerophylla

Uncinia sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved hookweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fibrous roots help it thrive in dry, rocky soils, and it is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

Uncinia sclerophylla is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient species in its native range.
