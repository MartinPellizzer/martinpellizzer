Utricularia lundii

Utricularia lundii, commonly known as the African bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland habitats in Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters and relies on these traps for essential nutrients. Its leaves are usually submerged, while its stems may float on the water surface.

Utricularia lundii is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, contributing to the control of microorganism populations.
