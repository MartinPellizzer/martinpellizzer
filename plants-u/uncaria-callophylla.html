Uncaria callophylla

Uncaria callophylla, commonly known as the yellow cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, claw-like stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other plants with similar medicinal properties.
