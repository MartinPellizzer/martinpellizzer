Uragoga triphylla

Uragoga triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe.

Uragoga triphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
