Uncinia compacta var. petriei

Uncinia compacta var. petriei, commonly known as the dwarf hook-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hook-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
