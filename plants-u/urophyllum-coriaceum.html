Urophyllum coriaceum

Urophyllum coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf urophyllum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru.

Urophyllum coriaceum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
