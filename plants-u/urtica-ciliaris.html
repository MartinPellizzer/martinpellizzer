Urtica ciliaris

Urtica ciliaris, commonly known as the velvetleaf or stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions around the world. Its flowers are small and green, often clustered in spikes, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Urtica ciliaris is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
