Urostigma validum

Urostigma validum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Urostigma validum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
