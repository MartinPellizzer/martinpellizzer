Utricularia paucifolia

Utricularia paucifolia, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features delicate, thread-like stems and small, leafless shoots that bear minute bladders used to trap prey. Its flowers are usually white or pink and are arranged in spikes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Utricularia paucifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
