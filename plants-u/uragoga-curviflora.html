Uragoga curviflora

Uragoga curviflora, commonly known as the curlyflower plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue, which are often clustered together. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Uragoga curviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
