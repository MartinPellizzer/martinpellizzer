Utricularia caerulea var. smithiana

Utricularia caerulea var. smithiana, commonly known as the blue horned waterwheel, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowering stems rise above the water surface.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lentibulariaceae and is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic life.
