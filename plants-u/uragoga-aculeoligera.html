Uragoga aculeoligera

Uragoga aculeoligera, commonly known as the spiny leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent, sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
