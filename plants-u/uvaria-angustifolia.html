Uvaria angustifolia

Uvaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Uvaria angustifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
