Urtica hispidula

Urtica hispidula, commonly known as the hairy nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica hispidula typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
