Urostachys bonapartei

Urostachys bonapartei, commonly known as the Bonaparte's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Urostachys bonapartei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
