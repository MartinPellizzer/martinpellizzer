Uva dulcis

Uva dulcis, commonly known as the date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, green fruits that ripen to a sweet, amber color. The fruit is rich in sugars, fiber, and nutrients, making it a valuable food source.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Arecaceae and is cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
