Unxia anthemifolia

Unxia anthemifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Unxia anthemifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
