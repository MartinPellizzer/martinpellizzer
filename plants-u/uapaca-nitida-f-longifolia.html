Uapaca nitida f. longifolia

Uapaca nitida f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved velvetbean, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
