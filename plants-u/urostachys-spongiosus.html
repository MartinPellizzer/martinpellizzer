Urostachys spongiosus

Urostachys spongiosus, commonly known as the spongey sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its natural environment.

Urostachys spongiosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
