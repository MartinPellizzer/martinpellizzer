Urtica bifida

Urtica bifida, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems that release an irritating sap when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Urtica bifida thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
