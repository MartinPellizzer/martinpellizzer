Uncaria domatiifera

Uncaria domatiifera, commonly known as the creeping climbing vine, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for traditional herbal use.
