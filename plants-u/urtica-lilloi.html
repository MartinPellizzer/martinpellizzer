Urtica lilloi

Urtica lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's nettle, is a species of stinging nettle native to South America.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Urtica lilloi thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its leaves contain irritant compounds, making contact with the skin cause a stinging sensation.
