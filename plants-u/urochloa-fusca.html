Urochloa fusca

Urochloa fusca, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed seed heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Urochloa fusca is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
