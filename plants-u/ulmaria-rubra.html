Ulmaria rubra

Ulmaria rubra, commonly known as red hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features a stout, short stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually reddish-purple in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. Ulmaria rubra is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
