Upopion cordatum

Upopion cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Upopion cordatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
