Uvedalia linearis var. lutea

Uvedalia linearis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow linear sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden green in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Uvedalia linearis var. lutea plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
