Urtica herniarifolia

Urtica herniarifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle or southern nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that cause a skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Urtica herniarifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.
