Univiscidiatus heptadactylus

Univiscidiatus heptadactylus, commonly known as the seven-fingered violet, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its unique morphology.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, arranged in a tight cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories.
