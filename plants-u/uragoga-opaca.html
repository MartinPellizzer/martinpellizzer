Uragoga opaca

Uragoga opaca, commonly known as the dark uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
