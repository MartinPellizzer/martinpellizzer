Unona ferruginea var. brevifolia

Unona ferruginea var. brevifolia, commonly known as the rusty ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, rust-colored bark and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
