Uragoga argentata

Uragoga argentata, commonly known as silverleaf uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Uragoga argentata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
