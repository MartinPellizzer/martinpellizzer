Urena lobata var. rhombifolia

Urena lobata var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Urena lobata var. rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
