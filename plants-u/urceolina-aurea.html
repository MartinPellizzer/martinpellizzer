Urceolina aurea

Urceolina aurea, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Urceolina aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
