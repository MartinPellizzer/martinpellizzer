Uapaca nitida var. mulengo

Uapaca nitida var. mulengo, commonly known as the Mulengo velvetball, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its edible fruits, which are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or woodland areas.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
