Uragoga zenkeri

Uragoga zenkeri, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically borne on erect stems and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Uragoga zenkeri is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
