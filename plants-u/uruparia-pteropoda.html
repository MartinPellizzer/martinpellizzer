Uruparia pteropoda

Uruparia pteropoda, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a deep root system that anchors it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming at night and attracting pollinators like bats and moths.

Uruparia pteropoda plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
