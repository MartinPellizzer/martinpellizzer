Uragoga longicuspis

Uragoga longicuspis, commonly known as the long-toothed uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and sharply serrated leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Uragoga longicuspis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
