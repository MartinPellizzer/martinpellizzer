Unona cordifolia

Unona cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a dense canopy, making it a significant component of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
