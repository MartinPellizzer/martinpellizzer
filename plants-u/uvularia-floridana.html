Uvularia floridana

Uvularia floridana, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its unique floral structure and native habitat make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
