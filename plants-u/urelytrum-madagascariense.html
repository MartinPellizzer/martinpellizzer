Urelytrum madagascariense

Urelytrum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers with vibrant purple and white petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
