Urtica fernandesiana

Urtica fernandesiana, commonly known as the Fernandes's nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, hair-like structures that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Urtica fernandesiana is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insect species.
