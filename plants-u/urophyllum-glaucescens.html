Urophyllum glaucescens

Urophyllum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.

Urophyllum glaucescens is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.
