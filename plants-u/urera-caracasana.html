Urera caracasana

Urera caracasana, commonly known as the Caribbean lily or wild lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
