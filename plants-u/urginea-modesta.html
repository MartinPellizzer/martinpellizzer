Urginea modesta

Urginea modesta, commonly known as the purple squill, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, and they wither after flowering.

Urginea modesta is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
