Unona bibracteata

Unona bibracteata, commonly known as the two-bract euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Unona bibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
