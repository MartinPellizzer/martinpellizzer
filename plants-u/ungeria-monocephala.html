Ungeria monocephala

Ungeria monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as savannas and along riverbanks.

Ungeria monocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
