Uva-ursi intricata

Uva-ursi intricata, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically red or dark green, and it produces tiny white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its intricate, branched structure, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Uva-ursi intricata is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
