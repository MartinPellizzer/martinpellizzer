Uragoga trisulcata

Uragoga trisulcata, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has long, slender stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree bark.

Uragoga trisulcata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
