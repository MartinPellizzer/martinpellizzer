Uvaria pyrifolia

Uvaria pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic and eye-catching appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

Uvaria pyrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
