Uragoga citrifolia

Uragoga citrifolia, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, powdery bloom. Its fruit, which resembles a small bread loaf, is edible when mature and has a starchy, sweet flavor.

The tree is highly valued for its nutritional content and plays a significant role in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
