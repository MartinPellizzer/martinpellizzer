Urtica recurvata

Urtica recurvata, commonly known as the recurved nettle, is a flowering plant in the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically in the summer months. Urtica recurvata is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed soils and along woodland edges.
