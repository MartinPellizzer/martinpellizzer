Ursinia glandulosa

Ursinia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Ursinia glandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
