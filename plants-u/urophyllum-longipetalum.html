Urophyllum longipetalum

Urophyllum longipetalum, commonly known as the long-petaled urnflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a dense, composite flower head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Urophyllum longipetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
