Uragoga globosa

Uragoga globosa, commonly known as the globe uragoga, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy fruit that resembles a small, round globe, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Uragoga globosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
