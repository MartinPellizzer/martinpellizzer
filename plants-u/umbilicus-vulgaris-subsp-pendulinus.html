Umbilicus vulgaris subsp. pendulinus

Umbilicus vulgaris subsp.

pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
