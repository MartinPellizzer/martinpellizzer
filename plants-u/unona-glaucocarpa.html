Unona glaucocarpa

Unona glaucocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, blue-black fruits that are edible and have a slightly tart flavor. Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.

Unona glaucocarpa thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
