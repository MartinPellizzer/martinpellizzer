Ulmus glabra var. pendula

Ulmus glabra var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping elm, is a cultivar of the American elm characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

It features a broad, spreading crown with long, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental tree is valued for its graceful appearance and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
