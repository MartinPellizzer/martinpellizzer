Uncaria parviflora

Uncaria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered climbing vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is often found growing in dense forest undergrowth, where it climbs trees for support.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal remedies.
