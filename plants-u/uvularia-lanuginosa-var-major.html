Uvularia lanuginosa var. major

Uvularia lanuginosa var. major, commonly known as the woolly bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodsy habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
