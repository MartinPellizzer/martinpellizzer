Uragoga corymbulosa

Uragoga corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Uragoga corymbulosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
