Uva cordata

Uva cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf muscadine grape, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for warm climates. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, wines, and other culinary applications.

Botanically, Uva cordata belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes other grape species.
