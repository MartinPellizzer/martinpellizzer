Utricularia perversa

Utricularia perversa, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in wet, marshy environments. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. These traps are triggered by water movement, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients from its surroundings.

Its rapid growth and unique trapping mechanism make it a fascinating subject in the study of carnivorous plants.
