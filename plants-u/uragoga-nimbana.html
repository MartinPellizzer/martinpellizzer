Uragoga nimbana

Uragoga nimbana, commonly known as the skyward bloom, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue petals that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. The plant has a woody stem with dense, velvety foliage that thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom only during the early morning hours, releasing a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family and is noted for its unique adaptations to high-altitude survival.
