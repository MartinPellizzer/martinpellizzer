Urtica madagascariensis

Urtica madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar nettle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica madagascariensis is often found in forest understories and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.
