Uvularia perfoliata var. major

Uvularia perfoliata var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, perfoliate leaves that are often fused around the stems, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
