Uncaria rhynchophylla var. kouteng

Uncaria rhynchophylla var. kouteng, commonly known as the Kouteng hook vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
