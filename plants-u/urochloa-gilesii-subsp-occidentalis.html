Urochloa gilesii subsp. occidentalis

Urochloa gilesii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as Western grass or African grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in agricultural and natural settings.
