Uvifera pubescens

Uvifera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

Uvifera pubescens is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
