Urtica moroides

Urtica moroides, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing a painful rash upon contact. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica moroides is also valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
