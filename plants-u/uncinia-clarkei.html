Uncinia clarkei

Uncinia clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Uncinia clarkei is known for its ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it a common weed in certain regions.
