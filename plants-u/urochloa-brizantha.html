Urochloa brizantha

Urochloa brizantha, commonly known as Panicum brizanthum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Urochloa brizantha is widely used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.
