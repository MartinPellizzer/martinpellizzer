Utricularia schinzii

Utricularia schinzii, commonly known as the aquatic bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that form bladders used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its roots are usually submerged, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white.

Utricularia schinzii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling populations of microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
