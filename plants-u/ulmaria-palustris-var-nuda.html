Ulmaria palustris var. nuda

Ulmaria palustris var. nuda, commonly known as slender swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream edges.
