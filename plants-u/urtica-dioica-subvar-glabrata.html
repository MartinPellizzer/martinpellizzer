Urtica dioica subvar. glabrata

Urtica dioica subvar. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth stinging nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its stinging hairs, which contain irritant compounds, are present on both the stems and leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
