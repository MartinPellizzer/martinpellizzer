Ulmus × crenata

Ulmus × crenata, commonly known as the Japanese cliff-leaf elm, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often lobed or toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–15 meters and has a spreading, rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Ulmus × crenata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
