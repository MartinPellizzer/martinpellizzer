Uncaria lanosa f. glabrescens

Uncaria lanosa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous cat's claw, is a variant of the cat's claw plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the typical hairy form of Uncaria lanosa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
