Urticastrum milnei

Urticastrum milnei, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Urticastrum milnei is known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
