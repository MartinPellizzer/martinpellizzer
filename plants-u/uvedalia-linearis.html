Uvedalia linearis

Uvedalia linearis, commonly known as the slender uvedalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
