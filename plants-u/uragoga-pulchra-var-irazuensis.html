Uragoga pulchra var. irazuensis

Uragoga pulchra var. irazuensis, commonly known as the Irazu beauty, is a striking bromeliad native to the highlands of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, often resembling flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, designed to collect and retain water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
