Uragoga nemorosa

Uragoga nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate forests of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Uragoga nemorosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland undergrowth.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
