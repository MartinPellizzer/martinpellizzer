Uragoga purpurascens

Uragoga purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent purple veins and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within rainforests. Its flowers bloom during the wet season and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Uragoga purpurascens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
