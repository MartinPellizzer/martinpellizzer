Uragoga peduncularis

Uragoga peduncularis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed uragoga, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Uragoga peduncularis is often found in shaded, forested areas where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
