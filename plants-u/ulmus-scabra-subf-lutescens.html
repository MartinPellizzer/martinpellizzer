Ulmus scabra subf. lutescens

Ulmus scabra subf.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-horned elm, is a subspecies of the warty elm native to parts of eastern China. It is characterized by its smooth, often yellowish bark and distinctive, elongated leaves with a rough upper surface.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is known for its moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
