Uragoga platyphylla

Uragoga platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the warm season. Uragoga platyphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
