Uragoga subpunctata

Uragoga subpunctata, commonly known as the spotted poinsettia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which are actually the true flowers of the plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often spiky structures. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Uragoga subpunctata is often cultivated for its festive appearance, especially during the holiday season.
