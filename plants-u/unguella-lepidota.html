Unguella lepidota

Unguella lepidota, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, though it prefers well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
