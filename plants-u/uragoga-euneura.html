Uragoga euneura

Uragoga euneura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Uragoga euneura is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
