Urceola reticulata

Urceola reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined urceola, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a dense network of veins on its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Urceola reticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
