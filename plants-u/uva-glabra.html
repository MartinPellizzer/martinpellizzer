Uva glabra

Uva glabra, commonly known as the smooth grape or white grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that are typically not consumed by humans.

Uva glabra is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
