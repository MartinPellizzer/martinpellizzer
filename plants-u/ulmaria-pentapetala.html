Ulmaria pentapetala

Ulmaria pentapetala, commonly known as five-petaled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Ulmaria pentapetala is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
