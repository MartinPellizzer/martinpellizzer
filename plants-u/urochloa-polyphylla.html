Urochloa polyphylla

Urochloa polyphylla, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, pastures, and along roadsides.

Urochloa polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth and spreading habit.
