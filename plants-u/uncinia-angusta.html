Uncinia angusta

Uncinia angusta, commonly known as the narrow thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Uncinia angusta is relatively drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its harsh native environment.
