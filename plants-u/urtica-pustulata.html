Urtica pustulata

Urtica pustulata, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a painful rash. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is found in temperate regions around the world. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and can be used in traditional medicine.

Urtica pustulata also produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels.
