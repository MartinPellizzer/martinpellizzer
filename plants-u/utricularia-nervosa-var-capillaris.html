Utricularia nervosa var. capillaris

Utricularia nervosa var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used to trap microscopic organisms. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its slender, thread-like stems and small leaves are adaptations for its specialized hunting mechanism.

It is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family and exhibits a unique reproductive strategy through both sexual and asexual means.
