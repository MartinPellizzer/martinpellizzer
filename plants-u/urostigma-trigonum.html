Urostigma trigonum

Urostigma trigonum, commonly known as the three-angled bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-angled stems and small, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often submerged, while the stems may rise to the water's surface.
