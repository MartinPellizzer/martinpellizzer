Ulmus forficata

Ulmus forficata, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, green fruits that mature to a dark brown color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Ulmus forficata is also valued for its resistance to urban conditions and pests.
