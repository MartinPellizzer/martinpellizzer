Ursinia matricariifolia

Ursinia matricariifolia, commonly known as the common ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late summer to autumn.

Ursinia matricariifolia is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
