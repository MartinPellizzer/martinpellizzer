Ulex parviflorus var. tenuior

Ulex parviflorus var. tenuior, commonly known as the lesser furrowed thorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
