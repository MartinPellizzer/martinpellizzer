Uragoga pachyphylla

Uragoga pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant's succulent structure is a key feature, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with limited rainfall.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Uragoga pachyphylla is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
