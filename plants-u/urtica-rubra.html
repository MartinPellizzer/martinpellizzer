Urtica rubra

Urtica rubra, commonly known as red nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and stems, which contain irritant chemicals that cause a skin reaction upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dark-colored berries.

Urtica rubra is often found in temperate regions of North America.
