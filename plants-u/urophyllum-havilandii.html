Urophyllum havilandii

Urophyllum havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
