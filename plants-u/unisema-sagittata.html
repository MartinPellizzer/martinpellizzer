Unisema sagittata

Unisema sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and have a smooth or serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Unisema sagittata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
