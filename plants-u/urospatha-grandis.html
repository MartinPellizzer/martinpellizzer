Urospatha grandis

Urospatha grandis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Urospatha within the family Araceae, which includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of rivers, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils.

Urospatha grandis is often cultivated for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to taro and can be cooked in various ways.
