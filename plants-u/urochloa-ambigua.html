Urochloa ambigua

Urochloa ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Urochloa ambigua is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
