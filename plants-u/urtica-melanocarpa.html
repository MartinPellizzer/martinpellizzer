Urtica melanocarpa

Urtica melanocarpa, commonly known as black nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blackish-purple flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Black nettle is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
