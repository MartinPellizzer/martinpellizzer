Uvaria cylindrica

Uvaria cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical satinflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Uvaria cylindrica is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
