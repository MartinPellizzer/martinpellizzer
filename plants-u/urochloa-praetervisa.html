Urochloa praetervisa

Urochloa praetervisa, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in pastureland due to its high forage value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
