Ulmus × webbiana

Ulmus × webbiana, commonly known as the Chinese field maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that belongs to the elm family, Ulmaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 10-15 meters tall.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with some variations depending on the age of the tree.
