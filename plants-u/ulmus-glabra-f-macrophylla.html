Ulmus glabra f. macrophylla

Ulmus glabra f. macrophylla, commonly known as the big-leaf elm, is a unique variant of the Wych elm.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This cultivar is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
