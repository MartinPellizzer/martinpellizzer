Ulex provincialis

Ulex provincialis, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, reddish stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

Ulex provincialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
