Ulex europaeus proles gallii

Ulex europaeus proles gallii, commonly known as the Scotch broom gall.

This plant is a type of flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaf structure. The gall, which is a growth caused by an insect, is a notable feature of this plant.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
