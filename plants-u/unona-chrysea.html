Unona chrysea

Unona chrysea, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Unona chrysea make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
