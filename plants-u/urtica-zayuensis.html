Urtica zayuensis

Urtica zayuensis, commonly known as the Yarlung hemp nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Yarlung Zangbo River valley in southern Tibet.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

Urtica zayuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
