Urceolina pendula var. fulva

Urceolina pendula var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
