Unifolium bifolium subvar. hirtellum

Unifolium bifolium subvar. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy leaf twinleaf, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features two opposite, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
