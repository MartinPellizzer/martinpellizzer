Uapaca pilosa f. subglabra

Uapaca pilosa f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-haired bush apple, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
