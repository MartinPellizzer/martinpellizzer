Ulex vaillantii

Ulex vaillantii, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems covered with small, spiny leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds.

Ulex vaillantii is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and rocky soils.
