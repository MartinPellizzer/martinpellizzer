Urtica evitata

Urtica evitata, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in humans. Urtica evitata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
