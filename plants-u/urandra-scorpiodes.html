Urandra scorpiodes

Urandra scorpiodes, commonly known as the scarlet flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Urandra scorpiodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
