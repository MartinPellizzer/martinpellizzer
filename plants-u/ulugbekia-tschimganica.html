Ulugbekia tschimganica

Ulugbekia tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimganica sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
