Urochloa semiundulata var. lanata

Urochloa semiundulata var. lanata, commonly known as hairy sicklegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its fibrous root system makes it a useful plant for erosion control and forage in certain regions.
