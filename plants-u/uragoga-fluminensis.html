Uragoga fluminensis

Uragoga fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Uragoga fluminensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
