Uragoga angusturensis

Uragoga angusturensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uragoga, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural research.
