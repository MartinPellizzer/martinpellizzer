Unciferia pompalis

Unciferia pompalis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, spiny fruit that ripens to a bright red color.

Unciferia pompalis is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in some regions.
