Uragoga insignis

Uragoga insignis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Uragoga insignis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ornate floral structure.
