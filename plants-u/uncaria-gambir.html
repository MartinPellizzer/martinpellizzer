Uncaria gambir

Uncaria gambir, commonly known as gambir or Malaysian gambier, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is most notable for its dark brown, woody pods that contain small, black seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Gambir is also cultivated for its use in the production of natural dyes and as a source of tannins.
