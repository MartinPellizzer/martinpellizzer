Uragoga lepiniana

Uragoga lepiniana, commonly known as the red-flowered uragoga, is a striking tropical plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant crimson petals and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with thorn-like protrusions, which serve as a defense mechanism.

Uragoga lepiniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
