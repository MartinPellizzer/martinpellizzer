Ulex spartioides

Ulex spartioides, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ulex spartioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
