Urtica globifolia

Urtica globifolia, commonly known as the globe weed or stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and small, greenish flowers that form in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its stem is upright, often branching, and covered with fine, irritating hairs.

Urtica globifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
