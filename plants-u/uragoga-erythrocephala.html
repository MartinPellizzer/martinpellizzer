Uragoga erythrocephala

Uragoga erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and woody stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences. Uragoga erythrocephala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
