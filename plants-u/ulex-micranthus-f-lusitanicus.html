Ulex micranthus f. lusitanicus

Ulex micranthus f. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese thorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environments and a common sight in Mediterranean landscapes.
