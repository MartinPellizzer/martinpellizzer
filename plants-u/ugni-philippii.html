Ugni philippii

Ugni philippii, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Ugni philippii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
