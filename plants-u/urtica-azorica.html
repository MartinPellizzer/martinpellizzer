Urtica azorica

Urtica azorica, commonly known as the Azorean nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Urtica azorica plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
