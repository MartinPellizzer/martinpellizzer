Ulmus fungosa

Ulmus fungosa, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Ulmus fungosa is relatively drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types, making it a hardy ornamental tree.

Its fast growth rate and resistance to many pests and diseases make it a popular choice for landscaping.
