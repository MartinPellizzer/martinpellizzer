Unona pygmaea

Unona pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
