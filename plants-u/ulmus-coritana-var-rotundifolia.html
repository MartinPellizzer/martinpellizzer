Ulmus coritana var. rotundifolia

Ulmus coritana var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf elm, is a variant of the European wych elm.

It is characterized by its distinctive round or broadly ovate leaves, which set it apart from other elm species. This variety is native to parts of Europe and has adapted to a range of soil types and climatic conditions. The tree typically grows to a moderate height and has a broad, spreading crown.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.
