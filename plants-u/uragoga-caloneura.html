Uragoga caloneura

Uragoga caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinct, narrow vein pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is mostly herbaceous, with a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Uragoga caloneura is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
