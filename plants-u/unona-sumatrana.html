Unona sumatrana

Unona sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran onion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

Unona sumatrana thrives in tropical climates and is typically cultivated in well-drained soils.
