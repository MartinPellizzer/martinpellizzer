Ulmus chinensis

Ulmus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Ulmus chinensis is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
