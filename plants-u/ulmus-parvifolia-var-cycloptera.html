Ulmus parvifolia var. cycloptera

Ulmus parvifolia var. cycloptera, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a variant of the Chinese elm.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often clustered in whorls. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
