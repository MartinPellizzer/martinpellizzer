Uncaria pilosa

Uncaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other medicinal plants such as the African bitters.
