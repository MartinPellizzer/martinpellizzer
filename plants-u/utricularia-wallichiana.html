Utricularia wallichiana

Utricularia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the alpine regions of Asia.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are often floating or submersed, with specialized structures for adhesion to substrates.

Utricularia wallichiana exhibits a unique adaptation to its environment, relying on its carnivorous behavior to supplement its nutrient intake.
