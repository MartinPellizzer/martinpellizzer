Ulex europaeus f. strictus

Ulex europaeus f. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Scotch broom, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Ulex europaeus f. strictus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
