Ulmus keaki

Ulmus keaki, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Ulmus keaki is also valued for its resilience to urban environments and resistance to certain pests and diseases.
