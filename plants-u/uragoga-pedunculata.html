Uragoga pedunculata

Uragoga pedunculata, commonly known as the red-flowered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and elongated peduncles that support its showy blooms. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Uragoga pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
