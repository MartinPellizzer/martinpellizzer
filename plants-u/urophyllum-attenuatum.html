Urophyllum attenuatum

Urophyllum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its stem is cylindrical and can branch out, forming a compact shrub.

Urophyllum attenuatum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
