Urginea secunda

Urginea secunda, commonly known as the purple star lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking violet or purple flowers with a star-like shape, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Urginea secunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
