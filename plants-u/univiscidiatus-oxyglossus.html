Univiscidiatus oxyglossus

Univiscidiatus oxyglossus, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety silver-green leaves that reflect light and give it a shimmering appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
