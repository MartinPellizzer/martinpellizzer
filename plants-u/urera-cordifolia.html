Urera cordifolia

Urera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf urera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Urera cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
