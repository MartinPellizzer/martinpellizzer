Uranostachys prostrata

Uranostachys prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate uranostachys, is a low-growing perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
