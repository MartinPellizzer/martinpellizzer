Uragoga lageniformis

Uragoga lageniformis, commonly known as the bottle-shaped orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
