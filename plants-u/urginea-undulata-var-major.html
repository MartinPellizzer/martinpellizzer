Urginea undulata var. major

Urginea undulata var. major, commonly known as the giant African snail, is a large herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a robust, fleshy stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often covered in a thick, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly succulent feel.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in some areas.
