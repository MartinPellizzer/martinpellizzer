Urtica alnifolia

Urtica alnifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact, causing irritation to the skin. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Urtica alnifolia is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
