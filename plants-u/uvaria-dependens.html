Uvaria dependens

Uvaria dependens, commonly known as the hanging hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that hang from the plant, giving it its common name. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
