Utricularia acicularis

Utricularia acicularis, commonly known as the needle bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and lack showy petals.

Utricularia acicularis plays a role in nutrient cycling in its habitat by capturing and digesting small aquatic organisms.
