Urtica heracleifolia

Urtica heracleifolia, commonly known as giant nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, with a robust stem that is also covered in stinging trichomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica heracleifolia is known for its showy, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.
