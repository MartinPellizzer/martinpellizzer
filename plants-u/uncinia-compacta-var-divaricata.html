Uncinia compacta var. divaricata

Uncinia compacta var. divaricata, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with bracts that are often more prominent than the actual petals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
