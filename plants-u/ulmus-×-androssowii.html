Ulmus × androssowii

Ulmus × androssowii, commonly known as the Chinese field maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Ulmaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by oval-shaped samaras as its fruit. It typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
