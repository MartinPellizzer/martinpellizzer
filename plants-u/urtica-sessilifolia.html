Urtica sessilifolia

Urtica sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-stalked nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface that can cause a skin irritation when touched. Its small green flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Urtica sessilifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
