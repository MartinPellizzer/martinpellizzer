Uvifera gracilis

Uvifera gracilis, commonly known as the slender grapevine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and minor fruit production.

It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
