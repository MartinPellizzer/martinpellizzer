Uleiorchis ulei

Uleiorchis ulei, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often among leaf litter or in rocky crevices. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single, narrow leaf and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
