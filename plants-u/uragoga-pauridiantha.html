Uragoga pauridiantha

Uragoga pauridiantha, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange floral displays. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are borne on long, arching inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique coloration and growth habits, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
