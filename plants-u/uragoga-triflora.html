Uragoga triflora

Uragoga triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm seasons. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with rough, bark-like texture.

Uragoga triflora is often found in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories.
