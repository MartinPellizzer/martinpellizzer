Urena virgata

Urena virgata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Urena virgata is known for its rapid growth and is considered a weed in many regions.
