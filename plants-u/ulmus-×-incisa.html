Ulmus × incisa

Ulmus × incisa, commonly known as the Japanese hackberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the Japanese elm (U. parvifolia) and the Chinese elm (U. parvifolia).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, green fruits that turn reddish-brown when mature.

It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
