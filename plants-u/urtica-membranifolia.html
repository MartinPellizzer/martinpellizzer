Urtica membranifolia

Urtica membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs and have a membranous texture. Its stems are usually hairy and can also cause a stinging sensation upon contact.

Urtica membranifolia typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
