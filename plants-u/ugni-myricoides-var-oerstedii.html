Ugni myricoides var. oerstedii

Ugni myricoides var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's huito, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
