Utricularia brennensis

Utricularia brennensis, commonly known as the Brennens bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a small, aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies. The plant features tiny, bladder-like structures called utricles, which trap small aquatic prey. These utricles are typically less than a millimeter in size and are used to capture and digest microorganisms.

Utricularia brennensis is notable for its rapid growth and unique adaptations to its nutrient-deficient environment.
