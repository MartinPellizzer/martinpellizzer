Uragoga noxia

Uragoga noxia, commonly known as the desert thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry environments.

Uragoga noxia is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
