Ucriana

Ucriana, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a tropical aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has a short, stout stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at dusk and closing at dawn, a feature that contributes to its spiritual significance in various cultures.

The Ucriana plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
