Ulmus pumila

Ulmus pumila, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types and climates.

Ulmus pumila is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
