Uvaria javanica

Uvaria javanica, commonly known as the Java apple or yellow wood apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by globose, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
