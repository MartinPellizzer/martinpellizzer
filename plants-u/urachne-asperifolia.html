Urachne asperifolia

Urachne asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved urachne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features rough, hairy foliage that gives it its specific epithet "asperifolia," meaning "rough-leaved.". It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Urachne asperifolia is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance.
