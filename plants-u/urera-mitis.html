Urera mitis

Urera mitis, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are typically pollinated by bees and other insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
