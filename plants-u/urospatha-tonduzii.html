Urospatha tonduzii

Urospatha tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and intricate, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Urospatha tonduzii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
