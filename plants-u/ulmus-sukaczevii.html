Ulmus sukaczevii

Ulmus sukaczevii, commonly known as the Sukaczev's elm, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and a distinctive, gnarled trunk that often develops deep fissures. This elm is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a valuable but endangered species in its native habitat.

The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
