Utricularia terrae-reginae

Utricularia terrae-reginae, commonly known as the queen's land bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, where it relies on its carnivorous adaptations to supplement its nutrient intake. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters.

Utricularia terrae-reginae is an example of the diverse evolutionary strategies employed by bladderworts to thrive in challenging environments.
