Uragoga ligustrina

Uragoga ligustrina, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
