Uvifera laurifolia

Uvifera laurifolia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a dense, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.

Uvifera laurifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand urban conditions.
