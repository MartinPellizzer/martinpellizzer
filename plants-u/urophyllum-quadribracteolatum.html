Urophyllum quadribracteolatum

Urophyllum quadribracteolatum, commonly known as the four-bract euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a cup-like structure called a cyathium. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
