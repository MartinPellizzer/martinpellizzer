Urera capitata

Urera capitata, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves and produces blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Urera capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
