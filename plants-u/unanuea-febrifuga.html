Unanuea febrifuga

Unanuea febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with dark centers, and its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture. Unanuea febrifuga is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating fever and headaches.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
