Uvaria bracteata

Uvaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate satinflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Uvaria bracteata produces small, smooth, dark blue or black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.
