Ulmus lesueurii

Ulmus lesueurii, commonly known as the Lesueur's elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it grows in a variety of habitats including forests and woodlands. The tree is characterized by its slender, upright form and its distinctive leaves, which are elongated and have a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The Lesueur's elm is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
