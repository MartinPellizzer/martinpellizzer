Uncinia brevicaulis var. macloviana

Uncinia brevicaulis var. macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian hook-sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
