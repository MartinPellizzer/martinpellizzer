Uncinia scaberrima

Uncinia scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
