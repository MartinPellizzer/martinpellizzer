Uncinia longifolia

Uncinia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
