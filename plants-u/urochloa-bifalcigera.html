Urochloa bifalcigera

Urochloa bifalcigera, commonly known as the two-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Urochloa bifalcigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
