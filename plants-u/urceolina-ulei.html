Urceolina ulei

Urceolina ulei, commonly known as the oil urn plant, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces a unique type of oil from its seeds, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines, adding a subtle, aromatic flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its practical uses.
