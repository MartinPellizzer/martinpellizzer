Uniyala gossypina

Uniyala gossypina, commonly known as the Indian cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
