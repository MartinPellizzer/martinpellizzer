Unona corniculata

Unona corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Unona corniculata is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and rocky areas.
