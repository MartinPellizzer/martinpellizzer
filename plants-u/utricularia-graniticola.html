Utricularia graniticola

Utricularia graniticola, commonly known as the granite bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to rocky, acidic environments.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor soils, often found in crevices of granite rocks. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

Utricularia graniticola plays a role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
