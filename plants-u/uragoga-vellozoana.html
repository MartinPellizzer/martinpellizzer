Uragoga vellozoana

Uragoga vellozoana, commonly known as the Vellozoan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
