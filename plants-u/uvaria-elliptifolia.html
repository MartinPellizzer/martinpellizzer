Uvaria elliptifolia

Uvaria elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptic uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Uvaria elliptifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
