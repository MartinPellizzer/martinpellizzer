Uva schweinfurthii

Uva schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert grape, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Uva schweinfurthii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
