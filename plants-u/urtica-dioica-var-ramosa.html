Urtica dioica var. ramosa

Urtica dioica var. ramosa, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Urtica dioica var. ramosa is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
