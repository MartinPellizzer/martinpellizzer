Unona hamata

Unona hamata, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Botanically, it is classified within the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
