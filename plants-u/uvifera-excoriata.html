Uvifera excoriata

Uvifera excoriata, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the summer months. Uvifera excoriata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
