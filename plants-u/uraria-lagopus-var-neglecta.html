Uraria lagopus var. neglecta

Uraria lagopus var. neglecta, commonly known as the desert blue pea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
