Urochloa paucispicata

Urochloa paucispicata, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and drooping inflorescences, which give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Urochloa paucispicata is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful, decorative form.
