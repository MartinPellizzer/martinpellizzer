Uraria arborea

Uraria arborea, commonly known as the tree uraria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
