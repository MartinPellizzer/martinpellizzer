Ursinia anethoides

Ursinia anethoides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

Ursinia anethoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
