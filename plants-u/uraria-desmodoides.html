Uraria desmodoides

Uraria desmodoides, commonly known as the Indian burr or wild senna, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of hairy, spiny pods. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and along roadsides.
