Urtica atrichocaulis

Urtica atrichocaulis, commonly known as the smooth stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

This plant is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
