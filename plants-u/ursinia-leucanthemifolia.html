Ursinia leucanthemifolia

Ursinia leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white thistle or white-leaved ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with a yellow center.

Ursinia leucanthemifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
