Uragoga pachysantha

Uragoga pachysantha, commonly known as the thick-leafed uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and fleshy leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Uragoga pachysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
