Urtica grandidentata subsp. lombok

Urtica grandidentata subsp.

lombok, commonly known as Lombok stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact with the skin.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
