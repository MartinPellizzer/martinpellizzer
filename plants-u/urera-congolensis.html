Urera congolensis

Urera congolensis, commonly known as African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
