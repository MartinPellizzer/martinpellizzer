Uvaria amplexicaulis

Uvaria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Uvaria amplexicaulis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
