Uragoga celastrinea

Uragoga celastrinea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Uragoga celastrinea is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.
