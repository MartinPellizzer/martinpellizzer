Urceolina narcissiflora

Urceolina narcissiflora, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble cups, which are borne on tall, branching stems. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
