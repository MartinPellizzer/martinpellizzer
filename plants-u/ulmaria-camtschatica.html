Ulmaria camtschatica

Ulmaria camtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticulture and ecological studies.
