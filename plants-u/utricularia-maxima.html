Utricularia maxima

Utricularia maxima, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest aquatic insects and other small organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, with the bladders attached to them for trapping prey.

Utricularia maxima is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, contributing to nutrient cycling through its predatory activities.
