Uragoga galloguianensis

Uragoga galloguianensis, commonly known as the Galloguian mistplant, is a rare and elusive species native to the dense, misty forests of the Galloguian Highlands.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that shimmer with a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue and violet, which bloom only during the early morning hours. Its roots are known to absorb moisture from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
