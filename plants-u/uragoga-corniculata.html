Uragoga corniculata

Uragoga corniculata, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. This plant is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that resemble horns, hence its common name. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Uragoga corniculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
