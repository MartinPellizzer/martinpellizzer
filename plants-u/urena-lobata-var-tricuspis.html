Urena lobata var. tricuspis

Urena lobata var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, three-lobed leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
