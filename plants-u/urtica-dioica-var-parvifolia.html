Urtica dioica var. parvifolia

Urtica dioica var. parvifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle with small leaves, is a variant of the common nettle found in temperate regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely serrated, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
