Urena lobata subsp. alba

Urena lobata subsp.

alba, commonly known as white urena, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by fluffy, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Urena lobata subsp.

alba is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
