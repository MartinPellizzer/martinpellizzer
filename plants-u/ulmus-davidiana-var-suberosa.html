Ulmus davidiana var. suberosa

Ulmus davidiana var. suberosa, commonly known as the Chinese cliff elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rugged, twisted trunk and branches that often form a dense, irregular crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This species is adapted to rocky and mountainous environments, showcasing its resilience and unique botanical features.
