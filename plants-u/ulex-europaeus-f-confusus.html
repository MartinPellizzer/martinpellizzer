Ulex europaeus f. confusus

Ulex europaeus f. confusus, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a variant of the common bladder campion.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its prostrate stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and coastal vegetation.
