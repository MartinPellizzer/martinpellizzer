Uncinia multifolia

Uncinia multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uncinia multifolia is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
