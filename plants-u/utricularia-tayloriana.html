It is characterized by its small, specialized bladder-like structures used for capturing prey. This aquatic or semi-aquatic plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are often submerged, with the bladders attached to the stems.

Utricularia tayloriana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor water environments.
