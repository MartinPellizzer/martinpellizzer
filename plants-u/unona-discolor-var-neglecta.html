Unona discolor var. neglecta

Unona discolor var. neglecta, commonly known as the neglected white woodbine, is a variant of the white woodbine, native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its pale green to white, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the wet season. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often twining around supports.

The leaves are heart-shaped and may have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
