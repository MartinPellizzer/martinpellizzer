Uragoga depressiglobosa

Uragoga depressiglobosa, commonly known as the globular bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive globular, bladder-like structures.

These specialized organs are used to trap small aquatic organisms, enabling the plant to obtain nutrients through carnivory. It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself with its root system. The plant's leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying adaptations in aquatic ecosystems.
