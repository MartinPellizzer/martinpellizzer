Utricularia charnleyensis

Utricularia charnleyensis, commonly known as the Charnley's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow water or damp soil. The plant features tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms for nutrition. Its flowers are usually small and pale, often with yellow or green hues.

Utricularia charnleyensis is part of the extensive Utricularia genus, which includes many species of carnivorous plants.
