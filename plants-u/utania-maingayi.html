Utania maingayi

Utania maingayi, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid native environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf burn.

Utania maingayi is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
