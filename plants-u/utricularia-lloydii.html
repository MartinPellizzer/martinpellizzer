Utricularia lloydii

Utricularia lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow water, where it can anchor itself with its root system. Its leaves are slender and often form dense mats on the water surface.

Utricularia lloydii is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and unique trapping mechanism.
