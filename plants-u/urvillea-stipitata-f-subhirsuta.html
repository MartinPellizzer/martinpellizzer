Urvillea stipitata f. subhirsuta

Urvillea stipitata f. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy urvillea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
