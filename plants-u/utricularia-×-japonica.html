Utricularia × japonica

Utricularia × japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bladderwort, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called utricles, which are used for capturing prey. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich water bodies, where it can thrive in both still and flowing conditions. Its leaves are usually submerged, while the flowering stems emerge above the water surface.

Utricularia × japonica is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is appreciated for its unique morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
