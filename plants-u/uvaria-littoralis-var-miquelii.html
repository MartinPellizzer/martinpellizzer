Uvaria littoralis var. miquelii

Uvaria littoralis var. miquelii, commonly known as the coastal uvaria, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in saline and humid environments, often found along shores and in mangrove forests. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

This species plays a role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
