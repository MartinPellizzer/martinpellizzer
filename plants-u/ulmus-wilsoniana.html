Ulmus wilsoniana

Ulmus wilsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to urban conditions.

Its fast growth rate makes it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation projects.
