Uncinia paludosa

Uncinia paludosa, commonly known as the bog hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of freshwater lakes. Its rhizomes help stabilize the soil, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Uncinia paludosa plays a role in maintaining the hydrological balance of its habitat and supports various invertebrate and bird species.
