Urtica palmata

Urtica palmata, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, green stems and serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs which can cause an irritating sting upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in shaded environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, black seeds.

Urtica palmata is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
