Urtica portulacina

Urtica portulacina, commonly known as the lesser nettle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like vein pattern and fine, stinging hairs on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica portulacina has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
