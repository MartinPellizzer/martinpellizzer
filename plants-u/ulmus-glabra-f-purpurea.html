Ulmus glabra f. purpurea

Ulmus glabra f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf elm, is a cultivar of the American elm.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish-bronze foliage, which turns reddish in the fall. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and moderate growth rate. Its bark is grey and becomes fissured with age.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in urban landscapes.
