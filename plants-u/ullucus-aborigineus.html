Ullucus aborigineus

Ullucus aborigineus, commonly known as the Andean ground cherry, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional Andean medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments and is well adapted to the cool, temperate climate of the Andes.
