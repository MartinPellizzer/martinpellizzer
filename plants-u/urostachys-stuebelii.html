Urostachys stuebelii

Urostachys stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered along the stem, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Urostachys stuebelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
