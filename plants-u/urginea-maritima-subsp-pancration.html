Urginea maritima subsp. pancration

Urginea maritima subsp.

pancration, commonly known as the sea onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features large, fleshy bulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in arid environments.

The plant produces tall, leafless stems that bear clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and sword-shaped, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
