Uncaria spinosa

Uncaria spinosa, commonly known as the climbing evergreen vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia, Africa, and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in small, hooked spines used for climbing. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Uncaria spinosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids in its roots and stems.
