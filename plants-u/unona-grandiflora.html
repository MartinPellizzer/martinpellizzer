Unona grandiflora

Unona grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered unona, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
