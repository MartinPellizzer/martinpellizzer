Urtica nepa

Urtica nepa, commonly known as the aquatic nettle, is a unique plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and long, slender stems that often float on the water's surface. The plant has stinging hairs on its leaves and stems, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. Urtica nepa is an aquatic plant that plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
