Ulmus montana var. dampieri-aurea

Ulmus montana var. dampieri-aurea, commonly known as the golden wahoo, is a striking ornamental tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping branches and its vibrant golden-yellow foliage, which stands out against the surrounding landscape. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is a cultivar of the wahoo tree, known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual interest and ecological benefits.
