Uncinia brevicaulis var. rigida

Uncinia brevicaulis var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid hookweed, is a variant of the hookweed species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly curled, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of greenish-white.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along streambanks.
