Uragoga angustifolia

Uragoga angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
