Ulmus campestris var. betulifolius

Ulmus campestris var. betulifolius, commonly known as the Betulifolius elm, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin, resembling those of birch trees, hence its common name. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and open areas. It has a straight, slender trunk and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and historical use in landscaping.
