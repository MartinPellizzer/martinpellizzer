Urtica silvatica

Urtica silvatica, commonly known as the forest nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Urtica silvatica is characterized by its stinging hairs, which release irritants when touched.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
