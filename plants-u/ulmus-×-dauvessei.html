Ulmus × dauvessei

Ulmus × dauvessei, commonly known as the Dauvesse hybrid elm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the American elm (U. americana) and the European elm (U. laevigata).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resistance to certain diseases compared to its parent species.
