Uvaria fruticosa

Uvaria fruticosa, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its dark blue to black berries, which are edible and have a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Uvaria fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
