Ulmus × superba

Ulmus × superba, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the American elm (U. americana) and the Chinese elm (U. parvifolia).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round, brown fruits.

It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to Dutch elm disease, making it a popular urban tree species.
