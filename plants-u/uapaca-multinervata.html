Uapaca multinervata

Uapaca multinervata, commonly known as the African wild coffee, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.
