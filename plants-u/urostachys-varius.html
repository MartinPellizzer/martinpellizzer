Urostachys varius

Urostachys varius, commonly known as the variable mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it blooms during the summer months.

Urostachys varius is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
