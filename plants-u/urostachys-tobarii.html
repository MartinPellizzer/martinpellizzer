Urostachys tobarii

Urostachys tobarii, commonly known as the Andean silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
