Urena ricinocarpa

Urena ricinocarpa, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a tropical annual herb belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas but has become an invasive species in many parts of the world.

Urena ricinocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
