Urtica oreophila

Urtica oreophila, commonly known as the mountain nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species known for their stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine, irritating hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

The flowers are small and green, arranged in clusters, and the plant is often found in forest understories.
