Ulmus procera var. australis

Ulmus procera var. australis, commonly known as the Australian black locust, is a variant of the European tree known as the wych elm.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pods containing seeds.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of the Australian landscape.
