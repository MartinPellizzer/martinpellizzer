Urticastrum sinuatum

Urticastrum sinuatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Urticastrum sinuatum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a resilient species in various habitats.
