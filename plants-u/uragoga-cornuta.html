Uragoga cornuta

Uragoga cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral spikes that bear showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Uragoga cornuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
