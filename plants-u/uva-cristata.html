Uva cristata

Uva cristata, commonly known as the cristate grape, is a rare and unique plant species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Uva cristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a symbol of the Canary Islands' unique biodiversity.
