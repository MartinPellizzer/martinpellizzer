Uragoga rufescens

Uragoga rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Uragoga rufescens is often found in tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
