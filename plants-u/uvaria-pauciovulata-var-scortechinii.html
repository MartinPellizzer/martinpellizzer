Uvaria pauciovulata var. scortechinii

Uvaria pauciovulata var. scortechinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uvaria, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
