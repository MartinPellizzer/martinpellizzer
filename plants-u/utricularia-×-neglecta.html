Utricularia × neglecta

Utricularia × neglecta, commonly known as the hybrid bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Utricularia gibba and Utricularia olivacea. The plant is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and specialized bladder-like structures used for capturing prey. Its leaves are typically whorled and possess the characteristic inflated bladders that function as traps.

Utricularia × neglecta is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor environments.
