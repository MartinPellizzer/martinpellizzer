Uncaria lanosa var. appendiculata

Uncaria lanosa var. appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a woody stem with dense, hairy pubescence, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with the bark containing alkaloids used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
