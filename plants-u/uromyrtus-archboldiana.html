Uromyrtus archboldiana

Uromyrtus archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold's hakea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which eat the fleshy, red fruits.

Uromyrtus archboldiana is well adapted to drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
