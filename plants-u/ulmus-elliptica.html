Ulmus elliptica

Ulmus elliptica, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. Ulmus elliptica is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
