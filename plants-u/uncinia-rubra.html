Uncinia rubra

Uncinia rubra, commonly known as the red hair curl, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

Uncinia rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
