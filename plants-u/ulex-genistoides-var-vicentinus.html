Ulex genistoides var. vicentinus

Ulex genistoides var. vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentine burr.

This variant is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its spiny, shrub-like growth habit. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of sharp, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
