Uvaria oligocarpa

Uvaria oligocarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor, making it a valuable resource in local cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
