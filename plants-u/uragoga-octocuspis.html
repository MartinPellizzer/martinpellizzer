Uragoga octocuspis

Uragoga octocuspis, commonly known as the eight-spined uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring eight spines along its stem and a cluster of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Uragoga octocuspis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
