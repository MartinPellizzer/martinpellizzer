Urostachys lecomteanus

Urostachys lecomteanus, commonly known as the Lecomte's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
