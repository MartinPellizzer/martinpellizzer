Uraria picta

Uraria picta, commonly known as the painted uraria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pattern of white veins, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
