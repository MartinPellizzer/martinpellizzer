Ulantha grandiflora

Ulantha grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora ulantha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a woody stem, making it a distinctive species within its genus. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ulantha grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
