Urginea maritima f. angustifolia

Urginea maritima f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea onion, is a botanical variant of the sea onion plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped white flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. It is a perennial herb with a robust underground bulb that supports its growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
