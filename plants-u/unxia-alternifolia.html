Unxia alternifolia

Unxia alternifolia, commonly known as the Chinese alternateleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
