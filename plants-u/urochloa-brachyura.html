Urochloa brachyura

Urochloa brachyura, commonly known as hairy signal grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, pastures, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a paniculate cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Urochloa brachyura is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
