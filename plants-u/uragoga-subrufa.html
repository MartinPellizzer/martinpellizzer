Uragoga subrufa

Uragoga subrufa, commonly known as the red-barked uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which becomes more prominent with age. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Uragoga subrufa produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.
