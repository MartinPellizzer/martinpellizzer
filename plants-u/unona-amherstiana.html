Unona amherstiana

Unona amherstiana, commonly known as the Amherst's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Unona amherstiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
