Uapaca kirkiana var. benguelensis

Uapaca kirkiana var. benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela date palm, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or processed into beverages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
