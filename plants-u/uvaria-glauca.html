Uvaria glauca

Uvaria glauca, commonly known as the blue uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its small, glaucous-blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
