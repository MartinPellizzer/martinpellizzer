Unona moniliformis

Unona moniliformis, commonly known as the bead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bead-like structures along its stems, which are formed by the fusion of leaf bases. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a cluster of grapes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Unona moniliformis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
