Uvularia grandiflora f. variegata

Uvularia grandiflora f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, recurved shape. The foliage is characterized by its variegated leaves, which display a mix of green and white or cream markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland settings.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
