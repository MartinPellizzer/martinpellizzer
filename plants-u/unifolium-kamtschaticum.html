Unifolium kamtschaticum

Unifolium kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale blue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense arrangement that helps it withstand harsh weather conditions.

Unifolium kamtschaticum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
