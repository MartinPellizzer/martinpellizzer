Utricularia madagascariensis

Utricularia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in wetland environments. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are often submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Utricularia madagascariensis plays a role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem by preying on microorganisms.
