Umbilicus erectus

Umbilicus erectus, commonly known as the upright navel plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive navel-like structure at the base of its stem.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating. The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white and pale green.

Umbilicus erectus is valued in horticulture for its unusual appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
