Uvaria macrantha

Uvaria macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered uvaria, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.

Uvaria macrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
