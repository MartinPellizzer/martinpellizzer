Uragoga laevifolia

Uragoga laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved uragoga, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
