Uragoga poliostemma

Uragoga poliostemma, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters. Uragoga poliostemma is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
