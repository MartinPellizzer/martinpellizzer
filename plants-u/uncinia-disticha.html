Uncinia disticha

Uncinia disticha, commonly known as the clustered hookweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a pointed tip, and it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Uncinia disticha is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.
