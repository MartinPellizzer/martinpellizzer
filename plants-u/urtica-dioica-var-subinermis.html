Urtica dioica var. subinermis

Urtica dioica var. subinermis, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, hollow stems and heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it is dioecious, meaning separate male and female plants.

The plant contains a variety of bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
