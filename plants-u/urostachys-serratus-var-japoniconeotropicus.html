Urostachys serratus var. japoniconeotropicus

Urostachys serratus var. japoniconeotropicus, commonly known as the Japanese mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments. It is often found in shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
