Utricularia siamensis

Utricularia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in stagnant or slow-moving water. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture tiny aquatic organisms for nourishment. Its leaves are typically submerged, while its flowering stems emerge above the water surface.

Utricularia siamensis is notable for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various water conditions.
