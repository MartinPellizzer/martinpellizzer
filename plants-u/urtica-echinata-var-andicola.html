Urtica echinata var. andicola

Urtica echinata var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean stinging nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that contain irritant chemicals, causing a painful rash upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features include serrated leaf margins and small, greenish flowers arranged in umbels.
