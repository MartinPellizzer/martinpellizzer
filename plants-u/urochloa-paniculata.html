Urochloa paniculata

Urochloa paniculata, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, bluish-gray leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Urochloa paniculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in erosion control due to its spreading growth habit.
