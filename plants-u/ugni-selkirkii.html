Ugni selkirkii

Ugni selkirkii, commonly known as the Selkirk honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is typically found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a distinct scent when crushed.
