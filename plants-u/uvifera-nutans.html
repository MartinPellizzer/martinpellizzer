Uvifera nutans

Uvifera nutans, commonly known as the hanging grapefruit, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small citrus fruits. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which help it adapt to arid environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Uvifera nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
