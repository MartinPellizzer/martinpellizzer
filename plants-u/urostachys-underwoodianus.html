Urostachys underwoodianus

Urostachys underwoodianus, commonly known as the Underwood's carpet grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological botany.
