Urtica lucifuga

Urtica lucifuga, commonly known as the ghost nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slight serration along the edges.

Urtica lucifuga is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
