Uragoga pancheri

Uragoga pancheri, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and fine hairs that can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Uragoga pancheri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
