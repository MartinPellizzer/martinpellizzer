Ucriana hamelliae

Ucriana hamelliae, commonly known as the Hamell's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.
