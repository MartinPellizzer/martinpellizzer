Urochloa nigropedata

Urochloa nigropedata, commonly known as black-stemmed blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and distinctive, flat, blade-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. It is relatively drought-tolerant and can grow in a variety of soil types.

Urochloa nigropedata is sometimes used in erosion control due to its rapid growth and dense root system.
