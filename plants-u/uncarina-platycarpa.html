Uncarina platycarpa

Uncarina platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited star-apple, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by green, fleshy berries. The berries turn yellow or orange when mature and contain numerous seeds enclosed in a gelatinous pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
