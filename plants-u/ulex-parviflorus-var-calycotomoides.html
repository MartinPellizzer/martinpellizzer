Ulex parviflorus var. calycotomoides

Ulex parviflorus var. calycotomoides, commonly known as the prickly hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
