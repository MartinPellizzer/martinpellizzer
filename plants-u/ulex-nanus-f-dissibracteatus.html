Ulex nanus f. dissibracteatus

Ulex nanus f. dissibracteatus, commonly known as the dwarf Scotch broom, is a shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is native to western Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is notable for its distinct leaf structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
