Urtica submitis

Urtica submitis, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing a painful rash upon contact. Urtica submitis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
