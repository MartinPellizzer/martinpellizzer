Uragoga corymbosa

Uragoga corymbosa, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
