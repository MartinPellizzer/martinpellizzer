Utricularia sandwithii

Utricularia sandwithii, commonly known as the Sandwich Islands bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Hawaii.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. The plant is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. These traps are triggered by the movement of prey, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients in nutrient-poor environments.

Utricularia sandwithii plays a unique role in its ecosystem as both a predator and a potential indicator of water quality.
