Uncasia perfoliata

Uncasia perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as the ceiba, through a specialized root system called a haustellum. It produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
