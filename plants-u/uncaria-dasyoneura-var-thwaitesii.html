Uncaria dasyoneura var. thwaitesii

Uncaria dasyoneura var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the thwaitesii variety of the climbing shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
