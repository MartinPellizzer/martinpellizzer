Urtica pilulifera var. balearica

Urtica pilulifera var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic stinging nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle species native to the Balearic Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and dense, stinging hairs on both the leaves and stems. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed habitats. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded fruits that resemble pills, giving it its common name.

The plant is known for its defensive stinging hairs, which release irritants when touched.
