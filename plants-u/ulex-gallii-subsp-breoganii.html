Ulex gallii subsp. breoganii

Ulex gallii subsp.

breoganii, commonly known as the Irish heath, is a shrub native to western Europe, particularly Ireland and parts of Britain. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife. Ulex gallii subsp.

breoganii is also known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
