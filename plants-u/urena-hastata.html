Urena hastata

Urena hastata, commonly known as the hairy urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Urena hastata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
