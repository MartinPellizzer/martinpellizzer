Ulmus simplicidens

Ulmus simplicidens, commonly known as the simple-leaf elm, is a species of flowering plant in the elm family, Ulmaceae.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The tree is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a smooth margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

The wood of Ulmus simplicidens is valued for its fine grain and is used in traditional woodworking and furniture making.
