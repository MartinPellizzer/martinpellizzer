Urtica takasagonis

Urtica takasagonis, commonly known as Japanese stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica takasagonis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
