Urtica portulacoides

Urtica portulacoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle or creeping nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a rash in humans. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Urtica portulacoides is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
