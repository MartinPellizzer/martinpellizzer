Urochloa gilesii

Urochloa gilesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Urochloa gilesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
