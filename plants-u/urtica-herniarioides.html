Urtica herniarioides

Urtica herniarioides, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains histamine and other irritants in its hairs, causing a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Urtica herniarioides is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
