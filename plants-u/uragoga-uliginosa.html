Uragoga uliginosa

Uragoga uliginosa, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Uragoga uliginosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
