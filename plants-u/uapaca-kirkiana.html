Uapaca kirkiana

Uapaca kirkiana, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.
