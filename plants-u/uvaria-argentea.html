Uvaria argentea

Uvaria argentea, commonly known as silver uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, yellowish-orange berries. Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.

Uvaria argentea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
