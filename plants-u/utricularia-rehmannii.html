Utricularia rehmannii

Utricularia rehmannii, commonly known as the Indian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually submerged, while its stems may float on the water surface.
