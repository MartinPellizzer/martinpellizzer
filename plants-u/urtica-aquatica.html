Urtica aquatica

Urtica aquatica, commonly known as water nettle, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. Its small, green, heart-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem.

Urtica aquatica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
