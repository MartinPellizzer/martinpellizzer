Unifolium bifolium

Unifolium bifolium, commonly known as the two-leafed single-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It possesses a single leaf at the base and a second, smaller leaf emerging from the stem, giving it its binomial name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Unifolium bifolium is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
