Unona penduliflora

Unona penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that hang in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 15 meters in height. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
