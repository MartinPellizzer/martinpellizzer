Urostachya pachystachya

Urostachya pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert landscapes.

Urostachya pachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and dry gardens.
