Ursinia nana

Ursinia nana, commonly known as the dwarf ursinia, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ursinia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
