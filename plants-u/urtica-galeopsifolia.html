Urtica galeopsifolia

Urtica galeopsifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release formic acid when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica galeopsifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
