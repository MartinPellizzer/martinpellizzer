Uvifera strobilulifera

Uvifera strobilulifera, commonly known as the mountain ash or European rowan, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Uvifera strobilulifera is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
