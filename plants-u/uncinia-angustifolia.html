Uncinia angustifolia

Uncinia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hookweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer. Its fibrous roots and rhizomes allow it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Uncinia angustifolia is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions.
