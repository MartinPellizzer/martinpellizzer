Ulmaria

Ulmaria, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces an aromatic, licorice-scented seed that is used in herbal medicine. Ulmaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
