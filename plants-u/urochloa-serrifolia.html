Urochloa serrifolia

Urochloa serrifolia, commonly known as the sawleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and tufted growth habit, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Urochloa serrifolia is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
