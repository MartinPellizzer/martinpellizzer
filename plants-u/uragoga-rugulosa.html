Uragoga rugulosa

Uragoga rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, textured leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, often pink or white blooms.

Uragoga rugulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
