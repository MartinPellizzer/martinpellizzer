Uncinia egmontiana

Uncinia egmontiana, commonly known as the Egmont's hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, high-altitude environments. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Uncinia egmontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
