Uragoga bakeriana

Uragoga bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's uragoga, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Uragoga bakeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
