Urospatha savannarum

Urospatha savannarum, commonly known as the savanna grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Urospatha savannarum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
