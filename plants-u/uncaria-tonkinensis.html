Uncaria tonkinensis

Uncaria tonkinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pairs of opposite, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties.
