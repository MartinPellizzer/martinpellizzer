Urochloa holosericea subsp. holosericea

Urochloa holosericea subsp.

holosericea, commonly known as hairy lemongrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
