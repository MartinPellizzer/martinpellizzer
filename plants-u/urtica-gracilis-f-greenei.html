Urtica gracilis f. greenei

Urtica gracilis f. greenei, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a variant of the common nettle found in various regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The leaves and stems are covered in stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
