Ugni myricoides f. grossa

Ugni myricoides f. grossa, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Chile and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The inflorescences are clustered and produce small, white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
