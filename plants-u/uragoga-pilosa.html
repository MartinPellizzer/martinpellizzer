Uragoga pilosa

Uragoga pilosa, commonly known as the hairy uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Uragoga pilosa thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded understory areas of forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
