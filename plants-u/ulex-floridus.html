Ulex floridus

Ulex floridus, commonly known as the Florida holly or Florida buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, evergreen leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Ulex floridus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
