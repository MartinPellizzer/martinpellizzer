Uapaca togoensis

Uapaca togoensis, commonly known as the Togo velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
