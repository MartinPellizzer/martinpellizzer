Uvaria angolensis subsp. guineense

Uvaria angolensis subsp.

guineense, commonly known as African blackberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverine areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
