Unonopsis rufescens

Unonopsis rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellowish in color.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental bark and medicinal properties.
