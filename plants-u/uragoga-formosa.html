Uragoga formosa

Uragoga formosa, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant yellow petals with a distinctive star-shaped pattern, making it visually appealing. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Uragoga formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
