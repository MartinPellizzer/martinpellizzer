Uragoga aemulans

Uragoga aemulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant has long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Uragoga aemulans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique ability to mimic other orchids.
