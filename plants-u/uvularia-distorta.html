Uvularia distorta

Uvularia distorta, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted flower stems that often curl and twist as they grow. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Uvularia distorta thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.
