Ulmus virgata

Ulmus virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The tree is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and long, drooping branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Ulmus virgata produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval fruits.
