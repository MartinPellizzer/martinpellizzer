Uva sumatrana

Uva sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran grapefruit, is a citrus species native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its thick, peeling rind and segmented, juicy flesh that ranges in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, while the fruit matures over several months.

Uva sumatrana is valued for its unique flavor and is used both for consumption and in traditional medicine.
