Ulex nanus var. lusitanicus

Ulex nanus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian furze, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by wind or animals.
