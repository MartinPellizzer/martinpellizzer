Urginea epigea

Urginea epigea, commonly known as the African giant lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust, tuberous root system that allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic appearance and unique blooming habit.
