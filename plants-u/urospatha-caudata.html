Urospatha caudata

Urospatha caudata, commonly known as the hairy cat's-tail, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that resemble a cat's tail, composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and hairy, contributing to its common name.

Urospatha caudata is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in high humidity and nutrient-rich soils.
