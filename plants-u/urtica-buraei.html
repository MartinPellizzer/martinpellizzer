Urtica buraei

Urtica buraei, commonly known as the Buraei nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain formic acid and can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Urtica buraei is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
