Ulmus nitens var. stricta

Ulmus nitens var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soils.
