Uncasia hyssopifolia

Uncasia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf hyssop, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Uncasia hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
