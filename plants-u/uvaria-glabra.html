Uvaria glabra

Uvaria glabra, commonly known as the smooth uvaria, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe, often containing a single seed.
