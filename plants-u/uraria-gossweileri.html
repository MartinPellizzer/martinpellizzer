Uraria gossweileri

Uraria gossweileri, commonly known as the African uraria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Uraria gossweileri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
