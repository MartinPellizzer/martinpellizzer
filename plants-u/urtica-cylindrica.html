Urtica cylindrica

Urtica cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hollow, and cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with serrated edges and also bear stinging hairs.

Urtica cylindrica is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks.
