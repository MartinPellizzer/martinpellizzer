Uromyrtus neomyrtoides

Uromyrtus neomyrtoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Uromyrtus neomyrtoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
