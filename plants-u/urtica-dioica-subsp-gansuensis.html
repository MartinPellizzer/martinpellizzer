Urtica dioica subsp. gansuensis

Urtica dioica subsp.

gansuensis, commonly known as the Gansu stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Gansu Province in China. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritating chemicals upon contact.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are wind-pollinated and followed by small, dry fruits.
