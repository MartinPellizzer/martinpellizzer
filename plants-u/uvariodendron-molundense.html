Uvariodendron molundense

Uvariodendron molundense, commonly known as the Molundense Olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use in local markets.
