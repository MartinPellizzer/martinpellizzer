Urachne aemulans

Urachne aemulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of other orchid species. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a complex structure that includes a prominent lip or labellum.

Urachne aemulans is noted for its ability to mimic the appearance of other orchids, which may aid in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
