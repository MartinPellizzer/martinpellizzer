Uragoga sphaerocephala

Uragoga sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed uragoga, is a unique plant species characterized by its spherical, fleshy fruit that resembles a small globe.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant's leaves are typically broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences that add to its ornamental appeal.
