Uvaria cordata

Uvaria cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries. Uvaria cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in tropical rainforests.
