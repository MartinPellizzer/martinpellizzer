Ursinia calenduliflora

Ursinia calenduliflora, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ursinia calenduliflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
