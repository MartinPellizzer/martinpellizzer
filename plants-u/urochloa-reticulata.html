Urochloa reticulata

Urochloa reticulata, commonly known as finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Urochloa reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
