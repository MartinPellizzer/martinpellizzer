Uncaria jasminiflora

Uncaria jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that resemble jasmine, typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
