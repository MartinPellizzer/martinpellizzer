Ulmus × dippeliana

Ulmus × dippeliana, commonly known as the Wych Elm hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is a hybrid between the Wych Elm (Ulmus glabra) and the English Elm (Ulmus procera), resulting in a tree with robust growth and adaptability. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, winged samaras as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in European landscapes.
