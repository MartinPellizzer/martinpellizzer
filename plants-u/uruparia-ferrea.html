Uruparia ferrea

Uruparia ferrea, commonly known as ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Uruparia ferrea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
