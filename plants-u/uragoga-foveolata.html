Uragoga foveolata

Uragoga foveolata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
