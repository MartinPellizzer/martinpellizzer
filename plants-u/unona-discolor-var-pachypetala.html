Unona discolor var. pachypetala

Unona discolor var. pachypetala, commonly known as the thick-leaved white woodbine, is a variant of the white woodbine species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and leathery leaves that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense foliage structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or semi-shaded environments.
