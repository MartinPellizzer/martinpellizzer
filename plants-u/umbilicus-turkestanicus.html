Umbilicus turkestanicus

Umbilicus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branching.

Umbilicus turkestanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
