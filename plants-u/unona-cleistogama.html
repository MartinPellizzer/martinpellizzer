Unona cleistogama

Unona cleistogama, commonly known as the cleistogamous unona, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique reproductive strategy.

It produces closed, self-pollinating flowers that do not open, ensuring efficient pollination without the need for external agents. The plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, while its stems are sturdy and often woody.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitats.
