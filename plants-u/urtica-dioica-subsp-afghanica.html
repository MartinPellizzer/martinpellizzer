Urtica dioica subsp. afghanica

Urtica dioica subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that cause irritation upon contact with the skin.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and can reach up to 15 cm in length.

It produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are often hidden among the leaves.
