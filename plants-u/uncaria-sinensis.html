Uncaria sinensis

Uncaria sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese climbing tea or hook vine, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and small, opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that contain elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
