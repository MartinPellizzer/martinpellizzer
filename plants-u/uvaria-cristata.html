Uvaria cristata

Uvaria cristata, commonly known as the crowned uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a smooth, branching stem. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Uvaria cristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
