Uragoga crocea

Uragoga crocea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Uragoga crocea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
