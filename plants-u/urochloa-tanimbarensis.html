Urochloa tanimbarensis

Urochloa tanimbarensis, commonly known as the Tanimbarensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Urochloa tanimbarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
