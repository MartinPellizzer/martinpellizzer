Uncinia rupestris

Uncinia rupestris, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments in South Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
