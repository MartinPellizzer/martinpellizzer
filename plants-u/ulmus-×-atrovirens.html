Ulmus × atrovirens

Ulmus × atrovirens, commonly known as the black elm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the American elm (Ulmus americana) and the European elm (Ulmus glabra).

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The tree grows to a height of 15–25 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. It is moderately tolerant of urban conditions, making it a suitable candidate for street tree planting.

However, it is susceptible to Dutch elm disease, which has significantly reduced its population in many regions.
