Uncaria lanosa

Uncaria lanosa, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and hairy foliage, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other medicinal plants like the African coffee tree.
