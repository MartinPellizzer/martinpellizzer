Uncinia phleoides var. laticarpa

Uncinia phleoides var. laticarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited hookweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
