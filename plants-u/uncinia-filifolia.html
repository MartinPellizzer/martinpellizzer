Uncinia filifolia

Uncinia filifolia, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Uncinia filifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
