Urostachys linifolius var. sanguineus

Urostachys linifolius var. sanguineus, commonly known as the bloodleaf or red-flowered tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
