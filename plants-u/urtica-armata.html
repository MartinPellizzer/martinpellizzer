Urtica armata

Urtica armata, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that release irritating chemicals when touched. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Urtica armata thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in forest clearings and along stream banks.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
