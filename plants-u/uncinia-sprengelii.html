Uncinia sprengelii

Uncinia sprengelii, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its unique structure, with hooked bracts resembling tiny pincushions, gives it its common name.

Uncinia sprengelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
