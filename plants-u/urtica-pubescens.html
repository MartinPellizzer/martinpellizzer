Urtica pubescens

Urtica pubescens, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. The plant contains fine hairs that release an irritant when touched, causing a stinging sensation. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica pubescens is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
