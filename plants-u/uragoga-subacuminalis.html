Uragoga subacuminalis

Uragoga subacuminalis, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a rare tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that have a distinctive silver sheen on the underside. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
