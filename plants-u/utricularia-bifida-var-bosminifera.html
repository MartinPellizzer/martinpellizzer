Utricularia bifida var. bosminifera

Utricularia bifida var. bosminifera, commonly known as the Bosmina bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where it relies on capturing small aquatic organisms for nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in stagnant water environments, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
