Ulmus × oblanceolata

Ulmus × oblanceolata, commonly known as the Chinese wahoo or Chinese elm, is a hybrid species of elm native to China.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
