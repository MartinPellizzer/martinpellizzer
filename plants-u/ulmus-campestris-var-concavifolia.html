Ulmus campestris var. concavifolia

Ulmus campestris var. concavifolia, commonly known as the concaveleaf elm, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically narrower and have a concave base, setting it apart from other elm species. It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The bark is often rugged and fissured, providing a textured appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in urban landscapes.
