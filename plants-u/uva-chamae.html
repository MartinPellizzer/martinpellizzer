Uva chamae

Uva chamae, commonly known as the creeping grape or ground grape, is a perennial vine native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known vines like grapes and kiwifruit. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance, while the fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

Uva chamae is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
