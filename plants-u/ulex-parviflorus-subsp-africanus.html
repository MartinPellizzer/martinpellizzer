Ulex parviflorus subsp. africanus

Ulex parviflorus subsp.

africanus, commonly known as the African sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
