Ulex bourgaeanus

Ulex bourgaeanus, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow berries, which are edible when fully ripe. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and colorful fruits.
