Uragoga longipedunculata

Uragoga longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the floral stems that bear the inflorescences. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Uragoga longipedunculata is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
