Urginea ensifolia

Urginea ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf arrowhead, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by large, showy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
