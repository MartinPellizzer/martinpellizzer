Uragoga tricholoba

Uragoga tricholoba, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
