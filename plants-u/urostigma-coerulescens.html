Urostigma coerulescens

Urostigma coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white petals with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Urostigma coerulescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
