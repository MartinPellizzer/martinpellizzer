Urtica morifolia

Urtica morifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritating chemicals when touched, causing a painful rash. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica morifolia is also known for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.
