Uragoga discolor

Uragoga discolor, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering season usually occurs during the warm months, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Uragoga discolor is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
