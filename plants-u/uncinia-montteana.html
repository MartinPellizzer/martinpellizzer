Uncinia montteana

Uncinia montteana, commonly known as the mountain hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces distinctive hooked fruits, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
