Uragoga pararawinensis

Uragoga pararawinensis, commonly known as the Pararawi orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected conservation areas.
