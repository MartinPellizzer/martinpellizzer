Ulmus glabra f. minor

Ulmus glabra f. minor, commonly known as the European wych elm.

It is a small, slow-growing elm species native to central and southern Europe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its limited size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
