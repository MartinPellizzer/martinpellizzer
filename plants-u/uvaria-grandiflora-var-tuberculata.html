Uvaria grandiflora var. tuberculata

Uvaria grandiflora var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate uvaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
