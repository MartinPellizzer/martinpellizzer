Urtica dioica subsp. eudioica

Urtica dioica subsp.

eudioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain formic acid and histamine, causing irritation upon contact with the skin.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Urtica dioica subsp.

eudioica is also valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
