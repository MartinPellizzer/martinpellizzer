Urtica variegata

Urtica variegata, commonly known as the variegated nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species known for their stinging hairs. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the family.

Urtica variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
