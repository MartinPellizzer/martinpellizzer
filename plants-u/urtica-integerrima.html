Urtica integerrima

Urtica integerrima, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs on the stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, irritating trichomes. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, pale fruits.

Urtica integerrima thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
