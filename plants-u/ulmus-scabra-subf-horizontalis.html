Ulmus scabra subf. horizontalis

Ulmus scabra subf.

horizontalis, commonly known as the roughleaf elm, is a subspecies of the European wych elm. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shady environments. It has a slow growth rate and is relatively hardy, though it is susceptible to certain fungal diseases.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
