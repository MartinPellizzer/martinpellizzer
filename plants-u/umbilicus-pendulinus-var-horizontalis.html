Umbilicus pendulinus var. horizontalis

Umbilicus pendulinus var. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal umbel, is a unique variant of the umbel plant characterized by its distinctive horizontal arrangement of flowers.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in a flat, umbrella-like structure, which gives it its common name. The leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to the delicate blooms.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
