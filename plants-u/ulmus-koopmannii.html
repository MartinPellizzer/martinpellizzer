Ulmus koopmannii

Ulmus koopmannii, commonly known as Koopmann's elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central China, specifically the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and parks.
