Urtica tetraphylla

Urtica tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Urtica tetraphylla typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is noted for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
