Uncaria jasminiflora var. macrophylla

Uncaria jasminiflora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf climbing vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. Its botanical features include tendrils that aid in climbing and the presence of swollen, club-shaped nodes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
