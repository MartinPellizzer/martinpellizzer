Uragoga korrowalensis

Uragoga korrowalensis, commonly known as the Korrowal rainforest vine, is a climbing plant native to the subtropical rainforests of Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found along the forest floor or on tree trunks.

Due to its ecological importance and unique appearance, it is a notable species in botanical studies of the region.
