Uhdea pyramidata

Uhdea pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorl at the base of the plant.

Uhdea pyramidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
