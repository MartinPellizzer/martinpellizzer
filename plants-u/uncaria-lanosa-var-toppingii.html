Uncaria lanosa var. toppingii

Uncaria lanosa var. toppingii, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw.

This variant of the cat's claw vine is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and woody stems. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
