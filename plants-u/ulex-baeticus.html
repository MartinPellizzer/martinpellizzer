Ulex baeticus

Ulex baeticus, commonly known as the Baetican burr, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical seed pods that burst open to disperse its seeds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Ulex baeticus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
