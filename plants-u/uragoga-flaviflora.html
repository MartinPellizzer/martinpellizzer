Uragoga flaviflora

Uragoga flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture.

Uragoga flaviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
