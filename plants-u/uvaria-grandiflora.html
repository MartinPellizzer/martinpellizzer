Uvaria grandiflora

Uvaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glandular-stippled leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
