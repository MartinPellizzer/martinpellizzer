Uragoga oxylepis

Uragoga oxylepis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with opposite leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Uragoga oxylepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
