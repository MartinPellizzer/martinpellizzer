Urochloa occidentalis

Urochloa occidentalis, commonly known as West African grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Urochloa occidentalis is valued for forage and soil stabilization in agricultural settings.
