Urtica magellanica var. glomeruliflora

Urtica magellanica var. glomeruliflora, commonly known as the Magellan nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
