Ulex argenteus proles ianthocladus

Ulex argenteus proles ianthocladus, commonly known as the silver furrowed hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
