Uraria guangxiensis

Uraria guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi uraria, is a flowering plant species native to southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and is adapted to humid tropical climates. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
