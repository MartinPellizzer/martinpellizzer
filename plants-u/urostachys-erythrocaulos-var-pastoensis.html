Urostachys erythrocaulos var. pastoensis

Urostachys erythrocaulos var. pastoensis, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
