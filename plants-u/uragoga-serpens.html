Uragoga serpens

Uragoga serpens, commonly known as the serpentine uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule containing minute seeds. Uragoga serpens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
