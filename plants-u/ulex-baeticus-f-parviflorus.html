Ulex baeticus f. parviflorus

Ulex baeticus f. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered furze, is a subspecies of the common furze, native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its foliage consists of leathery, evergreen leaves that provide habitat for various insects and birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to its native environment.
