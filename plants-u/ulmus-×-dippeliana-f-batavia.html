Ulmus × dippeliana f. batavia

Ulmus × dippeliana f. batavia, commonly known as the Batavia elm, is a hybrid species within the elm genus, Ulmus.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters. The tree features a rounded crown and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. It is native to the Netherlands and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resistance to certain diseases.

The Batavia elm is also valued for its adaptability to urban environments and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
