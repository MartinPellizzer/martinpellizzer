Utricularia natans

Utricularia natans, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that allow it to float on the water's surface. Utricularia natans is native to various regions across Asia, Africa, and Australia.

Its unique adaptations make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
