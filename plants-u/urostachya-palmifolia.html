Urostachya palmifolia

Urostachya palmifolia, commonly known as the palmleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves that resemble a palm tree's fronds, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Urostachya palmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
