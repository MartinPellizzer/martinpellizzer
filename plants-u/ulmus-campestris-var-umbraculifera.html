Ulmus campestris var. umbraculifera

Ulmus campestris var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the shade-tolerant elm, is a variant of the field elm characterized by its broader, more spreading leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and is adapted to grow in shaded environments, making it suitable for urban landscapes. The tree has a rounded crown and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinct base and are often dark green on top.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in less sunny conditions.
