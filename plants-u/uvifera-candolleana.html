Uvifera candolleana

Uvifera candolleana, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
