Ugni bridgesii

Ugni bridgesii, commonly known as the Andean huckleberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or dark purple in color, rich in antioxidants and used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
