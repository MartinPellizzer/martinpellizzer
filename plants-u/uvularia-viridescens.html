Uvularia viridescens

Uvularia viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
