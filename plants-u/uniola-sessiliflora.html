Uniola sessiliflora

Uniola sessiliflora, commonly known as flatleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, narrow grass-like leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in clusters. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms. Its fibrous root system helps prevent erosion, and its dense growth can suppress invasive species.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
