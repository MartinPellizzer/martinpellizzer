Unona ovata

Unona ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests. Its rhizome system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in moist, tropical understories.

The leaves are not only ornamental but also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
