Uragoga microdesmia

Uragoga microdesmia, commonly known as the small-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It attaches to host trees through its haustellum, which penetrates the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically small, thick, and glossy, adapted to reduce water loss in its often arid habitat.

Uragoga microdesmia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and influencing the growth of its host trees.
