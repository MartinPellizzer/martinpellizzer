Urostachys capellae

Urostachys capellae, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant characteristics. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Urostachys capellae is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
