Unona evecta var. intermedia

Unona evecta var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate unona, is a lesser-known species within the Unona genus.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a compact form that makes it suitable for small garden spaces.

The botanical significance of this variety lies in its potential role in ecological studies and its possible medicinal properties.
