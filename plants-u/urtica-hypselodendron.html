Urtica hypselodendron

Urtica hypselodendron, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, covered in fine, stinging hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Urtica hypselodendron thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
