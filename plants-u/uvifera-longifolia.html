Uvifera longifolia

Uvifera longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf uvilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
