Uragoga sapinii

Uragoga sapinii, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery coating of trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structure consists of spore-bearing capsules that are typically found on the undersides of its leaves.

Uragoga sapinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
