Uva rufa

Uva rufa, commonly known as the red grapefruit, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, red-orange rind and segmented, juicy orange flesh. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Uva rufa is valued for its high vitamin C content and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
