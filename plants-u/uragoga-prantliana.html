Uragoga prantliana

Uragoga prantliana, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs. Uragoga prantliana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
