Ulearum sagittatum var. viridispadix

Ulearum sagittatum var. viridispadix, commonly known as the green-veined arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments.

It features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive green veins, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist soil and partial shade, making it suitable for water gardens or bog landscapes.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
