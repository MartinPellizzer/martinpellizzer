Ulex australis

Ulex australis, commonly known as the Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to western Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and bloom in early spring.

Ulex australis has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
