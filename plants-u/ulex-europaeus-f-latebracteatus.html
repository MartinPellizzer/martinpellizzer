Ulex europaeus f. latebracteatus

Ulex europaeus f. latebracteatus, commonly known as the latebracteate furze, is a variant of the common furze, characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

This plant is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow and consist of a spiny calyx with five bracts that enclose the reproductive organs. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
