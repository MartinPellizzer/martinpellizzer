Urochloa supervacua

Urochloa supervacua, commonly known as the coastal lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in dune ecosystems where it helps stabilize sand dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh coastal climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
