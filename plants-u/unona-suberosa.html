Unona suberosa

Unona suberosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Unona suberosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
