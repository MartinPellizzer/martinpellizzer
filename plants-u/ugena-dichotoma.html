Ugena dichotoma

Ugena dichotoma, commonly known as the forked ugena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with stems splitting into two at the base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Ugena dichotoma is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
