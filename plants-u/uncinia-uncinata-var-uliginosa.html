Uncinia uncinata var. uliginosa

Uncinia uncinata var. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog hookweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in acidic, waterlogged soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slight curl at the tips, while its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting specialized insect and bird populations.
