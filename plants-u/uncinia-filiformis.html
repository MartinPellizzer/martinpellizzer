Uncinia filiformis

Uncinia filiformis, commonly known as the slender hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in undisturbed natural areas.
