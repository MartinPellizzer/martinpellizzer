Urochloa polystachya

Urochloa polystachya, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green blades and panicles that produce small, whitish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a clumping growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Its foliage and inflorescence contribute to its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
