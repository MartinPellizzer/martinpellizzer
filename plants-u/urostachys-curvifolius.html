Urostachys curvifolius

Urostachys curvifolius, commonly known as the curly-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Urostachys curvifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
