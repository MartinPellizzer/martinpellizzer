Utricularia nipponica

Utricularia nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and possesses specialized bladder-like traps used to capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wetlands, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it can thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Its leaves are usually slender and floating, with the bladders attached to the leaf tips.

Utricularia nipponica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling microorganism populations and contributing to the food web.
