Uvifera striata

Uvifera striata, commonly known as the striped tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with distinct white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional medicine.
