Ulmus procera f. vanhouttei

Ulmus procera f. vanhouttei, commonly known as the Weeping Elm, is a unique cultivar of the American elm.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a striking, umbrella-like appearance. The tree has heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, winged samaras that are dispersed by wind.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic value in parks and gardens.
