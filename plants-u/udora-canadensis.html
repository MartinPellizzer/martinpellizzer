Udora canadensis

Udora canadensis, commonly known as Canada violet, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem with hairy surfaces and produces small, fragrant blooms in early spring.

Udora canadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
