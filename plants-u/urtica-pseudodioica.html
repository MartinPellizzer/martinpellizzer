Urtica pseudodioica

Urtica pseudodioica, commonly known as false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and fine, stinging hairs on both sides. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

Urtica pseudodioica is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in woodland edges.
