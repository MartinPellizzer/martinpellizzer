Uromyrtus

Uromyrtus, commonly known as the white-flowered mistletoe, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These plants are hemiparasitic, meaning they derive some nutrients from their host plants while also performing photosynthesis. Uromyrtus species are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, where they thrive in harsh environmental conditions. Their small, white or pale flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and they produce small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals.

The genus includes several species, each with unique adaptations to survive in their specific ecological niches.
