Uvaria sambiranensis

Uvaria sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis gooseberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
