Ursinia tripartita

Ursinia tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellow disc florets surrounded by pink to purple ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Ursinia tripartita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
