Urtica nudicaulis

Urtica nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation. The plant contains stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica nudicaulis is often found in clusters and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
