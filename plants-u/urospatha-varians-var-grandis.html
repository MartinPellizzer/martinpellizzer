Urospatha varians var. grandis

Urospatha varians var. grandis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a spike, followed by edible, starchy tubers that are similar to taro. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other familiar plants like caladium and philodendron.
