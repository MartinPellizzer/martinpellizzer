Uragoga diplosphaerica

Uragoga diplosphaerica, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, waxy leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the year under optimal conditions.
