Uragoga viridis

Uragoga viridis, commonly known as the green uragoga, is a unique plant species characterized by its vibrant green foliage and distinct leaf morphology.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white and yellow.

Uragoga viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
