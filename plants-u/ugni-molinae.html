Ugni molinae

Ugni molinae, commonly known as molina's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible berries, which are used in traditional culinary practices.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
