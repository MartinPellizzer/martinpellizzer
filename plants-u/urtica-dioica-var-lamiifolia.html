Urtica dioica var. lamiifolia

Urtica dioica var. lamiifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, which contain irritant chemicals like histamine and acetylcholine. The plant has heart-shaped or ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
