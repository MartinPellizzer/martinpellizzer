Urania speciosa

Urania speciosa, commonly known as the Orchid Butterfly Plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically bright yellow with red and purple markings, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are broad and waxy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
