Ulex baeticus var. scaber

Ulex baeticus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough sea holly, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive addition to Mediterranean flora.
