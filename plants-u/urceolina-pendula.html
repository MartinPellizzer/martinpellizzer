Urceolina pendula

Urceolina pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
