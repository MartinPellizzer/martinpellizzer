Urena lobata var. americana

Urena lobata var. americana, commonly known as the American urena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, which are followed by three-lobed seed pods. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Urena lobata var. americana is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
