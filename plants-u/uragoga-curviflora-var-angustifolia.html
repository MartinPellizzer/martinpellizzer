Uragoga curviflora var. angustifolia

Uragoga curviflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed curving-flower, is a unique botanical specimen characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, curling inflorescences.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a complex floral structure with showy, often multicolored bracts that serve as an attraction for pollinators. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple branching stems that contribute to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
