Uvaria purpurea var. flava

Uvaria purpurea var. flava, commonly known as the yellow uvaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, yellow fruits. Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the genus Uvaria, often found in tropical rainforests.

The yellow uvaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
