Urostigma intramarginale

Urostigma intramarginale, commonly known as the margins-edge dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster near the ground. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender achene with a pappus, allowing for wind dispersal.

Urostigma intramarginale is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
