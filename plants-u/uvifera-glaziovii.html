Uvifera glaziovii

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a thick, succulent stem that helps it store water, adapting to its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss.

Uvifera glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
