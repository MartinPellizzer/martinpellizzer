Urtica dubia

Urtica dubia, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release formic acid, causing a painful rash upon contact. Urtica dubia typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a perennial plant that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, often forming dense clusters.
