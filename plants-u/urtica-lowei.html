Urtica lowei

Urtica lowei, commonly known as the Low's stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica lowei is often found in the southeastern region of the U.S., particularly in areas with rich, loamy soil.
