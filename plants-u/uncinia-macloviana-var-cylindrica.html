Uncinia macloviana var. cylindrica

Uncinia macloviana var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical hookweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant produces small, dry fruits.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
