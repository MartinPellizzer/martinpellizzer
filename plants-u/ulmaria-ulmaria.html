Ulmaria ulmaria

Ulmaria ulmaria, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shady woods and along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white, umbrella-shaped flower clusters. Its aromatic, licorice-scented seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ulmaria ulmaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
