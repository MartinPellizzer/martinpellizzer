Uragoga mandiocana

Uragoga mandiocana, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Marantaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its starchy roots are harvested for culinary use, similar to taro.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
