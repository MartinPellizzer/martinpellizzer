Usteria barclayana

Usteria barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Usteria barclayana produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
