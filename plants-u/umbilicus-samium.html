Umbilicus samium

Umbilicus samium, commonly known as the common umbilicus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its umbellate inflorescence, which consists of small white or greenish flowers arranged in a cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a mild, aromatic scent.
