Urena lobata

Urena lobata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by three-lobed seed pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Botanically, it is classified under the family Malvaceae, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
