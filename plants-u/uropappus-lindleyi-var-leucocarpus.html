Uropappus lindleyi var. leucocarpus

Uropappus lindleyi var. leucocarpus, commonly known as white-fruited burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, white, spherical fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and it plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
