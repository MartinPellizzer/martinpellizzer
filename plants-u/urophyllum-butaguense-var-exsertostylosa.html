Urophyllum butaguense var. exsertostylosa

Urophyllum butaguense var. exsertostylosa, commonly known as the exsertostylosa variant of the butaguense urrophyllum, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique floral morphology.

It features elongated, exserted stamens that extend beyond the corolla, a trait that distinguishes it from other variants of the species. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
