Usteria secunda

Usteria secunda, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Usteria secunda plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
