Ursinia albicaulis

Ursinia albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ursinia albicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate, daisy-like flowers.
