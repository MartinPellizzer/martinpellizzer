Uva timoriensis

Uva timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor grape, is a small, woody vine native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Uva timoriensis is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in traditional medicine.
