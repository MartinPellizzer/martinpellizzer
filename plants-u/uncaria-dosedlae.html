Uncaria dosedlae

Uncaria dosedlae, commonly known as the Brazilian cat's claw.

It is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its woody stems and dense, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
