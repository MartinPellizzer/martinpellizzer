Uralepis capensis

Uralepis capensis, commonly known as the Cape uraria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It has a creeping growth habit and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Uralepis capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
