Unona polycarpa

Unona polycarpa, commonly known as the tropical fruit tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen canopy and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with secondary growth and a fibrous root system.

Unona polycarpa is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
