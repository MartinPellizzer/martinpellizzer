Urtica maculata

Urtica maculata, commonly known as the spotted nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Urtica maculata has hollow, stinging hairs on its stems and leaves, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
