Urocarpidium albiflorum

Urocarpidium albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflowered urocarp, is a member of the family Urocarpaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, hard seeds.

The species is noted for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local insect populations.
