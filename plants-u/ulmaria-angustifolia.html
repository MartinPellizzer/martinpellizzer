Ulmaria angustifolia

Ulmaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and fern-like, finely divided leaves. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Ulmaria angustifolia is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
