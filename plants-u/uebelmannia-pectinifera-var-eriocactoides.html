Uebelmannia pectinifera var. eriocactoides

Uebelmannia pectinifera var. eriocactoides, commonly known as the Ervicactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in short, spiny bristles. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
