Utricularia dichotoma subsp. novae-angliae

Utricularia dichotoma subsp.

novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England whorled bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the eastern United States. It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor soils such as marshes and bogs.

The plant features whorled leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures used to trap prey.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
