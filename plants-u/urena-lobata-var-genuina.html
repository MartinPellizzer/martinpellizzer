Urena lobata var. genuina

Urena lobata var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
