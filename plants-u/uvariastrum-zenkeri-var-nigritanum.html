Uvariastrum zenkeri var. nigritanum

Uvariastrum zenkeri var. nigritanum, commonly known as the black-fruit uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark purple to black berries. The berries are typically clustered and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It is often found in humid forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
