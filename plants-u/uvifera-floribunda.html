Uvifera floribunda

Uvifera floribunda, commonly known as the flowering grapefruit tree, is a unique plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fruit that resembles a grapefruit in appearance but is typically smaller and has a more delicate flavor. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
