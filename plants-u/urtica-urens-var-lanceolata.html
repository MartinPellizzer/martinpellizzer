Urtica urens var. lanceolata

Urtica urens var. lanceolata, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant features heart-shaped or lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Urtica urens var. lanceolata is a perennial species that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often found in disturbed soils and along woodland edges.

Its botanical significance includes its role in traditional medicine and as a food source for certain insects.
