Uvaria littoralis

Uvaria littoralis, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Uvaria littoralis is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
