Ulmus kijimana

Ulmus kijimana, commonly known as the Kijimana elm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ulmaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a broad, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The bark of Ulmus kijimana is thick and fissured, providing some protection against pests and environmental stress.
