Urostachys deminuens

Urostachys deminuens, commonly known as the small-leaved stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Urostachys deminuens is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
