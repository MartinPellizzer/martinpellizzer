Uvaria chariensis

Uvaria chariensis, commonly known as the African star anise, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, often hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

Uvaria chariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
