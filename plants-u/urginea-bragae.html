Urginea bragae

Urginea bragae, commonly known as the giant squill, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground stem and tall, sword-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces showy, white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
