Ulmus pumila f. pendula

Ulmus pumila f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese elm, is a unique cultivar of the Chinese elm tree.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that droop downward, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically a dark green on the upper surface and lighter green on the underside. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
