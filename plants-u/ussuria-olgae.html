Ussuria olgae

Ussuria olgae, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of Eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of gymnosperms. The tree thrives in boreal and subarctic regions, where it plays a vital role in forest ecosystems. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Ussuria olgae is also important for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various species.
