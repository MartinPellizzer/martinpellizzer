Urceola montana

Urceola montana, commonly known as the mountain urn plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flower heads, which are typically blue to purple in color. The plant has hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Urceola montana is often found in open meadows and along mountain slopes where it blooms in late summer.
