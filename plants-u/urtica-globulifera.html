Urtica globulifera

Urtica globulifera, commonly known as the globe wheaton.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly found in the Caribbean and parts of Central America. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed pods, which contain small, round seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, which can cause mild irritation upon contact.

Urtica globulifera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
