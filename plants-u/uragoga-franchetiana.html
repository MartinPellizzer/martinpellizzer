Uragoga franchetiana

Uragoga franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Uragoga franchetiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
