Uvaria cinerascens

Uvaria cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of ash trees. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
