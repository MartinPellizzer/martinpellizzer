Uncinia araucana

Uncinia araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria nut, is a species of conifer native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, evergreen foliage that forms a pyramidal shape. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source for indigenous communities. Its leaves are needle-like and remain on the plant for several years before falling.

Uncinia araucana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
