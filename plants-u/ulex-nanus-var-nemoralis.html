Ulex nanus var. nemoralis

Ulex nanus var. nemoralis, commonly known as the creeping furze, is a low-growing shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a dry capsule containing several seeds.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed or open habitats.
