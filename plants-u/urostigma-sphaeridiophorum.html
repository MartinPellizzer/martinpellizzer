Urostigma sphaeridiophorum

Urostigma sphaeridiophorum, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

Urostigma sphaeridiophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
