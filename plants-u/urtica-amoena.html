Urtica amoena

Urtica amoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs that cause a skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Urtica amoena is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
