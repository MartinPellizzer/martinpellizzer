Urbanolophium dusenianum

Urbanolophium dusenianum, commonly known as the Dusen's urban lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in urban environments.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like thallus and a distinctive yellowish-green color. This lichen is often found on bark, stone, and other surfaces in cities, where it plays a role in air quality monitoring. Its structure consists of a fungal matrix intertwined with algal cells, which provide photosynthetic capability.

Urbanolophium dusenianum is an indicator species for pollution levels and ecological health in urban settings.
