Urtica montana

Urtica montana, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

The plant contains a mixture of formic acid and histamine in its stinging hairs, which cause irritation upon contact with the skin. Urtica montana thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a food source when cooked.
