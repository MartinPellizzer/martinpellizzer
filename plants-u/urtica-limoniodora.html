Urtica limoniodora

Urtica limoniodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that emit a distinct lemon-like fragrance when crushed. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica limoniodora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
