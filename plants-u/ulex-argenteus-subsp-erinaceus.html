Ulex argenteus subsp. erinaceus

Ulex argenteus subsp.

erinaceus, commonly known as the silver buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.
