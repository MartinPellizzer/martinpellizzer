Urodon capitatus

Urodon capitatus, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure that gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on tall, erect stems.

Urodon capitatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
