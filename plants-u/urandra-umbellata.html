Urandra umbellata

Urandra umbellata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruits are capsules that release seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

Urandra umbellata is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
