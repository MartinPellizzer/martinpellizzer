Urginea bequaertii

Urginea bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's giant bulb, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, fleshy, underground bulb that can grow up to several feet in diameter. It produces striking, white to pale purple flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
