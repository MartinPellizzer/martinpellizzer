Urochloa xantholeuca

Urochloa xantholeuca, commonly known as yellow leucaena, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically yellow-green in color, and its small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark brown seeds that are often used for forage and soil improvement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable plant in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.

Urochloa xantholeuca is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
