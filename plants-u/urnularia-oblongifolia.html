Urnularia oblongifolia

Urnularia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf urnularia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the moist, shaded environments it inhabits.

Urnularia oblongifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
