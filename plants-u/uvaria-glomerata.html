Uvaria glomerata

Uvaria glomerata, commonly known as the clustered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Uvaria glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
