Urtica carolinensis

Urtica carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which release irritants upon contact with the skin. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in woodland edges.

Urtica carolinensis is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants.
