Uvaria sphenocarpa

Uvaria sphenocarpa, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces black, red, and white berries, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its fruit contains a high concentration of essential oils and has been used for its purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Uvaria sphenocarpa make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
