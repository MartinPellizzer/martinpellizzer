Utricularia tortilis var. andongensis

Utricularia tortilis var. andongensis, commonly known as the Andong bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are usually slender and spirally arranged, with the bladders located at the base of the leaf stems.

It is known for its ability to float or grow submerged, depending on environmental conditions.
