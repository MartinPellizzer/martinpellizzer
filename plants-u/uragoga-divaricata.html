Uragoga divaricata

Uragoga divaricata, commonly known as the variegated ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by red berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Uragoga divaricata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb walls or trellises.
