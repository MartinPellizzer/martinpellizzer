Uncaria insignis

Uncaria insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, Uncaria insignis is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
