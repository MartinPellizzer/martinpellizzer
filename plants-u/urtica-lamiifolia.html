Urtica lamiifolia

Urtica lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and dense clusters of stinging hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

Urtica lamiifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
