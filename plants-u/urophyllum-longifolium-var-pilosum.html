Urophyllum longifolium var. pilosum

Urophyllum longifolium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf urophyllum, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
