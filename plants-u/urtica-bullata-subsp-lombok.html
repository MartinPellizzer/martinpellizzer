Urtica bullata subsp. lombok

Urtica bullata subsp.

lombok, commonly known as the Lombok stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Indonesian island of Lombok. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its stinging properties, it requires careful handling during collection or cultivation.
