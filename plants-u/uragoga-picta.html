Uragoga picta

Uragoga picta, commonly known as the painted uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that display intricate patterns of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Uragoga picta is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
