Urostachys hartwegianus var. viridissimus

Urostachys hartwegianus var. viridissimus, commonly known as the emerald leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively low maintenance and resistant to drought.
