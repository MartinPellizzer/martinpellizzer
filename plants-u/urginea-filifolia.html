Urginea filifolia

Urginea filifolia, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stem that resembles an elephant's foot, which stores water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
