Urtica cuneifolia

Urtica cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and a dense covering of stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Urtica cuneifolia is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height.
