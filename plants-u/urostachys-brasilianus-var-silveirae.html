Urostachys brasilianus var. silveirae

Urostachys brasilianus var. silveirae, commonly known as the Brazilian silvergrass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
