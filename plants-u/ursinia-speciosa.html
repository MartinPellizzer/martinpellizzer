Ursinia speciosa

Ursinia speciosa, commonly known as the golden quill or sweet sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are borne in dense, spike-like clusters and bloom in the spring and summer.

Ursinia speciosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
