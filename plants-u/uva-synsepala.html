Uva synsepala

Uva synsepala, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically five-lobed and yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate with a pointed tip and a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Uva synsepala is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a tart-sweet flavor.
