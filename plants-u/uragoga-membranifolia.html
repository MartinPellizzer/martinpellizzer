Uragoga membranifolia

Uragoga membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous-leaf uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, papery leaves that are often translucent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences that add to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
