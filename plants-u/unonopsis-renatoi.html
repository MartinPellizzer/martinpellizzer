Unonopsis renatoi

Unonopsis renatoi, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
