Ulmus carpinifolia f. sarniensis

Ulmus carpinifolia f. sarniensis, commonly known as the Sarnian field maple, is a rare and distinctive form of the field maple.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and slender, weeping growth habit. The plant is native to the Carpathian Mountains and is adapted to moist, calcareous soils. It exhibits unique morphological features, including its elongated branches and delicate foliage.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
