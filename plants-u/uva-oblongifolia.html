Uva oblongifolia

Uva oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian grape or Brazilian uva, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Uva oblongifolia thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity conservation.
