Unisema cordata f. angustifolia

Unisema cordata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heartleaf, is a variant of the species Unisema cordata, characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a pointed apex and a broad base, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
