Utricularia sprengelii var. acuticeras

Utricularia sprengelii var. acuticeras, commonly known as the acute-shelled bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where it absorbs nutrients from captured aquatic organisms. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and efficient trapping mechanism.
