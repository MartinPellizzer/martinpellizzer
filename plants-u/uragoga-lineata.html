Uragoga lineata

Uragoga lineata, commonly known as the lineated uragoga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, linear leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Uragoga lineata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
