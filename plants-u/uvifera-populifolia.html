Uvifera populifolia

Uvifera populifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaped areas.
