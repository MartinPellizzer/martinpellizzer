Umsema mucronata

Umsema mucronata, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy areas, often near water bodies. The plant features soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually with five pink or reddish petals.

The plant is valued for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
