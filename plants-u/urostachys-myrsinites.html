Urostachys myrsinites

Urostachys myrsinites, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features silver-gray leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Urostachys myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
