Uragoga paniculata

Uragoga paniculata, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a dark central spot. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of flowers on long, branching stalks. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Uragoga paniculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
