Unona ovata var. afzeliana

Unona ovata var. afzeliana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing hard seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The wood is valued for its durability and has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
