Uncinia brevicaulis f. montana

Uncinia brevicaulis f. montana, commonly known as the mountain hook-sedge, is a variant of the hook-sedge species native to alpine regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas of North America.
