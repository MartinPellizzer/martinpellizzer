Ulmus americana var. glabra

Ulmus americana var. glabra, commonly known as the American elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its broad, spreading crown with elongated, heart-shaped leaves. The tree is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

However, it is highly susceptible to Dutch elm disease, which has significantly reduced its population in many regions.
