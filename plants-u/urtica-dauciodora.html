Urtica dauciodora

Urtica dauciodora, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

Urtica dauciodora is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
