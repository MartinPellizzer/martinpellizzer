Unisema lancifolia f. trullifolia

Unisema lancifolia f. trullifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed unisema, is a variant of the unisema genus characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

This plant exhibits a distinct botanical feature in its leaf morphology, with the leaves being narrow and pointed, giving it a striking appearance. It is typically found in specific habitats, often in moist, shaded environments. The flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to certain growing conditions.
