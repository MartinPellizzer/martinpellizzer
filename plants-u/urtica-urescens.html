Urtica urescens

Urtica urescens, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Urtica urescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
