Uvaria confertiflora

Uvaria confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The fruits are fleshy berries that turn from green to red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds.

Uvaria confertiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
