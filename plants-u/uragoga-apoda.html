Uragoga apoda

Uragoga apoda, commonly known as the leafless balsam, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with most of its photosynthetic activity occurring through its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Uragoga apoda is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
