Urtica aureliana

Urtica aureliana, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
