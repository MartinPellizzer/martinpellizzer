Urochloa reptans

Urochloa reptans, commonly known as carpet grass, is a tropical and subtropical perennial grass species native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its ability to spread rapidly through stolons, making it a common weed in lawns and pastures. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and may have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Urochloa reptans is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.
