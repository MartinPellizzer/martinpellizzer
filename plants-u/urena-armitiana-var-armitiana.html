Urena armitiana var. armitiana

Urena armitiana var. armitiana, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a tropical and subtropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in many regions.
