Ulmus campestris var. mollis

Ulmus campestris var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved field elm, is a variant of the field elm native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly lobed. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse by wind.

This variety is less hardy than other elms and is susceptible to Dutch elm disease.
