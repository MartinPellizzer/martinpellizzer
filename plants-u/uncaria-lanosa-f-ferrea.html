Uncaria lanosa f. ferrea

Uncaria lanosa f. ferrea, commonly known as the Brazilian cat's claw.

This plant is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its woody stems and hirsute (hairy) texture. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects.
