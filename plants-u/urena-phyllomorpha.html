Urena phyllomorpha

Urena phyllomorpha, commonly known as the hairy urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, hairy seed pods. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with prominent veins and a slightly hairy surface.

Urena phyllomorpha is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in poor, arid conditions.
