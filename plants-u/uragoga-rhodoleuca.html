Uragoga rhodoleuca

Uragoga rhodoleuca, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. Its roots are reddish in color and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Uragoga rhodoleuca is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
