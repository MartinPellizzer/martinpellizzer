Uncaria membranifolia

Uncaria membranifolia, commonly known as the creeping or climbing cat's claw, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
