Ulex europaeus subsp. latebracteatus

Ulex europaeus subsp.

latebracteatus, commonly known as the latebracteate goat's-rhino, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, red, spherical fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
