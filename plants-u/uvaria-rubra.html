Uvaria rubra

Uvaria rubra, commonly known as the red uvaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or orange berries. Uvaria rubra is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
