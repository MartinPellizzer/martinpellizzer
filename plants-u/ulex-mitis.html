Ulex mitis

Ulex mitis, commonly known as the Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ulex mitis is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in ecological restoration projects.
