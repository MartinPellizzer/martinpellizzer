Uvularia pitsutu

Uvularia pitsutu, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is well adapted to partial shade.
