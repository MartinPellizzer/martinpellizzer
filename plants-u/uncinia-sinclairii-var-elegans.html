Uncinia sinclairii var. elegans

Uncinia sinclairii var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant hookweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features slender, hooked stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
