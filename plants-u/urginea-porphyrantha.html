Urginea porphyrantha

Urginea porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered African giant lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a thick, fleshy bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
