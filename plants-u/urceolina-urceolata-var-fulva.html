Urceolina urceolata var. fulva

Urceolina urceolata var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow cup-plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more elongated and less hairy. Its flowering stems are unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.
