Urachne megalantha

Urachne megalantha, commonly known as the giant urachne, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. Its adaptations to arid environments include thick, water-storing tissues and a shallow root system.

Urachne megalantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
