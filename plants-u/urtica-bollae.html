Urtica bollae

Urtica bollae, commonly known as the woolly nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Urtica bollae is a member of the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a distinctive species within its native range.
