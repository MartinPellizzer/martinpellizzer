Ulex minor subsp. gallii

Ulex minor subsp.

gallii, commonly known as the Scottish heath or lesser bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
