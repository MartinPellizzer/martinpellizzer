Unona diversifolia

Unona diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf unona, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that vary in color from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and culinary uses, it is a valued species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
