Uragoga sulcata

Uragoga sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its compact growth habit and ability to bloom year-round contribute to its ornamental value.

Uragoga sulcata is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which makes it easy to cultivate.
