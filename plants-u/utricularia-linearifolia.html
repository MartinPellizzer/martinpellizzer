Utricularia linearifolia

Utricularia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, thread-like leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used for trapping prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes.

Utricularia linearifolia is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters by supplementing its diet with captured microorganisms.
