Uragoga grandiflora

Uragoga grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with a branching stem and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom during the warmer months.

Uragoga grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
