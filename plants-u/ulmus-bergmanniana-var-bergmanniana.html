Ulmus bergmanniana var. bergmanniana

Ulmus bergmanniana var. bergmanniana, commonly known as the Bergmann's elm, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The leaves are elongated, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique form and seasonal interest.
