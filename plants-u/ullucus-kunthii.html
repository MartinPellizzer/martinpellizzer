Ullucus kunthii

Ullucus kunthii, commonly known as the Andean groundpea, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often brightly colored tubers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has a sprawling growth habit. Its tubers are used in traditional Andean cuisine and are valued for their nutty flavor and nutritional content.
