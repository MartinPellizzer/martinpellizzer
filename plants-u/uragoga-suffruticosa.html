Uragoga suffruticosa

Uragoga suffruticosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
