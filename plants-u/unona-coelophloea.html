Unona coelophloea

Unona coelophloea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
