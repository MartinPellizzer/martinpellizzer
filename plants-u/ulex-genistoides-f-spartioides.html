Ulex genistoides f. spartioides

Ulex genistoides f. spartioides, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Ulex genistoides f. spartioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
