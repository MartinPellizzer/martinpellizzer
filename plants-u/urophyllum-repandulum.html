Urophyllum repandulum

Urophyllum repandulum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Urophyllum repandulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
