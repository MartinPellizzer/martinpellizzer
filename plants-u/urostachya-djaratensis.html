Urostachya djaratensis

Urostachya djaratensis, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Its fibrous material has historically been used for making ropes and textiles by indigenous peoples.
