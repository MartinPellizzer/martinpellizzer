Uvifera urbaniana

Uvifera urbaniana, commonly known as the urban wild grape, is a woody vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found growing along walls or in rocky areas.

Uvifera urbaniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
