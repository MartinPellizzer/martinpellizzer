Urocarpus phebalioides

Urocarpus phebalioides, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Urocarpus phebalioides is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
