Utricularia panamensis

Utricularia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is a small, aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, nutrient-poor waters. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures called utricles, which it uses to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. Its roots are often submerged, and it can float on the water's surface.

Utricularia panamensis is known for its rapid growth and unique adaptive features that allow it to survive in its specific ecological niche.
