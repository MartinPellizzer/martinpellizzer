Uncaria horsfieldiana

Uncaria horsfieldiana, commonly known as the Horsfield's climbing shrub, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twisted, thorny stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
