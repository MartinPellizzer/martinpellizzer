Ulmus glaucescens

Ulmus glaucescens, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to urban conditions.

The Chinese elm is often used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
