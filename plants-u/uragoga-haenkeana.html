Uragoga haenkeana

Uragoga haenkeana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Uragoga haenkeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
