Urceola napeensis

Urceola napeensis, commonly known as the Napeen's urn cactus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique urn-shaped stems that store water. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
