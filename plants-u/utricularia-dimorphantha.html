Utricularia dimorphantha

Utricularia dimorphantha, commonly known as the red-bellied bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, translucent bladders used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, where it relies on its specialized structures for nutrition. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowering stems emerge above the water surface.

Utricularia dimorphantha plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling microbial populations and contributing to the food web.
