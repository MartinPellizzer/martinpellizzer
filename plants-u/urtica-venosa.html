Urtica venosa

Urtica venosa, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that release formic acid when touched. Urtica venosa is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
