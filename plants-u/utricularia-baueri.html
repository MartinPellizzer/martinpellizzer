Utricularia baueri

Utricularia baueri, commonly known as the Bauer's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, translucent bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, relying on its specialized structures for nutrition. Its leaves are usually floating or submerged, with the bladders attached to the leaf surfaces.

Utricularia baueri is an example of the diverse adaptations found in the Utricularia genus, showcasing its unique method of obtaining nutrients.
