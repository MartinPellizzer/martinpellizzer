Unona pilosa

Unona pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals and prominent stamens. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Unona pilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
