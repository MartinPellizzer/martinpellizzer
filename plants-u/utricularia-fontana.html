Utricularia fontana

Utricularia fontana, commonly known as the water whorl bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used for trapping prey. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive system involves both sexual and asexual reproduction, with flowers that are typically small and greenish-white in color.

Utricularia fontana plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
