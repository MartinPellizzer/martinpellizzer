Urtica urens

Urtica urens, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, nitrogen-rich soils. Urtica urens produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
