Uva aurita

Uva aurita, commonly known as the golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Uva aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
