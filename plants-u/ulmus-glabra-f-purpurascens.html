Ulmus glabra f. purpurascens

Ulmus glabra f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf elm, is a unique variant of the American elm.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant has a slender, upright growth habit with a spreading crown, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a smooth, waxy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
