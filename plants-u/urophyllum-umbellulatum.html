Urophyllum umbellulatum

Urophyllum umbellulatum, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features a tall, narrow stem with whorled leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
