Uvularia lanuginosa

Uvularia lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with hairy stems and leaves. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
