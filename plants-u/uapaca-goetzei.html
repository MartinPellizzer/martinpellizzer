Uapaca goetzei

Uapaca goetzei, commonly known as the African blackwood or kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Uapaca goetzei is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and as a source of the kola nut, which has cultural and economic significance.
