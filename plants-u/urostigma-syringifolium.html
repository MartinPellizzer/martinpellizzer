Urostigma syringifolium

Urostigma syringifolium, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly heart-shaped base.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
