Urceolaria pendula

Urceolaria pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features striking, pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive dark spot at the base. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
