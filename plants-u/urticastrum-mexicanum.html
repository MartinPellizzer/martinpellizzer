Urticastrum mexicanum

Urticastrum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in composite heads.

Urticastrum mexicanum is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
