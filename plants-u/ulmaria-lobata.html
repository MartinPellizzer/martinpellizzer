Ulmaria lobata

Ulmaria lobata, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, aromatic seed oil that is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ulmaria lobata is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
