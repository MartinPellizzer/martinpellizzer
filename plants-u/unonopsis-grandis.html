Unonopsis grandis

Unonopsis grandis, commonly known as the giant unon, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can span several meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. The plant produces large, ornamental inflorescences that are often used in horticultural displays.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is a popular choice for large gardens and botanical collections.
