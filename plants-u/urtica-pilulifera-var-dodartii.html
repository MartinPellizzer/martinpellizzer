Urtica pilulifera var. dodartii

Urtica pilulifera var. dodartii, commonly known as the stinging nettle variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a painful rash. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its growth habit includes a low, spreading form with upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.
