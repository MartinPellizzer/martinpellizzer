Urochloa arrecta

Urochloa arrecta, commonly known as upright grass or tall weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Urochloa arrecta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

It is also valued for its resilience to drought and moderate grazing.
