Uncifera verrucosa

Uncifera verrucosa, commonly known as the warty spongeplant, is a unique member of the family Monotropaceae.

It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and its ability to perform photosynthesis through its mycorrhizal associations with fungi. The plant has a fleshy, sponge-like appearance with a central stem and warty, leaf-like structures. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual symbiotic relationship and its role in forest ecosystems.
