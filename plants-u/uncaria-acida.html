Uncaria acida

Uncaria acida, commonly known as the prickly ash or Indian hawthorn, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, hooked prickles along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
