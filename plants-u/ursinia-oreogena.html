Ursinia oreogena

Ursinia oreogena, commonly known as the mountain ursinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Ursinia oreogena is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
