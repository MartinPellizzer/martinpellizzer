Ursinia scapiformis

Ursinia scapiformis, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Ursinia scapiformis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
