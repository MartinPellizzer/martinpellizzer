Unisema peduncularis

Unisema peduncularis, commonly known as the single-leaf pedunculate, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant is distinguished by its single, large leaf that emerges directly from the stem, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spring, and are usually white or pale green.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Unisema peduncularis is considered a species of conservation concern.
