Uvularia rosea

Uvularia rosea, commonly known as the rose bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to lavender flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in woodland environments, preferring partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Its showy flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
