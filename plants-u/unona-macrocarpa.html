Unona macrocarpa

Unona macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming at night. Its roots anchor it in shallow waters, while its floating leaves support a diverse ecosystem.

Unona macrocarpa plays an important role in water purification and serves as a food source for aquatic animals.
