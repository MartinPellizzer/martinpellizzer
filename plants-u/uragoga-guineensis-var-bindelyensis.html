Uragoga guineensis var. bindelyensis

Uragoga guineensis var. bindelyensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
