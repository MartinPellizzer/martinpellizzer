Ulmus davidiana f. levigata

Ulmus davidiana f. levigata, commonly known as the Chinese cliff elm, is a unique variant of the Chinese elm.

It is characterized by its smooth, thin bark and small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. This form is often found growing on rocky outcrops and cliff faces, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness, making it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
