Urtica ulmifolia

Urtica ulmifolia, commonly known as lesser nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica ulmifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
