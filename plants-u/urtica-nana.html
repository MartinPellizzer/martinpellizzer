Urtica nana

Urtica nana, commonly known as dwarf nettle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs that cause irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Urtica nana is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
