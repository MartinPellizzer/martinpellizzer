Uragoga leucocarpa

Uragoga leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited uragoga, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, fleshy fruits that resemble small, round lanterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a glossy surface.

The botanical interest of Uragoga leucocarpa lies in its unique fruit structure and its potential ecological role in its native habitat.
