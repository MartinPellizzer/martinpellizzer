Uncaria tomentosa

Uncaria tomentosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or cat's claw, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, fragrant white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the bark and roots containing alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
