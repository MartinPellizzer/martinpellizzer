Ulmus parvifolia

Ulmus parvifolia, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It has a fast growth rate and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

The bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
