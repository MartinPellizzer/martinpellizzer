Urtica dioica var. procera

Urtica dioica var. procera, commonly known as tall nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle characterized by its taller growth habit and larger leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America, often found in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features hollow, stout stems covered in fine stinging hairs, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences, and its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
