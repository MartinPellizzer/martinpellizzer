Urostachys verticillatus var. filiformis

Urostachys verticillatus var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
