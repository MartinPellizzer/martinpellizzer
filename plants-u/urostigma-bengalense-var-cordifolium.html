Urostigma bengalense var. cordifolium

Urostigma bengalense var. cordifolium, commonly known as the Bengal heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
