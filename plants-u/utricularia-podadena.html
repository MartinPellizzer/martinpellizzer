Utricularia podadena

Utricularia podadena, commonly known as the bladderworts, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures called utricles, which function as traps to capture tiny aquatic organisms. These utricles are inflated and release a jet of water to suck in prey, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Utricularia podadena is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
