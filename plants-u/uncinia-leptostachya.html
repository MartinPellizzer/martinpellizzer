Uncinia leptostachya

Uncinia leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hookgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks. The plant has thin, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, awned florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Uncinia leptostachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
