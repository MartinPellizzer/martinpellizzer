Uvaria moralesii

Uvaria moralesii, commonly known as the red-flowered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Uvaria moralesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
