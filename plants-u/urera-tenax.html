Urera tenax

Urera tenax, commonly known as the evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow center, and bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in tropical regions.
