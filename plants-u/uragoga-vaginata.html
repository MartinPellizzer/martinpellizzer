Uragoga vaginata

Uragoga vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Uragoga vaginata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
