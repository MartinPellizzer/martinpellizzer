Uragoga coronata

Uragoga coronata, commonly known as the crown coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

Uragoga coronata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
