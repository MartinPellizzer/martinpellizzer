Uvifera humboldtii

Uvifera humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's uvilla, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate temperatures and salt spray.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
