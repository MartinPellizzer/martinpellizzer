Umbilicus rupestris subsp. horizontalis

Umbilicus rupestris subsp.

horizontalis, commonly known as the rock umbilicus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its short, thick, fleshy stems that often lie horizontally along the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
