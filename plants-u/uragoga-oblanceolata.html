Uragoga oblanceolata

Uragoga oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga oblanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
