Umbilicus oppositifolius

Umbilicus oppositifolius, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny areas and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a creeping rootstock and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in gardens and fields.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
