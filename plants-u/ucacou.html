Ucacou

Ucacou, commonly known as the "flower of the forest," is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in dense, humid forests and thrives in shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a slight waxy coating.

Ucacou is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
