Urena armitiana

Urena armitiana, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas, roadsides, and waste places.

Urena armitiana is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
