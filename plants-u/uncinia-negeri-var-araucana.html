Uncinia negeri var. araucana

Uncinia negeri var. araucana, commonly known as the Araucanian hookgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
