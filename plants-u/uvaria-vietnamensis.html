Uvaria vietnamensis

Uvaria vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese starfruit, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both decorative and culinary purposes.
