Uvaria fluminensis

Uvaria fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet pepper or yellow uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Uvaria fluminensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
