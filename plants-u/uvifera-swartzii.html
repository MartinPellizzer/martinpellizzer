Uvifera swartzii

Uvifera swartzii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Uvifera swartzii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
