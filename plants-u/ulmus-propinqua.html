Ulmus propinqua

Ulmus propinqua, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds. It is relatively resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions further enhances its horticultural value.
