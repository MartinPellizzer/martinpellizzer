Urostachys australis

Urostachys australis, commonly known as the Australian feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems that form dense tufts, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Urostachys australis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
