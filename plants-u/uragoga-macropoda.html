Uragoga macropoda

Uragoga macropoda, commonly known as the giant uragoga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features robust, upright stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching profusely. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a deep green color. Its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, with small, tubular blossoms that are typically white or pale yellow.

Uragoga macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
