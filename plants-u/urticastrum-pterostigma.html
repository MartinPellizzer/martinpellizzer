Urticastrum pterostigma

Urticastrum pterostigma, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact, similar to other members of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodlands.

Urticastrum pterostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators.
