Ulex australis subsp. welwitschianus

Ulex australis subsp.

welwitschianus, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, spiny stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.

The Cape rush is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
