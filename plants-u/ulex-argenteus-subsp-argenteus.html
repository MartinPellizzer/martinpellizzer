Ulex argenteus subsp. argenteus

Ulex argenteus subsp.

argenteus, commonly known as the silver buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.
