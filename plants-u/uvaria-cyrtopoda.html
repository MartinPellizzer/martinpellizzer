Uvaria cyrtopoda

Uvaria cyrtopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue to black berries. Uvaria cyrtopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in shaded garden settings or as a hedge.
