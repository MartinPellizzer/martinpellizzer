Umbilicus thyrsiflorus

Umbilicus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the mountain umbilicus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a distinctive, umbilicated base that gives it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
