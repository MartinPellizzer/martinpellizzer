Urtica sicula

Urtica sicula, commonly known as Sicilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Urtica sicula is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
