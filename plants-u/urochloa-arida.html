Urochloa arida

Urochloa arida, commonly known as sand grass or desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showing remarkable adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often used for erosion control due to its dense root system and ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils.

Urochloa arida is also valued for forage in livestock grazing in dryland areas.
