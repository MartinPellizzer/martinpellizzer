Uncinia caespitosa var. viridis

Uncinia caespitosa var. viridis, commonly known as the green tufted grass, is a variant of the tufted grass species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming low, tufted clumps that are often found in open, disturbed areas. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically green to bluish-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
