Ulmus × fordii

Ulmus × fordii, commonly known as the Chinese elms, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ulmus parvifolia and Ulmus laciniata.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Ulmus × fordii is also valued for its resistance to urban environments and pests.
