Urginea aurantiaca

Urginea aurantiaca, commonly known as the red tulip or orange lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its bulbs are toxic and should not be ingested.

Urginea aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
