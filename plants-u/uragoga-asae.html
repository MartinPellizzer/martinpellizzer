Uragoga asae

Uragoga asae, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble raindrops, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Uragoga asae is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
