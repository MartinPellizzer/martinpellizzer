Utricularia nigrescens

Utricularia nigrescens, commonly known as the black water bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps that capture and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often submerged or partially submerged. Its leaves are usually slender and branched, with the bladders arranged along the stems.

The plant's ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters is due to its unique method of obtaining nutrients through predation.
