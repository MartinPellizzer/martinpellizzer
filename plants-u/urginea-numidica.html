Urginea numidica

Urginea numidica, commonly known as the Numidian giant lily, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically blooming in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
