Urospermum echioides

Urospermum echioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in prairies, roadsides, and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Urospermum echioides produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
