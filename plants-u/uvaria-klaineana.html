Uvaria klaineana

Uvaria klaineana, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic leaves, and a unique seed structure.

Uvaria klaineana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
