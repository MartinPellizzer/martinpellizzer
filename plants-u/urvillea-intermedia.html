Urvillea intermedia

Urvillea intermedia, commonly known as the yellow urvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
