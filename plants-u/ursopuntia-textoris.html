Ursopuntia textoris

Ursopuntia textoris, commonly known as the velvet cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety covering of fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. Its stems are typically short and branched, forming a compact, bushy structure.

Ursopuntia textoris is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and visually interesting plant for cultivation in dry climates.
