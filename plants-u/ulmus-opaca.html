Ulmus opaca

Ulmus opaca, commonly known as the American elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, vase-shaped crown. The tree has compound, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. Its bark is rough and fissured, becoming deeply grooved with age.

Ulmus opaca produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits.
