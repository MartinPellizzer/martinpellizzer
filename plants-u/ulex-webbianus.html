Ulex webbianus

Ulex webbianus, commonly known as the common goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 cm. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its sharp, prickly stems.

Ulex webbianus is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
