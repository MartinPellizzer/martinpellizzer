Uragoga latistipula

Uragoga latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptation to moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.
