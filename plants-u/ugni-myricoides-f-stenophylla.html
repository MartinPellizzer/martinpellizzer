Ugni myricoides f. stenophylla

Ugni myricoides f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a botanical variant of the myrtle plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
