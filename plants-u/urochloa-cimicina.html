Urochloa cimicina

Urochloa cimicina, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open habitats. Urochloa cimicina is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used for forage or soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
