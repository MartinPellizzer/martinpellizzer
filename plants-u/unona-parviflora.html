Unona parviflora

Unona parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered unona, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Unona parviflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
