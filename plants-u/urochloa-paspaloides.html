Urochloa paspaloides

Urochloa paspaloides, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and often have a bluish-green color.

Urochloa paspaloides is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
