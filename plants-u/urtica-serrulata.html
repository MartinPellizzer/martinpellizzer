Urtica serrulata

Urtica serrulata, commonly known as the serrated nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its root system is fibrous and shallow.

Urtica serrulata is often found in sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments.
