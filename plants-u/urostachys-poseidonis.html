Urostachys poseidonis

Urostachys poseidonis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for coastal gardens.

Urostachys poseidonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
