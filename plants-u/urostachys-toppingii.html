Urostachys toppingii

Urostachys toppingii, commonly known as the Tibetan feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, dry climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a subject of interest in both botanical studies and horticulture.
