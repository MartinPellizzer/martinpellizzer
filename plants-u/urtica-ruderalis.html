Urtica ruderalis

Urtica ruderalis, commonly known as the common nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain irritating chemicals that cause a skin reaction in humans. This plant is typically found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. Urtica ruderalis is an herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
