Urochloa eminii

Urochloa eminii, commonly known as the Emine grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a panicle inflorescence that is often used in ornamental gardening.

Urochloa eminii is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
