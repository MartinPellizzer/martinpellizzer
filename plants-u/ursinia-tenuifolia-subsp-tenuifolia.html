Ursinia tenuifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Ursinia tenuifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
