Uragoga vasivensis

Uragoga vasivensis, commonly known as the Crimson Veil Orchid, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with intricate veining that resembles a network of delicate threads. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its unique morphology includes a slender stem and clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
