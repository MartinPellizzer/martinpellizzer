Ulmus excelsa

Ulmus excelsa, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is a fast-growing species that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Ulmus excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
