Utricularia foliosa var. olygosperma

Utricularia foliosa var. olygosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic habitats.

It features small, floating leaves with specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, relying on captured prey for essential nutrients.

Its morphology is adapted for both aquatic and semi-aquatic conditions, making it a unique example of plant carnivory.
