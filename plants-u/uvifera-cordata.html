Uvifera cordata

Uvifera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf uvifera, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

Uvifera cordata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
