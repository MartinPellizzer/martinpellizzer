Ulmus davidiana var. levigata

Ulmus davidiana var. levigata, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a popular ornamental and street tree.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in urban and suburban landscapes.
