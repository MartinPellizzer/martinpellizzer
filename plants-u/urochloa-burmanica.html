Urochloa burmanica

Urochloa burmanica, commonly known as Burmese grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Urochloa burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
