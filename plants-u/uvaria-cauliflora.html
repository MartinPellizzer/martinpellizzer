Uvaria cauliflora

Uvaria cauliflora, commonly known as the clustered gooseberry, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Uvaria cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
