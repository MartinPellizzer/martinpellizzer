Uncinia banksii

Uncinia banksii, commonly known as the Banks' hookless plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a head. It is native to regions of Australia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its low-growing habit and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
