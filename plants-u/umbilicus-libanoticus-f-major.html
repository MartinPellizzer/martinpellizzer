Umbilicus libanoticus f. major

Umbilicus libanoticus f. major, commonly known as the Greater Cabbage Tree, is a distinctive plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, cabbage-like leaves that are typically green with a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
