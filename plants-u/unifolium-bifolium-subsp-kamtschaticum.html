Unifolium bifolium subsp. kamtschaticum

Unifolium bifolium subsp.

kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschaticum bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East and northeastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with showy blue to violet flowers. Its botanical classification places it within the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
