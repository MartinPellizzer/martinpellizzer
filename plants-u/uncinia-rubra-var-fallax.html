Uncinia rubra var. fallax

Uncinia rubra var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of the red hookweed, is a variant of the species Uncinia rubra, characterized by its distinctive reddish coloration and hooked growth pattern.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a dense inflorescence that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

It is primarily valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
