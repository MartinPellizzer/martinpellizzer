Urera obovata

Urera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed urera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to obovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
