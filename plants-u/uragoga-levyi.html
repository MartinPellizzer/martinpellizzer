Uragoga levyi

Uragoga levyi, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Uragoga levyi is known for its toxic properties, containing solanine and other alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
