Ulex tazensis

Ulex tazensis, commonly known as the Tazenda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ulex tazensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
