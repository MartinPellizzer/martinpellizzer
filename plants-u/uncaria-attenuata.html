Uncaria attenuata

Uncaria attenuata, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and twining growth habit, which allows it to climb and entangle other plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Uncaria attenuata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
