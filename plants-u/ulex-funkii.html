Ulex funkii

Ulex funkii, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathland and moorland environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.

Ulex funkii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
