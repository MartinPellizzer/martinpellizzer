Uncinia involuta

Uncinia involuta, commonly known as the hooked hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features slender, branching stems with hairy surfaces and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, with a rolled or curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Uncinia involuta is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
