Uvaria ovalifolia var. borneensis

Uvaria ovalifolia var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean oval-leaf uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
