Urochloa semialata

Urochloa semialata, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Urochloa semialata is often used for forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
