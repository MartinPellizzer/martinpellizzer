Unona pycnantha

Unona pycnantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a deciduous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

The golden wattle is an important part of the Australian flora and is the national floral emblem of Australia.
