Urginea maritima subsp. maura

Urginea maritima subsp.

maura, commonly known as the sea onion or false tulip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bulbous structure and produces striking violet or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
