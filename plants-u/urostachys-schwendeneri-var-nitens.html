Urostachys schwendeneri var. nitens

Urostachys schwendeneri var. nitens, commonly known as the silver pine or silver grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern China.

It features narrow, silvery-blue leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
