Urtica chamaedryoides

Urtica chamaedryoides, commonly known as false nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Urtica chamaedryoides is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides.
