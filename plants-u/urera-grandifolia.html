Urera grandifolia

Urera grandifolia, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in swampy areas. The plant features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Urera grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
