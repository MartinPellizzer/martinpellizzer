Urochloa acuminata

Urochloa acuminata, commonly known as hairy signal grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Urochloa acuminata is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, which contributes to its widespread distribution.
