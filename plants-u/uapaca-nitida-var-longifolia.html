Uapaca nitida var. longifolia

Uapaca nitida var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved velvetball, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and riverine habitats.
