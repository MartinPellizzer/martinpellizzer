Ulex parviflorus f. canescens

Ulex parviflorus f. canescens, commonly known as the grey thorn or creeping thorn, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in harsh environments.
