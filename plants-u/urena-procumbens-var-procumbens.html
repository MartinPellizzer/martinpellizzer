Urena procumbens var. procumbens

Urena procumbens var. procumbens, commonly known as false nettle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface and serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by fluffy, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many environments.
