Ulex luridus

Ulex luridus, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Ulex luridus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
