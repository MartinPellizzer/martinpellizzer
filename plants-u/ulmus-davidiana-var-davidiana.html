Ulmus davidiana var. davidiana

Ulmus davidiana var. davidiana, commonly known as the Chinese elm, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
