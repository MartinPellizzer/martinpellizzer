Uragoga lanceifolia

Uragoga lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Uragoga lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
