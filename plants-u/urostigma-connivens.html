Urostigma connivens

Urostigma connivens, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans alike.
