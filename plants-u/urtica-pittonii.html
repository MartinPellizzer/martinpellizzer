Urtica pittonii

Urtica pittonii, commonly known as the Patagonian stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Urtica pittonii is often found in grasslands and shrublands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
