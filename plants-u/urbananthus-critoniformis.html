Urbananthus critoniformis

Urbananthus critoniformis, commonly known as the cityscape flameflower, is a unique urban-adapted plant that thrives in disturbed environments such as sidewalks and construction sites.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its roots are fibrous and capable of stabilizing soil in urban areas prone to erosion.

Despite its resilience, it is considered a rare species with limited natural distribution.
