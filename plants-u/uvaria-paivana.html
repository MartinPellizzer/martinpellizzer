Uvaria paivana

Uvaria paivana, commonly known as the white uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
