Urostachys pflanzii

Urostachys pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish spikelets that are arranged in loose panicles, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Urostachys pflanzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
