Ulex europaeus subsp. europaeus

Ulex europaeus subsp.

europaeus, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a perennial shrub native to western Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces globose, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in coastal and sandy habitats, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in maritime ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
