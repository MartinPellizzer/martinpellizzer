Urtica canadensis var. minor

Urtica canadensis var. minor, commonly known as stinging nettle minor, is a variant of the stinging nettle plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs on both the stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, pale brown fruits.

The plant is known for its irritant properties, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
