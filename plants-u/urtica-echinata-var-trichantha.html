Urtica echinata var. trichantha

Urtica echinata var. trichantha, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. Urtica echinata var. trichantha thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
