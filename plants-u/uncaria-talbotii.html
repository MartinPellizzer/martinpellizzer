Uncaria talbotii

Uncaria talbotii, commonly known as the talbot's climbing shrub, is a vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
