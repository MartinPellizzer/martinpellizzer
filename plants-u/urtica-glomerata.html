Urtica glomerata

Urtica glomerata, commonly known as stinging nettle in some regions, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of its species. Urtica glomerata is native to various parts of Europe, Asia, and North America, often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in many people.
