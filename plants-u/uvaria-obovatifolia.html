Uvaria obovatifolia

Uvaria obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, obovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Uvaria obovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
