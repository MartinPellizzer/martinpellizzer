Unifolium amplexicaule

Unifolium amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It features a thallus that is typically greenish to yellowish in color, with a dense, leaf-like appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner, which contributes to its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its unique morphology includes a basal layer that clings tightly to the substrate, giving it the common name "clasping leaf.".

This lichen is often found on bark, stones, and soil in temperate regions, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling and habitat support.
