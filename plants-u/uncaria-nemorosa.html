Uncaria nemorosa

Uncaria nemorosa, commonly known as wild indigo or American wild indigo, is a climbing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces black, bean-like seeds enclosed in a hairy pod, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other climbing plants and shrubs.
