Ugni montana

Ugni montana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are tart and often used in culinary applications. Ugni montana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
