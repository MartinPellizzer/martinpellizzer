Uncinia bractata

Uncinia bractata, commonly known as the hooked bract grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. Its unique feature is the presence of hooked bracts, which give it its common name and aid in seed dispersal.

Uncinia bractata is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
