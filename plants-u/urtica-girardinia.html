Urtica girardinia

Urtica girardinia, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Urtica girardinia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
