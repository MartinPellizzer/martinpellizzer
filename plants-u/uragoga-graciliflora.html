Uragoga graciliflora

Uragoga graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem with smooth, pale bark.

Uragoga graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
