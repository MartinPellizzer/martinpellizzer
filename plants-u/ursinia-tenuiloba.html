Ursinia tenuiloba

Ursinia tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ursinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features slender, hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
