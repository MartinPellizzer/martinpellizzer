Urophyllum rostratum

Urophyllum rostratum, commonly known as the beak-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are elongated and have a narrow, beak-like tip. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange.

Urophyllum rostratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
