Urtica caravellana

Urtica caravellana, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, green stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. Urtica caravellana is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
