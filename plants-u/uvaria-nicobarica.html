Uvaria nicobarica

Uvaria nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar star fruit, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by yellowish or orange-colored berries. Its berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
