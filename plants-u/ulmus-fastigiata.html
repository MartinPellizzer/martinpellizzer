Ulmus fastigiata

Ulmus fastigiata, commonly known as the upright elm, is a species of flowering plant in the elm family, Ulmaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense foliage, making it a striking ornamental tree. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that are dispersed by wind.

This tree is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
