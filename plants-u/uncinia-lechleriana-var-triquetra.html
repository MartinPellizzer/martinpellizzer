Uncinia lechleriana var. triquetra

Uncinia lechleriana var. triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered hookweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, triquetrous (three-cornered) fruits that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
