Ulmus virens

Ulmus virens, commonly known as the green elm, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

Ulmus virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
