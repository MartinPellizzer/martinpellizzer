Urochloa maxima var. trichoglumis

Urochloa maxima var. trichoglumis, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features erect, slender stems with hairy foliage, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers in panicle clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock forage.
