Urophyllum macrophyllum var. corymbosa

Urophyllum macrophyllum var. corymbosa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its showy white flowers, which bloom at night, attract pollinators such as beetles and moths.

The plant's buoyant leaves and fibrous roots make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
