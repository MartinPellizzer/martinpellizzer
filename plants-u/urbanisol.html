Urbanisol

Urbanisol, commonly known as the urban soil variant, is a type of soil that develops in urban environments due to prolonged human activity.

It is characterized by a high concentration of anthropogenic materials such as concrete, asphalt, and construction debris. The botanical aspects of urbanisol include its poor nutrient content and low organic matter, which make it challenging for most plant species to thrive. However, some hardy plants have adapted to these harsh conditions by developing deep root systems and efficient water-use strategies.

Urbanisol often supports a unique ecosystem of pioneer species that can colonize disturbed urban areas.
