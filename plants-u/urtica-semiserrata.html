Urtica semiserrata

Urtica semiserrata, commonly known as the semi-serrated nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Urtica semiserrata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
