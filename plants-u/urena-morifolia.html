Urena morifolia

Urena morifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Urena morifolia is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, due to its adaptability.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in certain regions.
