Urophyllum pulchristipulum

Urophyllum pulchristipulum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Urophyllum pulchristipulum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
