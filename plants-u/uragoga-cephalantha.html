Uragoga cephalantha

Uragoga cephalantha, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features a basal rosette of strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
