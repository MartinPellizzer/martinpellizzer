Uvaria amherstiana

Uvaria amherstiana, commonly known as the Amherst's starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by yellow, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

Uvaria amherstiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
