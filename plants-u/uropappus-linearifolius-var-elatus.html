Uropappus linearifolius var. elatus

Uropappus linearifolius var. elatus, commonly known as the tall sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
