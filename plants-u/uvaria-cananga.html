Uvaria cananga

Uvaria cananga, commonly known as the Java candle or sweet sandlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
