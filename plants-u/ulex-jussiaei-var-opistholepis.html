Ulex jussiaei var. opistholepis

Ulex jussiaei var. opistholepis, commonly known as the southern bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottles, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
