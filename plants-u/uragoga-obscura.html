Uragoga obscura

Uragoga obscura, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the dense, shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that exhibit a unique pattern of light and dark green pigmentation. The plant thrives in high humidity and low light conditions, often growing along the bases of ancient trees or in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores released in a fine mist during the wet season.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, Uragoga obscura is considered a critically endangered species.
