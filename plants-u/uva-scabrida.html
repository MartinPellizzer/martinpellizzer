Uva scabrida

Uva scabrida, commonly known as the rough grape or scabrous grape, is a woody vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically green when mature. Uva scabrida is often found in moist, shaded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
