Urtica magellanica

Urtica magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan nettle, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and can reach up to 15 centimeters in length.

Urtica magellanica is often found in temperate regions and is part of the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species.
