Urochloa vittata

Urochloa vittata, commonly known as the spotted grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Urochloa vittata is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
