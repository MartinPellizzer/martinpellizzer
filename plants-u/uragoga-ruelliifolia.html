Uragoga ruelliifolia

Uragoga ruelliifolia, commonly known as the Ruelliifolia or the African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in arid conditions.

Uragoga ruelliifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
