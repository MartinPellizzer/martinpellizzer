Uvularia smithii

Uvularia smithii, commonly known as the bellflower vine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Uvularia smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
