Ulex × breoganii

Ulex × breoganii, commonly known as the hybrid common furze, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid between Ulex europaeus (common gorse) and Ulex parviflorus (small-flowered gorse), resulting in a plant with distinctive yellow, needle-like leaves and small, bright orange flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, thorny growth habit that provides habitat for various wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathland environments.
