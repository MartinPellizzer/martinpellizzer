Unona flagellaris

Unona flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized flagellated structures on its fronds.

These flagella, which resemble tiny tails, aid in the dispersal of spores through water, allowing the plant to propagate in moist environments. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, where it often grows in shaded, humid areas near water sources. Its fronds are typically delicate and feathery, with a distinctively segmented appearance.

Botanically, Unona flagellaris is classified within the family Polypodiaceae, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
