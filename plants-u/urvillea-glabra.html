Urvillea glabra

Urvillea glabra, commonly known as the smooth urvillea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on the branches. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Urvillea glabra is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
