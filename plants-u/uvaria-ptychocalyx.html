Uvaria ptychocalyx

Uvaria ptychocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uvaria, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically white or pale yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
