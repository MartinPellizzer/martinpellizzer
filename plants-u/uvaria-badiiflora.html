Uvaria badiiflora

Uvaria badiiflora, commonly known as the black-flowered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Uvaria badiiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
