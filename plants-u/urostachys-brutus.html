Urostachys brutus

Urostachys brutus, commonly known as the mountain rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the summer months. Urostachys brutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
