Urera subpeltata

Urera subpeltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
