Urtica obnoxia

Urtica obnoxia, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a painful rash. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Urtica obnoxia is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein, minerals, and vitamins.
