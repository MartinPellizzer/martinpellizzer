Unxia pratensis

Unxia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Unxia pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting biodiversity.
