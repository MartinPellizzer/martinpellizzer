Urera alceifolia

Urera alceifolia, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on long, slender stalks. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
