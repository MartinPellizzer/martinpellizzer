Ulticona aspera

Ulticona aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Ulticona aspera is often found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
