Ulmaria aruncus

Ulmaria aruncus, commonly known as goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, feathery white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has a tall, slender stem with hairy foliage, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Ulmaria aruncus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
