Ursinia pedunculosa

Ursinia pedunculosa, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its bright orange to red flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ursinia pedunculosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
