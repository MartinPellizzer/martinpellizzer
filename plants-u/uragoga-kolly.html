Uragoga kolly

Uragoga kolly, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Uragoga kolly is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
