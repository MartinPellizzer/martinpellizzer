Urtica pentandra

Urtica pentandra, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems covered in fine, hollow hairs that release an irritating substance when touched. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of streams.

Urtica pentandra is known for its nutritional value and has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a food source.
