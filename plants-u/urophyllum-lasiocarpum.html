Urophyllum lasiocarpum

Urophyllum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a dark center, and its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped. The plant produces elongated, woody fruits called achenes, which are covered in a rough, fibrous coating. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Urophyllum lasiocarpum is native to North America and is often found in the western United States.
