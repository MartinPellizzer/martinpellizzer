Utricularia platyptera

Utricularia platyptera, commonly known as the broad-leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually floating or submerged, with specialized structures called bladders that function as traps.

Utricularia platyptera plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling populations of microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
