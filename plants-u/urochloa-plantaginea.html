Urochloa plantaginea

Urochloa plantaginea, commonly known as parlor grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its fine foliage and ability to tolerate moderate foot traffic.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
