Uva microcarpa

Uva microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend support structures. Its small, dark purple berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
