Uvaria chamae

Uvaria chamae, commonly known as the creeping uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, yellowish drupe.

Uvaria chamae is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.
