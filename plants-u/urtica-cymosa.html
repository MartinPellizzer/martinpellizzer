Urtica cymosa

Urtica cymosa, commonly known as the clustered nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, giving it its common name.

Urtica cymosa typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
