Ulmus pumila f. androssowii

Ulmus pumila f. androssowii, commonly known as the Korean elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas along riverbanks. It has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental horticulture.
