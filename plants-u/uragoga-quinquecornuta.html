Uragoga quinquecornuta

Uragoga quinquecornuta, commonly known as the five-angled uragoga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled seed pods, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Uragoga quinquecornuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
