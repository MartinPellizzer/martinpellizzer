Urobotyra stapfiana

Urobotyra stapfiana, commonly known as the Stapf's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Urobotyra stapfiana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
