Urostigma tsjakela

Urostigma tsjakela, commonly known as the red-flowered urostigma, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and yellow central disk, which make it visually distinctive. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Urostigma tsjakela is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
