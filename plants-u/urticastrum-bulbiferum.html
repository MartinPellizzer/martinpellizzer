Urticastrum bulbiferum

Urticastrum bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbous goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils or open areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well.

Urticastrum bulbiferum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
