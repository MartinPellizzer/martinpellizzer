Urostigma pseudorubrum

Urostigma pseudorubrum, commonly known as the redstem ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. Its showy pink to red flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Urostigma pseudorubrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
