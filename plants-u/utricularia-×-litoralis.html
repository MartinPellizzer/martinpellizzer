Utricularia × litoralis

Utricularia × litoralis, commonly known as the hybrid water lentil, is a carnivorous plant species that combines traits from its parent species, Utricularia foliosa and Utricularia radiata.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features small, bladder-like traps that capture tiny aquatic organisms for nutrients. Its leaves are typically floating or submerged, with specialized structures for trapping prey.

Utricularia × litoralis is valued in aquatic horticulture for its ornamental appearance and unique predatory behavior.
