Uncinia phleoides

Uncinia phleoides, commonly known as the hooked sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Uncinia phleoides is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
