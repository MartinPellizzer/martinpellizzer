Univiscidiatus amplexicaulis

Univiscidiatus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping viscid vine, is a unique plant characterized by its viscid (sticky) surfaces and clasping stems.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky coating, which helps in trapping moisture and deterring herbivores. Its flowers are small and typically violet, blooming in clusters, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
