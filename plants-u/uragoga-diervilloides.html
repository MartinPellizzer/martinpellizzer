Uragoga diervilloides

Uragoga diervilloides, commonly known as the purple tangleweed, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Uragoga diervilloides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
