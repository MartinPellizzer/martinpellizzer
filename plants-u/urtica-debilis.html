Urtica debilis

Urtica debilis, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in many people. Urtica debilis is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils.
