Uvaria scabrida var. parviflora

Uvaria scabrida var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
