Uncaria lanosa f. toppingii

Uncaria lanosa f. toppingii, commonly known as the hairy cat's claw.

This plant is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It features woody stems with dense, white, lanate (hairy) covering that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife.
