Ulmus scabra subf. viminalis

Ulmus scabra subf.

viminalis, commonly known as the roughleaf weeping elm, is a variant of the common elm characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves and weeping growth habit. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, shady environments.

The plant has a rugged, gnarled appearance with a spreading crown and pendulous branches. Its leaves are small, rough-textured, and turn a yellowish-green in autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
