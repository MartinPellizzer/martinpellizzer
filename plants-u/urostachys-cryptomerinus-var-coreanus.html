Urostachys cryptomerinus var. coreanus

Urostachys cryptomerinus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Korean mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.
