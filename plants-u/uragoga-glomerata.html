Uragoga glomerata

Uragoga glomerata, commonly known as the clustered uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Uragoga glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
