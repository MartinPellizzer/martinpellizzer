Utricularia welwitschii var. microcalyx

Utricularia welwitschii var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a distinctive three-lobed structure.

The variety microcalyx is distinguished by its reduced calyx, giving it a unique appearance among bladderworts.
