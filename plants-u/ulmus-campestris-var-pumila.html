Ulmus campestris var. pumila

Ulmus campestris var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf field elm, is a small, compact tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a variety of the field elm, characterized by its slow growth and dense, rounded form. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
