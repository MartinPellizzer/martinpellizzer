Urtica dioica subsp. xingjiangensis

Urtica dioica subsp.

xingjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of China. It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain irritants like histamine and acetylcholine.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
