Urtica atrovirens var. floribunda

Urtica atrovirens var. floribunda, commonly known as the purple stinging nettle, is a variant of the stinging nettle species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. Its leaves and stems contain stinging hairs that release irritant chemicals when touched.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
