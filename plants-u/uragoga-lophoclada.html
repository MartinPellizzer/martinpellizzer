Uragoga lophoclada

Uragoga lophoclada, commonly known as the spiral leaf fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its unique, spirally arranged fronds.

Native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity. The plant features long, slender fronds that unfurl in a distinctive helical pattern, giving it an elegant appearance. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Uragoga lophoclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
