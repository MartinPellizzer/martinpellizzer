Uapaca goossensii

Uapaca goossensii, commonly known as the wild date palm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy drupes.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or processed into beverages, and the tree is valued for its ecological and economic significance in its native habitat.
