Urophyllum calycinum

Urophyllum calycinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Urophyllum calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
