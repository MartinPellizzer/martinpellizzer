Urostachys serratus var. sachalinensis

Urostachys serratus var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and marshy areas. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which help it thrive in wet conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
