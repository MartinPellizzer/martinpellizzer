Uraria barbaticaulis

Uraria barbaticaulis, commonly known as the hairy uraria, is a small shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, arid environments and is adapted to survive in poor soil conditions. It has a woody stem with hairy nodes, which gives it its common name.

Uraria barbaticaulis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
