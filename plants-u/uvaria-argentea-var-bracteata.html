Uvaria argentea var. bracteata

Uvaria argentea var. bracteata, commonly known as silver uvaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive silver-white foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are often followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.
