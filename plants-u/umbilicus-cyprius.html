Umbilicus cyprius

Umbilicus cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus umbilicus, is a rare and unique plant species native to Cyprus.

It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped flowers that resemble a belly button, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
