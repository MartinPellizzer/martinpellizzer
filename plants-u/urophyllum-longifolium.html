Urophyllum longifolium

Urophyllum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved urrophyllum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
