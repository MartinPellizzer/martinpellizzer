Unona latifolia

Unona latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf unona, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
