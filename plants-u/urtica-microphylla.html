Urtica microphylla

Urtica microphylla, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs.

The plant belongs to the Urticaceae family and is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its small greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are typically pollinated by wind. The plant contains a high concentration of proteins, minerals, and bioactive compounds, making it both a traditional food source and a medicinal plant.

Urtica microphylla thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
