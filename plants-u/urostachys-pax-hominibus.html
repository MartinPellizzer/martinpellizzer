Urostachys pax-hominibus

Urostachys pax-hominibus, commonly known as the peace grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and shady woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance plants.
