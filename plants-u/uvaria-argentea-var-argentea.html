Uvaria argentea var. argentea

Uvaria argentea var. argentea, commonly known as silver uvaria, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
