Uragoga simulans

Uragoga simulans, commonly known as the false arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and bloom during the rainy season.

Uragoga simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
