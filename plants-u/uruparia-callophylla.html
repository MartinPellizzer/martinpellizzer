Uruparia callophylla

Uruparia callophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the wet season. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Uruparia callophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
