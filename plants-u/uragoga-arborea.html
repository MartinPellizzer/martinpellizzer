Uragoga arborea

Uragoga arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. Uragoga arborea thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
