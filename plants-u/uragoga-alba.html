Uragoga alba

Uragoga alba, commonly known as the white uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Uragoga alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
