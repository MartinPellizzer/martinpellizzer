Uvaria dolichoclada

Uvaria dolichoclada, commonly known as the long-stalked uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
