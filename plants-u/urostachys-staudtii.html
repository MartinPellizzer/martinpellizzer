Urostachys staudtii

Urostachys staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

Urostachys staudtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hardy ground cover.
