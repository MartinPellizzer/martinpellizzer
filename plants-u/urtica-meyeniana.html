Urtica meyeniana

Urtica meyeniana, commonly known as the Mexican stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine, irritating hairs that cause a skin reaction upon contact. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica meyeniana is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
