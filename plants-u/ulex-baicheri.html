Ulex baicheri

Ulex baicheri, commonly known as the Kashmiri burr or red burr, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that produces distinctive spiny fruits, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of prickly, three-lobed burrs. Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.

Ulex baicheri is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and as an astringent.
