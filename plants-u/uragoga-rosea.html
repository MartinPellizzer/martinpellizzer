Uragoga rosea

Uragoga rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink petals and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Uragoga rosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
