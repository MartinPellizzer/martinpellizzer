Urostachys aqualupianus

Urostachys aqualupianus, commonly known as the aquatic stachys, is a unique plant characterized by its water-adapted morphology.

It typically features slender, elongated stems and narrow, ribbon-like leaves that reduce surface area to minimize water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with pale purple or white petals, which are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes or slow-moving streams.

Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from water, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
