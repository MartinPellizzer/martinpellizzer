Utricularia × spectabilis

Utricularia × spectabilis, commonly known as the hybrid bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Utricularia aurea and Utricularia gibba, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its small, floating bladders that trap aquatic prey, enabling it to supplement its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor water environments. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches.

Its morphology includes slender stems with whorled leaves and specialized bladders that are essential for its unique method of obtaining nutrients.
