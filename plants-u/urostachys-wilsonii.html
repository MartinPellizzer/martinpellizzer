Urostachys wilsonii

Urostachys wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Urostachys wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
