Urostigma cestrifolium

Urostigma cestrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used as a food source in various parts of the world.

Urostigma cestrifolium is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.
