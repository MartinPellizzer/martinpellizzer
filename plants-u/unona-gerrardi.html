Unona gerrardi

Unona gerrardi, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, toxic berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its poisonous nature, it is important to handle the plant with care.
