Uragoga collina

Uragoga collina, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Uragoga collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
