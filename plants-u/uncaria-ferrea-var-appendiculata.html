Uncaria ferrea var. appendiculata

Uncaria ferrea var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate climbing vine, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
