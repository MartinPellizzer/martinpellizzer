Urtica tenacissima

Urtica tenacissima, commonly known as giant nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Urtica tenacissima is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
