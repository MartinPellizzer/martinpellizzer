Ugena polymorpha

Ugena polymorpha, commonly known as the variable ugena, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a broad, ovate shape.

Ugena polymorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual display.
