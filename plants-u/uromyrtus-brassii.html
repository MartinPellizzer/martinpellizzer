Uromyrtus brassii

Uromyrtus brassii, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and small, needle-like leaves. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
