Urochloa whiteana

Urochloa whiteana, commonly known as whiteleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are often pale green or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Urochloa whiteana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
