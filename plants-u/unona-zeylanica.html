Unona zeylanica

Unona zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested and used as a spice. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries. Its bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its distinct flavor and medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is closely related to other cinnamon species.
