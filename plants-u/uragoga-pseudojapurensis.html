Uragoga pseudojapurensis

Uragoga pseudojapurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Uragoga pseudojapurensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
