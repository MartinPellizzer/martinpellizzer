Urtica gigas

Urtica gigas, commonly known as giant nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hollow, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine stinging hairs. Its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Urtica gigas is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
