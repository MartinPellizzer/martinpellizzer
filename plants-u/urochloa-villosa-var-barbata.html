Urochloa villosa var. barbata

Urochloa villosa var. barbata, commonly known as hairy leaf blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and coarse, upright growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Urochloa villosa var. barbata is known for its adaptability and resilience to various environmental conditions.
