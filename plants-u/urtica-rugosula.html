Urtica rugosula

Urtica rugosula, commonly known as rough stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Urtica rugosula is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes.
