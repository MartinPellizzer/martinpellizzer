Uragoga hygrophilodes

Uragoga hygrophilodes, commonly known as the swamp gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist soils and thrives in areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Uragoga hygrophilodes plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland environment.
