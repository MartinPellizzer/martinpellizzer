Urtica wallichiana

Urtica wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas.

Urtica wallichiana is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
