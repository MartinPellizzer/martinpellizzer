Ulex narcissi

Ulex narcissi, commonly known as the Scotch bonnet or hedgehog plant, is a perennial shrub native to the western Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces oval, leathery berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats.

Ulex narcissi is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in landscaping.
