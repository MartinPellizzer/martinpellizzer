Uruparia roxburghiana

Uruparia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers that are arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its stinging hairs, which contain irritants, are a notable botanical feature.
