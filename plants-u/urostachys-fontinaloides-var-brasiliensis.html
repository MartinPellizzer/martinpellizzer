Urostachys fontinaloides var. brasiliensis

Urostachys fontinaloides var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water starwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as streams and ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying aquatic ecosystems and plant adaptation to water environments.
