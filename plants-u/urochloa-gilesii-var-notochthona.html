Urochloa gilesii var. notochthona

Urochloa gilesii var. notochthona, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dryland agriculture.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and forage value for livestock.
