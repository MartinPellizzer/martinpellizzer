Ulex welwitschianus var. recurvatus

Ulex welwitschianus var. recurvatus, commonly known as the recurved burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's most distinctive feature is its recurved, barbed pods that release seeds when disturbed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
