Uragoga ovalis

Uragoga ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf uragoga, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct veining pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with smooth bark and a root system adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Uragoga ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
