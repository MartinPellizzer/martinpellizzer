Uvaria solanifolia

Uvaria solanifolia, commonly known as the purple-stemmed uvaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by smooth, oval berries that turn red when mature. Uvaria solanifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
