Uragoga trichocephala

Uragoga trichocephala, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Uragoga trichocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
