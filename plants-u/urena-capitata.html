Urena capitata

Urena capitata, commonly known as the clusterleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Urena capitata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.
