Urochloa adspersa

Urochloa adspersa, commonly known as hairy signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in pastures and agricultural fields. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Urochloa adspersa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm climates.
