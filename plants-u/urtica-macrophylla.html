Urtica macrophylla

Urtica macrophylla, commonly known as the giant nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Urtica macrophylla is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
