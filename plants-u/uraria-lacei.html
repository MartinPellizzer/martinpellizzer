Uraria lacei

Uraria lacei, commonly known as the velvetleaf uraria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Uraria lacei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
