Urtica cordifolia

Urtica cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine, hollow hairs that release an irritating sap when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Urtica cordifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
