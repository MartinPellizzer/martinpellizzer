Uncaria canescens

Uncaria canescens, commonly known as the white sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems that twine around supporting structures. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by red, berry-like fruits containing multiple seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including the treatment of various ailments.
