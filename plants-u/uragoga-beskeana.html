Uragoga beskeana

Uragoga beskeana, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Uragoga beskeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
