Unona asiatica

Unona asiatica, commonly known as the Asian senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
