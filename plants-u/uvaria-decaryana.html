Uvaria decaryana

Uvaria decaryana, commonly known as the red-flowered uvaria, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.

Uvaria decaryana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
