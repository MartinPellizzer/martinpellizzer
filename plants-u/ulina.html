Ulina

Ulina, commonly known as the "sweet potato vine," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to edible, tuberous roots similar to sweet potatoes. Ulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, making it both a decorative and edible plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
