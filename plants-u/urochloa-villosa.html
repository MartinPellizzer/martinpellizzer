Urochloa villosa

Urochloa villosa, commonly known as hairy signal grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas, including pastures and disturbed soils, and is well adapted to warm climates. It is often used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

Urochloa villosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
