Ubochea dichotoma

Ubochea dichotoma, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Ubochea dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
