Uragoga rubiginosa

Uragoga rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
