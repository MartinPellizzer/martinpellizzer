Urtica hamiltoniana

Urtica hamiltoniana, commonly known as Hamilton's nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica hamiltoniana is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
