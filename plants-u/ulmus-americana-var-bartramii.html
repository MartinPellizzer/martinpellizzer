Ulmus americana var. bartramii

Ulmus americana var. bartramii, commonly known as the Bartram's elm, is a variant of the American elm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The leaves are elongated, with a serrated margin and a dull green upper surface. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
