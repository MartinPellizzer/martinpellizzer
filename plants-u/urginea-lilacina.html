Urginea lilacina

Urginea lilacina, commonly known as the purple arum, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple spathe that encloses a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators such as flies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Urginea lilacina is also known for its large, fleshy leaves and its ability to store water, making it somewhat drought-tolerant once established.
