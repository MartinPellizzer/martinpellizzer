Unifolium trifolium

Unifolium trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf clover, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in lawns and meadows.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
