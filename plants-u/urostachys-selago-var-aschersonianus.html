Urostachys selago var. aschersonianus

Urostachys selago var. aschersonianus, commonly known as the Ascherson's sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of xerophytic species. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh, dry conditions.
