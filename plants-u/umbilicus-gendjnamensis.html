Umbilicus gendjnamensis

Umbilicus gendjnamensis, commonly known as the Gendjnamensis Umbilicus, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbilical-shaped root system, which is both its namesake and a key feature for identification. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to minimize water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
