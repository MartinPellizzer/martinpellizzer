Urginea cepifolia

Urginea cepifolia, commonly known as the giant bulbous plant or the African giant lily, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its massive, bulbous stem that can grow up to several meters in height, supporting a large, showy inflorescence. The plant is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and sword-shaped, and it produces striking white or pinkish flowers.

The bulb serves as a storage organ, allowing the plant to survive periods of drought.
