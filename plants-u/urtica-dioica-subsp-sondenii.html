Urtica dioica subsp. sondenii

Urtica dioica subsp.

sondenii, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its hollow, stout stems covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants upon contact.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks. Urtica dioica subsp.

sondenii is known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
