Urtica crenulata

Urtica crenulata, commonly known as the serrated nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Urtica crenulata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

It has a fibrous root system and can grow up to 1 meter in height, making it a notable species in its native habitats.
