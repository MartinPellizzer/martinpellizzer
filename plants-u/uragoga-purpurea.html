Uragoga purpurea

Uragoga purpurea, commonly known as the purple uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Uragoga purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
