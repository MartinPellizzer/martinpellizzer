Ulex genistoides var. spartioides

Ulex genistoides var. spartioides, commonly known as the creeping thorned broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in Mediterranean climates.
