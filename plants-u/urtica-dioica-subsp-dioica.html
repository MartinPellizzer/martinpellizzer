Urtica dioica subsp. dioica

Urtica dioica subsp.

dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its stinging hairs that contain formic acid. It typically grows in moist, nitrogen-rich soils and is found in various habitats such as woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals, and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's unique defensive mechanism and versatile uses make it an important species in both ecological and human contexts.
