Urtica virgata

Urtica virgata, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to various regions of the world.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine stinging hairs, which release irritants upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dark-colored berries.

Urtica virgata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
