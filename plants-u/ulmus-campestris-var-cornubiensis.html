Ulmus campestris var. cornubiensis

Ulmus campestris var. cornubiensis, commonly known as the Cornish elm, is a rare and ancient variety of elm native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and a gnarled, twisted trunk that gives it a unique appearance. The tree typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring acidic soils. It is a member of the Ulmaceae family and is known for its slow growth and longevity.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
