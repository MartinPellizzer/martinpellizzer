Urostachys vernicosus

Urostachys vernicosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Urostachys vernicosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
