Uragoga subscandens

Uragoga subscandens, commonly known as the climbing uragoga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb using adventitious roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Uragoga subscandens is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
