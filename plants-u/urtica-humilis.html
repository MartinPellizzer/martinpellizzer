Urtica humilis

Urtica humilis, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in most people. Urtica humilis is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils in temperate regions.

It is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
