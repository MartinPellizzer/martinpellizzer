Uragoga Johnsonii

Uragoga johnsonii is a rare and enigmatic plant species discovered in the remote jungles of Southeast Asia. Known for its striking bioluminescent properties, it emits a soft blue glow at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. This unique characteristic has made it a subject of fascination for botanists and ecologists alike. Despite its beauty, the plant remains poorly understood due to its elusive nature and limited habitat. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this fragile species from threats posed by deforestation and climate change.
