Urochloa leersioides

Urochloa leersioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bladygrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and degraded soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Urochloa leersioides is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
