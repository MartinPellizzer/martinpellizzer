Uncinia aspericaulis

Uncinia aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-leaved hookweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wiry, branching stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Uncinia aspericaulis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in disturbed habitats.
