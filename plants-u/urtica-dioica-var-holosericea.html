Urtica dioica var. holosericea

Urtica dioica var. holosericea, commonly known as the stinging nettle with silky hairs, is a variant of the common stinging nettle.

It is characterized by its dense, fine, and silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
