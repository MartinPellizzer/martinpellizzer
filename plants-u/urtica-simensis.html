Urtica simensis

Urtica simensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian nettle, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Urtica simensis is notable for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
