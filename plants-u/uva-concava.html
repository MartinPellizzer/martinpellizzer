Uva concava

Uva concava, commonly known as the concave grape or wild grape, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, concave-shaped berries that are typically green when immature and turn red or black when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. Uva concava thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
