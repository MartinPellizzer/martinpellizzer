Uncinia moseleyana

Uncinia moseleyana, commonly known as the red sandreed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to reddish-purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Uncinia moseleyana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
