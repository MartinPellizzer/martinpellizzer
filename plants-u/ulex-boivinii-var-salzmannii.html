Ulex boivinii var. salzmannii

Ulex boivinii var. salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
