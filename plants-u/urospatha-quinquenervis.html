Urospatha quinquenervis

Urospatha quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an inflorescence called a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.

Urospatha quinquenervis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
