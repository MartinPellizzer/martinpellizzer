Utricularia vulgaris var. formosana

Utricularia vulgaris var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor aquatic environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic life and its role in local ecosystems.
