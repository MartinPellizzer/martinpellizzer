Ulmus campestris var. corylifolia

Ulmus campestris var. corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese field elm, is a variant of the field elm native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm in size. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
