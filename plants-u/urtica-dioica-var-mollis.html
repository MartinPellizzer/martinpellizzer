Urtica dioica var. mollis

Urtica dioica var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along streambanks.

This plant is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein, minerals, and vitamins, making it a valuable forage plant for both humans and animals.
