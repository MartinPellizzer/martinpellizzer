Unona glauca

Unona glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
