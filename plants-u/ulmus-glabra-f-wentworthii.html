Ulmus glabra f. wentworthii

Ulmus glabra f. wentworthii, commonly known as the Wentworthii elm, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Wych elm.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the United Kingdom and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is considered a valuable specimen in botanical collections.
