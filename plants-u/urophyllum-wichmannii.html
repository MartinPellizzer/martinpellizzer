Urophyllum wichmannii

Urophyllum wichmannii, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is notable for its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a pincushion, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountain climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
