Uncinia variegata

Uncinia variegata, commonly known as the variegated hookweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive variegation pattern, often featuring white or pale green markings.

Uncinia variegata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
