Uebelinia crassifolia

Uebelinia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its stems are often woody and branched, providing structural support.

Uebelinia crassifolia is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
