Urtica gracilis subsp. incaica

Urtica gracilis subsp.

incaica, commonly known as the Inca nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted venation pattern.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
