Urospatha affinis

Urospatha affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Urospatha affinis is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
