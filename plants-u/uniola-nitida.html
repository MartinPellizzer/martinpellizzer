Uniola nitida

Uniola nitida, commonly known as slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
