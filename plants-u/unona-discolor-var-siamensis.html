Unona discolor var. siamensis

Unona discolor var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam golden vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its long, trailing stems. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.
