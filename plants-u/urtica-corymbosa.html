Urtica corymbosa

Urtica corymbosa, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hollow hairs that release an irritating chemical when touched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typical of its species. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Urtica corymbosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
