Urena aculeata

Urena aculeata, commonly known as the thorned urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a rough texture.

Urena aculeata is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
