Utania spicata

Utania spicata, commonly known as the spikeflower or spike grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Utania spicata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
