Umbilicus semiensis

Umbilicus semiensis, commonly known as the half-navel plant, is a rare and unusual species with a distinctive botanical structure.

It features a central umbilical-like structure at its base, which gives it its name. The plant has a unique arrangement of leaves that spiral around its stem in a semi-circular pattern. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is native to a specific region in Southeast Asia and is noted for its slow growth and limited distribution.
