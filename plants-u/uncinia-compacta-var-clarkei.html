Uncinia compacta var. clarkei

Uncinia compacta var. clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's compact uncinia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
