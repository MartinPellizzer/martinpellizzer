Uragoga infundibularis

Uragoga infundibularis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near streams. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a waxy surface.

Uragoga infundibularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
