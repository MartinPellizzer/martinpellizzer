Uvifera obtusifolia

Uvifera obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed uvilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Uvifera obtusifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
