Urochloa setigera var. albistyla

Urochloa setigera var. albistyla, commonly known as the white-stemmed signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often whitish stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as pastures, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its use in forage and soil stabilization in agricultural settings.
