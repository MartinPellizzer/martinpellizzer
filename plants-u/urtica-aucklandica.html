Urtica aucklandica

Urtica aucklandica, commonly known as the Auckland nettle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in damp, shaded environments. It has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Urtica aucklandica plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
