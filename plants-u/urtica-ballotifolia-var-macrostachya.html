Urtica ballotifolia var. macrostachya

Urtica ballotifolia var. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features stout, hairy stems that often grow upright, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically arranged in spikes along the stem. Its leaves are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
