Urophyllum acuminatum

Urophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Urophyllum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
