Urtica elata

Urtica elata, commonly known as tall nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its stinging hairs, which contain irritants, are a notable botanical feature.

Urtica elata is often found in disturbed soils and moist environments, making it a common weed in various habitats.
