Uva hirsuta

Uva hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow or orange berries that are covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. The plant has alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are oval to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Uva hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
