Unona camphorata

Unona camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is known for producing a valuable essential oil called camphor, which is extracted from its wood and leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in various traditional practices.
