Urodon

Urodon, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of broad, waxy leaves.

The leaves are typically green to reddish in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's structure allows it to float or remain partially submerged, facilitating light absorption for photosynthesis.

Urodon is often used in water gardens and as a natural water filtration aid due to its ability to absorb nutrients and pollutants.
