Urtica dasyphylla

Urtica dasyphylla, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, irritating hairs, which can cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Urtica dasyphylla is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein, minerals, and vitamins, making it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and chemical composition have made it a subject of interest in botanical and ecological studies.
