Umbilicus horizontalis subsp. gaditanus

Umbilicus horizontalis subsp.

gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
