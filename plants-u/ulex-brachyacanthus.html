Ulex brachyacanthus

Ulex brachyacanthus, commonly known as the common hedgehog cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that features dense clusters of spiny, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thick and capable of storing water.

Ulex brachyacanthus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
