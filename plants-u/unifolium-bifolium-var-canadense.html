Unifolium bifolium var. canadense

Unifolium bifolium var. canadense, commonly known as Canada's narrow-leafed twinleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinct two leaflets, which are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, which are visible in early spring.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
