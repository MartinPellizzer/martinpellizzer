Urena lobata subsp. sinuata

Urena lobata subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with wavy margins and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Urena lobata subsp.

sinuata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively in certain environments.
