Uvifera cylindrostachys

Uvifera cylindrostachys, commonly known as the cylinder-stemmed grapevine, is a climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to edible berries similar to grapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
