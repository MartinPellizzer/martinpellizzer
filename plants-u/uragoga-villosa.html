Uragoga villosa

Uragoga villosa, commonly known as the hairy uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Uragoga villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
