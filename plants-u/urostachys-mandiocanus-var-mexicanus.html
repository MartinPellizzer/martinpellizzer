Urostachys mandiocanus var. mexicanus

Urostachys mandiocanus var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Mexican arrowroot is valued for its edible, starchy roots, which have been used traditionally by indigenous communities.
