Uvifera pipericarpa

Uvifera pipericarpa, commonly known as the peppered grape plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive combination of grape-like fruits and pepper-like seeds.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, round berries that contain numerous tiny, peppery seeds, giving it its common name. Its foliage is typically broad and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Uvifera pipericarpa is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of unique culinary ingredients.
