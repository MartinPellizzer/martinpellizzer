Univiscidiatus sublestus

Univiscidiatus sublestus, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a rare and delicate plant characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. The plant's leaves are composed of numerous small, delicate pinnae that create a visually striking pattern. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized spore capsules, which develop on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
