Ulex latebracteatus

Ulex latebracteatus, commonly known as the bracted goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Ulex latebracteatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
