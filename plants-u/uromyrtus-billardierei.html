Uromyrtus billardierei

Uromyrtus billardierei, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, white to pinkish flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Uromyrtus billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
