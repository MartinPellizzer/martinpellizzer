Unona verrucosa

Unona verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

Unona verrucosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
