Upopion

Upopion, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it difficult to study. Its unique morphology and limited distribution have made it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Upopion is considered endangered, highlighting the need for further research and protection.
