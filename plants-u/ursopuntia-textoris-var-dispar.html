Ursopuntia textoris var. dispar

Ursopuntia textoris var. dispar, commonly known as the variable hairless cactus, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in sparse or absent spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many cacti adapted to arid environments.

This variety exhibits notable morphological variation, making it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation and taxonomy.
