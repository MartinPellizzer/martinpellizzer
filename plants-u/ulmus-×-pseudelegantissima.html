Ulmus × pseudelegantissima

Ulmus × pseudelegantissima, commonly known as the hybrid elm, is a deciduous tree that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Ulmus minor and Ulmus laevis, showcasing a blend of traits from both parent species. The tree typically has a broad, spreading canopy with heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

Ulmus × pseudelegantissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
