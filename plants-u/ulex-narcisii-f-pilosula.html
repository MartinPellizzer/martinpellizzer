Ulex narcisii f. pilosula

Ulex narcisii f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a botanical variant of the common goat's beard.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the British Isles and thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its unique appearance.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
