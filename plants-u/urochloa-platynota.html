Urochloa platynota

Urochloa platynota, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Urochloa platynota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
