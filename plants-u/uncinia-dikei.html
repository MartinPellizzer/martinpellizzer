Uncinia dikei

Uncinia dikei, commonly known as the dwarf hookweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in moist, sandy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Amaranthaceae, which includes other weedy and ornamental plants.
