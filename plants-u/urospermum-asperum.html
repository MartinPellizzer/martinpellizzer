Urospermum asperum

Urospermum asperum, commonly known as rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its rough, hairy stems and yellow-orange flower heads make it easily identifiable.

Urospermum asperum is often found in grasslands and along disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
