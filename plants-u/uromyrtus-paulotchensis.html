Uromyrtus paulotchensis

Uromyrtus paulotchensis, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, egg-shaped fruits that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Uromyrtus paulotchensis is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
