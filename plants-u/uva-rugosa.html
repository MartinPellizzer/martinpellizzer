Uva rugosa

Uva rugosa, commonly known as the rough grape or rock grape, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by its edible, tart fruits. Uva rugosa thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
