Uragoga viburnoides

Uragoga viburnoides, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
