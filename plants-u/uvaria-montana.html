Uvaria montana

Uvaria montana, commonly known as the mountain uvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Uvaria montana is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
