Uldinia ceratocarpa

Uldinia ceratocarpa, commonly known as the horned cup moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Peltasporaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped capsules that are surrounded by a thick, persistent peristome. Uldinia ceratocarpa is primarily found in temperate regions and plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
