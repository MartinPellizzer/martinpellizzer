Urginea insularis

Urginea insularis, commonly known as the Socotra aloe, is a unique plant native to the Socotra archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it conserve water in its arid environment. The plant produces a tall, branching inflorescence with small, white to pale yellow flowers. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal rainfall.

Its striking appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in the region's flora.
