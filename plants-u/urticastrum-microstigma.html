Urticastrum microstigma

Urticastrum microstigma, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Urticastrum microstigma is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
