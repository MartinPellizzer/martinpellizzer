Urandra luzoniensis

Urandra luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of montane forests, where it thrives in the cool, humid climate. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
