Ulmus × acuminatissima

Ulmus × acuminatissima, commonly known as the Chinese elms, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ulmus parvifolia and Ulmus davidiana.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often pointed at the tip, giving it the species name "acuminatissima.". The tree typically has a broad, spreading crown and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark is grey to brown and becomes fissured with age, while its twigs are usually smooth and reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
