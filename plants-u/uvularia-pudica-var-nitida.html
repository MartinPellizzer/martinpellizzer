Uvularia pudica var. nitida

Uvularia pudica var. nitida, commonly known as the shining bellvista, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a desirable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
