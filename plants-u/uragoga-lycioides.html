Uragoga lycioides

Uragoga lycioides, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Uragoga lycioides is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
