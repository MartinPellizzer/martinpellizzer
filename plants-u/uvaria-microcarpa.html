Uvaria microcarpa

Uvaria microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited satinflower, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Uvaria microcarpa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
