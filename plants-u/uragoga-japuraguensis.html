Uragoga japuraguensis

Uragoga japuraguensis, commonly known as the Japurá river fern, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, particularly in the Japurá River system. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its reproductive structures are hidden beneath the water, contributing to its limited visibility.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
