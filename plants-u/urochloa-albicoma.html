Urochloa albicoma

Urochloa albicoma, commonly known as whiteleaf bladygrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive white or silver underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Urochloa albicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability.
