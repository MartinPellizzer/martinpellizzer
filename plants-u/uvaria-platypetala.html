Uvaria platypetala

Uvaria platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Uvaria platypetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
