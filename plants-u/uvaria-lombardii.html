Uvaria lombardii

Uvaria lombardii, commonly known as the Lombardian starfruit, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
