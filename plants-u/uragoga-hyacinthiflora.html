Uragoga hyacinthiflora

Uragoga hyacinthiflora, commonly known as the hyacinth-flowered uragoga, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that resemble hyacinths.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a dense display of colors ranging from purple to blue, making it a popular ornamental species.

Uragoga hyacinthiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
