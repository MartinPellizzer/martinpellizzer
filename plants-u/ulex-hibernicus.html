Ulex hibernicus

Ulex hibernicus, commonly known as the Scottish heath or common hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heaths and grasslands. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Ulex hibernicus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
