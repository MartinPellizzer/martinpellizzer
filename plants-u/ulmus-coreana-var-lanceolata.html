Ulmus coreana var. lanceolata

Ulmus coreana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Korean lanceleaf elm, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically 5–10 cm long and 1–2 cm wide, with a smooth margin and a slightly pubescent underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
