Umbilicus mirus

Umbilicus mirus, commonly known as the wonder umbilical, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling growth pattern that resembles a coiled umbilical cord, giving it its intriguing common name. The plant features a central stem with multiple whorls of leaves that radiate outward in a symmetrical fashion. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues.

Due to its rarity and unusual morphology, Umbilicus mirus is of great interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
