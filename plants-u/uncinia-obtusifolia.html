Uncinia obtusifolia

Uncinia obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf hookweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its fibrous roots and rhizomes allow it to thrive in dry, disturbed soils.

Uncinia obtusifolia is often found in arid environments such as deserts and rocky slopes.
