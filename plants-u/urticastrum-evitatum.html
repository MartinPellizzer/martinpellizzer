Urticastrum evitatum

Urticastrum evitatum, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Urticastrum evitatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
