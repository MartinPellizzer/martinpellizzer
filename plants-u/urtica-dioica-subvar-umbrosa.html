Urtica dioica subvar. umbrosa

Urtica dioica subvar. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade nettle, is a variant of stinging nettle that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. This subspecies is often found in woodlands and along forested stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with separate male and female parts.

The plant is used in traditional herbal medicine and as a food source for some wildlife.
