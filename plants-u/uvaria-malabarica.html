Uvaria malabarica

Uvaria malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar satinwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Uvaria malabarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
