Urostachys pruinosus

Urostachys pruinosus, commonly known as the glossy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically covered in a waxy, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive glossy appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
