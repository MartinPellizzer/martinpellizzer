Uraria pseudoacuminata

Uraria pseudoacuminata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to regions of South Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan, and typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and pea-like, typical of the legume family, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental potential.
