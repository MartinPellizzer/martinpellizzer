Urtica inaequalis

Urtica inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal nettle, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, irritating hairs. Urtica inaequalis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.
