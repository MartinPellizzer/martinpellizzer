Uragoga leptophylla

Uragoga leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf uragoga, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Uragoga leptophylla is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
