Urtica magellanica subsp. lilloi

Urtica magellanica subsp.

lilloi, commonly known as the Magellan nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin and can vary in size.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by wind.
