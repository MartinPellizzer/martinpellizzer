Ugena microphylla

Ugena microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ugena, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Ugena microphylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
