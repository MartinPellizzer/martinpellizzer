Uvifera grandiflora

Uvifera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered uvifera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Uvifera grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
