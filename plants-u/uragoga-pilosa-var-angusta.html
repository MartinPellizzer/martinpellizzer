Uragoga pilosa var. angusta

Uragoga pilosa var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved uragoga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with adventitious roots, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
