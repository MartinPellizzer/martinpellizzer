Urtica javaensis

Urtica javaensis, commonly known as Java nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Urtica javaensis is known for its irritant hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
