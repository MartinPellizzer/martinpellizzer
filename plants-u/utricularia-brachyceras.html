Utricularia brachyceras

Utricularia brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like traps used to capture microscopic aquatic organisms. This species typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are narrow and often submerged, while its stems may be floating or rooted.

Utricularia brachyceras plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling populations of small invertebrates and contributing to nutrient cycling.
