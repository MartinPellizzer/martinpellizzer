Ulmus × longidens

Ulmus × longidens, commonly known as the Chinese elms, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ulmus parvifolia and Ulmus laciniata.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping due to its tolerance of urban conditions. The tree has a distinctive, spreading crown and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, winged seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to urban pollution.
