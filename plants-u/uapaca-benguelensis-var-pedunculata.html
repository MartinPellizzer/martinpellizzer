Uapaca benguelensis var. pedunculata

Uapaca benguelensis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Benguela uapaca, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
