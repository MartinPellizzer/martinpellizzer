Urtica finlaysoniana

Urtica finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Finlayson's nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging nettle species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Urtica finlaysoniana is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
