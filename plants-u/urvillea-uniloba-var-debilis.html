Urvillea uniloba var. debilis

Urvillea uniloba var. debilis, commonly known as the slender urvillea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are follicles that release small, winged seeds when mature.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and floral display.
