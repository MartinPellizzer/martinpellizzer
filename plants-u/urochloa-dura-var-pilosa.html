Urochloa dura var. pilosa

Urochloa dura var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy durra, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and dense foliage, with hairy nodes and leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Urochloa dura var. pilosa is also valued for its forage qualities and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
