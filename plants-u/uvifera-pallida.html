Uvifera pallida

Uvifera pallida, commonly known as the pale uvifera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Uvifera pallida is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
