Unona dasymaschala

Unona dasymaschala, commonly known as the silky tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
