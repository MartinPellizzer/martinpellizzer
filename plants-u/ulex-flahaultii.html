Ulex flahaultii

Ulex flahaultii, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. Ulex flahaultii is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, saline conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
