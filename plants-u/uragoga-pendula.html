Uragoga pendula

Uragoga pendula, commonly known as the hanging uragoga, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, flexible stems that trail downward, often forming cascading displays in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Uragoga pendula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
