Uvularia umbellata

Uvularia umbellata, commonly known as the bellflower vining plant, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a climbing perennial that produces small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its unique floral structure and climbing habit make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
