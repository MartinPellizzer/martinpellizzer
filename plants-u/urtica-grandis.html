Urtica grandis

Urtica grandis, commonly known as giant nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Urtica grandis is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
