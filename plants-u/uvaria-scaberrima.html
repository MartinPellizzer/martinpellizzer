Uvaria scaberrima

Uvaria scaberrima, commonly known as the rough uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Uvaria scaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
