Urtica triangularis subsp. triangularis

Urtica triangularis subsp.

triangularis, commonly known as the triangular stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its serrated, triangular leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
