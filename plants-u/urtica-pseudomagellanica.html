Urtica pseudomagellanica

Urtica pseudomagellanica, commonly known as the Magellan nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 1 meter in height. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica pseudomagellanica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
