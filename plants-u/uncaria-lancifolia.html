Uncaria lancifolia

Uncaria lancifolia, commonly known as the hook vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellowish flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, making it a significant species in traditional medicine.

Uncaria lancifolia is often used in herbal remedies for its purported effects on the nervous system.
