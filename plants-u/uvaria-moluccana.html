Uvaria moluccana

Uvaria moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a notable species in horticulture and local cuisine.
