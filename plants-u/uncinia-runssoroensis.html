Uncinia runssoroensis

Uncinia runssoroensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal hookweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Uncinia runssoroensis is often found in grassy areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
