Uva crassipetala

Uva crassipetala, commonly known as the broad-leafed grape, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Uva crassipetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
