Utricularia imerinensis

Utricularia imerinensis, commonly known as the Malagasy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species that thrives in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant features tiny, bladder-like traps that capture small aquatic organisms for nutrients. Its leaves are thin and floating, with specialized structures for trapping prey.

Utricularia imerinensis plays a unique ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling in water ecosystems.
