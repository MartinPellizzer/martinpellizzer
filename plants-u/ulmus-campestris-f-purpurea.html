Ulmus campestris f. purpurea

Ulmus campestris f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf field elm, is a cultivar of the field elm, characterized by its distinctive purple foliage.

This ornamental tree exhibits heart-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, adding visual interest to landscapes. It is typically a medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant is relatively slow-growing and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often used in urban and garden settings for its unique coloration.
