Uncinia ferruginea

Uncinia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty hook cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with dark green, ribbed stems that are often covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has clusters of short, hooked spines that give it its common name.

Uncinia ferruginea is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
