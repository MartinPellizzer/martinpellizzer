Uragoga squarrosa

Uragoga squarrosa, commonly known as the roughleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged foliage. Its leaves are typically spiny and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing protection from herbivores.

Uragoga squarrosa is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
