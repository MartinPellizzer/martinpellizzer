Uncinia filiformis var. debilior

Uncinia filiformis var. debilior, commonly known as the slender hookweed, is a variant of the hookweed species native to certain regions of North America.

It is a flowering plant in the family Poaceae, characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its morphology includes a slender stem with delicate, grass-like foliage, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
