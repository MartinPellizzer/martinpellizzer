Ulmus montana var. cebennensis

Ulmus montana var. cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebennensis elm, is a rare and ancient variety of elm native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and a distinctive, gnarled growth habit. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often reaching ages of over 200 years.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
