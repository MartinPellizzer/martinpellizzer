Urostylis gaillardioides

Urostylis gaillardioides, commonly known as the slender swampgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, which is common among grass-like plants.

Urostylis gaillardioides plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
