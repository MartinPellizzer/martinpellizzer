Urostigma cordatum

Urostigma cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Urostigma cordatum is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
