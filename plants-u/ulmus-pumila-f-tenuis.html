Ulmus pumila f. tenuis

Ulmus pumila f. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elm, is a variant of the Chinese elm characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

This form exhibits reduced leaf size and a more compact growth habit compared to the typical Ulmus pumila. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its foliage provides a delicate, lace-like appearance, making it a popular choice for landscaping in urban environments.
