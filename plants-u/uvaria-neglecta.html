Uvaria neglecta

Uvaria neglecta, commonly known as the slender uvaria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
