Urucu

Urucu, commonly known as achiote, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is cultivated for its bright red seeds, which are rich in carotenoids like beta-carotene. The plant belongs to the Crotonaceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The seeds are also a key ingredient in natural dyes and food coloring.
