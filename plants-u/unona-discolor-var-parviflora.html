Unona discolor var. parviflora

Unona discolor var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered white woodbine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, often waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and early summer. Its botanical features include a fragrant aroma and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
