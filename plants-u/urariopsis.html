Urariopsis

Urariopsis, commonly known as the false arrowhead, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of a single species, Urariopsis jumentorum, which is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The genus is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
