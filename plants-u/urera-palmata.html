Urera palmata

Urera palmata, commonly known as the golden palm, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes other palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Urera palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
