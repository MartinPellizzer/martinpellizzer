Utricularia dentata

Utricularia dentata, commonly known as the toothed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called utricles, which function as traps to capture tiny aquatic organisms. This species typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, where it relies on capturing prey to supplement its nutrient intake. Utricularia dentata has finely toothed leaves that help in anchoring it in its aquatic habitat.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
