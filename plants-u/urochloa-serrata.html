Urochloa serrata

Urochloa serrata, commonly known as serrated grass or broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaf blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas, including pastures, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is valued for its drought tolerance and use in forage and erosion control.

Urochloa serrata is also used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
