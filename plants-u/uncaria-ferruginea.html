Uncaria ferruginea

Uncaria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bark climbing shrub, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, rough, and rust-colored stems, which give it its common name. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in small umbels.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
