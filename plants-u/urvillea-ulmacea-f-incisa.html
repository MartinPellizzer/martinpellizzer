Urvillea ulmacea f. incisa

Urvillea ulmacea f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed urvillea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
