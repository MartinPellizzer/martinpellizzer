Umbilicus rupestris subsp. gaditanus

Umbilicus rupestris subsp.

gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
