Ulmus × arbuscula

Ulmus × arbuscula, commonly known as the Chinese wailu or Chinese field elm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the Chinese elm (Ulmus parvifolia) and the wailu elm (Ulmus wailu).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, ovate leaves and a distinctive, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The bark is typically smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a hardy and ornamental tree.

The plant is valued for its resilience and is often used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
