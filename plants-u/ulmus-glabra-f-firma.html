Ulmus glabra f. firma

Ulmus glabra f. firma, commonly known as the European Wych Elm.

It is a cultivar of the Wych Elm, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and reduced size compared to the wild species. The leaves are typically smaller and more upright, giving the plant a neat appearance. It is often used in urban landscapes due to its tolerance of pollution and compact form.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional hedgerows.
