Urostigma truncatum

Urostigma truncatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and are clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in the spring.

Urostigma truncatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
