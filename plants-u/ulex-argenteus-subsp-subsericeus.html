Ulex argenteus subsp. subsericeus

Ulex argenteus subsp.

subsericeus, commonly known as the silver buckthorn, is a perennial shrub native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically consumed by birds. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
