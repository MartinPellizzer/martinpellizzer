Urtica canadensis

Urtica canadensis, commonly known as Canadian nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which contain formic acid and cause a painful rash upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in woodlands, stream banks, and meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine, white veins.

Urtica canadensis produces small, greenish flowers in umbrella-like clusters, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
