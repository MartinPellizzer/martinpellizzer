Urtica pulus

Urtica pulus, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hollow, hair-covered stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant contains fine, venomous hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Urtica pulus belongs to the Urticaceae family and is known for its edible leaves when cooked.

The plant also plays a role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
