Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with fine, hollow hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with clusters of small greenish-white flowers in umbels. Urtica dioica is a member of the Urticaceae family and is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source when cooked.
