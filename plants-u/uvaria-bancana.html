Uvaria bancana

Uvaria bancana, commonly known as the blackberry tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Uvaria bancana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
