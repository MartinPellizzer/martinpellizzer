Ulex nanus var. confertus

Ulex nanus var. confertus, commonly known as the dense thorned broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
