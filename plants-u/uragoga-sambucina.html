Uragoga sambucina

Uragoga sambucina, commonly known as sweetwood or black elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale yellow. Uragoga sambucina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its berries.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
