Uncaria sterrophylla

Uncaria sterrophylla, commonly known as the climbing evergreen vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
