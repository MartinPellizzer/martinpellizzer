Ulmus glabra var. camperdownii

Ulmus glabra var. camperdownii, commonly known as the Camperdown elm, is a small, ornamental tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant is a cultivar of the Wych elm, known for its compact size and attractive foliage. It flowers in early spring with small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers, followed by small, round fruits.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
