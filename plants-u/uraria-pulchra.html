Uraria pulchra

Uraria pulchra, commonly known as the purple uraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and typically have a purple or pinkish hue.

Uraria pulchra is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in degraded areas.
