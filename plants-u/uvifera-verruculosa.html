Uvifera verruculosa

Uvifera verruculosa, commonly known as the rough uvilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
