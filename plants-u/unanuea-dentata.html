Unanuea dentata

Unanuea dentata, commonly known as the serrated leaf plant, is a tropical species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and well adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Unanuea dentata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
