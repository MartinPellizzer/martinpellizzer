Urophyllum

Urophyllum, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Urophyllum is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, woodland environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
