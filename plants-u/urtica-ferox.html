Urtica ferox

Urtica ferox, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, stinging hairs that release formic acid upon contact, causing skin irritation. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Urtica ferox is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
