Urochondra

Urochondra, commonly known as the common helleborine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and the presence of showy, often green or white flowers. The plant typically features a rhizomatous root system and has lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Urochondra species are native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, and they thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, they are notable for their complex floral structures and their role in mycorrhizal associations.
