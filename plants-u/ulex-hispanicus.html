Ulex hispanicus

Ulex hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish Heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing small, hard seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Ulex hispanicus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.
