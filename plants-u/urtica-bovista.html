Urtica bovista

Urtica bovista, commonly known as cattle nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Urtica bovista is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
