Ursinia eckloniana

Ursinia eckloniana, commonly known as the Cape ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Ursinia eckloniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
