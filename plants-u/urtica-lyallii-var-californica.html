Urtica lyallii var. californica

Urtica lyallii var. californica, commonly known as the California stinging nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, smooth fruits.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
