Ulex richteri

Ulex richteri, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Ulex richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
