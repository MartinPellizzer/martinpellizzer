Uragoga multinervata

Uragoga multinervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined uragoga, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinct, parallel veins. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Uragoga multinervata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
