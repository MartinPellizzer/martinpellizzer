Usionis pedicillaris

Usionis pedicillaris, commonly known as the slender pedicellate orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil, while its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

Usionis pedicillaris is noted for its subtle fragrance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
