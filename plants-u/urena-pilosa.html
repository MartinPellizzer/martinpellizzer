Urena pilosa

Urena pilosa, commonly known as hairy urena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

Urena pilosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
