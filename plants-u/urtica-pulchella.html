Urtica pulchella

Urtica pulchella, commonly known as the pretty nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted pattern on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Urtica pulchella is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
