Urceolina horsmannii

Urceolina horsmannii, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure that encloses its flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
