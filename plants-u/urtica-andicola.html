Urtica andicola

Urtica andicola, commonly known as the Andean nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Urtica andicola has been traditionally used in South American medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
