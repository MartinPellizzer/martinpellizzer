Uragoga capitata

Uragoga capitata, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.
