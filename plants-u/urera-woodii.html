Urera woodii

Urera woodii, commonly known as the Mexican elder or Mexican soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a spherical, spiny berry that ripens to a yellowish color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
