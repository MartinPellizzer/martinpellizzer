Umbilicus sempervivum

Umbilicus sempervivum, commonly known as the stonecrop or houseleek, is a succulent plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and rosettes of thick, waxy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and as a remedy for various ailments.
