29 Best Medicinal Herbs For Herbalists

1. Achillea millefolium (yarrow)

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a flowering medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including wounds, fever, and digestive issues.

Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties make it an effective natural remedy for soothing burns, cuts, and other skin irritations.

Additionally, yarrow has been traditionally used to ease menstrual cramps and promote relaxation, making it a popular ingredient in herbal teas and infusions.

achillea millefolium

2. Acorus calamus (sweet flag)

Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its numerous health benefits.

It is believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues, reduce stress and anxiety, and alleviate pain.

The root of the acorus calamus plant is commonly used in herbal remedies and is also a key ingredient in some traditional teas and tinctures.

acorus calamus

3. Aesculus hippocastanum (horse chestnut)

Aesculus hippocastanum, also known as horse chestnut, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health issues, including inflammation and circulatory problems.

The herb contains a number of bioactive compounds, including saponins and flavonoids, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Traditionally, horse chestnut has been used to treat conditions such as varicose veins, swelling, and pain, although its effectiveness for these uses has not been extensively scientifically proven.

aesculus hippocastanum

4. Allium sativum (garlic)

Allium sativum, also known as garlic, is a popular medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions due to its antimicrobial, antiviral, and antifungal properties.

Its active compounds, such as allicin, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects and help to reduce the risk of heart disease, certain cancers, and other health issues.

Additionally, garlic has been traditionally used to support digestive health and to alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and other respiratory infections.

allium sativum

5. Aloe vera (ghritkumari)

Aloe vera, also known as ghritkumari, is a widely used medicinal herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits for centuries.

The gel extracted from its leaves is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, which help to soothe burns, wounds, and skin irritations, promoting faster healing and reducing inflammation.

Aloe vera is also used to treat various internal health issues, including digestive problems, and is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

aloe vera

6. Alpinia officinarum (ginger)

Alpinia officinarum, also known as ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

The rhizome of this herb contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive properties.

Alpinia officinarum is commonly used to alleviate nausea, reduce pain and inflammation, and promote digestive health, making it a valuable natural remedy in many cultures.

alpinia officinarum

7. Ammi visnaga (sweet annie)

Ammi visnaga, also known as sweet annie, is a medicinal herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, valued for its potential health benefits and unique fragrance.

Traditionally used in herbal medicine, ammi visnaga is said to possess anti-inflammatory properties and is often used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Modern research has also investigated its potential in reducing inflammation and as a treatment for anxiety and insomnia.

ammi visnaga

8. Angelica sinensis (dong quai)

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to promote women's health and alleviate various health issues.

It is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce menstrual cramps, improve circulation, and support immune function.

In traditional Chinese medicine, dong quai is often used to balance the body's energy and promote overall well-being, particularly in women experiencing hormonal imbalances or menstrual disorders.

angelica sinensis

9. Apium graveolens (parsley)

Apium graveolens, also known as parsley, is a versatile medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions.

Its roots, stems, and leaves contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce swelling and alleviate symptoms associated with digestive issues and respiratory problems.

Additionally, parsley has been traditionally used to support kidney function and promote urinary health due to its diuretic properties.

apium graveolens

10. Arctium lappa (burdock)

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions, including skin issues and digestive problems.

The root of the plant contains inulin, a prebiotic fiber that can help support the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, promoting a healthy digestive system.

Burdock root is also traditionally used to treat eczema, acne, and other skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

arctium lappa

11. Artemisia absinthium (wormwood)

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, prized for its bitter compounds and traditional medicinal uses.

The herb has been used for centuries to treat various ailments, including digestive issues, fever, and even malaria, due to its active compound artemisinin.

However, wormwood should be used with caution, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects such as nausea and liver damage.

artemisia absinthium

12. Artemisia annua (sweet annie)

Artemisia annua, also known as sweet Annie, is a medicinal herb native to China and Southeast Asia, traditionally used to treat fever and malaria due to its high concentration of artemisinin, a potent antimalarial compound.

This herb has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, with its leaves and stems being harvested and processed to extract the artemisinin.

Studies have shown that artemisinin-based treatments can be highly effective in reducing the severity and mortality rate of malaria, making artemisia annua a valuable resource in global health efforts.

artemisia annua

13. Astragalus membranaceus (huang qi)

Astragalus membranaceus, also known as huang qi, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used to boost the immune system and enhance overall health.

It has been used for centuries to treat various conditions, including fatigue, respiratory issues, and digestive problems, due to its adaptogenic properties and ability to tonify the body's energy.

The root of the astragalus plant is rich in flavonoids, saponins, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal benefits.

astragalus membranaceus

14. Azadirachta indica (neem)

Azadirachta indica, also known as neem, is a medicinal herb native to the Indian subcontinent and has been used for centuries in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

It is renowned for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiparasitic properties, making it effective in treating a range of health issues, from skin conditions and digestive problems to fever and respiratory infections.

The oil extracted from the neem tree's seeds has also been used to repel insects and as a natural insecticide, due to its powerful insecticidal properties.

azadirachta indica

15. Barosma betulina (buchu)

Barosma betulina, also known as buchu, is a medicinal herb native to South Africa, traditionally used to treat a variety of health issues.

The plant has been utilized for centuries in traditional medicine to address problems such as urinary tract infections, digestive issues, and skin conditions due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its essential oil, extracted from the leaves, is still used today in the production of herbal remedies and skincare products.

barosma betulina

16. Bupleurum chinense (chinese thorowax)

Bupleurum chinense, also known as Chinese thorowax, is a medicinal herb native to China and other parts of East Asia, traditionally used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The herb has been used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its bioactive compounds and flavonoids.

Its extracts and roots are often used in herbal supplements and remedies to promote overall well-being and immune system function.

bupleurum chinense

17. Calendula officinalis (pot marigold)

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a flowering herb commonly used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's flowers and leaves are rich in antioxidants and have been traditionally used to treat a variety of skin conditions, including wounds, burns, and eczema.

Calendula's soothing and protective effects make it a popular ingredient in skincare products and ointments.

calendula officinalis

18. Capsicum annuum (cayenne)

Capsicum annuum, also known as cayenne, is a versatile medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions, including pain, inflammation, and digestive issues.

The active compound capsaicin found in cayenne pepper has potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a popular natural remedy for relieving symptoms of arthritis, migraines, and muscle soreness.

When used in moderation, cayenne pepper can also help to stimulate circulation, boost the immune system, and even aid in weight loss.

capsicum annuum

19. Cassia senna (cassia)

Cassia senna, also known as cassia, is a traditional medicinal herb derived from the leaves of the cassia tree, commonly used to stimulate bowel movements and relieve constipation.

The herb contains anthraquinone glycosides, which are responsible for its laxative properties and have been used for centuries in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine.

Cassia senna is often used in herbal remedies to treat digestive issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome and hemorrhoids, and is typically consumed as a tea or supplement.

cassia senna

20. Centella asiatica (gotu kola)

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a traditional medicinal herb that has been used for centuries in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine.

It is renowned for its numerous health benefits, including improving cognitive function, reducing inflammation, and promoting wound healing.

The herb's extracts have been found to contain compounds with antioxidant and anti-scarring properties, making it a popular natural remedy for skin and brain health.

centella asiatica

21. Chamomilla recutita (german chamomile)

Chamomilla recutita, also known as German chamomile, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat a variety of health issues, including insomnia, anxiety, and digestive problems.

The herb's soothing and calming effects are attributed to its high concentration of apigenin and luteolin, two potent flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory properties.

Traditionally, German chamomile is consumed as a tea or applied topically to relieve skin irritations and promote relaxation.

chamomilla recutita

22. Cimicifuga racemosa (black cohosh)

Cimicifuga racemosa, also known as black cohosh, is a medicinal herb traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of menopause, including hot flashes and mood swings.

Native American and European herbalists have utilized black cohosh for centuries to treat various conditions, including menstrual cramps, arthritis, and anxiety.

Research suggests that black cohosh may also have anti-inflammatory properties and may help to improve sleep quality and reduce stress.

cimicifuga racemosa

23. Cinchona officinalis (red bark)

Cinchona officinalis, also known as red bark, is a medicinal herb native to the Andean region of South America, where its bark has been used for centuries to treat malaria and other fevers.

The herb's active compound, quinine, has been isolated and used to develop various medications for treating malaria and other parasitic diseases.

Cinchona officinalis remains a valuable plant in traditional medicine and continues to be studied for its potential in treating various health conditions.

cinchona officinalis

24. Cinnamomum camphora (camphor tree)

Cinnamomum camphora, also known as camphor tree, is a medicinal herb that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

The herb's essential oil is derived from its leaves and contains compounds like camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that help alleviate pain and reduce inflammation.

Cinnamomum camphora is also used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and relieve stress, making it a popular herbal remedy for promoting overall well-being.

cinnamomum camphora

25. Codonopsis pilosula (dang shen)

Codonopsis pilosula, also known as dang shen, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to tonify the spleen and stomach, improving digestion and overall energy levels.

The root of the codonopsis pilosula plant is often used in herbal remedies to enhance the body's ability to absorb nutrients and fight off infections.

In traditional Chinese medicine, codonopsis pilosula is considered a gentle and non-invasive way to support the body's natural healing processes.

codonopsis pilosula

26. Coleus forskohlii (forskohlii)

coleus forskohlii

27. Commiphora molmol (myrrh)

Commiphora molmol, also known as myrrh, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including wounds, digestive issues, and respiratory problems.

The resin of the Commiphora molmol tree, which is typically harvested by making incisions in the bark, is rich in anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

commiphora molmol

28. Corydalis yanhusuo (yan hu suo)

Corydalis yanhusuo, also known as Yan Hu Suo, is a traditional medicinal herb used in Chinese medicine to treat various ailments, particularly pain and inflammation.

The root of the plant contains a compound called dehydrocorybulbine (DHCB), which has been shown to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Studies have demonstrated the potential of Corydalis yanhusuo in managing chronic pain and improving sleep quality, making it a promising natural remedy for individuals suffering from pain-related conditions.

corydalis yanhusuo

29. Crataegus oxyacantha (hawthorn)

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as hawthorn, is a flowering plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly in Europe, due to its potential health benefits.

Its berries, flowers, and leaves are commonly used in supplements and teas, although more research is needed to confirm the full extent of its medicinal effects.

crataegus oxyacantha
