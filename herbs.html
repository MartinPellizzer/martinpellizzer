1. achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Its fern-like foliage and white or yellow composite flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal purposes. Traditionally, yarrow has been used to treat wounds and reduce inflammation due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

achillea millefolium

2. acorus calamus

Acorus calamus, also known as sweet flag or calamus, is a perennial plant native to Asia and Europe, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and aromatic rhizome. The rhizome of Acorus calamus has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. In addition to its medicinal uses, the plant's essential oil is also used in aromatherapy and perfumery to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

acorus calamus

3. aesculus hippocastanum

Aesculus hippocastanum, also known as the horse chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkans and parts of Asia Minor. It is widely cultivated for its large, showy white or pink flowers and distinctive red conical seed capsule, which contains edible seeds but is often mistaken for a fruit. The horse chestnut tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate pollution and its long lifespan, often reaching up to 200 years in ideal conditions.

aesculus hippocastanum

4. allium sativum

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a species of plant in the onion family (Amaryllidaceae) that has been used for centuries for its culinary, medicinal, and cultural significance. Native to Central Asia, garlic has been cultivated for over 7,000 years and is now grown in many parts of the world for its edible bulbs, which are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. The strong flavor and aroma of garlic are due to the presence of sulfur compounds, which have been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral properties.

allium sativum

5. aloe vera

Aloe vera is a succulent plant known for its numerous health benefits, particularly in soothing and calming irritated skin. The gel from its leaves can be used to treat burns, minor cuts, and other skin irritations, promoting healing and reducing inflammation. Additionally, aloe vera has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions.

aloe vera

6. alpinia officinarum

Alpinia officinarum, also known as lesser galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a type of ginger that is commonly used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various Southeast Asian cuisines. The plant's rhizome has anti-inflammatory properties and is used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and respiratory infections.

alpinia officinarum

7. ammi visnaga

Ammi visnaga, also known as khella, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. Its seeds have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including the treatment of heart conditions and as a diuretic. The plant contains a number of bioactive compounds, including amides and flavonoids, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.

ammi visnaga

8. angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions. Native to China, this perennial plant is valued for its roots, which are rich in antioxidants, anti-inflammatory compounds, and other bioactive molecules. In traditional Chinese medicine, dong quai is often used to balance the body's energy and promote women's health, particularly in relation to menstrual and menopausal issues.

angelica sinensis

9. apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, commonly known as celery, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its edible stalks and seeds. The plant is a rich source of vitamins and minerals, particularly vitamin K and potassium, and is often used as a flavoring agent in soups, salads, and other dishes. In addition to its culinary uses, apium graveolens has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

apium graveolens

10. arctium lappa

Arctium lappa, also known as burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive purple flowers. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for centuries, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to support digestive health. Burdock root is also a popular ingredient in herbal teas and supplements, often used to promote detoxification and overall well-being.

arctium lappa

11. artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine, as well as in the production of absinthe, a spirit known for its unique flavor and psychoactive properties. However, wormwood contains thujone, a compound that can be toxic in large quantities, making its use and cultivation subject to regulatory restrictions in many countries.

artemisia absinthium
