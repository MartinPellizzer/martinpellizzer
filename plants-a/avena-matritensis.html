Avena matritensis

Avena matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid oat, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Avena matritensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
