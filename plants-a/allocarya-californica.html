Allocarya californica

Allocarya californica, commonly known as the California marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riparian areas.

Allocarya californica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
