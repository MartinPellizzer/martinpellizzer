Allium materculae subsp. graveolens

Allium materculae subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the garlic leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and umbel-like inflorescences.

The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers and has a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, adding a sharp flavor to dishes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and stronger aroma compared to other Allium species.
