Asplenium lividum

Asplenium lividum, commonly known as the silver spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in shaded, humid environments. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive silvery or bluish tint on the underside. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium lividum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
