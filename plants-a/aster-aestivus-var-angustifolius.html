Aster aestivus var. angustifolius

Aster aestivus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved summer aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
