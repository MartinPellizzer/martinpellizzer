Alyssum calycinum subsp. granatense

Alyssum calycinum subsp.

granatense, commonly known as Spanish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant has finely divided, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value. Alyssum calycinum subsp.

granatense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
