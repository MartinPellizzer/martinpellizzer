Astragalus ancistrocarpus

Astragalus ancistrocarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Astragalus ancistrocarpus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in arid environments.
