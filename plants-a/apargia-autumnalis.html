Apargia autumnalis

Apargia autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Apargia autumnalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
