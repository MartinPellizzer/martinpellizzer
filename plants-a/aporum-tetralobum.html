Aporum tetralobum

Aporum tetralobum, commonly known as the four-lobed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive four-lobed structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces a showy, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and rich, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
