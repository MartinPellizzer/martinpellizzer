Astragalus massiliensis var. salvatoris

Astragalus massiliensis var. salvatoris, commonly known as the Massilian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators and its role in soil stabilization.
