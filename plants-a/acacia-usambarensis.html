Acacia usambarensis

Acacia usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Tanzania and Kenya.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia usambarensis is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
