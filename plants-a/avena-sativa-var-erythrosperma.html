Avena sativa var. erythrosperma

Avena sativa var. erythrosperma, commonly known as red-spored oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its distinctive red-colored seeds.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, leafy stems and panicles of spikelets. Its seeds are smaller and more densely packed compared to standard oat varieties, contributing to its unique appearance.

The red coloration of the seeds is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which also provide antioxidant properties.
