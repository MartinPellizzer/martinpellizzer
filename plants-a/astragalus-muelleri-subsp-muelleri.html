Astragalus muelleri subsp. muelleri

Astragalus muelleri subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Astragalus muelleri subsp.

muelleri is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
