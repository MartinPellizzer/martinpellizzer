Actinodaphne furfuracea

Actinodaphne furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy bark and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
