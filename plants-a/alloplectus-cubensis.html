Alloplectus cubensis

Alloplectus cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban clover, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Alloplectus cubensis has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
