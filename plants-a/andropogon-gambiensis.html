Andropogon gambiensis

Andropogon gambiensis, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a tall perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological and potential agricultural uses.
