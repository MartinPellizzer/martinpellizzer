Althaea flexuosa

Althaea flexuosa, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Althaea flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
