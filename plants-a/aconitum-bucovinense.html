Aconitum bucovinense

Aconitum bucovinense, commonly known as the Bucovina aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are compound, with palmate divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum bucovinense contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
