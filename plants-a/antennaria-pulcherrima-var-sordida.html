Antennaria pulcherrima var. sordida

Antennaria pulcherrima var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate cold climates.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive appearance and its relation to the broader Antennaria genus.
