Ageratum solisii

Ageratum solisii, commonly known as the Solisii Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ageratum solisii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
