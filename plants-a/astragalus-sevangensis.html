Astragalus sevangensis

Astragalus sevangensis, commonly known as the Sevang milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus sevangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
