Adesmia melanocaulos

Adesmia melanocaulos, commonly known as the black-stemmed burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
