Astragalus glaucus

Astragalus glaucus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with 5–9 oval leaflets that are usually blue-green in color.

Astragalus glaucus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
