Acacia macrostachya

Acacia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant acacia or desert wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its long, straight thorns and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system and the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria.

Acacia macrostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
