Asarum caudatum f. chloroleucum

Asarum caudatum f. chloroleucum, commonly known as the chloroleucum form of the Indian snakeroot, is a variant of the Asarum genus native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spathate inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
