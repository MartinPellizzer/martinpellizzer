Aralia scaberula

Aralia scaberula, commonly known as rough aralia, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels. Its berries are edible and often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Aralia scaberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
