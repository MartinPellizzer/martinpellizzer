Avena carpatica

Avena carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian oat, is a perennial grass native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale green flowers, which bloom in late summer. Avena carpatica is adapted to grow in rocky and nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
