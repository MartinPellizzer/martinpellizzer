Amischotolype sphagnorrhiza

Amischotolype sphagnorrhiza, commonly known as the spongy sphagnum moss, is a unique species of moss belonging to the family Sphagnaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spongy growth form, which helps it retain moisture in wet, acidic environments. This plant thrives in peat bogs and other nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils, where it plays a crucial role in ecosystem development. Its rhizoids anchor it to the substrate, while its leafy structures contribute to the formation of peat.

The plant's ability to absorb and store large amounts of water makes it ecologically significant in maintaining wetland habitats.
