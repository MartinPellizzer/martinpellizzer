Aerva floribunda

Aerva floribunda, commonly known as Indian champa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branched, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Aerva floribunda is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
