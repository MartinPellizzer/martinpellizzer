Allium durieuanum

Allium durieuanum, commonly known as the Durieuan onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces round, bulbous structures that are used both ornamentally and for culinary purposes in some regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
