Aquilegia maimanica

Aquilegia maimanica, commonly known as the Mexican columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Aquilegia maimanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
