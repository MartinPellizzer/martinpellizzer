Amaranthus angustifolius proles silvestris

Amaranthus angustifolius proles silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seeds are often used as a grain, while the leaves are edible and rich in nutrients.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
