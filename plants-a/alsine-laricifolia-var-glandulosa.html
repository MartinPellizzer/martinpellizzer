Alsine laricifolia var. glandulosa

Alsine laricifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alsine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, which help in reducing water loss.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
