Acacia greggii

Acacia greggii, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to spines, and its yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which have been used by both humans and animals. Acacia greggii is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
