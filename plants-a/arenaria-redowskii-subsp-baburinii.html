Arenaria redowskii subsp. baburinii

Arenaria redowskii subsp.

baburinii, commonly known as the Karakorum sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
