Aconitum wulfenianum

Aconitum wulfenianum, commonly known as the Wulfen's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces erect, sparsely branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
