Abelmoschus marianus

Abelmoschus marianus, commonly known as the Indian okra or Marian's okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
