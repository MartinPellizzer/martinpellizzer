Alsophila quitensis

Alsophila quitensis, commonly known as the Quitense tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its rhizome system supports the growth of new shoots, contributing to its ability to form dense stands.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
