Artemisia lindleyana

Artemisia lindleyana, commonly known as bluegreen wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer, followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its leaves contain volatile oils, contributing to its strong scent and medicinal uses.
