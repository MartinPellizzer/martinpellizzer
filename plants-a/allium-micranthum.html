Allium micranthum

Allium micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in the spring. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

Allium micranthum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
