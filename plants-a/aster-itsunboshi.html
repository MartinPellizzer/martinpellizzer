Aster itsunboshi

Aster itsunboshi, commonly known as the Japanese aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. Itsunboshi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
