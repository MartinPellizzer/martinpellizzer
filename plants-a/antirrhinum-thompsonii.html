Antirrhinum thompsonii

Antirrhinum thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in areas with full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit.
