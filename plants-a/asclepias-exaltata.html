Asclepias exaltata

Asclepias exaltata, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods called follicles, which contain toxic, sticky seeds. It is a vital host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing nourishment for both larvae and adult butterflies.

Asclepias exaltata thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
