Asplenium filix-femina var. dentigerum

Asplenium filix-femina var. dentigerum, commonly known as the toothed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and China. The plant features delicate, lace-like fronds with a dark green hue and a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
