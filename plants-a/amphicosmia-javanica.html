Amphicosmia javanica

Amphicosmia javanica, commonly known as the Javanese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Amphicosmia javanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
