Acrocephalus sapinii

Acrocephalus sapinii, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which help it survive in harsh climatic conditions.

Acrocephalus sapinii plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and is an important part of alpine ecosystems.
