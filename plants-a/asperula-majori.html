Asperula majori

Asperula majori, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Asperula majori is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
