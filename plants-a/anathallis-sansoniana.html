Anathallis sansoniana

Anathallis sansoniana, commonly known as the Sanson's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and striking, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, showcasing a specialized mutualistic relationship.

Anathallis sansoniana is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
