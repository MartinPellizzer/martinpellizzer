Acalypha communis subsp. tracheliifolia

Acalypha communis subsp.

tracheliifolia, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fire plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, upright spikes of bright red bracts that resemble poker chips, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered at the base of the bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and red, adding visual interest to landscapes.
