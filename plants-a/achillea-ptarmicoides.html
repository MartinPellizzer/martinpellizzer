Achillea ptarmicoides

Achillea ptarmicoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves with a feathery texture and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Its historical use in medicine and as a garden plant highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
