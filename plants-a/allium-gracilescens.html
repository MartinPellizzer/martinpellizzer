Allium gracilescens

Allium gracilescens, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its mild garlic-like scent.

Allium gracilescens is also noted for its ability to naturalize and form dense clusters over time.
