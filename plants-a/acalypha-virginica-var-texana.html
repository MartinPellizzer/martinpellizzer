Acalypha virginica var. texana

Acalypha virginica var. texana, commonly known as the Texas ironweed, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and prairies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
