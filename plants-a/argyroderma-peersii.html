Argyroderma peersii

Argyroderma peersii, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
