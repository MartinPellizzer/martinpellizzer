Aeonium caespitosum

Aeonium caespitosum, commonly known as the Campfire Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically red or purple in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it both ornamental and easy to maintain.
