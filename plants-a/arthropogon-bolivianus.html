Arthropogon bolivianus

Arthropogon bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like morphology.

It is native to South America, particularly Bolivia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with slender, tufted shoots and narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicle-like structures.

Arthropogon bolivianus is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
