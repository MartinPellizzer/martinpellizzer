Aglycia

Aglycia, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant is typically found in shallow, still freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Aglycia lacks true leaves, instead having whorls of tiny, scale-like structures that aid in photosynthesis.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for studying aquatic plant ecology and carnivorous plant behavior.
