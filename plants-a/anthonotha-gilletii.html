Anthonotha gilletii

Anthonotha gilletii, commonly known as the red sandrat or red sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with limited rainfall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are small and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Anthonotha gilletii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
