Arisaema pictum

Arisaema pictum, commonly known as the painted arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the forests of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces showy, often colorful bracts that serve to attract pollinators. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema pictum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and striking floral display.
