Aegiphila gloriosa var. gloriosa

Aegiphila gloriosa var. gloriosa, commonly known as the golden bush or glory plant, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy inflorescences and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
