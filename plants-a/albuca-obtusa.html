Albuca obtusa

Albuca obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved albuca, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
