Acetosella lilacina

Acetosella lilacina, commonly known as purple springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically reaches a height of 5 to 15 centimeters, with slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are typically 5-lobed and have five stamens, with a distinctive elongated pistil.

Acetosella lilacina thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and it is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
