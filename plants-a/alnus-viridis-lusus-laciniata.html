Alnus viridis lusus laciniata

Alnus viridis lusus laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a variant of the green alder.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, winged seeds.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.
