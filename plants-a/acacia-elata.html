Acacia elata

Acacia elata, commonly known as the tall wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia elata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
