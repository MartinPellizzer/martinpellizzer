Achyranthes lappacea

Achyranthes lappacea, commonly known as the red root or red bryony, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Achyranthes lappacea is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various soil conditions.
