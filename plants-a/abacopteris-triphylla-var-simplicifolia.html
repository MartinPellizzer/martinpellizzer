Abacopteris triphylla var. simplicifolia

Abacopteris triphylla var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved three-leaf fern, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lanceolate lobes arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant reproduces via spores, typical of its fern classification, and thrives in humid, temperate climates.
