Amygdalus spinosissima var. urumiensis

Amygdalus spinosissima var. urumiensis, commonly known as the Uruma almond.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Pakistan and India. The plant is characterized by its spiny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a hard, woody drupe containing a single seed.

The species is adapted to survive in dry, saline soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
