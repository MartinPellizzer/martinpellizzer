Albizia pullenii

Albizia pullenii, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.

Albizia pullenii is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
