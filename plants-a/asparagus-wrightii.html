Asparagus wrightii

Asparagus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Wright's asparagus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
