Alpinia nutans var. longiramosa

Alpinia nutans var. longiramosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed ginger, is a variant of the alpine ginger plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
