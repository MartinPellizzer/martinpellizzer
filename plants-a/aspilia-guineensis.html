Aspilia guineensis

Aspilia guineensis, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Aspilia guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
