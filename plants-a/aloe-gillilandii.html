Aloe gillilandii

Aloe gillilandii, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe gillilandii is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots and a slow growth rate to conserve moisture.
