Anthericum peteri

Anthericum peteri, commonly known as the Peter's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Anthericum peteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
