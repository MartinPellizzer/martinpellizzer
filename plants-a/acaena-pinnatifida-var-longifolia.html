Acaena pinnatifida var. longifolia

Acaena pinnatifida var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved burr bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, prickly fruits containing seeds. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
