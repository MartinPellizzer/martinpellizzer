Aristida pseudobromus

Aristida pseudobromus, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in dry environments.
