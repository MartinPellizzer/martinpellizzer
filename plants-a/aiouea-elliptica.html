Aiouea elliptica

Aiouea elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Aiouea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
