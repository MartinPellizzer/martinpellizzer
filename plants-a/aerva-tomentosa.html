Aerva tomentosa

Aerva tomentosa, commonly known as white thornapple, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Aerva tomentosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
