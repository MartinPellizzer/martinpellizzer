Aristolochia weddellii subsp. rondoniana

Aristolochia weddellii subsp.

rondoniana, commonly known as the Rondonian Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, tropical environments with high rainfall and partial shade.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
