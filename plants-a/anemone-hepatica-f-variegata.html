Anemone hepatica f. variegata

Anemone hepatica f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive variegation of white and green, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland environments with partial shade and well-drained, moist soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-loving, early-flowering plant.
