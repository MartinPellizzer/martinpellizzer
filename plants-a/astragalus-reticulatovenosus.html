Astragalus reticulatovenosus

Astragalus reticulatovenosus, commonly known as the reticulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in dense clusters.

Astragalus reticulatovenosus is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
