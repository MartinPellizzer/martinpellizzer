Anemone coronaria proles grassensis

Anemone coronaria proles grassensis, commonly known as the Grassland Poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
