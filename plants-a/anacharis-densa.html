Anacharis densa

Anacharis densa, commonly known as the dense waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are often submerged, though they may emerge near the water's surface.

Anacharis densa is widely used in aquariums due to its rapid growth and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.
