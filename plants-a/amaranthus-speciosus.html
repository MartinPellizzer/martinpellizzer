Amaranthus speciosus

Amaranthus speciosus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to purple, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are sturdy and upright, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Amaranthus speciosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
