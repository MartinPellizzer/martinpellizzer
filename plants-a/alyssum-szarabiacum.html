Alyssum szarabiacum

Alyssum szarabiacum, commonly known as the Polish alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Alyssum szarabiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
