Anthoxanthum odoratum var. nipponicum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, tufted blades with a distinctive sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and aromatic qualities make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional herbal uses.
