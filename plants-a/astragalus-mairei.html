Astragalus mairei

Astragalus mairei, commonly known as Maire's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain small, round seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Astragalus mairei is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
