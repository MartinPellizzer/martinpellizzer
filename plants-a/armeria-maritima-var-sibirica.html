Armeria maritima var. sibirica

Armeria maritima var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Siberia.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate and colder regions.
