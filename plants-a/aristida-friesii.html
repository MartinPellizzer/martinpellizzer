Aristida friesii

Aristida friesii, commonly known as the golden threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida friesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
