Acacia lunata

Acacia lunata, commonly known as the moon acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery segments.

Acacia lunata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
