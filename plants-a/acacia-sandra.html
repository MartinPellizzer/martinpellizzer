Acacia sandra

Acacia sandra, commonly known as the sand acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Acacia sandra is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
