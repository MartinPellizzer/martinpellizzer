Allium buhseanum

Allium buhseanum, commonly known as the Buhse's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to onions and garlic, though they are less commonly cultivated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is gaining interest among horticulturists and gardeners.
