Atropa arborea

Atropa arborea, commonly known as the tree belladonna, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring broad, ovate leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that contain toxic seeds, making it a potentially dangerous species. Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its milky sap and strong, unpleasant odor.

Its name derives from the Greek word "atropa," meaning "unavoidable," referencing its toxic properties.
