Acalypha moggii

Acalypha moggii, commonly known as the red-flowered copperleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or near water sources.

Acalypha moggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
