Asplenium pekinense var. foeniculaceum

Asplenium pekinense var. foeniculaceum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the pekinense fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the structure of the common maidenhair fern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually a deep green, with a slightly glossy appearance.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in humid indoor conditions.
