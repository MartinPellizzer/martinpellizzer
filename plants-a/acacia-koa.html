Acacia koa

Acacia koa, commonly known as the Hawaiian koa tree, is a native species of Hawaii belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its straight, sturdy trunk and bipinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery fuzz. The tree produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain seeds encased in a hard, woody shell. Acacia koa is well adapted to the dry, volcanic soils of the Hawaiian Islands and is often found in lowland and upland forests.

Due to its historical and cultural significance, it is also a symbol of Hawaiian identity and heritage.
