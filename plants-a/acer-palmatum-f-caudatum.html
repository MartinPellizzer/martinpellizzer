Acer palmatum f. caudatum

Acer palmatum f. caudatum, commonly known as the Japanese weeping maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple characterized by its cascading, weeping branches.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a broad, spreading form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance makes it a striking addition to landscapes, though it requires protection from harsh winter conditions.
