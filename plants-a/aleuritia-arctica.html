Aleuritia arctica

Aleuritia arctica, commonly known as the Arctic elderberry, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Aleuritia arctica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
