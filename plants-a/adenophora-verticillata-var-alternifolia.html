Adenophora verticillata var. alternifolia

Adenophora verticillata var. alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
