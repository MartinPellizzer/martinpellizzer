Astragalus glaucacanthos

Astragalus glaucacanthos, commonly known as the blue thorned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus glaucacanthos is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
