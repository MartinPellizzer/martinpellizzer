Arrhenatherum cycladum

Arrhenatherum cycladum, commonly known as the Cycladic feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a soft, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
