Aotus subglauca var. subglauca

Aotus subglauca var. subglauca, commonly known as the blue-green spurge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
