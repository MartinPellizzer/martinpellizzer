Aeschynanthus pseudohybridus

Aeschynanthus pseudohybridus, commonly known as the hybrid wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a trailing evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble the flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Aeschynanthus pseudohybridus is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
