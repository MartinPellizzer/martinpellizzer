Agapetes muscorum

Agapetes muscorum, commonly known as the mossy agapetes, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and densely packed, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Agapetes muscorum is also noted for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a pleasant, fragrant aroma.
