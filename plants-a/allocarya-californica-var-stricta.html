Allocarya californica var. stricta

Allocarya californica var. stricta, commonly known as the California dwarf broom.

It is a flowering plant native to California, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, shrub-like form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure in the landscape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
