Agoseris poeppigii

Agoseris poeppigii, commonly known as the desert goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Agoseris poeppigii has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
