Astragalus arakawensis

Astragalus arakawensis, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in well-drained soils.

Astragalus arakawensis is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential source of medicinal compounds.
