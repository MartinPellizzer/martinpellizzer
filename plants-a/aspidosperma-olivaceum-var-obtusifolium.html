Aspidosperma olivaceum var. obtusifolium

Aspidosperma olivaceum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* var. obtusifolium, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a blunt tip, giving it its specific variety name.
