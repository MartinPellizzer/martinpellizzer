Atriplex thornberi

Atriplex thornberi, commonly known as the Thornberg saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and semi-desert habitats.

Atriplex thornberi plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
