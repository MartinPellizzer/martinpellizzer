Asplenium ensiforme var. bicuspe

Asplenium ensiforme var. bicuspe, commonly known as the bicusped maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern, characterized by its distinctive two-pointed lobes on the fronds.

It is a fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are usually slender and arching, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
