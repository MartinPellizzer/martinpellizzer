Annona diversifolia

Annona diversifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a green, spiny capsule that turns yellow when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet pulp.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
