Acacia cangaiensis

Acacia cangaiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

Acacia cangaiensis is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
