Allium roseum var. humile

Allium roseum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf purple onion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are grass-like and bluish-green in color, with a distinct garlic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and mild flavor.
