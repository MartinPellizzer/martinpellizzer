Acalypha glutinosa

Acalypha glutinosa, commonly known as sticky leaf or glue plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy or sticky leaves that can be used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
