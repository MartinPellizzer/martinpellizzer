Astragalus sanguinolentus var. illyricus

Astragalus sanguinolentus var. illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive red or bloody-colored flowers, which are a notable feature of the species.
