Arum pentaphyllum

Arum pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-lobed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and edible species. Its foliage and flowers are often cultivated for their aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.

The plant prefers warm, humid conditions and is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor environments.
