Arceuthos drupacea

Arceuthos drupacea, commonly known as the sticky seedball plant, is a unique species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical seed pods that contain sticky, gelatinous seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed through a mechanism called ballistic dispersal, where the seed pod explosively ejects the seeds upon maturity.

This botanical feature makes it an intriguing subject for ecological and botanical study.
