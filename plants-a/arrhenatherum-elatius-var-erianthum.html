Arrhenatherum elatius var. erianthum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. erianthum, commonly known as the silvergrass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
