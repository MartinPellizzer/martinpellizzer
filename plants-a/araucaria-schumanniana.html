Araucaria schumanniana

Araucaria schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumacher's Araucaria, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of dense, pyramidal branches. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, spherical seed structures. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
