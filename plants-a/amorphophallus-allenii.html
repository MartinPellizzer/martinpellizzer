Amorphophallus allenii

Amorphophallus allenii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, making it a visually impressive species. The plant produces a tall, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near rivers or in lowland forests.

The plant is also noted for its unique flowering cycle, which occurs once every few years, making it a rare and fascinating botanical specimen.
