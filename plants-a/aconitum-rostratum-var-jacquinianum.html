Aconitum rostratum var. jacquinianum

Aconitum rostratum var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's beak aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spires of flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
