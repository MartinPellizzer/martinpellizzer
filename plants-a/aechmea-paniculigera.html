Aechmea paniculigera

Aechmea paniculigera, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a type of bromeliad characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces a tall, branching inflorescence that bears showy, pinkish-white flowers, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaf rosette makes it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Morphology

Aechmea paniculigera has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, with a bluish-gray color and a waxy coating, arranged in a dense spiral at the base.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows from the base upward. Its inflorescence is a branched panicle, bearing small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by wind and insects. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind once it splits open.

The roots are fibrous and anchor the plant firmly in the soil, helping it absorb water and nutrients.
