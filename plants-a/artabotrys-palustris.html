Artabotrys palustris

Artabotrys palustris, commonly known as the swamp lantern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped inflorescences that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in humid and moist conditions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
