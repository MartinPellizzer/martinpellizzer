Amphiachyris spinosa

Amphiachyris spinosa, commonly known as the spiny amphiachyris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
