Acalypha pseudalopecuroides

Acalypha pseudalopecuroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
