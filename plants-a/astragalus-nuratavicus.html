Astragalus nuratavicus

Astragalus nuratavicus, commonly known as the Central Asian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus nuratavicus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
