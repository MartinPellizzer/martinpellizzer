Anonis fruticosa

Anonis fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Anonis fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and fruit-bearing qualities.
