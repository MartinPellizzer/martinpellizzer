Aspidosperma ramiflorum

Aspidosperma ramiflorum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
