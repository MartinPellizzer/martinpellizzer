Adiantum hendersonii

Adiantum hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that resemble delicate lace. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a distinctive, arching growth habit that adds a refined touch to any setting.

Adiantum hendersonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
