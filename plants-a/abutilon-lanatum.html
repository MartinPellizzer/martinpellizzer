Abutilon lanatum

Abutilon lanatum, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its hairy texture is due to dense pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

Abutilon lanatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
