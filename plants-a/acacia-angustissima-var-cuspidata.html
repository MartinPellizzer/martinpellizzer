Acacia angustissima var. cuspidata

Acacia angustissima var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular, and often spiny stems, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are narrow, linear, and sometimes pointed, giving the plant its specific epithet "cuspidata.". It produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
