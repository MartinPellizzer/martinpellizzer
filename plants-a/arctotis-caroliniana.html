Arctotis caroliniana

Arctotis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Arctotis caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
