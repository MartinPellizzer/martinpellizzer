Abies sacra

Abies sacra, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green needles that are typically two to three inches long. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is smooth and grayish-white, especially on younger trees. Abies sacra thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in subalpine and high-elevation forests.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
