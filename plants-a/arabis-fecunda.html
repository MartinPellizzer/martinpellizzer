Arabis fecunda

Arabis fecunda, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis fecunda is typically short-lived, completing its life cycle within one growing season.
