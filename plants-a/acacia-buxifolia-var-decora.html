Acacia buxifolia var. decora

Acacia buxifolia var. decora, commonly known as the Decorated Boxwood Acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy foliage. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its adaptability to various climates.
