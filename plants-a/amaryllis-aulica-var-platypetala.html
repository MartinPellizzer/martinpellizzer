Amaryllis aulica var. platypetala

Amaryllis aulica var. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled amaryllis, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and clusters of large, showy flowers with flat, petal-like tepals. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, typically in late spring to early summer. Native to South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
