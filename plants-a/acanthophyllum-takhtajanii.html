Acanthophyllum takhtajanii

Acanthophyllum takhtajanii, commonly known as the Afghan spiny saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its resilience, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
