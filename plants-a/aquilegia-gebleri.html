Aquilegia gebleri

Aquilegia gebleri, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a rough texture, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Aquilegia gebleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in naturalized habitats.
