Astragalus pereshkhoranicus

Astragalus pereshkhoranicus, commonly known as the Caucasus milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
