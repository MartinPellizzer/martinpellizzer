Amaryllis × atrorubens

Amaryllis × atrorubens, commonly known as the red-tipped amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis cilindrica.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically produces multiple blooms on a single stalk, with each flower having a distinctive red or orange tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.
