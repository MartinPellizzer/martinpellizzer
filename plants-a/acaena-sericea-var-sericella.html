Acaena sericea var. sericella

Acaena sericea var. sericella, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
