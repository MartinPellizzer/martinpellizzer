Astragalus rosae

Astragalus rosae, commonly known as the rose milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Astragalus rosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
