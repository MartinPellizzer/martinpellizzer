Acacia amputata

Acacia amputata, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its wood is lightweight and has been traditionally used for making furniture and tools.

Acacia amputata is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.
