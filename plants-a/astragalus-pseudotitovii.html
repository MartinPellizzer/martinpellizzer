Astragalus pseudotitovii

Astragalus pseudotitovii, commonly known as the Titov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus pseudotitovii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
