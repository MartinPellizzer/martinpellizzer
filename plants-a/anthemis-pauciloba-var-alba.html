Anthemis pauciloba var. alba

Anthemis pauciloba var. alba, commonly known as white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in a compact, daisy-like pattern. It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The foliage is gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
