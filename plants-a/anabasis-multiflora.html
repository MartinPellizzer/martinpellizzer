Anabasis multiflora

Anabasis multiflora, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adapted to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Anabasis multiflora is well-suited to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
