Arcytophyllum latifolium

Arcytophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sage, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
