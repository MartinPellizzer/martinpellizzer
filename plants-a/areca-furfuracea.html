Areca furfuracea

Areca furfuracea, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits, which are commonly consumed in various cultures. Areca furfuracea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental and landscaping purposes.
