Astragalus tortuosus

Astragalus tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Astragalus tortuosus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
