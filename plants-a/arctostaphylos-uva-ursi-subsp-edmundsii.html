Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. edmundsii

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

edmundsii, commonly known as the Edmunds manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
