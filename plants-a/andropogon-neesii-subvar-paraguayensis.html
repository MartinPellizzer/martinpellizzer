Andropogon neesii subvar. paraguayensis

Andropogon neesii subvar. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in full sun. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
