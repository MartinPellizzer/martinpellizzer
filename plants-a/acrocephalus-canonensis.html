Acrocephalus canonensis

Acrocephalus canonensis, commonly known as the California bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in areas with standing water.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
