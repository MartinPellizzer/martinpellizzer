Aerides falcata

Aerides falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Aerides falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
