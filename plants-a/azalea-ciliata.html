Azalea ciliata

Azalea ciliata, commonly known as the coastal azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy texture along the edges.

Azalea ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
