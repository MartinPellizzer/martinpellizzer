Acer fulvescens subsp. fuscescens

Acer fulvescens subsp.

fuscescens, commonly known as the red maple or swamp maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive red to orange-red foliage in autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental species.

The tree typically grows to a height of 10–25 meters, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, light gray bark. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five pointed lobes, and are usually 5–10 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.
