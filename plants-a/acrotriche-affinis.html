Acrotriche affinis

Acrotriche affinis, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming annual or perennial herb that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer.
