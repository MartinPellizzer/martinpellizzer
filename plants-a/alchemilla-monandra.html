Alchemilla monandra

Alchemilla monandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Alchemilla monandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
