Agrostophyllum mangkutanae

Agrostophyllum mangkutanae, commonly known as the Bornean sandalwood, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant is notable for its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it faces significant conservation challenges.

Its ecological importance underscores the need for protective measures to ensure its survival.
