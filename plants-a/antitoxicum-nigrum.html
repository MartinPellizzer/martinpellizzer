Antitoxicum nigrum

Antitoxicum nigrum, commonly known as black hellebore, is a toxic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of dark red or purple flowers. The plant contains various toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which can be lethal in high doses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in home gardens.
