Anthurium lehmannii

Anthurium lehmannii, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive mottled pattern resembling a leopard's coat. The plant produces an inflorescence called a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium lehmannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.
