Arracacia incisa

Arracacia incisa, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking yellowish-white spathe. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spadices containing numerous tiny berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
