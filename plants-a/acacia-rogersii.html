Acacia rogersii

Acacia rogersii, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. Acacia rogersii produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils and is often found in desert and scrubland habitats.
