Astragalus calycinus var. brevidentatus

Astragalus calycinus var. brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and sagebrush steppe. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of small, oblong leaflets that have short, blunt teeth.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
