Anaphalis sinica var. remota

Anaphalis sinica var. remota, commonly known as the remote anaphalis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, silver-gray foliage and produces small, fluffy flowers that resemble cotton. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
