Allium flavum subsp. flavum

Allium flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features a bulbous stem with a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are widely cultivated for culinary use, valued for their pungent aroma and flavor.

The plant is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
