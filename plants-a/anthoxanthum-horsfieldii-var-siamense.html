Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. siamense

Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. siamense, commonly known as the Siam meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wet meadows.
