Agropyron caninum var. richardsonii

Agropyron caninum var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife and livestock.
