Abelia chinensis var. hanceana

Abelia chinensis var. hanceana, commonly known as the Hance's abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
