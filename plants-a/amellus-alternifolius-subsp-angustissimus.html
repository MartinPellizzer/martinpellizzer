Amellus alternifolius subsp. angustissimus

Amellus alternifolius subsp.

angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed alternateleaf amellus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
