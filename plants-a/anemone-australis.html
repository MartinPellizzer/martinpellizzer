Anemone australis

Anemone australis, commonly known as the New Zealand anemone, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and subalpine areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Anemone australis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
