Artemisia argyrophylla

Artemisia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal.
