Acalypha dupraeana var. sylvicola

Acalypha dupraeana var. sylvicola, commonly known as the forest acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are borne in dense inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces or as a container plant.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
