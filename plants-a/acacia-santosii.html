Acacia santosii

Acacia santosii, commonly known as the Santos acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warmer climates.
