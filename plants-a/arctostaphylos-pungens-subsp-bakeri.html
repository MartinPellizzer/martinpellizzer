Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. bakeri

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
