Abronia carnea

Abronia carnea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Abronia carnea is an important species for pollinators and is considered a symbol of coastal resilience.
