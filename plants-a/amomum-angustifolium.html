Amomum angustifolium

Amomum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of small, pale green flowers that develop into dark brown, fragrant pods. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum angustifolium is also an important species in the family Zingiberaceae, closely related to ginger and turmeric.
