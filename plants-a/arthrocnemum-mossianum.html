Arthrocnemum mossianum

Arthrocnemum mossianum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It thrives in sandy soils and is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, including high temperatures and limited water availability. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Arthrocnemum mossianum plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
