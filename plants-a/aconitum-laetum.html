Aconitum laetum

Aconitum laetum, commonly known as the meadow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
