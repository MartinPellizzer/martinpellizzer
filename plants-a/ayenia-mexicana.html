Ayenia mexicana

Ayenia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy yellow or orange flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ayenia mexicana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure includes narrow, linear leaves and a woody stem, making it a distinctive member of its native flora.
