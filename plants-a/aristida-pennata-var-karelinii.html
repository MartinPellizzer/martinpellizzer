Aristida pennata var. karelinii

Aristida pennata var. karelinii, commonly known as Karelin's false brome, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
