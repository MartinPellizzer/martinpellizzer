Anthospermum crocyllis

Anthospermum crocyllis, commonly known as the golden bell.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
