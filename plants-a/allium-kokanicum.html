Allium kokanicum

Allium kokanicum, commonly known as the Kokanik garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, fleshy bulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are used in local cuisines and have been historically valued for their flavor and medicinal properties.

Allium kokanicum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold climate for optimal growth.
