Allium giganteum

Allium giganteum, commonly known as the giant garlic, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical bulbs and its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Allium giganteum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some culinary applications.
