Allium oreodictyum

Allium oreodictyum, commonly known as the alpine leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
