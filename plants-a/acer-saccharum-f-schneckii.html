Acer saccharum f. schneckii

Acer saccharum f. schneckii, commonly known as the red maple, is a variant of the sugar maple characterized by its distinctive red foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated margin and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree produces samaras, or winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
