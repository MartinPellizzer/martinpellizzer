Ancipitia

Ancipitia, commonly known as the orchid with a unique flower structure.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often showy floral morphology. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers often have specialized structures such as lip petals and column-like stamen, which are adaptations for pollination by specific insect species.

Ancipitia species are found in various tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in moist, shaded environments.
