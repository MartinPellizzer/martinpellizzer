Acacia metrosideriflora

Acacia metrosideriflora, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing numerous flat, winged seeds.

Acacia metrosideriflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
