Anthoxanthum odoratum var. verticillatum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. verticillatum, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright yellow to orange inflorescences that appear in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense, spiral clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic scent and distinctive color make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
