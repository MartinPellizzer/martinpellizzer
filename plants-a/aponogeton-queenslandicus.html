Aponogeton queenslandicus

Aponogeton queenslandicus, commonly known as the Queensland arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Australia.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the substrate, while its roots absorb nutrients from the water.

Aponogeton queenslandicus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.
