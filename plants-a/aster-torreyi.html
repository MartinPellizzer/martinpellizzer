Aster torreyi

Aster torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

Aster torreyi is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in native plant communities.
