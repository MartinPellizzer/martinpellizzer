Astragalus kurdicus

Astragalus kurdicus, commonly known as the Kurdish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus kurdicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
