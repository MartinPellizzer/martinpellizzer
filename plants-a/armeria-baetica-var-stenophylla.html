Armeria baetica var. stenophylla

Armeria baetica var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
