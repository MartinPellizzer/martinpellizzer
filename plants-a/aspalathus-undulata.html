Aspalathus undulata

Aspalathus undulata, commonly known as the Cape tea or African green tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often rolled and used in the production of herbal tea. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment. It is known for its high content of antioxidants and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Aspalathus undulata is also an important species in the fynbos biome, contributing to the region's unique biodiversity.
