Acacia armata var. linearifolia

Acacia armata var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
