Arundinaria shibuyana

Arundinaria shibuyana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria shibuyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
