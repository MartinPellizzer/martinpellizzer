Acer campestre f. ferrugineum

Acer campestre f. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty maple, is a variant of the field maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive rust-colored undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant autumn foliage and unique leaf texture.
