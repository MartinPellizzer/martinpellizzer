Atractocarpus simulans

Atractocarpus simulans, commonly known as the false mastic tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional use.
