Artemisia prilipkoana

Artemisia prilipkoana, commonly known as the Prilipko's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
