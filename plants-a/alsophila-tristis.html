Alsophila tristis

Alsophila tristis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, which helps in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Alsophila tristis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
