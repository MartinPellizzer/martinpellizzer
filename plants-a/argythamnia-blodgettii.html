Argythamnia blodgettii

Argythamnia blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's tamarisk, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
