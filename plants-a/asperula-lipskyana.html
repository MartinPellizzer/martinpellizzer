Asperula lipskyana

Asperula lipskyana, commonly known as the Armenian beauty or mountain azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Asperula lipskyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
