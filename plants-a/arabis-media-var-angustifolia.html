Arabis media var. angustifolia

Arabis media var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient addition to rock gardens.
