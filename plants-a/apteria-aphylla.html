Apteria aphylla

Apteria aphylla, commonly known as the naked leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for both water and nutrients.

It lacks true leaves and stems, instead having a flattened, leaf-like structure called a phyllode that performs photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to various regions in Australia and is often found growing on eucalyptus and acacia trees.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
