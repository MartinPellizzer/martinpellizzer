Adiantum lunulatum var. subjunonicum

Adiantum lunulatum var. subjunonicum, commonly known as the Junonia maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble lace, with small, spoon-shaped pinnae that are often curled at the tips. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is a perennial plant that produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings and indoor cultivation.
