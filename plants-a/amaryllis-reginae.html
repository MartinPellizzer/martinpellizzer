Amaryllis reginae

Amaryllis reginae, commonly known as the Queen of the Amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, and pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis reginae is popular as a flowering houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
