Aerva brachiata var. microstachys

Aerva brachiata var. microstachys, commonly known as the small-stalked horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are typically harvested when dry. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
