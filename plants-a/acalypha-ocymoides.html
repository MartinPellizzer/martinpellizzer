Acalypha ocymoides

Acalypha ocymoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts that can be red or purple in color. Its roots are fibrous and can become invasive, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Acalypha ocymoides is adapted to thrive in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and forest edges.
