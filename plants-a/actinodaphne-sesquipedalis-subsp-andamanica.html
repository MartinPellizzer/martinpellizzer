Actinodaphne sesquipedalis subsp. andamanica

Actinodaphne sesquipedalis subsp.

andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman nut or betel nut, is a member of the Lauraceae family. It is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, which is often used for its nut-like qualities.

The species is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in local communities.
