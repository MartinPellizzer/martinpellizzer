Artemisia murcica

Artemisia murcica, commonly known as Spanish wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Artemisia murcica has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
