Actinea lemmonii

Actinea lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent tentacles and a slender, flexible body. This marine organism typically inhabits rocky substrates in shallow coastal waters. Actinea lemmonii plays a role in the marine ecosystem as both predator and prey.

Its reproductive cycle involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its population dynamics in its habitat.
