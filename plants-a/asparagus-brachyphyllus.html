Asparagus brachyphyllus

Asparagus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces tiny, white flowers in clusters during the spring season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Asparagus brachyphyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
