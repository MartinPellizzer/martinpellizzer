Albuca somersetianum

Albuca somersetianum, commonly known as the Somerset bulb, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous form. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca somersetianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
