Azalea retusa

Azalea retusa, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Azalea retusa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
