Anthurium subcoerulescens

Anthurium subcoerulescens, commonly known as the blue anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, waxy, blue-green foliage and elongated, spadix-like inflorescence that produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.

Anthurium subcoerulescens is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
