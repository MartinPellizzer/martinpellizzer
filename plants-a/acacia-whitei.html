Acacia whitei

Acacia whitei, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular clusters. The leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in regions with limited rainfall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
