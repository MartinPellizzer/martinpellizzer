Aegilops neglecta var. ochreata

Aegilops neglecta var. ochreata, commonly known as the ochre wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a light green to yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
