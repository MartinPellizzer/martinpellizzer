Argyreia populifolia var. thwaitesii

Argyreia populifolia var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for climbing structures or as a ground cover.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
