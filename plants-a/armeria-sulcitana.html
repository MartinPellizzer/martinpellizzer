Armeria sulcitana

Armeria sulcitana, commonly known as the Sicilian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Armeria sulcitana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
