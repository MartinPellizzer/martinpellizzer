Amphilophium chocoense

Amphilophium chocoense, commonly known as the Chocoense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees in the understory of rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
