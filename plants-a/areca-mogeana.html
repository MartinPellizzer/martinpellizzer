Areca mogeana

Areca mogeana, commonly known as the golden areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by edible fruits resembling dates. Areca mogeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
