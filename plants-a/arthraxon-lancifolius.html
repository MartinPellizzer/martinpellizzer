Arthraxon lancifolius

Arthraxon lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
