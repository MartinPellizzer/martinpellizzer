Acacia planifrons

Acacia planifrons, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are flat and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia planifrons is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
