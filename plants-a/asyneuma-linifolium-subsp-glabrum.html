Asyneuma linifolium subsp. glabrum

Asyneuma linifolium subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf asyneuma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its linear, glabrous leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within the Asteraceae family.
