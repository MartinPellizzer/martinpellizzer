Ardisia Apodophylla

Ardisia apodophylla is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It is commonly found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is often used in ornamental gardening. Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it has gained attention among botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
