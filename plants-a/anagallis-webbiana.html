Anagallis webbiana

Anagallis webbiana, commonly known as the webb's pink, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a reddish underside.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
