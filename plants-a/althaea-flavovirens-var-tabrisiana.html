Althaea flavovirens var. tabrisiana

Althaea flavovirens var. tabrisiana, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
