Armeniaca sibirica f. abrupta

Armeniaca sibirica f. abrupta, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a cold-hardy shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its resilience to harsh climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
