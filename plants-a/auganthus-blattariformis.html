Auganthus blattariformis

Auganthus blattariformis, commonly known as the leaf-like auganthus, is a unique plant species characterized by its foliage that resembles broad, flat leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow or orange.

Auganthus blattariformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
