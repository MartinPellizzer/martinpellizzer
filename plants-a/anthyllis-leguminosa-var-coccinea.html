Anthyllis leguminosa var. coccinea

Anthyllis leguminosa var. coccinea, commonly known as the red anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
