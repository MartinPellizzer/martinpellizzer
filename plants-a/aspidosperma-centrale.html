Aspidosperma centrale

Aspidosperma centrale, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has a dense, rounded canopy and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The tree also plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
