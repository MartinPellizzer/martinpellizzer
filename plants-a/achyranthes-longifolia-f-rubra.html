Achyranthes longifolia f. rubra

Achyranthes longifolia f. rubra, commonly known as red foxglove, is a variant of the species Achyranthes longifolia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
