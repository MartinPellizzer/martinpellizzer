Aloe pulcherrima

Aloe pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking, colorful flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a rosette-like structure with segmented, leaf-like stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, orange, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Aloe pulcherrima prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil, thriving in moderate temperatures.
