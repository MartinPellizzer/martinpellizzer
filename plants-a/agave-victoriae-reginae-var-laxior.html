Agave victoriae-reginae var. laxior

Agave victoriae-reginae var. laxior, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a striking member of the Agavaceae family native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

This succulent plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and large, strap-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces dramatic night-blooming flowers, typically in shades of white or pale yellow, which attract moths and other nocturnal pollinators. The plant is relatively slow-growing and requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.
