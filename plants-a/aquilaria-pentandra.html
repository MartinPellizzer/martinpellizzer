Aquilaria pentandra

Aquilaria pentandra, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its unique scent and is used in traditional medicine and incense. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has compound leaves with several leaflets. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a resinous substance called agarwood when infected by certain fungi.
