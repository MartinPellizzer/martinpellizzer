Adenia manganiana

Adenia manganiana, commonly known as the Manganian Adenia, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Adenia manganiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
