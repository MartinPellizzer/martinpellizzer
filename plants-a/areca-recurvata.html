Areca recurvata

Areca recurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf areca, is a tropical palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-winged fruits, each containing a single seed. Areca recurvata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
