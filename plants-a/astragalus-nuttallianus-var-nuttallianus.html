Astragalus nuttallianus var. nuttallianus

Astragalus nuttallianus var. nuttallianus, commonly known as Nuttall's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
