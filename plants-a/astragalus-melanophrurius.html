Astragalus melanophrurius

Astragalus melanophrurius, commonly known as the black-horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in open habitats.
