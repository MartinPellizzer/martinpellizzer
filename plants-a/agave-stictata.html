Agave stictata

Agave stictata, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves that are thick and fleshy, with sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
