Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. tenoreanus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

tenoreanus, commonly known as the Southern African thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
