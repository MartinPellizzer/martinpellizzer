Ardisia novoguineensis

Ardisia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
