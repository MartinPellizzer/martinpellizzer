Andropogon condensatus subsp. elongatus

Andropogon condensatus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

This species typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers. Andropogon condensatus subsp.

elongatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
