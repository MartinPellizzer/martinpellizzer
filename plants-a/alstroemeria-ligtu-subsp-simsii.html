Alstroemeria ligtu subsp. simsii

Alstroemeria ligtu subsp.

simsii, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of blooms along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and horticultural collections.
