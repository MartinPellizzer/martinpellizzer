Afrothismia amietii

Afrothismia amietii, commonly known as the Amiet's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a dry, warm climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.
