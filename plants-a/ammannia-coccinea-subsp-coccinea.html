Ammannia coccinea subsp. coccinea

Ammannia coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features erect, branching stems that are typically red or purple in color, which are characteristic of its subspecies.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Ammannia coccinea subsp.

coccinea thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
