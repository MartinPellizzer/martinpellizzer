Astragalus ceramicus var. imperfectus

Astragalus ceramicus var. imperfectus, commonly known as the imperfect honeyvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
