Achillea capitata

Achillea capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and fern-like, and it produces small, herbaceous stems.

Achillea capitata is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
