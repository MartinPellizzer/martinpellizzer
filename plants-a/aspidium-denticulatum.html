Aspidium denticulatum

Aspidium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed water fern, is a member of the family Blechnaceae.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often covered in small, tooth-like projections. Aspidium denticulatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
