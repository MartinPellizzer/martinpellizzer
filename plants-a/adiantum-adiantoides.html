Adiantum adiantoides

Adiantum adiantoides, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum adiantoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
