Artemisia songarica

Artemisia songarica, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in compact clusters called heads, typical of the sunflower family.

Artemisia songarica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
