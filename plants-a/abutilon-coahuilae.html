Abutilon coahuilae

Abutilon coahuilae, commonly known as the Coahuila Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are yellow or orange, often clustered in terminal panicles. It produces capsules as fruit, which contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
