Astragalus chionobius var. hirtus

Astragalus chionobius var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and for its potential medicinal properties.
