Avenula pratensis var. vasconica

Avenula pratensis var. vasconica, commonly known as the Spanish grass or Vasconian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
