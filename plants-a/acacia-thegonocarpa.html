Acacia thegonocarpa

Acacia thegonocarpa, commonly known as the honey ant tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which enriches the surrounding environment. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia thegonocarpa is also valued for its role in providing food and shelter for various native animals.
