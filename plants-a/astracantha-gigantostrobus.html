Astracantha gigantostrobus

Astracantha gigantostrobus, commonly known as the giant thorned cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. Its dense clusters of spines provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
