Acanthus xiamenensis

Acanthus xiamenensis, commonly known as the Xiamen acanthus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, bracteate inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
