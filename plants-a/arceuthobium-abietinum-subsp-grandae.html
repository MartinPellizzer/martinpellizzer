Arceuthobium abietinum subsp. grandae

Arceuthobium abietinum subsp.

grandae, commonly known as the giant dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily infests coniferous trees such as firs and pines. It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis in its leaves.

The plant has a unique dispersal mechanism, where its seeds are propelled by the explosive release of its capsules when touched. It forms dense clusters on the branches of its host, often leading to reduced growth and vitality in the host tree.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific host requirements, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.
