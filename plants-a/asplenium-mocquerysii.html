Asplenium mocquerysii

Asplenium mocquerysii, commonly known as the Mocquery's spleenwort, is a rare and ancient fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, and often clustered fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or decaying wood in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
