Acrolophus mierghii

Acrolophus mierghii, commonly known as the Persian grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Acrolophus mierghii is adapted to drought conditions and is often used for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
