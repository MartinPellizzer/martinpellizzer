Allium gypsodictyum

Allium gypsodictyum, commonly known as the Gypsum leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, cylindrical flower stalks that bear clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
