Allium pulchellum var. pallens

Allium pulchellum var. pallens, commonly known as the pale onion or white garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale to white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and ornamental value.
