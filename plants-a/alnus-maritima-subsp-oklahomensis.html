Alnus maritima subsp. oklahomensis

Alnus maritima subsp.

oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are typical of the alder genus. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil stabilization and ecological restoration efforts.
