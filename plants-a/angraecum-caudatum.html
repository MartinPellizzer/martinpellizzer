Angraecum caudatum

Angraecum caudatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its unique floral structure, with a long, narrow spur, is adapted for pollination by specific moths.

Angraecum caudatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
