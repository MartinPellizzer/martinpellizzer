Amelanchier canadensis var. fernaldii

Amelanchier canadensis var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically turning from red to dark purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
