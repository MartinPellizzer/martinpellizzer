Adenophora paniculata var. dentata

Adenophora paniculata var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name "dentata.". The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
