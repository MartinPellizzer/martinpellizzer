Alcea rugosa

Alcea rugosa, commonly known as the hairy hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are palmately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Alcea rugosa is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of fiber.
