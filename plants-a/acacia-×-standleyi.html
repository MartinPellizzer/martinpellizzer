Acacia × standleyi

Acacia × standleyi, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a hybrid shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and long, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including fuel and construction.

Acacia × standleyi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
