Amomum borealiborneense

Amomum borealiborneense, commonly known as the northern cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark brown, ellipsoid fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal value.
