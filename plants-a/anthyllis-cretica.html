Anthyllis cretica

Anthyllis cretica, commonly known as creeping anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Anthyllis cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
