Allium tricoccum var. burdickii

Allium tricoccum var. burdickii, commonly known as Burdick's ramps, is a variant of the wild leek native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and pale green, bulbous stems that emerge in early spring. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique genetic variation and is closely related to other Allium species such as A. tricoccum and A. canadense.
