Asperula purpurea

Asperula purpurea, commonly known as purple woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Asperula purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
