Abies hickelii var. oaxacana

Abies hickelii var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca fir, is a variant of the Mexican fir native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain seeds surrounded by a winged aril. It thrives in cool, moist environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
