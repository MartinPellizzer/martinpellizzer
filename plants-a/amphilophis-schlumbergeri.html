Amphilophis schlumbergeri

Amphilophis schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Schlumbergera truncata, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in a variety of colors, typically during the winter months. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and blooming habits make it a favorite among succulent enthusiasts.
