Atriplex laciniata var. americana

Atriplex laciniata var. americana, commonly known as American saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing coastal and desert landscapes.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
