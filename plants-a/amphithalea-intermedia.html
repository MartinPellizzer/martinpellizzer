Amphithalea intermedia

Amphithalea intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Amphithalea intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
