Acer dasycarpum subvar. albomaculatum

Acer dasycarpum subvar. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are often adorned with white or pale yellow spots. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that have elongated wings. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

The white-spotted maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
