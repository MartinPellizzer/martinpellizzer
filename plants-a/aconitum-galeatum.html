Aconitum galeatum

Aconitum galeatum, commonly known as the meadow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Aconitum galeatum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
