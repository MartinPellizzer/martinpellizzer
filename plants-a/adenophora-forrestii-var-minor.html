Adenophora forrestii var. minor

Adenophora forrestii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser forest bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
