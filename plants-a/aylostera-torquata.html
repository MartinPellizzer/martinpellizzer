Aylostera torquata

Aylostera torquata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aylostera torquata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
