Antennaria dioiciformis subsp. dioiciformis

Antennaria dioiciformis subsp.

dioiciformis, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically hairy and lobed.

The plant is characterized by its dioecious nature, meaning male and female flowers are found on separate plants. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
