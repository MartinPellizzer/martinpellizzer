Acacia ricoae

Acacia ricoae, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Acacia ricoae is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native animals.
