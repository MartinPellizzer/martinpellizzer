Anacampta angulata

Anacampta angulata, commonly known as the angular anacampta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from red to purple, attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Anacampta angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
