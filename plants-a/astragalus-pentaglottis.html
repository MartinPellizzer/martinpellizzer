Astragalus pentaglottis

Astragalus pentaglottis, commonly known as the five-voiced milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pentaglottis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
