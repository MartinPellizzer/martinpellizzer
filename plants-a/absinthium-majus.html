Absinthium majus

Absinthium majus, commonly known as greater wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and wastelands.

Absinthium majus has been historically used in herbal medicine and is related to the plant used in the production of absinthe.
