Aster elliptica

Aster elliptica, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
