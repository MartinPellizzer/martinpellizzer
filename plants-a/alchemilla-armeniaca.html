Alchemilla armeniaca

Alchemilla armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla armeniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
