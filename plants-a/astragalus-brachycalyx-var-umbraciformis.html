Astragalus brachycalyx var. umbraciformis

Astragalus brachycalyx var. umbraciformis, commonly known as the shade-forming milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riparian areas. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to access groundwater in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to its specific ecological niche.
