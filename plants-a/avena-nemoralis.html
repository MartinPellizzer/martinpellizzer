Avena nemoralis

Avena nemoralis, commonly known as common oat, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Avena nemoralis is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
