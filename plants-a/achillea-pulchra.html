Achillea pulchra

Achillea pulchra, commonly known as the showy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rugged terrain.

Achillea pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
