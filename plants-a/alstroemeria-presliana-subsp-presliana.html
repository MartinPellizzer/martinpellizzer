Alstroemeria presliana subsp. presliana

Alstroemeria presliana subsp.

presliana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
