Alsophila paleolata var. nigrescens

Alsophila paleolata var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that darkens with age, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
