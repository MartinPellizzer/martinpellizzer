Astragalus turrillii

Astragalus turrillii, commonly known as the Turrill's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus turrillii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
