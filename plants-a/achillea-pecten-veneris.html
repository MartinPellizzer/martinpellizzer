Achillea pecten-veneris

Achillea pecten-veneris, commonly known as Venus' comb, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its finely divided, fern-like leaves and its showy, yellow or orange flower heads that resemble a comb, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant adds to its botanical significance.
