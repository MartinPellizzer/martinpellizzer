Artemisia tangutica var. tangutica

Artemisia tangutica var. tangutica, commonly known as Tangut wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in arid, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various ailments.
