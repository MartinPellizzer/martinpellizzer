Acacia declinata

Acacia declinata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia declinata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
