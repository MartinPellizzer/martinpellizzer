Anemone obtusiloba var. rotundifolia

Anemone obtusiloba var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features basal leaves that are round to ovate with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five to seven petals. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a cluster of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
