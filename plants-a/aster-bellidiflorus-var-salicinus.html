Aster bellidiflorus var. salicinus

Aster bellidiflorus var. salicinus, commonly known as the Salicin aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, typically in shades of purple and white. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
