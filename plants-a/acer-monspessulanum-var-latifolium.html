Acer monspessulanum var. latifolium

Acer monspessulanum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its distinctive foliage and seasonal color changes make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
