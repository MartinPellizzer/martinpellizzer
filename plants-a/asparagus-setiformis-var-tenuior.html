Asparagus setiformis var. tenuior

Asparagus setiformis var. tenuior, commonly known as the narrowleaf asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is closely related to other asparagus species.
