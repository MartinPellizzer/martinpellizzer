Alchemilla arvensis var. glabra

Alchemilla arvensis var. glabra, commonly known as smooth field lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy, and often hairy leaves that are smooth to the touch in this variety. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in damp meadows and gardens.
