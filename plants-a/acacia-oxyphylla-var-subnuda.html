Acacia oxyphylla var. subnuda

Acacia oxyphylla var. subnuda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow floral spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus and attract a variety of pollinators. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
