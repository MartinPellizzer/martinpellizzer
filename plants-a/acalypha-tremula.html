Acalypha tremula

Acalypha tremula, commonly known as the trembling mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the summer months. Acalypha tremula thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
