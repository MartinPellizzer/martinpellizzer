Adenia scheffleri

Adenia scheffleri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has thick, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths.

Adenia scheffleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
