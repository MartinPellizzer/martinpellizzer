Agave expansa

Agave expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its robust structure and ability to store water make it a hardy plant adapted to harsh conditions.
