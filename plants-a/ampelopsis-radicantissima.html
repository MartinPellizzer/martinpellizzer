Ampelopsis radicantissima

Ampelopsis radicantissima, commonly known as the creeping grapevines, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. Its fruits are small, blue-black berries that appear in late summer.

Ampelopsis radicantissima is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
