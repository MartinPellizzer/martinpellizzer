Aster macrophyllus var. excelsior

Aster macrophyllus var. excelsior, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the stems are upright and hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
