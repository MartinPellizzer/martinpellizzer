Arundinella hirta var. kutcharoensis

Arundinella hirta var. kutcharoensis, commonly known as the Kutch grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This species is often used in traditional practices and has potential ecological and agricultural applications.
