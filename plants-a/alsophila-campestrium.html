Alsophila campestrium

Alsophila campestrium, commonly known as the field huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its creeping rhizome and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are linear, arranged in two ranks, and often have a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
