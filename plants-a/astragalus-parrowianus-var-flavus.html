Astragalus parrowianus var. flavus

Astragalus parrowianus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture in arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
