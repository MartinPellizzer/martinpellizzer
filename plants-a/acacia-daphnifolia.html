Acacia daphnifolia

Acacia daphnifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia daphnifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
