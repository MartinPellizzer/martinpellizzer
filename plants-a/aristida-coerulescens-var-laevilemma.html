Aristida coerulescens var. laevilemma

Aristida coerulescens var. laevilemma, commonly known as the bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or silver flowers, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dryland gardens and erosion control.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
