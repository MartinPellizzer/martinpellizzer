Acacia basedowii

Acacia basedowii, commonly known as the West African acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, savannah-like environments. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, making it visually distinctive.

Acacia basedowii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
