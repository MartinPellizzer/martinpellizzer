Amorpha fruticosa var. fragrans

Amorpha fruticosa var. fragrans, commonly known as sweetcarpet or fragrant bush clover, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
