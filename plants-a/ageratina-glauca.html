Ageratina glauca

Ageratina glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves and fluffy, blue-white flower heads that resemble mist. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flowers. Ageratina glauca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
