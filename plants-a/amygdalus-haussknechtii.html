Amygdalus haussknechtii

Amygdalus haussknechtii, commonly known as the Caucasian almond, is a species of shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, stony seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
