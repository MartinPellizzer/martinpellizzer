Ageratina celestini

Ageratina celestini, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ageratina celestini is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
