Asparagus scoparius var. plocamoides

Asparagus scoparius var. plocamoides, commonly known as the clustered asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
