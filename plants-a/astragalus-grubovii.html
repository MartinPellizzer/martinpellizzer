Astragalus grubovii

Astragalus grubovii, commonly known as the Grubov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in early summer. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus grubovii is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
