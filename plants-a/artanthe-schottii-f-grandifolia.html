Artanthe schottii f. grandifolia

Artanthe schottii f. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically bright red or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in various regions for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
