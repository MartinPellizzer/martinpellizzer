Astragalus clandestinus

Astragalus clandestinus, commonly known as the hidden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus clandestinus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
