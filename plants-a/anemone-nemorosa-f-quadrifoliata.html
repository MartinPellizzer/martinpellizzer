Anemone nemorosa f. quadrifoliata

Anemone nemorosa f. quadrifoliata, commonly known as the four-leaf anemone, is a variant of the wood anemone.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover-like foliage, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

This form is a natural variation that showcases the genetic diversity within the Anemone genus.
