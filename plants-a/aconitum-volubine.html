Aconitum volubine

Aconitum volubine, commonly known as the climbing aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, often branched stems that climb using tendrils. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
