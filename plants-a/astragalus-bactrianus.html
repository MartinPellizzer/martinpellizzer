Astragalus bactrianus

Astragalus bactrianus, commonly known as the Bactrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Mongolia and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-shaped flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
