Aerva timoriensis

Aerva timoriensis, commonly known as Timor grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of greenish-white flowers.

Aerva timoriensis is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
