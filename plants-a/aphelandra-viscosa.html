Aphelandra viscosa

Aphelandra viscosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that range from yellow to red. Aphelandra viscosa thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
