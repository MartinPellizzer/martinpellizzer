Aegiphila perplexa

Aegiphila perplexa, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
