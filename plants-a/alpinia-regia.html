Alpinia regia

Alpinia regia, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like true ginger and turmeric. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the year in suitable climates. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Alpinia regia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
