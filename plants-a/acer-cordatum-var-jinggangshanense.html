Acer cordatum var. jinggangshanense

Acer cordatum var. jinggangshanense, commonly known as the Jinggangshan maple, is a variant of the heart-shaped maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and moderate climates. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
