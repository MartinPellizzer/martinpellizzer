Asperula hirsuta glabrescens

Asperula hirsuta glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that become glabrous as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are used in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
