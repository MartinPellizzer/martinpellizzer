Arctous rubra

Arctous rubra, commonly known as the red mountain-mahogany, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Arctous rubra thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
