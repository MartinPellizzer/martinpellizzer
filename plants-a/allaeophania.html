Allaeophania

Allaeophania, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, white, fragrant flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Allaeophania is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many areas.
