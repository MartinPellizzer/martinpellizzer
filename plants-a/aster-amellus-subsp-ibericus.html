Aster amellus subsp. ibericus

Aster amellus subsp.

ibericus, commonly known as the Iberian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, displaying purple or pinkish hues, and are visited by various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth compared to other Aster amellus varieties.
