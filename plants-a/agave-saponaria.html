Agave saponaria

Agave saponaria, commonly known as soap agave, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called bloom. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have sharp, spiny tips. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
