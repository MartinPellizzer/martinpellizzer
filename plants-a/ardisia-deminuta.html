Ardisia deminuta

Ardisia deminuta, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ardisia deminuta thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
