Anisopappus buchwaldii

Anisopappus buchwaldii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry conditions.
