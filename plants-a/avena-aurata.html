Avena aurata

Avena aurata, commonly known as the golden oat or golden grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Avena aurata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
