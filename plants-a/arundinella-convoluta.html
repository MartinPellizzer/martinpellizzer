Arundinella convoluta

Arundinella convoluta, commonly known as the curly reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical growth habit and its ability to form mats or clumps. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Arundinella convoluta produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, contributing to its ornamental value in certain landscapes.
