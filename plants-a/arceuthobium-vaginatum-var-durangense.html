Arceuthobium vaginatum var. durangense

Arceuthobium vaginatum var. durangense, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of grasses or other flowering plants. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a unique seed dispersal mechanism involving explosive seed pods. It is native to Mexico and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant interactions and ecological dependencies.
