Arenaria tatewakii

Arenaria tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Arenaria tatewakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
