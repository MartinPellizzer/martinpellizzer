Artemisia campestris

Artemisia campestris, commonly known as yellow wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Artemisia campestris is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
