Anemone gayana

Anemone gayana, commonly known as the mountain anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive central yellow cone, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Anemone gayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
