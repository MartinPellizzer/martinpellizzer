Asplenium violascens

Asplenium violascens, commonly known as the violet holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental horticulture.
