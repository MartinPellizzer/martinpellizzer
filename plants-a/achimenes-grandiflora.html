Achimenes grandiflora

Achimenes grandiflora, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes grandiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
