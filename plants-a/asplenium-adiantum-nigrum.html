Asplenium adiantum-nigrum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or shaded forest floors. The plant has a distinctive appearance with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum is often found in temperate regions and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
