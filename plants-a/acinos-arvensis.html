Acinos arvensis

Acinos arvensis, commonly known as the field dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, typically appearing in clusters. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
