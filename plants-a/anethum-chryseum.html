Anethum chryseum

Anethum chryseum, commonly known as the golden anise, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Anethum chryseum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
