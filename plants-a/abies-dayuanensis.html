Abies dayuanensis

Abies dayuanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically containing two seeds. Abies dayuanensis thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
