Alchemilla gerrardii var. hirsutopetiolata

Alchemilla gerrardii var. hirsutopetiolata, commonly known as the hirsute-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hirsute (hairy) leaves that are petiolate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
