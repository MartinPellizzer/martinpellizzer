Alchemilla vulgaris var. sylvestris

Alchemilla vulgaris var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are shield-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.
