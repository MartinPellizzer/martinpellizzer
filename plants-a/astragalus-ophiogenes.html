Astragalus ophiogenes

Astragalus ophiogenes, commonly known as the snake's head milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus ophiogenes plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
