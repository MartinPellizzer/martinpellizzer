Aristida pallens var. major

Aristida pallens var. major, commonly known as the tall dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest to landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant gardens.
