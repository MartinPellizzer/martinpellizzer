Artemisia × gayeriana

Artemisia × gayeriana, commonly known as mugwort or sweet wormwood, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-green, finely divided leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Artemisia × gayeriana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its essential oils contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
