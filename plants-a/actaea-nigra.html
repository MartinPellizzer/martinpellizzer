Actaea nigra

Actaea nigra, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear opposite, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Actaea nigra thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
