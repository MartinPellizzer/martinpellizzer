Artemisia sinanensis

Artemisia sinanensis, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are visited by various pollinators.

Artemisia sinanensis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
