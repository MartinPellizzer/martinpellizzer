Anagallis iraruensis

Anagallis iraruensis, commonly known as the Iraru's pink, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Anagallis iraruensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
