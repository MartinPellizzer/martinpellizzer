Asphodelus cerasifer f. comosus

Asphodelus cerasifer f. comosus, commonly known as the clustered cherry asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to southern Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and prolonged blooming period.
