Amorphophallus hirtus var. kiusianus

Amorphophallus hirtus var. kiusianus, commonly known as the Kiusi elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to considerable sizes. The plant produces a large, umbrella-shaped leaf with long, narrow segments. It blooms with an inflorescence that resembles a spadix, often accompanied by a large, showy bract.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
