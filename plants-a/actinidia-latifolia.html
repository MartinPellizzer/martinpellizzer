Actinidia latifolia

Actinidia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are typically broad and have a smooth texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
