Aconitum sanyoense var. edentatum

Aconitum sanyoense var. edentatum, commonly known as the Sanyoense Monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive purple underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
