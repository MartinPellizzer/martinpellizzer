Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. lactiflora

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

lactiflora, commonly known as the milk-pot bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are generally erect and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
