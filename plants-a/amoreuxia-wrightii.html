Amoreuxia wrightii

Amoreuxia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Amoreuxia wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
