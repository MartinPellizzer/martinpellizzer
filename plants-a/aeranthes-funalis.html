Aeranthes funalis

Aeranthes funalis, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Aeranthes funalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
