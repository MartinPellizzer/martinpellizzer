Amelanchier humilis var. typica

Amelanchier humilis var. typica, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a yellowish-red color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
