Amaryllis lapacensis

Amaryllis lapacensis, commonly known as the Lapacensis amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears multiple blooms, often in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Amaryllis lapacensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
