Adiantum wilesianum

Adiantum wilesianum, commonly known as the Wilesian maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that resemble the wings of a bird. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost translucent appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Adiantum wilesianum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
