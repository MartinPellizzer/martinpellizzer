Andropogon coeruleus

Andropogon coeruleus, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into fluffy, white to pale blue seed heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats.

Andropogon coeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
