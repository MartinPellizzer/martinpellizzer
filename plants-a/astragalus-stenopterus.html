Astragalus stenopterus

Astragalus stenopterus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus stenopterus is often found in grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky soils.
