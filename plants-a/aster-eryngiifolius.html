Aster eryngiifolius

Aster eryngiifolius, commonly known as the thistle-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that resemble those of thistles, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Its foliage and flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
