Arundinaria amara

Arundinaria amara, commonly known as sweet bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Arundinaria amara is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
