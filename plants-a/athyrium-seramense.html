Athyrium seramense

Athyrium seramense, commonly known as the Seram fern, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Seram.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Athyrium seramense is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
