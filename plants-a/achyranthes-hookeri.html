Achyranthes hookeri

Achyranthes hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its seed capsules are three-angled and contain small, flat seeds.

Achyranthes hookeri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
