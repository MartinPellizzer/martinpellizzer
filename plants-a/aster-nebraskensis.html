Aster nebraskensis

Aster nebraskensis, commonly known as the Nebraska aster, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, open prairies and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features erect, branching stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its showy, purple to blue-purple flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs.
