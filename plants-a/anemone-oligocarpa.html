Anemone oligocarpa

Anemone oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a distinct white flower cluster that blooms in early spring. Its flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a circular pattern, surrounded by a cluster of yellow stamens.

Anemone oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
