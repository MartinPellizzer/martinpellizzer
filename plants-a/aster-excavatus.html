Aster excavatus

Aster excavatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple or blue flower heads composed of many small florets, with a distinctive central disk. It typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Aster excavatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
