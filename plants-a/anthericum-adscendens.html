Anthericum adscendens

Anthericum adscendens, commonly known as the climbing arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize moist environments effectively. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Anthericum adscendens thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.
