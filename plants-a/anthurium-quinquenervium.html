Anthurium quinquenervium

Anthurium quinquenervium, commonly known as the Five-Nerved Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, a feature typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium quinquenervium is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
