Amaryllis fulvovirens

Amaryllis fulvovirens, commonly known as the yellow bell amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Amaryllis fulvovirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
