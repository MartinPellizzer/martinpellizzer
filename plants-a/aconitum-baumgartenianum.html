Aconitum baumgartenianum

Aconitum baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Baumgarten's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are hairy and often have a waxy coating.

Aconitum baumgartenianum is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

Morphology

Aconitum baumgartenianum has large, compound leaves that are palmately lobed, dark green in color, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is tall, cylindrical, and covered with a rough, bark-like texture, typically ranging from gray to brown in color. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, and have a spurred structure, blooming in late summer to early autumn, and are pollinated by insects such as bees.
