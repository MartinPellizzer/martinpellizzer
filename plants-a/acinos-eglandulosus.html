Acinos eglandulosus

Acinos eglandulosus, commonly known as the leafy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with basal leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and is characterized by its five-lobed leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members such as roses and strawberries.
