Aconitum variegatum subsp. pyrenaicum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmately lobed, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or yellow veining. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
