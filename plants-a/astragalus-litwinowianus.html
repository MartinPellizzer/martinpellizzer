Astragalus litwinowianus

Astragalus litwinowianus, commonly known as the Litwinowian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health purposes.
