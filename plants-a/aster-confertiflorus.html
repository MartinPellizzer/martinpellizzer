Aster confertiflorus

Aster confertiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
