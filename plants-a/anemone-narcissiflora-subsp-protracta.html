Anemone narcissiflora subsp. protracta

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

protracta, commonly known as the protracta anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona that resembles the petals of a daffodil.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodlands or rocky slopes.

The subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
