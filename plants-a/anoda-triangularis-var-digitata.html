Anoda triangularis var. digitata

Anoda triangularis var. digitata, commonly known as the narrowleaf horsegram, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
