Adesmia littoralis

Adesmia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal legume, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Adesmia littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.
