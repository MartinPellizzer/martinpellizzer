Aster sherriffianus

Aster sherriffianus, commonly known as the Arizona aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster sherriffianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
