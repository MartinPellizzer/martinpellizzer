Andropogon consanguineus

Andropogon consanguineus, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its striking purple tassels that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon consanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wildlife habitat and erosion control.
