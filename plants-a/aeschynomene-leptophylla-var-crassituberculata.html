Aeschynomene leptophylla var. crassituberculata

Aeschynomene leptophylla var. crassituberculata, commonly known as the thick-celled aeschynomene, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its potential in soil improvement and as a forage crop.
