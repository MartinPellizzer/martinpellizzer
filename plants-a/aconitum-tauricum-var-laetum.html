Aconitum tauricum var. laetum

Aconitum tauricum var. laetum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive helmet-shaped sepals. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
