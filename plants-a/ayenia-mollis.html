Ayenia mollis

Ayenia mollis, commonly known as the soft ayenia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ayenia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the genus Ayenia.
