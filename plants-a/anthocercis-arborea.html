Anthocercis arborea

Anthocercis arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Anthocercis arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
