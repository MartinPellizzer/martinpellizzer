Allophylus dummeri

Allophylus dummeri, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy in appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Allophylus dummeri is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in mild climates.
