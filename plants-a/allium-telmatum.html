Allium telmatum

Allium telmatum, commonly known as the marsh garlic, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its botanical features make it well adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Allium telmatum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
