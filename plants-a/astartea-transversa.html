Astartea transversa

Astartea transversa, commonly known as the Transverse Astartea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Astartaceae and is characterized by its unique, small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface, adapting to its humid habitat.

Astartea transversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
