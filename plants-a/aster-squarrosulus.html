Aster squarrosulus

Aster squarrosulus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
