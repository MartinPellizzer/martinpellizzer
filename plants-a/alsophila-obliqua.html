Alsophila obliqua

Alsophila obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that often curve in a zigzag pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in rainforests. Its rhizome produces roots that anchor it to the soil, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Alsophila obliqua plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
