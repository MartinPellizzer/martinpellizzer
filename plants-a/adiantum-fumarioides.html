Adiantum fumarioides

Adiantum fumarioides, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum fumarioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
