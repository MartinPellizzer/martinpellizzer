Anthurium spathiphyllum

Anthurium spathiphyllum, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking, spath-shaped inflorescences that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium spathiphyllum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
