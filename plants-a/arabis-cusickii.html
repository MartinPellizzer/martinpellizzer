Arabis cusickii

Arabis cusickii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, particularly in California and Nevada. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
