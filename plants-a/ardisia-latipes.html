Ardisia latipes

Ardisia latipes, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia latipes is valued for its ornamental berries and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
