Atriplex lindleyi subsp. quadripartita

Atriplex lindleyi subsp.

quadripartita, commonly known as the four-parted saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in salty, dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
