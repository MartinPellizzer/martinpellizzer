Apinagia aripecuruensis

Apinagia aripecuruensis, commonly known as the Peruvian yew, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of northern Peru.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits. The plant belongs to the family Taxaceae, which includes other yew species known for their medicinal and toxic properties. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest to pharmacological research.
