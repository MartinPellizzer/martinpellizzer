Andropogon foliatus

Andropogon foliatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon foliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
