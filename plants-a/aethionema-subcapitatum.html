Aethionema subcapitatum

Aethionema subcapitatum, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in compact clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
