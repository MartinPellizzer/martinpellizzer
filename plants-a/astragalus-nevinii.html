Astragalus nevinii

Astragalus nevinii, commonly known as Nevin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
