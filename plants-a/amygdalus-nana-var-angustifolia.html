Amygdalus nana var. angustifolia

Amygdalus nana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dwarf almond, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible, sweet, almond-like seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early blooming habit.
