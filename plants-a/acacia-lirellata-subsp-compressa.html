Acacia lirellata subsp. compressa

Acacia lirellata subsp.

compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
