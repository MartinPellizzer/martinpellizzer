Agave gracilispina

Agave gracilispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are adorned with sharp, spiny margins, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
