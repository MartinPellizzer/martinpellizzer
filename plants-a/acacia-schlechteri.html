Acacia schlechteri

Acacia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is adapted to sandy soils, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
