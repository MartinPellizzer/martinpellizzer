Astrocarpa purpurascens

Astrocarpa purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its stems are typically succulent and can store water, an adaptation to its often arid habitat.

The plant contains latex, which is a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family and can be toxic if ingested.
