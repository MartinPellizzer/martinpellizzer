Aira alba

Aira alba, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-white foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aira alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and soil stabilization.
