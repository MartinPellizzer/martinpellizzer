Atylosia rugosa

Atylosia rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue during the summer months. Atylosia rugosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
