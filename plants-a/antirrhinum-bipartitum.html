Antirrhinum bipartitum

Antirrhinum bipartitum, commonly known as the two-colored snapdragon, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with two distinct color zones, typically red and yellow, which give it its common name. The plant has opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves and produces elongated, sparsely branched flower spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
