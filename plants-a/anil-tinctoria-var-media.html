Anil tinctoria var. media

Anil tinctoria var. media, commonly known as the dwarf indigo plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
