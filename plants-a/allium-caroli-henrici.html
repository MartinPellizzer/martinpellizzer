Allium caroli-henrici

Allium caroli-henrici, commonly known as the Caroli-Henrici garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are aromatic and can be used in culinary applications.

Allium caroli-henrici prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
