Anthemis ormenioides

Anthemis ormenioides, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a white central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is known for its aromatic properties. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
