Althaea calvertii

Althaea calvertii, commonly known as the Maryland marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its roots produce a mucilaginous substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Althaea calvertii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland habitats.
