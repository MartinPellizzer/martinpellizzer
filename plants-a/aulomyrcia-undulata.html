Aulomyrcia undulata

Aulomyrcia undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf patterns. The plant features a stout, unbranched stem that remains underground, supporting a crown of large, pinnate leaves. Aulomyrcia undulata is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
