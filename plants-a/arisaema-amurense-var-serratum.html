Arisaema amurense var. serratum

Arisaema amurense var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated Chinese arum, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces arrow-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are typically greenish-yellow, with a spadix that bears both male and female flowers.
