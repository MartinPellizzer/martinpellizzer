Archidendron whitei

Archidendron whitei, commonly known as the White's legume, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine in its native region.
