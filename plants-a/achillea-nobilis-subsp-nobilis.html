Achillea nobilis subsp. nobilis

Achillea nobilis subsp.

nobilis, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of gray-green, aromatic leaves and produces dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong, pleasant fragrance and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal uses throughout history.

The subspecies nobilis is distinguished by its more compact growth and slightly smaller flowers compared to other subspecies.
