Annona amplexicaulis

Annona amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish-white fruits that ripen to a yellow or orange color.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, creamy flavor, making it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
