Abutilon indicum

Abutilon indicum, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the summer months. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Abutilon indicum is also valued for its fibrous stem, which has been used in traditional crafts and textile production.
