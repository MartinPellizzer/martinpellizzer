Athyrium ponapense

Athyrium ponapense, commonly known as the Ponape Japanese fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Micronesia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Athyrium ponapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
