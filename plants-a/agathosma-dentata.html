Agathosma dentata

Agathosma dentata, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its distinctive scent and medicinal properties make it a notable species in its native region.
