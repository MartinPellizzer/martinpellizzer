Annona arenaria var. obtusa

Annona arenaria var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves that are typically broader than they are long. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, yellowish fruits with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The fruit has a soft, creamy pulp and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
