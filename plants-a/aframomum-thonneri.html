Aframomum thonneri

Aframomum thonneri, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are valued for their spicy, aromatic flavor and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal importance.
