Allium oreophilum var. ostrowskianum

Allium oreophilum var. ostrowskianum, commonly known as the Ostrowski's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
