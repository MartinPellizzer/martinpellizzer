Asplenium stenochlaenoides

Asplenium stenochlaenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a dense layer of rhizome scales.

Asplenium stenochlaenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
