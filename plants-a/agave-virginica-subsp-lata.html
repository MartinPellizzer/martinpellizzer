Agave virginica subsp. lata

Agave virginica subsp.

lata, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, typically blooming once in its lifetime. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its robust growth and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
