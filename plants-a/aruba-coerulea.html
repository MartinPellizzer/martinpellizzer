Aruba coerulea

Aruba coerulea, commonly known as the blue arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant blue and white inflorescence.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a glossy surface that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Aruba coerulea is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
