Asperula aristata f. vulgaris

Asperula aristata f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common wood rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
