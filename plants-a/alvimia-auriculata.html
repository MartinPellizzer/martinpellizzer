Alvimia auriculata

Alvimia auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alvimia auriculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
