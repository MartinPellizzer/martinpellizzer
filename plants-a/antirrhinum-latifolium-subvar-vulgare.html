Antirrhinum latifolium subvar. vulgare

Antirrhinum latifolium subvar. vulgare, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Antirrhinum latifolium subvar. vulgare is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
