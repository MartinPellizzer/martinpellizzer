Astragalus henryi

Astragalus henryi, commonly known as Henry's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus henryi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its potential medicinal properties.
