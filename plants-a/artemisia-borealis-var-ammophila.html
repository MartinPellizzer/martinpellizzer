Artemisia borealis var. ammophila

Artemisia borealis var. ammophila, commonly known as the coastal wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy, coastal environments and is often found in tundra and dunes. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize coastal soils.

Its essential oils contribute to its strong scent and have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.
