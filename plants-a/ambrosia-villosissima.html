Ambrosia villosissima

Ambrosia villosissima, commonly known as the woolly ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Ambrosiaceae family and is known for its allergenic pollen, which can contribute to seasonal allergies.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential nuisance in agricultural settings.
