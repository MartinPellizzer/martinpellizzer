Acacia ficoides

Acacia ficoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia ficoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
