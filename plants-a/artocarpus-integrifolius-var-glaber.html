Artocarpus integrifolius var. glaber

Artocarpus integrifolius var. glaber, commonly known as breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The fruit is rich in carbohydrates and has been a staple food in many island cultures for centuries. Its botanical features include a smooth, bark-like rind and a starchy, edible pulp.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental value and has various traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
