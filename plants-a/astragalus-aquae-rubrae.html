Astragalus aquae-rubrae

Astragalus aquae-rubrae, commonly known as the red water milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, saline soils, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus aquae-rubrae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
