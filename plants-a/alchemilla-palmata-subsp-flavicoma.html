Alchemilla palmata subsp. flavicoma

Alchemilla palmata subsp.

flavicoma, commonly known as golden-haired lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
