Asplenium papaverifolium

Asplenium papaverifolium, commonly known as the poppy-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that resemble the petals of a poppy, giving it its common name. Native to the southeastern United States, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant's intricate frond structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and conservation efforts.
