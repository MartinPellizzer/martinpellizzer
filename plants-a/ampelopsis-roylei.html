Ampelopsis roylei

Ampelopsis roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of blue-black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Ampelopsis roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
