Asplenium auritum var. sulcatum

Asplenium auritum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the sulcate maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often arching or drooping. The leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, serrated or toothed margin. This fern prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
