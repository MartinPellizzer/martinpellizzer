Anogeissus pendula

Anogeissus pendula, commonly known as the hanging tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of tannins.
