Anthyllis apennina

Anthyllis apennina, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Anthyllis apennina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
