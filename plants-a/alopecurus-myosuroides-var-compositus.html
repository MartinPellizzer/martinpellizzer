Alopecurus myosuroides var. compositus

Alopecurus myosuroides var. compositus, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of tiny white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as lawns, fields, and along pathways. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which can suppress other vegetation.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
