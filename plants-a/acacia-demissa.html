Acacia demissa

Acacia demissa, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
