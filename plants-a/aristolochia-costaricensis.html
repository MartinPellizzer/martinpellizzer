Aristolochia costaricensis

Aristolochia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a hooded structure. The plant produces small, green berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, white pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
