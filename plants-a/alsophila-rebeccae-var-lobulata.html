Alsophila rebeccae var. lobulata

Alsophila rebeccae var. lobulata, commonly known as the Rebeccae's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.
