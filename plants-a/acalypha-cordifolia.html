Acalypha cordifolia

Acalypha cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its growth habit is usually herbaceous, with some varieties exhibiting semi-shrubby characteristics.

Acalypha cordifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
