Aspilia apensis

Aspilia apensis, commonly known as the African torch or red torch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange inflorescences that resemble torches, which are composed of numerous small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Aspilia apensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
