Angelica sinuata

Angelica sinuata, commonly known as the western angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, compound leaves with deeply notched or sinuate margins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences.

Angelica sinuata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
