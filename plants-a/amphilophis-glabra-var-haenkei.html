Amphilophis glabra var. haenkei

Amphilophis glabra var. haenkei, commonly known as the smooth amphilophis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wetland environments.
