Acomastylis arapahoensis

Acomastylis arapahoensis, commonly known as the Arapaho dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, oval seeds.

Acomastylis arapahoensis is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
