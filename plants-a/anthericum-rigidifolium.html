Anthericum rigidifolium

Anthericum rigidifolium, commonly known as rigid-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its stiff, upright leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a feature common in the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum rigidifolium is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it thrives in humid conditions.
