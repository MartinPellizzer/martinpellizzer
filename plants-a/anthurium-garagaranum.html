Anthurium garagaranum

Anthurium garagaranum, commonly known as the black anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves that contrast beautifully with its bright red spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium garagaranum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
