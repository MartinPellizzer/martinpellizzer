Alkanna subleiocarpa

Alkanna subleiocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red alkanna, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces distinctive red or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and a unique, aromatic resin that exudes from its bark. Alkanna subleiocarpa is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
