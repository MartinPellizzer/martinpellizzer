Adiantum convolutum

Adiantum convolutum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum convolutum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its graceful, fern-like foliage.
