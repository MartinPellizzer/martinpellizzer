Alyssum clausonis

Alyssum clausonis, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, providing a striking contrast to the flowers.

Alyssum clausonis is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
