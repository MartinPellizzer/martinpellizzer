Aconitum lycoctonum var. rubicundum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the red-hair aconite, is a variant of the European aconite characterized by its striking red-orange flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply cut. Its flowers are arranged in a spires and bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
