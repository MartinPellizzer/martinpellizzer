Alchemilla appendiculata

Alchemilla appendiculata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
