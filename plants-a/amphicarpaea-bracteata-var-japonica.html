Amphicarpaea bracteata var. japonica

Amphicarpaea bracteata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese false indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
