Ardisia quinquangularis

Ardisia quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
