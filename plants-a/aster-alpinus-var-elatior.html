Aster alpinus var. elatior

Aster alpinus var. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
