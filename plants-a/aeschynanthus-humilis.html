Aeschynanthus humilis

Aeschynanthus humilis, commonly known as the hanging ivy or the orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically opposite, oval-shaped, and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Aeschynanthus humilis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
