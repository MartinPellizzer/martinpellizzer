Acacia congesta

Acacia congesta, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, flat seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia congesta is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
