Acacia irrorata subsp. irrorata

Acacia irrorata subsp.

irrorata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
