Aphanostephus arkansanus

Aphanostephus arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas grass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and bluish-green foliage, which is often mistaken for a type of sedges or rushes. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, clay soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological value.

Aphanostephus arkansanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
