Acanthus glomeratus

Acanthus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
