Alpinia monopleura var. minor

Alpinia monopleura var. minor, commonly known as the lesser ginger or mountain ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal applications.
