Anthemis austriaca f. incrassata

Anthemis austriaca f. incrassata, commonly known as the Austrian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their medicinal properties.

The compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
