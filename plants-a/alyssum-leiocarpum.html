Alyssum leiocarpum

Alyssum leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Alyssum leiocarpum is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
