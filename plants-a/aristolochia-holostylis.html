Aristolochia holostylis

Aristolochia holostylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive shape and coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by specific insects, primarily flies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
