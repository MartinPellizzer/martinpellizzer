Anthemis libanotica

Anthemis libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Anthemis libanotica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
