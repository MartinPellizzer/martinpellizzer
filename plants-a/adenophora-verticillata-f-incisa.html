Adenophora verticillata f. incisa

Adenophora verticillata f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often slightly toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
