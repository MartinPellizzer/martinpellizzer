Andropogon sorghum var. lasiorhachis

Andropogon sorghum var. lasiorhachis, commonly known as the silky stalk sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its distinctive silky hairs on the leaf sheaths and stems.

It is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, which are typically pink or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique visual characteristics in gardens and landscapes.
