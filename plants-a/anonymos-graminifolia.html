Anonymos graminifolia

Anonymos graminifolia, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage.

It typically features narrow, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Anonymos graminifolia is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
