Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. vulnerarioides

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

vulnerarioides, commonly known as the lesser milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mediterranean regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure.

The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and slightly lobed.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a remarkable ability to thrive in poor soils.
