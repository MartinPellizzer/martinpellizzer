Acritopappus hagei

Acritopappus hagei, commonly known as the Hage's acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is distinguished by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads, giving it a unique appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged aesthetic.

Acritopappus hagei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
