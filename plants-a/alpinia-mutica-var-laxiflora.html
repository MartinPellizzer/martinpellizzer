Alpinia mutica var. laxiflora

Alpinia mutica var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warm season. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Alpinia mutica var. laxiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
