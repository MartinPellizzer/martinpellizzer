Aconitum napellus subsp. linnaeanum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, palmately compound leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a raceme at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
