Andinia bifida

Andinia bifida, commonly known as the twinleaf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its simple, bifid (two-lobed) leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family, which includes other carnivorous plants. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
