Anthocercis aromatica

Anthocercis aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic anthocercis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a distinct aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
