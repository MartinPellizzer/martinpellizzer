Agave attenuata

Agave attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave attenuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
