Andropogon pseudapricus

Andropogon pseudapricus, commonly known as the prairie grass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native grassland ecosystems.
