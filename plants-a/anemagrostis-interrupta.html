Anemagrostis interrupta

Anemagrostis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, and is characterized by its slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both spikelets and racemes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it blend into its wetland surroundings.

Anemagrostis interrupta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
