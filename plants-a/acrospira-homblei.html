Acrospira homblei

Acrospira homblei, commonly known as the Hombli's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common feature among Euphorbiaceae species.

Acrospira homblei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
