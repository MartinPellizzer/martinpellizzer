Aegiphila verrucosa

Aegiphila verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
