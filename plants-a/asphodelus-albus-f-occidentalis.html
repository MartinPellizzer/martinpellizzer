Asphodelus albus f. occidentalis

Asphodelus albus f. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, pale green to white leaves that are linear and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient plant for gardens and natural landscapes.
