Astragalus falconeri var. paucistrigosus

Astragalus falconeri var. paucistrigosus, commonly known as the Falconeri milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves with numerous tiny leaflets, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
