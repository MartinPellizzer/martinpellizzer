Althaea crispa

Althaea crispa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Althaea crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
