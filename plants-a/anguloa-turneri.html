Anguloa turneri

Anguloa turneri, commonly known as the purple anguloa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a strong fragrance. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, and it prefers cool, moist conditions.

Anguloa turneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
