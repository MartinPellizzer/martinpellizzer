Atriplex holocarpa var. spongiosa

Atriplex holocarpa var. spongiosa, commonly known as the spongy saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, spongy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Its structure and physiology make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
