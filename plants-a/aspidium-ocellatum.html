Aspidium ocellatum

Aspidium ocellatum, commonly known as the eye-spotted water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often adorned with small, eye-like spots, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are anchored to the substrate, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Aspidium ocellatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
