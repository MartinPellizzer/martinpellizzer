Aster ujiinsularis

Aster ujiinsularis, commonly known as the Uji Island aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding color to its natural habitat. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
