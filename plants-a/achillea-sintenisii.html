Achillea sintenisii

Achillea sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
