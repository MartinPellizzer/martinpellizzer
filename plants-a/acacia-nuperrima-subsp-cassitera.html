Acacia nuperrima subsp. cassitera

Acacia nuperrima subsp.

cassitera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is commonly found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
