Agapetes setigera var. parviflora

Agapetes setigera var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered silver fir, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in montane forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are white or pale pink, adding ornamental value.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
