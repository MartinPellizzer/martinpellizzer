Aegilops umbellulata var. transcaucasica

Aegilops umbellulata var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its potential in forage and as a model organism in genetic research.
