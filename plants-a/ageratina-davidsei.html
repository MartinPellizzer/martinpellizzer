Ageratina davidsei

Ageratina davidsei, commonly known as the David's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. Ageratina davidsei is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

It is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
