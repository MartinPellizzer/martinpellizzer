Artanthe secunda

Artanthe secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Artanthe secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
