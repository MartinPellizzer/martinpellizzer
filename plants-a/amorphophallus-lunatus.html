Amorphophallus lunatus

Amorphophallus lunatus, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, often foul-smelling inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy underground tuber that is used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in certain regions.
