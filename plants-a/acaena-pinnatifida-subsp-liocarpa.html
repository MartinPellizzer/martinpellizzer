Acaena pinnatifida subsp. liocarpa

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

liocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces distinctive achene fruits, which are surrounded by a persistent, woolly pappus that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and forest margins, and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
