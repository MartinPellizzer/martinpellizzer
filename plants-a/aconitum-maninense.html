Aconitum maninense

Aconitum maninense, commonly known as the Manin's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant has a toxic alkaloid-rich root system.

Aconitum maninense is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
