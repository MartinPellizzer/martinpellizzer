Acinodendron andinum

Acinodendron andinum, commonly known as the Andean acacia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its root system is adapted to thrive in poor, rocky soils typical of its mountainous habitat.

Acinodendron andinum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
