Apocopis mangalorensis

Apocopis mangalorensis, commonly known as the Mangalore goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of the region. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
