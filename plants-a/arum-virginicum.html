Arum virginicum

Arum virginicum, commonly known as Indian turnip, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive purple spathe that encloses a spadix. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.
