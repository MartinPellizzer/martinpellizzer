Astragalus pseudoversicolor

Astragalus pseudoversicolor, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of western North America, particularly in the Great Basin and surrounding areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Astragalus pseudoversicolor plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
