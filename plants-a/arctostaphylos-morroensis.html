Arctostaphylos morroensis

Arctostaphylos morroensis, commonly known as the Morro rock heath, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are favored by wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
