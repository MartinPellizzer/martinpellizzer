Asphodelus bracteatus

Asphodelus bracteatus, commonly known as the sword-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is part of the Asparagaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Asphodelus bracteatus is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
