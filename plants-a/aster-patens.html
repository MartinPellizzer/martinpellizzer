Aster patens

Aster patens, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster patens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
