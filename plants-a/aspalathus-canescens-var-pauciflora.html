Aspalathus canescens var. pauciflora

Aspalathus canescens var. pauciflora, commonly known as the silver bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in gardens.
