Androsace villosa f. longiscapa

Androsace villosa f. longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed mouse ear, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine white or gray pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The flowers are usually pink or white, clustered in dense inflorescences at the ends of the stems. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland or alpine areas.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders in horticultural settings.
