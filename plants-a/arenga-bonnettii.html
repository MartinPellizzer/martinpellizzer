Arenga bonnettii

Arenga bonnettii, commonly known as the sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of orange berries. Its trunk is fibrous and contains a starchy substance used in various traditional applications.

Arenga bonnettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has economic significance in some regions.
