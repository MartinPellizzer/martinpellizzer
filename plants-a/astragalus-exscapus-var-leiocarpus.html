Astragalus exscapus var. leiocarpus

Astragalus exscapus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its smooth, inflated pods are a distinguishing feature, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for certain wildlife.
