Allionia excelsa

Allionia excelsa, commonly known as the mountain alpine, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Allionia excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
