Aster ageratoides var. leiophyllus

Aster ageratoides var. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
