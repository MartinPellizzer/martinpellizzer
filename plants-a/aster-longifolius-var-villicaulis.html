Aster longifolius var. villicaulis

Aster longifolius var. villicaulis, commonly known as the silky aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name. This variety is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
