Ardisia quinquegona var. linearifolia

Ardisia quinquegona var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beautyberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces striking violet-black berries that are a key feature in its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are elongated and linear, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other ardisia species.
