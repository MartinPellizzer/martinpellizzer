Anisomeles salviifolia var. denudata

Anisomeles salviifolia var. denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf anisomeles, is a unique variant of the anisomeles plant characterized by its reduced foliage.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically features elongated, narrow leaves that are often sparse or absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, presenting in shades of pink or white.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
