Andropogon glaucescens var. tristachyus

Andropogon glaucescens var. tristachyus, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
