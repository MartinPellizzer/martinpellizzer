Alchemilla hoppeana

Alchemilla hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Alchemilla hoppeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.
