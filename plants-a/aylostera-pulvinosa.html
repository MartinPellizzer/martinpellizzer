Aylostera pulvinosa

Aylostera pulvinosa, commonly known as the purple-leafed acanthus, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant purple or reddish hue. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Aylostera pulvinosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and architectural form make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
