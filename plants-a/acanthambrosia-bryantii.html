Acanthambrosia bryantii

Acanthambrosia bryantii, commonly known as Bryant's thornbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, brown fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
