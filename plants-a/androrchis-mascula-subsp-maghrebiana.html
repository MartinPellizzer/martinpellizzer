Androrchis mascula subsp. maghrebiana

Androrchis mascula subsp.

maghrebiana, commonly known as the Maghreb orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in North Africa. It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafy stem and produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are short-lived, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
