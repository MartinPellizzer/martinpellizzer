Aberia mollis

Aberia mollis, commonly known as the silky abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Native Distribution

Aberia mollis is primarily native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

It is commonly found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. It is often associated with understory vegetation in these ecosystems.

Its natural range extends into parts of Indonesia and the Philippines as well.
