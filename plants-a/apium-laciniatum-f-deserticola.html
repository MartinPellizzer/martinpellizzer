Apium laciniatum f. deserticola

Apium laciniatum f. deserticola, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a variant of the wild carrot species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in rocky areas and along the edges of deserts.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
