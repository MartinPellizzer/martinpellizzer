Asystasia coleae

Asystasia coleae, commonly known as the purple-flowered asystasia, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Asystasia coleae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
