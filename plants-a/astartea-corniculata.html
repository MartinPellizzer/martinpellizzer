Astartea corniculata

Astartea corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Astartea corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
