Aralia sonchifolia

Aralia sonchifolia, commonly known as the American giant-leaf aralia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a compound umbel, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
