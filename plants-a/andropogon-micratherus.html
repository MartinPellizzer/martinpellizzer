Andropogon micratherus

Andropogon micratherus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon micratherus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
