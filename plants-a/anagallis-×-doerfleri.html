Anagallis × doerfleri

Anagallis × doerfleri, commonly known as the hybrid everlasting or the pink-flowered everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anagallis arvensis and Anagallis fimbriata. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
