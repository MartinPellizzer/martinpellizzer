Acer tenellum var. septemlobum

Acer tenellum var. septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with seven lobes, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
