Astragalus pallescens

Astragalus pallescens, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus pallescens is also of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience in harsh environments.
