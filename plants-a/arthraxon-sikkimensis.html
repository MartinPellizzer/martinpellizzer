Arthraxon sikkimensis

Arthraxon sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Arthraxon sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
