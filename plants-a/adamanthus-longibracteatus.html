Adamanthus longibracteatus

Adamanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract sycamore, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of its dioecious nature. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
