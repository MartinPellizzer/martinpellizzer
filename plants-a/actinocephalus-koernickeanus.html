Actinocephalus koernickeanus

Actinocephalus koernickeanus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are elliptical and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
