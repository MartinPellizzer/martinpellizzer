Aspidosperma multiflorum

Aspidosperma multiflorum, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
