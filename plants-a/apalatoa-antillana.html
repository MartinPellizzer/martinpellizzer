Apalatoa antillana

Apalatoa antillana, commonly known as the Antillean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
