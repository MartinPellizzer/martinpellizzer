Antrophora williamsii

Antrophora williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' antler plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like inflorescence that resembles the branching structure of a deer's antlers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its specialized habitat, Antrophora williamsii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
