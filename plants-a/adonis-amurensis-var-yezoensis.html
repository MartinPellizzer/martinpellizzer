Adonis amurensis var. yezoensis

Adonis amurensis var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo Adonis, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, unbranched stems bearing clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
