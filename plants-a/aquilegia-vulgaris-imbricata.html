Aquilegia vulgaris imbricata

Aquilegia vulgaris imbricata, commonly known as the imbricata columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and yellow. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated edge and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. It produces racemes of flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its growth habit is typically upright, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.
