Arabis malinvaldiana

Arabis malinvaldiana, commonly known as the Malinvaldian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to the harsh conditions of its natural habitat.
