Annona ambotay subsp. occidentalis

Annona ambotay subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers.

The plant produces a fruit similar to the soursop, with a smooth, green rind and a soft, white, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other Annona species.
