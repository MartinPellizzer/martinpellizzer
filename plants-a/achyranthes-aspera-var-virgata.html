Achyranthes aspera var. virgata

Achyranthes aspera var. virgata, commonly known as Indian knuckle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces oblong, smooth, and often ribbed fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
