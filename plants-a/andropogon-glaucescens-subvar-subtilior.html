Andropogon glaucescens subvar. subtilior

Andropogon glaucescens subvar. subtilior, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with a purplish hue.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in native landscapes.
