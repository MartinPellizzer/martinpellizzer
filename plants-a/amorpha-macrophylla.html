Amorpha macrophylla

Amorpha macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf honeyvine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Amorpha macrophylla is often found in moist, open habitats like woodlands and streambanks.
