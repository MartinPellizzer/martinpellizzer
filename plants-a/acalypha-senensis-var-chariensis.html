Acalypha senensis var. chariensis

Acalypha senensis var. chariensis, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in many regions.
