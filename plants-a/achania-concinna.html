Achania concinna

Achania concinna, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and succulent stems.

Achania concinna is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
