Astragalus ehdenensis

Astragalus ehdenensis, commonly known as the Ehden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and drought.
