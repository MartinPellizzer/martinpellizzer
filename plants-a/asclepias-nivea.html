Asclepias nivea

Asclepias nivea, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias nivea is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
