Astragalus kurdicus var. nudatus

Astragalus kurdicus var. nudatus, commonly known as the naked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many members of its family. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies.

The varietal name "nudatus" refers to the lack of pubescence on its stems, distinguishing it from other Astragalus species.
