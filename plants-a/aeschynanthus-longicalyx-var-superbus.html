Aeschynanthus longicalyx var. superbus

Aeschynanthus longicalyx var. superbus, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or the Christmas bell, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom during the winter months. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
