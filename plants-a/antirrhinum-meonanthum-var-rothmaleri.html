Antirrhinum meonanthum var. rothmaleri

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. rothmaleri, commonly known as the Rothmaler's snapdragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
