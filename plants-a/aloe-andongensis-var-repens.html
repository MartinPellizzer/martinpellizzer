Aloe andongensis var. repens

Aloe andongensis var. repens, commonly known as the creeping aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces orange or yellow flowers on tall, branching inflorescences during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
