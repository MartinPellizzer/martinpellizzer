Astragalus aphananthos

Astragalus aphananthos, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Astragalus aphananthos is part of the legume family and has adaptations that allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
