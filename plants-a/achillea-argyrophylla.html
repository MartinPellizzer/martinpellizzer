Achillea argyrophylla

Achillea argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Silverleaf yarrow is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.
