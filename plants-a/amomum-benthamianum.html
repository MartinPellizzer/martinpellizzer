Amomum benthamianum

Amomum benthamianum, commonly known as the Benthams' cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are followed by oval, dark blue-black berries. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
