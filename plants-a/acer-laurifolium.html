Acer laurifolium

Acer laurifolium, commonly known as the California maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that turns vibrant red or orange in the fall. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
