Arctostaphylos hooveri

Arctostaphylos hooveri, commonly known as Hoover's manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
