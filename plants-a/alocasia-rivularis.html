Alocasia rivularis

Alocasia rivularis, commonly known as the river alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length, often with a dark green coloration and a glossy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Alocasia rivularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique leaf patterns and vibrant coloration.
