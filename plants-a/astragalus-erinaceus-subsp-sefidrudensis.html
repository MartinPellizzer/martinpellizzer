Astragalus erinaceus subsp. sefidrudensis

Astragalus erinaceus subsp.

sefidrudensis, commonly known as the Sefidrudensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow or pale green flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations in its native habitat.
