Asarum asperum var. asperum

Asarum asperum var. asperum, commonly known as the rough asarum, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers clustered in a spathaceous bract, which are often hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
