Alpinia pterocarpa

Alpinia pterocarpa, commonly known as the mountain ginger or wild ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
