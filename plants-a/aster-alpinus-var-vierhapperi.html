Aster alpinus var. vierhapperi

Aster alpinus var. vierhapperi, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
