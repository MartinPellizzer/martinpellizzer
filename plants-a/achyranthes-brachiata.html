Achyranthes brachiata

Achyranthes brachiata, commonly known as horse date or Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by triangular, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Achyranthes brachiata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
