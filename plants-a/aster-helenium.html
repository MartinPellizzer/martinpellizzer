Aster helenium

Aster helenium, commonly known as the American sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its large, showy yellow or orange flower heads. The plant typically grows up to 3 to 5 feet tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
