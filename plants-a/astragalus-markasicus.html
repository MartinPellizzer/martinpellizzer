Astragalus markasicus

Astragalus markasicus, commonly known as the Markasik milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus markasicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
