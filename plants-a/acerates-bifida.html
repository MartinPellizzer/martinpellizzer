Acerates bifida

Acerates bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
