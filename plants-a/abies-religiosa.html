Abies religiosa

Abies religiosa, commonly known as the sacred fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal crown and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
