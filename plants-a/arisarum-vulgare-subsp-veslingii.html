Arisarum vulgare subsp. veslingii

Arisarum vulgare subsp.

veslingii, commonly known as the purple-flowered arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe. It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a purple tint.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple spathe, which is characteristic of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in shade-tolerant plants.
