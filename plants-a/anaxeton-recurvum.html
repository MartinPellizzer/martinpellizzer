Anaxeton recurvum

Anaxeton recurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting pea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anaxeton recurvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
