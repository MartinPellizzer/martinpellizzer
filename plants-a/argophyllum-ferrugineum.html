Argophyllum ferrugineum

Argophyllum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty argophyllum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

Argophyllum ferrugineum is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
