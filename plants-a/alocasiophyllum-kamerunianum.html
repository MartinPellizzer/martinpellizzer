Alocasiophyllum kamerunianum

Alocasiophyllum kamerunianum, commonly known as the Cameroon cycad, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Cameroon.

It belongs to the cycad family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, palm-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, and its reproductive structures are essential for its survival in the wild.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
