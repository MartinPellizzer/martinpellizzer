Anoda populifolia

Anoda populifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Anoda populifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
