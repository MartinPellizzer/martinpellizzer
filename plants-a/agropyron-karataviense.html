Agropyron karataviense

Agropyron karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavi needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions.

Agropyron karataviense is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and as a forage plant for livestock.
