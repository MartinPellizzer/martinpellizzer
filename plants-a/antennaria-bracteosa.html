Antennaria bracteosa

Antennaria bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted bellworts, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small bells. The plant features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its botanical structure includes a central stem with numerous branching stems and small, bract-like leaves that protect the flower heads.

Antennaria bracteosa is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland and wetland habitats.
