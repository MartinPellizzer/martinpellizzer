Astragalus incanus subsp. incurvus

Astragalus incanus subsp.

incurvus, commonly known as the bentleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its incurved leaflets, which give it its common name. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system for efficient water uptake.
