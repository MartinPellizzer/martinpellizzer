Albuca caudata

Albuca caudata, commonly known as the cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca caudata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in mild climates.
