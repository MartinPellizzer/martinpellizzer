Anisophyllum scordiifolium

Anisophyllum scordiifolium, commonly known as the garlic-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-scented leaves that are narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.
