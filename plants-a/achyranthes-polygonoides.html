Achyranthes polygonoides

Achyranthes polygonoides, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
