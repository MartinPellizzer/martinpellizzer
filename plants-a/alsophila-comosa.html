Alsophila comosa

Alsophila comosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots. The fronds are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
