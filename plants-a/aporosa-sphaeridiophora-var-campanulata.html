Aporosa sphaeridiophora var. campanulata

Aporosa sphaeridiophora var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped aporosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
