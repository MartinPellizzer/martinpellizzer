Arnica alpina var. plantaginea

Arnica alpina var. plantaginea, commonly known as the alpine tawnpiece, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in herbal remedies, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
