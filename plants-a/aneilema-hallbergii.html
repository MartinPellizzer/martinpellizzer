Aneilema hallbergii

Aneilema hallbergii, commonly known as the red-flowered arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, crimson bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.
