Allenanthus

Allenanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, giving it its common name. Allenanthus species produce small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
