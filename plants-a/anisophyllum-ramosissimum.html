Anisophyllum ramosissimum

Anisophyllum ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor, sandy soils.

Anisophyllum ramosissimum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
