Avena triseta

Avena triseta, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Avena triseta is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, making it a versatile and resilient plant.
