Astragalus jaliscensis

Astragalus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Jalisco milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus jaliscensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its unique botanical traits.
