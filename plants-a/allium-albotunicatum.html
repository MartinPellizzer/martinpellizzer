Allium albotunicatum

Allium albotunicatum, commonly known as the white tunic onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a bulbous stem that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to collections of ornamental alliums.
