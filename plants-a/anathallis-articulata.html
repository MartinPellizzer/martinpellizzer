Anathallis articulata

Anathallis articulata, commonly known as the articulated anathallia, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, elongated flower that resembles a small, delicate spike. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually greenish or yellowish with a distinctive, recurved lip.

Anathallis articulata is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
