Abutilon malvifolium

Abutilon malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Abutilon malvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
