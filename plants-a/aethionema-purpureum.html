Aethionema purpureum

Aethionema purpureum, commonly known as the purple flowering sandthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aethionema purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
