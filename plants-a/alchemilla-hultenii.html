Alchemilla hultenii

Alchemilla hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of shiny, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Alchemilla hultenii is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardiness.
