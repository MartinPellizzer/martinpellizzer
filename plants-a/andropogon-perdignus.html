Andropogon perdignus

Andropogon perdignus, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green color, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Andropogon perdignus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
