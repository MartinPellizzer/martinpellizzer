Adiantum capillus-veneris var. polyhymnia

Adiantum capillus-veneris var. polyhymnia, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green with a distinctive light green or silver vein pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in shaded forest floors. Its fronds are composed of small, lance-shaped segments that give it a lacy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
