Acaena lucida var. parvifolia

Acaena lucida var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
