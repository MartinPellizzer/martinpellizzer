Alyssum serpyllifolium var. incanum

Alyssum serpyllifolium var. incanum, commonly known as silvery matweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, lawns, and along roadsides.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
