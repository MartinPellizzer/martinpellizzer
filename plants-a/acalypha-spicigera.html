Acalypha spicigera

Acalypha spicigera, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, with bright red bracts surrounding small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often green with a reddish tinge, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acalypha spicigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
