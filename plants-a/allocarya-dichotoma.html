Allocarya dichotoma

Allocarya dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a tall, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. Allocarya dichotoma is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insect species.
