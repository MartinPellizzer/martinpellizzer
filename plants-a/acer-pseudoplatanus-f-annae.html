Acer pseudoplatanus f. annae

Acer pseudoplatanus f. annae, commonly known as the Ann Arbor maple, is a rare and distinctive form of the European maple.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves that are typically smaller and more rounded than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, showing some tolerance to urban conditions.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
