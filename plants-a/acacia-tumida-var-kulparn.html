Acacia tumida var. kulparn

Acacia tumida var. kulparn, commonly known as the kulparn wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are grey-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

The kulparn wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in local ecosystems.
