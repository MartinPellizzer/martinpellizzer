Aster harveyanus subsp. robustus

Aster harveyanus subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its sturdy stems and hairy foliage distinguish it from other members of the genus.
