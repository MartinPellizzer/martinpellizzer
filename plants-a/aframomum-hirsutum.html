Aframomum hirsutum

Aframomum hirsutum, commonly known as the African ginger or wild cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by red or orange berries containing numerous seeds.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
