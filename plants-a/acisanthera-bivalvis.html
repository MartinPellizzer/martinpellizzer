Acisanthera bivalvis

Acisanthera bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open into two valves, a feature that gives it its scientific name. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
