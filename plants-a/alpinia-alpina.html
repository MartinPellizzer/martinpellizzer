Alpinia alpina

Alpinia alpina, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia alpina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
