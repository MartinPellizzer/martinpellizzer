Amomis oblongata

Amomis oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Amomis oblongata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
