Asclepias lanceolata var. paupercula

Asclepias lanceolata var. paupercula, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive, hooked seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.
