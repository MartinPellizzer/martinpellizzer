Aconitum szeewaldianum

Aconitum szeewaldianum, commonly known as the Szeewald monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue, spurred flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant has palmately compound leaves with deeply cut lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shady to semi-shaded environments.

Aconitum szeewaldianum is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
