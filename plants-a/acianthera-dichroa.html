Acianthera dichroa

Acianthera dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored petals, typically featuring a combination of white and purple or pink hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Acianthera dichroa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
