Astragalus pseudovegetus

Astragalus pseudovegetus, commonly known as the false vegetation milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
