Axonopus paranaensis

Axonopus paranaensis, commonly known as Parana grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Axonopus paranaensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
