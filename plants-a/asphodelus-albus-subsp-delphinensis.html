Asphodelus albus subsp. delphinensis

Asphodelus albus subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure compared to the typical Asphodelus albus.
