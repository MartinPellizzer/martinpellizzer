Avena adzharica

Avena adzharica, commonly known as the Caucasian oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. Avena adzharica is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
