Acacia subcaerulea var. parvifolia

Acacia subcaerulea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, narrow, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
