Aegiceras floridum

Aegiceras floridum, commonly known as the Florida mangrove, is a halophytic tree native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It possesses a unique ability to tolerate and thrive in saline environments, making it an essential component of mangrove ecosystems. The plant has a buttressed trunk and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Aegiceras floridum plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
