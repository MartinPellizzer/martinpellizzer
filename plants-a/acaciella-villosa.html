Acaciella villosa

Acaciella villosa, commonly known as the hairy acaciella, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, white, velvety hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, adapted to conserve moisture.

Acaciella villosa is often used in local traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
