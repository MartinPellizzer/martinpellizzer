Anthericum arenarium

Anthericum arenarium, commonly known as the sand anthericum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Anthericum arenarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
