Agave mapisaga

Agave mapisaga, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with white to grayish-blue markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small greenish-white flowers, which bloom after many years.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
