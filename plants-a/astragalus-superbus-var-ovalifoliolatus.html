Astragalus superbus var. ovalifoliolatus

Astragalus superbus var. ovalifoliolatus, commonly known as the oval-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers. The leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations in its native environment.
