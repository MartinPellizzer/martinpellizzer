Alchemilla cuneata f. intermedia

Alchemilla cuneata f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in shady garden settings for its ornamental value.
