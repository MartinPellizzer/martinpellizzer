Amylocarpus platyspinus

Amylocarpus platyspinus, commonly known as the broad-spined arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Madagascar. The plant features a short rhizome with fleshy, tuberous roots that are used as a starchy food source. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and have a waxy surface, while its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

The plant is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible tubers.
