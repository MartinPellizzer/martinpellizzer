Arabis gegamica

Arabis gegamica, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing insulation against cold temperatures.

Arabis gegamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
