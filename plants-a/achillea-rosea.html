Achillea rosea

Achillea rosea, commonly known as the pink yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-green hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or rose-colored, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Achillea rosea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional medicine.
