Archidendron clypearia var. casai

Archidendron clypearia var. casai, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and economic purposes.
