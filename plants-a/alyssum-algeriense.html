Alyssum algeriense

Alyssum algeriense, commonly known as Algerian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in spring and early summer.

Alyssum algeriense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
