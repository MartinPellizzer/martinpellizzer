Astragalus wrightii

Astragalus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Astragalus wrightii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
