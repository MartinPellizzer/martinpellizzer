Aphelandra antioquiensis

Aphelandra antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
