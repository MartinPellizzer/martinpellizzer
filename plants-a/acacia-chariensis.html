Acacia chariensis

Acacia chariensis, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and small, globular yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. Acacia chariensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
