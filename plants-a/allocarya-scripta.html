Allocarya scripta

Allocarya scripta, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Allocarya scripta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
