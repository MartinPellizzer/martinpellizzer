Amerimnon cochinchinense

Amerimnon cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
