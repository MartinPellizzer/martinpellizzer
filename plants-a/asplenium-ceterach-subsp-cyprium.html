Asplenium ceterach subsp. cyprium

Asplenium ceterach subsp.

cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
