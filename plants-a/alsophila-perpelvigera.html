Alsophila perpelvigera

Alsophila perpelvigera, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps anchor the plant in moist, well-drained soil.

Alsophila perpelvigera is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
