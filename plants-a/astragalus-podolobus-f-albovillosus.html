Astragalus podolobus f. albovillosus

Astragalus podolobus f. albovillosus, commonly known as the white-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China.

This subspecies is notable for its pale, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
