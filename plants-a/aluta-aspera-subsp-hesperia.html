Aluta aspera subsp. hesperia

Aluta aspera subsp.

hesperia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in open meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, elongated seeds in its fruit. Aluta aspera subsp.

hesperia is often found in the Sierra Nevada mountain range, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
