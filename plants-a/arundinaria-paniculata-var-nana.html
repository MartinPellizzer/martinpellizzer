Arundinaria paniculata var. nana

Arundinaria paniculata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a compact variety of bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, panicle-like clusters of white to light green flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
