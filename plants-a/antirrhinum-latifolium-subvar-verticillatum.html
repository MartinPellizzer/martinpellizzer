Antirrhinum latifolium subvar. verticillatum

Antirrhinum latifolium subvar. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
