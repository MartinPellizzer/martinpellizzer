Aloysia castellanosii

Aloysia castellanosii, commonly known as the Peruvian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant produces fragrant, four-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Aloysia castellanosii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
