Arctostaphylos nochistlanensis

Arctostaphylos nochistlanensis, commonly known as the Chihuahuan manzanita, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions, showcasing its resilience and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
