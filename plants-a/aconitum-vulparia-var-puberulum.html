Aconitum vulparia var. puberulum

Aconitum vulparia var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are arranged in a raceme atop tall, stout stems. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are hairy, giving it the "puberulum" designation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
