Aspidium biseriatum

Aspidium biseriatum, commonly known as the two-row water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically divided into two rows of pinnae, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

Aspidium biseriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
