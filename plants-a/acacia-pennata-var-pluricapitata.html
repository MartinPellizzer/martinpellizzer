Acacia pennata var. pluricapitata

Acacia pennata var. pluricapitata, commonly known as the many-headed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and its distinctive yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feather-like leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
