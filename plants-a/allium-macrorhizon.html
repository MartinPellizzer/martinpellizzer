Allium macrorhizon

Allium macrorhizon, commonly known as the giant leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful crop in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
