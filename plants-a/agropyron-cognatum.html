Agropyron cognatum

Agropyron cognatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces dense spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Agropyron cognatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
