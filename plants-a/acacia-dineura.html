Acacia dineura

Acacia dineura, commonly known as the silky oak, is a tropical tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in landscaping and for its ornamental qualities.
