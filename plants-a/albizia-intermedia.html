Albizia intermedia

Albizia intermedia, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its feathery, bipinnate leaves and fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Albizia intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation projects.
