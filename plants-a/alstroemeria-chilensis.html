Alstroemeria chilensis

Alstroemeria chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped leaves and a robust root system.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
