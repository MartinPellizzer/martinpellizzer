Acacia varia var. crassinervis

Acacia varia var. crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, broad veins, which give it its specific epithet "crassinervis.". The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically during spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
