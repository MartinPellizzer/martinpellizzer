Asyneuma argutum subsp. argutum

Asyneuma argutum subsp.

argutum, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering stems are usually branched and bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Asyneuma argutum subsp.

argutum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.
