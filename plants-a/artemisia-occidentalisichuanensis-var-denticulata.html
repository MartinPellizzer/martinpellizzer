Artemisia occidentalisichuanensis var. denticulata

Artemisia occidentalisichuanensis var. denticulata, commonly known as the western Chinese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
