Andrachne filiformis

Andrachne filiformis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thin, thread-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, nutrient-poor environments.

Andrachne filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
