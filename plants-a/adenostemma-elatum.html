Adenostemma elatum

Adenostemma elatum, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Adenostemma elatum is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
