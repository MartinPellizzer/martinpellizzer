Acetosella cummingii

Acetosella cummingii, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces delicate white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers shady to partially sunny conditions. It is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas at high elevations.

Acetosella cummingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
