Alstroemeria epulauquensis

Alstroemeria epulauquensis, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and cool, humid climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Alstroemeria epulauquensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
