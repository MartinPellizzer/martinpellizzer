Aspilia macrorrhiza

Aspilia macrorrhiza, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies, composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.

Aspilia macrorrhiza is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
