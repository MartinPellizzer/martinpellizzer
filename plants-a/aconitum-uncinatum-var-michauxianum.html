Aconitum uncinatum var. michauxianum

Aconitum uncinatum var. michauxianum, commonly known as the Michaux's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a coarse texture and a distinctive, waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
