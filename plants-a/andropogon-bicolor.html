Andropogon bicolor

Andropogon bicolor, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted flower heads that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bracts that are often tinged with purple or pink.

Andropogon bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
