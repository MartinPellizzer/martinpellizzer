Alternanthera filifolia subsp. pintensis

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

pintensis, commonly known as the Brazilian threadleaf amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often bright red or purple, especially when grown in full sunlight.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.
