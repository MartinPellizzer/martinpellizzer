Achras tabogaensis

Achras tabogaensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, cylindrical trunk and sparse, spiky foliage. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its stem, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
