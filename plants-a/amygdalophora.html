Amygdalophora

Amygdalophora, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a hairy husk, which are harvested for their edible seeds. These seeds are rich in oil and are widely used in food and cosmetics.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
