Acalypha reticulata f. glabrescens

Acalypha reticulata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky mallow, is a variant of the species Acalypha reticulata.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in both garden and landscape settings.
