Alchemilla plocekii

Alchemilla plocekii, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla plocekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
