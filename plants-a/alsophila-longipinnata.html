Alsophila longipinnata

Alsophila longipinnata, commonly known as the long-pinnate tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that give it a graceful, tree-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila longipinnata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
