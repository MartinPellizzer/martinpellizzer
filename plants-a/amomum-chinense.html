Amomum chinense

Amomum chinense, commonly known as Chinese cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic compounds.
