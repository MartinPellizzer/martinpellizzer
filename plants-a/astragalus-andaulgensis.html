Astragalus andaulgensis

Astragalus andaulgensis, commonly known as the Andalusian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across suitable environments.
