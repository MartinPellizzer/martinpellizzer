Acacia argyrodendron

Acacia argyrodendron, commonly known as the silver-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia argyrodendron is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
