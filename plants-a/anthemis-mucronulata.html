Anthemis mucronulata

Anthemis mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, oval-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
