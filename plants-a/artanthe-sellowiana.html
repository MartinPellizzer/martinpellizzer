Artanthe sellowiana

Artanthe sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, colorful blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
