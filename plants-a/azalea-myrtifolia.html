Azalea myrtifolia

Azalea myrtifolia, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes, particularly in regions with mild, humid climates.

The leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
