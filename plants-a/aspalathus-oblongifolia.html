Aspalathus oblongifolia

Aspalathus oblongifolia, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought-resistant properties. It is widely cultivated for its unique leaves, which are used to make the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.

The botanical features of Aspalathus oblongifolia make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
