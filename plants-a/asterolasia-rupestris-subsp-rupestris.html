Asterolasia rupestris subsp. rupestris

Asterolasia rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock aster, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean and semi-arid climates.
