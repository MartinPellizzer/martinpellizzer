Astragalus corrugatus

Astragalus corrugatus, commonly known as the wrinkled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus corrugatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
