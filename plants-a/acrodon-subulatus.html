Acrodon subulatus

Acrodon subulatus, commonly known as the needleleaf acrodon, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Acrodon subulatus is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
