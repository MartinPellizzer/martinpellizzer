Aquifolium heterophyllum

Aquifolium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable holly.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Aquifoliaceae, characterized by its alternate, simple, and usually spiny leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan.

The leaves exhibit variation in shape and size, which contributes to its common name.
