Arenaria souliei

Arenaria souliei, commonly known as the Souchong sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
