Alchemilla parapersica

Alchemilla parapersica, commonly known as Persian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

Alchemilla parapersica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
