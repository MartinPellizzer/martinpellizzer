Astragalus hornii var. bahaeensis

Astragalus hornii var. bahaeensis, commonly known as the Bahai milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological resilience and potential medicinal properties.
