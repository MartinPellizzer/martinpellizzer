Acaena cadilla f. pusilla

Acaena cadilla f. pusilla, commonly known as the small-stemmed New Zealand bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, woolly coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
