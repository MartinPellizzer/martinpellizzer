Acronia diabolica

Acronia diabolica, commonly known as the devil's acorn, is a rare and striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and a unique, often twisted fruit that resembles a miniature acorn. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically along riverbanks or in dense undergrowth. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish, which contrasts with its dramatic fruit.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
