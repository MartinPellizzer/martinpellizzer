Andropogon scoparius var. polycladus

Andropogon scoparius var. polycladus, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking purple-tinted inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
