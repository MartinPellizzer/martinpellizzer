Adenocarpus parvifolius subsp. commutatus

Adenocarpus parvifolius subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the small-leaved burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
