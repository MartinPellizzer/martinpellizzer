Achillea seidlii

Achillea seidlii, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are aromatic and finely divided, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Achillea seidlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
