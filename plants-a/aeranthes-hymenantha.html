Aeranthes hymenantha

Aeranthes hymenantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers with prominent green veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
