Aconitum napellus subsp. burnatii

Aconitum napellus subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are palmately compound with finely divided lobes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
