Arrhenatherum setaceum

Arrhenatherum setaceum, commonly known as slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to wetland environments, with a rhizomatous growth habit that aids in spreading and survival.
