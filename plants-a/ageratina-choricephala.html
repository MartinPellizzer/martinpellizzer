Ageratina choricephala

Ageratina choricephala, commonly known as the purpletop ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink flower heads, which are surrounded by white or pale blue bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ageratina choricephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
