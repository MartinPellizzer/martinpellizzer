Astragalus pushtashanicus

Astragalus pushtashanicus, commonly known as the Pushtan hill milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Astragalus pushtashanicus is also noted for its potential ecological and medicinal significance in its native habitats.
