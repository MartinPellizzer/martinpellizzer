Aspidium moritzianum

Aspidium moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a refined appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Aspidium moritzianum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
