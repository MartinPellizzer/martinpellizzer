Alpinia ligulata

Alpinia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features upright, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance.

Alpinia ligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
