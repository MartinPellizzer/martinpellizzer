Adenodolichos oblongifoliolatus

Adenodolichos oblongifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets that give it its species name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by legumes containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions contributes to its ecological and agricultural significance.
