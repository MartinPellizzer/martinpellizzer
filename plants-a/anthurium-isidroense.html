Anthurium isidroense

Anthurium isidroense, commonly known as the Isidro's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the spiky, colorful structures that house the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium isidroense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
