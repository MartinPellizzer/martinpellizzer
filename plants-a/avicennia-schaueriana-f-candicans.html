Avicennia schaueriana f. candicans

Avicennia schaueriana f. candicans, commonly known as the candicans mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species Avicennia schaueriana.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, which are a key botanical feature. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, typically in coastal areas. Its leaves are ovate and have a thick cuticle, which helps in reducing water loss.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
