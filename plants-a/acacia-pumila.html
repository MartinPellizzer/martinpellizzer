Acacia pumila

Acacia pumila, commonly known as the stinkwood or white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs, and it produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia pumila is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
