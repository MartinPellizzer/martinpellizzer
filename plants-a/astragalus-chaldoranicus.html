Astragalus chaldoranicus

Astragalus chaldoranicus, commonly known as the Chaldaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus chaldoranicus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
