Asystasia dalzelliana var. alba

Asystasia dalzelliana var. alba, commonly known as white-veined asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white veins on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other varieties of Asystasia.
