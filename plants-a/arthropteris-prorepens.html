Arthropteris prorepens

Arthropteris prorepens, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stolons that allow it to spread across the forest floor. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate and subtropical regions.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
