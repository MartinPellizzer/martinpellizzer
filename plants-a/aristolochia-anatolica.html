Aristolochia anatolica

Aristolochia anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding floral structure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine, although it is toxic and should be handled with care.
