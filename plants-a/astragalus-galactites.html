Astragalus galactites

Astragalus galactites, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across suitable habitats.
