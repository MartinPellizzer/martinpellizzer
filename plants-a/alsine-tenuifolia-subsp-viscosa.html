Alsine tenuifolia subsp. viscosa

Alsine tenuifolia subsp.

viscosa, commonly known as sticky alsike, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
