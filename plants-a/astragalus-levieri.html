Astragalus levieri

Astragalus levieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as mountain slopes and open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, twisted pod that contains several seeds.

Astragalus levieri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
