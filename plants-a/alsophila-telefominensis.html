Alsophila telefominensis

Alsophila telefominensis, commonly known as the Telefomin tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, fibrous trunk, while its fronds contribute to the dense canopy of the forest.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in maintaining forest biodiversity.
