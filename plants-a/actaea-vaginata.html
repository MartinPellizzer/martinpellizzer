Actaea vaginata

Actaea vaginata, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its berries are dark blue and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Actaea vaginata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
