Alchemilla crassicaulis

Alchemilla crassicaulis, commonly known as broadstem lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, somewhat woody stems that are covered in dense, silver-gray fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a wavy margin and are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Alchemilla crassicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
