Acacia lugardiae

Acacia lugardiae, commonly known as the Lugard's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky soils.

Acacia lugardiae is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
