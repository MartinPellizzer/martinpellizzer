Actinocephalus bahiensis

Actinocephalus bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starburst cactus, is a unique species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that form a star-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid regions with suitable climates.
