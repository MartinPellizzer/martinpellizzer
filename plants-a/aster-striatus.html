Aster striatus

Aster striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

Aster striatus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
