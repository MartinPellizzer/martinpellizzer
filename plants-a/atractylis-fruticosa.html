Atractylis fruticosa

Atractylis fruticosa, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers are borne in compact clusters at the ends of the stems.

Atractylis fruticosa is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.
