Amaryllis formosa

Amaryllis formosa, commonly known as the "Coral Beauty," is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive coral-like hue. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Amaryllis formosa typically blooms in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year with proper care.
