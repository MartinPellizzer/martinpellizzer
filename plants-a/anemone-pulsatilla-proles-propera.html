Anemone pulsatilla proles propera

Anemone pulsatilla proles propera, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
