Abutilon hasslerianum f. johnsonii

Abutilon hasslerianum f. johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and broad, lobed foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
