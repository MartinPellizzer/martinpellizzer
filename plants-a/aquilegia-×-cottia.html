Aquilegia × cottia

Aquilegia × cottia, commonly known as the hybrid columbine, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a cross between Aquilegia vulgaris and Aquilegia formosa, resulting in a plant with distinctive, spurred flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and purple. The leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
