Anemone coronaria var. phaenicea

Anemone coronaria var. phaenicea, commonly known as the red anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, single or double flowers with bright red petals and a yellow center, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can naturalize in suitable climates.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive red color and its association with the broader Anemone coronaria species.
