Armeniaca minutiflora

Armeniaca minutiflora, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It produces small, orange-red fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet, chewy texture and nutritional value.
