Asparagus officinalis var. oxycarpus

Asparagus officinalis var. oxycarpus, commonly known as the Mexican asparagus or silver asparagus, is a perennial plant native to Central America.

It features slender, gray-green stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
