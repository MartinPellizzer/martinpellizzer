Alaternus × hybridus

Alaternus × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid alaternus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two species of the Alaternus genus.

It is characterized by its unique combination of traits from its parent species, often displaying variations in leaf morphology and flower structure. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
