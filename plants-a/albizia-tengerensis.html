Albizia tengerensis

Albizia tengerensis, commonly known as the Tenger's silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ornamental value, featuring delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, fluffy white flowers. Albizia tengerensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
