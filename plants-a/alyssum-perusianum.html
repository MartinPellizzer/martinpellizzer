Alyssum perusianum

Alyssum perusianum, commonly known as the Peruvian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Alyssum perusianum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
