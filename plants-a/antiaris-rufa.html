Antiaris rufa

Antiaris rufa, commonly known as the red antiaris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like calla lilies and philodendrons. The plant is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces striking yellowish-white spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Antiaris rufa is also known for its toxic sap, which contains calcium oxalate crystals, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
