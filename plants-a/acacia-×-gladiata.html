Acacia × gladiata

Acacia × gladiata, commonly known as the hybrid wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acacia aneura (mulga) and Acacia longifolia (coast wattle). The plant is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia × gladiata is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
