Acaena magellanica var. venulosa

Acaena magellanica var. venulosa, commonly known as the Magellanic burr or velvet burr, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright orange to red berries, which contain hard, barbed seeds enclosed in a fibrous coating. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
