Amyema angustifolia

Amyema angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed amyema, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils.

Amyema angustifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
