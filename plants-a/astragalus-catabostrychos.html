Astragalus catabostrychos

Astragalus catabostrychos, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with a narrow, linear shape, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus catabostrychos is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
