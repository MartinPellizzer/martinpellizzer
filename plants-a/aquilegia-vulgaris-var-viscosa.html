Aquilegia vulgaris var. viscosa

Aquilegia vulgaris var. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a hairy, glandular texture that gives it its "sticky" characteristic. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
