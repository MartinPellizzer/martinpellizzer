Avenula bromoides var. straminea

Avenula bromoides var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale yellow to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
