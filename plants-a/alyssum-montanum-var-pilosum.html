Alyssum montanum var. pilosum

Alyssum montanum var. pilosum, commonly known as the woolly alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners seeking drought-resistant and visually appealing flora.
