Aloe sakarahensis subsp. pallida

Aloe sakarahensis subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-green in color, which help it store water in its native habitat.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow to orange flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and potentially useful in traditional medicine.
