Ammi minus

Ammi minus, commonly known as lesser fennel, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and light, facilitating wind dispersal.

Ammi minus is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
