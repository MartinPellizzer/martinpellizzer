Astragalus ruscifolius

Astragalus ruscifolius, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus ruscifolius is often found in rocky soils and is considered a valuable plant for wildlife and soil stabilization.
