Acacia hooperiana var. glabriuscula

Acacia hooperiana var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Hooper's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in harsh environmental conditions.
