Androcentrum multiflorum

Androcentrum multiflorum, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong, bitter scent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
