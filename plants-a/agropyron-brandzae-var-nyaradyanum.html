Agropyron brandzae var. nyaradyanum

Agropyron brandzae var. nyaradyanum, commonly known as the Nyaradyanum wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
