Agropyron yezoense

Agropyron yezoense, commonly known as Yezo wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Agropyron yezoense is also valued for its resilience to cold climates and its potential in erosion control.
