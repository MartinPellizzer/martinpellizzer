Acrodiclidium geminiflorum

Acrodiclidium geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered candelabra cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that bears clusters of small, green, spiny leaves at the apex. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are borne on short, hairy pedicels. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
