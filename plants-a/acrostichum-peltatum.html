Acrostichum peltatum

Acrostichum peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, broad, and often slightly curled appearance. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
