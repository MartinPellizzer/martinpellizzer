Aria ochracea

Aria ochracea, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, producing a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism.

Aria ochracea is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
