Amelanchier vallisclausae

Amelanchier vallisclausae, commonly known as the valley alder or serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it has a distinctive branching pattern.

Amelanchier vallisclausae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
