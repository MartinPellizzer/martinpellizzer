Artemisia indica

Artemisia indica, commonly known as Indian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal uses. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Artemisia indica is valued for its traditional applications in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
