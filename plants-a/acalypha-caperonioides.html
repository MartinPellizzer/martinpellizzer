Acalypha caperonioides

Acalypha caperonioides, commonly known as the Cape rotundifolia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically rounded or ovate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which are surrounded by specialized bracts. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
