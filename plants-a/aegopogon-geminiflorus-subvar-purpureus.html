Aegopogon geminiflorus subvar. purpureus

Aegopogon geminiflorus subvar. purpureus, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
