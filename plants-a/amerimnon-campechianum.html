Amerimnon campechianum

Amerimnon campechianum, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
