Allium staticiforme

Allium staticiforme, commonly known as the static-form garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, cylindrical bulbs. The plant produces tall, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that resemble small bells. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short stem that supports the inflorescence.

Allium staticiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
