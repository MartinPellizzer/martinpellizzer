Aspalathus microcarpa

Aspalathus microcarpa, commonly known as African green tea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, making it resilient in arid environments. Its leaves are harvested and processed to make a popular herbal tea.

The botanical features of Aspalathus microcarpa contribute to its ecological significance and commercial value.
