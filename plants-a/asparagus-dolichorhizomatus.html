Asparagus dolichorhizomatus

Asparagus dolichorhizomatus, commonly known as the long-rooted asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its long, thick rhizomes, which contribute to its robust growth and adaptability. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
