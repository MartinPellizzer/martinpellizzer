Alchemilla strigosula

Alchemilla strigosula, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla strigosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
