Alyssum macropodum var. macropodum

Alyssum macropodum var. macropodum, commonly known as the large-leaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum macropodum var. macropodum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as butterflies and bees.
