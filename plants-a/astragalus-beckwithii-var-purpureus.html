Astragalus beckwithii var. purpureus

Astragalus beckwithii var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
