Acacia homaloclada

Acacia homaloclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like seed pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Acacia homaloclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
