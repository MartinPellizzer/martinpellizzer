Anoectochilus sumatranus

Anoectochilus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are notable for their unique shape, resembling a slipper, which is a common feature among orchids in the Anoectochilus genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
