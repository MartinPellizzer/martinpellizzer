Arachis triseminata

Arachis triseminata, commonly known as the three-seeded groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces underground pods containing three seeds each, which are used as a food source. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Arachis triseminata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
