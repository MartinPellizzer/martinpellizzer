Acacia lanceolata

Acacia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia lanceolata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
