Aligera insignis

Aligera insignis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Aligera insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
