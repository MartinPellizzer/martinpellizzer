Acoridium kingii

Acoridium kingii, commonly known as the King's acoridium, is a rare and unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
