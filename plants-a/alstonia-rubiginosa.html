Alstonia rubiginosa

Alstonia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed alstonia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Alstonia rubiginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
