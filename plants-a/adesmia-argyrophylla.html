Adesmia argyrophylla

Adesmia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is a low-growing shrub, typically reaching heights of about 30 centimeters.

Adesmia argyrophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
