Albizia brevipes

Albizia brevipes, commonly known as the small-headed mimosa, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in globular or racemose inflorescences. Albizia brevipes is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
