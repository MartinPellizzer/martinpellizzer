Andira anthelminthica var. ormosioides

Andira anthelminthica var. ormosioides, commonly known as the Ormosioides andira, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its potential medicinal properties, including anthelmintic effects, which have been studied for traditional use in treating parasitic infections.
