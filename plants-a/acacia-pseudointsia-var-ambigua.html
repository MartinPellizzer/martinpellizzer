Acacia pseudointsia var. ambigua

Acacia pseudointsia var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or open habitats. Its bark and leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, while its seeds are a food source for certain wildlife.

The species is notable for its variability in morphology, which contributes to its common name.
