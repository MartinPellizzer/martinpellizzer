Achillea ramosissima

Achillea ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and a branching, upright stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Achillea ramosissima is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
