Aegilonearum

Aegilonearum, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and other moist habitats. Aegilonearum plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.
