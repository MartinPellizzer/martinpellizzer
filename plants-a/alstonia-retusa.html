Alstonia retusa

Alstonia retusa, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its showy, panicle-like inflorescences produce small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
