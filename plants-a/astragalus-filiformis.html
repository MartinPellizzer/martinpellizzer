Astragalus filiformis

Astragalus filiformis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, bean-like pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.

Astragalus filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
