Agoseris longissima

Agoseris longissima, commonly known as the longstem goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

Agoseris longissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
