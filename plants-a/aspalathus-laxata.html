Aspalathus laxata

Aspalathus laxata, commonly known as the small-leaved African geranium, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Aspalathus laxata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
