Athyrium alpinum

Athyrium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine lady fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia, Europe, and North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
