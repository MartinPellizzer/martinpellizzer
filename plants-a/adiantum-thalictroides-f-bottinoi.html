Adiantum thalictroides f. bottinoi

Adiantum thalictroides f. bottinoi, commonly known as the Bottinoi maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with small, lanceolate pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
