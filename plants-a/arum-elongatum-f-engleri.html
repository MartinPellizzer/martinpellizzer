Arum elongatum f. engleri

Arum elongatum f. engleri, commonly known as the Engler's arrowleaf arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a large, spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are often a deep green with a glossy texture, and the plant can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
