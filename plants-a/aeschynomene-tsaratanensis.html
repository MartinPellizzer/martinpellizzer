Aeschynomene tsaratanensis

Aeschynomene tsaratanensis, commonly known as the Madagascar burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive burr-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has small, trifoliate leaves. It produces yellow flowers and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and potential use in traditional medicine.
