Abarema pahangensis

Abarema pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang legume, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through mechanical means.
