Amorpha apiculata

Amorpha apiculata, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Amorpha apiculata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
