Arnicastrum glandulosum var. glandulosum

Arnicastrum glandulosum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in glandular hairs, giving them a sticky texture. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its flowering stems are often hairy and may have a reddish tinge.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, open woodlands and meadows.
