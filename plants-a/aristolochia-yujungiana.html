Aristolochia yujungiana

Aristolochia yujungiana, commonly known as the Yujungian Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant produces a strong, often unpleasant odor to attract its pollinators, primarily flies. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
