Astragalus bakaliensis

Astragalus bakaliensis, commonly known as the Bakalensis milkvetch, is a species of legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus bakaliensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
