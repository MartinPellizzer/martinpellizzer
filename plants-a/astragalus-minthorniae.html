Astragalus minthorniae

Astragalus minthorniae, commonly known as Minthorn's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus minthorniae is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
