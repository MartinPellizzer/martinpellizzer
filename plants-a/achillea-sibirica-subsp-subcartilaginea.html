Achillea sibirica subsp. subcartilaginea

Achillea sibirica subsp.

subcartilaginea, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia. It features a compact growth habit with a dense rosette of gray-green, softly hairy leaves.

The plant produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine, cartilaginous hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
