Anemone laceratoincisa

Anemone laceratoincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone laceratoincisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
