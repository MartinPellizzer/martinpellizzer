Allium chamaemoly var. coloratum

Allium chamaemoly var. coloratum, commonly known as the purple onion grass or Japanese garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green with a slight purple tinge. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it popular in Asian cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
