Arnica lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Arnica lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces tall, leafless stems topped with yellow-orange flower heads composed of numerous disc flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
