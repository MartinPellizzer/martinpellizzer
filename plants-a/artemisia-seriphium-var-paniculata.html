Artemisia seriphium var. paniculata

Artemisia seriphium var. paniculata, commonly known as Panicled Artemisia, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a strong aromatic scent, typical of the Artemisia genus. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, forming panicles that bloom in late summer.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
