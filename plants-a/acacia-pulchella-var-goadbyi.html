Acacia pulchella var. goadbyi

Acacia pulchella var. goadbyi, commonly known as the Goadby's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
