Adenophora tetraphylla var. princeps

Adenophora tetraphylla var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
