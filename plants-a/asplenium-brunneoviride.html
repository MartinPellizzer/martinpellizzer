Asplenium brunneoviride

Asplenium brunneoviride, commonly known as the brown-green spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage that consists of closely arranged, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocks or in crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium brunneoviride is a hardy plant that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
