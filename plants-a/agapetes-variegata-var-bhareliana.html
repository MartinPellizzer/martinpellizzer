Agapetes variegata var. bhareliana

Agapetes variegata var. bhareliana, commonly known as the Bhareliana silverleaf evergreen, is a compact shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that exhibits a striking variegation of silver and green hues. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention in its high-altitude habitat. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to shaded conditions.
