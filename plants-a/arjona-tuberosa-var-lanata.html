Arjona tuberosa var. lanata

Arjona tuberosa var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly arjona, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
