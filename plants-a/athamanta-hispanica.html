Athamanta hispanica

Athamanta hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Athamanta hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
