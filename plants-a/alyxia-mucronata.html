Alyxia mucronata

Alyxia mucronata, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are a notable feature of its botanical appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Alyxia mucronata is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
