Aristea maitlandii

Aristea maitlandii, commonly known as the Maitland's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aristea maitlandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
