Ajuga hildebrandtii

Ajuga hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's bugleweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Ajuga hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in woodland gardens.
