Alyssum pannulosum

Alyssum pannulosum, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum pannulosum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
