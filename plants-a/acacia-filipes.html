Acacia filipes

Acacia filipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Acacia filipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
