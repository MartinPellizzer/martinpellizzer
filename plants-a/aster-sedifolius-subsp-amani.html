Aster sedifolius subsp. amani

Aster sedifolius subsp.

amani, commonly known as the Amanian aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the summer months.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
