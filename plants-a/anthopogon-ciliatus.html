Anthopogon ciliatus

Anthopogon ciliatus, commonly known as the silky grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to its modest appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Anthopogon ciliatus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for certain wildlife species.
