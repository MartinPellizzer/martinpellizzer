Adesmia cytisoides var. cytisoides

Adesmia cytisoides var. cytisoides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It features slender, often spiny stems and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
