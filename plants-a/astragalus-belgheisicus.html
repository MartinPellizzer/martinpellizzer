Astragalus belgheisicus

Astragalus belgheisicus, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, including parts of Greece, Albania, and Bulgaria. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus belgheisicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
