Acacia jupunba

Acacia jupunba, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves composed of multiple narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia jupunba is well adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also known for its use in the production of tannins and as a source of timber.
