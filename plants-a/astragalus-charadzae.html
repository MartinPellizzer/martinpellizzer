Astragalus charadzae

Astragalus charadzae, commonly known as the Charadza milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus charadzae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
