Asperula longifolia

Asperula longifolia, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring season. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Asperula longifolia is well adapted to acidic soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
