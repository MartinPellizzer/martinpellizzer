Aeollanthus rosulifolius

Aeollanthus rosulifolius, commonly known as the rose-leafed aeollanthus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
