Araucaria bidwillii var. pendula

Araucaria bidwillii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hoop pine, is a unique variant of the hoop pine tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading branches that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically green, with a smooth, dark bark that becomes flaky with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and can live for several decades, making it a long-term addition to landscapes.
