Acrostichum latifolium var. membranaceum

Acrostichum latifolium var. membranaceum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped, leathery leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with multiple layers of small, toothed pinnae that give it a lush, feathery appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and tropical landscapes.
