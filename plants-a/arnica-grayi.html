Arnica grayi

Arnica grayi, commonly known as Gray's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the genus Arnica in the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Arnica grayi is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is often used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
