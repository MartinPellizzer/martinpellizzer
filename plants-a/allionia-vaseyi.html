Allionia vaseyi

Allionia vaseyi, commonly known as the yellow-flowered allionia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Allionia vaseyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
