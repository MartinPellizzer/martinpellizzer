Astragalus bicarinatus

Astragalus bicarinatus, commonly known as the two-ribbed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is often deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus bicarinatus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
