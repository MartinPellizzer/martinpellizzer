Acanthopanax innovans

Acanthopanax innovans, commonly known as the Korean angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in forested areas. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
