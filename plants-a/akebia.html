Akebia

Akebia, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or five-leaf akebia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Akebia quinata, which is native to East Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Akebia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
