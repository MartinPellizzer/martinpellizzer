Aegiphila brasiliensis

Aegiphila brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
