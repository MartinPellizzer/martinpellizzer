Achillea pulchra f. rosea

Achillea pulchra f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea Yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and long blooming period.

The cultivar's distinct coloration sets it apart from other yarrow varieties, making it a popular choice among horticulturists.
