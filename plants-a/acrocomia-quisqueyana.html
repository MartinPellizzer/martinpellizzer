Acrocomia quisqueyana

Acrocomia quisqueyana, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Cuba and the Bahamas.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has pinnate leaves that are long and feathery, with a bright green color. Its inflorescences are clustered at the base of the leaves, producing small white flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny fruits.

The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.
