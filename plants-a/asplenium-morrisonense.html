Asplenium morrisonense

Asplenium morrisonense, commonly known as Morrison's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces spores in spherical clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium morrisonense is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
