Acer chunii subsp. dimorphophyllum

Acer chunii subsp.

dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves that change color throughout the seasons.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
