Acacia microcephala

Acacia microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals in its native range.
