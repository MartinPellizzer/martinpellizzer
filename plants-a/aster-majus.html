Aster majus

Aster majus, commonly known as the great aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers. Its blooms appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster majus is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
