Amygdalus fenzliana

Amygdalus fenzliana, commonly known as the Chinese peach or wild peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits that resemble peaches but are typically less fleshy and more tart. It is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
