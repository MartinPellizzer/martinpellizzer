Artanthe lineata f. laevigata

Artanthe lineata f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved artanthe, is a cultivar of the artanthe plant, which belongs to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

The smooth leaf texture of this variety distinguishes it from other forms of Artanthe lineata.
