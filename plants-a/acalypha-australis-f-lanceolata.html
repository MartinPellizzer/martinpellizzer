Acalypha australis f. lanceolata

Acalypha australis f. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with red or orange bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
