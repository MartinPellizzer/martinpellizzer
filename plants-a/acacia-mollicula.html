Acacia mollicula

Acacia mollicula, commonly known as the soft acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive appearance.
