Andryala × dichroa

Andryala × dichroa, commonly known as the hybrid of Andryala and Dichroa, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that mature to a dark purple color.

Andryala × dichroa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
