Avena persica var. glabrescens

Avena persica var. glabrescens, commonly known as Persian wheatgrass, is a variety of the species Avena persica, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly in livestock feed.

The leaves and young shoots are rich in nutrients, including proteins, vitamins, and minerals.
