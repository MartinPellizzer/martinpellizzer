Acacia sorophylla

Acacia sorophylla, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid conditions. Acacia sorophylla is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is also an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for birds and insects.
