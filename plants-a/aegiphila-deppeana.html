Aegiphila deppeana

Aegiphila deppeana, commonly known as the Chilean ironwood, is a shrub native to central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct, aromatic scent, which is used in traditional medicine.

Aegiphila deppeana is well adapted to dry, coastal environments and is often found in rocky soils.
