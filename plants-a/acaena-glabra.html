Acaena glabra

Acaena glabra, commonly known as the smooth thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, woody fruits called achenes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acaena glabra is often found in moist, shaded areas and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
