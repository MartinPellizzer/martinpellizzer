Arenaria hookeri

Arenaria hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic environments. The plant has small, succulent leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Arenaria hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
