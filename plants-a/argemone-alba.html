Argemone alba

Argemone alba, commonly known as white argemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves and yellow flowers that turn pink as they mature. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to tolerate arid environments.
