Aeranthes neglecta

Aeranthes neglecta, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem with a few narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in a spike, with pale green or yellowish petals.

Aeranthes neglecta is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated as a rare ornamental plant.
