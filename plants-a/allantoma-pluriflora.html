Allantoma pluriflora

Allantoma pluriflora, commonly known as the clustered allantoma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Allantoma pluriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
