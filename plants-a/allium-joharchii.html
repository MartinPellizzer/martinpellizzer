Allium joharchii

Allium joharchii, commonly known as the Kashmir garlic, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in traditional cuisines, particularly in South Asian countries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Allium joharchii is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
