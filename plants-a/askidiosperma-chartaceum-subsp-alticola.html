Askidiosperma chartaceum subsp. alticola

Askidiosperma chartaceum subsp.

alticola, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fibrous, peeling bark that resembles paper, which gives it its common name.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
