Aconitum glabrisepalum

Aconitum glabrisepalum, commonly known as the smooth-sepal aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, which are typically borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aconitum glabrisepalum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
