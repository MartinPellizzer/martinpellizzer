Allium oleraceum var. carinatum

Allium oleraceum var. carinatum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is used as a vegetable in some cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its mild garlic flavor and is used both ornately and culinarily.
