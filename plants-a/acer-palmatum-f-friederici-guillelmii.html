Acer palmatum f. friederici-guillelmii

Acer palmatum f. friederici-guillelmii, commonly known as the Japanese maple 'Friederici-Guillelmii', is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like foliage that turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

The leaves are palmately lobed and provide a striking visual display throughout the growing season.
