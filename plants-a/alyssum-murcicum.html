Alyssum murcicum

Alyssum murcicum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-green foliage and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Alyssum murcicum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
