Alchornea websteri

Alchornea websteri, commonly known as the Webster's alchornea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
