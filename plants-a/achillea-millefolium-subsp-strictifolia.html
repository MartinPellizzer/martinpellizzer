Achillea millefolium subsp. strictifolia

Achillea millefolium subsp.

strictifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive arrangement of feathery, finely divided lobes.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
