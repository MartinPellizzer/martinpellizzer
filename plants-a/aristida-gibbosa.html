Aristida gibbosa

Aristida gibbosa, commonly known as the tufted dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and tufted, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, sandy soils, and disturbed areas. Its small, pale green flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Aristida gibbosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.
