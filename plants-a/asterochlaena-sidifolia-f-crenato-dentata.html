Asterochlaena sidifolia f. crenato-dentata

Asterochlaena sidifolia f. crenato-dentata, commonly known as the serrated-lobed rock aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive serrated and lobed margin. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in its native habitat.
