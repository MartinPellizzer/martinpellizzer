Alyxia reinwardtii var. cinerea

Alyxia reinwardtii var. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf alyxia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or limestone areas.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.
