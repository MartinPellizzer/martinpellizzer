Asclepias nivea var. curassavica

Asclepias nivea var. curassavica, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release silky, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and dry habitats.

This species is also a vital food source for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
