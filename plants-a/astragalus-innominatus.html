Astragalus innominatus

Astragalus innominatus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water availability.
