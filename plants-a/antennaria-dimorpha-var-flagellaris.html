Antennaria dimorpha var. flagellaris

Antennaria dimorpha var. flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
