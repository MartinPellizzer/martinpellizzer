Aconitum uncinatum var. muticum

Aconitum uncinatum var. muticum, commonly known as the creeping aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and is often grown in controlled environments.
