Ayenia pilosa

Ayenia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ayenia pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
