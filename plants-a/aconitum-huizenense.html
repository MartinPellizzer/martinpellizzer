Aconitum huizenense

Aconitum huizenense, commonly known as the Huizen monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that are clustered in a spire-like arrangement. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, palmate structure.

Aconitum huizenense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
