Asarum kiusianum

Asarum kiusianum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
