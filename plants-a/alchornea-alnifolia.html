Alchornea alnifolia

Alchornea alnifolia, commonly known as the silky alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Alchornea alnifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
