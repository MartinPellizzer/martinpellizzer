Afroaster peglerae

Afroaster peglerae, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, while its flowers range from white to yellow.

Afroaster peglerae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
