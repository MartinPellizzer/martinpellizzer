Allium atrorubens

Allium atrorubens, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces spherical, inflated seed pods that turn brown as they mature, containing numerous small, dark seeds. Allium atrorubens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
