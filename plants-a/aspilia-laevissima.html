Aspilia laevissima

Aspilia laevissima, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a short, upright stem and is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact, showy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in arid or semi-arid conditions.

Aspilia laevissima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
