Aster altaicus var. taitoensis

Aster altaicus var. taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito mountain aster, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value.
