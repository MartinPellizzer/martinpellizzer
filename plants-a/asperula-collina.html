Asperula collina

Asperula collina, commonly known as mountain azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Asperula collina thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in temperate climates.
