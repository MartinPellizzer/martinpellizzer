Anisopappus hoffmannianus

Anisopappus hoffmannianus, commonly known as Hoffmann's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical foliage structure. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

Anisopappus hoffmannianus is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.
