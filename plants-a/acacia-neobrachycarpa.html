Acacia neobrachycarpa

Acacia neobrachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually narrow and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia neobrachycarpa is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought.
