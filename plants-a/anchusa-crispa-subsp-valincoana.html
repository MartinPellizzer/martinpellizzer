Anchusa crispa subsp. valincoana

Anchusa crispa subsp.

valincoana, commonly known as the Valincoan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
