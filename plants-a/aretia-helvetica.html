Aretia helvetica

Aretia helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Aretia helvetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
