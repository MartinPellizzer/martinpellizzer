Ageratina pichinchensis

Ageratina pichinchensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations.

Ageratina pichinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
