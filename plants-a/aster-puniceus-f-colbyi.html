Aster puniceus f. colbyi

Aster puniceus f. colbyi, commonly known as the Colby's pink aster, is a rare and striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flower heads, which are typically borne on slender, hairy stems. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
