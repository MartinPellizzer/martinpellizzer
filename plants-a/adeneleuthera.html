Adeneleuthera

Adeneleuthera, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its flowers are tubular with a prominent corolla.

Adeneleuthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
