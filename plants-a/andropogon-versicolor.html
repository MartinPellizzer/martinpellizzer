Andropogon versicolor

Andropogon versicolor, commonly known as the variable bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.

Andropogon versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
