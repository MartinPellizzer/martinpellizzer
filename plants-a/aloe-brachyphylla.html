Aloe brachyphylla

Aloe brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf aloe or narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features short, thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used for its soothing properties in traditional medicine.

Aloe brachyphylla is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
