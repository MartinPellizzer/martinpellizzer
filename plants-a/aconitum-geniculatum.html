Aconitum geniculatum

Aconitum geniculatum, commonly known as the zigzag monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with palmately compound leaves that have a distinctive, notched appearance. Aconitum geniculatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
