Antennaria dioica var. rosea

Antennaria dioica var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored mouse-ear.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are typically pink or rose-colored, giving it its common name. It produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
