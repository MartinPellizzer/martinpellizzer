Aloe arachnoides

Aloe arachnoides, commonly known as the spider aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered with a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on tall, branching stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe arachnoides is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
