Adiantum aquismonense

Adiantum aquismonense, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to rocky substrates or soil.

Adiantum aquismonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

Morphology

Adiantum aquismonense has fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, with a delicate, finely toothed margin, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

The leaves are typically small, ranging from 10 to 20 centimeters in length, and have a bright green color that remains consistent throughout the growing season. The stem is slender and upright, with a smooth, light green bark that grows in a simple, unbranched pattern. This plant does not produce flowers, as it is a spore-bearing fern, and instead reproduces through the development of sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Its roots are fibrous and rhizoidal, anchoring the plant in moist, shaded environments where it thrives.
