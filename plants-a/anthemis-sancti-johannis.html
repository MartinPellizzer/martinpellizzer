Anthemis sancti-johannis

Anthemis sancti-johannis, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant contains active compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which are believed to have antidepressant and anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
