Arracacia pubescens

Arracacia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arracacha, is a member of theApiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and clustered inflorescences.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a stout, branching stem covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and it produces edible roots that are used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Arracacia pubescens make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
