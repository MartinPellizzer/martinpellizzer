Actaea yunnanensis

Actaea yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, palmately compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Actaea yunnanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
