Atractylis candida

Atractylis candida, commonly known as white bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Atractylis candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
