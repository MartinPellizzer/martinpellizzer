Aeranthes lehmannii

Aeranthes lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Aeranthes lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
