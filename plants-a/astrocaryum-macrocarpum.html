Astrocaryum macrocarpum

Astrocaryum macrocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti, is a palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by large, edible fruits that are encased in a fibrous husk. Its fruit is a significant food source for various wildlife and is also used by indigenous communities.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
