Azalea burgeri

Azalea burgeri, commonly known as the Burgundy azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange in color. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with dense, leathery foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which occurs in the spring.
