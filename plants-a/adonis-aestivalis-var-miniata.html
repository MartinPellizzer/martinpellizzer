Adonis aestivalis var. miniata

Adonis aestivalis var. miniata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems rise above the foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple with white markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique coloration, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
