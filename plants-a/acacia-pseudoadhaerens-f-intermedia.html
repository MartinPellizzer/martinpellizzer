Acacia pseudoadhaerens f. intermedia

Acacia pseudoadhaerens f. intermedia, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
