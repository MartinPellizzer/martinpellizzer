Acalypha pubiflora subsp. australica

Acalypha pubiflora subsp.

australica, commonly known as the Australian or silky ironwood, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Acalypha pubiflora subsp.

australica is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
