Aquilegia atrata

Aquilegia atrata, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually dark red or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
