Asperula petraea

Asperula petraea, commonly known as the rock asperula, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Asperula petraea is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
