Ajax pseudonarcissus var. telamonius

Ajax pseudonarcissus var. telamonius, commonly known as the Telamonian daffodil, is a variant of the daffodil species characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features a single, trumpet-shaped corona that is typically longer than the tepals, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
