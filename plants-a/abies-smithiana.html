Abies smithiana

Abies smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dense, dark green needles that are typically arranged in pairs. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Abies smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of alpine flora.
