Amaranthus angustifolius subsp. aschersonianus

Amaranthus angustifolius subsp.

aschersonianus, commonly known as the aschersonian amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which eventually develop into seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is used in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
