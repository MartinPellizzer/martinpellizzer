Anemone litoralis

Anemone litoralis, commonly known as the sea anemone, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal regions.

It has delicate, petal-like structures called sepals that surround its reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in shallow waters and thrives in sandy or rocky substrates. Its flowers are often blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone litoralis plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
