Alopecurus fulvus var. interruptus

Alopecurus fulvus var. interruptus, commonly known as the yellow hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with yellowish to reddish flowers. Its leaves are usually covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
