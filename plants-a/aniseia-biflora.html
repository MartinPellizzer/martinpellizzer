Aniseia biflora

Aniseia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered anise, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often branched.

Aniseia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
