Achimenes divaricata

Achimenes divaricata, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow or orange throat. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are borne on erect stems, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Achimenes divaricata is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid environments.
