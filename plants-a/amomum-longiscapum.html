Amomum longiscapum

Amomum longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellow flowers, which are followed by small, triangular berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent scent and medicinal properties.

Amomum longiscapum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and economic importance.
