Aristolochia aucheri

Aristolochia aucheri, commonly known as the Persian-dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, tubular, and often twisted flower structure. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color and are known for their strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphology and chemical composition.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
