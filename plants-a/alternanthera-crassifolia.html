Alternanthera crassifolia

Alternanthera crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Alternanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features striking, broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
