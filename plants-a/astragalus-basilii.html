Astragalus basilii

Astragalus basilii, commonly known as Basil's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
