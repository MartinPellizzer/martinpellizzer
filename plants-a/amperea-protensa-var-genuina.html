Amperea protensa var. genuina

Amperea protensa var. genuina, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
