Achyranthes ryukyuensis

Achyranthes ryukyuensis, commonly known as Ryukyu knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are characterized by their greenish-white color.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its historical use in traditional medicine.
