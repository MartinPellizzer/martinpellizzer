Aspalathus cerrhantha

Aspalathus cerrhantha, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited to Mediterranean climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Aspalathus cerrhantha plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
