Ampelygonum malaicum

Ampelygonum malaicum, commonly known as the Malacca bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
