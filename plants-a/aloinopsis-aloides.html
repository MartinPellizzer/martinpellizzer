Aloinopsis aloides

Aloinopsis aloides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks with bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild laxative effect.

Aloinopsis aloides is well-suited for rock gardens and requires full sun and well-draining soil.
