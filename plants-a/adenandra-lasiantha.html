Adenandra lasiantha

Adenandra lasiantha, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Adenandra lasiantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental bark and drought tolerance.
