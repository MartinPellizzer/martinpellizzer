Andropogon hylophilus

Andropogon hylophilus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal panicle. It is well adapted to fire-prone ecosystems and plays a key role in maintaining ecological balance.

Andropogon hylophilus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in restoration projects.
