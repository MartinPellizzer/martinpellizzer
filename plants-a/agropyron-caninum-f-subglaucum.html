Agropyron caninum f. subglaucum

Agropyron caninum f. subglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its distinctive glaucous blue-green foliage.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and plant identification.
