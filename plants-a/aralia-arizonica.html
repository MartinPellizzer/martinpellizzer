Aralia arizonica

Aralia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona woodbine, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets that are often arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The botanical characteristics of Aralia arizonica make it a distinctive and resilient plant in its native habitat.
