Astragalus macroptilus var. brevilaciniatus

Astragalus macroptilus var. brevilaciniatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
