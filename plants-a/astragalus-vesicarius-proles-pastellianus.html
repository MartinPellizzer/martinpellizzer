Astragalus vesicarius proles pastellianus

Astragalus vesicarius proles pastellianus, commonly known as the pastellian milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
