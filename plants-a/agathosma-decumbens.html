Agathosma decumbens

Agathosma decumbens, commonly known as buchu, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, peppermint-like scent. The plant has small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and traditional medicinal use.
