Asteriscus morrisonii

Asteriscus morrisonii, commonly known as Morrison's asterisk, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
