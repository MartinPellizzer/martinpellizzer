Achoriphragma saposhnikovii

Achoriphragma saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with slender stems and lanceolate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
