Acacia celastrifolia

Acacia celastrifolia, commonly known as the strangled acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Acacia celastrifolia is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber in its native regions.
