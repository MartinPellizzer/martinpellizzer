Alopecurus laguroides

Alopecurus laguroides, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Alopecurus laguroides plays a role in maintaining soil stability and provides forage for various herbivorous animals.
