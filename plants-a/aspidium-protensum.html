Aspidium protensum

Aspidium protensum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a distinctive appearance.

Aspidium protensum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
