Aristotelea

Aristotelea, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescences. While not typically cultivated for its edible roots like true arrowroot (Maranta arundinacea), some species are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitats.
