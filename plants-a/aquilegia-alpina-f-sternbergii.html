Aquilegia alpina f. sternbergii

Aquilegia alpina f. sternbergii, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a hybrid form of the alpine columbine, characterized by its distinctive spurred petals.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and is known for its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in cooler, moist conditions.
