Astrocaryum huaimi var. orbignii

Astrocaryum huaimi var. orbignii, commonly known as the Huaimi palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark brown fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the ecosystems of the Amazon rainforest.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in sustainable forestry practices.
