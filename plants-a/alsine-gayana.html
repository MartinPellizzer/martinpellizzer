Alsine gayana

Alsine gayana, commonly known as the African sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Alsine gayana is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
