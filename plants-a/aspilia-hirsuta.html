Aspilia hirsuta

Aspilia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy aspilia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in a cluster. Aspilia hirsuta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
