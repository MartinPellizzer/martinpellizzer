Abutilon albidum subsp. fruticosum

Abutilon albidum subsp.

fruticosum, commonly known as the silverleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, low-maintenance plant.
