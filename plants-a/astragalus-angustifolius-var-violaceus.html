Astragalus angustifolius var. violaceus

Astragalus angustifolius var. violaceus, commonly known as the violetleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
