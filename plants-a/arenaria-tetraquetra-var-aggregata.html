Arenaria tetraquetra var. aggregata

Arenaria tetraquetra var. aggregata, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dunes. The plant has small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
