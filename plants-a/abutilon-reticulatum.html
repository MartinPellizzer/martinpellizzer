Abutilon reticulatum

Abutilon reticulatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon reticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
