Amorphophallus panomensis

Amorphophallus panomensis, commonly known as the Panomean arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a large, fleshy underground tuber that serves as its storage organ and is used for its starchy root. Its leaves are typically large and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in certain regions.
