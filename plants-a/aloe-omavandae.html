Aloe omavandae

Aloe omavandae, commonly known as the Namib Desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in extreme desert conditions. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom during the brief rainy season. Aloe omavandae is adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall, with a slow growth rate and minimal leaf shedding.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
