Arisaema robustum f. variegatum

Arisaema robustum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Indian turnip, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features a distinctive, leaf-like bract that encloses the spadix, which is adorned with small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant's foliage consists of heart-shaped, variegated leaves with white and green patterns, adding visual interest. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
