Alopecurus lasiostachyus

Alopecurus lasiostachyus, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus lasiostachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
