Astilbe japonica f. rubeola

Astilbe japonica f. rubeola, commonly known as the red-leaf Japanese astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery foliage that turns a striking reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces panicles of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shaded garden landscapes.
