Astragalus sclerocladus

Astragalus sclerocladus, commonly known as the hard-clad milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is adapted to dry conditions with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Astragalus sclerocladus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
