Asplenium chaseanum

Asplenium chaseanum, commonly known as the Chase's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows on the bark of trees or in moist, shaded environments. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium chaseanum is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, forested areas.
