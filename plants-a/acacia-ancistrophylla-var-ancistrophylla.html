Acacia ancistrophylla var. ancistrophylla

Acacia ancistrophylla var. ancistrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
