Anchusa strigosa var. tonsa

Anchusa strigosa var. tonsa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
