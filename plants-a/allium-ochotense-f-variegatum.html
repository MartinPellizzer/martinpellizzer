Allium ochotense f. variegatum

Allium ochotense f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated onion, is a botanical variant of the Allium ochotense species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green and white or yellow hues. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in areas of Siberia and the Russian Far East. The foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
