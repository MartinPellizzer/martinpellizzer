Anthurium purdieanum

Anthurium purdieanum, commonly known as the Purdie's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, often with a reddish or purple tint along the veins.

Anthurium purdieanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
