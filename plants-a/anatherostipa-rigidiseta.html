Anatherostipa rigidiseta

Anatherostipa rigidiseta, commonly known as the rigid seta grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant features dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of grasses. Its rigid, bristly hairs, especially on the stems and leaves, give it its common name and contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Anatherostipa rigidiseta is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
