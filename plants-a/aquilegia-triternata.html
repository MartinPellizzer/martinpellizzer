Aquilegia triternata

Aquilegia triternata, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in early spring. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals that are arranged in a bell shape.

Aquilegia triternata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
