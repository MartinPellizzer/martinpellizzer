Astragalus farmanfarmajanii

Astragalus farmanfarmajanii, commonly known as the Farmanfarmajan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deep within the ground.

Astragalus farmanfarmajanii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
