Arundinaria kongosanensis f. aureostriata

Arundinaria kongosanensis f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-striped bamboo, is a rare and ornamental bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow stripes on the culms, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in a clumping form, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its foliage is dense and lush, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and landscape design.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
