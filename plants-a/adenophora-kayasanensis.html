Adenophora kayasanensis

Adenophora kayasanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Adenophora kayasanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
