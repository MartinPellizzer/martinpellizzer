Artemisia dentata

Artemisia dentata, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Artemisia dentata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
