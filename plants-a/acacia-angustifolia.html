Acacia angustifolia

Acacia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia angustifolia is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
