Amirola mollis

Amirola mollis, commonly known as the silky leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Amirola mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
