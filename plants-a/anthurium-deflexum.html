Anthurium deflexum

Anthurium deflexum, commonly known as the Flexed Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture.

Anthurium deflexum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
