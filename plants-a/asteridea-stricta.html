Asteridea stricta

Asteridea stricta, commonly known as the upright aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a rough texture.

Asteridea stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
