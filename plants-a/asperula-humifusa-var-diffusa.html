Asperula humifusa var. diffusa

Asperula humifusa var. diffusa, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
