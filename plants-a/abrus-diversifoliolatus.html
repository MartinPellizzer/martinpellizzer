Abrus diversifoliolatus

Abrus diversifoliolatus, commonly known as the variable-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnate leaves that vary in shape and size, often with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, flat, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
