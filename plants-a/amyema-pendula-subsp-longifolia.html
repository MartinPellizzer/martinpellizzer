Amyema pendula subsp. longifolia

Amyema pendula subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ghost gum, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping habit and elongated, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer leaves compared to the typical form of Amyema pendula.
