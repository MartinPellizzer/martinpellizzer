Abies cephalonica var. parnassica

Abies cephalonica var. parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Pindus Mountains in Greece.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more rigid compared to other fir species. The cones are typically small and clustered, with reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. This variety is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, thriving in cool, moist environments with poor soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and human activity.
