Aechmea eriostachya

Aechmea eriostachya, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for various small organisms. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear showy, violet-blue flowers, which are often followed by colorful, long-lasting bracts. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aechmea eriostachya thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.
