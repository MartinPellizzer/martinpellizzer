Aira caryophyllaea

Aira caryophyllaea, commonly known as the red sandreed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish to reddish inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves.
