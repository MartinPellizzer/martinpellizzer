Agrostis coarctata subsp. trinii

Agrostis coarctata subsp.

trinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often prostrate or ascending, and its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and closely arranged.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
