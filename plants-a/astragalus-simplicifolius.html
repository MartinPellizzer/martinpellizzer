Astragalus simplicifolius

Astragalus simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Astragalus simplicifolius plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
