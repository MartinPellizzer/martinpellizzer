Allium mogoltavicum

Allium mogoltavicum, commonly known as the Mogoltav garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, spherical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
