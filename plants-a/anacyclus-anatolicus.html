Anacyclus anatolicus

Anacyclus anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian horsebean, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Anacyclus anatolicus thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
