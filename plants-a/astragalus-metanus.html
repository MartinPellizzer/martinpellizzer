Astragalus metanus

Astragalus metanus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus metanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitats.
