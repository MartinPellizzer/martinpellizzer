Astragalus varzobicus

Astragalus varzobicus, commonly known as the Varzob Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
