Achyronia dasyantha

Achyronia dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly achyronia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
