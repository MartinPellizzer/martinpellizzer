Allium aegaeum

Allium aegaeum, commonly known as the purple onion or Cyprus onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, flowering stems that bear small, spherical purple or pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Allium aegaeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
