Anemia phyllitidis var. hirta

Anemia phyllitidis var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a variant of the Anemia phyllitidis species, which belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a light green color and a slightly wavy margin.

This variant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.
