Agave guedeneyrii

Agave guedeneyrii, commonly known as the Guadalupe Island agave, is a species native to the island of Guadalupe in the East Pacific.

It is a succulent plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable subject in botanical studies.
