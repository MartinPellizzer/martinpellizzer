Acrostichum caenopteris

Acrostichum caenopteris, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a yellowish or golden underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with closely spaced, lance-shaped pinnae.

The golden shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
