Amomum warburgii

Amomum warburgii, commonly known as Java cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
