Adesmia oligophylla var. quadripinnata

Adesmia oligophylla var. quadripinnata, commonly known as the four-feather legume, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided into multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
