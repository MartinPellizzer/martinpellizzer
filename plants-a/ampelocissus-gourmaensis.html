Ampelocissus gourmaensis

Ampelocissus gourmaensis, commonly known as the Gourma fig, is a woody vine native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
