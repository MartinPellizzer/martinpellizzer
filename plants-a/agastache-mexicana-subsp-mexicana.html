Agastache mexicana subsp. mexicana

Agastache mexicana subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as Mexican hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
