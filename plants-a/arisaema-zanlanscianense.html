Arisaema zanlanscianense

Arisaema zanlanscianense, commonly known as the Zanlanshan arisaema, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
