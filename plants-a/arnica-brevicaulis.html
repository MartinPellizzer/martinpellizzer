Arnica brevicaulis

Arnica brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Arnica brevicaulis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
