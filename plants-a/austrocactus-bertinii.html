Austrocactus bertinii

Austrocactus bertinii, commonly known as the Bertini's cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Argentina.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 15 centimeters. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring.

Austrocactus bertinii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
