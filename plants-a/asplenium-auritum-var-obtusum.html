Asplenium auritum var. obtusum

Asplenium auritum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
