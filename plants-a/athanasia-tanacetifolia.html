Athanasia tanacetifolia

Athanasia tanacetifolia, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark green, finely toothed leaves. The plant produces small, spherical yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Athanasia tanacetifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
