Arum dioscoridis f. punctatum

Arum dioscoridis f. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted arum.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a unique inflorescence that resembles a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered within a fleshy, spadix. It is native to regions in Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
