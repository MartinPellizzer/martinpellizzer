Astragalus neomaassoumianus

Astragalus neomaassoumianus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
