Albizia atakataka

Albizia atakataka, commonly known as the red thorn acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Albizia atakataka thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
