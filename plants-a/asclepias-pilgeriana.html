Asclepias pilgeriana

Asclepias pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, hooded buds and spiral-shaped seed pods. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

Asclepias pilgeriana thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.
