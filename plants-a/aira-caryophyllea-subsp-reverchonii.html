Aira caryophyllea subsp. reverchonii

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's tufted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features tufts of spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pinkish to reddish flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver- or blue-gray in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
