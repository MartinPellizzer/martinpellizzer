Apicra × bicarinata

Apicra × bicarinata, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Apicra and bicarinata species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as red, pink, or white. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Apicra × bicarinata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal.
