Aloysia chiapensis

Aloysia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
