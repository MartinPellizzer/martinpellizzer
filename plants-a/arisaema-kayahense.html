Arisaema kayahense

Arisaema kayahense, commonly known as the Kayah arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arisaema kayahense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
