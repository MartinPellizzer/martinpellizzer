Abarema longipedunculata

Abarema longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled burrbean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
