Acradenia bosistoi

Acradenia bosistoi, commonly known as the Karijini ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species is also notable for its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
