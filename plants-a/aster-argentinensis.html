Aster argentinensis

Aster argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster argentinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
