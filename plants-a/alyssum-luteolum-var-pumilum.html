Alyssum luteolum var. pumilum

Alyssum luteolum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf yellow alyssum, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has delicate, hairy leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its cheerful appearance.

This variety is a hardy perennial that is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
