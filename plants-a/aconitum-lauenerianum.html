Aconitum lauenerianum

Aconitum lauenerianum, commonly known as the Lauener's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble a hood, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant features palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

However, all parts of the plant are highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids.
