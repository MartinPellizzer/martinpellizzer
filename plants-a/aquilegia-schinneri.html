Aquilegia schinneri

Aquilegia schinneri, commonly known as Schinner's columbine, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often hairy, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
