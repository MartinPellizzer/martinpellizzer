Aquilegia vulgaris var. normalis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. normalis, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically arranged in a cluster of five to ten blooms. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in woodland or rocky soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
