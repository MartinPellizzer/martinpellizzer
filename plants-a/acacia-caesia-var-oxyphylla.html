Acacia caesia var. oxyphylla

Acacia caesia var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional and contemporary uses.
