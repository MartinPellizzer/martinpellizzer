Aconitum paniculigerum

Aconitum paniculigerum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids such as aconitine.
