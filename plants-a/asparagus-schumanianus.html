Asparagus schumanianus

Asparagus schumanianus, commonly known as Schuman's asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Asparagus schumanianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
