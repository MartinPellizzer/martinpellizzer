Antidesma litorale

Antidesma litorale, commonly known as the coastal antidesma, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Antidesma litorale is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in coastal ecosystems.
