Aspalathus quinquefolia subsp. virgata

Aspalathus quinquefolia subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant produces small, round, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters.

Due to its unique properties, it is cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of rooibos tea.
