Annona australis

Annona australis, commonly known as the Australian soursop, is a tropical plant native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, spiky green fruits that turn yellow when ripe, containing a white, creamy pulp with numerous black seeds. Its flowers are fragrant and typically white or pale green, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Annona australis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
