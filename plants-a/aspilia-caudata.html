Aspilia caudata

Aspilia caudata, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia caudata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
