Achillea gussonei

Achillea gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic foliage and extended blooming period make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
