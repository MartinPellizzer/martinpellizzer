Acaena × ovina var. velutina

Acaena × ovina var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet burr, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a fine, velvety pubescence, contributing to its common name. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
