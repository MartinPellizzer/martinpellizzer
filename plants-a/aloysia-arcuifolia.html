Aloysia arcuifolia

Aloysia arcuifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Aloysia arcuifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
