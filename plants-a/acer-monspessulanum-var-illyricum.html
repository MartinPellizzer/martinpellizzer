Acer monspessulanum var. illyricum

Acer monspessulanum var. illyricum, commonly known as the Croatian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, winged samaras that disperse its seeds by wind. It prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate partial shade, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and urban forestry.
