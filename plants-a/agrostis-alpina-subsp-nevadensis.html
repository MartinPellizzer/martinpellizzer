Agrostis alpina subsp. nevadensis

Agrostis alpina subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of North America. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as alpine meadows and mountain slopes.

The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually short and compact.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges.
