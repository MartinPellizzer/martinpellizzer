Arnica austriaca

Arnica austriaca, commonly known as Austrian mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in meadows and mountain slopes. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Arnica austriaca is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
