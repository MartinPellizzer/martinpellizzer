Aletris foliosa

Aletris foliosa, commonly known as the silver root or false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root system that stores nutrients and allows it to survive in shaded, moist environments.

Aletris foliosa is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
