Arctocrania canadensis

Arctocrania canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian arctotropis, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.

Arctocrania canadensis is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
