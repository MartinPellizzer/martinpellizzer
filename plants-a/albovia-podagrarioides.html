Albovia podagrarioides

Albovia podagrarioides, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It has a fibrous root system that aids in stabilizing sandy soils.

Albovia podagrarioides is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
