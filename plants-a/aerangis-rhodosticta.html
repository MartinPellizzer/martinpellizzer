Aerangis rhodosticta

Aerangis rhodosticta, commonly known as the red-streaked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive red or pink streaking on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
