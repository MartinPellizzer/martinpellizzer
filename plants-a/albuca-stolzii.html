Albuca stolzii

Albuca stolzii, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Albuca stolzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
