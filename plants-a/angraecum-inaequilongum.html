Angraecum inaequilongum

Angraecum inaequilongum, commonly known as the long-nosed angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous white or pale pink flowers, which are adapted for pollination by moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum inaequilongum is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
