Asterophyllum galium

Asterophyllum galium, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features slender, hairy stems that produce small, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
