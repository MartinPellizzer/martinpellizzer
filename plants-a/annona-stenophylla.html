Annona stenophylla

Annona stenophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are white with a yellow center and are followed by fruits that are round and have a rough, waxy skin.

The fruit contains a sweet, creamy pulp with numerous small, hard seeds.
