Astragalus viscosus

Astragalus viscosus, commonly known as sticky milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in a sticky, glandular coating. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus viscosus is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in the western United States.
