Antimima fenestrata

Antimima fenestrata, commonly known as the window leaf or fenestrated antimony plant, is a unique member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fenestrated (hollowed-out) leaves that create a visually striking pattern. The plant originates from South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Antimima fenestrata is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
