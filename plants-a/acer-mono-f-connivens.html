Acer mono f. connivens

Acer mono f. connivens, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the mono maple.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a unique appearance compared to typical maple species. The leaves are typically smaller and more rounded, with a velvety texture. This form is often found in specific microhabitats where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and ecological niche.
