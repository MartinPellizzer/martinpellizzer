Astragalus taubertianus

Astragalus taubertianus, commonly known as Taubert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus taubertianus is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
