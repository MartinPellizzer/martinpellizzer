Astragalus semitarius

Astragalus semitarius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, hard seeds that are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Astragalus semitarius is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various pollinators.
