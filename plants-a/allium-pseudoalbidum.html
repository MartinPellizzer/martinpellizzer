Allium pseudoalbidum

Allium pseudoalbidum, commonly known as the white garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are similar in appearance to garlic but are less commonly cultivated for culinary purposes.

Allium pseudoalbidum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
