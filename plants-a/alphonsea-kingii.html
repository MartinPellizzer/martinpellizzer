Alphonsea kingii

Alphonsea kingii, commonly known as the King's alphonsea, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and it produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar to custard apples.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
