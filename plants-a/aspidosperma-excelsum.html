Aspidosperma excelsum

Aspidosperma excelsum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cupuaçu* tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's bark is thick and rugged, providing natural protection against environmental stressors. Its fruit, known as the cupuaçu, is a large, oblong pod that contains a sweet, acidic pulp rich in nutrients.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in the region.
