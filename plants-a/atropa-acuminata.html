Atropa acuminata

Atropa acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tailed nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils across the Mediterranean region. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually violet or blue, and bloom in the summer months.

The berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant a potential hazard to both humans and animals.
