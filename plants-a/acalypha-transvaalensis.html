Acalypha transvaalensis

Acalypha transvaalensis, commonly known as Transvaal glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, often displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Acalypha transvaalensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
