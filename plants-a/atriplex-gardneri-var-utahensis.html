Atriplex gardneri var. utahensis

Atriplex gardneri var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well-suited for saline soils and harsh desert conditions.

The species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
