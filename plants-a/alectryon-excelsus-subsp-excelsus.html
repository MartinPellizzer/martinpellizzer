Alectryon excelsus subsp. excelsus

Alectryon excelsus subsp.

excelsus, commonly known as the shining glorybower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and sclerophyllous forests.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
