Acaena latebrosa var. brachyphylla

Acaena latebrosa var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red, spherical fruits that are equipped with hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
