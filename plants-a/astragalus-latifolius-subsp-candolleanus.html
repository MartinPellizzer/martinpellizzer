Astragalus latifolius subsp. candolleanus

Astragalus latifolius subsp.

candolleanus, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant features compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
