Acacia caringa

Acacia caringa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical flowers that are usually yellow or orange, which appear in clusters during the warmer months. Acacia caringa thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
