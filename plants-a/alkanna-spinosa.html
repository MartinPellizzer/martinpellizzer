Alkanna spinosa

Alkanna spinosa, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. Alkanna spinosa is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
