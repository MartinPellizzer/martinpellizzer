Anemia affinis

Anemia affinis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in height.

Anemia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
