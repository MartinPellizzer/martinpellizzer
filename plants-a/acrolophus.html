Acrolophus, commonly known as the acrolophus moth or false tiger moths, is a genus of moths in the family Erebidae.

These insects are characterized by their distinctive wing patterns, often featuring bold, contrasting colors and intricate designs. While not plants themselves, they are often associated with specific plant species during their larval stages. Their larvae feed on a variety of plants, including grasses and sedges, making them important in ecological food webs.

The genus is distributed across various regions, including North America and parts of Asia.
