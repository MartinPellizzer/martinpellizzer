Allium jucundum

Allium jucundum, commonly known as the sweet leek, is a hardy perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant produces edible, bulbous roots that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
