Armeria villosa subsp. serpentinicola

Armeria villosa subsp.

serpentinicola, commonly known as the serpentine pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright pink to magenta flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, calcareous, or serpentine soils, making it a characteristic species of rocky and coastal habitats. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and strong coastal winds.
