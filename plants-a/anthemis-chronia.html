Anthemis chronia

Anthemis chronia, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are often surrounded by a ring of pale green leaves. Anthemis chronia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.
