Acacia cognata

Acacia cognata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia cognata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
