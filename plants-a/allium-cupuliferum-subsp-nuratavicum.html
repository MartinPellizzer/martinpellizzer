Allium cupuliferum subsp. nuratavicum

Allium cupuliferum subsp.

nuratavicum, commonly known as the Nuratav mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and geographic distribution.
