Acaena sericea var. gracilis

Acaena sericea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender burrplant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, needle-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, spiny fruits that are typically red or brown, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
