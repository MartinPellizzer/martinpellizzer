Anemia villosa var. deltoidea

Anemia villosa var. deltoidea, commonly known as the delta hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of large, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
