Acacia venulosa var. lanata

Acacia venulosa var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescent foliage, which gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia venulosa var. lanata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
