Aphaca disperma

Aphaca disperma, commonly known as the scattered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aphaca disperma thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
