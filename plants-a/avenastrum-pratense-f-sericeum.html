Avenastrum pratense f. sericeum

Avenastrum pratense f. sericeum, commonly known as the silky meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and features elongated, narrow leaves with a silky, hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This fern-like grass is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
