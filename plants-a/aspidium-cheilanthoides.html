Aspidium cheilanthoides

Aspidium cheilanthoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aspidium cheilanthoides is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.
