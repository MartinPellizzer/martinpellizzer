Aster lingulatus

Aster lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive narrow, pointed appearance.

Aster lingulatus produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
