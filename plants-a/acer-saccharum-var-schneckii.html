Acer saccharum var. schneckii

Acer saccharum var. schneckii, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which ranges from bright orange to deep crimson. The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, while its fruit is a pair of twin samaras.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
