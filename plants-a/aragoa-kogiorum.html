Aragoa kogiorum

Aragoa kogiorum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like blooms that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Aragoa kogiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
