Apocynum cannabinum var. bolanderi

Apocynum cannabinum var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's dogbane, is a variant of the perennial herb native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
