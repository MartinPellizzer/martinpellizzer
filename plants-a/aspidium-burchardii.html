Aspidium burchardii

Aspidium burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically pinnate or bipinnate in structure. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to substrates like rock or soil.

Aspidium burchardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
