Alchemilla amani

Alchemilla amani, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Alchemilla amani is often used in rock gardens and alpine plantings due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage.
