Aspalathus angustifolia

Aspalathus angustifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is most famous for its use in producing rooibos tea, which is rich in antioxidants and has a distinctive earthy flavor.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a key species in the tea industry.
