Amaryllis × breviflora

Amaryllis × breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis bellonis and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its compact size and produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, with trumpet-shaped flowers that are often speckled or streaked. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for a vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both cultivated gardens and botanical collections.
