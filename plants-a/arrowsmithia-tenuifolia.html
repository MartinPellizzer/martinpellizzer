Arrowsmithia tenuifolia

Arrowsmithia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically less than an inch in width. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
