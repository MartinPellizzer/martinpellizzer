Anemone alpina f. flavescens

Anemone alpina f. flavescens, commonly known as the Alpine anemone, is a variant of the alpine anemone native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, while its stem is usually slender and unbranched. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a central yellow stamen that gives the appearance of a crown.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
