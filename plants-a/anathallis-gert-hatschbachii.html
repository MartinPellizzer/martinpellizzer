Anathallis gert-hatschbachii

Anathallis gert-hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatzschbach's spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with elongated, slender flowers that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in its humid, shaded habitat. Its flowers are predominantly white with a subtle greenish tint, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
