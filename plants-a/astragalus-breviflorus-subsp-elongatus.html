Astragalus breviflorus subsp. elongatus

Astragalus breviflorus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the long-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated floral structure compared to the typical form of A. breviflorus. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and provides forage for wildlife.
