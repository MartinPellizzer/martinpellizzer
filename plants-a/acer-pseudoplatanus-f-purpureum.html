Acer pseudoplatanus f. purpureum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which develops throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges and a glossy texture. This form thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
