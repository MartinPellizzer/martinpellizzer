Asplenium serra var. incurvatum

Asplenium serra var. incurvatum, commonly known as the hooked spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or rolled at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a serrated edge, which is a key identifying feature.

This variety is relatively rare and is often found in specific geographic regions with suitable microclimates.
