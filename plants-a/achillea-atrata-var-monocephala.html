Achillea atrata var. monocephala

Achillea atrata var. monocephala, commonly known as the black yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
