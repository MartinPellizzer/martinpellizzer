Agrostis vinealis subsp. kudoi

Agrostis vinealis subsp.

kudoi, commonly known as Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its tolerance to moderate grazing.

Botanically, it is notable for its rhizomatous growth habit and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
