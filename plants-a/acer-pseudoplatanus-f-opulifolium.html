Acer pseudoplatanus f. opulifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus f. opulifolium, commonly known as the woolly maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, woolly appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically growing in temperate regions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
