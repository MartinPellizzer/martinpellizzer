Artemisia matricarioides

Artemisia matricarioides, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Artemisia matricarioides is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
