Anacharis occidentalis

Anacharis occidentalis, commonly known as the Brazilian waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are opposite, arranged in pairs along the stem, and are often oval-shaped.

Anacharis occidentalis is commonly used in aquariums due to its fast growth rate and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.
