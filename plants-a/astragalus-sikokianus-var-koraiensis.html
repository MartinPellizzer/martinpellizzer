Astragalus sikokianus var. koraiensis

Astragalus sikokianus var. koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its root system is notable for its deep taproot, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
