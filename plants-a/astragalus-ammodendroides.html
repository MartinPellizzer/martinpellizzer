Astragalus ammodendroides

Astragalus ammodendroides, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus ammodendroides is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
