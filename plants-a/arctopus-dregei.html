Arctopus dregei

Arctopus dregei, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of pale blue to purple disk florets.

Arctopus dregei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
