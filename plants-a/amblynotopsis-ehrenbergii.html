Amblynotopsis ehrenbergii

Amblynotopsis ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help store water, adapting it to survive in dry environments. It blooms during the cooler months, typically in winter or early spring.

Amblynotopsis ehrenbergii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
