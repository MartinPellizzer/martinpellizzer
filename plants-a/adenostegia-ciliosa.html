Adenostegia ciliosa

Adenostegia ciliosa, commonly known as the hairy stemless sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stem and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adenostegia ciliosa is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
