Ampelopsis palmiloba

Ampelopsis palmiloba, commonly known as the palm-leaf honeysuckle, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five palmately arranged leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its berries are red when mature and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Ampelopsis palmiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and naturalized areas.
