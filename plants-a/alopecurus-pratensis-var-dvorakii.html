Alopecurus pratensis var. dvorakii

Alopecurus pratensis var. dvorakii, commonly known as the Dvorak's meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe, characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of grassland ecosystems.
