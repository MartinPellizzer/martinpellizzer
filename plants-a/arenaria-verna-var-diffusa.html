Arenaria verna var. diffusa

Arenaria verna var. diffusa, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is considered rare in the wild.
