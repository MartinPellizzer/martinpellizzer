Adenostemma viscosum var. parviflorum

Adenostemma viscosum var. parviflorum, commonly known as sticky false flax, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its stems are often branching and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

It is part of the Acanthaceae family and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
