Aristida purpurea var. berlandieri

Aristida purpurea var. berlandieri, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, wiry stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
