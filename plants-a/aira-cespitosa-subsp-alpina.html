Aira cespitosa subsp. alpina

Aira cespitosa subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine feathergrass, is a grass species native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high elevations.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.
