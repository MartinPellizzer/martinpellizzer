Amellus oxylepis

Amellus oxylepis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Amellus oxylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
