Abutilon tiubae var. parviflorum

Abutilon tiubae var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered velvetleaf, is a variant of the Abutilon genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
