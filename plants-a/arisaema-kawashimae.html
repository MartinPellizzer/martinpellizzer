Arisaema kawashimae

Arisaema kawashimae, commonly known as the Kawashima arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into berries, which are edible when ripe.

Arisaema kawashimae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
