Astilbe shikokiana var. sikokumontana

Astilbe shikokiana var. sikokumontana, commonly known as the Shikoku astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features finely divided, fern-like foliage that adds a delicate texture to garden landscapes. The plant produces panicles of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
