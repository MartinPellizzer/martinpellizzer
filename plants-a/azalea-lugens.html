Azalea lugens

Azalea lugens, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea lugens prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
