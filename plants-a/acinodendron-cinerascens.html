Acinodendron cinerascens

Acinodendron cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ash-gray leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and often flaky, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
