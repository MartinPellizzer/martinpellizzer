Atractylodes cuneata

Atractylodes cuneata, commonly known as Chinese Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

The rhizomes are the primary part used, containing compounds like volatile oils and sesquiterpenes.
