Antidesma macrophyllum

Antidesma macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
