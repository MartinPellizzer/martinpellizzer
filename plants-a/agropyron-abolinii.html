Agropyron abolinii

Agropyron abolinii, commonly known as the Transcaucasian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Agropyron abolinii is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
