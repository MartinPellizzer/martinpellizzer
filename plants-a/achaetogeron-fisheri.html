Achaetogeron fisheri

Achaetogeron fisheri, commonly known as the Fisher's ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, almost translucent fronds that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments, typically in shaded forest understories. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with sporangia clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
