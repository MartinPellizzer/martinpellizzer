Athyrium kawakamii

Athyrium kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami's lady fern, is a rare and endangered fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
