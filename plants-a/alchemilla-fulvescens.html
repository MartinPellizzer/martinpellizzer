Alchemilla fulvescens

Alchemilla fulvescens, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Alchemilla fulvescens is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded areas.

It is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
