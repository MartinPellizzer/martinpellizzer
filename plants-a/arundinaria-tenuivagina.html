Arundinaria tenuivagina

Arundinaria tenuivagina, commonly known as the narrow sheath bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria tenuivagina is valued for its durability and flexibility, making it a useful resource in various cultural and practical applications.
