Asplenium anthraxacolepis

Asplenium anthraxacolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually narrow and have a distinctive, creeping growth habit.

Asplenium anthraxacolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
