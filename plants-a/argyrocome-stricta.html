Argyrocome stricta

Argyrocome stricta, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a member of the Lecanoraceae family.

It is a foliose lichen that forms a thin, crust-like growth on rock surfaces, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a distinctive silver-gray appearance due to the presence of a dense layer of calcium oxalate crystals on its upper surface. It is typically found in regions with low humidity and high sunlight exposure.

Argyrocome stricta plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a bioindicator of environmental conditions.
