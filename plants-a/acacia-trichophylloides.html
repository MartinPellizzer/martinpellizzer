Acacia trichophylloides

Acacia trichophylloides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
