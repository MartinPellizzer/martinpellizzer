Anaphalis staintonii

Anaphalis staintonii, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and fluffy, white capitula that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, achene-like fruits.

Anaphalis staintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
