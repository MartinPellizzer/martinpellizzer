Arabis pendulocarpa

Arabis pendulocarpa, commonly known as the hanging cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and toothed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Arabis pendulocarpa typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
