Aconitum brevicalcaratum var. parviflorum

Aconitum brevicalcaratum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are typically covered in short, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
