Anathallis edmeiae

Anathallis edmeiae, commonly known as the Edmeia's spider orchid, is a rare and exquisite flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, showcasing a fascinating mutualistic relationship.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Anathallis edmeiae is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
