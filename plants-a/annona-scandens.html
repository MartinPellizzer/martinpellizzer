Annona scandens

Annona scandens, commonly known as the custard apple vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant's fruit, resembling a small, green apple, is edible and has a creamy, custard-like texture when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences.
