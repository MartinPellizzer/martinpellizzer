Adamia sylvatica

Adamia sylvatica, commonly known as the redwood fern, is a unique plant native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a type of fern that exhibits a distinctive, upright growth habit with finely divided, lace-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its ecological importance and slow growth rate, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
