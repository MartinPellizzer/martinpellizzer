Acer mono f. horizontale

Acer mono f. horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal maple, is a unique variant of the single-trunk maple.

It is characterized by its horizontally spreading branches that create a distinctive, rounded canopy. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, red samaras that are released in the spring.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
