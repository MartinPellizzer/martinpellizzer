Abacopteris glandulosa

Abacopteris glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky matted rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, glandular leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
