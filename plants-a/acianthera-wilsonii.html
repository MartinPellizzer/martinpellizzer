Acianthera wilsonii

Acianthera wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers with prominent lips, which are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and montane regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
