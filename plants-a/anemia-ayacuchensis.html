Anemia ayacuchensis

Anemia ayacuchensis, commonly known as the Ayacuchensis plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.

Anemia ayacuchensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
