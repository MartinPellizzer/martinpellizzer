Allium roxburghii

Allium roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan leek, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
