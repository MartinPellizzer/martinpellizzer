Agathomeris amaranthoides

Agathomeris amaranthoides, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, with succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its growth habit is often prostrate or spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in xerophytic gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also appreciated in horticulture.
