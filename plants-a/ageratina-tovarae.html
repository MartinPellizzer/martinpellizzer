Ageratina tovarae

Ageratina tovarae, commonly known as the Tovar's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or purple in color.

Ageratina tovarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
