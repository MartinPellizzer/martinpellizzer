Acacia hainanensis

Acacia hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Hainan Island in China.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading crown and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Acacia hainanensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
