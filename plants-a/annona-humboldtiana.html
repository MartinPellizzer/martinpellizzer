Annona humboldtiana

Annona humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, spiny fruit with a thick, yellowish skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet, and aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
