Asplenium insolitum

Asplenium insolitum, commonly known as the rare fern, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Asplenium insolitum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
