Amauropelta atrorubens

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that display a striking red and black coloration, particularly on the underside. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
