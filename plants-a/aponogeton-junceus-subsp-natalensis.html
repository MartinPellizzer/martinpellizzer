Aponogeton junceus subsp. natalensis

Aponogeton junceus subsp.

natalensis, commonly known as Natal waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to southern Africa. It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 10–30 cm in length and 1–2 cm in width.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often submerged, though they may occasionally emerge near the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
