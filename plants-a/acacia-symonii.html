Acacia symonii

Acacia symonii, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, thorn-like leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions. Its unique appearance has made it a subject of botanical interest and conservation efforts.

Acacia symonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
