Aurantium × myrtifolium

Aurantium × myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtifolium orange, is a hybrid citrus plant that combines traits from *Citrus aurantium* and *Citrus myrtifolia*.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, orange-colored fruits. The fruit has a thick, segmented rind and a juicy, acidic pulp, making it suitable for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
