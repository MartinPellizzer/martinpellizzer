Amomum aquaticum

Amomum aquaticum, commonly known as water ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emerging from water or moist soil. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum aquaticum plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
