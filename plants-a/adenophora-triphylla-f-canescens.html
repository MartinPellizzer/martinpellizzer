Adenophora triphylla f. canescens

Adenophora triphylla f. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

The species is part of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.
