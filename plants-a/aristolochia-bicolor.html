Aristolochia bicolor

Aristolochia bicolor, commonly known as the yellow Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a yellow interior. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is known for its unique floral structure and is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.
