Ageratina molinae

Ageratina molinae, commonly known as Molina's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flower heads that are often surrounded by white or pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Ageratina molinae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in garden settings in regions with a suitable climate.
