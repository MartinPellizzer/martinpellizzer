Abutilon membranifolium

Abutilon membranifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Abutilon membranifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
