Abarema clypearia var. angulata

Abarema clypearia var. angulata, commonly known as the angular legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
