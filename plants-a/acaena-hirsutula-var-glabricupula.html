Acaena hirsutula var. glabricupula

Acaena hirsutula var. glabricupula, commonly known as the smooth cupule acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, cup-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. Its foliage is generally hairy, though the variety glabricupula has reduced hair on the cupules.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
