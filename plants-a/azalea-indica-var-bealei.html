Azalea indica var. bealei

Azalea indica var. bealei, commonly known as the Beale azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
