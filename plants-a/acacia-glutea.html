Acacia glutea

Acacia glutea, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia glutea is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in savannas and rocky areas.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of fiber and in traditional medicine.
