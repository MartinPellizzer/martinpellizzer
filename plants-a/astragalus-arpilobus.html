Astragalus arpilobus

Astragalus arpilobus, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds with persistent, hooked tendrils, aiding in seed dispersal.

Astragalus arpilobus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
