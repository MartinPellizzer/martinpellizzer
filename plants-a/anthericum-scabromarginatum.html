Anthericum scabromarginatum

Anthericum scabromarginatum, commonly known as the rough-margin nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf morphology and unique floral structure.
