Acacia poilanei

Acacia poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

Acacia poilanei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
