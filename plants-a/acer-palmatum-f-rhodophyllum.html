Acer palmatum f. rhodophyllum

Acer palmatum f. rhodophyllum, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking foliage.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in winter, and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a compact growth habit with slender, weeping branches that add ornamental value to gardens.

Botanically, it is valued for its unique leaf coloration and is often used in horticultural displays.
