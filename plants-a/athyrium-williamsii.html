Athyrium williamsii

Athyrium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often divided into numerous small, triangular pinnae. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of fine, hairy tissue, which helps in water retention.

Athyrium williamsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
