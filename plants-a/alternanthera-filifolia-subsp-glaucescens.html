Alternanthera filifolia subsp. glaucescens

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mist flower, is a perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is often used in aquatic gardens or as a foreground plant in water features due to its compact growth habit.
