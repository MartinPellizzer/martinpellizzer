Allium drobovii

Allium drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii onion, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.

Allium drobovii is also noted for its ornamental value due to its delicate floral display.
