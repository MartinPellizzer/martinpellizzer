Anthemis bulgarica

Anthemis bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Anthemis bulgarica is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
