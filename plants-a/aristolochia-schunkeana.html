Aristolochia schunkeana

Aristolochia schunkeana, commonly known as the Schunke's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, typically greenish-yellow with a prominent purple or brown throat. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique foliage.
