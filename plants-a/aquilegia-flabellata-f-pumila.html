Aquilegia flabellata f. pumila

Aquilegia flabellata f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf garden hellebore, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
