Azalea atlantica var. luteoalba

Azalea atlantica var. luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow-white azalea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to white in color. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
