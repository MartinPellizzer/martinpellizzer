Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp. australe

Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the southern common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble small snout-like structures.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats like roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
