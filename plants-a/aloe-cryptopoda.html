Aloe cryptopoda

Aloe cryptopoda, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the sandy soils it inhabits.

Aloe cryptopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
