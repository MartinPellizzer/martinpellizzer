Acacia stenophylla var. linearis

Acacia stenophylla var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
