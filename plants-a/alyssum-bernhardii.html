Alyssum bernhardii

Alyssum bernhardii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Alyssum bernhardii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
