Astragalus pileh-khasehensis

Astragalus pileh-khasehensis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.
