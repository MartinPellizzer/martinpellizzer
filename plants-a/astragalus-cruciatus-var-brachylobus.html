Astragalus cruciatus var. brachylobus

Astragalus cruciatus var. brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
