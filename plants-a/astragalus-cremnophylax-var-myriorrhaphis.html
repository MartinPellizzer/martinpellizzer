Astragalus cremnophylax var. myriorrhaphis

Astragalus cremnophylax var. myriorrhaphis, commonly known as the Sierra lily or mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
