Aspidium paucijugum

Aspidium paucijugum, commonly known as the few-lobed water fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its spores are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its natural habitat.

The plant's botanical structure includes a rhizome that supports its growth and helps in nutrient absorption.
