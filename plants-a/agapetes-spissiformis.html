Agapetes spissiformis

Agapetes spissiformis, commonly known as the dense-leaved silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery bloom. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Agapetes spissiformis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
