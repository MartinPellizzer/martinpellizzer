Aster calendulaceus

Aster calendulaceus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Aster calendulaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
