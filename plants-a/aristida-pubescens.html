Aristida pubescens

Aristida pubescens, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often pale green to brownish in color.

Aristida pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
