Asphodelus parviflorus

Asphodelus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spikes of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, fleshy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Asphodelus parviflorus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
