Andropogon glabriusculus

Andropogon glabriusculus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
