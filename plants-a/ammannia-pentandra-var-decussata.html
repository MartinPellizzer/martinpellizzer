Ammannia pentandra var. decussata

Ammannia pentandra var. decussata, commonly known as the redroot sandbarren, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
