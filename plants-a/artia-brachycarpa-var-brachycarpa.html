Artia brachycarpa var. brachycarpa

Artia brachycarpa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited artia, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a fibrous root system. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces capsule fruits that remain attached to the plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
