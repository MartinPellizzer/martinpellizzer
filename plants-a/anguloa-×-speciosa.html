Anguloa × speciosa

Anguloa × speciosa, commonly known as the hybrid angel's trumpets, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anguloa elegans and Anguloa pubescens.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is native to the Andes region of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces striking, fragrant blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in length, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide variety of flower colors and growth forms, enhancing its appeal in horticulture.
