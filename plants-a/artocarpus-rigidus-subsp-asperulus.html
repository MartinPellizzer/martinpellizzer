Artocarpus rigidus subsp. asperulus

Artocarpus rigidus subsp.

asperulus, commonly known as the rough-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a fibrous husk. The fruit is typically consumed when ripe, offering a starchy, sweet pulp.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional uses in local cuisines.
