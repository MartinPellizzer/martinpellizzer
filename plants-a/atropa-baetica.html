Atropa baetica

Atropa baetica, commonly known as the Spanish nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dark purple berries and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are bell-shaped and range in color from white to purple, and it produces toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
