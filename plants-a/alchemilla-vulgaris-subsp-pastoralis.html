Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. pastoralis

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

pastoralis, commonly known as the common lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and damp gardens. Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

pastoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
