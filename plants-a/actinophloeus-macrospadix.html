Actinophloeus macrospadix

Actinophloeus macrospadix, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, making it a prominent feature in its habitat. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater stands. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

The flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, are typically found in clusters and play a role in its reproductive cycle.
