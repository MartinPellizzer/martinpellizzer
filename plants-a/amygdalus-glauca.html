Amygdalus glauca

Amygdalus glauca, commonly known as the Chinese apricot or sand apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that mature into red, fuzzy drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Amygdalus glauca is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
