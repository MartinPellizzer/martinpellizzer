Astragalus lusitanicus

Astragalus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Iberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus lusitanicus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
