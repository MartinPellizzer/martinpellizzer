Aethionema graecum var. pseudogracile

Aethionema graecum var. pseudogracile, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a compact, low-growing plant with dense clusters of small, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. The plant typically features gray-green, woolly leaves and produces flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
