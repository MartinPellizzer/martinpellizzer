Axonopus fissifolius var. coronatus

Axonopus fissifolius var. coronatus, commonly known as the crowned carpetgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive light green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Its varietal name, *coronatus*, refers to the crown-like appearance of its leaf bases.
