Alpinia oblonga

Alpinia oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters at the base of the plant.

Alpinia oblonga is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
