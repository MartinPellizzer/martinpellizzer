Abama californica

Abama californica, commonly known as the California abalone, is a species of sea snail found in the coastal waters of California.

It belongs to the family Muricidae and is characterized by its spiral-shaped shell and vibrant coloration. The shell is typically adorned with intricate patterns and ridges, providing camouflage in its marine environment. This species plays an important role in the ecosystem by contributing to the biodiversity of rocky intertidal zones.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
