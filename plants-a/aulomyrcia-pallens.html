Aulomyrcia pallens

Aulomyrcia pallens, commonly known as the white-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers have five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Aulomyrcia pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
