Acarpha australis

Acarpha australis, commonly known as the Australian grass or coastal grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense clusters at the base of the stems. Acarpha australis is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
