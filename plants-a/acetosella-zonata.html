Acetosella zonata

Acetosella zonata, commonly known as the spotted sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
