Alstroemeria secundifolia

Alstroemeria secundifolia, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb with sword-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a flowering stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Alstroemeria secundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
