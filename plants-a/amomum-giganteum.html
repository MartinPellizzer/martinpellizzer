Amomum giganteum

Amomum giganteum, commonly known as giant cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Amomum giganteum is often cultivated for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
