Allium eulae

Allium eulae, commonly known as the Eula onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like daffodils and snowdrops. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its bulbs are small and white, often used in traditional medicine.

Allium eulae is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in herbal remedies.
