Apuleia leiocarpa

Apuleia leiocarpa, commonly known as the yellow poinciana, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often marked with fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
