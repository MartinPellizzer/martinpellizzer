Adiantopsis × australopedata

Adiantopsis × australopedata, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Adiantopsis australis and Adiantopsis pedata.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates. Its fronds are bi-lobed and exhibit a graceful, arching growth habit.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
