Aster chamissonis

Aster chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster chamissonis is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
