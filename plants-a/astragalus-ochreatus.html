Astragalus ochreatus

Astragalus ochreatus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Astragalus ochreatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
