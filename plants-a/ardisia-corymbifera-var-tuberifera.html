Ardisia corymbifera var. tuberifera

Ardisia corymbifera var. tuberifera, commonly known as the tuberous ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant's tuberous roots contribute to its ability to store moisture and nutrients, aiding its survival in diverse soil conditions.
