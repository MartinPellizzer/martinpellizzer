Arctium tomentellum

Arctium tomentellum, commonly known as the downy burdock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a cluster, typical of the daisy family.

The plant produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.
