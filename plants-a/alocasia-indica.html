Alocasia indica

Alocasia indica, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. Alocasia indica is a member of the Araceae family, which includes plants like peace lilies and calla lilies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
