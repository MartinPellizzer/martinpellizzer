Argyrolobium incanum

Argyrolobium incanum, commonly known as the silver globular bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and spherical clusters of yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Argyrolobium incanum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
