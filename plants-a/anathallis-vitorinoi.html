Anathallis vitorinoi

Anathallis vitorinoi, commonly known as the Vitorino's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinct, intricate structure that facilitates specific pollination by flies.

Due to habitat loss, Anathallis vitorinoi is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
