Archidendron ellipticum

Archidendron ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its woody stems are often branched and covered in a rough, bark-like texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
