Agave mooreana

Agave mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, blue-green, sword-like leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Agave mooreana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
