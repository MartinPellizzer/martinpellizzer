Amaranthus angustifolius var. graecizans

Amaranthus angustifolius var. graecizans, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1-2 meters in height. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue due to the presence of a waxy coating. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense panicles, and the plant produces tiny, three-angled seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
