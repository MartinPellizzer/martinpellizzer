Astragalus australis var. cottonii

Astragalus australis var. cottonii, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
