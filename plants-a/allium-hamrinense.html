Allium hamrinense

Allium hamrinense, commonly known as the Hamrin onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its stout, cylindrical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Allium hamrinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
