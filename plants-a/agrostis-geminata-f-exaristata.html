Agrostis geminata f. exaristata

Agrostis geminata f. exaristata, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass genus characterized by its distinctive tufted growth habit.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense, mat-like turf, making it suitable for specialized horticultural uses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
