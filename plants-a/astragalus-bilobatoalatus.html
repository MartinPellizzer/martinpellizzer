Astragalus bilobatoalatus

Astragalus bilobatoalatus, commonly known as the two-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
