Amaranthus paniculatus var. sanguineus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the blood amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly earthy flavor, making it a valuable crop for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its use in sustainable agriculture.
