Aalius pubescens

Aalius pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aalius, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aalius pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
