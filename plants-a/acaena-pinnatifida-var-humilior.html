Acaena pinnatifida var. humilior

Acaena pinnatifida var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf New Zealand knapweed, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, prickly fruits known as achenes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
