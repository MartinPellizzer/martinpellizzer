Aster canus subsp. rossicus

Aster canus subsp.

rossicus, commonly known as the Ross's golden aster, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats, often in alpine or tundra environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish to reddish, and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, blooming in late summer.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.
