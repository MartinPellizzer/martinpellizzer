Astragalus khoshjailensis

Astragalus khoshjailensis, commonly known as the Khoshjail Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, exhibiting deep root systems to access moisture. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

However, it is a rare species with limited distribution and requires specific ecological conditions to thrive.
