Acer saccharum f. villipes

Acer saccharum f. villipes, commonly known as the red maple, is a cultivar of the sugar maple, characterized by its striking red foliage in the fall.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are palmate, with three to five lobes, and are often adorned with red or orange veins during the autumn season. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring, while its fruits are samaras that disperse seeds via wind.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
