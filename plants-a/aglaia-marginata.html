Aglaia marginata

Aglaia marginata, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, also known as the mahogany family, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by round, green fruits containing smooth, dark brown seeds.

Aglaia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
