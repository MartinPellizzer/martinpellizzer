Agave monostachya

Agave monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing numerous white to pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave monostachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for its fibers.
