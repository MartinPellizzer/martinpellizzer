Asclepias rafaelensis

Asclepias rafaelensis, commonly known as Rafael's milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a common feature among milkweeds. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid environments.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological importance.
