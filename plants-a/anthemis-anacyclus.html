Anthemis anacyclus

Anthemis anacyclus, commonly known as the goatweed or field chamomile, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features small, yellow flowers with a white center, resembling those of chamomile, and has a characteristic scent when crushed. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Anthemis anacyclus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and calming properties.
