Acrocomia panamensis

Acrocomia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian palm, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
