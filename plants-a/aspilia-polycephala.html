Aspilia polycephala

Aspilia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features a short, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in clusters at the top of the plant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aspilia polycephala is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
