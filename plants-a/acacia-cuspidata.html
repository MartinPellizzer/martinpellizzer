Acacia cuspidata

Acacia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its roots form nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
