Amelanchier alnifolia var. subintegra

Amelanchier alnifolia var. subintegra, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It features simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

The berries are valued for their sweet flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
