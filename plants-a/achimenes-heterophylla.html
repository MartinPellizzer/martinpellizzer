Achimenes heterophylla

Achimenes heterophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf *achimenes*, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with contrasting yellow throats. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens and greenhouses.

Achimenes heterophylla is known for its ability to produce numerous tubers, which allow it to survive in adverse conditions.
