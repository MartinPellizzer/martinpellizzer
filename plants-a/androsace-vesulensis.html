Androsace vesulensis

Androsace vesulensis, commonly known as the Vesuvius sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the volcanic regions of southern Italy.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features a dense rosette of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, forming a showy cluster at the center of the plant.

Androsace vesulensis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
