Aster megalanthus

Aster megalanthus, commonly known as the giant blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features sturdy, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with toothed edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster megalanthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
