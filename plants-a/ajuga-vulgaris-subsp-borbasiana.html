Ajuga vulgaris subsp. borbasiana

Ajuga vulgaris subsp.

borbasiana, commonly known as carpet bugle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens and naturalized settings.
