Achillea calcarea

Achillea calcarea, commonly known as limestone yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in flat-topped clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
