Acaena caespitosa

Acaena caespitosa, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, mat-forming growth habit, characterized by its spiny stems and small, lobed leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, spherical, and covered in hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Acaena caespitosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
