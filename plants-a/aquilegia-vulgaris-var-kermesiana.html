Aquilegia vulgaris var. kermesiana

Aquilegia vulgaris var. kermesiana, commonly known as the Kermesian columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that form a striking blue or purple coloration. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, which are dark green and slightly hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
