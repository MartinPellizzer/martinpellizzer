Aristolochia hirsuta

Aristolochia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding flower structure.
