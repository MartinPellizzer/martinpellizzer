Amerimnon acutum

Amerimnon acutum, commonly known as the sharpleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Amerimnon acutum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
