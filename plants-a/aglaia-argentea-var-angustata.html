Aglaia argentea var. angustata

Aglaia argentea var. angustata, commonly known as silver flame tree or narrow-leaf aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its lanceolate, silver-mottled leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, yellowish-orange fruits. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.
