Alchemilla pseudodecumbens

Alchemilla pseudodecumbens, commonly known as the creeping lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla pseudodecumbens is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
