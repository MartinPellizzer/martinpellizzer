Aethionema stylosum

Aethionema stylosum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive central gland. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

Aethionema stylosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
