Allium maximowiczii var. yezomonticola

Allium maximowiczii var. yezomonticola, commonly known as the Yezomonticola garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
