Albizia tonkinensis

Albizia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and parks.
