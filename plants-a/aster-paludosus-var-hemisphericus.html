Aster paludosus var. hemisphericus

Aster paludosus var. hemisphericus, commonly known as the swamp aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and small animals.
