Acetosella multicaulis

Acetosella multicaulis, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Acetosella multicaulis is often found growing in rocky or calcareous soils.
