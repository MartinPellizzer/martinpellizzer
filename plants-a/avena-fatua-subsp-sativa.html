Avena fatua subsp. sativa

Avena fatua subsp.

sativa, commonly known as cultivated oat, is a domesticated variant of the wild oat species. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which develop into oval, husked grains. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutritious seeds used in food and animal feed.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop with agricultural and nutritional significance.
