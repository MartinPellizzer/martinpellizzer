Anemone narcissiflora subsp. typica

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

typica, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
