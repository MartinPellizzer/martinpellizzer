Amygdalus gaertneriana

Amygdalus gaertneriana, commonly known as the Gaertner's almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a woody husk, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
