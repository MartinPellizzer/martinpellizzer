Astragalus brauntonii

Astragalus brauntonii, commonly known as Braunton's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
