Attalea agrestis

Attalea agrestis, commonly known as the wild palm or Brazilian buriti, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a slender, unbranched trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of black, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
