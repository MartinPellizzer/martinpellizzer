Annona acuminata

Annona acuminata, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellowish fruit with a sweet and creamy pulp, often speckled with small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
