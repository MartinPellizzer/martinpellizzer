Alstroemeria peregrina

Alstroemeria peregrina, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria peregrina is also valued for its ability to tolerate cooler climates and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
