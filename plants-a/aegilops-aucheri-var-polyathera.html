Aegilops aucheri var. polyathera

Aegilops aucheri var. polyathera, commonly known as the Armenian grass or Armenian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in ecological restoration projects.
