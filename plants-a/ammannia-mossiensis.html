Ammannia mossiensis

Ammannia mossiensis, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Ammannia mossiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
