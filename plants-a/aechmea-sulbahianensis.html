Aechmea sulbahianensis

Aechmea sulbahianensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Aechmea sulbahianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Morphology

Aechmea sulbahianensis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color with a waxy coating.

The leaves are arranged in a spiral around the central stem, which is short and often covered in a fibrous, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces inflorescences with long, spiky bracts that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and yellow, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds. The fruit is a berry that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in rocky or sandy soils.
