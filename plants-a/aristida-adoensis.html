Aristida adoensis

Aristida adoensis, commonly known as the African grass or slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
