Adelobotrys latifolius

Adelobotrys latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf adelobotrys, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
