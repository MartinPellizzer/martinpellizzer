Alsine rostrata proles lanuginosa

Alsine rostrata proles lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly alsike, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
