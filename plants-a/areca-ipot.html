Areca ipot

Areca ipot, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Areca ipot is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are sometimes harvested for thatching or weaving purposes.
