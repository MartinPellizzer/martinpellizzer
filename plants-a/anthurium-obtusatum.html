Anthurium obtusatum

Anthurium obtusatum, commonly known as the blunt-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that house the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium obtusatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
