Averrhoa

Averrhoa, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a bright red outer shell enclosing segmented, white, and sweet flesh. The botanical features of Averrhoa include its aromatic essential oils and the presence of bioactive compounds in its fruit.

It is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its medicinal properties.
