Asplenium petrarchae

Asplenium petrarchae, commonly known as the Petrarch's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium petrarchae is a rare and ancient species, with a long evolutionary history dating back to the Paleozoic era.
