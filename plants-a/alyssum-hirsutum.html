Alyssum hirsutum

Alyssum hirsutum, commonly known as hairy alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features compact clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has fuzzy, hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum hirsutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
