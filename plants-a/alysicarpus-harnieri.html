Alysicarpus harnieri

Alysicarpus harnieri, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moderately wet conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
