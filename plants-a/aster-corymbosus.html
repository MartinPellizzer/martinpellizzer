Aster corymbosus

Aster corymbosus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Aster corymbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
