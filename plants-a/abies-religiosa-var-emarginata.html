Abies religiosa var. emarginata

Abies religiosa var. emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a variant of the sacred fir native to Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dark green, with a fragrant aroma, and the cones are small and cylindrical. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
