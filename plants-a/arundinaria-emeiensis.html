Arundinaria emeiensis

Arundinaria emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei bamboo, is a rare and ancient species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are long and narrow with a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority.
