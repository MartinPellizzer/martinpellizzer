Atriplex rusbyi

Atriplex rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Atriplex rusbyi also plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
