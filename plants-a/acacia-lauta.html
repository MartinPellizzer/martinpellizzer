Acacia lauta

Acacia lauta, commonly known as the giant wattle, is a tall, woody shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, phyllodes that are typically straight or slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia lauta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
