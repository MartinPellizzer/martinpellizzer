Arrhenatherum elatius var. aristatum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. aristatum, commonly known as the aristate false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
