Arthrosamanea leptophylla var. guineensis

Arthrosamanea leptophylla var. guineensis, commonly known as the African palm or Guinean palm, is a flowering plant species native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, which eventually develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
