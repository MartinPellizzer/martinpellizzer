Alseis pickelii

Alseis pickelii, commonly known as the Pickel's alseis, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Alseis pickelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
