Ageratina reserva

Ageratina reserva, commonly known as the velvetleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant is known for its hairy, velvety leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Ageratina reserva produces small, pale blue or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
