Acosta calvescens

Acosta calvescens, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Acosta calvescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
