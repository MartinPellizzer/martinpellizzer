Anthurium cerropirrense

Anthurium cerropirrense, commonly known as the Cerro Pirre anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. Its foliage is often a deep green with a metallic sheen, and the spathe can range from red to orange in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
