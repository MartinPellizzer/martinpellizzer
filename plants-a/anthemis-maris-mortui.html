Anthemis maris-mortui

Anthemis maris-mortui, commonly known as the sea death flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often found in areas with strong sea winds and limited freshwater availability.
