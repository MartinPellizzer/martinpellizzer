Apteria aphylla var. hymenanthera

Apteria aphylla var. hymenanthera, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for stabilizing sandy soils and supporting desert ecosystems.
