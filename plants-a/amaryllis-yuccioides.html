Amaryllis yuccioides

Amaryllis yuccioides, commonly known as the Yucca-like amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
