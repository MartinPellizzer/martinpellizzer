Aconitum × exaltatum

Aconitum × exaltatum, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-shaped cluster, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost poisonous appearance.

Aconitum × exaltatum prefers well-drained, fertile soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
