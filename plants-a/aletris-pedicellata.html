Aletris pedicellata

Aletris pedicellata, commonly known as the pink root or purple cornsnake plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple, depending on the variety. Aletris pedicellata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
