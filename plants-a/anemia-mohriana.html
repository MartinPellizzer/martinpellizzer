Anemia mohriana

Anemia mohriana, commonly known as the Mohriana anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Anemia mohriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
