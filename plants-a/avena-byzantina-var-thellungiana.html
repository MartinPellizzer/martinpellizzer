Avena byzantina var. thellungiana

Avena byzantina var. thellungiana, commonly known as the Turkish oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in traditional landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
