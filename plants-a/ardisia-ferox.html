Ardisia ferox

Ardisia ferox, commonly known as the red-rooted evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping. Its flowers are white and clustered in small umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ardisia ferox is valued for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
