Adenium somalense var. crispum

Adenium somalense var. crispum, commonly known as the Somalian adenium or the crinkled adenium, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
