Andropogon consimilis

Andropogon consimilis, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a common component of native grasslands and prairies.

Andropogon consimilis plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
