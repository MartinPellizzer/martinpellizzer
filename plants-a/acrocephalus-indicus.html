Acrocephalus indicus

Acrocephalus indicus, commonly known as the Indian reed, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are often seen rising above the foliage, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus indicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
