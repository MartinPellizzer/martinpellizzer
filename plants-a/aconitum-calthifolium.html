Aconitum calthifolium

Aconitum calthifolium, commonly known as the lady's mantle aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream sides.

Aconitum calthifolium contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution when handling.
