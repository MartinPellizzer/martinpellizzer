Aster scabridus

Aster scabridus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aster scabridus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
