Alectorolophus × puberulus

Alectorolophus × puberulus, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its textured appearance.

Alectorolophus × puberulus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
