Aniba cinnamomiflora

Aniba cinnamomiflora, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or cinnamomum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and twigs, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
