Asparagus aphyllus var. bovei

Asparagus aphyllus var. bovei, commonly known as the Bove's asparagus, is a rare and unique variant of the asparagus plant.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage, with only a few small leaves emerging from the base. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are typically slender and branching, forming a compact, shrub-like structure.

This variety is native to specific regions in southern Africa and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
