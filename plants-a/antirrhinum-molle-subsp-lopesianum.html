Antirrhinum molle subsp. lopesianum

Antirrhinum molle subsp.

lopesianum, commonly known as the Lopesian toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy flowers.
