Alsophila stuebelii

Alsophila stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps anchor the plant in moist, shaded environments.

Alsophila stuebelii is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
