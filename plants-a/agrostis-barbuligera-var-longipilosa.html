Agrostis barbuligera var. longipilosa

Agrostis barbuligera var. longipilosa, commonly known as the long-haired bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, low-growing habit, often forming mats on the ground. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are covered with long, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
