Acanthopanax setulosus

Acanthopanax setulosus, commonly known as the rough-septed dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
