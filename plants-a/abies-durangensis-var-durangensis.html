Abies durangensis var. durangensis

Abies durangensis var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often growing in dense stands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
