Abutilon pauciflorum var. canotomentosum

Abutilon pauciflorum var. canotomentosum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic value.
