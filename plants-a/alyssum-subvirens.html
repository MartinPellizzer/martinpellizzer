Alyssum subvirens

Alyssum subvirens, commonly known as greenleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a dense, compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Alyssum subvirens is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as ladybugs and lacewings.
