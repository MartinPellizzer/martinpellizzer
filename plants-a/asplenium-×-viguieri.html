Asplenium × viguieri

Asplenium × viguieri, commonly known as the hybrid shield fern, is a naturally occurring intergeneric hybrid between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and the presence of sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are typically clustered in groups of three. The plant exhibits a variety of leaf shapes, ranging from lanceolate to ovate, and often displays a dark green to bluish-green coloration. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

As a hybrid, it demonstrates genetic diversity and adaptability, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
