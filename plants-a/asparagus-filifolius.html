Asparagus filifolius

Asparagus filifolius, commonly known as the silver asparagus or silver filifolius, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Asparagus filifolius thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
