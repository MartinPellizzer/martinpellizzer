Anthericum pilosum

Anthericum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-winged nuts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anthericum pilosum is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks.
