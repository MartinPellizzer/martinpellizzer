Aster phyllodes

Aster phyllodes, commonly known as the silky bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, fern-like leaves that are often mistaken for stems, and its showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and the plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Aster phyllodes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
