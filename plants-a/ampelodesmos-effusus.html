Ampelodesmos effusus

Ampelodesmos effusus, commonly known as the silky vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, flexible stems that allow it to twine around supports, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Ampelodesmos effusus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover trellises or walls.
