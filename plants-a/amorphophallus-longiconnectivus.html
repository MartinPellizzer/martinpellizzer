Amorphophallus longiconnectivus

Amorphophallus longiconnectivus, commonly known as the long-stemmed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and inflorescence. The plant produces a swollen underground stem called a tuber, which is the primary storage organ. The leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and can grow up to several meters in length.

This species is valued for its starchy tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.
