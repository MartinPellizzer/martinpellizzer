Acer microphyllum

Acer microphyllum, commonly known as the western red cedar, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
