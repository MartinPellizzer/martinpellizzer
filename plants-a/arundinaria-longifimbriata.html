Arundinaria longifimbriata

Arundinaria longifimbriata, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a species native to eastern China.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are often adorned with long, feathery bracts. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
