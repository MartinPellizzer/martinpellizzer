Apodanthera succulenta

Apodanthera succulenta, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It has a compact growth habit, making it popular as a houseplant.

Its botanical features include a short stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-draining soil.
