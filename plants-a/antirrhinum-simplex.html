Antirrhinum simplex

Antirrhinum simplex, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, hence its common name. The flowers are typically pink, red, or yellow, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Antirrhinum simplex is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
