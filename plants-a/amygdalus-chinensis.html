Amygdalus chinensis

Amygdalus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peach, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by peach-like fruits that are edible and widely consumed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional Chinese medicine.
