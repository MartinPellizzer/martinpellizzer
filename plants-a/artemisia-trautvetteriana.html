Artemisia trautvetteriana

Artemisia trautvetteriana, commonly known as Russian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features silvery-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
