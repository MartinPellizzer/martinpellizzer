Allium porrum var. ampeloprasum

Allium porrum var. ampeloprasum, commonly known as tree leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces tall, cylindrical flower stalks topped with umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbous base is often used in cooking, similar to leeks and onions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
