Acmadenia juniperina

Acmadenia juniperina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acmadenia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers during the autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Acmadenia juniperina is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
