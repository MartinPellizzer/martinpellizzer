Anemone alpina subsp. apiifolia

Anemone alpina subsp.

apiifolia, commonly known as the Alpine garden anemone, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of finely cut, fern-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations. It is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to mountainous landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
