Ampelocissus dekindtiana

Ampelocissus dekindtiana, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal grape, is a woody vine native to South Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its edible, sweet-tart berries that are often consumed raw or used in traditional remedies. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
