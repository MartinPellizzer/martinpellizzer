Amphicosmia parkeri

Amphicosmia parkeri, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Amphicosmia parkeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
