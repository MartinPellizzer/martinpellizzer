Anthyllis dillenii var. bulgarica

Anthyllis dillenii var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian anthyllis, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
