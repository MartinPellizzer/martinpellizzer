Acacia allenii

Acacia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia allenii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
