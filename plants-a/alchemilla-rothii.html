Alchemilla rothii

Alchemilla rothii, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cymes.

Alchemilla rothii is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
