Ablania digitata

Ablania digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are compound, with multiple divisions that are finely serrated.

Ablania digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
