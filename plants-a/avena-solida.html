Avena solida

Avena solida, commonly known as wild oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds, which are enclosed in a protective hull, are often dispersed by wind and can germinate in a variety of soil types.

Avena solida is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
