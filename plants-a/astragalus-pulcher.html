Astragalus pulcher

Astragalus pulcher, commonly known as the handsome milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus pulcher is often found in prairies, sagebrush steppe, and other open habitats.
