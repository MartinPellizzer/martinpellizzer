Adenandra linifolia

Adenandra linifolia, commonly known as the silky bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that give it its scientific name "linifolia," meaning "leaf-like.". The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

Adenandra linifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
