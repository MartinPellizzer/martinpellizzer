Azalea lateritia

Azalea lateritia, commonly known as the rock azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers ranging from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Azalea lateritia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
