Adonis involucrata

Adonis involucrata, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a single pistil and showy petals, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Adonis involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
