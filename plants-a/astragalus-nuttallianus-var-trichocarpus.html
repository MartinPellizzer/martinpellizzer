Astragalus nuttallianus var. trichocarpus

Astragalus nuttallianus var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is of interest in ecological and medicinal studies.
