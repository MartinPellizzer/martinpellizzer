Alsophila impressa

Alsophila impressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green with a smooth, leathery texture and have a distinctive, narrow shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
