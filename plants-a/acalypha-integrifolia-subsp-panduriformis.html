Acalypha integrifolia subsp. panduriformis

Acalypha integrifolia subsp.

panduriformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved spurge or panduriform acalypha, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
