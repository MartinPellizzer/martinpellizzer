Aristida damarensis

Aristida damarensis, commonly known as the Damara grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Due to its ecological resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
