Aegilops triaristata subsp. elongata

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with three to five bracts at the base of each spikelet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
