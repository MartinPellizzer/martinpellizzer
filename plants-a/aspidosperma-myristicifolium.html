Aspidosperma myristicifolium

Aspidosperma myristicifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cupania myristicifolia*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed.
