Anthurium hygrophilum

Anthurium hygrophilum, commonly known as the water anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often speckled with white or silver markings. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ability to tolerate moist conditions makes it well-suited for environments with high atmospheric moisture.
