Allium lavandulare

Allium lavandulare, commonly known as lavender garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like leaves that are blue-gray in color and produces clusters of small, lavender-pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic scent, which is reminiscent of both garlic and lavender. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
