Aerva persica

Aerva persica, commonly known as Persian spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other leafy greens and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.
