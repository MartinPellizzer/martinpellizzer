Acrostemon barkerae

Acrostemon barkerae, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines, characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Acrostemon barkerae is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
