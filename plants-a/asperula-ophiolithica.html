Asperula ophiolithica

Asperula ophiolithica, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often in clusters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Asperula ophiolithica is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
