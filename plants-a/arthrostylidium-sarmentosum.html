Arthrostylidium sarmentosum

Arthrostylidium sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping sandverbena, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Arthrostylidium sarmentosum thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
