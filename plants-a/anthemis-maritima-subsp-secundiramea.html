Anthemis maritima subsp. secundiramea

Anthemis maritima subsp.

secundiramea, commonly known as the sea ragwort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to salt-laden environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in maritime habitats and is considered a hardy, resilient plant.
