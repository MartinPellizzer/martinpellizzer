Aloysia ternifolia f. oppositifolia

Aloysia ternifolia f. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Native to South America, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It produces small, four-angled fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its propagation.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
