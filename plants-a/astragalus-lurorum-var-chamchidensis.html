Astragalus lurorum var. chamchidensis

Astragalus lurorum var. chamchidensis, commonly known as the Chamchidian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variant is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique geographical distribution and ecological adaptations.
