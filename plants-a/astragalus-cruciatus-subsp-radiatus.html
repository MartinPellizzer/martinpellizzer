Astragalus cruciatus subsp. radiatus

Astragalus cruciatus subsp.

radiatus, commonly known as the radiate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
