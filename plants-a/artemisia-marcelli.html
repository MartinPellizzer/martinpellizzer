Artemisia marcelli

Artemisia marcelli, commonly known as Marcell's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic, often pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
