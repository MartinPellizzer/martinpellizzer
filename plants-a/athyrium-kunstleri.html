Athyrium kunstleri

Athyrium kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium kunstleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
