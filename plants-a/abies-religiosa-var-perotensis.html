Abies religiosa var. perotensis

Abies religiosa var. perotensis, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The foliage is typically dark green, with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

This species plays a vital role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
