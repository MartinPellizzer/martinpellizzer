Adinandra oblonga

Adinandra oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing small, black seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Adinandra oblonga plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
