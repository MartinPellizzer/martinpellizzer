Amaranthus retroflexus f. rubricaulis

Amaranthus retroflexus f. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed pigweed, is a variant of the common amaranth species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and branching structure. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive trait is the presence of red or purple stems, which give it its common name.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
