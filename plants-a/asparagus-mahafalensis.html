Asparagus mahafalensis

Asparagus mahafalensis, commonly known as Mahafal asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and branched stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
