Aglaia grandis

Aglaia grandis, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which aid in its dispersal.

Aglaia grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
