Alchemilla pectinata var. mexicana

Alchemilla pectinata var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
