Aria nivea var. lanata

Aria nivea var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
