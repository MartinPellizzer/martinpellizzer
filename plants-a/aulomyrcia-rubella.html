Aulomyrcia rubella

Aulomyrcia rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.
