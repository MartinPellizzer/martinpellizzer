Androrchis pinetorum

Androrchis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Androrchis pinetorum is characterized by its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which aids in nutrient absorption.
