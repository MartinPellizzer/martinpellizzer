Absinthium chinense

Absinthium chinense, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its historical use in herbal remedies and its ecological role in supporting pollinators make it an important plant in its native habitat.
