Aralia elata var. elata

Aralia elata var. elata, commonly known as Japanese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmate shape, often divided into five to nine lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. Its berries are red and edible, though often consumed by birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
