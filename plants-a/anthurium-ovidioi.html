Anthurium ovidioi

Anthurium ovidioi, commonly known as the Ovidio's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium ovidioi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
