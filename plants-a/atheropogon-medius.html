Atheropogon medius

Atheropogon medius, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments.

Atheropogon medius is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of ecological conditions.
