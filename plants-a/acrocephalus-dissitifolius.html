Acrocephalus dissitifolius

Acrocephalus dissitifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are borne on elongated, slender stalks, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
