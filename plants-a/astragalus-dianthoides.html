Astragalus dianthoides

Astragalus dianthoides, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus dianthoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
