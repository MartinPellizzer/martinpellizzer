Acacia clydonophora

Acacia clydonophora, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The silky acacia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
