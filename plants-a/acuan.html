Acuan

Acuan, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries. The plant has hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
