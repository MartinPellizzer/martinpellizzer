Astracantha spiciformis

Astracantha spiciformis, commonly known as the spiky aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a spiky, cylindrical shape, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
