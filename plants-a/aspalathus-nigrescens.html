Aspalathus nigrescens

Aspalathus nigrescens, commonly known as African black tea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to produce a dark-colored infusion when steeped, despite not being a true tea. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its distinctive flavor and limited cultivation, it is considered a rare and valuable species in the botanical world.
