Anemone vesicatoria

Anemone vesicatoria, commonly known as the stinging anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the presence of glandular hairs that can release a mild irritant when touched.

This plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.
