Acer pseudoplatanus var. anomalum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. anomalum, commonly known as the Cork-leaf Maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features lobes that are more rounded and less deeply cut compared to the typical maple. The leaves are often a vibrant green during the growing season and turn striking shades of red and orange in autumn. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
