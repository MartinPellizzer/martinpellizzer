Acaena stricta var. gracilis

Acaena stricta var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender burrplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny bracts that form the characteristic burrs, which are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
