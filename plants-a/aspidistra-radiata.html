Aspidistra radiata

Aspidistra radiata, commonly known as the Cast-iron plant, is a hardy evergreen perennial native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy, strap-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spadix and a modified leaf called a spathe. It is well adapted to low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its resilience and elegant appearance make it a favored ornamental plant in both tropical and temperate climates.
