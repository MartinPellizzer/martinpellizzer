Amelanchier sera

Amelanchier sera, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.

Amelanchier sera is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
