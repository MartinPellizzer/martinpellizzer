Amygdalus mongolica

Amygdalus mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Amygdalus mongolica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
