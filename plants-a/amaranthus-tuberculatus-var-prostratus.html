Amaranthus tuberculatus var. prostratus

Amaranthus tuberculatus var. prostratus, commonly known as pigweed, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its prostrate habit and hairy stems.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with prominent veins and a rough texture. This species is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
