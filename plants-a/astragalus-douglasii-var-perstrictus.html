Astragalus douglasii var. perstrictus

Astragalus douglasii var. perstrictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in racemes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
