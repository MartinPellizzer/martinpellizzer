Allium aureum

Allium aureum, commonly known as the golden garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a strong garlic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a pungent flavor similar to garlic.

Allium aureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
