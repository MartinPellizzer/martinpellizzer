Artemisia codonocephala

Artemisia codonocephala, commonly known as the mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
