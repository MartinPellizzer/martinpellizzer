Agauria salicifolia f. adenantha

Agauria salicifolia f. adenantha, commonly known as the adenantha form of the willow-leaf agauria, is a unique variant of this plant species.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features narrow, willow-like leaves with a silvery underside. This form is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it adapts to harsh conditions through its succulent leaves. The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit, often forming dense clumps that help retain moisture.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
