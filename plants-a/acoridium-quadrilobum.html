Acoridium quadrilobum

Acoridium quadrilobum, commonly known as the four-lobed arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy, with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family.

Acoridium quadrilobum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
