Abutilon pubescens

Abutilon pubescens, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

Abutilon pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
