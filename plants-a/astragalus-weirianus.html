Astragalus weirianus

Astragalus weirianus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and meadows, and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves and distinctive flower structure are typical of the genus Astragalus.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
