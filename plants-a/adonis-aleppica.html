Adonis aleppica

Adonis aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo gloryflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and southwestern Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle red or purple tinge. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adonis aleppica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
