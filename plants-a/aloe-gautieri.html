Aloe gautieri

Aloe gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces tall, branched stems with yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe gautieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
