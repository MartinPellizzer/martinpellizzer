Astragalus tragacantha subsp. terraccianoi

Astragalus tragacantha subsp.

terraccianoi, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is known for producing a viscous, gum-like substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.
