Aira micrantha

Aira micrantha, commonly known as little air grass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Aira micrantha is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
