Allium charaulicum

Allium charaulicum, commonly known as the Kashmiri garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium charaulicum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes in mountainous regions.
