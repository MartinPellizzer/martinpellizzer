Alpinia versicolor

Alpinia versicolor, commonly known as the flame ginger or red ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences that range from red to orange, which appear during the warmer months. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alpinia versicolor is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in culinary and medicinal applications.
