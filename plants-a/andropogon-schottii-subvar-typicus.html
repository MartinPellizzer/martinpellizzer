Andropogon schottii subvar. typicus

Andropogon schottii subvar. typicus, commonly known as the Schott's bluestem, is a grass species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
