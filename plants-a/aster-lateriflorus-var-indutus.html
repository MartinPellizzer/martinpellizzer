Aster lateriflorus var. indutus

Aster lateriflorus var. indutus, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, dense pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
