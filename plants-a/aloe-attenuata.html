Aloe attenuata

Aloe attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. Aloe attenuata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and can be harvested for their gel-like substance.
