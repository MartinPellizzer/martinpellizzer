Acianthera ruizii

Acianthera ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers that are often a mix of white and purple hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
