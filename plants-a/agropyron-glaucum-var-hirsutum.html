Agropyron glaucum var. hirsutum

Agropyron glaucum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Agropyron glaucum var. hirsutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
