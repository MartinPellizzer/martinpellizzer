Alchemilla vulgaris var. glabrescens

Alchemilla vulgaris var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a dense rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that become smooth with age, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
