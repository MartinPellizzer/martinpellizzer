Amauropelta rustica

Amauropelta rustica, commonly known as the rusty shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a distinctive rusty-colored underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Amauropelta rustica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
