Athyrium keysseri

Athyrium keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's lady fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arched or gracefully drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thin layer of soil, and the fronds can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Athyrium keysseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
