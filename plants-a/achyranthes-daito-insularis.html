Achyranthes daito-insularis

Achyranthes daito-insularis, commonly known as the Ryukyu achyranthes, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
