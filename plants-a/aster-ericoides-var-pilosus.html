Aster ericoides var. pilosus

Aster ericoides var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky mountain aster, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. Its showy, blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and are composed of both ray and disk flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
