Adesmia inflexa var. sericeophylla

Adesmia inflexa var. sericeophylla, commonly known as the silky-leafed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, characterized by its small, spiny stems and dense foliage. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
