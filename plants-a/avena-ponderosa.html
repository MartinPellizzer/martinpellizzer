Avena ponderosa

Avena ponderosa, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which eventually develop into oval, bristly seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Avena ponderosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its use in restoration projects.
