Azalea nudiflora var. coccinea

Azalea nudiflora var. coccinea, commonly known as the redleaf azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
