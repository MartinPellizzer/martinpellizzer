Agave howardii

Agave howardii, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave howardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a landscape plant.
