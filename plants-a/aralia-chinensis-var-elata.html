Aralia chinensis var. elata

Aralia chinensis var. elata, commonly known as the Chinese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several leaflets, and its clusters of small, white to greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
