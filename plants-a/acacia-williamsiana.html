Acacia williamsiana

Acacia williamsiana, commonly known as Williams' acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing numerous small, flattened seeds.

Acacia williamsiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
