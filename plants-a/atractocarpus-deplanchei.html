Atractocarpus deplanchei

Atractocarpus deplanchei, commonly known as the Amazonian ebony, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is part of the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
