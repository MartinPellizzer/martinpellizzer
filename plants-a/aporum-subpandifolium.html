Aporum subpandifolium

Aporum subpandifolium, commonly known as the pandifoliate aporum, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant for its aesthetic value.
