Alyssum pulvinare

Alyssum pulvinare, commonly known as the pulvinate alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
