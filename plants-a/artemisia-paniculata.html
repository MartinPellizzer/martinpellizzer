Artemisia paniculata

Artemisia paniculata, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.
