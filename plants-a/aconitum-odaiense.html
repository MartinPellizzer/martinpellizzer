Aconitum odaiense

Aconitum odaiense, commonly known as the Japanese aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum odaiense is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
