Apteria hymenanthera

Apteria hymenanthera, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

A. hymenanthera is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
