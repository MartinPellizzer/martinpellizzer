Agave bahamana

Agave bahamana, commonly known as the Bahamian agave, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves with a grayish-green color and sharp, spiny edges. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Agave bahamana is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
