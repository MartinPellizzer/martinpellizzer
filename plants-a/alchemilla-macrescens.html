Alchemilla macrescens

Alchemilla macrescens, commonly known as the sticky alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, sticky coating.

Alchemilla macrescens is often found in moist, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
