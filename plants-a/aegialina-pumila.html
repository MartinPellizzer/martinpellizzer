Aegialina pumila

Aegialina pumila, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in sandy, salt-tolerant environments. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sprawling and vine-like.

Aegialina pumila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune soils and providing habitat for small insects and birds.

Morphology

Aegialina pumila has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.
