Andropogon fragilis var. malayanus

Andropogon fragilis var. malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan bluestem, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
