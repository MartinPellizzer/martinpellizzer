Adenocarpus intermedius f. viridis

Adenocarpus intermedius f. viridis, commonly known as the green-headed burr, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits. Its foliage is noted for its bright green coloration, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
