Astracantha griseosericea

Astracantha griseosericea, commonly known as the gray-hairy aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
