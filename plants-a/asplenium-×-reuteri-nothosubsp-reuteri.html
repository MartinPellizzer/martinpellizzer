Asplenium × reuteri nothosubsp. reuteri

Asplenium × reuteri nothosubsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the hybrid shield fern, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium scolopendrium. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with small, spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid in the Aspleniaceae family.
