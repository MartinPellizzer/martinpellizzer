Aconitum × cammarum var. tricoronense

Aconitum × cammarum var. tricoronense, commonly known as the Three-Corona Aconite, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, erect stems covered in rough, hairy foliage. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, sparsely branched inflorescence, with each bloom having three sepals that are often adorned with distinctive patterns.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
