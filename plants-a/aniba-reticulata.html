Aniba reticulata

Aniba reticulata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

Aniba reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrance.
