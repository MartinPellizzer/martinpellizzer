Artemisia pineticola

Artemisia pineticola, commonly known as the mountain wormwood, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, resinous scent.

Artemisia pineticola is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
