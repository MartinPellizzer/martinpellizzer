Asplenium unilaterale var. bivalvatum

Asplenium unilaterale var. bivalvatum, commonly known as the two-lobed spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into two lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its fronds are usually short and clustered.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
