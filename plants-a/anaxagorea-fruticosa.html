Anaxagorea fruticosa

Anaxagorea fruticosa, commonly known as the fragrant wood or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its parts.
