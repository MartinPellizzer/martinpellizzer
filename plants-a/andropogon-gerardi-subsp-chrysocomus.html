Andropogon gerardi subsp. chrysocomus

Andropogon gerardi subsp.

chrysocomus, commonly known as golden threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive golden hue along the midrib, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
