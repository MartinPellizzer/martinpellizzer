Acidosasa paucifolia

Acidosasa paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved acidbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring. It is adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in harsh, cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
