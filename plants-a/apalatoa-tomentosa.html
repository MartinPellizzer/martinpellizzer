Apalatoa tomentosa

Apalatoa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly apalatoa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Apalatoa tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
