Apargia rigens

Apargia rigens, commonly known as the slender bluets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairless and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are small, blue to violet in color, and are arranged in loose clusters at the top of the stems.

Apargia rigens is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
