Acacia assimilis

Acacia assimilis, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a striking silver-gray color during the dry season. The plant produces yellow spherical flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are woody and contain a single, edible seed.

Acacia assimilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
