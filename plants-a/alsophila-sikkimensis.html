Alsophila sikkimensis

Alsophila sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountain forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk, while the fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Alsophila sikkimensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
