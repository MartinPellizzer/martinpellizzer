Ariocarpus retusus subsp. horacekii

Ariocarpus retusus subsp.

horacekii, commonly known as the Horacek's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with spiny areoles and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible, red, fleshy fruits that are highly prized for their flavor and nutritional value. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
