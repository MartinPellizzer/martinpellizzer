Aster longifolius var. major

Aster longifolius var. major, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
