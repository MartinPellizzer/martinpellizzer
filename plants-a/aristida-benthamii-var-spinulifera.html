Aristida benthamii var. spinulifera

Aristida benthamii var. spinulifera, commonly known as the spinylifera grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This grass is often found in native grasslands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
