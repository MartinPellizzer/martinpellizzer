Allium stellerianum subsp. tuvinicum

Allium stellerianum subsp.

tuvinicum, commonly known as the Tuvinian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
