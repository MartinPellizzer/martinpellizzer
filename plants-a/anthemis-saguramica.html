Anthemis saguramica

Anthemis saguramica, commonly known as the Georgian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Anthemis saguramica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
