Allium gilanense

Allium gilanense, commonly known as Gilan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caspian region of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

Allium gilanense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
