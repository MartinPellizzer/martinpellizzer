Astragalus shockleyi

Astragalus shockleyi, commonly known as Shockley's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
