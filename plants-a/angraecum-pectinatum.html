Angraecum pectinatum

Angraecum pectinatum, commonly known as the Pectinatum Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous white, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its blooms typically appear during the rainy season, showcasing the plant's adaptation to its tropical environment.

Angraecum pectinatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity.
