Astragalus thracicus subsp. trojanus

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

trojanus, commonly known as the Trojan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and narrow leaves, which are adapted to its arid and rocky habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
