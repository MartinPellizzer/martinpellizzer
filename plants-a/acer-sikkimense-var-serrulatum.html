Acer sikkimense var. serrulatum

Acer sikkimense var. serrulatum, commonly known as the Sikkim maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.
