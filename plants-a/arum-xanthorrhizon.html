Arum xanthorrhizon

Arum xanthorrhizon, commonly known as the yellow arrow arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with the showy spathe serving as a pollinator attractant.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
