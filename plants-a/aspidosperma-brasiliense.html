Aspidosperma brasiliense

Aspidosperma brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red berries that contain a single seed. Its latex is toxic and contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
