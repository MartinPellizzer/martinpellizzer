Acanthopsis horrida

Acanthopsis horrida, commonly known as the rough acanthus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough and textured appearance.

Acanthopsis horrida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
