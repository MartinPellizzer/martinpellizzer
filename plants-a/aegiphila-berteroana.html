Aegiphila berteroana

Aegiphila berteroana, commonly known as the Peruvian mountain mint, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, attracting various pollinators.
