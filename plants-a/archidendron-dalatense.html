Archidendron dalatense

Archidendron dalatense, commonly known as the Dalat legume, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
