Anthemis leucolepis

Anthemis leucolepis, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has finely divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, highlighting its ecological importance.
