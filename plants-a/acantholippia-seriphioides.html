Acantholippia seriphioides

Acantholippia seriphioides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
