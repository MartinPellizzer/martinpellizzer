Aporum xanthoacron

Aporum xanthoacron, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, often displaying a vibrant yellow-green coloration. The plant produces showy, yellow spore-bearing spikes that emerge from the base of the leaves, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of air humidity and indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor tropical gardens.
