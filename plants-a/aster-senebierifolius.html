Aster senebierifolius

Aster senebierifolius, commonly known as the Senebier's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster senebierifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
