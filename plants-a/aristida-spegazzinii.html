Aristida spegazzinii

Aristida spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in dry climates.
