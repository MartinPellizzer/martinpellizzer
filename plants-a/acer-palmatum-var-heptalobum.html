Acer palmatum var. heptalobum

Acer palmatum var. heptalobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are palmately divided into seven lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, red or yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red samaras in the fall.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
