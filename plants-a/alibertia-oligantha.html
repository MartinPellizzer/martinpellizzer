Alibertia oligantha

Alibertia oligantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark, woody fruits containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species.
