Acaena pinnatifida var. uspallatensis

Acaena pinnatifida var. uspallatensis, commonly known as the Chilean burr bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are often referred to as "burrs.". The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its fruit is an important food source for various bird species, contributing to seed dispersal.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
