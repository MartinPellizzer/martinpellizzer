Acropteris septentrionalis

Acropteris septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.

Acropteris septentrionalis plays a role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
