Archidendron brevicalyx

Archidendron brevicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved legume or the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous racemes of flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, brown pods containing several seeds. Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical gardens.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
