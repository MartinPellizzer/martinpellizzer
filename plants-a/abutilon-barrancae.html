Abutilon barrancae

Abutilon barrancae, commonly known as the Barranca Abutilon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring. Its fibrous bark and mucilaginous sap have been used in traditional medicine.

Abutilon barrancae is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low water availability.
