Acacia kerryana

Acacia kerryana, commonly known as the Kerry acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia kerryana is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
