Astragalus curvirostris

Astragalus curvirostris, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its roots can fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus curvirostris is often found in grasslands and shrublands, playing a role in local ecosystems.
