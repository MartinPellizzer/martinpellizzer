Alexis grandiflora

Alexis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered alexis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
