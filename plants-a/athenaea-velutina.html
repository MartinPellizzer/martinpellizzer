Athenaea velutina

Athenaea velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf athenaea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
