Alkanna angustifolia

Alkanna angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alkanna, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright red to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Alkanna angustifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
