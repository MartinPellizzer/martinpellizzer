Aconitum thalianum

Aconitum thalianum, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically deeply lobed, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. Aconitum thalianum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
