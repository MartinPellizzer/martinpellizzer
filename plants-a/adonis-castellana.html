Adonis castellana

Adonis castellana, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
