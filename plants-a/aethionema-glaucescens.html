Aethionema glaucescens

Aethionema glaucescens, commonly known as the pale sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. It is well adapted to survive in poor soils and high temperatures, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies of desert flora.
