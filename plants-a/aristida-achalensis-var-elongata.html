Aristida achalensis var. elongata

Aristida achalensis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated false grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of India.

It typically grows in open habitats such as rocky slopes, dry forests, and grasslands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is characterized by its elongated lemma and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, drought-prone environments.
