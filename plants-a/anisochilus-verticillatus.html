Anisochilus verticillatus

Anisochilus verticillatus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls along the stem. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Anisochilus verticillatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
