Amaranthus blitum var. oleraceus

Amaranthus blitum var. oleraceus, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture, while the stems are sturdy and branching. This variety is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and protein.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens and agricultural systems.
