Arisaema sadoense

Arisaema sadoense, commonly known as the Sado Island arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically the island of Sado.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries.

Arisaema sadoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
