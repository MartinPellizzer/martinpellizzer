Alchemilla litwinowii

Alchemilla litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
