Acer barbatum var. floridanum

Acer barbatum var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The Florida maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
