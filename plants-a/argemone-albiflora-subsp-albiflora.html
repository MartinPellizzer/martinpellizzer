Argemone albiflora subsp. albiflora

Argemone albiflora subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered argemone, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China. It features hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
