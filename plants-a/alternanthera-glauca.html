Alternanthera glauca

Alternanthera glauca, commonly known as blue velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alternanthera glauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
