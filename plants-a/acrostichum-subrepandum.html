Acrostichum subrepandum

Acrostichum subrepandum, commonly known as the spreading fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that often trail along the ground, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are covered in a fine layer of hair.

Acrostichum subrepandum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its lush, green foliage.
