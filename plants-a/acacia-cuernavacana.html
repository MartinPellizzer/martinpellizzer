Acacia cuernavacana

Acacia cuernavacana, commonly known as the Cuernavaca acacia, is a species of flowering plant native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in cylindrical spikes, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Acacia cuernavacana is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
