Acer saccharum var. nigrum

Acer saccharum var. nigrum, commonly known as the black sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
