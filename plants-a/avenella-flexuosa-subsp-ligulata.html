Avenella flexuosa subsp. ligulata

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved avenella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender stems that often lie prostrate or form dense mats. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.
