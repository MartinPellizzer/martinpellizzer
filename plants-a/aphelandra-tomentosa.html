Aphelandra tomentosa

Aphelandra tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf torch plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, orange-yellow bracts. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, though it is not a true palm.
