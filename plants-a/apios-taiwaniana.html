Apios taiwaniana

Apios taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan sunchokes, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and tuberous roots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like pods that contain seeds.

Apios taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
