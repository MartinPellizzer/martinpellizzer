Asplenium reticulatum

Asplenium reticulatum, commonly known as the nettle spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense, compact rosette. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with a network of fine roots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asplenium reticulatum is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
