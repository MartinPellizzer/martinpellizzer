Alsophila lepidoclada

Alsophila lepidoclada, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, lacy appearance that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
