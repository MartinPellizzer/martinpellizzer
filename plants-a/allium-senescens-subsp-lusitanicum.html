Allium senescens subsp. lusitanicum

Allium senescens subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to Mediterranean climates, with a dormant period during colder months.
