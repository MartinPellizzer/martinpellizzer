Aspilia setosa var. reflexa

Aspilia setosa var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow disc flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its compact growth habit and attractive floral display.
