Acrodiclidium elaeophorum

Acrodiclidium elaeophorum, commonly known as the silver grass.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and drought tolerance.
