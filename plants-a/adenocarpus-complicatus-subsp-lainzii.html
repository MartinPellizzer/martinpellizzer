Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. lainzii

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz's thornveld, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its morphology reflects its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
