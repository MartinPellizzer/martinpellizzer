Acaena anserinifolia subsp. pusilla

Acaena anserinifolia subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the small silverweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern South America. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its fruits are burrs with hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
