Anguria grandiflora

Anguria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant-flowered cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces elongated, edible fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
