Amorphophallus claudelii

Amorphophallus claudelii, commonly known as the Claudel's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking, spatulate-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of many aroid species. It is cultivated for its edible tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
