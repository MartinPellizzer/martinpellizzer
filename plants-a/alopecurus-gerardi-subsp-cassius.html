Alopecurus gerardi subsp. cassius

Alopecurus gerardi subsp.

cassius, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a herbaceous perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
