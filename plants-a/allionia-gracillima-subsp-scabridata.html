Allionia gracillima subsp. scabridata

Allionia gracillima subsp.

scabridata, commonly known as the roughleaf allionia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
