Achillea breynina

Achillea breynina, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in flat-topped clusters, while its leaves are finely divided and silvery-gray in appearance.

Achillea breynina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
