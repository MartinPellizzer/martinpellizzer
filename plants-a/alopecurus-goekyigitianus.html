Alopecurus goekyigitianus

Alopecurus goekyigitianus, commonly known as the Goekyigit's hairgrass, is a rare and endangered species of grass native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and is adapted to specific soil and climatic conditions. Its conservation status highlights the need for habitat protection and ecological research.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
