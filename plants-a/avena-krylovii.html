Avena krylovii

Avena krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's oat, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender stems and delicate, awned spikelets. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with a structure typical of the grass family Poaceae.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
