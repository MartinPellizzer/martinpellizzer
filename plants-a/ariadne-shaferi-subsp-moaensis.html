Ariadne shaferi subsp. moaensis

Ariadne shaferi subsp.

moaensis, commonly known as the Moa Mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence with yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
