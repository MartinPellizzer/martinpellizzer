Avena alba subvar. minor

Avena alba subvar. minor, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with white or pale florets, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It is often cultivated as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
