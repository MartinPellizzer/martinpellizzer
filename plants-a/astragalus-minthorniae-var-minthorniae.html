Astragalus minthorniae var. minthorniae

Astragalus minthorniae var. minthorniae, commonly known as Minthorn's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
