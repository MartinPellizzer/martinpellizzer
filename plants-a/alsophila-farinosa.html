Alsophila farinosa

Alsophila farinosa, commonly known as the farinosa tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender fern that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a central trunk covered in fibrous, brownish scales. The fronds are pinnate, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, fuzzy appearance on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique, elegant form.
