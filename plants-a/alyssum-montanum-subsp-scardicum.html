Alyssum montanum subsp. scardicum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

scardicum, commonly known as mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Alyssum montanum subsp.

scardicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
