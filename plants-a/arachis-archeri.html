Arachis archeri

Arachis archeri, commonly known as the mountain groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like structures containing edible seeds. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Arachis archeri is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential agricultural value.
