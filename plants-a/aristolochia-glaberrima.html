Aristolochia glaberrima

Aristolochia glaberrima, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various health purposes.
