Aloe arborescens var. milleri

Aloe arborescens var. milleri, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, woolly, purple-tinged bracts that resemble a lion's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a characteristic typical of many succulents. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is often cultivated for its decorative appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
