Anapeltis nitida

Anapeltis nitida, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Anapeltis nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
