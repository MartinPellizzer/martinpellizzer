Aerides multiflora var. lobbii

Aerides multiflora var. lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers shady, moist environments and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
