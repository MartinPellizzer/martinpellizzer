Armeniaca vulgaris var. oblonga

Armeniaca vulgaris var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and the Himalayas.

It produces edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamins and minerals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to oblong in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters, and it is typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested in late summer or early autumn.
