Amaranthus × ozanonii subsp. ralletii

Amaranthus × ozanonii subsp.

ralletii, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red to purple foliage and inflorescences, which are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both decorative and ecological landscaping.
