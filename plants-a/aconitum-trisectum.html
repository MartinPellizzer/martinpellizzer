Aconitum trisectum

Aconitum trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant to handle without proper precautions.
