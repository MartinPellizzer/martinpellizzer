Arundinaria variegata var. oroshima

Arundinaria variegata var. oroshima, commonly known as the Oroszima bamboo, is a rare and ornamental grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that exhibit a striking variegation of green and white along their length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and unique foliage, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical conservation efforts.
