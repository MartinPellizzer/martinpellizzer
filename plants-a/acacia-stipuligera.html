Acacia stipuligera

Acacia stipuligera, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. Acacia stipuligera is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays an important role in providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for native wildlife.
