Astragalus kolaensis

Astragalus kolaensis, commonly known as the Kola milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Astragalus kolaensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and savannas.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life in its habitat.
