Aristida capillacea

Aristida capillacea, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often concealed within the foliage, making it less conspicuous. Its fibrous root system aids in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in degraded landscapes.

Aristida capillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators and wildlife.
