Acer cappadocicum f. rubrocarpum

Acer cappadocicum f. rubrocarpum, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn. The plant is native to Turkey and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
