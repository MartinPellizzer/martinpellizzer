Athamanta hirsuta var. mutellinoides

Athamanta hirsuta var. mutellinoides, commonly known as the hairy athamanta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
