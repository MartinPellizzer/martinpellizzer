Acalypha macrophylla

Acalypha macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf spurflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Acalypha macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
