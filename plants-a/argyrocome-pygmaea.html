Argyrocome pygmaea

Argyrocome pygmaea, commonly known as the silverleaf pygmy, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its growth habit is low and compact, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptation to extreme conditions, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
