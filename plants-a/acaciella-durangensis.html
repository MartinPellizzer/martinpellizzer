Acaciella durangensis

Acaciella durangensis, commonly known as the Durango acacia, is a small shrub native to the Mexican state of Durango.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
