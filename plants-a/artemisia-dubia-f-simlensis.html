Artemisia dubia f. simlensis

Artemisia dubia f. simlensis, commonly known as the Serbula Artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
