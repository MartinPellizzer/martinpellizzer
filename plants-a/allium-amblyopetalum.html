Allium amblyopetalum

Allium amblyopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in the Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense umbel. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is also cultivated as an ornamental due to its delicate appearance.
