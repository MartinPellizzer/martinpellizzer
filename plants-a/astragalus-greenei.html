Astragalus greenei

Astragalus greenei, commonly known as Greene's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus greenei is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
