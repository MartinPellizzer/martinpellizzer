Asparagus plocamoides

Asparagus plocamoides, commonly known as the silver asparagus or mountain asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by needle-like berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive, slightly zigzagged margin.

Asparagus plocamoides is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
