Astragalus striatus var. echegarayi

Astragalus striatus var. echegarayi, commonly known as the Echegaray's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its scientific classification includes the species Astragalus striatus, with the specific variety echegarayi.
