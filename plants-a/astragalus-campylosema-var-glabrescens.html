Astragalus campylosema var. glabrescens

Astragalus campylosema var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smooth, glabrous appearance.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.
