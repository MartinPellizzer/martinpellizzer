Anemia pyrenaea

Anemia pyrenaea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive cone-shaped cluster of bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Anemia pyrenaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also a source of nectar for pollinators.
