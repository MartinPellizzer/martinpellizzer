Aechmea aguadocensis, commonly known as the Guaduca bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its humid, shaded environment.

Aechmea aguadocensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
