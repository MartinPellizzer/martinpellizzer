Aquilegia leptoceras

Aquilegia leptoceras, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces showy, nodding blooms in early to mid-summer.

The species is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
