Acacia ixiophylla

Acacia ixiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia ixiophylla is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
