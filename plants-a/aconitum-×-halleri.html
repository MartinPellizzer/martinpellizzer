Aconitum × halleri

Aconitum × halleri, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum × halleri is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle.
