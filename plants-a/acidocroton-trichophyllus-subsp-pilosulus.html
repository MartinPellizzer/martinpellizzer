Acidocroton trichophyllus subsp. pilosulus

Acidocroton trichophyllus subsp.

pilosulus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The flowers are clustered in panicles and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
