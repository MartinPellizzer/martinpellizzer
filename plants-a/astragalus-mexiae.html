Astragalus mexiae

Astragalus mexiae, commonly known as Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus mexiae is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and desert scrub.
