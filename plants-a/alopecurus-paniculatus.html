Alopecurus paniculatus

Alopecurus paniculatus, commonly known as purple false oatgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, purple or pinkish flowers. Its inflorescence is usually erect and branched, with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

Alopecurus paniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
