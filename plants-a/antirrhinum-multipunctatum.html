Antirrhinum multipunctatum

Antirrhinum multipunctatum, commonly known as the spotted toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
