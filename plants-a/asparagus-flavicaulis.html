Asparagus flavicaulis

Asparagus flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and small, grass-like leaves. It produces delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences.

Asparagus flavicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
