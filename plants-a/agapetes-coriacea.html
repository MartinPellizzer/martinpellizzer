Agapetes coriacea

Agapetes coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skinned mountain heath, is a shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in late summer.

Agapetes coriacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
