Aeschynanthus kingii

Aeschynanthus kingii, commonly known as the king's ivy, is a tropical climbing plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or shelves.
