Agrostophyllum confusum

Agrostophyllum confusum, commonly known as the confused grass-leaf, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, parallel venation in the leaves, and spikelets that are often tightly packed.

Agrostophyllum confusum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
