Aquilegia jonesii var. elatior

Aquilegia jonesii var. elatior, commonly known as the tall Colorado lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of spurred, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are covered in fine hairs, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
