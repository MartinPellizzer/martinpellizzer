Aldina speciosa

Aldina speciosa, commonly known as the showy aldina, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinct basal rosette arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
