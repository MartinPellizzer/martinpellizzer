Armeniaca vulgaris var. albida

Armeniaca vulgaris var. albida, commonly known as the white peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its white or pale yellow flesh and yellow to orange skin.

It is a member of the Prunus genus and is closely related to the common peach (Prunus persica). The plant is typically a small deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is prized for its delicate flavor and aroma, and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
