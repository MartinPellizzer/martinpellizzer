Aira phleoides

Aira phleoides, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
