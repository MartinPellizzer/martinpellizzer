Astartea arbuscula

Astartea arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring a compact, bushy structure with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Astartea arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
