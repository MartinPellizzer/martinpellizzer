Aconitum alboflavidum

Aconitum alboflavidum, commonly known as the white-yellow aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture.

Aconitum alboflavidum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
