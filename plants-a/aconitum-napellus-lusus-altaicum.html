Aconitum napellus lusus altaicum

Aconitum napellus lusus altaicum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
