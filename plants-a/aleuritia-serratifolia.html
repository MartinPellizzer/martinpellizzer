Aleuritia serratifolia

Aleuritia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrate-leaf alder, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aleuritia serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
