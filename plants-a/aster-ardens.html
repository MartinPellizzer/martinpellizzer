Aster ardens

Aster ardens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features sturdy, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster ardens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
