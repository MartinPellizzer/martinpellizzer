Acer heldreichii f. macropterum

Acer heldreichii f. macropterum, commonly known as the large-winged maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmately lobed leaves, which are typically large and have prominent, broad wing-like lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
