Aralia warmingiana

Aralia warmingiana, commonly known as the Chinese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with a broad, palmate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
