Acacia tephroloba

Acacia tephroloba, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia tephroloba thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
