Aconitum firmum subsp. skerisorae

Aconitum firmum subsp.

skerisorae, commonly known as Skerisorae monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture and a strong, toxic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
