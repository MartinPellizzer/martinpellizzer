Ararocarpus velutinus

Ararocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
