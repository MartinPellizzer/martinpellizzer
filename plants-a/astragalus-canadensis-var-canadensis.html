Astragalus canadensis var. canadensis

Astragalus canadensis var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
