Astragalus gremlii

Astragalus gremlii, commonly known as Gremli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, usually yellow or greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus gremlii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
