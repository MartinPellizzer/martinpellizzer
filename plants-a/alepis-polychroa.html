Alepis polychroa

Alepis polychroa, commonly known as the multicolored sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, adding to its visual appeal. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Alepis polychroa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic value.
