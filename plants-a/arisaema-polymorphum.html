Arisaema polymorphum

Arisaema polymorphum, commonly known as the variable arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its unique, often spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a hooded spathe. Its leaves are typically large, arrow-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish or purple veining.

Arisaema polymorphum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
