Aloinopsis loganii

Aloinopsis loganii, commonly known as the Logan's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Aloinopsis loganii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
