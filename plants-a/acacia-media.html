Acacia media

Acacia media, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Acacia media is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
