Anurus nissolia

Anurus nissolia, commonly known as the silverleaf anurus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
