Arundinella lasiostoma var. hirsutissima

Arundinella lasiostoma var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving the plant its common name. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
