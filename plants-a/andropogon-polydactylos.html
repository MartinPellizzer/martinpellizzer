Andropogon polydactylos

Andropogon polydactylos, commonly known as the feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
