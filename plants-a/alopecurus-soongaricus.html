Alopecurus soongaricus

Alopecurus soongaricus, commonly known as the Siberian mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Alopecurus soongaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
