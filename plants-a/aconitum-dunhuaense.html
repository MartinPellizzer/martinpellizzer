Aconitum dunhuaense

Aconitum dunhuaense, commonly known as the Dunhua monkshood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
