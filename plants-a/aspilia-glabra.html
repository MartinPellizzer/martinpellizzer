Aspilia glabra

Aspilia glabra, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescence composed of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
