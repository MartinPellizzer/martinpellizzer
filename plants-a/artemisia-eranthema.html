Artemisia eranthema

Artemisia eranthema, commonly known as golden thread, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Artemisia eranthema thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal uses.
