Aechmea brachyclada

Aechmea brachyclada, commonly known as the compact pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a dense rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is covered in fine, white trichomes that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces vibrant orange to red flower spikes that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Aechmea brachyclada thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush, tropical appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea brachyclada has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green color and a leathery texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead forming a compact, low-growing rosette with a thick, fibrous base that resembles a pseudostem. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of red or pink bracts that enclose small, tubular flowers with three petals and three sepals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by birds. The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or dry soil.
