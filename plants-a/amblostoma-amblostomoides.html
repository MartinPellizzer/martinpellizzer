Amblostoma amblostomoides

Amblostoma amblostomoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
