Aconitum cochleare

Aconitum cochleare, commonly known as the spiral aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum cochleare is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
