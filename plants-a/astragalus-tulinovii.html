Astragalus tulinovii

Astragalus tulinovii, commonly known as the Tulinov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora in its native region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
