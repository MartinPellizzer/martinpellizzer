Allium collinum

Allium collinum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is used for propagation. Allium collinum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
