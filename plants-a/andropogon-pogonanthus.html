Andropogon pogonanthus

Andropogon pogonanthus, commonly known as the switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats.

Andropogon pogonanthus is valued for its ecological role and is also used in bioenergy production.
