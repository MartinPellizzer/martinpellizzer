Amyris gileadensis

Amyris gileadensis, commonly known as the Gilead sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Amyris gileadensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
