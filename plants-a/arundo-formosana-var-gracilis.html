Arundo formosana var. gracilis

Arundo formosana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender giant reed, is a variant of the giant reed species native to eastern Asia.

It is a tall, reed-like grass that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and sturdy, hollow stems. This plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshes, where it often forms dense stands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful plant for erosion control and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in summer, which are arranged in panicles and contribute to its reproductive success.
