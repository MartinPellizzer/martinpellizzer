Amomum trispicatum

Amomum trispicatum, commonly known as the three-bristled cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elongated rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish flowers followed by red berries.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
