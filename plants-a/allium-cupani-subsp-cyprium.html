Allium cupani subsp. cyprium

Allium cupani subsp.

cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters.

The plant has a distinct garlic odor and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its bulbs are edible and can be used similarly to other Allium species.
