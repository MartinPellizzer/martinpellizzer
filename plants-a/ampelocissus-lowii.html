Ampelocissus lowii

Ampelocissus lowii, commonly known as the African climbing fig, is a member of the Moraceae family.

It is a woody vine native to southern Africa, characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Ampelocissus lowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
