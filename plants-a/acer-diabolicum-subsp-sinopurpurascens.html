Acer diabolicum subsp. sinopurpurascens

Acer diabolicum subsp.

sinopurpurascens, commonly known as the Sinopurpurascens maple, is a rare and striking deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage, which displays intense colors during the autumn season.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a glossy surface that enhances their visual appeal. This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

However, due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
