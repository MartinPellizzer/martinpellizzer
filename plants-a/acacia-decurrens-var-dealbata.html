Acacia decurrens var. dealbata

Acacia decurrens var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
