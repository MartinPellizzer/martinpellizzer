Ageratum glanduliferum var. albiflorum

Ageratum glanduliferum var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in glandular structures, contributing to its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects and its use in floral arrangements.
