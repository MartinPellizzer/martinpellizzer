Acacia angustissima var. chisosiana

Acacia angustissima var. chisosiana, commonly known as the Chisos acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and produces yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its bark is often fissured, and it has a woody, twisted trunk.

This variety is part of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility in its native habitat.
