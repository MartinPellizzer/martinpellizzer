Allium alpinarii

Allium alpinarii, commonly known as the alpine garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces a bulbous structure that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for alpine gardens.

Allium alpinarii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
