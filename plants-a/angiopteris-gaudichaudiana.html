Angiopteris gaudichaudiana

Angiopteris gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and colonization of new habitats.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
