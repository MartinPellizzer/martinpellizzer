Aquilegia corsica

Aquilegia corsica, commonly known as the Corsican columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are often reflexed. The plant has palmately compound leaves, with three to five leaflets that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aquilegia corsica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
