Alyxia oliviformis f. subacuta

Alyxia oliviformis f. subacuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyxia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and have a distinct pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other flowering plants with milky sap.
