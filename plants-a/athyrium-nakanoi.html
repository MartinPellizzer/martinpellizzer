Athyrium nakanoi

Athyrium nakanoi, commonly known as the Nakano's male fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium nakanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
