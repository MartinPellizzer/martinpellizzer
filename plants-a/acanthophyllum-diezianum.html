Acanthophyllum diezianum

Acanthophyllum diezianum, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in survival in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Acanthophyllum diezianum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
