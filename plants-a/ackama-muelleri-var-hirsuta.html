Ackama muelleri var. hirsuta

Ackama muelleri var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ackama, is a variant of the ackama plant native to certain regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and stems, which provide it with a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of yellow or green, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
