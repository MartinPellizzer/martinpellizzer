Aspalathus retroflexa var. parviflora

Aspalathus retroflexa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bushveld pea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
