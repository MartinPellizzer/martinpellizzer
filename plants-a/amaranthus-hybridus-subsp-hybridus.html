Amaranthus hybridus subsp. hybridus

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

hybridus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a reddish tint, and the plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional cuisines for its nutritious seeds and leaves.
