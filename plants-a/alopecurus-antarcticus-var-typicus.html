Alopecurus antarcticus var. typicus

Alopecurus antarcticus var. typicus, commonly known as the Antarctic meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. This plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is adapted to withstand low temperatures and strong winds.
