Agrostis huttoniae

Agrostis huttoniae, commonly known as Hutton's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces slender spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Agrostis huttoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
