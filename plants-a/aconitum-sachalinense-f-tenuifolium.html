Aconitum sachalinense f. tenuifolium

Aconitum sachalinense f. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with finely divided, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
