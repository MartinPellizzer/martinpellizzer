Aster turczaninowii

Aster turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typical arrangement of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its foliage is generally basal, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by petal-like rays.
