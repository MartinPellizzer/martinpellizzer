Allantodia dilatata

Allantodia dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf allantodia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Allantodia dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
