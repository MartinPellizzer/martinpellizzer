Aristea glauca

Aristea glauca, commonly known as the blue aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance.

Aristea glauca is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
