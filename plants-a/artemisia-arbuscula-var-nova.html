Artemisia arbuscula var. nova

Artemisia arbuscula var. nova, commonly known as the Cape garlic bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in winter. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping in arid regions.
