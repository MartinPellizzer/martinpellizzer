Achyroseris albertoregelia

Achyroseris albertoregelia, commonly known as the Albert Regel's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers, which mature into fluffy seeds for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native habitat's ecological balance.
