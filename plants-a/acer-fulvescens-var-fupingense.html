Acer fulvescens var. fupingense

Acer fulvescens var. fupingense, commonly known as the Fuping maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and a smooth upper surface. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
