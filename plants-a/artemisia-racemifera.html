Artemisia racemifera

Artemisia racemifera, commonly known as the mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia racemifera is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
