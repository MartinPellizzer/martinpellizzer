Alpinia celebica

Alpinia celebica, commonly known as the Celebes ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Alpinia celebica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal applications.
