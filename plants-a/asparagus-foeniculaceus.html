Asparagus foeniculaceus

Asparagus foeniculaceus, commonly known as the lemon asparagus or sweet asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fern-like, compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, tender shoots that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus foeniculaceus is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible qualities.
