Adenia senensis

Adenia senensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
