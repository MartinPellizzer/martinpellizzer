Achyranthes lorentzii

Achyranthes lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and slightly hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is known for its adaptability to wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
