Arabis cebennensis subsp. pedemontana

Arabis cebennensis subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
