Aronia arbutifolia var. baenitziana

Aronia arbutifolia var. baenitziana, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and vibrant red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The berries are edible and can be used in jams or as a natural dye.
