Aylostera polypetala

Aylostera polypetala, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aylostera polypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
