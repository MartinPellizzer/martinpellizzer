Arisaema tortuosum subsp. tortuosum

Arisaema tortuosum subsp.

tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia. It features a distinctive spiral-shaped leaf, which is one of its most notable botanical characteristics.

The plant produces an unusual spadix surrounded by a spathe that is typically green with a red or purple base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and growth habit make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
