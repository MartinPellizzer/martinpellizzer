Achyrocline saturejoides var. saturejoides

Achyrocline saturejoides var. saturejoides, commonly known as the white sage of the Canary Islands, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the island of Tenerife and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. It has a compact, shrub-like form with multiple stems and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Mediterranean and Macaronesian flora.
