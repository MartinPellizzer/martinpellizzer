Agropyron repens var. pilosum

Agropyron repens var. pilosum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
