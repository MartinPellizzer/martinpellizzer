Amaranthus × turcicensis

Amaranthus × turcicensis, commonly known as turkey corn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces numerous small, three-lobed seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it both an attractive garden plant and a valuable agricultural crop.
