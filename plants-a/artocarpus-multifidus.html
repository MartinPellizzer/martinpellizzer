Artocarpus multifidus

Artocarpus multifidus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and large, spiky fruits. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is typically harvested when it is still green and unripe. Botanically, it belongs to the family Moraceae and is known for its high nutritional value.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
