Aglaia minutiflora var. travancorica

Aglaia minutiflora var. travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore aglaia, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
