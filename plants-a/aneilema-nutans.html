Aneilema nutans

Aneilema nutans, commonly known as the hanging arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Aneilema nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
