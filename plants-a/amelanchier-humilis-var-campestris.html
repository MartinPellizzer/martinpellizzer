Amelanchier humilis var. campestris

Amelanchier humilis var. campestris, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible berries that are typically purple or red when ripe, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and usually have three to five leaflets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and wildlife habitat.
