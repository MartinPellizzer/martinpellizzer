Acinodendron lindenii

Acinodendron lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

Acinodendron lindenii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
