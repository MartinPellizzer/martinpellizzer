Asparagus maximus

Asparagus maximus, commonly known as giant asparagus or asparagus fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching stems and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Asparagus maximus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
