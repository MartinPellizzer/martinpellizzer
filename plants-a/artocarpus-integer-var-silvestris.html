Artocarpus integer var. silvestris

Artocarpus integer var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, smooth, green leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically round and fibrous, contains a soft, starchy pulp surrounded by a tough outer husk. This species is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines, and for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
