Alstroemeria aquidauanica

Alstroemeria aquidauanica, commonly known as the Aquidauana lily, is a striking flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Alstroemeria aquidauanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
