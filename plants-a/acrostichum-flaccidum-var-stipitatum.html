Acrostichum flaccidum var. stipitatum

Acrostichum flaccidum var. stipitatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
