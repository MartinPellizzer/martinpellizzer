Actinochloa gracilis

Actinochloa gracilis, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three small, spikelet-like structures.

Actinochloa gracilis is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
