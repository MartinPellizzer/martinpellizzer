Allium cycladicum

Allium cycladicum, commonly known as the Cycladic garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its geographical origin, highlighting its significance in regional horticulture.
