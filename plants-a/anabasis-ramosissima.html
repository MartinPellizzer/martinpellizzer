Anabasis ramosissima

Anabasis ramosissima, commonly known as the jointed glassworts, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Anabasis ramosissima is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
