Alchemilla trunciloba var. pilosula

Alchemilla trunciloba var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a low-growing rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often silver-gray in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
