Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. ochroleuca

Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow sweetclover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional medicine.
