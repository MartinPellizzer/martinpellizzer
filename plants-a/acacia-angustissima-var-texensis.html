Acacia angustissima var. texensis

Acacia angustissima var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower clusters that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

The Texas acacia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
