Angelica laevis

Angelica laevis, commonly known as the smooth angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica laevis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent.
