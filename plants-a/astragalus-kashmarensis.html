Astragalus kashmarensis

Astragalus kashmarensis, commonly known as the Kashmar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus kashmarensis is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
