Andinia spiralis

Andinia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped arrangement of leaves and flowers, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying shades of white and green.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, Andinia spiralis is considered a rare and endangered species.
