Andropogon blakei

Andropogon blakei, commonly known as Blake's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon blakei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
