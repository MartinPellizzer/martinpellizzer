Asplenium eberhardtii

Asplenium eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with tightly packed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Asplenium genus.

This species is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.
