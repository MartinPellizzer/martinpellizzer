Alchemilla transiens var. corsica

Alchemilla transiens var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
