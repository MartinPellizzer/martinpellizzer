Arenaria otitoides

Arenaria otitoides, commonly known as the sandstone flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
