Astragalus lasiostylus

Astragalus lasiostylus, commonly known as the rough-styled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus lasiostylus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
