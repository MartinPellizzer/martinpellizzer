Arisaema sikokianum

Arisaema sikokianum, commonly known as the Siksika Indian Arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features a tuberous root system and possesses arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
