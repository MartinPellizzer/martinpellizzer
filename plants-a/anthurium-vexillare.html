Anthurium vexillare

Anthurium vexillare, commonly known as the flag anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a metallic sheen, and the inflorescence can range from white to red, depending on the variety.

Anthurium vexillare prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in humid environments with well-draining soil.
