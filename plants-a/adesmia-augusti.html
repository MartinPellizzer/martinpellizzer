Adesmia augusti

Adesmia augusti, commonly known as the August's legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which splits open to release the seeds.

Adesmia augusti plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for certain wildlife species.
