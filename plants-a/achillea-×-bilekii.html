Achillea × bilekii

Achillea × bilekii, commonly known as the Bilek's yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
