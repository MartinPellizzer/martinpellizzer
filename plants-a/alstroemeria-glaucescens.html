Alstroemeria glaucescens

Alstroemeria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a yellow throat, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Alstroemeria glaucescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
