Anacardium subcordatum

Anacardium subcordatum, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or the wild cashew, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruits containing a single seed surrounded by a toxic, resinous aril. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its bark is rough and fissured.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
