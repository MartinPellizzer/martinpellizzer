Adelia haemiolandra

Adelia haemiolandra, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
