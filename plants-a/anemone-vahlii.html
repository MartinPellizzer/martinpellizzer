Anemone vahlii

Anemone vahlii, commonly known as the desert anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and basal rosettes of fleshy, lobed leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
