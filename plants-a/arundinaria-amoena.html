Arundinaria amoena

Arundinaria amoena, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth, waxy texture.

Arundinaria amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
