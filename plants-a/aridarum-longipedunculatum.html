Aridarum longipedunculatum

Aridarum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the desert stalked orchid, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to arid environments.

It features elongated, slender peduncles that support its small, delicate flowers, which are typically pale green or yellowish in color. The plant's leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils or along dry riverbeds, where it can access limited moisture.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of particular interest to botanists studying desert flora.
