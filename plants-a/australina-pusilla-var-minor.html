Australina pusilla var. minor

Australina pusilla var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Australian lily, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, and it produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and elegant blooms.
