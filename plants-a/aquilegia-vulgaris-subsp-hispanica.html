Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. hispanica

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, and its stems are often branched and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
