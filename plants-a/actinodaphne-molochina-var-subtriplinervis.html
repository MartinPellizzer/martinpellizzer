Actinodaphne molochina var. subtriplinervis

Actinodaphne molochina var. subtriplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined actinodaphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves with a distinctive three-veined structure. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
