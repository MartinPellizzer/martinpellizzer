Aristida vestita var. eckloniana

Aristida vestita var. eckloniana, commonly known as the Ecklon's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
