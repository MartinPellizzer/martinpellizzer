Astragalus multijugus

Astragalus multijugus, commonly known as the many-juged milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Astragalus multijugus is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
