Aphanamixis

Aphanamixis, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Aphanamixidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescences that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Aphanamixis species are known for their small, inconspicuous flowers and their ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Botanically, they are of interest due to their unusual morphology and limited distribution.
