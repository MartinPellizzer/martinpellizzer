Astephanus fruticosus

Astephanus fruticosus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos biome. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, with a slightly hairy texture.

Astephanus fruticosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
