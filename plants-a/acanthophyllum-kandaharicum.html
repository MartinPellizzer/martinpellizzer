Acanthophyllum kandaharicum

Acanthophyllum kandaharicum, commonly known as the Kandahar spiny shrub, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in desert ecosystems and is used for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
