Avenula pratensis subsp. hirtifolia

Avenula pratensis subsp.

hirtifolia, commonly known as hairy meadow oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are hairy, especially along the edges, giving it its subspecies name. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

It plays an important role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
