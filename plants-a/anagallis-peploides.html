Anagallis peploides

Anagallis peploides, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer.
