Asparagus asiaticus

Asparagus asiaticus, commonly known as Chinese asparagus or Asian asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in traditional medicine and cuisine.
