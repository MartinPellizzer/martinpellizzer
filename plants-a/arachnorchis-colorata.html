Arachnorchis colorata

Arachnorchis colorata, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features a dense, hairy stem that supports its showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, with intricate patterns that resemble spider webs, giving the plant its genus name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique and colorful blooms.
