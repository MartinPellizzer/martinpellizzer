Anisostachya armandii

Anisostachya armandii, commonly known as the Armand's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically beige or pale green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
