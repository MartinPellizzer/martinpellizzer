Aglaonema minus var. scortechinii

Aglaonema minus var. scortechinii, commonly known as the Silver Queen plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves with prominent silver or white veins, which give it its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and prefers indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are not typically displayed.

Aglaonema minus var. scortechinii is known for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in humid environments.
