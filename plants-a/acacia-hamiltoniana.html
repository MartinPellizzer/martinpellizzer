Acacia hamiltoniana

Acacia hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its golden-yellow floral spikes. The plant features compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Acacia hamiltoniana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
