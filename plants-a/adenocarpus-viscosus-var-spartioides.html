Adenocarpus viscosus var. spartioides

Adenocarpus viscosus var. spartioides, commonly known as the sticky cress, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically covered in a sticky, glandular substance, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
