Aristida flaccida

Aristida flaccida, commonly known as the drooping grass or slender threeawn, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous growth habit, which help it establish in various environments.

Aristida flaccida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
