Albizia lophantha var. montana

Albizia lophantha var. montana, commonly known as mountain albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Albizia lophantha var. montana is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in humid, forested environments.
