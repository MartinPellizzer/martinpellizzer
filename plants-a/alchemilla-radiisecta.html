Alchemilla radiisecta

Alchemilla radiisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.
