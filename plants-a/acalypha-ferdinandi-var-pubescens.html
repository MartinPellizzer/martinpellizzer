Acalypha ferdinandi var. pubescens

Acalypha ferdinandi var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or flameleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to bronze, often with a contrasting lighter margin. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
