Albizia vaughanii

Albizia vaughanii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia vaughanii is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
