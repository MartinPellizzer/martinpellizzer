Asplenium arboreum var. pinnatum

Asplenium arboreum var. pinnatum, commonly known as the tree fern spleenwort, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender, and often branched fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a central trunk-like structure covered in a mass of roots, resembling a tree. Its fronds are typically pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in dense forests.
