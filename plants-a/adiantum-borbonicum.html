Adiantum borbonicum

Adiantum borbonicum, commonly known as the Bourbon maidenhair fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a heart. The plant features slender, wiry stems with small, bright green leaves that have a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Adiantum borbonicum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
