Aconitum confertiflorum

Aconitum confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant has a toxic root system containing potent alkaloids.

Aconitum confertiflorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
