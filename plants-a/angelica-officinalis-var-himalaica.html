Angelica officinalis var. himalaica

Angelica officinalis var. himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features large, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in complex umbels. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and is often found in moist, mountainous regions. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
