Anchusa officinalis subsp. angustifolia

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf honesty, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
