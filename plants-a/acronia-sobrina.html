Acronia sobrina

Acronia sobrina, commonly known as the silverleaf acronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive silver-green hue with prominent veins. It produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
