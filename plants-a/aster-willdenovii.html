Aster willdenovii

Aster willdenovii, commonly known as the Willdenow's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster willdenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
