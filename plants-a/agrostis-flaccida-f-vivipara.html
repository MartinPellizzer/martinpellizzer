Agrostis flaccida f. vivipara

Agrostis flaccida f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bentgrass, is a rare and unique variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous seeds, which are capable of germinating while still attached to the parent plant. This trait is uncommon in grasses and gives it a distinctive reproductive strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual reproductive mechanism, making it a subject of interest in plant biology and ecology.
