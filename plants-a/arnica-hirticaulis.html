Arnica hirticaulis

Arnica hirticaulis, commonly known as the rough-haired arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Arnica hirticaulis is valued for its medicinal properties, though it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
