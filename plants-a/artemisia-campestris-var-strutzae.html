Artemisia campestris var. strutzae

Artemisia campestris var. strutzae, commonly known as the creeping tarragon, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a sharp, herbal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value and ability to deter pests make it a popular choice in horticulture.
