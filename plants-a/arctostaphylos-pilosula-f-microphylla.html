Arctostaphylos pilosula f. microphylla

Arctostaphylos pilosula f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf manzanita, is a compact shrub native to California.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pinkish flowers in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the summer months.

This variety is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
