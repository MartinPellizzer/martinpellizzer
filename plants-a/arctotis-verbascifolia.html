Arctotis verbascifolia

Arctotis verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arctotis verbascifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
