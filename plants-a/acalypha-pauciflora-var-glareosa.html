Acalypha pauciflora var. glareosa

Acalypha pauciflora var. glareosa, commonly known as the glossy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often brightly colored leaves, which can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is particularly notable for its glossy texture and vibrant coloration.
