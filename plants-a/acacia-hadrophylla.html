Acacia hadrophylla

Acacia hadrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Acacia hadrophylla is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of nectar for native pollinators.
