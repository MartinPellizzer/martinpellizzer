Andrachne telephioides

Andrachne telephioides, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves and stems allow it to store water, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Andrachne telephioides is often found in sandy soils and is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
