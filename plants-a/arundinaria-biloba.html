Arundinaria biloba

Arundinaria biloba, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or sweet bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Arundinaria biloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
