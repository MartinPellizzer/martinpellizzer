Athamanta macedonica

Athamanta macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Athamanta macedonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
