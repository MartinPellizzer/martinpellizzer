Angraecum panicifolium

Angraecum panicifolium, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green with intricate patterns. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences that bear multiple blooms, which are pollinated primarily by the hawkmoth. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Angraecum panicifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
