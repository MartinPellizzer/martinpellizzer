Acalypha paxiana

Acalypha paxiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive silver or gray-green coloration. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Acalypha paxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
