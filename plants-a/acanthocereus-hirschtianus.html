Acanthocereus hirschtianus

Acanthocereus hirschtianus, commonly known as the desert night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and clustered flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
