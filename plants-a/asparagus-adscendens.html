Asparagus adscendens

Asparagus adscendens, commonly known as the silver asparagus or mountain asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, particularly in southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. Its inflorescence consists of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
