Aster novae-angliae

Aster novae-angliae, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy composite flowers that consist of ray florets and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its vibrant purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
