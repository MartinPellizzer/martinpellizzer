Astragalus sericoleucus

Astragalus sericoleucus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Astragalus sericoleucus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
