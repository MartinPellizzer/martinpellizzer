Adonis annua var. baetica

Adonis annua var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetica Crown Imperial, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, arranged in tall, branched inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, fleshy fruit containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period and historical use in herbal medicine.
