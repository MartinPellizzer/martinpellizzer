Artabotrys porphyrifolius

Artabotrys porphyrifolius, commonly known as the red-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
