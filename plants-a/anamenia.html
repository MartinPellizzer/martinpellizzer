Anamenia

Anamenia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the woodlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Anamenia is a perennial herb with lance-shaped leaves and is often found in shaded environments.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
