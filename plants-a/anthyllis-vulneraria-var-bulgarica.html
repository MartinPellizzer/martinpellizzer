Anthyllis vulneraria var. bulgarica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
