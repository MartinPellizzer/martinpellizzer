Adenostemma tinctorium

Adenostemma tinctorium, commonly known as redroot false flax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its roots are characterized by their reddish color and are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Adenostemma tinctorium is also valued for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in ecological restoration projects.
