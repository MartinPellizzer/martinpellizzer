Asparagus dichotomus

Asparagus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique growth pattern and adaptability make it a distinctive member of the Asparagaceae family.
