Acanthocalycium aurantiacum

Acanthocalycium aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-tipped barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall and relies on its succulent stems for water storage.

Its spines provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration.
