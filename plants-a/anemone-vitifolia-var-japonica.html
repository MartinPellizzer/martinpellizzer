Anemone vitifolia var. japonica

Anemone vitifolia var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating called bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in natural habitats.
