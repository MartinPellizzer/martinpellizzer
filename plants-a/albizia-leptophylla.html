Albizia leptophylla

Albizia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The leaves are pinnately compound, with slender leaflets that give the plant its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
