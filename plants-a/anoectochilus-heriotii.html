Anoectochilus heriotii

Anoectochilus heriotii, commonly known as the Heriot's slipper orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped orchid lip, which is typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
