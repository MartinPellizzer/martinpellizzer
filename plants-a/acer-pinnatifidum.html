Acer pinnatifidum

Acer pinnatifidum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
