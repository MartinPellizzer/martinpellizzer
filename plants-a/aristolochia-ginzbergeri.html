Aristolochia ginzbergeri

Aristolochia ginzbergeri, commonly known as the Ginzberger's arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisting growth habit. Its flowers are adapted for specific pollination by certain insects, and the plant contains toxic compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

However, due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated or used in traditional herbal medicine.
