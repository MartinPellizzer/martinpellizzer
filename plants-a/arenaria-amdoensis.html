Arenaria amdoensis

Arenaria amdoensis, commonly known as the Amdo sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in high-altitude, arid environments. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Arenaria amdoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
