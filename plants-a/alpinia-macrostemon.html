Alpinia macrostemon

Alpinia macrostemon, commonly known as the giant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in terminal panicles. Its botanical features include a complex inflorescence and a strong, spicy aroma reminiscent of ginger.

Alpinia macrostemon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
