Artemisia abyssinica

Artemisia abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains essential oils and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
