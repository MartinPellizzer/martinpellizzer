Acacia leptostachya

Acacia leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow or pale green inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Acacia leptostachya is also notable for its role in providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
