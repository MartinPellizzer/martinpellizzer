Anthistiria ciliata var. hispida

Anthistiria ciliata var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy anthistiria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
