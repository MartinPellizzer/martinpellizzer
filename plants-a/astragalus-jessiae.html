Astragalus jessiae

Astragalus jessiae, commonly known as Jessiae milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus jessiae is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
