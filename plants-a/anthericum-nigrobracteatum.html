Anthericum nigrobracteatum

Anthericum nigrobracteatum, commonly known as the black bract arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety bracts that enclose the spadix, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant features a large, heart-shaped leaf that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by specific insects, particularly flies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
