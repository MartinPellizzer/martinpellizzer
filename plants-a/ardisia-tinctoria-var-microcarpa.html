Ardisia tinctoria var. microcarpa

Ardisia tinctoria var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf Christmasberry, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green oval leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are visually striking and often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting, it is a popular choice for landscape design in tropical and subtropical regions.
