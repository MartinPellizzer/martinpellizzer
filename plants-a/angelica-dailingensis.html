Angelica dailingensis

Angelica dailingensis, commonly known as the Dailing angelica, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica dailingensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its roots, which are used to treat various ailments.
