Abies eichleri

Abies eichleri, commonly known as the Eichler's fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its branches. The cones of Abies eichleri are typically conical and woody, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the montane ecosystems in its native range.
