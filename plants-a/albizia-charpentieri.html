Albizia charpentieri

Albizia charpentieri, commonly known as the African shrub or white acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. It produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Albizia charpentieri is often found in savannas and woodland areas, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
