Arundinella zollingeri

Arundinella zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's bamboo, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly grassy aroma.

Arundinella zollingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
