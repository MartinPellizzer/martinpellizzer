Abolboda acicularis var. granularis

Abolboda acicularis var. granularis, commonly known as the granular needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in compact clumps, with short, rhizomatous stems that help it survive in harsh, dry environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is well adapted to low-nutrient soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
