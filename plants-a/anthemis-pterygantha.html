Anthemis pterygantha

Anthemis pterygantha, commonly known as the winged anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The leaves are linear and sometimes covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
