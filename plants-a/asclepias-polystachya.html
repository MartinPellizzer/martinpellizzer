Asclepias polystachya

Asclepias polystachya, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of bright orange to red flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is a common feature in the milkweed genus. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Asclepias polystachya is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and serves as a food source for monarch butterfly larvae.
