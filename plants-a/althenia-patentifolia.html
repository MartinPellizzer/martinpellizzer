Althenia patentifolia

Althenia patentifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf althenia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Althenia patentifolia is typically found in rocky soils and dry environments, thriving in full sun.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
