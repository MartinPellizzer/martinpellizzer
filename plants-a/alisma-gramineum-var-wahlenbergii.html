Alisma gramineum var. wahlenbergii

Alisma gramineum var. wahlenbergii, commonly known as Wahlenberg's water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps it thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
