Atriplex muelleri var. stipitata

Atriplex muelleri var. stipitata, commonly known as the mueller's saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant typically grows in coastal and inland saline soils, thriving in areas with high salt content. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of harsh environmental conditions.
