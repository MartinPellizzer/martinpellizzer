Arundinaria kumasasa

Arundinaria kumasasa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Arundinaria kumasasa is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
