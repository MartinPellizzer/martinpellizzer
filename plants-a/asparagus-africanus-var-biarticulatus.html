Asparagus africanus var. biarticulatus

Asparagus africanus var. biarticulatus, commonly known as the African asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with a distinctive branching pattern, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
