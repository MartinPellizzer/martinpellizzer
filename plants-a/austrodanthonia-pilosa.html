Austrodanthonia pilosa

Austrodanthonia pilosa, commonly known as wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive silver-blue to grey-green coloration. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense, low-growing clumps. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Austrodanthonia pilosa is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
