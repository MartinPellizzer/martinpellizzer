Asplundia gamotepala

Asplundia gamotepala, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their starchy texture.

Asplundia gamotepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
