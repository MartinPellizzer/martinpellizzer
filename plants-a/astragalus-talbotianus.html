Astragalus talbotianus

Astragalus talbotianus, commonly known as Talbot's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
