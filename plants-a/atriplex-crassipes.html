Atriplex crassipes

Atriplex crassipes, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to saline and arid environments, making it a common species in coastal and desert regions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Atriplex crassipes plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
