Acacia strigulosa

Acacia strigulosa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia strigulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
