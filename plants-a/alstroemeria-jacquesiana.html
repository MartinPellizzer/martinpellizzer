Alstroemeria jacquesiana

Alstroemeria jacquesiana, commonly known as the Jacques's alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
