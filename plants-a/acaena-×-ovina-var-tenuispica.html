Acaena × ovina var. tenuispica

Acaena × ovina var. tenuispica, commonly known as the slender bristled burr, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
