Agrostis tsitondroinensis

Agrostis tsitondroinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agrostis tsitondroinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
