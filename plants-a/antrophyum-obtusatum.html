Antrophyum obtusatum

Antrophyum obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed cycad, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its reproductive structures include male and female cones, which are typical of cycads.

Antrophyum obtusatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
