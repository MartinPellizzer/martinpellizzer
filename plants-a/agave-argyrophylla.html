Agave argyrophylla

Agave argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf agave, is a perennial succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which features a waxy coating to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom once in its lifetime. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water.

Agave argyrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
