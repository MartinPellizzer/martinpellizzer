Aponogeton dinteri

Aponogeton dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's arrowhead, is a rare aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that resemble arrowheads, typically floating on the water surface with the base submerged. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water, adding a delicate aesthetic to its habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light exposure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
