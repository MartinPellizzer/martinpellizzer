Arundinaria acerba

Arundinaria acerba, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Arundinaria acerba is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
