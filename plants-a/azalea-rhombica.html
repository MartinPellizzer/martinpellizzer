Azalea rhombica

Azalea rhombica, commonly known as the rhombic azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known shrubs like rhododendrons. Azalea rhombica typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
