Acanthocereus chiapensis

Acanthocereus chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
