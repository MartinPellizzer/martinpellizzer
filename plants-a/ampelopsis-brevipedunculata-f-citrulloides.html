Ampelopsis brevipedunculata f. citrulloides

Ampelopsis brevipedunculata f. citrulloides, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a cultivar of the species Ampelopsis brevipedunculata, which is native to eastern North America.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine characterized by its bright yellow to orange foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and as a decorative element in gardens.
