Alstroemeria angustifolia var. conferta

Alstroemeria angustifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the narrowleaf alstroemeria, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of colorful, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
