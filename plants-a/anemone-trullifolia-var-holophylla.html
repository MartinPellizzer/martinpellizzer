Anemone trullifolia var. holophylla

Anemone trullifolia var. holophylla, commonly known as the ghost anemone, is a striking perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
