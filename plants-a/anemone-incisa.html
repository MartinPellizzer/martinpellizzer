Anemone incisa

Anemone incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive notched or cut appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Anemone incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
