Andropogon monticola

Andropogon monticola, commonly known as mountain bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which turns golden in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red.

Andropogon monticola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
