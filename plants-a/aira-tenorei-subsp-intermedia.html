Aira tenorei subsp. intermedia

Aira tenorei subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate air grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
