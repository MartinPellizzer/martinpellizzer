Aetheilema imbricatum var. verticillare

Aetheilema imbricatum var. verticillare, commonly known as the clustered leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
