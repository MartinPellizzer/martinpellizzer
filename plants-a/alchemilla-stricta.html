Alchemilla stricta

Alchemilla stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in branched inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla stricta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
