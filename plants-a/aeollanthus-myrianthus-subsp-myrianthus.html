Aeollanthus myrianthus subsp. myrianthus

Aeollanthus myrianthus subsp.

myrianthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a low-growing rosette. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
