Amorpha colorata

Amorpha colorata, commonly known as the scarlet beeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive red calyx that becomes showy as the flowers age.

Amorpha colorata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
