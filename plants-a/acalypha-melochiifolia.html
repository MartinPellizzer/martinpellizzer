Acalypha melochiifolia

Acalypha melochiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or melochiifolia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests or along stream banks.

Acalypha melochiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
