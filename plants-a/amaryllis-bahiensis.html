Amaryllis bahiensis

Amaryllis bahiensis, commonly known as the Holy Night lily, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem that bears multiple blooms, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Amaryllis bahiensis is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
