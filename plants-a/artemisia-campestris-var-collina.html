Artemisia campestris var. collina

Artemisia campestris var. collina, commonly known as field wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, finely divided leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.
