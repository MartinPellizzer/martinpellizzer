Adonis riparia

Adonis riparia, commonly known as the river adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Adonis riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
