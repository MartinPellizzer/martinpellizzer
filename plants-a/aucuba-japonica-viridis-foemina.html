Aucuba japonica viridis-foemina

Aucuba japonica viridis-foemina, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features broad, waxy, dark green leaves with a striking yellow margin that becomes more pronounced in winter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it an excellent specimen plant for garden borders or foundation plantings.
