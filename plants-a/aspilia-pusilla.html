Aspilia pusilla

Aspilia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf flame plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Aspilia pusilla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
