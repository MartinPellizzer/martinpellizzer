Aristolochia longa subsp. paucinervis

Aristolochia longa subsp.

paucinervis, commonly known as the slender Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are often adorned with a complex, intricate structure. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced venation compared to other Aristolochia species.
