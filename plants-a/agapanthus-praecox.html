Agapanthus praecox

Agapanthus praecox, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a ornamental plant with both aesthetic and ecological value.
