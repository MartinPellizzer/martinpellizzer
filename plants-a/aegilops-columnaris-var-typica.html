Aegilops columnaris var. typica

Aegilops columnaris var. typica, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a flowering plant in the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically found at the base of the spikelets. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
