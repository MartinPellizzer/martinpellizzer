Aralia chinensis var. longibractea

Aralia chinensis var. longibractea, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or long-bract aralia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by distinctive, elongated bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
