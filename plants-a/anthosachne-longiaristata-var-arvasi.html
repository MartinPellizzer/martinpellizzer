Anthosachne longiaristata var. arvasi

Anthosachne longiaristata var. arvasi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Anthosachne.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-parted flowers in clusters. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
