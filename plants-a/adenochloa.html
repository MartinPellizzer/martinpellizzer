Adenochloa

Adenochloa, commonly known as the spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of spikelets that form the inflorescence. The leaves are typically narrow and blade-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Adenochloa species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in their native habitats.
