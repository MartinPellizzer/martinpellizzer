Alchemilla splendens proles fulgens

Alchemilla splendens proles fulgens, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
