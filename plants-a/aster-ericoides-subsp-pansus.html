Aster ericoides subsp. pansus

Aster ericoides subsp.

pansus, commonly known as the purple mountain aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America. It typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine habitats.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
