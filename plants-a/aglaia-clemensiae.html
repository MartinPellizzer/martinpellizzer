Aglaia clemensiae

Aglaia clemensiae, commonly known as the Philippine aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Aglaia clemensiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
