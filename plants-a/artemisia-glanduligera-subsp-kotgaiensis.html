Artemisia glanduligera subsp. kotgaiensis

Artemisia glanduligera subsp.

kotgaiensis, commonly known as the Kotgai wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
