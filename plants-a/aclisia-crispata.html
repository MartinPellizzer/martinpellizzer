Aclisia crispata

Aclisia crispata, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant.
