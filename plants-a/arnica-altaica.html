Arnica altaica

Arnica altaica, commonly known as high mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its stems are upright and often branched, while its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette.

Arnica altaica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
