Agastache palmeri

Agastache palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Agastache palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
