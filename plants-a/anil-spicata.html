Anil spicata

Anil spicata, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Anil spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
