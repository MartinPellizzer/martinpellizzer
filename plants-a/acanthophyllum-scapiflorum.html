Acanthophyllum scapiflorum

Acanthophyllum scapiflorum, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow or green flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Acanthophyllum scapiflorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
