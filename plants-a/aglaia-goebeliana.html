Aglaia goebeliana

Aglaia goebeliana, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Aglaia goebeliana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
