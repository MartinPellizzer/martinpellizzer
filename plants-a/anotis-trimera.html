Anotis trimera

Anotis trimera, commonly known as the common plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anotis trimera is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
