Asplenium macrocarpon

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the leaves, and it produces large, fleshy, edible spores that are a food source for various invertebrates.

Asplenium macrocarpon is found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
