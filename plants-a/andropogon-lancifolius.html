Andropogon lancifolius

Andropogon lancifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering stalks produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually blue to purple in color.

Andropogon lancifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
