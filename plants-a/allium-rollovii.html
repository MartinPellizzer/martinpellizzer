Allium rollovii

Allium rollovii, commonly known as the Turk's cap leek, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, rounded bulb, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium rollovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal practices.
