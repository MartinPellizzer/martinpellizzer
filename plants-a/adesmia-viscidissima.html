Adesmia viscidissima

Adesmia viscidissima, commonly known as the sticky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sticky glandular hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Adesmia viscidissima is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
