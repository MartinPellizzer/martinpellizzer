Amaryllis falcata

Amaryllis falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring or early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
