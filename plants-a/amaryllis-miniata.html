Amaryllis miniata

Amaryllis miniata, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces clusters of flowers on tall, sturdy stems, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Amaryllis miniata is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
