Arenaria jamesoniana

Arenaria jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in maritime climates. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Arenaria jamesoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
