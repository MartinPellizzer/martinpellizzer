Aspalathus alpestris

Aspalathus alpestris, commonly known as the whitebosch, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Aspalathus alpestris is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
