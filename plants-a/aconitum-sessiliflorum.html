Aconitum sessiliflorum

Aconitum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
