Arundinaria flabellata

Arundinaria flabellata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green, with a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated.

Arundinaria flabellata is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its ornamental appearance and use in landscaping.
