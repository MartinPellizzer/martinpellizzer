Athamanta tenuifolia

Athamanta tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
