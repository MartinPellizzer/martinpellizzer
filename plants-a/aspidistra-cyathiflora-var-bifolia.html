Aspidistra cyathiflora var. bifolia

Aspidistra cyathiflora var. bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved aspidistra, is a rare and striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically elongated and strap-like. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

The varietal name "bifolia" refers to its distinctive two-leaf structure, which is a notable feature in its botanical description.
