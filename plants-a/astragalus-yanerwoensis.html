Astragalus yanerwoensis

Astragalus yanerwoensis, commonly known as the Yanerwo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for containing bioactive compounds, which have been of interest in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable but vulnerable species.
