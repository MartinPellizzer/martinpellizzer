Artemisia seriphium var. effusa

Artemisia seriphium var. effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, slightly resinous leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
