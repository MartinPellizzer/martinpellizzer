Aeluropus mucronatus

Aeluropus mucronatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Aeluropus mucronatus is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions.
