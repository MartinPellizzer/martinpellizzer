Aphanostephus skirrhobasis f. quasigigantiusculus

Aphanostephus skirrhobasis f. quasigigantiusculus, commonly known as the giant form of the common steppe grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppe and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
