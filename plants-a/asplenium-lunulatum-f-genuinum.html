Asplenium lunulatum f. genuinum

Asplenium lunulatum f. genuinum, commonly known as the moon's crescent fern, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its unique, crescent-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are narrow and have a distinct, undulating pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
