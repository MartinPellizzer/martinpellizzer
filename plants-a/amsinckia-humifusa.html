Amsinckia humifusa

Amsinckia humifusa, commonly known as the desert bluecurls, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, sprawling stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Amsinckia humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
