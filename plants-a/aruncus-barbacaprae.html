Aruncus barbacaprae

Aruncus barbacaprae, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features striking, feathery plumes of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has long, slender leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
