Astragalus anamurensis

Astragalus anamurensis, commonly known as the Anamur milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus anamurensis plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and supporting pollinator populations.
