Aster asagrayi

Aster asagrayi, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its showy purple or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in regions with mild, dry climates.
