Acer laisuense

Acer laisuense, commonly known as the Laishu maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
