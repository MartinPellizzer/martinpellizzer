Acinos suaveolens f. tinctus

Acinos suaveolens f. tinctus, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or tinctured sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its aromatic, fragrant foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The 'tinctus' form is distinguished by its more intense fragrance and slightly darker foliage compared to the typical variety.
