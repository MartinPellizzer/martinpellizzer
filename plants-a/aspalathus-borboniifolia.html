Aspalathus borboniifolia

Aspalathus borboniifolia, commonly known as the Bourbon tea plant, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a unique herbal tea.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate.
