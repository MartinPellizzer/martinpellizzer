Arabis lyrata subsp. kamchatica

Arabis lyrata subsp.

kamchatica, commonly known as the Kamchadenskaya rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia and the Russian Far East. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats, where it contributes to the local flora's resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
