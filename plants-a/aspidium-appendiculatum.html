Aspidium appendiculatum

Aspidium appendiculatum, commonly known as the marginal aspidium, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Aspidium appendiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
