Acacia papulosa

Acacia papulosa, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, papillate (bumpy) structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia papulosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is a hardy species that can withstand drought conditions.
