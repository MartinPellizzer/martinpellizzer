Astragalus aleppicus var. bserinensis

Astragalus aleppicus var. bserinensis, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
