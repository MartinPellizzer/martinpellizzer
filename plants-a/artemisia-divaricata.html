Artemisia divaricata

Artemisia divaricata, commonly known as silver dust sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia divaricata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
