Arundinaria tsukubensis

Arundinaria tsukubensis, commonly known as the Tsukubensis bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
