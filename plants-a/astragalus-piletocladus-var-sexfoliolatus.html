Astragalus piletocladus var. sexfoliolatus

Astragalus piletocladus var. sexfoliolatus, commonly known as the six-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with six leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-desert and steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
