Ageratum corymbosum var. lactiflorum

Ageratum corymbosum var. lactiflorum, commonly known as the milkflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, adding a decorative touch to landscapes and floral arrangements.
