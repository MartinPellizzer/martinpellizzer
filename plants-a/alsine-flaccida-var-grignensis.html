Alsine flaccida var. grignensis

Alsine flaccida var. grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan alsine, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, pale green to blue-green leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and road sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaranthaceae family.
