Ampelopsis delavayana var. gentiliana

Ampelopsis delavayana var. gentiliana, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its long, twisting vines that can climb trees or trellises. Its berries, which turn red in autumn, are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
