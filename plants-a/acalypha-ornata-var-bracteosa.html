Acalypha ornata var. bracteosa

Acalypha ornata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bracteosa spurflower, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bright red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are surrounded by the showy red bracts, making it popular in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded conditions make it a favored choice for gardeners seeking visual interest.
