Allium pseudobodeanum

Allium pseudobodeanum, commonly known as the Bodeanum garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant features bulbous stems that produce small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are grass-like and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making them a valuable addition to culinary and medicinal practices.
