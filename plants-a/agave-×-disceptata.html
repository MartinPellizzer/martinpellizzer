Agave × disceptata

Agave × disceptata, commonly known as the blue agave, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Agave americana and Agave angustifolia.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and tall, spire-like inflorescences. The plant produces large, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. It is a succulent with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Agave × disceptata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in the production of tequila.
