Alhagi napaulensium

Alhagi napaulensium, commonly known as the Indian burweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Alhagi napaulensium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor nutrient environments.
