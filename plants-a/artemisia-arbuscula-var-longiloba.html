Artemisia arbuscula var. longiloba

Artemisia arbuscula var. longiloba, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features silvery-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive minty scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
