Acnistus arborescens

Acnistus arborescens, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and its showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly hairy surface.

Acnistus arborescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
