Aglaia heterotricha

Aglaia heterotricha, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aglaia heterotricha is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
