Agastache pallidiflora var. neomexicana

Agastache pallidiflora var. neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
