Apteranthes europaea subsp. europaea

Apteranthes europaea subsp.

europaea, commonly known as European bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
