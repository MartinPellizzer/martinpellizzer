Adicea verbascifolia

Adicea verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
