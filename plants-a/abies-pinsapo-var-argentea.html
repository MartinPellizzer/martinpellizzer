Abies pinsapo var. argentea

Abies pinsapo var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the Sierra de Grazalema in southern Spain.

It is characterized by its slow growth, evergreen foliage, and distinctive silver-white variegation on its needles. The tree has a conical shape with a dense crown and produces small, cylindrical cones. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
