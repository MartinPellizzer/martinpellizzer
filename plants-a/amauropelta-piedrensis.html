Amauropelta piedrensis

Amauropelta piedrensis, commonly known as the piedrensis shield fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the humid forests of southeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. Its unique morphology and restricted range make it an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
