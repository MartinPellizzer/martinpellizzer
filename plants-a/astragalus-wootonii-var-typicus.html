Astragalus wootonii var. typicus

Astragalus wootonii var. typicus, commonly known as Wooton's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces compound leaves with numerous leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
