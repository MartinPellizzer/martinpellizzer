Areca triandra

Areca triandra, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that grow in a crown at the top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, oval fruits that contain a single seed. Areca triandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
