Astragalus asper var. leucotrichus

Astragalus asper var. leucotrichus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
