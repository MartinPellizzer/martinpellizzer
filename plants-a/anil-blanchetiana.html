Anil blanchetiana

Anil blanchetiana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anil blanchetiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
