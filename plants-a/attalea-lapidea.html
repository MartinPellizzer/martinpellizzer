Attalea lapidea

Attalea lapidea, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into dark purple, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are used for thatching and weaving, while the fruit is consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
