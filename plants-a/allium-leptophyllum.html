Allium leptophyllum

Allium leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments.

Allium leptophyllum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
