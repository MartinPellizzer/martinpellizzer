Agathosma serratifolia

Agathosma serratifolia, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.
