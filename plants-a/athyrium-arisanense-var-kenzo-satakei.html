Athyrium arisanense var. kenzo-satakei

Athyrium arisanense var. kenzo-satakei, commonly known as the Kenzo-satakei Japanese shield fern, is a striking fern species native to Japan.

It features elegant, lanceolate fronds that are dark green and often adorned with a silvery sheen. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
