Astragalus guestii

Astragalus guestii, commonly known as the Colorado gorse, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Astragalus guestii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
