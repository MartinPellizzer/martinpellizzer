Acrocomia odorata

Acrocomia odorata, commonly known as the oil palm or the Brazilian oil palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves and large, clustered inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a high amount of oil, making it economically important.

The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is adapted to tropical climates.
