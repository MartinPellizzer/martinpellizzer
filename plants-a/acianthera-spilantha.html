Acianthera spilantha

Acianthera spilantha, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Acianthera spilantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
