Acacia penninervis var. falciformis

Acacia penninervis var. falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
