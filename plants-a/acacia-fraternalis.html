Acacia fraternalis

Acacia fraternalis, commonly known as the sacred acacia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, feathery leaves and yellow or golden-green flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems. Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices, and the plant has cultural significance in various indigenous communities.

Acacia fraternalis also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.
