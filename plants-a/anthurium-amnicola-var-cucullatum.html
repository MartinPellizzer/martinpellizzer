Anthurium amnicola var. cucullatum

Anthurium amnicola var. cucullatum, commonly known as the helmet anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant spathes, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which enclose the small, clustered flowers. Its foliage and inflorescence make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
