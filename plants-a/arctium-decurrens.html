Arctium decurrens

Arctium decurrens, commonly known as the tall burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its most distinctive feature is the large, spiny fruit, which contains seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk.

Arctium decurrens is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
