Antrophyopsis bivittata

Antrophyopsis bivittata, commonly known as the two-striped antrophyopsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive two-toned leaves, which often display contrasting green and yellow stripes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
