Abies umbellata

Abies umbellata, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone-like structures that hang in clusters. The tree's bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, while its branches are dense and often droop downward. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The white fir plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
