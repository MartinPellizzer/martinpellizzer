Avena strigosa proles brevis

Avena strigosa proles brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
