Astephanus ovatus

Astephanus ovatus, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Astephanus ovatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
