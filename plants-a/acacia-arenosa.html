Acacia arenosa

Acacia arenosa, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, bean-like pods and feathery, bipinnate leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that access groundwater.

Acacia arenosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood in its native habitats.
