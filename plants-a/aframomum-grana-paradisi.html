Aframomum grana-paradisi

Aframomum grana-paradisi, commonly known as the African grain of paradise, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent aroma and potential medicinal properties.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both culinary and ethnobotanical contexts.
