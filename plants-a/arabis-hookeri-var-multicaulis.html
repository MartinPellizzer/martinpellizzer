Arabis hookeri var. multicaulis

Arabis hookeri var. multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
