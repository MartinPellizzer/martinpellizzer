Alyssum orophilum

Alyssum orophilum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hair.

Alyssum orophilum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
