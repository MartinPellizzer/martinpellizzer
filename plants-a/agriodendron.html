Agriodendron

Agriodendron, commonly known as the desert rose, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of southern Asia. The plants are characterized by their succulent stems and showy, often brightly colored flowers. They are typically shrubs or small trees, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Agriodendron species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their resilience and aesthetic beauty.
