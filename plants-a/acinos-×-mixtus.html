Acinos × mixtus

Acinos × mixtus, commonly known as the hybrid alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Acinos × mixtus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
