Ammannia hexandra

Ammannia hexandra, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the warm season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia hexandra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
