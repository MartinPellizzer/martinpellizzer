Achnatherum effusum

Achnatherum effusum, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Achnatherum effusum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is used in restoration ecology.
