Adansonia bozy

Adansonia bozy, commonly known as the baobab tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its massive, swollen trunk and thick, fibrous bark. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall and has a lifespan that can exceed 1,000 years. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the evening.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp.
