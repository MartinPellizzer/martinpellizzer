Andropogon pseudoischaemum

Andropogon pseudoischaemum, commonly known as eastern wood sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant has tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces dark brown, three-lobed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
