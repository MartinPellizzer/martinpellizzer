Acalypha glandulosa var. brevistachya

Acalypha glandulosa var. brevistachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, clustered inflorescences that range from red to orange in color. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
