Alsophila congoensis

Alsophila congoensis, commonly known as the Congo tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to central Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila congoensis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
