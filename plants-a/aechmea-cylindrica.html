Aechmea cylindrica

Aechmea cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces striking pink or red flower spikes that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and moderate humidity.

Morphology

Aechmea cylindrica has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, but it has a short, sturdy base with a thick, smooth bark-like covering. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with violet petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a cylindrical capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for water storage, typical of bromeliads.
