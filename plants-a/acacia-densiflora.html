Acacia densiflora

Acacia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform photosynthesis. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
