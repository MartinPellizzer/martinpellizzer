Astragalus onobrychis f. albiflorus

Astragalus onobrychis f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bur clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and its distinctive, pod-like fruit that splits open to release seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
