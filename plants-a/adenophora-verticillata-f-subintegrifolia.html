Adenophora verticillata f. subintegrifolia

Adenophora verticillata f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear whorled clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other forms of Adenophora. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
