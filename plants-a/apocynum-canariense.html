Apocynum canariense

Apocynum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Islands milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Apocynum canariense is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.
