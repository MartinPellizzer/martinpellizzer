Antirrhinum breweri var. ovalifolium

Antirrhinum breweri var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, oval-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
