Alsine imbricata f. glandulosa

Alsine imbricata f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alsike clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
