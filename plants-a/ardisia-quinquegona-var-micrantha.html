Ardisia quinquegona var. micrantha

Ardisia quinquegona var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are visually striking. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

The berries are edible for birds but toxic to humans, adding to its ecological significance.
