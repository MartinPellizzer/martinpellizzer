Archidendron kunstleri subsp. kunstleri

Archidendron kunstleri subsp.

kunstleri, commonly known as the African wisteria, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa. It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces pendant, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
