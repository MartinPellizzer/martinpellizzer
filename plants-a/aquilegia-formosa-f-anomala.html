Aquilegia formosa f. anomala

Aquilegia formosa f. anomala, commonly known as the Sierra formosa columbine, is a variant of the formosa columbine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is known for its showy, often colorful flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
