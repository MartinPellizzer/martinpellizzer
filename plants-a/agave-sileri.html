Agave sileri

Agave sileri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave sileri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
