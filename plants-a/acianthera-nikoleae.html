Acianthera nikoleae

Acianthera nikoleae, commonly known as Nikolea's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy roots adapted to its humid, shaded forest habitat. Its floral structure includes specialized pollination mechanisms, making it an important subject in botanical and ecological studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
