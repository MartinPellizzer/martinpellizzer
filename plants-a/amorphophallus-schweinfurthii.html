Amorphophallus schweinfurthii

Amorphophallus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or purple. It thrives in moist, swampy habitats and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native environment.
