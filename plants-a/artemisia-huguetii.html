Artemisia huguetii

Artemisia huguetii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia huguetii is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of natural insect repellents.
