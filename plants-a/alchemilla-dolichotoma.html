Alchemilla dolichotoma

Alchemilla dolichotoma, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla dolichotoma is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
