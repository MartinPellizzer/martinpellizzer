Amianthium macrotox

Amianthium macrotox, commonly known as the giant swamp lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as swamps and marshes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for wetland ecosystems and botanical gardens.
