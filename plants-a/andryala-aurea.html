Andryala aurea

Andryala aurea, commonly known as golden andryala, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Andryala aurea is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also appreciated for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
