Acanthophyllum persicum

Acanthophyllum persicum, commonly known as Persian spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
