Aneurolepidium karataviense

Aneurolepidium karataviense, commonly known as the Karatav Valley grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, temperate regions and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand arid conditions and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
