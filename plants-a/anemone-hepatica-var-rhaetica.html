Anemone hepatica var. rhaetica

Anemone hepatica var. rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian liverwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit, with bright yellow flowers emerging from rosettes of dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive liver-shaped appearance, giving rise to its common name.

The flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
