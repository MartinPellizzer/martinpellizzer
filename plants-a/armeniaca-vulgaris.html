Armeniaca vulgaris

Armeniaca vulgaris, commonly known as the peach, is a deciduous fruit tree native to southwest Asia.

It produces a sweet, fuzzy fruit with a hard pit inside, which contains the seed. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other stone fruits like plums and cherries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Armeniaca vulgaris is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
