Agapetes flava var. flava

Agapetes flava var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly yellow, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
