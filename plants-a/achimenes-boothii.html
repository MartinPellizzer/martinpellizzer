Achimenes boothii

Achimenes boothii, commonly known as the velvetleaf achimenes, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Achimenes boothii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Morphology

Achimenes boothii has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender, slightly hairy, and has a reddish-brown bark that becomes smoother with age. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent spur, coming in shades of pink and purple, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adventitious, helping the plant anchor itself and absorb nutrients from the soil.
