Astragalus striatellus var. macrocarpus

Astragalus striatellus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the broad-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
