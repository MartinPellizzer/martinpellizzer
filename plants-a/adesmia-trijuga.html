Adesmia trijuga

Adesmia trijuga, commonly known as the three-angled legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Adesmia trijuga is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
