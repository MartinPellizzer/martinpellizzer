Astragalus gryphus

Astragalus gryphus, commonly known as the Greek milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus gryphus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
