Astragalus syrticola

Astragalus syrticola, commonly known as the Syrian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Astragalus syrticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
