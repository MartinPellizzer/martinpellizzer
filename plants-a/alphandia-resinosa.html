Alphandia resinosa

Alphandia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous alphandia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, resinous coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Alphandia resinosa is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments, and its resinous properties make it resistant to drought and insect damage.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
