Artemisia namanganica

Artemisia namanganica, commonly known as Namangan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs.

Artemisia namanganica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
