Antidesma pentandrum var. pseudopentandrum

Antidesma pentandrum var. pseudopentandrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved antidesma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
