Alseis schippii

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Alseis schippii is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
