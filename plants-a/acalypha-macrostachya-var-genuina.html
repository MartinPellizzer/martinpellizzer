Acalypha macrostachya var. genuina

Acalypha macrostachya var. genuina, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
