Astragalus angustidens var. strictifolius

Astragalus angustidens var. strictifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in dry conditions.
