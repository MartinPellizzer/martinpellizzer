Agrostis alba subsp. vinealis

Agrostis alba subsp.

vinealis, commonly known as silvergrass or white grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and roadside areas. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are wind-pollinated. Agrostis alba subsp.

vinealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
