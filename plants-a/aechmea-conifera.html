Aechmea conifera

Aechmea conifera, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces striking red or orange bracts that enclose its flowers. Aechmea conifera thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique structure and vibrant appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea conifera has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green surface and a silvery underside.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, fibrous rhizome that anchors it to the substrate. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts, producing small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, pollinated by wind and insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red or orange berry that contains numerous small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by animals feeding on the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil, typical of epiphytic growth habits.
