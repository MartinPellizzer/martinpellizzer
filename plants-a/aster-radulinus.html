Aster radulinus

Aster radulinus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet to purple petals surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and branching.

Aster radulinus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
