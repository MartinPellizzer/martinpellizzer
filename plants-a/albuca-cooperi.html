Albuca cooperi

Albuca cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces tall, slender flower stalks with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
