Astragalus lonchocarpus var. hamiltonii

Astragalus lonchocarpus var. hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
