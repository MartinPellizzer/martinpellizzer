Alcantarea geniculata

Alcantarea geniculata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, hibiscus-like flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features a woody stem with a distinctive zigzag pattern, which gives it its scientific name "geniculata," meaning "kinked" or "bent.". Its leaves are broad, palmate, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its native arid environment.

Alcantarea geniculata is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
