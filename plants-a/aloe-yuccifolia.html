Aloe yuccifolia

Aloe yuccifolia, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered with a thick layer of wax to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe yuccifolia is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
