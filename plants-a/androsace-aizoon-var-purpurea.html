Androsace aizoon var. purpurea

Androsace aizoon var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
