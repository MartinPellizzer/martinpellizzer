Astragalus leioclados

Astragalus leioclados, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
