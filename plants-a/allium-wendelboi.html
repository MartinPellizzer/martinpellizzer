Allium wendelboi

Allium wendelboi, commonly known as the Wendelboi garlic, is a rare and unique species within the Allium genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are small and often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinct floral structure.
