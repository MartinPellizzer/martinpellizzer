Averrhoa bilimbi f. papuana

Averrhoa bilimbi f. papuana, commonly known as the African eggplant or bitter gourd, is a variant of the bilimbi plant native to tropical regions.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that are typically green and turn yellow when mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
