Aegilops triaristata var. bispiculata

Aegilops triaristata var. bispiculata, commonly known as the two-tailed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive two-tailed inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
