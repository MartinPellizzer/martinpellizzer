Aropsis palustris

Aropsis palustris, commonly known as the marsh arthropod, is a rare flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
