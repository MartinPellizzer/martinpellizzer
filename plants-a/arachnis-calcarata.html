Arachnis calcarata

Arachnis calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a few flowers, each with a unique, elongated lip that resembles a spur. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Arachnis calcarata is often found in sunny, calcareous habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
