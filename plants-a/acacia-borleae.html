Acacia borleae

Acacia borleae, commonly known as the Bornean acacia, is a shrub native to the islands of Borneo and Sumatra.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are compound and divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like seed pods containing minute, hard seeds. Acacia borleae thrives in tropical rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
