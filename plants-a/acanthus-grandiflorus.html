Acanthus grandiflorus

Acanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered acanthus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
