Astragalus vexillaris

Astragalus vexillaris, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus vexillaris is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
