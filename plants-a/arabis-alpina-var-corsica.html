Arabis alpina var. corsica

Arabis alpina var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
