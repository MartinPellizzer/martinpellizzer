Asclepias albiflora

Asclepias albiflora, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is toxic to some animals. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias albiflora plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly monarch butterflies.
