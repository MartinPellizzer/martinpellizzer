Azedarach odoratum

Azedarach odoratum, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The Persian lilac is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
