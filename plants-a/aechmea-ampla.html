Aechmea ampla

Aechmea ampla, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, known as a rosette, which collects water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with prominent white or silver veins, and they are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. Aechmea ampla produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea ampla has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and pointed, arranged in a compact spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a silvery underside and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, brownish bark, growing upright from the base of the leaf rosette. Its flowers are borne in a dense, spike-like inflorescence, with violet or purple petals and yellow stamens, blooming from late winter to early spring, and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
