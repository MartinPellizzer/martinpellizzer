Abutilon sessiliflorum

Abutilon sessiliflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
