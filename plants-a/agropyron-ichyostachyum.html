Agropyron ichyostachyum

Agropyron ichyostachyum, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert grasslands and shrublands. Its deep root system helps it survive in low-water environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Agropyron ichyostachyum plays a role in preventing soil erosion and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
