Aconitum napellus f. paniculatum

Aconitum napellus f. paniculatum, commonly known as the European monkshood paniculate form, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves and produces panicle-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Like its parent species, it contains toxic alkaloids and requires caution when handling.
