Astragalus ceratoides var. montanus

Astragalus ceratoides var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, dry pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
