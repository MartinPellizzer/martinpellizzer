Aeschynanthus monetarius

Aeschynanthus monetarius, commonly known as the silver lace vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aeschynanthus monetarius is also valued for its ability to produce fragrant blooms, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
