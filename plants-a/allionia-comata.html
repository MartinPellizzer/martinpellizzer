Allionia comata

Allionia comata, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Allionia comata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
