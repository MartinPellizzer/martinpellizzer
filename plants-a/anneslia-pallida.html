Anneslia pallida

Anneslia pallida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its pale blue to white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Anneslia pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
