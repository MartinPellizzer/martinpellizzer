Abelmoschus divaricatus

Abelmoschus divaricatus, commonly known as the Chinese okra or African okra, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible pods, which are used in various culinary traditions.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and floral display.
