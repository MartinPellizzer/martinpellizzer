Aira tremula

Aira tremula, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, blade-like, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Aira tremula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
