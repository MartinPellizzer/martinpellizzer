Arctium majus subsp. nemorosum

Arctium majus subsp.

nemorosum, commonly known as the field thistle or common hogweed, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, and it is known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
