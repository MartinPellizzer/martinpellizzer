Allium taiwanianum

Allium taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
