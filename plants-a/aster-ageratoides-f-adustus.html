Aster ageratoides f. adustus

Aster ageratoides f. adustus, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and marshes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
