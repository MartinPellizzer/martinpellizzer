Arum trilobatum

Arum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a deep green color. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate parts of the spadix.

Arum trilobatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
