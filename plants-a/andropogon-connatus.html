Andropogon connatus

Andropogon connatus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Andropogon connatus is often found in association with other wetland plants, playing a role in maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
