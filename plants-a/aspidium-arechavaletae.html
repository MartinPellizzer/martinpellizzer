Aspidium arechavaletae

Aspidium arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's water fern, is a species of polypodiales fern native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aspidium arechavaletae plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
