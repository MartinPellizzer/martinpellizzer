Alchemilla kosiarensis

Alchemilla kosiarensis, commonly known as the Kosiarensis lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, translucent glands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Alchemilla kosiarensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
