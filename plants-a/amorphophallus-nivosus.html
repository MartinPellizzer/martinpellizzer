Amorphophallus nivosus

Amorphophallus nivosus, commonly known as the snow umbrella, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, umbrella-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces a single, spadix-type inflorescence that is often surrounded by a showy, white spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
