Armeria maritima subsp. purpurea

Armeria maritima subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of western North America. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces dense clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
