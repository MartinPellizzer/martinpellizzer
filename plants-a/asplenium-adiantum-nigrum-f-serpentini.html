Asplenium adiantum-nigrum f. serpentini

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum f. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that resemble the tail of a serpent.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a finely serrated margin that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its graceful appearance and adaptability.
