Ambrosina bassii var. maculata

Ambrosina bassii var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted ambrosia.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The species features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value.
