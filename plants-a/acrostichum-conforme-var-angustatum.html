Acrostichum conforme var. angustatum

Acrostichum conforme var. angustatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf brake fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf brake fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fern with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing fronds that are typically narrow and strap-like. The plant features a dense, evergreen foliage that thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to humid, low-light conditions.
