Aucuba albopunctifolia var. albopunctifolia

Aucuba albopunctifolia var. albopunctifolia, commonly known as the white-spotted aucuba, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are adorned with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a specimen plant in shaded landscapes.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its unique foliage and potential ornamental appeal.
