Astragalus douglasii var. parishii

Astragalus douglasii var. parishii, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Its flowers are often visited by native pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
