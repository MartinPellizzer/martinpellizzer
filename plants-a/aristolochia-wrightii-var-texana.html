Aristolochia wrightii var. texana

Aristolochia wrightii var. texana, commonly known as the Texas pipevine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to the southeastern United States.

The species is known for its chemical compounds, some of which are used in traditional medicine.
