Asyneuma virgatum subsp. virgatum

Asyneuma virgatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the slender leaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with proper conditions.
