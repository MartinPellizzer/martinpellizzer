Alternanthera piptantha

Alternanthera piptantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves that often display a variegated coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
