Athamanta alata

Athamanta alata, commonly known as the winged athamanta, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Athamanta alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
