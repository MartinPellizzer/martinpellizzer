Acanthopanax stenophyllus f. dilatatus

Acanthopanax stenophyllus f. dilatatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed Chinese angel's trumpet, is a variant of the Chinese angel's trumpet plant.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often slightly lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by red berries. It is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
