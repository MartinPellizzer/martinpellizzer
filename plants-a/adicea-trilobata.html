Adicea trilobata

Adicea trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, trifoliate leaves that are usually lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

Adicea trilobata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
