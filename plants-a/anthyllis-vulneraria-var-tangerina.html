Anthyllis vulneraria var. tangerina

Anthyllis vulneraria var. tangerina, commonly known as the Tangerina bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its common name reflects its distinctive appearance, resembling a bird's foot with its clusters of small leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
