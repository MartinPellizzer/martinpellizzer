Annona spinescens

Annona spinescens, commonly known as the spiny custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a spiny exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp inside. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
