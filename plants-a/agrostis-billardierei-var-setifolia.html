Agrostis billardierei var. setifolia

Agrostis billardierei var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and supporting local wildlife.
