Alyssum obovatum var. microphyllum

Alyssum obovatum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its compact growth habit and profuse flowering.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry, sunny environments.
