Alsophila gastonyi

Alsophila gastonyi, commonly known as the Giant Tree Fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it one of the tallest tree ferns. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically light green and contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
