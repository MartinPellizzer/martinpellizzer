Amauropelta muscicola

It is characterized by its compact, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures, giving it its common name. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spores for propagation.

Amauropelta muscicola is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
