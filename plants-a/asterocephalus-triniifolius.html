Asterocephalus triniifolius

Asterocephalus triniifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved asterhead, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
