Armeria filicaulis var. longifolia

Armeria filicaulis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often longer than those of the typical variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
