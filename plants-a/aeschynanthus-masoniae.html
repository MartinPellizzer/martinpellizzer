Aeschynanthus masoniae

Aeschynanthus masoniae, commonly known as the Mason's jewel vine, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a trailing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Aeschynanthus masoniae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
