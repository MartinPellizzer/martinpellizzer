Abutilon julianae

Abutilon julianae, commonly known as the Julian's abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
