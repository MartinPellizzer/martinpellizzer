Acanthus villaeanus

Acanthus villaeanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Acanthus villaeanus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.

It is also a popular choice for garden design due to its architectural form and long-lasting foliage.
