Allium roseum subsp. gulekense

Allium roseum subsp.

gulekense, commonly known as the Kashmiri chive, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional cuisine for their mild onion-like flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is well adapted to mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other Allium species.
