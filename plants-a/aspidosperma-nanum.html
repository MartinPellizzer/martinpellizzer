Aspidosperma nanum

Aspidosperma nanum, commonly known as the dwarf aspidosperma, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a species of conservation concern.
