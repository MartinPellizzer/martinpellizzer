Abutilon hulseanum

Abutilon hulseanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
