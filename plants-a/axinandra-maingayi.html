Axinandra maingayi

Axinandra maingayi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Axinandra maingayi is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
