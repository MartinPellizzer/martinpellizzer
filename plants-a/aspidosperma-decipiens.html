Aspidosperma decipiens

Aspidosperma decipiens, commonly known as the false yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
