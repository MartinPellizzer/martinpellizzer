Adiantum galeottianum

Adiantum galeottianum, commonly known as the Galeotti's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often glossy and slightly curled. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, which contributes to its ornamental value.

Adiantum galeottianum is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
