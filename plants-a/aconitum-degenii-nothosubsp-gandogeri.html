Aconitum degenii nothosubsp. gandogeri

Aconitum degenii nothosubsp.

gandogeri, commonly known as the Gandogeri monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly in suitable habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
