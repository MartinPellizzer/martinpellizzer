Avellinia tenuicula

Avellinia tenuicula, commonly known as the slender acorn bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Avellinia tenuicula plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
