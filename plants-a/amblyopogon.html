Amblyopogon

Amblyopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, which are often spike-like in structure. Amblyopogon species are native to various regions of Africa and are often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
