Aesculus hippocastanum f. pyramidalis

Aesculus hippocastanum f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the columnar horse chestnut, is a compact, upright form of the common horse chestnut.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, pyramidal shape, giving the plant a striking, tree-like appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing shiny, orange-red seeds.

This cultivar is often used in urban landscaping due to its elegant form and ornamental value.
