Acacia undulifolia var. piligera

Acacia undulifolia var. piligera, commonly known as the woolly wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has finely serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
