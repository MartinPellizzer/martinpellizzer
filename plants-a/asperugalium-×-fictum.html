Asperugalium × fictum

Asperugalium × fictum, commonly known as the hybrid asperugalium, is a unique plant resulting from the cross between Aspergillus and a fungal species.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with a complex morphology that includes a fibrous, spongy structure. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments where it can thrive on decaying organic matter. Its growth pattern is slow and deliberate, often forming dense clusters over time.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not commonly cultivated but is of interest to mycologists and botanists.
