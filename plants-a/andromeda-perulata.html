Andromeda perulata

Andromeda perulata, commonly known as the dwarf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing evergreen coverage. Its floral structure is typical of the Ericaceae family, with showy petals and prominent stamens.

Andromeda perulata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
