Adelobotrys intonsus

Adelobotrys intonsus, commonly known as the slender false ivy, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
