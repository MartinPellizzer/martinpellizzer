Acetosa scutata

Acetosa scutata, commonly known as the shieldleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features succulent, shield-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. Acetosa scutata is often found in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and in meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
