Astragalus hamosus f. falcatus

Astragalus hamosus f. falcatus, commonly known as the curlyleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with the terminal leaflet being notably curled or falcate, giving the plant its distinguishing feature. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.
