Andryala arenaria subsp. parvipila

Andryala arenaria subsp.

parvipila, commonly known as the small-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It typically forms dense mats of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. It is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.

Its botanical features make it an important component of xerophytic ecosystems.
