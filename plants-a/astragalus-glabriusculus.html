Astragalus glabriusculus

Astragalus glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus glabriusculus is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
