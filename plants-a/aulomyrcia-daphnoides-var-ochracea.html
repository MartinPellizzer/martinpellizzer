Aulomyrcia daphnoides var. ochracea

Aulomyrcia daphnoides var. ochracea, commonly known as the yellow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that exhibit a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
