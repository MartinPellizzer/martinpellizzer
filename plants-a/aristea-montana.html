Aristea montana

Aristea montana, commonly known as the mountain aristea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Aristea montana is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
