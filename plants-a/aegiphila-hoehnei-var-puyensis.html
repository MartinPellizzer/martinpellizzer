Aegiphila hoehnei var. puyensis

Aegiphila hoehnei var. puyensis, commonly known as Puyaninha, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings.
