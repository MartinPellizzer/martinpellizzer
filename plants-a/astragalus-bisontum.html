Astragalus bisontum

Astragalus bisontum, commonly known as the bison milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the grasslands of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and disturbed soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its historical significance includes use by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
