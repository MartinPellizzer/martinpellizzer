Alchemilla gracilis var. granvikii

Alchemilla gracilis var. granvikii, commonly known as the slender alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy choice for gardeners seeking a unique, native plant.
