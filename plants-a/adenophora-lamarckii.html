Adenophora lamarckii

Adenophora lamarckii, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora lamarckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
