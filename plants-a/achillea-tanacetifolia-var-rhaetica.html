Achillea tanacetifolia var. rhaetica

Achillea tanacetifolia var. rhaetica, commonly known as Rhaetian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in alpine gardens and rockeries.
