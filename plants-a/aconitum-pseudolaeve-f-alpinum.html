Aconitum pseudolaeve f. alpinum

Aconitum pseudolaeve f. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
