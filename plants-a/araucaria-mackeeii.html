Araucaria mackeeii

Araucaria mackeeii, commonly known as the Mackee's araucaria, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude regions of central Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar trunk and dense, feather-like foliage that forms a broad, rounded crown. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red aril, which is an adaptation for animal dispersal. Its slow growth rate and limited geographic range make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, Araucaria mackeeii is considered a living fossil and a key species in the study of conifer evolution.
