Anthemis marschalliana subsp. pectinata

Anthemis marschalliana subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the woolly anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its subspecies pectinata is distinguished by its more pronounced pectinate leaves, which are arranged in a rosette form.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
