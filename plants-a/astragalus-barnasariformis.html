Astragalus barnasariformis

Astragalus barnasariformis, commonly known as the Barnasari's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
