Anaphalis margaritacea var. revoluta

Anaphalis margaritacea var. revoluta, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
