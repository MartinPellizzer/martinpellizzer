Anthericum torreyi var. lanceolatum

Anthericum torreyi var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spadix and a spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
