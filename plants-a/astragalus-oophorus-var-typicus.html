Astragalus oophorus var. typicus

Astragalus oophorus var. typicus, commonly known as the "eggplant milkvetch," is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
