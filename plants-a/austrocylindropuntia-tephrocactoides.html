Austrocylindropuntia tephrocactoides

Austrocylindropuntia tephrocactoides, commonly known as the Desert Cylindropuntia, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in spring, and it produces small, edible fruits.

This species is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
