Archeria combrei

Archeria combrei, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The species is well-suited for cultivation in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
