Achyranthes longifolia

Achyranthes longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf achyranthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Achyranthes longifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
