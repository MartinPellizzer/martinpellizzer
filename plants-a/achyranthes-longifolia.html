Achyranthes longifolia

Achyranthes longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf achyranthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Achyranthes longifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in loose clusters, blooming from June to August, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant anchor in sandy or loamy soils.

Native Distribution

Achyranthes longifolia is primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia.
