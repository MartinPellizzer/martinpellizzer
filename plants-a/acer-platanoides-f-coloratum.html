Acer platanoides f. coloratum

Acer platanoides f. coloratum, commonly known as the white maple or coloratum maple, is a variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale green leaves, which are often tinged with red or pink on the edges. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its striking foliage and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
