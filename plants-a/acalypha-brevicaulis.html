Acalypha brevicaulis

Acalypha brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically inconspicuous and borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.

Acalypha brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
