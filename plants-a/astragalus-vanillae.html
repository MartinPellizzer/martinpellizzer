Astragalus vanillae

Astragalus vanillae, commonly known as vanilla milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
