Apteranthes tessellata

Apteranthes tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Apteranthes tessellata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
