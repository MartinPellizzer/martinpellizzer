Alpinia vitellina var. cannifolia

Alpinia vitellina var. cannifolia, commonly known as the banana ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow or white flowers, followed by edible, banana-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing characteristics with other gingers.
