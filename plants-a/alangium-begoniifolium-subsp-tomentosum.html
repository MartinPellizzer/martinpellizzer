Alangium begoniifolium subsp. tomentosum

Alangium begoniifolium subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the tomentose alangium, is a member of the Schisandraceae family. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in an alternate pattern.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and bear a dense covering of fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant its tomentose appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
