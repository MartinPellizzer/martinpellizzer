Acanthonychia ramosissima var. rosetta

Acanthonychia ramosissima var. rosetta, commonly known as the Rosetta thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and sharp, spiny leaves that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to reduce water loss in dry environments.

The Rosetta thornbush is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental appearance.
