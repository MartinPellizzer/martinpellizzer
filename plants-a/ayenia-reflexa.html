Ayenia reflexa

Ayenia reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of white to pink.

Ayenia reflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
