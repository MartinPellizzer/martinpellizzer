Armeria atlantica subvar. major

Armeria atlantica subvar. major, commonly known as the Atlantic thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This hardy species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
