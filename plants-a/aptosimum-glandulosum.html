Aptosimum glandulosum

Aptosimum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, often hairy leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant produces tiny, greenish to brownish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

Aptosimum glandulosum is adapted to drought conditions, with succulent stems and leaves that store water efficiently.
