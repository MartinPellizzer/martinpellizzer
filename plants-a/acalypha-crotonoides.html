Acalypha crotonoides

Acalypha crotonoides, commonly known as the redroot croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange roots and its palmate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
