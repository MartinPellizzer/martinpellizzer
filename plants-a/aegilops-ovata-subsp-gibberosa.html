Aegilops ovata subsp. gibberosa

Aegilops ovata subsp.

gibberosa, commonly known as the woolly wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and hairy, oval-shaped spikelets.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential as a forage crop and its genetic relationship to wheat.
