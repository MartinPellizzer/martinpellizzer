Arisaema negishii

Arisaema negishii, commonly known as the Negishi arisaema, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of arrow-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
