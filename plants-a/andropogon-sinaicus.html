Andropogon sinaicus

Andropogon sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Israel and Egypt.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Andropogon sinaicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
