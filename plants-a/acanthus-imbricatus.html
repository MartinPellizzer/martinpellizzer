Acanthus imbricatus

Acanthus imbricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves with a distinctive ribbed structure and its showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Acanthus imbricatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and historical use in classical architecture and decorative arts.
