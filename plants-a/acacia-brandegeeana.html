Acacia brandegeeana

Acacia brandegeeana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a vibrant red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces long, yellow to orange flower spikes that bloom in the summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and as a street tree in warmer regions.
