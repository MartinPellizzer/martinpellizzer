Astragalus albertshoferi

Astragalus albertshoferi, commonly known as the Albertshofer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus albertshoferi is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
