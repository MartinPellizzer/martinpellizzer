Aspidosperma velutinum

Aspidosperma velutinum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
