Avena malzevii

Avena malzevii, commonly known as the slender oat, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves, which are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
