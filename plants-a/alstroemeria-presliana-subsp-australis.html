Alstroemeria presliana subsp. australis

Alstroemeria presliana subsp.

australis, commonly known as the New Zealand alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability have made it a valued species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
