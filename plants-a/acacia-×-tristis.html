Acacia × tristis

Acacia × tristis, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acacia aneura and Acacia longifolia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

Due to its unique form and adaptability, it is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
