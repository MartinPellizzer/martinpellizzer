Anathallis soratana

Anathallis soratana, commonly known as the purple-flowered anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet petals and a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak, which is an adaptation for pollination by specific insects. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are ephemeral, blooming for only a short period, which makes it a rare sight in the wild.

Anathallis soratana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
