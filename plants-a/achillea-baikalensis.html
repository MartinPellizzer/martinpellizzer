Achillea baikalensis

Achillea baikalensis, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its resilience to cold climates makes it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
