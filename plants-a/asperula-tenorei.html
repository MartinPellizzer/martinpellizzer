Asperula tenorei

Asperula tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink to red blossoms that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula tenorei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticultural displays.
