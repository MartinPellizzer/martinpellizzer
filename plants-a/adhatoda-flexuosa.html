Adhatoda flexuosa

Adhatoda flexuosa, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, including vasicine and ajmalicine, which have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable botanical species.
