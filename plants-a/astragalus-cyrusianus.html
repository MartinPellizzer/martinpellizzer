Astragalus cyrusianus

Astragalus cyrusianus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Astragalus cyrusianus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
