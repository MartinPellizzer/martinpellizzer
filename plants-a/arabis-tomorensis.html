Arabis tomorensis

Arabis tomorensis, commonly known as the Toomer's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many well-known edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
