Anerincleistus pauciflorus var. pauciflorus

Anerincleistus pauciflorus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the rare mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its habitat. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
