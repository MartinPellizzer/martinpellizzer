Artemisia jacquinii

Artemisia jacquinii, commonly known as the silver dust sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
