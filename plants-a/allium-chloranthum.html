Allium chloranthum

Allium chloranthum, commonly known as the green garlic or garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium chloranthum is valued both for its edible parts and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
