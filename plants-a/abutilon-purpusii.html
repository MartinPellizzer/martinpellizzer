Abutilon purpusii

Abutilon purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both native and cultivated landscapes.
