Aster tenellus var. robustus

Aster tenellus var. robustus, commonly known as the robust aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy surface, and it prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
