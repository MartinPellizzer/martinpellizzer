Amaranthus hierichuntinus

Amaranthus hierichuntinus, commonly known as desert amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, branched stems and clusters of small, often red or green flowers. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable food source. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Its ability to grow in harsh environments makes it an important plant in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
