Astragalus pendulipodus

Astragalus pendulipodus, commonly known as the hanging milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pendulipodus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
