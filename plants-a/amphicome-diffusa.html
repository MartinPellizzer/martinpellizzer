Amphicome diffusa

Amphicome diffusa, commonly known as the water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its feathery, branching leaves that resemble the structure of clover leaves.

It thrives in freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and marshes. The plant's leaves are thin and delicate, allowing for efficient light absorption and gas exchange in water. Amphicome diffusa is also notable for its ability to absorb heavy metals from its surroundings, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.

Its reproductive system involves the production of small, floating fruits that disperse via water currents, aiding in its spread.
