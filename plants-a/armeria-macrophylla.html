Armeria macrophylla

Armeria macrophylla, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its salt tolerance allows it to flourish in harsh maritime environments.

Armeria macrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
