Alchemilla indica var. sibthorpioides

Alchemilla indica var. sibthorpioides, commonly known as sibthorpioid lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features dense rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its common name reflects its historical use in folk medicine and its ornamental value in gardens.
