Achillea barrelieri subsp. mucronulata

Achillea barrelieri subsp.

mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear basal leaves with finely toothed margins.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
